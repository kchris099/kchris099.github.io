Destination Guide — Reykjavik

Explore Reykjavik

A Brief History

Believed to be the first permanent settlement in Iceland founded in 874, Reykjavik remained a small fishing and trading village until the 18th century. It rapidly industrialized in the 20th century, growing into a modern Nordic capital fiercely independent in its culture. Today, this complex historical evolution remains significantly embedded in the civic architecture and vibrant daily life of the community.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

Hallgrimskirkja

Hallgrimskirkja, a Lutheran church located in the heart of Reykjavik, is an architectural marvel that beautifully blends modern design with Icelandic tradition. Completed in 1986 after nearly five decades of construction, this structure stands at an impressive height of 74.5 meters (244 feet), making it the tallest church in Iceland.

Reservation required

The Reykjavik Food Walk

The Reykjavik Food Walk is a very rated culinary tour that guides visitors through the historic streets of Iceland's capital. Led by knowledgeable local guides, the tour features stops at various restaurants and food stalls to sample authentic Icelandic cuisine, including fresh seafood, traditional lamb dishes, and local delicacies.

Free

Sun Voyager

Sun Voyager is a stainless-steel sculpture located on the granite shores of Reykjavik, Iceland. Created by Jón Gunnar Árnason, it resembles a Viking longship and was unveiled in 1990 to celebrate the city's 200-year anniversary. Despite its appearance, the sculpture is actually an ode to the sun, symbolizing hope and light in a place with long hours of winter darkness.

Free

Höfði House

Höfði House, built in 1909 and once the residence of Icelandic poet Einar Benediktsson, holds historical significance as the site of a crucial political summit. While many tourists are drawn to the nearby Sun Voyager sculpture, a stroll along the sea towards Höfði House offers its own charm. This area is popular among locals for running and cycling.

1500 ISK

Laugardalslaug

Nestled in the vibrant Laugardalur area of Reykjavik, Laugardalslaug is a must-visit destination for both locals and travelers alike. This expansive outdoor pool complex boasts year-round heated pools, including an impressive Olympic-sized swimming pool and several smaller options tailored for children. The facility features a delightful array of hot tubs with varying temperatures, perfect for relaxation after a swim or workout.

3300 ISK

National Museum of Iceland

The National Museum of Iceland offers a captivating journey through the country's history and culture, showcasing artifacts from the Settlement Age to modern times. Visitors can explore exhibits that provide a comprehensive overview, with a particular focus on the Settlement Era, including chieftains' rule and the advent of Christianity. Notable highlights include swords, drinking horns, silver hoards, and a striking bronze figure of Thor.

13990 ISK

Sky Lagoon

Sky Lagoon is a new and magical geothermal spa located in Karsnes Harbour, Kopavogur, near Reykjavik. Despite its close proximity to the city center, it offers a remote hideaway experience with all the elements of a luxury spa retreat. The themed meeting rooms provide unique views and atmospheres, such as overlooking the Eiffel Tower or relaxing in a cozy ski lodge.

7090 ISK

Perlan

Perlan is a prominent landmark and museum in Reykjavik, situated on Öskjuhlíð hill. The striking glass dome structure, built over six massive water tanks, houses the 'Wonders of Iceland' exhibition. Visitors can explore a realistic indoor ice cave, a planetarium showing the Northern Lights, and an observation deck offering 360-degree views of the city.

6590 ISK

Lava Show

The Icelandic Lava Show in Reykjavik provides a unique, safe opportunity to experience the intense heat and power of flowing lava up close. The educational exhibition melts real volcanic rock and pours it into a showroom, allowing visitors to feel the heat, hear the crackling, and learn about Iceland's volatile geological environment.

5690 ISK

FlyOver Iceland

FlyOver Iceland is a cutting-edge entertainment venue located in the vibrant Grandi harbour district of Reykjavik. It offers a unique experience where visitors are seated in motion seats and surrounded by a wraparound screen, creating the sensation of flying over Iceland's landscapes. The simulation includes natural wonders such as lava plains, geysers, mountain tops, and fjords. Special effects like wind, mist, and scents enhance the immersive experience.
