Destination Guide — Plovdiv

Explore Plovdiv

A Brief History

Continuously inhabited for over 8,000 years, Plovdiv flourished under Thracian, Roman, and Ottoman rule. Its magnificent Roman theater and preserved National Revival houses trace the evolution of a vital crossroads city that bridged ancient empires and modern Bulgaria. Decades of careful preservation have ensured that this profound historical narrative remains a vital part of the modern experience.

Top Sights & Activities

Monument of the Red Army "Alyosha"

The Monument of the Red Army, commonly known as Alyosha, is a massive 11-meter tall reinforced concrete statue of a Soviet soldier standing on Bunardzhik Hill in Plovdiv. Erected in the 1950s, it commemorates the Soviet casualties during the liberation of Bulgaria in World War II. The monument dominates the city skyline and offers expansive panoramic views of Plovdiv from its base.

Free

"Bunardzhika" Hill

"Bunardzhika" Hill is a documented natural feature in Plovdiv, Bulgaria, listed in regional geographic and conservation records for its geological formation and protected status. The site is accessible via marked trails and is open to visitors during daylight hours. Travellers follow the principal path from the trailhead and combine the visit with nearby viewpoints and forest walks.

Free (3D film 6 BGN extra)

Ancient Stadium of Philipopolis

Nestled in the heart of Plovdiv, Bulgaria, the Ancient Stadium of Philipopolis is a remarkable testament to Roman engineering and history. Dating back to the 2nd century AD, this impressive structure was designed in a horseshoe shape and could host an astonishing 30,000 spectators for various sporting events, including athletic competitions and gladiatorial contests.

Free

Sveta Marina church

Sveta Marina Church, located just off Knyaz Alexander Street, is a historic Orthodox Christian church with a wooden bell tower reminiscent of a Chinese Pagoda. The late 19th-century church features a blend of Bulgarian and Byzantine architectural styles, adorned with intricate carvings on the outside and colorful frescoes depicting scenes from the life of Saint Marina inside. This ancient church has been destroyed and rebuilt multiple times over the centuries.

7 BGN

Ancient Theatre of Philippopolis

Ancient Theatre of Philippopolis is a documented performance venue in Plovdiv, Bulgaria, recognised in regional cultural listings for its programming and architectural significance. The venue hosts scheduled performances and admits visitors during box-office hours. Travellers approach from the surrounding cultural district and combine the visit with nearby galleries and historic theatres.

Free

Old Town of Plovdiv

The Old Town of Plovdiv is a very preserved historic district situated on three of the city's hills. It is renowned for its winding cobblestone streets and distinctive Bulgarian National Revival architecture, featuring colorful, richly decorated houses. The area also contains significant ancient ruins, including the Roman theater, reflecting the city's multi-layered history spanning thousands of years.

Free

Singing Fountains

The Singing Fountains in Plovdiv are a captivating sight to behold. Nestled in Tsar Simeons Garden, this attraction features synchronized lights and water jets on a picturesque lake, creating a mesmerizing display. Visitors can bask in the beauty of nature, explore hiking trails, and delight in children's playgrounds before being enchanted by the fountains' choreographed performances set to music.

Free

“Tsar Simeon Garden” Park

Tsar Simeon Garden, established in 1892 for the First International Fair in Bulgaria, is a picturesque and expansive park located at the heart of Plovdiv city center. This historic green space offers a tranquil retreat from the city's heat and hosts various social and cultural events. The park features century-old trees, vibrant flowerbeds, and charming water fountains.

Free

Roman Forum of Philippopolis

The Roman Forum of Philippopolis, located at the opposite end of Knyaz Alexander Street, is an impressive collection of ancient ruins dating back to the 1st century AD. Covering a vast area of 20 hectares, with 11 hectares already excavated, it stands as the largest Roman forum in Bulgaria.

Free

Monument of Unification

The Monument of Unification in Plovdiv commemorates the 1885 unification of the Principality of Bulgaria with the province of Eastern Rumelia. The striking bronze statue depicts a young woman holding a laurel wreath, symbolizing peace and unity. Located in Saedinenie Square, the monument is a focal point for national celebrations and a symbol of Bulgarian independence and solidarity.
