Plovdiv

Settlement on the three hills predates Rome; Thracian, Roman, and Ottoman rulers each left theatres, mosques, and merchant houses. Nineteenth-century National Revival mansions fill the old town above the Maritsa. Plovdiv ranks among Europe's longest-inhabited urban sites, a claim of continuous occupation rather than a single founding myth. The Roman theatre hosts performances again above the modern tram streets.

Monument of the Red Army "Alyosha"

An eleven-metre Soviet soldier stands on Bunardzhik Hill above Plovdiv at the monument commonly called Alyosha. Erected in the 1950s, it commemorates the Soviet army's role in Bulgaria during the Second World War and remains politically contested; broad steps and terraces lead to the base, where the city, Rhodope foothills, and surrounding plain spread below.

"Bunardzhika" Hill

Bunardzhika Hill, also called the Hill of the Liberators, rises above Plovdiv with monuments and walking paths. Trails climb to viewpoints over the Thracian Plain and the city's tiled roofs. A large Soviet-era soldier statue crowns one of the summits used for evening walks. Evening light turns the soldier monument into a silhouette against the plain.

Ancient Stadium of Philipopolis

The Ancient Stadium of Philipopolis lies partly under Plovdiv's main street, with excavated marble seating visible at street level. Built in the Roman period for the city then called Philippopolis, it once held thousands for races and games. Visitors view the exposed curve of seats from the pedestrian zone without a modern stadium bowl above.

42.1476° N / 24.7481° E

04 AdmissionFree

Sveta Marina church

Saint Marina Church sits just off Plovdiv's main pedestrian street within the historic metropolitan complex. The present nineteenth-century building contains a richly carved iconostasis, painted walls, galleries, and an enclosed courtyard. A separate wooden bell tower rises in tiered stages beside the church, giving the site a distinctive profile among central shops and offices.

42.1462° N / 24.7504° E

Ancient Theatre of Philippopolis

Roman seating at the Ancient Theatre of Philippopolis still hosts scheduled performances in Plovdiv's old town, with box-office hours for admissions. Approaches come from the surrounding cultural district and nearby historic streets. Stone cavea and stage wall remain the clear archaeological frame.

42.1472° N / 24.7512° E

06 AdmissionFree

Old Town of Plovdiv

The Old Town of Plovdiv is a very preserved historic district situated on three of the city's hills. It is known for its winding cobblestone streets and distinctive Bulgarian National Revival architecture, featuring colorful, richly decorated houses. The area also contains significant ancient ruins, including the Roman theater, reflecting the city's multi-layered history spanning thousands of years.

42.1500° N / 24.7526° E

07 AdmissionFree

Singing Fountains

Plovdiv's Singing Fountains combine choreographed water jets with light and music in a public park setting. Playgrounds and paths around the basin fill before evening shows start. Families treat the fountain plaza as both daytime green space and nighttime spectacle. Seating rings the basin so families can watch without standing the whole show.

42.1406° N / 24.7460° E

08 AdmissionFree

“Tsar Simeon Garden” Park

Tsar Simeon Garden is Plovdiv's central city park, laid out in the late nineteenth century with paths, lakes, and flower beds. The Singing Fountains and lawn areas draw evening walkers from the nearby pedestrian streets. Named for medieval ruler Tsar Simeon, the park remains the principal green square of the modern city center.

42.1419° N / 24.7472° E

09 AdmissionFree

Roman Forum of Philippopolis

The Roman forum of Philippopolis occupied the administrative and commercial center of the ancient city from an early century onward. Excavated colonnades, paving, shops, an odeon, and public structures survive in separate sections around Plovdiv's modern central post office; street level cuts across the archaeological area, revealing how the Roman plan lies beneath the contemporary city.

42.1426° N / 24.7495° E

10 AdmissionFree

Youth Hill

Youth Hill is one of Plovdiv's syenite hills with forest paths and open clearings above the neighborhoods. Walkers and joggers use marked routes for exercise and wide city views. The wooded slopes stay cooler than the flat streets during summer heat. Picnic clearings appear where the trees thin near the ridge.

42.1369° N / 24.7311° E

Monument of Unification

The Monument of Unification in Plovdiv commemorates the 1885 unification of the Principality of Bulgaria with the province of Eastern Rumelia. The striking bronze statue depicts a young woman holding a laurel wreath, symbolizing peace and unity. Located in Saedinenie Square, the monument is a focal point for national celebrations and a symbol of Bulgarian independence and solidarity.

42.1513° N / 24.7445° E
