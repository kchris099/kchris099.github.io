Destination Guide — Veliko Tarnovo

Explore Veliko Tarnovo

A Brief History

Serving as the capital of the Second Bulgarian Empire in the 12th century, Veliko Tarnovo was a medieval powerhouse of Eastern Orthodox culture. The formidable Tsarevets fortress and dramatic river gorge topography reflect its enduring legacy as a symbol of Bulgarian sovereignty. Today, this complex historical evolution remains significantly embedded in the civic architecture and vibrant daily life of the community.

Top Sights & Activities

Monument of Mother Bulgaria

The Monument of Mother Bulgaria, located in the center of Veliko Tarnovo, is a tribute to fallen warriors from five wars. Unveiled in 1935, it was funded by donations from the local population. Situated near attractions like Asen's Monument and the State Art Gallery 'Boris Denev,' this spot offers a great view of the city's architecture and nature.

Free

Monument to the Assen Dynasty

In the heart of Veliko Tarnovo stands the magnificent Monument to the Assen Dynasty, a tribute to the kings Assen, Peter, Kaloyan and Ivan Asen II. Erected in 1985 to mark the 800th anniversary of their rebellion, this towering obelisk with statues of the four kings on horseback is a symbol of Bulgaria's rich history.

Free

Samovodska charshia market

Samovodska Charshia market, also known as Old Town, is a charming historic street located in the heart of Veliko Tarnovo, Bulgaria. This vibrant area is packed with traditional craft shops and souvenir stores offering handmade items such as woodcarvings, charms, and jewelry. Visitors can immerse themselves in the old-world atmosphere while exploring the unique offerings of this authentic market.

Tsarevets Fortress

Tsarevets Fortress, perched on a hill overlooking Veliko Tarnovo, is a renowned medieval stronghold that offers a glimpse into Bulgaria's rich history. Dating back to the 12th century, it served as the primary fortress of the Second Bulgarian Empire and played a pivotal role in shaping the nation's destiny. The fortress boasts robust stone walls that once witnessed medieval power struggles and is surrounded by historical treasures such as Orthodox temples and residential houses.

BGN 10

Holy Forty Martyrs Church

Nestled in the historic city of Veliko Tarnovo, the Holy Forty Martyrs Church stands as a testament to Bulgaria's rich medieval heritage. Constructed in 1230 by Tsar Ivan Asen II, this remarkable church commemorates his significant victory at the Klokotnitsa Battle against Byzantine forces. The battle marked a pivotal moment for Bulgaria, establishing it as a dominant power in the Balkans during that era.

BGN 10

Trapezitsa Fortress

Nestled atop the scenic Trapezitsa Hill, the Trapezitsa Fortress is a remarkable medieval site that offers a glimpse into Bulgaria's rich history. This impressive fortress, strategically located on the banks of the Yantra River, features ruins, including a tower and an ancient church adorned with beautiful frescoes.

BGN 15

Baldwin's Tower

When visiting the historic Tsarevets Fortress, consider include a stop at the Baldwin Tower. Perched at one of the fortress's farthest points, this ancient structure offers panoramic views of the surrounding cityscape and nearby attractions like Mini Bulgaria. The tower is not just a visual delight; it also holds fascinating historical significance that adds depth to your exploration.

Free

Hotnitsa Waterfall

Hotnitsa Waterfall, also known as Kaya Bunar, is a natural wonder located 15 kilometers from Veliko Tarnovo. The area features wooden walkways that wind through a gorge and lead to a cascade flowing into a picturesque blue-green pool. The eco-trail offers shade from the forested surroundings, making it an ideal spot for a leisurely hour-and-a-half walk.

Free

"Tsar Asen I" Square

Tsar Asen I Square is a prominent public space located in front of the Tsarevets Fortress in Veliko Tarnovo. It serves as the main approach to the historic stronghold and is often the gathering point for visitors attending the dramatic Sound and Light show projected onto the fortress walls. The square provides an excellent vantage point for viewing the medieval architecture and the surrounding landscape.

VIP Hall and room Sound and Light

VIP Hall and room Sound and Light is a show that takes place in Veliko Tarnovo, Bulgaria. It is usually performed on public holidays or by private request for a fee. The show features amazing music and lights, creating a magical experience that is enjoyed without distractions like phone usage. Visitors describe the show as magnificent, with the music complementing the light display.

Free

Monastery of the Holy Transfiguration of God

Nestled in the countryside, the Monastery of the Holy Transfiguration of God is a 19th-century marvel adorned with vibrant frescoes depicting biblical scenes. Visitors to Veliko Tarnovo can explore this sacred site along with other attractions such as Tsarevets Fortress, Holy Forty Martyrs Church, and Samovodska street crafts shops. A picturesque hike leads to this tranquil monastery, where travelers can admire the Circle of Life mural adorning the church's outer wall.

HillView Veliko Tarnovo - Off-road tours, city tours and rentals

HillView Veliko Tarnovo - Off-road tours, city tours and rentals is a documented natural feature in Veliko Tarnovo, Bulgaria, listed in regional geographic and conservation records for its geological formation and protected status. The site is accessible via marked trails and is open to visitors during daylight hours. Travellers follow the principal path from the trailhead and combine the visit with nearby viewpoints and forest walks.
