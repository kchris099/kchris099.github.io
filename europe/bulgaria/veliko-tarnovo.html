Veliko Tarnovo

Veliko Tarnovo was the capital of the Second Bulgarian Empire in the twelfth century and a major Eastern Orthodox centre. Tsarevets fortress and the Yantra river gorge still dominate the skyline from that medieval period. After Ottoman rule and nineteenth-century nation-building, museums and restored houses on the old hills keep the imperial and crafts record visible.

Monument of Mother Bulgaria

The Monument of Mother Bulgaria, located in the center of Veliko Tarnovo, is a tribute to fallen warriors from five wars. Unveiled in 1935, it was funded by donations from the local population. Situated near attractions like Asen's Monument and the State Art Gallery 'Boris Denev,' this spot offers a great view of the city's architecture and nature.

Monument to the Assen Dynasty

In Veliko Tarnovo stands the Monument to the Assen Dynasty, a tribute to the kings Assen, Peter, Kaloyan and Ivan Asen II. Erected in 1985 to mark the 800th anniversary of their rebellion, this towering obelisk with statues of the four kings on horseback is a symbol of Bulgaria's rich history.

Tsarevets Fortress

Tsarevets Hill held the palace, patriarchal complex, churches, houses, and defenses of Tarnovo when it was capital of the Second Bulgarian Empire. Reconstructed walls and gates trace the medieval citadel above the Yantra River. Paths climb through archaeological foundations to the patriarchal church, while evening sound-and-light programs use the fortress slopes as a large historical stage.

Holy Forty Martyrs Church

Tsar Ivan Asen II built the Church of the Holy Forty Martyrs in 1230 to commemorate victory at Klokotnitsa. Stone columns carry important medieval inscriptions, including texts linked to earlier Bulgarian rulers. The riverside church later passed through periods of conversion, damage, and restoration, and now preserves royal burials and evidence of Tarnovo's sacred and political life.

Trapezitsa Fortress

Trapezitsa Fortress crowns a hill opposite Tsarevets in Veliko Tarnovo, part of the medieval Bulgarian capital's twin strongholds. Excavated churches, walls, and terraces open when the site admits visitors along marked paths. From the ridge, views take in the Yantra bends and the restored Tsarevets complex across the river.

Baldwin's Tower

Baldwin's Tower stands at a far corner of Tsarevets Fortress in Veliko Tarnovo. Tradition links the tower to the captivity of Latin Emperor Baldwin after medieval battles. Rebuilt stonework and a steep approach make it a final stop on fortress circuits. From the battlements the Yantra loops and tiled roofs of the old town spread below.

Also called Kaya Bunar, Hotnitsa Waterfall sits at the end of a shaded eco-trail of about ninety minutes through forest near Veliko Tarnovo. Marked tracks lead to viewing platforms above the cascade. Tree cover keeps the walk cool on the approach to the fall.

"Tsar Asen I" Square

Tsar Asen I Square forms the broad approach to Tsarevets Fortress in Veliko Tarnovo. Open paving gathers pedestrians before the fortified gate and provides an unobstructed view of the walls, towers and hillside above the Yantra River. Evening sound-and-light programs use the square as a viewing area, projecting historical scenes across the medieval stronghold.

The Monastery of the Holy Transfiguration sits in a gorge near Veliko Tarnovo among wooded cliffs. Church frescoes and monastic buildings occupy a terrace above the river. Visitors often pair the monastery with Tsarevets and the old town's craft street. Cliff walls rise tightly around the terrace, isolating the courtyard from road noise.
