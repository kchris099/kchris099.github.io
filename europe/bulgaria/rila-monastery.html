Rila Monastery

Rila Monastery was founded in the 10th century by Saint John of Rila in the Rila Mountains south of Sofia. Rebuilt in the 19th century after fire, its striped arcades and frescoed church became a symbol of Bulgarian Orthodox culture during Ottoman rule. The site preserved manuscripts and religious art when national institutions were limited. UNESCO listed the monastery as a World Heritage site in 1983.

Rila Monastery

Founded in the 10th century by disciples of hermit Ivan of Rila, this monastery became a center of Bulgarian literacy and art under Ottoman rule. Striped arcades and frescoed church walls date largely from the 19th century. Set in the Rila Mountains, it remains an active religious community and UNESCO site.

Hrelyo's Tower

Hrelyo's Tower was built in the fourteenth century as a defensive refuge within the early Rila Monastery. Thick stone walls enclose stacked chambers and a small upper chapel decorated with medieval frescoes. The square tower survived fires and later rebuilding that replaced most surrounding structures, making it a historic standing element in the present monastic courtyard.

42.1334° N / 23.3403° E

The Cave of Saint John of Rila

Two kilometers from the monastery, this narrow cave is where Ivan of Rila lived as a hermit in the 10th century. Pilgrims climb steps to a chapel built against the rock face. The forest path and stream beside the site form part of a quiet walking route in the Rila range.
