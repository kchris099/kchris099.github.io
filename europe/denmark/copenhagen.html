Explore Copenhagen

A Brief History

Copenhagen was founded as a fishing village and grew after Bishop Absalon built a fortress on Slotsholmen in the 12th century. It became Denmark's capital under Christian IV, who added Renaissance buildings and canals in the 17th century. The 19th century brought neoclassical institutions and Tivoli Gardens. Neutral in both world wars, the city expanded with modernist housing and harbor redevelopment while retaining its medieval core.

Top Sights & Activities

Rosenborg Castle

Christian IV built this Dutch Renaissance summer residence in the early 1600s amid royal gardens north of the medieval core. It now displays crown jewels, coronation thrones, and royal regalia from the Danish monarchy. Towering hedges and flower beds surround the compact brick castle in Kongens Have.

Free

David Collection

Billionaire C.L. David's former townhouse holds Islamic art spanning from the 8th to 19th centuries, plus European 18th-century furniture and early modern painting. Free admission supports the founder's wish for public access. Galleries are arranged by region and dynasty across three floors near Nørreport.

Free (kr50)

Frederik's Church

Called the Marble Church for its Danish marble cladding, this neo-classical dome church completed in 1894 crowns the Frederiksstaden district. Its copper-green dome is among the largest church domes in northern Europe. Visitors can climb to the outer walkway for views toward Amalienborg and the harbour.

kr120

Amalienborg

Four identical rococo palace façades surround an octagonal courtyard where the Danish royal guard changes posts daily. Built in the 1750s for noble families, the complex became the monarchy's winter residence. One palace wing is open as a museum tracing recent royal history and ceremonial traditions.

kr90

Thorvaldsens Museum

Denmark's oldest purpose-built museum, opened in 1848 to house Bertel Thorvaldsen's neoclassical sculptures and personal collections. Colorful Pompeian-inspired interiors contrast with white marble figures. The building faces Christiansborg on Slotsholmen, near the artist's Copenhagen studio site.

Free

The Christiansborg Tower

At 106 meters, this baroque revival tower atop Christiansborg Palace is Copenhagen's tallest viewpoint. Free elevator access leads to an open platform over the rooftops. Below, the same complex houses parliament, the supreme court, and ruins of earlier castles on Slotsholmen island.
