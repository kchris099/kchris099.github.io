Destination Guide — Aarhus

Explore Aarhus

A Brief History

Founded by Vikings at the mouth of the Aarhus River in the 8th century, the settlement grew into a prominent medieval diocese. Industrialization and a bustling modern port later expanded the city, balancing its deep Viking roots with forward-looking urban development. The synthesis of these diverse historical influences has produced a notably distinct and resilient civic character.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

Musikhuset Aarhus

Nestled in the heart of Aarhus, Musikhuset Aarhus stands as Scandinavia's largest concert hall and a true cultural gem. This architectural marvel, designed by Kjaer & Richter and completed in 1982, boasts an impressive array of nine stages and six halls that cater to diverse musical tastes—from pop to opera, rock to musicals. Each year, it welcomes nearly a million guests for over 1,500 concerts and events.

Free

Ridehuset

Ridehuset is a large multi-purpose hall that hosts various events, including markets like the clothing market by Day. It's a popular spot for big events and has bike parking available nearby. The atmosphere is pleasant, making it a great place to purchase local products and enjoy the summer festival. Additionally, it's known for hosting lively 90s parties with great DJs. If you're in the area, consider check out Ridehuset and its vibrant offerings.

Free

Marselisborg Castle

Marselisborg Castle, also known as Marselisborg Slott, is a summer residence for the Danish Royal Family. Nestled in the Memorial Park with picturesque views of Marselisborg Forest, this over 100-year-old palace boasts English-style gardens, sprawling lawns, and beautiful lakes.

Free

The Infinite Bridge

'The Infinite Bridge' in Aarhus, also known as Den Uendelige Bro, offers a serene and intimate experience with its circular wooden pier that extends into the sea. Visitors can enjoy seaside views and a sense of solitude while taking a leisurely stroll along the bridge. Originally part of the Sculpture by the Sea festival in 2015, this art installation became so beloved by the locals that it was reconstructed as a permanent fixture.

Free

Marselisborg Deer Park

Marselisborg Deer Park is a 22-hectare nature preserve and zoo located south of Aarhus city. It features free-ranging wild boar and deer, including sika and fallow deer. The park is adjacent to the Marselisborg Forests, offering a peaceful retreat among flora, fauna, and wildlife. Visitors can explore the surrounding gardens and parklands of the impressive Marselisborg Palace, which has been the summer residence of Danish royalty since 1902.

Free

Tivoli Friheden

Tivoli Friheden is a family amusement park located around 2 km south of Aarhus city center, close to the woodlands of Marselisborg. The park offers traditional carousel and Ferris wheel rides, as well as 5D cinematic experiences and laser games. It's a perfect change of pace for children tired of historical landmarks and museums in Aarhus.

Free

Aarhus Cathedral

Aarhus Cathedral, a Gothic church dating back to the 12th century, is dedicated to Saint Clement and features fresco-covered arches. The city of Aarhus offers a plethora of attractions including ARoS - Aarhus Museum of Art, The Old Town open-air museum, Botanical Garden, Marselisborg Palace, Moesgaard Museum, Tivoli Friheden Theme Park and more.

Free

Aarhus Theatre

Aarhus Theatre, inaugurated in 1900, is a architectural gem designed by renowned architect Hack Kampmann. The decorative facade and lavish interiors make it a for architecture enthusiasts. Situated in the heart of the city center, this historic building hosts various performances throughout the year across its four different stages. Inside, visitors can admire intricate paintings and ceiling details while waiting for shows to start.

Free

Endless Connection

Endless Connection is a captivating fountain located on the harbor square near Toldboden. This artistic masterpiece, completed in 2017 during Aarhus' tenure as the European Capital of Culture, was designed by Danish artist Jeppe Hein and funded with a generous donation from the Salling funds. The fountain features two-meter high water walls that intermittently emerge from the ground, allowing visitors to stand inside without getting wet or run through them for an exhilarating experience.

Free

Øst for Paradis

East of Eden is a beautifully restored cinema in the hip city of Aarhus, Denmark. The historic cinema features comfortable chairs and modern audio/visual equipment, making it perfect for a Sunday morning brunch with friends. The theater also shows many new movies, making it a great way to explore different options. Tickets are fairly priced, making East of Eden popular movie theaters in Aarhus.
