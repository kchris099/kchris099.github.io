Explore Aarhus

A Brief History

Aarhus was founded as a fortified Viking settlement in the eighth century at the mouth of the Aarhus River. It became an important bishopric and trade center during the Middle Ages, evidenced by the construction of Aarhus Cathedral. The city expanded significantly during the industrial revolution in the nineteenth century, developing a major port and railway connections. Today, Aarhus is Denmark's second-largest city, known for its university, modern architecture, and cultural institutions.

Musikhuset Aarhus

Located Aarhus, Musikhuset Aarhus is Scandinavia's largest concert hall and a true cultural gem. This notable building, designed by Kjaer & Richter and completed in 1982, has an array of nine stages and six halls that cater to diverse musical tastes, from pop to opera, rock to musicals. Each year, it welcomes nearly a million guests for over 1,500 concerts and events.

Free

Ridehuset

Ridehuset is a visitor site in Aarhus, Denmark. Ridehuset is a large multi-purpose hall that hosts various events, including markets like the clothing market by Day. It's a popular spot for big events and has bike parking available nearby. If visitors are in the area, consider check out Ridehuset and its active offerings. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Marselisborg Castle

Marselisborg Castle, also known as Marselisborg Slott, is a fortified historic site in Aarhus, Denmark. Marselisborg Castle, also known as Marselisborg Slott, is a summer residence for the Danish Royal Family. Walls, courtyards, or towers form the main visitor route when the grounds are open.

Free

The Infinite Bridge

The Infinite Bridge, also known as Den Uendelige Bro, is a bridge in Aarhus, Denmark. Records associated with the site include 2015. Visitors can enjoy seaside views and a sense of solitude while taking a leisurely stroll along the bridge. Originally part of the Sculpture by the Sea festival in 2015, this art installation became so beloved by the locals that it was reconstructed as a permanent fixture. Pedestrian decks and adjacent streets provide the usual viewing points.

Free

Marselisborg Deer Park

Marselisborg Deer Park is a public park in Aarhus, Denmark. Records associated with the site include 1902. Marselisborg Deer Park is a 22-hectare nature preserve and zoo located south of Aarhus city. The park is adjacent to the Marselisborg Forests, offering a peaceful retreat among flora, fauna, and wildlife. Paths, lawns, and shaded areas make up the main public facilities.

DKK 225

Tivoli Friheden

Tivoli Friheden is a family amusement park located around 2 km south of Aarhus city center, close to the woodlands of Marselisborg. The park offers traditional carousel and Ferris wheel rides, as well as 5D cinematic experiences and laser games. It's a perfect change of pace for children tired of historical landmarks and museums in Aarhus.

Free (tower DKK 20)

Aarhus Cathedral

Aarhus Cathedral is a religious building in Aarhus, Denmark. The city of Aarhus offers a many of attractions including ARoS - Aarhus Museum of Art, The Old Town open-air museum, Botanical Garden, Marselisborg Palace, Moesgaard Museum, Tivoli Friheden Theme Park and more. The exterior and main interior spaces are usually viewable outside service times.

DKK 160

Aarhus Theatre

Aarhus Theatre, inaugurated in 1900, is a architectural gem designed by known architect Hack Kampmann. The decorative facade and lavish interiors make it a for architecture enthusiasts. Situated in the city center, this historic building hosts various performances throughout the year across its four different stages. Inside, visitors can admire detailed paintings and ceiling details while waiting for shows to start.

Free

Endless Connection

Endless Connection is a visitor site in Aarhus, Denmark. It opened or was completed around 2017. This artistic masterpiece, completed in 2017 during Aarhus' tenure as the European Capital of Culture, was designed by Danish artist Jeppe Hein and funded with a generous donation from the Salling funds. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

DKK 105
