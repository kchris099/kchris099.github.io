Explore Košice

A Brief History

Košice developed as a major trading post on the route connecting the Baltic to the Balkans, becoming the first European town to be granted a municipal coat of arms in 1369. As the most important city in eastern Slovakia, it thrived under Hungarian rule and later the Habsburgs, with its wealth funding the construction of the easternmost Gothic cathedral in Europe. Despite shifting borders in the twentieth century, its medieval and Renaissance core survived. The city's prosperous past is anchored by St. Elisabeth Cathedral, the State Theatre, and the elongated, spindle-shaped main square.

Jakab Palace

Nestled between the old town and the Main Station in Košice, Jakab Palace is a neo-Gothic structure that exudes an enchanting fairy-tale charm. Built in 1899 by an industrialist, this castle-like palace features striking spiky green roofs that evoke memories of whimsical castles from theme parks.

Free

The Singing Fountain

The Singing Fountain in Kosice is a captivating water feature located in the heart of the city. It offers a mesmerizing display of dancing jets, music, and colorful illuminations, creating a magical spectacle that captivates both locals and visitors. The fountain performs every hour, choreographing its water show to the pulse of bells from St. Elizabeth's Cathedral or popular music like The Beatles.

Košice Zoo

Nestled in the picturesque landscapes of Košice, the Košice Zoo is a sprawling haven for animal lovers and nature enthusiasts alike. This expansive zoo, in Europe, specializes primarily in European and Asian wildlife. Visitors can meander along wooded paths that connect various exhibits where animals roam freely in environments designed to mimic their natural habitats.

Observation Tower Košice

Observation Tower Košice, located in Slovakia, offers panoramic views of the entire city. Visitors can enjoy a pleasant walk to the tower, passing by charming castle ruins along the way. The area also features a free platform with binoculars for an alternative city view. While there is an entrance fee for the tower itself, it's worth it for the scenery. The surrounding forest provides a shady and refreshing atmosphere, making it an ideal spot for a leisurely stroll.

€5

Old Town Hall

The Old Town Hall in Kosice is a must-visit destination, serving as the city's visitor center and tourist office. This historic building, completed in 1780 during the transition from Baroque to Neoclassical architecture, boasts intricate Gothic and Renaissance detailing with stone sculptures. It holds significance as the temporary residence of Russian field marshal Mikhail Kutuzov after his defeat at the Battle of Austerlitz.

€15 (event ticket)

Steel Arena

Steel Arena, located in Kosice, Slovakia, is a state-of-the-art venue that serves as the largest ice hockey arena in the country. In addition to being the home base for the HC Kosice ice hockey team, it also hosts a variety of events including concerts, exhibitions, and cultural gatherings. With a seating capacity of 8,377 spectators, it offers an electrifying atmosphere during sports matches and other events.

€15 (performance ticket)

National Theatre Košice

National Theatre Košice, located in the heart of Main Street, is a architectural gem blending neo-baroque and neo-renaissance styles. Once a popular café and social hub, it now hosts captivating opera, drama, and ballet performances that attract both locals and tourists. The area surrounding the theatre buzzes with activity, especially during summer when the fountain with music becomes a major draw. Inside, visitors are treated to an enchanting historical ambiance.

€8 (film ticket)

Kino Úsmev Košice

Kino Úsmev Košice is a recommended destination for travelers visiting Kosice, offering a unique atmosphere within an old cinema building that is still operational. The venue boasts a great bar inside, adding to its distinct charm. Visitors can enjoy the retro vibes of the cinema and bar, while appreciating the friendly staff. A notable feature is the ability to bring drinks from the bar into the cinema, creating a comfortable and enjoyable movie experience.

€5

Botanical Garden UPJŠ

The Botanical Garden UPJŠ, situated in the heart of Kosice, is a vast green space divided into several sections, each with its own significance. It showcases flora and fauna from around the world. This 20-hectare garden, just a short walk from the city center, features not only local plants but also tropical specimens housed in greenhouses. While it may be considered somewhat hidden, efforts are underway to enhance visitor experience and horticultural education.
