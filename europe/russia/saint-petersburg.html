Destination Guide — Saint Petersburg

Explore Saint Petersburg

A Brief History

Saint Petersburg was founded in 1703 as Peter the Great's window on the Baltic, with canals and baroque palaces planned on marshland. Hermitage collections, fortress cathedrals, and Nevsky Prospekt record imperial capital culture until the Soviet era. Peterhof fountains and canal boat routes extend baroque imperial vistas along the Neva delta. Walking routes through the historic centre help visitors relate these monuments to wider patterns of settlement and civic life in Russia.

Top Sights & Activities

₽750

Peter and Paul Fortress

The Peter and Paul Fortress is the original citadel of St. Petersburg, founded by Peter the Great in 1703. The star-shaped fortress on Hare Island never saw combat but served as a notorious political prison. Today, it is a major museum complex, featuring the Peter and Paul Cathedral, which houses the ornate tombs of nearly all the Russian emperors.

Free

Palace Square

Palace Square is the vast, monumental central square of St. Petersburg, serving as the historic and architectural heart of the city. It is bordered by the magnificent green-and-white Winter Palace and the sweeping curve of the General Staff Building. The center is dominated by the Alexander Column, commemorating the Russian victory over Napoleon.

₽700

Winter Palace

The Winter Palace is a colossal, opulent Baroque palace in St. Petersburg that served as the official residence of the Russian Emperors from 1732 to 1917. The magnificent green-and-white facade dominates Palace Square and the Neva River embankment. Today, it houses the core collection of the State Hermitage Museum, one of the largest and most prestigious art museums in the world.

₽450

St. Isaac's Cathedral

St. Isaac's Cathedral is the largest Russian Orthodox basilica in St. Petersburg, renowned for its massive, gold-plated dome that dominates the city skyline. Completed in 1858 after 40 years of construction, the opulent interior is lavishly decorated with malachite, lapis lazuli, and intricate mosaics. Visitors can climb the colonnade for dramatic panoramic views of the city.

₽1000

Yusupov Palace

The Yusupov Palace on the Moika River is one of the most notable aristocratic residences in St. Petersburg, known for its very preserved, opulent 19th-century interiors and its private theater. The palace is most famous historically as the site of the dramatic assassination of Grigori Rasputin in 1916, an event detailed in a dedicated exhibition in the palace's basement.

₽700

Cruiser Aurora

Cruiser Aurora sits within the Cruiser Aurora area of Saint Petersburg, documented in local heritage registers for its civic or cultural role. Visitors approach from the main access road and combine the visit with neighbouring heritage buildings and public spaces in the district.

₽550

Grand Maket Russia

Grand Maket Russia is a massive, very detailed interactive miniature layout located in St. Petersburg. Covering 800 square meters, it represents the diverse regions, cities, and landscapes of the Russian Federation. The intricate model features moving trains, cars, and thousands of miniature figures, complete with a simulated day-and-night cycle, offering a unique geographical overview of the country.

₽450

Savior on the Spilled Blood

The Church of the Savior on Spilled Blood is one of St. Petersburg's most iconic landmarks, featuring a bright, colorful exterior of classic Russian Revival architecture and onion domes. It was built on the exact spot where Emperor Alexander II was assassinated in 1881. The interior is entirely covered with over 7,000 square meters of intricate, vibrant mosaics.

₽600

The State Russian Museum, Mikhailovsky Palace

The State Russian Museum, housed in the grand neoclassical Mikhailovsky Palace in St. Petersburg, contains the world's largest collection of Russian fine art. The extensive exhibits trace the development of Russian art from ancient religious icons to avant-garde masterpieces of the 20th century, including significant works by Ilya Repin, Kazimir Malevich, and Wassily Kandinsky.

₽1000

Bol'shoy Sankt-Peterburgskiy Gosudarstvennyy Tsirk

The Great St. Petersburg State Circus, also known as the Ciniselli Circus, is one of the oldest and most prestigious permanent circuses in Russia. Opened in 1877, the elegant, circular building features a lavish interior and hosts dramatic, high-quality performances involving acrobatics, clowns, and trained animals, continuing a long tradition of Russian circus arts.

Free

Park 300-Letiya Sankt-Peterburga

The Park of the 300th Anniversary of St. Petersburg is a large, modern recreational area located on the coast of the Gulf of Finland. Established in 2003, the park features a wide sandy beach, extensive walking and cycling paths, and a large central fountain. It offers excellent views of the modern Lakhta Center skyscraper and the new Gazprom Arena.

Free

Primorskiy Park Pobedy

Primorsky Park of Victory is a vast, beautifully landscaped public park located on Krestovsky Island in St. Petersburg. Established to commemorate the Allied victory in World War II, the park features wide, tree-lined avenues, numerous ponds, and formal gardens. It is a very popular destination for outdoor recreation, cycling, and accessing the adjacent Divo Ostrov amusement park.

Free

Greenhouse Tauride Gardens

The Greenhouse of the Tauride Gardens is a historic, elegant glass-and-iron conservatory located in central St. Petersburg. Dating back to the 18th century, it houses a lush, tropical environment filled with exotic plants, palm trees, and vibrant flowers. The serene, warm atmosphere provides a popular, picturesque retreat, especially during the long Russian winters.
