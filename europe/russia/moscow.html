Destination Guide — Moscow

Explore Moscow

A Brief History

Moscow became the political centre of Muscovy after the fourteenth century and later the Soviet Union. Kremlin cathedrals, Red Square processions, and metro palaces record how Orthodox ritual, autocratic rule, and twentieth-century state power shaped the Russian heartland. Bolshoi Theatre precincts and Arbat pedestrian lanes extend Kremlin ritual into wider Muscovite boulevards.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

Cathedral of Christ the Saviour

The Cathedral of Christ the Saviour stands as a testament to Russian architecture and spirituality, with its gleaming domes and intricately frescoed interiors. Rebuilt in the 1990s after its original construction was completed in the 19th century, this magnificent structure serves not only as a place of worship but also as a historic landmark commemorating Russia's victory over Napoleon in 1812.

₽700

Poklonnaya Hill

Poklonnaya Hill, one of Moscow's highest points, holds historical significance as a place where Westerners used to pay homage before entering the city. It has withstood invasions by Napoleon and Hitler and now stands as a symbol of Russia's military strength. At the top lies Victory Park, featuring World War II displays, a war museum, and memorials for war victims. The park also offers scenic walks and houses a memorial mosque and synagogue.

Free

Triumfal'naya Arka

Triumfal'naya Arka, also known as the Triumphal Arch of Moscow, is the oldest surviving triumphal arch in Russia. It was built to commemorate Russia's victory over Napoleon. Located near the Museum of the Great Patriotic War, it offers a beautiful sight with Christmas decorations during winter. The area also features modern developments like 'Moscow City', an international business center.

Free

GUM

GUM, short for "Main Universal Store," is a historic 3-story shopping arcade in Moscow with a semicircular glass roof and international brands. It is located near landmarks such as the Kremlin, Lenin's Mausoleum, and St. Basil's Cathedral. Built in the 19th century in a pseudo-Russian style, GUM is not only a shopping destination but also an art space known for its decorations.

Free

Red Square

Red Square is a historic landmark in Moscow, Russia, known for its sites such as the Kremlin, St. Basil's Cathedral, and Lenin's mausoleum. While there are paid attractions within the Kremlin complex, the surrounding area offers free exploration opportunities including Kazan Cathedral and Aleksandrovsky Garden with its enchanting fountains. Visitors can also stroll through GUM shopping center and witness the change of guards at the Eternal Flame.

Free

Kazan Cathedral

The Kazan Cathedral is a Russian Orthodox church that was rebuilt after it was destroyed in 1936. The original church was built in 1633 to celebrate the liberation of Russia from Polish invaders. Today, the cathedral still houses an icon of Lady of Kazan which is said to have helped Prince Dmitry Pozharsky remove Polish occupiers.

Free

Marshal Zhukov monument

Marshal Zhukov monument sits within the GUM area of Moscow, documented in local heritage registers for its civic or cultural role. Visitors approach from the main access road and combine the visit with neighbouring heritage buildings and public spaces in the district.

₽2000

St. Basil's Cathedral

St. Basil's Cathedral, also known as Pokrovsky Cathedral or The Cathedral of Intercession of the Virgin by the Moat, is a 16th-century former cathedral located in Red Square, Moscow. It now houses a museum of the church and is recognized for its multicolored domes that create a unique and appearance.

Free

Moscow Cathedral Mosque

The Moscow Cathedral Mosque is a modern structure built in traditional Islamic style, featuring a striking golden dome, intricate Islamic motifs, and a grand chandelier. It holds historical significance as it replaced the original mosque built in 1904 by the Tatar community. This magnificent landmark represents the cultural heritage of the Muslim people and offers a tranquil and cozy atmosphere for prayer and reflection.

₽500

Bolshoi Theatre

The Bolshoi Theatre is a lavish neoclassical repertory theater in Moscow, Russia, renowned for hosting the Bolshoi Ballet and Bolshoi Opera. While visiting Moscow, it's to explore landmarks like the Kremlin and St. Basil's Cathedral. The area around Red Square offers significant points of interest, including the grand GUM department store and panoramic views from the Children's World department store rooftop.

₽500

Moscow Art Theatre

Nestled in the heart of Moscow, the Moscow Art Theatre stands as a testament to the rich cultural tapestry of Russia. Established in 1898 by visionary educators, this elegant venue has become synonymous with theatrical naturalism. It showcases an impressive repertoire featuring works from literary giants like Tolstoy, Shakespeare, and Ibsen, alongside contemporary playwrights such as Gorky and Maeterlinck.

₽1000

Novodevichy Convent

Novodevichy Convent is a 1520s monastery located by the riverside in Moscow. It is famous for its fresco-lined 5-domed cathedral and ornate soaring bell tower. The convent offers free admission regardless of the day of the week, making it a must-visit attraction in Moscow.

Free

Vorob'yevy gory

Gorky Park is a large park located in the centre of Moscow, Russia. The park features a number of attractions such as the zoo, an amusement park and a theater. The park is also home to several sculptures and monuments, including one to founder Mikhail Lomonosov. Sparrow Hills, another popular attraction in the park, offers views of the city skyline and the Moscow River.

₽1000

Great Moscow State Circus

The Great Moscow State Circus is the largest circus in Europe, featuring animal acts, clowns, and acrobats. The venue has a canteen where visitors can purchase water and snacks. It's recommended to store coats before entering the circus. The performances are visually and audibly captivating, with mostly Russian audiences. Tickets can be purchased through various channels, including online platforms for tourists. Advance booking is advised during holidays.

Free

Patriarch's Ponds

Patriarch's Ponds is an elegant pond surrounded by a park and is famously featured in Mikhail Bulgakov's novel 'The Master and Margarita.' It's a charming district with small streets, trendy restaurants, and historical significance. The area is perfect for leisurely strolls, as it's not too close to metro stations but still within walking distance of Red Square and the Kremlin.

₽700

Tchaikovsky Concert Hall

Tchaikovsky Concert Hall is a documented performance venue in Moscow, Russia, recognised in regional cultural listings for its programming and architectural significance. The venue hosts scheduled performances and admits visitors during box-office hours. Travellers approach from the surrounding cultural district and combine the visit with nearby galleries and historic theatres.

₽1700

Moscow Zoo

Located in Moscow, the Moscow Zoo is a conservation and educational institute that houses over 8,000 animals from around the world. Initially established as a small park with only 286 mammal species in the mid-19th century, it has since grown into one of Europe's most visited zoos and Russia's top zoo destination. The diverse collection includes gorillas, polar bears, elephants, white whales, pandas, Siberian tigers, and giraffes among others.

Free

Atrium

If you're on the hunt for a shopping experience in Moscow, look no further than Atrium Mall. This expansive and modern indoor complex boasts four floors filled with an array of shops and restaurants, making it a perfect destination for both retail therapy and dining delights. With numerous clothing and sports stores to explore, you’re sure to find something that suits your style.

Free

Danilov Monastery 2

Danilov Monastery, named after its founder Daniel of Moscow, has a rich history dating back to 1282. The monastery's defensive walls were built in the mid-17th century and it is now enclosed by seven towers. Initially founded in the late 13th century, it was relocated multiple times before Ivan the Terrible ordered the construction of a new church near Daniel's grave.

Free

Donskoy Monastery

Donskoy Monastery, one of Moscow's oldest cloisters, was established in 1591 following a victory over a Crimean khan. The red-walled monastery has been expanded over the years by tsars and nobility. During the 1812 siege of Moscow, it was occupied by soldiers who damaged the monastery and looted valuable items. Notable figures like Aleksandr Solzhenitsyn are buried on its grounds.

Free

Gorky Central Park of Culture and Leisure

Gorky Central Park of Culture and Leisure, located along the Moskva River, is a popular destination in Moscow. This expansive park features 18th-century gardens, an open-air theater, and various dining options. Visitors can explore cultural institutions such as the Garage Museum of Contemporary Art and enjoy pop-up exhibitions and festivals.

₽1200

Ostankino Television Tower

Located in North Moscow, between the city center and Sheremetyevo Airport, Ostankino Television Tower is a rocket-like structure with 45 levels. It was built in 1967 to commemorate the 50th anniversary of the Soviet Revolution and stands at an impressive height of 540 meters. This makes it Europe's tallest free-standing structure.

Free

Park Ostankino

Ostankino Park is a large, historic recreational park in Moscow, adjacent to the VDNKh exhibition center and the Ostankino TV Tower. The park features extensive oak and linden forests, a large boating pond, and the beautifully preserved 18th-century Ostankino Palace. It provides a vast, peaceful green space for walking, cycling, and outdoor leisure activities.

Free

Vostok Rocket

The Vostok Rocket monument is a prominent landmark located at the VDNKh exhibition center in Moscow. It is a full-scale replica of the R-7 launch vehicle that carried Yuri Gagarin, the first human in space, into orbit in 1961. The towering, authentic rocket serves as a powerful symbol of the Soviet Union's pioneering achievements in space exploration.

Free

All-Russian Exhibition Center

The All-Russian Exhibition Center, also known as VVTs or VDNKh, is a historic exhibition complex that dates back to 1939. Originally established as the All-Union Agricultural Exhibition, it has evolved into a high-profile venue for fairs, congresses, and expositions. The center boasts halls capable of accommodating up to 500 people and spans an impressive 136 hectares with over 250 buildings.

Free

Main Botanical Garden of the Russian Academy of Sciences

The Main Botanical Garden of the Russian Academy of Sciences, established in 1945, spans a vast 340-hectare area and is home to thousands of plant and tree species. The garden features well-kept gardens, charming ponds with turtles and waterfowl, and a rare collection of trees. Visitors can explore enormous greenhouses as well as lakeside walkways.

Free

Church of the Ascension

The Ascension Church, also known as the White Column, is a huge tent-like church that was built to commemorate the coronation of Ivan the Terrible in 1532. It's perched on a hill overlooking Moscow and is an amazing place to visit, with its traditional Russian village market inside and acres of beautiful gardens and parkland outside. The estate museum showcases Russian nature at its year-round.

₽500

Vakhtangov Theater

The Vakhtangov Theater, located on Arbat Street, is a historic and elegant venue that showcases a diverse range of Russian and international drama and comedy performances. It is renowned for its celebrated production of Carlo Gozzi's play featuring the golden figure of Princess Turandot. Despite being one of the oldest theaters in Moscow, it offers a cozy and intimate atmosphere with no social distancing measures in place during performances.

₽1100

The Moscow Kremlin

The Moscow Kremlin, also known as The Kremlin, is a massive fortified complex In Moscow. Originally built as the tsar's residence, it now serves as the official residence of the President of the Russian Federation. This historic site houses ancient churches used for royal ceremonies and various museums showcasing Russian state regalia and art.

Free

Alexander Garden

Nestled alongside the Kremlin's western wall, Alexander Garden is a delightful oasis in the heart of Moscow. This expansive park comprises three distinct gardens adorned with summer blooms and meticulously arranged flower beds that shift colors with the seasons. Visitors can marvel at several monuments, including a tribute to Marshal Georgy Zhukov and the poignant Tomb of the Unknown Soldier, where an eternal flame burns and a ceremonial changing of the guard occurs every hour.

₽500

Moscow Manege

Nestled in the heart of Moscow, just a stone's throw from the Kremlin and Red Square, the Moscow Manege stands as a testament to history and culture. Originally built in 1817 to celebrate Russia's triumph over Napoleon, this neoclassical gem was designed by Spanish engineer Agustin de Betancourt. With its expansive column-free interior, it serves as an exceptional venue for various large-scale exhibitions and cultural events.

₽400

Novospassky Monastery

Novospassky Monastery sits within the Novospassky Monastery area of Moscow, documented in local heritage registers for its civic or cultural role. Visitors approach from the main access road and combine the visit with neighbouring heritage buildings and public spaces in the district.

₽500

Kuskovo Estate Museum

Kuskovo Estate Museum is a grand 18th-century country estate located in Moscow. It features a majestic palace, landscaped gardens, and a ceramics museum. The estate was once a village that was later transformed into a hunting reserve before Count Boris Sheremetev decided to build his summer residence there. The park and palace were further developed by his son Petr, who dedicated his life and wealth to the estate and the theater.

Free

Izmailovsky Park

Izmailovsky Park is a vast green space in Moscow, offering a variety of attractions and activities for visitors of all ages. The park features a Ferris wheel, fairground rides, play areas, and skating facilities. Additionally, it boasts a recreated Russian village with mini-museums, bakeries, and a market where find affordable souvenirs.

Zaryadye Park

Zaryadye Park, located near the Kremlin walls in Moscow, is a modern urban park that seamlessly blends nature and architecture. It offers views of the Kremlin and features a floating bridge over the river Moscow. The park showcases various natural landscapes found across Russia, including mixed forests, tundra, and the steppe. Visitors can explore different geographical zones within the park's boundaries.

₽4600

Sanduny

Nestled in the heart of Moscow, Sanduny Baths is a historical gem that has been captivating visitors since its establishment in 1808. This Russian bathhouse seamlessly blends traditional banya experiences with opulent interiors, featuring vaulted ceilings and elegant marble staircases. A visit here is not just about relaxation; it's an immersion into a rich cultural tradition where indulge in steam rooms and pools while enjoying top-notch service.
