Destination Guide — Southern Spain

Explore Seville

A Brief History

Seville developed as a Roman port on the Guadalquivir and later capital of Muslim al-Andalus under the Almohads before Christian reconquest in 1248. Its cathedral, alcázar, and archive of the Indies grew with Atlantic commerce after 1492, when the city licensed voyages to the Americas. Plaza districts, watchtowers, and parish churches record civic life in Andalusia's largest city.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

Plaza de España

The Plaza de España is a large, monumental square located at the western end of the Gran Vía in Madrid. It features a massive central monument dedicated to the Spanish novelist Miguel de Cervantes, including bronze statues of his famous characters, Don Quixote and Sancho Panza. The square is bordered by two of Madrid's most prominent early skyscrapers.

€13

Catedral de Sevilla

Seville Cathedral was built from 1401 on the site of the Almohad mosque after reconquest. Its Gothic volume and Giralda bell tower incorporate the former minaret. The church houses the tomb of Christopher Columbus and remains the seat of the Archbishop of Seville.

€9

Iglesia Colegial del Divino Salvador

El Salvador is a parish church on Plaza del Salvador rebuilt in Baroque style in the 17th and 18th centuries on earlier mosque and church foundations. Its interior retablo and dome illustrate Seville's post-reconquest religious architecture in the dense historic center north of the cathedral.

Free

Quiosco plaza del Salvador

The bandstand on Plaza del Salvador provides a focal point in one of Seville's older commercial squares. The plaza developed around the parish church and market streets of the medieval center. Outdoor seating and events use the open space between historic facades.

Free

Fuente de Híspalis de Puerta de Jerez

The Hispalis fountain stands at Puerta de Jerez near the entrance to María Luisa Park. Installed in the early 20th century, it references the Roman name for Seville and decorates a traffic junction beside historic city gates. The square connects the old center with the park and riverfront.

Free

Christopher Columbus Monument

The Columbus monument rises at the center of Plaza de Colón near the Guadalquivir embankment. Erected in the early 20th century, it commemorates the explorer's association with Seville and Atlantic voyages. The column and surrounding gardens mark a ceremonial space between the historic center and river.

€12

Setas de Sevilla

Setas de Sevilla is a contemporary wooden structure by Jürgen Mayer over Plaza de la Encarnación. Completed in 2011, its walkways and viewing platforms rise above Roman and medieval remains displayed in the archaeological museum below. The installation reshaped a central square in the old town.

€3

Torre del Oro

Torre del Oro is a 13th-century Almohad watchtower on the Guadalquivir riverbank. It once anchored a chain barrier across the river protecting the port. The tower now houses a maritime museum and offers views along the waterfront toward the Triana district.

€10

Plaza de Toros de la Real Maestranza de Caballería de Sevilla

La Maestranza bullring was built in the 18th century for the Royal Cavalry Corps of Seville. Its Baroque facade and oval arena remain an active venue for corridas during the April Fair season. A museum presents the history of bullfighting in Andalusia.

€16

Royal Alcázar of Seville

The Alcázar of Seville began as a Muslim fortress and was expanded by Christian kings in Mudéjar style from the 14th century. Palaces, patios, and gardens record court life under Pedro I and later monarchs. The complex remains a royal residence and UNESCO World Heritage Site.

€12

Palacio de las Dueñas

Palacio de las Dueñas is a 15th-century noble house with later additions in central Seville. Its patios, chapel, and collections belonged to the House of Alba and include art and memorabilia linked to the family and city society. Guided visits cover rooms arranged around flower-filled courtyards.

€4

Iglesia de San Luis de los Franceses

Iglesia de San Luis de los Franceses is a Baroque church and former Jesuit novitiate completed between 1699 and 1731 to designs by Leonardo de Figueroa. The Diputación de Sevilla has owned the complex since the 19th century. Its church, crypt, and novitiate rooms preserve Spanish Baroque architecture on Calle San Luis, northwest of the cathedral quarter.

Free

La Giralda

La Giralda is a Spanish Renaissance-style minaret with a Gothic and baroque bell tower. It's part of the diverse and beautiful monuments in Seville, including the Cathedral, Reales Alcazares, Torre del Oro, Plaza de Espana, Maria Luisa Park, and more. Seville offers an array of tourism options from historical tours to tapas and wine tours.

Free

Hermandad de la Macarena

The Basílica de la Macarena is situated in the northern part of Seville's city center, within the Macarena neighborhood. It houses the revered religious figure, La Virgen de la Esperanza Macarena (The Virgin of Hope Macarena), commonly known as La Macarena.

Free

Parque de María Luisa

Parque de María Luisa is a significant urban park in Seville, offering scenic plazas with landscaped gardens, fountains, and monuments. The park is part of an area that includes various notable landmarks such as the Giralda, the Cathedral, Reales Alcazares, Torre del Oro, Plaza de España, and more. It's a must-visit destination in Seville and provides a comprehensive insight into the essence of the city.

Free

Plaza de América

Plaza de América is a historic square in Seville, built for the 1929 Ibero-American Exhibition. It features ornate pavilions surrounding a pond and gardens. The square is located within Maria Luisa Park, which also houses three architectural gems from the Expo: the Mudejar Pavilion (now the Museum of Popular Arts and Customs), the Renaissance Palace (now the Archaeological Museum), and the Royal Pavilion.

Free

Sevilla Aquarium

The Acuario de Sevilla is a modern aquarium located along the Guadalquivir River. The exhibits are thematically organized around the 16th-century global voyage of Ferdinand Magellan, showcasing the diverse marine environments encountered during the expedition. The facility features a massive, deep ocean tank housing various species of sharks and rays, emphasizing marine conservation.

Free

Tablao Flamenco Las Setas

Tablao Flamenco Las Setas offers an flamenco experience in Seville. The intimate venue is adorned with elegant decor and dim lighting, setting the stage for a passionate Andalusian art form performance. Guests can enjoy a complimentary cocktail upon arrival and choose from premium or VIP tickets for an extended experience, including an exclusive meet-and-greet with the talented artists.

Free

Ramon Sanchez-Pizjuan Stadium

Ramon Sanchez-Pizjuan Stadium, also known as La Bombonera, is the home ground of Sevilla Futbol Club. This historic stadium, named after a former club president, offers guided tours that provide access to the trophy and dressing rooms. With a capacity of around 45,000 supporters, it's one of Europe's beautiful stadiums. The stadium and its museum are open for visits on non-game days.

Free

Triana Bridge

Puente de Triana, also known as the Isabel II Bridge, is a historic iron arch bridge that was completed in 1852. It connects the charming neighborhood of Triana to the heart of Seville, spanning the Guadalquivir River. Before its construction, there was a pontoon bridge made of boats dating back to Moorish times. The area has a unique vibe with Roman and Moorish influences due to its history.

Free

Mercado de Triana

Nestled in the heart of Triana, the Mercado de Triana is a charming indoor market that captures the essence of Andalusian culture. This vibrant bazaar features an array of vendor stalls brimming with fresh produce, meats, and seafood, making it a paradise for food lovers. Built on the remnants of an ancient castle, one can still glimpse its historical wall while enjoying the lively atmosphere.

Free

Capilla de los Marineros

Nestled in the vibrant Triana neighborhood of Seville, the Capilla de los Marineros, or Chapel of the Sailors, is a captivating gem that beckons travellers with its rich history and Baroque architecture. Established in 1759, this chapel honors the Virgen del Carmen, patron saint of sailors, and serves as a significant spiritual hub for locals and pilgrims alike. The intricate decorations and exquisite stained-glass windows create an atmosphere of reverence and beauty.

Free

Amphitheatre of Italica

The Amphitheatre of Italica is a massive ancient structure that once hosted gladiator fights. It's part of the Roman Ruins of Italica, located near Seville, and offers guided tours that also cover the Roman theater and ancient baths. travellers can marvel at the well-preserved amphitheater and explore its vaulted gallery adorned with intricate mosaics. The site is free for EU citizens and provides a fascinating glimpse into the history of this Roman city.

Free

Conjunto Arqueológico de Itálica

The Conjunto Arqueológico de Itálica is an expansive and well-preserved ancient Roman city that boasts temple ruins and a huge amphitheater. Founded in 206 BC, it was the birthplace of emperors Trajan and Hadrian. The site features broad paved streets leading to the remains of houses with mosaic-laid patios, as well as a 20,000-seat amphitheater, ever built.

Free

Monasterio de San Isidoro del Campo

The Monasterio de San Isidoro del Campo is a medieval monastery located in Santiponce, near Seville. The town was originally founded around the monastery due to continuous floods along the River Guadalquivir. Built in 1301, the monastery has a rich history and served as a refuge for survivors of natural disasters. The site features excellent repair and restoration, minimal tourist crowds, and surroundings that offer a glimpse into suburban Spanish life.

Free

Flamenco Dance Museum

The Flamenco Dance Museum, also known as Museo del Baile Flamenco, is housed in an 18th-century building in Seville. It offers a rich visual display of flamenco history and culture, featuring costumes worn by famous flamenco stars. The museum was opened by renowned Sevillana flamenco dancer Cristina Hoyos and includes interactive exhibitions on the first and second floors. travellers can also enjoy live performances in the courtyard or basement vault.

Free

AIRE Ancient Baths Sevilla

Nestled in the Santa Cruz neighborhood of Sevilla, Spain, AIRE Ancient Baths Sevilla is housed in a historic Mudejar style mansion. The spa offers a unique experience with its tour of water rooms at varying temperatures and candlelit ambiance. Guests can indulge in ancient bathing rituals, including thermal baths and steam rooms, culminating in an outdoor pool with views of the Alcazar.

Free

Casa de Salinas

Casa de Salinas is a 16th-century palace that offers guided tours, allowing travellers to explore its ornate architecture and elegant interiors. The courtyard is a smaller, less crowded version of the Alcazar, making it a unique historical gem. The tour provides insights into the property's long and unique history, ancient artifacts, and remarkable architecture. With knowledgeable guides and well-maintained surroundings, the entrance fee contributes to the preservation of this special place.

Free

Royal Tobacco Factory of Seville

The Royal Tobacco Factory of Seville, a neoclassical building that once served as a tobacco factory, is now home to the offices of Seville University and features an array of paintings. This historical site opened in 1758 and was the first tobacco factory in Europe, becoming the second-largest building in Spain at the time. The factory employed up to 6,000 workers and had its own chapel and prison.

Free

Murillo Gardens

Murillo Gardens is a serene urban park in Seville, featuring well-maintained pathways, shaded seating areas, and historic monuments. It's part of the must-visit attractions in Seville, along with other landmarks like Reales Alcazares and the Cathedral of Seville. The gardens are named after the 17th-century painter Murillo and offer a peaceful retreat from the city's hustle and bustle.

Free

El Corte Ingles

El Corte Ingles is a versatile shopping destination offering a wide range of products including clothing, home decor, groceries, and prepared foods. The rooftop area provides an elegant and non-pretentious space to enjoy traditional Spanish dry-cured ham and cava while taking in views of Seville. The department store's location makes it convenient to find transportation options such as cabs and ride-sharing services.

Free

Seville Museum of Fine Arts

The Seville Museum of Fine Arts is housed in a 17th-century palace that was originally a convent. Founded by St. Peter Nolasco, it's the second most important Spanish art museum in Spain and boasts an impressive collection of Sevillan Baroque paintings from the 17th century Golden Age. The museum features works by renowned artists such as Greco, Velazquez, Zurbaran, Murillo, Valdes Leal, and Francisco de Herrera the younger.

Free

Hercules Columns

Hercules Columns, located at the Alameda de Hercules, are a pair of ancient columns topped with statues of Hercules and Julius Caesar. These columns were originally part of a Roman temple dedicated to Lord March and have been standing in their original location on Calle Marmoles since the 21st century. The statues were sculpted by Diego de Pesquera, with one being a 1574 marble copy by Hercules Farnese.

Free

Isla Mágica

Isla Mágica is a New World-themed adventure park located outside the city center, offering a Spanish colonial atmosphere. The park features thrilling roller coasters, water slides, live shows, and a lake for travellers of all ages to enjoy. Kids can engage in water battles with jet cannons on boats while older children can experience the adrenaline rush of a 50 miles-per-hour rollercoaster and brave the 220-feet free-fall Desafio ride.

Free

Casa de Pilatos

Casa de Pilatos is a palace located at no 1, Plaza de Pilatos, dating back to the fifteenth century. It was created through the union of two noble families and reflects their love for Renaissance art. The palace features internal courtyards adorned with Greek and Roman statues, as well as an impressive collection of artworks. Over time, it underwent comprehensive remodeling that incorporated Romantic elements and features.

Free

Palacio de San Telmo

Palacio de San Telmo is a 17th-century baroque palace in Seville, now serving as a government building. Its red-and-yellow facade and elaborate portal make it a example of Sevillian Baroque architecture. Adjacent to the palace are the gardens of San Telmo Palace and the Real Fábrica de Tabacos, which is now Seville's university.

Free

Puerta del León

The Puerta del León (Lion's Gate) is the primary public entrance to the Real Alcázar of Seville. The historic gateway is integrated into the ancient Almohad defensive walls and is distinguished by a striking 19th-century tile mural depicting a crowned lion holding a cross. It marks the transition from the bustling city into the serene, opulent palace complex.

Free

Archivo de Indias

The Archivo de Indias is a Renaissance building that has been designated as a World Heritage site. It houses an extensive collection of documents related to the Spanish Empire. The city of Seville, where the archive is located, is known for its rich history and architectural wonders such as the Giralda, the Cathedral, Reales Alcazares, Torre del Oro, and many more.
