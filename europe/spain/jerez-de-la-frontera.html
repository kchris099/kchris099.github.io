Explore Jerez De La Frontera

A Brief History

Jerez De La Frontera lies in Spain. A heavily contested border town during the medieval Reconquista, Jerez later channeled its vast agricultural wealth into global sherry exports. The opulent bodegas and long running equestrian traditions highlight a legacy of 19th-century mercantile success and Andalusian culture. Museums, markets, and street plans in Jerez De La Frontera still show layers from those periods.

Castillo de San Marcos

Castillo de San Marcos is a visitor site in Jerez De La Frontera, Spain. Construction and early use date to around 1260. Located along the banks of the Guadalete River, Castillo de San Marcos is a evidence of history and architecture. This medieval fortress, constructed in 1260, has crenelated walls and towers that rise majestically against the active blue Andalusian sky. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Iglesia Mayor Prioral, located in Plaza de Espana, El Puerto de Santa Maria, is a Gothic church with elements of Baroque architecture. Claustros de Santo Domingo is a visitor site in Jerez De La Frontera, Spain. Iglesia de San Marcos

Iglesia de San Marcos is a Gothic church dating back to the 14th century, featuring a Mudéjar portal and a polygonal apse. The church has a single nave covered with star-shaped ribbed vaults and houses the oldest Mudejar-style baptismal chapel. It also has a main baroque altarpiece with paintings on biblical themes and an 18th-century baroque sacramental chapel.

Teatro Villamarta

Teatro Villamarta is a historic theater in Jerez de la Frontera, Andalusia, known for its Andalusian regionalist architecture. It hosts the International Flamenco Festival where known artists give master classes and nightly performances. The venue also showcases ballet and other performances. Visitors can explore nearby shops selling flamenco attire. The theater offers various seating options, with some guests recommending seats a few rows back for an optimal view of the stage.

Mercado Central de Abastos

When visiting Jerez de la Frontera in Andalucia, a trip to the Mercado Central de Abastos is a must for an authentic experience of the local food scene. This busy market, located in town, offers a wide array of fresh seafood and regional produce. It's a favorite spot among locals for their weekly grocery shopping.

Iglesia de San Miguel

Iglesia de San Miguel is a Renaissance-era church in Jerez. Its Gothic facade and intricately carved ceiling make it a sight to behold. Situated in the San Miguel neighborhood, this church holds historical significance as one of the city's most important churches before the cathedral was built. The interior has a blend of Baroque, Renaissance, and Gothic architecture with wood, metal, and stone work.
