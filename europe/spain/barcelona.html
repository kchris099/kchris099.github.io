Explore Barcelona

A Brief History

Barcelona was founded as a Roman colony Barcino and later became the capital of Catalonia. Medieval lanes in the Gothic Quarter surround the cathedral. The 1888 and 1929 expositions spurred urban projects, including works by Antoni Gaudí such as the Sagrada Família. The city hosted the 1992 Summer Olympics along its waterfront. Catalan language and institutions shape civic life alongside Spanish administration.

Basílica de la Sagrada Família

Antoni Gaudí took over this basilica project in 1883 and worked on it until his death in 1926. Nativity and Passion facades, soaring nave, and hyperbolic forms blend Gothic structure with Art Nouveau ornament. Construction continues through ticket revenue and private donations, with a targeted completion in the 2030s.

Free

Bateries antiaèries del Turó de la Rovira

On Turó de la Rovira hill, concrete gun platforms and shelters were built in 1937 to defend Barcelona from fascist air raids during the Civil War. Ruins and panels explain how the city organized civil defense. The summit also offers open views over the grid of Eixample and the Mediterranean.

Montjuïc Castle

This fortress crowns Montjuïc hill above the port, with origins in the 1640 Reapers' War. It later served as a prison and execution site under Franco. Restored ramparts and a military museum explain its role in the 1714 siege of Barcelona and subsequent garrisons.

€35

Casa Batlló

Gaudí remodeled this apartment building on Passeig de Gràcia in 1904–1906 for the Batlló family. Bone-like balconies, scaled roof tiles, and trencadís mosaic on the facade evoke marine and skeletal forms. Interior visits include the light well, attic catenary arches, and rooftop chimneys.

€35

Casa Milà

La Pedrera, completed in 1912 for the Milà family, was Gaudí's last civil building. Undulating stone walls and forged iron balconies wrap a corner block on Passeig de Gràcia. The attic and rooftop with sculpted chimneys are open to visitors, along with a restored early 20th-century apartment.

€15

Museu Picasso de Barcelona

Opened in 1963 in five adjoining medieval palaces of the Born quarter, this museum traces Picasso's years in Barcelona from age 14 onward. Holdings include Blue Period works, early portraits, and studies for Las Meninas. The collection grew through donations by the artist and his secretary Jaume Sabartés.

Free

Arc de Triomf

Built in reddish brick for the 1888 Universal Exposition, this arch marked the fair entrance from Parc de la Ciutadella. Neo-Mudéjar decoration covers friezes and spandrels with terracotta reliefs. It now stands at the top of Passeig de Lluís Companys, framing views toward the zoo and parliament.

Free

El Born Centre de Cultura i Memòria

Beneath a restored iron market hall in El Born, excavations reveal streets and houses buried after the 1714 siege. A raised walkway circles the site while exhibits explain daily life before the neighborhood was demolished to build the citadel. The center also hosts temporary exhibitions on Catalan history.

€5

Basílica de Santa Maria del Mar

Merchants of the Ribera district funded this church between 1329 and 1383, hauling stone from Montjuïc quarries. Catalan Gothic lines emphasize height and uniform bare stone inside. The symmetrical facade and rose window face a small square a short walk from the Born quarter.

€14

Catedral de Barcelona

Barcelona's cathedral, dedicated to the Holy Cross and Saint Eulalia, grew from a 13th-century Gothic choir to a full basilica by the 15th century. A neo-Gothic facade was added in the 1880s. Cloisters with thirteen white geese recall the patron saint's age at martyrdom.

Free

La Rambla

La Rambla follows a former stream bed from Plaça de Catalunya to the Columbus monument at the port. Kiosks, flower stalls, and street performers line the pedestrian boulevard. It divides the Gothic Quarter from El Raval and fills with crowds, especially on weekends and festival days.

Free

Plaça d'Espanya

Created for the 1929 International Exposition, this circular square sits at the foot of Montjuïc where major avenues meet. Venetian towers flank the approach to the fairgrounds. Metro lines, buses, and fountains make it a busy gateway to the MNAC museum and Magic Fountain shows.

Free

Torres Venecianes

These paired 47-meter towers, built for the 1929 exposition, echo the campanile of St Mark's in Venice. They frame the avenue leading uphill toward the Palau Nacional. Brick cladding and open arches mark the transition from the Eixample grid to the Montjuïc fairgrounds.

€12

Museu Nacional d'Art de Catalunya

The Palau Nacional on Montjuïc, built for 1929, holds Catalonia's national art museum. Romanesque mural sections reconstructed from Pyrenean churches form the core collection. Gothic altarpieces, modernista rooms, and a rooftop terrace with city views round out the visit.

Free

Torre de Comunicacions de Montjuïc

Santiago Calatrava designed this telecommunications tower for the 1992 Olympics on Montjuïc. Its white steel mast tilts like a calçot, a Catalan spring onion, and supports broadcast equipment. The structure is visible from much of the city and stands near the Olympic stadium complex.

Monumental

Monumental is a historic art nouveau bullring in Barcelona that has been repurposed into a museum and event space. The building was featured in the movie The Gunman, although the actual bullfight scenes were filmed in Madrid due to the ban on bullfighting in Catalonia. Today, Monumental hosts events like the Monumental Club, a popular daytime festival with fashion stalls, jewelry, and handmade items from local artisans.

€18

Recinte Modernista de Sant Pau

Sant Pau Recinte Modernista is a modernist hospital in Barcelona, featuring colorful Moorish-style pavilions and beautiful gardens. Designed by the modernist architect Domenech Muntaner, it was originally built to replace medieval facilities and featured innovative patient distribution by sex and illness type. The hospital's outer courtyard boasts majestic buildings in Catalan Art Nouveau style, offering plenty of photo opportunities.

€20

Palau de la Música Catalana

The Palau de la Música Catalana is a example of Art Nouveau architecture, celebrated for its intricate facade and lavish auditorium featuring a stained-glass dome. Nestled in the vibrant El Born neighborhood, this concert hall stands as a testament to the creative genius of Lluis Domenech Montaner, completed in 1908. As one of Barcelona's seven UNESCO World Heritage sites, it harmoniously blends modernist, gothic, and Moorish styles.

Free

El Corte Inglés Plaça de Catalunya

When you arrive at Plaza Catalunya in Barcelona, one of the first sights to greet you is the impressive El Corte Inglés Plaça de Catalunya. This expansive seven-story department store is a must-visit for shoppers of all ages. It's often likened to a Spanish version of Macy's and offers an extensive range of products including designer clothing, accessories, cosmetics, footwear, furniture, electronics, and more.

Free

Plaça de Catalunya

Plaça de Catalunya is a vibrant and bustling square that serves as the heart of Barcelona, seamlessly blending civic life with cultural activities. This tree-lined plaza is adorned with sculptures and surrounded by an array of shops and cafes, making it a perfect spot to relax or people-watch. As you cross Las Ramblas, find yourself in this lively hub where various events are frequently held, adding to its dynamic atmosphere.

Free

Magic Fountain of Montjuïc

The Magic Fountain of Montjuïc is a renowned circular fountain in Barcelona known for its scheduled shows of water and light choreographed to music. Located on the slopes of Montjuic hill, it is considered one of the city's symbols. In the evening, the fountain transforms into an enchanting display as water jets dance to melodies from composers like Bach, Beethoven, and Tchaikovsky.

Free

Arenas de Barcelona

Arenas de Barcelona, located in Placa Espana, was originally a bullfighting arena but has been transformed into a five-floor shopping mall with over 100 stores offering a wide range of products. The rooftop features a 360-degree promenade that provides views of Barcelona and Montjuic, along with eateries and bars. travellers can also enjoy a large cinema showcasing Spanish films.

Christopher Columbus monument

Nestled at the southern tip of La Rambla in Barcelona, the Columbus Monument, or Monument a Colom, is an impressive tribute to Christopher Columbus and his historic journey to the Americas. Erected in 1888 for the Universal Exposition, this ornate Corinthian column reaches a height of 60 meters (197 feet) and features a striking bronze statue of Columbus himself, gesturing towards the sea as if guiding future explorers.

Plaça del Rei

Nestled in the heart of Barcelona's Gothic Quarter, Plaça del Rei is a captivating square steeped in history and medieval charm. This tranquil space is surrounded by impressive structures that tell tales of the past, including the Palau Reial Major, which served as the royal residence for Catalan counts and Aragonese kings.

Temple d'August

MUHBA Temple d'August is a hidden gem in the Gothic Quarter of Barcelona, featuring preserved columns from the 1st century BC Roman Temple of Augustus. The temple stood at the center of the forum until the 5th century AD when it fell into decline with the rise of Christianity. travellers can explore this peaceful courtyard for free and learn about its historical significance through informative signs.

Free

Casa de les Punxes

Casa de les Punxes, also known as Casa Terradas, is a modernist mansion in Barcelona designed by architect Josep Puig i Cadafalch in 1905. The building's design is inspired by the Neuschwanstein Castle and features a mix of materials including stained glass windows, ceramics, ironwork, and sculptures. travellers can take tours to explore the interiors and learn about the history of the building through innovative floor-to-ceiling projections.

Free

Port Olímpic

Port Olímpic, constructed for the 1992 Summer Olympics, offers various amenities for boats. It is a hub of vibrant nightlife with bars like CDLC, Shoko, and Bestial attracting a cosmopolitan crowd for cocktails and dancing to groovy music. The area is known for its upmarket experience in the summer. Additionally, clubs like Opium, Pacha, and Catwalk provide entertainment until the early hours of the morning.

Spotify Camp Nou

The Spotify Camp Nou is the home ground of the renowned Barcelona football club. It was inaugurated in 1957 and is known for having the greatest capacity in Europe, making it stadiums globally. The stadium offers regular matches as well as tours and a museum, attracting football enthusiasts from around the world. travellers can explore the locker rooms, tunnels, and field on a tour, providing an immersive experience into the team's environment.

Pedralbes Royal Palace

Pedralbes Royal Palace is an elegant historic palace set in beautiful gardens, now housing a ceramics museum and hosting various cultural events. The site is catalogued as a palace. Pedralbes Royal Palace is listed among principal sites for Barcelona within Spain.

Parc de Cervantes

Parc de Cervantes is famous for its extensive rose garden, showcasing over 10,000 rose bushes, making it a picturesque spot for nature lovers. The site is catalogued as a tourist attraction. Parc de Cervantes is listed among principal sites for Barcelona within Spain.

Monastery of Sant Cugat

The Monastery of Sant Cugat is a historic Benedictine monastery dating back to the 9th century. It features a bell tower, Romanesque cloister, and a museum that showcases the monastery's history and art. The Monastery Museum is the main exhibition space of the Museum of Sant Cugat, offering insights into the abbey, Romanesque monasteries, medieval monks, as well as temporary art exhibitions and workshops.

Sant Cugat Shopping center

Nestled in the heart of Sant Cugat, the Sant Cugat Shopping Center is a vibrant hub that promises an experience for all travellers. This expansive mall features trendy retail shops and casual eateries, alongside a well-stocked supermarket and a state-of-the-art movie theater. As you stroll through its lively atmosphere, discover not only modern stores but also charming historic establishments with shop windows.

Parc del Mirador del Poble-sec

Parc del Mirador del Poble-sec is a serene park located at the top of a mountain, offering well-maintained grounds and panoramic views of Barcelona. It is often featured in lists of the viewpoints in the city. The park provides an up-close look at the marina and cruise ship port, making it an ideal spot for nighttime vistas.

Casa Vicens Gaudí

Casa Vicens Gaudí is a four-story residence that marks the beginning of Antoni Gaudí's illustrious career as an architect. Constructed between 1883 and 1885 for stockbroker Manel Vicens Montaner, this vibrant home showcases a captivating facade adorned with green and white checkered tiles alongside intricate floral patterns.

Park Güell

Park Güell is an enchanting destination that captures the essence of Barcelona's artistic spirit. Nestled in the Gracia district, this vibrant park showcases the genius of Antoni Gaudí through its mosaic-covered structures, whimsical sculptures, and grand staircases. Originally envisioned as a residential complex by Eusebio Güell between 1900 and 1914, it never fully materialized but was transformed into a public park in 1926.

€24

Gaudí House Museum

The Gaudí House Museum, located within Park Guell, was once the residence of the renowned architect Antoni Gaudí. Although it may appear traditional from the outside, this house served as a showhouse for the Park Guell development and now houses a small museum featuring furniture, objects, and documents by Gaudí. While not a typical museum experience, travellers can explore recreated rooms that reflect how Gaudí lived and worked in this space.

Free

MUHBA Turó de la Rovira

MUHBA Turó de la Rovira, also known as Bunkers del Carmel, is a popular spot in Barcelona offering panoramic views of the city and the ocean. It was a site of anti-aircraft bunkers during the Spanish Civil War and still retains historical remnants. The area stands 262m high and provides 360-degree views of Barcelona.

Temple of the Sacred Heart of Jesus

Perched atop Mount Tibidabo, the Temple of the Sacred Heart of Jesus is a 20th-century church designed by Catalan architect Sagnier. The church offers panoramic views of Barcelona and, on clear days, even glimpses of the Balearic Islands. Constructed between 1902 and 1961, it showcases a blend of architectural styles and materials sourced from both the mountain and Girona.
