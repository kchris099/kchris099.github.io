Explore Barcelona

A Brief History

Barcelona possesses a profound historical depth stretching back to its founding as the Roman colony of Barcino. Over the centuries, it grew into a dominant medieval maritime power within the Crown of Aragon, profoundly shaping the Mediterranean trade network. Today, its cultural identity is powerfully defined by its immense Gothic Quarter and the spectacular 19th-century Modernista architectural movement spearheaded by Antoni Gaudí.

Basílica de la Sagrada Família

An unfinished Catholic minor basilica designed by Catalan architect Antoni Gaudí. Construction began in 1882, combining Gothic and curvilinear Art Nouveau forms, and relies solely on private donations.

A historic military site featuring concrete remnants of anti-aircraft defenses constructed in 1937 during the Spanish Civil War to defend Barcelona from aerial bombings.

Montjuïc Castle

A military fortress dating back to 1640, built on a hill overlooking the harbor. It historically served as a prison and military command post, playing a central role in the War of the Spanish Succession.

€35

Casa Batlló

A residential building in the center of Barcelona, remodeled by Antoni Gaudí in 1904. Its exterior features a skeletal organic design and a colorful mosaic facade made from broken ceramic tiles.

€35

Casa Milà

Known as La Pedrera, this is a modernist building constructed between 1906 and 1912 by Antoni Gaudí. It was the last private residence designed by him, noted for its undulating stone facade and wrought iron balconies.

€15

Museu Picasso de Barcelona

An art museum housing an extensive collection of artworks by the 20th-century Spanish artist Pablo Picasso, with a particular focus on his formative years and early relationship with the city of Barcelona.

Free

Arc de Triomf

A triumphal arch built in the Neo-Mudéjar style by architect Josep Vilaseca i Casanovas. It served as the main access gate for the 1888 Barcelona Universal Exposition.

Free

El Born Centre de Cultura i Memòria

A cultural facility housed in a 19th-century cast-iron market building. It preserves archaeological remains of the streets and houses from 1700, destroyed during the War of the Spanish Succession.

€5

Basílica de Santa Maria del Mar

A church in the Ribera district, constructed between 1329 and 1383 at the height of the Crown of Aragon's maritime and mercantile expansion. It is a prominent example of Catalan Gothic architecture.

€14

Catedral de Barcelona

The Gothic cathedral and seat of the Archbishop of Barcelona, constructed from the 13th to 15th centuries over a former Romanesque church. The neo-Gothic facade was added in the late 19th century.

Free

La Rambla

A tree-lined pedestrian street stretching 1.2 kilometers through central Barcelona. Originally a seasonal stream, it became a central promenade in the 15th century and serves as a boundary between the Gothic Quarter and El Raval.

Free

Plaça d'Espanya

One of Barcelona's primary squares, designed by Josep Puig i Cadafalch and built for the 1929 International Exposition at the foot of Montjuïc. It serves as a major transit hub.

Free

Torres Venecianes

Two 47-meter-tall towers situated at the junction of Plaça d'Espanya and Avinguda de la Reina Maria Cristina, modeled after the campanile of St Mark's Basilica in Venice for the 1929 International Exposition.

€12

Museu Nacional d'Art de Catalunya

The national museum of Catalan visual art located in the Palau Nacional, which was built for the 1929 International Exposition. It houses a vast collection of Romanesque church paintings.

Free

Torre de Comunicacions de Montjuïc

A telecommunications tower built for the 1992 Summer Olympics to transmit television coverage. Designed by Santiago Calatrava, its structure resembles an athlete holding the Olympic Flame.
