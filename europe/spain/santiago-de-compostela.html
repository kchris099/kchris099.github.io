Explore Santiago de Compostela

A Brief History

Santiago de Compostela grew around the shrine of Saint James, whose relics drew pilgrims from the 9th century along routes crossing Christian Europe. The Romanesque cathedral with its Baroque façade remains the goal of the Camino de Santiago. Archbishopric power, university foundations, and regional government shaped granite plazas, monasteries, and civic buildings in Galicia's administrative capital.

Top Sights & Activities

Cathedral of Santiago de Compostela

The Cathedral of Santiago de Compostela is a Romanesque structure and a known pilgrimage site, featuring the carved arched portico by Master Mateo. The cathedral has undergone various extensions and incorporates elements of Renaissance, Baroque, and Neo-Classical styles. Its different facades showcase diverse architectural influences, from Baroque to Neoclassical.

€15

Porch of the Glory

Porch of the Glory is a visitor site in Santiago de Compostela, Spain. This Romanesque masterpiece greeted pilgrims upon their arrival and holds religious importance as it is believed to be the burial site of Saint James the Great. The cathedral also marks the end of the Camino de Santiago pilgrimage route. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Free

Palace of Fonseca

Palace of Fonseca, also known as Pazo de Fonseca, is a fortified historic site in Santiago de Compostela, Spain. Records associated with the site include 1544. The Palace of Fonseca, also known as Pazo de Fonseca, is a historic building in Santiago. Originally the ancestral home of Archbishop Alonso de Fonseca, it was transformed into the University of Compostela in 1544. Walls, courtyards, or towers form the main visitor route when the grounds are open.

€5

Museum of the Galician People

The Museo del Pueblo Gallego is a museum dedicated to the history and culture of Galicia, housed within a 13th-century convent complex. For a small fee, visitors can explore this ethnographic museum featuring artifacts dating back to prehistoric times. The museum offers interactive exhibits and performances that bring Galician traditions to life.

Free

Colegiata de Santa María de Sar

Colegiata de Santa María de Sar is a visitor site in Santiago de Compostela, Spain. The church has three basilical structures divided by pillars and showcases a leaning interior columns phenomenon. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Free

Praza de Galicia

Praza de Galicia lies beside Santiago's old town with views toward the cathedral towers and the Hostal dos Reis Católicos. Plane trees shade benches used by pilgrims arriving on the Camino. The square links the historic centre to modern administrative buildings along the Rúa do Vilar axis.

Free

As Duas Marias

As Duas Marias is a visitor site in Santiago de Compostela. As Dúas Marías is a bronze statue in the Alameda park commemorating two local sisters known for their daily walks in traditional dress. The sculpture stands among paths lined with stone benches and pollarded trees. The monument has become a meeting point between the university quarter and the historic centre.

Free

Parque da Alameda

Parque da Alameda is a visitor site in Santiago de Compostela, Spain. This elegant green space comprises three distinct areas: Paseo da Alameda, the Oak Grove of Santa Susana, and Paseo da Ferradura. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Free

Monumento al caminante

The Monumento al Caminante on the Alameda honours pilgrims who complete the Camino de Santiago. The bronze figure strides toward the cathedral spires visible above the rooftops. The work stands at a viewpoint where walkers traditionally first sight the city's towers.

Free

Convento de San Francisco de Santiago

Convento de San Francisco de Santiago, originally a 13th-century monastery for Franciscan friars, has been transformed into a refined hotel and restaurant. The historic building is located near a Gothic cathedral and notable monuments. The former convent now offers excellent facilities, delicious food with a special Pilgrim menu featuring typical monastery dishes, and well equipped service.
