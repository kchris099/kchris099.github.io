Segovia

Segovia was a significant Celtic and Roman settlement before becoming an important medieval textile center and a favored residence of the Kings of Castile. In 1474, Isabella I was proclaimed Queen of Castile within the city. The wealth generated by the wool trade funded the construction of numerous Romanesque churches and a large late-Gothic cathedral within the walled core. Visitors trace this Roman and royal history through the monumental Roman Aqueduct, the Alcázar fortress perched on a rocky crag, and the historic Jewish Quarter.

The Royal Palace of La Granja of San Ildefonso is a 17th-century baroque palace located in the town of San Ildefonso, just 12 kilometers east of Segovia.
