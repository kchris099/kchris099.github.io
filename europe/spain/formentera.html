Formentera

Pirate pressure kept Formentera thinly settled until the seventeenth century. Without a deep harbour, the island avoided mass infrastructure until a 1960s counterculture discovered its beaches. Salt pans and low white houses record that late, light settlement pattern. Ses Illetes' sandbars draw day boats from Ibiza without restoring a deep historic port.

Far de la Mola

The Far de la Mola is a historic lighthouse perched on the highest point of Formentera, at the eastern edge of the island. Standing atop sheer, 120-meter-high cliffs, the lighthouse offers dramatic, sweeping views of the Mediterranean Sea. The dramatic location is known for expansive sunrises and was notably featured in a novel by Jules Verne.

38.6634° N / 1.5838° E

Cap de Barbaria

Cap de Barbaria is the rugged, southwestern tip of Formentera, characterized by a stark, lunar-like landscape of flat, rocky terrain. The area is dominated by the well known Cap de Barbaria Lighthouse and the nearby Cova Foradada, a small cave leading to a balcony overlooking the sea. It is a very popular and evocative destination for watching the sunset.

Cala en Baster

Cala en Baster is a unique, rocky cove located on the northern coast of Formentera. The bay is characterized by its high, sheer limestone cliffs, into which local fishermen have carved traditional wooden boat slipways (escars) directly into the rock face. The clear, deep waters make it an excellent, secluded spot for snorkeling and diving.

Torre de la Punta Prima

The Torre de Punta Prima is an 18th-century defensive watchtower located on the northern coast of Formentera. It was part of a network of coastal towers built to warn the island's inhabitants of approaching pirate fleets. The well-preserved stone structure stands on a rocky promontory, offering panoramic views of the sea and the neighboring island of Ibiza.

38.7229° N / 1.4707° E
