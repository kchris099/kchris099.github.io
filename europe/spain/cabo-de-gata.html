Explore Cabo De Gata

A Brief History

Cabo de Gata has a history shaped by its arid climate and strategic Mediterranean location. In the nineteenth century, the area saw a brief mining boom before returning to its isolated, rural character. Today, the region is protected as a natural park, celebrated for its pristine volcanic beaches and stark, untouched landscapes. Museums, markets, and street plans in Cabo De Gata still show layers from those periods.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

Playa de San José

Playa de San José is the main urban beach of the coastal village of San José, located within the Cabo de Gata-Níjar Natural Park in Almería. The wide, sandy beach offers calm, shallow waters and excellent facilities, making it a popular and accessible destination for families, while maintaining the relaxed atmosphere typical of the protected natural park.

Free

Faro de Cabo de Gata

The Cabo de Gata Lighthouse is an well known landmark situated on the rugged, southeastern tip of the Iberian Peninsula, within the Cabo de Gata-Níjar Natural Park. Built in 1863 on the ruins of an ancient castle, the lighthouse stands atop dramatic volcanic cliffs, offering sweeping, panoramic views of the Mediterranean Sea and the treacherous Arrecife de las Sirenas reef below.

Free

Cala de Abril

Cala de Abril is a visitor site in Cabo De Gata, Spain. Cala de Abril is a small, secluded cove located within the Cabo de Gata-Níjar Natural Park. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest. Cala de Abril remains part of the public visitor circuit in Cabo De Gata.

Free

Cape Town Fund

The Cape Town Fund is a prominent landmark located in the Cabo de Gata-Níjar Natural Park. It is a popular spot for visitors to take photographs and enjoy the coastal scenery. It is a popular destination for tourists and locals alike, offering a unique and memorable experience.

Free

Cabo De Gata

Cabo De Gata is a visitor site in Cabo De Gata, Spain. Cabo de Gata-Níjar Natural Park is a vast, protected coastal area in southeastern Spain, known for its stark, volcanic landscapes, hidden coves, and pristine beaches. The park remains largely undeveloped, offering a dramatic and unspoiled natural environment for hiking and eco-tourism. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Free

Mirador Las Salinas

Mirador Las Salinas is a viewpoint in Cabo De Gata, Spain. The Mirador Las Salinas is a prominent observation point located within the Cabo de Gata-Níjar Natural Park. It offers expansive, panoramic views over the vast coastal salt flats (salinas), which are an important wetland habitat for numerous migratory bird species. A short approach path leads to the main overlook.

Free (€6 parking fee for restricted beaches in summer)

Parque Natural del Cabo de Gata-Níjar

Parque Natural del Cabo de Gata-Níjar sits within the Parque Natural del Cabo de Gata-Níjar area of Cabo De Gata, documented in local heritage registers for its civic or cultural role. Visitors approach from the main access road and combine the visit with neighbouring heritage buildings and public spaces in the district.

Free

Cala de la Media Luna

Cala de la Media Luna is a, crescent-shaped beach located in the Cabo de Gata-Níjar Natural Park. Characterized by its fine, dark volcanic sand and clear, calm waters, the secluded cove is surrounded by low, rocky hills. It offers a peaceful, natural setting for swimming and relaxing, accessible via an unpaved coastal road.

Free

San Felipe Castle

The Castle of San Felipe is an 18th-century coastal fortress located in the village of Los Escullos, within the Cabo de Gata-Níjar Natural Park. Built to defend the coastline against Barbary pirate attacks, the well-preserved battery stands on a fossilized sand dune overlooking the Mediterranean Sea, providing a historic landmark amidst the dramatic coastal scenery.
