Cabo De Gata

Volcanic headlands and aridity limited settlement; a brief nineteenth-century mining pulse left towers and spoil before the coast emptied again. Natural-park status now protects beaches and steppe. Cabo de Gata's story is extraction then conservation, not a continuous urban chronicle. Spaghetti western crews once used the same stark volcanic badlands as standing sets.

Playa de San José

San Jose's main beach curves beside the village within Cabo de Gata-Nijar Natural Park. Sand and relatively sheltered water make it more accessible than many isolated coves, while the adjoining promenade, harbor and services connect directly with the settlement. Summer crowds, wind, sea conditions and parking pressure vary and protected-area status does not guarantee calm or unrestricted recreation every day.

36.7627° N / 2.1064° W

02 AdmissionFree

Faro de Cabo de Gata

The Cabo de Gata Lighthouse is an well known landmark situated on the rugged, southeastern tip of the Iberian Peninsula, within the Cabo de Gata-Níjar Natural Park. Built in 1863 on the ruins of an ancient castle, the lighthouse stands atop dramatic volcanic cliffs, offering sweeping, panoramic views of the Mediterranean Sea and the treacherous Arrecife de las Sirenas reef below.

36.7218° N / 2.1929° W

03 AdmissionFree

Cala de Abril

Cala de Abril is a small cove inside Cabo de Gata-Nijar Natural Park where volcanic rock and clear water meet a narrow beach. Access usually involves walking from coastal tracks rather than a developed resort road. Swimmers and snorkelers use the sheltered water when seas are calm, keeping the cove quieter than the park's larger named beaches.

36.7223° N / 2.1814° W

04 AdmissionFree

Cape Town Fund

At Spain's southeastern edge, the Cabo de Gata headland projects into the Mediterranean within a dry volcanic landscape. The lighthouse, cliffs and offshore rock formations around the Arrecife de las Sirenas mark the coastal approach, while salt pans and low steppe extend inland. Strong wind, exposed roads and protected habitats shape travel through this remote section of the natural park.

36.7385° N / 2.2082° W

05 AdmissionFree

Cabo De Gata

Cabo de Gata names both the volcanic headland and the wider Cabo de Gata-Nijar Natural Park on Spain's southeastern coast. Arid hills, hidden coves, and long undeveloped beaches define a landscape kept free of high-rise tourism. Hiking paths and coastal tracks link salt flats, cliffs, and fishing hamlets across protected ground.

36.7746° N / 2.2387° W

06 AdmissionFree

Mirador Las Salinas

A roadside overlook beside the Cabo de Gata salt pans reveals shallow lagoons, embankments, working salt areas, and the mountain-backed coast. Flamingos and other migratory birds may feed at a distance depending on season and water conditions, while nearby hides offer more controlled observation; the open setting is sensitive habitat.

36.7790° N / 2.2330° W

Parque Natural del Cabo de Gata-Níjar

Cabo de Gata-Nijar Natural Park protects a dry volcanic coastline of cliffs, coves, reefs, salt flats, steppe, villages and former mining landscapes in Almeria. Roads and paths connect beaches and settlements, but heat, wind, limited shade, seasonal traffic and marine conditions require planning.

36.7420° N / 2.1285° W

08 AdmissionFree

Cala de la Media Luna

Cala de la Media Luna is a, crescent-shaped beach located in the Cabo de Gata-Níjar Natural Park. Characterized by its fine, dark volcanic sand and clear, calm waters, the secluded cove is surrounded by low, rocky hills. It offers a peaceful, natural setting for swimming and relaxing, accessible via an unpaved coastal road.

36.7314° N / 2.1517° W

09 AdmissionFree

San Felipe Castle

San Felipe Castle stands on the coast at Los Escullos, where an eighteenth-century battery guarded shipping and nearby settlements from raids, and the structure is normally understood from its exterior and setting, while protected-landscape rules and restoration work may limit interior access. A low stone fort, gun platform, and walls occupy a raised fossil dune above the Mediterranean.

36.8023° N / 2.0627° W
