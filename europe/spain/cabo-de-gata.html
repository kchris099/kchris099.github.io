Cabo De Gata

Volcanic headlands and aridity limited settlement; a brief nineteenth-century mining pulse left towers and spoil before the coast emptied again. Natural-park status now protects beaches and steppe. Cabo de Gata's story is extraction then conservation, not a continuous urban chronicle. Spaghetti western crews once used the same stark volcanic badlands as standing sets.

San Felipe Castle

The Castle of San Felipe is an 18th-century coastal fortress located in the village of Los Escullos, within the Cabo de Gata-Níjar Natural Park. Built to defend the coastline against Barbary pirate attacks, the well-preserved battery stands on a fossilized sand dune overlooking the Mediterranean Sea, providing a historic landmark amidst the dramatic coastal scenery.
