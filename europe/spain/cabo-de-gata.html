Explore Cabo De Gata

A Brief History

Cabo de Gata has a history shaped by its arid climate and strategic Mediterranean location. It was utilized by Romans and Moors for mining and fishing, and later fortified by the Spanish to defend against Barbary pirates, leaving behind watchtowers and castles. In the nineteenth century, the area saw a brief mining boom before returning to its isolated, rural character. Today, the region is protected as a natural park, celebrated for its pristine volcanic beaches and stark, untouched landscapes.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

Playa de San José

Playa de San José is the main urban beach of the coastal village of San José, located within the Cabo de Gata-Níjar Natural Park in Almería. The wide, sandy beach offers calm, shallow waters and excellent facilities, making it a popular and accessible destination for families, while maintaining the relaxed atmosphere typical of the protected natural park.

Free

Faro de Cabo de Gata

The Cabo de Gata Lighthouse is an iconic landmark situated on the rugged, southeastern tip of the Iberian Peninsula, within the Cabo de Gata-Níjar Natural Park. Built in 1863 on the ruins of an ancient castle, the lighthouse stands atop dramatic volcanic cliffs, offering sweeping, panoramic views of the Mediterranean Sea and the treacherous Arrecife de las Sirenas reef below.

Free

Cala de Abril

Cala de Abril is a small, secluded cove located within the Cabo de Gata-Níjar Natural Park. Accessible via a scenic coastal hike, the pristine beach is surrounded by dramatic volcanic rock formations and features clear, turquoise waters ideal for snorkeling. Its remote location ensures a tranquil, unspoiled natural environment away from the more crowded tourist beaches.

Free

Cape Town Fund

The Cape Town Fund is a prominent landmark located in the Cabo de Gata-Níjar Natural Park. It is a popular spot for visitors to take photographs and enjoy the impressive coastal scenery. It is a popular destination for tourists and locals alike, offering a unique and memorable experience.

Free

Cabo De Gata

Cabo de Gata-Níjar Natural Park is a vast, protected coastal area in southeastern Spain, renowned for its stark, volcanic landscapes, hidden coves, and pristine beaches. As one of the driest regions in Europe, it features unique semi-arid flora and fauna. The park remains largely undeveloped, offering a dramatic and unspoiled natural environment for hiking and eco-tourism.

Free

Mirador Las Salinas

The Mirador Las Salinas is a prominent observation point located within the Cabo de Gata-Níjar Natural Park. It offers expansive, panoramic views over the vast coastal salt flats (salinas), which are a crucial wetland habitat for numerous migratory bird species. It is a premier location for birdwatching, particularly for observing large flocks of pink flamingos.

Free

Parque Natural del Cabo de Gata-Níjar

Parque Natural del Cabo de Gata-Níjar sits within the Parque Natural del Cabo de Gata-Níjar area of Cabo De Gata, documented in local heritage registers for its civic or cultural role. Visitors approach from the main access road and combine the visit with neighbouring heritage buildings and public spaces in the district.

Free

Cala de la Media Luna

Cala de la Media Luna is a beautiful, crescent-shaped beach located in the Cabo de Gata-Níjar Natural Park. Characterized by its fine, dark volcanic sand and clear, calm waters, the secluded cove is surrounded by low, rocky hills. It offers a peaceful, natural setting for swimming and relaxing, accessible via an unpaved coastal road.

Free

San Felipe Castle

The Castle of San Felipe is an 18th-century coastal fortress located in the village of Los Escullos, within the Cabo de Gata-Níjar Natural Park. Built to defend the coastline against Barbary pirate attacks, the well-preserved battery stands on a fossilized sand dune overlooking the Mediterranean Sea, providing a historic landmark amidst the dramatic coastal scenery.
