Explore Burgos

A Brief History

Burgos formed the heart of the medieval Kingdom of Castile and the starting point of many Camino de Santiago pilgrims crossing the Meseta. Its thirteenth-century cathedral, one of the first Gothic churches in Spain, crowns a hill above the Arlanzón where Visigothic and later Christian councils met. Chroniclers and local tradition link the city to El Cid, while arcaded streets, the Miraflores Charterhouse, and the archaeological site of Atapuerca nearby anchor Burgos in Castilian state-building.

Cartuja de Miraflores

The Cartuja de Miraflores in Burgos is a Gothic palace-turned-monastery that dates back to the 1400s. Once a royal residence, it now stands as captivating monuments from the late European Gothic era. The monastery houses remarkable tombs of Isabel la Catolica's parents, crafted by Gil de Siloe, and features an ornate gold altarpiece brought from Christopher Columbus' second trip to the Americas.

Burgos Cathedral

Burgos Cathedral is a Gothic structure in Burgos, Spain, known for its rich history and architecture. It played a significant role in introducing the Gothic style to the country during the Middle Ages. The cathedral features 38 altarpieces, numerous chapels, and intricate religious artwork. It also serves as the final resting place of El Cid, adding to its historical importance.

Free

Church of San Nicolás de Bari

Saint Nicolás of Bari church is a in Burgos, offering travellers a unique experience to learn about the city's emblematic monuments. The church is located near the Castle of Burgos and provides white stone carvings inside, making it stand out from other embellished churches in the area. Additionally, travellers can explore the small museum and sacristy at the back of the church as part of their entry fee.

Free

Mirador Del Castillo

When visiting Burgos, Spain, make sure to include Mirador Del Castillo in itinerary. This popular attraction offers panoramic views of the city and its surroundings from the top of Burgos Hill. Located at Castillo de Burgos, a medieval castle with remaining walls dating back to 884 AD, this viewpoint provides an excellent vantage point for planning exploration of the city. While at the castle, take time to appreciate its historical significance as a defensive structure.

Free

Castillo de Burgos

Castillo de Burgos is a medieval fortress in Burgos, Spain. It was built as a defensive structure in the year 884 by count Diego Porcelos and played an important role in the Reconquista, which was a historical process of Christian Kingdoms seeking to control the Muslim rule. Today, the ruins of the castle stand at 75m above the city and have been inhabited since prehistory.

Free

Casa Quintanilla

on the Calle Paloma, just a stone's throw from Plaza Mayor, Casa Quintanilla is a delightful culinary gem that transports back to 1880. This quaint shop is a haven for food lovers eager to indulge in local delicacies. Here, savor Burgos' renowned black pudding and fresh cheese, alongside an impressive selection of regional wines, oils, and dried meats.

Free

Arco de Santa María

Arco de Santa María is a medieval gate that was restored in the 16th century and features intricate carved figures. It has been repurposed for art exhibitions and cultural events. The gate is one of the twelve entrances to the city from the Middle Ages, showcasing its historical significance.

€9

The Huelgas Monastery

The Huelgas Monastery, a Cistercian convent dating back to 1187, is a must-visit in Burgos. The monastery boasts ornate cloisters, royal tombs, and a textile museum. While exploring Burgos, travellers can also check out the Cathedral, the Miraflores Carthusian Monastery, the Evolution Museum, San Esteban Church, and the Retablo Museums.

Free

Iglesia de San Lesmes Abad

Iglesia de San Lesmes Abad, In Burgos, Spain, is a popular historical landmark dedicated to the city's patron saint. The church is known for its and ornate altars and houses the remains of San Lesmes. It's recommended to visit this church as it offers a glimpse into the city's rich cultural heritage. Adjacent to it is Paseo del Espolon, another must-visit spot in Burgos.

Free

Freedom Square

in Burgos, Restaurante El Fogón de Jesusón is a cozy culinary gem that blends 20 years of experience and Oriental influences. This family-run establishment, born from the dream of a father and son, aims to leave every diner with a smile. The restaurant's proposal harmoniously fuses the of Mediterranean and Asian cuisine, using local products with a distinctive Asian touch.
