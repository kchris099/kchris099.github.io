Burgos

Burgos formed the the medieval Kingdom of Castile and the starting point of many Camino de Santiago pilgrims crossing the Meseta. Its thirteenth-century cathedral, one of the first Gothic churches in Spain, crowns a hill above the Arlanzón where Visigothic and later Christian councils met. Chroniclers and local tradition link the city to El Cid, while arcaded streets, the Miraflores Charterhouse, and the archaeological site of Atapuerca nearby anchor Burgos in Castilian state-building.
