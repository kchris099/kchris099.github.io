Destination Guide — Eastern Spain

Explore Tarragona

A Brief History

Established as a regional settlement beyond an older fortified core, the city developed through industrialization in the late 19th century, post-war reconstruction, and modern civic expansion that reshaped its public spaces, markets, and surrounding landscape. Restored religious buildings, civic buildings, and natural features now define the contemporary visitor experience.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

The Ferreres Aqueduct

The Ferreres Aqueduct, also known as Pont de les Ferreres and Pont del Diable, is an ancient Roman aqueduct located just 4 kilometers north of Tarragona. This UNESCO World Heritage Site was built in the 1st century to supply water to the city of Tarraco. Today, it is a popular spot for scenic walks and picnics, offering a breath of fresh air away from the crowds.

Free

Roman Amphitheater of Tarragona

The Amfiteatre de Tarragona is a historic Roman amphitheater located on the coast, overlooking the Mediterranean Sea. Dating back to the late 1st century or early 2nd century BC, it was once a center for gruesome gladiator fights and public spectacles, as well as animal hunts. With a seating capacity of around 14,000 people, it was a prominent venue for entertainment and important events during its prime.

Free

Balcón del Mediterráneo

The Mediterranean Balcony, also known as Balcon del Mediterraneo, is a popular observation deck located in Tarragona, Spain. Positioned at the end of Rambla Nova, it offers views of the Mediterranean Sea, the port, the beach, and the Roman amphitheater. travellers can reach this spot by taking a gradual ascent via stairs or an elevated walkway from the train station's western side.

Free

Circ Roma

Circ Romà is an ancient Roman circus dating back to the 1st century CE, serving as a venue for chariot racing events in Tarragona. Today, ongoing excavation work provides insight into its vibrant past. travellers can explore the ruins and access a free audio guide detailing the site's history and usage. The circus once accommodated up to 30,000 spectators and was gradually encompassed by medieval Tarragona.

Free

Catedral de Tarragona

The Catedral de Tarragona, also known as Santa Maria del Miracle, is a architectural marvel that combines Romanesque and Gothic styles. Built between the 12th and 14th centuries on the grounds of a former Roman temple and mosque, this cathedral showcases the transition from the serene Romanesque apse to the dynamic Gothic architecture.

Free

Muralla Romana de Tarragona

The medieval walled city of Tarragona is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and contains many ancient Roman structures, including the Roman Circus, Amphitheatre, and the Roman and paleo-Christian necropolis. The wall surrounding the city is approximately 3.5 kilometers long, dating back to the 3rd century BCE. The walk takes about an hour and is well worth it for any history buff!.

Free

Central Market of Tarragona

The Central Market of Tarragona, also known as Mercat Central de Tarragona, is a lively indoor market hall that features a variety of vendors including butchers, bakers, and artisanal food stalls. The market is located in the medieval Carmen neighborhood, surrounded by historical landmarks such as the Cathedral and its famous bell tower.

Free

Castellers Monument

The Monument als Castellers is an 11-meter tall bronze sculpture created by Francesc Angles Garcia, dedicated to the tradition of Castells. These are human towers formed through a combination of teamwork and strength. The monument is located in Tarragona, Catalonia and is a popular tourist destination. travellers can also explore the nearby Roman Amphitheatre and chariot tracks while enjoying walks in this warm and welcoming area with friendly locals.

Free

Roman theatre of Tàrraco

The Roman theatre of Tàrraco is a significant historical site in Tarragona, known for its well-preserved ruins dating back to the 2nd century. Designated as a UNESCO World Heritage Site, it features remnants of an amphitheater, aqueducts, religious buildings, city walls, and part of the Aurelian Way. The theater complex also includes the Arch of Bera and the Tower of the Scipios along the Via Augusta.

Free

Plaça del Mercadal

Plaça del Mercadal is a must-visit spot on the Modernista Route, offering a great starting point with its emblematic square. Here, admire the Casa Navas designed by Domenech Montaner and explore its fabrics shop with original fittings from the early 20th century.

Free

Plaça de les Peixateries Velles

Old Fishmongers' Square is a historical and charming square that offers a tranquil setting to savor an authentic Vermut beverage outdoors. To enhance your visit, indulge in the delightful combination of Vermut, olives, and crisps. This Sunday midday tradition is widely visited! The ambiance is pleasant and inviting, with an array of delectable tapas and snacks available. Additionally, delight in their excellent selection of house wine at a reasonable price.

Free

Cala Fonda

Cala Fonda, also known as Waikiki Beach, is a secluded cove near Tarragona, Catalonia. It is surrounded by a fragrant pine forest and towering yellow limestone cliffs. The beach features fine golden sand and shallow warm waters, making it an ideal spot for nature lovers and nudists.

Free

PortAventura World

Located in Tarragona, Spain, PortAventura World is a seaside theme park that offers an array of attractions for travellers. The park is divided into six thematic areas: the Mediterranean, Polynesia, China, Mexico, the Far West and Sesamo Aventura. Each area features rides and shows related to its theme. From state-of-the-art roller coasters like Dragon Khan in China to live entertainment and water slides, there's something for everyone at this popular European destination.

Free

PortAventura Park

PortAventura Park is a world-building theme park divided into five areas representing different continents, each with buildings and rides reflecting the culture of the country. The park features traditional entertainment facilities, roller coasters, rapids, 4D cinemas, and more. With over 1 million square meters of landscape and 30,000 plant species, it offers a captivating experience.

Free

Karting Salou

Karting Salou is a popular attraction in the area, offering races suitable for both young adults and children. The circuit spans 900 meters for adults and 300 meters for children aged 4 to 13. One of its main draws is the Bungee Rocket, which launches two people to a height of 90 meters at a speed of 180 km/h in just 1.3 seconds.

Free

Mercat Central de Reus

The Mercat Central de Reus, built in 1915, is a vibrant upscale market offering a wide variety of goods including produce, fruit, baked goods, flowers and take-out food. The historic building now features modern escalators leading to an underground grocery store. It stands out as and most popular markets in the area. travellers can immerse themselves in local flavors by exploring the fresh products such as fruits, vegetables, fish and shellfish available here.

Free

Bar Jardí de la Casa Rull

Nestled in the charming city of Reus, Bar Jardí de la Casa Rull is a delightful escape from the hustle and bustle. Housed within a building characterized by its brown brick facade and intricate stone detailing, this bar has transformed into a vibrant cultural hub. The new management has elevated its ambiance with lighting and an inviting musical backdrop that enhances your experience.

Free

Diadegranja com

The Museu Arqueològic Salvador Vilaseca is a delightful destination for art and history enthusiasts alike. On the ground floor, travellers can marvel at an impressive exhibition dedicated to the works of Mariano Fortuny, showcasing a captivating array of paintings, prints, and drawings that highlight his artistic genius.

Free

Institut Pere Mata de Reus

Institut Pere Mata is a attraction in Reus, known for its outstanding modernist architecture. The building was constructed in 1900 as Gaudi's first private commission and showcases the Noucentisme style with architecture by renowned designers. travellers can take a stroll through Reus to explore nearly 80 modernist buildings, including the Institut Pere Mata.
