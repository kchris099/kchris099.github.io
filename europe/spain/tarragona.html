Explore Tarragona

A Brief History

Tarragona is in Spain. Established as a regional settlement beyond an older fortified core, the city developed through industrialization in the late 19th century, post-war reconstruction, and modern civic expansion that reshaped its public spaces, markets, and surrounding landscape. Museums, markets, and street plans in Tarragona still show layers from those periods.

Top Sights & Activities

The Ferreres Aqueduct

The Ferreres Aqueduct, also known as Pont de les Ferreres and Pont del Diable, is an ancient Roman aqueduct located just 4 kilometers north of Tarragona. This UNESCO World Heritage Site was built in the 1st century to supply water to the city of Tarraco. Today, it is a popular spot for scenic walks and picnics, offering a breath of fresh air away from the crowds.

€5

Roman Amphitheater of Tarragona

The Amfiteatre de Tarragona is a historic Roman amphitheater located on the coast, overlooking the Mediterranean Sea. Dating back to the late 1st century or early 2nd century BC, it was once a center for gruesome gladiator fights and public spectacles, as well as animal hunts. With a seating capacity of around 14,000 people, it was a prominent venue for entertainment and important events during its prime.

Free

Balcón del Mediterráneo

The Mediterranean Balcony, also known as Balcon del Mediterraneo, is a popular observation deck located in Tarragona, Spain. Positioned at the end of Rambla Nova, it offers views of the Mediterranean Sea, the port, the beach, and the Roman amphitheater. Travellers can reach this spot by taking a gradual ascent via stairs or an elevated walkway from the train station's western side.

Free

Circ Roma

Circ Romà is an ancient Roman circus dating back to the 1st century CE, serving as a venue for chariot racing events in Tarragona. Today, ongoing excavation work provides insight into its active past. Travellers can explore the ruins and access a free audio guide detailing the site's history and usage. The circus once accommodated up to 30,000 spectators and was gradually include by medieval Tarragona.

€12

Catedral de Tarragona

Catedral de Tarragona, also known as Santa Maria del Miracle, is a visitor site in Tarragona, Spain. The Catedral de Tarragona, also known as Santa Maria del Miracle, is a notable building that combines Romanesque and Gothic styles. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Free

Muralla Romana de Tarragona

The medieval walled city of Tarragona is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and contains many ancient Roman structures, including the Roman Circus, Amphitheatre, and the Roman and paleo-Christian necropolis. The wall surrounding the city is approximately 3.5 kilometers long, dating back to the 3rd century BCE. The walk takes about an hour and is well worth it for any history buff!

Free

Central Market of Tarragona

Central Market of Tarragona, also known as Mercat Central de Tarragona, is a market in Tarragona, Spain. The market is located in the medieval Carmen neighborhood, surrounded by historical landmarks such as the Cathedral and its famous bell tower. Stalls sell food and goods during regular market hours.

Free

Castellers Monument

The Monument als Castellers is an 11-meter tall bronze sculpture created by Francesc Angles Garcia, dedicated to the tradition of Castells. These are human towers formed through a combination of teamwork and strength. The monument is located in Tarragona, Catalonia and is a popular tourist destination. Travellers can also explore the nearby Roman Amphitheatre and chariot tracks while enjoying walks in this warm and welcoming area with friendly locals.

Free

Roman theatre of Tàrraco

Roman theatre of Tàrraco is a theatre in Tarragona, Spain. The Roman theatre of Tàrraco is a significant historical site in Tarragona, known for its well-preserved ruins dating back to the 2nd century. The theater complex also includes the Arch of Bera and the Tower of the Scipios along the Via Augusta. Public access is usually through ticketed performances or daytime visits.

Free

Plaça del Mercadal

Plaça del Mercadal is a must-visit spot on the Modernista Route, offering a great starting point with its emblematic square. Here, admire the Casa Navas designed by Domenech Montaner and explore its fabrics shop with original fittings from the early 20th century.

Free

Plaça de les Peixateries Velles

Plaça de les Peixateries Velles is a visitor site in Tarragona, Spain. To enhance a visit, indulge in the combination of Vermut, olives, and crisps. This Sunday midday tradition is widely visited! The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Free

Cala Fonda

Cala Fonda, also known as Waikiki Beach, is a visitor site in Tarragona, Spain. Cala Fonda, also known as Waikiki Beach, is a secluded cove near Tarragona, Catalonia. It is surrounded by a fragrant pine forest and towering yellow limestone cliffs. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Free

PortAventura World

PortAventura World is a waterfront area in Tarragona, Spain. Located in Tarragona, Spain, PortAventura World is a seaside theme park that offers an array of attractions for travellers. The park is divided into six thematic areas: the Mediterranean, Polynesia, China, Mexico, the Far West and Sesamo Aventura. Quays and embankment paths form the main walking route.

Free

PortAventura Park

PortAventura Park is a public park in Tarragona, Spain. PortAventura Park is a world-building theme park divided into five areas representing different continents, each with buildings and rides reflecting the culture of the country. With over 1 million square meters of landscape and 30,000 plant species, it offers a experience. Paths, lawns, and shaded areas make up the main public facilities.

Free

Karting Salou

Karting Salou is a popular attraction in the area, offering races suitable for both young adults and children. The circuit spans 900 meters for adults and 300 meters for children aged 4 to 13. One of its main draws is the Bungee Rocket, which launches two people to a height of 90 meters at a speed of 180 km/h in just 1.3 seconds.

Free

Mercat Central de Reus

Mercat Central de Reus is a visitor site in Tarragona, Spain. Construction and early use date to around 1915. The Mercat Central de Reus, built in 1915, is a active upscale market offering a wide variety of goods including produce, fruit, baked goods, flowers and take-out food. It stands out as and most popular markets in the area. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Free

Bar Jardí de la Casa Rull

Bar Jardí de la Casa Rull is a visitor site in Tarragona, Spain. Housed within a building characterized by its brown brick facade and detailed stone detailing, this bar has transformed into a active cultural hub. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Free

Diadegranja com

Diadegranja com is a visitor site in Tarragona. The Museu Arqueològic Salvador Vilaseca is a destination for art and history enthusiasts alike. On the ground floor, travellers can marvel at an exhibition dedicated to the works of Mariano Fortuny, showing a array of paintings, prints, and drawings that highlight his artistic genius.

Free

Institut Pere Mata de Reus

Institut Pere Mata is a attraction in Reus, known for its outstanding modernist architecture. The building was constructed in 1900 as Gaudi's first private commission and showcases the Noucentisme style with architecture by known designers. Travellers can take a stroll through Reus to explore nearly 80 modernist buildings, including the Institut Pere Mata.
