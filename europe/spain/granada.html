Destination Guide — Southern Spain

Explore Granada

A Brief History

Granada was the last Muslim kingdom on the Iberian Peninsula until its surrender to the Catholic Monarchs in 1492, ending centuries of Nasrid rule centered on the Alhambra palaces and Generalife gardens. The Albaicín quarter, cathedral, and royal chapel preserve Moorish street patterns and later Christian institutions. Snow-fed irrigation and mountain trade routes linked the city to the Sierra Nevada and the coast.

Top Sights & Activities

Alhambra

The Alhambra is a Nasrid palace and fortress complex begun in the 13th century on Sabika hill above Granada. Halls, courtyards, and walls combine Islamic geometry with later Christian additions after 1492. Timed tickets regulate access to the palaces, fortress, and surrounding gardens.

€19

Generalife Gardens

The Generalife served as a summer estate and orchard for Nasrid rulers beside the Alhambra. Terraced gardens, cypress alleys, and irrigation channels illustrate Andalusian landscape design. Paths connect pavilions and viewpoints above the Darro valley and city below.

€19

Patio de la Acequia

Patio de la Acequia is the main courtyard of the Generalife with a long pool flanked by flower beds and arcades. The channel supplied water to terraced gardens on the hillside. The patio remains one of the most photographed spaces within the Nasrid garden complex.

€5

Catedral de Granada

Granada Cathedral was built from 1523 over the former mosque after the Catholic Monarchs took the city. Diego de Siloé and later architects shaped its Renaissance and Baroque interior. The Royal Chapel beside the cathedral holds tombs of Ferdinand and Isabella.

€5

Palacio de los Córdova

Palacio de los Córdova is a 16th-century mansion on the edge of the Albaicín with later modifications. Its patios and gardens overlook the Alhambra from the opposite hill. The building now hosts municipal cultural uses and public access to terraces with panoramic views.

€2

Palacio de la Madraza

La Madraza was founded in 1349 as a Nasrid university beside the cathedral. Surviving oratory decoration and later Baroque chapel illustrate the transition from Islamic to Christian institutions in the city center. The building belongs to the University of Granada.

Free

Corral del Carbón

Corral del Carbón is a 14th-century Nasrid caravanserai near the present market streets. Its arched entrance and courtyard served merchants storing goods in the city center. Restored in the 20th century, it now hosts cultural events and remains an early example of Islamic commercial architecture.

Free

Basílica Virgen de las Angustias

The basilica of Our Lady of Sorrows is Granada's principal Baroque church and focus of local devotion. Built from the 17th century on a pilgrimage site, its interior houses the venerated image of the Virgin. The church stands near the Genil river in the expanded city center.

€3

Huerta de San Vicente

Huerta de San Vicente was the summer house of Federico García Lorca's family on the edge of Granada. The modest villa and garden preserve rooms where the poet spent periods before his death in 1936. A museum presents manuscripts, furniture, and family history within the orchard setting.

Free

Carmen de los Mártires

Carmen de los Mártires is a 19th-century estate that invites travellers to explore its beautifully landscaped gardens, complete with enchanting statues and fountains. Spanning seven hectares on the picturesque Sabika hill, adjacent to the Alhambra, this park features diverse areas such as a French Baroque garden centered around a grand pond with a Neptune statue, and the Palm Garden adorned with an impressive three-tiered fountain.

Free

Nasrid Palaces

Nestled within the enchanting Alhambra, the Nasrid Palaces are a testament to Moorish architecture and history. travellers can wander through opulent courtyards, grand reception halls, and regal quarters that showcase intricate designs and exquisite craftsmanship. The palaces offer a captivating glimpse into the past with their unique inscriptions, vibrant painted decorations, and an array of beautiful antiques.

Free

Patio de Los Leones

Patio de Los Leones is a courtyard located within the Alhambra complex in Granada, Spain. The centerpiece of this Moorish palace is the famed Fountain of Twelve Lions made of white marble, surrounded by finely decorated columns and graceful archways. The lions guarding the fountain make it an incomparable spectacle that has captivated travellers for years. The intricate details carved into each tile add to the beauty of this unique place.

Free

Palace of Charles V

The Palace of Charles V is a Renaissance-style residence located within the Alhambra complex. Built between 1526 and 1637, it holds special significance for Charles V, serving as a tribute to his grandparents and symbolizing Catholic dominance in Granada. The grand circular courtyard with its highest colonnade completed in 1619 offers a striking view of the Andalucian sky. Today, the palace houses museums and stands as a reminder of historical power struggles in Spain.

Free

El Partal

El Partal is a captivating area within the Alhambra complex that holds the remnants of King Muhammad III's former palace. The site features the striking El Partal palace with its five-arched portico, a central pool, and the ancient Torre de las Damas. This well-preserved example of Nasrid architecture showcases traditional Islamic design and materials, reflecting the legendary paradise gardens of Moorish culture.

€6

Alcazaba

The Alcazaba is a 13th-century military citadel located within the Alhambra complex in Granada. The name "Alcazaba" comes from the Arabic word for Citadel and it served as a strategic fortification with sweeping views from its main watchtower, Torre de la Vela. This ancient structure was used by Moorish rulers to oversee their territory and is characterized by its massive walls and old ramparts.

Free

Mirador de San Nicolás

Mirador de San Nicolás is a bustling square situated on an elevated hilltop in Granada, offering panoramic views of the Alhambra. It's renowned for its dramatic sunset vistas and is considered spots to admire the fortress from afar. The square is located in an old neighborhood and provides unobstructed views of the entire Alhambra, making it a must-visit location.

Free

Mezquita Mayor de Granada

The Mezquita Mayor de Granada, built in 2003, is the first mosque constructed in the city since the 1400s. It offers various services and community outreach programs. The mosque features a prayer hall, surrounding gardens, an Islamic studies center, and a library. travellers can attend lectures, Arabic classes, exhibitions, and courses on Islam to learn about the culture and spirituality of the religion.

Free

Puerta de Elvira

Puerta de Elvira is a historic city gate in Granada, dating back to the 11th century when the city was under Islamic rule. It has witnessed centuries of history and reflects diverse cultural influences. Named after Princess Elvira, it served as the official gate to the city during Moorish rule. Despite renovations, its location and structure have remained largely unchanged. The gate is a in Granada, offering a glimpse into the region's rich and storied past.

Free

Palacio de Dar al-Horra

Nestled in the historic Albaicin district, the Palacio de Dar al-Horra stands as a testament to the rich tapestry of Granada's past. Constructed in the 15th century atop remnants of an earlier Zirid palace from the 11th century, this intimate residence was home to Aisha, a significant figure in Nasrid history and mother of Boabdil, Granada’s last emir. The architecture beautifully reflects typical Spanish-Islamic styles while offering views of the city.

Free

Mirador de San Cristóbal

Mirador de San Cristóbal is a stone observation point in Granada that offers panoramic views of the city. It is wheelchair accessible and provides a less crowded alternative to other viewpoints. From here, travellers can see the old city wall and landmarks such as the Alhambra, cathedral, Palace of Dar al-Horra, and Convent of Santa Isabel la Catolica. The location also allows for a picturesque view of Granada from the west, framed by the Sierra Nevada mountain.

Free

Cuarto Real de Santo Domingo

Cuarto Real de Santo Domingo is a 13th-century Nasrid palace in Granada, once belonging to Axa, the mother of King Boabdil. The palace features a beautifully decorated qubba, or reception hall, and original excavations. It's not widely known but offers a peaceful atmosphere with Islamic history and lovely gardens. The palace has decorations similar to the Alhambra and hosts temporary art exhibitions in its modern extension.

Free

Fuente de las Batallas

The Fuente de las Batallas (Fountain of the Battles) is a prominent, historic fountain located in the bustling center of Granada, at the intersection of major avenues. The ornate fountain serves as a popular meeting point and a traditional site for public celebrations, particularly for fans of the local football club after significant victories.

Free

Tablao Flamenco La Alborea Granada

Nestled in the heart of Granada, just a stone's throw from Plaza Nueva, Tablao Flamenco La Alborea offers an enchanting experience for those eager to immerse themselves in the passionate world of flamenco. This intimate venue accommodates around 50 guests, ensuring that every seat provides an unobstructed view of the captivating performances.

Free

Mirador Placeta de Carvajales

The Mirador Placeta de Carvajales is a beautiful cobblestone plaza with views of Alhambra, which is famous medieval palaces in Spain. This spot is popular with dog owners and public drinkers alike, and has a pretty atmosphere with trees and a reflecting pond.

Free

Palacio de los Olvidados

In Granada's Arabic neighborhood, Palacio de los Olvidados is a museum housed in a historic aristocratic home. It delves into Spain's dark past, particularly the period of deadly extremism following the overthrow of the Moors. The main exhibit showcases torture devices used during this time, offering a glimpse into this shameful era without trivializing it.

Free

Hammam Al Ándalus

Hammam Al Ándalus is a serene venue offering traditional Turkish baths and other spa services. The exquisite Arab baths provide a perfect setting to relax and immerse yourself in the unique atmosphere of the Nasrid era. With various pools of different temperatures, steam baths, and relaxing massages, it offers a rewarding experience.

Free

Basílica de San Juan de Dios

Nestled in the heart of Granada, the Basílica de San Juan de Dios is a example of 18th-century Baroque architecture that simply cannot be missed. Constructed between 1737 and 1759, this magnificent basilica dazzles with its opulent gold and silver embellishments that adorn nearly every inch of its interior.

Free

Monasterio de San Jerónimo, Granada

The Monastery of San Jerónimo is a magnificent Renaissance architectural complex located in central Granada. Founded by the Catholic Monarchs in the late 15th century, the monastery features a impressive, very decorated church interior with a massive, intricate altarpiece. It also houses the tomb of the 'Great Captain,' Gonzalo Fernández de Córdoba.

Free

Alcaicería

Alcaicería is a historic Arabic-style bazaar in Granada, now located in narrow alleyways between the cathedral and Plaza Bib-Rambla. Once a sprawling marketplace for North African spices, silks, fabrics, and ceramics, it was reduced to its current size after a fire in the 19th century. Despite its smaller scale, Alcaicería still offers boutique stalls selling shawls, scarves, jewelry, and handbags with intricate Moorish designs.

Free

Plaza Isabel La Católica

Plaza Isabel La Católica sits within the Corral del Carbón area of Granada, documented in local heritage registers for its civic or cultural role. Visitors approach from the main access road and combine the visit with neighbouring heritage buildings and public spaces in the district.

Free

Biodomo Granada

The Biodomo is an immersive, indoor bio-park located within the Parque de las Ciencias in Granada. The facility recreates tropical environments from the Amazon, Madagascar, and the Indo-Pacific, housing over 250 species of animals and plants. It focuses on environmental education and conservation, allowing visitors to walk through lush, climate-controlled habitats.

Free

San Miguel Alto viewpoint

Saint Michael Viewpoint, also known as Mirador de San Miguel, is a popular destination in Granada offering panoramic views of the city and the Alhambra. Located at Ermita de San Miguel Alto, it requires a bit of effort to reach as travellers need to climb up a small mountain. The viewpoint provides photographic opportunities and is situated in a more rural area beyond the narrow streets of The Albaicin.

Free

Monasterio de Nuestra Señora de la Asunción "La Cartuja"

Monasterio de Nuestra Señora de la Asunción "La Cartuja" sits within the Monasterio de Nuestra Señora de la Asunción "La Cartuja" area of Granada, documented in local heritage registers for its civic or cultural role. Visitors approach from the main access road and combine the visit with neighbouring heritage buildings and public spaces in the district.

Free

Plaza de Toros de Granada

The Plaza de Toros de Granada is an impressive bullring that showcases the rich cultural heritage of Spain. With its striking Neo-Mudejar architecture and grand brick archways, this venue can accommodate up to 14,500 spectators for thrilling bullfights and lively outdoor concerts. travellers can enjoy guided tours that delve into the history of this site, allowing them to step inside the ring and even hold a traditional pink cape.

Free

Generalife

The Generalife, an ancient Muslim palace, is renowned for its exquisite ornamental gardens that feature serene pools, charming courtyards, and a riot of vibrant blooms. When visiting Granada, it's essential to dedicate at least three to four hours to explore this complex. If time allows, easily spend an entire day marveling at the intricate Moorish architecture found throughout both the Alhambra and Generalife.

Free

The Moor’s Chair (Saint Helen's Castle)

The Moor’s Chair (Saint Helen's Castle) sits within the Generalife area of Granada, documented in local heritage registers for its civic or cultural role. Visitors approach from the main access road and combine the visit with neighbouring heritage buildings and public spaces in the district.

Free

Carmen de la Victoria

Nestled in the enchanting Albaicín district, El Carmen de la Victoria is a hidden gem owned by the University of Granada. This charming building offers views of the Alhambra and features beautifully maintained gardens, fountains, and an orchard that invite you to unwind. Accessing this serene spot is simple; just knock on the door for free entry. Once inside, savor a delightful drink at the café while soaking up the tranquil atmosphere.

Free

P.º de los Tristes

Paseo de los Tristes, or the Promenade of the Sad Ones, is a charming and vibrant walkway in Granada that beautifully captures the essence of this historic city. Nestled alongside the River Darro, it meanders between two picturesque bridges—the Moorish Las Chirimias and El Aljibillo—offering views of the Alhambra.

Free

Trinity Square

Trinity Square (Plaza de la Trinidad) is a lively, historic public square located in the center of Granada. It is surrounded by elegant 19th-century buildings and features a central fountain and numerous outdoor cafes. The square is a popular gathering place for locals and students, offering a relaxed atmosphere in the heart of the city's commercial district.

Free

Royal Chapel of Granada

The Royal Chapel of Granada, built in the 1500s, is a mausoleum for Catholic royalty and also serves as a museum. It is a popular tourist attraction located next to the Granada Cathedral. The chapel is famous for being the burial place of Isabella and Ferdinand II, two powerful Catholic Monarchs who completed the reconquering of Spain from the Moors. The beautiful tomb made from alabaster was designed by Italian sculptor Domenico Fancelli.
