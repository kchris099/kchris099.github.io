Explore Malmo

A Brief History

Malmö lies at the eastern end of the Öresund strait opposite Copenhagen and was a Danish town until the Treaty of Roskilde in 1658 transferred Skåne to Sweden. St. Peter's Church and the Malmöhus castle date from the Middle Ages. Shipbuilding and industry grew in the 19th century. The Öresund Bridge opened in 2000, linking the city by rail and road to Denmark. Turning Torso tower marks recent waterfront development.

Top Sights & Activities

Malmo Castle

Founded in 1434 and rebuilt by Christian III of Denmark in the 1530s, this waterfront fortress is among Scandinavia's oldest surviving Renaissance castles. Its mint, dungeons, and cannons recall wars between Denmark and Sweden. Museums inside cover city history, shipping, and natural science.

Free

Malmo City Hall

Built in Dutch Renaissance style and completed around 1546, Malmo's town hall faces Stortorget with a stepped gable and ornate entrance. Council chambers and courtrooms still operate inside. The facade reflects the city's Hanseatic trade wealth when Skåne belonged to Denmark.

Free

Stortorget Malmo

Stortorget has served as Malmo's main square since the 16th century, surrounded by town hall, merchants' houses, and cafes. Markets and public events still fill the cobbled space. A bronze equestrian statue of Karl X Gustav recalls the Swedish conquest of Skåne in 1658.

Free

Optimistorkestern

Yngve Lundell's 1985 sculpture shows seven bronze musicians marching across Gustav Adolfs torg. The Optimist Orchestra became a popular meeting point in the city center. Children often climb the figures while shoppers pass along the adjoining pedestrian streets.

Free

Centralposthuset

Designed by Ferdinand Boberg and opened in 1906, this National post office faces the central station with a tower and granite base. Telegraph and sorting halls have given way to shops and offices, but arched windows and brick ornament remain landmarks of early 20th-century civic design.

Free

Turning Torso

Turning Torso is a remarkable and energy-efficient tower block located in Vastra hamnen, Malmo. Designed by Santiago Calatrava, this neo-futurist skyscraper stands at 190 meters with 54 stories, making it the tallest building in the Nordics. Its distinctive twisted shell turns 90 degrees from bottom to top, creating a magnificent sight for travellers.

Free

Kungsparken, Malmö

Kungsparken is a picturesque park in Malmö, featuring beautiful gardens, sculptures, and a serene atmosphere perfect for relaxation. The site forms part of Kungsparken, Malmö's documented civic and cultural landscape within Sweden, with archives and visitor information available from municipal or regional heritage authorities.

Free

Malmö City Library

The Malmö City Library is a captivating structure located in a riverside park, featuring a blend of historic and modern architecture. Comprising three distinct buildings, including The Castle with its Renaissance-inspired design, The Cylinder housing the entrance and a cozy cafe, and The Calendar of Light which hosts the main book collection and study areas.

Free

Malmö Opera

In Malmo, Sweden, Malmö Opera is a striking architectural marvel with its sleek glass facade and unique shape. The opera house offers a diverse range of music theatre performances, focusing on opera, musicals, and contemporary musical dramas. It also showcases newly composed Swedish operas and musicals to support emerging talents in the industry.

Free

St. Peter's Church

When it comes to Protestant churches, one cannot go wrong by visiting St. Peter's Church in Goran Olstagan. This historical church is steeped in history and features beautiful bright Romanesque inspired interiors that are sure to please travellers of all faiths. Besides its historical significance, the church also boasts charming displays, chandeliers, and sacred sculptures that make it a for anyone visiting the area.

Free

Emporia Shopping Center

Nestled in the vibrant Hyllie neighborhood, Emporia Shopping Center stands as a beacon of modern retail and architectural brilliance. Spanning an impressive 70,000 square meters, this massive indoor mall boasts over 160 shops, restaurants, and cafes that cater to every shopping whim imaginable—from fashion to home decor. Designed by renowned architect Gert Wingardh, its striking wave-like exterior and amber-hued entrance create a visual spectacle that beckons travellers inside.

Free

Malmö Arena

Malmö Arena is a versatile venue that hosts various events such as sporting competitions, concerts, conferences, and family shows. It features a restaurant area with 3,250 seats and 20 fast food outlets. The arena is also the home field of the local hockey team Redhawks. travellers can enjoy performances by renowned artists in solo concerts. Situated in the Oresund Region, it offers top-notch facilities for wining and dining or grabbing a quick snack.

Free

Katrinetorp Landeri

Nestled on the outskirts of Malmo, Katrinetorp Landeri is a charming 19th-century manor that offers a serene escape into nature. This picturesque estate features expansive gardens and an inviting restaurant and café, where travellers can savor authentic Swedish cuisine made from fresh produce grown right on-site. The tranquil surroundings include lovely walking paths through lush forests and peaceful ponds, making it an ideal spot for relaxation.

Free

Øresund Bridge

The Øresund Bridge is an engineering marvel connecting Sweden and Denmark, offering views of the surrounding waters. The site forms part of Øresund Bridge's documented civic and cultural landscape within Sweden, with archives and visitor information available from municipal or regional heritage authorities.
