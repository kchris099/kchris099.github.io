Explore Visby

A Brief History

Visby emerged as the preeminent center of the Hanseatic League in the Baltic Sea during the twelfth to fourteenth centuries, controlling trade between Russia and Western Europe. Its immense wealth financed the construction of magnificent limestone merchant houses and numerous churches within a massive defensive ring wall. The city's prominence declined following a Danish invasion in 1361 and subsequent pirate raids. Today, this frozen medieval prosperity is visible in the extraordinarily well-preserved city wall, the ruins of St. Nicholas Church, and the medieval street network.

Visby Town Wall

Visby Town Wall, also known as Ringmuren, is a well-preserved medieval defensive structure encircling the charming town of Visby on the island of Gotland in Sweden. Dating back to the 14th century, this remarkable stone fortification spans 3.4 kilometers and incorporates defensive towers. It was meticulously constructed over two centuries to safeguard the city against potential invasions.

Free

St. Nicolai Ruin

St. Nicolai Ruin is a well-preserved medieval church ruin in Visby, Sweden, dating back to the 13th century. It is one of the 12 church ruins in the area and stands out for its remarkable Gothic architecture. The walls are still relatively intact, adding to its historical charm. This historic site has been repurposed into an outdoor concert hall, making it a popular venue for music enthusiasts and history buffs alike.

Visby Cathedral

When visiting Visby, don't miss the awe-inspiring Visby Cathedral, also known as Sankta Maria kyrka. Dating back to the late 12th and early 13th centuries, this historic church boasts whimsical towers topped by baroque cupolas. Admire its beautiful stained-glass windows, ornate carved reredos, and carved floor slabs. The cathedral is a popular venue for intimate music concerts in the summer.

Lummelunda Cave

Lummelunda Cave offers guided tours through an ancient cave featuring fossils, stalactites, and captivating exhibitions. The friendly and knowledgeable staff provide an interesting discovery adventure story amidst beautiful rock formations. The surrounding area includes a nature trail leading to the beach and through the forest, making it a great place to spend warm or rainy days. The museum is fascinating and kid-friendly, offering a variety of tour options suitable for different preferences.

DBW's Botanical Garden

DBW's Botanical Garden is a picturesque oasis in Visby, featuring a diverse collection of native and exotic plants and trees. Established in 1855 by the Sallskapet DBW, the garden has undergone several renovations, including a major overhaul in the 1930s that shaped its current enchanting layout. Visitors can stroll along meandering paths and admire unusual plant species such as fig trees, mulberry trees, and a wide variety of roses that bloom well into late autumn.

South Hällarna Nature Reserve

Södra hällarna is a destination that attracts visitors from around the world. It offers views and diverse wildlife, including eagles, rabbits, hawks, foxes, woodpeckers, and sea gulls. Visitors are encouraged to maintain the cleanliness of the area by taking their garbage with them and showing respect for the environment. It's an ideal location for leisurely walks amidst nature's beauty.
