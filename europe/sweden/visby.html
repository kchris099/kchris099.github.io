Visby

Visby emerged as the preeminent center of the Hanseatic League in the Baltic Sea during the twelfth to fourteenth centuries, controlling trade between Russia and Western Europe. Its large wealth financed the construction of limestone merchant houses and numerous churches within a massive defensive ring wall. The city's prominence declined following a Danish invasion in 1361 and subsequent pirate raids. Today, this frozen medieval prosperity is visible in the extraordinarily well-preserved city wall, the ruins of St. Nicholas Church, and the medieval street network.

Visby Town Wall

Visby's stone wall encircles much of the medieval town with towers, gates, battlements and sections built from the thirteenth century onward. Defensive needs, trade wealth, conflict and later repair produced variations around the circuit. Roads and houses interrupt some views, while recognition covers the Hanseatic town as a whole.

02 AdmissionFree

St. Nicolai Ruin

St Nicolai in Visby is a thirteenth-century church ruin with walls still standing high enough to host outdoor concerts in summer. The roofless nave sits inside the medieval town. Grass floors and stone arches make it one of the clearer Hanseatic church shells on Gotland.

57.6442° N / 18.2985° E

03 AdmissionFree

Great Square

Stora Torget anchors the walled medieval core of Visby on Gotland, with cafes, shops, and civic buildings lining the open pavement. Market stalls and outdoor seating appear in warmer months, while the surrounding facades show layers of Hanseatic-era stone and later plaster. From the square, lanes lead toward the cathedral, the ring wall, and the harbour below the old town.

57.6406° N / 18.2961° E

04 AdmissionFree

Visby Cathedral

Visby Cathedral began as a church for German merchants and became the island's cathedral after the Reformation; unlike Visby's many ruined medieval churches, it remains in regular use, so services, concerts and parish activity shape access. Romanesque and Gothic forms, later towers, chapels, furnishings and restoration reflect a long active religious history within the walled town.

57.6419° N / 18.2980° E

Lummelunda Cave

Lummelunda Cave extends through limestone north of Visby, formed by underground water and revealed in stages through exploration, and water levels, weather and safety rules affect tours. Guided routes enter a prepared section with passages, chambers, streams and mineral formations, while most of the system remains inaccessible to ordinary visitors.

57.7382° N / 18.4059° E

06 AdmissionFree

Österport (Eastern Gate)

Osterport opens through the eastern side of Visby's medieval wall, connecting the old town with modern streets and shopping areas beyond. Pedestrians and nearby traffic keep the gate active as a threshold in the present city. Its pointed passage, adjoining towers and defensive masonry reflect several construction phases and later repairs. The passage still links two distinct parts of Visby.

57.6385° N / 18.2985° E

07 AdmissionFree

DBW's Botanical Garden

Founded in 1855 by Sällskapet DBW and reshaped in a major 1930s renovation, Visby's botanical garden grows fig and mulberry trees plus roses that bloom into late autumn. Meandering paths lead through native and exotic plantings. The enclosed oasis sits inside the walled town's green belt.

57.6438° N / 18.2939° E

08 AdmissionFree

South Hällarna Nature Reserve

Södra hällarna is a destination that attracts visitors from around the world. It offers views and diverse wildlife, including eagles, rabbits, hawks, foxes, woodpeckers, and sea gulls. Visitors are encouraged to maintain the cleanliness of the area by taking their garbage with them and showing respect for the environment. It's an ideal location for leisurely walks amidst nature's beauty.

57.6227° N / 18.2741° E

09 AdmissionFree

Söderport (Southern Gate)

Soderport guards the southern approach to Visby's walled town, where the road once arrived from rural Gotland, and traffic now passes close to the monument, while the gate's importance comes from its position in the surviving circuit. A vaulted opening, tower, wall walks, and nearby defensive sections show medieval construction modified by later use and restoration.

57.6348° N / 18.2919° E

10 AdmissionFree

Galgberget, Visby

Galgberget is a limestone height north of Visby associated with the town's medieval and early modern execution site. Stone pillars remaining from the gallows stand in open ground with views toward the wall and sea. The landscape carries a history of punishment and exclusion.

57.6504° N / 18.3075° E
