Explore Gothenburg

A Brief History

Gothenburg was founded in 1621 as a fortified Dutch-planned port on Sweden's west coast to control trade with the North Sea and Atlantic. Canals, warehouses, and shipyards shaped its grid before 19th-century industry and later design institutions made it a centre of manufacturing and Scandinavian urban planning. Municipal archives, parish records, and surviving architecture help explain how the city connected to wider regional trade routes, court patronage, and modern civic rebuilding after industrial and wartime change.

Skansen Kronan

Skansen Kronan is a historic hilltop fortress in Gothenburg, built in 1697 to defend against Danish invasions. Over the years, it has served as a prison and military museum but now houses a restaurant and conference center. The adjacent former army barracks features a seasonal café with views over the city and harbor. Despite the challenging hike to reach the top, visitors are rewarded with a 360-degree panoramic view of Gothenburg.

Free

Feskekörka

Feskekörka is a historic indoor fish market in Gothenburg, known as the 'Fish Church' due to its architectural resemblance to a long chapel. Built in 1874 without pillars, it features various seafood counters and new restaurants offering national and international cuisine. Visitors can enjoy classic home cooking with street food twists, warm and familiar smoked flavors, herring, schnapps, oysters, and west coast tastes.

Free (guided tour kr175)

Gunnebo Palace and Gardens

Gunnebo Palace and Gardens is a Neoclassical country house in Molndal with landscaped grounds. The restaurant is also situated on the premises and offers food from the kitchen gardens. The site forms part of Gunnebo Palace and Gardens's documented civic and cultural landscape within Gothenburg, with archives and visitor information available from municipal or regional heritage authorities.

Liseberg

Liseberg is the largest amusement park in Scandinavia and a top tourist attraction in Gothenburg, Sweden. With over three million visitors annually, it offers a wide range of entertainment including theater shows, musical performances, and various rides such as Balder, a wooden roller coaster, the Liseberg Wheel for panoramic views, and thrilling attractions like Helix and AtmosFear.

Poseidon

Embark on a romantic adventure starting at the Poseidon Statue, a bronze sculpture that graces Gotaplatsen in central Gothenburg. Created by Carl Milles and unveiled in 1931, this captivating figure of the sea god is not just an artistic marvel but also a symbol of protection for sailors. After soaking in its beauty, meander over to the Gothenburg Museum of Art, where explore an impressive array of Nordic masterpieces spanning centuries.

Free

Gothenburg Cathedral

Nestled in the vibrant heart of Gothenburg, the Gothenburg Cathedral, or Gustavi Domkyrka, stands as a remarkable testament to early 19th-century architecture. This structure beautifully blends Gothic and neoclassical styles, making it a visual delight for visitors. Beyond its architectural allure, the cathedral serves as an essential hub for the local community, hosting a variety of events from religious services to captivating concerts and cultural gatherings.

Free

The Market Hall

The Market Hall, also known as Stora Saluhallen, is a historic indoor market in Gothenburg. Built in 1889, it's a remarkable architectural gem and the city's largest indoor marketplace. The hall features an array of vendors offering gourmet delights such as cheeses, baked goods, deli sandwiches, fresh fish, meat, produce, fruits and spices. Additionally, there are numerous restaurants where visitors can enjoy a delicious lunch after exploring the stalls.

Free

Gothenburg Botanical Garden

Gothenburg Botanical Garden, also known as Botaniska Tradgarden, is a sprawling and tranquil oasis that offers a serene escape from the city's hustle and bustle. Established in 1916, this expansive 432-acre garden is one of Europe's largest botanical gardens. It features over 16,000 plant species, including rare and endangered ones, housed in greenhouses with exhibitions.

IKEA Göteborg Bäckebol

IKEA Göteborg Bäckebol is a Scandinavian furniture chain offering ready-to-assemble furniture, textiles, lighting, and home decor. Despite expectations of it being cheap and efficient, the experience was quite the opposite due to Sweden's expensive nature. The store offers a relaxed atmosphere with helpful staff and a self-scan cash register system. Additionally, visitors can enjoy quality yet affordable food at the bistro with a wide range of vegetarian options.

Free

Göteborgsoperan

Göteborgsoperan, also known as the Gothenburg Opera House, is a versatile concert space that has been open since 1994. Situated in the heart of Gothenburg, this modern architectural marvel is a hub for various performing arts including opera, ballet, and musical theater. The venue's performances and innovative productions attract audiences from across Sweden and beyond.

Free

Masthugget Church

Masthugget Church, situated in the Masthugget district of Gothenburg, Sweden, is a remarkable example of Nordic architecture. This monumental brick church, completed in 1914 and designed by architect Sigfrid Ericson, boasts a 60-meter tower and offers panoramic views of Gothenburg. The church's Art Nouveau style and hilltop location make it a prominent city landmark.
