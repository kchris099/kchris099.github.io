Gothenburg

Gothenburg was founded in 1621 as a fortified Dutch-planned port on Sweden's west coast to control trade with the North Sea and Atlantic. Canals, warehouses, and shipyards shaped its grid before 19th-century industry and later design institutions made it a centre of manufacturing and Scandinavian urban planning. Municipal archives, parish records, and surviving architecture help explain how the city connected to wider regional trade routes, court patronage, and modern civic rebuilding after industrial and wartime change.
