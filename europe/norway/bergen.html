Bergen

Bergen was founded around 1070 and served as Norway's medieval capital and a leading Hanseatic kontor from the 14th century. Bryggen wharf warehouses, the harbour, and surrounding seven mountains record North Sea trade in stockfish and later shipping industry. As western Norway's largest city, Bergen links fjord tourism with a long record of seafaring, commerce, and civic institutions.

Bryggen

Bryggen, also known as the Hanseatic Wharf, is a medieval wharf located in Bergen's historic harbor district. It is known for colorful wooden-clad boat houses and is a evidence of the legacy of the Hanseatic League's trading empire dating back to the 14th century. Despite being ravaged by fires numerous times over many years, Bryggen remains an attraction and is protected as a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Fishmarket in Bergen

Bergen Fish Market is the place to go if visitors are looking for fresh seafood. It's located in Bryggen, which is a UNESCO World Heritage Site, and it has been a popular tourist destination since the Middle Ages. The market is open every day except Sunday and it's always busy with people sampling the delights available. Travelers can also take advantage of the numerous transportation options that connect Oslo with Trondheim, including the Bergen Railway and the Dovre Railway.

Bergenhus Fortress

Bergenhus Fortress, dating back to 1240, looms over the Bergen Harbour entrance and has served as a protective stronghold for centuries. While only a medieval hall and defensive tower remain, it is still in use by the Royal Norwegian Navy. Visitors can explore Haakons Hall and climb the Rosenkrantz Tower, which offers views of the harbor. The fortress also houses a museum showing Bergen's history during WWII.

Fantoft Stave Church

Fantoft Stave Church is a church in Bergen, Norway. Fantoft Stave Church is a reconstruction of an early medieval wooden church that was originally built in 1150 and later destroyed in a fire in 1992. The church showcases the careful ornamentation typical of Norwegian stave churches, with detailed designs and carved dragon heads. The exterior and main interior spaces are usually viewable outside service times.
