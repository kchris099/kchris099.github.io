Bergen

Bergen was founded around 1070 and served as Norway's medieval capital and a leading Hanseatic kontor from the 14th century. Bryggen wharf warehouses, the harbour, and surrounding seven mountains record North Sea trade in stockfish and later shipping industry. As western Norway's largest city, Bergen links fjord tourism with a long record of seafaring, commerce, and civic institutions.

Bryggen

Bryggen's narrow rows of timber buildings preserve the plan of Bergen's Hanseatic trading quarter along Vagen harbor. Fires repeatedly destroyed the structures, so most visible buildings are later reconstructions on medieval foundations; warehouses now contain museums, workshops, shops and offices, while the surviving urban form records centuries of northern European trade.

Fishmarket in Bergen

Bergen's fish market occupies a waterfront area beside Vagen harbor, with seafood, produce and prepared food sold from indoor and outdoor stalls. Trading has taken place around the harbor for centuries, although the present facilities are modern and strongly oriented toward tourism. Bryggen stands across the water from the market. Together they frame the city's historic commercial harbor.

60.3947° N / 5.3252° E

Bergen Tourist Information

Bergen Tourist Information occupies a central waterfront location near the Fish Market and Bryggen. Staff provide maps, local transport guidance and planning help for city sights, fjord excursions and travel across western Norway. The center is a practical starting point for understanding routes and distances, especially when a visit extends beyond Bergen into the surrounding coastal region.

04 AdmissionFree

Festplassen

Festplassen forms a broad civic square between Bergen's city center and Lille Lungegardsvannet. Markets, celebrations, concerts, demonstrations, festivals and seasonal installations use the paving, while the lake; museums and administrative buildings frame its edges. Event structures come and go, leaving the square's role as flexible public ground as the lasting description.

60.3911° N / 5.3258° E

05 AdmissionFree

St. John's Church

St John's Church (Johanneskirken) in Bergen is a neo-Gothic brick church on a rise south of the central harbour districts, with a tall spire over Nygård. The Lutheran parish interior is plain beneath high vaults. Outside services the doors commonly open for a short visit.

60.3886° N / 5.3194° E

06 Admissionkr120

Bergenhus Fortress

Bergenhus occupies the harbor entrance on ground used by Norway's medieval royal administration. Hakon's Hall, Rosenkrantz Tower, walls, archaeological remains, military buildings and open grounds record royal, defensive and wartime history. The complex still has military functions, while museums and monument interiors keep separate schedules and the fortress includes more than two surviving medieval structures.

60.4000° N / 5.3185° E

07 AdmissionNOK 380

Akvariet i Bergen - The National Aquarium

Bergen Aquarium has operated on Nordnes since 1960, using tanks, outdoor pools, education and research programs to interpret marine and terrestrial animals. Norwegian coastal life forms an important part of the collection, while species and presentations change with animal care and renovation. The institution combines public exhibits with conservation and educational work.

60.3997° N / 5.3034° E

08 AdmissionNOK 90

Fantoft Stave Church

After arson destroyed the relocated medieval church in 1992, Fantoft was carefully reconstructed of a medieval church moved from Fortun to Bergen in the nineteenth century and destroyed by arson in 1992. The rebuilt structure restores steep roofs, carved portals, dragon-head ornament, and dark timber interiors. It is a heritage replica on private grounds, not the surviving original fabric described by its twelfth-century date.

60.3393° N / 5.3533° E

09 AdmissionNOK 200

Fløibanen Upper Station

Floibanen's upper station opens onto Mount Floyen above Bergen, where terraces, trails, forest, play areas, and services overlook the city and fjords. The funicular provides frequent urban transport as well as sightseeing. Weather can erase the view, and accessibility varies across mountain paths even when the railway itself accommodates many mobility needs.

60.3948° N / 5.3428° E

10 Admissionkr145

Fløyen

Mount Fløyen rises above Bergen with forest trails and a summit area reached in about six minutes on the Fløibanen funicular to roughly 320 meters. Platforms look over the harbor, islands, and fjord arms. Hikers also climb on foot from the city streets below.

60.3983° N / 5.3459° E
