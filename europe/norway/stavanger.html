Destination Guide — Stavanger

Explore Stavanger

A Brief History

Established as a regional settlement beyond an older fortified core, the city developed through industrialization in the late 19th century, post-war reconstruction, and modern civic expansion that reshaped its public spaces, markets, and surrounding landscape. Restored religious buildings, civic buildings, and natural features now define the contemporary visitor experience.

Gamle Stavanger

Gamle Stavanger, also known as Old Town, is a historic district in Stavanger featuring well-preserved 18th- and 19th-century white wooden cottages painted in unique pastel hues. Initially slated for demolition after World War II, the area was saved by a forward-thinking city architect who recognized its potential for tourism. Today, it's a trendy residential area and a popular spot for travellers to wander through the narrow cobblestone streets and admire the picturesque houses.

Free

Stavanger tourist Information

Stavanger Tourist Information is the perfect place to start your exploration of the city. The office offers free information materials and brochures to guide you through the Stavanger Region. The friendly and attentive staff are ready to assist with selling tickets for city and fjord sightseeing, as well as providing helpful insights and advice.

Free

Stavanger Concert Hall

Begin your day with a leisurely walk from the Stavanger Concert Hall, an architectural marvel in the city. Take a stroll through Old Stavanger, passing charming white picket fences and vibrant gardens before arriving at the IDDIS Museum, home to the Norwegian Printing Museum and the Norwegian Canning Museum. The concert hall also features a cafe offering an array of delicious food and drinks.

Free

Stavanger Cathedral

Stavanger Cathedral, the oldest in Norway, is an elegant medieval structure with a blend of Gothic and Romanesque features. Completed in 1125, it stands as a testament to the city's rich history. The surrounding area boasts 18th- and 19th-century wooden houses that add to its cultural heritage. The cathedral's architecture reflects its long history, showcasing a mix of Romanesque and Gothic styles.

Free

Flor og Fjære

Flor og Fjære is a captivating destination that offers an experience in Norway. This enchanting estate features meticulously curated gardens bursting with vibrant flowers, lush greenery, and serene ponds connected by charming pathways. travellers can embark on guided tours led by engaging guides who share the island's rich history while creating a relaxed atmosphere.

Free

Vålandstårnet

The Vålandstårnet is a historical landmark built in 1885 in the town of Stavanger, Norway. The tower is accessible by walking up from the center of town and has amazing views across Greater Stavanger area. There is also an annual soapbox race for kids down the hill.

Free

Sverd i fjell

Nestled on a picturesque peninsula jutting into the serene Hafrsfjord in Stavanger, Sverd i fjell, or Swords in Rock, is a striking monument that pays homage to Norway's rich Viking history. Erected in 1983 by renowned sculptor Fritz Roed and unveiled by King Olav V, this impressive installation features three towering bronze swords, each standing at an awe-inspiring 10 meters tall.

Free

Stavanger Botanical Garden

Stavanger Botanical Garden, located near the Sormarka forest just 4 km from the city center, offers a serene oasis for nature enthusiasts. The garden features themed gardens, beautiful paths surrounded by diverse plants and trees, and even a pond with a small herd of salamanders. Families can also enjoy plenty of space for children to play and have a picnic.

Free

Brutt lenke

Brutt lenke is a truly enchanting place, especially for watching the sunset. It features small benches scattered around where travellers can linger for hours, taking in the tranquility of the surroundings. The area is serene with few people, making it perfect for those seeking some peaceful moments. Additionally, it offers great walking paths with landscapes and ocean views. The site also houses an original monument commemorating a tragic event, adding to its unique appeal.

Free

Lyse Arena

Sola flytekniske museum, located near the Seaplane Port in Stavanger, is a unique attraction that offers a glimpse into the history of aviation in Norway. Housed in one of the last remaining aircraft hangars built by the German Luftwaffe during World War II, the museum showcases a diverse collection of preserved aircraft and artifacts from different eras, including World War II to modern times.
