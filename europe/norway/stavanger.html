Stavanger

A medieval bishopric left a cathedral at the core; herring and canning later lined the harbour with white wooden warehouses in Gamle Stavanger. North Sea oil from the 1960s added towers and the Petroleum Museum. The town's arc is bishopric to fishery to energy capital on Norway's west coast. Canning museum exhibits in old factories explain the sardine decades before oil.

Gamle Stavanger

White and pastel wooden cottages from the eighteenth and nineteenth centuries pack Gamle Stavanger's cobbled lanes. Postwar demolition plans failed after a city architect argued for keeping the district, which now mixes residents with walkers. Narrow streets reward slow looking at timber facades.

Stavanger tourist Information

Stavanger Tourist Information is the place to start a visit of the city. The office offers free information materials and brochures to guide visitors through the Stavanger Region. The friendly and attentive staff are ready to assist with selling tickets for city and fjord sightseeing, as well as providing helpful insights and advice.

Stavanger Concert Hall

Two main halls and waterfront foyers form Stavanger Concert Hall, which opened in 2012 for orchestral music, touring concerts, conferences and cultural events. The city's resident orchestra is based in the complex. Old Stavanger and IDDIS lie within walking distance but remain separate attractions, while the hall itself is defined by its architecture, acoustics and performance program.

04 AdmissionFree

Stavanger Cathedral

Stavanger Cathedral began as a Romanesque church in the early twelfth century and gained a Gothic choir after a medieval fire. Stone carving, stained glass, chapels, pulpit, and restoration campaigns record centuries of worship. The cathedral anchors the old center but is not surrounded solely by eighteenth- and nineteenth-century wooden houses, which are concentrated elsewhere.

Flor og Fjære

Flor og Fjære is a subtropical garden island near Stavanger reached by boat, with guided tours that explain how exotic plantings were established on the rocky islet. Paths wind among palms, flowers, and lawns above the fjord. The visit combines a short sea crossing with a landscaped garden walk.

06 AdmissionFree

Vålandstårnet

Valandstarnet stands on a hill above Stavanger, built in the late nineteenth century as a lookout associated with the city's waterworks and fire observation. Paths and roads reach the tower through a residential park, with views across the city and coast in clear weather. Interior access and special events vary, while the surrounding hill remains open for walking.

58.9572° N / 5.7284° E

07 AdmissionFree

Sverd i fjell

Sculptor Fritz Roed embedded three bronze swords in rock beside Hafrsfjord, unveiling the monument in 1983. Sverd i fjell commemorates the Battle of Hafrsfjord, traditionally linked with Harald Fairhair's consolidation of Norway, although exact date and details remain debated. The fixed swords symbolize peace after conflict and frame a public shoreline landscape.

58.9414° N / 5.6719° E

Stavanger Botanical Garden

Stavanger Botanical Garden on the Ullandhaug slopes holds themed plant collections with views toward the city and fjord. Paths, lawns, and play areas give families space for walks and picnics among the beds. Greenhouse and outdoor sections support both research display and public recreation.

09 AdmissionFree

Brutt lenke

Brutt lenke is a Stavanger memorial sculpture whose broken-chain form marks a tragic local event. The monument sits in a public setting meant for quiet reflection rather than recreation. Stone and metal elements make the break in the chain the central image of the site.

58.9705° N / 5.5939° E

Lyse Arena

Lyse Arena is Viking FK's football stadium in Stavanger's Jattavagen district. Opened in 2004 as Viking Stadion, it later carried the SR-Bank name before adopting its current sponsor name in 2025. Steep covered stands surround an artificial pitch used by the club's men's and women's teams, while the complex also accommodates concerts and large gatherings beside the local rail station.
