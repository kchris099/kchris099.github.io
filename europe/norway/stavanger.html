Explore Stavanger

A Brief History

Stavanger is in Norway. Established as a regional settlement beyond an older fortified core, the city developed through industrialization in the late 19th century, post-war reconstruction, and modern civic expansion that reshaped its public spaces, markets, and surrounding landscape. Museums, markets, and street plans in Stavanger still show layers from those periods.

Gamle Stavanger

Gamle Stavanger, also known as Old Town, is a historic district in Stavanger featuring well-preserved 18th- and 19th-century white wooden cottages painted in unique pastel hues. Initially slated for demolition after World War II, the area was saved by a forward-thinking city architect who recognized its potential for tourism. Today, it's a trendy residential area and a popular spot for travellers to wander through the narrow cobblestone streets and admire the houses.

Stavanger Concert Hall

Stavanger Concert Hall is a visitor site in Stavanger, Norway. Begin visitor day with a leisurely walk from the Stavanger Concert Hall, an notable building in the city. Take a stroll through Old Stavanger, passing white picket fences and active gardens before arriving at the IDDIS Museum, home to the Norwegian Printing Museum and the Norwegian Canning Museum. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Stavanger Cathedral

Stavanger Cathedral is a religious building in Stavanger, Norway. It opened or was completed around 1125. Completed in 1125, it is a evidence of the city's rich history. The surrounding area has 18th- and 19th-century wooden houses that add to its local history. The cathedral's architecture reflects its long history, showing a mix of Romanesque and Gothic styles. The exterior and main interior spaces are usually viewable outside service times.

Flor og Fjære

Flor og Fjære is a visitor site in Stavanger, Norway. Travellers can began on guided tours led by engaging guides who share the island's rich history while creating a relaxed atmosphere. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Vålandstårnet

The Vålandstårnet is a historical landmark built in 1885 in the town of Stavanger, Norway. The tower is accessible by walking up from the center of town and has views across Greater Stavanger area. There is also an annual soapbox race for kids down the hill.

Sverd i fjell

Sverd i fjell is a visitor site in Stavanger, Norway. Records associated with the site include 1983. Erected in 1983 by known sculptor Fritz Roed and unveiled by King Olav V, this installation has three towering bronze swords, each standing at an awe-inspiring 10 meters tall. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Stavanger Botanical Garden is a public park in Stavanger, Norway. Families can also enjoy plenty of space for children to play and have a picnic. Paths, lawns, and shaded areas make up the main public facilities. Stavanger Botanical Garden remains part of the public visitor circuit in Stavanger.

Brutt lenke is a visitor site in Stavanger, Norway. The site also houses an original monument commemorating a tragic event, adding to its unique appeal. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest. Brutt lenke remains part of the public visitor circuit in Stavanger.

Lyse Arena

Lyse Arena is a visitor site in Stavanger. Sola flytekniske museum, located near the Seaplane Port in Stavanger, is a unique attraction that offers a glimpse into the history of aviation in Norway. Housed in one of the last remaining aircraft hangars built by the German Luftwaffe during World War II, the museum showcases a diverse collection of preserved aircraft and artifacts from different eras, including World War II to modern times.
