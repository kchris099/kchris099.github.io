Explore Oslo

A Brief History

Oslo traces its origins to a medieval settlement at Bjørvika and was renamed and moved after fires in the 17th century. Union with Denmark and later Sweden shaped its harbour and Akershus fortress before full independence in 1905. Postwar expansion along the fjord, the City Hall, Vigeland sculptures, and museums on Bygdøy illustrate Oslo's development as Norway's capital and maritime gateway.

Top Sights & Activities

Free (castle admission NOK 160)

Akershus Fortress

Akershus Fortress, originally built in the 13th century, was strategically positioned to protect Oslo from external threats. Over the years, it has evolved into a complex that includes a medieval castle, military fortress, Renaissance palace, and active military installations. The fortress also houses two military museums and hosts various events. While the castle is currently closed for renovations, visitors can still explore the sprawling complex and enjoy its historical significance.

Free

The Royal Palace

The Royal Palace in Oslo, Norway, is a significant 19th-century residence that has played a pivotal role in the country's history since 1814. It serves as the primary residence of the King and Queen and is where the King presides over the Council of State. During summer, guided tours offer visitors access to important rooms such as the Council Chamber and Banqueting Hall. The palace shares its park with the public, reflecting Norwegian values of humility and closeness to its people.

Free

Ekebergparken

Ekebergparken is a sprawling wooded park in Oslo, featuring over 40 classic and contemporary sculptures scattered throughout its grounds. Visitors can enjoy leisurely walks along the park's walkways while taking in panoramic views of the city. The park offers a combination of vast green spaces and captivating sculptures, making it an ideal destination for art enthusiasts and nature lovers alike.

Free

Oslo Cathedral

Oslo Cathedral, a 1600s church in Norway, is not only a place for regular services but also hosts royal and state events. It's known for its historical significance and is where the Royal family gets married. The cathedral's surroundings offer opportunities for shopping traditional souvenirs at places like Den Norske Husfliden store.

Free

The Tiger

In the heart of Oslo, stands The Tiger, a striking 4.5-meter bronze sculpture created by Norwegian artist Elena Engelsen. This majestic tiger was presented to the city in honor of its millennium celebration in 2000 and has since become one of Oslo's most landmarks. Positioned outside Oslo Central Station, it captivates visitors with its powerful presence and serves as a popular spot for photos and gatherings.

Free

Oslo Central Station

Oslo S, also known as Oslo Central Station, is a historic transportation hub that offers a streamlined set of retail shops, eateries, and lodging. The station features a comprehensive network of travel conveniences including shops, food outlets, restaurants, and specialty stores. It is easy to navigate and provides straightforward connectivity to other modes of transport. Visitors can find train information at various points throughout the platform complex.

Free

Oslo Opera House

The Oslo Opera House is a striking and contemporary architectural masterpiece located by the waterfront. It serves as the home to the Norwegian National Opera & Ballet. Designed by Snohetta, it resembles a glacier floating in the Oslofjord and is considered one of Scandinavia's most buildings. The interior can be fully appreciated through guided tours, offering insights into its thoughtful design that reflects Norway's cultural significance and public engagement.

kr350

The Norwegian Opera and Ballet

The Norwegian Opera and Ballet in Oslo is a modern architectural wonder that has become a signature work for the Norwegian National Ballet. The building's spacious and airy design makes it a for culture, art, and history enthusiasts. Visitors can enjoy opera or ballet performances by reserving tickets in advance, and may even have the chance to go up on the roof to capture views of Oslo's port and city lights.

Free

Deichman Bjørvika

Located in Bjørvika, Oslo, the Deichman Bjørvika is a new public library that offers a unique and captivating experience. The building's simple exterior gives way to an inviting and vibrant interior, adorned with beautiful decorations and well-lit spaces. The atmosphere is described as soothing, making it an ideal place for indulging in long hours of reading.

200 NOK

The Norwegian Museum of Cultural History

The Norwegian Museum of Cultural History is a vast open-air museum that features an impressive collection of medieval buildings and indoor exhibits showcasing culture and lifestyle. It is museums in Europe, offering visitors the perfect opportunity to explore Norway's rich history in a day. The museum showcases a stoic stave church built in AD among other notable attractions such as The National Gallery, The Viking Ship Museum, and the Nobel Peace Center.

Free

Frogner Park

Frognerparken, located in Oslo, is a public space and sculpture garden famous for the Vigeland Sculpture Park. The park features an open-air museum showcasing the works of Gustav Vigeland, with a myriad of sculptures that capture intricate expressions in bronze. Visitors can wander through this whimsical wonderland, surrounded by lush greenery and sun-kissed sculptures. The park offers surprises at every turn, from hidden benches to playful fountains and blooming flowers.
