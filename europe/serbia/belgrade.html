Destination Guide — Belgrade

Explore Belgrade

A Brief History

Belgrade stands at the confluence of the Sava and Danube rivers, with Belgrade Fortress occupying a ridge contested by Romans, Byzantines, Ottomans, and Habsburgs. The city was capital of Yugoslavia and later Serbia. Bombing in 1999 damaged bridges and buildings. Kalemegdan park, Skadarlija quarter, and socialist-era blocks record layers from the 19th century to the present.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

Church of Saint Sava

Dedicated to Saint Sava, founder of the Serbian Orthodox Church, this domed church on Vračar Hill has been under construction since 1935. White marble façades and unfinished interior spaces reflect decades of interrupted work. When completed, it will rank among the largest Orthodox church buildings in the Balkans.

Free

Belgrade Fortress

At the confluence of the Sava and Danube, this fortress occupies a bluff fortified since Roman times. Celts, Byzantines, Ottomans, and Austrians each rebuilt its walls. Today the upper fort contains museums, parks, and the Pobednik monument with wide river views.

Free

St. Petka Chapel

This small chapel inside the fortress covers a spring believed to have healing properties since Ottoman times. Icons and candles fill the dim interior where worshippers collect water. It sits near the ramparts above the rivers, away from the busier upper fort paths.

Free

Zindan Gate

Called Zindan or dungeon gate, this entry to the lower fort is flanked by two large round towers from the 15th century. Captives were once held in chambers below. The passage connects the riverside park to inner courtyards where cannons and medieval walls are displayed.

Free

Pobednik

Ivan Meštrović sculpted this bronze figure in 1928 to honor Serbia's Balkan Wars and World War I soldiers. Standing on a pedestal in the upper fortress, it overlooks the Sava-Danube confluence. Locals call it Pobednik, meaning the Victor, and treat it as a city emblem.

Free

St. Mark Orthodox Church

Built in Serbo-Byzantine style between 1931 and 1940, this church near Tašmajdan Park holds the tomb of medieval emperor Stefan Dušan. Mosaic decoration and a tall bell tower dominate the surrounding streets. It serves as a parish church and memorial to Serbia's 14th-century Nemanjić dynasty.

Free

Gardoš Tower

Tower Gardos, Zemun, also known as the Millennium Tower, is a must-visit spot in Belgrade. Built in the late 1800s to celebrate Hungary's 1,000-year rule in the region, this 36-meter high tower offers panoramic views of Zemun and the Danube. travellers can climb up steep spiral stairs for a small fee to enjoy the scenery from the top.

Free

Kalemegdan

Kalemegdan is a sprawling park that houses a historic fortress, complete with a museum and zoo. The park offers scenic walking paths and views of the nearby rivers. Adjacent to the park, travellers can explore a charming street lined with ice cream shops, coffee houses, bakeries, and various retail spaces offering handcrafted and vintage items. Belgrade's top attraction, Kalemegdan Park or Belgrade Fortress, provides panoramic views of the city and its surroundings.

Free

Ružica Church

Nestled within the historic Kalemegdan Fortress, Ružica Church stands as a testament to Belgrade's rich heritage. This Serbian Orthodox church, dedicated to Saint Mary, is not only the oldest in the city but also one of Serbia's most significant religious sites. Its origins trace back to the 13th century, with its first documented mention appearing in the 1400s.

RSD 500

Belgrade Zoo

Belgrade Zoo, located on the Danube River, is a charming wildlife park that spans 7 hectares and houses around 800 animals from 210 different species. It's a great place to visit with children, offering thrilling experiences such as observing birds, predatory animals, and wild creatures. The zoo is part of the Belgrade Fortress/Kalemegdan Park area and is known for its variety of birds as well as its wolves, white lions, and tigers.

Free

Republic Square

Republic Square is a bustling central square and transportation hub in Belgrade's old town, featuring the equestrian statue of Prince Michael. It is conveniently located near Knez Mihailova Street, offering easy access to dining and shopping options. The area is known for its vibrant nightlife, with nearby restaurants like Impression Lanzhou and Panda Tianfu. Additionally, the square is home to prestigious cultural institutions such as the National Museum and National Theatre.

Free

National Theatre in Belgrade

The National Theatre in Belgrade, located on Republic Square, is a historic venue that has been showcasing Serbian culture since 1869. Despite war damage and architectural updates, it remains a revered symbol of the country's artistic heritage. travellers can take backstage tours during the day to witness wardrobe and scenery maintenance and enjoy performances by resident singers. The square itself is a popular meeting point with landmarks like the National Museum and Prince Mihailo monument.

Free

Cathedral Church of Saint Michael the Archangel

Nestled in the heart of Belgrade, the Cathedral Church of Saint Michael the Archangel stands as a testament to the city's rich history and architectural beauty. Constructed in the 1830s, this Serbian Orthodox cathedral showcases neoclassical design and is -preserved monuments from that era. Its grand altar houses relics of Serbian saints, making it a significant spiritual site for many.

Free

Partizan Stadium

Partizan Stadium, the second largest football stadium in Serbia, is home to FC Partizan and has an official capacity of 32,710 seats. Located not far from Belgrade, it's a classic old-school stadium with massive lights illuminating the arena and a worn-out construction adorned with graffiti. The ticket office can be inefficient during peak times, so arranging tickets early is recommended.

Free

Rakovica Monastery

Rakovica Monastery, also known as the monastery of the Obrenovic dynasty, has its origins dating back to the 13th century and was likely renovated in the 14th century. This Baroque-style monastery is located in Rakovica, on the outskirts of Belgrade. The peaceful gardens and chapels adorned with mosaics create a serene atmosphere. The well-groomed territory features beautiful roses and is home to the grave of Patriarch Paul.

Free

Ada Ciganlija

Nestled on a picturesque river peninsula, Ada Ciganlija is the ultimate leisure destination in Belgrade, especially during the summer months. This locale boasts a 4.2 km long artificial lake that serves as a playground for both kids and adults. Here, indulge in various activities such as sailing, swimming, and waterskiing or simply bask in the sun on its sandy beaches.

Free

Belgrade Old Railway Station

The Belgrade Old Railway Station, a neoclassical structure established in 1884, stands as a testament to the city’s rich history. Once a bustling hub for travelers, it now serves as an intriguing relic of the past since train operations ceased in mid-2018. Despite its disuse, the station's art nouveau charm continues to captivate travellers and photographers alike.

Free

New Palace

Novi Dvor, or New Palace, is a significant landmark in central Belgrade. It was once the royal residence for the Karadordevic dynasty and played a role in the history of Serbia and Yugoslavia. Situated across from Stari Dvor (Old Palace), both structures are part of a grand ensemble on Kralja Milana. The Old Palace boasts Beaux-Arts architecture and interiors imported from Vienna.

Free

House of the National Assembly of the Republic of Serbia

The House of the National Assembly of the Republic of Serbia is a Beaux-Arts structure that stands as a testament to the country's rich history and architectural beauty. Nestled in Belgrade, this impressive building was constructed on the site of the former Batal Mosque after Serbia's independence in 1878. Its construction faced numerous delays due to historical events like wars and economic challenges, but it ultimately emerged as a symbol of national pride.

Free

Tašmajdan

Tasmajdan is a vibrant park located in the downtown area, offering a mix of historical and modern attractions. It features paved jogging paths, shaded areas with trees, and a large sports complex. The park is surrounded by landmarks such as St. Mark's Church, the country's Parliament, and the main Post Office building. Tasmajdan has an interesting history and is situated above famous city caves.

Free

Flower Square

Flower Square, also known as Cvetni Trg, is a charming and lively square in Belgrade. It originated as a small park in the late 19th century and later transformed into an open market surrounded by attractive buildings. The square is adorned with oak trees and offers various seating areas, making it a popular spot for locals and travellers to enjoy cafes, eateries, and outdoor terraces. In the past, it was renowned for its numerous flower shops.

Free

Jevremovac Botanical Garden

Nestled in the heart of Belgrade, Botanical Garden "Jevremovac" is a captivating oasis boasting lush vegetation, a serene Japanese garden, and an enchanting old greenhouse. Spanning approximately 5 hectares, this historic garden is home to over 350 tree and bush species from around the world. Initially established in the 19th century at the behest of Josif Pancic, it exudes cultural and architectural significance as one of the oldest botanical gardens globally.

Free

Bajloni Market Belgrade

Bajloni Market, also known as Skadarlija Market, is a beloved open-air market In Belgrade. This vibrant marketplace hosts popular monthly events featuring live music and a variety of stalls offering delicacies, homeware, wine, and art. The market's picturesque setting at the bottom of Skadarska Street adds to its charm and makes it a favorite among locals and travellers alike.

Free

Kneza Mihaila

Kneza Mihaila, a vibrant pedestrian street in Belgrade, is a must-visit for anyone exploring the city. This bustling thoroughfare stretches approximately 700 meters and connects the historic Kalemegdan Fortress to Republic Square, making it an essential part of your journey. Lined with impressive old buildings and international brand stores, Kneza Mihaila offers a delightful blend of shopping and sightseeing.

RSD 400

Ethnographic Museum

The Ethnographic Museum in Belgrade, Serbia, is a treasure trove of artifacts and exhibits dedicated to Serbian culture and ethnography. Established in 1901, it stands as one of the oldest museum buildings in the region. With a vast collection of over 150,000 items including jewelry, clothing, weapons, folk costumes, trade devices, crafts, furniture and dishes from different historical periods such as ancient history and the Middle Ages.

Free

Bajrakli Mosque

Bajrakli Mosque, the only mosque in Belgrade, dates back to the 1600s when it was built during the Ottoman Empire. It features an octagonal dome and a minaret. The mosque has a rich history as it was converted into a Catholic church during the Austro-Hungarian occupation but later restored to its original purpose when the Ottomans regained control of Serbia.
