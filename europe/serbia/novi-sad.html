Destination Guide — Novi Sad

Explore Novi Sad

A Brief History

Founded in the late 17th century by Orthodox merchants seeking refuge across the Danube from the Ottoman Empire, Novi Sad blossomed into the 'Serbian Athens'. Its elegant Habsburg architecture and the formidable Petrovaradin Fortress reflect a rich legacy of cross-cultural flourishing. The remarkable survival of these cultural and architectural milestones provides a powerful window into the region's storied past.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

Holy Name of Mary Catholic Church

The Church of the Name of Mary, also called the Novi Sad Cathedral, stands as a prominent Gothic Revival landmark in Novi Sad, Serbia. This Roman Catholic parish church was constructed between 1892 and 1894 and boasts a remarkable 72-meter tall bell tower with an intricately designed spire.

Free

Monument of Svetozar Miletić

The Monument of Svetozar Miletić stands in Novi Sad, Serbia's Liberty Square. Crafted by sculptor Ivan Strović and unveiled in 1939, the bronze statue captures the dynamic presence of Svetozar Miletić, a significant Serbian figure known for his leadership during the struggle for autonomy under Austro-Hungarian rule.

Free

Serbian National Theatre

Located near the Cathedral of St. George in Novi Sad, Serbia, the Serbian National Theatre is a grand theater building with two halls and a small blackbox theater hall. The exterior has been renovated for Novi Sad's designation as the cultural capital of 2021, while the interior still retains its dated charm. The main hall features hand-operated stage machinery and can accommodate around 950 seats.

Free

Velika Remeta Monastery

Velika Remeta Monastery is a centuries-old landmark with a rich history and beautiful architecture. The monastery features a dark underground wine cellar and a pharmacy that has been in operation for hundreds of years. Visitors can explore the gorgeous outdoor trails, interact with friendly monks, and purchase icons, honey, and rakija made by the monastery. The estate exudes peace and tranquility, offering a serene environment for both adults and children to enjoy.

Free

Grgeteg Monastery

Grgeteg Monastery is a serene religious retreat nestled in a natural setting near Sremski Karlovci. Founded in 1471, this female Serb Orthodox monastery boasts a beautiful chapel, well-preserved library with ancient religious texts, and a peaceful cloister. Visitors can explore the monastery's picturesque complex and purchase local products at the souvenir shop.

Free

St. Nicholas Cathedral

St. Nicholas Cathedral, nestled in the charming city of Sremski Karlovci, is a example of baroque-classical architecture and holds significant historical value. This magnificent structure features an impressive facade adorned with sculptures of the twelve Apostles and houses the tomb of its patron saint. Originally built between 1758 and 1762 after replacing a smaller church dedicated to Saint Nicholas, it showcases exquisite Serbian Baroque paintings that captivate visitors.

Free

Futoška pijaca

Futoška pijaca, located in the vibrant city of Novi Sad, is a must-visit for anyone who appreciates fresh produce and local goods. This bustling open-air market showcases an impressive array of area-sourced fruits and vegetables, alongside beautiful flowers and artisanal products. As the capital of Vojvodina, Novi Sad benefits from its fertile soil and favorable climate, which support numerous small organic farms that supply this lively market.

Free

Fruška Gora National Park

Fruška Gora National Park is a beautiful, forested mountain range located in the Vojvodina region of Serbia. Known as the 'Jewel of Serbia,' the park features extensive hiking trails, diverse flora and fauna, and numerous historic Serbian Orthodox monasteries hidden within the woods. It is a major destination for nature lovers, wine enthusiasts, and spiritual pilgrims.

Free

Saint George's Cathedral

Saint George's Cathedral is a significant religious and cultural landmark located in Novi Sad's Old Town. The cathedral boasts a unique architectural style that combines traditional Serbian and Byzantine influences, setting it apart from other city landmarks. Inside, visitors can admire beautiful frescoes, colorful mosaics, and ornate chandeliers. This Orthodox church features a belfry with an ornate black & gold spire and a gilded iconostasis.

Free

Pozorište mladih Novi Sad

Pozorište mladih Novi Sad, or the Youth Theatre of Novi Sad, is a delightful cultural gem that captivates audiences with its vibrant performances tailored for children and families. Nestled in a cozy venue, this theatre has become a beloved weekend destination for parents and kids alike. The energetic actors bring stories to life through engaging visuals and dynamic storytelling, making it accessible even to those who may not fully grasp the language of the performance.

Free

Danube Park

Danube Park, also known as Dunavski Park, is a public park located near the Danube River in Novi Sad. Spanning over 7 hectares, this beautiful park offers a perfect blend of nature and urban landscape. Visitors can enjoy well-maintained walking paths, native trees, and colorful flowerbeds as they stroll through the immaculate grounds. The park features a charming pond with fishes and swans, along with picturesque little islets.

Free

Liman market (Limanska pijaca)

Limanska pijaca is a bustling market in NS where find a variety of goods at affordable prices. The market offers fresh fruits, vegetables, poultry, and household items. It's known for its friendly vendors and is considered a hidden gem for organic produce. The days to visit are Friday through Sunday when all the merchants are present, offering a wide range of products.

Free

Karađorđe Stadium

Karađorđe Stadium, also known as FC Vojvodina's home ground, is a historic sports venue in Novi Sad. It is named after the renowned Serbian revolutionary leader, Karađorđe Petrović. The stadium holds significant cultural and sporting importance for the city, being the oldest football stadium in Novi Sad. Despite its age and need for repairs, it remains a point of pride for FC Vojvodina and has witnessed many historic football moments.

Free

Petrovaradin Fortress Clock

Petrovaradin Fortress's Clock, situated on the outskirts of Novi Sad and overlooking the Danube, is a renowned historical site. As the second largest fortress in Europe, its construction spanned from the late 17th to late 18th century under Ottoman and Austrian control. Visitors can access the tower via a car road or explore its surroundings for picturesque views of Novi Sad and the Danube River.
