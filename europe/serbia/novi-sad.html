Novi Sad

Orthodox merchants founded Novi Sad across the Danube from Ottoman Belgrade in the late seventeenth century, earning the nickname Serbian Athens. Petrovaradin Fortress and Habsburg streetscapes express that frontier-cultural boom on the edge of empire. EXIT festival later reused Petrovaradin's ramparts for mass culture on the same fortress. The Name of Đura Jakšić and other writers fixed the Athens nickname in culture.
