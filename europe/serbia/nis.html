Park Bubanj

Park Bubanj is a public park in Nis, Serbia. The park is dedicated to the memory of over 10,000 people who were mass executed by the Germans during the war. Situated just over two kilometers from the Red Cross Concentration Camp, it holds historical significance as a location where numerous atrocities took place. Paths, lawns, and shaded areas make up the main public facilities.

Čegar

The Čegar Monument is a historic site located near Niš, commemorating the Battle of Čegar during the First Serbian Uprising in 1809. It honors the heroic sacrifice of the Serbian commander Stevan Sinđelić and his soldiers, who blew up their own powder magazine to destroy the advancing Ottoman forces. The monument is a profound symbol of Serbian resistance and independence.

Nazi Concentration Camp "Red Cross”

The Red Cross Concentration Camp in Niš is a preserved historical site that operated during the Axis occupation of Serbia in World War II. It served as a transit camp for Jews, Romani people, and Serbian partisans before they were deported to larger extermination camps or executed locally at Bubanj Hill. The site now functions as a memorial museum, documenting the harsh conditions and the tragic history of the Holocaust in the region.
