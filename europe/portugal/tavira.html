Destination Guide — Southern Portugal

Explore Tavira

A Brief History

A thriving maritime center during the Moorish occupation and later a dominant port for the tuna industry, Tavira suffered heavily in the 1755 earthquake. Rebuilt with elegant pyramidal roofs, its quiet riverside streets preserve a notably intact slice of traditional Algarve history. The remarkable survival of these cultural and architectural milestones provides a powerful window into the region's storied past.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

Castelo de Tavira

Castelo de Tavira is a picturesque 11th-century castle with views of the town and surrounding park from its ancient battlement walls. The fortress offers panoramic vistas of Tavira's terracotta rooftops, the meandering Gilao river, and the distant ocean. With remnants dating back to Phoenician times and extensive reconstruction during the Portuguese Reconquest, this Moorish castle is one of Europe's oldest.

Free

Camera Obscura - Tavira EYE

The Camera Obscura, or Tavira Eye, is located inside a converted water tower in the historic center of Tavira. Using a simple system of lenses and mirrors, it projects a live, 360-degree panoramic image of the city onto a large concave table in a darkened room. A guide provides a detailed commentary on the city's history and landmarks during the presentation.

Free

Tavira Old Bridge

Tavira Old Bridge is a documented piece of civic infrastructure in Tavira, Portugal, recognised in local engineering and heritage listings for its construction period and continued public use. The structure is accessible from the surrounding streets and is visible from adjacent viewpoints. Travellers cross or view the site while moving between nearby districts and transit connections.

Free

Mercado Municipal de Tavira

Mercado Municipal de Tavira is a bustling marketplace offering an array of fresh produce, seafood, meats, spices, and local crafts. The daily market showcases the vibrant Portuguese daily life and offers great bargains on various goods. On weekends, the outdoor area features antiques, artisan designs, clothing, and more. Visitors can explore wide aisles filled with an impressive variety of local products including fish, fruits, vegetables, spices, honey and salt.

Free

Praia do Barril

Praia do Barril is a charming beach located near the historic town of Tavira in the eastern Algarve region of Portugal. This family-friendly destination offers long stretches of white sand, calm and clear waters perfect for swimming, and picturesque dunes. The area's rich fishing history is reflected in the old tuna-boat anchors that adorn the beach and the converted fishermen's cottages that now house cafes and restaurants.

Free

Praia da Ilha de Tavira

Praia da Ilha de Tavira is a popular sand bar offering ocean swims and beachfront cafes serving delicious seafood. The small town of Tavira, crossed by the Gilao river, has become a tourist hotspot with an array of restaurants, terraces, music festivals, and fairs. Visitors can enjoy the picturesque Roman bridge reflected in the river and explore the Mercado da Ribeira for dining and shopping experiences.

Free

Praia dos Tesos

Praia dos Tesos sits within the Praia da Ilha de Tavira area of Tavira, documented in local heritage registers for its civic or cultural role. Visitors approach from the main access road and combine the visit with neighbouring heritage buildings and public spaces in the district.

Free

Praia de Cabana

Praia de Cabanas, also known as Ilha de Cabanas-Mar or simply Cabanas Beach, is a serene and expansive 7-kilometer stretch of unspoiled white sand. Accessible by a short water taxi from the boardwalk of Cabanas, this beach offers tranquility and relaxation for sunbathers. The island has its own amenities including a cafe-bar, showers, toilet facilities, and options to rent sunshades.

Free

Cabanas de Tavira

Cabanas de Tavira is a charming coastal village located on the eastern side of Tavira Island. It boasts a long stretch of beach and a picturesque waterfront where fishing boats can be seen anchored in the Ria Formosa. The village has its origins in tuna fishing and maintains its traditional charm. A short ferry ride from Cabanas takes visitors to the pristine Praia de Cabanas, known for its relaxing ambiance and lined with cafes and restaurants.

Free

Benamor Golf

Benamor Golf is a renowned golf course located in the Eastern Algarve, offering views of the Serra do Caldeirao mountains and the Atlantic Ocean. The course features a unique design with water coming into play on eleven of its eighteen holes, creating a challenging layout with par-4s, par-3s, and par-5s.
