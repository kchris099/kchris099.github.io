Tavira

Moorish then Portuguese Tavira thrived on tuna and coastal trade until the 1755 earthquake forced a rebuild with characteristic pyramidal roofs. The Gilão riverfront and island beaches nearby kept an Algarve town scale that mass tourism resorts elsewhere erased. The island ferry to Tavira Island beaches extends the historic town into a dual river–sea setting.

Castelo de Tavira

Castelo de Tavira is a 11th-century castle with views of the town and surrounding park from its ancient battlement walls. The fortress offers panoramic vistas of Tavira's terracotta rooftops, the meandering Gilao river, and the distant ocean. With remnants dating back to Phoenician times and extensive reconstruction during the Portuguese Reconquest, this Moorish castle is one of Europe's oldest.

Camera Obscura - Tavira EYE

The Camera Obscura, or Tavira Eye, is located inside a converted water tower in the historic center of Tavira. Using a simple system of lenses and mirrors, it projects a live, 360-degree panoramic image of the city onto a large concave table in a darkened room. A guide provides a detailed commentary on the city's history and landmarks during the presentation.

Praia do Barril

Praia do Barril is a beach located near the historic town of Tavira in the eastern Algarve region of Portugal. This family-friendly destination offers long stretches of white sand, calm and clear waters perfect for swimming, and dunes. The area's rich fishing history is reflected in the old tuna-boat anchors that adorn the beach and the converted fishermen's cottages that now house cafes and restaurants.
