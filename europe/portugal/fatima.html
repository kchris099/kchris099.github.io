Fátima

Fátima was a small serra village until reported apparitions of the Virgin Mary to three shepherd children in 1917 transformed it into one of Catholicism's principal pilgrimage destinations. The basilicas, colonnaded prayer esplanade, and chapels of the Apparitions rose through the twentieth century as millions arrived on foot and by road. While the surrounding heathland still recalls rural Estremadura, the planned sanctuary city records how modern Portuguese faith, dictatorship-era patronage, and international devotion reshaped this landscape.

Castle of Ourem

Castle of Ourem, located in a small village, is a medieval hilltop fortress offering panoramic views of the surrounding area. The castle was rebuilt in the 15th century using Italian military architectural styles and is a national monument with great historical significance. Originally a Muslim fortification, it was reconquered from the Moors in 1136.

Mira de Aire Cave

The Limestone Caves in Mira de Aire are a natural formation featuring stalactites, stalagmites, waterfalls, and an underground lake. The caves have been expertly preserved and maintained with atmospheric lighting that enhances the beauty of the attraction. At the end of the trail is a spacious deck suitable for ceremonies such as weddings or parties.

Basilica of the Most Holy Trinity

The Basilica of the Holy Trinity opened in 2007 to accommodate large indoor services at the Sanctuary of Fatima. Its broad circular plan and low profile contrast with the older basilica across the esplanade. Numerous entrances lead toward a large altar. Modern religious artworks create a contemporary worship space designed for major pilgrim gatherings.

39.6297° N / 8.6753° W

04 AdmissionFree

Chapel of the Apparitions

A small chapel marks the place where three shepherd children reported apparitions of the Virgin Mary in 1917. Built soon after the events, it was later reconstructed following an attack and became the devotional center of the Fatima sanctuary. Its simple form keeps attention on the reported apparition site. Masses, candle processions, offerings and private prayer gather around it.

39.6317° N / 8.6733° W

Basilica of Our Lady of the Rosary of Fatima

The Basilica of Our Lady of the Rosary anchors the Fatima sanctuary built after the 1917 Marian apparitions reported by three local children. Pilgrims gather in the wide forecourt and enter the long nave for Mass and private prayer; the facade and interior remain open outside major ceremonies and candle processions.

Wax Museum of Fátima

The Wax Museum of Fátima stages the 1917 Marian apparitions and related local history with life-size figures, and includes ethnographic displays on daily life in the sanctuary town. Galleries walk visitors through sequential scenes. It sits a short walk from the main pilgrimage esplanade.

07 AdmissionFree

Way of the Little Shepherds

The Way of the Little Shepherds traces paths around Fátima linked to the 1916 and 1917 apparitions reported by Lúcia de Jesus, Francisco Marto, and Jacinta Marto. Markers guide a meditative walk between sites tied to the three children. Open countryside surrounds the pilgrimage route.

39.6245° N / 8.6679° W

08 AdmissionFree

Lucia's House

Lucia dos Santos spent her childhood in a modest family house in Aljustrel near Fatima. Preserved rooms, furniture and farm objects illustrate rural domestic life before the sanctuary transformed the surrounding region. The property is closely tied to Lucia's family and the 1917 apparition narrative. Its small scale contrasts with the monumental pilgrimage buildings that later developed nearby.

39.6156° N / 8.6649° W

09 AdmissionFree

Francisco and Jacinta's House

Francisco and Jacinta's House holds historical significance as the birthplace of the young shepherds who reportedly witnessed apparitions of the Lady of the Rosary in Fatima, Portugal. The house, located just 200 meters from Lucia's residence, was acquired by the Shrine in November 1996 and subsequently reconstructed. This site is a key pilgrimage destination for those interested in the events that unfolded during May to October 1917.

39.6163° N / 8.6635° W

10 AdmissionFree

Parish Church of Fatima

The parish church of Fatima served the villages around Aljustrel before the apparition sanctuary was built. Francisco and Jacinta Marto were baptized here, and Lucia also participated in parish life. A modest nave, font, cemetery, and local devotional setting contrast with the monumental pilgrimage complex, making the church relevant to the children's ordinary community.

39.6179° N / 8.6525° W
