Explore Aveiro

A Brief History

Aveiro was first documented in the tenth century and grew prosperous through salt production and maritime trade. A severe storm in 1575 closed its connection to the sea, leading to economic decline until a new canal was opened in the nineteenth century. The city is characterized by its network of canals navigated by traditional moliceiro boats and its striking Art Nouveau architecture. Today, Aveiro is known as the "Venice of Portugal," blending its maritime heritage with a modern university presence.

Top Sights & Activities

Farol da Barra

Farol da Barra is a visitor site in Aveiro. Maré Cheia is a seafood restaurant located in Aveiro, Portugal, known for its quality and fresh fish dishes. The restaurant takes advantage of the city's rich maritime resources to offer delicious grilled fish, fish stews, and eel stew. It has garnered praise from locals for its impeccable service and welcoming atmosphere.

Free

Casas Típicas da Costa Nova

The Casas Típicas da Costa Nova are well known, brightly painted, striped wooden houses located in the coastal village of Costa Nova, near Aveiro. Originally used by fishermen to store gear, these 'palheiros' have been beautifully restored and are now a defining symbol of the region, creating a active and very photogenic streetscape.

Free

Praia da Costa Nova

Praia da Costa Nova is a beach destination that draws visitors with its unique charm. This stretch of sandy coastline, extending over 9 kilometers along the western peninsula, is known for its active striped houses that create a colorful backdrop against the golden sands and rolling dunes. The beach offers ample space for sunbathing and relaxation, even during peak summer months when the northern section tends to attract more crowds.

Free

Reserva Natural das Dunas de São Jacinto

Reserva Natural das Dunas de São Jacinto sits within the Farol da Barra area of Aveiro, documented in local heritage registers for its civic or cultural role. Visitors approach from the main access road and combine the visit with neighbouring heritage buildings and public spaces in the district.

Free

Ponte dos Laços de Amizade

Ponte dos Laços de Amizade, also known as Ponte dos Carcavelos, is one of Aveiro's oldest and most bridges. Its yellow color and the city coat of arms featuring an eagle make it a spot along the canal in Bairro do Beira Mar. Visitors can enjoy a leisurely stroll around Aveiro, often referred to as the Venice of Portugal, and take boat trips through the canals.

Free

Confeitaria Peixinho

Confeitaria Peixinho is a pastry shop known for its local sweets and pastries, including the famous ovos moles. The shop, open since 1856, offers a cozy atmosphere with high ceilings, painted walls, and chandeliers. In addition to ovos moles, they also sell a variety of cakes and meringues featuring the sweet delicacy. Visitors can also sample the lesser-known local biscuits Raivas and Alemaes.

Free

Moliceiros Pier

The Moliceiros Pier in Aveiro is the central docking area for the city's traditional, brightly painted boats, known as moliceiros. Originally used for harvesting seaweed, these gondola-like vessels now offer scenic tours through the city's network of urban canals. The pier is a busy hub of activity and the starting point for exploring the 'Venice of Portugal.'.

Free

Beira Mar - Jardim do Rossio

Jardim do Rossio is a pleasant, palm-lined public park located along the main canal in the Beira Mar district of Aveiro. The park offers a relaxing environment with views of the passing moliceiro boats and the historic Art Nouveau architecture along the waterfront. It frequently hosts local craft markets and cultural events.

Free

Ponte dos Carcavelos

Ponte dos Carcavelos is a visitor site in Aveiro, Portugal. Ponte dos Carcavelos is a significant bridge in Aveiro, known for its historical importance and romantic charm. It was constructed over a previous bridge that collapsed and served as an important crossing for workers heading to the salt fields. The main structures and grounds are the principal points of interest.

Free

Aveiro station

Aveiro Station, also known as Estacao Ferroviaria de Aveiro, is a modern central train station located near the city center. It operates daily with various timetables for Alfa Pendular, Intercity, Regional, Suburban, and Vouga Regional trains. The new design of the station stands in contrast to the old azulejo-decorated building nearby. There are plans to convert the classic structure into a museum or regional information office.
