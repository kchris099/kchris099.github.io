Explore Marvao

A Brief History

Marvao is in Portugal. Established as a regional settlement beyond an older fortified core, the city developed through industrialization in the late 19th century, post-war reconstruction, and modern civic expansion that reshaped its public spaces, markets, and surrounding landscape. Museums, markets, and street plans in Marvao still show layers from those periods.

Castle of Marvão

The Castle of Marvão, a stately stone fortress originally constructed in the 8th century and rebuilt in the 17th century, is a for tourists. Situated on a rocky hill, it offers views of the Alentejo plain. The village of Marvao itself is, with whitewashed houses lining the mountain ridge. The castle walls provide views of the surrounding area, showing human ingenuity and engineering from centuries ago.

Free

Castle of Castelo de Vide

The Castle of Castelo de Vide is a must-visit in the Alto Alentejo region, near the Spanish border. It offers views over the border and was where a king sealed his marriage to Queen Isabel of Aragon. The castle allows travellers to climb up to the tower and walk over its roofs for village and landscape views. The museum inside showcases military history, but unfortunately, information is only available in Portuguese.

Free

River beach Portagem

River beach Portagem is a beach in Marvao, Portugal. Access is typically on foot from nearby roads or beachfront paths. River beach Portagem remains part of the public visitor circuit in Marvao. Local maps identify River beach Portagem within Marvao, Portugal.

Free

Sé de Portalegre

The Municipal Museum in Portalegre offers a guided tour that provides valuable insight into the history of the city. The visit is accompanied by a helpful guide who explains everything in detail. The museum showcases a unique collection, including the first car from Portalegre, which is on display. While there is an entrance fee, travellers can enjoy free admission on Sundays until 1 pm.

Free

Cidade Romana de Ammaia

Cidade Romana de Ammaia is a visitor site in Marvao, Portugal. This museum showcases an array of artifacts that span from the Paleolithic era to modern times, providing travellers with a rich understanding of the region's history. While exploring its well-curated exhibitions, uncover archaeological treasures and traditional items that tell the story of Marvao's past. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.
