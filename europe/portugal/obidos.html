Óbidos

Óbidos was a strategic hilltop settlement gifted by Portuguese kings to their queens from the thirteenth century, which helped preserve its circuit of walls and whitewashed houses. Romans and later Visigoths knew the site before the castle rebuilt under the first dynasty controlled the road between Lisbon and Leiria. Medieval gates, Manueline pelourinho, and baroque churches within the enclosure show how a fortified borough became a literary and festival town without losing its compact plan.

Castelo de Óbidos

Castelo de Óbidos is a grand medieval castle with origins dating back to the 9th century. The castle offers hotel rooms for travellers, allowing them to experience a unique stay within its historic walls. From the top of the castle, one can admire the streets below, adorned with Moorish roof tiles and old trees that create a setting.

02 AdmissionFree

Porta da Vila

Porta da Vila is the main entrance to the old town of Obidos, featuring cobbled streets leading to the castle and adorned with whitewashed walls covered in colorful bougainvillea and geraniums. The area also includes the central square with Santa Maria Church and the Municipal Museum, travellers can explore Direita for souvenirs and sample ginja de Obidos cherry liquor.

Medieval Market of Óbidos

The Medieval Market of Óbidos fills streets and castle yards during its seasonal fair with costumed stalls selling crafts, food, and drink. Timber booths and temporary stages turn the walled town into a short-lived market circuit distinct from everyday shop hours on Rua Direita and the quieter winter lanes.

39.3582° N / 9.1581° W

04 AdmissionFree

Aqueduto de Usseira

The Aqueduto de Usseira, also known as the Obidos Aqueduct, is a notable building that has been quenching the thirst of the town since its construction in the 1570s under Queen Catherine of Austria. Spanning approximately three kilometers, this structure channels water from a natural spring near Usseira to the Chafariz Real on Praca de Santa Maria.

39.3576° N / 9.1570° W

05 AdmissionFree

Sanctuary of the Lord of the Stone

The Sanctuary of the Lord of the Stone (Senhor Jesus da Pedra) outside Óbidos is an unfinished eighteenth-century Baroque church with incomplete tower details. Inside, chapels include the main Calvary chapel and side dedications such as Our Lady of Conception. The round plan stands apart from the walled town.

39.3646° N / 9.1499° W

Parque Dom Carlos I

Parque Dom Carlos I in the Óbidos area combines landscaped grounds with cultural and historical markers named for King Carlos. Paths link plantings and open lawns for walking. The park mixes outdoor leisure with commemorative landscaping near the lagoon and town approaches.

Praça da Fruta das Caldas da Rainha

Praca da Republica in Caldas da Rainha hosts the produce market commonly called Praca da Fruta. Stalls sell fruit, vegetables, flowers, bread and regional goods among tiled and Art Nouveau facades. The continuing market links nearby farms with the town center and reflects Caldas da Rainha's long commercial life, while ceramics and small shops extend into the surrounding streets.

08 AdmissionFree

Chafariz das Cinco Bicas

As visitors near the end of a visit, includes the Chafariz das Cinco Bicas, a 18th-century fountain commissioned by King João V to enhance Lisbon's water supply. Located between Praça da República and a modern hospital, this historic site is not just an notable building but also holds tales from the past, like Queen Leonor washing her hands here. The detailed design and beauty of this fountain make it a for anyone visiting Portugal.

39.4046° N / 9.1319° W

09 AdmissionFree

Praia da Foz do Arelho

At Foz do Arelho, the Obidos Lagoon meets the Atlantic through shifting sandbars and channels. A broad beach offers calmer lagoon water on one side and exposed surf on the other, with boardwalks and seasonal services along the settlement. Currents, wind, water quality, and channel conditions vary, so swimming suitability cannot be generalized for all ages or every day.

39.4312° N / 9.2321° W

10 AdmissionFree

Lagoa de Óbidos

Lagoa de Óbidos is a coastal lagoon west of the walled town, opening toward the Atlantic through a shifting sandbar. Birdlife, windsurfers, and shoreline walks share the water and reed edges. Villages around the lagoon depend on both fishing traditions and seasonal beach use.

39.4126° N / 9.2148° W
