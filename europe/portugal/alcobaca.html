Alcobaça

Alcobaça developed around a Cistercian monastery founded in 1153 by Afonso Henriques after he took Santarém from Moorish rule, with royal tombs placed in its austere nave. The abbey's kitchens, cloisters, and water channels organised one of Portugal's richest agricultural estates through the Middle Ages. The town that spread along the Alcoa stream later supplied labour to the monastery's farms and, after secularisation, to ceramics and light industry in the Leiria district.

Monastery of Alcobaça

Cistercians founded Alcobaça Monastery in the twelfth century under royal patronage, creating an austere Gothic church, cloisters and monastic rooms with later additions. The tombs of Pedro I and Inês de Castro give the complex national historical importance. Heritage recognition covers the monastery as a whole, whose architectural value comes from scale, engineering and disciplined spatial design.

Miradouro do Suberco

Located Nazare, Portugal, the Miradouro do Suberco is a viewpoint that stands 110 meters above sea level. This lookout offers panoramic vistas of both Praia de Banhos Beach and Praia da Nazare Beach, making it an essential stop for anyone visiting this coastal town. Known for its landscapes and active fishing culture, Nazare draws travelers with its delicious seafood and wooden fishing boats called 'bateiras.'.

Sanctuary of Our Lady of Nazareth

The Sanctuary of Our Lady of Nazareth stands in Sitio above the coastal town of Nazare. Medieval pilgrimage traditions center on a small Marian image and the legend of Dom Fuas Roupinho, while later Baroque rebuilding produced the present church and tile-covered interior.

Farol da Nazaré

Nazare Lighthouse occupies the Fort of Sao Miguel Arcanjo on a headland above Praia do Norte. The fort's rooms interpret local maritime history and the development of large-wave surfing, while the light and cliff edge overlook the underwater canyon responsible for exceptional swell. Weather and safety barriers limit access, and the museum's display changes as new surf records and equipment are added.

39.6045° N / 9.0853° W

North Beach

Facing the Nazaré cliffs near Alcobaça, North Beach gained surfing fame after Garrett McNamara's record big-wave ride in 2011. Steep approaches and open sand favor swell watching as much as swimming. Walkers usually reach the strand on foot from coastal roads and paths above the cliffs.

Praia de São Martinho do Porto

A scallop-shaped bay holds the wide white sand of Praia de São Martinho do Porto, where calm water suits families and small boats. Nearby beaches add views toward Mangues Mountain and openings for wind sports. The enclosed shape keeps the main strand more sheltered than open Atlantic coasts.

39.5093° N / 9.1351° W

Praia do Salgado

Praia do Salgado stretches as a wide Atlantic beach near Alcobaça, with long sands suited to walking while strong surf makes swimming hazardous. Parking fills quickly on warm days. Wind and open swell keep the shore feeling exposed compared with more sheltered coves nearby.

39.5473° N / 9.1114° W

Praia da Nazaré

Praia da Nazaré forms the main town beach of Nazaré, with a broad sandy arc facing the Atlantic. Lifeguarded zones allow swimming when conditions permit, though swell remains constant. The beach sits below the cliffs that made the town famous for large winter waves.

39.5971° N / 9.0735° W

Miradouro da Pederneira

Miradouro da Pederneira is one of the three main districts in Nazare, Portugal. It is a mainly residential area accessible by car or a funicular carriage that takes passengers up the sharp slope for a small fee. The site is catalogued as a viewpoint.

39.5961° N / 9.0648° W

Monte de São Bartolomeu

Located a large pine forest, Monte de São Bartolomeu is a destination that draws travellers for both its natural beauty and cultural significance. Each year, the village of Nazaré comes alive as locals gather to celebrate tradition with bonfires and communal feasts featuring grilled pork, chorizo, and black pudding served alongside crusty Portuguese bread.
