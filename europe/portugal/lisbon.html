Explore Lisbon

A Brief History

Lisbon, Europe's second-oldest capital, is a spectacular, sun-kissed city beautifully built across seven steep hills overlooking the Tagus River. Famously known for its remarkably scenic viewpoints (miradouros), historic yellow trams, and stunning Manueline architecture, it is a city deeply rich in maritime history. Visitors are utterly captivated by its melancholic Fado music, incredible seafood, and vibrant, winding neighborhoods.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

Praça do Comércio

Historically the site of the Royal Palace before the 1755 earthquake, this large harbor-facing plaza was the entry point for maritime trade and returning explorers.

Free

Rua Augusta Arch

A triumphal arch built to commemorate the reconstruction of Lisbon after the 1755 earthquake, featuring historical figures and classical allegories.

€10

Jeronimos Monastery

A masterpiece of Manueline architecture commissioned in 1501 to commemorate Vasco da Gama's successful voyage to India, reflecting Portugal's maritime wealth.

Free

Empire Square Garden

A large landscaped square built in 1940 for the Portuguese World Exhibition, designed to celebrate the nation's historical reach and imperial history.

Free

Torre de Belem

A 16th-century fortification that served as both a defensive bulwark for the Tagus estuary and a ceremonial gateway to Lisbon for departing explorers.

Free

Monument to the Discoveries

Erected in 1960 to mark the 500th anniversary of Henry the Navigator's death, honoring the key figures of the Portuguese Age of Discovery.

Free

Museu do Tesouro Real

A modern high-security museum that permanently exhibits the Portuguese Crown Jewels and the historical treasures of the royal family.

Free

Palácio Nacional da Ajuda

A neoclassical palace that became the official royal residence in the 19th century, remaining unfinished when the monarchy was abolished in 1910.

Free

Castelo de São Jorge

A historic Moorish castle that was later captured by Christian crusaders in 1147, serving as a royal palace and military barracks throughout its history.

Free

Eduardo VII Park

Lisbon's largest central park, originally established in the early 20th century and later named to honor King Edward VII of the UK to reaffirm the Anglo-Portuguese Alliance.

Free

Estufa Fria

A botanical greenhouse complex built in the 1930s within an old basalt quarry, originally created to shelter sensitive exotic plants arriving from global colonies.
