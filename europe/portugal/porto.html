Explore Porto

A Brief History

Porto is a highly charismatic, steeply terraced coastal city famous worldwide for its namesake Port wine and stunning, highly colorful riverfront. The city's historic Ribeira district beautifully features narrow cobblestone streets, vibrant tiled facades, and spectacular views across the Douro River. It perfectly balances a deeply authentic, slightly gritty historic charm with incredible culinary sophistication.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

Porto Cathedral

A Romanesque monument dating back to the 12th century, serving as one of the oldest and most important religious edifices in the city's history.

Free

Chapel of Souls

An 18th-century chapel notable for its exterior, which was covered in 1929 with thousands of azulejo tiles depicting the lives of Saint Francis and Saint Catherine.

Free

Bolhão Market

A historic, neoclassical municipal market opened in 1914, remaining a central part of Porto's daily commercial and culinary life.

Free

Jardins do Palacio de Cristal

A 19th-century landscaped garden overlooking the Douro River, originally built to surround an industrial exhibition palace that was later demolished.

Free

Miradouro da Ponte da Arrábida

A viewing platform established on the concrete arch of the Arrábida Bridge, which was the largest concrete arch in the world when it opened in 1963.

Free

Cais da Ribeira

The historic riverside district of Porto, long functioning as a bustling commercial port for the export of port wine and traditional goods.

Free

Luís I Bridge

A double-deck metal arch bridge opened in 1886, designed by a disciple of Gustave Eiffel to connect Porto with the port wine lodges of Vila Nova de Gaia.

Free

Clérigos Church

A Baroque church completed in 1750, recognized for its towering 75-meter bell tower that historically served as a navigational landmark for ships on the Douro.
