Explore Porto

A Brief History

Porto lies on the Douro River estuary and gave its name to Portugal. Medieval trade and wine export from the upper Douro shaped its economy. The historic center with the cathedral, Ribeira quays, and Dom Luís I bridge was inscribed as a UNESCO World Heritage site in 1996. Port wine cellars in Vila Nova de Gaia aged barrels for British merchants from the 18th century onward.

Porto Cathedral

A historic, formidable Romanesque fortress-church masterfully standing high above the winding riverfront. It beautifully features, intensely detailed blue azulejo tile cloisters and provides, sweeping views of the vibrant city below.

Free

Chapel of Souls

The Chapel of Santa Catarina, also known as the Chapel of Souls, is a chapel located on the shopping street of Rua de Santa Catarina, in the former parish of Santo Ildefonso, in the city of Porto, in Portugal. It is particularly noted for the blue azulejo tiles on its exterior walls.

Free

Bolhão Market

A historic, vibrant neoclassical marketplace that has served as the culinary heart of the city since 1914. It brilliantly showcases the rich, authentic traditional gastronomy and daily commerce of northern Portugal.

Free

Jardins do Palacio de Cristal

The Crystal Palace in Portugal's second largest city of Porto was inspired by The Crystal Palace in London. It was inaugurated in 1865 to host the 1865 International Exhibition. Eventually falling into disrepair, it was demolished in 1951 to make way for a Sports Pavilion, today known as the Rosa Mota Pavilion, named after the Portuguese marathon runner.

Free (€18)

Miradouro da Ponte da Arrábida

The Fort of Paimogo, also known as the Fort of Our Lady of the Angels of Paimogo, stands in a dominant position on Paimogo beach near the town of Lourinhã, Lisbon District in Portugal Constructed in 1674, it ceased to serve a military function in 1834 and is now under threat from coastal erosion. The fort presents an almost unique example of a fort from that period that has not undergone subsequent modification.

Free

Cais da Ribeira

The Luiz I Bridge, commonly known as Dom Luís I Bridge, is a double-deck metal arch bridge that spans the river Douro between the cities of Porto and Vila Nova de Gaia in Portugal. At its construction, its 172 metres span was the longest of its type in the world. It can be confused with the nearby Maria Pia Bridge, a railway bridge that was built 9 years earlier to the east), which is similar in aspect to the Luiz I bridge.

Clérigos Church

The Clérigos Church is a Baroque church in the city of Porto, in Portugal. Its 75-meter-tall bell tower, the Torre dos Clérigos, can be seen from various points of the city and is one of its most characteristic symbols.
