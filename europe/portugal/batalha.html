Explore Batalha

A Brief History

Batalha owes its fame to the Dominican monastery King João I vowed to build after the 1385 victory at Aljubarrota secured Portuguese independence from Castile. Royal tombs, Founder's Chapel, and the unfinished Capelas Imperfeitas display Flamboyant Gothic and Manueline carving paid for by pepper profits from Africa and Asia. The small town that grew beside the abbey remained tied to court ceremony, craft guilds, and later agricultural life on the Estremadura plateau.

Top Sights & Activities

€15

Batalha Monastery

The Batalha Monastery is a masterpiece of late Gothic and Manueline architecture, recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage site. Built to commemorate the Portuguese victory at the Battle of Aljubarrota in 1385, the vast complex features the impressive Unfinished Chapels, intricate stone tracery, and the magnificent Founder's Chapel containing royal tombs.

€9

Centro de Interpretação da Batalha de Aljubarrota

Centro de Interpretação da Batalha de Aljubarrota sits within the Batalha Monastery area of Batalha, documented in local heritage registers for its civic or cultural role. Visitors approach from the main access road and combine the visit with neighbouring heritage buildings and public spaces in the district.

Free

Source of the River Lis

Source of the River Lis is a documented body of water in Batalha, Portugal, recognised in regional geographic listings for its ecological and recreational role. The shoreline is accessible from the surrounding district and supports walking paths and boat launches. Travellers approach from the adjacent promenade and combine the visit with nearby parks and waterfront districts.

€2

Castelo de Leiria

Nestled atop a cliff, the Castelo de Leiria is a medieval fortress that offers travellers an enchanting glimpse into Portugal's rich history. This remarkable site features beautiful gardens and the remnants of a Gothic church, all while providing panoramic views of the city below.

Free

Our Lady of the Immaculate Conception Cathedral, Leiria

Our Lady of the Immaculate Conception Cathedral, located in Leiria, Portugal, is a example of Joanine architecture from the 16th century. This cathedral shares its design with two other cathedrals in Portugal and is considered a hallmark of late Renaissance inspiration. The Mannerist-style building has undergone several changes over the years due to natural disasters and historical events.

Free

Jardim Luís de Camões

Jardim Luís de Camões is a charming urban park located in the town center, offering amenities such as drinking fountains, well-maintained restrooms, and an array of inviting bars and cafes. travellers can also benefit from the presence of an informative tourist office and seasonal attractions. The park is renowned for hosting a captivating medieval festival during certain times of the year, making it an ideal spot to immerse oneself in local culture.

Free

Fonte Luminosa

The Fonte Luminosa (Luminous Fountain) is a monumental fountain located in the Alameda Dom Afonso Henriques in Lisbon. Built in the 1940s, the grand structure features cascading water displays and is beautifully illuminated at night. It serves as a prominent landmark and a popular gathering space in the eastern part of the city.

Free

Jardim Almoínha Grande

Jardim Almoínha Grande is a modern, well-maintained public park located in the city of Leiria. The park features expansive green lawns, walking paths, contemporary water features, and a large children's playground. It provides a significant recreational space for local residents and a peaceful environment for outdoor activities.

Free

Rota do Vale do Lapedo

The Rota do Vale do Lapedo is a scenic hiking trail near Leiria that winds through a lush, deep limestone gorge. The valley is known for its dense vegetation, unique geological formations, and the archaeological site where the 'Lapedo Child,' a significant prehistoric skeleton, was discovered. The trail offers a beautiful and educational nature walk.

Free

Pia do Urso

Pia do Urso is a restored, traditional stone village located in the hills near Batalha. The village features a unique sensory eco-park designed for visually impaired visitors, with tactile elements and aromatic plants. The area is also known for its natural limestone basins ('pias'), which, according to legend, were used by bears for drinking.
