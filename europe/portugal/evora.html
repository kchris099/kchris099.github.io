Explore Évora

A Brief History

Évora grew from the Roman colony of Liberalitas Julia into a favoured residence of Portuguese kings during the Avis dynasty, when the Jesuit university and royal palace filled its walled centre. The Temple of Diana, cathedral, and sixteenth-century aqueduct span layers from antiquity through the Counter-Reformation. As capital of the Alentejo, Évora administered vast wheat and cork estates, leaving a compact city of whitewashed houses and arcaded squares that later gained UNESCO recognition.

Roman Temple of Évora

The Roman Temple of Évora, located in the Conde Vila Flor square, is a remarkable ancient ruin built in the 1st century A.D. Initially dedicated to Emperor Augustus, it features impressive Corinthian pillars and stands as a symbol of Roman worship. Over the centuries, it served various purposes including as a mosque during the Moorish period and later as a market and slaughterhouse.

Cathedral of Évora

The Cathedral of Évora, completed in 1746, is a prominent Roman Catholic cathedral offering scenic city views from its tower. It is significant cathedrals in Portugal and sits on the highest point of the city, making it a prominent feature of the cityscape.

Free

Giraldo Square Fountain

Giraldo Square Fountain is located in the central district and faces the basilica of St. Andrew. It was named after Giraldo the Fearless, who defeated the Moors in 1165 and became the governor of Evora. The square used to be a Roman market and was later renamed in his honor. The fountain originally sourced water from an aqueduct for public use in ancient times.

€6

Igreja e Mosteiro de São Francisco

Igreja e Mosteiro de São Francisco is a Gothic cathedral in Evora, built in honor of St. Francis during the reign of Manuel I. Adjacent to the cathedral is the famous Chapel of Bones, constructed from human skulls and bones collected from overcrowded cemeteries. The chapel's walls are adorned with motifs and its white brick ceiling features death-themed frescoes.

Free

Ruínas Fingidas

Ruínas Fingidas, or "Faux Ruins," may sound like a deceptive attraction at first, but it's actually a collection of constructed ruins made from pieces of real ruins found in the surrounding area. These faux ruins are roped off, preventing travellers from entering or climbing them, but they offer a picturesque and nostalgic sight within the beautiful park. While not historically significant themselves, these ruins contribute to the overall ambiance of the park.

Free

Theatro Garcia de Rezende

I regret not being able to explore the interior of Theatro Garcia de Rezende as it was closed during my visit. However, I admired its exterior from a distance. The theater company, known for its multitude of events and friendly staff, creates a vibrant atmosphere within the establishment. The place is visually and overall pleasant.

Free

Aqueduct Água de Prata

The Agua de Prata Aqueduct, also known as the Evora aqueduct, is a stone structure designed by Francisco de Arruda in the 1530s. Extending for nearly 9 kilometers through various parts of the city's historic center, this well-preserved water canal offers a picturesque path alongside it. The aqueduct showcases an elegant blend of old and new, with its rough stone construction seamlessly integrated into the modern town.

Free

Alto de São Bento

Alto de São Bento, just a short drive from Evora's old town, is a popular viewpoint offering panoramic views of the city and its surroundings. Locals often visit for picnics or to enjoy the sunset in a relaxed atmosphere. The area also features little white windmills and a Museological Center dedicated to preserving natural and cultural heritage. It's an ideal spot for a leisurely walk with historical information available on-site.

Free

Great Dolmen of Zambujeiro

Nestled near the historic city of Évora, the Great Dolmen of Zambujeiro stands as a testament to ancient engineering and communal practices from the late Neolithic period, around 4000-3000 BCE. This remarkable megalithic burial chamber is constructed from enormous granite slabs that create a striking stone chamber topped with an impressive capstone. As dolmens in Europe, it offers travellers a glimpse into prehistoric rituals and burial customs.

Free

Almendres Cromlech

Located just west of Evora near Guadalupe, the Almendres Cromlech, also known as Cromleque dos Almendres, is a Neolithic-era monument dating back over 6,000 years. It is the largest megalithic site on the Iberian Peninsula and predates Stonehenge in England.
