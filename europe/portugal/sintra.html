Sintra

Portuguese royalty summered in Sintra's forested hills northwest of Lisbon. The National Palace with twin chimneys mixes Moorish and Manueline fabric; Ferdinand II added Pena Palace on a hilltop ruin in the nineteenth century. Romantic estates and gardens spread through the municipality. UNESCO listed the town and its surrounding palace parks as a World Heritage site in 1995.

Centro historico de Sintra

The historic center of Sintra is a, labyrinthine district located the foothills of the Sintra Mountains. It is characterized by narrow, steep cobblestone streets, traditional Portuguese architecture, and numerous artisan shops and cafes. The area is the gateway to the region's famous palaces and is dominated by the distinctive twin conical chimneys of the Sintra National Palace.

Palácio Nacional da Pena

The Pena Palace is a Romanticist palace in São Pedro de Penaferrim, in the municipality of Sintra, on the Portuguese Riviera. The castle stands on the top of a hill in the Sintra Mountains above the town of Sintra, and on a clear day it can be easily seen from Lisbon and much of its metropolitan area. It is a national monument and constitutes one of the major expressions of 19th-century Romanticism in the world.
