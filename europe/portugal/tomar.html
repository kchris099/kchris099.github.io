Explore Tomar

A Brief History

Tomar was established in the twelfth century within the lands granted to the Knights Templar, serving as their Portuguese headquarters during the Reconquista. After the Templars were dissolved, the Portuguese Order of Christ took over the town, playing a crucial role in funding Portugal's maritime discoveries in the fifteenth century. The town expanded in a grid pattern under Prince Henry the Navigator. This legacy of crusaders and explorers dominates the city, culminating in the monumental Convent of Christ, the Templar Castle, and the historic 15th-century synagogue.

Convent of Christ

Perched atop a hill in the picturesque town of Tomar, Portugal, the Convent of Christ is a Roman Catholic convent adorned with intricate Manueline-style sculptures and medieval cloisters. The convent is part of a cluster of monuments located on one of the highest points in the city. Accessible via Avenida Dr Vieira Guimaraes, this historical site offers visitors a journey back in time to explore its rich connection to the legendary Knights Templar.

€15

Tomar castle

Castelo de Tomar, established in the mid-12th century, served as a defensive fortress and symbolized the Knights Templars' dominance. Strategically located on a hill, it played a pivotal role in Portugal's Reconquista against Moorish incursions. The nearby Convent of Christ is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and marvel of medieval architecture. Visitors can immerse themselves in history and imagine the warrior monks who once inhabited this place.

Free

Seven Hills National Forest

Seven Hills National Forest, also known as Tomars Mata dos Sete Montes National Woodland, is a serene 39-hectare park that connects to the castle and was once used for cultivation by the Order of Christ. The forest features ancient trees and a cylindrical stone tower called Charolinha, resembling the lantern towers of the Convent of Christ. It is a favorite among locals and tourists alike, offering beautiful nature walks below or around the castle walls.

Free

Church Santa Maria do Olival

The Church Santa Maria do Olival in Tomar was built as the pantheon for the Knights Templar leaders, and it is where the city's founder, Gualdim Pais, is buried. Remodelled during the 13th century, it showcases early Gothic architecture with a rose window and a freestanding bell tower. The church still holds services and features remarkable history and tombs.

Aqueduto do Convento de Cristo - Troço Pegões Altos

An impressive aqueduct built to supply water to the Convent of Christ. Visitors can admire its engineering marvel and enjoy the scenic views from its arches. It remains a notable local point of interest situated in Tomar, Portugal. Visitors can observe the surrounding area and its connection to the city's broader historical landscape. Local guides sometimes include it on walking routes through the centre.

Free

Igreja de São João Baptista

Igreja de São João Baptista is a 16th-century church located in the old town of Tomar, Portugal. The church features a Manueline doorway and an impressive octagonal bell tower adorned with Manueline motifs such as the armillary sphere and the Templar Cross. Inside, visitors can admire paintings by Gregorio Lopes and exquisite 17th-century diamond-point azulejos.

Free

Sinagoga de Tomar

The Synagogue of Tomar, dating back to the 15th century, is a historical gem with vaulted ceilings and religious artifacts on display. Renovated yet preserving its ancient manuscripts, it offers a glimpse into the rich religious history of the town. As the only synagogue of its kind in Portugal, it stands as a testament to Tomar's Jewish heritage and is a must-visit for those interested in exploring this aspect of the city's past.

Free
