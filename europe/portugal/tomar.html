Tomar

Tomar was established in the twelfth century within the lands granted to the Knights Templar, serving as their Portuguese headquarters during the Reconquista. After the Templars were dissolved, the Portuguese Order of Christ took over the town, playing an important role in funding Portugal's maritime discoveries in the fifteenth century. The town expanded in a grid pattern under Prince Henry the Navigator. This legacy of crusaders and explorers dominates the city, culminating in the monumental Convent of Christ, the Templar Castle, and the historic 15th-century synagogue.

Convent of Christ

Perched atop a hill in the town of Tomar, Portugal, the Convent of Christ is a Roman Catholic convent adorned with detailed Manueline-style sculptures and medieval cloisters. The convent is part of a cluster of monuments located on one of the highest points in the city. Accessible via Avenida Dr Vieira Guimaraes, this historical site has a journey back in time to explore its rich connection to the legendary Knights Templar.

Tomar castle

Castelo de Tomar, established in the mid-12th century, served as a defensive fortress and symbolized the Knights Templars' dominance. Strategically located on a hill, it played a important role in Portugal's Reconquista against Moorish incursions. The nearby Convent of Christ is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and marvel of medieval architecture. Visitors can immerse themselves in history and imagine the warrior monks who once inhabited this place.

The Synagogue of Tomar, dating back to the 15th century, is a historical gem with vaulted ceilings and religious artifacts on display. Renovated yet preserving its ancient manuscripts, it offers a glimpse into the rich religious history of the town.
