Destination Guide — Kilkenny

Explore Kilkenny

A Brief History

Kilkenny developed around a monastic settlement founded by St. Canice before becoming the medieval capital of Norman Ireland in the twelfth century. The powerful Butler family ruled from Kilkenny Castle for centuries, holding the influential Confederation of Kilkenny in the 1640s during the Irish Confederate Wars. The city managed to preserve much of its Norman street plan and merchant houses despite the turbulence of the Cromwellian conquest. Today, visitors walk the Medieval Mile between the imposing castle and the Gothic St. Canice's Cathedral.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

Kilkenny Castle Park

Kilkenny Castle Park, a top tourist spot in Kilkenny, is set on 50 acres of land overlooking the River Nore. The park features formal and informal gardens, including a renowned rose garden and an ornamental lake. Visitors can explore the fortified Augustinian monastery ruins at their leisure, enjoying the serene atmosphere and picturesque surroundings.

€7

St Canice's Cathedral

St Canice's Cathedral is a 13th-century Catholic cathedral located in Kilkenny, Ireland. It is the second-largest cathedral in the country and has been well-preserved through careful restoration efforts. Adjacent to the cathedral is a 9th-century round tower with panoramic views of the city and surrounding countryside, which can be reached by climbing its 121 steps. The cathedral, along with Kilkenny Castle, forms part of Ireland's -preserved medieval town center.

Free

The Black Abbey

The Black Abbey, a Dominican church and priory established in 1225, stands as a testament to Kilkenny's rich medieval heritage. This remarkable site has been beautifully restored, showcasing its impressive multi-colored Rosary Window—Ireland's largest stained-glass window—crafted in 1892. The vibrant colors come alive when illuminated by sunlight, creating an enchanting atmosphere within the abbey.

Free

St. Mary's Cathedral

St. Mary's Cathedral, located in Kilkenny, Ireland, is a grand Roman Catholic cathedral completed in 1857. Situated on the town's highest point, this imposing limestone landmark features a 60-meter tall tower that dominates the skyline and is beautifully illuminated at night. The cathedral was designed by William Deane Butler and is considered magnificent churches in Kilkenny. Its design is said to have been inspired by Gloucester Cathedral.

€25

Smithwick's Experience

Immerse yourself in the rich history of Kilkenny and its brewery at the Smithwick's Experience. This interactive guided tour takes you through an 18th-century brewery, offering a fascinating insight into the heritage and brewing process of Smithwick's ale. Explore holographics, old machinery, and hops while being guided by passionate locals with deep roots in the brewery.

€8

Kilkenny Castle

Kilkenny Castle, located in the county town of Kilkenny, is a riverside castle with a beautifully restored interior that offers tours to visitors. The castle is surrounded by a large garden and features an art gallery and tearoom. The town itself boasts Ireland's most preserved Medieval center, along with the impressive Castle and Cathedral.

€12

St. Mary’s Medieval Mile Museum

Immerse yourself in the rich history of Kilkenny by visiting the St. Mary’s Medieval Mile Museum, housed within a 13th-century church and graveyard. The museum showcases an array of medieval sculptures, Renaissance-era tombs, and artifacts dating back over 800 years. Highlights include high crosses, stone-carved tombs, and significant relics discovered during the church's restoration.

€17

The National Reptile Zoo

Located in Kilkenny City, The National Reptile Zoo is a unique attraction dedicated to the conservation of reptiles and their habitats. With over 50 exhibits, visitors can get up close to snakes, crocodiles, geckos, and turtles while learning about these fascinating creatures. The zoo offers supervised handling sessions for both kids and adults, providing an educational experience for all ages. Additionally, there are opportunities for family fun with soft play areas available on-site.

€5

Jerpoint Abbey

A Cistercian abbey with intricate stone carvings and a rich history dating back to the 12th century. It remains a notable local point of interest situated in Kilkenny, Ireland. Visitors can observe the surrounding area and its connection to the city's broader historical landscape. Local guides sometimes include it on walking routes through the centre.

€15

Jurassic Newpark

Jurassic Newpark at the Newpark Hotel is a thrilling experience that takes you back in time to the era of dinosaurs. The life-sized animatronic dinosaurs, including the mighty T-Rex, create an immersive environment where visitors can watch them move and hear their roars. In addition to the prehistoric creatures, there's a pet farm with adorable animals like llamas and emus.
