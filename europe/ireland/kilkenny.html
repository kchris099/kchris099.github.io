Kilkenny

Kilkenny grew around St Canice's monastic settlement before becoming the medieval capital of Norman Ireland in the twelfth century. The Butler family ruled from Kilkenny Castle for centuries and hosted the Confederation of Kilkenny in the 1640s. The Norman street plan and merchant houses survived Cromwellian conquest; the Medieval Mile still runs between the castle and Gothic St Canice's Cathedral.

Kilkenny Castle Park

Riverside grounds around Kilkenny Castle open lawns and paths beside the restored castle that offers interior tours. Walls, courtyards, and parkland form the walking route when grounds are open. The park extends the castle's public presence into green space along the River Nore in the county town.

St Canice's Cathedral

St. Canice's Cathedral stands within Kilkenny's medieval quarter on a site of much earlier Christian worship, and the narrow tower climb depends on weather and capacity, and size rankings among Irish cathedrals require careful definitions. The thirteenth-century church contains tombs, effigies, stained glass, carved stone, and memorials, while a separate round tower beside it predates the cathedral.

The Black Abbey

Dominicans founded Kilkenny's Black Abbey in the thirteenth century, and the church returned to Catholic worship after centuries of suppression, alteration and restoration. A broad nave, cloister traces and carved tombs record its medieval origins. The large nineteenth-century Rosary Window adds color to an interior still used for regular services.

St. Mary's Cathedral

St. Mary's Cathedral was completed in 1857 as the Roman Catholic cathedral of Kilkenny. William Deane Butler designed the Gothic Revival church around a tall central tower, with pinnacles, lancet windows and a long nave shaping its skyline presence. Stained glass, carved altars and devotional furnishings fill the interior, while the elevated site makes the tower visible across much of the city.

Smithwick's Experience

Immerse oneself in the rich history of Kilkenny and its brewery at the Smithwick's Experience. This interactive guided tour takes visitors through an 18th-century brewery, offering a fascinating insight into the heritage and brewing process of Smithwick's ale. Explore holographics, old machinery, and hops while being guided by passionate locals with deep roots in the brewery.

Kilkenny Castle

Kilkenny Castle developed from an Anglo-Norman stronghold into the principal residence of the Butler family, and nineteenth-century remodeling and modern restoration shape what is seen today, while self-guided and guided areas may follow different access rules. Towers, courtyards, state rooms, a picture gallery, library, and reconstructed interiors overlook extensive parkland beside the River Nore.

St. Mary’s Medieval Mile Museum

Kilkenny's Medieval Mile Museum occupies the former St. Mary's parish church and its graveyard. Burials, carved tombs, civic records, sculpture, archaeological finds, and modern displays interpret the merchants and families who shaped the medieval city. The adapted church itself is central to the museum.

The National Reptile Zoo

The National Reptile Zoo in Kilkenny is a private collection focused on reptiles, amphibians and invertebrates. Enclosures, education talks, breeding work and supervised contact may form part of visits, though species and handling policies change. Animal welfare, hygiene, staff training and current licensing are more important than fixed exhibit counts or promises that every age group can hold animals.

Jerpoint Abbey

Near Thomastown, a twelfth-century Cistercian ruin keeps cloister carvings and sculpted tombs under roofless naves. Stone columns still carry detailed figures in the open air. The rural abbey sits apart from Kilkenny city's medieval core, rewarding a short countryside detour for carved monastic fabric.

Jurassic Newpark

Jurassic Newpark occupies part of the grounds of Kilkenny's Newpark Hotel as a commercial family attraction. Animatronic dinosaurs and themed paths create a staged prehistoric setting. Play areas and a small animal section add separate forms of recreation. Seasonal opening, hotel events and changes to the animal collection affect an experience managed independently from Kilkenny's historic attractions.
