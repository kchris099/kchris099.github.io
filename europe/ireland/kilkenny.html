Kilkenny

Kilkenny developed around a monastic settlement founded by St. Canice before becoming the medieval capital of Norman Ireland in the twelfth century. The powerful Butler family ruled from Kilkenny Castle for centuries, holding the influential Confederation of Kilkenny in the 1640s during the Irish Confederate Wars. The city managed to preserve much of its Norman street plan and merchant houses despite the turbulence of the Cromwellian conquest. Today, visitors walk the Medieval Mile between the imposing castle and the Gothic St. Canice's Cathedral.
