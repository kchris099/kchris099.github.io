A Brief History

Vilnius served as the capital of the Grand Duchy of Lithuania from the 14th century, with castle hill, cathedral square, and university founded in 1579 shaping a multiconfessional baroque cityscape. Polish-Lithuanian Commonwealth and Russian imperial rule added palaces, churches, and later industrial quarters. The city was a center of Jewish learning before the Holocaust and Soviet occupation altered its population and built fabric.

Top Sights & Activities

Vilnius Cathedral

Vilnius Cathedral stands on the historic ceremonial square at the foot of castle hill and has been rebuilt across many centuries. It remains one of the main religious and civic monuments of Lithuania. The neoclassical facade and interior chapels preserve the site where grand dukes were honored and where Catholic life has centered since the Middle Ages.

€6

Bell Tower of Vilnius Cathedral

The detached bell tower on Cathedral Square incorporates older defensive and civic functions into the cathedral precinct. Its form records the long development of the city's ceremonial center. Visitors can climb the tower for views over the square, castle hill, and the baroque church towers of the old town below.

€8

Gediminas Castle

Gediminas Castle Tower crowns the upper castle site above Vilnius and remains the clearest surviving part of the medieval fortifications. The hilltop position is central to narratives of Lithuanian statehood. A museum inside the tower presents archaeological finds and history of the grand duchy, while the summit offers wide views over the Neris and Vilnia river valleys.

Free

Church of St. Anne

St Anne's Church is a late Gothic church long associated with the old town and nearby Bernardine complex. Its brick facade remains one of the main monuments of Vilnius architecture. The slender towers and intricate masonry drew attention from visitors and rulers since the 16th century, including Napoleon, who reportedly wished to carry the church to Paris.

Free

Gates of Dawn

The Gates of Dawn preserve one of the former city gates of Vilnius and contain a chapel with a revered Marian image. The site marks the southern edge of the old town and its pilgrimage tradition. Devotees and tourists pass through the gate daily to see the silver-clad icon visible from the street and to visit the chapel above the arch.

Free

Holy Trinity Church & Basilian Gate

The Holy Trinity complex and Basilian Gate reflect the Uniate and monastic history of Vilnius near the route from the Gates of Dawn. The ensemble preserves religious architecture tied to the eastern Christian traditions of the city. The gate, church, and monastery buildings show how Ruthenian and Orthodox communities shaped the multicultural fabric of early modern Vilnius.

Free

Saint Catherine's Church

Saint Catherine's Church was rebuilt in the Baroque period for the Benedictine convent and remains part of the dense churchscape of old Vilnius. Its facade and interior record the Catholic rebuilding of the early modern city. The church now serves as a concert venue, linking sacred architecture with Vilnius traditions of choral and organ music.
