Explore Vilnius

A Brief History

Vilnius served as the capital of the Grand Duchy of Lithuania from the 14th century, with castle hill, cathedral square, and university founded in 1579 shaping a multiconfessional baroque cityscape. Polish-Lithuanian Commonwealth and Russian imperial rule added palaces, churches, and later industrial quarters. The city was a center of Jewish learning before the Holocaust and Soviet occupation altered its population and built fabric.

Vilnius Cathedral

Vilnius Cathedral stands on the historic ceremonial square at the foot of castle hill and has been rebuilt across many centuries. It remains one of the main religious and civic monuments of Lithuania. The neoclassical facade and interior chapels preserve the site where grand dukes were honored and where Catholic life has centered since the Middle Ages.

Gediminas Castle

Gediminas Castle Tower crowns the upper castle site above Vilnius and remains the clearest surviving part of the medieval fortifications. The hilltop position is central to narratives of Lithuanian statehood. A museum inside the tower presents archaeological finds and history of the grand duchy, while the summit offers wide views over the Neris and Vilnia river valleys.

Free

Church of St. Anne

Church of St. Anne is a church in Vilnius, Lithuania. St Anne's Church is a late Gothic church long associated with the old town and nearby Bernardine complex. Its brick facade remains one of the main monuments of Vilnius architecture. The slender towers and detailed masonry drew attention from visitors and rulers since the 16th century, including Napoleon, who reportedly wished to carry the church to Paris. The exterior and main interior spaces are usually viewable outside service times.

Gates of Dawn

The Gates of Dawn preserve one of the former city gates of Vilnius and contain a chapel with a revered Marian image. The site marks the southern edge of the old town and its pilgrimage tradition. Devotees and tourists pass through the gate daily to see the silver-clad icon visible from the street and to visit the chapel above the arch.

Free

Holy Trinity Church & Basilian Gate

The Holy Trinity complex and Basilian Gate reflect the Uniate and monastic history of Vilnius near the route from the Gates of Dawn. The ensemble preserves religious architecture tied to the eastern Christian traditions of the city. The gate, church, and monastery buildings show how Ruthenian and Orthodox communities shaped the multicultural fabric of early modern Vilnius.

Saint Catherine's Church

Saint Catherine's Church was rebuilt in the Baroque period for the Benedictine convent and remains part of the dense churchscape of old Vilnius. Its facade and interior record the Catholic rebuilding of the early modern city. The church now is a concert venue, linking sacred architecture with Vilnius traditions of choral and organ music.

Vilnius TV Tower

Vilnius TV Tower is a visitor site in Vilnius, Lithuania. It opened or was completed around 1980. The Vilnius TV Tower, standing at 326.5m tall, is the tallest structure in Lithuania and one of the tallest TV towers in Europe. It was completed in 1980 and has since become a symbol of the city. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Three Crosses Monument

The Three Crosses Monument, also known as the Hill of Three Crosses, is a historic site located on one of the highest old town hills in Vilnius. According to legend, three monks erected the crosses in the 17th century to honor fellow monks who were martyred in the 14th century. The monument has undergone several reconstructions over time and offers panoramic views of Vilnius from across the river Vilnele.

Bernardine Garden is a known park in Vilnius, offering a riverfront experience with various attractions. Travellers can enjoy chess tables, a play area, and musical fountain shows. The park's lush green spaces and numerous water ponds provide ample opportunities for relaxation and leisurely strolls. Situated in the Old Town, it provides easy access to landmarks such as Gediminas Castle and the Cathedral.

Orthodox Church of the Holy Spirit, Vilnius

The Orthodox Church of the Holy Spirit in Vilnius has been a significant religious and cultural hub for over four centuries. It was established in 1567 by Helena, the wife of Alexander Jagiellon, and later expanded with a monastery, school, and printing shop. The church houses an ornate Baroque reliquary containing the relics of three saints: Anthony, John, and Eustathius.

Hall Market, also known as Hales Turgus, is a historic market hall in Vilnius that has been serving locals since 1906. Situated between the train station and the Gates of Dawn, it offers a wide selection of fresh produce, meat, cheese, and homemade products at very affordable prices. The century-old building itself is quite fascinating and resembles a train station.
