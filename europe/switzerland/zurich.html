Destination Guide — Zurich

Explore Zurich

A Brief History

Evolving from a Roman customs post on the Limmat River, Zurich became an early, powerful member of the Swiss Confederacy. The aggressive industrialization and banking reforms of the 19th century transformed the historic guild city into one of the world's leading financial powerhouses. Decades of careful preservation have ensured that this profound historical narrative remains a vital part of the modern experience.

Lindenhof

Lindenhof is a historic hilltop gathering place in Zurich, situated on the site of a former Roman fort. Visitors can enjoy outdoor chess boards and city views from this elevated plaza adorned with linden trees, benches, and giant chess sets. The park offers sweeping vistas of the Limmat River, Old Town's red-tiled rooftops, and the distant Swiss Alps. Despite a somewhat steep climb to reach the park area, the panoramic views make it truly rewarding.

Free

Jules Verne Panoramabar

Located in downtown Zurich, Jules Verne Panoramabar offers a sophisticated and lively atmosphere with panoramic views of the old town. This upscale bar is known for its international wine selection and cocktails, making it a perfect spot to unwind after a day of sightseeing. Situated near Bahnhofstrasse, it's an ideal pitstop while exploring the city.

Free

Bahnhofstrasse

Bahnhofstrasse is Zurich's 'Main Street,' a bustling pedestrianized boulevard that runs from the main train station to Burkliplatz at the head of the lake. This 1,200-meter street is lined with upscale cafes and luxury designer boutiques, making it one of Europe's most attractive shopping streets. The shops offer high-end goods like furs, fashion, and jewelry.

Fraumünster Church

Fraumünster Church, located in the Old Town of Zurich, is a historic and elegant church with a distinctive copper-topped spire. It was founded in 853 by King Louis the German and was once inhabited by female members of European aristocracy. The church is renowned for its stained glass windows designed by Marc Chagall, which create a kaleidoscope of colors inside the church.

Free

Confiserie Sprüngli

Confiserie Sprüngli is a renowned confectionery located on Bahnhofstrasse in Zurich. This family-run emporium has been offering exquisite treats since 1859 and is currently managed by the sixth generation. The flagship store showcases an array of Grand Cru chocolates, cakes, truffles, and more.

Free

Grossmünster

Grossmünster, also known as the "Great Minster," is an twin-towered Romanesque cathedral located in Zurich, Switzerland. It was built on the site of a Roman burial ground and has been a prominent landmark since its opening in 1220. The cathedral's tall twin towers are considered one of Zurich's most recognizable features and have stood for centuries.

Free

Zürich Opera House

Immerse yourself in the world of classic and contemporary opera, ballet, music concerts, and theatrical performances at the Zürich Opera House. This historic venue, which opened its doors in 1891, is renowned for its architecture and rich artistic legacy. Whether you're a seasoned opera enthusiast or a newcomer to the theater scene, the Zurich Opera House promises an experience with its acclaimed productions and talented performers from around the globe.

Free

Chinagarten Zürich

The Chinese Garden (Chinagarten) in Zurich is a beautiful, authentic classical Chinese garden located on the shores of Lake Zurich. It was a gift from Zurich's partner city, Kunming, in recognition of technical assistance provided to the Chinese city. The tranquil garden features traditional pavilions, a pond, and intricate woodwork, offering a peaceful cultural retreat.

Free

Platzspitz

Platzspitz Park, located in Zurich, Switzerland, has undergone a transformation in recent years and is now a family-friendly space. Situated between the Sihl River and the Limmat River, it offers a picturesque setting with tree-lined paths and an art deco gazebo for concerts. The park hosts various events during the summer months and is becoming increasingly popular.

Free

Prime Tower

The Prime Tower is a modern 126-meter building in Zurich, Switzerland, featuring shops and dining options, including an upscale restaurant on the 35th floor. It was once the tallest structure in Switzerland until the completion of the Roche-Turm 2 tower in Basel. Situated near Hardbrucke railway station, it is part of a developing business and residential district known as Zurich West.

Free

Aussichtsturm Uetliberg

The Aussichtsturm Uetliberg, also known as the Uetliberg Lookout Tower, is a striking steel-truss landmark offering an open observation platform with panoramic views of Zurich and the Alps. Perched atop Uetliberg Mountain, this captivating tower provides visitors with 360-degree vistas of the sprawling cityscape, the shimmering waters of Lake Zurich, and the majestic snow-capped peaks of the Alps in the distance.

Free

Zoo Zürich

Located in Zurich, Switzerland, Zoo Zürich is a renowned animal conservation facility that has been welcoming visitors since 1929. The zoo boasts an impressive collection of over 370 animal species living in carefully designed habitats. From the Lewa Savana to the Masoala Rainforest and the Kaeng Krachan Elephant Park, visitors can explore diverse natural environments and observe animals living as close to their natural habitats as possible.

Free

Loorenkopf

Loorenkopf tower, a 33-meter high wood lattice tower located just 25 minutes from Zurich HBF, offers a view of the Swiss countryside. The circular trail leading to the observation tower takes visitors through dense forest and is accessible via public transport. The trek can be tiring but rewards hikers with views all the way to Jungfrau, Monch, and Eiger. Clean water is available from a nearby fountain, and benches provide a place to relax.

Free

Lindt Factory Outlet Kilchberg

The Lindt Factory Outlet Kilchberg is a chocolate shop with many different types of chocolates to see and enjoy. The prices are not as cheap as at the museum, but they do have some discounts available. The factory itself is very interesting, with chocolate test giveaways all around. I enjoyed my time there, and the ticket price was definitely worth it. However, I did not appreciate the parking lot being free only for those who had purchased tickets!.

Free

Theater 11

The Theater 11 is a contemporary venue that hosts a diverse range of international shows, from musicals to dance performances. It also features a restaurant-bar for visitors to enjoy. The theater is conveniently located and easily accessible by public transportation or car, with nearby parking options available. While the seating is comfortable, some feel that drink holders are lacking. The stage size is ideal for the space, offering great views from every seat.
