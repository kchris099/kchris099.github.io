Zurich

A Roman customs post on the Limmat became a leading Confederate city of guilds. Nineteenth-century industry and banking remade the lakeside town into a financial centre. Grossmünster and the Bahnhofstrasse trace Reformation civic religion beside modern capital. Niederdorf lanes keep a medieval street grain below the banking towers. Zwingli preached the Reformation from the Grossmünster pulpit above the river.

Lindenhof

Lindenhof is a historic hilltop gathering place in Zurich, situated on the site of a former Roman fort. Visitors can enjoy outdoor chess boards and city views from this elevated plaza adorned with linden trees, benches, and giant chess sets. The park offers sweeping vistas of the Limmat River, Old Town's red-tiled rooftops, and the distant Swiss Alps. Despite a somewhat steep climb to reach the park area, the panoramic views make it truly rewarding.
