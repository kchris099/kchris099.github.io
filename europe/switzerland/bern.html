Bern

Bern was founded in 1191 on a peninsula in the Aare when the Zähringen dukes laid out a planned city that later joined the Swiss Confederacy in 1353. Medieval arcades, the Zytglogge clock tower, and the Federal Palace express how a provincial capital became the seat of Switzerland's federal government in 1848. Bear pits, sandstone façades, and the rose garden above the river record Bern's role linking Alpine passes to the Swiss plateau without heavy industrial growth.

BearPark

Bern's Bear Park extends along the Aare below the old town, providing sloped outdoor enclosures connected with the city's heraldic bear tradition. The facility replaced the cramped historic bear pit but still keeps wild animals in captivity within an urban setting.

02 AdmissionFree

Rosengarten Bern

The Rosengarten sits on a hillside above Bern's Aare bend, mixing rose beds with open lawns and a broad viewpoint across the old town roofs. Paths loop through seasonal plantings, and evening light often draws walkers who come as much for the city panorama as for the flowers. The terrace overlooks the cathedral tower, the river, and the Alpine horizon beyond the capital.

03 AdmissionFree

Zytglogge

Bern's Zytglogge began as a medieval gate tower and later became the city's astronomical clock landmark after fires reshaped the old town, including work tied to the early 1400s. Painted dials, moving figures, and a bell chamber mark the hours for Kramgasse below. The tower still divides the lower and upper old town streets and remains a fixed meeting point under the arcades.

46.9480° N / 7.4478° E

04 AdmissionFree

Kindlifresserbrunnen

In Bern's Old Town, Switzerland, stands the Kindlifresserbrunnen, a historic fountain dating back to 1545. This unique and somewhat eerie sculpture depicts a giant devouring a sack full of children, sparking intrigue and debate among historians and locals about its origins and meaning. Located in Kornhausplatz, this fountain is just one of many whimsical statue water fountains in Bern that travellers can explore while strolling through the medieval town.

05 AdmissionFree

Parliament Building

The Bundeshaus, completed in 1902, holds the Swiss Federal Assembly and Federal Council behind a domed facade facing Bundesplatz in Bern. Cupola, sandstone wings, and formal halls house debates and state ceremonies for the federal government. Public tours explain bicameral procedure and the building's role as the working center of Swiss politics rather than a ceremonial palace alone.

46.9466° N / 7.4443° E

06 AdmissionFree

Käfigturm

Kafigturm began as a medieval gate tower and later became a prison after Bern's defenses expanded. The present seventeenth-century structure stands on Marktgasse with a clock, passage, and upper rooms now used for political exhibitions and events. Its name recalls incarceration, while heavy pedestrian traffic through the arch shows how a former defensive threshold became part of the commercial city center.

46.9482° N / 7.4439° E

Cathedral of Bern

Bern Minster is Switzerland's tall late Gothic church, begun in the fifteenth century and completed over later generations, and the tower provides a high view when open, but stairs, weather, worship and maintenance regulate access to the active Reformed church. Its west portal depicts the Last Judgment, while the interior contains ribbed vaults, stained glass, choir stalls, organs and memorials.

46.9472° N / 7.4515° E

08 AdmissionFree

Münsterplattform Bern

Behind Bern Minster, the Münsterplattform spreads as a tree-lined terrace above the Aare, with benches, picnic tables, and a small playground. The paved square opens toward Alpine ridges on clear days and toward the cathedral's stone bulk on the city side. Locals use it for lunch breaks and weekend sun, while travellers pause between climbing the tower and walking the arcaded streets.

46.9468° N / 7.4518° E

09 AdmissionFree

UNESCO - Bern Old Town

Bern's historic old town occupies a loop of the Aare with arcaded streets, fountains, towers, sandstone facades and a medieval street pattern reshaped after the 1405 fire. The World Heritage designation covers a living urban district where homes, shops, transport and government continue to operate. Many buildings also reflect later reconstruction and alteration.

Dählhölzli

Dählhölzli, established in 1937, is a 15-hectare animal park featuring a petting zoo, vivarium, and bear park. The walk to the zoo begins at BearPark and takes visitors through the historic Matte district to Marzili pool and Schonausteg before reaching Dählhölzli Zoo. Here, travellers can enjoy refreshments at the restaurant while observing over 220 animal species including ibex and wild boars.

46.9344° N / 7.4505° E

11 AdmissionFree

Freibad Marzili

Marzili is a seasonal public river pool complex below Bern's federal buildings, with lawns, changing rooms, pools and direct access to the Aare. Strong current makes river swimming suitable for confident swimmers who understand local entry and exit points. Water temperature, flood conditions, weather and municipal rules affect use.

46.9424° N / 7.4438° E
