Destination Guide — Basel

Explore Basel

A Brief History

Basel stands on the Rhine where Switzerland meets France and Germany. Its university, founded in 1460, is among the country's oldest. The city joined the Swiss Confederation in 1501 and developed as a printing and trade center. Erasmus is buried in the cathedral. Chemical and pharmaceutical firms established headquarters here in the 19th and 20th centuries. Art museums hold works from Holbein to contemporary collections.

Basel Town Hall

Basel Town Hall is a visitor site in Basel, Switzerland. Construction and early use date to around 1501. Constructed in the early 16th century following a devastating earthquake, this red-sandstone building reflects the city's entry into the Swiss Confederation in 1501. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Free (tower climb CHF6)

Basel Cathedral

Basel Cathedral is a church in Basel, Switzerland. Rebuilt in the late 12th century after an earthquake, this red sandstone cathedral blends Romanesque and Gothic architectural styles. Historically the site of the influential Council of Basel, it houses the tomb of humanist scholar Erasmus, reflecting long-running religious significance and deep intellectual heritage. The exterior and main interior spaces are usually viewable outside service times.

Free

Sevogel Brunnen

Created in the 19th century, this fountain commemorates Henman Sevogel, a prominent 15th-century councilor and military leader who died at the Battle of St. Jakob an der Birs. The monument was part of a movement to celebrate local heroes, keeping the memory of medieval political struggles alive.

Free

Mittlere brucke

Mittlere brucke is a visitor site in Basel. First constructed in 1226, this was one of the earliest bridges across the Rhine, boosting the city's role as a major trading hub. The original structure was replaced by a stone bridge in 1905 to accommodate modern traffic, serving as a historical symbol of strategic and commercial development.

Free

Spalentor

Spalentor is a majestic 15th-century gateway that is one of the three remaining structures from the ancient walls of the city. It is located in Basel, a city known for its significance in the chemical and pharmaceutical industry, with major companies like Novartis and Hoffmann-La Roche being among Switzerland's largest employers.

Free

Tinguely Fountain

Tinguely Fountain is a visitor site in Basel, Switzerland. Records associated with the site include 1977. Tinguely-Brunnen, created in 1977 by Swiss artist Jean Tinguely, is a fusion of sculptural machines and water. The mechanical figures are in constant motion and interaction with each other, reminiscent of the performers who once graced the theater stage. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

CHF26

Zoo Basel

Established in 1874, Zoo Basel is the oldest zoo in Switzerland and a must-visit attraction. Known as "Zolli" by locals, it houses a diverse range of animals including gorillas, snow leopards, lions, elephants, giraffes, and chimpanzees. The well-maintained park offers a pleasant summer stroll while the animal houses and vivarium are popular during winter.

Free

St. Jakob-Park

St. Jakob-Park is a modern and comfortable stadium located in Basel, Switzerland. It is the home of FC Basel 1893 and offers guided tours that include access to the subs bench and VIP areas. The stadium has a capacity of over 6,000 seated spectators as well as standing spaces for additional travellers. St. Jakob-Park hosts various football games, sporting events, athletics games, and concerts throughout the year.

Free

Tierpark Lange Erlen

Tierpark Lange Erlen is a public park in Basel, Switzerland. Records associated with the site include 1871. The park is home to a variety of local and exotic animals such as deer, goats, peacocks, wild boars, donkeys, ponies, guinea pigs and rabbits. It offers spacious enclosures and scenic walking trails for wildlife enthusiasts and families to enjoy. Paths, lawns, and shaded areas make up the main public facilities.

Free

Border Triangle Basel

Dreiländereck, also known as the Three-Country Point, is a significant landmark where the borders of Germany, France, and Switzerland intersect. This spot is marked by a monument that symbolizes the friendship and harmony between these nations. Travellers can relax by the Rhine River and immerse themselves in nature while taking in views of the surrounding landscapes.
