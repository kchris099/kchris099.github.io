Destination Guide — Lausanne

Explore Lausanne

A Brief History

Lausanne originated as a Roman military camp called Lousanna before moving to its current hilly location for defense. During the Middle Ages, it became an important bishopric, culminating in the construction of its magnificent Gothic cathedral. In 1536, the city was conquered by Bern, introducing the Reformation and establishing a renowned Protestant academy. Today, Lausanne is a dynamic university city and the headquarters of the International Olympic Committee, overlooking Lake Geneva.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

Lausanne Cathedral

Lausanne Cathedral, consecrated in 1275, is a Gothic masterpiece that has been an important landmark in Switzerland since medieval times. The exterior of the cathedral is impressive with its ornate painted portal, belfry, and vibrant rose window. Inside, visitors can admire the grand organs, 13th-century choir stalls, and ancient stained glass windows. It also serves as a stop for pilgrims following the Camino de Santiago within Switzerland.

Free

Place de la Palud

Nestled in the heart of Lausanne, Place de la Palud is a charming cobbled square that beautifully captures the essence of European al fresco life. This vibrant locale is home to a 16th-century fountain adorned with a statue of justice, which adds historical flair to your visit. Every hour from 9 am to 7 pm, consider catch the delightful cuckoo clock performance that narrates tales from the Vaud region's rich history.

Free

Lausanne Opera

Lausanne Opera stands as a beacon of cultural richness, captivating music lovers and art enthusiasts alike. This modern venue is renowned for its exceptional operatic performances, alongside an array of concerts and ballets that showcase both classical and contemporary artistry. The opera house boasts architecture paired with top-notch acoustics, ensuring every performance resonates beautifully.

Free

Esplanade de Montbenon

Esplanade de Montbenon is a picturesque park in Lausanne, Switzerland, offering views of Lake Geneva and the surrounding Alps. The site features a statue of William Tell, known for its legendary hero equipped with a crossbow. This expansive terrace provides an extraordinary range of environments along a flat stretch covering over half a kilometer. Visitors can enjoy leisurely strolls and picnics in well-manicured gardens while taking in the city and lake views.

Free

Sauvabelin Tower

Perched atop a hill in Sauvabelin Park, the Sauvabelin Tower offers visitors an experience with its 302-step spiral staircase leading to a viewing platform. This impressive 35-meter wooden tower provides panoramic vistas of Lausanne and its surroundings, including Lake Geneva, the Swiss Alps, and the Jura region. Originally built in 2003 to replace the original tower from 1904, it stands as a symbol of the turn of the millennium.

Free

AQUATIS Aquarium-Vivarium

AQUATIS Aquarium-Vivarium is a captivating destination that showcases the diverse ecosystems of five continents, featuring remarkable creatures such as Komodo dragons and crocodiles. Situated in Switzerland, known as Europe's freshwater reservoir, this attraction offers an immersive journey through the evolution of species and prehistoric animals. Visitors can explore African and Asian crocodiles, vipers, and delve into the rich flora and fauna of the Amazon forest.

Free

Pavillon Thaïlandais

The Thai Pavilion in Lausanne is a beautiful and impressive tribute to the close diplomatic ties between Thailand and Switzerland. The pavilion is built in the style of a mini-version of Bangkok's royal palace, and it features many exquisite details that are sure to impress visitors.

Free

Parc de Valency

Parc de Valency sits within the Lausanne Cathedral area of Lausanne, documented in local heritage registers for its civic or cultural role. Visitors approach from the main access road and combine the visit with neighbouring heritage buildings and public spaces in the district.

Free

Parc Louis Bourget

Parc Louis Bourget is a large, forested public park located on the shores of Lake Geneva in Lausanne. It offers expansive green spaces, walking trails, and a nature reserve that provides a habitat for diverse birdlife. The park is a popular destination for family picnics, barbecues, and enjoying the tranquil lakeside environment away from the city center.

Free

Pyramides de Vidy

The Pyramides de Vidy are a series of striking, modern pyramidal structures located near the waterfront in Lausanne. Originally built for the 1964 Swiss National Exhibition, they now serve as a prominent architectural landmark and a venue for various cultural and community events, reflecting the city's mid-century modern design heritage.
