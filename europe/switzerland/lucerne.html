Destination Guide — Lucerne

Explore Lucerne

A Brief History

Serving as a crucial medieval transit hub along the Gotthard trade route, Lucerne grew extremely wealthy from trans-alpine commerce. Its iconic covered wooden bridges and preserved patrician houses highlight its steadfast historical role as the Catholic heartland of central Switzerland. This continuous historical development established a lasting framework that strongly informs the area's modern cultural evolution.

Top Sights & Activities

Lion Monument

The Lion Monument in Lucerne, Switzerland is a poignant and enduring tourist attraction. It was created to honor the Swiss mercenaries who lost their lives defending the Tuileries Palace during an attack in 1792. The monument features a sculpture of a dying lion impaled by a spear, carved into natural sandstone rock above a pond. The names of the fallen officers and soldiers are engraved nearby, adding to the melancholic atmosphere of the setting.

Free

Hofkirche St. Leodegar

Hofkirche St. Leodegar, also known as the Church of St. Leodergar, is a monumental 17th-century church located in the Old Town area. It features twin Gothic bell towers and a vaulted white stone interior. The church has a rich history dating back to the 8th century when it was originally a Benedictine monastery.

Free

Chapel Bridge

The Chapel Bridge, also known as Kapellbrucke, is a 14th-century wooden bridge in Lucerne that is considered one of the oldest covered wooden bridges in Europe. It is a significant part of the city's medieval fortifications and was named after St. Peter's Chapel nearby. The bridge crosses the River Reuss and features a roof adorned with 17th-century paintings depicting local history.

Free

Jesuitenkirche Luzern

Nestled in the heart of Lucerne, the Jesuitenkirche is a 17th-century Baroque church that stands as a testament to the city's rich architectural and religious heritage. This magnificent structure, also known as the Church of St. Francis Xavier, features painted ceilings and intricate frescoes that captivate all who enter. travellers can admire its opulent interior adorned with marble columns and elaborate altars, creating an atmosphere of peace amidst the vibrant city life.

Free

Lucerne Needle Dam

The Lucerne Needle Dam, also known as the Reuss Weir, is a remarkable 19th-century engineering marvel that controls water flow from Lake Lucerne using thin wooden strips called needles. It is one of the few remaining needle dams globally and was converted into a hydroelectric power plant in 1998, generating enough electricity for 1,500 households annually.

Free

Spreuerbrücke

Spreuerbrücke is a covered pedestrian bridge dating back to the 15th century, known for its series of paintings depicting death motifs. The bridge features 45 paintings of the Dance of Death, created between 1616 and 1637 by painter Kaspar Meglinger and his students. These black paintings are accompanied by verse descriptions and the names of their patrons.

Free

Museggmauer

Museggmauer, also known as the Musegg Wall, is an old city wall in Lucerne. It features nine towers, with four of them open to the public and offering views from the top. The well-preserved medieval fortifications stretch about 2,800 feet long from the north bank of the Reuss almost to the north shore of Lake Lucerne.

Free

Meggenhorn

Nestled in the picturesque hills above Lake Lucerne, Meggenhorn Castle is a 19th-century architectural gem that draws travellers with its views and rich history. Modeled after the grand Chateau de Chambord from France's Loire Valley, this castle offers a unique glimpse into the past. While guided tours are available only on Sundays, guests can explore its beautiful grounds year-round for free.

Free

Pilatus-Bahnen AG

Pilatus-Bahnen AG offers a unique experience in Switzerland, with the steepest cogwheel railway in the world taking travellers to the top of Mount Pilatus. Despite occasional fog, the mountain provides views and various activities such as hiking, dining, and shopping. Accessible by bus and cable car, it's a must-visit destination when in Lucerne. Even on rainy days, the journey is still enjoyable and can be complemented with a Swiss Travel Pass for added convenience.

Free

Dreilindenpark (Konsipark)

Dreilindenpark, also known as Konsipark, is an English garden that offers views of Lake Lucerne and the Alps. The park features beautiful tiny castles with their own gardens, old trees, and interesting statues scattered throughout. It's a tranquil place with many seating options and basic facilities like toilets. travellers can enjoy picnics or join in on activities like a singing group for kids at fixed times every week.
