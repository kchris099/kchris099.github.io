Interlaken

Initially developing around an influential 12th-century Augustinian monastery, Interlaken was completely redefined by 19th-century romantic tourism. The arrival of the railways unlocked its dramatic alpine valleys, transforming the quiet settlement into Switzerland's premier adventure hub. Visitors today can clearly trace the detailed layers of this unique heritage throughout the preserved streets and monuments.

Yash Chopra Statue

The Yash Chopra Statue in Interlaken, Switzerland pays tribute to the known Indian film director who extensively used the Swiss Alps as a backdrop for his movies. The bronze statue, weighing over 350 kg, stands in Kursaal Garden and is a gesture of appreciation from the Swiss government for Chopra's role in popularizing the scenic beauty of Switzerland to South Asia.
