Destination Guide — Geneva

Explore Geneva

A Brief History

An independent republic significantly transformed by the Protestant Reformation, Geneva became a legendary European refuge for religious and intellectual dissidents. Its longstanding tradition of neutrality eventually established the city as the premier global headquarters for diplomacy and peace. Museums, markets, and street plans in Geneva still show layers from those periods.

Brunswick Monument

Brunswick Monument is a monument in Geneva, Switzerland. Construction and early use date to around 1877. Located in the Jardin des Alpes near Lake Geneva, the Brunswick Monument is a neo-Gothic tomb honoring Duke Charles II of Brunswick. The mausoleum, built in 1877, has an equestrian statue of the duke and is constructed from materials like Carrara marble and Verona stone. The memorial stands in an open plaza or roadside setting.

Free

Broken Chair

The Broken Chair is a 12-meter-high wooden sculpture located in front of the Palais des Nations, created by artist Daniel Bursett in 1997. Commissioned by Handicap International, it is a powerful protest against cluster bombs and landmines, symbolizing the devastating impact of these weapons on civilians during times of war. The chair's broken leg represents the loss of human limbs caused by landmine explosions.

CHF25

Palais des Nations

Palais des Nations is a significant United Nations office complex located in a park near the lake. The 1930s building, which is the European headquarters of the UN, is UN centers globally. Visitors can join guided tours available in 15 languages to explore various areas such as the Assembly Hall, Council Chamber, and Human Rights and Alliance of Civilisations Room featuring an ceiling painted by Spanish artist Miguel Barcelo.

Free

The Geneva Water Fountain

The Jet d'Eau is the well known symbol of Geneva, Switzerland. Located where Lake Geneva empties into the Rhône River, the massive water fountain propels 500 liters of water per second to an altitude of 140 meters. Originally a safety valve for a hydraulic power network, it is now a dramatic, internationally recognized landmark visible from across the city.

L'Horloge Fleurie

L'Horloge Fleurie is a visitor site in Geneva, Switzerland. Records associated with the site include 1955. The Flower Clock, also known as L'horloge fleurie, is an outdoor clock in Geneva that was designed in 1955. The flowers reportedly open and close their petals according to the time of day. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Jardin Anglais

Jardin Anglais is a visitor site in Geneva, Switzerland. Records associated with the site include 1855, 1862. Jardin Anglais is a English-inspired park located along the shores of Lake Geneva. The park has an 1862 fountain, a flower-clock sculpture, and beautifully-tended floral displays. In addition to being a popular venue for open-air concerts in summer, it hosts Noel au Jardin, Geneva's main Christmas market. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Free

Reformation Wall

Reformation Wall is a visitor site in Geneva, Switzerland. The Reformation Wall, located in the Parc Des Bastions in Geneva, is a significant monument honoring the Protestant Reformation and its influential figures. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Free

Place du Bourg-de-Four

Place du Bourg-de-Four, Geneva's oldest square, is a busy area with high-end shops, cafes with patios, and fountains. The Old Town offers ancient passageways and romantic cobblestone squares, with Place du Bourg-de-Four being the busiest example. Visitors can explore ritzy shopping promenades and indulge in chocolate shops like Rhone du Chocolatier.

Free

Victoria Hall

Victoria Hall is a visitor site in Geneva, Switzerland. It hosts music concerts, making it a must-visit for classical music enthusiasts. Known orchestras and performers such as the Geneva Chamber Music Orchestra and the Orchestre de la Suisse Romande frequently grace its stage. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Free

Pointe de la Jonction

Pointe de la Jonction is a visitor site in Geneva, Switzerland. The striking contrast between the clear turquoise waters of the Rhone and the muddy brown flow of the Arve creates a mesmerizing sight. This riverfront hub offers opportunities for swimming, relaxation, and recreation, with seasonal chair rentals available along with a small bar. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Free

Basilica Notre-Dame of Geneva

Basilica Notre-Dame of Geneva is a religious building in Geneva, Switzerland. Basilica Notre-Dame, located in Geneva, Switzerland, is a 19th-century Gothic-style Catholic church and a significant stopover point for pilgrims heading to Santiago de Compostela. Situated on the site of a former fort, this landmark is home to numerous important artworks, including stained glass windows by known artists like Claudius Lavergne.
