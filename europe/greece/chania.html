Explore Chania

A Brief History

Built on the ancient Minoan settlement of Kydonia, Chania was heavily fortified by the Venetians and later shaped by centuries of Ottoman rule. The distinct blend of defensive walls, minarets, and waterfront arsenals highlights its long-running strategic value in the Mediterranean. The survival of these cultural and architectural milestones provides a powerful window into the region's storied past.

Top Sights & Activities

Venizelos Graves

Venizelos Graves, located on a hilltop overlooking Chania and the sea, is the final resting place of two influential Greek politicians, Eleftherios Venizelos and his son Sophocles Venizelos. The site was chosen by Eleftherios Venizelos himself before his body was brought here from Paris. Despite being outside the city, the graves are an integral part of Chanioti's identity.

Free

Old Venetian Port of Chania

The Old Venetian Port of Chania is a historic harbor that dates back to the 13th century and was used for trade by the Venetians and Ottomans. The busy waterfront is filled with fish restaurants offering views of the port and its elegant antique lighthouse. Visitors can enjoy a leisurely stroll around the harbor, savoring seafood meze or grilled octopus while admiring the setting.

Free

Catholicon of the Venetian Monastery of St. Francis | Καθολικό Ενετικής Μονής Αγίου Φραγκίσκου

Catholicon of the Venetian Monastery of St. Francis | Καθολικό Ενετικής Μονής Αγίου Φραγκίσκου is a visitor site in Chania, Greece. The Catholicon of the Venetian Monastery of St. Francis in Chania is a significant historical structure dating back to the Venetian period. It now houses the Archaeological Museum of Chania. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Free

Holy Catholic Church of the Assumption of the Blessed Virgin Mary

Holy Catholic Church of the Assumption of the Blessed Virgin Mary is a religious building in Chania, Greece. Located Chania, the Holy Catholic Church of the Assumption of the Blessed Virgin Mary is a gem that often goes unnoticed. Visitors can attend mass throughout the week, with services held in Greek and some English translations available on Sundays. The exterior and main interior spaces are usually viewable outside service times.

Closed

Old Chania Market

Old Chania Market, also known as Agora, is a market in Chania, Greece. It opened or was completed around 1913. This historic market, also known as Agora, opened its doors in 1913 and has a unique cross-shaped design inspired by Marseille's market hall. While it may not be vast, it offers an authentic glimpse into Cretan daily life with vendors selling everything from fresh cheese and meats to aromatic herbs and spices. Stalls sell food and goods during regular market hours.

€3

Bougatsa Iordanis

Located near the Municipal Market of Chania, Iordanis Bougatsa is a family-owned bakery that has been serving bougatsa for over a century. Established in 1924, it is the oldest bougatsa store in Chania. The traditional bougatsa here comes in two varieties - sweet with custard and savory with myzithra, a local goat and sheep cheese.

Free

Lighthouse of Chania

The Lighthouse of Chania is a historic structure originally built by the Venetians in the 16th century and later modified by the Egyptians in 1839. It stands on a mole protruding into Chania harbor, offering views of the old port and Chania's old town.

Free

Firka Venetian Fortress | Ενετικό φρούριο Φιρκά

Firka Venetian Fortress | Ενετικό φρούριο Φιρκά is a fortified historic site in Chania, Greece. Construction and early use date to around 1629. The Firka Venetian Fortress is a historic coastal fortification located at the entrance of the old harbor in Chania. Built in 1629 to protect the city from pirate attacks, the fortress has thick stone walls and subterranean barracks. Walls, courtyards, or towers form the main visitor route when the grounds are open.

Free

Talos Square

Talos Square in Chania, Greece is a historic and location that offers a blend of cultural and natural beauty. The square is situated in the old town of Chania, featuring traditional buildings, alleys, and monuments from various historical periods. Visitors can enjoy views of the harbor and the sea while taking leisurely strolls through the area. In the evening, it becomes a popular spot to witness sunsets from the seawall.

Free

Sabbionara Bastion

The Sabbionara Bastion is a prominent section of the Venetian fortifications surrounding the old town of Chania. Located on the eastern side of the harbor, it was built to defend the city against Ottoman attacks. The well-preserved bastion now is a venue for cultural events and exhibitions, offering panoramic views of the sea and the historic waterfront.
