Chania

Chania occupies the site of Minoan Kydonia on Crete's northwest coast. Venetian engineers raised harbour walls and arsenals; Ottoman rule added mosques and minarets along the same waterfront. That stacked fortification, not a single empire, explains the old town's mix of bastions, Venetian shipyards, and narrow lanes around the inner harbour used by ferries and cafes today.

Venizelos Graves

Venizelos Graves, located on a hilltop overlooking Chania and the sea, is the final resting place of two influential Greek politicians, Eleftherios Venizelos and his son Sophocles Venizelos. The site was chosen by Eleftherios Venizelos himself before his body was brought here from Paris. Despite being outside the city, the graves are an integral part of Chanioti's identity.

Old Venetian Port of Chania

The Old Venetian Port of Chania is a historic harbor that dates back to the 13th century and was used for trade by the Venetians and Ottomans. The busy waterfront is filled with fish restaurants offering views of the port and its elegant antique lighthouse. Visitors can enjoy a leisurely stroll around the harbor, savoring seafood meze or grilled octopus while admiring the setting.

Catholicon of the Venetian Monastery of St. Francis | Καθολικό Ενετικής Μονής Αγίου Φραγκίσκου

The catholicon of the Venetian Monastery of St. Francis in Chania is a former Franciscan church from the Venetian period that later housed the Archaeological Museum. Stone halls display finds from across western Crete. The building sits within the old harbour city's layered Christian and civic history.

Holy Catholic Church of the Assumption of the Blessed Virgin Mary

A quieter Catholic parish in Chania holds weekday masses in Greek, with some English help on Sundays for mixed congregations. The modest facade sits among denser Orthodox landmarks in the old quarters. Exterior and main interior spaces remain viewable outside services.

Old Chania Market

Opened in 1913 on a cross-shaped plan inspired by Marseille's market hall, the Agora still sells everyday Cretan goods. Vendors fill the arms of the cross with food and household items. The compact hall shows daily trade more than tourist spectacle for locals shopping.

Bougatsa Iordanis

Iordanis has specialized in Chania-style bougatsa since the early twentieth century near the city center. The shop is known particularly for a savory pastry filled with local mizithra cheese and finished to order, while drinks and limited additional items support a focused breakfast trade.

07 AdmissionFree

Lighthouse of Chania

Chania's lighthouse stands at the end of the long harbor mole, marking the entrance to the Venetian port. A sixteenth-century Venetian foundation was substantially reshaped during Egyptian administration in the nineteenth century, producing the minaret-like form seen today; the exterior can be approached along an exposed breakwater when conditions allow, while interior access is generally restricted.

35.5196° N / 24.0167° E

Firka Venetian Fortress | Ενετικό φρούριο Φιρκά

Built in 1629 at the old harbor mouth to guard against pirates, the Venetian fort keeps thick stone walls and coastal embrasures. Early use dates to that seventeenth-century defense. Walks along the ramparts look back over the Venetian mole and town.

09 AdmissionFree

Talos Square

Talos Square lies near the western edge of Chania's old harbor and Venetian fortifications. Open paving, a monument, cafes and sea walls connect the waterfront with lanes of the old town, while the west-facing setting draws evening activity. Roads and parking interrupt parts of the space, giving it the character of a working harbor junction as well as a public viewpoint.

35.5188° N / 24.0142° E

10 AdmissionFree

Sabbionara Bastion

The Sabbionara Bastion is a prominent section of the Venetian fortifications surrounding the old town of Chania. Located on the eastern side of the harbor, it was built to defend the city against Ottoman attacks. The well-preserved bastion now is a venue for cultural events and exhibitions, offering panoramic views of the sea and the historic waterfront.
