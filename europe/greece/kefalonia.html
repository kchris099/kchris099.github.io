Destination Guide — Kefalonia

Explore Kefalonia

A Brief History

Established as a regional settlement beyond an older fortified core, the city developed through industrialization in the late 19th century, post-war reconstruction, and modern civic expansion that reshaped its public spaces, markets, and surrounding landscape. Restored religious buildings, civic buildings, and natural features now define the contemporary visitor experience.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

Platia Kampanas

Platia Kampanas, or Bell Square, is a charming pedestrian area In Argostoli. This delightful square features a historic Clock Tower that was beautifully reconstructed after the 1953 earthquake. Surrounded by quaint cafes and shops, it invites travellers to relax and soak in the vibrant atmosphere. The square is particularly enchanting in the evening when it comes alive with bustling street bars and lively crowds.

Free

Vallianos Square

Vallianou Square, In Argostoli, is a modern and vibrant central square surrounded by restaurants and cafes. The square is paved with trees and palms, providing a lively yet peaceful atmosphere for travellers to enjoy coffee or food. In the evening, the square comes to life with good lighting and entertainment options such as bars and restaurants. It's an ideal place to rest and eat while exploring Argostoli.

Free

Monument to the Fallen of the Acqui Division

The Monument to the Fallen of the Acqui Division stands as a poignant tribute to the Italian soldiers who lost their lives during World War II. Nestled on a hill above Argostoli, this beautifully maintained memorial serves not only as a reminder of the tragic events that unfolded but also highlights the respect and honor bestowed upon these fallen heroes by the local Kefalonians.

Free

Saint Theodore Lighthouse

Makris Gialos Beach is a picturesque sandy stretch with calm aqua waters and views. It's an ideal spot for families, offering comfy lounge chairs for rent. The name translates to 'long sandy beach,' capturing its serene atmosphere. travellers can bask in the warm Mediterranean sun or find shade under umbrellas while enjoying the beautiful surroundings.

Free

Old Vlachata

Kefalonia Compass Rent a Boat offers an experience for exploring the inaccessible beaches of Kefalonia. The friendly and helpful staff take pride in their boats and provide good instruction on boat handling. The boats are fully equipped with powerful engines, making it easy to reach desired destinations quickly. Customers appreciate the clean, spacious boats and the thorough training provided by the staff.

Free

Karavomilos Lake

Karavomilos Lake, located near Sami in Kefalonia, is a picturesque and enchanting spot that showcases the island's geological wonders. The lake was formed through limestone erosion and the karst landscape of Kefalonia, making it a result of ancient geological processes. It is also of great biological importance, home to many endemic species. travellers can enjoy a leisurely walk from Sami to the lake, passing by charming bridges and a little port along the way.

Free

Pirates Rent A Boat

Pirates Rent A Boat in Sami, along with Kefalonia Compass Rent a Boat, offers self-drive boat rentals and day trips on the Sami Star. Customers have praised Pirates for their instant response to bookings and for providing well-maintained boats like the Blackbeard and Flying Dutchman. The staff is described as accommodating, providing thorough safety briefings and ensuring customers are comfortable operating the boats.

Free

Paralia Petani

Paralia Petani, located in Kefalonia, Greece, is a charming pebble beach nestled amidst lush green hills. The beach boasts crystal-clear waters and sun loungers for travellers to relax on. While it may not be as secluded as some other beaches, it offers a serene atmosphere with the option to enjoy delicious seafood at nearby restaurants like Erasmia's tavern. travellers can also catch a sunset and should be cautious of potentially strong waves near the beachfront.

Free

Makris Gialos Beach

Makris Gialos Beach (secondary site) is a documented coastal feature in Kefalonia, Greece, recognised in regional geographic listings for its natural formation and public access. The shoreline is open to visitors throughout the year and connects to adjacent beachfront districts. Travellers approach from the coastal road or boardwalk and combine the visit with nearby harbour areas and seaside markets.

Free

Platis Gialos Beach

Platis Gialos Beach is a picturesque coastal spot located 3 km from Argostoli, adjacent to Makrys Gialos beach. The area is embraced by verdant hills and abundant vegetation. This beach boasts pristine white sand and crystal-clear waters, earning it the prestigious Blue Flag for cleanliness and excellent facilities. It's not overly crowded, making it perfect for snorkeling enthusiasts.
