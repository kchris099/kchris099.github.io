Kefalonia

Kefalonia, the largest Ionian island, held Mycenaean and classical settlements before Venetian and British rule left fortresses such as Assos and ports like Argostoli. The 1953 earthquake destroyed most towns; concrete rebuilding followed while some villages were abandoned. Olive groves, Melissani cave, and Myrtos cliffs define the postwar landscape. Ferry links to the mainland and fellow Ionian islands frame a history of quakes, empires, and recovery.
