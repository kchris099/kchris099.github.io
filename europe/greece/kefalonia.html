Explore Kefalonia

A Brief History

Kefalonia is in Greece. Established as a regional settlement beyond an older fortified core, the city developed through industrialization in the late 19th century, post-war reconstruction, and modern civic expansion that reshaped its public spaces, markets, and surrounding landscape. Museums, markets, and street plans in Kefalonia still show layers from those periods.

Vallianos Square

Vallianos Square is a public square in Kefalonia, Greece. Vallianou Square, In Argostoli, is a modern and active central square surrounded by restaurants and cafes. In the evening, the square comes to life with good lighting and entertainment options such as bars and restaurants. It's an ideal place to rest and eat while exploring Argostoli. Monument to the Fallen of the Acqui Division

The Monument to the Fallen of the Acqui Division is a tribute to the Italian soldiers who lost their lives during World War II. Located on a hill above Argostoli, this beautifully maintained memorial serves not only as a reminder of the tragic events that unfolded but also highlights the respect and honor bestowed upon these fallen heroes by the local Kefalonians.

Karavomilos Lake

Karavomilos Lake, located near Sami in Kefalonia, is a and enchanting spot that showcases the island's geological wonders. The lake was formed through limestone erosion and the karst landscape of Kefalonia, making it a result of ancient geological processes. It is also of great biological importance, home to many endemic species. Travellers can enjoy a leisurely walk from Sami to the lake, passing by bridges and a little port along the way.
