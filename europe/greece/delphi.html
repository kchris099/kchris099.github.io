Delphi

Greeks treated Delphi as the world's navel; the oracle's counsel shaped interstate politics for centuries. Christian closure and landslides buried the sanctuary until nineteenth-century excavators uncovered the temple terrace and stadium. The archaeological site on Mount Parnassus is therefore a recovered classical religious capital, not a continuous living city.

Temple of Apollo

Temple of Apollo is a religious building in Delphi, Greece. Dating back to the 300s BCE, its six restored Doric columns evoke a sense of awe and wonder. According to legend, this sacred site is intertwined with the story of Leto, who sought refuge from Hera's wrath while pregnant with twins Apollo and Artemis. The exterior and main interior spaces are usually viewable outside service times.

Tholos of Athena Pronaia

Tholos of Athena Pronaia is a visitor site in Delphi, Greece. The Tholos of Athena Pronaia is an ancient Greek temple located at the Sanctuary of Athena Pronaia, dating back to the 4th century BCE. Pilgrims would traditionally begin their visit here before proceeding to the Temple of Apollo. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Ancient Stadium of Delphi

The Ancient Stadium of Delphi offers a unique experience for visitors, serving as the venue for various cultural events such as the Summer Arts Festival organized by the European Cultural Centre of Delphi. This historic site was once home to the Pythian Games and could accommodate up to 6,000 spectators on its tiered stone seating. The stadium's unpaved trail leads visitors to discover ancient starting blocks and other remnants from its glorious past.

The Athenian Treasury

The Athenian Treasury is an ancient marble structure located at the Temple of Apollo in Delphi. It is the -preserved treasury among others, showing monuments and gifts from victorious city-states. The construction dates back to 510-480 BCE, dedicated by Athenians to commemorate their triumph over the Persians. Positioned along the Sacred Way leading to the temple and oracle of Apollo, it housed votive offerings.

Delphi Archaeological Museum

The Delphi Archaeological Museum is a treasure trove that showcases the rich history of the known Delphic sanctuary, once home to the most famous oracle of ancient Greece. Visitors can explore an collection of architectural sculptures, statues, and detailed micro-art pieces that were dedicated by worshippers over centuries.

European Cultural Centre of Delphi

The European Cultural Centre of Delphi is a modern hub for cultural activities in the city, established to promote culture and make Delphi an international cultural reference point. The center hosts cultural events every two years, featuring ancient tragedies, musical concerts, and theatrical performances in the ancient theater of Delphi.

Maritime Museum of Galaxidi

The Maritime Museum of Galaxidi is housed in a historic 19th-century mansion and documents the town's rich naval history. The collection includes detailed ship models, nautical instruments, historic photographs, and figureheads from the era when Galaxidi was a major shipbuilding and trading center. The museum provides valuable insight into Greece's maritime heritage.
