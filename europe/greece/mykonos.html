Explore Mykonos

A Brief History

Mykonos was historically a modest agricultural and maritime island, overshadowed by the nearby sacred island of Delos in antiquity. It experienced periods of Venetian and Ottoman rule, which influenced its distinctive Cycladic architecture and labyrinthine streets designed to thwart pirates. In the mid-twentieth century, Mykonos transformed into a cosmopolitan destination, attracting international artists and travelers. Today, it is renowned for its iconic windmills, vibrant nightlife, and pristine beaches.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

Windmills of Mykonos

The Windmills of Mykonos, located at Kato Mili and within walking distance from Mykonos Town, are an feature of the island. Perched on the highest hill, these 16 historic windmills offer a plethora of entertainment options and are a popular spot for capturing sunset views. Seven of these 17th-century windmills have been beautifully restored in the Kato Mili region, providing visitors with a glimpse into their historical significance.

Free

Armenistis Lighthouse

Armenistis Lighthouse, perched atop a steep hill, offers views of the sea, sunsets, and neighboring islands. It is a must-visit attraction in Mykonos, where visitors can immerse themselves in the island's rich maritime history while gazing at the unique rugged landscape from the lighthouse's stone-fenced courtyard.

Free

Manto Mavrogenous Statue

At the end of the Old Harbour in Mykonos, find Manto Mavrogenous Square, featuring an elegant bust that pays tribute to Manto Mavrogenous, a remarkable woman known for her beauty and passion for Greece's liberation from the Ottomans. The square is also the starting point of Matogianni Street, home to designer boutiques and jewelry shops. Despite its luxurious offerings, this area also caters to those seeking local items or a relaxing café experience.

€20

Archaeological Site of Delos

Archaeological Site of Delos is a documented piece of civic infrastructure in Mykonos, Greece, recognised in local engineering and heritage listings for its construction period and continued public use. The structure is accessible from the surrounding streets and is visible from adjacent viewpoints. Travellers cross or view the site while moving between nearby districts and transit connections.

Free

Mykonos Harbour View

Mykonos Old Port is a bustling area with picturesque views, making it the most popular place to stay on the island. It offers a vibrant atmosphere with plenty of hotels and accommodations, as well as numerous shops, restaurants, and taverns. Visitors can explore the fish market for fresh seafood and enjoy fascinating exhibitions at the Archaeological Museum of Mykonos. This area is also home to landmarks such as Little Venice and the Kato Mili Windmills.

€2

Monastery of Tourliani

The Monastery of Tourliani, dating back to the 16th century, is a whitewashed church and monastery located southeast of Mykonos Town. It is famous for its intricate marble carvings and a grand Italian baroque altar screen. Visiting this monastery offers a glimpse into the more authentic side of Mykonos, away from the touristy areas.

Free

Holy Church of Panagia Paraportiani

The Church of Panagia Paraportiani is a unique complex of five joined churches located in the Kastro neighborhood. Built in the 17th century, it stands out as an admirable architectural monument and a characteristic image of Cycladic temples. The church's whitewashed exterior and unconventional design reflect the island's vernacular architecture and Cycladic flair.

Free

View of Alefkandra (Little Venice)

View of Alefkandra, also known as Little Venice, is a charming and picturesque neighborhood in Mykonos Town on the Greek island of Mykonos. This area is famous for its unique architecture, vibrant atmosphere, and waterfront views that resemble the city of Venice. The district began to be built in the 18th century and was home to wealthy merchants and captains. Visitors can enjoy beautiful sunsets in Greece from this location.

Free

Paradise Beach

Paradise Beach is a documented coastal feature in Mykonos, Greece, recognised in regional geographic listings for its natural formation and public access. The shoreline is open to visitors throughout the year and connects to adjacent beachfront districts. Travellers approach from the coastal road or boardwalk and combine the visit with nearby harbour areas and seaside markets.

Free

Ornos Beach

Ornos Beach, located just 2 km southwest of Mykonos Town, is a popular and trendy destination known for its family-friendly atmosphere. Unlike some of the other beaches on the island, Ornos offers calm waters and soft sand, making it ideal for young visitors. The area is lined with rental chairs and umbrellas, beach bars, restaurants, and hotels. It's a picturesque setting with plenty of beach hotel rooms, cafes, taverns, and activities like diving.
