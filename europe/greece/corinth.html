Explore Corinth

A Brief History

Ancient Corinth controlled the narrow isthmus connecting the Peloponnese to mainland Greece, amassing large wealth through the portage of ships between the Aegean and Ionian seas. Destroyed by the Romans in 146 BC, it was rebuilt by Julius Caesar to become the prosperous capital of Roman Greece and an early site of Christian preaching by the Apostle Paul. Subsequent Byzantine, Frankish, and Ottoman rulers fortified the looming Acrocorinth. Visitors explore the extensive Roman forum, the archaic Temple of Apollo, and the massive fortifications of the upper citadel.

Temple of Apollo

The Temple of Apollo, a limestone architectural site dating back to around 560 B.C.E., is situated on a commanding rocky hill in Ancient Corinth. Although only seven monolithic limestone columns remain today, the temple originally boasted six columns at the front and back, and fifteen along each side. This historically rich site offers insights into ancient Greek commerce, development, and societal attitudes.

€15

Archaeological Museum of Ancient Corinth

Archaeological Museum of Ancient Corinth is a museum in Corinth, Greece. Visitors are particularly impressed by the mosaics made with sea pebbles and the interactive sensor that plays ancient poetry at just the right volume. Galleries and public rooms are open on posted hours in Corinth.

Free

Acrocorinth

Located just an hour and a half from Athens, Acrocorinth is a monumental evidence of Greece's rich history. This medieval fortress rises 575 meters above the ancient city of Corinth, offering views of both the Corinthian and Saronic Gulfs. Acrocorinth is a visitor site in Corinth, Greece with public access during posted hours.

Free

Corinth Canal

The Corinth Canal, a feat of engineering completed in 1893, separates the Peloponnese from mainland Greece. The canal offers views of its sheer rock walls and blue waters, enjoyed from a sturdy footbridge or on a tour boat. For the adventurous, bungee jumping provides an entirely different perspective. Nearby attractions include the Gulf of Corinth and traditional Greek villages offering local food and wine experiences.

€15

Splash Waterpark Isthmos

Splash Waterpark Isthmos is a destination for families looking to enjoy a fun-filled day near Athens. With its clean and well-organized facilities, this water park offers a variety of pools and slides that keep kids entertained for hours. The attentive lifeguards ensure safety while the friendly staff adds to the welcoming atmosphere. Visitors can savor delicious food options, including tasty souvlaki, at the on-site restaurant or grab a refreshing coffee at Utopia.

Free
