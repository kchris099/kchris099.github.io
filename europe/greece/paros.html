Paros

Paros was a significant maritime power in antiquity, known for its semi-translucent Parian marble used in classical sculpture. The island was later ruled by the Venetians, who built the distinctive Kastro in Parikia, and then by the Ottomans. It played an active role in the Greek War of Independence in the 1820s. Today, Paros is celebrated for its traditional whitewashed villages, historic Byzantine churches, and active coastal culture.

Holy Shrine of the Virgin Mary Ekatontapyliani

The Holy Shrine of the Virgin Mary Ekatontapyliani, also known as the Church of a Hundred Doors, is a significant Byzantine-era stone church located in Parikia. Dating back to the 4th century, it is one of the oldest and -preserved Christian monuments globally. The church houses rare icons and historic artwork, including pieces from an ancient temple within its courtyard. It is also home to the Byzantine Museum.

Frankish Castle Paroikia

In Parikia, the main harbor and commercial center of Paros, lies the Frankish Castle. This historic site is surrounded by important landmarks such as Panagia Ekatontapiliani and the Archaeological Museum. The castle's outer walls were constructed in 1260 by Venetian Duke Marco Sanudo using stones from ancient buildings, including remnants from an ancient temple of Athena and an Ionic temple dating back to the 5th century BC.

Butterflies Nature Reserve

Butterflies Nature Reserve is a public park in Paros, Greece. The Butterflies Nature Reserve (Petaloudes) in Paros is a lush, shaded valley that is a summer habitat for thousands of Jersey Tiger Moths. The unique microclimate, characterized by dense vegetation and natural springs, attracts the insects from June to September. Paths, lawns, and shaded areas make up the main public facilities.

Venetian Castle of Paros

The Venetian Castle in Naoussa, Paros is a significant landmark dating back to the early 14th century. Originally built as a fortress by the Venetian Duke of Naxos, it served as protection against invaders and pirate attacks. The castle's strategic location on a thin strip of land in Naoussa cove allowed for surveillance and defense of the port. Unlike traditional medieval castles, this structure resembles a settlement with detached houses.

37.1258° N / 25.2374° E

Kolympethres Beach

Kolympethres Beach in Paros is known for its rock formations, pristine golden sands, and clear turquoise waters. Accessible by public buses or small fishing boats from Naoussa Port, the times to visit are early morning or at sunset. The beach offers facilities such as beach bars, tavernas, and water sports.
