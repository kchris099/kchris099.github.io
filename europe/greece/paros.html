Explore Paros

A Brief History

Paros was a significant maritime power in antiquity, renowned for its semi-translucent Parian marble used in classical sculpture. The island was later ruled by the Venetians, who built the distinctive Kastro in Parikia, and then by the Ottomans. It played an active role in the Greek War of Independence in the 1820s. Today, Paros is celebrated for its traditional whitewashed villages, historic Byzantine churches, and vibrant coastal culture.

Holy Shrine of the Virgin Mary Ekatontapyliani

The Holy Shrine of the Virgin Mary Ekatontapyliani, also known as the Church of a Hundred Doors, is a significant Byzantine-era stone church located in Parikia. Dating back to the 4th century, it is one of the oldest and -preserved Christian monuments globally. The church houses rare icons and historic artwork, including pieces from an ancient temple within its courtyard. It is also home to the Byzantine Museum.

Free

Frankish Castle Paroikia

In the heart of Parikia, the main harbor and commercial center of Paros, lies the Frankish Castle. This historic site is surrounded by important landmarks such as Panagia Ekatontapiliani and the Archaeological Museum. The castle's outer walls were constructed in 1260 by Venetian Duke Marco Sanudo using stones from ancient buildings, including remnants from an ancient temple of Athena and an Ionic temple dating back to the 5th century BC.

Free

Lefkes village, Paros

Lefkes is a picturesque, traditional mountain village situated in the center of Paros. Once the island's capital, it features narrow, winding alleys, whitewashed houses with colorful doors, and the impressive Church of Agia Triada. The village is surrounded by pine forests and olive groves, offering a peaceful, authentic Cycladic atmosphere away from the coastal resorts.

€4

Butterflies Nature Reserve

The Butterflies Nature Reserve (Petaloudes) in Paros is a lush, shaded valley that serves as a summer habitat for thousands of Jersey Tiger Moths. The unique microclimate, characterized by dense vegetation and natural springs, attracts the insects from June to September. Visitors can walk along designated paths to observe the resting moths in a tranquil natural setting.

Free

Venetian Castle of Paros

The Venetian Castle in Naoussa, Paros is a significant landmark dating back to the early 14th century. Originally built as a fortress by the Venetian Duke of Naxos, it served as protection against invaders and pirate attacks. The castle's strategic location on a thin strip of land in Naoussa cove allowed for surveillance and defense of the port. Unlike traditional medieval castles, this structure resembles a settlement with detached houses.

Free

Kolympethres Beach

Kolympethres Beach in Paros is renowned for its rock formations, pristine golden sands, and clear turquoise waters. Accessible by public buses or small fishing boats from Naoussa Port, the times to visit are early morning or at sunset. The beach offers facilities such as beach bars, tavernas, and water sports.

Free

Παραλία Μικρή Σάντα Μαρία

Mikri Santa Maria is a beautiful, sheltered beach located on the northeastern coast of Paros. Known for its fine golden sand and crystal-clear, shallow waters, it offers a more relaxed and intimate atmosphere compared to the larger neighboring beaches. It is a popular spot for swimming, snorkeling, and enjoying the natural coastal beauty of the island.
