Explore Rhodes

A Brief History

Rhodes town preserves a Gothic and Ottoman layered harbour capital built by the Knights of Saint John after 1309. Palace of the Grand Master, Street of the Knights, and mosques record crusader fortification and later Ottoman Aegean governance. Harbour mandraki walks and medieval inns extend crusader street grids along the Aegean waterfront. Walking routes through the historic centre help visitors relate these monuments to wider patterns of settlement and civic life in Greece.

Medieval City of Rhodes

The Medieval City of Rhodes is a well-preserved walled town with grand palaces, Byzantine-era mosques, and piazzas lined with cafes. It's considered the -preserved and most medieval city in Europe. The mystifying Street of the Knights leads to the Grand Master Palace, which has 160 rooms to explore. Other historical sites include the Archaeological Museum, Virgin of the Castle, Turkish baths, and mosques with tall minarets in small squares.

Free

Street of the Knights of Rhodes

Rhodes is a treasure trove of historical sites, and the Street of the Knights of Rhodes is a must-visit. This cobbled medieval street is lined with seven inns, each representing the countries of origin for the Knights of St. The architecture along this street is considered examples of Gothic urbanism, making it a and historically significant area to explore.

Palace of the Grand Master of the Knights of Rhodes

Located at the pinnacle of the historic Street of the Knights, the Palace of the Grand Master of the Knights of Rhodes is a evidence of medieval architecture and history. Originally constructed by the Knights Hospitaller in the early 14th century, this imposing fortress was built atop an earlier Byzantine stronghold from the 7th century. Once serving as both a grand residence and administrative hub for these valiant knights, it has witnessed centuries of change.

€5

Medieval Clock Tower

The Medieval Clock Tower, originally built in the 7th century, is a prominent landmark in Rhodes Town. The tower was reconstructed in 1852 with baroque elements after being damaged by an explosion. Visitors can climb to the observation deck for panoramic views of the medieval layout of the town and beyond.

Free

Saint Athanasios Gate

Saint Athanasios Gate is a historic arched stone gate located in the defensive walls of Rhodes, built in the mid-15th century. It is also known as Saint Francis Gate due to its proximity to the church of Saint Francis of Assisi. The round tower of Saint Mary, constructed by Grand Master Jean de Lastic in 1441, overlooks this entrance. According to Turkish tradition, it was through this gate that Suleiman the 's troops entered Rhodes.

Free

Sea Gate

Sea Gate is a historic stone entryway from the Middle Ages, featuring double crenellated towers and Christian reliefs. Built in the 14th century by Grand Master Juan Fernandez de Heredia, it bears his coat of arms on top. The Ottoman administration later demolished its side towers to widen access to Kolona harbour. Today, it facilitates swift vehicle flow between the harbour and the new town through direct connection with Liberty Gate.

Free

Hippocrates Square

Hippocrates Square (Plateia Ippokratous) is the busy central square of the medieval Old Town of Rhodes. It is characterized by the Castellania building, a historic fountain, and numerous cafes and shops. The square is a major meeting point and a active hub for visitors exploring the cobblestone streets and historic architecture of the walled city.

Acropolis of Rhodes

Acropolis of Rhodes is a visitor site in Rhodes, Greece. The Acropolis of Rhodes is a significant ancient site located in the capital of the island, on Monte Smith. Nature enthusiasts can explore the Valley of Butterflies with its unique flora and fauna. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Ancient Stadium of Rhodes

Ancient Stadium of Rhodes is a stadium in Rhodes, Greece. The influence of Hellenistic culture is evident through the remains of temples dedicated to Greek gods such as Zeus, Athena, Apollo, and Helios nearby. Visitors can also explore a renovated section of the theater with new marble elements. Event access follows posted ticket and gate procedures.

Free

Colossus of Rhodes

The Myth Colossus of Rhodes, once a towering 33-meter statue of the Greek sun-god Helios, stood at the entrance to Mandraki Harbour. It was built in 280 BC to commemorate Rhodes' successful defense against an attack. Unfortunately, it collapsed during an earthquake in 226 BC. Today, two bronze statues of a stag and a doe stand as memorials at the harbor entrance.

Free

Windmills of Rhodes

Rhodes Windmills, also known as the Windmills of Mandraki, are located on the outskirts of busy Mandraki Harbour. They offer views and serve as a starting point for boat trips to other coastal areas like Lindos village. The windmills have been well restored and provide views across the harbor to the walls and palace of the grandmasters. It's a emblem with rustic beauty that offers a scenic viewpoint.

Free

Nea Agora

Nea Agora is a visitor site in Rhodes, Greece. This marketplace features an array of shops and stalls brimming with local treasures, from fresh produce and artisanal cheeses to fragrant spices and golden honey. The main structures and grounds are the principal points of interest.

Kallithea Springs

Kallithea Springs is a visitor site in Rhodes, Greece. Kallithea Springs, located by the seaside, is a historic spa with Art Deco architecture and a beach. Dating back to the 7th century B.C., it has been a popular destination for those seeking the therapeutic benefits of its thermal waters. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Paralia Antoni Kouin is a visitor site in Rhodes. Filerimos Monastery, located on Filerimos Hill in the Greek island of Rhodes, is a historical site with religious significance dating back to the 8th century BC. The monastery was built in the 10th century AD by the Order of the Knights of St. John and has been restored in the early 20th century. Surrounded by lush greenery, this stone structure is a popular tourist attraction and has a glimpse into its medieval architecture with domed arches.

Saint Nectarios Church is a religious building in Rhodes, Greece. Construction and early use date to around 1967. Built in 1967, it has a classic Greek architecture with sand-colored stone walls, colonnaded arcades, and red-tiled roofs. The church also includes a lush green courtyard made of Finta stone and a bell tower as its highlight. The exterior and main interior spaces are usually viewable outside service times.

The ruins of Church of the Virgin Mary of the Burgh

Located at the eastern edge of Rhodes Old City, in the Jewish Quarter, the Church of the Virgin Mary of the Burgh is a significant example of ecclesiastical architecture in Dodecanese and Asia Minor. Dating back to the 14th century, it was originally a three-aisled basilica with Gothic ribbed cross vaults. Although it suffered damage during World War II and was later converted into houses, its grand original Gothic style still impresses visitors.
