Explore Naxos

A Brief History

A powerful center of early Cycladic civilization and later the seat of a Venetian Duchy, Naxos drew sustained wealth from its fertile lands and emery exports. The enduring medieval citadel and ancient marble quarries highlight a long legacy of commercial and cultural dominance. The remarkable survival of these cultural and architectural milestones provides a powerful window into the region's storied past.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

Temple of Apollo

Naxos, the largest of the Cyclades islands, is a treasure trove of history and natural beauty. The island boasts seven museums showcasing artifacts from various periods, including the Archaeological Museum with exhibits dating back to the Neolithic period. Visitors can explore ancient ruins like the doorlike remains of the Temple of Apollo and wander through Chora's Venetian castle.

Free

Venetian Castle of Naxos

The Venetian Castle of Naxos is a remarkable edifice with thick walls made of large rocks, showcasing traditional medieval architecture. The narrow streets are now lined with shops and Greek tavernas offering exceptional local produce and meats. Visitors can explore the Mycenaean City of Grotta, various museums, the Catholic Church, and the Central Tower within the Chora and main square.

Free (sunbeds €15)

Agios Prokopios Beach

Agios Prokopios Beach in Naxos is a popular destination known for its beauty and family-friendly atmosphere. The southern end offers a tranquil setting with minimal infrastructure, making it perfect for a peaceful beach day. Visitors can relax on the long stretch of golden sandy shore and enjoy the crystal-clear turquoise waters, which are protected from strong winds. This Blue Flag awarded beach also provides opportunities for water sports and features a diving club.

Free

Cedar Forest of Alyko

The Cedar Forest of Alyko is a protected natural area located on the southwestern coast of Naxos. The unique coastal ecosystem features rare cedar trees growing amidst sand dunes, leading down to pristine, secluded beaches. The area is largely undeveloped, offering a tranquil environment for nature walks, swimming, and enjoying the rugged coastal landscape.

Free

Hawaii Beach

Hawaii Beach in Naxos, Greece is a remote and serene sandy strip with smooth rock formations and views of nearby hotel ruins. The beach is known for its vertical reddish cliffs that create a striking contrast with the yellow sand and blue waters. It's a tranquil spot where naturists may be spotted, although they tend to prefer the more isolated Kedros beach nearby. Accessible from Alyko, there are no public buses directly to the beach, but visitors can walk from there.

Free

Mikri Vigla Beach

Mikri Vigla Beach is a documented coastal feature in Naxos, Greece, recognised in regional geographic listings for its natural formation and public access. The shoreline is open to visitors throughout the year and connects to adjacent beachfront districts. Travellers approach from the coastal road or boardwalk and combine the visit with nearby harbour areas and seaside markets.

€5

Bazeos Tower

Bazeos Tower, originally a 17th-century monastery, now stands as a cultural hub and historical landmark on the island of Naxos. After being abandoned by monks in the 19th century, it became home to several families of potters. Today, the tower hosts various cultural activities and serves as an art gallery featuring works by both local and international artists. Visitors can explore its well-preserved chapel and learn about its rich history from friendly staff members.

€5

Ancient Temple of Demeter

The Ancient Temple of Demeter, located near the village of Sangri on Naxos, is a significant archaeological site dating back to the 6th century BC. Built entirely of Naxian marble, the partially restored temple provides early examples of classical Greek architecture. It was dedicated to the goddess of agriculture, reflecting the fertile nature of the surrounding valley.

Reservation required

Saint Anna Winery

Saint Anna Winery is a charming winery located on Naxos, offering a delightful experience for wine enthusiasts. Established in 2016 by a passionate young entrepreneur, the winery sits on land that has been in the family for generations. Visitors can enjoy tastings of exquisite wines and locally produced olive oil while savoring a spread of homegrown olives, cheese, and tomatoes.

Free

Apollonas Kouros

The Apollonas Kouros is a massive, unfinished marble statue dating from the 8th century BC, located in an ancient quarry near the village of Apollonas on Naxos. Measuring over 10 meters in length, the statue depicts a male figure, possibly the god Dionysus or Apollo. It offers a fascinating glimpse into the techniques and scale of ancient Greek marble carving.
