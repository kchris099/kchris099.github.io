Explore Meteora

A Brief History

Meteora is in Greece. Established as a regional settlement beyond an older fortified core, the city developed through industrialization in the late 19th century, post-war reconstruction, and modern civic expansion that reshaped its public spaces, markets, and surrounding landscape. Museums, markets, and street plans in Meteora still show layers from those periods.

Meteora

Meteora is a visitor site in Meteora, Greece. Located central Greece, Meteora is a destination that draws travellers with its towering rock formations and ancient monasteries. The name "Meteora," meaning 'lofty' or 'elevated,' encapsulates the essence of this locale. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Holy Monastery of Rousanos - Saint Barbara

The Holy Monastery of Rousanos, also known as Saint Barbara, is a striking monastic complex perched on a tall rock formation. Originally dedicated to the 'Transfiguration' but now honoring Saint Barbara, it was likely built in 1288 and later renovated into a monastery in 1545. Despite being heavily damaged during WWII, it was restored in the 1980s. The monastery enforces a strict dress code and offers views from its balcony.

Monastery of Varlaam

Perched majestically atop a cliff, the Monastery of Varlaam dates back to the mid-14th century and is a example of Byzantine architecture. Travellers can explore its beautifully adorned chapel featuring frescoes and look at its museum that showcases fascinating artifacts. For those looking to enhance their experience, local guides in Kalabaka offer private tours tailored to various interests.

Holy Monastery of the Great Meteoron - Transfiguration of the Saviour

Perched dramatically on a rocky outcrop, the Holy Monastery of the Great Meteoron is a evidence of 14th-century architecture and spirituality. As the largest of Meteora's monasteries, it has undergone significant restoration over many years, particularly in 1483 and 1552. Travellers can explore its frescoes and historical refectory while learning about its rich history through local guides who offer tailored tours.

Holy Monastery of Saint Nicholas Anapafsas at Meteora

Holy Monastery of Saint Nicholas Anapafsas at Meteora is a visitor site in Meteora, Greece. Construction and early use date to around 1368. Located atop a sandstone cliff, the Holy Monastery of Saint Nicholas Anapafsas at Meteora is a medieval site founded in 1368. Just a kilometer from Kastraki, this monastery offers views and is an ideal spot for photography enthusiasts. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Monastery of the Holy Trinity

The Monastery of the Holy Trinity at Meteora is a historic and awe-inspiring site located on a dramatic rocky point with sweeping views. The name "Meteora" reflects the suspended heavenly appearance of the rock formations. This UNESCO World Heritage site consists of six surviving monasteries, dating back to the 9th century when hermits began cave-dwelling in this area. Despite being less visited by tourists on bus tours, it offers spiritual tranquility and preserved quietness.

Main Observation Deck of Meteora

The Main Observation Deck of Meteora offers panoramic views of the rocky plain and towns below, making it a prime spot for photography. For the compositions, head to the small parking area further down the road. The beauty of the rock formations and monasteries, along with local shops and restaurants, make Meteora a must-visit destination. Whether at sunrise or sunset, the view from this observation deck is truly and not to be missed.

Holy Church of the Dormition of the Virgin Mary

Holy Church of the Dormition of the Virgin Mary is a religious building in Meteora, Greece. While relatively modest clothing is required, it's worth visiting for its spiritually inspirational atmosphere and rich history. The exterior and main interior spaces are usually viewable outside service times.

Greek Education Museum

Greek Education Museum is a museum in Meteora, Greece. Travellers are welcomed by enthusiastic guides who share stories about the Greek school system, allowing guests to engage with hands-on activities like spinning tools and writing in traditional styles. Galleries and public rooms are open on posted hours in Meteora.
