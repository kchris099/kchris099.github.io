Explore Corfu

A Brief History

Unlike most of Greece, Corfu remained under Venetian protection for centuries, successfully repelling Ottoman sieges. This long Italianate influence, followed by brief British and French administration, left an indelible mark on its baroque architecture and musical traditions. Museums, markets, and street plans in Corfu still show layers from those periods.

Top Sights & Activities

€10

Old Fortress

The Old Fortress of Corfu is a historic site located near the island's capital. It consists of scenic ruins, artifacts on display, a seating area, and a church. Easter is an especially festive time to visit, with unique church services and processions. The dropping of colorful clay pots from balconies in the Old Town on Saturday morning is a tradition not to be missed.

€7

The Achilleion Palace

The Achilleion Palace is a fortified historic site in Corfu, Greece. Construction and early use date to around 1890. The Achilleion Palace in Corfu was built in 1890 as a summer retreat for Empress Elisabeth of Austria (Sisi). The opulent interiors and the panoramic views of the Ionian Sea reflect the Empress's deep appreciation for Greek culture and mythology. Walls, courtyards, or towers form the main visitor route when the grounds are open.

Free

Holy Monastery of Panagia Vlacherna

The Holy Monastery of Panagia Vlacherna is a and site located in the middle of a bay in Corfu. It offers views of airport traffic overhead and is connected to the mainland by a footbridge. The monastery's setting makes it a must-visit attraction on any trip to Corfu. While its interior details remain unknown, the exterior exudes charm and beauty, making it an essential stop for visitors exploring the island's and natural attractions.

Free

Holy Church of Saint Spyridon

Holy Church of Saint Spyridon is a religious building in Corfu, Greece. The Holy Church of Saint Spyridon is a 16th-century Greek Orthodox church located in Corfu, known for its bell tower and painted ceiling. It houses the relics of St. Spyridon, brought to Corfu City from Constantinople in the 15th century. The exterior and main interior spaces are usually viewable outside service times.

€5

New Fortress

The New Venetian Fortress, also known as the Samuels Fortress, is a historic 16th-century fortified castle that offers views of the city and the ocean. It stands tall above Ohrid and Lake Ohrid, providing visitors with an unparalleled vantage point. Originally built in the late 16th century, this fortress is a well-preserved example of Venetian military architecture.

Free

Spianada Square

Spianada Square, also known as the Esplanade, is a prominent city square in Corfu, Greece. It offers views of both the old and new ports of the city. The square is situated between the edge of Corfu's old town and the old fortress on the coast. Visitors can take leisurely strolls in the park or relax at one of its many cafes while enjoying some people-watching.

Free

Kaiser William II Observatory

The Kaiser William II Observatory, also known as the Kaiser's Throne (Kaiser-Thron), is a scenic viewpoint located near the village of Pelekas in Corfu. It was a favorite spot of the German Emperor Wilhelm II, who purchased the Achilleion Palace in 1907. The elevated location offers sweeping, 360-degree views of the island's lush landscape and the surrounding sea.

Free

The Douglas Obelisk

The Douglas Obelisk is a visitor site in Corfu. Ionian Seaways offers a directly and enjoyable ferry experience between Corfu and Saranda. The staff at their office are helpful, providing assistance with ticketing and even offering cold water while waiting. They also offer luggage storage and helpful recommendations for nearby cafes. The ferry itself is clean, and the crossing is described as flawless, making for a travel experience.

Free

Maitland Monument

Maitland Monument sits within the Maitland Monument area of Corfu, documented in local heritage registers for its civic or cultural role. Visitors approach from the main access road and combine the visit with neighbouring heritage buildings and public spaces in the district.

Free

Anemomilos Windmill

The Anemomilos Windmill is a, restored traditional windmill located on the coastal promenade of Garitsa Bay in Corfu Town. It is a reminder of the island's agricultural past and is a popular landmark for walking and photography. The surrounding area offers pleasant views of the Old Fortress and the sea.
