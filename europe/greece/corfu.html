Corfu

Venice held Corfu for centuries and kept Ottoman armies off the island, so arcaded streets and fortresses look more Italian than mainland Greek. Brief French and British administrations added further layers. The Old Fortress and Liston arcade summarise that western Ionian exception. Cricket on the Esplanade is a leftover habit of the British protectorate years.

Old Fortress

The Old Fortress of Corfu is a historic site located near the island's capital. It consists of scenic ruins, artifacts on display, a seating area, and a church. Easter is an especially festive time to visit, with unique church services and processions. The dropping of colorful clay pots from balconies in the Old Town on Saturday morning is a tradition not to be missed.

The Achilleion Palace

Empress Elisabeth of Austria (Sisi) raised the 1890 summer retreat to honor Greek myth, especially Achilles. Opulent interiors open toward Ionian Sea views from the hillside terraces. Gardens and statues carry the same classical theme across the estate grounds.

Holy Monastery of Panagia Vlacherna

A narrow causeway links Corfu's Vlacherna Monastery with the Kanoni shoreline. The small whitewashed church and bell tower occupy an islet beside the airport approach, while boats depart nearby for Pontikonisi. Aircraft pass low overhead and active worship continues, so the site combines a distinctive coastal setting with noise, limited space.

Holy Church of Saint Spyridon

A 16th-century Orthodox church holds a tall bell tower, a painted ceiling, and the relics of St. Spyridon brought from Constantinople in the 15th century. Outside service times the exterior and main nave stay viewable. The silver casket draws steady local devotion.

New Fortress

Corfu's New Fortress rises above the old harbor as a Venetian defensive complex developed mainly in the sixteenth and seventeenth centuries. Bastions, tunnels, gates, barracks, walls and upper terraces overlook Corfu Town and the sea. The New Venetian Fortress, also known as the Samuels Fortress, is a historic 16th-century fortified castle that offers views of the city and the ocean.

Spianada Square

Spianada forms a broad esplanade between Corfu's old town and Old Fortress. Lawns, paths, cricket ground, arcades, monuments, cafes and ceremonial spaces reflect Venetian fortification clearances and later French and British planning. It remains the town's principal open public landscape, while separate sections such as the Liston have distinct architectural histories.

Kaiser William II Observatory

Kaiser's Throne is a stone viewing platform above Pelekas on one of Corfu's central hills; emperor Wilhelm II favored the location after acquiring the Achilleion estate, though the platform is far from that palace. Roads and steps lead toward broad island and sea views when weather is clear, while the site is a viewpoint.

The Douglas Obelisk

The Douglas Obelisk in Corfu commemorates Howard Douglas, a nineteenth-century British Lord High Commissioner of the Ionian Islands. The stone monument stands in a public garden setting recalling the British protectorate era. It belongs to Corfu Town's cluster of colonial-period memorials.

Maitland Monument

A circular neoclassical pavilion called the Maitland Monument stands at the edge of Corfu's Spianada. Twenty Ionic columns surround a domed structure erected in the early nineteenth century to honor British Lord High Commissioner Thomas Maitland. The monument reflects the period of British rule in the Ionian Islands.

Anemomilos Windmill

The Anemomilos Windmill is a, restored traditional windmill located on the coastal promenade of Garitsa Bay in Corfu Town. It is a reminder of the island's agricultural past and is a popular landmark for walking and photography. The surrounding area offers pleasant views of the Old Fortress and the sea.
