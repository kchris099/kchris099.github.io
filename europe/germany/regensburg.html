Regensburg

Regensburg stands at the Danube's northernmost navigable point where Romans built Castra Regina and where medieval emperors held Reichstag assemblies in patrician palaces along the river. Stone bridge from the twelfth century and the cathedral's Gothic spires anchored trade between Italy and the North Sea. Largely spared heavy bombing, the city preserves one of Germany's densest ensembles of Roman, Romanesque, and baroque fabric as a Bavarian administrative centre.

Steinerne Brücke

Regensburg's Stone Bridge carried trade across the Danube from the twelfth century and helped direct routes into the medieval city. Repeated floods, repairs, loss of original arches and modern restoration altered the structure over time, while a surviving bridge tower marks the old-town end. Pedestrian use now replaces most traffic, opening views toward the cathedral, river islands and historic waterfront.

Schloss St. Emmeram, Thurn und Taxis

The Thurn und Taxis residence grew from the former Benedictine monastery of St. Emmeram after the princely family acquired it in the nineteenth century. Cloisters, state rooms, a ballroom, private chapels, collections, courtyards and service wings form an extensive palace complex still partly occupied. Guided tours distinguish residential interiors from the separate abbey church and broader Regensburg old town.

Haidplatz

Medieval jousts once filled this triangular Old Town square. The Golden Cross, an early Gothic house with 13th-century origins, faces the paving. Historic inns and walkable lanes surround the plaza toward St. Peter's and the river, keeping the space in daily use.

04 AdmissionFree

Altes Rathaus Regensburg

Altes Rathaus Regensburg housed imperial assemblies of the Perpetual Diet from 1663 until 1806 in rooms still shown on guided tours. Gothic and later chambers face the historic market streets. The building remains central to understanding the city's role in the Holy Roman Empire.

St. Peter Cathedral

Regensburg Cathedral rises above the old town as Bavaria's principal mature Gothic cathedral. Construction began in the thirteenth century and continued across centuries, producing twin spires, sculpture; stained glass, a high nave, choir, treasury and cloister. The Regensburger Domspatzen choir remains connected with worship and music, while services and rehearsals shape access to the active Catholic church.

06 AdmissionFree

Porta Praetoria

Erected in 179 AD, the monumental gateway was the northern entrance to the Roman camp Castra Regina. Stone arches still stand in the Old Town fabric beside later houses. Quay and embankment paths pass near the surviving Roman masonry along the Danube edge.

Basilica of the Nativity of Our Lady Regensburg

The Basilica of the Nativity of Our Lady, usually called the Alte Kapelle, stands near Regensburg's former ducal and royal precinct. An early medieval foundation was transformed through Romanesque and Gothic phases, then given especially rich rococo decoration. Stucco, altars, music and collegiate worship surround an older architectural core tied to the city's court history. The decorative interior contrasts with the restrained exterior.

08 AdmissionFree

Donau Arena

The Donau Arena is a large ice skating rink that is popular for hockey and ice skating. It was built in 1999 and replaced the old Eisstadion as Regensburg's home stadium for Ice Hockey. There are beginner and professional courses available to learn ice skating here, as well as skate rentals for those who want to practice on their own. The atmosphere at the Donau Arena is friendly and family-friendly, with good sightlines from most seats.

09 AdmissionFree

Scots Monastery

The Scots Monastery in Regensburg, founded for Irish Benedictine monks, centres on a Romanesque church of St. James with a celebrated carved north portal. The monastery complex sits just outside the medieval core. Stone sculpture on the portal ranks among Bavaria's major Romanesque works.

Staatstheater Regensburg

The Theater Regensburg, established in 1804, is an important venue for operas, operettas, ballets, musicals, plays, and orchestral concerts. It also hosts open-air performances during the summer season. The theater's bar/cafe is inviting and provides a pleasant pre-show or intermission experience. While surtitles would have been beneficial during the performance of Der Freischutz, the staging, orchestra, and singing were.
