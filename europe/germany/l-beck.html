Explore Lübeck

A Brief History

Lübeck, celebrated rightfully as the 'Queen of the Hanseatic League', was the absolute economic and cultural powerhouse of Baltic maritime trade during the Middle Ages. Its immense, historic wealth is still visible in its iconic skyline of seven towering church spires and magnificent brick Gothic architecture, most notably the famous Holstentor gate. As the historic marzipan capital of the world and a protected UNESCO World Heritage site, Lübeck flawlessly preserves the awe-inspiring grandeur of northern Germany’s mercantile golden age.

Top Sights & Activities

Varies

Holstentor (Holsten Gate)

The iconic, slightly leaning brick Gothic gate that serves as the symbol of Lübeck. It houses a fascinating museum detailing the city's power and immense wealth during the Hanseatic League era.

Free

St. Mary's Church (Marienkirche)

A towering masterpiece of brick Gothic architecture that set the standard for countless other Baltic churches. Inside, you can view the hauntingly preserved broken bells that fell during WWII bombings.

Free

Lübeck Old Town

A UNESCO World Heritage island surrounded by the Trave river. Wander through the intricate network of hidden medieval courtyards and alleyways (Gänge und Höfe) tucked behind the grand merchant houses.

€ 12.00

Niederegger Marzipan Museum

Lübeck is the world capital of marzipan. Visit the historic Café Niederegger to view life-sized marzipan sculptures in their free museum, and indulge in their legendary, world-class almond treats.

Varies

Lübeck Town Hall (Rathaus)

One of the oldest, most beautiful, and largest town halls in Germany. Its striking dark brick facade, Renaissance staircases, and ornate details reflect the immense pride of the merchant class.

€ 12.00

European Hansemuseum

A highly interactive, modern museum that brilliantly brings to life the 800-year history of the Hanseatic League, showcasing Lübeck's role as a global medieval trading superpower.
