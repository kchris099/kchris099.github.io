Dortmund

Dortmund rose as an imperial city on the Hellweg trade route and became a Hanseatic member in the 14th century, shipping beer and steel across northern Europe. Coal mining and brewing dominated the 19th century, while Borussia Dortmund and the rebuilt city centre after 1945 shaped modern identity. Deindustrialisation left pithead frames, the Phoenix lake district, and the Ruhr's largest football stadium as landmarks of structural change.

Signal Iduna Park

Signal Iduna Park, opened in 1974 as Westfalenstadion, is the home ground of Borussia Dortmund and seats over 81,000 spectators, making it Germany's largest football stadium. The south terrace, known as the Yellow Wall, holds about 25,000 standing places. Self-guided stadium walks run on non-match days through the players' tunnel, dressing rooms, and press areas.

Borusseum

The Borusseum beside Signal Iduna Park is Borussia Dortmund's club museum, opened in 2008 to document more than a century of BVB history. Exhibits include trophies, jerseys, and interactive displays on the 1966 European Cup Winners' Cup and recent Bundesliga titles. The museum sits in the stadium's northeast corner and closes before evening home matches.

Thier-Galerie Dortmund

Thier-Galerie is a city-centre shopping mall on Westenhellweg, opened in 2010 on the site of a former department store. Three retail levels and a food court house international fashion chains and local retailers beneath a glass roof. The complex connects to Dortmund's pedestrian shopping streets and the main railway station within a few minutes' walk.

Wasserschloss Haus Dellwig

Haus Dellwig is a moated manor in northwest Dortmund whose origins reach to the 12th century, with the present brick and sandstone house rebuilt after a fire in 1661. A Renaissance gatehouse and surrounding park border the Dellwiger Bach stream. The Heimatmuseum Lütgendortmund inside displays local history on limited Sunday opening hours.

Rombergpark Botanical Garden

Rombergpark botanical garden covers about 65 hectares south of Dortmund's centre, combining arboretum plantings with geological and Chinese garden sections. The von Romberg family landscaped the estate from the 1820s before the city acquired it in 1927. Glass plant houses display tropical and succulent collections on weekends within the wider park.

Florianturm

The Florianturm telecommunications tower rises 219 metres in Westfalenpark and was built for the 1959 Federal Garden Show. Two observation platforms offer views across the Ruhr valley and Dortmund's skyline. A revolving restaurant operates on the upper deck during opening hours, and the tower reopened after renovation in 2020.

Westfalenpark

Westfalenpark spans 70 hectares around the Florianturm and hosts the German Rosarium with thousands of rose varieties. Created for the 1959 Bundesgartenschau, the park stages seasonal festivals including the Festival of Lights in winter. Themed gardens, playgrounds, and event lawns draw visitors from across the Ruhr conurbation throughout the year.

Phoenix-See

Phoenix-See is an artificial lake completed in 2011 on the former Phoenix Ost steelworks site in Dortmund's Hörde district. A 3.2-kilometre shoreline path circles the water, linking new housing, a marina, and restaurants built during the post-industrial conversion. The lake forms part of the Emscher landscape park regeneration programme across the Ruhr.

Casino Hohensyburg

The Spielbank Hohensyburg occupies a modern glass building on the Syberg hill south of Dortmund, beside medieval castle ruins overlooking the Ruhr and Lenne rivers. The casino opened in 1985 and offers table games and slot machines under state licence. The surrounding parkland includes the Kaiser Wilhelm I monument and woodland trails through the Hohensyburg nature reserve.

Hohensyburg Burgruine

The Hohensyburg castle ruins crown the Syberg at about 245 metres above sea level, on the site of a Saxon refuge fort captured by Charlemagne in 775. Remaining walls and the keep outline a fortress held by the lords of Syberg until the 16th century. The Vincketurm observation tower beside the ruins charges a small fee for its viewing platform.
