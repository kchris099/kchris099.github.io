Berlin

Berlin became the capital of Prussia and later the German Empire after unification in 1871. Boulevards, museums, and the Reichstag expanded through industrial growth. The city was divided after 1945, with the Berlin Wall separating East and West from 1961 until 1989. Reunification restored it as the federal capital. Museumsinsel, Brandenburg Gate, and surviving neighborhoods record Prussian, Weimar, Nazi, and Cold War layers.

Kaiser Wilhelm Memorial Church

Berlin left the bomb-damaged tower of Kaiser Wilhelm Memorial Church standing as a warning against war. Egon Eiermann's 1960s church and bell tower surround the ruin with blue stained-glass walls, creating a deliberate contrast between imperial-era masonry and postwar modernism. Memorial displays, worship, and concerts occupy the ensemble at Breitscheidplatz amid the commercial center of former West Berlin.

East Germany completed Berlin's television tower at Alexanderplatz in 1969 as a broadcasting facility and symbol of the socialist capital.
