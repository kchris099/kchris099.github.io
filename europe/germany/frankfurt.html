Explore Frankfurt

A Brief History

Frankfurt developed as a free imperial city, a major trade fair center, the site of imperial elections and coronations, and later one of Germany's main financial capitals. The station, cathedral quarter, Romerberg, churches, museums, and river crossings show the city's mix of medieval core, bourgeois culture, constitutional history, and modern commerce.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

Frankfurt Hauptbahnhof

Frankfurt's main station is one of Germany's great late-19th-century rail terminals and the usual gateway into the inner city. Its scale reflects Frankfurt's role as a transport hub linking trade, finance, and long-distance travel.

Free

Frankfurt Cathedral

Frankfurt Cathedral was the coronation church of Holy Roman emperors and dominates the eastern side of the old center. It is one of the clearest surviving reminders of the city's ceremonial role within the empire.

Free

Römerberg

The Romerberg is Frankfurt's historic market square and the ceremonial heart of the reconstructed old town. It concentrates civic memory, tourism, and some of the city's most recognizable facades in one compact space.

Free

Frankfurter Römer

The Romer, Frankfurt's long-serving city hall, represents the continuity of municipal government in the city center. Its stepped facade became one of the lasting civic symbols of Frankfurt across changing political eras.

Free

Old St Nicholas Church

The Old St Nicholas Church is the small Gothic church facing the Romerberg and closely tied to the square's civic history. Its modest scale helps preserve the ecclesiastical balance within an otherwise strongly civic setting.

€8

Historical Museum Frankfurt

The Historical Museum traces Frankfurt's urban development beside the Romerberg and the Main riverfront. Its collections help place the nearby medieval core within the city's wider commercial, social, and political history.

Free

St. Paul's Church

St Paul's Church is remembered above all for the Frankfurt Parliament of 1848 and the history of German constitutional politics. It turns one urban church into a national site of political memory.

€12

Frankfurter Goethe-Haus

Goethe House reconstructs the family home in which Johann Wolfgang von Goethe spent his youth in Frankfurt. It ties the city center to Frankfurt's role in German literary and intellectual history.

Free

Goethe-Denkmal

The Goethe monument near Goetheplatz reflects Frankfurt's pride in its most celebrated writer. It also shows how 19th-century public sculpture turned literary memory into part of the modern cityscape.

Free

Alte Oper

The Alte Oper is Frankfurt's restored 19th-century opera house and one of the main monumental buildings of the inner city. Its rebuilding also reflects postwar decisions about memory, preservation, and cultural life in the center.

Free

Große Bockenheimer Str.

Grosse Bockenheimer Strasse, often called the Fressgass, is a central shopping and dining street west of the old core. It represents the commercial Frankfurt that developed beyond the medieval ceremonial center.

Free

Bulle & Bär

The Bull and Bear sculpture near the stock exchange symbolizes Frankfurt's modern identity as Germany's financial capital. It is a small but clear marker of the city's later economic role.

Free

St. Catherine's Church

St Catherine's Church is the main Protestant church on the Hauptwache and a landmark on one of Frankfurt's busiest urban nodes. Its survival places an older parish history within a heavily commercial modern center.

Free

Liebfrauen, Frankfurt

Liebfrauen is a Gothic city church whose courtyard and devotional traditions survive in the dense commercial center. It offers a quieter religious counterpoint to the retail and office districts around it.

€19

Städel Museum

The Stadel Museum is one of Germany's major art museums and anchors Frankfurt's Museumsufer on the south bank of the Main. It shows how the city's cultural institutions extend beyond the medieval old town into later riverfront districts.

Free

Eiserner Steg

The Eiserner Steg is the main pedestrian bridge across the Main and a classic viewpoint back toward Frankfurt's skyline and old center. It links the historic north bank with the museum quarter and river promenades to the south.
