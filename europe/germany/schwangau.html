Explore Schwangau

A Brief History

Inhabited since the Stone Age and shaped by Roman road networks, Schwangau's medieval legacy was dominated by the Knights of Schwangau. In the 19th century, King Ludwig II transformed its dramatic alpine ridges with monumental romantic castles, securing its global fame. The survival of these cultural and architectural milestones provides a powerful window into the region's storied past. Museums, markets, and street plans in Schwangau still show layers from those periods.

Top Sights & Activities

€21

Neuschwanstein Castle

Neuschwanstein Castle, a 19th-century hilltop fortress commissioned by King Ludwig II, is a attraction located the Bavarian Alps near the Austrian border. It's one of Germany's most visited sites and offers tours of its lavishly furnished rooms. While it's a popular tourist spot, some travelers recommend exploring other day trips from Munich for a more rewarding experience.

Free

Marienbrücke

Marienbrücke, also known as the Pollat bridge, is a pedestrian bridge that offers views of Neuschwanstein Castle. Named after Queen Marie, it spans the Pollat gorge and was originally constructed in 1845 by King Maximillian II. The wooden footbridge was later replaced with an iron structure by King Ludwig II in 1866.

€24

Hohenschwangau Castle

Hohenschwangau Castle is a fortified historic site in Schwangau, Germany. Hohenschwangau Castle, a Gothic-style summer residence of King Ludwig II, is located near the Pollat Gorge. Located a landscape alongside Neuschwanstein, Hohenschwangau offers insight into the royal upbringing of King Ludwig II. Walls, courtyards, or towers form the main visitor route when the grounds are open.

€32

Tegelbergbahn

Tegelbergbahn is a visitor site in Schwangau. The Tegelberg Cable Car is a modern and well-serviced cable car that takes visitors to the top of Tegelberg Mountain, reaching a height of 1,730 meters. The ride offers views of Neuschwanstein Castle and the Ammer Mountains and takes approximately 10 minutes. Each cabin can accommodate up to 44 people, but due to COVID-19 measures, they are not filled to maximum capacity to ensure social distancing.

Free

St. Coloman

St. Coloman is a visitor site in Schwangau, Germany. Coloman Church is a baroque church located in the village of Hohenschwangau, Germany. Built in the 17th century, it stands on the spot where St. Coloman, an Irish monk, rested during his pilgrimage to Jerusalem. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

€6

Hohes Schloss

Hohes Schloss is a visitor site in Schwangau, Germany. Hohes Castle, located in the German town of Füssen at the end of the famous Romantic Avenue, offers rural views and a glimpse into regional 1500s art. Visitors can explore its ornate banqueting hall, trompe-l'oeil paintings in the inner courtyard, an art gallery, prison tower, and a small chapel. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

€15

Reptilienzoo Allgäu

Reptilienzoo Allgäu, located in Fussen, offers a unique experience with its diverse collection of exotic reptiles, fish, birds, and mammals. The zoo is situated on an old industrial site and houses various animals such as tortoises, snakes, iguanas, caiman, spiders, frogs, and mongoose. Visitors can witness the active behavior of the animals in beautifully designed enclosures.

Free

Lechfall

Lechfall is a stepped waterfall located in Fussen, Germany, on the chalk blue-green Lech river. It's just a 20-minute walk from Fussen train station through a lovely park. The falls were constructed in the 18th century to prevent flooding in Fussen. The Lech river flows from Austria through Germany and eventually reaches Augsburg before joining the Danube.

Free (Treetop Walk €5 extra)

Forest Experience Center Ziegelwies

Forest Experience Center Ziegelwies offers a range of family-friendly activities, including adventure trails and treetop walks. The exhibition center provides informative displays and public amenities. While some trails may be affected by weather conditions, the bergwaldpfad and treetop walk are recommended. Visitors can explore suspension bridges at treetop level and access free entry with the "Konigskarte" pass available from local hotels.

Free

Hopfensee

Hopfensee, also known as the Allgauer Riviera, is a visitor site in Schwangau, Germany. It offers views of the Allgäu Alps and has warmer waters than other lakes in the region, making it an ideal summer retreat. Visitors can relax on the bathing beach Hopfen or take a leisurely walk or bike ride around the lake. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.
