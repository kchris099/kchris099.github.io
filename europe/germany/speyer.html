Speyer

Speyer is among the oldest cities on the Rhine, with Roman roots and a medieval role as an imperial and episcopal center under Salian emperors. Its Romanesque cathedral, consecrated in 1061, became a burial church for German rulers and a symbol of imperial ambition. Old town streets, guild houses, and memorial churches record free imperial city life and the confessional divisions of the early modern period.

Speyerer Schuh

On Speyer's Maximilianstrasse, a small marker records the Speyerer Schuh, a local unit of length used before metric measurement. One Speyer shoe equaled about 28 centimeters and remained in circulation until the early nineteenth century, recalling a period when merchants and craftspeople relied on city-specific standards within the commercial center near the cathedral.

Historische Altstadt Speyer

Speyer's old town preserves the street network and civic fabric that grew around the cathedral and the former free imperial city. Its squares, lanes, and shopfront streets connect the monumental church landscape with the daily urban life of the center.

03 AdmissionFree (Crypt €5)

Speyerer Dom

Speyerer Dom is a vast Romanesque imperial cathedral founded under the Salian dynasty, with a crypt that holds emperors' tombs. Successive building campaigns produced the long nave and twin-pair towers visible across the Rhine plain. The church remains Speyer's defining monument.

49.3172° N / 8.4424° E

04 AdmissionFree

Gedächtniskirche, Speyer

Speyer's Memorial Church was built around 1900 to commemorate the Protestant princes and cities that issued the Protestation at the 1529 Diet of Speyer. A tall neo-Gothic spire, stained-glass cycles, statues, portals, and a hall interior present a nineteenth-century interpretation of Reformation history. The building contrasts deliberately with the older Romanesque cathedral while continuing active Protestant worship.

49.3149° N / 8.4299° E
