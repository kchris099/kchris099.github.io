Explore Speyer

A Brief History

Speyer is among the oldest cities on the Rhine, with Roman roots and a medieval role as an imperial and episcopal center under Salian emperors. Its Romanesque cathedral, consecrated in 1061, became a burial church for German rulers and a symbol of imperial ambition. Old town streets, guild houses, and memorial churches record free imperial city life and the confessional divisions of the early modern period.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

Speyerer Schuh

Speyerer Schuh is a small urban landmark in the old center that helps orient a walk through the commercial streets around the cathedral precinct. Stops like this show how Speyer's historic core is experienced at street level rather than only through its largest monuments.

Free

Historische Altstadt Speyer

Speyer's old town preserves the street network and civic fabric that grew around the cathedral and the former free imperial city. Its squares, lanes, and shopfront streets connect the monumental church landscape with the daily urban life of the center.

Free

Speyerer Dom

Speyer Cathedral is one of the main Romanesque churches of Europe and a major monument of Salian imperial patronage. Its scale, crypt, and long building history make it central to understanding both the city and the medieval empire.

Free

Gedächtniskirche, Speyer

The Memorial Church was built in the late 19th century to commemorate the Protestation of Speyer of 1529. Its tall neo-Gothic form marks the Protestant layer of the city's later religious and political memory.
