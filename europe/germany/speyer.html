Speyer

Speyer is among the oldest cities on the Rhine, with Roman roots and a medieval role as an imperial and episcopal center under Salian emperors. Its Romanesque cathedral, consecrated in 1061, became a burial church for German rulers and a symbol of imperial ambition. Old town streets, guild houses, and memorial churches record free imperial city life and the confessional divisions of the early modern period.

Speyerer Dom

Speyerer Dom is a visitor site in Speyer, Germany. Speyer Cathedral is one of the main Romanesque churches of Europe and a major monument of Salian imperial patronage. Its scale, crypt, and long building history make it central to understanding both the city and the medieval empire. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Gedächtniskirche, Speyer

Gedächtniskirche, Speyer is a visitor site in Speyer, Germany. Construction and early use date to around 1529. The Memorial Church was built in the late 19th century to commemorate the Protestation of Speyer of 1529. Its tall neo-Gothic form marks the Protestant layer of the city's later religious and political memory. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.
