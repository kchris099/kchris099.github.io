Baden-Baden

Baden-Baden developed around thermal springs used since Roman times in the northern Black Forest. Medieval margraves of Baden built a residence here, and 19th-century spa culture drew European visitors to its Kurhaus and colonnaded baths. The town remained part of the Grand Duchy of Baden until 1918. Postwar years preserved its parks, casino, and belle-époque villas while rail links connected it to Karlsruhe and Strasbourg.

Altstadt (Old Town)

Baden-Baden's Altstadt climbs the hillside above the spa quarter with lanes of shops, galleries, and cafés among historic townhouses. Church towers and small squares punctuate the slope toward the market area. The old town remains the pedestrian shopping and strolling core beside the Kurhaus gardens.

Friedrichsbad (Spa)

Of course, a visit to Baden-Baden would not be complete without a bathing for oneself. After visitors see how the Romans did it, visitors can pop upstairs to Friedrichsbad to it in the 21st century. Built in 1877, visitors can bath in style in what was considered the most modern bathhouse in Europe when it opened.

03 AdmissionFree

Florentinerberg

Florentinerberg rises directly above Baden-Baden's old center around the New Castle and historic thermal springs. Terraces, walls and steep streets link the planted hillside with the market square and spa quarter below. The name covers a compact urban slope containing several paths and viewpoints. Its changing elevation reveals how the old town fits between the castle hill and the Oos valley.

48.7636° N / 8.2416° E

04 AdmissionFree

Stiftskirche (Collegiate Church)

The Collegiate Church stands high on Baden-Baden's Florentinerberg above the market square. Romanesque beginnings, Gothic rebuilding, later alterations, tombs of the margraves of Baden, sculpture, stained glass and a prominent tower document its long role as parish and dynastic church. Active services continue, while the steep approach and surrounding cemetery-like memorial setting distinguish it from the lower spa architecture.

Römische Badruinen (Roman Bath Ruins)

Visitors can go back to where it all began with this one of the sightseeing activities in Baden-Baden. The area offers the ruins of the old Roman baths. While the Friedrichsbad (coming up next) was built on top of these ruins, some of them have been preserved as a museum and open to visitors them.
