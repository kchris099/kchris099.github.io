Explore Oberammergau

A Brief History

Oberammergau is a Bavarian town with roots dating back to the Middle Ages, traditionally known for its woodcarving industry. It gained international fame following a vow made during the bubonic plague in 1633 to perform a Passion Play every ten years if the village was spared. This tradition has continued for nearly four centuries, influencing the town's culture and economy. Today, Oberammergau is celebrated for its painted houses (Luftlmalerei) and its enduring theatrical heritage.

Linderhof Palace

Linderhof Palace is the smallest of the three palaces built by King Ludwig II of Bavaria and the only one he lived to see completed. Located near Ettal, the very ornate palace is inspired by the Palace of Versailles and features opulent Rococo interiors. The surrounding formal gardens include the dramatic Venus Grotto and the Moorish Kiosk.

€16

Olympic Ski Jump

Nestled in the Bavarian Alps, the Olympic Ski Jump in Garmisch-Partenkirchen is a modern marvel built on the historic site of a 1920s ski jump. This impressive structure features a towering 100-meter start ramp and serves as one of the region's most popular attractions. Known for its rich history dating back to the 1936 Winter Olympics, it continues to be a hub for winter sports enthusiasts today.

€78

Bayerische Zugspitzbahn Mountain Railway

The Bayerische Zugspitzbahn Mountain Railway is a narrow-gauge cogwheel railway that takes you to Germany's highest point at 2650 meters above sea level. bring your furry friend on the train and some cable cars, and further information can be obtained at the reception. The ticket from Garmisch costs 63 euros per adult, and it's advisable to bring a sweater as it gets cooler at the top.

€8

Garmisch Olympia Ice Sport Stadium

The Garmisch Olympia Ice Skating Rink is a great place to go ice skating if you're looking for a fun and adrenaline-filled experience. The rink is well maintained and has plenty of space for all types of skaters, from beginners to experts. Plus, the staff are friendly and trained in various skill ranges, so your family can have lots of fun no matter their level of expertise.

Wankbahn

Wankbahn is a popular landmark in Garmisch-Partenkirchen, offering a summit plateau reached by cable car with panoramic views, hiking trails, and paragliding opportunities. Visitors can immerse themselves in the city's rich history and culture by exploring nearby attractions such as Partnachklamm, Graseckseilbahn, Burgruine Werdenfels, and Spielbank Garmisch-Partenkirchen.

Free

Burgruine Werdenfels

Burgruine Werdenfels, located just north of Garmisch-Partenkirchen in Germany, is a 13th-century castle ruin perched 80 meters above the valley. Visitors can take a leisurely walk through the forest from Berggasthof Pflegersee to reach the ruins and explore its history through information boards along the way. The area around Lake Eibsee offers mountain biking opportunities with diverse trails and views.

Free

Kuhfluchtwasserfälle

Kuhflucht Waterfalls, located near Farchant in Germany, is a natural attraction featuring three steep and rocky waterfalls set amidst forested walking trails. These falls are among the tallest in the country, with a height of 270 meters. The area offers picturesque views of Garmisch and the Zugspitze, making it perfect for outdoor enthusiasts and photography lovers.

Free

Ettal Abbey

Ettal Abbey, located just a few minutes away from Oberammergau, is a renowned Benedictine monastery with a Baroque church featuring a frescoed dome. The abbey has been brewing beer for over 400 years and offers its famous Weissbrau. Visitors can explore the chapel, browse unique items in the monastery shop, and visit the cheese factory/store behind the monastery. The abbey also houses a boarding school, hotel, distillery, and bookshop.

€19

Alpine Coaster

The Alpine Coaster offers a thrilling toboggan ride with mountain views as you coast through meadows and down slopes. Accessible via a scenic 15-minute ski lift, the top of the mountain features amenities like a play area, restaurant, and park. Riders can control their speed using handbrakes on the fast and exhilarating slide that lasts around 3-4 minutes. While not suitable for young kids, it's recommended for adults seeking an adrenaline-pumping experience.
