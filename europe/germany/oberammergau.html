Oberammergau

Oberammergau is a Bavarian town with roots dating back to the Middle Ages, traditionally known for its woodcarving industry. It gained international fame following a vow made during the bubonic plague in 1633 to perform a Passion Play every ten years if the village was spared. This tradition has continued for nearly four centuries, influencing the town's culture and economy. Today, Oberammergau is celebrated for its painted houses (Luftlmalerei) and its long-running theatrical heritage.

Linderhof Palace

Linderhof Palace is a fortified historic site in Oberammergau, Germany. Linderhof Palace is the smallest of the three palaces built by King Ludwig II of Bavaria and the only one he lived to see completed. The surrounding formal gardens include the dramatic Venus Grotto and the Moorish Kiosk. Walls, courtyards, or towers form the main visitor route when the grounds are open.

Bayerische Zugspitzbahn Mountain Railway

The Bayerische Zugspitzbahn Mountain Railway is a narrow-gauge cogwheel railway that takes visitors to Germany's highest point at 2650 meters above sea level. Bring visitor furry friend on the train and some cable cars, and further information can be obtained at the reception. The ticket from Garmisch costs 63 euros per adult, and it's advisable to bring a sweater as it gets cooler at the top.

Ettal Abbey, located just a few minutes away from Oberammergau, is a known Benedictine monastery with a Baroque church featuring a frescoed dome.
