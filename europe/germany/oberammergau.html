Oberammergau

Oberammergau is a Bavarian town with roots dating back to the Middle Ages, traditionally known for its woodcarving industry. It gained international fame following a vow made during the bubonic plague in 1633 to perform a Passion Play every ten years if the village was spared. This tradition has continued for nearly four centuries, influencing the town's culture and economy. Today, Oberammergau is celebrated for its painted houses (Luftlmalerei) and its long-running theatrical heritage.

Linderhof Palace

Linderhof was a major palace King Ludwig II lived to see substantially completed. An intimate but ornamented residence, formal terraces, fountains, park buildings, the Moorish Kiosk, and the artificial Venus Grotto express his private theatrical world. Guided interior visits and restoration closures shape access, while the gardens extend well beyond the small central palace.

Olympic Ski Jump

Oberammergau's Olympic-era ski jump belongs to the same 1930s Alpine sports infrastructure wave as neighbouring valleys, still used for training against the Ammergau peaks. The inrun structure is visible from approaches to the Passion Play village. Winter sport and tourism share the valley floor.

Bayerische Zugspitzbahn Mountain Railway

The Bavarian Zugspitzbahn is a cogwheel railway from Garmisch-Partenkirchen toward the Zugspitze plateau, connecting with cable-car infrastructure below the summit. Tunnels, steep gradients, mountain stations and changing alpine weather make it both transport and engineering work.

Garmisch Olympia Ice Sport Stadium

The Olympic Ice Sports Centre in Garmisch-Partenkirchen supports public skating, hockey, curling, training and competitions across several rinks, and its history reaches back to facilities associated with the 1936 Winter Olympics, although later rebuilding created the present complex. Session schedules, rentals, events and maintenance determine which ice areas are open to the public.

Pfarrkirche St. Martin

Pfarrkirche St. Martin in Oberammergau, often called the Old Church, stands on early medieval roots with the present Gothic fabric largely of 1280. Wall paintings include a large figure of Saint Christopher. The parish church anchors the painted-house streets of the Passion Play town.

Wankbahn

Wankbahn rises from Garmisch-Partenkirchen to open slopes and hiking routes on Wank mountain, not from Oberammergau. The upper area faces the Wetterstein peaks and Loisach valley, with paths, restaurants and paragliding activity varying by season; wind, maintenance and operating periods affect the cable car, while Partnachklamm and other valley sites remain geographically separate.

Burgruine Werdenfels

Burgruine Werdenfels, located just north of Garmisch-Partenkirchen in Germany, is a 13th-century castle ruin perched 80 meters above the valley. Visitors can take a leisurely walk through the forest from Berggasthof Pflegersee to reach the ruins and explore its history through information boards along the way. The area around Lake Eibsee offers mountain biking opportunities with diverse trails and views.

Kuhfluchtwasserfälle

Forest paths above Farchant approach the Kuhflucht waterfalls through lower cascades and increasingly steep terrain, and the combined descent is often cited at about 270 metres, while the visible water breaks into several separate drops. Rain, snowmelt, mud and ice change both the flow and the condition of the route near Garmisch-Partenkirchen.

Ettal Abbey

Ettal Abbey is an active Benedictine monastery in a mountain valley north of Oberammergau. Its Baroque church centers on a large frescoed dome, while monastic buildings support worship, education; hospitality, a shop, brewing, distilling and other enterprises. Commercial facilities and tours change, but the basilica, community and long history since the fourteenth-century foundation provide the stable core.

Alpine Coaster

The Alpine Coaster above Oberammergau is a wheeled toboggan ride on rails through meadows, with handbrakes for speed control on a run lasting a few minutes. Mountain views open between trees and open slopes. It adds a managed downhill thrill beside hiking and village sightseeing.
