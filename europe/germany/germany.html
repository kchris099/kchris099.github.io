Germany Guide

Modern Germany took shape through unification in 1871 from numerous principalities, free cities, and regional crowns that had long governed the heart of Europe. The Holy Roman Empire, Reformation, princely courts, Hanseatic trade, and later industrial cities left a dense record in castles, cathedrals, town halls, and museums across Rhine, Alpine, and northern landscapes.
