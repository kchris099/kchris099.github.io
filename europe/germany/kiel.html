Explore Kiel

A Brief History

Kiel became the capital of the Prussian Baltic fleet after 1865 and later of the Imperial German Navy, with shipyards and the Kiel Canal linking the North Sea to the Baltic. The city hosted the 1936 Olympic sailing events and the 1918 naval mutiny that hastened Germany's defeat. Rebuilt after wartime bombing, Kiel developed research institutes and the Kiel Week regatta while harbour quays and government buildings record its role as Schleswig-Holstein's largest port.

Naval Memorial

The Naval Memorial, also known as the Laboe Naval Memorial, is a significant war memorial featuring a tower and observation deck. Originally built in 1936 to honor sailors of World War I, it now symbolizes all seafarers who lost their lives in both world wars. The towering structure offers a small exhibition on the ground floor and panoramic views from its platform.

Free

Laboe

Laboe, a municipality in Schleswig-Holstein, Germany, is located on the Baltic Sea coast just 10 km northeast of Kiel. The area has a beach along the Kiel Fjord, offering shallow waters that make it used for children to enjoy digging and playing. Travellers can also explore the Laboe Naval Memorial and the U-boat U-995 within the municipality.

Free

Olympic Center Schilksee Kiel Landeshauptadt

The Olympic Center Schilksee Kiel Landeshauptadt is a waterfront complex that includes a club house and sports hall, making it the perfect venue for beach volleyball championships and regattas. This sailing harbor served as a yacht harbor during the 1972 Olympics and offers facilities, restaurants, viewpoints, and even a bit of beach. Travellers can enjoy the gorgeous landscape while exploring adjacent attractions such as restaurants, cafes, and a promenade.

Free

Leuchtturm Kiel-Holtenau

Leuchtturm Kiel-Holtenau is a visitor site in Kiel, Germany. Construction and early use date to around 1895. Leuchtturm Kiel-Holtenau is a 24-meter lighthouse that has stood proudly since its construction in the 1890s, specifically in 1895. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Free

MERKUR Ostseehalle

MERKUR Ostseehalle is a visitor site in Kiel. Wunderino Arena is a prominent landmark in Kiel, Germany, and is celebrated for its role as a multi-purpose venue. Conveniently located near the train station, it's easily accessible for both locals and travellers alike. This facility is the home ground for THW Kiel and hosts various events ranging from thrilling handball matches to electrifying concerts.

Free

Kiel Opera House (Theatre Kiel)

In Kiel, the Kiel Opera House (Theatre Kiel) is a historic gem that has been audiences for over a century. This venue is not only known for its architecture but also is a hub for opera and ballet performances. Travellers can immerse themselves in an array of shows, from grand operatic productions to contemporary theater, all without the constraints of a strict dress code, just come as are and enjoy!

Free

St. Nikolai Church

St. Nikolai Church in Kiel has a rich history, with its construction dating back to the city's founding in 1242. The church has undergone several reconstructions and extensions over the years, including a restoration after being devastated by Allied bomb raids during World War II. Despite the destruction, ecclesiastical treasures and artworks had been removed prior to the bombings. In 1950, the church was reconstructed using modern elements and materials.

Aquarium GEOMAR

Aquarium GEOMAR is a visitor site in Kiel, Germany. Aquarium GEOMAR is a compact outdoor aquarium located in Kiel, offering a diverse range of marine life. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest. Aquarium GEOMAR remains part of the public visitor circuit in Kiel.

Free

Botanical Garden

Botanical Garden is a public park in Kiel, Germany. Within the Kiel University campus, the Botanical Garden is a natural and cultural monument with a history dating back 350 years. Paths, lawns, and shaded areas make up the main public facilities. Botanical Garden remains part of the public visitor circuit in Kiel.
