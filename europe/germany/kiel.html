Destination Guide — Northern Germany

Explore Kiel

A Brief History

Kiel became the capital of the Prussian Baltic fleet after 1865 and later of the Imperial German Navy, with shipyards and the Kiel Canal linking the North Sea to the Baltic. The city hosted the 1936 Olympic sailing events and the 1918 naval mutiny that hastened Germany's defeat. Rebuilt after wartime bombing, Kiel developed research institutes and the Kiel Week regatta while harbour quays and government buildings record its role as Schleswig-Holstein's largest port.

Naval Memorial

The Naval Memorial, also known as the Laboe Naval Memorial, is a significant war memorial featuring a tower and observation deck. Originally built in 1936 to honor sailors of World War I, it now symbolizes all seafarers who lost their lives in both world wars. The towering structure offers a small exhibition on the ground floor and panoramic views from its platform.

Free

Laboe

Laboe, a municipality in Schleswig-Holstein, Germany, is nestled on the Baltic Sea coast just 10 km northeast of Kiel. The area boasts a beach along the Kiel Fjord, offering shallow waters that make it used for children to enjoy digging and playing. travellers can also explore the Laboe Naval Memorial and the U-boat U-995 within the municipality.

Free

Olympic Center Schilksee Kiel Landeshauptadt

The Olympic Center Schilksee Kiel Landeshauptadt is a waterfront complex that includes a club house and sports hall, making it the perfect venue for beach volleyball championships and regattas. This sailing harbor served as a yacht harbor during the 1972 Olympics and offers facilities, restaurants, viewpoints, and even a bit of beach. travellers can enjoy the gorgeous landscape while exploring adjacent attractions such as restaurants, cafes, and a promenade.

Free

Leuchtturm Kiel-Holtenau

Leuchtturm Kiel-Holtenau is a 24-meter lighthouse that has stood proudly since its construction in the 1890s, specifically in 1895. This structure, built from reclaimed bricks, serves as a vital navigation aid at the entrance of the Kiel Fjord and boasts a striking red and white exterior. The surrounding area features well-kept lawns and benches where travellers can relax while soaking up the sun or enjoying views of the bustling Kiel Canal.

Free

MERKUR Ostseehalle

Wunderino Arena stands as a prominent landmark in Kiel, Germany, and is celebrated for its role as a multi-purpose venue. Conveniently located near the train station, it’s easily accessible for both locals and travellers alike. This impressive facility serves as the home ground for THW Kiel and hosts various events ranging from thrilling handball matches to electrifying concerts.

Free

Kiel Opera House (Theatre Kiel)

in the heart of Kiel, the Kiel Opera House (Theatre Kiel) stands as a historic gem that has been captivating audiences for over a century. This venue is not only renowned for its architecture but also serves as a hub for opera and ballet performances. travellers can immerse themselves in an array of shows, from grand operatic productions to contemporary theater, all without the constraints of a strict dress code—just come as are and enjoy!.

Free

St. Nikolai Church

St. Nikolai Church in Kiel has a rich history, with its construction dating back to the city's founding in 1242. The church has undergone several reconstructions and extensions over the years, including a restoration after being devastated by Allied bomb raids during World War II. Despite the destruction, ecclesiastical treasures and artworks had been removed prior to the bombings. In 1950, the church was reconstructed using modern elements and materials.

Aquarium GEOMAR

Aquarium GEOMAR is a compact outdoor aquarium located in Kiel, offering a diverse range of marine life. It is known for its seals and features various fish and crustaceans from the Baltic, Mediterranean, and tropical regions. The aquarium provides an immersive experience with lifelike environments showcasing Nordic and tropical swimmers. travellers can explore the informative exhibits to learn about the diversity of marine creatures and their habitats.

Free

Botanical Garden

within the Kiel University campus, the Botanical Garden is a natural and cultural monument with a history dating back 350 years. This hidden gem boasts an impressive collection of approximately 14,000 plant species, making it an public space for both scientific research and student training.
