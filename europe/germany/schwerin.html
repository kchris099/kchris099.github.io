Destination Guide — Northern Germany

Explore Schwerin

A Brief History

Established as a Slavic fortress in the 11th century, Schwerin served as the seat of the powerful Dukes of Mecklenburg. Its sweeping lakes and romantic 19th-century palace reflect a long history of aristocratic influence shaping the cultural landscape of northern Germany. Today, this complex historical evolution remains significantly embedded in the civic architecture and vibrant daily life of the community.

Top Sights & Activities

€13

Schwerin Castle

Nestled on an island in Lake Schwerin, the captivating Schwerin Castle is a example of Neo-Renaissance architecture. This fairy-tale castle boasts ornate rooms and houses a remarkable European art collection. The town of Schwerin itself is steeped in history, offering cobbled streets, historic buildings, and serene lakeside views.

Free

Marketplace

Schwerin Marktplatz is a charming historic marketplace located just a short stroll from Schwerin Cathedral. Built in 1651, this attractive town square offers a delightful setting to relax and explore. Visitors can enjoy the picturesque views of the cathedral and town hall while strolling through the square's small stores and cafes. The marketplace is also known for hosting demonstrations, outdoor markets, and events, making it an exciting spot to visit.

Free

Schweriner Weihnachtsmarkt

The Schweriner Weihnachtsmarkt is a delightful destination that features a warm and inviting atmosphere, making it the perfect gathering spot for people of all ages. This charming market has been thoughtfully designed to adapt to its surroundings, creating a cozy environment where visitors can enjoy the festive spirit. Friendly vendors are eager to assist, contributing to an overall relaxed vibe filled with good cheer.

Free

Schelf Church

The Paul Friedrich Monument, funded through donations, was crafted by Berlin sculptor Christian Daniel Rauch and unveiled in 1849. The Schwerins had ample reason to express gratitude towards Grand Duke Paul Friedrich, as he relocated the residence from Ludwigslust back to Schwerin in 1837. This sculpture is notable for its exceptional craftsmanship and should be admired alongside the museum's impressive facade.

Reservation required

Mecklenburg State Theatre

In the heart of Schwerin's Alter Garten, find the Mecklenburg State Theatre, a Neo-Renaissance building dating back to 1886. With its main theater capable of seating 650 guests and a smaller stage for puppet shows and kid-friendly events, this theater offers a diverse range of performances including plays, operas, musicals, and ballet. The grand-ducal ambiance is complemented by modern comfort with plush armchairs and ornate decor.

€4

Schleifmühle Schwerin

Schleifmühle Schwerin is a hidden gem in the city, offering a unique experience for visitors. This old water mill, dating back to 1704, provides insight into historical milling technology and working conditions of the past. The museum is recommended by many due to its enthusiastic guides who passionately explain the mill's operations. Situated close to the castle gardens, it offers a lovely setting for visitors to admire from the outside.

€16

Zoo Schwerin

Zoo Schwerin, located in the south of the state capital Schwerin, is a medium-sized year-round zoo that attracts almost 217,000 visitors annually. The zoo spans about 25 hectares and is home to over 2118 animals from 144 species. Visitors can observe a wide variety of animals including elephants, tigers, giraffes, lemurs, and kangaroos in their natural habitats.

€8

Internationales Feuerwehrmuseum Schwerin eV

The Internationales Feuerwehrmuseum Schwerin eV is an impressive museum dedicated to showcasing the history and significance of firefighting. Situated in Schwerin, it offers a captivating exhibition that chronicles the evolution of fire services globally. The museum's collection includes various artifacts, exhibits, and interactive displays that illustrate the techniques and equipment employed by firefighters throughout time.

€5

Open Air Museum of Ethnography Schwerin Mueß

The Open Air Museum of Ethnography Schwerin Mueß, also known as Freilichtmuseum & Geschichtliche Sammlungen, is a fascinating attraction that offers an interesting collection of original historic buildings from the 19th century. Situated in the former fishing and farming village of Muess, this open-air museum showcases farmhouses, barns, a blacksmith shop, bakery, school, vegetable garden, and more.

€15

Flippermuseum Schwerin

The Flippermuseum Schwerin is a gaming museum that exhibits restored pinball machines, jukeboxes, and arcade games. It also features a café for visitors to enjoy. Whether you are a pinball enthusiast or not, this place has something for everyone. The museum is run by friendly staff and tends to be less crowded, even on busy days like hot Saturdays in July.
