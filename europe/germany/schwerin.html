Schwerin

A Slavic fortress of the eleventh century became the residence of the dukes of Mecklenburg. The island palace rebuilt in the nineteenth century and the surrounding lakes define Schwerin's aristocratic geography in northern Germany more than any Hanseatic warehouse row. The state parliament now occupies parts of the palace the dukes once reserved for court.

Schelf Church

Schelf Church is a religious building in Schwerin, Germany. Records associated with the site include 1849, 1837. The Paul Friedrich Monument, funded through donations, was crafted by Berlin sculptor Christian Daniel Rauch and unveiled in 1849. The Schwerins had ample reason to express gratitude towards Grand Duke Paul Friedrich, as he relocated the residence from Ludwigslust back to Schwerin in 1837.
