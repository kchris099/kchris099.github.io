Schwerin

A Slavic fortress of the eleventh century became the residence of the dukes of Mecklenburg. The island palace rebuilt in the nineteenth century and the surrounding lakes define Schwerin's aristocratic geography in northern Germany more than any Hanseatic warehouse row. The state parliament now occupies parts of the palace the dukes once reserved for court.

Schwerin Castle

Schwerin Castle occupies an island between lakes as both a historic palace and the seat of Mecklenburg-Vorpommern's state parliament. The present neo-Renaissance exterior, courtyards, chapel, state rooms, gardens, and museum collections grew from medieval and later residences heavily rebuilt in the nineteenth century. Parliamentary security and restoration affect interior routes, while bridges and shore paths frame the elaborate silhouette.

Marketplace

Schwerin Marktplatz is a historic marketplace located just a short stroll from Schwerin Cathedral. Built in 1651, this attractive town square offers a setting to relax and explore. Visitors can enjoy the views of the cathedral and town hall while strolling through the square's small stores and cafes. The marketplace is also known for hosting demonstrations, outdoor markets, and events, making it an exciting spot to visit.

Schweriner Weihnachtsmarkt

Schweriner Weihnachtsmarkt fills squares around the old town and castle approaches each Advent with timber stalls, seasonal food, and lights. The market layout follows the historic streets rather than a single open field. Evening visits coincide with the illuminated Schloss skyline.

Schelf Church

Schelfkirche (St. Nicholas) is Schwerin's Baroque brick church for the Schelfstadt district, completed in the early eighteenth century. Tall windows and a unified hall plan distinguish it from the cathedral. Nearby, Christian Daniel Rauch's 1849 Paul Friedrich monument recalls the grand duke who returned the court to Schwerin in 1837.

Mecklenburg State Theatre

Mecklenburg State Theatre faces Schwerin's Alter Garten between the castle and museum. The late nineteenth-century building contains a main auditorium and supports drama, opera, ballet, concerts, musical theater, puppetry and festivals through several venues. Wartime survival and later renovation shaped the house, while access outside performances depends on scheduled tours or public events.

Schleifmühle Schwerin

Schleifmühle Schwerin is a historic water mill from 1704 interpreted as a museum of milling technology and working life. Machinery demonstrations and guided explanation show how grain was processed with water power. The site sits among the lakes that define Schwerin's landscape.

Zoo Schwerin

Zoo Schwerin, located in the south of the state capital Schwerin, is a medium-sized year-round zoo that attracts almost 217,000 visitors annually. The zoo spans about 25 hectares and is home to over 2118 animals from 144 species. Visitors can observe a wide variety of animals including elephants, tigers, giraffes, lemurs, and kangaroos in their natural habitats.

Internationales Feuerwehrmuseum Schwerin eV

Schwerin's fire-service museum brings together vehicles and equipment from different periods of emergency response. Full-size engines, pumps and ladders occupy the larger display areas. Uniforms, protective gear and communication devices provide a closer view of changing working methods, while documents and models emphasize German and regional brigade history.

Open Air Museum of Ethnography Schwerin Mueß

An open-air museum in the former village of Muess preserves rural life near Schwerin. Farmhouses, barns and workshops stand beside a school and cultivated plots, creating the outline of a small settlement, and furnished interiors, tools and seasonal demonstrations explain fishing, agriculture and craft work from the nineteenth and early twentieth centuries.

Flippermuseum Schwerin

Restored pinball machines, jukeboxes, and arcade games fill this gaming museum. Staff keep the rooms approachable even on busy summer Saturdays. Galleries invite play as much as quiet looking at the machines, so visits often last longer than a quick walk-through.
