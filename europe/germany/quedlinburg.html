Explore Quedlinburg

A Brief History

Quedlinburg was a Saxon stronghold and later a collegiate foundation tied to the Ottonian dynasty in the 10th century. More than a thousand half-timbered houses survive on cobbled lanes. The castle hill and St. Servatius Church hold royal memorials. Mining in the nearby Harz supported medieval wealth. The old town was added to the UNESCO World Heritage list in 1994 for its preserved urban fabric.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

Altstadt (Old Town)

Altstadt (Old Town) is a visitor site in Quedlinburg, Germany. The Altstadt of Celle is known for its concentration of beautifully preserved half-timbered (Fachwerk) houses. The pedestrianized streets offer a atmosphere filled with shops and cafes. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Free

Sternkiekerturm (Lindenbein Tower)

Just to the north-west of Marktplatz is Sternkiekerturm, an observation tower with great views of the Old Town thanks to being 42 metres high. It is next to the city wall and used to be part of the defensive system of Quedlinburg. The current design of the tower dates to the late 19th century when the eclectic style was in vogue.

€5

Fachwerkmuseum Im Ständerbau (Half Timbered Museum)

For travellers who want to learn more about Germany's half-timbered houses (it's hard not to when visitors see so many of them here!), this museum is the place to head. The Half Timbered Museum calls one of the oldest half-timbered houses in the city home. It dates back to 1310.

Free

Rathaus (Town Hall)

Rathaus (Town Hall) sits within the Quedlinburg area of Quedlinburg, documented in local heritage registers for its civic or cultural role. Visitors approach from the main access road and combine the visit with neighbouring heritage buildings and public spaces in the district.

Free

Marktkirche St. Benedikti (Market Church Of St Benedict)

Located behind the Rathaus is Marktkirche, the place Quedlinburg’s merchants went in the 10th century to worship. With Romanesque stonework, it’s a building that could have been consecrated as early as 1173. It’s evolved over the years and today, it has many architectural styles and furnishings.
