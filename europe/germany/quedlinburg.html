Quedlinburg

Quedlinburg was a Saxon stronghold and later a collegiate foundation tied to the Ottonian dynasty in the 10th century. More than a thousand half-timbered houses survive on cobbled lanes. The castle hill and St. Servatius Church hold royal memorials. Mining in the nearby Harz supported medieval wealth. The old town was added to the UNESCO World Heritage list in 1994 for its preserved urban fabric.

Altstadt (Old Town)

Quedlinburg's old town contains a dense collection of timber-framed houses spanning many construction periods beneath the castle hill. Medieval street lines, market squares, churches, courtyards, later restorations, shops and residences form a historic urban landscape.

02 AdmissionFree

Sternkiekerturm (Lindenbein Tower)

Just to the north-west of Marktplatz is Sternkiekerturm, an observation tower with great views of the Old Town thanks to being 42 metres high. It is next to the city wall and used to be part of the defensive system of Quedlinburg. The current design of the tower dates to the late 19th century when the eclectic style was in vogue.

Fachwerkmuseum Im Ständerbau (Half Timbered Museum)

The Fachwerkmuseum occupies one of Quedlinburg's earliest surviving timber-frame houses, traditionally dated to the early fourteenth century. Exposed posts, infill, joints and repairs make the building itself the main exhibit. Models, tools and displays explain how construction methods changed across the town's many half-timbered structures, while the small rooms preserve the scale of a medieval domestic interior.

51.7883° N / 11.1405° E

04 AdmissionFree

Rathaus (Town Hall)

Quedlinburg Town Hall faces the market square with a stone facade, steep roof, corner tower and Renaissance alterations to an older civic building. A statue of Roland and later decoration connect the structure with municipal rights and government. Administrative use limits casual interior access, while markets, ceremonies and surrounding timber-framed houses keep the square active around it.

51.7898° N / 11.1419° E

05 AdmissionFree

Marktkirche St. Benedikti (Market Church Of St Benedict)

Marktkirche St. Benedikti developed beside Quedlinburg's market as the parish church of merchants and townspeople. Romanesque origins, Gothic rebuilding, twin towers, chapels, furnishings, and later restoration reveal a longer history than one uncertain consecration date. Tower or interior access may be seasonal, while the church's position behind the town hall explains its close tie to civic trade.

51.7903° N / 11.1423° E
