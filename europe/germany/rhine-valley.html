Explore Rhine Valley

A Brief History

The Upper Middle Rhine Valley is a legendary, culturally profound stretch of the Rhine River, celebrated worldwide for its dramatic gorges, ancient terraced vineyards, and an unparalleled density of majestic medieval hilltop castles. This crucial European trade artery has inspired centuries of romantic art, timeless literature, and classical music, including the famous, haunting legend of the Lorelei siren. Recognized rightfully as a UNESCO World Heritage site, it perfectly captures the quintessential, romantic spirit of Germany's deeply intertwined historical and natural beauty.

Top Sights & Activities

Marksburg Castle

The only hilltop castle in the Middle Rhine Valley that was never destroyed. Perched high above Braubach, it provides an authentic, immersive look into daily medieval fortress life.

€15

Rheinfels Castle

Explore the massive, labyrinthine ruins of what was once the largest and most powerful fortress on the Rhine. Bring a flashlight to wander through its fascinating network of underground mining tunnels.

Varies

Rüdesheim am Rhein

A quintessential, vibrant winemaking town. Stroll down the famous Drosselgasse, a narrow cobblestone lane packed with lively traditional taverns, live music, and excellent local Riesling.

Varies

Lorelei Rock

A dramatic, soaring slate cliff towering 120 meters above the Rhine. It is famously steeped in German romantic legend as the home of a beautiful, singing siren who lured sailors to their doom.

Free

Rhine River Cruise

The absolute best way to experience the UNESCO World Heritage valley. Drift past dozens of romantic ruined castles, steep vineyards, and picturesque villages along the legendary river.

Free

Niederwald Monument

A colossal, patriotic monument Built in the late 19th century to commemorate the unification of Germany. You can reach it via a scenic cable car ride directly over the sprawling vineyards.
