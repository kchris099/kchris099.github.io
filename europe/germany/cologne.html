Explore Cologne

A Brief History

Cologne, one of Germany's oldest cities, was founded by the Romans in the 1st century AD. It flourished throughout the Middle Ages as a major religious and commercial center, famously anchored by its monumental Gothic Cathedral. Despite extensive World War II devastation, the city has meticulously restored its historic landmarks and vibrant Rhineland culture.

Top Sights & Activities

Free (€6)

Cologne Cathedral

Construction of this High Gothic cathedral began in 1248 to house the relics of the Three Wise Men, making it a major pilgrimage center. Completed in 1880, it survived severe World War II bombings and stands as a UNESCO World Heritage site representing European Christianity.

Free

Hohenzollern Bridge

Originally built between 1907 and 1911, this bridge crossing the Rhine was a crucial railway link and a symbol of Prussian engineering. Rebuilt after World War II for rail and pedestrian use, it is famously adorned with thousands of 'love padlocks' left by couples.

Free

Hahnentorburg

Dating back to the early 13th century, this medieval gate was a vital component of the defensive city walls. Historically, it served as the western entrance through which Holy Roman Emperors passed after their coronation, providing a link to the city's past as a fortified imperial center.

Free

Alter Markt

This central square has been the heart of civic and commercial life since the Middle Ages. Historically the site of the main market and political gatherings, it was heavily reconstructed after World War II but retains its historical footprint, hosting traditional festivals and markets.

€14

Schokoladenmuseum Koln

Opened in 1993, this museum explores the 5,000-year history of chocolate, from its origins in Mesoamerican cultures to modern industrial production. The building reflects modern architectural design integrated into a historic port area, detailing historical trade routes and manufacturing processes.

Free

Kranhauser im Rheinauhafen

Completed in 2010, these three distinct buildings were designed to resemble the historic harbor cranes that once operated along the Rhine. They are central to the revitalization of the old port district, bridging the city's industrial maritime past with its modern urban development.

Free

Flora und Botanischer Garten Koln

Established in 1864, this formal park was created for recreation and botanical study following the destruction of older city gardens. It features diverse historical garden styles, including French Baroque and English landscape designs, serving as an important center for botanical research.
