Explore Konstanz

A Brief History

Konstanz traces its origins to a Roman fort established in the fourth century. It flourished as a major trade center and bishopric during the Middle Ages, notably hosting the Council of Constance from 1414 to 1418, which ended the Western Schism. The city largely escaped destruction during World War II due to its proximity to neutral Switzerland. Today, Konstanz is a vibrant university town known for its well-preserved medieval center and scenic location on Lake Constance.

Island Mainau

Mainau Island, located near Konstanz in Germany, is a picturesque destination featuring an 18th-century castle and a butterfly house. The island is renowned for its diverse floral landscapes, offering a mesmerizing display of colorful flowers throughout the year. Visitors can explore the Mainau Palace and enjoy leisurely walks through the lush gardens. Additionally, the island offers various attractions such as a petting animal farm, playgrounds, and captivating views.

Free

Schloss Mainau

Schloss Mainau is an impressive baroque castle and residence located on an island in Lake Constance. The site features a palm house, events hall, café, playgrounds for children, and picturesque paths to stroll along the water and through trees and flowers. Visitors can explore the butterfly house and admire the thousands of tulips during Easter holidays. Inside the main house, there is a nice café and exposition of handcrafted items with a view of Lake Konstanz.

Free

Mainau Kinderland - Insel Mainau

Mainau Kinderland is a dedicated family area on the 'Flower Island' of Mainau in Lake Constance. It features extensive playgrounds, a petting zoo, and interactive water play areas designed for children. The attraction provides a fun, engaging environment that complements the island's famous botanical gardens and floral displays.

€12

Historische Altstadt Meersburg am Bodensee

Meersburg Altstadt, or the Old Town of Meersburg, is a captivating destination that transports visitors back to medieval times. With its charming cobblestone streets and architecture, this picturesque area offers views of Lake Constance and the Alps. As you wander through quaint shops and cozy cafes, find delightful spots to relax and soak in the atmosphere.

Free

Kaiserbrunnen

Nestled in the vibrant heart of Konstanz, Germany, the Kaiserbrunnen, or Emperors Fountain, stands as a striking red stone obelisk adorned with captivating sculptures that pay homage to historical figures. This landmark not only highlights the city's pivotal role in the Holy Roman Empire but also serves as a delightful gathering spot for locals and visitors alike.

Free

Historische Hafenuhr

Historische Hafenuhr is a charming historic landmark located in a city with beautiful sights of lakes and rivers. It's a great place to explore on foot, especially on cold winter days when the warmth of the people and the lively atmosphere contrast with the weather. The view of the lake and the Alps is truly amazing, making it a perfect spot to spend some restful time and take in the beauty of nature.

Free

Imperia

Imperia is a famous landmark in Konstanz, located at the end of the city's pier. This nine-meter-high concrete statue, created by local sculptor Peter Lenk, depicts a woman holding miniature statues of Pope Martin V and Emperor Sigismund. The statue rotates around its axis once every four minutes, attracting visitors with its audacious mechanism and aesthetic appeal. Imperia is known for its controversial yet intriguing portrayal of a barely dressed woman wearing a fool's cap.

Free

Stadtgarten

Stadtgarten is a picturesque city park located in the heart of Konstanz, offering a peaceful retreat with its well-maintained flower beds and meandering pathways. Situated by the waterfront, it provides photo opportunities along the lakeshore and captures the essence of urban life in Konstanz during summer. In the evenings, it becomes a vibrant social hub with music adding to its enchanting ambiance.

Free

Münster Unserer Lieben Frau

The Minster of Our Lady in Konstanz is a prominent Gothic church located in the historic city center. It served as the cathedral of the Diocese of Konstanz until 1821. The building features a mix of Romanesque and Gothic elements, a richly decorated interior, and a tower that offers expansive views of Lake Constance and the surrounding region.

€20

Bodensee-Therme Konstanz

Bodensee-Therme Konstanz is a modern spa and sports pool located near Lake Constance. With 3,000 square meters of water, it offers various amenities for visitors to swim, bathe, and relax. The facility includes family-friendly features such as low showers and small changing facilities for young children. Additionally, the spa boasts indoor and outdoor pools, a thermal pool with views of the lake, a sauna area, and an open-air pool with warm temperatures.
