Konstanz

Konstanz grew from a fourth-century Roman fort into a medieval trade centre and bishopric on the Rhine outlet of Lake Constance. The Council of Constance (1414–1418) ended the Western Schism here and briefly made the lakeside city a European diplomatic stage. Proximity to neutral Switzerland spared the old town heavy wartime bombing, so Münster, harbour warehouses, and alley fabric survive beside a postwar university campus.

Island Mainau

Mainau occupies an island in Lake Constance developed as a botanical garden and visitor estate under the Bernadotte family. Seasonal flower displays, mature trees, Mediterranean planting. A butterfly house, palace, church, play areas and lake paths cover a compact but carefully managed landscape. Blooms vary through the year, while crowds, ferry links and long walking routes shape the visit.

Schloss Mainau

A Baroque palace and church stand at the upper center of Mainau Island's gardens. The Teutonic Order built the residence in the eighteenth century, and parts remain associated with the Bernadotte family while other rooms support exhibitions, dining, or events. Exterior terraces connect the architecture with lake views and planting, but access to private or event spaces may be limited.

Mainau Kinderland - Insel Mainau

Mainau Kinderland provides outdoor play areas within the island's larger botanical estate. Climbing structures, water play, rafts, animal encounters and age-specific zones operate beside gardens and paths intended for family use. Weather, wet clothing and maintenance closures affect some facilities, while the play areas remain secondary to Mainau's plant collections.

Historische Altstadt Meersburg am Bodensee

Meersburg Altstadt, or the Old Town of Meersburg, is a destination that transports visitors back to medieval times. With its cobblestone streets and architecture, this area offers views of Lake Constance and the Alps. As visitors wander through quaint shops and cozy cafes, find spots to relax and soak in the atmosphere.

Kaiserbrunnen

Kaiserbrunnen on Konstanz's harbour recalls the city's role in Imperial diets and the medieval church council era through sculpted rulers and allegories. The fountain sits near the lake edge as a popular meeting spot. Bronze details reward a closer look between ferry traffic and the skyline.

Historische Hafenuhr

Historische Hafenuhr is the historic harbour clock on Konstanz's lakefront, a timekeeper facing the piers and Alpine horizon across the water. The instrument belongs to the working portscape rather than a museum interior. Benches nearby invite a pause between Old Town and boat landings.

Imperia

Peter Lenk's Imperia rotates slowly at the end of Konstanz's harbor pier. The nine-metre figure, installed in 1993, holds small caricatures of a pope and emperor while referring satirically to the Council of Constance and a literary character from Balzac. Nudity, exaggerated scale, and political mockery explain the work's controversy more accurately than treating it as a conventional historical heroine.

Stadtgarten

Stadtgarten Konstanz runs along the lakeshore with flower beds, lawns, and paths between the old town and harbour. Summer planting and water views make it a favoured strolling ground for residents and day visitors. The park ties civic greenery directly to the Bodensee promenade.

Münster Unserer Lieben Frau

Konstanz Minster developed over centuries on the high ground of the old town and served as the cathedral of a major diocese until 1821. Romanesque structure, Gothic additions, chapels, choir stalls, sculpture, crypt spaces and a climbable tower reflect its long ecclesiastical role. Events of the Council of Constance connect the church with European religious politics, while Catholic worship continues.

Bodensee-Therme Konstanz

Bodensee-Therme Konstanz is a modern spa and sports-pool complex near the lake with about 3,000 square metres of water surface. Swim lanes, warm baths, and sauna areas serve day visitors and residents. The facility extends Konstanz's leisure offer beyond the Old Town streets.
