Explore Saarbrücken

A Brief History

Saarbrücken grew from a medieval bridgehead and castle on the Saar River into the seat of the Counts of Nassau-Saarbrücken. In the eighteenth century, architect Friedrich Joachim Stengel reshaped the residence into a cohesive Baroque ensemble of palace, churches, and civic squares. Coal and steel later industrialised the Saar basin, and the city's border location left it contested between France and Germany through the twentieth century until Saarland joined West Germany in 1957. Today visitors encounter that layered past at Saarbrücken Castle, the Baroque Basilica of St. Johann, and the nearby UNESCO-listed Völklingen Ironworks.

Völklingen Ironworks

Völklingen Ironworks, located about twelve kilometers from Saarbrücken, is a historic industrial site that has been preserved in its original state. This UNESCO World Heritage Site offers a 5km-long trail for visitors to explore the extensive iron and steel production facilities dating back to the 19th century. The ironworks ceased operations in 1986 and has since been transformed into an industrial monument and science museum.

Free

Saarbrücken Castle

Nestled in the heart of Saarbrücken, Saarbrücken Castle is a 18th-century palace that offers visitors a glimpse into the region's rich history. This magnificent structure, originally established in the medieval era and later transformed by architect Friedrich Joachim Stengel into its Baroque style, boasts an intriguing network of underground tunnels and chambers that have been opened to the public as part of the Saar Historic Museum.

€15 (theatre ticket)

Saarland State Theatre

Nestled in the vibrant heart of Saarbrücken, the Saarland State Theatre stands as a beacon of cultural richness and artistic expression. This magnificent venue is not just a theater; it’s a hub for performing arts enthusiasts, showcasing an eclectic mix of plays, musicals, ballet performances, and operas. The theatre's schedule is brimming with diverse events that cater to all tastes—from captivating concerts to engaging workshops led by talented dancers from its esteemed ballet troupe.

Free

Basilica of St. John the Baptist

The Basilica of St. John the Baptist, also known as Saarbrucken Basilica, is a Baroque Catholic church located in St. John's market in Saarbrucken, Germany. Built by architect Friedrich Joachim Stengel between 1754 and 1758, it suffered damage during Allied bombing raids but has since been meticulously reconstructed to its original grandeur.

Free

Rathaus St. Johann

Rathaus St. Johann is a neo-Gothic town hall located in Saarbrucken, boasting intricate sculptures depicting ancient crafts and a captivating carillon that plays twice daily. Designed by Georg Von Hauberrisser, the building's Gothic Revival architecture features ornate traceried windows, pinnacles, and other elaborate decorations on its facade.

€20 (event ticket)

Saarlandhalle

Nestled on the outskirts of Saarbrücken, the Saarlandhalle opened its doors in 1967 after a year and a half of construction. This versatile venue is surrounded by greenery and boasts a large main hall along with eight smaller rooms, making it an ideal location for a variety of events including cultural shows, sports competitions, conferences, and exhibitions. Visitors can enjoy not only the spacious seating and cozy atmosphere but also easy access from nearby transport links.

€15

Calypso Aquatic Center and Sauna

Calypso is a spa facility that offers both indoor and outdoor swimming pools, as well as a range of children's areas, saunas, and slides. It is an excellent destination for families or groups of friends looking to have some fun. There are five water slides to choose from, along with cocktail bars, jacuzzis, and restaurants. Additionally, there are two dedicated kids' areas available at an affordable price.

€20 (alpaca walk)

Saar alpaca GbR

Saar Historical Museum, located next to Saarbrucken Castle, is a deceivingly large museum. The ground level features a narrow building from 1988, concealing historic underground chambers and tunnels excavated in the 1990s and 2000s. On the upper floors, visitors can explore the last 300 years of Saarland through art, design, and everyday items. The museum also occupies the castle's basement and a modern complex next door.

€15

Saarbrücken Zoo

Saarbrücken Zoo is a popular attraction known for its unique features that draw numerous visitors each year. The zoo is home to over 1,000 animals from 160 different species, showcasing creatures from the African savannah, rainforests of Africa and Amazon, South American coast, Patagonia, and Southeast Asia-Oceania. Visitors can explore the diverse accommodations nearby and enjoy attractions such as petting enclosures, pony rides, and night tours.

Free

Wildpark

Nestled in the St. Johann forest, Wildpark Saarbrucken spans 17 hectares and has been a home to around 25 species and nearly 150 animals and birds since its establishment in 1929. Visitors can encounter various creatures such as birds of prey, red deer, fallow deer, peacocks, and ibex while strolling through the park's educational paths. The well-maintained enclosures provide an up-close experience with friendly wildlife.
