Destination Guide — Southern Germany

Explore Munich

A Brief History

Munich developed from a 12th-century market settlement under the Wittelsbach dynasty into the capital of Bavaria, with residence palaces, churches, and civic gates expanded through Renaissance and Baroque patronage. The 19th century added museums, boulevards, and the Oktoberfest tradition tied to a royal wedding celebration of 1810. Wartime destruction and postwar reconstruction reshaped parts of the center while preserving major dynastic monuments.

Top Sights & Activities

€10

Nymphenburg Palace

Nymphenburg Palace was begun in 1664 as a summer residence west of Munich and expanded into a Baroque complex with pavilions and canals. State rooms, the Gallery of Beauties, and park buildings trace Wittelsbach court life. The grounds remain open parkland popular for walks and museum visits.

Free

Marienplatz and the New Town Hall

Marienplatz has been Munich's central square since the city's founding and is dominated by the Neo-Gothic New Town Hall completed in 1909. The Glockenspiel performs daily for crowds in the square below. Markets, festivals, and orientation for the old town still concentrate here.

€10

Residenzmuseum (Munich Residence)

The Munich Residence was the seat of Bavarian dukes, electors, and kings from the 16th to the 19th century and now houses a museum of state apartments and treasuries. Rooms span Renaissance, Baroque, and Rococo decoration with collections of porcelain and regalia. The complex forms the core of the palace district east of Odeonsplatz.

Free

St. Peter's Church (Peterskirche)

St Peter's Church, called Alter Peter, stands on the oldest parish site in Munich with a tower climb offering views over the center. The present building mixes Gothic and Baroque elements from centuries of rebuilding. It remains an active parish church steps from Marienplatz.

Free

English Garden (Englischer Garten)

The English Garden was created from 1789 as a public landscape park north of the old walls, among the largest city parks in Europe. Meadows, streams, and the Chinese Tower beer garden draw residents and visitors year-round. It records how Munich expanded beyond its medieval limits for recreation.

€12

BMW Museum and BMW Welt

BMW Museum and BMW Welt stand beside the Olympic Park in the north of Munich, presenting the company's vehicles, design, and engineering history. The bowl-shaped museum building and adjacent delivery center opened in the 2000s. The site connects Munich with postwar automotive industry and corporate architecture.

Free

Sendlinger Tor

Sendlinger Tor is a surviving city gate of the late medieval fortifications, marking the southern entrance to the walled town. The twin towers and arch still frame a busy traffic axis into the center. It remains a reference point in Munich's historic street network south of Marienplatz.

Free

Asamkirche

Asamkirche, dedicated to St Johann Nepomuk, was built in 1733–1746 by the Asam brothers as a private church attached to their house. Ceiling frescoes, sculpture, and gilded decoration fill a narrow plot on Sendlinger Straße. The church is a key example of Bavarian Baroque patronage in the dense old town.

€25

Allianz Arena

Allianz Arena opened in 2005 in Fröttmaning as the home stadium of FC Bayern Munich and TSV 1860 München, seating about 75,000. Its inflated facade panels can be lit in team colors. Stadium tours and a museum explain the building's design and matchday operations.

Free (dome lift €5)

New Town Hall

The New Town Hall, located at the heart of Munich's historic center in Marienplatz, is a neo-Gothic building with a towering spire offering panoramic city views. The Glockenspiel housed within the town hall comes to life daily with moving figurines and chimes, adding to the vibrant atmosphere of Marienplatz.

Free

Alter Hof

In the heart of Munich, Germany stands the Alter Hof, a historic castle with a rich legacy. Constructed between 1253 and 1255 as the first fortified residence for the Wittelsbachs, it served as their imperial residence until the 15th century. The castle features five wings and is adorned with wall inscriptions from different historical eras and an impressive collection of antiques.

Free

Hofbräuhaus München

The Hofbräuhaus in Munich is one of the most famous beer halls in the world, originally founded in 1589 as the royal brewery of the Kingdom of Bavaria. The vast, historic hall features traditional Bavarian music, long communal tables, and hearty local cuisine. It remains a central institution of Munich's beer culture and a popular gathering place.

€15

Bavarian State Opera

The Bavarian State Opera, also known as the Bayerische Staatsoper, is a prestigious opera company and theater situated in Munich, Germany. Established in 1653, it is one of the oldest opera companies in Europe and is housed within the neoclassical-style National Theater. The opera house is celebrated for its top-notch productions, exceptional vocalists, and impressive orchestra.

Free

Hofgarten

Hofgarten is a serene and elegant city park located near the Munich Residenz, offering a peaceful retreat in the midst of the bustling city. Originally designed as the private court garden for Bavarian rulers in the 17th century, it features formal lawns, well-maintained flower beds, and a central Temple of Diana. The park's Renaissance-style layout includes symmetrical paths and an ornamental pavilion at its center.

Free

Feldherrnhalle

Feldherrnhalle is a 19th-century Italianate monument located in Odeonsplatz, near the Residenz. It was built to house statues of Bavarian generals and is infamous for its association with Hitler's 1923 Beer Hall Putsch, a pivotal moment in Nazi history. The site also features the nearby Theatinerkirche, a Roman Baroque church.

Free

Theatine Church

The Theatine Church, also known as the Church of St. Cajetan, is a Catholic church located on Odeon Square in Munich. Its striking yellow facade and ornate white stucco interior make it a for travellers to the city. Construction began in 1663 to celebrate the birth of Maximilien-Emmanuel, future monarch, and was completed in 1690 with the addition of the Elector's family tomb and two towers.

Free (tower climb €8)

Frauenkirche

Frauenkirche, also known as the Church of Our Lady, is a restored Gothic church in Munich with two towers that top its structure. The church is famous for its legendary Devil's footprint, which has several variations of the story behind it. One version tells how the Prince of Hell helped the architect build the church and was to collect his soul as a reward after completion. However, the clever master builder outwitted the devil.

Free (crypt €3)

St. Michael München

St. Michael's Church in Munich is the largest Renaissance church north of the Alps, built by Duke William V of Bavaria in the late 16th century. The church features a massive barrel-vaulted ceiling and an ornate facade adorned with statues of the Wittelsbach rulers. Its crypt contains the tombs of several Bavarian monarchs, including King Ludwig II.

Free

Wittelsbacher Brunnen

The Old Botanical Garden, also known as the Botanischer Garten Munchen, is a serene oasis nestled in the heart of Munich. Originally established as a botanical garden in the 19th century, it now serves as a popular gathering place for both locals and tourists. The park boasts majestic trees, well-maintained flower beds, and the charming Neptune Fountain. travellers can take pleasure in a leisurely stroll through this lush green space or relax at the cafe and beer garden.

Free

Königsplatz

Königsplatz is a historic square in Munich, featuring grand neo-classical buildings such as the Propylaen gateway and two temple-like museums. Originally designed by Leo von Klenze and later expanded by the Nazis, the square now offers a peaceful and green space for travellers to enjoy concerts and open-air cinema during the summer. Flanked by neoclassical structures housing Munich's antiquities, Königsplatz also serves as a starting point for walking tours through the city.

€25

Deutsches Theater

Deutsches Theater, a historic late-19th century venue in Munich, offers a modern interior and hosts a variety of performances including big-name global musicals, plays, operas, dance shows, and more. The theater is rooted in history since its opening in 1896. English performances are also featured. travellers can enjoy the vibrant local scene by visiting nearby beer gardens or beer halls for music and entertainment.

Free

Viktualienmarkt

Viktualienmarkt is a lively and vibrant 200-year-old farmers' market in Munich, offering a wide variety of regional culinary delights. With over 100 stalls selling everything from fresh produce to artisanal cheeses and meats, it's a paradise for food lovers and anyone interested in experiencing Bavarian flavors. Families can enjoy the diverse array of food options and the open-air ambiance.

Free

Gärtnerplatz

Gärtnerplatz, originally constructed in 1860 as a city square, has evolved into Munich's trendiest area. With its Mediterranean ambiance, it's the perfect spot for people-watching and mingling with locals. The surrounding streets are adorned with boutique shops and various dining options. In the evening, the square becomes a hub for socializing before travellers head to nearby bars and clubs. This charming location features a fountain, flower-filled gardens, and ample seating for relaxation.

Free

Isar Gate

The Isartor (Isar Gate) is one of the four original main gates of the medieval city wall of Munich. It is the only gate that has preserved its main tower and flanking towers. The gate features a large fresco depicting the victorious return of Emperor Louis the Bavarian after the Battle of Mühldorf. Today, it houses the Valentin Karlstadt Musäum.

€10

Japanese Teahouse Kanshoan

Japanese Teahouse Kanshoan is a traditional teahouse located on a small island within a beautifully landscaped Japanese-style garden. travellers can witness and participate in traditional tea ceremonies held at the teahouse during certain months. The teahouse is accessible via a narrow bridge and offers a serene setting surrounded by trees, bushes, and a pond with ducks.

Free

Monopteros im Englischen Garten

In the heart of Munich's Englischer Garten lies the Monopteros, a circular replica Greek temple built in the 19th century by King Ludwig I. Perched atop a 49-foot hill, this Grecian-style bandstand offers views of central Munich and its landmarks. While the garden is known for activities like river surfing and sunbathing, the Monopteros provides a serene spot to watch the sunset over Munich's skyline.

Free

Siegestor

Siegestor, a 19th-century triumphal arch in Munich, is reminiscent of the Brandenburg Gate and was commissioned by King Ludwig as a tribute to victory. Resembling the Arch of Constantine, it suffered significant damage during World War II but has been partially restored over a 30-year period. The arch features a bronze sculpture of Bavaria with four lions and stands at the north end of Ludwigstrasse.

Free

Münchner Freiheit

Münchner Freiheit is a prominent public square located in the Schwabing district of Munich. It is a lively urban hub, surrounded by cafes, restaurants, and boutiques, reflecting the area's bohemian history. The square is also a major transportation node and hosts a popular Christmas market during the holiday season.

Free

Angel of Peace

The Angel of Peace, also known as Friedensengel, is a significant monument in Munich commemorating 25 years of peace following the Franco-Prussian War. This landmark features a golden angel atop a grand Corinthian column and an open square temple. travellers can enjoy panoramic views from the Prinzregent-Luitpold-Terrasse and witness sunsets over Munich's historical center.

Free

Olympiapark München

Olympiapark München is a sprawling complex that was built for the 1972 Olympic Games and has since become one of Munich's most popular attractions. The park features structures such as the Olympic Stadium, Olympic Hall, and the towering Olympic Tower with its observation deck. travellers can enjoy guided tours, concerts, and a variety of sports activities within the 850,000 square meter park.

€20

Hellabrunn Zoo

Hellabrunn Zoo, located on the banks of the river Isar, is a nature reserve-style zoo that houses over 750 species from around the world. The zoo covers 89 acres and was the first to group animals based on their place of origin. With more than 1,900 animals living in large open spaces that replicate their native habitats, Hellabrunn Zoo provides a humane environment for its inhabitants.

Free

Flaucher

Flaucher is a delightful green oasis nestled along the Isar River in Munich, perfect for those seeking relaxation and outdoor fun. This popular park features expansive lawns, scenic waterfront paths, and designated grilling areas that invite travellers to enjoy a sunny day with friends or family. The atmosphere here is vibrant during summer months, as sunbathers soak up rays while children splash in shallow waters and families gather for picnics.

€7

Neues Schloss Schleißheim

Neues Schloss Schleißheim, located in Oberschleissheim, north of Munich, is a German palace complex comprising three palaces: Old Schleissheim Palace, New Schleissheim Palace, and Lustheim Palace. The extensive grounds offer a great family day out with ornamental canals where Venetian gondolas can be found during the summer months. The bier garten is a favorite spot for travellers.

Free

Isar

The Isar River in Munich offers a safe and scenic route for cycling, with attractions such as the Deutsches Museum, Mullersches Volksbad public swimming pool and spa, and the Eisbach wave where surfers showcase their skills. The riverbank is a popular spot for barbecuing from Flaucher to Oberfohring, attracting sun worshippers and swimmers who enjoy evening barbecues by the water.

€6

Botanical Garden Munich-Nymphenburg

The Botanical Garden Munich-Nymphenburg is a sprawling 52-acre complex that features a diverse range of themed gardens, greenhouses, and ponds inhabited by turtles. travellers can also enjoy the amenities of a cafe and gift shop on the premises. Established in 1914, this botanical garden and arboretum is home to an impressive collection of over 19,000 plant species and subspecies. The garden serves as a vital sanctuary for rare plants and European bee species.

Free

Schlosspark Nymphenburg

The Nymphenburg Palace Park is a vast, meticulously landscaped garden complex in Munich, originally designed in the French formal style and later adapted to an English landscape garden. The park features a grand canal, numerous ornate pavilions (such as the Amalienburg and Badenburg), and extensive wooded areas, providing a serene retreat within the city.
