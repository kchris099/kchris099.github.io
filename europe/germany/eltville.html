Explore Eltville

A Brief History

Eltville am Rhein developed as a wine town and electoral Mainz stronghold from the Middle Ages, with vineyards terraced on the slopes of the Rheingau. Its riverside castle, Gothic parish church, and timber-framed old town preserve the compact fabric of a walled Rhine settlement tied to archbishopric administration and viticulture. The town became a center of Riesling production and river trade, with guild houses and market lanes reflecting prosperous burgher life.

Eltville Castle

Eltville Castle stands on the Rhine bank and served the archbishops and electors of Mainz from the 14th century as a residence within the Rheingau wine region. A tower, courtyards, and riverfront walls survive from successive building phases. The site now hosts events and recalls electoral rule in a compact wine town.

Free

Parish Church of Saints Peter and Paul

The parish church of Saints Peter and Paul rises above the old town with a Gothic choir and later alterations. It anchored religious life in an electoral Mainz settlement known for viticulture and river trade. The church links the castle quarter with the surrounding timber-framed streets and market lanes.

Free

Historische Altstadt Eltville

Eltville's old town preserves narrow lanes, half-timbered houses, and wine taverns within the former wall circuit on the Rhine. Guild signs and cellar doors recall the Riesling and Pinot Noir production of the surrounding hills. Walking the center shows how river traffic, viticulture, and civic life shaped a small Rheingau town.
