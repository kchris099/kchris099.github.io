Destination Guide — Southern Germany

Explore Berchtesgaden

A Brief History

Governed for centuries by powerful Prince-Provosts enriched by extensive salt mining, Berchtesgaden maintained deep autonomy in the Bavarian Alps. Its dark 20th-century association with the Third Reich eventually gave way to a modern era focused on national park preservation. The synthesis of these diverse historical influences has produced a notably distinct and resilient civic character.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

Kehlsteinhaus

Perched at an elevation of 1834m, The Eagle's Nest was originally built as a mountain retreat for Hitler and has since been repurposed into a restaurant with panoramic views. Accessible only by a special shuttle bus from the Kehlsteinhaus bus station, this historic site boasts a rich World War II history and was once used by top officials of the Third Reich regime.

Free

Almbachklamm

Almbachklamm is a picturesque gorge near the village of Marktschellenberg, offering a 2-kilometer hike through natural scenery. The trail features catwalks, bridges, and narrow paths alongside a beautiful green-blue stream with numerous waterfalls. While the hike provides impressive viewpoints and is perfect for hot days, it's important to note that there are sections without guardrails.

Free

Rossfeld Panorama Strasse

The Rossfeld Panorama Strasse is a picturesque mountain road that spans 15.4 kilometers along the high alpine terrain, offering views of the Berchtesgaden Alps, Dachstein Massif, and Salzkammergut. It is the highest panoramic road in Germany, reaching an altitude of 1,600 meters with winding hairpin turns.

Free

Salt Mine Berchtesgaden

Salt Mine Berchtesgaden offers a family-friendly tour of a 1500s salt mine, featuring miners' slides, a salt lake, and an illuminated grotto. Once owned by Berchtesgaden's princely rulers, the mine now welcomes travellers to experience its rich history. The hour-long tour includes a ride on a miniature train deep into the mountain, where an enormous chamber reveals the mining process.

Free

Berghof

Berghof, once the mountain retreat of Adolf Hitler, was a beautiful chalet that hosted not only Hitler but also his companion Eva Braun, her friends, and the families of Nazi officials. Although the original Berghof no longer stands due to bombing during World War II, travellers can stay at Hotel zum Turken nearby and explore underground bunkers that once connected to Hitler's residence.

Free

Alte Saline

Alte Saline, also known as the Bad Reichenhaller Saltmuseum, is a 19th-century industrial saltworks located in Bad Reichenhall. It played a significant role in the town's growth and prosperity from ancient Celtic times to the present day. The site features an underground grotto, a salt museum, and a gift shop offering various flavors of kitchen salt and bath salts.

Free

Gradierhaus

The Gradierhaus is a tranquil location with a serene atmosphere, ideal for unwinding and admiring the saline. It boasts a picturesque garden for leisurely strolls. While it caters predominantly to an older demographic and may require more amenities in the playground, this deliberate approach ensures a peaceful environment conducive to relaxation.

Free

RupertusTherme

Rupertus Therme in Bad Reichenhall is a modern health and fitness complex located at the Rupertuspark, offering views of the Alpine scenery. The facility features a large outdoor area with warm brine thermal pools, including a unique brine grotto. travellers can enjoy saunas, massage therapy, and a child-friendly pool with flumes. For adults seeking tranquility, there is an exclusive area without kids for peaceful relaxation.

Free

Ramsau Malerwinkel

Ramsau Malerwinkel is a hidden gem that beckons travelers with its scenery, especially during the summer months. Nestled in a charming village, this picturesque spot features crystal-clear waters flowing alongside a quaint chapel, creating an idyllic setting for relaxation and reflection. While it may be just a small bus stop with a bridge lacking handrails, the views make it worth your while.

Free

Pilgrimage Church of St. Bartholomew

Saint Bartholomew's Church, situated near Konigsee Lake in Bavaria, is a picturesque pilgrimage church dating back to 1697. The church boasts red onion-domed towers set against the backdrop of the Bavarian Alps, creating a sight. Inside, travellers can admire intricate woodwork, ornate altars, and beautiful stained glass windows in a peaceful atmosphere.

Free

Königssee

Königssee is a large, clear lake known for its beauty and St. Bartholomew church located on a peninsula. Surrounded by the picturesque hiking trails of the Berchtesgaden Alps, this destination offers a serene escape in nature. The area also features family-run hotels offering cozy rooms and delicious Bavarian and international cuisine.

Free

Malerwinkl

Malerwinkel is a serene forest area with walking trails and a popular viewing spot that offers views of Königssee lake. The trail leads to the Malerwinkel viewpoint, providing an excellent vantage point overlooking the lake and extending all the way to Sankt Bartholoma. While it may be crowded during peak season, the short 20-minute trek from the parking lot makes it accessible for most travellers.

Free

Nationalpark Berchtesgaden

Nationalpark Berchtesgaden is a alpine national park in Germany, known for its numerous trails and lakeside Church of Saint Bartholomew. One of the highlights is Lake Hintersee, with its crystal-clear turquoise waters and picturesque mountain backdrop. The area also offers a scenic circular hiking trail and unique boulder formations along the shore.
