Explore Berchtesgaden

A Brief History

Berchtesgaden was established in the twelfth century around an Augustinian monastery and grew wealthy through salt mining. It became an independent prince-provostry within the Holy Roman Empire before being annexed by Bavaria in 1810. The region's dramatic alpine scenery attracted Bavarian royalty and, later, tourists. Today, Berchtesgaden is known for its historic salt mines, the Watzmann mountain, and the pristine Konigssee.

Top Sights & Activities

Free (elevator round trip €35)

Kehlsteinhaus

Perched at an elevation of 1834m, The Eagle's Nest was originally built as a mountain retreat for Hitler and has since been repurposed into a restaurant with panoramic views. Accessible only by a special shuttle bus from the Kehlsteinhaus bus station, this historic site has a rich World War II history and was once used by top officials of the Third Reich regime.

Free

Almbachklamm

Almbachklamm is a visitor site in Berchtesgaden, Germany. Almbachklamm is a gorge near the village of Marktschellenberg, offering a 2-kilometer hike through natural scenery. While the hike provides viewpoints and is perfect for hot days, it's important to note that there are sections without guardrails. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

€10

Rossfeld Panorama Strasse

The Rossfeld Panorama Strasse is a mountain road that spans 15.4 kilometers along the high alpine terrain, offering views of the Berchtesgaden Alps, Dachstein Massif, and Salzkammergut. It is the highest panoramic road in Germany, reaching an altitude of 1,600 meters with winding hairpin turns.

Free

Salt Mine Berchtesgaden

Salt Mine Berchtesgaden offers a family-friendly tour of a 1500s salt mine, featuring miners' slides, a salt lake, and an illuminated grotto. Once owned by Berchtesgaden's princely rulers, the mine now welcomes travellers to experience its rich history. The hour-long tour includes a ride on a miniature train deep into the mountain, where an enormous chamber reveals the mining process.

Free

Berghof

Berghof, once the mountain retreat of Adolf Hitler, was a chalet that hosted not only Hitler but also his companion Eva Braun, her friends, and the families of Nazi officials. Although the original Berghof no longer stands due to bombing during World War II, travellers can stay at Hotel zum Turken nearby and explore underground bunkers that once connected to Hitler's residence.

Free

Alte Saline

Alte Saline, also known as the Bad Reichenhaller Saltmuseum, is a visitor site in Berchtesgaden, Germany. Alte Saline, also known as the Bad Reichenhaller Saltmuseum, is a 19th-century industrial saltworks located in Bad Reichenhall. It played a significant role in the town's growth and prosperity from ancient Celtic times to the present day. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Free

Gradierhaus

Gradierhaus is a visitor site in Berchtesgaden, Germany. It has a garden for leisurely strolls. While it caters predominantly to an older demographic and may require more amenities in the playground, this deliberate approach ensures a peaceful environment conducive to relaxation. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Free

RupertusTherme

RupertusTherme is a visitor site in Berchtesgaden, Germany. Rupertus Therme in Bad Reichenhall is a modern health and fitness complex located at the Rupertuspark, offering views of the Alpine scenery. Travellers can enjoy saunas, massage therapy, and a child-friendly pool with flumes. For adults seeking tranquility, there is an exclusive area without kids for peaceful relaxation. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Free

Ramsau Malerwinkel

Ramsau Malerwinkel is a visitor site in Berchtesgaden, Germany. While it may be just a small bus stop with a bridge lacking handrails, the views make it worth visitor while. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest. Ramsau Malerwinkel remains part of the public visitor circuit in Berchtesgaden.

Free

Pilgrimage Church of St. Bartholomew

Saint Bartholomew's Church, situated near Konigsee Lake in Bavaria, is a pilgrimage church dating back to 1697. The church has red onion-domed towers set against the backdrop of the Bavarian Alps, creating a sight. Inside, travellers can admire detailed woodwork, ornate altars, and stained glass windows in a peaceful atmosphere.

Free

Königssee

Königssee is a visitor site in Berchtesgaden, Germany. Bartholomew church located on a peninsula. Surrounded by the hiking trails of the Berchtesgaden Alps, this destination offers a quiet shoreline in nature. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Malerwinkl

Malerwinkl is a visitor site in Berchtesgaden, Germany. The trail leads to the Malerwinkel viewpoint, providing an excellent vantage point overlooking the lake and extending all the way to Sankt Bartholoma. While it may be crowded during peak season, the short 20-minute trek from the parking lot makes it accessible for most travellers. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Free

Nationalpark Berchtesgaden

Nationalpark Berchtesgaden is a alpine national park in Germany, known for its numerous trails and lakeside Church of Saint Bartholomew. One of the highlights is Lake Hintersee, with its clear turquoise waters and mountain backdrop. The area also offers a scenic circular hiking trail and unique boulder formations along the shore.
