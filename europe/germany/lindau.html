Lindau

Lindau occupies an island and lakeshore on the eastern end of Lake Constance where Bavaria meets Austria and Switzerland. Its harbor entrance carries a Bavarian lion and lighthouse. Medieval lanes and a Gothic town hall reflect membership in the Holy Roman Empire. Lindau joined Bavaria in 1802. Steamers and rail links connected the town to regional markets in the 19th century. Today it is a county seat and ferry port.

Minster of Our Lady (Münster Unserer Lieben Frau)

Lindau's Minster of Our Lady stands on earlier foundations from around 810 but was rebuilt in Baroque form between 1748 and 1752 after a town fire, then restored again after a 1922 blaze. The parish church anchors the island's Catholic skyline. Stuccoed interiors reflect those successive rebuilds.

02 AdmissionFree

Bavarian Lion Statue

A six-metre stone lion faces Lake Constance from the end of Lindau's harbor entrance. Sculptor Johann von Halbig created the Bavarian emblem in the 1850s, pairing it with the new lighthouse across the channel after the railway reached the island. The monument marks state authority and harbor identity through scale and position, while boats pass directly between the two structures.

03 AdmissionFree

Seepromenade

Lindau's Seepromenade follows the harbour edge of the island town on Lake Constance, with views to the Austrian and Swiss Alps on clear days. Cafés and boat traffic line the quay beside the lion statue and lighthouse. The walk is the island's main waterfront circuit.

Lindau Lighthouse

Lindau's nineteenth-century lighthouse stands opposite the Bavarian Lion at the harbor mouth. A clock is set into the tower, and an interior stair may open seasonally toward views of the island town, lake, and Alps. Although often called Germany's southernmost lighthouse, its principal story is the planned harbor entrance created after Bavarian railway and shipping development.

Lindau City Museum (Stadtmuseum)

Lindau's Stadtmuseum interprets the island city's art, civic life, trade and regional history through collections assembled over many years. Paintings, decorative objects and local material connect the museum with Lake Constance and Lindau's former role as a free imperial city, and its traditional home, the Baroque Haus zum Cavazzen, is itself an important part of the city's architectural setting.

06 AdmissionFree

Lake Constance

Lake Constance, the Bodensee, washes Lindau's island shores at the meeting of Germany, Austria, and Switzerland. Ferries, sailing craft, and promenades use the water as a shared regional space. Alpine ridges often close the southern horizon beyond the open lake.
