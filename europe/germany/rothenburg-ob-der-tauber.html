Explore Rothenburg ob der Tauber

A Brief History

Rothenburg ob der Tauber became a free imperial city on a plateau above the Tauber River in Franconia. Its intact walls and towers enclose a market square with the Renaissance town hall and St. Jakob's Church, which holds a carved altarpiece by Tilman Riemenschneider. The Thirty Years' War halted growth, helping preserve the medieval streetscape. Today the town is a county seat in Bavaria along the Romantic Road.

Altstadt (Old Town)

Altstadt (Old Town) is a visitor site in Rothenburg ob der Tauber, Germany. The Altstadt of Celle is known for its concentration of beautifully preserved half-timbered (Fachwerk) houses. The pedestrianized streets offer a atmosphere filled with shops and cafes. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Mittelalterliches Kriminalmuseum (Medieval Crime Museum)

Mittelalterliches Kriminalmuseum (Medieval Crime Museum) is a museum in Rothenburg ob der Tauber, Germany. The top things to do in Rothenburg Ob Der Tauber don’t come more macabre than this! Located in the historic former St. John’s Commandery, this building is a piece of history itself and was built between 1393 and 1410. Permanent galleries and temporary displays are open on posted hours.

Free

Marktplatz

Marktplatz (Market square) is the centre of town and is surrounded by buildings. Many historic events have taken place here. The Town Hall is here, timber-framed houses and the Georgsbrunnen (George Fountain) which has been here since 1446. Marktplatz is open to visitors in Rothenburg ob der Tauber, Germany, during posted hours.

€5

Deutches Weihnachtsmuseum (Christmas Museum)

Things to see in Rothenburg Ob Der Tauber don’t get more festive than this! The Deutches Weihnachtsmuseum is the ultimate place to visit for anyone who loves Christmas. Käthe Wohlfahrt Weihnachtsdorf is a popular Christmas chain of stores that open year round.

Free

Rathausturm (Town Hall Tower)

Rathausturm (Town Hall Tower) is a visitor site in Rothenburg ob der Tauber, Germany. It opened or was completed around 1578. Located on Marktplatz, Rothenburg's Town Hall (Rathaus) has two parts. It started being Built in Gothic style in the 14th century before being completed with a Renaissance building in 1578. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.
