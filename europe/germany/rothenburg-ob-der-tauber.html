Explore Rothenburg ob der Tauber

A Brief History

Rothenburg ob der Tauber is globally renowned as the ultimate, idyllic fairytale German medieval town. Reaching the peak of its immense economic power in the 15th century as a Free Imperial City, its sudden economic decline during the devastating Thirty Years' War inadvertently saved it from modern architectural development, freezing its captivating aesthetics in time. Today, its completely intact medieval defensive walls, plunging cobblestone alleys, and enchanting, colorful half-timbered houses make it the undisputed crown jewel of Franconian cultural history.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

Altstadt (Old Town)

Far and away the number 1 thing you will want to pick from the answers to what to do Rothenburg Ob Der Tauber is to explore the Old Town. It is just stunning! Most items in this list of Rothenburg places to visit are in the Old Town so you’ll see a big chunk of it just by walking between them.

€12

Mittelalterliches Kriminalmuseum (Medieval Crime Museum)

The top things to do in Rothenburg Ob Der Tauber don’t come more macabre than this! Located in the historic former St. John’s Commandery, this building is a piece of history itself and was built between 1393 and 1410.

Varies

Marktplatz

Marktplatz (Market square) is the centre of town and is surrounded by beautiful buildings. Many historic events have taken place here. The Town Hall is here, timber-framed houses and the Georgsbrunnen (George Fountain) which has been here since 1446.

€12

Deutches Weihnachtsmuseum (Christmas Museum)

Things to see in Rothenburg Ob Der Tauber don’t get more festive than this! The Deutches Weihnachtsmuseum is the ultimate place to visit for anyone who loves Christmas. Käthe Wohlfahrt Weihnachtsdorf is a popular Christmas chain of stores that open year round.

Varies

Rathausturm (Town Hall Tower)

Located on Marktplatz, Rothenburg’s Town Hall (Rathaus) has two parts. It started being Built in Gothic style in the 14th century before being completed with a Renaissance building in 1578. It makes for quite a sight.
