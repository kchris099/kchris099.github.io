Explore Lake Constance

A Brief History

Lake Constance, or Bodensee, borders Germany, Austria, and Switzerland and was shaped by glacial action after the last ice age. Roman and medieval settlements traded along its shores. The Council of Constance from 1414 to 1418 addressed church reform in the lakeside city. Steam navigation began in the 19th century, linking vineyards, orchards, and textile towns. Reichenau Island's early medieval monastery is a UNESCO World Heritage site.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

Lindau Old Town

Located on an island in Lake Constance connected by bridges, Lindau's Old Town has a rich history of medieval architecture and history. As visitors stroll through its streets, visitors'll discover well known landmarks such as the Lindau Lighthouse and busy squares filled with cafes and shops. The waterfront views add an extra layer of allure.

€23

Mainau Island

Located on Lake Constance, Mainau Island is known as the Island of Flowers. Here, visitors find gardens and landscapes that change with the seasons. The expansive array of floral displays, including rare and exotic plants, makes it a must-visit for nature lovers.

Free

Lake Constance Boat Tour

Taking a boat tour across Lake Constance is an excellent way to the area's charm. Visitors'll gain unique perspectives of the surrounding landscapes and catch glimpses of lakeside towns that dot the coastline. The tours offer relaxation as well as educational insights into the lake's rich history and geography.

€13

Meersburg Castle

Located in the town of Meersburg on the shores of Lake Constance, Meersburg Castle is a example of medieval architecture. As visitors approach the castle, the towering walls and ancient towers immediately capture visitor imagination, transporting visitors back in time. Inside, visitors are greeted with well-preserved interiors that showcase a rich collection of medieval artifacts, including armor, weapons, and period furniture.

Free

Reichenau Abbey

Reichenau Abbey is located on Reichenau Island, a UNESCO World Heritage site. This well-preserved monastery offers a fascinating look into early medieval monastic life and architecture. It’s a place of historical significance, dating back to the Carolingian Empire.

€14

Zeppelin Museum

Zeppelin Museum is a museum in Lake Constance, Germany. Situated in Friedrichshafen, the Zeppelin Museum is a treasure trove for history and aviation enthusiasts. Galleries and public rooms are open on posted hours in Lake Constance. Zeppelin Museum remains part of the public visitor circuit in Lake Constance.
