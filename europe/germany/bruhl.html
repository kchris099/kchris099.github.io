Brühl

Brühl developed around the court culture of the prince-electors of Cologne, whose Augustusburg and Falkenlust palaces were built in the 18th century with formal gardens and axial avenues. Palace churches, parks, and later museums turned a small residence town into a showcase of Baroque court life, now recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage ensemble. Nearby Phantasialand and rail links added 20th-century leisure and commuter functions. The town's street plan, fountains, and palace vistas still express the ceremonial reach of the Cologne electorate into the Rhineland countryside.

Schlösser Brühl

Schloss Augustusburg at Brühl is a Rococo residence built for Archbishop Clemens August of Cologne from 1725 and forms a UNESCO World Heritage ensemble with Falkenlust. State apartments, staircase, and gardens illustrate 18th-century court culture on the edge of Cologne's electorate. Guided tours cover the main palace rooms during opening hours.

St. Maria von den Engeln

St Maria von den Engeln is the former Franciscan church beside Augustusburg, consecrated in the 15th century and later tied to the electoral court. Its nave and furnishings sit within walking distance of the palace entrance. The church shows how religious houses were absorbed into princely residence settings.

Schlosspark Brühl

Schlosspark Brühl is a public park in Brühl, Germany. The Schlosspark Brühl extends behind Augustusburg with formal parterres, canals, and tree-lined avenues designed in the 18th century. The park is open to visitors and forms the landscaped core of the Brühl residence ensemble. Paths, lawns, and shaded areas make up the main public facilities.

Falkenluster Allee

The Falkenluster Allee is a straight chestnut-lined avenue about two kilometres long, linking Augustusburg with the hunting lodge of Falkenlust. Created in the 1740s, it structured movement between court and hunt within the electoral estate. Walking or cycling the axis shows how Baroque planners connected separate buildings through landscape.

50.8235° N / 6.9100° E

Schloss Falkenlust

Schloss Falkenlust is a compact hunting lodge completed in 1749 for falconry and court retreats, paired with Augustusburg as a UNESCO site. Interior stucco and panelled rooms survive from the Rococo period. The lodge sits at the southern end of the Falkenluster Allee, set within its own wooded grounds.

Phantasialand

Phantasialand is a theme park opened in 1967 on the edge of Brühl, with themed areas, rides, and seasonal shows. It draws large numbers of visitors independently of the town's palace heritage. The park represents a major modern leisure industry beside Brühl's older court architecture and museums.

Max Ernst Museum

The Max Ernst Museum on Commedienstraße presents the work of the surrealist artist born in Brühl in 1891. Paintings, sculptures, and documents trace his career from the Rhineland to Paris and the United States. The museum adds a 20th-century art identity to a town known mainly for its Baroque palaces.
