Explore Hamburg

A Brief History

Hamburg grew from a 9th-century settlement into a leading Hanseatic port on the Elbe River. The Speicherstadt warehouse district and Chilehaus reflect 19th- and 20th-century trade wealth. Fire in 1842 and Allied bombing in 1943 destroyed large parts of the center, followed by reconstruction. The city-state joined the Federal Republic in 1949. St. Michaelis Church, canals, and museums document maritime commerce and migration.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

Elbphilharmonie

From Speicherstadt and HafenCity, proceed to the awe-inspiring Elbphilharmonie. This architectural marvel, designed by Herzog & de Meuron, has become a symbol of Hamburg. Its distinctive glass façade and wave-like structure are captivating.

Free

St. Michaelis Church (Michel)

Begin your adventure in Hamburg at the iconic St. Michaelis Church, affectionately known as the “Michel.” This magnificent baroque church stands as one of Hamburg’s most treasured landmarks. The climb to the tower’s observation deck, reaching an impressive 132 meters, is an unforgettable experience.

Free

Landungsbrücken

Next, head towards the Landungsbrücken, or St. Pauli Piers, an essential stop to truly Hamburg’s vibrant waterfront life. This bustling area is the heart of Hamburg’s maritime activities.

€15

Hamburg Rathaus

Continue your exploration with a visit to the Hamburg Rathaus, or City Hall, a masterpiece of neo-Renaissance architecture. The grandeur of the Rathaus is immediately evident in its opulent exterior, adorned with intricate statues and sculptures. Consider joining a guided tour to delve into the rich history and significance of this building as the seat of Hamburg’s government.

€20

Miniatur Wunderland

Your next destination is Miniatur Wunderland, nestled in Hamburg’s historic Speicherstadt district. This expansive model railway exhibition is the largest of its kind globally, making it a attraction. As you step inside, prepare to be amazed by the intricately detailed miniature replicas depicting various countries and iconic landmarks.

Free

Speicherstadt and HafenCity

After immersing yourself in the wonder of Miniatur Wunderland, venture into the neighboring Speicherstadt and HafenCity districts. Speicherstadt, noted for being the world’s largest warehouse district, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site brimming with history. Its striking red-brick buildings and narrow canals create a picturesque, timeless ambiance.
