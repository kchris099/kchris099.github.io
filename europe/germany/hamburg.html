Destination Guide — Northern Germany

Explore Hamburg

A Brief History

Hamburg developed from a Carolingian mission settlement into a leading Hanseatic port commanding trade on the Elbe. The Speicherstadt warehouse quarter and Chilehaus express 19th-century shipping wealth, while the 2017 Elbphilharmonie marks HafenCity regeneration. Allied bombing in 1943 devastated the centre; post-war rebuilding preserved landmarks such as St. Michaelis Church. The city-state joined the Federal Republic in 1949 as a major gateway for emigration and cargo.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

Elbe Tunnel

The St. Pauli Elbtunnel opened in 1911 as continental Europe's first major underwater road tunnel, linking the Landungsbrücken with Steinwerder island. Two tiled tubes stretch 426 metres beneath the Elbe, reached by historic lifts or a ceremonial staircase. Pedestrians and cyclists pass free around the clock, with harbour views from the south portal.

Free

St. Pauli Piers

The St. Pauli Landungsbrücken are floating piers on the Norderelbe used by harbour ferries, cruise ships, and excursion boats since the 19th century. Green copper roofs and clock towers frame views of the Elbe and container terminals. River buses connect the piers with the Speicherstadt, Elbphilharmonie, and Finkenwerder.

Free (tower €8)

St. Michael's Church

St. Michaelis, known as the Michel, is a Baroque main church completed in 1912 after earlier fires destroyed predecessors. Its copper-clad tower rises 132 metres and offers a viewing platform over the port and Altstadt. The crypt houses exhibitions on Hamburg church history and maritime disasters.

Free

Elbphilharmonie Hamburg

The Elbphilharmonie concert hall by Herzog and de Meuron crowns a 1960s warehouse in HafenCity with a wave of curved glass completed in 2016. The publicly accessible Plaza at 37 metres is reached via the curved Tube escalator. The vineyard-style Grand Hall seats 2,100 for concerts by the NDR Elbphilharmonie Orchestra.

€22

Miniatur Wunderland

Miniatur Wunderland in the Speicherstadt is the world's largest model railway layout, opened in 2001 and expanded across themed sections from Scandinavia to the United States. Detailed miniature airports, harbours, and cities animate with moving trains and ships. The attraction occupies several floors of a historic warehouse block.

Free

Treppenviertel Blankenese

Blankenese's Treppenviertel climbs the Elbe bluff in western Hamburg with more than 5,000 steps linking villas, gardens, and river paths. Merchant houses and captains' homes from the 18th and 19th centuries line winding lanes above sandy beaches. The district was an independent fishing village until annexed by Hamburg in 1927.

€20

Hamburg BUNKER

The St. Pauli bunker on the Heiligengeistfeld is a wartime flak tower now crowned with a rooftop park planted with thousands of trees. Guided tours explain its construction in 1942, post-war use as a media bunker, and recent green conversion. A spiral path leads to panoramic views over the harbour and Millerntor stadium.

€16

Millerntor-Stadion

Millerntor-Stadion is the home ground of FC St. Pauli on the Heiligengeistfeld in the St. Pauli district, rebuilt several times since 1910. Stadium tours visit the Gegengerade stand, dressing rooms, and the club museum documenting supporter culture. The ground seats about 29,500 and lies steps from the Reeperbahn.

€29

Hagenbeck Zoo

Tierpark Hagenbeck in Stellingen was founded in 1907 by Carl Hagenbeck as one of the first zoos without barred cages, using moats and naturalistic enclosures. The park combines a zoo, tropical aquarium, and ethnographic displays across landscaped hills. Historic entrance buildings and the Neptune fountain date from the early 20th century.

Free

Chilehaus

The Chilehaus on the Kontorhausviertel is a 1924 expressionist office block designed by Fritz Höger for a shipping merchant trading with Chile. Its sharp prow and stepped brick façade resemble a steamship's bow. The Kontorhaus district with the Sprinkenhof and Chilehaus is a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

€7

St. Nikolai Memorial

The Mahnmal St. Nikolai preserves the burnt-out spire of a Gothic church destroyed in the 1943 Operation Gomorrah air raids. A lift ascends the tower for views over the wartime ruins, while the crypt museum documents the bombing and Nazi persecution. The church was once among the tallest buildings in the world.

Free

St. Catherine Church

St. Katharinen is a brick Gothic church in the harbour quarter, with a nave dating from the 13th century and a copper spire visible from the Speicherstadt. It served Hanseatic merchants and shipwrights and houses medieval artefacts including the lion-head door. The church was heavily damaged in 1943 and restored after the war.

Free (guided tour €8)

Hamburg Townhall

The Hamburger Rathaus on Rathausmarkt was completed in 1897 in neo-Renaissance style as the seat of the city-state senate and parliament. The 647-room building features the Bürgerschaft chamber and ornate state rooms with frescoes of Hamburg's history. The inner courtyard and portico are open to the public when sessions allow.

Free

Town Hall Market

Rathausmarkt is the central square before the town hall, used for markets, political rallies, and seasonal events including the Christmas market. The open space is framed by the Rathaus façade, Alsterarkaden arcades, and commercial buildings of the Altstadt. A fountain and flagpoles mark the civic heart of the Free and Hanseatic City.

Free

Alster Arcade

The Alsterarkaden are neoclassical shopping arcades built in 1842 along the Kleine Alster canal beside Rathausmarkt. Colonnades shelter boutiques and cafés above the water, rebuilt after the 1842 Great Fire. The arcades link the town hall quarter with Jungfernstieg promenade along the Binnenalster lake.

Free

Stage Theater an der Elbe

The Stage Theater an der Elbe is a riverside musical theatre in the harbour opened in 2000 on the site of former docks. Glass façades face the Elbe and the passing harbour traffic. The venue stages large-scale musical productions with seating for about 1,800 spectators.

Free

Planten un Blomen

Planten un Blomen is a 47-hectare park in the city centre combining botanical gardens, greenhouses, and themed landscapes from Japanese gardens to rose beds. Summer water-light concerts animate the central lake, while tropical plants fill the Schaugewächshäuser in winter. The park replaced fortifications after Hamburg's walls were demolished in the 19th century.

Free

Alsterfontäne

The Alsterfontäne is a programmable fountain at the Binnenalster lake that shoots water jets up to 60 metres during scheduled displays. Installed in 1989, the fountain marks the head of the Inner Alster and is visible from Jungfernstieg. Displays run on selected afternoons and during city festivals.

Free

Laeiszhalle

The Laeiszhalle concert hall on Johannes-Brahms-Platz opened in 1908 as the Musikhalle, named for shipping magnate Carl Laeisz. The neo-baroque main hall hosts the Hamburg Philharmonic and visiting orchestras. Brahms, who spent his last years in Hamburg, is commemorated in the adjoining square.

€16

Wildpark Schwarze Berge

Wildpark Schwarze Berge in the Harburg hills south of Hamburg is a woodland wildlife park showing native European species including wolves, lynx, and wild boar in large enclosures. Walking trails loop through forest on the site of a former forester's estate. The park targets families with feeding demonstrations and play areas.

Free

Water Art Elbinsel Kaltehofe

Wasser-Erlebnis Elbinsel Kaltehofe on a harbour island documents Hamburg's historic water filtration plant built in 1893 to supply safe drinking water to the growing port. Listed red-brick filter halls and ponds now host exhibitions on urban water management. The site lies beside nature trails on the Elbe island of Kaltehofe.

€10

Panoptikum

The Panoptikum on Spielbudenplatz is Germany's oldest wax museum, founded in 1879 and rebuilt after wartime destruction. Figures from Hamburg musicians to political leaders occupy themed rooms across two floors near the Reeperbahn. The cabinet-of-curiosities tradition continues with horror chambers and local celebrity tableaux.

Free

Reeperbahn

The Reeperbahn is St. Pauli's main entertainment mile, named for the rope-making trade that once lined the street. Neon clubs, theatres, and bars occupy 19th-century merchant houses between Millerntor and Spielbudenplatz. The Beatles performed in clubs here in 1960–62, and the annual Reeperbahn Festival draws music industry visitors.

Free

St. Peter's Church

St. Petri am Berge is a five-naved hall church in the Altstadt whose tower was the city's tallest structure before the Michel. The brick Gothic building dates largely from the 14th century and houses a bronze baptismal font from 1457. Medieval wall paintings were uncovered during post-war restoration of the nave.

Free

Thalia Theater

The Thalia Theater on Alstertor is one of Hamburg's three municipal theatres, housed in a 1912 neo-baroque building destroyed in 1943 and rebuilt in 1960. The repertoire spans drama, comedy, and contemporary German plays across two stages. The company is among the largest spoken-theatre ensembles in Germany.

Free

Hamburg State Opera

The Hamburgische Staatsoper on Dammtorstraße is the city's opera house, opened in 1827 and rebuilt after wartime bombing in 1955. The company stages opera and ballet in a 1,690-seat auditorium. Gustav Mahler served as conductor here in the 1890s before moving to Vienna.

Free

Port of Hamburg

The Port of Hamburg stretches along the Lower Elbe as Germany's largest seaport, handling containers, cruise ships, and bulk cargo since the Middle Ages. Viewing points at Landungsbrücken, Elbphilharmonie Plaza, and harbour tours reveal the working docks and Speicherstadt warehouses. The port drives much of the city-state's economy.

Free

Stadtpark

Stadtpark in Winterhude is a 148-hectare landscaped park opened in 1914 with meadows, woods, and the open-air Stadtparksee stage. The Planetarium occupies a former water tower, and sculpture gardens dot the grounds. Hamburg's largest inner-city park draws joggers, picnickers, and concert audiences throughout the year.

Free

Isemarkt

The Isemarkt is a weekly street market along the Isebekkanal in Eppendorf, held on Tuesdays and Fridays under the elevated U-Bahn tracks. Stalls sell produce, fish, flowers, and prepared food along one of Europe's longest open-air market rows. The market has operated in its covered form since the 1980s.

Free

Deutsches SchauSpielHaus Hamburg

The Deutsches SchauSpielHaus on Hartungstraße is Hamburg's principal spoken-theatre company, based in a 1900 art-nouveau building in Rotherbaum. The repertoire includes classics, new German drama, and international works on the main stage and Kammerspiele. The theatre employs one of the largest permanent acting ensembles in the German-speaking world.
