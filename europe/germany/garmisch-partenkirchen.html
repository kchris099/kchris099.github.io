Explore Garmisch-Partenkirchen

A Brief History

Garmisch-Partenkirchen, nestled spectacularly in the shadow of the Zugspitze—Germany's highest peak—was formed in 1935 when two fiercely independent, historic alpine towns were merged for the Winter Olympics. For centuries prior, they were vital, wealthy stops on the medieval trade route between Italy and Augsburg. Today, the town is a premier destination for alpine sports, profoundly renowned for its breathtaking mountain vistas, beautifully painted traditional Bavarian houses (Lüftlmalerei), and deeply rooted, authentic alpine cultural traditions.

Top Sights & Activities

Zugspitze

Visiting the Zugspitze is a must if you’re seeking a striking alpine adventure. As Germany’s highest peak, reaching 2,962 meters, it offers unparalleled panoramic views of the Bavarian Alps and Austria. Your journey can start with an exciting ride on the cogwheel train or cable car, both of which climb steep inclines and reveal stunning landscapes.

Varies

Partnach Gorge

Immerse yourself in the natural beauty of Partnach Gorge, a geological wonder located near Garmisch-Partenkirchen. This site is not just a spectacle; it tells a story of water power and geological evolution with its high rock faces and rushing river cutting through the landscape. The gorge, with its history dating back millions of years, offers an engaging experience as you walk along its illuminated paths.

Free

Historic Town Center

Garmisch-Partenkirchen’s historic town center is a delightful mix of Bavarian tradition and modern charm. Walking through its cobbled streets feels like stepping back in time, where every corner reveals traditional Bavarian architecture and the vibrant art of Lüftlmalerei—uniquely painted exteriors that tell local tales. As you explore, you’ll find an array of cozy cafes and unique local shops that offer a true taste of Bavarian culture.

Varies

Wank Mountain

Wank Mountain provides a perfect escape for those who enjoy hiking with awe-inspiring scenery. The mountain is famous for its gentle climbing paths that make it accessible to both seasoned hikers and families looking for a pleasant day outdoors. As you ascend, whether on foot or by cable car, you’ll be treated to sweeping views of the Wetterstein mountains and the lush valleys below.

Varies

Olympic Ski Stadium

When you visit the Olympic Ski Stadium, you’re stepping into a piece of history dating back to the 1936 Winter Olympics. This stadium isn’t just significant for its sporting past but also as a historical artifact with stories about its design and the events it has hosted over the decades. A guided tour will immerse you in the heritage of this iconic site, offering insights into its construction, importance during the Olympics, and its role today.
