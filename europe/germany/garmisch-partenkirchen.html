Explore Garmisch-Partenkirchen

A Brief History

Garmisch-Partenkirchen formed in 1935 from two market towns in the Bavarian Alps beneath Germany's highest peak, Zugspitze. The 1936 Winter Olympics brought an ice stadium and international attention. Earlier centuries saw salt routes and pilgrimage traffic through the Loisach valley. Painted frescoes on Partenkirchen houses reflect local craft traditions. The area remains a base for skiing, hiking, and access to nearby castles.

Top Sights & Activities

€78

Zugspitze

Visiting the Zugspitze is a must if you’re seeking a striking alpine adventure. As Germany’s highest peak, reaching 2,962 meters, it offers unparalleled panoramic views of the Bavarian Alps and Austria. Your journey can start with an exciting ride on the cogwheel train or cable car, both of which climb steep inclines and reveal landscapes.

€10

Partnach Gorge

Immerse yourself in the natural beauty of Partnach Gorge, a geological wonder located near Garmisch-Partenkirchen. This site is not just a spectacle; it tells a story of water power and geological evolution with its high rock faces and rushing river cutting through the landscape. The gorge, with its history dating back millions of years, offers an engaging as you walk along its illuminated paths.

Free

Historic Town Center

Garmisch-Partenkirchen’s historic town center is a delightful mix of Bavarian tradition and modern charm. Walking through its cobbled streets feels like stepping back in time, where every corner reveals traditional Bavarian architecture and the vibrant art of Lüftlmalerei— painted exteriors that tell local tales. As you explore, you’ll find an array of cozy cafes and unique local shops that offer a true taste of Bavarian culture.

€30

Wank Mountain

Wank Mountain provides a perfect escape for those who enjoy hiking with awe-inspiring scenery. The mountain is famous for its gentle climbing paths that make it accessible to both seasoned hikers and families looking for a pleasant day outdoors. As you ascend, whether on foot or by cable car, you’ll be treated to sweeping views of the Wetterstein mountains and the lush valleys below.

Free

Olympic Ski Stadium

on arrival the Olympic Ski Stadium, you’re stepping into a piece of history dating back to the 1936 Winter Olympics. This stadium isn’t just significant for its sporting past but also as a historical artifact with stories about its design and the events it has hosted over the decades. A guided tour will immerse you in the heritage of this iconic site, offering insights into its construction, importance during the Olympics, and its role today.
