Garmisch-Partenkirchen

Garmisch-Partenkirchen formed in 1935 from two market towns in the Bavarian Alps beneath Germany's highest peak, Zugspitze. The 1936 Winter Olympics brought an ice stadium and international attention. Earlier centuries saw salt routes and pilgrimage traffic through the Loisach valley. Painted frescoes on Partenkirchen houses reflect local craft traditions. The area remains a base for skiing, hiking, and access to nearby castles.

Zugspitze

Visiting the Zugspitze is a must For travellers seeking a striking alpine adventure. As Germany's highest peak, reaching 2,962 meters, it offers unparalleled panoramic views of the Bavarian Alps and Austria. Visitor journey can start with an exciting ride on the cogwheel train or cable car, both of which climb steep inclines and reveal landscapes.

Partnach Gorge

Immerse oneself in the natural beauty of Partnach Gorge, a geological wonder located near Garmisch-Partenkirchen. This site is not just a spectacle; it tells a story of water power and geological evolution with its high rock faces and rushing river cutting through the landscape. The gorge, with its history dating back millions of years, offers an engaging as visitors walk along its illuminated paths.

Historic Town Center

Garmisch-Partenkirchen's historic town center is a mix of Bavarian tradition and modern charm. Walking through its cobbled streets feels like stepping back in time, where every corner reveals traditional Bavarian architecture and the active art of Lüftlmalerei, painted exteriors that tell local tales. While exploring, visitors find an array of cozy cafes and unique local shops that offer a true taste of Bavarian culture.

Wank Mountain

Wank Mountain provides a perfect escape for those who enjoy hiking with awe-inspiring scenery. The mountain is known for gentle climbing paths that make it accessible to both seasoned hikers and families looking for a pleasant day outdoors. As visitors ascend, whether on foot or by cable car, visitors'll be treated to sweeping views of the Wetterstein mountains and the lush valleys below.

Olympic Ski Stadium

On arrival the Olympic Ski Stadium, visitors are stepping into a piece of history dating back to the 1936 Winter Olympics. This stadium isn't just significant for its sporting past but also as a historical artifact with stories about its design and the events it has hosted over the decades. A guided tour will immerse visitors in the heritage of this well known site, offering insights into its construction, importance during the Olympics, and its role today.
