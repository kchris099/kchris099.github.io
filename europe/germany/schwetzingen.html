Schwetzingen

Palatine electors made Schwetzingen their summer seat in the eighteenth century. Formal gardens, a mosque folly, and a court theatre express Enlightenment princely taste beside the Rhine plain. The town exists historically as an appendage of that palace garden, not as an independent free city. Mozart and other court musicians performed in the theatre still used for summer festivals.

Schwetzingen Palace

Schwetzingen Palace, located just 15 minutes west of Heidelberg, is a vast 17th-century palace with ornamental gardens that have been preserved since their original design. The palace was the summer residence for a prominent family and reached its peak under Prince Elector Carl Theodor. It has a rococo theater and is known for its lavish antiquities.

Mosque in The Palace Garden

The 'Mosque' in the Schwetzingen Palace Garden is an 18th-century folly designed by Nicolas de Pigage. It was built in a Moorish-Islamic style to reflect the Enlightenment era's fascination with the Orient, rather than for religious use. The ornate structure and its surrounding gardens add a unique, exotic element to the extensive palace grounds.
