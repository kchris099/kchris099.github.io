Destination Guide — Southern Germany

Explore Schwetzingen

A Brief History

Growing alongside its magnificent palace, Schwetzingen flourished in the 18th century as the summer residence of the Palatine Electors. The expansive formal gardens and baroque civic structures remain a vivid legacy of Enlightenment-era courtly culture and musical patronage. This rich historical tapestry established a strong foundation that continues to shape the city's dynamic contemporary identity.

Top Sights & Activities

€12

Schwetzingen Palace

Schwetzingen Palace, located just 15 minutes west of Heidelberg, is a vast 17th-century palace with ornamental gardens that have been preserved since their original design. The palace was the summer residence for a prominent family and reached its peak under Prince Elector Carl Theodor. It has a rococo theater and is known for its lavish antiquities.

€9

Mosque in The Palace Garden

The 'Mosque' in the Schwetzingen Palace Garden is an 18th-century folly designed by Nicolas de Pigage. It was built in a Moorish-Islamic style to reflect the Enlightenment era's fascination with the Orient, rather than for religious use. The ornate structure and its surrounding gardens add a unique, exotic element to the extensive palace grounds.

Reservation required

SAP Arena

The SAP Arena in Mannheim is a modern glass and aluminum stadium that serves as a versatile venue for various events, including ice hockey matches, handball games, major concerts, and more. It is the home arena of the Adler Mannheim ice hockey team and the Rhein-Neckar Löwen handball team. As part of the UNESCO Creative Cities Network with the title of "UNESCO City of Music," Mannheim boasts a smart city infrastructure with an advanced electrical grid.

Free

Wasserturm Mannheim

Wasserturm Mannheim is a sandstone water tower and historic landmark adorned with sculptures, dating back to 1889. Located in a city square, it offers numerous options for dining out and features a beautifully maintained park ideal for picnics and walks during warm weather when the water flows. However, it is currently undergoing refurbishment work which may detract from its appeal. In winter months, the trees and flowers lose their vitality.

€11

Mannheim Baroque Palace

Mannheim Baroque Palace, a grand 18th-century structure, was once the residence of the Prince Electors of the Palatinate. Today, it is primarily utilized by Mannheim University and also features a museum that showcases the palace's former glory. Visitors can explore restored halls displaying historical artifacts, elegant furniture, and classical pieces.

Free

Jesuit Church, Mannheim

The Jesuit Church in Mannheim, completed in 1760, is a example of Baroque architecture. Designed by Alessandro Galli Bibiena as a replica of the Church of the Gesu in Rome, it boasts twin bell towers and an opulent interior with polychrome marble. Despite being heavily damaged during World War II, the church has been meticulously reconstructed to its former glory. The lavish altar, royal boxes, frescos and paintings make for a visually captivating experience.

Reservation required

Capitol

Nestled in the heart of Mannheim, the Capitol Theatre stands as a example of art deco architecture, originally built in 1927. Once Germany's largest cinema, this venue has transformed into a vibrant hub for entertainment. Hosting an array of events from concerts and comedy shows to literature readings and corporate gatherings, it offers something for everyone.

Free

Old Bridge Heidelberg

The Old Bridge Heidelberg, constructed in 1788, is an pedestrian bridge that offers views of the city's landmarks. It connects the Neckar River to Neuenheim Old Town and is made of classic red sandstone. The bridge leads to the romantic ruins of an old castle that once served as a heavily fortified Gothic stronghold and later as one of Germany's most magnificent residences. Unfortunately, much of the structure was destroyed during wars in the 17th century and never rebuilt.

Free

Heiliggeistkirche

Nestled in the heart of Heidelberg's vibrant Market Square, the Heiliggeistkirche, or Church of the Holy Spirit, stands as a magnificent testament to Gothic architecture. Dating back to its first mention in 1239 and later expanded by Elector Ruprecht III in 1398, this grand structure has served as both a place of worship and a communal hub for centuries.

Free

Heidelberger Marktplatz

Heidelberger Marktplatz is a historic market square located in Altstadt, known for its Gothic Church of the Holy Spirit and a fountain dating back to the 1700s. This landmark has been significant since the Middle Ages, once serving as the site for public courts of justice and even executions. Heidelberg itself is famous for attractions like Heidelberg Castle and Heidelberg University, which is the oldest university in Germany.

€11

Heidelberg Castle

Heidelberg Castle, a 16th-century red sandstone fortress, stands proudly on a hillside overlooking the Altstadt and the Neckar River. Visitors can enjoy guided tours of the castle's interior and marvel at its Renaissance architecture. The castle is accessible via a steep cobbled trail or by taking the Bergbahn cogwheel train from Kornmarkt station.

Free

Philosophers’ Way

The Philosophers' Way, situated on a hill overlooking the Neckar River in Heidelberg, offers a picturesque and serene setting for leisurely walks and picnics. This winding path traverses through woodlands, vineyards, and gardens, providing views of the city. Named after renowned philosophers who frequented it during the Romantic Era, this route leads to the university and features scenic viewpoints along the Schlangenweg.

Free

Speyerer Dom

The Speyer Cathedral (Speyerer Dom) is a massive Romanesque basilica and a UNESCO World Heritage site. Founded in 1030 by Emperor Conrad II, it is one of the largest and most significant Romanesque buildings in Europe. The cathedral features a striking red sandstone exterior, a vast crypt containing the tombs of several Holy Roman Emperors, and a majestic, austere interior.

Free

Historische Altstadt Speyer

Speyer Altstadt, or the Old Town of Speyer, is a hidden gem that offers an enchanting blend of traditional German charm and modern flair. Just a short drive from Frankfurt, this picturesque area features narrow cobblestone streets perfect for leisurely strolls. Visitors can explore an array of cozy wine taverns, vibrant bars, and delightful restaurants. The architecture includes the Speyer Dom, which looks particularly at sunset.

Free

Altpörtel

Am Altpörtel is a historic city gate located in Speyer, Germany. It is one of the main attractions in the city, along with the Speyer Cathedral and several churches. Visitors can climb the tower as part of a public tour to get a panoramic view of Speyer. The gate itself is described as beautiful and adds to the charm of this old German town.

€14

Luisenpark

Luisenpark in Mannheim is a beautifully landscaped park featuring thousands of flowers, a tropical hall, a butterfly house, and animal enclosures. Visitors can enjoy the park's attractions while sitting at the cafes and bars around Friedrichsplatz. The park offers leisure activities for families and children, including an amusement park, green spaces for relaxation, flower gardens, a picturesque lake, and various sculptures.

Reservation required

Carl-Benz-Stadion

The Carl-Benz-Stadion, home to SV Waldhof Mannheim, was the venue for a game that left mixed impressions. The stadium itself received praise, but the process of reaching the seats was inconvenient. In order to enter and exit, one had to go through the main entrance and traverse the parking lot. This aspect discouraged further bookings in that area. However, SVW's performance on the field garnered appreciation as they created a great atmosphere despite being a 3rd league club.

Free

Königstuhl

Königstuhl, standing at 568 meters, is the highest point in Heidelberg and offers a view of the city and the Neckar valley. Accessible via a funicular or by climbing the Himmelsleiter (Ladder to Heaven), this scenic hill also features two special forest paths, Via naturae and the forest adventure trail, offering opportunities for learning and exploration for visitors of all ages.

€16

Heidelberg Zoo

Heidelberg Zoo is a beloved animal park located on the picturesque banks of the Neckar River. It houses over 1,100 animals from more than 250 species, including elephants, tigers, gorillas, sea lions, red pandas, and brown bears. The zoo actively participates in conservation efforts through programs like WAPCA and EEP to raise awareness about endangered species.

Free

Michaelskloster Heiligenberg

Michaelskloster Heiligenberg is a historic site located in southern Germany, with ruins dating back to the 5th century BC. The monastery was built in 1023 and offers panoramic views from its tower. The area is perfect for a peaceful hike, and visitors can explore the well-preserved ruins of the monastery. The site provides an opportunity to learn about the history of the monks who once lived there.

Free

Thingstätte Heidelberg

Thingstätte Heidelberg is a historic open-air amphitheater located on the Holy Mountain (Heiligenberg) in Heidelberg. Built in 1933-34 by the Nazi Party as part of their propaganda movement, it was one of 45 similar venues constructed across Germany. Today, it has been transformed into a public park while retaining its original architecture. Visitors can explore its history, enjoy panoramic views of the surrounding area, and take leisurely walks through the nearby forest.

Free

Handschuhsheim, Tiefburg

Nestled just a short tram ride from the bustling Altstadt, Handschuhsheim is a charming neighborhood that once thrived as an independent village. Its rich history dates back to 765, and notice the distinctive coat of arms featuring a glove with red lining throughout the area. Despite its name translating to 'glove home,' it’s not known for glovemaking but rather reflects the heritage of its original Franconian inhabitants.
