Explore Dinkelsbühl

A Brief History

Dinkelsbühl became a free imperial city in the Holy Roman Empire and fortified its center with walls and towers still visible today. The Gothic St. George's Minster dominates the market square. During the Thirty Years' War the town changed hands several times. Its preserved medieval street plan and half-timbered houses draw visitors along the Romantic Road. Annual festivals recall historical guild traditions and local folklore.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

Old Town Walls & Gates

Dinkelsbühl's medieval walls remain largely intact, enclosing the old town with 18 towers and four main gatehouses. Visitors can walk sections of the ramparts and climb towers such as the Segringer Tor for views over red-tiled roofs. The fortifications date mainly from the 14th and 15th centuries.

Free

St. George's Minster

St. George's Minster is a late-Gothic hall church whose tall nave and minimal interior decoration reflect the style of southern German sacred architecture. The Romanesque west tower predates the Gothic choir and nave. The church anchors the old town and hosts concerts throughout the year.

Free

Deutsches Haus

The Deutsches Haus on Weinmarkt is a half-timbered building from 1440 with an elaborately carved and painted facade. Its decorative figures and inscriptions illustrate medieval civic pride and craftsmanship. The house is privately owned but remains one of the most photographed buildings in the Road towns.

Free

Weinmarkt (Wine Market)

Weinmarkt is the central square of Dinkelsbühl, framed by gabled patrician houses and the town hall. Markets and festivals, including the annual Kinderzeche historical pageant, take place here. The square's layout has changed little since the town's peak as a Free Imperial City.

Free

House of History (Haus der Geschichte)

The House of History occupies the Old Town Hall and traces more than 800 years of Dinkelsbühl's past. Exhibits cover the cloth trade, town government, and the siege of 1632 during the Thirty Years' War, when a children's procession is said to have persuaded the Swedish army to spare the town.

Free

Stadtpark & Rothenburger Weiher

Stadtpark and the Rothenburger Weiher lie just outside the walls, where a small pond reflects the medieval fortifications. Shaded paths and benches make it a quiet place to rest after walking the old town. The park is a short stroll from the Segringer Tor gate.
