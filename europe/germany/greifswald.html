Greifswald

Hanseatic merchants on the Ryck built brick-Gothic churches that still dominate the skyline. The university of 1456 made Greifswald a scholarly town under Swedish Pomerania and later Prussia; Caspar David Friedrich studied here before painting the nearby coast. Market gables and harbour warehouses belong to that Baltic trading and university culture.

Museum Harbor (Museumshafen)

The Museum Harbor on the Ryck river holds a collection of restored traditional sailing vessels, including flat-bottomed coastal boats once used in the Baltic trade. Wooden masts and rigging line the quay, giving the waterfront a historic character. Boat trips and maritime events take place here in summer.

St. Nicholas Cathedral

St. Nicholas Cathedral, called 'Tall Nicholas' locally, is a brick Gothic church whose tower dominates Greifswald's skyline. Climbing the tower rewards visitors with views across the town toward the Baltic coast and the island of Rügen. The interior retains medieval furnishings and a large organ.

Pomeranian State Museum

The Pomeranian State Museum covers the natural and cultural history of the region from prehistory to the present. Its art holdings include works by Caspar David Friedrich, who was born in Greifswald in 1774. Exhibits also address Hanseatic trade, university life, and the changing borders of Pomerania.

Market Square & Town Hall

The Market Square is surrounded by brick Gothic gabled houses and the red-painted Town Hall with its stepped gable. Outdoor cafés fill the square in warm weather. The ensemble reflects the prosperity Greifswald enjoyed as a Hanseatic port and university town from the Middle Ages onward.

05 AdmissionFree

Eldena Abbey Ruins

Eldena Abbey lies in ruins about three kilometers from the city center, founded as a Cistercian monastery in 1199. Caspar David Friedrich painted the ivy-covered walls in the early 19th century, helping to make the site a symbol of landscape art. A short forest path leads to the remains of the choir and nave.

54.0921° N / 13.3852° E

Wieck Fishing Village

Wieck is a fishing village at the mouth of the Ryck where a historic wooden bascule bridge crosses to the Baltic shore. Small boats still moor along the quay, and restaurants serve locally caught fish. The village offers a quieter waterfront alternative to the busier city harbor upstream.
