Destination Guide — Eastern Germany

Explore Dresden

A Brief History

Dresden was the capital of Saxony under the Wettin dynasty, with August the Strong patronizing baroque architecture such as the Zwinger and Frauenkirche in the 18th century. The city was heavily bombed in February 1945, destroying much of the historic center. East German reconstruction and post-1990 rebuilding restored the church and royal buildings. Museums on the Elbe hold collections of porcelain, painting, and scientific instruments.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

Brühl's Terrace

Brühl's Terrace is a raised promenade above the Elbe, built on the remains of the city's 16th-century fortifications. Nicknamed the 'Balcony of Europe,' it offers views across the river toward the Neustadt. Statues, balustrades, and the Albertinum museum line the walkway.

Free (dome viewing platform €10)

Frauenkirche Dresden

The Frauenkirche is a Baroque Lutheran church in the Neumarkt, destroyed in the 1945 bombing and rebuilt between 1994 and 2005. Original stones were incorporated into the new structure, and the golden cross atop the dome was a gift from Britain. The interior holds one of Europe's largest stone domes.

Free

Martin Luther Statue

The Martin Luther statue in Neumarkt was erected in 1885 to commemorate the Reformation. The bronze figure survived the wartime destruction of the square and was restored after reunification. It stands near the Frauenkirche, linking Dresden to the wider Protestant history of Saxony.

€8

Stallhof Dresden

The Stallhof is a Renaissance courtyard within the Residenzschloss, once used for tournaments and court festivities in the 16th century. Its arcaded galleries and sgraffito decoration illustrate Saxon equestrian culture. Today it hosts events including the annual Medieval Christmas Market.

Free

Fürstenzug

The Fürstenzug is a 102-meter porcelain mural on the outer wall of the Stallhof, depicting 35 rulers of the House of Wettin from 1127 to 1904. Created from over 23,000 Meissen tiles between 1904 and 1907, it is one of the largest porcelain artworks in the world.

Free

Cathedral St. Trinitatis

The Cathedral of the Holy Trinity, also called the Hofkirche, was built in the 18th century as Dresden's Catholic court church under Augustus III. The crypt holds the remains of many Wettin rulers. Silbermann organs and a Rococo interior make it a notable stop on the historic city walk.

Free

Theaterplatz

Theaterplatz is Dresden's main civic square, bordered by the Semperoper, the Zwinger, and the Residenzschloss. Equestrian statues and open space give it a formal character. Most of the surrounding buildings were rebuilt after wartime damage while preserving their original facades.

Free (courtyard museums €14)

Dresdener Zwinger

The Zwinger is an 18th-century palace complex designed by Matthäus Daniel Pöppelmann for Augustus the Strong. It originally served as an orangery and festival venue. Today it houses the Old Masters Picture Gallery, the Porcelain Collection, and the Mathematics-Physics Salon.

€8

Kronentor

The Kronentor, or Crown Gate, is the southeastern entrance to the Zwinger, topped by a replica of the Polish royal crown. It symbolizes Augustus the Strong's election as King of Poland in 1697. Sculptural groups on the gate represent the four seasons and four elements.

Free

Augustusbrücke

The Augustusbrücke spans the Elbe between the Altstadt and Neustadt. A bridge has stood at this site since the 12th century; the current stone structure largely dates from the 19th century. From the bridge, walkers can see the skyline of spires, domes, and the riverfront terraces.

Free

Goldener Reiter

The Goldener Reiter is a gilded equestrian statue of Augustus the Strong, erected in 1736 on the Neustädter Markt. The figure faces east toward Poland, dressed in Roman armor. It is one of the most recognizable landmarks of Dresden's Neustadt district.

Free

The Grand Garden Palace

Located within the Grand Garden of Dresden, this palace is an excellent example of Baroque architecture and hosts various cultural events. The site forms part of The Grand Garden Palace's documented civic and cultural landscape within Germany, with archives and visitor information available from municipal or regional heritage authorities.

Free

The Grand Garden of Dresden

The Grand Garden of Dresden is a picturesque and expansive park In the city. It was built in early Baroque style and is the largest park in Dresden. The garden features baroque sculptures, a summer palace, large grassy lawns, and a beautiful pond surrounding the main palace. travellers can enjoy leisurely strolls or bike rides through the charming garden, as well as explore attractions such as the Dresden Zoological Garden and Dresden Botanical Garden within its grounds.

€19

Zoo Dresden

Zoo Dresden is home to over 2000 wild animals representing 300 species from around the world, all housed in naturalistic enclosures. The prestigious 16th-century Johanneum building hosts the Dresden Transport Museum, a favorite among families. It showcases a diverse collection of exhibits spanning different eras and includes interactive stations like the Transport Garden and Experiments Room. travellers can also enjoy a ride on the railway staffed by local school children dressed in uniform.

Free

Kunsthofpassage Dresden

Kunsthofpassage Dresden is a network of creatively decorated courtyards that host outdoor cafes, artists' studios, and independent boutiques. This art revitalization project was completed in 2001 and offers travellers different themed areas to explore. The Court of the Elements features a blue building adorned with pipes that create music in the rain, while the Court of Lights showcases multimedia presentations in a courtyard illuminated by sun-reflecting mirrors.

Free

Dresdner Molkerei Gebrüder Pfund GmbH

Dresdner Molkerei Gebrüder Pfund GmbH, also known as Pfunds Molkerei, is a historic dairy shop located in Dresden, Germany. Established in 1879 by the Pfund brothers, this 19th-century store is renowned for its elaborately hand-painted tiles and rich history. Despite being destroyed during World War II, it was faithfully reconstructed and continues to attract travellers with its ornate decor.

Free

Loschwitz Bridge

The Loschwitz Bridge, also known as the "Blue Wonder," is a historic steel bridge that crosses the Elbe River and connects the districts of Blasewitz and Loschwitz in Dresden. Built in the late 19th century, it was a remarkable engineering achievement at the time. The bridge offers views of the river and surrounding villas, making it a popular spot for pedestrians, motorists, and cyclists.

€4

Dresden Suspension Railway

The Dresden Suspension Railway is a unique and historic monorail system that offers travellers a one-of-a-kind experience. As one of the oldest suspension railways in the world, it ascends to an observation deck where travelers can enjoy panoramic views of the Dresden Elbe Valley. The 280-meter-long railway climbs over 84 meters to Oberloschwitz, providing a quick but scenic four-minute trip. For those who prefer hiking, there's also the option to make the ascent on foot.

Free (palace tours €8)

Albrechtsberg Palace

The Albrechtsberg Palace is a neoclassical building with an amazing location overlooking the river Elbe. It offers beautiful events spaces for private and corporate gatherings, and is a great place to enjoy the city at night. The site forms part of Albrechtsberg Palace's documented civic and cultural landscape within Germany, with archives and visitor information available from municipal or regional heritage authorities.

Free

Residenzschloss

Dresden Castle, also known as the Royal Palace, has a rich history dating back over 600 years. Despite being heavily damaged during World War II, it was meticulously rebuilt and now houses the Historic Green Vault, the New Green Vault, and various museums within the Dresden State Art Collections. The castle's architecture showcases a blend of styles including Baroque and Neo-Renaissance, with an domed courtyard adorned with Renaissance-style sgraffito paintings.

Free

Hausmannsturm

Nestled within the enchanting Dresden Castle, the Hausmannsturm stands as a testament to medieval architecture, dating back to 1400. This impressive octagonal tower reaches a height of 100.27 meters and is crowned with a Welsh Dome adorned in copper, complete with an open lantern that adds to its charm.

Free

Schlossplatz

Schlossplatz is a charming cobbled square in Dresden, with origins dating back to the 18th century. It is a must-visit destination in the city center, offering views of the Dresden Castle, Dresden Cathedral, Augustus Bridge, and the Elbe River promenade. Although it was heavily damaged during World War II, Schlossplatz has been meticulously restored to its historical grandeur. The square boasts impressive architectural beauty and serves as a scenic stop for travellers exploring Dresden.

Free

Semperoper Dresden

Situated in Dresden Old Town, the Semperoper is a renowned opera house known for its opulent interior and exceptional acoustics. Originally built in 1841 by architect Gottfried Semper, it has a rich history including destruction by fire in 1869 and bombing in 1945. Despite these setbacks, the opera house was rebuilt and continues to host performances. The 1,300-seat auditorium offers an intimate setting with excellent views from every seat.

Free

Holy Cross Church

The Holy Cross Church, also known as Kreuzkirche, is a example of late Baroque architecture in Dresden. Its 92m high tower offers a viewing platform at 54m, providing views of the city and the main Christmas Market. The church has been restored after being damaged during World War II and now showcases beautiful art. travellers can climb to the top for panoramic vistas of Dresden.

Free

Altmarkt

Altmarkt is a historic square in Dresden, known for its lively Christmas market and other seasonal events throughout the year. The square has been a hub of activity since 1434 and is surrounded by hotels, restaurants, and banks. During the festive season, it transforms into a winter wonderland with charming decorations, diverse food and beverage offerings, and handcrafted gifts from local vendors. travellers can also enjoy concerts featuring Christmas music.

Free

Filmnächte am Elbufer

Filmnächte am Elbufer is the largest open-air cinema festival in Germany, taking place every summer since 1991 on the banks of the River Elbe in Dresden's Neustadt district. This event attracts tens of thousands of movie enthusiasts annually and offers a unique experience with a colossal stage set against the backdrop of Dresden's skyline. The festival showcases internationally acclaimed films and hosts performers, providing an outdoor concert experience.

€8

Pillnitz Castle

Pillnitz Castle, a Renaissance palace that has been restored after a fire, is a must-visit destination in Dresden. The castle houses a botanical palm house and a decorative arts museum. It is part of the renowned collections presented by the Staatliche Kunstsammlungen Dresden, showcasing masterpieces acquired over centuries by the Princes of Saxony and Kings of Poland.
