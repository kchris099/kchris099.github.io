Destination Guide — Northern Germany

Explore Wolfsburg

A Brief History

Wolfsburg was planned in 1938 as a town for workers at the Volkswagen plant founded by the Nazi-era German Labour Front. The factory produced the Beetle and later became a symbol of West German recovery. The modernist Stadt des KdF-Wagens layout was renamed after the castle Wolfsburg in 1945. Today the city houses Volkswagen headquarters, an automobile museum, and the Autostadt exhibition complex.

Top Sights & Activities

€15

Wolfsburg Castle

Wolfsburg Castle is a Weser Renaissance water castle dating to the 13th century, predating the automobile industry that defines the modern city. It houses the Stadtmuseum with exhibits on local history and a municipal art gallery. The moat and red-brick towers contrast with the postwar architecture nearby.

Free

Autostadt

Autostadt is an automotive theme park adjacent to the Volkswagen factory, opened in 2000. Brand pavilions display current and concept vehicles, and visitors can watch new cars collected from the factory towers. Interactive exhibits cover design, production, and the history of mobility.

Free

Phaeno Science Center

The Phaeno Science Center was designed by Zaha Hadid and opened in 2005. Its concrete structure spans the landscape on angled pillars, and inside more than 350 hands-on experiments explore physics and perception. The building is a landmark of contemporary architecture in Lower Saxony.

€12

Volkswagen AutoMuseum

The Volkswagen AutoMuseum traces the company's history from the 1930s to the present. Exhibits include early Beetles, rare prototypes, and vehicles associated with major model lines. The collection is housed in a modern building near the factory and complements the larger Autostadt experience.

Free

Allerpark

Allerpark is a recreation area built around the Allersee lake on the city's western edge. Paths for walking and cycling circle the water, and a sandy beach attracts swimmers in summer. The park provides open space between the residential districts and the Autostadt and Phaeno cultural sites.

€12

Kunstmuseum Wolfsburg

The Kunstmuseum Wolfsburg occupies a glass-and-steel building near the city center and focuses on modern and contemporary art. Large-scale temporary exhibitions have featured works from the 20th and 21st centuries. The museum opened in 1994 as part of the city's cultural development alongside the Autostadt.
