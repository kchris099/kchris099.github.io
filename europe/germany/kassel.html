Kassel

Kassel served as the capital of the Landgraviate and later Electorate of Hesse-Kassel. Landgrave Karl founded Bergpark Wilhelmshöhe in the 18th century with cascades and the Hercules monument. Brothers Grimm collected folk tales while working in the city library. Documenta, a contemporary art exhibition, began in 1955 after wartime destruction. Bergpark was added to the UNESCO World Heritage list in 2013.

Löwenburg Castle

Löwenburg Castle is a castle in Kassel, Germany. Löwenburg Castle, designed to look like the ruins of a medieval knight’s fortress, is a fantastical creation of the late 18th century. Located above Wilhelmshöhe Castle, it is also in Bergpark Wilhelmshöhe. Walls, courtyards, or towers form the usual visitor route when open.

Orangerie in Karlsaue Park

Standing majestically in the large Karlsaue Park is the Orangerie, a striking Baroque castle which is different to what visitors may expect given the name. It was built at the start of the 18th century and, during winter, orange trees and other plants were kept inside here. Today, it is home to the Astronomy and Physical Cabinet and Hesse's largest planetarium.

Bergpark Wilhelmshöhe

Bergpark Wilhelmshöhe is a public park in Kassel, Germany. It's the #1 must-visit attraction in Kassel. Paths, lawns, and shaded areas make up the main public facilities. Bergpark Wilhelmshöhe remains part of the public visitor circuit in Kassel. Local maps identify Bergpark Wilhelmshöhe within Kassel, Germany.

Fulda River

Kassel's Fulda River is a ribbon of tranquility that courses through the city, offering a route for leisurely strolls. Edged by lush green paths, it cuts through the urban landscape, providing a natural sanctuary from the city buzz. Those looking to unwind can find pockets of peace as they watch the gentle waters flow, perhaps catching sight of local wildlife or kayakers navigating the currents.

Herkules Monument

The Herkules Monument, crowning Bergpark Wilhelmshöhe, is a colossal homage to the Greek demi-god known for his strength and far-reaching mythical feats. This grand statue, cast in copper, stands atop an octagonal stone base, offering visitors panoramic views of Kassel and beyond. It was built between 1707 and 1717.

51.2859° N / 9.2767° E

Schloss Wilhelmshöhe

At the foot of Bergpark Wilhelmshöhe resides Schloss Wilhelmshöhe, not just another palace but a repository of cultural treasures. This splendid neoclassical structure, once home to Kaiser Wilhelm II, now is a proud landmark of the region's artistic heritage. Inside, it houses the Gemäldegalerie Alte Meister, a gallery teeming with masterpieces by illustrious European painters.
