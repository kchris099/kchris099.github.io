Explore Black Forest

A Brief History

The Black Forest (Schwarzwald) is a mountainous region in southwest Germany, long shrouded in mystical folklore and natural majesty. Known for its dense, evergreen forests, dramatic waterfalls, and picturesque timber-framed villages, it has inspired countless fairy tales, including those of the Brothers Grimm. Beyond its legendary cuckoo clocks and rich culinary traditions, the region boasts a deep cultural history of mining, glassmaking, and forestry, remaining one of Europe’s most beloved and culturally immersive natural retreats for hikers and nature enthusiasts.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

Mummelsee

Mummelsee is a small cirque lake formed during the Ice Age, sitting at about 1,036 meters on the Hornisgrinde ridge. Local folklore speaks of water spirits said to dwell in its depths. Visitors can rent paddleboats in summer or walk the shore path for views of the surrounding spruce forest.

Free

Triberg Waterfalls

Triberg Waterfalls drop about 163 meters over seven natural tiers through dense forest near the town of Triberg. Marked trails lead to viewing platforms at different heights, and the falls are lit in the evening during the summer months. The site is one of the most visited natural attractions in the Black Forest.

Free

Schwarzwaldhochstraße

The Schwarzwaldhochstraße, or Black Forest High Road, runs for roughly 60 kilometers between Freudenstadt and Baden-Baden along the ridge tops. Drivers and cyclists pass viewpoints over the Rhine Valley and the Vosges, with access to trailheads, lakes, and small villages along the route.

€12

German Clock Museum

The German Clock Museum in Furtwangen documents the history of timekeeping from sundials to modern precision instruments. Its collection includes a large number of traditional Black Forest cuckoo clocks and explains how clockmaking shaped the regional economy from the 18th century onward.

€12

Vogtsbauernhof Open Air Museum

The Vogtsbauernhof Open Air Museum near Gutach preserves six historic farmsteads moved from across the Black Forest. Furnished interiors, workshops, and seasonal demonstrations show how rural families lived, farmed, and practiced crafts such as woodworking and textile production from the 16th to 19th centuries.

Free

Lake Titisee

Lake Titisee is a glacial lake near Titisee-Neustadt, popular for swimming, rowing, and lakeside walks. A promenade lined with cafés and shops runs along the shore, where visitors often try regional Black Forest cake. Boat trips operate in summer, and trails connect the lake to nearby hills.
