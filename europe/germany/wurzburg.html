Würzburg

Würzburg was the seat of prince-bishops who built the Residence palace in the 18th century with frescoes by Giovanni Battista Tiepolo. The Marienberg fortress above the Main River predates the baroque city below. The university was founded in 1402. Allied bombing in March 1945 destroyed much of the center, followed by decades of restoration. The Residence was inscribed as a UNESCO World Heritage site in 1981.

Old Main Bridge (Alte Mainbrücke)

The Old Main Bridge is a 15th-century stone arch bridge lined with Baroque statues of saints and rulers. Local residents gather on the bridge in the evening with glasses of Franconian wine, a tradition that has continued for generations. Views extend to the fortress on the hill and the cathedral towers.

Würzburg Cathedral (Kiliansdom)

Würzburg Cathedral, dedicated to St. Kilian, is a Romanesque basilica with a rebuilt interior after severe wartime damage. Restorations blended medieval structure with modern stained glass and furnishings. The cathedral is the seat of the bishop and anchors the Domstraße procession route.

03 AdmissionFree

Juliusspital

The Juliusspital is a charitable foundation established in 1576 that operates one of Germany's larger wine estates. Historic wine cellars beneath the complex can be toured, and the courtyard gardens are open to visitors. Proceeds from wine sales have funded social services in Würzburg for over four centuries.

49.7979° N / 9.9313° E

04 AdmissionFree

Käppele

The Käppele is a pilgrimage church on a hill above the city, reached by a tree-lined Stations of the Cross path. Built in the 18th century as part of a hospital foundation, it shares the tradition of winery ownership with the Juliusspital. The Rococo interior and terrace views draw visitors year-round.

Würzburg Residence

The Würzburg Residence is a Baroque palace designed by Balthasar Neumann for the prince-bishops, inscribed as a UNESCO World Heritage site. The grand staircase is crowned by a ceiling fresco by Giovanni Battista Tiepolo, among the largest frescoes ever created. Formal gardens extend behind the main building.

49.7928° N / 9.9388° E

Marienberg Fortress

Marienberg Fortress rises on a hill across the Main from the old town, occupying the site of a Celtic fort and medieval castle. It served as the seat of the prince-bishops for centuries before they moved to the Residence. The fortress museum and ramparts offer views over the city's churches and vineyards.

49.7898° N / 9.9192° E
