Göttingen

Göttingen received town rights in the early 13th century and became known for its university, founded in 1737 by George II of Great Britain as Elector of Hanover. The institution attracted mathematicians and physicists including Gauss. Half-timbered lanes surround churches and the old town hall. The city lay in the British occupation zone after 1945 and remains a research center in Lower Saxony.

Old Town Hall (Altes Rathaus)

Göttingen's Altes Rathaus on the Marktplatz dates to the thirteenth century with later Gothic and Renaissance work. Frescoes in the main hall depict scenes from the town's Hanseatic past. The building still hosts civic ceremonies beside the Gänseliesel fountain.

Gänseliesel Fountain

The Gänseliesel fountain on Göttingen's Marktplatz, installed in 1901, shows a goose girl with her flock and has become the city's emblem. Doctoral graduates traditionally climb to place flowers and kiss the statue. The ritual ties university life to the central market square.

03 AdmissionFree

St. John's Church (Johanniskirche)

St. John's Church is a Gothic hall church with two towers of unequal height at the west end. The north tower can be climbed for views over the old town and the university buildings. The church has been the main Protestant parish church of Göttingen since the Reformation.

51.5330° N / 9.9338° E

04 AdmissionFree

Kiessee Lake

Kiessee is a gravel-pit lake south of the city center, converted into a recreation area with beaches, boat rentals, and walking paths. Locals swim and sail here in summer. Cafés along the shore make it a common weekend destination for students and families from Göttingen.

51.5343° N / 9.9350° E

05 AdmissionFree

Göttingen City Walls

Sections of Göttingen's medieval city wall survive as tree-lined embankments encircling the historic center. Walkers can follow the ramparts past towers and gates, looking down on half-timbered houses and cobbled lanes. The walls were strengthened in the 14th century when the town joined the Hanseatic League.

51.5314° N / 9.9338° E

06 AdmissionFree

Botanical Garden of Göttingen University

The Botanical Garden of the University of Göttingen began in 1736 and moved to its present grounds in the nineteenth century. Greenhouses and outdoor beds show plants from several climate zones for teaching and research. The garden forms a green walk a short distance from the historic university town centre.
