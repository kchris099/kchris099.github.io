Moselle Valley

The Moselle valley carries one of Europe's older wine regions, with slopes terraced since Roman times around towns such as Trier and Cochem. Medieval castles guarded river crossings and tolls. Trier, founded as Augusta Treverorum, served as a western capital of the Roman Empire. Steep vineyards, half-timbered villages, and Roman ruins line the river from France into Germany. Wine fairs and river navigation shaped local economies for centuries.

Cochem Castle (Reichsburg)

Cochem Castle, or Reichsburg, sits on a hill above the Moselle and the town of Cochem. Rebuilt in neo-Gothic style in the 19th century on medieval foundations, it offers guided tours of furnished rooms. The terrace provides wide views of the river bend and the steep vineyard slopes on either side.

Geierlay Suspension Bridge

The Geierlay Suspension Bridge spans 360 meters across the Mörsdorfer Bachtal near the Moselle region. Opened in 2015, it hangs about 100 meters above the valley floor and is accessible via a forest trail from the village of Mörsdorf. The crossing takes roughly ten minutes on foot.

Porta Nigra (Trier)

Large sandstone blocks form Trier's Porta Nigra, a Roman gate built around the late second century without mortar in much of its masonry. Medieval conversion into a church helped preserve the structure before nineteenth-century restoration returned it toward a Roman appearance; towers, passages, upper galleries, and street views reveal the scale of Augusta Treverorum's northern entrance within the city's ensemble.

49.7597° N / 6.6442° E

Burg Eltz

Burg Eltz stands in a side valley of the Moselle, hidden from the main road and surrounded by forest. The medieval castle has been owned by the same family for over 800 years and retains original furnishings and armor collections. A shuttle bus and walking path connect the parking area to the castle entrance.

50.2051° N / 7.3368° E

05 AdmissionFree

Bernkastel-Kues

Bernkastel-Kues is a wine town on a bend of the Moselle, known for its medieval market square with narrow, gabled half-timbered houses. Wine taverns and cellars offer Riesling from the steep slopes above the river. A ruined castle overlooks the town from the vineyard-covered hill called the Landshut.

49.9157° N / 7.0708° E

06 AdmissionFree

Moselle River Cruise

River cruises on the Moselle pass terraced vineyards, small villages, and hilltop castle ruins along one of Germany's most scenic wine routes. Boats operate between towns such as Cochem, Bernkastel, and Koblenz during the warmer months. The slow pace allows views of the valley that are difficult to see from the roads above.

49.7838° N / 6.8172° E
