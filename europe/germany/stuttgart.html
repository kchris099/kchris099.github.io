Stuttgart

Stuttgart originated as a stud farm in the 10th century and became the capital of Württemberg. The New Palace and Solitude Palace reflect ducal and royal patronage. Industrial firms including Daimler and Porsche established headquarters here in the 20th century. The city was heavily bombed in 1944 and rebuilt with modern districts alongside restored churches. Vineyards on city hills continue a local wine tradition.

Schlossplatz and Neues Schloss

Schlossplatz forms Stuttgart's principal civic lawn between the New Palace, Konigsbau, museums and central shopping streets. Fountains and a jubilee column organize the former court grounds, while gardens support daily recreation. Events and demonstrations regularly use the square. The eighteenth-century palace facade frames the space, with most interiors serving state government.

Ludwigsburg Residential Palace

Ludwigsburg Residential Palace stands north of Stuttgart as a vast court complex begun in 1704. Baroque, rococo and neoclassical apartments surround courtyards with theaters, chapels and museum spaces, while formal gardens extend beyond the main buildings. Guided routes reveal how successive rulers expanded the residence and changed its decorative program.

Stuttgart TV Tower

Stuttgart's television tower opened in 1956 as an early telecommunications tower to use a reinforced-concrete shaft with an elevated observation structure. Engineer Fritz Leonhardt's design influenced later towers worldwide, while viewing decks and a cafe look across the basin, vineyards and surrounding hills when weather allows. Wind, maintenance and cloud can affect access.

Esslingen Old Town

Esslingen's old town lies southeast of Stuttgart and preserves a dense mix of medieval churches, timber-framed houses, canals, guild buildings, wine cellars, towers and later civic architecture. Hillside fortifications and vineyards rise above the Neckar-side center. Evening lighting and restaurants may support a visit, but the district's substance comes from its urban history.

Altes Schloss and the Württemberg State Museum

Across from Schlossplatz, the historic Altes Schloss holds the Wurttemberg State Museum. Collections run from Stone Age finds to modern objects across successive galleries. Opening follows posted hours inside the former ducal residence facing the square.

Mercedes-Benz Museum

Stuttgart is famous for being the home of the automotive industry so exploring this past is a great way to start the day. The Mercedes-Benz Museum opens its doors at 9am and I recommend visitors arrive right on opening to get the most from visitor day in Stuttgart. This museum offers a deep look at the evolution of the automobile, featuring an extensive collection of over 160 vehicles.
