Explore Aachen

A Brief History

Aachen, the westernmost city in Germany, is steeped in profound imperial history and cultural significance. Known as the favored residence of Charlemagne, who built his magnificent palatine chapel here in the 8th century, it served as the sacred coronation site for German kings for over 600 years. Famous for its healing thermal hot springs since Roman times, Aachen successfully bridges its majestic, aristocratic past with its present role as a leading European center of technological innovation and vibrant university life.

Historic Old Town

When you first arrive at Aachen at the central station, you may be wandering if you have visited an ancient city at all. But as you get closer to the heart of the city, around the Cathedral, this pretty Old Town is sure to be one you’ll remember. Dominated by landmarks like the iconic Elisenbrunnen (historic thermal baths) and ancient churches, the Old Town is also a hub for enjoying Aachen’s café culture and artisan shops.

Free

Aachen Cathedral

When you find yourself wandering through the charming streets of Aachen, Germany, the Aachen Cathedral, also known as the Kaiserdom, is a treasure you simply must uncover. This majestic structure, nestled in the heart of the city, not only commands attention with its intricate architecture but also carries with it centuries of history. Stepping through the ornate doors, your gaze will soar to the heights of the cathedral’s grandiose interior.

Printen at Aachen’s Bakeries

Printen, Aachen’s version of gingerbread, transcends mere confectionery to become an edible emblem of the city’s culinary heritage. This crunchy delicacy, with its specialized recipes safeguarded by local bakeries, infuses an unmistakable flavor of molasses and a blend of aromatic spices. Much more than a seasonal treat, Printen signifies the sweet essence of Aachen’s gastronomic tradition.

Varies

Aachen Rathaus (Town Hall)

Step into Aachen’s history at the Rathaus, the city’s grand town hall. Once the palatine seat of Charlemagne, this Gothic masterpiece is a treasure trove of art and history. Imagine the coronations that once echoed through its halls as you explore its ornate chambers.
