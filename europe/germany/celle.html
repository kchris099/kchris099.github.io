Explore Celle

A Brief History

Celle served as a residence of the Dukes of Brunswick-Lüneburg from the 14th century and briefly housed the exiled Queen Wilhelmine Amalia of Spain in the early 18th century. Its ducal palace and timber-framed old town survived wartime bombing with limited damage. The Schloss houses a baroque theater and art collections. Celle remains a county seat in Lower Saxony, linked by rail to Hanover and Hamburg.

Altstadt (Old Town)

Altstadt (Old Town) is a visitor site in Celle, Germany. There are also plenty of shops, restaurants, bars and more. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest. Altstadt (Old Town) remains part of the public visitor circuit in Celle.

€8

Bomann-Museum Celle

Opposite Celle Castle in the Old Town, Bomann-Museum should be visitor next stop. This museum traces the history of the people in the region of Lower Saxony from Bronze Age archaeological finds to today. There's a great range of exhibits including a re-created 19th century farmhouse to help visitors understand rural life at the time, information on the life of the 19th century bourgeoise, a great textile collection and exhibits of foreign migration to this area.

Free

Stadtkirche Sankt Marien (St Marien Town Church)

A minute's walk away is visitor next Celle attraction, Stadtkirche Sankt Marien or St Marien Town Church. This 700 year old Protestant-Lutheran town church doesn't just have a pretty exterior and an even prettier interior but also has great views if walkable the 235 steps up to the top of the church steeple. At 52 metres high, visitors have great views over the Old Town.

Free

Altes Rathaus (Old Town Hall)

Altes Rathaus (Old Town Hall) is a visitor site in Celle. A moment's walk from the church will find visitors in Celle's central Marktplatz and Old Town Hall. When Otto II built Celle in 1292, he probably also built this town hall on the main Marktplatz (Market Square). It was first mentioned in documents in 1378 before being expanded in 1380.

€8

Kunstmuseum Celle (Celle Art Museum)

Next door to the Bomann-Museum is Celle’s innovative Art Museum. Advertised as the world’s first 24 hour art museum, the inside is only open during the day. However, every evening, all night long on the hour, the foyer changes colours for several minutes.
