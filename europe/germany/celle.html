Explore Celle

A Brief History

Celle, a picturesque town in Lower Saxony, is celebrated as the historic southern gateway to the Lüneburg Heath. For centuries, it served as the royal residence of the Dukes of Brunswick-Lüneburg, a grand heritage reflected in its stunning Renaissance-style ducal palace and historic court theater. Undamaged by World War II, Celle boasts the largest, most spectacular ensemble of half-timbered houses in Europe, offering visitors a perfectly preserved, storybook atmosphere steeped in over a thousand years of aristocratic and merchant history.

Top Sights & Activities

€ 15.00

Schloss Celle (Celle Castle)

The number one tourist attraction in Celle has to be Schloss Castle. Even in a country full of castles, this one stands out and is definitely worth your time. Originally built around 980, it was expanded by Otto II in 1292.

Free

Altstadt (Old Town)

Celle’s Old Town is just beautiful and you’d be crazy to come to Celle without a wander through its pretty streets. With over 400 half-timbered houses, you won’t know where to look first. There are also plenty of shops, restaurants, bars and more.

€ 12.00

Bomann-Museum Celle

Opposite Celle Castle in the Old Town, Bomann-Museum should be your next stop. This museum traces the history of the people in the region of Lower Saxony from Bronze Age archaeological finds to today. There’s a great range of exhibits including a re-created 19th century farmhouse to help you understand rural life at the time, information on the life of the 19th century bourgeoise, a great textile collection and exhibits of foreign migration to this area.

Free

Stadtkirche Sankt Marien (St Marien Town Church)

A minute’s walk away is your next Celle attraction, Stadtkirche Sankt Marien or St Marien Town Church. This 700 year old Protestant-Lutheran town church doesn’t just have a pretty exterior and an even prettier interior but also has great views if you can walk the 235 steps up to the top of the church steeple. At 52 metres high, you have great views over the Old Town.

Free

Altes Rathaus (Old Town Hall)

A moment’s walk from the church will find you in Celle’s central Marktplatz and beautiful Old Town Hall. When Otto II built Celle in 1292, he probably also built this town hall on the main Marktplatz (Market Square). It was first mentioned in documents in 1378 before being expanded in 1380.

€ 12.00

Kunstmuseum Celle (Celle Art Museum)

Next door to the Bomann-Museum is Celle’s innovative Art Museum. Advertised as the world’s first 24 hour art museum, the inside is only open during the day. However, every evening, all night long on the hour, the foyer changes colours for several minutes.
