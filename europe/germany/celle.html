Celle

Celle served as a residence of the Dukes of Brunswick-Lüneburg from the 14th century and briefly housed the exiled Queen Wilhelmine Amalia of Spain in the early 18th century. Its ducal palace and timber-framed old town survived wartime bombing with limited damage. The Schloss houses a baroque theater and art collections. Celle remains a county seat in Lower Saxony, linked by rail to Hanover and Hamburg.

Schloss Celle (Celle Castle)

Celle Castle grew from a medieval fortress into the residence of the dukes of Brunswick-Lüneburg. Renaissance and baroque wings surround a courtyard, with state rooms, a chapel, collections, and a small court theater preserving different phases of dynastic life. Guided routes clarify which interiors survive or were reconstructed, while the surrounding park opens the palace toward Celle's old town.

Altstadt (Old Town)

Celle's Altstadt is dense with half-timbered Fachwerk houses along pedestrian streets around the ducal castle and parish church. Shops, cafés, and inns occupy the ground floors of carefully kept facades. The old town ranks among Lower Saxony's most complete timber-framed centres.

Bomann-Museum Celle

Opposite Celle Castle in the Old Town, Bomann-Museum should be visitor next stop. This museum traces the history of the people in the region of Lower Saxony from Bronze Age archaeological finds to today. There's a great range of exhibits including a re-created 19th century farmhouse to help visitors understand rural life at the time, information on the life of the 19th century bourgeoise, a great textile collection and exhibits of foreign migration to this area.

Stadtkirche Sankt Marien (St Marien Town Church)

A minute's walk away is visitor next Celle attraction, Stadtkirche Sankt Marien or St Marien Town Church. This 700 year old Protestant-Lutheran town church doesn't just have a pretty exterior and an even prettier interior but also has great views if walkable the 235 steps up to the top of the church steeple. At 52 metres high, visitors have great views over the Old Town.

Altes Rathaus (Old Town Hall)

Celle's Altes Rathaus faces the Marktplatz a short walk from the castle church. Documents mention the town hall by 1378, with expansion around 1380 after Otto II founded the planned town in 1292. The stepped gable building anchors civic life on the market square.

Kunstmuseum Celle (Celle Art Museum)

Kunstmuseum Celle uses light as both a collection theme and part of its architecture. Contemporary works appear inside during normal museum hours, while illuminated glass facades, windows, and exterior installations allow selected art to remain visible after closing, inspiring the institution's twenty-four-hour concept. The phrase does not mean every gallery stays physically open through the night.
