Explore Bayreuth

A Brief History

Bayreuth rose to prominence in the eighteenth century under Margravine Wilhelmine, who transformed the provincial town into a center of Enlightenment culture and Rococo architecture. The city's musical legacy was permanently cemented in the nineteenth century when Richard Wagner chose it to construct his innovative Festspielhaus, establishing the long-running Bayreuth Festival. This dual heritage of aristocratic patronage and operatic history defines the modern city, visible in the opulent Margravial Opera House, the Hermitage gardens, and Wagner's former residence, Wahnfried.

Eremitage

Eremitage, also known as the Hermitage, is a fortified historic site in Bayreuth, Germany. Originally a gift from the margrave to his well-educated princess, it was later expanded by her to include additional fountains, the Temple of the Sun, and an orangery. Walls, courtyards, or towers form the main visitor route when the grounds are open.

€6

Hermitage New Palace

The Hermitage New Palace, also known as Neues Schloss, is a historical gem left by Wilhelmine in Bayreuth. After the old palace was destroyed by fire in 1753, a more grandiose replacement was constructed while the couple traveled to Italy. Despite financial constraints, this palace is a evidence of opulence and elegance. The palace and its surrounding park offer an ever-changing spectacle throughout the seasons, making it a destination year-round.

€6

Hermitage Old Palace

Hermitage Old Palace is a fortified historic site in Bayreuth, Germany. Visitors can explore the city's museums, landmarks, parks, and gardens including the Eremitage and the Hofgarten. Walls, courtyards, or towers form the main visitor route when the grounds are open.

€24

Lohengrin Therme Bayreuth

Lohengrin Therme Bayreuth is a beach in Bayreuth, Germany. Visitors can indulge in an array of sauna experiences, including themed Aufguss sessions that incorporate music or soothing meditation sounds, along with a rotating selection of essential oils and natural branches to enhance the experience. Access is typically on foot from nearby roads or beachfront paths.

€10

Margravial Opera House

Margravial Opera House is a theatre in Bayreuth, Germany. Construction and early use date to around 1745. Constructed between 1745 and 1750 by architect Giuseppe Galli Bibiena and his son Carlo for Margravine Wilhelmine of Brandenburg-Bayreuth, this opera house is celebrated for its opulent interior adorned with woodwork and frescoes. Public access is usually through ticketed performances or daytime visits.

Free

Stadtkirche Heilig Dreifaltigkeit

Stadtkirche Heilig Dreifaltigkeit is a religious building in Bayreuth, Germany. The exterior and main interior spaces are usually viewable outside service times. Stadtkirche Heilig Dreifaltigkeit remains part of the public visitor circuit in Bayreuth.

Free

Hofgarten

Hofgarten is a visitor site in Bayreuth. A historic park located next to the Neues Schloss, featuring well-maintained gardens, fountains, and statues, ideal for a leisurely walk. Hofgarten is a visitor site in Bayreuth, Germany with public access during posted hours.

Free

Röhrensee Animal Park

Röhrensee Animal Park is a destination for families, offering a blend of animal enclosures and active flower beds set around a lake. Just a quick 7-minute drive away, this park is perfect for leisurely strolls or active outings throughout the year. Springtime visits are particularly enchanting as witness adorable baby ducklings and other young animals making their debut.

Free

Ecological Botanical Garden

Ecological Botanical Garden is a public park in Bayreuth, Germany. The Ecological Botanical Garden is a large 40-hectare sanctuary that safeguards rare plant species within its greenhouses, cultivation area, and research sections. Paths, lawns, and shaded areas make up the main public facilities.

€12 (guided tour)

Bayreuth Festival Theatre

Located on the Green Hill in Bayreuth, the Bayreuth Festival Theatre is a evidence of 19th-century architectural brilliance and musical innovation. Constructed between 1872 and 1875 under the visionary guidance of composer Richard Wagner, this venue was careful designed for his operatic masterpieces.
