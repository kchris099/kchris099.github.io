Erfurt

Erfurt was a trading center on the Via Regia in the Middle Ages and joined the Hanseatic League. Its Krämerbrücke, lined with inhabited houses, dates from the 15th century. Martin Luther studied at the university founded in 1392. The city remained part of Thuringia through changing German states. The cathedral and Severi Church stand on a hill above the old quarter, while preserved synagogues record a medieval Jewish community.

Petersberg Citadel (Zitadelle Petersberg)

Petersberg Citadel, a example of European baroque military architecture, reigns as a formidable structure reflecting Erfurt’s historical strategic relevance. It’s one of Europe’s preserved and largest baroque fortresses. Built in the 17th century atop Petersberg hill, it represents centuries of military history, with its intact bastions, casemates, and underground passages.
