Explore Bonn

A Brief History

Bonn was an electoral residence on the Rhine, the birthplace of Beethoven, and later the capital of West Germany. Churches, civic squares, university buildings, and palace gardens show the city's courtly, academic, and modern political history in a compact urban core.

Beethoven-Haus Bonn

The Beethoven House preserves the birthplace and memorial museum of Bonn's most internationally known native son. It ties the city directly to the musical and intellectual culture associated with Beethoven's early life.

Free

St. Remigius

St Remigius is a historic parish church in central Bonn and belongs to the city's older Catholic urban fabric. It also helps frame the small-scale network of streets and squares around the market and minster.

Free

Altes Rathaus

Bonn's old town hall fronts the market square and is one of the clearest civic buildings from the electoral period. In the federal capital era it also became a backdrop for state visits and public political ceremony.

Free

Kurfürstliches Schloss

The Kurfurstliches Schloss was the residence of the prince-electors of Cologne and now anchors the main university quarter. Its reuse illustrates how former court architecture shaped the later academic city.

Free

Hofgarten Bonn

The Hofgarten is the broad lawn and promenade in front of the former electoral palace and central to Bonn's university setting. It became an important public gathering space in both academic and political life.

Free

Bonner Münster

Bonn Minster is one of the Rhineland's major Romanesque churches and a central monument of the old city. Its long history places the electoral and modern capital layers within a much older ecclesiastical landscape.

Free

Beethoven-Denkmal

The Beethoven monument on Munsterplatz marks the composer's enduring place in Bonn's civic identity. It also reflects the 19th-century culture of public commemoration that reshaped many German city centers.

Free

HARIBO Store

The Haribo store reflects Bonn's connection with the confectionery company founded in the city in the 20th century. It adds a modern commercial note to a center otherwise dominated by court, church, and university history.

Free

Poppelsdorf Palace

Poppelsdorf Palace is a baroque branch residence at the end of Bonn's planned palace axis and now belongs to the university campus. Its relationship with the main palace helps explain the spatial ambitions of electoral Bonn.

Free

Botanische Gärten der Universität Bonn

The university botanical gardens occupy the grounds around Poppelsdorf Palace and extend Bonn's scientific and horticultural traditions. They show how former residence landscapes were adapted for teaching, research, and public strolling.
