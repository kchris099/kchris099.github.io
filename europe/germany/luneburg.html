Destination Guide — Northern Germany

Explore Luneburg

A Brief History

Established as a regional settlement beyond an older fortified core, the city developed through industrialization in the late 19th century, post-war reconstruction, and modern civic expansion that reshaped its public spaces, markets, and surrounding landscape. Restored religious buildings, civic buildings, and natural features now define the contemporary visitor experience.

Top Sights & Activities

Old crane in the Lüneburg harbor

The Old Crane in the Lüneburg harbor is a centuries-old industrial landmark that dates back to 1346. This historic harbor crane was used to lift valuable goods such as salt and firewood. It features two hidden wooden carrying wheels and is still fully functional, despite becoming redundant after Lüneburg was connected to the railway network in 1846.

Free

Am Sande

Am Sande is a picturesque market square located in the historic old town of Luneburg, Germany. The square is surrounded by charming gabled buildings that house various shops and eateries, adding to its medieval charm. St John's Church with its leaning spire overlooks the cobblestone plaza, creating a backdrop. In the Middle Ages, Am Sande was covered in sand rather than cobbles, making it easier for merchants to navigate their carts to the market.

Free

St. John's Church

St. John's Church, located in the historic center of Luneburg, Germany, is a magnificent Brick Gothic church with a rich history dating back to 927. The church is known for its 14th-century tilted steeple and the legend surrounding it. With its 108-meter bell tower dominating the skyline, St. John's features striking Gothic-style architecture and is home to the world's highest brick vaulted ceiling.

Free

Lüneburg water tower observation deck

The observation deck of the Lüneburg water tower offers a fascinating glimpse into history with its impressive 14th-century architecture. The view from the top is truly magnificent and well worth a visit. The staff are friendly and helpful, even if there is a language barrier. While most exhibits are in German, this should not discourage non-German speakers as the fourth floor speaks for itself and translation apps can easily assist in understanding the content.

Free

St. Michaelis Church

St. Michaelis Church, dating back to the 14th-15th century, holds historical significance as it was where Johann Sebastian Bach attended school and sang in the church choir. The church's acoustic qualities are praised, making it a popular venue for events like Christmas services. travellers can explore both the interior and exterior of this centuries-old church while enjoying its medieval surroundings.

Free

Kalkberg

Kalkberg is a hidden gem that offers a serene escape from the hustle and bustle of Luneburg. This lush garden, situated atop an ancient gypsum hill, provides panoramic views of the city’s rooftops. Once towering at 80 meters, Kalkberg has been shaped by centuries of gypsum mining, which ceased in 1923. Today, it stands as one of Germany's first nature reserves since its designation in 1932.

Free

German Salt Museum

The German Salt Museum, also known as the Luneburg Salt Museum or Salzmuseum, is a captivating venue that showcases the 1,000-year history of salt mining in Luneburg. Situated on the former saline site, this museum delves into the city's rich past as a prominent salt producer and its significance within the Hanseatic League.

Free

Theater Lüneburg

The Theater Lüneburg is a place that combines both innovative and traditional performances, making it a unique and appealing destination. It caters to all ages, including the youngest audience members. The German adaptation of Bonnie and Clyde that was showcased there demonstrated the high quality of their productions, providing great entertainment at an affordable price. Each performance showcases talent from both male and female actors.

Free

Lüneburg Town Hall

Lüneburg Town Hall is a historic building with medieval origins and a baroque-era facade. Construction of the town hall began in 1230, incorporating architectural elements from various periods. The original Gothic facade was replaced after a storm in 1703, resulting in the front seen today. Guided tours offer travellers the chance to explore the lavish interior, featuring murals, frescoes, and intricate wood carvings.

Free

Liebesgrund

Liebesgrund is a park located in Luneburg, known for its historical significance as the former moat of the Luneburg Wall. travellers can catch a glimpse of the Wall while enjoying various activities on the spacious lawn, such as soccer and frisbee. For families with young children, there is a charming and publicly accessible kindergarten with a large playground. Liebesgrund provides an ideal atmosphere for relaxation after shopping or outings near Am Sande.
