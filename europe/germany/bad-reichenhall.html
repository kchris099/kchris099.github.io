Destination Guide — Southern Germany

Explore Bad Reichenhall

A Brief History

Established as a regional settlement beyond an older fortified core, the city developed through industrialization in the late 19th century, post-war reconstruction, and modern civic expansion that reshaped its public spaces, markets, and surrounding landscape. Restored religious buildings, civic buildings, and natural features now define the contemporary visitor experience.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

Alte Saline

Alte Saline, also known as the Bad Reichenhaller Saltmuseum, is a 19th-century industrial saltworks located in Bad Reichenhall. It played a significant role in the town's growth and prosperity from ancient Celtic times to the present day. The site features an underground grotto, a salt museum, and a gift shop offering various flavors of kitchen salt and bath salts.

Free

Königlicher Kurgarten

Nestled in the heart of Bad Reichenhall, the Königlicher Kurgarten is a spa garden that has captivated travellers since its inception in 1868 by Royal Bavarian Court Gardener Carl von Effner. Renowned as one of Central Europe's most exquisite gardens, it boasts an enchanting backdrop of majestic mountains and is framed by elegant structures like the Concert Rotunda and Royal Spa House.

Free

Gradierhaus

The Gradierhaus is a tranquil location with a serene atmosphere, ideal for unwinding and admiring the saline. It boasts a picturesque garden for leisurely strolls. While it caters predominantly to an older demographic and may require more amenities in the playground, this deliberate approach ensures a peaceful environment conducive to relaxation.

Free

Wandelhalle

The Wandelhalle is a captivating gem nestled within the spa gardens, offering travellers an enchanting experience. This elegant pavilion features a striking misting green facade that draws salt from local saline springs, creating a refreshing atmosphere. A stroll along the impressive 540-foot Gradierhaus allows guests to inhale therapeutic salty air, known for its benefits to lung health.

Free

RupertusTherme

Rupertus Therme in Bad Reichenhall is a modern health and fitness complex located at the Rupertuspark, offering views of the Alpine scenery. The facility features a large outdoor area with warm brine thermal pools, including a unique brine grotto. travellers can enjoy saunas, massage therapy, and a child-friendly pool with flumes. For adults seeking tranquility, there is an exclusive area without kids for peaceful relaxation.

Free

Burgruine Karlstein

Nestled in the landscape of Bad Reichenhall, Germany, Burgruine Karlstein stands majestically atop Pankrazfelsens, offering travellers an experience. This historic castle ruin is just a stone's throw from the charming Wallfahrtskirche St. Pankraz, a beautiful example of Baroque architecture that enhances the scenic beauty of the area.

Free

Almbachklamm

Almbachklamm is a picturesque gorge near the village of Marktschellenberg, offering a 2-kilometer hike through natural scenery. The trail features catwalks, bridges, and narrow paths alongside a beautiful green-blue stream with numerous waterfalls. While the hike provides impressive viewpoints and is perfect for hot days, it's important to note that there are sections without guardrails.

Free

Salt Mine Berchtesgaden

Salt Mine Berchtesgaden offers a family-friendly tour of a 1500s salt mine, featuring miners' slides, a salt lake, and an illuminated grotto. Once owned by Berchtesgaden's princely rulers, the mine now welcomes travellers to experience its rich history. The hour-long tour includes a ride on a miniature train deep into the mountain, where an enormous chamber reveals the mining process.

Free

Schloss Berchtesgaden

Schloss Berchtesgaden, once a medieval monastery and later the summer residence of the Bavarian royal family, is now home to a permanent collection of rare family treasures. The fine Renaissance rooms showcase sacred art, including wood sculptures by renowned late-Gothic artists. travellers can explore two weaponry rooms displaying hunting tools and rifles from the 19th century, as well as enjoy the beautiful rose garden on the grounds.

Free

Nationalparkzentrum Haus der Berge

The House of Mountains, also known as the Berchtesgaden National Park Centre, is a captivating travellers center and museum that offers an immersive experience for those interested in learning about the Berchtesgaden Alps. The center features multimedia exhibits, a cinema, and a library where travellers can explore the rich history and natural beauty of the area.
