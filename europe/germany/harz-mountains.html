Explore Harz Mountains

A Brief History

The Harz Mountains stand as the highest and most dramatic mountain range in Northern Germany, a region deeply steeped in medieval mining history and mystical pagan folklore. For centuries, its rich silver and copper veins funded empires and spurred the creation of intricate, historic water management systems. Revered in German romantic literature as a wild gathering place for witches—most notably the Brocken peak in Goethe's 'Faust'—the Harz today enchants visitors with its dense forests, half-timbered towns, and historic narrow-gauge steam railways.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

Rappbode Dam & Suspension Bridge

Walk across one of the longest pedestrian suspension bridges in the world, stretching over the massive Rappbode reservoir. It offers a thrilling, vertigo-inducing perspective of the dramatic landscape.

Free

Quedlinburg Old Town

A UNESCO World Heritage site situated on the edge of the mountains, featuring over 1,300 perfectly preserved half-timbered houses and romantic cobblestone streets.

Varies

Hexentanzplatz (Witches' Dance Floor)

A dramatic cliff plateau high above the Bode Gorge, steeped in local folklore and pagan history. It offers an incredible viewpoint and features an outdoor theater and a gondola lift.

Varies

Rammelsberg Mines

A fascinating UNESCO World Heritage site in Goslar. Explore over 1,000 years of continuous mining history by descending deep underground to see the ancient, preserved tunnel systems.

Varies

The Brocken

The highest peak in Northern Germany, famously shrouded in mist and witchcraft legends. You can hike to the summit or take the historic steam-powered narrow-gauge railway for spectacular views.

Varies

Harz Narrow Gauge Railways

Experience the romance of a bygone era by riding these beautifully maintained historic steam trains that wind their way through deep forests, dramatic valleys, and picturesque mountain towns.
