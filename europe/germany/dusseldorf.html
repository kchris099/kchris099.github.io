Explore Düsseldorf

A Brief History

Düsseldorf became the capital of the Duchy of Berg and later of North Rhine-Westphalia after 1946. The Altstadt along the Rhine retains narrow lanes once tied to brewing and trade. Elector Jan Wellem commissioned the equestrian statue on the market square in the early 18th century. Postwar industry and fashion fairs shaped the modern economy. The Königsallee and MedienHafen district reflect 19th- and 20th-century urban expansion.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

Altstadt (Old Town)

Altstadt (Old Town) is a visitor site in Düsseldorf, Germany. Düsseldorf's Altstadt, or Old Town, covers a compact area south of the Rhine with narrow streets and traditional pubs. Locals call it 'the longest bar in the world' because of the concentration of breweries serving Altbier, a top-fermented ale particular to the region. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Free

Königsallee (The Kö)

The Königsallee, known as the Kö, is a tree-lined boulevard running along a canal in the city center. Luxury boutiques, department stores, and cafés line both sides. The avenue has been a shopping address since the 19th century and remains a focal point for fashion and leisure in western Germany.

€13

Rhine Tower (Rheinturm)

The Rhine Tower rises 240 meters above the Media Harbor and functions as a telecommunications mast. An observation deck at 168 meters provides views over the city, the Rhine, and on clear days the surrounding lowlands. A revolving restaurant operates in the tower's upper section.

€12

Schloss Benrath

Schloss Benrath is a pink Rococo palace completed in 1770, set in formal gardens and parkland south of the city center. The east wing houses the Museum of European Garden Art, and the west wing contains a natural history collection. The reflecting pool in front of the palace is a popular photo spot.

Free

Rheinuferpromenade

The Rheinuferpromenade is a landscaped walkway along the east bank of the Rhine, connecting the Altstadt to the modern harbor district. Cyclists and pedestrians share the path, and steps lead down to the water. Sunset views toward the Oberkassel district draw both residents and visitors.

Free

MedienHafen (Media Harbor)

MedienHafen (Media Harbor) is a waterfront area in Düsseldorf, Germany. The MedienHafen transformed a former industrial harbor into a district of offices, hotels, and restaurants. The area illustrates how Düsseldorf shifted from port commerce to media, design, and corporate headquarters after the 1990s. Quays and embankment paths form the main walking route.
