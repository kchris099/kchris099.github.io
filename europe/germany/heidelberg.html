Explore Heidelberg

A Brief History

Heidelberg, situated romantically along the lush banks of the Neckar River, is the absolute epitome of German Romanticism. Miraculously spared from the bombing of World War II, it retains its idyllic, winding Baroque old town and the majestic, haunting ruins of Heidelberg Castle, which overlook the city. As the home of Germany’s oldest university, founded in 1386, the city has long been a cultural haven for renowned philosophers, poets, and scientists, maintaining a vibrant, intellectual atmosphere amid breathtaking historic scenery.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

Old Bridge (Karl-Theodor-Brücke)

Next up in this guide to what to do in Heidelberg in one day is the historic Old Bridge, officially known as Karl-Theodor-Brücke. Built in 1788, this elegant stone bridge is a symbol of Heidelberg, bridging not just the two riverbanks, but also eras of history and culture. A leisurely walk across the bridge allows you to appreciate its architectural significance and the beautiful statues that adorn it, including Prince Elector Karl Theodor himself.

€ 15.00

Heidelberg Castle (Schloss Heidelberg)

Your 1 day itinerary Heidelberg will start at its most iconic point – Heidelberg Castle. Majestically perched on Königstuhl hill, it is an essential starting point for your Heidelberg day trip. This iconic landmark offers a window into the city’s rich history and its strategic importance in the region.

Varies

Heidelberg University

Heidelberg University, founded in 1386, is Germany’s oldest and one of Europe’s most prestigious universities. A visit here offers a fascinating glimpse into the academic heritage that has shaped the city. Explore the historic university library, which houses an impressive collection of old manuscripts and rare books.

Free

Market Square (Marktplatz)

Descending from the castle, venture into the heart of the Old Town where you’ll find the bustling Market Square, or Marktplatz. This historic square is a perfect spot to immerse yourself in the local culture. Surrounded by colorful baroque buildings and lively cafes, the square has been a central meeting place for centuries.

Free

Church of the Holy Spirit (Heiliggeistkirche)

Adjacent to Market Square is the impressive Church of the Holy Spirit, or Heiliggeistkirche. This grand church is a significant historical and architectural gem in Heidelberg. Dating back to the 14th century, it has served both Catholic and Protestant congregations over the centuries.

Free

Hauptstrasse (Main Street)

Next in this guide to places to visit in Heidelberg in one day, take a leisurely walk down Hauptstrasse, Heidelberg’s main pedestrian street. As one of Europe’s longest shopping streets, it stretches for over a mile through the Old Town. Hauptstrasse is lined with an array of shops, from high-end boutiques to charming local stores, offering everything from fashion and jewelry to souvenirs and books.
