Destination Guide — Southern Germany

Explore Mittenwald

A Brief History

Prospering in the Middle Ages as an essential staging post on the trade route between Venice and Augsburg, Mittenwald later became a legendary center for violin making. Its ornate frescoed facades and traditional crafts reflect a long legacy of Alpine mercantile wealth. The remarkable survival of these cultural and architectural milestones provides a powerful window into the region's storied past.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

Partnachklamm

Partnachklamm, located in Garmisch-Partenkirchen, is a narrow and picturesque gorge featuring waterfalls and a walkway, making it a popular destination for hikers. During winter, torch hiking tours are available in the area, offering an exciting way to experience the beauty of the gorge. The 703m long and 80m high Alpine wonder provides views of spraying waterfalls, rapids, and calm basins.

Free

Partnach Gorge

Partnach Gorge, located along the Partnach River, is a natural wonder with waterfalls and river rapids. To reach this beautiful place, take a train from Munich central station to Garmisch Partenkirchen and then a bus to the stadium before walking for about 10 minutes to the entrance. The trail through the gorge is relatively easy and offers views of the surrounding environment.

Free

Olympic Ski Jump

Nestled in the Bavarian Alps, the Olympic Ski Jump in Garmisch-Partenkirchen is a modern marvel built on the historic site of a 1920s ski jump. This impressive structure features a towering 100-meter start ramp and serves as one of the region's most popular attractions. Known for its rich history dating back to the 1936 Winter Olympics, it continues to be a hub for winter sports enthusiasts today.

Free

Kuhfluchtwasserfälle

Kuhflucht Waterfalls, located near Farchant in Germany, is a natural attraction featuring three steep and rocky waterfalls set amidst forested walking trails. These falls are among the tallest in the country, with a height of 270 meters. The area offers picturesque views of Garmisch and the Zugspitze, making it perfect for outdoor enthusiasts and photography lovers.

Free

Leutascher Geisterklamm und Wasserfallsteig

Leutasch Gorge, also known as Gleisterklamm, is a forested ravine featuring boardwalks and a viewing bridge that offer views of whirlpools, rapids, and a waterfall. The 970-meter-long series of steel walkways and bridges provide an exhilarating experience as you traverse the gorge. While exploring the area, consider visit the Klammgeist Restaurant located near the gorge for a delightful dining experience.

Free

Leutasch-Klamm Wasserfallsteig

Leutasch-Klamm Wasserfallsteig is a narrow gorge located partially in Germany and mostly in Austria, making it a must-visit highlight of Bavaria. The trail features paths, bridges, and cliff-hugging walkways offering views of rushing rapids below. It's an exciting and educational experience for both kids and adults, with opportunities to learn about nature and the importance of preservation. The ghost trail, goblin trail, and forest add to the adventure.

Free

Mittenwald Altstadt

The Altstadt (Old Town) of Mittenwald is famous for its beautifully painted houses, known as Lüftlmalerei, which depict religious and historical scenes. The historic center features cobblestone streets, traditional Bavarian architecture, and the prominent Church of St. Peter and Paul. The town is also renowned for its long history of violin making.

Free

Karwendel

The Karwendel is a major mountain range in the Northern Limestone Alps, accessible from Mittenwald via the Karwendelbahn cable car. The area offers extensive hiking and climbing routes in the summer, and ski touring in the winter. The rugged peaks and deep valleys provide a dramatic alpine landscape and a habitat for diverse mountain wildlife.

Free

Wankbahn

Wankbahn is a popular landmark in Garmisch-Partenkirchen, offering a summit plateau reached by cable car with panoramic views, hiking trails, and paragliding opportunities. Visitors can immerse themselves in the city's rich history and culture by exploring nearby attractions such as Partnachklamm, Graseckseilbahn, Burgruine Werdenfels, and Spielbank Garmisch-Partenkirchen.

Free

Garmisch Olympia Ice Sport Stadium

The Garmisch Olympia Ice Skating Rink is a great place to go ice skating if you're looking for a fun and adrenaline-filled experience. The rink is well maintained and has plenty of space for all types of skaters, from beginners to experts. Plus, the staff are friendly and trained in various skill ranges, so your family can have lots of fun no matter their level of expertise.
