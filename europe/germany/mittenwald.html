Mittenwald

Medieval pack trains between Venice and Augsburg stopped at Mittenwald. From the seventeenth century the town specialised in violin making, and frescoed façades advertised prosperous craft households. The Geigenbaumuseum and the painted street walls are the local evidence of Alpine trade turning into lutherie. Mathias Klotz is credited with bringing Tyrolean lutherie methods that local workshops adapted.

Partnachklamm

Partnachklamm cuts through the mountains south of Garmisch-Partenkirchen, where the Partnach River runs between steep rock walls. A secured path uses tunnels and bridges to pass waterfalls, rapids and narrow sections shaped by water and ice. The gorge forms part of the approach toward the Wetterstein mountains and has long connected local recreation with the alpine landscape. Moist rock and echoing water shape the enclosed sections.

Partnach Gorge

Partnach Gorge, located along the Partnach River, is a natural formation with waterfalls and river rapids. To reach this place, take a train from Munich central station to Garmisch Partenkirchen and then a bus to the stadium before walking for about 10 minutes to the entrance. The trail through the gorge is relatively easy and offers views of the surrounding environment.

Olympic Ski Jump

Mittenwald's Olympic ski jump recalls winter-sport facilities developed for the 1936 Olympic era in the Bavarian Alps. The structure still frames training and competition on the valley slopes. From below, the inrun stands out against the Karwendel ridges above town.

Kuhfluchtwasserfälle

Three linked cascades descend through forest above Farchant at the Kuhflucht waterfalls. A marked trail reaches lower viewpoints and climbs toward steeper sections, with water volume changing after rain and snowmelt. The often quoted 270-metre figure refers to the combined drop.

Leutascher Geisterklamm und Wasserfallsteig

Leutascher Geisterklamm und Wasserfallsteig follows steel walkways and bridges through a forested gorge on the German–Austrian edge near Mittenwald. About 970 metres of path overlook rapids, whirlpools, and a waterfall. A restaurant near the entrance serves hikers after the boardwalk circuit.

Leutasch-Klamm Wasserfallsteig

A narrow gorge straddles the German–Austrian border near Mittenwald, with marked tracks to viewing platforms above the water. Spray and rock walls define the walk more than a single cascade. Families use the secured path to follow the stream through the cleft.

Mittenwald Altstadt

Mittenwald's Altstadt is lined with Lüftlmalerei, painted facades showing religious and historical scenes on Alpine houses. Violin making has shaped the town's craft identity for centuries. Pedestrian lanes around the church and market keep the painted streetscape as the main visitor focus.

Karwendel

Karwendel is a broad limestone mountain range spanning the German-Austrian border. From Mittenwald, the Karwendelbahn reaches a high station above the Isar valley near hiking and climbing routes. Protected alpine terrain extends far beyond the lift area, with weather, snow, wildlife rules and route difficulty shaping travel through the range.

Wankbahn

A cable car climbs to a summit plateau used for hiking and paragliding above Garmisch-Partenkirchen. Trails fan out from the upper station across open Alpine ground. Nearby Partnachklamm, Graseckseilbahn, Burgruine Werdenfels, and the Spielbank sit in the same mountain basin below.

Garmisch Olympia Ice Sport Stadium

Garmisch-Partenkirchen's Olympic Ice Sports Centre contains several rinks used for public skating, ice hockey, curling, training and events. The complex developed from facilities associated with the 1936 Winter Olympics and has been modernized repeatedly.
