Moritzburg

Moritzburg is known for a baroque hunting lodge built for Augustus the Strong of Saxony on an island in a lake north of Dresden between 1722 and 1736. The ensemble includes pheasant gardens and a faux lighthouse on the shore. Earlier medieval fortifications gave way to the present palace. The surrounding town developed with rail links in the 19th century. The setting has appeared in films but remains a state museum.

Schloss Moritzburg

Schloss Moritzburg is a Baroque hunting lodge built on an artificial island in 1542 by Duke Moritz of Saxony. Augustus the Strong later remodeled it as a summer residence with opulent stucco interiors and a collection of hunting trophies and feather-decorated rooms. The setting among lakes and woods reflects Saxon court leisure traditions.

Schlosspark

The Schlosspark surrounds Moritzburg Castle with formal gardens that were later expanded into an English-style landscape park. Tree-lined avenues, ponds, and open lawns frame views of the castle from different angles. Paths connect the main island to adjacent ponds where pheasants and waterfowl are often seen.
