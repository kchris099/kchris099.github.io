Explore Mühlhausen

A Brief History

Mühlhausen became a free imperial city in the Middle Ages and joined the Hanseatic trade network. Its five Gothic churches include St. Mary's with a tall tower visible across the Thuringian plain. Thomas Müntzer preached here during the Peasants' War of 1525. The city later lay in Prussian and East German territory. Timber-framed houses and medieval gates survive in the old center, which suffered limited wartime damage.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

St. Mary's Church (Marienkirche)

St. Mary's Church is the second-largest church in Thuringia, with a tall spire visible across the old town. During the Peasants' War of 1525, the radical reformer Thomas Müntzer preached here to crowds of insurgents. The Gothic interior includes a large nave and historical memorials to the conflict.

Free

Divi Blasii Church

Divi Blasii Church is a Gothic parish church where Johann Sebastian Bach served as organist in 1707 before moving to Weimar. The building houses historic organs and a vaulted interior typical of Thuringian church architecture. Concerts and organ recitals continue the town's musical tradition.

Free

Historic City Walls

Mühlhausen's medieval walls still encircle much of the old town, with several towers open to visitors. Walkways along the ramparts give views of red-tiled roofs and church spires. The fortifications were expanded in the 13th and 14th centuries when Mühlhausen became a Free Imperial City.

Free

Popperode Spring

The Popperode Spring lies just outside the city walls and has supplied drinking water to Mühlhausen for centuries. A 19th-century well house shelters the source, and a short path through meadows leads to the site. The spring remains part of the town's water supply system today.

Free

Mühlhausen Town Hall

Mühlhausen Town Hall grew over several centuries around a central courtyard, combining Gothic, Renaissance, and Baroque elements. The building houses municipal offices and historical archives. Its arcaded courtyard and stepped gables face the central market area of the old town.

€12

Peasants' War Museum (Kornmarktkirche)

The Peasants' War Museum is housed in the former Kornmarktkirche, a Franciscan church converted after the Reformation. Exhibits cover the 1525 uprising, Thomas Müntzer's role, and the social conditions that fueled the revolt. Mühlhausen was one of the main centers of the movement in Thuringia.
