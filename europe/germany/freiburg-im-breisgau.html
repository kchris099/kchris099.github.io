Freiburg Im Breisgau

Freiburg was founded in 1120 by the Zähringen dukes as a market town at the edge of the Black Forest. Its Gothic minster and Bächle street channels mark medieval planning. Habsburg rule, French occupations, and Baden statehood followed. Heavy bombing in 1944 destroyed much of the centre, rebuilt on the old street plan. The university, dating from 1457, and surrounding vineyards keep Freiburg a regional capital of Baden-Württemberg.

Freiburg Cathedral

Freiburg Minster dominates Münsterplatz with a Gothic tower whose openwork stone spire rises above the old town. Construction extended from the Romanesque period into later medieval centuries, producing sculpted portals, stained glass, chapels, choir art, and a market-facing exterior marked by red sandstone. The church remains active, while tower access and services govern when different interior areas can be seen.

Bächle

As visitors walk around the Old Town, visitors won't be able to miss Bächle, the little waterways that flow through the streets. These were created in 1170 from the Dreisam River to provide industrial and cleaning water to the town as well as a way to stop fires, although they were also used as open sewers. These days, there are still 15.5 kilometres of these channels, some of which are underground.

Augustinermuseum

A former Augustinian monastery provides the architectural setting for Freiburg's Augustinermuseum. Medieval sculpture, cathedral figures, stained glass, paintings, decorative arts and later regional works appear across the old church, cloister areas and modern interventions. Large objects are displayed at multiple heights, helping relate museum fragments to the ecclesiastical spaces for which many were made.

Altstadt (Old Town)

Water runs through narrow Bachle channels beside the lanes of Freiburg's pedestrian old town. The minster and market form its historic core, joined by surviving gates and civic buildings. University facilities, reconstructed facades and residential streets reveal later layers, while wartime destruction and rebuilding explain why medieval fragments stand beside modern commercial life.

Bertoldsbrunnen

Bertoldsbrunnen marks one of Freiburg's busiest tram and pedestrian junctions with an abstract equestrian monument. Nikolaus Röslmeir's 1965 work replaced an earlier statue destroyed during the Second World War and commemorates the Zähringen duke associated with the city's foundation. Constant traffic and meeting activity make the site more useful as an orientation point than as a quiet sculpture garden.

Martinstor

Martinstor began as a medieval gate in Freiburg's first city wall and received its much taller present profile during late nineteenth-century reconstruction. A clock, steep roof, arched passage, and adjoining commercial buildings now frame tram and pedestrian movement through the old center; the surviving lower masonry is older than the upper tower, a distinction often obscured when the whole structure is called original.

Stadtgarten Freiburg

Stadtgarten Freiburg is a central park linked to the Schlossberg by a pedestrian bridge into forested hillside paths. Lawns, play areas, and tree cover give the city a green pause near the historic core. Views open toward the cathedral tower from higher garden edges.

Aussichtsturm Schlossberg

Steel latticework forms the Schlossbergturm above Freiburg on the wooded Schlossberg. A long climb through the hill's paths and then up the open stair reaches views across the city, Rhine plain, Vosges, and black Forest when weather is clear. There is no funicular railway to the tower itself; a small inclined lift reaches a lower hillside point, after which walking remains necessary.

Dreisamstadion

Dreisamstadion is the long-time football ground of SC Freiburg beside the Dreisam river, with stands close to the pitch and Black Forest slopes visible beyond. The compact venue is known for atmosphere more than luxury fittings. Matchdays dominate the neighbourhood calendar.

Messe Freiburg

Messe Freiburg is the city's exhibition and events campus for trade fairs, conferences, concerts, markets, and festivals. Halls and outdoor areas host rotating programmes from food shows to music. It functions as a flexible civic venue rather than a single-purpose stadium.

Vita Classica Therme

Vita Classica Therme in Bad-Krozingen, just a short train ride from Freiburg, is a luxurious wellness complex featuring both indoor and outdoor thermal pools with massage jets, as well as a whirlpool and sauna. The spa offers a dreamy setting inspired by ancient Rome. Despite some noise from children in the jacuzzi area, the facilities are modern and affordably priced. The hygienic and impeccably clean environment is complemented by outstanding customer service.

Steinwasen Park

In Oberried, about twenty minutes from Freiburg, families find more than thirty animal species and a long adventure rope bridge. Paths and lawns link the enclosures. The hillside site mixes wildlife viewing with play structures rather than a formal city park.

Schauinslandbahn

The Schauinslandbahn carries gondolas from Horben to high slopes of Schauinsland south of Freiburg. Opened in 1930 and later modernized, the line provides access to hiking routes, mountain views and winter conditions without driving to the summit area.
