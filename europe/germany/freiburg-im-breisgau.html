Explore Freiburg Im Breisgau

A Brief History

Freiburg Im Breisgau lies in Germany. Founded in 1120 as a free market town, Freiburg grew on medieval silver mining and the establishment of its known university in 1457. Rebuilt after severe World War II bombing, the city successfully merged its Gothic heritage with pioneering environmental modernism. Museums, markets, and street plans in Freiburg Im Breisgau still show layers from those periods.

Top Sights & Activities

Freiburg Cathedral

Freiburg Cathedral is a religious building in Freiburg Im Breisgau, Germany. Freiburg Cathedral (Freiburger Münster) is the well known landmark of Freiburg im Breisgau, known for detailed Gothic architecture and its towering 116-meter spire. The exterior and main interior spaces are usually viewable outside service times.

Free

Bächle

As visitors walk around the Old Town, visitors won't be able to miss Bächle, the little waterways that flow through the streets. These were created in 1170 from the Dreisam River to provide industrial and cleaning water to the town as well as a way to stop fires, although they were also used as open sewers. These days, there are still 15.5 kilometres of these channels, some of which are underground.

Free

Rathausplatz (Town Hall Square)

A areas to visit in Freiburg is Rathausplatz or Town Hall Square. It's a great spot to take a time out from exploring, have a drink and soak in the atmosphere. Don't miss the Old Town Hall which dates back to the 16th century and is Built in the Renaissance style in red.

€12

Augustinermuseum

Augustinermuseum is a visitor site in Freiburg Im Breisgau. Art lovers will not want to miss the Augustiner Museum particularly if visitors are looking for things to do in Freiburg on a rainy day as this is a great option. This museum showcases work from the Middle Ages to the 19th century in a former Augustinian monastery. The building itself has been well renovated making a great home for this collection.

Free

Altstadt (Old Town)

The Altstadt of Freiburg is known for its beautifully preserved historic buildings and atmosphere. The pedestrianized streets are filled with shops, cafes, and the well known Bächle water channels. The compact center links Freiburg Cathedral, Rathausplatz, historic lanes, shops, and museums, making it a practical walking area for exploring the city's older quarters and surviving medieval gates.

Free

Bertoldsbrunnen

Located in the historic old town of Freiburg, Bertoldsbrunnen is an abstract equestrian monument that was unveiled in 1965 to replace a 19th-century statue destroyed during World War II. The statue is named after Bertold III, who founded Freiburg as a free city in 1120.

Free

Martinstor

Martinstor is a reconstructed gate tower originally built for defense in the early 13th century, featuring a large clock. It is one of the surviving gates of the former city fortifications in Freiburg. The oldest part of a 66-meter-high tower, Martinstor was rebuilt at the end of the 19th century to accommodate traffic flow.

Free

Stadtgarten Freiburg

Stadtgarten Freiburg is a visitor site in Freiburg Im Breisgau, Germany. The Stadtgarten is a central public park in Freiburg, providing a peaceful green space for residents and visitors. The park is connected to the Schlossberg via a pedestrian bridge, offering easy access to the forested hill and its panoramic views. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Free

Aussichtsturm Schlossberg

Aussichtsturm Schlossberg, also known as Schlossbergturm, is a steel observation tower located atop the Schlossberg hill in Freiburg. With 153 steps to climb, it offers panoramic views of the city and its surroundings, including the Black Forest mountains and the Rhine valley. Visitors can reach the top by taking the Schlossbergbahn funicular railway or hiking scenic trails.

€8

Dreisamstadion

Dreisamstadion is a visitor site in Freiburg Im Breisgau, Germany. Dreisamstadion is a popular attraction in Freiburg im Breisgau, offering a unique experience for visitors. While the infrastructure and service may be considered prehistoric, the stadium's charm lies in its history and surroundings, with the mountains and Black Forest adding to its special atmosphere. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Free

Messe Freiburg

Messe Freiburg is a visitor site in Freiburg Im Breisgau, Germany. Messe Freiburg is a versatile event venue and exhibition center in the city, hosting a wide array of trade shows, conferences, cultural events, food festivals, tech fairs, flea markets, music festivals and theater performances. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

€22

Vita Classica Therme

Vita Classica Therme in Bad-Krozingen, just a short train ride from Freiburg, is a luxurious wellness complex featuring both indoor and outdoor thermal pools with massage jets, as well as a whirlpool and sauna. The spa offers a dreamy setting inspired by ancient Rome. Despite some noise from children in the jacuzzi area, the facilities are modern and affordably priced. The hygienic and impeccably clean environment is complemented by outstanding customer service.

€29

Steinwasen Park

Steinwasen Park is a public park in Freiburg Im Breisgau, Germany. Steinwasen Park, located in Oberried just 20 minutes from Freiburg, offers a variety of attractions for families. Visitors can enjoy observing over 30 different animal species and walking across the world's largest adventure rope bridge. Paths, lawns, and shaded areas make up the main public facilities.

€15

Schauinslandbahn

The Schauinslandbahn is a gondola lift that has been in operation since 1930 and is the longest continuous motion cable car in Germany. It provides easy access to the Schauinsland, one of the highest mountains in the Black Forest and a popular destination near Freiburg. The ride offers great views and a pleasant experience, with not too crowded cars and short wait times.
