Explore Goslar

A Brief History

Goslar prospered from silver mines in the Harz Mountains after their discovery around 968, becoming an imperial residence for German kings. The Romanesque Kaiserpfalz and timber-framed old town reflect that medieval wealth. Mining declined in the 16th century but left Rammelsberg ore works, later a museum. The historic town and mines were inscribed as a UNESCO World Heritage site in 1992.

Top Sights & Activities

€15

Imperial Palace (Kaiserpfalz)

When you visit the Imperial Palace in Goslar, you’re stepping back into medieval Germany. This reconstructed structure, originally Built in the 11th century, was once the seat of power for German emperors. As you walk through the grand halls, imagine the decisions that shaped the Holy Roman Empire being made within these walls.

Free

Altstadt

Wandering through Goslar’s Old Town is like stepping into a fairytale. Its cobblestone streets are lined with half-timbered houses and quaint shops. As you stroll, you’ll find architectural treasures at every turn, each telling its own story from the Hanseatic period to modern times.

Free

Domvorhalle

The Domvorhalle is a historically significant site that once belonged to the majestic Goslar Cathedral, or Kaiserpfalz. Although the cathedral itself no longer stands, the Domvorhalle gives you a sense of the grandeur that once was. This vestibule, with its Romanesque architecture and atmospheric remnants, invites you to imagine the splendor of the medieval era.

€12

Tin Figure Museum (Zinnfiguren-Museum)

At the Tin Figure Museum, immerse yourself in a miniature world rich with history and artistry. Located in the old tower of the Imperial Palace, this unique museum showcases an impressive collection of meticulously crafted tin figures. These miniature artworks depict scenes from various historical periods, local folklore, and everyday life, offering you a glimpse into the past through captivating displays.

€12

Goslar Museum (Goslarer Museum)

At the Goslar Museum, you’ll dive deep into the rich tapestry of the town’s history and culture. The museum is housed in a beautiful historic building that itself is part of the story. As you explore, you’ll find exhibitions that span from prehistoric times to modern-day Goslar.
