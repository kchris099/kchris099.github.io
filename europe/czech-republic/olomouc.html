Olomouc

Olomouc served as the historical capital of Moravia until the seventeenth century, standing as a primary center of religious and political power in the Czech lands. The city was severely damaged during the Thirty Years' War when occupied by Swedish forces, leading to its subsequent reconstruction in a triumphant Baroque style. As a major fortress city under the Habsburgs and the seat of an archbishopric, it attracted prominent artists and scholars. Today, the city's ecclesiastical wealth is visible in the Holy Trinity Column, Saint Wenceslas Cathedral, and numerous Baroque fountains.

Hradisko Monastery

The Hradisko Monastery, located in the northern part of Olomouc, Czech Republic, is a historic building with a rich and varied past. Originally established as a Benedictine monastery in 1078, it later came under the ownership of the Premonstratensian order in the mid-12th century.

Upper Square

Upper Square, or Horni Namesti, is a must-visit destination for anyone exploring Olomouc. This active square is brimming with architectural marvels that tell the city's rich history. At its heart stands the 15th-century baroque Town Hall, complete with a unique astronomical clock that has an intriguing past shaped by both war and ideology.

49.5939° N / 17.2509° E

Olomouc Astronomical Clock

Olomouc's astronomical clock occupies an arched recess on the town hall facade. A medieval mechanism existed here for centuries, but wartime damage led to a 1950s redesign by Karel Svolinský using mosaics of workers, scientists, athletes, and rural labor. At noon, moving figures emerge to a musical sequence, making the clock a distinct example of socialist-era public art within an older civic building.

49.5941° N / 17.2515° E

Church of St. Maurice

The Church of St. Maurice stands near Olomouc's Upper Square, where asymmetrical towers mark a Gothic building developed mainly during the fifteenth and sixteenth centuries. Its broad hall interior contains chapels, sculpture and a large historic organ associated with the city's musical life. A tower stair may open views across the center, while worship and concerts continue below.

Marian Column

Olomouc's Marian Column, raised in 1723 on Dolni namesti, gives thanks for the city's survival of a plague outbreak. The tall Baroque pillar and surrounding sculptures occupy the lower square among cafes and townhouses. It complements the Holy Trinity Column nearby as part of the city's plague-monument heritage.

49.5922° N / 17.2528° E

Andrův stadium

Andrův stadium is a unique venue, featuring residential apartments as part of its stands and a strikingly curved opposing section. This unconventional design creates an authentic Czech football atmosphere that appeals to fans. The stadium is known for offering affordable beer and wurst, adding to the overall enjoyable experience. Despite not being overly spacious, the stadium has beauty and charm that any football enthusiast would appreciate.

Zoo Olomouc

Zoo Olomouc houses more than 1,800 animals from over 350 species and takes part in European Endangered Species Programme breeding efforts. Wooded park grounds spread across a hillside above the city, with paths linking large mammal yards and smaller indoor exhibits.

Unofficial Minor Basilica of the Visitation of the Virgin Mary on Svatý Kopeček

The Basilica of the Visitation of the Virgin Mary crowns Svatý Kopeček northeast of Olomouc. Premonstratensians developed the Baroque pilgrimage complex in the seventeenth and eighteenth centuries around a revered Marian image. Twin towers, curved wings, frescoes, sculpture, gilded altars and surrounding cloisters create a ceremonial approach, while terraces overlook the plains below the hill.

49.6292° N / 17.3381° E

Saint Wenceslas Cathedral Olomouc

St Wenceslas Cathedral rises within Olomouc's former castle precinct, where a Romanesque church was founded in the twelfth century. Gothic rebuilding and a nineteenth-century neo-Gothic restoration produced its tall spires, pointed arches, chapels, and long interior; the adjoining chapter buildings and archaeological remains connect the cathedral with Moravian church government and the 1306 murder of King Wenceslas III nearby.

49.5978° N / 17.2626° E

Archbishop's Palace

Olomouc's Archbishop's Palace developed beside the cathedral as the residence and administrative seat of Moravian bishops and archbishops. Baroque state rooms, staircases, portraits, furniture, and ceremonial halls recall visits by rulers and major political events.
