Kutna Hora

Kutná Hora mined silver that financed Bohemian kings and minted Prague groschen from the thirteenth century. St. Barbara's Church, Italian Court, and Sedlec ossuary record Gothic patronage and medieval Central European mining wealth east of Prague. Silver-mine tours and ossuary chapels extend Gothic patronage through the Bohemian plateau east of Prague.

Sedlec Ossuary

Human bones from the overcrowded cemetery of Sedlec were arranged inside the lower chapel of the Church of All Saints during the nineteenth century. Garlands, pyramids, a chandelier, and the Schwarzenberg coat of arms use remains from thousands of individuals in a deliberate funerary design. The ossuary belongs to a consecrated religious site, so its meaning centers on mortality and burial history.

02 AdmissionCZK 220

Cathedral of Assumption of Our Lady and St. John the Baptist

Cistercians founded the Church of the Assumption in Sedlec around the turn of the fourteenth century, introducing ambitious Gothic forms to Bohemia. Hussite forces burned the monastery, and jan Blažej Santini-Aichel later rebuilt the church in a Baroque Gothic style; high vaults, a long light-filled nave, art, and monastic history connect it with nearby Kutná Hora and the historic cultural landscape.

49.9598° N / 15.2902° E

03 AdmissionCZK 180

St Barbara's Church

Silver-mining wealth financed St Barbara's Church on the ridge above Kutna Hora beginning in 1388. Construction continued in stages for centuries, producing a late Gothic interior of ribbed vaults, chapels, murals and stained glass linked to miners and the town's religious life. Nineteenth- and early twentieth-century work completed the present roofline and western facade after long periods of interruption.

49.9449° N / 15.2636° E

04 AdmissionCZK 100

Jesuit College

In Kutna Hora, visitors can explore the Gothic Cathedral of St. Barbara, the Baroque Jesuit College adorned with statues reminiscent of Prague's Charles Bridge, and the Silver Museum featuring a 280-meter mining tunnel. The area also includes historic sites like Vlassky dvur, Gothic churches, and Sedlec with its unique ossuary chapel decorated with 40,000 human remains.

49.9462° N / 15.2641° E

Gothic Stone Fountain

A twelve-sided stone reservoir stands in Rejsek Square as evidence of Kutná Hora's medieval struggle to secure drinking water. Master Matěj Rejsek designed the richly carved Gothic fountain in the 1490s, feeding it through wooden pipes from a distant spring; the roof no longer survives, but the high walls and decorative stonework show that the structure was both public infrastructure and a civic monument.

49.9488° N / 15.2639° E

06 AdmissionFree

Plague Column

Kutna Hora's plague column, built between 1713 and 1715, commemorates victims of the epidemic that struck the silver town. Modelled on Vienna's Holy Trinity column, the baroque monument rises above a square near the historic centre with saints and clouds carved in stone.

49.9494° N / 15.2669° E

07 AdmissionCZK 260

Italian Court Kutna Hora

The Italian Court developed from a fortified royal residence into the central mint of medieval Bohemia after silver transformed Kutná Hora. Coiners produced the Prague groschen here under royal supervision and later halls hosted government and ceremonial functions; courtyards, a chapel, mint exhibits and demonstrations explain the connection between mining, currency, monarchy and the growth of the town.

49.9483° N / 15.2683° E

08 AdmissionCZK 150

Bobsleigh Kutna Hora

A stainless-steel alpine coaster descends a hillside on the edge of Kutná Hora, using individual carts whose riders control speed with hand brakes. The track adds a modern recreation stop to a destination better known for medieval and Baroque monuments, and other family activities may share the grounds, but the gravity-driven run through the slope remains the site's defining feature.

49.9455° N / 15.2782° E

Sion castle ruins

Sion's ruined walls near Kutna Hora keep remains that include war carriages and a statue of Jan Rohac of Dube. When open, routes follow courtyards and towers of the former stronghold. The hilltop fragments read as a military site tied to Hussite-era memory rather than a rebuilt palace.

49.8892° N / 15.2110° E

Kačina

Kacina is an early-nineteenth-century Empire-style chateau east of Kutna Hora, with a long central block and pavilion wings set in parkland. Interiors present period rooms and museum displays, while the exterior reads as a formal neoclassical country seat.
