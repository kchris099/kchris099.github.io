Destination Guide — Telč

Explore Telč

A Brief History

Telč was transformed after 1550 when Renaissance nobleman Zacharias of Hradec rebuilt his water-surrounded chateau and ordered burgher houses with arcades around a triangular square following a fire. The uniform façades and ponds reflect Italianate planning on a Moravian crossroads between Vienna and Prague. Subsequent Habsburg and Czechoslovak rule left the town a county seat whose historic core survived with little modern intrusion, preserving one of Bohemia-Moravia's most intact Renaissance ensembles.

Top Sights & Activities

CZK 260

Telč Chateau

Telč Chateau is a Renaissance water castle on the edge of Zacharias of Hradec Square, rebuilt after 1550 by the nobleman who reshaped the town. State rooms, a chapel, and museum displays record Moravian court life under Habsburg rule. The complex anchors the UNESCO-listed historic core and opens onto the chateau garden.

CZK 30

Observation Tower Oslednice

The Oslednice observation tower rises on a wooded hill south of Telč's old town. A short climb rewards visitors with views over the chateau roofs, ponds, and the Vysočina countryside. The timber structure is a popular local lookout above the historic square.

CZK 150

Roštejn Castle

Roštejn Castle is a Gothic hunting lodge with a tall octagonal tower in forested hills north of Telč. Built for the nobility in the 14th century and later fortified, it served as a seasonal residence and lookout over the border woodlands. The tower and chapel remain the main surviving elements of the medieval seat.

Free

Štamberk (Sternberg)

Štamberk is a ruined castle above the forested valley near Telč, named for the Sternberg family who held the site in the medieval period. Low walls and foundations mark the former keep and residential wings. The ruin offers a quiet woodland walk with glimpses of the surrounding Moravian hills.
