Ostrava

Ostrava expanded in the nineteenth century when coal seams and ironworks of the Moravian-Silesian basin drew migrants to furnaces owned by the Rothschilds and later Czechoslovak state firms. Shafts, slag heaps, and workers' districts clustered at the Ostravice–Odra confluence, peaking under socialism before deindustrialisation. Heritage routes through Dolní Vítkovice and the mining museum interpret that heavy-industry border city near Poland.

Ostrava Zoo

Forested slopes and broad walking routes surround animal habitats at Ostrava Zoo in the city's eastern district; botanical trails and large grounds make movement part of the visit, while exhibits, animal numbers and access change as enclosures are renovated. The collection includes African and Asian mammals, birds, reptiles and regional species, with conservation breeding coordinated through European programs.

Halda Ema

Halda Ema is a 315-metre spoil heap from Ostrava's mining era, now climbed for views over the industrial city and surrounding hills. The ascent takes roughly 45 to 60 minutes from central districts and draws walkers and mountain bikers to the bare summit ridge.

Ostravar Aréna

Ostravar Arena seats about 10,000 for ice hockey and concerts and is home to HC Vitkovice Ridera, a club with roots back to 1928. The venue hosted world championship games in 2004 and 2015, and the season runs from autumn into spring in the Vitkovice district.

04 AdmissionFree

Forum Nová Karolina

Forum Nová Karolina opened on redeveloped industrial land between Ostrava's historic center and the former Vítkovice works. A glass-fronted complex contains retailers, restaurants, a cinema, supermarket, fitness facilities, services and parking. Its value is primarily contemporary shopping and leisure, though the location illustrates how former mining and factory districts have been folded into the modern city.

05 AdmissionFree

Antonín Dvořák Theatre

Opened in 1907 as a German-language municipal theater, the Antonín Dvořák Theatre later became part of Ostrava's National Moravian-Silesian Theatre; changes in name and repertory reflect the city's Czech, German, industrial and postwar history. A neo-Baroque auditorium, decorative facade, balconies and stage facilities support opera, ballet, drama and musical productions.

The lower Vítkovice Area

Lower Vitkovice preserves blast furnaces, a gasometer, and related ironworks structures as a cultural and education campus in Ostrava. Converted halls host concerts, museums, and science exhibits among the blackened industrial frames that once defined the city's steel economy.

The Small World Of Technology U6 appears in Ostrava civic listings as a regional commerce and browsing stop. Standard market hours cover entry from surrounding streets. People often combine the halls with nearby food vendors and craft stalls on the same outing.

Science & Technology Centrum

Interactive galleries at Ostrava's Science and Technology Center explain energy, engineering, medicine, nature, and everyday physics through experiments and large installations. The museum occupies the Lower Vítkovice industrial area beside former blast furnaces and mining structures, and its exhibits use the surrounding factory heritage as context, but the center itself is a modern educational facility.

09 AdmissionCZK 320

Bolt Tower

Bolt Tower rises above Blast Furnace No. 1 in the Lower Vítkovice industrial complex. Architect Josef Pleskot added a steel-and-glass superstructure and elevated walkways to the decommissioned furnace, allowing guided access through machinery once used in iron production. A cafe and lookout occupy the upper level, where views extend across Ostrava's factories, housing districts, and surrounding hills.

49.8196° N / 18.2768° E

DinoPark Ostrava

Full-size moving dinosaur models line outdoor paths at DinoPark Ostrava near Doubrava. Sound effects, play areas, a small train, film presentations, and fossil-themed displays create a family amusement park. The grounds are seasonal and exposed to weather, while the creatures represent popular reconstructions that may not reflect the latest research in every detail.

49.8524° N / 18.4557° E
