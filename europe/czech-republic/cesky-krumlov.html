Český Krumlov

Český Krumlov developed around a bend of the Vltava beneath a castle that became a major seat of the Rosenberg and later Schwarzenberg families from the 13th century. Its preserved old town, church towers, and cloister districts record Bohemian urban life through Gothic, Renaissance, and Baroque rebuilding under Habsburg rule. The castle theatre and bear moat recall court entertainments of the early modern period. Since the Velvet Revolution, heritage conservation and cultural festivals have renewed the town as a living museum of Central European townscape without heavy industrial overlay.

State Castle & Chateau Český Krumlov

Český Krumlov Castle grew from a Gothic core into a large residence with Renaissance and Baroque wings above the Vltava bend. State rooms, a theater, and tower provide views over the old town. The complex remained a noble seat until the 20th century and is now a national heritage site.

Cloak Bridge

The Cloak Bridge is a multi-level covered walkway connecting castle buildings across a deep ravine. Built in the 18th century, its arcades and stairs allowed movement between the upper castle and theater without descending to the valley. The structure is a distinctive element of the castle silhouette.

St. Jost Church

The former Church of St Jost stands in Český Krumlov's Latrán quarter below the castle. Founded in the medieval period and rebuilt several times, it ceased regular religious use after reforms under Emperor Joseph II. A tall tower and surviving Gothic and Renaissance fabric remain visible, while shops and private uses now occupy much of the complex.

Budějovická Gate

Budějovická Gate survives in the Latrán fortifications on the road toward České Budějovice. The tower and passage once controlled entry to the district between castle and river. Pedestrians still use it as a northern approach into Český Krumlov's historic quarter.

Kláštery Český Krumlov

The monastery district in Český Krumlov includes buildings of the former Minorite and other religious houses near the castle gardens. Cloisters and courts illustrate monastic presence in the town from the medieval period. Parts of the area are open for cultural use and public walks.

Church of St. Vitus

St Vitus Church in Český Krumlov's inner town has a tall Gothic nave with net vaulting begun in the fifteenth century and finished in later phases. It faces the main square below the castle rock. The parish remains the historic centre's principal church for services and quiet visits.

The Castle Garden

Terraces extend west of Český Krumlov Castle through a large Baroque garden created in the seventeenth century; the revolving theater occupies part of the grounds, while winter closures and restoration work can limit access to paths and ornamental areas. Formal parterres, fountains, wooded sections, ponds, and the Bellaria summerhouse organize a gradual movement away from the palace.

Maiden stone

The Maiden Stone viewpoint above Cesky Krumlov is reached by a long stair to an observation deck over the Old Town's red roofs and the bend of the Vltava. The perch sits outside the main castle circuit, giving a wide angle on the river loop and packed historic core.

Český Krumlov Castle Tower

Painted Renaissance decoration wraps the cylindrical castle tower above Český Krumlov's Latrán district. The lower structure has medieval origins, while sixteenth-century rebuilding created the arcaded upper gallery and colorful facade associated with the town's skyline. An internal stair leads to an open viewing level over castle courtyards, the Vltava bends, church roofs, and dense old-town streets.

Lazebnický most

Lazebnický most, or Lazebnick Bridge, crosses toward views of Český Krumlov's town roofs and castle. Shops and restaurants cluster around the bridge approaches. Boat and raft trips on the river give another angle on the same bend, useful on hot summer days.

Stations of the Cross

A sequence of small devotional stations climbs Křížová hora above Český Krumlov toward the Chapel of Our Lady of Sorrows and the Holy Cross; the route developed as a pilgrimage path in the eighteenth century and passes through open slopes and woodland. Views back toward the castle and town appear along the ascent, but the religious landscape extends beyond a single hilltop photo point.

Fountain and Plague Column

On Cesky Krumlov's town square, a fountain stands near the Marian plague column raised in the early eighteenth century after a local epidemic. The baroque column's saints and clouds rise above market paving, marking civic thanks for the plague's end.

Regional Museum in the Czech Krumlov

Collections inside Český Krumlov's Regional Museum trace settlement, craft, trade, religious life and social change across southern Bohemia. The museum occupies a former Jesuit seminary above the old town and includes archaeological material, art, household objects and historical documents; a large ceramic model reconstructs the town's historic form, helping relate individual buildings to the river, castle and surrounding hills.

48.8107° N / 14.3177° E

14 AdmissionFree

Museum Fotoatelier Seidel

Josef Seidel and his son František photographed families, landscapes, businesses and everyday life across the Šumava region from their Český Krumlov studio. The preserved house contains glass negatives, cameras, darkrooms, backdrops, furniture and business records. Exhibits explain both photographic technique and the multilingual communities documented before war and border changes transformed the region.
