Destination Guide — Český Krumlov

Explore Český Krumlov

A Brief History

Český Krumlov developed around a bend of the Vltava beneath a castle that became a major seat of the Rosenberg and later Schwarzenberg families from the 13th century. Its preserved old town, church towers, and cloister districts record Bohemian urban life through Gothic, Renaissance, and Baroque rebuilding under Habsburg rule. The castle theatre and bear moat recall court entertainments of the early modern period. Since the Velvet Revolution, heritage conservation and cultural festivals have renewed the town as a living museum of Central European townscape without heavy industrial overlay.

Top Sights & Activities

300 CZK

State Castle & Chateau Český Krumlov

Český Krumlov Castle grew from a Gothic core into a large residence with Renaissance and Baroque wings above the Vltava bend. State rooms, a theater, and tower provide views over the old town. The complex remained a noble seat until the 20th century and is now a national heritage site.

Free (interior tour 260 CZK)

Cloak Bridge

The Cloak Bridge is a multi-level covered walkway connecting castle buildings across a deep ravine. Built in the 18th century, its arcades and stairs allowed movement between the upper castle and theater without descending to the valley. The structure is a distinctive element of the castle silhouette.

Free

St. Jost Church

St. Jost is a Gothic church with a tall tower in the Latrán quarter below the castle. Founded in the 14th century, it served parish and guild communities outside the inner town walls. The tower remains a landmark when approaching the old center from the north.

Free

Budějovická Gate

Budějovická Gate is a surviving town gate in the Latrán fortifications on the road toward České Budějovice. The tower and passage controlled entry to the district between the castle and river. It forms part of the pedestrian route into the historic quarter from the north.

Free

Kláštery Český Krumlov

The monastery district in Český Krumlov includes buildings of the former Minorite and other religious houses near the castle gardens. Cloisters and courts illustrate monastic presence in the town from the medieval period. Parts of the area are open for cultural use and public walks.

Free

Church of St. Vitus

St. Vitus Church is a Gothic parish church in the inner town with a tall nave and net vaulting. Construction began in the 15th century and continued across later phases. The church anchors the main square and remains the principal religious building of the historic center.

Free

The Castle Garden

The Castle Garden in Český Krumlov is a sprawling 17th-century baroque garden surrounding the State Castle. It offers a sense of calmness and tranquility, with its symmetrical and well-groomed layout. The historical landmark boasts impeccable maintenance and views, making it a must-visit. The garden features cut hedges, a fountain, and areas dedicated to horse training.

Free

Maiden stone

The Český Krumlov Castle Tower is a Renaissance structure that stands as a prominent feature of the town's skyline. With its long staircase leading to an observation deck, travellers are rewarded with views of the charming streets and red-roofed buildings below, along with the winding Vltava River.

280 CZK

Český Krumlov Castle Tower

The Český Krumlov Castle Tower is the most recognizable landmark of the historic town, featuring a vibrant Renaissance facade painted in pastel colors. Visitors can climb the 162 steps to the viewing gallery, which offers expansive panoramic views of the castle complex, the winding Vltava River, and the red-roofed houses of the medieval town.

Free

Lazebnický most

Lazebnický most, also known as Lazebnick bridge, is a picturesque spot in the Czech Republic that offers views of the town and its castle. The area around the bridge is dotted with charming shops and restaurants, making it a great place to explore. For a different perspective of the city, travellers can take a boat or raft ride along the river, which is also perfect for cooling off on hot summer days.

Free

Stations of the Cross

The Stations of the Cross is a hilltop viewpoint in Cesky Krumlov that offers a vista of the city and its surroundings. The walk to reach this spot takes about 25-30 minutes from the city center, with the second half involving a climb up a hill. While some parts of the path leading to the top are currently under construction, travellers can still enjoy sitting on the lawn and taking in the magnificent view.

Free

Fountain and Plague Column

In the heart of the town, find the Fountain and Plague Column, two remarkable landmarks in the main square. The fountain, a example of Baroque architecture from the 17th century, boasts intricate carvings and a crowning statue of the Virgin Mary. Nearby stands the Plague Column, also known as the Marian Column, erected in gratitude for the end of a devastating plague in the early 18th century.

Free

Regional Museum in the Czech Krumlov

The Regional Museum in the Czech Krumlov, located in a former school building, offers an insightful journey through the regional and local history of Cesky Krumlov. Housed in a rebuilt Baroque structure of the former Jesuit College, it showcases collections and exhibits tracing the area's history from prehistoric times to the late nineteenth century. One of its highlights is an impressive ceramic model of the city, claimed to be the largest of its kind globally.

Free

Museum Fotoatelier Seidel

Museum Fotoatelier Seidel is a must-visit for photography enthusiasts, offering a glimpse into the 19th-century roots of European photography. Housed in the original studios of Josef and Frantisek Seidels, this captivating museum showcases historic photos, glass plate negatives, cameras, and darkroom equipment. The preserved studio provides an immersive experience with its original furnishings and personal artifacts.
