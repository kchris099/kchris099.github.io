Prague

Prague became the seat of Bohemian kings and, from 1344, an archbishopric centered on Prague Castle above the Vltava River. Charles IV expanded the New Town and founded Charles University in 1348. Hussite wars and Habsburg rule added Gothic, Renaissance, and baroque fabric across the river districts. Czechoslovak independence in 1918 and later division in 1993 kept Prague as the national capital; its historic center is a UNESCO site.

Prague Castle

Prague Castle is a castle complex in Prague, Czech Republic serving as the official residence and workplace of the president of the Czech Republic. Built in the 9th century, the castle has long served as the seat of power for kings of Bohemia, Holy Roman emperors, and presidents of Czechoslovakia. As such, the term "Prague Castle" or simply "Hrad" are often used as metonymy for the president and his staff and advisors.
