Explore Prague

A Brief History

Prague became the seat of Bohemian kings and, from 1344, an archbishopric centered on Prague Castle above the Vltava River. Charles IV expanded the New Town and founded Charles University in 1348. The Hussite wars and Habsburg rule followed, leaving Gothic, Renaissance, and baroque layers. Czechoslovak independence in 1918 and later division in 1993 kept Prague as the national capital. Its historic center is a UNESCO site.

Prague Castle

Prague Castle is a castle complex in Prague, Czech Republic serving as the official residence and workplace of the president of the Czech Republic. Built in the 9th century, the castle has long served as the seat of power for kings of Bohemia, Holy Roman emperors, and presidents of Czechoslovakia. As such, the term "Prague Castle" or simply "Hrad" are often used as metonymy for the president and his staff and advisors.

Free (Great South Tower CZK 200)

St. Vitus Cathedral

The Metropolitan Cathedral of Saints Vitus, Wenceslaus and Adalbert is a Catholic metropolitan cathedral in Prague, and the seat of the Archbishop of Prague. Until 1997, the cathedral was dedicated only to Saint Vitus, and is still commonly named only as St. Vitus Cathedral.

Free

Zámecké schody

The historic 'Castle Stairs' wind directly up the steep hillside, historically serving as the primary pedestrian route connecting the Lesser Town to the gates of Prague Castle. The ancient stone steps offer scenic views of the surrounding red-tiled roofs.

Free

Church of Our Lady Victorious and The Infant Jesus of Prague

The Infant Jesus of Prague is a 16th-century wax-coated wooden statue of the Child Jesus holding a globus cruciger of Spanish origin, now located in the Discalced Carmelite Church of Our Lady of Victories in Malá Strana, Prague, Czech Republic. First appearing in 1556, pious legends claim that the statue once belonged to Teresa of Ávila and was consequently donated to the Carmelite friars by Princess Polyxena of Lobkowicz in 1628.

Free

Charles Bridge

Charles Bridge is a medieval stone arch bridge that crosses the Vltava river in Prague, Czech Republic. Its construction started in 1357 under the auspices of King Charles IV, and finished in the early 15th century. The bridge replaced the old Judith Bridge built 1158–1172 that had been severely damaged by a flood in 1342.

Free (Old Town Hall tower CZK 300)

Prague Astronomical Clock

A historic, famously beautiful medieval clock spectacularly mounted directly on the Old Town Hall. It powerfully stands as the oldest wonderfully operating astronomical clock globally, drawing daily crowds. The site is catalogued as a tourist attraction.

Free

Staroměstské náměstí

The incredible historic, central square acting as the vibrant heart of the city since the 12th century. It famously showcases an, beautiful array of historic architectural styles. Staroměstské náměstí is listed among principal sites for Prague within Czech Republic.

Free

Church of Our Lady before Tyn

The Church of the Mother of God before Týn or just Týn), or Church of Our Lady before Týn, is a Gothic church and a dominant feature of the Old Town of Prague, Czech Republic. It has been the main church of this part of the city since the 14th century. The church's two towers are 80 m high, and each tower's spire is topped by eight smaller spires in two layers of four.

Free

Prague Metronome

The Prague Metronome is a 75-foot-tall functioning metronome in Letná Park, overlooking the Vltava River and the city center of Prague. The kinetic sculpture was erected in 1991, on the plinth left vacant by the late-1962 demolition of an enormous monument to former Soviet leader Joseph Stalin. The silent red metronome was designed by international artist Vratislav Novák, and officially named "Time Machine".

Free

Franz Kafka

The Head of Franz Kafka, also known as the Statue of Kafka, is an outdoor kinetic sculpture by David Černý depicting Czech German-language writer Franz Kafka, installed on 31 October 2014 outside of the Quadrio shopping mall in Prague, Czech Republic. The price was 30 million crowns, paid by the investor CPI Property Group together with the adjacent Quadrio complex. Kafka himself worked in the nearby building of a saving bank.

Free

Statue of Saint Wenceslas Riding an Upside-Down Dead Horse

A satirical sculpture by David Černý displayed inside the Lucerna Palace. The piece subverts traditional Bohemian equestrian monuments by depicting the patron saint seated on the belly of an inverted, lifeless horse. Statue of Saint Wenceslas Riding an Upside-Down Dead Horse is listed among principal sites for Prague within Czech Republic.

Free

Petrin Tower

Petrin Tower is a famous landmark in Prague, offering a panoramic view of the city and its skyline. It resembles the Eiffel Tower and is located on Petrin Hill. travellers can enjoy a funicular ride to reach the tower and then climb 299 wooden steps to reach the top. From there, they can admire majestic sights such as Charles Bridge, the Dancing House, and Prague Castle.

Free

Dancing House

Dancing House, also known as Tancici dum, is a modern architectural marvel located in Prague. Designed by Czech architect Valdo Milunic and Canadian Frank Gehry, this unique building stands out amidst the city's centuries-old architectural styles. Resembling two dancers in motion, it features dynamic and static elements that give the impression of a female dancer swaying with her male partner.

Free

Old Town Bridge Tower

The Old Town Bridge Tower, a Gothic structure dating back to the 14th century, stands proudly at one end of the Charles Bridge. With its grand arched gateway and intricate ornamentation, it captures the essence of medieval architecture. While many travellers admire it from afar as they stroll across the bridge, few realize that this gem can be explored further.

Free

Lennon Wall

Nestled near the Charles Bridge, the Lennon Wall in Prague is a vibrant canvas that has become a symbol of peace and hope since the 1980s. Following John Lennon's tragic death, fans began to adorn this wall with colorful graffiti, heartfelt messages, and lyrics celebrating his legacy as a peace advocate. Despite attempts by authorities to erase these expressions during Czechoslovakia's communist era, the wall persisted as a testament to resilience and creativity.

Free

Municipal house

Obecní Dům, also known as Municipal House, is a art nouveau venue in the heart of Prague. This architectural gem took seven years to build and now stands as beautiful buildings in the Czech Republic. It boasts a 2,000-seat concert hall, salons, offices, and restaurants adorned with lavish decorative work. The venue hosts classical concerts, opera, ballet performances, and various events like fashion shows.

Free

Prague Zoo

Prague Zoo, also known as Zoo Praha, is a renowned zoo founded in 1931 and located in the picturesque Drahan-Troja Nature Park. Spanning 58 hectares of hilly landscape, it is home to an impressive collection of nearly 700 species, making it zoological collections in Europe. The zoo's challenging setting has been utilized to its full potential, providing habitats for over 4000 animals representing over 600 species.

Free

Vítkov

Vítkov Hill, located in Prague 3, offers a unique and view of the city. The hill is home to the National Memorial, which provides insight into Czechoslovakia's history and its role in World War I. travellers can explore interactive exhibitions inside the memorial. The hill also features a 1929 equestrian statue of Jan Zizka, offering an off-the-beaten-path adventure for travelers.

Free

Prague Main Train Station

Prague Main Station, also known as Praha Hlavni nadrazi, is a centrally located and well-connected transportation hub that offers a convenient gateway to the captivating city of Prague. The station boasts impressive historic architecture alongside modern amenities such as shops, restaurants, and cafes.

Free

The Vrtba Garden

The Vrtba Garden, built between 1715 and 1720 in Italian baroque style, is a picturesque terraced garden adorned with sculptures and a pool. Nestled in Prague's Lesser Quarter, this UNESCO-listed gem designed by Frantisek Maximilian Kanka offers views of the surrounding landscape.

Free

Žižkov Television Tower

The Žižkov Television Tower is an landmark in Prague, designed by Vaclav Aulicky and completed in 1992. Despite being considered one of the ugliest buildings globally, it remains a fascinating attraction due to its distinctiveness. The tower offers a viewing platform with 360-degree views, along with restaurants and a luxury hotel. Situated in the trendy Žižkov neighborhood, the sky-high restaurant and bar provide international cuisine and city views.

Free

Klementinum

Klementinum is a historic complex located at the eastern end of the Charles Bridge, featuring the Mirror Chapel, Baroque Library, and an Astronomical Tower with an Atlas statue. Originally a Jesuit dormitory in the 16th century, it expanded to include a library, university, and scientific institutions. Guided tours showcase its opulent areas such as the Baroque library adorned with frescoes, Meridian Hall, and the Astronomical Tower offering city views.

CZK 600

Spanish Synagogue

Completed in 1868, the Spanish Synagogue stands in Josefov as a magnificent example of Moorish Revival architecture. Its opulent interior is covered in intricate gold and geometric stucco motifs inspired by the Alhambra in Spain. Today, it operates as a venue for classical music concerts and houses part of the Jewish Museum's permanent collection detailing modern Jewish history in the Czech lands.

Free

National Theatre

The National Theatre, also known as Narodni Divadlo in Czech, is a significant cultural institution in Prague. It holds a rich artistic tradition and is considered the Alma Mater of Czech Opera. The venue showcases nationally sponsored new and classical ballet, opera, and theater performances. Situated just a five-minute walk from the hotel, this historical late 19th-century building boasts magnificent interiors that are truly captivating.

Free

Memorial to the Victims of Communism

The Memorial to the Victims of Communism in Prague features a series of 7 bronze statues that symbolize the political prisoners who suffered during the Communist era in Czechoslovakia from 1948 to 1989. Situated at the base of Petrin hill on Ujezd street, this controversial and striking memorial was unveiled in 2002, designed by Czech sculptor Olbram Zoubek and architects Jan Kerel and Zdenek Holzel.

Free

National Museum

Národní Muzeum, also known as the National Museum, is a grand institution established in 1818 with a diverse array of natural science and history exhibits. The museum's origins are tied to the contributions of Czech aristocrats and the pivotal role played by Count Kaspar Maria von Sternberg, a prominent Bohemian botanist. Housed in an impressive neo-Renaissance building at Wenceslas Square, it stands as a symbol of Czech national revival.

Free

The Powder Tower

Prašná brána, also known as the Powder Tower, is a historic city gate in Prague that dates back to the 15th century. Originally built in the 11th century and later reconstructed, it served as one of the original entrances to Old Town Prague. During the 17th century, it was repurposed for storing gunpowder, hence its name.

Free

Orthodox Cathedral of Saints Cyril and Methodius

Ss. Cyril and Methodius Cathedral is a significant Orthodox church in the former Czechoslovakia, featuring a war resistance museum in its lower level. Located in Prague's New Town, amidst Art Nouveau and Revival Architecture, this Baroque Age architectural gem stands out. travellers can explore the eerie yet moving crypt and watch a film about the paratroopers and Heydrich's assassination.

Free

Aircraft museum Prague-Kbely

The Aircraft Museum Prague-Kbely, located on a historic airfield, is a vast indoor and outdoor museum boasting an extensive collection of aircraft. It is the largest aviation museum in the Czech Republic and one of the biggest in Europe, featuring 275 planes across four buildings.

Free

Lesser Town Bridge Tower

The Lesser Town Bridge Tower is a must-visit photo spot in Prague, located at the western end of the Charles Bridge. Offering views over the city, it connects to the lesser town and provides unique perspectives of the Charles Bridge, Mala Strana, Prague Castle, and St. Nicholas Church's dome. This tower was built in 1464 as an entrance gate to the Lesser Town and offers a less crowded but equally captivating experience compared to its counterpart, the Old Town Bridge Tower.

Free

Vyšehrad

Perched on a rocky promontory overlooking the Vltava River, Vyšehrad is a historic fortress complex steeped in Czech mythology. The expansive public grounds contain the neo-Gothic Basilica of St Peter and St Paul, ancient defensive walls, and tranquil parks. The Vyšehrad Cemetery serves as the final resting place for numerous prominent Czech cultural figures, including Antonín Dvořák and Alfons Mucha.

Free

State Opera

The Prague State Opera, also known as the Former New German Theatre, is a historic and elegant venue with a rich cultural legacy. Built in 1888, it offers opera and ballet performances. Managed by the National Theatre, this neo-Renaissance building boasts a gilded stage and interior. Despite its unfortunate location across from a major highway, it remains one of Central Europe's most important music venues.

CZK 600

The Old-New Synagogue

Built in 1270, the Old-New Synagogue is Europe's oldest active synagogue and a remarkable example of early Gothic architecture in Prague. Its heavy stone vaults and austere interior have survived fires, floods, and historical turmoil in the Josefov district. According to enduring local legend, the attic of this sacred building serves as the resting place of the mythical Golem of Prague.

Free

The Estates Theatre

The Estates Theatre, part of the National Theatre family, is an elegant and historic venue in Prague that has been running since 1783. It gained fame for hosting the world premiere of Mozart's Don Giovanni opera in 1787 and has continued to stage important Czech opera and drama productions.

Free

Old Royal Palace

The Old Royal Palace, located within Prague Castle, is a late Gothic palace with Renaissance elements. It features the impressive Vladislav Hall, where royal events were held. The palace has a rich history as the residence of Czech monarchs and Bohemian princes throughout the centuries. Its architectural transformation from a simple wooden building to encompassing various styles such as Gothic, Romanesque, Renaissance, and Baroque is truly remarkable.

Free

Museum of Prague

Located in a grand neo-Renaissance building in the Florenc district, the City of Prague Museum chronicles the capital's urban development from prehistory to the 20th century. Recently reopened after an extensive five-year modernization, the museum seamlessly blends historic architecture with interactive displays. Its centerpiece is Langweil's Model, an intricate 19th-century paper and wood miniature of the city.

Free

Basilica of St. James the Greater

The Basilica of St. James, also known as Bazilika Sv. Jakuba, may seem unassuming from the outside, but its interior is a display of baroque art and architecture. The long nave is adorned with gilded statues and paintings under a beautifully frescoed ceiling depicting stories of the Virgin Mary's life. One of its most revered treasures is the bejeweled Madonna Pietatis highlighted by blue light at the altar.

Free

Karel Zeman Museum

The Karel Zeman Museum, located in Prague, Czech Republic, is an interactive and family-friendly exhibition showcasing the renowned work of Czech film director and animator Karel Zeman. The museum focuses on Zeman's pioneering use of special effects in his fantasy films from the 20th century. travellers can explore his famous movies such as 'Journey to the Prehistoric,' 'Invention of Destruction,' and 'Baron Prasil.'.

Free

Letna Park

Letná Park is a vast elevated green space offering expansive panoramic views across the Vltava River and Prague's historic bridges. The park features a functioning giant metronome situated on the former site of a massive Stalin monument, symbolizing the city's transition through time. Locals frequent the park's shaded, tree-lined paths for leisurely strolls, sports, and outdoor relaxation.

Free

Czech Museum of Music

Nestled within the Baroque Church of Santa Maria Magdalena, the Czech Museum of Music is a treasure trove for music enthusiasts. This remarkable museum showcases an extensive array of musical instruments crafted by both Czech and international artisans, alongside fascinating manuscripts and interactive displays that highlight the rich musical heritage of the Czech Republic.
