Destination Guide — Salzburg

Explore Salzburg

A Brief History

Salzburg developed as an independent prince-bishopric on the Salzach River from the 8th century until its integration into Austria in 1816. Hohensalzburg Fortress, Baroque churches, and residential quarters reflect centuries of ecclesiastical rule and salt-trade wealth. Wolfgang Amadeus Mozart was born here in 1756, and the city later shaped its public culture around music festivals and heritage tourism. Alpine hills frame a compact historic core whose street plan, fountains, and palace gardens still express the former bishops' ceremonial and administrative authority.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

Mirabell Palace

Mirabell Palace was built by Prince-Archbishop Wolf Dietrich von Raitenau in the early 17th century as a residence beside the Salzach. Its Marble Hall and state rooms host concerts and civic events. The palace faces gardens that extend toward the fortress hill across the river.

Free

Mirabell Gardens

Mirabell Gardens were laid out in the 18th century as formal terraces, statues, and parterres below the palace. Geometric beds and fountains frame views toward Hohensalzburg Fortress and the cathedral domes. The gardens remain open public space on the right bank of the Salzach.

Free

Pegasusbrunnen

The Pegasus Fountain stands in Kapitelplatz below the cathedral and fortress approach. Baroque sculpture represents the mythical horse and forms a focal point on the square used for markets and events. The site lies along the pedestrian route through Salzburg's ecclesiastical center.

€12

Mozart-Wohnhaus

Mozart's residence on Makartplatz was the family home from 1773 to 1787 after leaving the birth house. Exhibits present instruments, documents, and domestic life during Mozart's years in Salzburg and early Vienna period. The museum occupies the reconstructed townhouse on the square.

Free

Marko-Feingold-Steg

Marko-Feingold-Steg is a pedestrian bridge over the Salzach linking the old town with the Steingasse quarter. Named for a Salzburg Holocaust survivor and educator, it replaced earlier crossings for foot traffic between both banks. The bridge offers views toward the fortress and riverfront facades.

€13

Mozarts Geburtshaus

Mozart's Birthplace on Getreidegasse is a townhouse museum where Wolfgang Amadeus Mozart was born in 1756. Rooms present family life, early instruments, and documents from his childhood in Salzburg. The yellow facade with guild sign marks one of the city's most visited addresses.

€30

Untersbergbahn Cable Car

The Untersbergbahn ascends from St. Leonhard on Salzburg's southern edge to stations on the Untersberg massif. Opened in 1961, the cable car provides access to alpine viewpoints and hiking paths above the city. Valley and summit stations connect with regional bus routes.

Free

Stift Nonnberg

Stift Nonnberg is a Benedictine convent founded in the 8th century on the eastern slope below the fortress. Its Romanesque church and Gothic additions make it one of the oldest continuously operating convents in the German-speaking world. The site is linked with The Sound of Music traditions.

€14

Festung Hohensalzburg

Hohensalzburg Fortress crowns the Festungsberg above the old town and was expanded by prince-archbishops from the 11th century. Ramparts, courtyards, and museums record military and court functions. A funicular and walking path connect the summit with Kapitelplatz below.

Free

Hellbrunn Palace

Schloss Hellbrunn is a Renaissance villa with beautifully landscaped gardens, famous for its trick water fountains and folklore museum. Built by Markus Sittikus von Hohenems in the early 17th century, this baroque palace was designed as a day residence without bedrooms. The estate is known for its 'Watergames,' where unsuspecting guests are squirted with water from hidden fountains.

Free

Salzburg Zoo Hellbrunn

Salzburg Zoo Hellbrunn is a family-friendly destination featuring over 1,500 animals from various continents. Originally established in the 15th century as an archbishopric zoological park, it now showcases 150 different species. The zoo's unique topography, nestled next to a cliff leading to the large African savannah, adds to its charm.

Free

Salzburg Cathedral

Salzburg Cathedral, with its twin spires and baroque-style architecture, is a monumental symbol of the city's religious significance and historical wealth. The cathedral houses a museum that displays sacred treasures, adding to its allure. The building has undergone several reconstructions over the centuries, with the current structure showcasing early Baroque ecclesiastical architecture at its.

Free

Residenzplatz

Residenzplatz is a magnificent 16th-century square featuring a baroque fountain and serving as the backdrop for various cultural events. It holds historical significance, being featured in famous films like The Sound of Music. travellers can explore nearby attractions such as Mirabell Palace and Garden, Felsenreitschule, St. Peters cemetery, Schloss Leopoldskron, and Nonnberg Convent.

Free

Mozartplatz

Mozartplatz is a vibrant and historic square in Salzburg, renowned for its striking memorial statue of the legendary composer Wolfgang Amadeus Mozart. This expansive courtyard, established in the early 17th century, invites travellers to immerse themselves in its charming atmosphere filled with delightful cafes and horse-drawn carriage rides.

Free

St. Peter's Abbey

St. Peter's Abbey, situated in Salzburg, Austria, is a renowned Benedictine monastery and Catholic church with a rich history dating back to the 7th century. Established by Saint Rupert, it stands as one of the oldest abbey-church and monastery complexes in the German-speaking world. The Romanesque St. Peter's Church, built in 1143 and later restored with Baroque and Rococo elements, showcases ornate altarpieces by Martin Johann Schmidt.

Free

Europark Salzburg

Europark Salzburg is a modern and stylish shopping center housing over 100 stores, including global retail chains and casual eateries. Situated in Salzburg's old town, it serves as a vibrant meeting place where traditional manufactories like bakeries, liqueur makers, tailors, and more enliven the narrow streets with their long-standing craftsmanship. The mall offers a wide range of shopping options from clothing brands to bookstores and various dining establishments.

Free

Casino Salzburg

Located in the baroque palace of Schloss Klessheim, Casino Salzburg offers a luxurious gambling experience in Wals-Siezenheim, Austria. The casino boasts a 25,000 sq ft gaming area with an array of modern slot machines and live table games such as American Roulette, Blackjack, Macau Baccarat, and Poker. Additionally, travellers can enjoy open-air games on the terrace overlooking the beautiful English garden.

Free

Fortress Hohensalzburg

Perched on a hilltop with commanding views of the city and the Alps, Fortress Hohensalzburg is an impressive 11th-century complex that draws in over 1.4 million travellers annually. To avoid the crowds, it's to arrive early and take a glass funicular or embark on a steep 20-minute walk up Festungsgasse in the Old Town to reach this landmark.

Free

Stiegl-Brauwelt

Stiegl-Brauwelt is a must-visit destination for beer enthusiasts in Salzburg. The brewery offers a multi-sensory exploration of the world of beer, featuring exhibits on brewing history and a modern brewhouse where travellers can witness the bottling process. Additionally, guests can indulge in Austrian cuisine at the brewpub and restaurant. Stiegl, a family-owned brewery since 1492, is renowned for its popular beers that are widely available throughout Austria.

Free

Richterhöhe

Richterhöhe, also known as Judges Height Lookout, is a hidden gem in Salzburg named after surveyor Eduard Richter. This spot offers views of the Alps and a grassy plain without the usual tourist crowds. From this vantage point, travellers can enjoy unique perspectives of Festungsberg mountain, the fortress, Gaisberg, and even catch a glimpse of Leopoldskron where scenes from 'The Sound of Music' were filmed.

Free

Pferdeschwemme (Hofmarstallschwemme)

Pferdeschwemme, also known as Hofmarstallschwemme, is a historic horse bath located in Salzburg's old town. The fountain features a striking sculpture of a prancing horse and is surrounded by several horse-themed murals, all set against the backdrop of the Monchsberg cliff walls. This picturesque site has been well-preserved since its construction in 1695 by Johann Bernhard Fischer von Erlach and served as a watering hole for the Archbishop's riding stables.

Free

Great Festival House

Great Festival House, also known as Grosses Festspielhaus, is a grand concert hall in Salzburg that was built in 1960. It mainly hosts classical music and opera performances. The building is located near the former royal stables and features a magnificent high baroque watering pond with a dramatic sculpture of a horse at its center.

Free

Felsenreitschule

The Felsenreitschule, also known as the Rock Riding School, is a historic 17th-century riding school turned theater that serves as a prominent stage for the Salzburg music festival. Carved out of the Monchsberg Hill cliffside in 1693, it features arched arcades and a natural stone wall backdrop. The theater was even featured in the filming of 'The Sound of Music.'.
