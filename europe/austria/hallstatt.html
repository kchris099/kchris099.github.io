Explore Hallstatt

A Brief History

Hallstatt's prosperity originated in prehistoric times with the exploitation of its extensive salt deposits, giving its name to the early Iron Age Hallstatt culture. The settlement remained a crucial salt mining center under the Habsburgs, with wealth driving the construction of its distinctive Alpine architecture squeezed between the lake and steep mountain slopes. Before modern road access in the late nineteenth century, the town relied almost entirely on boat transport. Visitors can still explore this ancient industrial heritage at the world's oldest salt mine and the historic market square.

Panoramic Viewpoint - Hallstatt Skywalk

The Panoramic Viewpoint - Hallstatt Skywalk is an awe-inspiring observation deck that extends over the charming village of Hallstatt in Austria. Situated at a height of 1,148 feet amidst the Dachstein Alps, it offers vistas of the UNESCO World Heritage site. Visitors can admire the deep blue waters of Hallstatter See, lush green valleys, and majestic mountain ranges from this elevated vantage point.

Free

Marketplace Hallstatt

Marktplatz Hallstatt, also known as the market place in Hallstatt, is a picturesque square at the heart of the historic town. Surrounded by majestic mountains and overlooking a beautiful lake, this UNESCO World Heritage Site is considered Austria's -kept secret. The square features the Holy Trinity Statue, a symbol of gratitude and protection from natural disasters. Visitors can explore small cafes, restaurants, and souvenir shops offering traditional Austrian goods.

Free

Panoramic Viewpoint - Hallstatt

Panoramic Viewpoint - Hallstatt is a picturesque town nestled amidst the mountains, accessible via the A1 motorway from Vienna. Despite rumors linking it to Disney's Frozen and a replica in China, it remains a destination. Visitors can expect an atmospheric ambiance, especially during overcast weather, adding mystery and tranquility to the experience. The time to visit may be early morning on Sundays to avoid crowds.

Free

Hallstätter See

Nestled in the mountains, Hallstätter See is a picturesque lake with a charming village on its shores. The area offers various water sports and boating activities, as well as nearby hiking trails. Visitors can enjoy culinary delights at restaurants like 'Zum Salzbaron' and 'Kaiserstuberl,' which offer a mix of Austrian and Mediterranean cuisine, including freshly caught fish from the lake.

€61

Dachstein-Gletscher (Schladming-Dachstein) - Talstation der Bergbahn

Dachstein-Gletscher (Schladming-Dachstein) is a popular destination for alpine and cross-country skiing, offering views and an ice palace experience. The Dachstein & Eishohlen GmbH & Co in Obertraun was established to preserve the unique ice caves of the Dachstein mountain range, providing guided tours for visitors. It's recommended to book tickets in advance due to high demand, especially during peak seasons.

€73

Dachstein Skywalk

The Dachstein Skywalk is a architectural wonder located on the Dachstein Glacier in Austria. It offers an otherworldly experience as you step onto the glass platform, providing an exhilarating view of the glacier and surrounding peaks. Visitors can also explore the Ice Palace and take a walk on the glacier. It's recommended to make cable car reservations early due to high demand, and visitors should be prepared for chilly winds by wearing layers and bringing a raincoat.

€66

5 Fingers

5 Fingers is a one-of-a-kind observation platform located on the Dachstein Mountains in Upper Austria. Accessible by cable cars, it offers views and an adrenaline rush as you stand on its hand-shaped "fingers" that stretch 400 meters above the ground. The hike to reach this platform takes about 25-30 minutes and provides panoramic views of Hallstatt town, the lake, and the glacier remnants from the ice age.

€66

World Heritage Spiral (Welterbespirale)

The World Heritage Spiral (Welterbespirale) is a must-visit attraction in Dachstein, offering a 360-degree panoramic view of Hallstatt and its surroundings from atop a 2,100-meter peak. Accessible via the Dachstein World Heritage Cable Car, this site provides an opportunity to explore the Giant Ice Cave and Mammoth Cave as well as witness three and a half thousand years of cultural heritage.

€50

Dachstein Rieseneishöhle

Discover the wonders of the Dachstein Giant Ice Cave, with its impressive ice formations and fascinating underground landscapes. It remains a notable local point of interest situated in Hallstatt, Austria. Visitors can observe the surrounding area and its connection to the city's broader historical landscape. Local guides sometimes include it on walking routes through the centre.

Free
