Feldkirch

Counts of Montfort held Feldkirch from the thirteenth century as a toll and market point toward Switzerland and Liechtenstein. Habsburg officials later took the town. Schattenburg castle on the ridge above the Ill River is the landmark that makes that border-county past readable in one climb. Market lanes under the castle still serve a town that lives off cross-border traffic.

Schattenburg

Schattenburg Castle was founded in the 13th century on a ridge above Feldkirch and served for centuries as the seat of local lords in Vorarlberg. The hilltop position controlled routes toward Switzerland and Liechtenstein. Today the restored rooms, courtyard, and museum trace the town's medieval and early modern history.

Rectory St Nicholas

The rectory beside the Cathedral of St Nicholas belongs to one of the oldest parish complexes in Feldkirch's walled old town. The building sits within the ecclesiastical quarter that grew around the market streets below Schattenburg Castle, linking daily parish life with the town's long religious and civic history.

Landesgericht Feldkirch

The Landesgericht is the regional court building that reflects Feldkirch's later role as an administrative center within Vorarlberg. Its civic architecture extends the historic core beyond the medieval market and church ensemble, showing how 19th- and 20th-century state institutions took shape in this western Alpine town.
