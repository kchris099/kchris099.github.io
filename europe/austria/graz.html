Explore Graz

A Brief History

Graz served as a Habsburg residence and later capital of Inner Austria, with its Schlossberg fortifications controlling the Mur valley on routes from the Balkans to the Alps. Renaissance arcaded courtyards, the Landhaus, and the cathedral reflect patronage by archdukes who rivalled Vienna before the nineteenth century brought industry and universities. Designated a UNESCO City of Design, Graz layers baroque façades, modernist exhibition halls, and Styrian wine culture on a historic trade crossroads.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

Uhrturm

The Clock Tower on Schlossberg Hill is a landmark visible across Graz and dates from the 16th century with later alterations. It survived demolition plans when the fortress above was dismantled in the 19th century. A path and lift connect the tower with viewpoints over the Mur and rooftops.

€3

Schlossberglift

The Schlossbergbahn funicular ascends from the city center to Schlossberg Hill beside the clock tower. Rebuilt in modern form, it provides quick access to ramparts, gardens, and cafés on the former fortress site. The lower station stands near the Mur shore in the historic core.

Free

Hauptplatz der Stadt Graz

Hauptplatz is the main square of Graz with the Rathaus and town houses around a central equestrian monument. Markets and civic events have filled the space since the medieval period. Arcades and façades illustrate the city's commercial and political center on the Mur.

€8

Opera Graz

Opera Graz is the city's opera house rebuilt after wartime destruction and reopened in the late 20th century on the banks of the Mur. It hosts opera, ballet, and concert productions for Styria. Guided tours present backstage areas and the auditorium when performances allow.

Free

Dreifaltigkeits-Pestsäule

The Trinity Plague Column on Hauptplatz was erected in the late 17th century to commemorate deliverance from plague and fire. Baroque sculpture rises in a column dedicated to the Holy Trinity at the center of the square. It remains a focal point of Graz's main public space.

€4

Mausoleum Kaiser Ferdinands II.

The mausoleum of Emperor Ferdinand II adjoins the cathedral and was built in the 17th century as a dynastic burial church. Its dome and interior decoration reflect early Baroque court patronage in Graz. The building forms part of the ecclesiastical ensemble near the Landeszeughaus.

€4

Grazer Landhaus

The Landhaus is the Renaissance seat of the Styrian provincial estates built in the 16th century with an arcaded courtyard. Its architecture influenced regional palace design. The building still houses Styrian state assembly functions and can be visited on guided tours.

Free

Stadtpfarrkirche zum Heiligen Blut

The parish church of the Holy Blood stands on Hauptplatz with a Gothic core and later Baroque furnishings. Its name refers to a venerated relic associated with Graz's medieval parish life. The church serves the old town congregation beside the civic buildings of the square.
