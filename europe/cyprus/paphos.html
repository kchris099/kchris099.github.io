Explore Paphos

A Brief History

Paphos is in Cyprus. Established as a regional settlement beyond an older fortified core, the city developed through industrialization in the late 19th century, post-war reconstruction, and modern civic expansion that reshaped its public spaces, markets, and surrounding landscape. Museums, markets, and street plans in Paphos still show layers from those periods.

Archaeological Site of the Tombs of the Kings

The Archaeological Site of the Tombs of the Kings is a vast necropolis dating back to the 3rd century BCE. It has underground tombs that were careful carved out of rock. The site offers a glimpse into ancient burial practices and architectural ingenuity, travellers can explore nearby attractions such as the Medieval Castle, waterfront restaurants, ice-cream shops, cafes, and bars with views across the water and harbor.

Free

Paphos Castle

Paphos Castle, originally a Byzantine fort, has a rich history of destruction and reconstruction. Over the years, it has served as a prison and warehouse for salt. The castle stands at the edge of Paphos harbor and is one of the city's most recognizable landmarks. It hosts various events throughout the year, including operas during the Paphos Aphrodite Festival.

Archaeological Site of Nea Paphos

The Archaeological Site of Nea Paphos is a large area filled with ancient ruins, including tombs, villas, and a fortress. The site also has well-preserved Roman mosaics depicting ancient mythology. Travellers can explore the Medieval Castle and enjoy views of the water and harbor. The area is busy with waterfront restaurants, shops, and yachts, creating a active atmosphere that blends directly with its rich historical significance.

Free

Paphos Mosaics

Paphos Mosaics are ancient Roman structures in Cyprus, showing well-preserved mosaics depicting mythological scenes. The mosaics date back to the Hellenistic period and are considered among the in the world. Agia Solomoni Catacomb

Agia Solomoni Catacomb, also known as the Catacombs of Saint Solomonia or Hagia Solomonia, is a significant site for Christian believers in Cyprus and a popular tourist destination. This large cave Christian temple was initially used as catacombs for stone extraction before being repurposed as a burial site and later transformed into a Christian Orthodox Church in the 9th century.

Agia Kyriaki Chrysopolitissa

Agia Kyriaki Chrysopolitissa is a historical site in Paphos, Cyprus, featuring the ruins of a 13th-century church known for its colorful mosaic floor. The site was once home to one of Pafos' largest religious structures, with the remaining foundations showing the size and grandeur of the original church.

Paphos Aphrodite Waterpark

Paphos Aphrodite Waterpark is a public park in Paphos, Cyprus. Paphos Aphrodite Waterpark is the largest water park in the Paphos area, offering a unique natural landscape for aquatic adventures. Located in Kato Paphos, it is easily accessible by car or local bus. Paths, lawns, and shaded areas make up the main public facilities.

Byzantine Church of Agia Paraskevi

Byzantine Church of Agia Paraskevi is a religious building in Paphos, Cyprus. This five-domed basilica is one of only two such structures on the island and showcases interior wall paintings that span several centuries, from the 8th to the 15th century. The exterior and main interior spaces are usually viewable outside service times.
