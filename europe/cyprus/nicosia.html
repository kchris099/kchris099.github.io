Explore Nicosia

A Brief History

Nicosia is in Cyprus. Established as a regional settlement beyond an older fortified core, the city developed through industrialization in the late 19th century, post-war reconstruction, and modern civic expansion that reshaped its public spaces, markets, and surrounding landscape. Museums, markets, and street plans in Nicosia still show layers from those periods.

Top Sights & Activities

Kyrenia Gate

Kyrenia Gate is a historical landmark in Nicosia, originally built by the Venetians in 1567 and later restored by the Ottomans. It now is a tourism information office and is surrounded by restaurants, shops, and hotels. The area is busy with activity and offers a mix of historical buildings frequented by tourists. It's also a convenient spot for orientation, being the last point of old Nicosia.

Free

Büyük Han

Büyük Han is a visitor site in Nicosia, Cyprus. Built in the early 1570s at the request of the island's first Ottoman governor, it has undergone recent renovations and now hosts traditional shops and handicraft workshops. Originally a caravanserai for weary travelers along the Silk Road, Büyük Han is now a celebrated landmark and arts and crafts market with a central café. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Free

Selimiye Camii

Selimiye Camii, located in North Nicosia, is a blend of Gothic architecture and Islamic design. Originally a Catholic cathedral dating back to the 13th century, it was converted into a mosque in 1570. This airy mosque with white interior walls is an symbol of Cyprus's cultural duality.

Free

Eleftheria Square

Eleftheria Square is a public square in Nicosia, Cyprus. The backdrop of massive Venetian walls adds to its allure. Travellers can also enjoy shopping at kiosks offering newspapers, magazines, sweets, drinks, and postcards. Open pavement and surrounding facades define the visitor setting.

Free

The Liberty Monument

The Liberty Monument is a monument in Nicosia, Cyprus. Records associated with the site include 1955, 1959. The Liberty Monument, located near the Venetian walls, is a symbol of Cypriot independence and commemorates the fighters who participated in the EOKA struggle against British rule from 1955 to 1959. The memorial stands in an open plaza or roadside setting.

Free

St. John's Cathedral

John's Cathedral, also known as Agios Ioannis Cathedral, is a significant landmark in Nicosia, Cyprus. This historic Gothic cathedral dates back to the 17th century and is located the city. It has an unassuming exterior that conceals a lavish interior adorned with detailed 18th-century frescoes.

Free

Hamam Omerye

Immerse oneself in the local history of Nicosia by visiting the 16th-century Ottoman bath, Hamam Omerye. This historical site has been transformed into a modern hammam and beauty spa, offering a range of luxurious treatments for ultimate relaxation. The distinctive dome and aromas create an experience that reflects the town's diverse identity. Indulge in a rejuvenating massage on the marble slab under star-shaped openings that allow sunlight to diffuse over visitor body.

Free

Famagusta Gate

Famagusta Gate is a visitor site in Nicosia, Cyprus. Located on the eastern side of South Nicosia, Cyprus, Famagusta Gate is a prominent old gateway landmark that underwent restoration in the early 1980s. It is more than just an entrance, also hosting outdoor concerts and exhibitions. With its main exhibition area and two side halls, there's always something interesting to see at Famagusta Gate. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Free

Acropolis Park

Acropolis Park is a public park in Nicosia, Cyprus. Acropolis Park, situated in Strovolos near the city center, is a and spacious park offering various outdoor activities for travellers of all ages. For those seeking relaxation, the park offers clear walkways, bridges, and fountains creating a quiet setting. Paths, lawns, and shaded areas make up the main public facilities.

Free

Nicosia Municipal Gardens

Nicosia Municipal Gardens, also known as Cyta Park, is a public park in Nicosia, Cyprus. Nicosia Municipal Park, also known as Cyta Park, is a active and diverse urban oasis located near the busy Ledra Street. It is a popular meeting place for immigrants from South Asia on Sundays, travellers can enjoy cosmetic services and sample homemade international cuisine. Paths, lawns, and shaded areas make up the main public facilities.
