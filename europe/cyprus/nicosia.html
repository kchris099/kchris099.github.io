Nicosia

Nicosia served as capital under Lusignan kings, Venetian fortifiers, and Ottoman governors, each leaving walls, mosques, and churches inside a star-shaped enceinte. British colonial rule from 1878 modernised administration around the old core. Since 1974 a UN buffer zone has divided the city between the Republic of Cyprus and the north. Crossing points, museums, and the Venetian walls show how partition reshaped a once-continuous urban fabric.

Kyrenia Gate

Kyrenia Gate is a historical landmark in Nicosia, originally built by the Venetians in 1567 and later restored by the Ottomans. Büyük Han is a visitor site in Nicosia, Cyprus. Built in the early 1570s at the request of the island's first Ottoman governor, it has undergone recent renovations and now hosts traditional shops and handicraft workshops. Originally a caravanserai for weary travelers along the Silk Road, Büyük Han is now a celebrated landmark and arts and crafts market with a central café.
