Nicosia

Nicosia served as capital under Lusignan kings, Venetian fortifiers, and Ottoman governors, each leaving walls, mosques, and churches inside a star-shaped enceinte. British colonial rule from 1878 modernised administration around the old core. Since 1974 a UN buffer zone has divided the city between the Republic of Cyprus and the north. Crossing points, museums, and the Venetian walls show how partition reshaped a once-continuous urban fabric.

Kyrenia Gate

Venetians built Kyrenia Gate in 1567; Ottoman restorers later renewed the portal at old Nicosia's edge. A tourism information office now occupies the structure among restaurants, shops, and hotels. The gate still works as an orientation landmark where the historic core meets later streets.

02 AdmissionFree

Büyük Han

Ottoman governor Muzaffer Pasha ordered Büyük Han soon after the conquest of Cyprus in the 1570s. Two levels of arcaded rooms surround a large courtyard and a small prayer structure raised over a fountain, recalling the building's use by merchants, animals and travelers. Restored workshops, galleries, cafes and craft shops now occupy the former lodging cells within northern Nicosia's walled city.

35.1763° N / 33.3626° E

03 AdmissionFree

Selimiye Camii

Selimiye Camii, located in North Nicosia, is a blend of Gothic architecture and Islamic design. Originally a Catholic cathedral dating back to the 13th century, it was converted into a mosque in 1570. This airy mosque with white interior walls is an symbol of Cyprus's cultural duality.

35.1765° N / 33.3645° E

04 AdmissionFree

Eleftheria Square

Eleftheria Square sits against Nicosia's massive Venetian walls with open paving for gatherings. Kiosks sell newspapers, magazines, sweets, drinks, and postcards around the hardscape. Surrounding facades and the wall backdrop keep the square tied to both civic life and the fortified circuit.

05 AdmissionFree

The Liberty Monument

The Liberty Monument near Nicosia's Venetian walls commemorates EOKA fighters in the 1955-1959 struggle against British rule. Sculpted figures rise on a stepped composition along the bastion line. The memorial faces the old city fortifications as a public statement of Cypriot independence memory.

35.1716° N / 33.3703° E

06 AdmissionFree

St. John's Cathedral

Agios Ioannis Cathedral, St John's, dates to the seventeenth century within Nicosia's old city. A plain exterior hides an interior covered with detailed eighteenth-century frescoes. The small Gothic plan remains the archbishopric's cathedral church for Orthodox services.

07 AdmissionFree

Hamam Omerye

Immerse oneself in the local history of Nicosia by visiting the 16th-century Ottoman bath, Hamam Omerye. This historical site has been transformed into a modern hammam and beauty spa, offering a range of luxurious treatments for ultimate relaxation. The distinctive dome and aromas create an experience that reflects the town's diverse identity. Indulge in a rejuvenating massage on the marble slab under star-shaped openings that allow sunlight to diffuse over visitor body.

35.1725° N / 33.3654° E

08 AdmissionFree

Famagusta Gate

The Famagusta Gate forms the eastern entrance through Nicosia's sixteenth-century Venetian walls. Its barrel-vaulted passage, central domed chamber and side rooms once controlled the road toward Famagusta. Restoration in the early 1980s adapted the interior for exhibitions, concerts and civic programs, allowing the defensive architecture to function as a cultural venue without erasing its original plan.

35.1743° N / 33.3710° E

09 AdmissionFree

Acropolis Park

In Strovolos near central Nicosia, Acropolis Park spreads walkways, bridges, and fountains across open lawns. Outdoor activity areas suit mixed ages, while quieter paths offer shade away from traffic. Paths and planted ground form the main public facilities rather than a single enclosed attraction.

35.1464° N / 33.3616° E

10 AdmissionFree

Nicosia Municipal Gardens

Also called Cyta Park, Nicosia Municipal Gardens sit near Ledra Street with lawns, shade, and walking paths. On Sundays the park is a common meeting place for South Asian immigrant communities. Homemade international food and informal services often appear alongside the greenery on those days.

35.1732° N / 33.3553° E
