Tartu

Tartu emerged as a medieval trading hub and joined the Hanseatic League in the thirteenth century, but its defining historical turn came when Swedish King Gustavus Adolphus founded its university in 1632. The city evolved into the intellectual center of the region, fostering the Estonian national awakening in the nineteenth century even while under Russian imperial rule. Despite heavy destruction during World War II, Tartu rebuilt its academic heart. Visitors can experience this intellectual heritage on Toome Hill, the classicist University Main Building, and the ruined Cathedral.
