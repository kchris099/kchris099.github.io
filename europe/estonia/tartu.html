Explore Tartu

A Brief History

Tartu emerged as a medieval trading hub and joined the Hanseatic League in the thirteenth century, but its defining historical turn came when Swedish King Gustavus Adolphus founded its university in 1632. The city evolved into the intellectual center of the region, fostering the Estonian national awakening in the nineteenth century even while under Russian imperial rule. Despite heavy destruction during World War II, Tartu rebuilt its academic heart. Visitors can experience this intellectual heritage on Toome Hill, the classicist University Main Building, and the ruined Cathedral.

Ruins of Tartu Cathedral

The Ruins of Tartu Cathedral, also known as the University of Tartu Museum, stand as a significant historical and architectural landmark in Estonia. Dating back to the 13th century, this impressive cathedral has undergone various transformations over time. Originally planned as a basilica, it now features high choirs, columns, and arches in the brick Gothic style. The cathedral is surrounded by a wall that once separated the bishop's residence from the rest of the city.

Free

Cathedral Hill Park

Cathedral Hill Park, located in Tartu, Estonia, holds historical significance as the site of the ancient Tarbatu Fortress and the medieval Tartu Cathedral. The cathedral, dating back to the 13th-16th century, has endured plunder and raids throughout history. Visitors can enjoy views from this park and engage in various outdoor activities such as off-road longboarding and cycling. Additionally, there are recreational amenities like an outdoor pingpong table.

Free

Tartu Town Hall Square

Tartu Town Hall Square, situated in the heart of Tartu, Estonia, is a captivating destination for visitors. The square is surrounded by well-preserved buildings that showcase the city's history and charm. The Tartu Town Hall with its Gothic-style facade stands as a prominent feature. Lined with cafes and restaurants, the square offers a charming atmosphere for exploration.

Free

Skulptuur "Suudlevad tudengid" / 'The Kissing Students' sculpture and fountain

This sculpture and fountain, located in Town Hall Square, symbolizes the vibrant student life of Tartu and is a popular photo spot. It remains a notable local point of interest situated in Tartu, Estonia. Visitors can observe the surrounding area and its connection to the city's broader historical landscape. Local guides sometimes include it on walking routes through the centre.

Free (tower €3)

St. John Church, Tartu

St. John Church in Tartu is a gothic brick Lutheran church located in the Old Town. While the interior may not be particularly , it offers panoramic views of the city from its tower, making it a must-visit attraction. Climbing to the top is relatively manageable with a small fee for entry. The church is adorned with terracotta sculptures, some of which are well-preserved and can be admired inside.

Free

St. Paul's Church

St. Paul's Church is an imposing and aesthetically pleasing structure, with an exterior that exudes a sense of menace while still retaining its beauty. However, upon stepping inside, one encounters a complete contrast with a candid and serene interior. As a Lutheran church, the decorations are kept minimal, including the altarpiece. Nevertheless, being within the church in complete silence can be a moving experience, akin to entering another world entirely.

€9

Tagurpidi Maja (Peapeal OÜ)

Experience a unique and disorienting visit to Tagurpidi Maja, an upside-down house where everything is inverted, providing a fun and quirky attraction for all ages. It remains a notable local point of interest situated in Tartu, Estonia. Visitors can observe the surrounding area and its connection to the city's broader historical landscape. Local guides sometimes include it on walking routes through the centre.

Free

Raadi manor

Raadi Manor, located next to Estonia's National Museum, is a historic site with a melancholic yet interesting atmosphere. The ruins of the manor, once owned by the von Liphart family, stand as a reminder of its past grandeur. The surrounding park offers a manageable space for leisurely walks and features several statues. Additionally, the area boasts fields and a lake, making it an ideal spot for walking and running.

Free

Peetri kirik

Peetri kirik, a church located just across the river from Tartu's historic old town, is a gem of Gothic European architecture that has stood for around 150 years. Visitors often rave about its beauty and the warm atmosphere created by the friendly community. While some have noted that it could benefit from a fresh coat of paint, this does not detract from its charm.

Closed

Aura Waterpark

If you're planning a trip to Tartu, make sure to include Aura Waterpark in your itinerary! This popular destination is the largest water park in the region and offers an array of attractions that cater to families and thrill-seekers alike. Open daily from 10:00 AM to 10:00 PM, it features exciting water slides, various pools, and a family-friendly atmosphere perfect for kids.
