Lyon

Lyon developed from the Roman colony of Lugdunum, founded around 43 BC at the confluence of the Rhône and Saône. It later became a major ecclesiastical seat, silk-weaving center, and industrial hub, with traboules, hidden passage-ways, linking Renaissance merchant quarters. Hillside basilicas, riverfront civic buildings, and preserved Roman theatres show the long continuity of urban life.

Basilica of Notre Dame of Fourvière

The Basilica of Fourvière was built in the late 19th century on the hill that overlooks Lyon's old city. Its hilltop position links modern pilgrimage architecture with the historic Fourvière ridge, where Roman Lugdunum once stood. The church and terrace offer wide views over the confluence of the Rhône and Saône.

Théâtre Gallo Romain

Roman theaters on Fourvière hill mark the former capital of Lugdunum. The larger theater and the smaller odeon beside it show the scale of public entertainment in imperial Gaul. The excavated seating and stage areas form part of the Lugdunum museum network on Lyon's western ridge.

45.7602° N / 4.8191° E

03 AdmissionFree

Hôtel de Ville de Lyon

Lyon's city hall was built in the 17th century on Place des Terreaux and rebuilt in part after a fire in the 19th century. Its classical facade and courtyard remain a civic landmark at the political center of the city, facing the square where public festivals, markets, and municipal events still take place.

45.7673° N / 4.8349° E

04 AdmissionFree

Lyon Zoo

Lyon Zoo sits inside Parc de la Tête d'Or, the large nineteenth-century urban park developed by the city on the Rhône. Animal enclosures share the same public green as lakes, lawns, and tree avenues rather than forming a separate gated complex. Paths link the zoo circuit to the rest of the park used daily by Lyonnais walkers.

45.7793° N / 4.8546° E

05 AdmissionFree

Porte des Enfants du Rhône

The Porte des Enfants du Rhône is one of the main ornamental entrances to the Parc de la Tête d'Or. It reflects the 19th-century design of Lyon's major urban park and its formal approach from the city. The gate marks the transition from boulevard traffic to the lawns, lake, and paths of the park interior.

45.7738° N / 4.8526° E

06 AdmissionFree

Traboules du Vieux Lyon

Les Traboules du Vieux Lyon, often referred to as the Secret Passageways of Old Lyon, are a fascinating maze of covered walkways that weave through the city's historic Renaissance district. Dating back to the 4th century, these unique passages were initially designed to offer residents sheltered routes between their homes and the busy River Saône.

45.7627° N / 4.8279° E

07 AdmissionFree

Place Bellecour

Place Bellecour occupies the wide middle of Lyon's Presqu'île between the Rhône and the Saône. Tree-lined walks, seasonal flower stalls, and a bronze equestrian statue of Louis XIV give the open square its everyday character. Paths lead south toward the Confluence district where the rivers meet.

45.7577° N / 4.8321° E

08 AdmissionFree

Mur des Canuts

A trompe-l'oeil streetscape covers the Mur des Canuts in Lyon's Croix-Rousse district, home of the city's historic silk workers. CitéCréation first painted the mural in 1987 and has revised it several times as imagined residents age and the surrounding neighborhood changes; painted stairs, windows, shops, and figures merge with real architecture, turning the wall into an evolving record of local life.

45.7780° N / 4.8280° E

09 AdmissionFree

Confluence

Confluence occupies the tip of Lyon's 2nd arrondissement where the Rhône and Saône join. Recent redevelopment replaced former industrial docks with retail blocks, housing, and the Musée des Confluences. Broad quays and new streets make the river junction readable as a modern city edge.

45.7411° N / 4.8173° E

10 AdmissionFree

Parc de la Tête d'Or

Parc de la Tête d'Or opened in the nineteenth century on Lyon's left bank as a large urban landscape of lawns, woodland, a lake, gardens and curving paths. The botanical garden and municipal zoo occupy substantial sections, while boats, sports, playgrounds and rose gardens serve everyday recreation. Several gates connect the park with the Rhône, university district and Cité Internationale.

45.7772° N / 4.8551° E

Grand Parc Miribel Jonage

Grand Parc Miribel Jonage stretches along the Rhône northeast of Lyon as a wide nature and recreation reserve. Lakes, woodland, and open meadows support cycling, walking, and swimming in designated areas. The park functions as the city's main large-scale green belt outside the dense urban grid.

12 AdmissionFree

Bartholdi Fountain

Frédéric Auguste Bartholdi's fountain was unveiled in 1892 on Place des Terreaux in Lyon. Bronze figures and cascading basins face the Hôtel de Ville and the Musée des Beaux-Arts. At night the ensemble is lit, and by day it anchors the square's civic open space.

45.7676° N / 4.8335° E

13 AdmissionFree

Opéra National de Lyon

Jean Nouvel's early 1990s reconstruction preserved the nineteenth-century facade of Lyon's opera house while adding a tall glazed barrel roof. Opera, ballet, concerts, rehearsals, workshops, and administrative spaces stack vertically within the constrained city-center site. The black upper volume and public plaza distinguish the building from a conventional historic auditorium, even though the original foyer and exterior remain visible.

45.7678° N / 4.8363° E

14 AdmissionFree

Palais de la Bourse de Lyon

The Palais de la Bourse faces Place des Cordeliers in central Lyon as a nineteenth-century commercial palace built for the city's chamber of commerce. A sculpted facade and grand interior halls recall Lyon's silk and banking wealth. The building still hosts events and offices beside the busy shopping streets.

45.7640° N / 4.8363° E

15 AdmissionFree

Westfield La Part-Dieu

Westfield La Part-Dieu is the major retail mall of Lyon's Part-Dieu business district, beside the high-speed rail station. Recent renovation refreshed shops, food courts, and family amenities inside a large multi-level complex that serves both commuters and residents of the eastern centre.

45.7617° N / 4.8563° E

16 AdmissionFree

Auditorium-Orchestre National de Lyon

The Auditorium-Orchestre National de Lyon, designed by Henri Pottier and renovated in 1993, is the concert home of the city's national orchestra at La Part-Dieu. The hall programmes more than 160 concerts a year from late Baroque to contemporary work, with a strong French repertoire. Its concrete shell anchors the modern business district east of the historic centre.
