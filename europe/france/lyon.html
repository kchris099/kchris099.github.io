Lyon

Lyon developed from the Roman colony of Lugdunum, founded around 43 BC at the confluence of the Rhône and Saône. It later became a major ecclesiastical seat, silk-weaving center, and industrial hub, with traboules, hidden passage-ways, linking Renaissance merchant quarters. Hillside basilicas, riverfront civic buildings, and preserved Roman theatres show the long continuity of urban life.

Basilica of Notre Dame of Fourvière

The Basilica of Fourvière was built in the late 19th century on the hill that overlooks Lyon's old city. Its hilltop position links modern pilgrimage architecture with the historic Fourvière ridge, where Roman Lugdunum once stood. The church and terrace offer wide views over the confluence of the Rhône and Saône.
