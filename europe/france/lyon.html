Explore Lyon

A Brief History

Lyon developed from the Roman colony of Lugdunum, founded around 43 BC at the confluence of the Rhône and Saône. It later became a major ecclesiastical seat, silk-weaving center, and industrial hub, with traboules—hidden passage-ways—linking Renaissance merchant quarters. Hillside basilicas, riverfront civic buildings, and preserved Roman theatres show the long continuity of urban life.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

Basilica of Notre Dame of Fourvière

The Basilica of Fourvière was built in the late 19th century on the hill that overlooks Lyon's old city. Its hilltop position links modern pilgrimage architecture with the historic Fourvière ridge, where Roman Lugdunum once stood. The church and terrace offer wide views over the confluence of the Rhône and Saône.

Free

Théâtre Gallo Romain

The Roman theaters on Fourvière preserve the remains of Lugdunum, the Roman capital of Gaul. The larger theater and adjacent odeon reflect the scale of urban and cultural life on the hill during the imperial period. The site is part of the Lugdunum museum network and can be visited without charge.

Free

Hôtel de Ville de Lyon

Lyon's city hall was built in the 17th century on Place des Terreaux and rebuilt in part after a fire in the 19th century. Its classical facade and courtyard remain a civic landmark at the political center of the city, facing the square where public festivals, markets, and municipal events still take place.

Free

Lyon Zoo

Lyon Zoo opened in the Parc de la Tête d'Or and forms part of a larger 19th-century urban park developed by the city. The zoo is integrated into the public green space rather than operating as a separate gated complex, and visitors can walk the park paths and animal enclosures without an admission fee.

Free

Porte des Enfants du Rhône

The Porte des Enfants du Rhône is one of the main ornamental entrances to the Parc de la Tête d'Or. It reflects the 19th-century design of Lyon's major urban park and its formal approach from the city. The gate marks the transition from boulevard traffic to the lawns, lake, and paths of the park interior.

Free

Traboules du Vieux Lyon

Les Traboules du Vieux Lyon, often referred to as the Secret Passageways of Old Lyon, are a fascinating maze of covered walkways that weave through the city's historic Renaissance district. Dating back to the 4th century, these unique passages were initially designed to offer residents sheltered routes between their homes and the bustling River Saône.

Free

Place Bellecour

Place Bellecour is a vast and picturesque public square located in Lyon, France. It is known for its tree-lined avenues, flower stalls, and a small art museum. The square stretches from the lower part of the Presqu'ile between Lyon's two rivers to the Confluences area where the Rhone and Saone meet. It is considered Europe's largest square and features an impressive equestrian statue of Louis XIV.

Free

Mur des Canuts

The Mur des Canuts, located on the Boulevard des Canuts in Lyon's Croix Rousse district, is a massive mural that showcases the history of the neighborhood, once inhabited by silk weavers known as canuts. Painted in trompe-l'oeil style, it seamlessly integrates with its surroundings. Initially created in 1987, this mural has been periodically updated to reflect changes to nearby structures.

Free

Confluence

Confluence is a modern retail complex in Lyon's 2nd arrondissement, situated at the convergence of the Rhone and Saone rivers. Once an industrial area, it has been revitalized with contemporary buildings designed by renowned architects. The district features a vibrant commercial artery, Cours Charlemagne, offering trendy restaurants and cafes like Maison Pepin and Platypus Brewpub.

Free

Parc de la Tête d'Or

Parc de la Tête d'Or is one of the largest and most beautiful urban parks in France, located in Lyon. Covering 117 hectares, it features a large boating lake, a free zoological park, and extensive botanical gardens, including a renowned international rose garden. The park is a beloved destination for recreation, nature observation, and family outings.

Free

Grand Parc Miribel Jonage

Grand Parc Miribel Jonage is a vast natural park located near Lyon, perfect for outdoor activities such as cycling, walking, and picnicking. It features beautiful lakes, beaches, and a golf course, offering a serene escape into nature. The site is catalogued as a tourist attraction.

Free

Bartholdi Fountain

Bartholdi Fountain, created by the renowned French sculptor Frédéric Auguste Bartholdi, was unveiled in 1892. It is located at Place des Terreaux in Lyon and is a attraction. The fountain's design exudes a sense of movement and energy, making it a remarkable sight for travellers. During the day, its refreshing coolness provides relief from the heat, while at night it is illuminated with cozy lighting.

Free

Opéra National de Lyon

The Opéra National de Lyon is a grand neo-Renaissance opera house In Lyon. Its historic facade, adorned with ornate columns and arched entryways, sets the stage for the opulent interior featuring gilded decorations and plush velvet seating. The magnificent auditorium can accommodate over 1,000 patrons. Architect Jean Nouvel's reconstruction in the early 90s preserved the original facade from 1831 while adding a huge cylindrical canopy that now defines its appearance.

Free

Palais de la Bourse de Lyon

The Palais de la Bourse is a magnificent 19th-century building located in the Cordeliers district of Lyon. Originally built to house the Chamber of Commerce and the Stock Exchange, it features opulent Second Empire architecture, including a very decorated central hall. Today, it serves as a prestigious venue for conferences, exhibitions, and cultural events.

Free

Westfield La Part-Dieu

Westfield La Part-Dieu, located in Lyon, France, has recently undergone a renovation and now offers a variety of activities for families. The shopping center features numerous international restaurants, a colorful climbing gym for children to learn and play, and a large cinema for entertainment. Additionally, the area is known as Lyon's leading business district with excellent accessibility through public transport and an airport nearby.

Free

Auditorium-Orchestre National de Lyon

The Auditorium-Orchestre National de Lyon, designed by Henri Pottier and renovated in 1993, hosts over 160 concerts annually, featuring a diverse range of music genres from late Baroque to contemporary pieces with a focus on French music. The venue offers free admission for students under 28, job seekers, RSA holders with disabilities and children aged 6 to 12 accompanied by an adult.
