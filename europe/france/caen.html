Explore Caen

A Brief History

Caen rose around the abbeys William the Conqueror and his wife Matilda founded in the eleventh century to atone for their consanguineous marriage, giving the city two Romanesque churches that still frame civic space. As capital of Normandy, Caen developed textile mills and a university that survived the Wars of Religion and Revolution. Heavy bombing in 1944 destroyed much of the centre before reconstruction preserved the abbeys and rebuilt avenues that connect the city to D-Day beaches nearby.

Sword Beach - Queen Red

Sword Beach, the easternmost of the D-Day landing zones on June 6, 1944, is a significant historical site where British and French forces bravely landed amidst fierce German resistance. This stretch of Normandy coastline between Ouistreham and Saint-Aubin-sur-Mer was pivotal for the 3rd British Infantry Division and commandos from the 1st Special Service Brigade as they fought to secure their foothold in France.

Free

Lighthouse of Ouistreham

The Lighthouse of Ouistreham is a renowned historical landmark that attracts travellers from all over the world. This structure, located in Ouistreham, France, offers views from its summit and provides an experience for those who brave the approximately 70 steps to reach the top. Open on select days, this lighthouse allows travellers to admire the picturesque town of Ouistreham and its surroundings from a unique vantage point.

Free

Abbaye-aux-Hommes

L'Abbaye-aux-Hommes, also known as the Abbey of Saint-Etienne, is a remarkable 11th-century Romanesque abbey in Caen, built by William the Conqueror. It was constructed using light yellow Jurassic limestone called Caen stone and is considered a prime example of Romanesque architecture. The abbey now serves as part of the City Hall and is famous for being the burial site of William the Conqueror.

Free

Caen Botanical Garden

Caen Botanical Garden, also known as Jardin des Plantes, is a must-visit attraction in the heart of the city. It features local flora, tropical greenhouses, and a medicinal section. travellers can participate in workshops and enjoy guided tours for groups. The garden is home to many rare specimens and offers a serene environment for leisurely strolls. While in the area, includes the opportunity to explore the medieval streets or visit the lovely marina in the city center.

€5

Caen Castle

Caen Castle, constructed on the remains of a fortress by William the Conqueror, is among Europe's largest medieval enclosures. It served as a gathering place for the Dukes of Normandy and Kings of England and was a key stronghold during the Hundred Years War. The castle now houses two museums that offer an in-depth look into Normandy's history, while its ramparts provide views of the city.

Free

St Peter's Catholic Church

St. Peter's Catholic Church is a remarkable landmark nestled in the heart of Caen, dating back to the 13th century. With its intricate facade and recently restored spire, this church stands as a testament to the city's rich history. travellers can explore its interior adorned with sculptures and stained glass windows that tell stories of yesteryears.

Free

Abbaye aux Dames de Caen

The Abbey of Sainte-Trinité is an 11th century monastery located in Caen, known for its arcaded courtyard and as the final resting place of Queen Mathilda. Commissioned by William the Conqueror and his wife to appease religious concerns about their marriage, the abbey has a fascinating history. The building was renovated in 1990 and now serves as the provincial government's office while also being open to travellers.

Free

Zenith Caen

Zenith Caen is a large performing arts hall that hosts a variety of stage events, including musicals and concerts. It offers great acoustics, comfortable seating, and reasonably priced food and drinks. The venue is conveniently located near good hotels and has excellent transport links. travellers have praised the cleanliness of the facilities and the friendliness of the staff.

Colline aux Oiseaux Park

in the northwest of Caen, Park of Colline aux Oiseaux, or Bird Hill, offers a delightful escape with its well-maintained grounds and captivating views of the city. The park boasts various attractions including a rose garden, playgrounds, picnic spots, mini-golf course, café and a small farm. travellers can also explore nearby landmarks such as the Caen Memorial Museum and gardens at Le Jardin des Plantes.
