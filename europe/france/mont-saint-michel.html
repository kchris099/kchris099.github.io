Mont Saint-Michel

Mont Saint-Michel began as a rocky tidal island dedicated to the archangel Michael after a reported vision in 708, with Benedictine monks building the abbey that crowns the mount from the eleventh century. Pilgrims crossed dangerous sands at low tide to reach a shrine that Norman dukes and French kings endowed through the Middle Ages. Prison use after the Revolution gave way to restoration and, in the twenty-first century, to a causeway redesign that lets the bay's tides once again isolate the monument.

Mont-Saint-Michel Abbey

Mont-Saint-Michel Abbey crowns the tidal rock above a dense village and surrounding bay. Benedictine builders developed Romanesque and Gothic churches, cloisters, halls, refectories, crypts and defensive spaces over several centuries. Stairs and narrow passages climb sharply through the complex, whose changing uses as monastery, fortress, prison and restored monument remain visible in the masonry.

Church of Saint-Pierre - Le Mont-Saint-Michel

The parish Church of Saint-Pierre stands halfway up Mont-Saint-Michel's Grande Rue below the abbey. A compact nave, side chapels, statues, stained glass, and a small cemetery serve the village and pilgrims in an intimate space. The church is dedicated partly to St Michael but remains distinct from the monastic church at the summit, with regular worship continuing amid heavy pedestrian traffic.

Tour du Nord

Tour du Nord, located in the Normandy region, offers views and access to the abbey. Travellers can enjoy vistas and explore the 13th-century North Tower for a unique perspective on the tides. The northeast tower provides great views of the tidal area and mainland. While it's a bit of a walk from the car park, it's worth it to experience this grand and massive site with its many restaurants and hotels.

Moulin de Moidrey

The Moulin de Moidrey is a fully restored, working windmill located just a few kilometers from Mont Saint-Michel. Dating back to 1806, it was careful rebuilt in 2003 and now produces traditional flour using wind power. Visitors can tour the mill, learn about the milling process, and enjoy excellent views of Mont Saint-Michel from its elevated position.

48.5852° N / 1.5061° W

Barrage du Mont-Saint-Michel

Barrage du Mont Saint Michel offers three paths leading to the Place du Barrage, providing a 30-minute walking trip. It's a great spot for capturing shots of Mont Saint Michel with the waterway in the center. The location is ideal for long exposure night photography, especially from the raised section of the barrier at the back. Travellers recommend visiting during sunrise or sunset for views and colors.

48.6176° N / 1.5122° W

Tourist Information Centre

The visitor information center on the mainland near Mont-Saint-Michel helps orient travelers before they cross the bay-side causeway. Maps, transport guidance, tide explanations and accessibility information clarify the relationship between the parking area, shuttle route and medieval mount. Introductory displays also outline the bay's environment and the abbey's history.

48.6125° N / 1.5067° W

07 AdmissionFree

Écomusée de la Baie du Mont-Saint-Michel

The Écomusée de la Baie at Vains interprets Mont Saint-Michel Bay through exhibits on tides, salt working, fishing, and shoreline livelihoods. Displays explain how people adapted to the extreme tidal range around the mount. It pairs well with a bay walk before or after visiting the abbey island.

48.6642° N / 1.4459° W
