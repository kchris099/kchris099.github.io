Explore Provins

A Brief History

Provins prospered as one of the Champagne fairs that linked Flemish cloth merchants to Italian bankers in the twelfth and thirteenth centuries, storing wealth in underground passages beneath its upper town. Counts of Champagne fortified walls, tithe barns, and the Caesar Tower to control tolls on roads toward Paris. When fair traffic declined, Provins kept its ramparts and rose cultivation, later recognised as a rare surviving ensemble of medieval trade infrastructure near the Brie plateau.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

The Eagles of the ramparts - Vol Libre Production

"The Eagles of the Ramparts - Vol Libre Production" is a captivating show set in the historical town of Provins. The performance features majestic birds flying overhead, creating a experience for travellers. Although it's presented only in French, the visuals and skilled handlers make it an event. The open-air setting adds to the charm, but be prepared for potential distractions like bugs.

Free

The Legend of the Knights show Equestrio

Experience the excitement of The Legend of the Knights show Equestrio, a thrilling equestrian jousting tournament at the foot of the ramparts. Watch as knights engage in impressive tumbles and sword fighting, providing guaranteed thrills within a captivating medieval setting. This show combines theater and acrobatics to bring to life the nuances of medieval France.

€5

Caesar Keep

Caesar Keep, also known as Tour Cesar, is a 12th-century watchtower located in Provins, a town with well-preserved medieval character. The tower offers panoramic views of the town and its surroundings from the top floors. travellers can explore the rooms of this historical site and gain insight into its original purpose. The steep and narrow original stairs lead to the top floor, where travellers can enjoy 360-degree views of Provins and its monuments.

Free

Saint-Quiriace collegiate church

Saint-Quiriace Collegiate Church is an imposing 12th-century church that was never completed due to financial difficulties. It features a dome and a superb nave, attracting travellers with its grandeur. The church once hosted Joan of Arc and French kings for mass, adding to its historical significance. travellers can explore the unfinished cathedral-like structure and admire its architectural marvels.

€6

Les souterrains de Provins

Les souterrains de Provins are a network of underground tunnels that connect various basements, featuring graffiti dating back to the 16th century. The guided tour provides a wealth of historical information, although some travellers found it challenging due to language barriers and group size. The constant 12-degree temperature in the tunnels requires travellers to dress warmly.

€5

Grange aux dîmes

Grange aux Dîmes is a historical site located in a major trading center with well-preserved three-storey merchants' houses that were used for storage and sales. The site offers an informative exhibition on Medieval Provins fairs, showcasing the lifestyle of medieval merchants. Additionally, travellers can explore La Maison Romane, which has been transformed into a museum featuring art from different periods.

Free

Priory Church of Saint-Ayoul at Provins

The Priory Church of Saint-Ayoul in Provins is a captivating historical site that offers travellers a glimpse into the past. Originally housing an organ built for the Jouy abbey in 1777, this instrument was moved to Saint-Ayoul in 1792 and retains much of its original material from the Ancien Regime despite various modifications over time.
