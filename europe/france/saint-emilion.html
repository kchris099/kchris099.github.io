Explore Saint-Émilion

A Brief History

Saint-Émilion grew around a limestone bluff where the monk Émilion settled in the eighth century, carving hermitages and a monolithic church that later pilgrims visited beneath medieval vineyards. English rule during the Hundred Years' War strengthened the wine trade whose classified estates now surround the town. Collegiate church, cloisters, and king's keep tower record how a small ecclesiastical borough became the ceremonial heart of one of Bordeaux's oldest viticultural jurisdictions.

Monolithic Church of Saint-Emilion

The Monolithic Church of Saint-Emilion is an underground church carved from limestone, with a towering 68-meter bell tower. It dates back to the 12th century and is said to be made from a single stone. The majority of the church is located underground, and travellers can explore it on official tours organized by the Office of Tourism. The village was named after Emilion, an 8th-century hermit who established a religious community there.

Château Ambe Tour Pourret

Château Ambe Tour Pourret is a estate located just a few minutes from the medieval town of Saint-Emilion in France. Acquired in 2007 by Francoise Lannoye, this small winery offers an exquisite Sunday brunch on the first Sunday of each month from June to October, featuring a buffet of regional specialties and themed dishes. travellers can also enjoy wine tastings and picnics on the grounds while taking in the picturesque surroundings.

Ferme la BARBANNE

Ferme la Barbanne is an educational farm and agricultural attraction located near Saint-Émilion.
