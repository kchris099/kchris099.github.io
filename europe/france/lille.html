Explore Lille

A Brief History

Lille grew at the crossroads of Flemish cloth towns and French royal authority, passing between Burgundian, Spanish, and French crowns before Louis XIV seized it in 1667. Vauban fortified the citadel, while nineteenth-century mills and railways made it an industrial hub of the Nord. Flemish step-gables, the Grand Place belfry, and the Palais des Beaux-Arts reflect how a border city blended Low Countries commerce with French administrative and mining wealth.

Top Sights & Activities

Decathlon Arena - Stade Pierre Mauroy

Stade Pierre Mauroy is a prominent stadium in Lille, serving as the home ground for LOSC Lille Metropole soccer team. With a seating capacity of 50,000, it has hosted various events including major concerts, rugby matches, and indoor sports competitions. The stadium gained recognition during EURO 2016 by hosting six matches, including the quarter-final between Wales and Belgium.

Free

Centre Commercial V2

Centre Commercial V2 is a popular shopping destination for locals, known as the largest shopping center in the city. It offers free parking and accessible bathrooms. The supermarket is expansive with two floors and there are various shops including a large bookshop, clothing stores, and more. Decathlon V2 is a highlight with its wide range of sports offerings under one roof. The staff are friendly and the overall experience is enjoyable.

Free

La Vieille Bourse

La Vieille Bourse is a visitor site in Lille, France. Its ornate architecture and gold Mercury statue on the campanile add to its grandeur. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest. La Vieille Bourse remains part of the public visitor circuit in Lille.

Free

Furet du Nord

Furet du Nord is a known bookstore located in Lille, offering an extensive collection of books and other items across its eight floors. It is often compared to the French equivalent of Barnes and Noble, attracting a large crowd due to its unmatched selection of books, games, and various related products, the store has a vast array of children's books suitable for all age groups.

Free

Lille Opera

Lille Opera is a theatre in Lille, France. Construction and early use date to around 1907. Lille Opera, situated in Lille, France, is a neoclassical building that was constructed between 1907 and 1913 to replace the former opera house destroyed by fire in 1903. The opera house is a prominent venue for opera, ballet, and classical music performances. Public access is usually through ticketed performances or daytime visits.

Free

Notre-Dame-de-la-Treille Cathedral

Notre-Dame-de-la-Treille Cathedral in Lille is a 19th-century Gothic revival structure dedicated to the Virgin Mary. Construction began in 1854 but was only completed in 1999, with a unique main facade designed by local architect Pierre-Louis Carlier and engineer Peter Rice. The cathedral houses a crypt and a sacred art museum featuring works by artists such as Warhol and Baselitz.

Free

Westfield Euralille

Westfield Euralille is a visitor site in Lille, France. Westfield Euralille is a modern and expansive urban complex located 750 meters east of the city center. It is situated between Lille's main train stations and encompasses a business district, residential area, hotels, shops, restaurants, and entertainment venues. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Free

Porte de Paris

Porte de Paris is a waterfront area in Lille, France. The Porte de Paris is a grand arch monument in Lille, built in the 17th century to commemorate Louis XVI's capture of the city. It was part of the fortified walls constructed after Louis XIV captured Lille, and it remains one of the few remaining gates from that time. Quays and embankment paths form the main walking route.

€8

Beffroi de Lille

The Beffroi de Lille, also known as the Belfry of Lille, is a towering structure and a significant landmark in Lille, France. This bell tower stands at an height of 104 meters and offers panoramic vistas of the city and valley from its observation deck. Designed by known architect Emile Dubuisson, it showcases mesmerizing Art Deco architecture and is a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Free

Zoo de Lille

In a 3.5-hectare green setting, Zoo de Lille is a compact and lush zoo that showcases a diverse range of birds, mammals, and reptiles in themed areas. It has gained recognition as one of the top parks in Lille, France for its collection of animals from various species. Travellers can immerse themselves in an enriching and exotic experience while exploring over 100 wild species from around the world.

Free

Citadelle de Lille

Citadelle de Lille is a fortified historic site in Lille, France. This historic site offers monthly guided tours and is surrounded by a scenic public park. Walls, courtyards, or towers form the main visitor route when the grounds are open. Citadelle de Lille remains part of the public visitor circuit in Lille.

€11

Villa Cavrois

Villa Cavrois, a 1932 modernist mansion located between Roubaix and Croix, has been careful restored and is now open to the public. Designed by architect Robert Mallet-Stevens for industrialist Paul Cavrois, this Art Deco house has sleek lines and period furniture. The villa's interior reflects a colorful and geometric style that captures the essence of its time.

Free

Marché de Wazemmes

Marché de Wazemmes is a visitor site in Lille, France. Marché de Wazemmes is a and traditional market located in Place Nouvelle Aventure, considered important markets in northern France. The market is known for its large outdoor gatherings on Tuesday, Thursday, and Sunday mornings. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.
