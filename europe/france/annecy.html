Explore Annecy

A Brief History

Annecy developed as a strategic settlement between Geneva and Chambery, serving as the capital of the County of Geneva from the thirteenth century. It became a center of the Catholic Counter-Reformation in the sixteenth century under Francis de Sales. The city's historic core is characterized by its medieval castle, Palais de l'Ile, and a network of canals. Today, Annecy is celebrated for its preserved old town and the pristine waters of Lake Annecy.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

Pont des Amours

The Pont des Amours, also known as the Lover's Bridge, is a romantic footbridge in Annecy that has been crossed by countless lovers. It offers views of the city, canal, and Lake Annecy. Whether you approach it from Paquier or Jardins de l'Europe, taking a walk along the water to reach this bridge is a delightful experience.

Free

Jardins de l'Europe

Jardins de l'Europe is a picturesque lakeside park in Annecy, offering views of Lake Annecy and the surrounding mountains. This 19th-century English-style garden provides a peaceful retreat for leisurely strolls, picnics, and relaxation. The park boasts century-old plantations, providing a perfect balance of light and shade that appeals to nature lovers and artists alike.

€6

Gorges du Fier

The Gorges du Fier is a dramatic natural wonder near Annecy, featuring a narrow, deep canyon carved by the Fier River. Visitors traverse the gorge via a suspended footbridge attached to the sheer rock face, offering dramatic views of the rushing water below and the unique geological formations of the canyon walls.

€7

Musée-Château d'Annecy

The Musée-Château d'Annecy is housed in a restored medieval castle that once served as the residence of the Counts of Geneva. Overlooking the old town and Lake Annecy, the museum features diverse collections including regional history, contemporary art, and alpine ecology. The historic architecture of the château itself, with its massive towers and defensive walls, is a major draw for visitors exploring the region's heritage.

Free

Église Notre Dame de Liesse

Located near the Canal du Thiou, Église Notre Dame de Liesse was completed in 1851 and is dedicated to the Virgin Mary. It has become a sanctuary where the counts of Geneva buried their families. The church features a 16th-century clock tower and Gothic stained glass windows from the 15th century, adding to its beauty and historical significance.

Free

Basilique de la Visitation

Perched on a rocky peak of the Semnoz massif in southeastern France, Basilique de la Visitation is a striking Catholic basilica with commanding views of the city, Lake Annecy, and the surrounding mountains. Built in the early 20th century, this architectural gem offers visitors an opportunity to hike and enjoy panoramic vistas. Its design and rich history make it a must-visit attraction in Annecy.

Free

Lake Annecy

Nestled in the heart of the Alps, Lake Annecy is a destination offering a variety of activities for nature lovers and adventure seekers. The crystal clear waters make it perfect for swimming and water sports, while the surrounding mountains provide views. Visitors can explore picturesque villages along the lakeshore and enjoy leisurely bike rides through adjacent parks.

€27

ACRO' AVENTURES TALLOIRES

If you're looking for an adventure, look no further than Les Grands Espaces - Parapente Annecy. Open year-round, this paragliding school is particularly recommended for first-time flyers between March and September. Guests rave about their experiences, highlighting the friendly and professional staff who make you feel at ease from the moment you arrive.

€100

Les Grands Espaces - Parapente Annecy

Les Grands Espaces is a premier paragliding school and tour operator based near Lake Annecy, renowned as one of Europe's top paragliding destinations. They offer tandem flights that launch from the surrounding mountain peaks, providing passengers with expansive aerial views of the pristine lake and the dramatic French Alps.

Free

Col de la Forclaz

Col de la Forclaz, also known as Col de la Forclaz de Montmin, is a picturesque mountain pass that offers views of the lake and surrounding mountains. Whether you choose to drive, cycle, or hike to the top from various starting points, be treated to panoramic vistas and opportunities to spot paragliders taking off. The area features restaurants, souvenir shops, and viewing terraces for visitors to enjoy.
