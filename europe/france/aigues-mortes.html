A Brief History

Established as a regional settlement beyond an older fortified core, the city developed through industrialization in the late 19th century, post-war reconstruction, and modern civic expansion that reshaped its public spaces, markets, and surrounding landscape. Restored religious buildings, civic buildings, and natural features now define the contemporary visitor experience.

Towers and Walls of Aigues-Mortes

The Towers and Walls of Aigues-Mortes are a fascinating medieval defensive structure surrounding the historic town of Aigues-Mortes in the Languedoc-Roussillon region of France. Built in the 13th century by King Louis IX, this limestone fortress offers views over courtyards and the surrounding salt marshes, including the Salinas de Aigues Mortes.

Free

Constancy Tower

Constancy Tower, also known as Tour de Constance, is a significant site for Protestant Christians. It stands as a lasting monument to the faith of 30 women who were imprisoned for their evangelical beliefs, some enduring captivity for up to 40 years. The words "resister," etched by Marie Durand, one of the leaders among the women, are still visible on the stone. The tower offers personalized guided tours that enhance the experience and provide insights into its historical significance.

Free

Gardette Gate

The Gardette Gate is a prominent landmark in the town of Aigue Morte, located in the south of France. The gate was built in about 11th century and is one of the earliest surviving examples of medieval walled towns in France. The gate is also notable for its beautiful Gothic architecture, which makes it a popular tourist attraction.

Free

Maison du Grand Site de France de la Camargue Gardoise

Nestled in the landscapes of the Grand Site de France de la Camargue Gardoise, the Maison du Grand Site offers an experience for nature lovers and adventure seekers alike. explore a scenic 6 km Greenway that stretches between Aigues-Mortes and Le Grau du Roi, where you’ll be captivated by vibrant colors from lush greenery, shimmering ponds, and expansive salt flats. Birdwatchers will delight in spotting various species, including magnificent pink flamingos.

Free

Tour Carbonnière

Nestled in the heart of the Camargue, Tour Carbonnière is a captivating 13th-century stone tower that stands as a testament to history and natural beauty. Originally built as a defensive structure for Aigues-Mortes, this remarkable site offers travellers an elevated lookout terrace with views over the surrounding marshes. The journey to reach it can be made on foot, by bike, or by car—though parking is limited and free.

€18

Seaquarium Institut Marin

Seaquarium Institut Marin, located in Le Grau-du-Roi, is a family-friendly destination open year-round. Situated on the left bank of Grau du Roi in Petite Camargue, it's easily accessible with elevators for travellers with reduced mobility. The massive aquarium houses over 200 different marine species including sharks, turtles, seals, and sea lions.

Free

Arènes du Grau du Roi

Arènes du Grau du Roi is a cultural hub hosting various events, with the most notable being the traditional Camargue bull races where participants attempt to remove ribbons from the bulls' horns. During the patron saint festival, there are bull releases adding to the excitement. The property's location offers easy access to Seaquarium and Amigoland Amusement Park, as well as natural attractions like Plage du Point Zero Public Beach and Plage Rive Gauche Park.

Free

Port de la Grande-Motte

Port de la Grande-Motte is a bustling marina In La Grande-Motte, France. The main entry point for travellers is through Port de la Gardette, offering a rare open area that extends to the Chateau and leads into the walled town. The marina itself is a picturesque sight with boats of all sizes neatly lined up in parallel docks, creating long perspectives.

Free

Pasino Partouche Grande Motte

The Casino Grande Motte is a sleek and contemporary establishment that offers a variety of gambling options, including slot machines, poker, and roulette. In addition to the gaming facilities, the casino also features a restaurant and a 1,200-seat theater. travellers have praised the helpful and efficient staff who maintain a discreet presence. The venue itself is well-suited for events such as dance festivals, with multiple high-quality spaces available both upstairs and downstairs.

Free

Sunset Beach

Sunset Beach, located in La Grande Motte, is a beautiful and expansive shoreline known for its fine, soft sand and considerable length. It's a popular destination for families and older individuals seeking a peaceful and serene environment. The beach offers ample space for swimming and volleyball, with nearby dining options available. travellers can also enjoy the convenience of various amenities close by.
