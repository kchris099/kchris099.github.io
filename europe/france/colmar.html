Destination Guide — Northern France

Explore Colmar

A Brief History

Established as a regional settlement beyond an older fortified core, the city developed through industrialization in the late 19th century, post-war reconstruction, and modern civic expansion that reshaped its public spaces, markets, and surrounding landscape. Restored religious buildings, civic buildings, and natural features now define the contemporary visitor experience.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

La Petite Venise

La Petite Venise in Colmar, France is a charming area known for its canals and historic buildings. The fisherman's section features half-timbered 16th- and 17th-century buildings along the Quai de la Poisonnerie, with the River Lauch flowing through. Swans gracefully float on the water while weeping willows line the banks, creating a picturesque scene reminiscent of a fairy tale illustration.

Free

Marché Couvert Colmar

Marché Couvert Colmar is a historic covered market dating back to the mid-1800s, located in the Petite Venice area. The building features a charming mix of brick and metalwork, preserving its original purpose as a marketplace for fresh produce like fruits, vegetables, meat, and fish. travellers can also find local specialties and dairy products here. The market is an ideal spot to grab ingredients for cooking or pick up ready-made items for a quick meal.

Free

Colmar Old Town

Vieille Ville, also known as the Old Town of Colmar, is a picturesque and charming area that feels like something out of a fairy tale. The streets are lined with colorful half-timbered houses, creating a storybook-like atmosphere. travellers can wander through narrow cobblestone streets and explore local cafes, bakeries, and shops offering crafts. St. Martin's Church stands as an impressive architectural marvel in this historic district.

Free

Église des Dominicains

The Dominican Church in Colmar is a restored 14th-century Gothic church that houses remarkable Alsatian artworks. Construction began in 1283, and despite necessary renovations over the years, the church retains its fascinating history. Notable pieces include revolutionary painter Grunewald's artwork and Martin Schongauer's famous 'Madonna of the Rose Bush.' The stained-glass windows are a for art enthusiasts, depicting religious scenes.

Free

Parc du Champ de Mars

Parc du Champ de Mars is a spacious and green park In Colmar, offering a peaceful retreat with its tree-filled surroundings, fountain, and statues of notable French figures. Throughout the year, travellers can enjoy leisurely strolls, picnics, and even ice skating during winter. The park also hosts a charming Christmas market and festive activities during the holiday season.

Free

Statue de la Liberté

The Statue of Liberty in Colmar, France is a 12-metre resin replica and serves as a memorial to Auguste Bartholdi, the sculptor who was born in Colmar. This tourist attraction is unique to the area and can be found at the northern suburbs roundabout. While it may not be as well-known as its larger counterpart in New York, it remains a beautiful imitation that showcases Bartholdi's talent.

Free

The Three Castles of Eguisheim

The Three Castles of Eguisheim are a collection of pink sandstone ruins situated atop hills, accessible via hiking trails. travellers can also indulge in wine tasting at Maison Wolfberger Cave & Boutique d'Eguisheim and explore the central square with its fountain, Eguisheim castle, and Saint-Leon-IX chapel. The area offers great hiking trails through beautiful woodland, leading to the fascinating castle ruins with excellent valley views.

Free

Village Historique de Riquewihr

Nestled in the Vosges Mountains, Riquewihr is a charming village surrounded by Grand Cru vineyards. Its cobblestone streets and colorful half-timbered houses make it a picturesque destination, perfect for history enthusiasts and culture lovers. The village's medieval charm is enhanced by its hillside vineyards and renowned wineries.

Free

Château de Kaysersberg

Nestled in the picturesque town of Kaysersberg, the Château de Kaysersberg stands as a testament to medieval architecture, dating back to around 1200. Although much of the castle is in ruins, it offers an adventurous climb up its tower, which features a daunting spiral staircase with 100 steps. The effort is rewarded with panoramic views of the charming village below and the surrounding vineyards.

Free

Château du Giersberg

Château du Giersberg is one of the castle ruins in Alsace, with parts dating back to the 13th century. It was once owned by a powerful family who ruled the area for centuries. The highlight of visiting this smaller-sized ruin is the view it offers, overlooking Ulrichsburg, the town of Ribeauville, and the Rhine plain. On a clear day, travellers can even see as far as Freiburg.
