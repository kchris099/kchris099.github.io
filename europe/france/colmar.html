Colmar

As a free imperial city, Colmar sold Rhine wine from half-timbered houses on the Lauch. Louis XIV annexed it to France; German rule after 1871 and the return in 1918 left bilingual street names. The Unterlinden Museum's Isenheim Altarpiece and the Petite Venise canals are the town's two most visited historic anchors. Bartholdi, sculptor of the Statue of Liberty, was born in this Alsatian wine town.

La Petite Venise

La Petite Venise is the canal quarter of Colmar where half-timbered houses line the River Lauch and flower boxes hang over the water. Swans and weeping willows appear along the banks, and pedestrian bridges link the market halls to the denser old-town lanes.

02 AdmissionFree

Marché Couvert Colmar

Colmar's covered market, dating from the mid-nineteenth century beside Petite Venise, sells produce, cheeses, meats, and Alsatian specialties under a brick hall. Locals buy ingredients for home cooking or take ready foods for a quick meal along the canal.

48.0747° N / 7.3598° E

03 AdmissionFree

Colmar Old Town

Colmar's old town preserves a dense network of medieval and early modern streets shaped by trade between France, the German lands and the Rhine plain. Half-timbered houses, canals, churches, guild buildings, courtyards, shops and former merchants' residences vary from block to block. Districts such as the Tanners' Quarter and Little Venice show specific histories beyond the generalized image of colorful facades.

48.0753° N / 7.3596° E

04 AdmissionFree

Église des Dominicains

The Dominican Church in Colmar, begun in 1283, holds Martin Schongauer's Madonna of the Rose Bush and other notable works, with stained glass that narrates biblical scenes. Gothic structure and later repairs frame an interior still used for exhibitions and worship.

48.0783° N / 7.3564° E

05 AdmissionFree

Parc du Champ de Mars

Parc du Champ de Mars in Colmar spreads trees, a fountain, and statues of notable French figures across open lawns. Strolls, picnics, and winter ice skating share the grounds through the year. A Christmas market and holiday events take over parts of the park in season.

48.0756° N / 7.3528° E

06 AdmissionFree

Statue de la Liberté

A twelve-meter resin replica of the Statue of Liberty stands in a roundabout at Colmar's northern approach. Installed in 2004 to mark the centenary of sculptor Auguste Bartholdi's death, it recalls that the designer of the New York monument was born in the city. The roadside setting limits close observation, but the figure acts as a clear civic marker on the route toward Strasbourg.

48.1084° N / 7.3636° E

07 AdmissionFree

The Three Castles of Eguisheim

The Three Castles of Eguisheim are a collection of pink sandstone ruins situated atop hills, accessible via hiking trails. Travellers can also indulge in wine tasting at Maison Wolfberger Cave & Boutique d'Eguisheim and explore the central square with its fountain, Eguisheim castle, and Saint-Leon-IX chapel. The area offers great hiking trails through woodland, leading to the fascinating castle ruins with excellent valley views.

48.0389° N / 7.2731° E

08 AdmissionFree

Village Historique de Riquewihr

Historic Riquewihr is a fortified wine village on the Alsace route north of Colmar, with cobbled streets and colourful half-timbered houses inside medieval walls. Vineyards climb the hills immediately beyond the gates, and cellars open for tastings along the main street.

48.1660° N / 7.2959° E

09 AdmissionFree

Château de Kaysersberg

The castle ruin above Kaysersberg crowns a vineyard hill overlooking the Alsace wine town, with a keep and wall fragments reached by a short climb from the streets below. From the heights the view takes in the valley roofs and the slopes that feed local cellars.

48.1404° N / 7.2623° E

10 AdmissionFree

Château du Giersberg

Château du Giersberg is one of the castle ruins in Alsace, with parts dating back to the 13th century. It was once owned by a powerful family who ruled the area for centuries. The highlight of visiting this smaller-sized ruin is the view it offers, overlooking Ulrichsburg, the town of Ribeauville, and the Rhine plain. On a clear day, travellers can even see as far as Freiburg.

48.2034° N / 7.3072° E
