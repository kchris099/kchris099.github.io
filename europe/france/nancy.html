Nancy

Nancy flourished as capital of the duchy of Lorraine, especially under exiled Polish king Stanislas Leszczyński, who rebuilt squares, gates, and a fine arts academy in the mid-eighteenth century. The medieval old town and Renaissance ducal palace preserve earlier Burgundian and Habsburg ties. Art Nouveau workshops led by Émile Gallé and Louis Majorelle later gave Nancy a decorative arts reputation, while universities and steelmaking linked the city to modern Lorraine industry.

Porte de la Craffe

Restored on the Grande Rue approach, Porte de la Craffe is a twin-towered fourteenth-century gate from Nancy's old wall. Thick walls, arrow slits, and a vaulted passage mark the medieval town limit. The gate stands inland among streets, not on a river quay.

48.6988° N / 6.1777° E

02 AdmissionFree

Place Stanislas

Stanisław Leszczyński commissioned Place Stanislas in the eighteenth century to link Nancy's medieval and newer districts while honoring Louis XV. Balanced classical facades, gilded gates by Jean Lamour, fountains by Barthélemy Guibal and the city hall enclose the paved square; cafes, ceremonies, light displays and public events continue within a historic urban ensemble that includes Place de la Carrière and Place d'Alliance.

48.6936° N / 6.1832° E

03 AdmissionFree

Opéra national de Lorraine

The Opéra national de Lorraine occupies an early twentieth-century theater on Place Stanislas. A classical exterior leads to a decorated auditorium used for opera, concerts, recitals and related programs, while ballet in Nancy is primarily associated with a separate national choreographic center. Production workshops, orchestra, chorus and guest artists make the house an active regional opera institution.

48.6940° N / 6.1840° E

04 AdmissionFree

Alliance Square

Place de l'Alliance sits a short walk from Place Stanislas as a quieter classical square framed by eighteenth-century façades and a central fountain. Open paving and clipped trees give a formal garden feel without market stalls. The ensemble continues Nancy's planned ducal townscape of aligned plazas and gated corners.

48.6939° N / 6.1858° E

05 AdmissionFree

Place de la Carrière

Place de la Carrière is a square with a rich history dating back to the 16th century. It is flanked by historical buildings such as the Palais du Gouverneur and Fermee par l'Arc Here. The square has been a venue for various events, from medieval tournaments to modern-day competitions like the 24 Heures de Stan' and literary events like Le Livre sur la Place.

48.6955° N / 6.1819° E

06 AdmissionFree

Basilica of Saint Epvre of Nancy

A tall neo-Gothic spire marks the Basilica of Saint-Epvre on the edge of Nancy's old town. Rebuilt in the nineteenth century, the church received stained glass, sculpture, furnishings and donations from European rulers and church leaders; its vertical nave, chapels, organ and art collection reflect an ambitious historicist project beside streets once tied to the ducal city.

48.6960° N / 6.1799° E

07 AdmissionFree

Government Palace - Palace Garden

The former Government Palace closes the long perspective of Place de la Carrière behind Nancy's triumphal arch. Built for royal administration in the eighteenth century, it now forms part of the wider Stanislas architectural ensemble. The Government Palace and its garden form part of Nancy's Place Stanislas ensemble, capital of the dukes of Lorraine and a UNESCO World Heritage Site since 1983.

48.6972° N / 6.1811° E

08 AdmissionFree (€6 for greenhouses)

Jean-Marie Pelt Botanical Garden

The Jean-Marie Pelt Botanical Garden on Nancy's outskirts groups themed plant collections, greenhouses, and research plots linked to the university. Paths cross lawns, shade trees, and specialist beds rather than a formal French parterre. Labels and seasonal displays make it a working botanical site as much as a public park.

48.6624° N / 6.1554° E

09 AdmissionFree

Nancy Cathedral

Nancy Cathedral was built during the eighteenth century after the medieval town expanded beyond its older parish churches. Giovanni Betto and later architects shaped a Baroque and classical facade, broad nave, dome, chapels, paintings, and organ. The building serves the Roman Catholic diocese and stands within ordinary commercial streets, a short walk from Place Stanislas but separate from the ducal palace.

48.6915° N / 6.1859° E

10 AdmissionFree

Parc de la Pépinière

Parc de la Pépinière began as a royal nursery under Duke Stanislas and opened as a public park in 1835. The original layout of walks and planting beds still structures the grounds beside Place Stanislas. Lawns, a small zoo area, and tree-lined paths serve everyday recreation in central Nancy.

48.6978° N / 6.1846° E
