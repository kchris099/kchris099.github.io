Explore Nancy

A Brief History

Nancy flourished as capital of the duchy of Lorraine, especially under exiled Polish king Stanislas Leszczyński, who rebuilt squares, gates, and a fine arts academy in the mid-eighteenth century. The medieval old town and Renaissance ducal palace preserve earlier Burgundian and Habsburg ties. Art Nouveau workshops led by Émile Gallé and Louis Majorelle later gave Nancy a decorative arts reputation, while universities and steelmaking linked the city to modern Lorraine industry.

Porte de la Craffe

Porte de la Craffe is a waterfront area in Nancy, France. Porte de la Craffe is a restored gate located in the 14th-century defense wall of the city. Quays and embankment paths form the main walking route. Porte de la Craffe remains part of the public visitor circuit in Nancy.

Free

Place Stanislas

Place Stanislas is a visitor site in Nancy, France. Place Stanislas is a neoclassical square in Nancy, France, recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Surrounded by elegant 18th-century buildings like the Opera National de Lorraine and the City Hall, travellers can admire gilded gates crafted by Jean Lamour and rococo fountains designed by Guibal. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Free

Opéra national de Lorraine

Opéra national de Lorraine, located in Nancy, is a venue that offers ballet performances by the Centre Choregraphique National Ballet de Lorraine. The building houses an auditorium where the Nancy combination Orchestra provides enchanting musical accompaniment. Travellers can easily book tickets online for reasonably priced shows and workshops, making it a must-visit destination for an evening of dance and music.

Free

Alliance Square

Alliance Square is a public square in Nancy, France. Open pavement and surrounding facades define the visitor setting. Local maps identify Alliance Square within Nancy, France. Posted signs mark approaches and opening information for Alliance Square.

Free

Place de la Carrière

Place de la Carrière is a square with a rich history dating back to the 16th century. It is flanked by historical buildings such as the Palais du Gouverneur and Fermee par l'Arc Here. The square has been a venue for various events, from medieval tournaments to modern-day competitions like the 24 Heures de Stan' and literary events like Le Livre sur la Place.

Free

Basilica of Saint Epvre of Nancy

Basilica of Saint Epvre of Nancy is a religious building in Nancy, France. Endowed with riches by notable figures such as Napoleon III and Pope Pius, the basilica also houses a collection of paintings contributed by artists from various European countries. The exterior and main interior spaces are usually viewable outside service times.

Free

Government Palace - Palace Garden

The Government Palace, also known as the Palace Garden, is a must-visit destination in Nancy. This historical site is part of the capital of the Dukes of Lorraine and has been recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage Site since 1983. The palace has architecture with striking golden doors and Rococo fountains surrounding it.

Jean-Marie Pelt Botanical Garden

Jean-Marie Pelt Botanical Garden is a public park in Nancy, France. Paths, lawns, and shaded areas make up the main public facilities. Jean-Marie Pelt Botanical Garden remains part of the public visitor circuit in Nancy. Local maps identify Jean-Marie Pelt Botanical Garden within Nancy, France.

Free

Nancy Cathedral

Nancy Cathedral is a religious building in Nancy, France. Nancy Cathedral, a Baroque masterpiece dating back to the 1700s, is a sight to behold in the city of Nancy. Located near the Place Stanislas and the opulent Ducal Palace, this cathedral stands out with its Italian-inspired design by Giovanni Betto. The exterior and main interior spaces are usually viewable outside service times.

Free

Parc de la Pépinière

Parc de la Pépinière, also known as La Pep by locals, is a visitor site in Nancy, France. Records associated with the site include 1835. Originally a royal nursery established by Stanislas, it was transformed into a public park in 1835 while preserving its original layout. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.
