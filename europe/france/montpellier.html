Montpellier

Montpellier grew from a 10th-century trading settlement into a university city under medieval lords of the Languedoc. Its medical faculty, founded in the 12th century, made it a centre of Mediterranean scholarship. Baroque promenades, arcaded squares, and 19th-century expansion toward the coast reflect how regional commerce, Protestant-Catholic conflict, and modern French civic planning reshaped the Hérault capital.

Place de la Comédie

Place de la Comédie is a spacious pedestrian plaza surrounded by historic buildings, offering outdoor cafe seating and street performances. The area has grand art nouveau architecture reminiscent of Paris, alongside medieval structures dating back to the 1500s and 1600s. Just a short distance away, modern architectural marvels like L'Arbre Blanc and Philippe Starck Cloud provide a striking contrast.

Le Corum

Le Corum in Montpellier holds concerts, touring shows, and conferences in large auditoriums with strong media systems. Poster-session halls and varied room sizes support different event scales. City views open from parts of the complex between sessions.

03 AdmissionFree

Arc de Triomphe

Montpellier's Arc de Triomphe, also called the Porte du Peyrou, crowns the Promenade du Peyrou where rue Foch meets the royal square. Completed in 1693 to designs linked with François d'Orbay, the limestone arch celebrates Louis XIV with relief panels of his victories. It frames views over the esplanade, the equestrian statue of the Sun King, and the Aqueduc Saint-Clément beyond.

43.6111° N / 3.8724° E

04 AdmissionFree

Promenade du Peyrou

Promenade du Peyrou is Montpellier's elevated royal square, with an equestrian statue of Louis XIV and an ornamental water tower designed by Henri Pitot. An aqueduct arcade approaches the terrace from the west. The open plateau looks over the city's historic roofs toward the coastal plain.

43.6112° N / 3.8708° E

05 AdmissionFree

Aqueduc Saint-Clément

The Saint-Clément Aqueduct carried spring water into Montpellier during the eighteenth century. Long stone arcades approach the Château d'Eau at the end of the Peyrou promenade, where the supply fed fountains and public distribution points. Although commonly viewed from streets and paths, the structure belongs to a wider hydraulic system extending beyond the visible arches.

43.6114° N / 3.8688° E

06 AdmissionFree

Place de La Canourgue

Place de la Canourgue sits among seventeenth-century townhouses in Montpellier's old centre. Plane trees shade a garden-like square with benches and a quiet residential character. Aristocratic mansions frame the space, making it one of the calmer historic plazas away from the busier shopping streets.

43.6117° N / 3.8746° E

Planet Ocean Montpellier

Planet Ocean Montpellier in Port Marianne combines an aquarium and interactive marine museum. Tanks present Mediterranean and tropical habitats with sharks, rays, penguins, and reef species among more than 400 animals on display. The modern building is part of the newer eastern districts along the Lez.

43.6028° N / 3.9171° E

08 AdmissionFree

Esplanade de l'Europe

Ricardo Bofill's Antigone district opens toward the Lez River through the broad Esplanade de l'Europe. Curving neoclassical-inspired facades frame lawns, paths, fountains, cafes and apartment buildings along a planned urban axis from central Montpellier. Large scale, symmetry and the riverfront setting define this late twentieth-century public landscape. The river terminates the district's long sequence of plazas.

43.6077° N / 3.8951° E

09 AdmissionFree

Zoo de Montpellier

Montpellier Zoological Park covers a large hilly estate around the former Lunaret property north of the center, and walking routes connect habitats for mammals, birds, and other species with areas of Mediterranean vegetation. Montpellier Zoological Park, established in 1964 on the historic estate of Henri de Lunaret, is a haven for wildlife enthusiasts.

43.6396° N / 3.8738° E

Château d'O

Fountains and pools, formal approaches, a theater and cultural programming occupy Chateau d'O, a municipal property northwest of central Montpellier. The estate developed around a historic residence and landscaped grounds used for public events. Areas open to visitors vary with restoration and programming, while similarly named private wine estates around the city are separate places.

43.6331° N / 3.8389° E
