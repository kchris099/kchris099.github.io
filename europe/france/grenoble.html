Explore Grenoble

A Brief History

Grenoble developed at the foot of the French Alps where the Isère valley opens toward the Rhône, serving as capital of the Dauphiné after its union with France in 1349. Fortifications of the Bastille hill and later magistrates' courts anchored a city of glove-makers, hydroelectric engineers, and universities. Nineteenth-century industry and the 1968 Winter Olympics expanded suburbs while the historic core retained arcaded streets linking Alpine trade to Mediterranean France.

Bastille

Bastille is a visitor site in Grenoble, France. The Fort de La Bastille à Grenoble is a 19th-century military fort located on Mount Rachais, overlooking the city of Grenoble. Accessible by a scenic cable car ride, the fortress offers panoramic views of the city and the River Isere. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Free

Fontaine au Lion

Fontaine au Lion is a visitor site in Grenoble, France. Records associated with the site include 1870. Crafted by Victor Sappey in 1870, this striking fountain has a lion fiercely battling a serpent, symbolizing the Drac and Isère rivers respectively. This artistic representation reflects the historical struggles faced by locals due to flooding from these waterways. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Free

Jardin de Ville

Jardin de Ville is a visitor site in Grenoble, France. Records associated with the site include 1719. The park is adorned with century-old trees and is home to various sculptures and monuments, including busts of Jean-Jacques Rousseau and Pierre Baiteux. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Free

Fromagerie Les Alpages

Fromagerie Les Alpages is a visitor site in Grenoble, France. Records associated with the site include 1984. Since 1984, this shop has been a go-to destination for both connoisseurs and casual enthusiasts. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Free

Stade des Alpes

The Stade des Alpes, located in Grenoble, France, is a notable stadium that is the home ground for both the Grenoble Foot 38 football club and the FC Grenoble rugby team. This versatile venue caters to both football and rugby enthusiasts alike. Situated approximately 460 meters northeast of Perret tower, the Stade des Alpes has an architectural design, particularly evident in its striking roof structure. The stadium's exterior appears well-maintained and visually appealing.

Free

Porte de France

The Porte de France, completed in 1620, is a significant city gate adorned with bas-relief sculptures and a World War I memorial. Situated in the Saint-Laurent district on the right bank of the Isere, it stands alongside the Saint-Laurent gate and footbridge. This Renaissance structure is a attraction in Grenoble's city center and offers free access to travellers.

Free

Jardin des Dauphins

Jardin des Dauphins is a visitor site in Grenoble, France. Records associated with the site include 1620. The Jardin des Dauphins is a Mediterranean-style park located below the hilltop Bastille fort in Grenoble. It has a 1620 gate, winding paths, and offers views of the city. The park provides an ideal setting for leisurely walks, picnics, and outdoor activities. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Free

Parc de Fiancey

Parc de Fiancey is a spacious public park located in the municipality of Saint-Égrève, near Grenoble. It offers a variety of recreational amenities, including a large lake, walking paths, sports fields, and playgrounds. The park is a central hub for community activities and provides a scenic natural space with views of the surrounding Chartreuse mountains.

Free

Maison de la culture de Grenoble

In Grenoble, the Maison de la culture de Grenoble is a cultural hub that hosts an array of events, including the known Festival of Detours de Babel. This festival showcases contemporary music and jazz over two weeks in April, featuring performances like Thierry Pecou's contemporary opera "Nahasdzaan in the Glittering World," which draws inspiration from Navajo culture and environmental stewardship.

Free

Parc des Champs-Élysées

Parc des Champs-Élysées is a visitor site in Grenoble, France. The main structures and grounds are the principal points of interest. Local maps mark Parc des Champs-Élysées inside Grenoble. Approaches are signed from nearby streets in Grenoble, France.
