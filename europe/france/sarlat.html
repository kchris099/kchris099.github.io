Sarlat

A Carolingian abbey seeded Sarlat; medieval and Renaissance merchants then filled the golden-stone lanes with townhouses. Because heavy industry never arrived, twentieth-century restoration could treat the centre as a near-complete medieval market town in the Dordogne. Truffle and foie gras markets keep the mercantile calendar alive in those same lanes.
