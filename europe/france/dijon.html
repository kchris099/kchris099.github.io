Dijon

Dijon was seat of the Valois dukes of Burgundy, whose fourteenth- and fifteenth-century patronage left the palace, Chartreuse de Champmol, and sculpted tombs that drew Flemish and Italian artists. The city controlled wine routes from the Côte d'Or and hosted a parliament after annexation by France in 1477. Half-timbered houses, the Church of Notre-Dame, and mustard and cassis commerce still express how a ducal capital became a regional administrative and gastronomic centre.

Porte Guillaume

Porte Guillaume faces Parc Darcy at the centre of Dijon's main shopping district and is visible from all sides of the square. The gate is a frequent photograph stop among the surrounding streets. It remains one of the city's most visited landmarks despite older mislabels as a waterfront feature.

02 AdmissionFree

BHV Dijon

BHV Dijon occupies a central position on Rue de la Liberte, the main shopping street through Dijon's historic center. The department store carries clothing, accessories, beauty products and household goods across several floors. Its commercial role is contemporary, but the location also places it among the arcades, stone facades and pedestrian routes between Place Darcy and the old town.

03 AdmissionFree

Halles centrales et marché central

Dijon's central covered market fills a restored hall with stalls of produce, cheeses, meats, baked goods, and regional wines. Morning shoppers pack the aisles for Burgundy ingredients, and the building remains a working food hub rather than a souvenir gallery.

04 AdmissionFree

The Owl of Dijon

The Owl of Dijon is a small stone carving on a buttress of Notre-Dame church, worn smooth by touch from passers-by who treat it as a luck charm. The figure is easy to miss without looking up, yet it remains one of the city's most sought street details.

47.3228° N / 5.0416° E

05 AdmissionFree

Saint Michael's Church

Église Saint-Michel fronts a Dijon square with a Renaissance façade of columns and niches unusual among the city's Gothic churches. Inside, a hall church plan and later fittings show successive rebuilds. The west front faces street traffic; doors often open outside services for a look at the nave and choir.

47.3211° N / 5.0449° E

06 AdmissionFree

Darcy Garden

Laid out in the nineteenth century in neo-Renaissance style over a reservoir, Darcy Garden holds greenery, seasonal flowers, and a fountain into an oval basin. Paths and lawns form the main walking areas. The planted square sits beside central Dijon shopping streets.

47.3244° N / 5.0319° E

Puits de Moïse

The Well of Moses at the Chartreuse de Champmol preserves Claus Sluter's early-fifteenth-century prophets around a hexagonal base, among the few major survivals of the Carthusian monastery. The carved figures remain in situ for close viewing in the former monastic precinct.

47.3212° N / 5.0167° E

08 AdmissionFree

Colombière Park

Long straight avenues of trees define Parc de la Colombiere on Dijon's southern edge, reached along the historic Cours du Parc. Designed in the seventeenth century in a formal French style, the grounds include lawns, woodland, playgrounds and an animal enclosure. Remnants of the Roman road toward Chalon add an archaeological layer to a park used mainly for everyday recreation. The straight avenues preserve the garden's formal order.

47.3000° N / 5.0468° E

09 AdmissionFree

Zenith Dijon

Zenith Dijon is a modern live music venue with a cavernous space that can accommodate up to 9000 people. Since its opening in 2005, it has become a hub for various entertainment events including pop and rock concerts, stand-up comedy, musicals, shows, and conventions. The venue offers modern facilities and is conveniently located near the center of Dijon, easily accessible by tram.

47.3553° N / 5.0582° E

10 AdmissionFree

Pouilly Castle Park

Parc du Château de Pouilly occupies former estate grounds on Dijon's outskirts, with lawns, tree belts, and residual castle masonry set among walking paths. Families use the open grass for picnics while longer routes circle the wooded edges. The site reads more as a municipal park than a fortified museum circuit.

47.3474° N / 5.0509° E
