Explore Stratford Upon Avon

A Brief History

Stratford Upon Avon is in United Kingdom. Established as a regional settlement beyond an older fortified core, the city developed through industrialization in the late 19th century, post-war reconstruction, and modern civic expansion that reshaped its public spaces, markets, and surrounding landscape. Museums, markets, and street plans in Stratford Upon Avon still show layers from those periods.

Warwick Castle

Warwick Castle is a fortified historic site in Stratford Upon Avon, United Kingdom. Records associated with the site include 1345. Warwick Castle is a known Medieval fortress with over 1,100 years of history, offering an array of interactive attractions and walk-through adventures.

St Mary’s Church

St Mary's Church is a religious building in Stratford Upon Avon, United Kingdom. Construction and early use date to around 1000. St Mary's Church, with a history spanning over 1000 years, is a significant landmark in Warwick. Originally built in 1123 by Roger de Beaumont, the second Earl of Warwick, it was nearly destroyed in the Great Fire of Warwick in 1693. The exterior and main interior spaces are usually viewable outside service times.

Free

Charlecote Park

Charlecote Park is a historic 12th-century house located on the banks of the River Avon, just east of Stratford. The estate has a rich history dating back to the 13th century and is known for its connections to the Lucy family and William Shakespeare. The park encompasses over 180 acres of parkland, including a deer park with free-roaming fallow deer. Travellers can explore the Victorian furnishings, family portraits, and carriage collection in the stables.

£5

Holy Trinity Church

Holy Trinity Church is a medieval church in Stratford-upon-Avon, known for being the final resting place of William Shakespeare. The church also houses his baptism and burial records. It is one of the attractions in the area, along with other famous sights like Shakespeare Birthplace Trust, Shakespeare's Homes, Tudor World Museum & the Falstaff Experience, and Royal Shakespeare Company (RSC).

Free

Royal Shakespeare Theatre

Royal Shakespeare Theatre is a theatre in Stratford Upon Avon, United Kingdom. The Royal Shakespeare Theatre, located in Stratford-upon-Avon, is a striking red-brick building dedicated to showing Shakespearean works. The venue has received recognition for its architectural design and change, with London-based theatre design consultancy Charcoalblues being awarded for their work on the project. Public access is usually through ticketed performances or daytime visits.

Stratford Butterfly Farm

Located in Stratford-upon-Avon, the Stratford Butterfly Farm is a popular attraction that draws numerous travellers each year. It has a rainforest-like greenhouse where thousands of colorful butterflies flutter around. The Insect City houses rare invertebrates, while the discovery zone showcases every stage of the butterfly's life cycle, travellers can explore Minibeast Metropolis to encounter snakes, reptiles, amphibians, and other creepy crawlies.

Shakespeare's Birthplace

Shakespeare's Birthplace is a visitor site in Stratford Upon Avon, United Kingdom. Shakespeare's Birthplace, located on Henley Street in Stratford-upon-Avon, is a restored 16th-century half-timbered house where William Shakespeare was born and spent his early years. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Free

Stratford-upon-Avon Racecourse

Located close to the River Avon and just a mile from Stratford-on-Avon town center, Stratford On Avon Racecourse offers a thrilling day out with top jockeys competing for prize money. The racecourse has been operating since 1755 and hosts numerous races during the summer, providing plenty of entertainment for children. The compact layout allows for excellent views of the races, and travellers praise the friendly staff and reasonably priced food.

Free

Hatton Locks

Hatton Locks, also known as the "stairway to heaven, is a visitor site in Stratford Upon Avon, United Kingdom. Construction and early use date to around 1799. Hatton Locks, also known as the "stairway to heaven," is a series of 21 locks on the Grand Union Canal in Warwickshire. Originally constructed in 1799 and later expanded in the 1930s, these locks were designed to lift or drop the canal by an 146.5 feet over less than 2 miles.

Free

Hatton Adventure World

Hatton Adventure World is a visitor site in Stratford Upon Avon, United Kingdom. Hatton Adventure World is a family-friendly destination that offers a mix of educational farm experiences and entertainment. Travellers can enjoy interacting with animals, including the guinea pig village, as well as exotic experiences like holding snakes or cuddling chinchillas, there are indoor play areas such as the soft play and slide mania center.
