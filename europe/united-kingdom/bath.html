Royal Crescent

Royal Crescent is a visitor site in Bath, United Kingdom. Construction and early use date to around 1767. It is featured in the popular series Bridgerton, capturing the elegance of the era. Travellers can leisurely explore the city, taking in sights like the Roman Baths and Prior Park. The row of 30 terraced houses built between 1767 and 1774 showcases Georgian architecture, with No.1 Royal Crescent now serving as a museum for travellers to experience its grandeur firsthand.

£16

No. 1 Royal Crescent

No. 1 Royal Crescent is a visitor site in Bath, United Kingdom. 1 Royal Crescent is an 18th-century Palladian-style townhouse that has been transformed into a museum, offering travellers a glimpse into life during the Regency period in Bath. Through restored townhouse furnishings and a soundscape of imagined conversations between family members and staff, travellers can gain insight into the concerns and lifestyle of that time.

Free

Prior Park

Prior Park is a public park in Bath, United Kingdom. It's a popular spot for romantic outings and even weddings. Accessible via the city's bus network or through the Skyline route, it's an ideal destination to explore while visiting Bath. Paths, lawns, and shaded areas make up the main public facilities.

Free

Pulteney Bridge

Pulteney Bridge, constructed in 1774 by Robert Adam, is a and historic landmark in Bath. It spans the River Avon and is adorned with rows of shops on both sides, reminiscent of Venice's Rialto. The bridge's notable architecture, designed in a symmetrical Palladian style during the Georgian era, makes it a popular destination for sightseers and photographers alike. Its location offers views from Parade Bridge, Parade Gardens, and the weir.

Free

Bath Abbey

Bath Abbey is a religious building in Bath, United Kingdom. Bath Abbey is a late Medieval church with a Victorian Gothic interior designed by Sir George Gilbert Scott. The Victorian Gothic interior includes a soaring vaulted stone ceiling above the nave, added by Scott in the 1860s. The exterior and main interior spaces are usually viewable outside service times.

£24

The Roman Baths

The Roman Baths in Bath is a historical site featuring a well-preserved bathhouse and temple with natural spring water. Travellers can explore the museum and enjoy an audio tour designed for kids, immersing them in interactive ancient history. The site offers family-friendly activities including age-specific trails and a children's audioguide narrated by Michael Rosen.

£44

Thermae Bath Spa

Thermae Bath Spa is a visitor site in Bath, United Kingdom. Thermae Bath Spa is a modern spa that offers the unique experience of bathing in natural hot-spring water, including an open-air rooftop pool. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Free

Theatre Royal, Bath

The Theatre Royal, Bath is a Georgian playhouse adorned with plush red and gold décor. It was built in 1805 and is considered important examples of Georgian theatre architecture. Over the years, it has hosted performances by known actors and continues to draw audiences with a diverse range of entertainment including drama, West End productions, opera, comedy, and dance across its three auditoriums.

Free

A garden in the Frome Valley, with terraces, statues and ponds, is often used for summer opera, jazz and concerts. The gardens open from May to September and have a gentle Italian feel. The site is catalogued as a park. The site is catalogued as a park.

Farleigh Hungerford Castle remains part of the public visitor circuit in Bath. Local maps identify Farleigh Hungerford Castle within Bath, United Kingdom.

The Circus - Bath

The Circus in Bath is a historic architectural landmark featuring a circular grassy area surrounded by townhouses. Designed by John Wood the Elder and completed by his son, it is considered a masterpiece with three classical orders stacked on top of one another. The houses were once home to famous residents like painter Thomas Gainsborough. The design incorporates carved acorns atop the houses, referencing the myth of Prince Bladud founding Bath with the help of an errant pig rooting for acorns.

Free

National Trust - Bath Assembly Rooms

The National Trust - Bath Assembly Rooms offers a glimpse into the opulent lifestyle of the 18th century elite. Designed by John Wood the Younger, these rooms were at the Bath's social scene, hosting dances, card games, and tea gatherings. The Fashion Museum housed within adds to its allure. Frequented by known figures like Jane Austen, it remains a time capsule of history where travellers can immerse themselves in the lavish past.

Free

Dundas Aqueduct

Dundas Aqueduct is a visitor site in Bath, United Kingdom. It opened or was completed around 1810. Just three miles southeast of Bath, the Dundas Aqueduct is a late-18th-century stone marvel that gracefully carries the Kennet and Avon Canal over the River Avon. Completed in 1810, this Scheduled Monument showcases architecture designed by John Rennie and his team. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Free

Bath City Farm

Bath City Farm is a visitor site in Bath, United Kingdom. Bath City Farm is a 37-acre pastoral oasis located just a short distance from the city center. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest. Bath City Farm remains part of the public visitor circuit in Bath.
