London

London grew from Roman Londinium on the Thames into the capital of England and later the United Kingdom. Medieval Westminster and Tudor commerce gave way to Georgian expansion, Victorian industry, and 20th-century reconstruction after wartime bombing. The Tower of London and St Paul's Cathedral mark that long sequence beside parliamentary Westminster and the City's financial core.

Tower of London

William the Conqueror's fortress beside the Thames grew into a royal palace, prison, armory, and menagerie that once held lions and even a polar bear. Today the White Tower, walls, and Crown Jewels displays form a dense historic complex. Yeoman Warder tours and the ravens remain part of the working ceremonial site.

Tower Bridge

Tower Bridge opened in 1894 as a combined bascule and suspension bridge beside the Tower of London. Twin towers, high-level walkways, engine rooms, and lifting road spans explain how river traffic and street transport were balanced in the late Victorian city. The bridge remains a working crossing, while exhibition entry and lift schedules are separate from walking across at street level.
