St Ives

St Ives began as a Cornish fishing harbour whose pilchard cellars and granite cottages packed the shoreline. From the late 19th century painters settled for the light, forming a colony that later included Barbara Hepworth and the St Ives School. Tate St Ives opened in 1993 on the seafront. Narrow lanes, Porthmeor Beach, and studio museums show how fishing and modern art reshaped the same harbour.

St Michael's Mount

St Michael's Mount, located in Marazion, is a rocky island with a village, medieval church, and castle. It is a known landmark in the area and lets visitors explore its rich history through tours of the house and grounds. The 360-degree panoramas add to the allure of this medieval monastery. Marazion beach provides a backdrop for swimming, making it an experience.

Lanyon Quoit

Lanyon Quoit is a Neolithic chambered tomb on open moorland near Madron, west of Penzance. A large capstone rests on three upright stones, but the present arrangement dates from an 1824 reconstruction after the monument collapsed during a storm. One support was removed and the capstone was reset at a lower height, making the surviving form an altered version of the prehistoric structure.

50.1474° N / 5.5991° W

Morrab Gardens

Morrab Gardens is a subtropical public garden developed around a former estate. Mild coastal conditions support palms, tree ferns, flowering shrubs and other tender plants among lawns, ponds and winding paths. The landscaped grounds also contain civic and community uses, including a nearby library, while seasonal planting and mature specimens connect the garden with the horticultural history of Cornwall's western coast.

50.1161° N / 5.5372° W

Penzance Promenade

Penzance Promenade follows the shore from Jubilee Pool toward Newlyn as a seafront walk used for daytime strolling and evening fish-and-chip stops. In storms, waves break over the road, drawing watchers to the railings. The path links the art-deco lido to the working harbor district.

St Ives Harbour

Working fishing boats move in and out of St Ives Harbour while a sandy beach, small shops, and fish-and-chip counters line the quay. At low tide colorful hulls rest on the sand in front of the town. Harbour paths keep walkers beside the daily catch traffic.

Porthmeor Beach

Porthmeor Beach, located near the Tate St Ives building, is a sandy beach that attracts surfers and swimmers. It has seasonal lifeguards and dog-friendly access with some restrictions. The beach offers convenient facilities such as a cafe, toilets, and a surf school. Its easy accessibility includes a slipway on the east side. Porthmeor's golden sands and waves make it one of the top surfing beaches in West Cornwall.

50.2155° N / 5.4824° W

Porthgwidden Beach

A small sheltered sandy cove in central St Ives, Porthgwidden suits paddling, sandcastles, and quiet sunbathing close to town. Streets and beach paths reach the sand without a long transfer. Families often choose it over the more exposed bay beaches nearby.

Carbis Bay Beach

Carbis Bay Beach lies east of St Ives beneath steep wooded slopes, with a sandy shore, sheltered water, hotel development and access from the coastal railway and paths. The tide changes the width of exposed sand and reveals rocks along parts of the bay. Resort facilities share the shoreline with a distinctly Cornish coastal setting framed by headlands and wooded hills.

50.1989° N / 5.4660° W

National Trust - Trengwainton Garden

Located on the outskirts of town, Trengwainton Garden is a 16th-century country home with gardens that offer scenic woodland trails, exotic plants, and views across Mount Bay and the Lizard. Originally established in the 19th century, it flourished in the early 20th century when exotic plants from Asia were introduced.

50.1273° N / 5.5673° W

Trewidden Garden

Fifteen acres near Penzance, Trewidden Garden winds through mixed borders that peak in summer color along shaded paths. Loops under tree cover connect the planted rooms. The garden works as a half-day horticultural stop for travellers based around St Ives.

50.1103° N / 5.5784° W
