St Ives

St Ives began as a Cornish fishing harbour whose pilchard cellars and granite cottages packed the shoreline. From the late 19th century painters settled for the light, forming a colony that later included Barbara Hepworth and the St Ives School. Tate St Ives opened in 1993 on the seafront. Narrow lanes, Porthmeor Beach, and studio museums show how fishing and modern art reshaped the same harbour.

St Michael's Mount, located in Marazion, is a rocky island with a village, medieval church, and castle. Lanyon Quoit

Lanyon Quoit is an ancient megalithic tomb located in a rural setting near a road in Cornwall. This Neolithic burial tomb, dating back to 3500-2500 BC, is one of the oldest stone monuments in Cornwall. Although it was reconstructed after a storm in the 1800s and looks different from its original form, it remains an site to visit.

Penzance Promenade is a visitor site in St Ives, United Kingdom. Penzance Promenade is an walkway that stretches along the waterfront from Jubilee Pool to Newlyn. Carbis Bay Beach is a and sheltered sandy beach located just a short drive from Riviere Sands caravan park and east of St Ives.
