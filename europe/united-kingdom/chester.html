Explore Chester

A Brief History

Established as a massive Roman legionary fortress in the 1st century, Chester later became an essential medieval port before the River Dee silted up. The complete preservation of its ancient defensive walls and unique two-tiered Tudor galleries highlight an exceptionally intact urban history. Museums, markets, and street plans in Chester still show layers from those periods.

Top Sights & Activities

Chester Zoo

Chester Zoo is a known destination, home to over 20,000 animals from 400 different species. The zoo's founder aimed to create a space without bars, using natural barriers like ditches and moats. Visitors can explore the various habitats on foot, by monorail, or even by boat. The zoo is dedicated to conservation efforts worldwide. Surrounding Chester are miles of lush countryside, making it an ideal location for weddings with backdrops for photos.

Free

Chester Cathedral

Chester Cathedral, dating back to 1092, is a medieval marvel located on the site of an ancient church and Benedictine Abbey. The cathedral showcases a blend of Norman Romanesque and Gothic architecture. Visitors can explore the interior before ascending the 216-step tower for panoramic views spanning two countries and five counties. Nearby, Watergate Street has half-timbered houses like Gods Providence House and Leche House.

Free

Storyhouse

Storyhouse is a visitor site in Chester, United Kingdom. Storyhouse is a versatile venue in Chester, England, offering a library, theatre, cinema, and cultural events alongside a modern restaurant. It's the cultural and creative Chester with an array of entertainment options for children. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Free

Chester City Walls

Chester City Walls are a historic marvel, offering a 2-mile-long walkway with well-preserved Roman and Medieval fortifications. The walls, originally built in 80 AD to defend the city, now provide an excellent vantage point for panoramic views of Chester and its surroundings. Visitors can access the walls through several gates and towers, including Northgate, Eastgate, Watergate, and Bridgegate.

Free

Eastgate Clock

The Eastgate Clock, a Victorian turret clock situated above a Georgian arch, is a historic landmark in Chester. Erected in 1899 to commemorate Queen Victoria's Diamond Jubilee, it has become an symbol of the city. Despite its age, the clock is a evidence of Chester's rich history, with the original gateway dating back to Roman times.

Free

Chester Cross

Chester Cross is a historic and busy meeting point located at the junction of Watergate Street, Bridge Street, and Eastgate Street in Chester, Cheshire. This central spot offers access to four of the city's main streets and hosts a medieval monument. The area is steeped in history, dating back to Roman times when these streets served as vital arteries for the fortress.

Free

Chester Roman Amphitheatre

Chester Roman Amphitheatre is the largest Roman amphitheatre in Britain, dating back to 1st century AD. It was used for military training, executions, and entertainment, with a capacity of about 7,000 spectators. The site was discovered in the 1950s after being hidden beneath buildings for 2,000 years. Today, visitors can explore the ruins for free and witness military re-enactments during summer.

Free

Chester Racecourse

Chester Racecourse, established in 1539, holds the title of being the oldest racecourse still in operation globally. The one-mile course attracts visitors from around the world to witness horses navigating its tight turns. Over time, the racecourse has expanded to include various facilities such as a restaurant serving delectable treats and an extensive wine collection.

Free

Telford's Warehouse

Telford's Warehouse is a canalside bar and restaurant known for its live music and arts events. It offers a wide selection of European beers, cask ales, and cocktails. The venue has a spacious and entertaining atmosphere with friendly staff. Situated by the canal, it provides an ideal setting for enjoying late-night food and drinks while watching canal boats pass by. Telford's Warehouse is popular among Chester students due to its known cask ales and weekly music nights.

Free

Designer Outlet Cheshire Oaks

Cheshire Oaks Designer Outlet is a large village-style site offering discounts on designer and high-street fashion brands. With over 140 boutiques, restaurants, and cafes spread across a quarter-million square feet of retail space, it's the largest outlet of its kind in the UK. From athletic labels like Nike and Adidas to fashion brands such as Burberry and Hugo Boss, there's something for every taste.

Free

Blue Planet Aquarium

Blue Planet Aquarium is a must-visit for aquarium and fish tank enthusiasts, offering over 100 living displays, including one of Europe's largest shark collections. Visitors can explore the Flooded Forest to see exotic freshwater fish from South America and stroll through the 71m long Underwater Shark Tunnel where sharks and fish swim overhead.

Free

The Ice Cream Farm is a visitor site in Chester, United Kingdom. The Ice Cream Farm is a major family attraction located in the Cheshire countryside near Chester. The expansive site also includes numerous children's play areas, an indoor sand and water play zone, and a large outdoor adventure park. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Free

The Crocky Trail

The Crocky Trail is a visitor site in Chester, United Kingdom. The Crocky Trail is a rugged outdoor adventure park located near Chester, designed to encourage physical activity and muddy fun. It is a very popular destination for energetic family outings. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.
