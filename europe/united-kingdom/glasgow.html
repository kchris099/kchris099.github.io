Destination Guide — Scotland

Explore Glasgow

A Brief History

Glasgow expanded from a medieval bishopric on the Clyde into Britain's leading industrial city during the 19th-century shipbuilding and textile boom. Georgian and Victorian sandstone architecture, the University of Glasgow, and regenerated waterfront districts record engineering wealth and later deindustrialisation. As Scotland's largest city, Glasgow combines mercantile history with contemporary design, music, and civic museums.

Top Sights & Activities

£19

Celtic Park

Celtic Park, also known as "Paradise" by fans, is the largest football stadium in Scotland and home to Celtic FC. Even if you're not a football enthusiast, a visit to this state-of-the-art stadium is recommended. The guided tour has been described as "fantastic," allowing visitors to explore areas like the boardroom, tunnel, dressing room, and dugout while being accompanied by knowledgeable staff.

Free

Glasgow Green

Glasgow Green is a historic park spanning 55 hectares and featuring landmarks such as the triumphant arch and the terracotta Doulton Fountain in front of People's Palace. As the oldest park in Glasgow, it boasts attractions like Nelson's Monument and the St. Andrew's Suspension Bridge. Visitors can enjoy a pleasant walk along well-kept pathways by the River Clyde, passing boathouses, bridges, and greenhouses attached to The People's Palace.

Free

Glasgow Cathedral

Glasgow Cathedral, also known as St. Mungo's, is a significant example of Scottish Gothic architecture and the oldest building in Glasgow. It was consecrated in 1197 and is the only medieval cathedral to have survived the Protestant Reformation in 1560. The lower crypt houses the tomb of Glasgow's patron saint, St. Mungo. Visitors are captivated by its stained glass windows and ornate interior.

Free

The Glasgow Necropolis

The Glasgow Necropolis is a Victorian-era cemetery that houses the graves and mausoleums of prominent figures from Glasgow's past. Spanning 37 acres, it is a rich source of captivating architecture, sculptures, and historical narratives from the city's Victorian era. With an estimated 50,000 burials and around 3,500 monuments marking them, this cemetery has been described as a 'city of the dead.'.

Free

George Square

George Square, a civic square in Glasgow named after King George III, was established in 1781 and serves as a vibrant hub of history and culture. This location is adorned with an impressive array of statues honoring notable Scots such as Queen Victoria, the beloved poet Robert Burns, the innovative James Watt, and Prime Minister William Gladstone.

Free

The Glasgow Royal Concert Hall

The Glasgow Royal Concert Hall, a striking stone building located at the intersection of the city's main shopping streets, is a versatile venue that hosts an array of events throughout the year. Operated by Glasgow Life, it features a large auditorium with seating for 2,475 people and numerous smaller performance spaces. The hall showcases performances across various genres including classical, jazz, rock, pop, and folk music as well as spoken-word events.

£25

Theatre Royal Glasgow

Theatre Royal Glasgow, a historic establishment dating back to 1867, is a prominent theater in the heart of Glasgow. It boasts Victorian plasterwork and a chandelier-adorned auditorium where visitors can enjoy a diverse range of performances including opera, ballet, musicals, and live concerts. As the home to Scotland's resident companies - Scottish Opera and Scottish Ballet - it offers first-class entertainment for all.

£12

Glasgow Film Theatre

Glasgow Film Theatre, also known as GFT, is a beloved independent cinema and registered charity that serves as the heart of film in Glasgow. It offers a diverse selection of films including classics, independent movies, documentaries, foreign language films, and new releases. The theater is renowned for hosting various annual film festivals and events.

Free

OVO Hydro

Located in Glasgow, OVO Hydro is a state-of-the-art indoor arena with a modern design that hosts various sporting events and concerts. Set to open in September 2013, this 12,000-seat national arena at SECC is expected to be one of the world's top concert venues. Designed by Foster and Partners, the structure combines ancient inspiration with modern technology, featuring a glowing exterior made from space industry-developed material.

Free

Glasgow Botanic Gardens

Glasgow Botanic Gardens is a renowned 27-acre park featuring diverse plant collections, a functional vegetable plot, and impressive Victorian cast-iron glasshouses. As one of the city's top attractions, it offers a refreshing outdoor experience for locals and tourists alike. The highlight of the gardens is the striking Kibble Palace greenhouse. Visitors can explore well-maintained collections and enjoy the picturesque layout.

Free

Kibble Palace

Kibble Palace is a historical greenhouse situated in the heart of Glasgow Botanic Gardens. This popular public park is a beloved spot for both locals and visitors, offering a serene escape from the urban hustle. The gardens boast diverse flora from around the world, including species from South Africa, Australia, New Zealand, China, and Japan. Visitors can enjoy leisurely activities like picnicking or taking romantic strolls amidst this botanical paradise.

Free

Hunterian Art Gallery

The Hunterian Art Gallery, nestled within the University of Glasgow, is a treasure trove for art and history enthusiasts alike. This remarkable venue houses over a million items, ranging from meteorites to mummies, showcasing an eclectic mix of artifacts that span centuries and cultures. As Scotland's oldest public museum, it offers visitors a unique glimpse into the past with its extensive collection that includes everything from Roman relics to dinosaur fossils.

£9

Bothwell Castle

Bothwell Castle is a 13th-century fortress located on the banks of the River Clyde in South Lanarkshire, about 10 miles from Glasgow. The castle features a massive cylindrical donjon and played a significant role in Scotland's wars of independence. Visitors can explore the ruins and learn about its history while enjoying scenic views of the river. The site also offers walking trails along the riverbank, making it a great destination for outdoor enthusiasts.
