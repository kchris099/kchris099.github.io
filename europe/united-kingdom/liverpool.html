Liverpool

Liverpool rose as Britain's major Atlantic port in the 18th and 19th centuries, shipping cotton, slaves, and emigrants before becoming a centre of transatlantic liners. Albert Dock warehouses, two cathedrals, and football clubs record mercantile wealth, Irish immigration, and post-industrial culture recognised by UNESCO on the waterfront. Municipal archives, parish records, and surviving architecture help explain how the city connected to wider regional trade routes, court patronage, and modern civic rebuilding after industrial and wartime change.
