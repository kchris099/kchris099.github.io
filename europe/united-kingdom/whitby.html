Explore Whitby

A Brief History

A prominent medieval religious center that hosted the consequential Synod of Whitby in 664, the town later thrived as a major whaling and shipbuilding port. Its striking ruined abbey and steep cobblestone streets inspired deep maritime folklore and legendary Gothic literature. Decades of careful preservation have ensured that this profound historical narrative remains a vital part of the modern experience.

Top Sights & Activities

£14

Whitby Abbey

Whitby Abbey, perched on a clifftop overlooking the town, is a historic site with ties to English poetry and Bram Stoker's Dracula. The 199 Steps lead up to the imposing ruins, while nearby attractions include the Captain Cook Memorial Museum and Whitby Museum. The landmark church of St. Mary's, where Captain James Cook once worshipped, overlooks the town from East Cliff.

Free

Whitby Brewery Ltd

Nestled in the shadow of Whitby Abbey and overlooking the North Sea, Whitby Brewery Ltd was established in 2013 by four passionate brewers. The brewery offers a selection of hand-pulled beers and operates a bottle shop. Situated next to the historic abbey, it has quickly gained local acclaim for its Yorkshire ales, which are served in various establishments around Whitby. Housed in a converted barn, the brewery's small bar serves cask beers and bottles produced on-site.

Free

Whalebone Arch

The Whalebone Arch in Whitby is a celebrated replica of a whale's jaw bone that replaced the original arch erected in 1853. It commemorates the dangerous whaling industry of the town's past, with its massive archway framing an exceptional view of Whitby Abbey and the town's red-roofed cottages across the bay.

Free

Whitby Beach

Whitby Beach is a spacious and family-friendly destination that offers summer activities like donkey rides, fish & chips, beach huts, and views of the lighthouse. The renowned Magpie Cafe has recently reopened after a fire and is celebrated for its fresh seafood sourced from Whitby boats. Visitors can also bring their dogs to several beaches in the area, each offering unique views of the North Yorkshire Coastline.

Free

North Yorkshire and Cleveland Heritage Coast

North Yorkshire and Cleveland Heritage Coast is a preserved coastline that attracts photography enthusiasts with its ruins, waterfalls, and pastoral views. The area is divided into five regions, each offering unique landscapes and attractions. Visitors can explore castles, country churches, museums, and stately homes to immerse themselves in the region's history. For adventure seekers, Go Ape! High Wire Forest Adventure in Dalby Forest provides an exhilarating experience.

Free

199 Steps

The 199 Steps in Whitby are a famous landmark leading up to the abbey, offering views of the harbor and town. These steps were originally built to test Christian worshippers' dedication at St. Mary's Church on the clifftop. Along the way, strategically placed benches provide resting spots for climbers. Nearby attractions include Whitby Beach and Harbour, where visitors can enjoy the Gothic atmosphere and delicious local food such as fish and chips.

Free

Fortune's Kippers

Fortune's Kippers is a renowned smokehouse and shop located in the historic town of Whitby. Established in 1872, this family-run business has been using traditional kipper smoking methods passed down through five generations. The aroma of oak wood smoke fills the air around Henrietta Street, leading visitors to this establishment at the bottom of the 199 Steps.

Free

Robin Hood's Bay and Beach

Robin Hood's Bay and Beach is a documented coastal feature in Whitby, United Kingdom, recognised in regional geographic listings for its natural formation and public access. The shoreline is open to visitors throughout the year and connects to adjacent beachfront districts. Travellers approach from the coastal road or boardwalk and combine the visit with nearby harbour areas and seaside markets.

Free

Whitby Coastal Cruises Ltd

Whitby Coastal Cruises operates boat tours departing from the historic harbor of Whitby. The short cruises provide passengers with excellent views of the dramatic North Yorkshire coastline, the iconic ruins of Whitby Abbey perched on the cliff, and the bustling fishing port. The tours often include commentary on local maritime history and folklore.

Free

The Cinder Track

The Cinder Track is a 21-mile traffic-free trail that follows the old railway line from Whitby to Scarborough. It offers a quieter and scenic route through North York Moors countryside and coastal coves, including the picturesque Hayburn Wyke with its hidden waterfall. For a shorter walk, consider the 6-mile circular route from Whitby to Hawsker ending at Whitby Abbey.
