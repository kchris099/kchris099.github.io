Whitby

The Synod of 664 made Whitby's abbey a pivot of English church history; later whaling and shipbuilding filled the harbour. The ruined abbey above the 199 steps and the jet jewellery trade fed both maritime folklore and Gothic literary fame. Dracula associations from Bram Stoker's stay layered literary tourism onto the abbey ruins. Captain Cook learned seamanship in Whitby before his Pacific voyages.

Whitby Abbey

Whitby Abbey, perched on a clifftop overlooking the town, is a historic site with ties to English poetry and Bram Stoker's Dracula. The 199 Steps lead up to the imposing ruins, while nearby attractions include the Captain Cook Memorial Museum and Whitby Museum. The landmark church of St. Mary's, where Captain James Cook once worshipped, overlooks the town from East Cliff.

Whalebone Arch

The Whalebone Arch in Whitby is a celebrated replica of a whale's jaw bone that replaced the original arch erected in 1853. It commemorates the dangerous whaling industry of the town's past, with its massive archway framing an view of Whitby Abbey and the town's red-roofed cottages across the bay.

Whitby Beach

Whitby Beach is a spacious and family-friendly destination that offers summer activities like donkey rides, fish & chips, beach huts, and views of the lighthouse. The known Magpie Cafe has recently reopened after a fire and is celebrated for its fresh seafood sourced from Whitby boats. Visitors can also bring their dogs to several beaches in the area, each offering unique views of the North Yorkshire Coastline.

199 Steps

The 199 Steps in Whitby are a famous landmark leading up to the abbey, offering views of the harbor and town. These steps were originally built to test Christian worshippers' dedication at St. Mary's Church on the clifftop. Along the way, strategically placed benches provide resting spots for climbers. Nearby attractions include Whitby Beach and Harbour, where visitors can enjoy the Gothic atmosphere and delicious local food such as fish and chips.

Fortune's Kippers

Fortune's Kippers is a known smokehouse and shop located in the historic town of Whitby. Established in 1872, this family-run business has been using traditional kipper smoking methods passed down through five generations. The aroma of oak wood smoke fills the air around Henrietta Street, leading visitors to this establishment at the bottom of the 199 Steps.

The Cinder Track

The Cinder Track is a 21-mile traffic-free trail that follows the old railway line from Whitby to Scarborough. It offers a quieter and scenic route through North York Moors countryside and coastal coves, including the Hayburn Wyke with its hidden waterfall. For a shorter walk, consider the 6-mile circular route from Whitby to Hawsker ending at Whitby Abbey.
