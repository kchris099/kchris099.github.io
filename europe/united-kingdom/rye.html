Explore Rye

A Brief History

Established as a regional settlement beyond an older fortified core, the city developed through industrialization in the late 19th century, post-war reconstruction, and modern civic expansion that reshaped its public spaces, markets, and surrounding landscape. Restored religious buildings, civic buildings, and natural features now define the contemporary visitor experience.

Rye Harbour Nature Reserve

Rye Harbour Nature Reserve, located just 2 miles from the historic town of Rye, was voted Britain's favorite nature reserve in 2016. This vast conservation site is home to rare species like the Spangled Button Beetle and has been recognized for its importance as a wetland on an international scale. The reserve offers diverse coastal habitats including shingle, salt marsh, saline lagoons, and grazing marsh, making it a haven for wildlife.

Spike Milligan's gravestone is located at St Thomas's Church in Winchelsea, East Sussex. The comedian passed away in 2002 at the age of 83, and his famous epitaph "I told you I was ill" was added to his gravestone after a dispute among his family members. This witty inscription marks the end of a bitter feud and serves as a humorous tribute to the beloved ex-Goon.

Free

Camber Sands

Camber Sands is a beach in East Sussex, England, boasting 2 miles of shoreline with beautiful sand dunes. The area offers various water sports and is patrolled by lifeguards for safety. Additionally, travellers can enjoy delicious dining experiences at restaurants like The Gallivant and The Owl. For accommodation, there are unique design cottages that offer splendid views of the sea and dunes.

Free

Smallhythe Place

Smallhythe Place, a 16th-century home of actress Ellen Terry, is now a museum showcasing her personal and theatrical collection. The Tudor architecture and half-timbered structure make it one of Kent's most beautiful historic houses. The property includes a rose garden, vintage tea room, and a charming 17th-century Barn Theatre. travellers can explore the fascinating exhibits and enjoy varied shows in the working theatre.

Free

Church of Saint Mary, Rye

The Church of Saint Mary in Rye, a historic Episcopal church dating back to the 12th century, offers travellers a rich experience. The church features a travellers center providing ample information and the opportunity to climb its bell tower for views of Rye's rooftops, harbor, and sea. This classic English village church boasts over 900 years of history and showcases various architectural styles.

Free

National Trust - Lamb House

National Trust - Lamb House is a charming red-brick Georgian residence that was once the home of renowned American novelist Henry James. The house features a tranquil walled garden, adding to its allure. Built in 1772, it has a rich history and has been visited by notable literary figures such as HG Wells and Rudyard Kipling.

Free

Rye Castle Museum - Ypres Tower

Rye Castle Museum, also known as Ypres Tower, is a 14th-century tower located by the River Rother in Rye. It offers harbor views and houses exhibits and artifacts that depict medieval life. The museum provides insights into Rye's history as a Cinque Port defending England against invasion and its role as the smuggling capital of England.

Free

Rye Heritage Centre

Rye Heritage Centre, also known as Strand Quays Heritage Centre, serves as the perfect introduction to the rich history of Rye. travellers can explore a town model and enjoy a captivating 20-minute sound and light show that narrates the story of Rye through the ages. The centre also features a vintage penny arcade with classic amusement machines like the famous Laughing Sailor. Additionally, travellers can rent audio guides or pick up themed maps for self-guided tours around town.

Free

Camber Castle

Camber Castle, also known as Winchelsea Castle, is a historic artillery fort constructed by Henry VIII in the 16th century to defend against French invaders. Located near Rye, it features concentric circles of ruins and offers guided tours. The castle is surrounded by picturesque countryside with grazing cows and sheep. travellers can enjoy a scenic walk from Rye to the castle while observing wildlife such as birds, sheep, and cows.
