Rye

Rye was a medieval Cinque Port on the Sussex coast, though silting left the town stranded inland above marshes. Half-timbered streets, the Landgate, and St Mary's Church recall its harbour wealth and French-raid fears. Smuggling stories cling to the 18th century. Cobbles of Mermaid Street and views over Romney Marsh still present a fortified hill town once tied to Channel trade.

Rye Harbour Nature Reserve

Rye Harbour Nature Reserve, located just 2 miles from the historic town of Rye, was voted Britain's favorite nature reserve in 2016. This vast conservation site is home to rare species like the Spangled Button Beetle and has been recognized for its importance as a wetland on an international scale. The reserve offers diverse coastal habitats including shingle, salt marsh, saline lagoons, and grazing marsh, making it a haven for wildlife.
