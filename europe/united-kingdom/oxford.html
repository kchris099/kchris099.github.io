Oxford

From the thirteenth century colleges acquired chapels, libraries, and quadrangles along the Thames and Cherwell, organising the town around teaching. Civil War royalist headquarters and later Cowley car works widened the economic base. The Bodleian and enclosed college courts remain the university city's defining institutions. Blackwell's and the covered market supply a commercial street life beside the colleges.

Blenheim Palace

Blenheim Palace is a grand 17th-century stately home located in Oxfordshire, UK. The palace is set amidst beautifully designed gardens by Capability Brown and has over 2000 acres of parkland to explore. As the birthplace of Winston Churchill and a UNESCO World Heritage Site, it gives visitors a rich historical experience. Guests can tour the opulent stately rooms, stroll through botanic gardens, and began on walking trails across the estate's countryside.

Carfax Tower

Carfax Tower, a 23-meter-high bell tower, is a historic landmark in Oxford, England. It was originally part of St. Martin's Church dating back to the 12th century and offers panoramic views of the city from its top. Despite the church being demolished to make way for traffic, the tower remains well-preserved. Travellers can climb 99 steps to reach the peak and enjoy vistas of Oxford's city center.

Bodleian Library

The Bodleian Library, established in 1602, is the UK's second largest library and one of the oldest in Europe. It houses an astonishing collection of over 11 million works, including up to 13 million printed works. Located in central Oxford's Radcliffe Square, it was founded by a bishop in the 14th century and expanded by Thomas Bodley to become a leading source of combined human knowledge.
