Isle Of Wight

Roman villas, Carisbrooke Castle, and Tudor forts faced the Solent long before Victoria made Osborne a royal summer home. Cowes yachting and Victorian pier towns followed. The island's compact history mixes defence, crown retreat, and holiday traffic. The Needles batteries extend the defence story into nineteenth-century Palmerston forts. Fossil hunter Mary Anning's era made the island cliffs a geology classroom.
