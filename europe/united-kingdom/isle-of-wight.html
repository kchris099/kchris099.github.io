A Brief History

Isle Of Wight is in United Kingdom. Established as a regional settlement beyond an older fortified core, the city developed through industrialization in the late 19th century, post-war reconstruction, and modern civic expansion that reshaped its public spaces, markets, and surrounding landscape. Museums, markets, and street plans in Isle Of Wight still show layers from those periods.

Top Sights & Activities

Tapnell Farm Park

Tapnell Farm Park is a public park in Isle Of Wight, United Kingdom. Tapnell Farm Park is a farm-themed amusement destination located near Yarmouth in West Wight, perfect for families seeking adventure and fun. Kids can explore the petting zoo, where they can interact with both familiar farm animals and exotic creatures like wallabies and meerkats. Paths, lawns, and shaded areas make up the main public facilities.

Free

Model Village Godshill

Located in the village of Godshill, the Model Village Godshill is a popular attraction featuring immaculately kept miniature buildings and landscapes. Travellers can enjoy strolling through this careful crafted village, which exudes a sense of pride and joy. The site also offers a chance to watch handmade chocolates being made before indulging in some sweet treats at the chocolate shop.

Free

Isle Of Wight Donkey Sanctuary

The Isle of Wight Donkey Sanctuary is a haven for rescued donkeys, horses, and ponies, offering travellers the chance to meet these animals in a setting. As a charity-run attraction, it relies on donations and on-site purchases to support its cause. The sanctuary provides a home for unwanted donkeys and offers the opportunity for travellers to adopt or donate to these animals.

£21

Amazon World Zoo Park

Amazon World Zoo is the largest exotic animal attraction on the Isle of Wight, featuring over 200 species including tropical birds, meerkats, penguins, flamingos, otters, and monkeys. Travellers can experience jungle rainforests with a variety of exotic birds, crocodiles, and penguins while learning about conservation efforts to protect their natural habitats.

Free

Quarr Abbey

Quarr Abbey is a religious building in Isle Of Wight, United Kingdom. Travellers of all ages are welcome to explore the grounds and enjoy cream teas at the coffee shop. The abbey also offers free tours, and travellers can feed pigs with food purchased on-site, there's a woodland trail suitable for an off-road pushchair, and kids can make the most of their visit by hiring an activity pack. The exterior and main interior spaces are usually viewable outside service times.

Free

Isle of Wight Steam Railway - Havenstreet Station

Isle of Wight Steam Railway - Havenstreet Station is a visitor site in Isle Of Wight, United Kingdom. The Isle of Wight Steam Railway, located at Havenstreet, is a destination that offers a journey through time with its well-preserved exhibits, steam-powered locomotives, and train stations. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Free

Wight Karting Go-Kart Track

Wight Karting Go-Kart Track is a visitor site in Isle Of Wight, United Kingdom. Wight Karting Go-Kart Track, located near Ryde, is a fantastic destination for families seeking an exhilarating day out. This family-run establishment offers various go-kart options suitable for everyone aged eight and up, ensuring that even the youngest racers can join in on the fun without feeling overwhelmed by older competitors. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Free

Shanklin Chine

Shanklin Chine is a visitor site in Isle Of Wight, United Kingdom. Shanklin Chine is a coastal ravine that has been a popular destination for over two centuries. Travellers can explore the chine's natural and human history at the heritage center. Notable figures like Jane Austen and John Keats were said to be fans of this tree-lined gorge. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Free

Shanklin Theatre

Shanklin Theatre is a theatre in Isle Of Wight, United Kingdom. Records associated with the site include 1879. Located just a stone's throw from the seafront and the Shanklin Old Village, Shanklin Theatre is a landmark of culture on the Isle of Wight. This Victorian gem has been audiences since 1879, showing an eclectic mix of professional and amateur performances throughout the year. Public access is usually through ticketed performances or daytime visits.

Free

Shanklin Beach

Shanklin Beach is a beach in Isle Of Wight, United Kingdom. Records associated with the site include 1987. Shanklin Beach, located on the south east coast of the Isle of Wight, is a popular destination known for its golden sands and traditional English seafront promenade. Despite losing its pier in 1987, the beach now has a semi-circular vantage point with benches. Access is typically on foot from nearby roads or beachfront paths.
