Explore Isle Of Wight

A Brief History

Established as a regional settlement beyond an older fortified core, the city developed through industrialization in the late 19th century, post-war reconstruction, and modern civic expansion that reshaped its public spaces, markets, and surrounding landscape. Restored religious buildings, civic buildings, and natural features now define the contemporary visitor experience.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

Tapnell Farm Park

Tapnell Farm Park is a delightful farm-themed amusement destination located near Yarmouth in West Wight, perfect for families seeking adventure and fun. This vibrant park features an array of activities that cater to all ages, from exhilarating go-kart rides to thrilling sledge slides. Kids can explore the petting zoo, where they can interact with both familiar farm animals and exotic creatures like wallabies and meerkats.

Free

Model Village Godshill

Located in the charming village of Godshill, the Model Village Godshill is a popular attraction featuring immaculately kept miniature buildings and landscapes. travellers can enjoy strolling through this meticulously crafted village, which exudes a sense of pride and joy. The site also offers an opportunity to watch handmade chocolates being made before indulging in some sweet treats at the chocolate shop.

Free

Isle Of Wight Donkey Sanctuary

The Isle of Wight Donkey Sanctuary is a haven for rescued donkeys, horses, and ponies, offering travellers the chance to meet these animals in a picturesque setting. As a charity-run attraction, it relies on donations and on-site purchases to support its cause. The sanctuary provides a home for unwanted donkeys and offers the opportunity for travellers to adopt or donate to these animals.

Free

Amazon World Zoo Park

Amazon World Zoo is the largest exotic animal attraction on the Isle of Wight, featuring over 200 species including tropical birds, meerkats, penguins, flamingos, otters, and monkeys. travellers can experience jungle rainforests with a variety of exotic birds, crocodiles, and penguins while learning about conservation efforts to protect their natural habitats.

Free

Quarr Abbey

Quarr Abbey is a serene Benedictine monastery located near the Fishbourne ferry port. travellers of all ages are welcome to explore the grounds and enjoy cream teas at the coffee shop. The abbey also offers free tours, and travellers can feed pigs with food purchased on-site. Additionally, there's a woodland trail suitable for an off-road pushchair, and kids can make the most of their visit by hiring an activity pack.

Free

Isle of Wight Steam Railway - Havenstreet Station

The Isle of Wight Steam Railway, located at Havenstreet, is a captivating destination that offers a journey through time with its well-preserved exhibits, steam-powered locomotives, and train stations. travellers can immerse themselves in the rich history of railway transportation while enjoying rides on beautifully restored Victorian and Edwardian carriages. The site also features accessible facilities for wheelchair users and families with young children.

Free

Wight Karting Go-Kart Track

Wight Karting Go-Kart Track, located near Ryde, is a fantastic destination for families seeking an exhilarating day out. This family-run establishment offers various go-kart options suitable for everyone aged eight and up, ensuring that even the youngest racers can join in on the fun without feeling overwhelmed by older competitors. The track spans 500 meters and features exciting curves that challenge drivers of all skill levels.

Free

Shanklin Chine

Shanklin Chine is a picturesque coastal ravine that has been a popular destination for over two centuries. This 400-meter-long gorge features lush vegetation, waterfalls, and pathways carved into the cliffs for views. travellers can explore the chine's natural and human history at the heritage center. Notable figures like Jane Austen and John Keats were said to be fans of this tree-lined gorge.

Free

Shanklin Theatre

Nestled just a stone's throw from the picturesque seafront and the charming Shanklin Old Village, Shanklin Theatre stands as a beacon of culture on the Isle of Wight. This Victorian gem has been captivating audiences since 1879, showcasing an eclectic mix of professional and amateur performances throughout the year. With its intimate atmosphere, it’s an ideal venue for everything from West End touring productions to lively tribute acts, comedy shows, and family-friendly entertainment.

Free

Shanklin Beach

Shanklin Beach, located on the south east coast of the Isle of Wight, is a popular destination known for its golden sands and traditional English seafront promenade. Despite losing its pier in 1987, the beach now features a semi-circular vantage point with benches. The area exudes a traditional seaside charm with colorful esplanades and typical amusements. travellers can enjoy water sports or simply relax on the sandy shores while taking in the vibrant atmosphere.
