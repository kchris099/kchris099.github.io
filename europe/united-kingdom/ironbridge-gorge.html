Ironbridge Gorge

The Ironbridge Gorge became the crucible of the Industrial Revolution in the early eighteenth century when Abraham Darby pioneered the commercial smelting of iron using coke instead of charcoal. This breakthrough triggered a massive expansion of foundries, brickworks, and rail networks along the River Severn. The area's global industrial significance was crowned in 1779 with the construction of the world's first cast-iron bridge. Today, this foundational industrial landscape is preserved across a network of museums, the original Darby furnace, and the well known Iron Bridge itself.
