Explore Ironbridge Gorge

A Brief History

The Ironbridge Gorge became the crucible of the Industrial Revolution in the early eighteenth century when Abraham Darby pioneered the commercial smelting of iron using coke instead of charcoal. This breakthrough triggered a massive expansion of foundries, brickworks, and rail networks along the River Severn. The area's global industrial significance was crowned in 1779 with the construction of the world's first cast-iron bridge. Today, this foundational industrial landscape is preserved across a network of museums, the original Darby furnace, and the iconic Iron Bridge itself.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

The Iron Bridge

The Iron Bridge, constructed in 1779, stands as a remarkable symbol of Britain's industrial heritage. The project involved various stakeholders and partners, including Historic England and the local authority. Funding was provided by English Heritage and donors like the Hermann Reemtsma Foundation from Germany. This bridge, located in Severn Gorge, revolutionized bridge construction with its single cast iron arch spanning 100 feet across the gorge.

Free

Buildwas Abbey

Buildwas Abbey is a remarkable 12th-century Cistercian abbey featuring well-preserved ruins, including an intact church and intricately carved chapter house. Situated in a serene wooded setting near the Severn River, it offers a picturesque and tranquil atmosphere for exploration. Visitors can wander through the grounds with their leashed dogs and admire the architectural grandeur of the abbey.

Free

Bridgnorth Castle and Gardens

Nestled in the heart of Bridgnorth, Bridgnorth Castle and Gardens is a captivating destination that beautifully marries history with nature. This impressive fortress, dating back to around 896, stands as a testament to the town's rich past. The castle's intriguing architecture gives it a unique charm, often described as looking like it's on the verge of toppling over! Surrounded by meticulously maintained gardens, visitors can enjoy views of the Severn Valley Railway and beyond.

£8

Wenlock Priory

Wenlock Priory, a medieval ruin nestled in a serene green area, offers visitors a glimpse into its rich history. The 13th-century church and the intricately carved Norman chapter house stand as testaments to the priory's former grandeur. As you explore the tranquil grounds, encounter ornate stone carvings and remnants of the monks' living quarters, each narrating a part of the priory's story.

£18

Hoo Zoo and Dinosaur World

The Hoo Farm Animal Kingdom is a family-focused zoo with animals from meerkats to farmyard favourites, while Dinosaur World is a small zoo featuring dinosaurs. Both are great for food, staff and animals and would make an excellent tourist attraction.

Free

Lilleshall Abbey

Lilleshall Abbey is a beautiful and well-preserved abbey, which is accessible to the public. The abbey was founded in 1148 and remains an important historical site. The abbey was severely damaged during a Parliamentarian siege in the English Civil War, but much of the church still survives. The processional door is particularly impressive.

£15

Telford Steam Railway

Telford Steam Railway, located near Ironbridge, offers delightful rides on vintage steam and diesel locomotives. The railway operates from March to September, allowing visitors to travel along a mile-long stretch of the historic Wellington to Craven Arms Railway. The experience includes stops at three stations and features two steam locomotives and two diesel engines.

£20 (performance ticket)

Telford Theatre

Telford Theatre is a modern playhouse that offers a variety of entertainment, including popular music, comedy, family shows, ballet, and pantomime. It's a great place to unwind and enjoy quality time with loved ones. The theater hosts a range of performances from comedies to dramas and pantomimes, ensuring there's always something new to watch. Visitors praise the friendly staff and clean facilities at this decently sized venue with good acoustics.

£15

Exotic Zoo Wildlife Park

Exotic Zoo Wildlife Park, located in Telford, is a community-driven and family-led zoo that prioritizes sustainability, education, and conservation. Despite being smaller than commercialized zoos, it offers a unique experience where visitors can get up close to the animals. The park features various zones such as Desert Zone, Rainforest Zone, Nocturnal Area, and a petting farm.

Free

Apley Woods

Apley Woods is a nature preserve with lovely woods, grasslands and lakes. It's perfect for a quiet walk with your dog, and the space is big enough to run around without getting cramped. In the spring, the trees are in full bloom and it's lovely to wander through.
