Explore Canterbury

A Brief History

Canterbury's cathedral has been the senior church of the Church of England since Augustine's mission of 597 CE. Roman walls, medieval cloisters, and pilgrimage routes associated with Thomas Becket record how ecclesiastical authority shaped Kentish history. St Augustine's Abbey ruins and city walls frame the cathedral precinct within Kent's historic landscape.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

Reculver Towers and Roman Fort

Reculver Towers and Roman Fort, located in Herne Bay, offers a picturesque setting for history enthusiasts and casual visitors alike. The 12th-century towers stand as a testament to the area's rich past, having served as an Anglo-Saxon monastery and later a parish church. While access to the interior of the towers is limited, the site's historical significance is undeniable. Visitors can also enjoy scenic walks along the seafront and take in sunset views over the skyline.

Free

Herne Bay Pier

Herne Bay Pier stands as a beloved emblem of this charming seaside town, offering a delightful blend of nostalgia and fun. Originally constructed in 1831, the pier has undergone several transformations due to storms and fires but continues to be a hub for visitors.

Free

Herne Bay Clock Tower

Nestled along the picturesque waterfront, the Herne Bay Clock Tower stands as a testament to the town's rich history since its construction in 1837. This Grade II listed landmark is one of the earliest free-standing clock towers in the UK and was funded by Mrs. Ann Thwaytes as a tribute to those who fell during the Second Boer War. Its original bell still chimes, adding charm to this structure that draws visitors for both its historical significance and architecture.

£18

Wildwood Trust

Wildwood Trust, located in Herne Bay, is a top British wildlife park spanning 40 acres of ancient woodland. The park houses over 200 animals, including native species like deer, owls, and red squirrels as well as rarer creatures such as lynx and wild horses. Visitors can enjoy various events such as talks, feeds, zoo keeper days, photography days, and festive activities during the Christmas season.

£18

Canterbury Cathedral

Canterbury Cathedral, a Norman-built Church of England place of worship located in Canterbury, UK, is one of the oldest religious sites in England with a history dating back over 1400 years. It gained notoriety as the location where Archbishop Thomas Becket was tragically killed in 1170. Despite enduring fires and restoration efforts, the cathedral's Gothic towers and exquisite stained-glass windows continue to captivate visitors.

£25

Canterbury Punting Company

The Canterbury Punting Company offers guided river tours along the historic Great Stour river, which flows directly through the center of Canterbury. The traditional flat-bottomed punts provide a unique, peaceful perspective of the city's medieval architecture, hidden gardens, and the iconic Canterbury Cathedral, accompanied by historical commentary.

Free

Franciscan Gardens

Franciscan Gardens sits within the Canterbury Cathedral area of Canterbury, documented in local heritage registers for its civic or cultural role. Visitors approach from the main access road and combine the visit with neighbouring heritage buildings and public spaces in the district.

Free

Westgate Gardens

Nestled beside the serene River Stour, Westgate Gardens is a picturesque haven that invites visitors to unwind amidst its natural beauty. This enchanting spot features a magnificent 200-year-old plane tree and an intriguing Victorian Tower House, adding historical charm to the landscape. As you stroll through these gardens, find yourself immersed in the rich history of Canterbury, especially as you approach the monumental Westgate—the largest surviving medieval gatehouse in England.

£9

St Augustine's Abbey

St Augustine's Abbey, dating back to AD 597, was originally established as a burial site for Anglo-Saxon kings and played a significant role in the revival of Christianity in southern England. The abbey now stands as impressive ruins but offers visitors a chance to explore its rich history through a museum, free audio tour, and virtual reality experience. With its connection to Canterbury Castle and the serene surroundings outside the city walls, it makes for an intriguing visit on a sunny day.

Free

Harbour Market Whitstable

Harbour Market Whitstable is a and community-based open-air market located in the picturesque fishing town of Whitstable. Situated along the working harbour and dock, this market includes locally hand-crafted goods, fine art, and tempting food stalls serving various cuisines. Visitors can sample the local delicacy of fried fish while enjoying the waterfront location. The market showcases premium, locally sourced products ranging from food and drink to art and home goods.
