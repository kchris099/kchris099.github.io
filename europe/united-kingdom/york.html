Explore York

A Brief History

York is in United Kingdom. Established as a regional settlement beyond an older fortified core, the city developed through industrialization in the late 19th century, post-war reconstruction, and modern civic expansion that reshaped its public spaces, markets, and surrounding landscape. Museums, markets, and street plans in York still show layers from those periods.

Top Sights & Activities

York Minster

York Minster, also known as The Cathedral and Metropolitical Church of Saint Peter in York, is a Gothic cathedral with stained-glass windows. Travellers can explore the interactive Undercroft exhibition to learn about the cathedral's history and construction. Tower tours are available for those who want to climb up and enjoy views of the city.

£9

Clifford's Tower, York

Clifford's Tower, In York, is a 13th-century castle keep perched on a grass mound. Originally part of York Castle built by William the Conqueror, it has served as a prison and royal mint before becoming a popular tourist attraction. Travellers can climb to the top for panoramic views of the city and beyond to the North York Moors. The tower offers a glimpse into York's medieval past and is an excellent starting point for exploring the area.

Free

Designer Outlet York

Designer Outlet York, located in Fulford just a little further from York City Centre, is a haven for fashion enthusiasts seeking great deals on international designer and high street fashion brands as well as homeware. With over 120 major names like Calvin Klein, Boss, Adidas, Levis and Lacoste, shoppers can enjoy significant discounts of up to 50 or 60%. The beautifully landscaped garden setting adds to the charm of this top fashion and shopping destination.

Free

York City Walls

York City Walls are remnants of a stonework enclosure around a rectangular fortification, built in 71 CE. York has a rich history dating back to its founding in 71 AD as the capital of the Roman province in Britain. It later became famous for trade and commercial centers during the Middle Ages. The city developed into an important center for rail networks, manufacturing, education, and health services. Today, tourism plays a significant role in the local economy.

Free

Shambles Market

Shambles Market is a well-established outdoor market In York, open every day of the week. It offers a wide range of products including fresh produce, crafts, and clothing. The market has been recognized with the Outdoor Market award at the Great British Market Awards. Travellers can indulge in a culinary adventure at the dedicated food court where various food stands serve up delicious street food daily.

Free

York Racecourse

York Racecourse is a visitor site in York, United Kingdom. York Racecourse hosts flat race meetings from May to October, offering a grandstand and restaurants with track views. It's a great place for friends to enjoy dressing up, sipping drinks, and betting on horses. Notable events include The John Smiths Cup, Music Showcase Weekend, and the Welcome to Yorkshire Ebor Festival in October. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Free

York Theatre Royal

York Theatre Royal is a historic theater in York, established in 1744. It hosts a variety of shows from new writing to classics and youth work, including the annual Mystery Plays. The theater attracts audiences from all over England and tourists alike. In addition to York Theatre Royal, the city is home to other theaters such as Grand Opera House Theater and Joseph Rowntree Theater, making it an important hub for arts and literature.

Free

York Naburn Lock Caravan Park

The York Dungeon is a thrilling and entertaining attraction in York that offers a unique way to explore the city's dark past. Travellers can expect to encounter costumed characters, modern scares, and immersive sets as they journey through 2000 years of history. From infamous figures like Guy Fawkes and local witch Isabella Billington to the Vikings and the Plague, the experience brings York's terrifying history to life through ten live shows.

Free

York Barbican

In York, York Barbican is the city's largest music and arts venue offering a diverse range of entertainment. From live music and comedy to sports events and children's activities, there is something for everyone. The venue hosts a variety of events including musical performances by known bands like The Waterboys, opera shows by the Ukrainian National Opera, and comedy nights featuring BBC Radio's award-winning panel show 'I'm Sorry I Haven't a Clue.'.

Free

Bootham Bar

Bootham Bar is an ancient gatehouse in York, England, and is one of the four original entrances into the city. It has a history of over 800 years and is considered important landmarks in York. Travellers can start their walk on the York City Walls from Bootham Bar, enjoying up-close views of York Minister and vistas along the route.
