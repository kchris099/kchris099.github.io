Explore Durham

A Brief History

Durham was established in the late tenth century when monks from Lindisfarne sought a secure resting place for the relics of St. Cuthbert. The city grew around its magnificent Norman cathedral and castle, which served as the seat of the powerful Prince-Bishops who defended the English border against the Scots. In the nineteenth century, Durham became a center for coal mining and established one of England's oldest universities. Today, its historic peninsula remains a UNESCO World Heritage site and a center of academic excellence.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

Durham Cathedral

Durham Cathedral is a masterpiece of Norman architecture and a UNESCO World Heritage site, dominating the skyline above the River Wear. Founded in 1093, it houses the shrine of St. Cuthbert and the tomb of the Venerable Bede. The cathedral's massive stone pillars and pioneering ribbed vaulting make it one of the most significant religious buildings in Europe.

Free

Durham Castle

Durham Castle, originally built in 1072 after the Norman invasion, is now a college offering B&B accommodations. The castle is located next to Durham Castle and offers views of the city and river. Visitors can also explore nearby attractions such as Beamish Museum and the National Railway Museum in Shildon for a more tranquil break or historical experience.

Free

Durham Market Hall

Durham Market Hall, housed under a metal and glass canopy, has been operating since 1852. Following a restoration in 1996, the market now hosts over 50 traders rooted in the Durham community. Most businesses source their products locally, supporting both quality and the local economy. Despite the rise of online shopping, this Victorian Indoor City Market Hall remains vibrant with its bustling Saturday market offering a wide array of goods.

Free

National Trust - Crook Hall Gardens

National Trust - Crook Hall Gardens is a hidden gem located just a short distance from Durham's lively city center. The medieval manor house, dating back to the 13th century, is surrounded by five acres of picturesque English-style gardens. Visitors can explore various themed gardens such as The Secret Walled Garden, Silver and White Garden, Shakespeare Garden, and The Maze and Quiet Corner.

Free

Hall Hill Farm

Hall Hill Farm is an excellent family-friendly attraction that features a range of enjoyable activities for children. Visitors can interact with a variety of animals, including cows, rabbits and guinea pigs, and there is also a play barn and toddler area for younger kids. Donkey rides are also available for those looking for a more unique experience.

Free

Finchale Priory

Nestled along the picturesque River Wear in County Durham, England, Finchale Priory is a captivating 13th-century monastery ruin that offers a glimpse into medieval monastic life. Originally founded in 1196, this former Benedictine monastery provides visitors with centuries of history within its ancient walls. The priory's serene natural surroundings make it an ideal spot for leisurely walks and exploration.

Free

Adventure Valley

Adventure Valley is a large family adventure park located near Durham. It offers a wide mix of indoor and outdoor activities, including expansive play zones, giant slides, a petting farm, and a miniature railway. The park provides comprehensive, year-round entertainment for children and families in a rural setting.

Free

Brancepeth Castle

Brancepeth Castle is a fascinating historical site with centuries of history. The current owners are dedicated to preserving and enhancing the castle's use. Visitors can book tours to explore its rich history, from its conception through the ups and downs over the centuries. The castle, which may appear as a Victorian folly, has undergone significant renovations but still retains its ancient roots. The owner-led tour provides an authentic insight into the castle's extensive and noteworthy past.

Free

Diggerland Durham

Diggerland Durham is a unique construction-themed amusement park where children and adults can ride, drive, and operate full-sized construction machinery, including diggers, dumpers, and JCBs. The park offers a hands-on, interactive experience with modified heavy equipment, providing an unconventional and very entertaining day out.

Free

Down At The Farm

"Down At The Farm" is a delightful destination for families, offering an array of both domestic and exotic animals that visitors can interact with through handling and feeding sessions. Kids will love the outdoor adventure play areas featuring jumping pillows, a sandpit, and even a tube maze! Thrilling activities like tractor-trailer rides, quad biking, barrel rides, and racing car tracks ensure there's never a dull moment.
