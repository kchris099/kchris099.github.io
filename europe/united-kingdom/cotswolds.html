Cotswolds

Medieval wool profits paid for the stone churches and manor houses of Bibury, Broadway, and Chipping Campden. Enclosure quieted the cloth trade; honey-coloured limestone villages remained. Twentieth-century rail and car tourism rediscovered that rural building stock. Dry-stone walls parcel the same hills the flocks once crossed between pasture and market.

Woodchester Mansion Trust

An unfinished Victorian Gothic house in a Cotswolds valley, Woodchester Mansion keeps raw stone rooms open under trust care for guided exploration. Bats roost in sections of the structure as a conservation interest. Estate paths and parking support walkers who continue into the surrounding woodland.

Painswick Rococo Garden

Eighteenth-century asymmetrical design at Painswick Rococo Garden uses winding paths, hidden follies, and staged viewpoints as a private pleasure ground. Abandoned in the 1950s, the garden was later restored by historians and owners. Ornamental planting now again fills the valley rooms.

Giffords Circus

Giffords Circus is a spectacle that brings together an enchanting mix of acrobats, jugglers, magicians, musicians, and clowns. This summer, the circus will grace Minchinhampton Common from August 8 to 19 with its Avalon tour, immersing audiences in a active medieval area filled with Arthurian legends. Expect costumes and an opulent set accompanied by live music that enhances the magical atmosphere.

Shambles Market, Stroud

Shambles Market in Stroud operates on scheduled days with independent stalls selling food, crafts, clothing, antiques and household goods. The setting and vendor mix vary by market date, weather and local events, while nearby streets extend the town's commercial activity. It is a recurring community market whose character comes from changing traders, with no fixed permanent collection. Each market day has a different balance of stalls.

St. John Baptist Cirencester

The parish church of St John Baptist in Cirencester grew from an Augustinian foundation recorded around 1117 into one of the great Cotswold wool churches, with a tall tower over the market place. Fan vaulting and merchant endowments mark late medieval prosperity. The churchyard and porch sit at the heart of the town's historic streets.

Cirencester Open Air Swimming Pool

Cirencester's open-air pool, established in 1869, still uses natural spring water warmed to about 27°C in a town-centre outdoor basin. Seasonal swimming draws locals and travellers who want a historic lido experience. Changing rooms and lawns surround the pool as a summer civic amenity.

Cirencester Amphitheatre

Cirencester Amphitheatre, located on the outskirts of the town on Cotswold Avenue, is an ancient Roman-era monument managed by English Heritage. Dating back to the 2nd century, it was amphitheatres in the country, accommodating around 8,000 spectators who gathered to witness various public performances.

Cerney House Gardens

A family-run Cotswolds garden mixes formal country-house planting with a working kitchen garden, orchard, herb beds, wildflower bank, woodland, and parkland. A Victorian walled garden and nineteenth-century conservatory sit at the core. Paths link those rooms across the wider estate green.

Cotswold Water Park Hire

Cotswold Water Park Hire is a commercial equipment and activity business within the wider lake district formed from former gravel workings. Rentals may include kayaks, paddle-sport equipment, bicycles or other outdoor gear. The available activities depend on the season, weather and safety requirements at specific lakes. The surrounding water park remains a broad landscape managed by many separate organizations.
