Explore Cotswolds

A Brief History

Established as a regional settlement beyond an older fortified core, the city developed through industrialization in the late 19th century, post-war reconstruction, and modern civic expansion that reshaped its public spaces, markets, and surrounding landscape. Restored religious buildings, civic buildings, and natural features now define the contemporary visitor experience.

Top Sights & Activities

£13

Woodchester Mansion Trust

Woodchester Mansion Trust is a 19th-century Gothic mansion that offers tours showcasing masterful carving and stone masonry craftsmanship. The estate provides ample parking, albeit with a slightly rough driveway, and allows dogs on leads while cautioning travellers of livestock in some areas. With its rolling hills and scenic trails, the property offers views to travellers who come recommended to enjoy its peaceful ambiance.

£13

Painswick Rococo Garden

Painswick Rococo Garden is a picturesque 18th-century garden located in the Cotswolds, UK. It is known for its ornamental decoration and asymmetrical design, featuring winding paths, hidden follies, and scenic viewpoints. Originally created as a pleasure garden for a wealthy landowner, it was abandoned in the 1950s but has since been restored to its former glory by dedicated garden historians and owners.

Free

Giffords Circus

Giffords Circus is a delightful spectacle that brings together an enchanting mix of acrobats, jugglers, magicians, musicians, and clowns. This summer, the circus will grace Minchinhampton Common from August 8 to 19 with its Avalon tour, immersing audiences in a vibrant medieval realm filled with Arthurian legends. Expect costumes and an opulent set accompanied by live music that enhances the magical atmosphere.

Free

Shambles Market, Stroud

Shambles Market in Stroud has transformed from a traditional meat market to a vibrant hub of arts, crafts, and local produce. The town's unique high street offers independent food shops, bakeries, and delis. travellers can explore the eclectic range of goods including organic food, books, jewelry, and crafts. The Saturday market is a must-visit with an array of offerings such as cheeses, vegetables, dog treats, cakes, and more.

Free

St. John Baptist Cirencester

St. John Baptist Church in Cirencester is an Anglican church that was established in 1117 as part of an Augustinian monastery. The walled precinct and the church are situated at the center of a charming Cotswold town. The building, constructed in 1480, is typical of 15th century architecture with elaborate details and stained glass windows. Inside, there are ornate features and colorful wall paintings to admire. A gift shop inside offers religious items for purchase.

Free

Cirencester Open Air Swimming Pool

Cirencester Open Air Swimming Pool, established in 1869, offers a unique experience of swimming in natural spring water heated to an average of 27C. Situated on the outskirts of Cirencester Park, the facility features a 28-meter main pool with a slide, a separate children's paddling pool, and a sunbathing patio. travellers can also enjoy hot and cold refreshments on-site.

Free

Cirencester Amphitheatre

Cirencester Amphitheatre, located on the outskirts of the town on Cotswold Avenue, is an ancient Roman-era monument managed by English Heritage. Dating back to the 2nd century, it was amphitheatres in the country, accommodating around 8,000 spectators who gathered to witness various public performances.

Free

The Organic Farm Shop Cirencester

The Organic Farm Shop is a charming and secluded destination for a meal, nestled in converted farm buildings. The interior is tastefully furnished and boasts two spacious log burning stoves that provide warmth in all weather conditions. While dining, guests can enjoy the view of the picturesque fields beyond. The shop offers an array of fascinating products, predominantly ethical and organic foods, but also including clothing and gifts.

Free

Cerney House Gardens

Nestled in the picturesque Cotswolds near Cirencester, Cerney House Gardens is a charming Victorian garden offering enchanting woodland walks adorned with snowdrops every February. This family-run gem boasts a delightful mix of formal country house garden elements and an organic allure. travellers can explore a working kitchen garden, orchard, herb garden, wildflower bank, woodlands, and parkland surrounding a Victorian walled garden with a 19th-century conservatory.

Free

Cotswold Water Park Hire

Cotswold Water Park Hire is a fantastic destination for water sports enthusiasts, offering an array of activities on its expansive lakes. With experienced staff ready to assist, travellers can rent kayaks and canoes to navigate the shorelines and observe local wildlife, including various waterfowl. The facility boasts ample parking, clean amenities, and options for open water swimming throughout the year.
