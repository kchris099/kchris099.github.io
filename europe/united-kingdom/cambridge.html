Explore Cambridge

A Brief History

Evolving around one of the world's oldest continuous universities founded in 1209, Cambridge has been shaped entirely by profound academic legacy. The majestic Gothic colleges and peaceful river 'Backs' reflect eight centuries of extensive wealth, royal patronage, and intellectual dominance. Visitors today can clearly trace the detailed layers of this unique heritage throughout the preserved streets and monuments.

Top Sights & Activities

The Church of Our Lady of the Assumption and the English Martyrs, Cambridge

The Church of Our Lady of the Assumption and the English Martyrs, Cambridge is a religious building in Cambridge, United Kingdom. It opened or was completed around 1890. Located at the busy crossroads of Hills Road and Lensfield Road in Cambridge, the Church of Our Lady of the Assumption and the English Martyrs is a example of Gothic Revival architecture. The exterior and main interior spaces are usually viewable outside service times.

Free

Cambridge Market Square

Cambridge Market Square is a historic outdoor market that has been a central gathering place for locals since medieval times. It offers a wide variety of goods, including gourmet food stalls, fresh produce, herbs and spices, preserves, oils, condiments, and locally made crafts. The market reflects the city's active multicultural community with its diverse range of offerings from around the world.

Free

Great St Mary's

Great St Mary's is a visitor site in Cambridge, United Kingdom. Great St Mary's is a 15th-century church located in Cambridge, offering panoramic views of the city's known colleges from its tower. Notable attractions include 18th-century galleries and a tall bell tower that provides superb panoramic views of the city center. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

£16

King's College Chapel

King's College Chapel is a example of late Gothic architecture located in Cambridge. This 16th-century chapel has an fan-vaulted ceiling, detailed wood carvings, and original stained-glass windows. It took nearly a century to complete this monumental structure, which was founded by Henry VI and later finished by five English monarchs.

Free

The Corpus Clock

The Corpus Clock is a visitor site in Cambridge, United Kingdom. Records associated with the site include 2008. The Corpus Clock, located outside Cambridge's Corpus Christi College, is a monument created by John C Taylor and unveiled in 2008. Instead of traditional hands, the time is displayed through backlit LED lights in concentric rings. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Free

Cambridge Corn Exchange

The Cambridge Corn Exchange is a large and historic venue that hosts a variety of events, including popular and orchestral music performances, comedy shows, dance performances, and plays. The city of Cambridge offers a scene with attractions like the Arts Theatre, Cambridge Junction, and the ADC Theatre, visitors can explore museums such as the Fitzwilliam and enjoy festivals like the Beer Festival and Folk Festival during the summer months.

Free

Newton's Apple Tree

Newton's Apple Tree is a visitor site in Cambridge, United Kingdom. This living descendant of the tree that inspired Sir Isaac Newton's theories on gravity has a chance to connect with the past and gain insight into the origins of Newton's Laws of Gravity. The careful maintained grounds and growing tree serve as a evidence of the long running significance of this historical site. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Free (via queens' college £4)

Mathematical Bridge

Mathematical Bridge is a bridge in Cambridge, United Kingdom. The Mathematical Bridge is a historical footbridge in Cambridge, known for its unique construction using only straight timbers and precise engineering. It is located near the River Cam, passing through the Backs area where several colleges are situated. Pedestrian decks and adjacent streets provide the usual viewing points.

Free

Lets Go Punting Cambridge

Lets Go Punting Cambridge is a documented piece of civic infrastructure in Cambridge, United Kingdom, recognised in local engineering and heritage listings for its construction period and continued public use. The structure is accessible from the surrounding streets and is visible from adjacent viewpoints. Travellers cross or view the site while moving between nearby districts and transit connections.

Free

Castle Mound

Castle Hill is a grassy mound located in the centre of Cambridge, providing panoramic views of the city. The car park is very cheap and provides great access to the site, making it perfect for those who want to visit the usual tourist spots before hiking up to the top for an view.
