Brighton

Sea-bathing fashion and George IV's patronage lifted a fishing village into a Regency resort; the railway then locked in London day-trip traffic. The Royal Pavilion's Indian-Gothic fantasy is the architectural emblem of that royal leisure economy on the Channel. The West Pier's ruin and the Palace Pier show how seaside capital rose and weathered. The Lanes' tangled alleys predate the Regency squares behind the seafront.

Lewes Priory

Lewes Priory, dating back to the 11th century, was the first Cluniac house in England dedicated to traditional monastic life. The site encourages visitors to explore the ruins and follow in the footsteps of medieval monks. The herb garden still grows today, offering a glimpse into where medicinal plants and herbs were once cultivated. Lewes itself is a market town with a rich history, featuring landmarks like Lewes Castle and hosting events such as the Lewes Bonfire celebrations.
