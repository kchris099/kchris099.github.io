Kotor

Kotor sits at the head of a fjord-like bay on the Adriatic and was ruled by Byzantines, Venetians, and later Austrian administrations. Its walls climb the hillside above a maritime old town with Romanesque churches and a cathedral dedicated to Saint Tryphon from 1166. The Bay of Kotor served medieval trade routes. UNESCO listed the natural and cultural area as a World Heritage site in 1979.

Kotor Fortress

Known as St. John's Fortress, this citadel climbs the slope above Kotor's old town. Walls and switchback paths were expanded by Venetians and later Austrian engineers from the 9th century onward. The strenuous hike rewards climbers with views over red rooftops, the bay, and cruise ships at the pier.

Kotor Bay Panorama

Viewpoints above Kotor look onto a winding Adriatic inlet often called Europe's southernmost fjord, though it is a submerged river canyon. Settlements ring the shoreline beneath steep mountains. The panorama shows how the bay sheltered shipping routes under Venetian and later Austro-Hungarian rule.

Kampana Tower

Kampana Tower guards the point where Kotor's western walls meet the Skurda River and waterfront; paths near the tower reveal its relationship to gates and harbor. Venetian-period masonry, crenellations, and adjacent ramparts form part of the historic fortification system surrounding the old town.

Cathedral of Saint Tryphon

Twin towers flank the facade above a small old-town square, while the treasury preserves sacred art and relics associated with Saint Tryphon. Kotor Cathedral was consecrated in 1166 and rebuilt after several earthquakes, leaving a Romanesque core with Baroque and later additions. Active Catholic worship continues within one of Montenegro's historic major churches.

42.4239° N / 18.7716° E

Clock Tower

Erected in 1602 under Venetian rule, the clock tower rises beside the main gate into Kotor's old town. A stone pillory once stood before it for public punishment. The mechanism still chimes the hours above the narrow square where markets and festivals gather.
