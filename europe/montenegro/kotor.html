Explore Kotor

A Brief History

Kotor is a heavily fortified coastal town whose strategic importance was recognized since antiquity. For nearly four centuries, it functioned as a highly autonomous part of the Venetian Republic, which profoundly influenced its distinctly Italianate architectural character. Its massive defensive walls, integrated directly into the steep surrounding mountains, reflect the continuous historical necessity of defending its valuable maritime trade routes against Ottoman expansion.

Kotor Fortress

Also known as St. John's Fortress, a defensive structure integrated into the steep mountain behind Kotor. The fortifications were gradually developed from the 9th to the 19th century by the Venetians and Austrians.

Kotor Bay Panorama

The scenic vantage points overlooking the Bay of Kotor, a ria of the Adriatic Sea. The area has been inhabited since antiquity and served as a crucial naval and commercial harbor during the Venetian Republic.

Free

Kampana Tower

A distinctive defensive tower built by the Venetians in the 13th and 14th centuries as part of the city walls of Kotor to protect the town from sea-based attacks and pirate raids.

€3

Cathedral of Saint Tryphon

A Roman Catholic cathedral consecrated in 1166, dedicated to Saint Tryphon, the patron and protector of the city. It is one of the oldest churches in Europe and features Romanesque architecture.

Free

Clock Tower

Built in 1602 during the Venetian period, this tower features a mix of Baroque and Gothic architectural elements. A historical pillory structure stands directly in front of the tower, historically used to punish offenders.
