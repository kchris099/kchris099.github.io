Budva

Budva is one of the oldest settlements on the Adriatic coast, with origins dating back over 2,500 years to Illyrian times. It was later ruled by the Romans, Venetians, and the Austro-Hungarian Empire, which heavily influenced its fortified Old Town (Stari Grad) and Mediterranean architecture. The town suffered significant damage in a 1979 earthquake but was careful reconstructed. Today, Budva is Montenegro's premier coastal resort, known for its historic center and active beaches.

Budva ballerina

The Dancing Girl Statue in Budva is a bronze sculpture located between the old town and Mogren Beach. It's a symbol of the city, inspired by a local legend about a girl who waited for her sailor lover to return but tragically died on the same rocks where her statue now stands. Some believe it depicts a drowned girl, while others see it as an ordinary statue. The elegant pose of the topless girl has become, with many visitors attempting to imitate it for photos.

Fort Mogren

Fort Mogren, also known as Jaz Troop, is a historical site built in 1860 by the Austro-Hungarian Empire to protect Budva's western borders. Despite its historical significance, the fort is set amidst natural surroundings. Accessible via an asphalt road with free parking, the neglected fort suffered heavy damage during a 1979 earthquake. However, it offers views of Budva and the nearby Jazz beach.

The Podmaine Monastery, a 15th-century Serbian Orthodox monastery, is situated in a hillside location and has active interior frescoes. Built by the Crnojevi noble family, it comprises two churches - a smaller one from the same period dedicated to the Presentation of the Mother of God and a larger one built in 1747 devoted to the Dormition of the Mother of God.

Cetinje Monastery

Cetinje Monastery is a significant Christian Orthodox site in Montenegro, built in 1704 on medieval ruins. Located in Cetinje, this monastery holds sacred relics and is the seat of the Metropolitanate of Montenegro. As the former capital of Montenegro, Cetinje has rich history and cultural attractions, including the Mausoleum of Prince Danilo and The Royal Theater Zetski dom.
