Venice

Venice grew from lagoon settlements in the 5th century into a maritime republic linking the Adriatic with the eastern Mediterranean through trade and naval power. Palaces, churches, bridges, and institutions such as the Arsenal reflect mercantile government, artistic patronage, and engineering shaped by canals and tidal islands. Decline after the 16th century and Austrian rule preceded incorporation into Italy in 1866.

Palazzo Ducale

Palazzo Ducale is a visitor site in Venice. The Doge's Palace combined the residence of the doge with council chambers, courts, and administrative rooms of the Venetian Republic. The present Gothic complex was built mainly in the 14th and 15th centuries and remains a record of the city's civic government. The Scala d'Oro, Senate hall, and Bridge of Sighs route show how Venice governed its maritime empire.
