Explore Venice

A Brief History

Venice grew from lagoon settlements into a maritime republic that linked the Adriatic with the eastern Mediterranean. Palaces, churches, bridges, and trading institutions reflect centuries of mercantile government, artistic patronage, and engineering shaped by canals and tidal islands.

Top Sights & Activities

From €30

Palazzo Ducale

The Doge's Palace combined the residence of the doge with council chambers, courts, and administrative rooms of the Venetian Republic. The present Gothic complex was built mainly in the 14th and 15th centuries and remains a record of the city's civic government.

Free

Ponte di Rialto

The Rialto Bridge was completed in 1591 as the main stone crossing of the Grand Canal at Venice's historic commercial center. Its arcades and location near the market made it central to trade and daily movement within the city.

Free

Ponte dei Sospiri

The Bridge of Sighs was completed in the early 17th century to connect the Doge's Palace with the New Prison. Its enclosed passage became one of the most recognized symbols of Venice and its judicial institutions.

Free

Piazza San Marco

Saint Mark's Square developed as the ceremonial center of Venice beside the basilica, campanile, and procuratie. It was the public forecourt of the republic and remains the city's main civic gathering space.

€6

Basilica di San Marco

Saint Mark's Basilica took shape from the 11th century as the state church of the Venetian Republic and shrine of Saint Mark. Its mosaics, domes, and marble surfaces reflect trade links with Byzantium and the eastern Mediterranean.

€10

Ca' Pesaro International Gallery of Modern Art

Ca' Pesaro is a Baroque palace on the Grand Canal completed in the 18th century and now home to the International Gallery of Modern Art. The museum connects Venetian palatial architecture with later collections of modern painting and sculpture.

€10

Biblioteca Nazionale Marciana

The Marciana Library grew from the humanist collections given to Venice in the 15th century and occupies Sansovino's library building on Saint Mark's Square. It remains one of Italy's major research libraries for manuscripts and early printed books.

Free

Basilica Santa Maria della Salute

Santa Maria della Salute was built after the plague of 1630 as a votive church at the entrance to the Grand Canal. Its domed profile by Baldassare Longhena became one of the main landmarks of Baroque Venice.

€10

Ca' Rezzonico

Ca' Rezzonico is an 18th-century palace on the Grand Canal that now houses the Museum of 18th Century Venice. Its rooms, paintings, and furnishings present the social and artistic culture of the late republican city.

€10

Murano Glass Museum

The Murano Glass Museum is housed in Palazzo Giustinian and traces the island's long role in glass production under Venetian rule. Murano workshops shaped the city's export economy and craft identity for centuries.
