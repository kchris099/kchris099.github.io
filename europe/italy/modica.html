Modica

Modica cascades down steep valleys in southeastern Sicily where Arab and Norman counts built churches and castles before the 1693 earthquake required baroque rebuilding. Cacao introduced from Spanish America under Aragonese rule evolved into a distinctive cold-worked chocolate tradition still sold along Corso Umberto. Split into Modica Alta and Bassa, the town preserves stairways, bridges, and civic palaces that connect feudal hilltops to valley-floor markets in the Hyblaean hills.

Ragusa Ibla

A deep earthquake-cut chasm separates this older quarter from modern Ragusa Superiore. The Basilica of San Giorgio crowns the lower town's Baroque streets. Caffetteria Donnafugata and the two-Michelin-starred Duomo restaurant draw visitors who follow Inspector Montalbano filming locations through the same lanes.

Giardino Ibleo

Built in 1858 by local volunteers, Giardino Ibleo occupies a rock spur about 385 meters above sea level overlooking Ragusa Ibla's valley. Palm-lined paths, churches, and belvederes organize the small public garden. It remains a quiet overlook above the baroque townscape.

03 AdmissionFree (bell tower €2)

Duomo di San Giorgio

A long staircase rises through Modica Alta to the Duomo di San Giorgio, whose eighteenth-century facade forms one of the city's principal Sicilian Baroque compositions. Rebuilding followed the 1693 earthquake, though the church retains older foundations and sacred objects. The interior's tall nave, paintings, and silver reliquary connect the dramatic urban setting with devotion to Modica's patron saint.

Pizzo viewpoint

Also called Pizzo Belvedere, the overlook sits near Belvedere Principe di Piemonte and the church of San Giovanni. From the edge, Modica's stacked roofs and surrounding hills fill a single sweep. Approach paths are short walks from nearby roads or parking, so the climb stays modest.

05 AdmissionFree

Belvedere di San Benedetto

The viewpoint clarifies the steep geography that separates Modica Alta from lower districts. From a terrace near San Benedetto in upper Modica, the belvedere looks across tightly packed houses, church domes, stairways, and the valley dividing the historic town. Reaching it involves uphill streets and steps, and the attraction is the urban panorama.

Cattedrale di San Giovanni Battista

The Cathedral of San Giovanni Battista in Ragusa crowns a staircase-lined square with an eighteenth-century Baroque facade rebuilt after the 1693 earthquake. Twin towers and a broad nave dominate the upper town. Evening light catches the limestone front that faces the sloping piazza.

Church of St Mary of the Stairs

The Church of St Mary of the Stairs, located in Ragusa Ibla, Italy, is a old town with a less maintained but atmosphere. The view from the top, framed by the bell tower of the church and local homes, is truly magical and worth returning to see in different lights.

08 AdmissionFree

Antica Dolceria Bonajuto

Modica's Bonajuto family has operated a confectionery business since the nineteenth century, producing chocolate associated with the city's cold-working tradition. The shop sells bars flavored with ingredients such as vanilla, cinnamon, or chili alongside pastries and other sweets; its historical displays and production story explain how granular Modica chocolate differs in texture from fully conched industrial styles.

09 AdmissionFree

Duomo di San Pietro Apostolo

Duomo di San Pietro in Modica was rebuilt in Baroque form after the 1693 earthquake destroyed earlier fabric. A long stairway climbs to a facade framed by statues of the apostles. The church remains a principal parish landmark in the lower town's stone streetscape.

36.8607° N / 14.7608° E

10 AdmissionFree

Chiesa Collegiata di Santa Maria di Betlem

Near Piazza Principe di Napoli, Santa Maria di Betlem traces origins traditionally to the early Christian period and later medieval rebuilding in Modica. Early twentieth-century mudslides damaged the fabric before restoration. The collegiate church still anchors a neighborhood of steep lanes above the torrent bed.
