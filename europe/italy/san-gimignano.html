Explore San Gimignano

A Brief History

San Gimignano prospered on the Via Francigena pilgrimage route between Florence and Siena, when merchant families built tower houses in the twelfth and thirteenth centuries to display wealth and defend factions. Plague and the loss of independent commune status froze much of the skyline that later visitors nicknamed a medieval Manhattan. Surrounding saffron fields and Vernaccia vineyards supported the hill town after trade routes shifted, preserving walls and public frescoes above the Elsa valley.

Piazza della Cisterna

Piazza della Cisterna is a triangular plaza surrounded by medieval buildings and named after its 13th-century cistern. It is one of the principal squares in San Gimignano, along with Piazza del Duomo. The plaza offers access to several notable attractions, including the Palazzo Comunale and the Basilica Collegiata di Santa Maria Assunta, known for its Romanesque architecture and fresco cycles by known artists.

Porta Nuova

Porta Nuova, also known as Porta Volterrana or Porta Salis, is a majestic city gate located in the middle of Colle di Val d'Elsa. Built in 1479 as part of the defensive system for the castle of Colle, it is attributed to Giuliano da San Gallo. The gate gives the impression of being an entrance to a fortress rather than a city when viewed from outside.

Sentierelsa Trail

Sentierelsa Trail is a visitor site in San Gimignano, Italy. The Sentierelsa Trail is a 4km-long route that follows the turquoise-colored river Elsa. One of its highlights is the 15-meter high Diborrato waterfall cascading into a deep blue lake. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Free

Diborrato Waterfall

Diborrato Waterfall is a terraced cascade that stands 15 meters tall, offering an exhilarating experience for nature lovers and adventure seekers alike. At the end of the Elsa Trail, this spot rewards hikers with a refreshing swim in its rocky pool after a scenic 40-minute trek. The color of the river adds to its charm, making it a place for families to enjoy despite cooler water temperatures.
