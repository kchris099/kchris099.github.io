Vicenza

Vicenza flourished under the Venetian Republic as a country seat for landowners who hired Andrea Palladio to redesign palaces, villas, and the Basilica loggias in the sixteenth century. Roman roads and medieval guild halls preceded that classical revival, which spread Palladian proportions across Europe and North America. The Teatro Olimpico, completed in 1585, anchors a centre where stone arcades, silk workshops, and later manufacturing linked the Veneto plain to Alpine timber routes.

Olympic Theater

Andrea Palladio designed Teatro Olimpico for Vicenza's Accademia Olimpica near the end of his life. Vincenzo Scamozzi completed the interior and its permanent perspective scenery for the 1585 opening. A classical cavea, sculpted architectural screen, and receding stage streets create the illusion of a much deeper city, preserving the earliest surviving enclosed theater of the Renaissance.

Palazzo Chiericati

Palazzo Chiericati is a sixteenth-century Palladian palace that now houses Vicenza's civic art museum, with works from the thirteenth to twentieth centuries. Venetian school paintings by Tintoretto, Veronese, and others hang in the galleries. The porticoed facade faces the open Campo dell'Isola near the Teatro Olimpico.

Chiesa di Santa Corona

Vicenza's Santa Corona was founded in the thirteenth century to hold a relic believed to come from Christ's Crown of Thorns. The Gothic church later received Renaissance chapels, including the Valmarana Chapel designed by Palladio. Bellini's Baptism of Christ, works by Veronese and Montagna, tombs, and archaeological remains beneath the church broaden its artistic and religious significance.

Palladian Basilica

Palladio wrapped Vicenza's medieval civic hall with two levels of white stone loggias, creating the building now called the Basilica Palladiana. Serlian arches regularize the irregular older structure and frame Piazza dei Signori. The vast upper hall and terraces host exhibitions and events, while the exterior became one of the clearest statements of Palladio's architectural language.

Cathedral Santa Maria Annunciata

Vicenza Cathedral developed through repeated rebuilding on an early Christian site. Its Gothic nave and later chapels stand beneath a dome associated with Andrea Palladio, while archaeological areas preserve earlier church remains. Artworks include a triptych by Lorenzo Veneziano, and the long south side faces Piazza del Duomo within a complex that also includes the bishop's palace.

Church of San Lorenzo

A deep Gothic portal and rose window front Vicenza's Church of San Lorenzo, begun for the Franciscans in the thirteenth century. The broad brick interior contains tombs, chapels, frescoes, and sculpture from several periods, including work associated with Bartolomeo Montagna; its scale reflects the order's urban presence, but comparisons with the cathedral add little to understanding the church itself.

Basilica of Saints Felice and Fortunato

Early Christian foundations, mosaics, sarcophagi and later Romanesque work meet at the Basilica of Saints Felice and Fortunato west of central Vicenza. The complex developed around an ancient burial area and preserves a crypt, nave and bell tower shaped by several rebuilding phases. Archaeological evidence beneath and around the church traces local Christianity back to late antiquity.

Retrone

The Retrone is a river running through Vicenza, meeting the Bacchiglione near the historic center and once powering mills along its banks. Bridges and riverside walks thread between Palladian facades and quieter residential edges. The watercourse remains a defining natural line through the city plan.

45.5317° N / 11.5260° E

Teatro civico

Schio's Teatro Civico opened in the early twentieth century during a period of industrial prosperity linked to the town's textile economy. Art Nouveau decoration, a horseshoe auditorium, boxes and stage spaces define the building. Restoration and cultural programming have returned the theater to use for performances and community events within Schio's historic center.

Duomo di San Pietro Apostolo

On Gorzone hill, the cathedral overlooks Alessandro Rossi Square with a bell tower carrying fifteenth-century detailing. Inside, classical columns divide five aisles, and the Chapel of the Coronation stands out among the side spaces. At night the mass of the building still reads clearly above the town.
