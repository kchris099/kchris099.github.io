Explore Florence

A Brief History

Florence emerged as a major commune in medieval Tuscany, expanding through banking, wool trade, and republican government before the Medici consolidated ducal rule in the 16th century. Its cathedral dome, palaces, guild churches, and civic squares preserve the political and artistic landscape of the Renaissance. Figures such as Dante, Brunelleschi, and Michelangelo shaped the city's global cultural reputation.

Top Sights & Activities

Cathedral of Santa Maria del Fiore

Florence Cathedral was begun in 1296 and completed across several centuries, with Brunelleschi's dome added in the 15th century. The church became the main religious monument of the republic and remains central to the city's skyline and civic identity.

Free

Piazzale Michelangelo

Piazzale Michelangelo is a 19th-century square in Florence, featuring a bronze replica of Michelangelo's David and offering panoramic views of the city. It's a popular spot for romantic dates, where travellers can enjoy the bird's-eye view while sipping on wine and listening to musicians play favorites during sunset.

Free

Basilica di San Miniato

San Miniato al Monte is a Romanesque basilica on the hill above Florence, built over earlier religious foundations and closely tied to the cult of Saint Minias. Its facade, choir, and hilltop setting make it one of the major historic churches of the city.

€10

Basilica di Santa Croce di Firenze

Santa Croce was founded for the Franciscans in the late 13th century and became a major burial church for notable Italians including Michelangelo and Galileo. Frescoes, chapels, and tombs connect the basilica with Florence's religious and intellectual history.

Free

Piazza della Repubblica

Piazza della Repubblica took its present form during 19th-century redevelopment after Florence became capital of unified Italy. The square occupies the site of the Roman forum and marks the shift from medieval street fabric to modern boulevards and arcades.

€13

Palazzo Vecchio

Palazzo Vecchio, originally built to house the city's government officials and later transformed into the Medici family's residence, is a 13th-century fortified palace in Florence. It has lavishly decorated chambers, ornate courtyards, and a towering presence atop the Piazza della Signoria.

Free

Piazza della Signoria

Piazza della Signoria formed around the civic the Florentine republic and remains tied to Palazzo Vecchio, Loggia dei Lanzi, and a long tradition of public sculpture. The square has functioned as a political and ceremonial space since the medieval period.

€15

Palazzo Strozzi

Palazzo Strozzi was built from the late 15th century for the Strozzi family and is one of the main Renaissance palaces of Florence. Its courtyard and rusticated exterior show the city's elite domestic architecture, while the building now hosts cultural exhibitions.

Free

Ponte Vecchio

Ponte Vecchio, a medieval bridge with Roman roots, is not just an notable building but also a active hub of culture and history. First documented in 996, this resilient structure has weathered the test of time, surviving floods and even World War II. Rebuilt in the 14th century after a devastating flood, it became home to exclusive shops selling jewelry and souvenirs when Ferdinand decreed that only goldsmiths could operate here in 1593.

Free

Piazza del Duomo

Piazza del Duomo is a square that is the Florence, surrounded by some of the city's most historical and artistic landmarks. At its center stands the Santa Maria del Fiore Cathedral, known for its dome, the largest brick dome ever constructed, designed by the brilliant Renaissance architect Filippo Brunelleschi. This notable building is complemented by Giotto's Campanile on one side and the Baptistery of St.

Free

Baptistery of St. John

Baptistery of St. John is a visitor site in Florence, Italy. John is an octagonal basilica in Florence, known for its striking marble facade, bronze doors, and mosaic ceiling. It holds a significant place in the spiritual center of Florence, located in Piazza del Duomo and Piazza San Giovanni alongside the Church of Santa Maria del Fiore. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Free

Brunelleschi's dome

Brunelleschi's Dome, an symbol of the Renaissance, stands majestically atop the Cathedral of Santa Maria del Fiore in Florence. Designed by Filippo Brunelleschi between 1420 and 1436, this notable building reaches a height of 91 meters and has a unique octagonal shape formed by inner and outer concentric domes.

Free

Fontana del Porcellino

Fontana del Porcellino is a visitor site in Florence, Italy. Fontana del Porcellino is a famous bronze fountain in Florence, located near Piazza della Repubblica. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest. Fontana del Porcellino remains part of the public visitor circuit in Florence.

Free

Loggia dei Lanzi

Loggia dei Lanzi, also known as Loggia della Signoria, is a 14th-century open-air gallery of Renaissance sculpture located in Piazza della Signoria in Florence. This ceremonial building with wide arches houses important Renaissance art and has been a part of the city since 1382. The area was used to display sculptures as a political message next to Florence's seat of power during the rule of the Grand Duchy of Tuscany.

Free

Porta San Niccolò

Porta San Niccolò, a towering stone gate from Florence's 1300s fortification walls, offers panoramic views from its crenellated top. Located in the Oltrarno near San Niccolò, this historical landmark is known for its architecture and rich history. It is part of the well-preserved fortifications that once protected the city and is a evidence of Florence's medieval past.

Free

Basilica of Santa Croce in Florence

The Basilica of Santa Croce in Florence is a Neo-Gothic church that is a evidence of the city's rich artistic and historical heritage. Designed by Arnolfo di Cambio in 1294 for the Franciscan order, this structure is not only known for its Giotto frescoes but also is the final resting place for some of Italy's most illustrious figures, including Michelangelo and Galileo.

Free

Forte di Belvedere

Forte di Belvedere, a 16th-century hilltop fortress in Florence, offers panoramic views of the city's skyline and surrounding hills. Originally built by Buontalenti on the orders of the Medici family as a refuge and treasure hideout, it now hosts art exhibitions and provides a 360-degree view of Florence, including landmarks like the Duomo, Ponte Vecchio, and Palazzo Vecchio.

Free

Villa Bardini

Villa Bardini is a 17th-century villa in Florence that houses artworks by painter Pietro Annigoni and is surrounded by a known garden. The villa offers a quiet shoreline from the busy city, with its manicured gardens providing a backdrop for leisurely strolls. Travellers can wander through wisteria-covered paths and explore the terraced Rose and Iris Gardens, which are part of the UNESCO extension.

Free

Giardino Bardini

Giardino Bardini is a visitor site in Florence, Italy. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest. Giardino Bardini remains part of the public visitor circuit in Florence. Local maps identify Giardino Bardini within Florence, Italy.

Free

David

David, the known Renaissance-era marble sculpture by Michelangelo, is a masterpiece that depicts the biblical character David. The statue is celebrated for its detail and accuracy in portraying the human form. While the original can be found at the Galleria dell'Accademia, a replica stands at the entrance of Palazzo Vecchio. Travellers to Florence can also admire other works by Michelangelo inside the gallery.

Free

Piazza della Santissima Annunziata

Piazza della Santissima Annunziata is a example of Renaissance architecture, featuring elegant porticoes and a striking central statue of Ferdinando I. This square is a backdrop for the active 'rificolona' festival, which celebrates the arrival of travellers from the Casentino and Pistoia mountains. As they journeyed to Florence, these travelers illuminated their path with colorful lanterns, creating a magical atmosphere in both the countryside and city streets.

Free

Basilica of Santa Maria Novella

In Florence, Italy, stands the Basilica of Santa Maria Novella, a 15th-century Dominican church known for its frescoes by Gothic and early Renaissance masters. Situated on the northern side of the river, this area is home to some of Florence's major attractions.

Free

Officina Profumo - Farmaceutica di Santa Maria Novella

Officina Profumo - Farmaceutica di Santa Maria Novella is a visitor site in Florence, Italy. Officina Profumo - Farmaceutica di Santa Maria Novella is a historic herbal pharmacy located in Florence, Italy. Housed in a 13th-century frescoed chapel, it offers an array of fragrant products including soaps, lotions, and potions made from a mix of spices, herbs, and flowers. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Free

Chiesa di San Salvatore in Ognissanti

Located a neighborhood of Florence, the Chiesa di San Salvatore in Ognissanti is a example of early Baroque architecture with deep historical roots. Originally established as a 13th-century structure, it underwent significant renovations during the 16th and 17th centuries and was restored after the devastating flood of 1966.

Free

Ponte Santa Trinita

Ponte Santa Trinita, also known as St Trinity Bridge, is a old stone bridge in Florence that crosses the River Arno. It was designed by Bartolomeo Ammanati and reconstructed after World War II. The bridge is adorned with lovely statues and is surrounded by two other stone bridges, Ponte Alla Carraia and the famous Ponte Vecchio.

Free

Palazzo Corsini

Palazzo Corsini is a historical venue that encapsulates the grandeur of Florentine architecture. This palace has witnessed centuries of artistic evolution, making it an ideal backdrop for cultural events and celebrations. Recently, it hosted a recital featuring known artists Julia Lynch and James Platt, showing its versatility as a performance space.

Free

Strozzi Palace

Located just a stone's throw from Piazza della Repubblica, the Strozzi Palace (Palazzo Strozzi) is a evidence of Renaissance architecture in Florence. Built between 1489 and 1538 for the affluent Strozzi family, who were notable rivals of the Medici clan, this grand palace is not only steeped in history but also is a active cultural hub.

Free

Boboli Gardens

Boboli Gardens is a public park in Florence, Italy. The Medici family transformed the gardens into a Italian-style oasis, setting an example for European courts. The open-air museum is adorned with Renaissance statues, fountains like Neptune Fountain and the large Statue of Abundance, and a grand avenue lined with cypress trees and statues leading to Isolotto square. Paths, lawns, and shaded areas make up the main public facilities.

Free

Palazzo Pitti

Palazzo Pitti is a grand Renaissance royal palace in Florence that houses an extensive collection of Italian and European masterpieces. It comprises six museums, including the Palatine Gallery, which has twenty-eight rooms filled with late Renaissance and Baroque art by known artists such as Raphael, Andrea del Sarto, Rubens, and Tintoretto. The Gallery of Modern Art offers a display of artworks within the former residence of the Lorraine family on the second floor of the palace.

Free

Chiesa di Santa Maria del Carmine

Chiesa di Santa Maria del Carmine is a restored baroque church located near the Church of Santo Spirito in the Oltrarno District. Despite its unfinished facade, it is known for housing the Brancacci Chapel, a masterpiece of Renaissance painting. The chapel's frescoes by Masaccio and Masolino are considered groundbreaking for their use of depth, perspective, and monumental figures.

Free

Parco di Villa Il Ventaglio

Villa Park 'The Fan' is a terraced park located at Villa Il Ventaglio, offering hanging gardens and a small lake with islets. The park provides a quiet shoreline with its natural surroundings, the area is surrounded by several historic palaces in Florence, such as Palazzo Pitti and Palazzo Vecchio, adding to the allure of the location.

Free

Parco delle Cascine

Parco delle Cascine is a visitor site in Florence, Italy. Parco delle Cascine is Florence's largest city park, spanning 116 hectares and offering a variety of activities for locals and travellers. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Free

Cappelle Medicee

The Cappelle Medicee, or the Medici Chapels, is a notable building that is the final resting place for the illustrious Medici family. Since 1429, this opulent site has been a evidence of the family's wealth and influence during the Renaissance. Travellers are often left in awe of its extravagant decor featuring active polychrome marbles, granite, and sparkling semi-precious stones that embellish the tombs within its mausoleum and crypts.

Free

Medici Riccardi Palace

The Medici Riccardi Palace, a 15th-century mansion designed by Michelozzo for the Medici family, is now home to an art museum and a famous chapel. The palace hosts an exhibition featuring works by Italian artist Oscar Ghiglia, known for his ties to Tuscany and European artistic developments. The palace's architecture stands out with its arched windows, partially shuttered loggia, rusticated floors, and large cornice capping the roofline.
