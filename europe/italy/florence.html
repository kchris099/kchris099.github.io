Florence

Florence rose as a medieval Tuscan commune on banking, wool, and republican institutions before the Medici turned it into a duchy in the sixteenth century. Brunelleschi's cathedral dome, the Palazzo Vecchio, guild churches, and the Uffizi corridor record that mercantile and Medici patronage. Dante, and later Michelangelo, fixed the city's place in European letters and art.

Medici Riccardi Palace

Michelozzo designed Palazzo Medici in the fifteenth century for Cosimo de' Medici, using graduated rustication and a broad classical cornice to shape an influential urban residence.
