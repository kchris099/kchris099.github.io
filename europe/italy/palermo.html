Palermo

Palermo served as capital of Norman Sicily after Arab engineers and Byzantine mosaicists helped Roger II build palaces and churches that blended Latin, Greek, and Islamic traditions in the twelfth century. Spanish viceroys later fortified harbours and baroque churches above Phoenician and Roman street grids. Twentieth-century migration, Allied invasion in 1943, and anti-mafia campaigns reshaped the city while markets, oratories, and Quattro Canti intersections still organise life on the Tyrrhenian coast.
