Explore Siena

A Brief History

Siena rivalled Florence as a banking republic until defeat at Montaperti in 1260 and later plague and Medici pressure ended its independence. The fan-shaped Piazza del Campo, Palazzo Pubblico frescoes, and striped cathedral embody Gothic civic art funded by the Monte dei Paschi bank founded in 1472. Seventeen contrade still stage the Palio horse race, preserving neighbourhood rivalries that date to medieval guild organisation in southern Tuscany.

Siena Cathedral

Siena Cathedral, also known as the Duomo, is a 13th-century marvel known for its striking facade with symbolic black and white marble stripes. It is the main place of worship in Siena and dominates the city's skyline alongside the Torre del Mangia. The cathedral has a rich history, originally built to replace a church dedicated to Mary on a site that was once a temple for the worship of Minerva.

Basilica Cateriniana di San Domenico

Basilica Cateriniana San Domenico is a striking Gothic cathedral in Siena, Italy.
