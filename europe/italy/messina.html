Messina

Messina faces the Strait of Messina on Sicily's northeastern coast and was founded as a Greek colony called Zancle. Earthquakes in 1783 and 1908 destroyed much of the city, leading to wide rebuilding. The cathedral bell tower carries an astronomical clock from 1933. Ferries link the city to mainland Italy. Norman and baroque churches recall periods of Byzantine, Arab, and Spanish influence on the island.

Duomo di Messina

Messina's cathedral was founded by the Normans in 1197 on the site of earlier churches. Earthquakes in 1783 and 1908, plus wartime bombing, required repeated rebuilding. Its campanile houses an astronomical clock that stages animated figures at noon, drawing crowds to the Piazza del Duomo.

Tempio di Cristo Re

Completed in 1937 on the former Matagrifone castle hill, Tempio di Cristo Re commemorates Christ the King and Italy's war dead. An octagonal dome and historicist detailing dominate Messina's skyline above the strait. Memorial plaques and tombs from the World Wars fill parts of the interior.

38.1969° N / 15.5539° E

03 AdmissionFree

Stele della Madonna della Lettera

Erected in 1582 at the harbor mouth, this votive column carries a gilded statue of the Madonna della Lettera, patron of Messina. According to tradition, she exchanged letters with the Virgin of Nazareth. Sailors have long regarded the monument as a landmark when entering the Strait of Messina.
