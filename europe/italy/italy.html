Italy

Rome first united much of the peninsula within an empire whose roads and amphitheaters survived long after political collapse in the west. Medieval communes and Renaissance courts turned Florence and Venice into competing centers, while Siena followed its own civic tradition and the papacy remade Rome. Unification in 1861 created a national state, but fascism and the Second World War ended the monarchy and produced the republic of 1946.
