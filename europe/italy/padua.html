Padua

The university founded in 1222 drew scholars including Galileo and made Padua an intellectual capital of northern Italy. Giotto's Scrovegni Chapel frescoes and the wooden anatomical theatre belong to that scholarly city. Venetian rule later folded Padua into a terraferma network without erasing the university's centrality. Prato della Valle's elliptical square is among Europe's largest, framing later civic life.

Palazzo Bo

Palazzo Bo is a Renaissance palace and home to an elegant anatomy theater, forming part of the city's university. Originally owned by the Papafava family, it was later gifted to a butcher in 1405 for supplying meat during a siege. The building housed an inn before becoming the university in 1539. The name "Palazzo del Bo" persists due to the university's decision to preserve a bull's skull as part of its emblem.
