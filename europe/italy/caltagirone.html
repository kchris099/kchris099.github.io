Explore Caltagirone

A Brief History

Caltagirone has produced pottery since antiquity on a hill between Catania and Gela, with Arab and Norman rulers fostering kilns that supplied tiles across Sicily. The seventeenth-century Staircase of Santa Maria del Monte, lined with decorated risers, links the lower town to the upper after earthquake damage. Baroque churches and ceramic workshops express how a craft town rebuilt in the Val di Noto while keeping majolica at the centre of its economy and civic festivals.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

Staircase of Santa María del Monte

The Staircase of Santa María del Monte, also known as Scalinata di Santa Maria del Monte, is a renowned urban stairway in Caltagirone, Italy. Built in the 1600s and adorned with ceramic tiles in the 1950s, it has become an landmark. The staircase consists of 142 steps, each covered with hand-painted tiles that create a chromatic effect.

Free

Cathedral of Saint Julian

The Cathedral of Saint Julian, also known as Cattedrale di San Giuliano, is a must-visit in the town of Caltagirone. This Norman-origin cathedral boasts an art nouveau facade from the early 20th century and a striking dome covered in light blue tiles. Inside, travellers can admire paintings and effigies that are typical of Italian and Sicilian religious architecture.

Free

Giardino Pubblico

Giardino Pubblico, also known as the Public Garden of Caltagirone, is a 19th-century garden designed by engineer Luigi Spagna and expanded by Michele Fragapane. It features a music stage and panoramic terrace, with manicured avenues leading to a pavilion inspired by Moorish architecture. Adjacent to the Museo della Ceramica, it offers a tranquil retreat from the city center, used for enjoying an ice cream or drink at the park bar.

Free

Public Garden Villa Comunale

Public Garden Villa Comunale in Caltagirone is a garden surrounded by greenery, offering a relaxing escape and an public space for a leisurely walk. The garden, designed by architect Basil in the late 19th century, is considered gardens in Italy. travellers can enjoy the well-kept upper level and take a quick tour on the tourist train to experience its enchanting beauty.
