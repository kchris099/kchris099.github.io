Explore Turin

A Brief History

Turin served as the first capital of unified Italy in 1861 and long capital of the House of Savoy. Baroque squares, royal palaces, and the Egyptian Museum reflect ducal patronage and industrial investment by Fiat and other manufacturers. The Mole Antonelliana and Alpine views mark a city that linked Piedmont to French and Swiss trade routes. Municipal archives, parish records, and surviving architecture help explain how the city connected to wider regional trade routes, court patronage, and modern civic rebuilding after industrial and wartime change.

Porta Palazzo

Porta Palazzo, also known as Piazza della Repubblica, is the largest open-air market in Europe located in Turin. It offers a active mix of Italian and foreign traditions with a wide range of products including produce, flowers, apparel, and art. The market reflects the multiethnic nature of the city and is home to various associations and LGBT groups, it hosts events like La Madre for the LGBTQ+ community and Casa Arcobaleno which offers cultural activities.

Basilica of Superga

Perched atop Superga hill, the Basilica of Superga is a baroque church with a commanding dome that offers views of the Alpine surroundings. Designed by known architect Filippo Juvarra in the early 18th century, this grand basilica replaced an existing church and holds great historical significance as it is the burial site for the royal House of Savoy.

Free

Chiesa di Santa Maria del Monte dei Cappuccini

Chiesa di Santa Maria del Monte dei Cappuccini is a 16th-century church located on the Monte dei Cappuccini, a hill standing at 325 meters high near the Po River in Turin. The hill offers panoramic views of the city, river, and sunsets. It's conveniently close to the historic center and can be reached by crossing the Vittorio Emanuele Bridge.

Free

Chiesa Parrocchiale della Gran Madre di Dio

Located along the banks of the Po River in Turin, the Chiesa Parrocchiale della Gran Madre di Dio is a evidence of neoclassical architecture. Established in 1831, this church was commissioned by local administrators to commemorate King Vittorio Emanuele's return after Napoleon's defeat. Its design draws inspiration from Rome's Pantheon, featuring grand columns and an dome that captures the eye of every passerby.

Free

Sindone Chapel

The Cappella della Sacra Sindone, located within Turin Cathedral in Italy, is a Baroque chapel designed by Guarino Guarini in the 17th century to house the Shroud of Turin, believed to bear the image of Jesus. The chapel's interior has lavish decorations including frescoes and stucco work. Although it suffered damage from a fire in 1997, it is currently undergoing restoration.

La Venaria Reale

La Venaria Reale, located about a forty-five-minute drive from Turin, is an enormous palace estate and part of the UNESCO World Heritage-designated Savoy royal residences. It is one of Italy's most popular museums and has a Baroque masterpiece designed by Filippo Juvarra. The vast structure underwent an ambitious restoration project twenty years ago, making it the largest of its kind in Europe.

Allianz Stadium

Allianz Stadium, home to Juventus FC, is a modern all-seater stadium with a capacity of 41,000. It offers guided tours and houses a club museum for sports enthusiasts to explore the team's history. Conveniently located just 9 km from the Allianz Juventus Stadium, it is easily accessible from the nearest airport and Porta Susa station. The stadium is a must-visit for football fans, offering an immersive experience through museum visits and guided tours.

Castello Reale di Moncalieri

Castello Reale di Moncalieri is a visitor site in Turin, Italy. Castello Reale di Moncalieri is a historic hilltop palace in Moncalieri, Piedmont, Italy. Originally built as a fortress by Thomas of Savoy in the 12th century, it later became a ducal home and a favorite residence of Vittorio Amedeo III and Vittorio Emanuele. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Santuario della Consolata

Located Turin, the Santuario della Consolata is a evidence of Baroque artistry and rich history. This Marian sanctuary, also known as the Church of the Virgin of Consolation, is one of the city's oldest churches, dating back to its origins in the 5th century. Visitors are greeted by an classical portico that leads into a interior adorned with polychrome marble.

Free

Piazza Castello

Piazza Castello is a central square in Turin surrounded by historic buildings such as the Royal Palace, Palazzo Madama, and the Royal Theatre. It is a hub for cultural events and gatherings. The site forms part of Piazza Castello's documented civic and cultural landscape within Turin, with archives and visitor information available from municipal or regional heritage authorities.

€10

Palazzo Madama

Palazzo Madama on Piazza Castello was a Roman gate, medieval fortress, and later Savoy royal residence before becoming Turin's city museum of ancient and medieval art. Its baroque facade by Juvarra contrasts with the medieval tower behind it. Collections range from Roman sculpture to Renaissance painting and decorative arts displayed across four floors.

Free

Chiesa di San Lorenzo

Real Chiesa di San Lorenzo is a octagonal Catholic church built in 1687, featuring baroque architecture, frescoes, statues, and a dome. It was originally conceived as a chapel for Palazzo Reale in the central Piazza Castello and designed by Guarini. The church's style reflects the influence of Borromini and showcases Guarini's innovative approach to form.

Free

Teatro Regio di Torino

Teatro Regio di Torino is a grand opera house located in the city, staging full-scale productions from October to June. The building is part of the historic Piazza Castello and shares its space with other significant landmarks such as Biblioteca Reale and Armeria Reale. The opera house has a rich history dating back to the 18th century and offers a cultural experience that reflects the active soul of Torino.

€18

Mole Antonelliana

The National Museum of Cinema is located in the Mole Antonelliana, a 19th-century building that is a symbol of Turin. The museum offers an extensive collection showing the evolution of cinema, from pre-cinematic optical equipment to modern films and memorabilia. Visitors can explore different floors to learn about the history and techniques of filmmaking, view various sets, and engage in interactive experiences such as 3D simulations and reenacting movie scenes.

€8

Museo della Radio e della Televisione Rai

The Museum of Radio and Television RAI, located in Turin, offers a fascinating journey through the history of Italian radio and TV. Despite its small size, the museum has an collection of vintage TV and radio equipment from different eras. Visitors can explore interactive exhibits that showcase the evolution of communication and television technology. The museum is recommended for families, young kids, groups of friends, or solo travelers.

Free

Casa Scaccabarozzi

Casa Scaccabarozzi, nicknamed the Fetta di Polenta for its narrow wedge shape, is a five-storey residential block on Corso San Maurizio designed by Alessandro Antonelli in 1840. The facade is only about one metre wide at one end, creating one of Turin's most unusual street silhouettes. The polychrome townhouse remains a private landmark of the Risorgimento-era city centre.

Free

Mastio della Cittadella

The Mastio della Cittadella is the pentagonal brick keep at the centre of Turin's Parco della Citadella, built in the 1730s as part of the Savoy arsenal complex. The tower once guarded munitions workshops and barracks; it now anchors public gardens with the park zoo and lawns spreading around the former fortress ditch.

Free

Piazza Solferino

Piazza Solferino is a tree-lined square in central Turin named for the 1859 battle and framed by 19th-century apartment blocks. The Alfieri monument and nearby cafés make it a regular meeting point between Porta Nuova station and the Po riverside. Trams cross the square on routes linking the historic centre with the San Salvario district.

Free

Giardini Reali di Torino

The Giardini Reali di Torino are the Royal Gardens of Turin, offering lush greenery, fountains, and a peaceful escape from the city's hustle and bustle. The site forms part of Giardini Reali di Torino's documented civic and cultural landscape within Turin, with archives and visitor information available from municipal or regional heritage authorities.

Galleria Subalpina

Galleria Subalpina, located at the end of Via Roma in Turin, is a glass-roofed shopping arcade that exudes opulence and charm. Designed in the Art Nouveau style by Pietro Carrera, it has detailed ironwork and decorative mosaics, creating a active atmosphere.

Free

Villa della Regina

Villa della Regina is a 17th-century royal palace located on the hills surrounding Turin. It was originally built as a vineyard and gardens complex for Prince-Cardinal Maurice of Savoy, but later expanded and adorned by Luisa Ludovica of Savoy, who made it her personal residence. The villa has been home to several Savoy queens over many years. Its Baroque beauty is complemented by frescoes, paintings, and Chinese cabinets.
