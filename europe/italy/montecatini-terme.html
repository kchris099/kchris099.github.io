Montecatini Terme

Montecatini Terme grew in the eighteenth and nineteenth centuries around thermal springs that Grand Duke Leopold of Tuscany promoted as a regulated spa destination on the road between Florence and Lucca. Liberty-style bath pavilions, hotels, and the funicular to the upper medieval castle formed a planned resort for bourgeois and later international guests. While surrounding marshland was drained for agriculture, the spa quarter kept its arcades and park avenues as Tuscany's principal belle-époque watering place.

Terme Tettuccio

Arcades, fountains, decorated halls and mature gardens form the monumental setting of Terme Tettuccio in Montecatini Terme. The complex grew from an eighteenth-century spa and was extensively rebuilt in the early twentieth century by Ugo Giovannozzi; drinking halls once organized therapeutic use of local mineral waters, while ceramics, murals and colonnades record the town's international spa culture.

Stazione Funicolare di Montecatini Terme

Red carriages climb a steep single-track line between Montecatini Terme and the hill village of Montecatini Alto, and service is seasonal and timetable-dependent. Opened in 1898, the funicular retains passing tracks and historic station buildings while covering the elevation change in a short journey.

Nuovo Teatro Verdi Srl

Teatro Verdi stands on Montecatini Terme's Viale Verdi near the spa gardens, continuing a performance tradition that began in the nineteenth century. Renovations have adapted the auditorium for concerts, drama, musicals, comedy, and touring productions. Nuovo Teatro Verdi Srl is the operating company, while access to the building depends on scheduled performances.

Borgo di Montecatini Alto

In the hills, Borgo di Montecatini Alto is a destination that feels like stepping into a fairytale. Once the seat of the municipality until 1905, this enchanting village has historical ruins and artifacts that transport back in time. A ride on the funicular railway offers views as ascend to this locale.

Zoo of Pistoia

The Zoo of Pistoia is a venerable zoo and amusement park that houses over 600 animals, including mammals, birds, reptiles, and amphibians. The environment aims to closely replicate the natural habitats of the animals. Travellers can encounter various species such as brown bears, lynx, tigers, lions, giraffes, colorful parrots, rare lemurs from Madagascar and other endangered species from around the world.

Piazza della Sala

Six days a week a produce market fills the square with fruit and vegetables from nearby growers; Sundays stay clear. Surrounding facades and open pavement keep the space usable when stalls are gone. The market rhythm makes this the town's everyday civic floor rather than a ceremonial plaza.

Shrine Basilica of Our Lady of Humility

This fifteenth-century shrine basilica anchors devotion to Our Lady of Humility in Montecatini Terme's historic fabric. Stone volumes and a measured facade mark it among the town's older worship sites. Outside service hours the exterior and main interior spaces are usually available to walk through.

Museo dello Spedale del Ceppo

In Pistoia's Piazza Giovanni XXIII, the Museo dello Spedale del Ceppo occupies a hospital complex with roots in the thirteenth century and a sixteenth-century loggia. A glazed terracotta frieze of virtues and works of mercy wraps the facade. Exhibits interpret care and charity in the historic institution.

Il Quercione

A broad-crowned ancient oak marks a rural meeting point for walks through olive groves and woodland. Picnic clearings and farm tracks radiate from the trunk into the surrounding terrain. Locals treat the tree as a fixed landmark when describing routes outside the spa-town centre.

Fortezza di Montecarlo

Medieval walls and towers at Montecarlo still show how the fort controlled approaches through this part of Tuscany. Surviving fabric still shows gateways, curtain lines, and elevated walkways without modern reconstruction covering every surface. The site's strategic role in regional contests is the main story told on site.

43.8521° N / 10.6669° E
