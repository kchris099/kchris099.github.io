Explore Matera

A Brief History

Established as a regional settlement beyond an older fortified core, the city developed through industrialization in the late 19th century, post-war reconstruction, and modern civic expansion that reshaped its public spaces, markets, and surrounding landscape. Restored religious buildings, civic buildings, and natural features now define the contemporary visitor experience.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

Cathedral Basilica of Matera “Most Holy Mary of the Bruna”

The Cattedrale di Maria Santissima della Bruna e Sant'Eustachio is a 1200s cathedral with a commanding bell tower and intricate interior decorations. Situated high atop Piazza Duomo, it offers panoramic views of Matera from the ridge between the two sassi districts. The city boasts numerous other remarkable churches, such as Santa Maria di Idris, San Pietro Barisano, and San Pietro Caveoso, each showcasing unique architectural features.

Free

Church of Saint Peter 'Barisano'

The Church of Saint Peter 'Barisano' is a 13th-century church located in the Sassi area of Matera. Despite being plundered in the 1960s and 70s, it still holds historical significance. The church is entirely carved out of rock and features catacombs, making it the largest of the three rock churches in Matera. travellers can purchase a combined ticket to explore this and other churches as well as other casa attractions.

Free

Convent of Saint Agostino

The Convent of Saint Agostino, located at the northern end of the Sassi, was founded in 1592 by the Eremitani monks. The complex suffered damage from an earthquake in 1734 and underwent various uses during the nineteenth century. Today, it houses offices of the Ministry for Cultural Heritage and Activities.

Free

Chiesa di San Giovanni Battista

The Chiesa di San Giovanni Battista, also known as the Church of Saint John the Baptist, is a centuries-old stone church in Matera, Italy. This Gothic and Romanesque architectural gem overlooks a small square and is a prominent religious landmark within Matera's ancient cityscape. travellers can admire its Byzantine architecture and reflect in its simple and peaceful ambiance.

Free

Long Cistern

The "Palombaro Lungo" is a remarkable subterranean cistern located beneath the bustling Piazza Vittorio in Matera. Dating back to the 19th century, this expansive water reserve was designed to collect drinking water for the residents of "Sasso Caveoso." With a depth of 15 meters and a capacity of approximately 5,000 cubic meters, it represents an impressive feat of hydraulic engineering.

Free

Crypt of Original Sin

Crypt of Original Sin is a unique cave church featuring frescoes from the 8th and 9th centuries depicting biblical scenes and angels. Accessing the site can be a bit challenging, with limited lighting and some language barriers for English speakers. travellers have noted that the audio devices for English speakers may not be of high quality, and some paintings are missing or deteriorated. It's recommended to make reservations in advance, as access may require prior arrangements.

Free

Belvedere di Piazza Giovanni Pascoli

Belvedere di Piazza Giovanni Pascoli is a must-visit terrace in the Upper Town of Matera, offering an incredible panoramic view of the cave cityscape. It provides a vantage point to appreciate the scale and expanse of Matera's cave houses. The viewpoint at Piazzetta Pascoli between two districts offers a unique perspective to observe the city's particularities.

Free

Sasso Caveoso

Sasso Caveoso is a in Matera, located in the labyrinthian area of the city. It houses Musma, Italy's most important sculpture museum, showcasing works inside Palazzo Pomarici and excavated chambers. The neighboring Sasso Barisano district and Murgia Materana Park offer additional exploration opportunities.

Free

Chiesa di Santa Lucia alle Malve

Chiesa di Santa Lucia alle Malve is a significant site in Matera, dating back to the eighth century. It was the first female monastic settlement of the Benedictine Order and has played a crucial role in the city's historical and urban development over a millennium. The former monastic complex features entrances leading into internal cavities along the rocky wall.

Free

Church of Saint Mary of Idris

The Church of Saint Mary of Idris is an exceptional Catholic church built into a rocky hilltop. It boasts a exterior and features preserved frescoes in the crypt. Although small and not well-preserved inside, it remains a unique and hauntingly beautiful example of Christian history. travellers should note that photos are not allowed during visits, which cost 3.50 for just a few minutes.
