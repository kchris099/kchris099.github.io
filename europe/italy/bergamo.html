Bergamo

Bergamo divides between a Venetian-walled Città Alta on the hill and a lower town expanded after the fifteenth century along the roads to Milan and the Alps. The Venetian Republic fortified walls, gates, and the Piazza Vecchia when Bergamo guarded the eastern approaches to its mainland territories. Silk mills, music conservatories, and Orio al Serio airport later tied the city to Lombard industry while funiculars still link medieval palaces to modern boulevards below.

Porta San Giacomo

White Zandobbio marble frames Porta San Giacomo, one of the principal gates through Bergamo's sixteenth-century Venetian walls. A stone causeway approaches its arched facade from the lower city, opening directly into Citta Alta, and the gate and surrounding fortifications belong to the transnational listing of Venetian defensive works built between the sixteenth and seventeenth centuries.

02 AdmissionFree

Mura Veneziane

Bergamo's Venetian Walls encircle the upper town in a sixteenth-century defensive circuit built under the Republic of Venice. Bastions and gates such as Porta San Giacomo still define the ridge skyline above the lower city. Walkways along the ramparts give continuous views across the Lombardy plain.

45.7020° N / 9.6615° E

03 AdmissionFree

Bergamo Cathedral

Bergamo Cathedral faces Piazza Duomo beside Santa Maria Maggiore and the Colleoni Chapel. Dedicated to Saint Alexander, the episcopal church took its present form through Renaissance, Baroque and nineteenth-century rebuilding over earlier foundations. A domed crossing, richly furnished chapels and archaeological remains beneath the floor connect the upper city's religious history across several periods.

45.7035° N / 9.6627° E

04 AdmissionFree

Antico Lavatoio

Built in 1881 below Bergamo Alta, the Antico Lavatoio is a public washhouse with a long white marble trough under an iron roof. Water inlets, overflow drains, and spray gutters supported communal laundry work. The small structure survives as a civic utility monument beside the climb toward the Venetian walls.

45.7036° N / 9.6638° E

05 AdmissionFree

Piazza Mercato delle Scarpe

Piazza Mercato delle Scarpe is a square that is the starting point for an enchanting tour. From here, began on a journey to Rocca Castle, offering views of the Lower Town. As continue walk, encounter Piazza Vecchia and Piazza Duomo, where notable landmarks like the Basilica of Santa Maria Maggiore and the Colleoni Chapel await.

06 AdmissionFree

Palazzo e Giardini Moroni

A seventeenth-century palace in Bergamo Alta fills rooms with Baroque architecture, frescoes, and a substantial art collection, with landscaped gardens behind the house. Self-guided routes move through several interiors before opening onto the grounds. The Moroni residence remains a private-collection palace visit above the lower city.

45.7033° N / 9.6678° E

07 AdmissionFree

Porta Nuova

Twin neoclassical buildings define Porta Nuova at the center of modern Bergamo. Erected in the 1830s as customs barriers, the matching propylaea frame a direct view uphill toward the towers and walls of Citta Alta. Streets, bus routes, and pedestrian crossings radiate from the square, making the former gate an orientation point between the railway station and upper town.

45.6943° N / 9.6704° E

08 AdmissionFree

Monument To Partisan

Giacomo Manzu created Bergamo's Monument to the Partisan as a bronze relief dedicated to resistance fighters killed during the Second World War; its placement in a busy civic area connects the memory of anti-fascist struggle with the everyday movement of the modern city. Unveiled in 1977, the work sets restrained human figures against a broad wall near Porta Nuova.

45.6947° N / 9.6699° E

09 AdmissionFree

Gaetano Donizetti Theater

Opera composer Gaetano Donizetti gives his name to Bergamo's principal theater, whose origins reach the late eighteenth century, and rebuilding and later restoration shaped the present auditorium, boxes, stage and neoclassical facade on Piazza Cavour. Opera, drama, concerts and the annual Donizetti festival keep the venue tied to the city's musical history.

45.6953° N / 9.6709° E

10 AdmissionFree (funicular €2)

Castello di San Vigilio

Ruined walls and underground passages crown San Vigilio hill above Bergamo's Citta Alta. The fortress developed from an earlier stronghold and was expanded under Venetian rule to watch routes across the valleys and plain. A funicular reaches the nearby village, after which paths lead around the surviving ramparts and viewpoints.

45.7092° N / 9.6510° E
