Como

Como sits at the southern tip of its lake where Romans founded Novum Comum and Julius Caesar settled colonists on the Alpine road to Raetia. Medieval commune wars and silk weaving, revived in the nineteenth century, shaped lakeside villas and rationalist architecture. Funiculars and steamer routes connected the city to Bellagio and the Swiss border; cathedral spires, rationalist monuments, and waterfront promenades still frame Lombardy's mountain trade.

I Giardini di Villa Melzi

The gardens of Villa Melzi d'Eril line the Bellagio shore of Lake Como around a nineteenth-century neoclassical villa. Paths pass exotic plantings, statues, and lakefront terraces with views across the water. The estate remains a horticultural stop on the central lake circuit between ferry landings.

San Giovanni

San Giovanni is an annual celebration that takes place on the last weekend of June at Lake Como. This event marks the beginning of summer and is rooted in the history of Isola Comacina and the Lario region. It commemorates the assault on the island during battles with Barbarossa, offering a reenactment of this historical event. The festival is a significant cultural experience that brings together locals and travellers to celebrate and honor this historical legacy.

Bellagio

Bellagio occupies the promontory where Lake Como divides into its Como and Lecco branches. Stone lanes climb between the ferry landing, parish church, gardens, and residential terraces, while boats connect the town with both shores. Its strategic position has shaped the settlement's long history, and the lake junction is easier to understand from the northern tip than from the commercial center.

Menaggio

Menaggio occupies the western shore of Lake Como in Lombardy, facing Bellagio across the central basin. A lakeside promenade, ferry pier, and climbing streets organize the town between water and hillside. Walkers use it as a base for lake ferries and roads toward Switzerland and the upper lake.

Castello di Vezio

Castello di Vezio stands on a promontory above Varenna as a twelfth-century military outpost overlooking the middle of Lake Como. The main tower and garden terrace look down on the village roofs and ferry lanes. Falconry displays and museum corners occupy parts of the restored fortification.

Villa Monastero garden

On Lake Como, the estate's House Museum shows nineteenth-century decor across fourteen furnished rooms, while garden paths and shade structure outdoor walks. Travellers combine interiors with lakeside plantings. The villa grounds sit near Castello di Vezio routes as a house-and-garden stop rather than a public city park alone.

Villa del Balbianello

Cardinal Angelo Maria Durini began this eighteenth-century villa at Lenno; later owners, including explorer Guido Monzino, filled it with art and expedition artifacts. A loggia, frescoes, and gardens face Lake Como. Boat or path approaches reach the wooded promontory, and the collection rooms extend the visit beyond the garden terraces.

Punta Spartivento

At Bellagio's northern point, Punta Spartivento looks across the meeting of Lake Como's three principal directions; a small waterfront area faces the northern basin while the Como and Lecco branches separate behind the promontory. Mountain ridges enclose the view, and the level approach from the old town ends at a simple public overlook.

Basilica of St. Giacomo

Bellagio's Basilica of San Giacomo stands near the upper end of the historic center, with Romanesque origins dating to the eleventh and twelfth centuries. A square bell tower rises beside a stone facade, while later interior changes introduced altars, paintings, and devotional objects. The church is an active parish and an architectural record of medieval life on the lake promontory.

Greenway del Lago di Como

Greenway del Lago di Como is a 10 km trail along the western shore of Lake Como, used for leisurely walks and family outings. The route starts in Colonno and ends in Griante, passing through villages like Sala Comacina and Ossuccio. Along the way, hikers can admire baroque churches, traditional Italian architecture, and lakeside gardens.

45.9561° N / 9.1502° E

Life Electric

Daniel Libeskind designed Life Electric as a stainless-steel monument to physicist Alessandro Volta, who was born in Como. Installed at the end of the Diga Foranea pier, the tall curved form reflects water, sky, and the city's lakefront buildings; the walk along the breakwater separates the sculpture from Piazza Cavour and gives it a changing silhouette against the surrounding mountains.

45.8154° N / 9.0803° E

Villa Olmo

Villa Olmo is a grand 18th-century neoclassical villa located on the shores of Lake Como. It was commissioned by Innocenzo Odescalchi and has hosted notable figures such as Napoleon Bonaparte and Giuseppe Garibaldi. The villa's park once featured a famous ancient tree that inspired its name. Travellers can enjoy the lakeside promenade from Villa Geno to Tavernola, passing by villas and taking in views.

45.8190° N / 9.0654° E
