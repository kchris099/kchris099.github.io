Explore Ravenna

A Brief History

Ravenna served as the capital of the Western Roman Empire in the fifth century, a refuge chosen for its defensive marshlands. It subsequently became the capital of the Ostrogothic Kingdom under Theodoric and later the center of Byzantine power in Italy. This unparalleled sequence of late antique and early medieval rule endowed the city with the collection of early Christian mosaics in Europe. Visitors witness this transition from the Roman to the Byzantine world within the glowing interiors of the Basilica of San Vitale, the Mausoleum of Galla Placidia, and Sant'Apollinare Nuovo.

Basilica of San Vitale

Basilica of San Vitale is a religious building in Ravenna, Italy. The transition from naturalistic representations to mosaics with golden backgrounds reflects the ideological and stylistic changes of the time. The exterior and main interior spaces are usually viewable outside service times.

Mausoleo di Galla Placidia

Mausoleo di Galla Placidia is a 5th-century chapel in the shape of a Latin cross, adorned with active Roman mosaics. It is located in the churchyard alongside Basilica di San Vitale and holds three sarcophagi believed to contain the remains of Aelia Galla Placidia, Emperor Constantius III, Emperor Valentinian III or Emperor Onorio.

Battistero Neoniano (o degli Ortodossi)

Battistero Neoniano (o degli Ortodossi), also known as the Orthodox Baptistery, is a religious building in Ravenna, Italy. This ancient monument showcases a significant shift in artistic representation from naturalistic motifs to mosaics with golden backgrounds, symbolizing transcendence and the glory of Christ. The exterior and main interior spaces are usually viewable outside service times.

Basilica di Sant'Apollinare in Classe

Located just outside Ravenna, this basilica is known for its mosaics and is an excellent example of early Christian basilicas in Italy. It remains a notable local point of interest situated in Ravenna, Italy. Visitors can observe the surrounding area and its connection to the city's broader historical landscape. Local guides sometimes include it on walking routes through the centre.

Basilica di Sant'Apollinare Nuovo

Basilica di Sant'Apollinare Nuovo is a religious building in Ravenna, Italy. The mosaics showcase a transition from naturalistic representations to those with golden backgrounds, symbolizing transcendence and the glory of Christ. The exterior and main interior spaces are usually viewable outside service times.

San Giovanni Evangelista, Ravenna

San Giovanni Evangelista, Ravenna is a religious building in Ravenna, Italy. Founded by the Roman Empress Galla Placidia in 425 AD as an expression of gratitude for her safe passage across the Adriatic Sea, this church has witnessed centuries of change. The exterior and main interior spaces are usually viewable outside service times.

Arian Baptistery

Arian Baptistery is a monument in Ravenna, Italy. Battistero degli Ariani is an octagonal structure built by the Ostrogothic ruler Theodoric, now situated about 2 meters below the current ground level. Originally part of a larger complex, this small building still showcases its beauty despite the loss of the rest. The memorial stands in an open plaza or roadside setting.

Mausoleo di Teodorico

Mausoleo di Teodorico is a visitor site in Ravenna. The Mausoleum of Theodoric is a significant historical site in Ravenna, Italy. Built in 520 for the Great Theodoric, it is a circular gothic structure with a monolithic dome. Legend has it that the red porphyry tub on the top floor is where the emperor took his last breath before being struck by lightning and thrown into Mount Etna by a black horse.

Free

Rocca Brancaleone

Rocca Brancaleone is a public park in Ravenna, Italy. Rocca Brancaleone is a historic fortress in the city, now transformed into expansive gardens and a community space. Inside the ancient walls, visitors can enjoy a playground with various toys and ample space for kids to run around. Paths, lawns, and shaded areas make up the main public facilities.

€35

Mirabilandia

Mirabilandia is a public park in Ravenna, Italy. It offers a perfect solution for families with kids and teenagers to have an exciting day out. Paths, lawns, and shaded areas make up the main public facilities. Mirabilandia remains part of the public visitor circuit in Ravenna.

Safari Ravenna

Safari Ravenna is a wildlife sanctuary that has the chance to drive through the park and began on guided train tours while admiring various animals. There are also opportunities to feed the creatures and explore the reptile house. The unique experience of driving through a zoo makes it worth visiting if visitors happen to be in the area. Overall, Safari Ravenna is an excellent destination for animal lovers seeking an adventure with exotic wildlife.
