Ravenna

Ravenna served as the capital of the Western Roman Empire in the fifth century, a refuge chosen for its defensive marshlands. It subsequently became the capital of the Ostrogothic Kingdom under Theodoric and later the center of Byzantine power in Italy. This unparalleled sequence of late antique and early medieval rule endowed the city with the collection of early Christian mosaics in Europe. Visitors witness this transition from the Roman to the Byzantine world within the glowing interiors of the Basilica of San Vitale, the Mausoleum of Galla Placidia, and Sant'Apollinare Nuovo.

Basilica of San Vitale

Inside, mosaic programs shift from more naturalistic figures toward saints and court scenes set on gold grounds, a change that tracks Byzantine taste in Ravenna. The octagonal church remains one of the city's principal early Christian monuments. Outside liturgy, exterior and main spaces are usually open to view.

Mausoleo di Galla Placidia

In the churchyard beside San Vitale, this fifth-century chapel holds three sarcophagi traditionally linked to Aelia Galla Placidia, Constantius III, and Valentinian III or Honorius. Starry blue mosaics cover the vaults above the stone chests. The small volume rewards a slow look at ceiling and walls.

Battistero Neoniano (o degli Ortodossi)

The Neonian Baptistery preserves a fifth-century mosaic of Christ's baptism beneath an octagonal dome in central Ravenna. Apostles, architectural motifs, and symbolic scenes radiate around the composition above marble revetment and stucco. Built beside the former cathedral complex, the small structure belongs to Ravenna's historic early Christian monuments and differs from the Arian Baptistery across town.

Basilica di Sant'Apollinare in Classe

Sant'Apollinare in Classe stands near Ravenna's ancient port area on a cemetery site associated with Saint Apollinaris, an early bishop and patron of the city. Built in the sixth century, the basilica has a long columned nave leading to an apse mosaic of the saint in a green landscape beneath a jeweled cross. A detached cylindrical bell tower and open surroundings reinforce its link with the former port city of Classe.

44.3802° N / 12.2324° E

Basilica di Sant'Apollinare Nuovo

Processions of martyrs and virgins move across the gold mosaic walls of Sant'Apollinare Nuovo in Ravenna. Theodoric the Great built the basilica in the early sixth century for Arian worship, after which Byzantine authorities altered imagery and rededicated it. Palace and harbor scenes, repeated figures, and the long nave preserve visible evidence of that political and religious transition.

San Giovanni Evangelista, Ravenna

Roman empress Galla Placidia founded the church in 425 AD after a safe crossing of the Adriatic, dedicating it in thanks. Later centuries altered fabric while keeping the dedication. Outside service hours the exterior and main interior spaces are usually viewable.

Arian Baptistery

Ostrogothic ruler Theodoric built this octagonal baptistery, now sunk about two metres below modern street level. Once part of a larger Arian complex, the small building alone survives with its mosaic program. It sits in an open setting where the drop to original floor level is obvious.

44.4187° N / 12.2024° E

Mausoleo di Teodorico

Built around 520 for Theodoric the Great, the Mausoleum of Theodoric is a two-story stone rotunda outside Ravenna topped by a single huge monolithic dome. A porphyry basin inside is tied to later legends about the king's death. The structure stands apart from the city's mosaic churches as an Ostrogothic royal tomb.

Rocca Brancaleone

Venetian fortress walls now enclose gardens and a community park rather than a garrison yard. Inside the circuit, a playground and open lawns give children space to run between the old bastions. Paths and shade trees follow the defensive outline through the green interior.

Mirabilandia

Mirabilandia is a large amusement park on the Ravenna plain with roller coasters, flat rides, and themed zones aimed at families. Lakeside paths and show arenas sit among the ride clusters outside the historic city. Seasonal calendars set which attractions and evening events run on a given day.

Safari Ravenna

Near Mirabilandia, Safari Ravenna is a drive-through wildlife park where animals occupy large managed enclosures viewed from private cars or park vehicles. A separate walking area contains smaller species and educational displays. The operator manages the animal collection and visitor routes within a commercial recreation facility centered on captive wildlife.
