Explore Genoa

A Brief History

Genoa was a maritime republic whose merchants and bankers financed expeditions across the Mediterranean from the 11th century onward. Christopher Columbus was born here, and the city's palazzi dei rolli, rebuilt port, and narrow caruggi record republican government, Spanish Habsburg rule, and 19th-century industrial growth along the Ligurian coast.

Porta Soprana

Porta Soprana is a grand gate with two circular towers that served as an entrance to the city and were part of the 12th-century defensive walls in Genoa. It has been restored in the 19th and 20th centuries and is also known as St. Andrea Towers due to its proximity to an ancient convent. Visitors can purchase a combined ticket for access to both the towers and the House of Columbus, which is located nearby.
