Cagliari

Cagliari occupies a hill above the Gulf of Angels that held Phoenician, Roman, and Pisan strongholds before Spanish and Savoyard rule. The Castello quarter, bastions, and cathedral crown the ridge. As capital of Sardinia it gathered administration, a university, and a harbour serving the island's south. Roman amphitheatre remains, salt flats, and modern waterfront districts show how successive Mediterranean powers used the site.

In the historic Castello quarter of Cagliari, Italy stands the Torre dell'Elefante, a 14th-century limestone tower adorned with a sculpted elephant. Closed for renovation works for some time, it has now reopened to the public. Built as part of the city's medieval defenses by the Pisans, it gives visitors memorable views over the port and sea. The Cattedrale di Santa Maria Assunta e Santa Cecilia is a 13th-century cathedral located in Cagliari, Sardinia.
