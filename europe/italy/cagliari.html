Explore Cagliari

A Brief History

Established as a regional settlement beyond an older fortified core, the city developed through industrialization in the late 19th century, post-war reconstruction, and modern civic expansion that reshaped its public spaces, markets, and surrounding landscape. Restored religious buildings, civic buildings, and natural features now define the contemporary visitor experience.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

Bastione di Saint Remy

Bastione di Saint Remy is a significant structure featuring a limestone arch, pillars, terrace, and promenade for cultural events. It is located in the districts of Marina, Stampace, and Villanova. travellers can explore various squares and outdoor areas for traditional celebrations. One can start their Cagliari tour by ascending the marble steps of Passeggiata Coperta or taking the free elevator from Piazza della Costituzione to reach Bastione San Remy.

Free

Torre dell'Elefante

In the historic Castello quarter of Cagliari, Italy stands the impressive Torre dell'Elefante, a 14th-century limestone tower adorned with a sculpted elephant. Closed for renovation works for some time, it has now reopened to the public. Built as part of the city's medieval defenses by the Pisans, it offers travellers memorable views over the port and sea. The tower is an important piece of medieval engineering and provides a glimpse into Cagliari's rich history.

Free

Cattedrale di Santa Maria Assunta e Santa Cecilia

The Cattedrale di Santa Maria Assunta e Santa Cecilia is a 13th-century cathedral located in Cagliari, Sardinia. Originally built as a church dedicated to Santa Cecilia, it was later elevated to the rank of cathedral during the Aragonese domination. The cathedral boasts a graceful Gothic exterior and a lavish interior adorned with baroque decor. Inside, travellers can explore the vast marble pulpit and crypt containing royal tombs.

Free

Palazzo Regio

Palazzo Regio, also known as Casteddu e Susu, is a grand palace with a rich history as the former royal residence in Sardinia. Over the centuries, it has witnessed various dominations and influences, leaving an indelible mark on its architectural structure and cultural significance. In the old town within the il Castello complex, this opulent palace now hosts exhibitions, concerts, and public visits.

Free

Porta Cristina

Porta Cristina is a historic gateway to the Castello district in Cagliari. Built in 1825, it replaced the Gate of Soccorso and was named after Queen Maria Cristina of Bourbon. The area offers open spaces and is a major crossroad for drivers, yet it remains relatively uncrowded. It serves as a landmark for navigating the city and provides access to various attractions such as St. Pancratius Tower, Piazza Palazzo, Cathedral S.

Free

Basilica di San Saturnino

Nestled in the heart of Cagliari, the Basilica di San Saturnino is a remarkable testament to early Christian architecture, dating back to the 5th century. This church features an impressive domed roof and apse that tower majestically above its surroundings. Dedicated to Saint Saturninus, the city's patron saint who met his martyrdom during Diocletian's reign, this basilica offers travellers a glimpse into the rich history of Christianity in Sardinia.

Free

Civic Market of San Benedetto

The Civic Market of San Benedetto is a bustling and vibrant covered food market located in Cagliari, Italy. It is renowned as the largest covered market in Italy and Europe, spanning two stories and 8,000 square meters. The market features separate departments for produce, fish, meat, and more.

Free

Parco della Musica

Parco della Musica is a delightful urban oasis that beautifully blends modern design with natural elements. Inaugurated in 2011, this park features expansive open spaces, innovative playgrounds, and scenic pathways lined with trees. It serves as a vibrant gathering spot for families and locals alike, often hosting concerts and cultural events during the warmer months.

Free

Santuario di Nostra Signora di Bonaria

Located on a hill southeast of the city, Santuario di Nostra Signora di Bonaria is a revered religious complex with a storied history. The sanctuary houses a venerated statue of the Virgin Mary and Christ, which is said to have miraculously saved a shipwrecked crew during a storm in 1307.

Free

Marina di Bonaria

Marina di Bonaria - Su Siccu is a promenade located at the foot of the Basilica of Bonaria in Cagliari. It's well-maintained, equipped with benches, street lamps, and a cycle path, making it perfect for leisurely walks and outdoor activities. The area is also popular for amateur fishing and offers beautiful views of boats coming from distant places. travellers can enjoy spotting white flamingos and savoring sea urchins in winter.

Free

Parco di San Michele

Parco di San Michele is a picturesque green space located in Cagliari, offering travellers a variety of attractions. The park features jogging trails, lawns, and towering pine trees, creating an ideal setting for outdoor activities. Situated on a hilltop fortress, it provides panoramic views of the city, mountains, and ocean. The park's serene atmosphere makes it a popular spot for picnics and enjoying sunsets.

Free

Fortino di Sant'Ignazio

Fortino di Sant'Ignazio, also known as Forte di Sant'Elia, is a former military fort situated atop the Sant'Elia hill in Cagliari. Offering unobstructed panoramic views of the city and its surroundings, it is renowned as vantage points to witness mesmerizing sunsets.

Free

Tuvixeddu necropolis

Tuvixeddu necropolis, located on a large hillside, is an archaeological site featuring Carthaginian and Roman burial chambers. This ancient burial ground, over 2,500 years old and built by the Phoenicians, showcases unearthed decorations and riches from the site. The Romans also established their own nearby burial ground.

Free

Basilica di Sant'Elena Imperatrice

The Easter service at 7.30, following a heartfelt confession on the eve, was held in the Basilica di Sant'Elena Imperatrice. The service adhered to strict Anticovid regulations, ensuring social distancing and sanitization of hands even before the Eucharist. The organization of the event was exemplary, with a focus on safety and compliance. Although not overly large in size, the basilica's beauty captivated all who attended.
