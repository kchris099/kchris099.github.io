Explore Pisa

A Brief History

Pisa was a maritime republic by the 11th century, competing in Mediterranean trade before decline after defeat at Meloria in 1284. The cathedral, baptistery, and leaning campanile form the Piazza dei Miracoli, begun in 1063. The university, founded in 1343, remains one of Italy's oldest. Allied bombing in 1944 damaged parts of the city. The cathedral square was inscribed as a UNESCO World Heritage site in 1987.

Tower of Pisa

This freestanding campanile for Pisa Cathedral began in 1173 and took nearly two centuries to finish. Soft subsoil caused the famous lean, already noticeable when work resumed after a pause in the 12th century. Stabilization work in the 1990s reduced the tilt; timed tickets regulate climbs to the upper galleries.

Free (monument complex €20)

Cattedrale di Pisa

Founded in 1064 with spoils from naval campaigns, Pisa's cathedral anchors the Piazza dei Miracoli. Its Romanesque facade combines Byzantine, Islamic, and classical motifs in marble. Inside, a coffered ceiling and pulpit by Giovanni Pisano illustrate the maritime republic's wealth and artistic ambition.

Free

Piazza dei Cavalieri

Medieval Pisa governed from this square before Florentine rule. Cosimo I de' Medici rebuilt it in the 1560s as headquarters for the Knights of Saint Stephen, a military order that fought Ottoman corsairs. The Palazzo della Carovana, designed by Vasari, now houses the Scuola Normale Superiore.

Free

Piazza del Duomo

Piazza del Duomo, also known as Piazza dei Miracoli or Square of Miracles, is a renowned 8.87-hectare area in Pisa, Tuscany. This walled square is a significant center of medieval art and boasts some of the architectural complexes globally. The site is catalogued as a square.

Free

Battistero di San Giovanni

Located in the Square of Miracles, Battistero di San Giovanni is a medieval baptistery that often goes unnoticed next to its towering neighbors. This round building boasts an impressive interior with a baptism font at its center and offers a unique vantage point from its top floor overlooking the cathedral. Constructed in the 12th century in Romanesque style, it features Gothic and Renaissance influences as well.

Free

Chiesa di Santa Caterina d'Alessandria

Chiesa di Santa Caterina d'Alessandria is a 13th-century Gothic-style Catholic church located south of the Arno River. It underwent renovations in the 19th century, resulting in a nave with a gabled roof and plastered walls, except for the lower part of the X and XI century apse. The church is famous for housing the pulpit from which St. Thomas Aquinas preached.

Free

Church of Saint Mary of the Thorn

Chiesa di Santa Maria della Spina is a small Gothic church in Pisa, situated on the southern bank of the river Arno. It was constructed in the 1200s by the Gualandi family and originally named Santa Maria di Pontenovo. The church's title "della Spina" refers to a thorn from Christ's crown of thorns, which was housed there until the 19th century.

Free

Logge dei Banchi

Logge dei Banchi is a historic loggia in Pisa, located near the Ponte di Mezzo. Built in 1605, it used to host markets for silk, wool, money changers' stalls, and cereals. Today, it still serves as a market space, especially during the Christmas season. The loggia offers picturesque views along the Arno River and is a popular spot for locals and tourists alike.

Free

Tuttomondo (Keith Haring)

Tuttomondo, a vibrant masterpiece by the renowned American street artist Keith Haring, is a gem nestled in Pisa. Just a stone's throw from the bustling Corso Italia and near the train station, this mural spans an impressive 180 square meters on the wall of the Church of Sant Antonio Abate. Created in just four days back in 1989, it stands as Haring's final work before his untimely passing.

Free
