Perugia

Etruscan walls and a Roman plateau underlie a medieval commune dominated by warring families and papal governors. The Palazzo dei Priori and underground fortress passages record that turbulent civic politics. Umbrian hilltop power, not coastal trade, explains Perugia's historic form. The Corso and Rocca Paesina tunnels show how papal engineers literally overbuilt the commune.

The Fontana Maggiore

Fontana Maggiore stands in Perugia's main square as a thirteenth-century fountain with two polygonal basins carved by Nicola and Giovanni Pisano. Reliefs show months, zodiac signs, biblical scenes, and civic allegories. The fountain remains the sculptural centerpiece between the cathedral and Palazzo dei Priori.

02 AdmissionFree

Perugia Cathedral

Perugia Cathedral faces Piazza IV Novembre with an unfinished side wall. Dedicated to Saint Lawrence, the church developed mainly from the fourteenth century onward and contains chapels, choir stalls, paintings, and relics associated with the Holy Ring. The Fontana Maggiore and Palazzo dei Priori place it within the city's civic core, not the separate Abbey of San Pietro.

43.1125° N / 12.3893° E

03 AdmissionFree

Church of Saint Michael the Archangel

A circular plan, ring of reused ancient columns, and central dome distinguish Perugia's Church of San Michele Arcangelo. Often dated to the fifth or sixth century, the compact building stands north of the medieval center within a small green area; its early Christian form and continued religious use make it different from the city's later Gothic churches and Baroque chapels.

43.1201° N / 12.3853° E

04 AdmissionFree

Etruscan Arch

The Etruscan Arch, or Porta Augusta, is a travertine city gate from Perugia's ancient walls, later inscribed under Augustus. Massive blocks and a later loggia mark the northern entry to the hill town. The gate still funnels traffic between the historic center and lower modern streets.

43.1145° N / 12.3896° E

05 AdmissionFree

Acquedotto medievale di Perugia

Perugia's medieval aqueduct once carried water from Monte Pacciano to the Fontana Maggiore. After the hydraulic system fell out of use, part of its raised route was converted into a pedestrian walkway between houses and arches. The present Via dell'Acquedotto offers an unusual approach to the historic center.

43.1139° N / 12.3884° E

06 AdmissionFree

Sciri Tower

Torre degli Sciri is a medieval family tower in Perugia to survive nearly to its original height. The square stone shaft belonged to the Sciri household and rose above a neighborhood once crowded with similar defensive residences; restoration has allowed limited scheduled access to upper levels, while the exterior remains visible along Via dei Priori.

43.1126° N / 12.3848° E

07 AdmissionFree

Church of Saint Francis 'al Prato'

San Francesco al Prato began as a major Franciscan church in the thirteenth century but suffered repeated structural problems on unstable ground. Much of its roof and interior were lost before a long restoration adapted the building as an auditorium; the preserved facade and renewed hall now support concerts and public events, while the adjacent square remains part of Perugia's university district.

43.1140° N / 12.3834° E

08 AdmissionFree

Rocca Paolina

After Vatican forces took Perugia, this fortress was raised to assert papal power and reinforce the walls. It stayed under Vatican control until 1860. Today the underground galleries and remaining fabric hold exhibitions on the city's history, turning a former instrument of control into a cultural route through the old bastions.

43.1086° N / 12.3879° E

09 AdmissionFree

Piazza Italia

This elevated civic square opens toward long views over the Umbrian plain and the city's newer districts below. Surrounding nineteenth-century buildings frame the paved floor used for gatherings and everyday crossings. From the edge, Perugia's ridge-top plan becomes clearer than it is from the narrower medieval lanes inland.

43.1090° N / 12.3882° E

10 AdmissionFree

Carducci Gardens

Beside Palazzo Della Provincia, these elevated gardens look across rooftops toward the valley and distant hills. Cypress and lawn paths make the terrace a regular sunset stop. The outlook takes in parts of the historic centre and the wider Umbrian countryside without leaving the upper town.

43.1081° N / 12.3881° E
