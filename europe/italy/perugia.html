Explore Perugia

A Brief History

An influential Etruscan stronghold eventually conquered by Rome, Perugia evolved into a dominant medieval commune. Its formidable civic palazzi and dark subterranean fortresses reflect a turbulent history defined by powerful warring families and frequent papal interventions. This rich historical tapestry established a strong foundation that continues to shape the city's dynamic contemporary identity.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

The Fontana Maggiore

The Fontana Maggiore is a remarkable 13th-century fountain In Perugia, Italy. Commissioned to celebrate the city's freedom, this multifaceted monument features ornate carvings that depict a wide array of events and subjects. Designed with two levels and twenty-five sides, it was adorned with sculptures representing zodiac signs, prophets, saints, monthly rural tasks, Roman history, and biblical and mythological characters.

Free

Perugia Cathedral

Perugia Cathedral, also known as the basilica di San Pietro, is a 15th-century cathedral located in the main square of Perugia. It is part of the Catholic abbey established in 996 and stands on ancient sacred grounds dating back to the Etruscans and Romans. The cathedral features beautiful paintings and frescoes, including a remarkable ceiling adorned with art. Additionally, it houses an adjacent museum displaying artifacts and manuscripts.

Free

Church of Saint Michael the Archangel

Chiesa di San Michele Arcangelo is a centuries-old Catholic church located in Perugia. It has a circular shape, with a dome and an interior adorned with ornate columns. Situated north of the city center, it is believed to be one of the oldest churches in the region, dating back to around the 5th century. The church stands out for its unique architecture and peaceful surroundings, offering travellers a serene place to contemplate and relax.

Free

Etruscan Arch

The Etruscan Arch, also referred to as the Arch of Augustus, stands proudly in Perugia as a remarkable relic from the 3rd century BC. This grand travertine structure serves not only as a city gate but also as an emblem of the city's rich Etruscan heritage. As you approach this monumental archway, you're stepping into history—an era when Perugia was a vital hub within ancient Italy's communication networks.

Free

Acquedotto medievale di Perugia

Step back in time and wander along the Acquedotto medievale di Perugia, an ancient Roman aqueduct that slices through the city. This elevated pathway offers some of the most vistas in Perugia, making it a prime spot for capturing Instagram-worthy photos. The walkway leads to the University buildings and provides a mesmerizing panorama of the city and its surroundings.

Free

Sciri Tower

Sciri Tower, located within the Rocca Paolina in Perugia, is a historical fortress built in 1543 for Pope Paul III. The tower has a rich and sometimes tumultuous history, adding to its allure. travellers can enjoy views from the top of the 42-meter tower, especially during sunset. While entry is free, donations are encouraged to support the maintenance of this historic site.

Free

Church of Saint Francis 'al Prato'

The Church of Saint Francis 'al Prato' is a unique and relaxing square in the medieval town of Perugia. It serves as a favorite spot for students and children to unwind. The church has been transformed into a cultural space, hosting conferences, concerts, and shows during events like Umbria Jazz and the International Journalism Festival. Renovated beautifully, it now offers accessibility for people with disabilities.

Free

Rocca Paolina

Rocca Paolina is a historic fortress in Perugia, Italy, built in the mid-16th century by Pope Paul III. It was constructed as a demonstration of power and to strengthen the city's defensive walls after the Vatican forces occupied Perugia. The fortress stands as a symbol of papal authority and remained under Vatican control until 1860. Today, Rocca Paolina serves as a cultural hub with an exhibition about the city's history.

Free

Piazza Italia

Palazzo del Capitano del Popolo, also known as Palazzo del Bargello, is a 13th-century building located on Piazza Matteotti in Perugia. The palace features a remarkable portal adorned with twisted columns and griffins, as well as a captivating central sculpture of Justice. It served as the seat of the judiciary during the municipal era and seamlessly blends medieval elements with Renaissance motifs.

Free

Carducci Gardens

Carducci Gardens, dedicated to the Nobel Prize-winning poet Giosue Carducci, offer a tranquil and peaceful setting for relaxation. The elevated position provides sweeping views of the rooftop and mountains, making it a popular spot for watching sunsets. With its cypress-covered rolling hills, it's also an ideal location for capturing Instagram-worthy shots. travellers can enjoy views of the valley and parts of Perugia from this garden, which is adjacent to Palazzo Della Provincia.
