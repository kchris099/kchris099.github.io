Explore Perugia

A Brief History

An influential Etruscan stronghold eventually conquered by Rome, Perugia evolved into a dominant medieval commune. Its formidable civic palazzi and dark subterranean fortresses reflect a turbulent history defined by powerful warring families and frequent papal interventions. Museums, markets, and street plans in Perugia still show layers from those periods.

Top Sights & Activities

The Fontana Maggiore

The Fontana Maggiore is a visitor site in Perugia, Italy. Designed with two levels and twenty-five sides, it was adorned with sculptures representing zodiac signs, prophets, saints, monthly rural tasks, Roman history, and biblical and mythological characters. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Free

Perugia Cathedral

Perugia Cathedral, also known as the basilica di San Pietro, is a religious building in Perugia, Italy. Perugia Cathedral, also known as the basilica di San Pietro, is a 15th-century cathedral located in the main square of Perugia. It is part of the Catholic abbey established in 996 and stands on ancient sacred grounds dating back to the Etruscans and Romans. The exterior and main interior spaces are usually viewable outside service times.

Free

Church of Saint Michael the Archangel

Church of Saint Michael the Archangel is a visitor site in Perugia. Chiesa di San Michele Arcangelo is a centuries-old Catholic church located in Perugia. It has a circular shape, with a dome and an interior adorned with ornate columns. Situated north of the city center, it is believed to be one of the oldest churches in the region, dating back to around the 5th century.

Free

Etruscan Arch

Etruscan Arch is a visitor site in Perugia, Italy. This grand travertine structure serves not only as a city gate but also as an emblem of the city's rich Etruscan heritage. As visitors approach this monumental archway, visitors are stepping into history, an era when Perugia was a vital hub within ancient Italy's communication networks. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Free

Acquedotto medievale di Perugia

Step back in time and wander along the Acquedotto medievale di Perugia, an ancient Roman aqueduct that slices through the city. This elevated pathway offers some of the most vistas in Perugia, making it a prime spot for capturing Instagram-worthy photos. The walkway leads to the University buildings and provides a mesmerizing panorama of the city and its surroundings.

Free

Sciri Tower

Sciri Tower is a visitor site in Perugia, Italy. Construction and early use date to around 1543. Sciri Tower, located within the Rocca Paolina in Perugia, is a historical fortress built in 1543 for Pope Paul III. While entry is free, donations are encouraged to support the maintenance of this historic site. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Free

Church of Saint Francis 'al Prato'

The Church of Saint Francis 'al Prato' is a unique and relaxing square in the medieval town of Perugia. It is a favorite spot for students and children to unwind. The church has been transformed into a cultural space, hosting conferences, concerts, and shows during events like Umbria Jazz and the International Journalism Festival. Renovated beautifully, it now offers accessibility for people with disabilities.

Free

Rocca Paolina

Rocca Paolina is a historic fortress in Perugia, Italy, built in the mid-16th century by Pope Paul III. It was constructed as a demonstration of power and to strengthen the city's defensive walls after the Vatican forces occupied Perugia. The fortress is a symbol of papal authority and remained under Vatican control until 1860. Today, Rocca Paolina is a cultural hub with an exhibition about the city's history.

Free

Piazza Italia

Piazza Italia, also known as Palazzo del Bargello, is a public square in Perugia, Italy. Palazzo del Capitano del Popolo, also known as Palazzo del Bargello, is a 13th-century building located on Piazza Matteotti in Perugia. Open pavement and surrounding facades define the visitor setting.

Free

Carducci Gardens

Carducci Gardens is a public park in Perugia, Italy. The elevated position provides sweeping views of the rooftop and mountains, making it a popular spot for watching sunsets. With its cypress-covered rolling hills, it's also an ideal location for capturing Instagram-worthy shots. Travellers can enjoy views of the valley and parts of Perugia from this garden, which is adjacent to Palazzo Della Provincia. Paths, lawns, and shaded areas make up the main public facilities.
