Explore Pienza

A Brief History

Pienza was rebuilt from 1459 when humanist Pope Pius II, born Enea Silvio Piccolomini, transformed his birthplace Corsignano into an ideal Renaissance city above the Orcia valley. Bernardo Rossellino aligned cathedral, papal palace, and municipal buildings around a trapezoidal piazza that embodied classical proportion. The project remained small in scale yet became a reference for planned towns across Italy as older village lanes survived within new civic geometry.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

Cipressi di San Quirico d'Orcia

near the town of San Quirico d'Orcia, the Cipressi di San Quirico d'Orcia is a grove of cypress trees that has become an symbol of the Tuscan landscape. This picturesque site features a contemporary sculpture atop a hill, offering views over the undulating countryside. Historically significant, these trees were reportedly planted for each newborn in nearby villages during a time when many left rural life for urban living.

Free

Chapel Vitaleta

Chapel Vitaleta is a small, secluded church located outside the village of Vitaleta in Tuscany. It is surrounded by cypress trees and offers views of the countryside. The origins of this church are shrouded in mystery, but it has been restored and is now recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Currently undergoing renovation, the area also features an outdoor sculpture exhibition by artist Helidon Xhixha.

Free

Horti Leonini

Horti Leonini is a historic Italian garden located in San Quirico d'Orcia, dating back to 1581. It was originally gifted to Diomede Leoni by Francesco de Medici and has since retained its original layout. Divided into two sections, the garden features both natural and manicured areas, adorned with statues including one of Cosimo III de Medici.

Free

Sanctuary of the Madonna di San Biagio

The Sanctuary of the Madonna di San Biagio is a 16th-century domed church located in Montepulciano, Italy. The church overlooks the picturesque countryside and is surrounded by cypress-lined roads. travellers can take a leisurely walk through Montepulciano, admiring historical palaces, churches, and Renaissance artwork along the main street.

Free

Porta al Prato

Porta al Prato is an ancient city gate in Montepulciano, marking one of the main entrances to the historic center. It's a great starting point for exploring the town's medieval streets and architecture. The site is catalogued as a tourist attraction.

Free

Piazza Pio II

Piazza Pio II is the heart of Pienza, a Renaissance masterpiece envisioned by Pope Pius II. The square features the impressive Duomo and Palazzo Piccolomini, showcasing architectural perfection. travellers can also find the tourist office at Palazzo Vescovile. Nearby, Cattedrale di Santa Maria Assunta and Palazzo Piccolomini stand in all their fifteenth-century glory.

Free

Pienza Cathedral

Pienza Cathedral, also known as the Duomo of Pienza, is a church that combines Gothic and Renaissance architectural elements. The travertine facade is a masterpiece of Renaissance style, featuring three portals and a tympanum with the coat of arms of Pius II. Inside, the cathedral maintains a Gothic influence with three naves and an apse divided into chapels.

Free

Centro Storico Pienza

in the picturesque Tuscan hills, Centro Storico Pienza is a historic village that captivates travellers with its architecture and rich cultural heritage. As wander through its quaint streets, discover churches, intriguing museums, and delightful shops. The centerpiece of this enchanting town is the Palazzio Pio, where Pope Pius once resided, offering a glimpse into its storied past.

Free

Agriturismo Poderuccio

Agriturismo Poderuccio offers an dining experience with excellent service. Guests rave about the delicious and beautifully seasoned salads, exquisite main courses, and excellent wine selection. The view of the Tuscan mountainside adds to the overall ambiance. travellers also praise the location of the apartments, which are within walking distance to Pienza and offer a view.

Free

Corsignano

Corsignano is a 12th-century Romanesque stone church located just outside Pienza in the Val d'Orcia region of Italy. This ancient parish church, dating back to the year 714, is considered the primordial nucleus from which Pienza developed and showcases exemplary Romanesque art with its decorated doorways, cylindrical bell tower, and intricate sculptures. Notably, it is where popes Pius II and Pius III were baptized.
