Destination Guide — Northern Italy

Explore Ferrara

A Brief History

Established as a regional settlement beyond an older fortified core, the city developed through industrialization in the late 19th century, post-war reconstruction, and modern civic expansion that reshaped its public spaces, markets, and surrounding landscape. Restored religious buildings, civic buildings, and natural features now define the contemporary visitor experience.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

Ferrara Cathedral

The Cathedral of Ferrara, also known as the Basilica Cattedrale di San Giorgio or Duomo di Ferrara, is a 12th-century structure located in Piazza della Cattedrale. Its ornate three-tiered marble facade showcases a blend of Romanesque, Gothic, Renaissance, and Baroque architectural styles. The cathedral houses a baroque interior and is connected to the Archbishops Palace by a secret passage.

Free

Estense Castle

Estense Castle, also known as the Castle of San Michele, is a grand 14th-century moated castle in Ferrara, Italy. Originally built for political and military purposes, it later became the residence of the Este family. The castle boasts four impressive towers and holds over six centuries of history within its walls. travellers can explore lavish private chambers, a chapel, prison, and a museum showcasing precious artworks.

Free

Teatro Comunale di Ferrara

Teatro Comunale di Ferrara, also known as Claudio Abbado Theatre, is an opera house with a rich history. Built between 1786 and 1797, it exudes the charm of old-style concert venues. The seating mainly consists of boxes rising around the stage, providing a unique viewing experience. While not the most comfortable for those in the second row of a box, the theater's architecture is undeniably beautiful and makes for concert backdrops.

Free

Acquedotto di Ferrara

The Acquedotto di Ferrara, built in 1932, is a majestic Italian monument located in the beautiful Rione Giardino area of Ferrara. Surrounded by gardens and near the stadium and Po di Volano river, it offers ample space for children to play. The area also features a monument and an ancient church nearby.

Free

Stadio Paolo Mazza

Stadio Paolo Mazza is the home of the football team in Ferrara, offering a modern and beautiful setting for sports enthusiasts. Despite some stands being temporarily closed due to legal and safety issues, the stadium provides an outstanding experience for Serie C matches. The atmosphere during games is described as electrifying, with fans eagerly returning after a long absence.

Free

Piazza Ariostea

Piazza Ariostea is a charming square in Ferrara, Italy, featuring a walking track, lawns, and a monument dedicated to poet Ludovico Ariosto. The square hosts various events throughout the year, such as the Palio di San Giorgio - the oldest in the world - where participants in Renaissance costumes parade and compete in games between eight contrade. Another highlight is the Ferrara Buskers Festival at the end of August, which fills the streets with music and color.

Free

Palazzo dei Diamanti

Palazzo dei Diamanti is a Renaissance palace in Ferrara, Italy, known for its diamond-shaped marble facade. It houses the renowned National Picture Gallery and is considered an architectural jewel of downtown. The historic center of the city is like an open-air museum with medieval and Renaissance buildings such as the sumptuous Estense Castle and Palazzo Schifanoia.

Free

Chiesa di San Cristoforo alla Certosa

Chiesa di San Cristoforo alla Certosa is a terracotta church located outside the city center of Ferrara, easily accessible by foot or bus. Construction began in 1498 and was completed in 1551 under the supervision of Bagio Rosetti. The church is a despite being a historical and religious monument.

Free

Mura di Ferrara

The Mura di Ferrara, also known as the Walls of Ferrara, is a 9-kilometer stretch of 16th-century city walls that encircle the historic center. These medieval defensive structures feature earthworks, moats, city gates, bastions, and towers. Starting from Castello Estense, travellers can explore this impressive site on foot or by bike.

Free

Basilica Santa Maria in Vado

Basilica Santa Maria in Vado is a renowned church located in the northern quarter of Ferrara, known as the 'Herculean Addition'. It was developed by Ercole d'Este in the 15th century and is home to the Pinacoteca Nazionale di Ferrara. The church gained fame due to a miracle that occurred on Easter day in 1171. Its richly decorated interior showcases Renaissance and Baroque influences.
