Ferrara

Este dukes between the fourteenth and sixteenth centuries walled Ferrara, raised Castello Estense, and planned a Renaissance extension north of the medieval core. Poets and painters worked at that court; papal rule followed the dynasty. Brick palaces and bicycle-filled streets on the Po plain are the everyday residue of the ducal capital. The diamond-shaped walls of the Addizione Erculea remain a textbook of Renaissance planning.

Ferrara Cathedral

Ferrara Cathedral faces the city's central piazza with a Romanesque lower facade, Gothic upper tiers, and an elaborate sculpted portal. Founded in the twelfth century and dedicated to Saint George, the building absorbed substantial changes over later centuries, including a Baroque interior. Its long southern side carries the Loggia dei Merciai, historically lined with shops.

Estense Castle

Also called the Castle of San Michele, this fourteenth-century moated fortress later became the Este family residence, with four towers holding chambers, a chapel, prison, and museum galleries of artworks. Six centuries of court history fill the interiors. Travellers cross the moat into a complete urban castle still central to Ferrara.

Teatro Comunale di Ferrara

Teatro Comunale di Ferrara, also known as Claudio Abbado Theatre, is an opera house with a rich history. Built between 1786 and 1797, it exudes the charm of old-style concert venues. The seating mainly consists of boxes rising around the stage, providing a unique viewing experience. While not the most comfortable for those in the second row of a box, the theater's architecture is undeniably and makes for concert backdrops.

Acquedotto di Ferrara

Ferrara's former aqueduct monument anchors Piazza XXIV Maggio with a circular basin, tall arches, and sculptural groups completed in the early 1930s. Architect Carlo Savonuzzi and sculptor Arrigo Minerbi turned a piece of water infrastructure into a public landmark; lawns and paths now surround the structure in the Giardino district west of the historic center.

Stadio Paolo Mazza

SPAL plays its home matches at Stadio Paolo Mazza, a football ground near Ferrara's old walls and railway corridor. Opened in the twentieth century and repeatedly renovated, the stadium combines covered stands with sections rebuilt for changing league and safety requirements.

Piazza Ariostea

Lawns, a walking track, and a monument to poet Ludovico Ariosto fill the square that hosts the Palio di San Giorgio, with Renaissance costumes and games among eight contrade, and the Ferrara Buskers Festival each August. Event days transform the open green. Quiet weeks leave the monument and paths for everyday local use.

Palazzo dei Diamanti

Diamond-pointed marble blocks cover the Renaissance facade that houses Ferrara's National Picture Gallery. The palace sits among medieval and Renaissance streets near Estense Castle and Palazzo Schifanoia. Art visitors enter for the gallery collections, while the faceted stone exterior remains the building's street signature.

Chiesa di San Cristoforo alla Certosa

Chiesa di San Cristoforo alla Certosa is a terracotta church located outside the city center of Ferrara, easily accessible by foot or bus. Construction began in 1498 and was completed in 1551 under the supervision of Bagio Rosetti. The church is a despite being a historical and religious monument.

09 AdmissionFree

Mura di Ferrara

Ferrara's sixteenth-century walls run about nine kilometers around the historic center, beginning near Castello Estense. Tree-lined ramparts support walking and cycling circuits above the surrounding streets. Gates and bastions mark successive expansions of the Este city plan.

44.8335° N / 11.6131° E

10 AdmissionFree

Basilica Santa Maria in Vado

Santa Maria in Vado is linked to a Eucharistic miracle traditionally dated to Easter 1171, when blood was said to have marked the church vault during Mass. The present Ferrara basilica was largely rebuilt in the late fifteenth and early sixteenth centuries. Renaissance proportions, later decoration, and the small sanctuary associated with the event distinguish it from museums in the nearby Palazzo dei Diamanti.
