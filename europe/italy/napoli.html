Explore Napoli

A Brief History

Naples, or Napoli, was founded as a Greek settlement Neapolis and later became a Roman resort town with nearby Pompeii and Herculaneum. Angevin, Aragonese, and Spanish viceroys ruled the Kingdom of Naples from the Middle Ages to 1860. The historic center contains castles, churches, and underground Roman remains. UNESCO inscribed the centro storico as a World Heritage site in 1995.

Mount Vesuvius

Mount Vesuvius is a visitor site in Napoli. This active somma-stratovolcano rises above the Bay of Naples within a national park. Its eruption in 79 AD buried Pompeii and Herculaneum under ash and pumice. Trails lead to the crater rim from the ticketed entrance at about 1,000 meters; guides explain ongoing monitoring of seismic activity.

Free

Lungomare Mergellina

This waterfront walk links Mergellina harbor to Posillipo along the Gulf of Naples. Once a fishing village, the strip gained villas and cafes in the 19th century. Views extend toward Vesuvius and Capri; small beaches and seafood restaurants line the road beside the marina.

Castel Nuovo

Castel Nuovo is a visitor site in Napoli. Charles I of Anjou began this castle in 1279 as a royal seat beside the port. Known as Maschio Angioino, it later housed Aragonese and Spanish viceroys. Five round towers and a triumphal arch entrance survive; the interior includes a Renaissance chapel and museum rooms on Neapolitan history.

Free

Piazza Dante

Named for Dante Alighieri, whose bronze statue stands at the center, this square was once the market square of Mercatello outside the medieval walls. Port'Alba book stalls open off one side. Students from the nearby university fill the cafes that ring the porticoed space.

Free

Castel dell'Ovo

Ovo Castle, also known as Castel dell'Ovo, is an imposing fortress and former royal residence located in Naples. It is the oldest remaining fortification in the city, dating back to the 6th century BC. The castle's name originates from a legend about Virgil, a known Roman poet who was believed to have placed a magical egg into its foundation. Another version suggests that it was named for its unique shape.

Free

Chiesa del Gesù Nuovo

Chiesa del Gesù Nuovo is a example of Neapolitan Baroque architecture, located the Piazza del Gesu Nuovo. This church, built between 1584 and 1601, has a unique stone facade that stands out with its quirky design reminiscent of Renaissance palaces. Inside, travellers are treated to an opulent display of religious art, including detailed frescoes by known Italian artists like Francesco Solimena.

San Carlo Theatre

San Carlo Theatre, located near the Royal Palace and Galleria Umberto in Naples, is the oldest continuously running public opera venue globally. Commissioned by King Charles III of Naples and restored after a fire in 1816, it has a Neoclassical building with an opulent horseshoe-shaped auditorium and red and gold furnishings. The venue offers guided tours that include visits to foyers, the main hall, and the royal box.

Free

Murale Diego Armando Maradona - Quartieri Spagnoli

The Murale Diego Armando Maradona in the Quartieri Spagnoli district of Naples is a legendary tribute to the footballer. This mural, considered the most famous of all, is an altar for Neapolitans' adoration of Maradona. The square in front of the mural has been transformed into an open-air museum celebrating his legacy, with outdoor shops offering souvenirs and jerseys.

Archaeological Park of Herculaneum

The Archaeological Park of Herculaneum is a site that preserves the remains of the ancient city destroyed by Mount Vesuvius. It is part of a larger group of attractions in Naples and its surroundings. The park offers a unique insight into the daily life and culture of the ancient inhabitants through its excavations and precious artifacts. The Archaeological Museum of Herculaneum holds an collection that further enriches travellers' understanding of this civilization.

Villa Campolieto

Located along the Miglio d'Oro, Villa Campolieto is a 18th-century villa that showcases the grandeur of its time. Originally constructed in 1775 and designed by the known architect Luigi Vanvitelli, this villa has frescoes and remnants of formal gardens that whisper tales of its glorious past. Although it may appear somewhat forlorn today, with empty rooms and a sense of neglect, travellers can still appreciate its staircase and artwork.

Catacombs of San Gaudioso

Catacombs of San Gaudioso is a visitor site in Napoli, Italy. The Catacombe di San Gaudioso is a 3rd-century underground burial site in Naples, Italy. The catacombs are located beneath the Basilica of Santa Maria della Sanita and were used as a burial site for St. Gaudioso, a bishop who lived in Naples until his death in 453 AD. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Underground Naples

Exploring Underground Naples is like stepping into a hidden world beneath the busy streets of the city. This fascinating network, known as Napoli Sotteranea, spans over 40 miles and showcases ancient tunnels that date back to Greek times when they were initially carved out for stone extraction. The Romans later expanded these passageways, transforming them into aqueducts to supply water to the growing population.
