Amalfi Coast

Amalfi was a maritime republic that rivalled Genoa and Pisa in the early Middle Ages, earning wealth from Mediterranean trade. After political decline, terraced lemon villages clung to the cliffs until nineteenth- and twentieth-century elites made the coast a resort. Ravello's villas and Amalfi's cathedral square recall both republic and belle-époque tourism.

Garden of the Fugitives

The Garden of the Fugitives, also known as the Garden of the Skeletons, is a former vineyard in Pompeii where 13 victims of Mount Vesuvius' eruption were found and cast in plaster. The site is named after these plaster casts, which depict the haunting last moments of the victims' lives. Over time, layers of ash and pumice preserved their bodies before they rotted away, leaving voids that were later filled with plaster to create these displays.
