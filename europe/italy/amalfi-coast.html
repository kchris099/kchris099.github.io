Destination Guide — Southern Italy

Explore Amalfi Coast

A Brief History

Once an independent maritime republic that rivaled Genoa and Pisa in the 10th century, the Amalfi Coast derived extensive wealth from Mediterranean trade. Following its medieval decline, the dramatic terraced villages were preserved, eventually drawing global elite tourism. Today, this complex historical evolution remains significantly embedded in the civic architecture and vibrant daily life of the community.

Top Sights & Activities

Amphitheatre of Pompeii

The Amphitheatre of Pompeii is a remarkable ancient Roman ruin that once held up to 20,000 spectators for gladiatorial combats and other performances. This historic site offers visitors a chance to step back in time and experience the reality of living in a Roman settlement. Built around 70 BC, it predates the Colosseum by over a century, making it the oldest known amphitheatre of its kind.

€20

Garden of the Fugitives

The Garden of the Fugitives, also known as the Garden of the Skeletons, is a former vineyard in Pompeii where 13 victims of Mount Vesuvius' eruption were found and cast in plaster. The site is named after these plaster casts, which depict the haunting last moments of the victims' lives. Over time, layers of ash and pumice preserved their bodies before they rotted away, leaving voids that were later filled with plaster to create these poignant displays.

€20

Teatro Grande

The Teatro Grande, also known as the Large Theatre, is a horseshoe-shaped Roman theater in Pompeii that was preserved by the eruption of Mount Vesuvius. With seating for 5,000 spectators and views over Pompeii and Mount Vesuvius, it offered fantastic acoustics for musical performances and plays. Dating back to the Samnite era, this grand structure features a cavea divided into three levels and a velarium to protect spectators from the elements.

€20

The brothel of Pompeii

Lupanare di Pompei, also known as the 'wolfs den,' is a significant site within the ruins of Pompeii. This Roman brothel, consisting of two levels with five small cells on each floor, offers a glimpse into the intimate side of Roman life. Each cell features a stone bed topped with a mat or cushion and an erotic fresco above the door, likely depicting the specialization of the prostitute working in that cell.

€20

Archaeological Park of Pompeii

The Archaeological Park of Pompeii is a remarkable site that preserves the remains of the ancient Roman city, which was buried under ash and lava during the eruption of Mount Vesuvius in 79 AD. The vastness of the excavated ruins showcases the impressive extent of Roman civilization frozen in time. Rediscovered in 1599, detailed archaeological investigations began two centuries later, revealing a wealth of historical treasures.

€20

Casa dei Vettii

The Casa dei Vettii is one of the most famous, luxurious, and well-preserved residences in the ancient Roman city of Pompeii. Owned by two wealthy freedmen, the house is renowned for its exquisite, vibrant frescoes depicting mythological scenes and its beautifully reconstructed peristyle garden. It provides profound insight into the domestic life and art of the Roman elite.

€20

Forum of Pompeii

Foro di Pompei, located in the ancient city of Pompeii, offers a captivating glimpse into Roman life. The well-preserved ruins include temples, municipal buildings, and the basilica at the social and civic center of the town. Buried under volcanic ash from Vesuvius' eruption in AD 79, visitors can wander through Roman streets and explore houses, shops, cafes, amphitheaters, and even a brothel.

€20

Scavi di Pompei

When visiting Scavi di Pompei, it's important to be mindful of the train stations, as some maps may only list them as 'Pompei.' Booking a guide is recommended for a more comprehensive experience, especially considering the vastness of the site. The upper entrance near the train station is convenient for access. The ruins are expansive and not very accessible for those with mobility issues.

€25

Villa of the Mysteries

Villa dei Misteri, a Roman villa dating back to the 2nd century BCE, is located at the northwestern edge of Pompeii. The villa boasts well-preserved and richly decorated frescoes that depict the initiation of a young bride into the mysteries of the cult of Dionysus. These vibrant frescoes, painted in glowing Pompeiian red, are found throughout many rooms, with the ones adorning the triclinium.

€8

Villa Rufolo

Villa Rufolo is a 13th-century villa with Moorish influences, renowned for its cascading gardens and terraces that offer views of the sea. Over the years, it has become a significant tourist attraction, especially after hosting the first editions of Wagner's Concerts in its gardens. The Villa's international reputation grew further when it was acquired by the Provincial Tourist Board of Salerno and opened to the public.

€10

Villa Cimbrone

Villa Cimbrone is an elegant hilltop lodging located in a medieval manor offering luxurious rooms, fine dining, and a pool. The hotel features nineteen suites set in large gardens, each uniquely decorated with coastal views, fireplaces, marble floors, and frescoed ceilings. Guests can enjoy exquisite meals prepared by a Michelin star chef at the onsite restaurant and relax by the swimming pool in the central courtyard.

€10

Villa Cimbrone Gardens

The gardens of Villa Cimbrone in Ravello are among the most dramatic in Italy, perched high on a cliff above the Amalfi Coast. The expansive, meticulously landscaped grounds feature classical statues, temples, and vibrant flower beds. The highlight is the 'Terrace of Infinity,' a dramatic balcony lined with marble busts that offers expansive views of the Mediterranean Sea.

Free

Amalfi Coast

The Amalfi Coast is a renowned stretch of coastline in southern Italy, characterized by its dramatic, sheer cliffs, terraced vineyards, and picturesque pastel-colored fishing villages. The winding coastal road offers expansive views at every turn. The region is famous for its historic maritime republics, production of limoncello, and luxurious, romantic atmosphere.

€3

Cathedral of St Andrew Apostle

The Cathedral of St Andrew Apostle, located in the heart of Amalfi, is a architectural marvel that has undergone numerous renovations since its inception in the 9th century. Originally constructed by Duke Manson in 987, it was built adjacent to an earlier church on the remnants of a temple, symbolizing Amalfi's rise as a maritime power.

Free

Villa Arianna

Villa Arianna is a Roman villa that was buried in the eruption of Mt. Vesuvius in 79 CE. It is one of three villas located outside Herculaneum and Pompei, which visitors can explore today. Although the Bourbons damaged and stole many of the villa's frescoes, there are still partially preserved rooms with beautiful views to be admired.

Free

Cassa Armonica

When exploring the charming town, consider stop by the Cassa Armonica, an exquisite bandstand designed by Eugenio Cosenza in 1901. This Liberty-style masterpiece stands tall and proud, offering a picturesque spot to unwind as you soak in the sunset views. While nearby attractions like the private castle and Villa Quisiana are not open for tours, this pavilion serves as a lovely focal point in the square.

Reservation required

Medieval Castle - Castellammare di Stabia

The Castellammare di Stabia medieval castle, dating back to the 9th century, boasts gardens and panoramic views of the gulf and Mount Vesuvius. Its location provides the vistas in town, making it an ideal spot for enjoying a beautiful sunset after hiking up a flight of stairs.

Free

Chiesa della Santissima Annunziata e Museo diocesano

The Chiesa della Santissima Annunziata is a 14th-century Catholic church with Gothic architecture and a bell tower situated on a cliff overlooking the sea. Its terrace offers views of the sea and town, making it a popular destination for weddings, including those from foreign couples. The church also hosts classical music concerts that enhance its charm and romantic atmosphere, making it panoramic churches in the world.

Free

Fiordo di Furore

The Fiordo di Furore is a striking, narrow coastal gorge on the Amalfi Coast, created by the Schiato stream cutting through the limestone cliffs. A small, hidden beach lies at the base of the fjord, sheltered by an arched stone bridge that carries the coastal highway above. It is a very photographed and unique natural feature of the region.

€7

Grotta dello Smeraldo

Grotta dello Smeraldo, also known as the Emerald Grotto, is a underwater cave located on Italy's Amalfi Coast. Discovered in 1932, this hidden gem offers boat tours that allow visitors to admire its unique blue-green light and marvel at the stalactites, stalagmites, and even an underwater ceramic manger. While it may be less famous than Capri's Blue Grotto, it still impresses with its natural beauty.

Free

Palazzo Mezzacapo Gardens

Palazzo Mezzacapo Gardens, located in the heart of Maiori, is a serene oasis within the town. The gardens feature subterranean waterways that create a unique and refreshing atmosphere, making it an ideal place for relaxation and leisurely strolls. The 18th-century Palazzo and its well-kept grounds offer visitors a glimpse of architecture and tranquility. It's a perfect spot for anyone seeking a peaceful escape or looking to capture some picturesque moments.

Free

Path of the Lemons

Embark on an enchanting journey along the Path of the Lemons, a picturesque hiking trail that connects the towns of Minori and Maiori. This family-friendly route winds through lush lemon groves and offers vistas of mountains and sea. The leisurely walk takes about an hour each way, providing ample opportunities to savor the tranquility of nature while admiring charming villages along the path.

Free

Church of Saint Mary of the Assumption

Chiesa di Santa Maria Assunta, also known as the Church of Santa Maria, is a storied and church located in Positano. The church is dedicated to the Virgin Mary and features a Byzantine icon of her adorning its interior walls. It was once part of a monastery and holds great cultural significance in Italian Catholicism. The church's elegant architecture, including a beautiful tiled dome, adds to the town's picturesque charm.
