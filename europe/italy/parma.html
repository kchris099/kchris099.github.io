Parma

Parma was established as a Roman colony in 183 BC and grew into a significant medieval commune. In the sixteenth century, it became the capital of the Duchy of Parma and Piacenza under the Farnese family, who fostered a golden age of art and architecture. The city is known for its Romanesque cathedral, the octagonal Baptistery, and its deep culinary traditions. Today, Parma is celebrated as a cultural and gastronomic center in the Emilia-Romagna region.
