Explore Parma

A Brief History

Parma was established as a Roman colony in 183 BC and grew into a significant medieval commune. In the sixteenth century, it became the capital of the Duchy of Parma and Piacenza under the Farnese family, who fostered a golden age of art and architecture. The city is renowned for its Romanesque cathedral, the octagonal Baptistery, and its deep culinary traditions. Today, Parma is celebrated as a cultural and gastronomic center in the Emilia-Romagna region.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

Parma Cathedral

Cattedrale di Parma is a remarkable Romanesque cathedral featuring a vast interior adorned with art and crowned by a frescoed cupola. The city of Parma exudes excellence through its charming streets, typical houses, and sunny squares. It boasts sumptuous palaces, fashionable shopping streets, and a ducal legacy that defines the Parmesan style.

Free

Baptistery of Parma

The Baptistery of Parma is a pink-marble octagonal structure adorned with medieval paintings and carvings, serving as a venue for baptisms. When visiting Parma, take the time to explore its historic center, where discover architectural marvels such as Palazzo della Pilotta, Duomo, Basilica of Santa Maria della Steccata, Farnese Theater, and National Gallery.

Free

San Giovanni Evangelista

San Giovanni Evangelista is a Baroque church located in Parma, Italy, known for its frescoed dome painted by Correggio. The complex includes the church, a monastery, and the ancient pharmacy of San Giovanni. The church boasts remarkable frescoes by Correggio, particularly on the dome where he created an illusion of an open sky with apostles resting on clouds and Christ at the center.

Free

Ennio Tardini Stadium

Ennio Tardini Stadium, located on the edge of the city center, offers an opportunity to experience a calcio match with your family. catch Serie B Parma squad in action if you're not ready for a Serie A match. The stadium exudes the flair of big football despite its low number of spectators. While there are some drawbacks like limited refreshments and facilities, the well-trained security staff ensures a safe and enjoyable experience.

Free

Chiesa di San Francesco del Prato

Chiesa di San Francesco del Prato, located in Parma, Italy, is a historic Catholic church with a tranquil ambiance and remarkable architecture. Originally constructed in the 13th century and expanded over time, the church has been an integral part of the area's religious and artistic heritage. Adorned with murals by renowned Italian Renaissance artist Correggio, it offers visitors a glimpse into the region's cultural legacy.

Free

Teatro Regio

The Teatro Regio, a lavish neo-classical venue established in 1829, is an opulent setting adorned with red velvet and gilded accents. It stands as the premier location in Parma for experiencing ballet, opera, and classical concerts. The theater hosts a diverse array of performances including the renowned annual Verdi Festival showcasing classical music.

Free

Piazza della Pace

Piazza della Pace is a large, modern public square located in the center of Parma, adjacent to the monumental Palazzo della Pilotta. The square was extensively redesigned in the late 20th century to feature wide lawns, contemporary fountains, and a monument to the partisan resistance. It serves as a major gathering space and a venue for outdoor cultural events.

Free

Basilica di Santa Maria della Steccata

Basilica di Santa Maria della Steccata is a significant church in Parma, designed in the shape of a Greek cross with an ornate interior and royal crypt. It holds deep connections to Parmigianino, who worked meticulously on its construction. The basilica was built between 1521 and 1539, with Parmigianino initially planned to carry out the entire fresco decoration.

Free

Palazzo della Pilotta

The Palazzo della Pilotta is a 16th-century palace complex in Parma, Italy, which now houses the National Gallery, Teatro Farnese, and two museums. The Galleria Nazionale within the complex is a must-visit for art enthusiasts as it showcases Parmigianino's greatest masterpieces such as the Turkish Slave Girl and A Mystic Marriage of St. Catherine.

Free

Palazzo Pigorini

Palazzo Pigorini is a historic building located in the center of Parma, notable for its elegant 18th-century architecture. The palace now functions as a prominent civic exhibition space, hosting temporary art exhibitions, photography displays, and cultural events. It plays a significant role in the contemporary cultural life of the city.
