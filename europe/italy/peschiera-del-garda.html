Peschiera del Garda

Peschiera del Garda stands where the Mincio leaves Lake Garda, a choke point fortified by Scaligeri, Venetians, and especially the Habsburg Quadrilatero after the Congress of Vienna. Bastions, canals, and barracks turned the town into a garrison guarding routes between Milan and the Tyrol. Unification battles in 1859 and later lakeside tourism reshaped the ramparts, but star-shaped walls and harbour quays still record how military planners treated this waterside crossing.

Frescoes from several medieval periods cover the modest interior of San Pietro in Mavino on Sirmione's northern peninsula. Grotte di Catullo e Museo Archeologico di Sirmione

The Archaeological site of Grotte di Catullo, located on the Sirmione peninsula in northern Italy, is a significant Roman villa dating back to the 1st century BC. Despite its name, it's not a cave but rather the remains of a grand Roman domus. While traditionally associated with the poet Catullus, there's uncertainty about this claim.

Castello di Desenzano del Garda

Desenzano Castle occupies a hill above Lake Garda on the footprint of a medieval defensive enclosure used as refuge for residents, and cultural events use the grounds and access to elevated sections provides views over tiled roofs, the harbor and the lake basin. Ponte Visconteo

Gian Galeazzo Visconti ordered the fortified bridge at Borghetto sul Mincio in the late fourteenth century as part of a defensive system linked with Valeggio's castle. Long walls, towers, and a dam-like crossing cut across the river valley above watermills and the village. The structure is appreciated mainly from surrounding paths and roads.

Porta Verona

Porta Verona opens through Peschiera del Garda's angular Venetian fortifications toward the historic road to Verona. Built in the sixteenth century, the gate combines rusticated stonework, military emblems, and a passage beside canals that helped defend the fortress town. The surrounding walls belong to the historic Venetian Works of Defence and remain integrated with daily traffic.
