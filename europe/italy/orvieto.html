Explore Orvieto

A Brief History

Orvieto was founded by the Etruscans on a dramatic tuff cliff and later became a significant Roman and medieval center. During the Middle Ages, it served as a papal refuge and developed a powerful commune, culminating in the construction of its magnificent Gothic cathedral in the thirteenth century. The city's history is also reflected in its extensive network of underground caves and tunnels. Today, Orvieto is celebrated for its striking topography, historic architecture, and regional wines.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

Orvieto Cathedral

The Duomo di Orvieto is a 1300s cathedral with a gold facade adorned with glittering mosaics. The cathedral is famous for its chapel filled with intricate frescoes. The construction of the cathedral began in 1290 and involved several architects over the years. A museum opposite the Duomo offers an opportunity to explore Italy's significant collection of Etruscan artifacts, making it a must-visit destination for history enthusiasts.

Free

Orvieto Underground

Orvieto Underground is a captivating network of tunnels, caves, and wells located beneath the city of Orvieto in Umbria. Dating back 2.5 to 3 millennia ago, this subterranean world comprises over 1,200 artificial cavities that were rediscovered by local speleologists in the late 1970s. The underground space served various purposes throughout history, including storage areas for wine and olive oil.

Free

Rocca Monaldeschi della Cervara

Rocca Monaldeschi della Cervara is a 15th-century fortress located in the charming village of Bolsena. The fortress, built on ancient Etruscan ruins dating back to 300 BC, houses the Territorial Museum and the Collegiate Church of Santa Cristina, featuring Romanesque architecture with Renaissance embellishments.

Free

Basilica di Santa Cristina

Nestled a bit downhill from the picturesque lakeside, the Basilica di Santa Cristina stands as a testament to centuries of faith and history. This remarkable structure not only houses the medieval shrine dedicated to St. Cristina but also features ancient Roman catacombs that whisper tales of the past. It is here that one can reflect on the Miracle of Bolsena, an event pivotal in establishing the Catholic feast of Corpus Christi.

Free

Torre del Moro

The Torre del Moro in Orvieto is a historic clock tower that offers panoramic views of the city and the surrounding countryside. Adjacent to the tower is the Seven building, once owned by the papacy and now serving as a cultural center. The area around the tower is filled with tile shops, art galleries, and museums, including Museo Claudio Faina, Museo Emilio Greco, and Museo dell'Opera del Duomo.

Free

Palazzo Del Capitano Del Popolo

In the charming town of Orvieto, Italy, stands the historic Palazzo Del Capitano Del Popolo. This 14th-century palace was once an administrative center for the Captain of the People and later served as a mayor's residence. Today, it captivates visitors with its medieval architecture and rich history. The palace is part of a picturesque square, Piazza del Popolo, where bars and restaurants offer a delightful view of this landmark.

Free

Labirinto Di Adriano

The Labirinto Di Adriano is an extensive underground network of caves and tunnels located in the historic center of Orvieto. Discovered and excavated by a local resident, the labyrinth reveals centuries of Etruscan, medieval, and Renaissance history, including ancient wells, cisterns, and workshops. Guided tours offer a fascinating glimpse into the subterranean life of the city.

Free

Pozzo di San Patrizio

Pozzo di San Patrizio, also known as St. Patrick's Well, is a attraction in Orvieto. This 16th-century circular well features a remarkable 248-step spiral staircase for descending into its depths and ascending back up. Legend has it that the well's design was inspired by a cavity where Saint Patrick retreated for prayer, with rumors of access to Purgatory and even glimpses of Paradise.

Free

Rocca Albornoziana, Orvieto

Nestled beside the cable car station and the entrance to St. Patrick's Well, Rocca Albornoziana is a park that beckons visitors with its historical charm and views. Dating back to the 14th century, this fortress was commissioned by Cardinal Egidio Albornoz as a defensive stronghold. Today, it serves as a lush green space where soak in panoramic vistas of Orvieto’s striking tufa hills and the rolling Umbrian countryside below.

Free

Funicolare di Orvieto - Scalo

The Funicolare di Orvieto - Scalo is a convenient and affordable way to reach the top of the historical city of Orvieto. It's easy to use and offers a scenic view as it ascends. The ticket, which costs 1.30 euros, includes a bus ride into town, but many prefer the lovely walk instead. While it runs on a schedule like local transit, some find that it stops running too early in the evening.
