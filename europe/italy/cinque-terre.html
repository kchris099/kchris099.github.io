Destination Guide — Northern Italy

Explore Cinque Terre

A Brief History

Established as a regional settlement beyond an older fortified core, the city developed through industrialization in the late 19th century, post-war reconstruction, and modern civic expansion that reshaped its public spaces, markets, and surrounding landscape. Restored religious buildings, civic buildings, and natural features now define the contemporary visitor experience.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

Parco Nazionale delle Cinque Terre

Parco Nazionale delle Cinque Terre is a coastal gem in Italy, where vibrant fishing villages cling to steep cliffs overlooking the sparkling sea. This UNESCO World Heritage Site offers views and a unique blend of natural beauty and cultural charm. The region is explored on foot, with the famous Cinque Terre Walk being a highlight for travellers.

Free

Manarola viewpoint

Manarola Overlook Viewpoint is a must-visit spot in Cinque Terre, offering panoramic views of the coastal town and its picturesque surroundings. This enchanting village is often considered the most scenic in the national park, with its colorful houses, shops, and restaurants. The viewpoint provides the most beautiful view in all of Cinque Terre, especially near sunset. While it can get quite busy during peak season, the vistas make it worth a visit.

Free

Castello di Riomaggiore

Perched above the sea, Castello di Riomaggiore is a 13th-century stone castle originally built for defense against pirates and invaders. Over the centuries, it was transformed into a residence for noble families. Today, travellers can explore its well-preserved rooms and halls showcasing the village's history. The castle's terrace offers sweeping views of Riomaggiore and its surroundings. Despite being a bit of a climb to reach, the vistas make it worth the effort.

Free

Castello di Vernazza

The Doria Tower castle ruins, believed to have been constructed circa 1000 CE, can be accessed via steep stairs and offer an impressive panoramic vista. While the castle itself may not be particularly noteworthy, travellers are treated to views of the colorful surrounding town. Located along the blue route leading towards Corniglia, this village is a must-visit destination within Cinque Terre. Additionally, travellers can browse various spice and herb shops here.

Free

Chiesa di San Giovanni Battista

The Parrocchia di S. Giovanni Battista is a 14th-century Catholic church located in Cinque Terre, Italy. The Gothic-style facade of the church showcases alternating black and white marble stripes, as well as a large circular window adorned with delicate lace-like details. As one of the oldest churches in the region, it boasts an impressive interior featuring an intricately designed marble floor that leads up to an ornate altar.

Free

Spiaggia Levanto

Spiaggia Levanto is a renowned beach in Cinque Terre, easily accessible and boasting a long, wide seaside area. The beach is surrounded by lush green hills and offers clear waters for swimming. While the entrance to the sea can be rocky, the water is exceptionally clear. travellers can enjoy fresh food in the area and relax with their families while taking in beautiful sea and mountain landscapes. Spiaggia Levanto also provides sunsets and is ideal for leisurely walks even during winter.

Free

Portovenere

Portovenere, a picturesque village nestled along the Ligurian coast of northwestern Italy, is a true gem of the Mediterranean. This charming destination boasts vibrant 12th-century houses that line its harbor, creating an enchanting atmosphere reminiscent of days gone by. The La Palazzata, a row of tall and slender tower houses, stands as a testament to the village's history and was originally built for protection against pirates.

Free

Doria Castle

Doria Castle stands as a magnificent remnant of history, perched high above the sea and offering panoramic views. travellers can stroll along the ancient carrugio, which connects the castle to the village through charming craft shops and delightful taverns. This 12th-century path leads to notable sites like the Romanesque Sanctuary of Madonna Bianca and culminates at Doria Castle itself, one of Genoa's most impressive defensive structures.

Free

Grotta di Lord Byron

Grotta di Lord Byron is a rocky cove named after the famous English poet who found inspiration in its waters. The area, once visited by Lord Byron and Percy B. Shelly, offers natural beauty and an opportunity for swimming. Although there's no designated entry point into the water and the rocks can be covered with algae and moss, it's still a great experience for those who approach carefully.

Free

Parco Naturale Regionale di Porto Venere

Porto Venere Regional Natural Park is a wild and rugged island reserve that boasts several hiking trails, quaint village restaurants, and the remnants of a 19th-century fort. The blue-green sea surrounds it, adding to the park's beauty. A small church overlooking the tranquil sea provides a picturesque location for weddings or peaceful contemplation.
