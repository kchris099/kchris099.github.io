Prizren

Prizren is a historical city situated in southern Kosovo, located on the banks of the Bistrica river at the foot of the Šar Mountains. Throughout its history, the city has been a vital administrative and trade center under the Byzantine, Serbian, and Ottoman empires. The varied periods of rule resulted in an architectural landscape that includes medieval fortresses, Orthodox churches, and Ottoman-era mosques. Prizren also played a central role in the nineteenth-century Albanian national awakening.

Prizren Fortress

Located atop a hill, Prizren Fortress, or Kalaja e Prizrenit, is a evidence of the rich history of this active town in Kosovo. Once the the Serbian Empire, this medieval fortress offers panoramic views that capture the essence of Prizren's multicultural history. The town is home to a harmonious blend of Albanians, Serbs, and Turks who coexist amidst religious architecture.

Sinan Pasha Mosque

Sinan Pasha Mosque is a religious building in Prizren, Kosovo. It opened or was completed around 1615. Completed in 1615 by Sofi Sinan Pasha, this mosque is not just a place of worship but also an essential part of the city's cultural fabric. The exterior and main interior spaces are usually viewable outside service times.

Albanian League of Prizren

The Albanian League of Prizren, also known as Muzeu Lidhja Shqiptare e Prizrenit, is considered the birthplace of modern Albanian nationalism. The museum complex is housed in the buildings where the league met and showcases paintings and sculptures depicting the Albanian national struggle.
