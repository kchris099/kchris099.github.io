Destination Guide — Eger

Explore Eger

A Brief History

Founded by King Stephen I in the tenth century as an episcopal see, Eger became a flourishing center of Renaissance and Baroque culture. The city is most famous for its heroic 1552 defense against a massive Ottoman army, though it eventually fell and endured nearly a century of Turkish rule, which left behind minarets and thermal baths. Following the Habsburg reconquest, returning bishops rebuilt the city in an ornate Baroque style. This resilient history is reflected in the medieval Eger Castle, the northernmost Ottoman minaret, and the grand minorite churches.

Top Sights & Activities

HUF 1,000

Kethüda Minaret of Eger

The Kethüda Minaret of Eger, also known as the Eger Minaret, is a historic tower that served as part of a mosque during the Ottoman Empire's rule. It stands as one of the oldest surviving minarets in Europe, dating back to the 17th century. Kethüda Minaret of Eger is catalogued as a historical landmark within the historic quarters of Eger.

Free

Dobó István Square

Dobó István Square, located in the heart of Eger, is a vibrant and lively destination that offers a mix of history, culture, and entertainment. Named after the renowned Hungarian warrior Istvan Dobo, who defended Eger against the Ottoman armies in 1552, this square is a bustling hub with outdoor cafes, a summer fountain, and the Baroque Church of Anthony of Padua. It's a popular social spot for locals and visitors alike.

Free

Church of Anthony of Padua

Nestled in the vibrant heart of Eger's old town, the Church of Anthony of Padua, also known as the Minorite Church, is a example of Baroque architecture that captivates visitors with its grandeur. Completed in 1771 by the talented Bohemian architect Kilian Ignaz Dientzenhofer, this church boasts an impressive curved facade and two striking towers that enhance its visual appeal.

HUF 6,000

Castle of Eger

The Castle of Eger, originally built in the 13th century to defend against Turkish invasion, now houses a history museum. Visitors can explore the renovated dungeons, outer Esplanade, and armoury while imagining the historical figures who once walked its grounds. Nearby attractions include Geza Gardonyis former house turned museum and the city center with its Archbishops Palace and Basilica.

Free

Cathedral Basilica of St. John the Apostle, Eger

The Cathedral Basilica of St. John the Apostle in Eger is a neoclassical Catholic basilica that was built in 1836. Its soaring 40-meter dome and seasonal organ concerts make it a attraction. The basilica's imposing dome and towering spires dominate the city skyline, showcasing its Baroque design masterpiece. Inside, visitors are treated to beautiful paintings, elaborate altars, and towering columns that evoke awe and wonder.

HUF 3,500

Eger Archiepiscopal Palace Turistical and Visitor Centre

The Eger Archiepiscopal Palace Turistical and Visitor Centre is a 18th-century Baroque residence that offers visitors a fascinating glimpse into the lives of bishops and archbishops who have influenced this historic town. Nestled next to the Basilica, this palace serves as both a museum and visitor center, showcasing an impressive collection of religious art, decorative items, and historical artifacts.

HUF 3,900

City Under The City

Discover the hidden world beneath Eger, Hungary with a visit to the City Under The City. This unique attraction offers guided tours through the expansive cellar network located beneath the town square, featuring impressive volcanic stone and cement arches. Learn about the history of this subterranean space, originally built by Bishop Gyorgy Fenessy in the late 17th century as an alternative residence to Eger Castle.

Free

Valley of the Beautiful Woman

The Valley of the Beautiful Woman, also known as Szepasszonyvolgy, is a charming and picturesque small valley located in one of the country's top wine regions. It is nestled within the Bermuda Triangle of Matra-Eger-Bukk and boasts a wide range of vineyards that contribute to its rich winemaking tradition. The area is steeped in history, with Turkish memories, Baroque splendor, and Hungarian gulags adding to its allure.

HUF 4,900

Turkish Bath

When in Eger, a visit to the Turkish Bathhouse is a must for those interested in immersing themselves in the city's rich history. This relic from the Ottoman era offers a glimpse into the lavishness and architectural brilliance of Turkish design. The Torok Furdo, built at the beginning of the 17th century, is the only surviving bathhouse from that period and has been renovated while still maintaining its original Turkish-style pools.

Free

Szmrecsányi Lajos Kert

Szmrecsányi Lajos Kert is a known location in Eger, Hungary. It remains a notable local point of interest situated in Eger. Visitors can observe the surrounding area and its connection to the city's broader historical landscape. Local guides sometimes include it on walking routes through the centre.
