Eger

Founded by King Stephen I in the tenth century as an episcopal see, Eger became a flourishing center of Renaissance and Baroque culture. The city is most known for heroic 1552 defense against a massive Ottoman army, though it eventually fell and endured nearly a century of Turkish rule, which left behind minarets and thermal baths. Following the Habsburg reconquest, returning bishops rebuilt the city in an ornate Baroque style. This resilient history is reflected in the medieval Eger Castle, the northernmost Ottoman minaret, and the grand minorite churches.

Kethüda Minaret of Eger

The Kethüda Minaret of Eger, also known as the Eger Minaret, is a historic tower that served as part of a mosque during the Ottoman Empire's rule. It is one of the oldest surviving minarets in Europe, dating back to the 17th century. Kethüda Minaret of Eger is catalogued as a historical landmark within the historic quarters of Eger.

Dobó István Square

Dobó István Square is a public square in Eger, Hungary. Records associated with the site include 1552. Named after the Hungarian warrior Istvan Dobo, who defended Eger against the Ottoman armies in 1552, this square is a hub with outdoor cafes, a summer fountain, and the Baroque Church of Anthony of Padua. Open pavement and surrounding facades define the visitor setting.

Castle of Eger

The Castle of Eger, originally built in the 13th century to defend against Turkish invasion, now houses a history museum. Visitors can explore the renovated dungeons, outer Esplanade, and armoury while imagining the historical figures who once walked its grounds. Nearby attractions include Geza Gardonyis former house turned museum and the city center with its Archbishops Palace and Basilica.

Cathedral Basilica of St. John the Apostle, Eger

The Cathedral Basilica of St. John the Apostle in Eger is a neoclassical Catholic basilica that was built in 1836. Its soaring 40-meter dome and seasonal organ concerts make it a attraction. The basilica's imposing dome and towering spires dominate the city skyline, showing its Baroque design masterpiece. Inside, visitors are treated to paintings, elaborate altars, and towering columns that evoke awe and wonder.

City Under The City

Discover the hidden world beneath Eger, Hungary with a visit to the City Under The City. This unique attraction offers guided tours through the expansive cellar network located beneath the town square, featuring volcanic stone and cement arches. Learn about the history of this subterranean space, originally built by Bishop Gyorgy Fenessy in the late 17th century as an alternative residence to Eger Castle.

Turkish Bath

When in Eger, a visit to the Turkish Bathhouse is a must for those interested in immersing themselves in the city's rich history. This relic from the Ottoman era offers a glimpse into the lavishness and architectural brilliance of Turkish design. The Torok Furdo, built at the beginning of the 17th century, is the only surviving bathhouse from that period and has been renovated while still maintaining its original Turkish-style pools.
