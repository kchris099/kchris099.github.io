Destination Guide — Pécs

Explore Pécs

A Brief History

Pécs preserves Roman Sopianae cemeteries, an Ottoman mosque, and nineteenth-century civic buildings in southern Transdanubia. Early Christian burial chambers and Zsolnay ceramic heritage record Roman Pannonia, Ottoman occupation, and Hungarian industrial culture. Early Christian burial chambers and Zsolnay porcelain yards extend Roman and Ottoman layers in Transdanubia.

Top Sights & Activities

HUF 1590

Pécs TV Tower

The Pécs TV Tower, standing at 197 meters high atop the 535-meter Misina, has been in operation since 1973. Apart from its broadcasting responsibilities, it boasts an observation deck offering panoramic views of Pécs and its surroundings. On clear days, visitors can admire the Villanyi Mountains and even catch a glimpse of Croatian Papuk Hill or Badacsony. At night, the tower is illuminated with colors.

HUF 4200

Pécs Zoo

Pécs Zoo, located in the southern part of Pecs, Hungary, is a popular attraction offering a diverse range of animal species from around the world. The hilltop facility features animal exhibits, a petting zoo, an aquarium, and a park showcasing local plants. Visitors can observe majestic creatures like lions, tigers, and leopards up close. The zoo is well-organized and clean with friendly English-speaking staff.

Free

Nike Statue

Nestled in the hills of Pecs, the Nike Statue stands as a remarkable tribute to art and history, inviting visitors to explore its enigmatic presence. Erected in 1975 by Memos Makris as a Liberation monument, this statue offers panoramic views of the city and surrounding landscapes. While some may find the site shrouded in mystery regarding its origins and purpose, it remains a popular spot for tourists seeking both beauty and tranquility.

Free

Zsolnay Fountain

Zsolnay Fountain is a significant monument in Pecs, dedicated to Vilmos Zsolnay, the renowned designer of Zsolnay ceramics. Donated by his son in 1908 and designed by Andor Pilch, this fountain is a when visiting the city. It's located on Szechenyi Ter and stands in front of the Catholic church. The fountain is especially captivating during warmer months when it's operational, offering a refreshing oasis for visitors.

HUF 6000

National Theatre of Pécs

When exploring the cultural landscape of Pécs, the National Theatre of Pécs stands out as a must-visit destination. This architectural gem not only captivates with its design but also serves as a hub for the city's dynamic performing arts scene. Home to the renowned Pécs Ballet Company, it offers an array of performances ranging from drama and opera to ballet, ensuring there's something for everyone.

Free

Downtown Candlemas Church of the Blessed Virgin Mary

Downtown Candlemas Church of the Blessed Virgin Mary, formerly known as the Pasha Gazi Kassim Mosque, is a remarkable 16th-century building in Pecs. Originally a mosque during the Turkish occupation, it was later converted into a Catholic church. The structure features Islamic elements such as Turkish ogee arches and a mihrab, alongside beautiful geometric frescoes.

Free

Basilica of Pécs

Pécs Cathedral, also known as the Cathedral Basilica of the Assumption of the Blessed Virgin Mary, is a Catholic church with origins dating back to the 10th century. In Pecs, Hungary, this active cathedral boasts beautiful gardens and exquisite religious art. Its rich history and magnificent architectural design make it a top tourist attraction in the city. The cathedral's Romanesque Revival look from the 19th century adds to its allure.

Free

Barbican

Barbican is a well-preserved circular fortress gate that dates back centuries and offers city views from the top. Similar to the Krakow Barbican, the Pecs Barbican was once part of a city wall system and is situated in the northwest corner of the medieval wall surrounding Pecs city center. It is Hungary's only remaining barbican and was constructed in the 15th century as a defense against Ottoman occupation.

Free

Vilmos Zsolnay Statue

Zsolnay Statue is a fascinating attraction located in Pecs, Hungary. The statue is made of durable porcelain, a material invented by the Zsolnay family. Visitors can explore the Zsolnay Family and Factory History Exhibition to learn about the industrial dynasty's journey from producing simple ceramic items to creating ornate decorative pieces. Additionally, at the factory's exit in Pecs, there is a museum and a functioning factory site that can be visited.

HUF 10800

Zsolnay Mausoleum

The Zsolnay Mausoleum is a cultural center dedicated to the renowned ceramics company, showcasing modern art and offering dining options. The mausoleum itself is a burial chamber of the Zsolnay family, featuring exquisite porcelain made with innovative techniques known as "eosin." Visitors recommend exploring the entire cultural center first to understand the history and significance of the mausoleum.

Free

Tettye Ruins

Tettye Ruin Garden, located in the picturesque city of Pecs, Hungary, is a captivating blend of history and natural beauty. Nestled on the slopes of Misina Hill, this historic site offers panoramic views of the city below. Once a grand palace, it now showcases ancient ruins surrounded by lush vineyards that evoke the Renaissance era.
