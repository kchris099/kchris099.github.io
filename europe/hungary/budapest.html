Destination Guide — Budapest

Explore Budapest

A Brief History

Budapest united the towns of Buda, Óbuda, and Pest in 1873 along the Danube, combining a royal castle district, merchant quarters, and Habsburg-era boulevards. The parliament, chain bridge, and thermal baths built in the late 19th century expressed Hungarian nationhood within the dual monarchy. Defeat in World War I, siege damage in 1944–45, and socialist reconstruction left distinct scars and housing estates. Since 1989 the riverfront has revived as a cultural and tourist corridor where Gothic, Ottoman, and Art Nouveau layers meet cafés, festivals, and civic life on both banks of the Danube.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

Buda Castle

Buda Castle occupies the plateau above the Danube where Hungarian kings and later Habsburg rulers built and rebuilt a royal residence. The present ensemble includes the National Gallery and History Museum within courtyards and wings from medieval foundations to 19th-century reconstruction.

Free

Fountain of King Matthias

The Matthias Fountain stands in the western forecourt of Buda Castle and was completed in 1904 with bronze figures of King Matthias Corvinus and hunters. Neo-Baroque sculpture decorates the terrace above the Danube bend. The fountain forms a ceremonial focal point before the palace entrance.

HUF 1700

Fisherman's Bastion

Fisherman's Bastion is a Neo-Romanesque terrace on Castle Hill built in the early 20th century beside Matthias Church. Turrets and arcades frame views over the Danube, Parliament, and Pest embankment. The structure commemorates the medieval fishermen's guild that defended this section of walls.

Free

Széchenyi Chain Bridge

The Chain Bridge opened in 1849 as the first permanent crossing between Buda and Pest. Designed by William Tierney Clark and built with Scottish engineering support, its stone lions and suspension chains became symbols of united Budapest. Pedestrians and vehicles share the span above the Danube.

Free

Inner-City Church of the Franciscans

The Inner City Parish Church on Pest's Danube embankment is one of the oldest buildings in central Pest with Romanesquin and Gothic layers. Franciscan friars have served the parish for centuries. The church stands beside the Elizabeth Bridge approach on the riverfront.

Free

St. Gerard Sagredo Statue

The statue of Saint Gerard stands on the Gellért Hill slope above the Elisabeth Bridge. It commemorates the Italian bishop martyred in the 11th century during the Christianization of Hungary. The monument marks a viewpoint on the climb toward the Citadella.

Free

Elisabeth Bridge

Elisabeth Bridge connects Buda and Pest at the narrowest point of the Danube in Budapest. The present white cable-stayed bridge opened in 1964 after wartime destruction of the earlier chain structure. It carries traffic and pedestrians between Gellért Square and the inner city.

Free

Citadella

The Citadella is a 19th-century fortress on Gellért Hill built after the 1848–49 revolution. Its walls and the Liberty Statue above overlook the Danube and city panorama. The site is reached by paths from the embankment and remains a major viewpoint on the Buda side.

HUF 14000

Hungarian Parliament Building

The Hungarian Parliament Building, a example of neo-Gothic architecture, is a landmark in Budapest. Situated along the Danube River, it boasts lavishly decorated rooms and towering spires that captivate architectural enthusiasts and history buffs alike. Families with older children interested in history and politics will also find the site intriguing. To avoid long lines, booking a tour in advance is recommended.

Free

Heroes' Square

Heroes' Square at the end of Andrássy Avenue was laid out for the 1896 millennium celebrations of the Hungarian state. The Millennium Monument and colonnades commemorate chieftains and rulers. The square opens toward City Park and the Museum of Fine Arts.

HUF 2800

Vajdahunyad Castle

Vajdahunyad Castle in City Park was built in 1896 as a composite of Hungarian architectural styles for the millennium exhibition. Its courtyards and chapel reference Transylvanian and regional landmarks. The Agricultural Museum occupies the main wing within the park.

HUF 4500

St. Stephen's Basilica

St. Stephen's Basilica is a grand Roman Catholic church in Budapest, built between 1851 and 1905. It holds regular concerts and is a significant site in Hungarian history, as it stands on Saint Stephen Square, named after the first Hungarian king. The square is also the center of celebrations for Hungary's greatest holiday on August 20th, dedicated to King Stephen.

HUF 11900

Széchenyi Thermal Bath

Széchenyi Thermal Bath in City Park opened in 1913 in Neo-Baroque style around outdoor and indoor pools fed by thermal springs. Medicinal waters and grand halls reflect Budapest's spa culture under the monarchy. The complex remains one of the largest bathhouses in Europe.

Free

Central Market Hall

The Great Market Hall on Fővám Square was designed by Samu Pecz and opened in 1897 with a steel-framed hall and Zsolnay tile roof. Vendors sell produce, paprika, and prepared foods on three levels near the Liberty Bridge. The building remains a working market and visitor destination.

Free

Dohány Street Synagogue

The Dohány Street Synagogue, located in Budapest's Jewish Quarter, is a example of Moorish-style architecture. It is the largest synagogue in Europe and features ornate domes, a museum, cemetery, and Holocaust memorial. The complex also includes the Jewish Museum, Heroes Temple, and Raoul Wallenberg Memorial Park. travellers can explore the synagogue's tranquil garden and reflect at the touching Raoul Wallenberg Memorial Garden.

Free

Elizabeth Lookout

Perched atop Janos Hill in the Buda Hills, Elizabeth Lookout is a historic limestone tower offering panoramic views of Budapest. Built in 1911, this landmark features a cafe and can be reached by bus or car followed by a short hike. While there's a small fee to enter the tower, it's family-friendly with adventurous climbs and sweeping vistas suitable for all ages.

Free

Kossuth Lajos Square

Kossuth Lajos Square, also known as Kossuth Ter, is a significant city square in Hungary with a rich historical and political background. It is surrounded by impressive public buildings such as the Hungarian Parliament building and the Ministry of Agriculture. The City Park now features a modern museum building on the former Felvonulasi Square site, offering an extensive collection displayed on a floor space three times larger than its previous venue on Kossuth Square.

Free

Liberty Square

Liberty Square (Plaza de la Libertad) is a modern public space in Taipei, Taiwan, featuring extensive green areas, water features, and contemporary art installations. It serves as a popular venue for cultural events, open-air concerts, and community gatherings, providing a vibrant urban oasis amidst the bustling commercial district.

Free

Vörösmarty Square

Vörösmarty tér is a picturesque square in the heart of Pest, Budapest. It's famous for its Christmas market and features a statue of poet Mihály Vörösmart. The area is popular among both locals and tourists, offering upscale boutiques and shops along Vaci utca and pedestrian streets. The official Budapest Christmas market takes place here, showcasing handmade ornaments, hand-crafted mugs, and delicious food.

Free

Vigadó Concert Hall

Vigadó Concert Hall, located just a block away from the Danube River in Budapest, is a grand concert venue with a rich history. Built in 1865 to replace its predecessor destroyed by fire, it quickly became a social hub hosting prestigious concerts and elegant events. Despite suffering damage during World War II, it was painstakingly restored over 36 years and declared a national monument.

Free

Ferris Wheel of Budapest

The Ferris Wheel of Budapest is a popular attraction located in a public park, offering panoramic views of the city. It has become enjoyable ways to take in the sights of Budapest, although it may be slightly terrifying for those afraid of heights. travellers can time their visit to coincide with sunset for an even more experience.

Free

Elizabeth Square

Elizabeth Square, or Erzsebet Ter, is a vibrant green oasis nestled in the heart of Pest. This spacious park features an ornamental fountain and charming cafes, making it an ideal spot for relaxation. travellers can enjoy city views from the Budapest Eye Ferris wheel nearby. The square serves as a hub for guided tours that explore landmarks like St. Stephen's Basilica and Matthias Church while offering entertaining anecdotes about Hungarian culture and cuisine.

Free

City Park

City Park is a sprawling green space in the heart of Budapest, offering a variety of attractions for travellers. The park features tree-lined paths, expansive lawns, and a large lake. At its entrance lies Heroes Square, a major landmark adorned with an statue complex depicting Hungarian national leaders and the Memorial Stone of Heroes. Within the park grounds, travellers can explore Budapest Zoo, Vajdahunyad Castle housing the Museum of Hungarian Agriculture, and the Museum of Fine Arts.

Free

Holy Trinity Statue

The Holy Trinity Column is a reminder of the plague that swept through Budapest in the early 1800s, killing 600 people. The pillar is a sturdy column with two statues beneath and very few beautiful angels on top. The site is catalogued as a historical landmark.

Free

Church of Saint Mary Magdalene

The Church of Saint Mary Magdalene in Budapest is a historic site with a restored late-Gothic tower that offers views of the city. Originally commissioned by Bela IV of Hungary, it was one of the first Gothic buildings in Budapest and served the Hungarian community. Although damaged during WWII bombings and subsequently torn down by the Communist regime, the four-story tower remains as a memorial to its past.

Free

Sándor Palace

Sándor Palace, a magnificent neoclassical edifice constructed in 1806, stands as the official residence and office of Hungary's president. Designed by Mihaly Pollack, this grand structure has a rich history that includes serving as a government residence since the Compromise of 1867. Although it faced devastation during World War II, Sándor Palace was meticulously restored to its former splendor in 2002.

Free

Castle Garden Bazaar

Várkert Bazár is a beautifully restored 19th-century Neo-Renaissance complex located between Castle Hill and the Danube's bank in Budapest. It offers panoramic views, expansive lawns, restful cafes, and temporary exhibitions both indoors at YBL6 Muveszeti Ter and outdoors. Direct access to the Royal Castle allows travellers to combine their visit with other nearby attractions.

Free

Hungarian State Opera

The Hungarian State Opera House in Budapest is a neoclassical venue adorned with ceiling frescoes and statues of composers, offering opera and ballet performances as well as guided tours. It's a captivating destination for opera and theater enthusiasts, architecture aficionados, and those who appreciate grandeur and artistic heritage. The opulent interiors and performances make it a must-visit for anyone seeking a unique cultural experience.

Free

Rudas Thermal Bath

The Rudas Thermal Bath, dating back to the 16th century and boasting traditional Turkish baths, is a must-visit in Budapest. Nestled in the shadow of the majestic Buda Castle, this historic spa complex features six thermal pools, a swimming pool, and a wellness center. The authentic Ottoman architecture with domed ceilings and colored glass creates a meditative atmosphere for travellers.

Free

Citadel

Nestled atop Gellert Hill in Budapest, the Citadella stands as a powerful symbol of the city’s history and resilience. Constructed following the Hungarian Revolution of 1848, this impressive fortress not only showcases architecture but also offers panoramic views of both Buda and Pest. At its summit, travellers can admire the Liberty Monument, a tribute to Hungary's liberation from Nazi occupation after World War II.

Free

Citadel Lookout

The Citadel Lookout, located in Budapest, Hungary, offers a mesmerizing panoramic view of the city. The landmark has a diverse historical significance and was used as an anti-aircraft position during World War II. Climbing to the top reveals the construction's history, which was prompted by Hungarian patriots' uprising against Habsburg rule. travellers can enjoy views of Pest from the hilly Buda side and can even witness sunsets over the Danube.

Free

Gellért Hill

Gellért Hill in Budapest offers panoramic views of the Danube River and the city's landmarks. travellers can enjoy a leisurely walk up the hill and find balconies with picturesque vistas, as well as snack kiosks to grab a quick bite. The area is part of the UNESCO World Heritage site and provides an enchanting backdrop for a romantic evening or a peaceful escape from the bustling city.

Free

Garden of Philosophers

Nestled on a tranquil hilltop in Budapest, the Garden of Philosophers, also known as Taban Garden, is a hidden sanctuary featuring statues of renowned religious and philosophical figures. Just a short walk from the Elisabeth Bridge and the Citadel, this secret gem offers panoramic views of the Danube River and the Buda Castle.

Free

Gellért Thermal Bath

Gellért Thermal Bath is a part of the renowned Hotel Gellert complex and boasts an opulent Art Nouveau design that reflects its pre-World War era origins. The indoor and outdoor bathing experience offers a unique blend, with travellers starting in the indoor chambers adorned with Zsolnay pyrogranite and porcelain features.

Free

Metropolitan Ervin Szabó Library

The Metropolitan Ervin Szabó Library is a grand public library complex housed in a former 19th-century palace. Once the residence of Count Frigyes Wenckheim, it was acquired by the City Council and opened to the public. travellers can explore various rooms, including a reading room in the former Dining Room and a Smoking Room with elegant bookshelves and a chandelier.

Free

Margaret Island

Nestled in the heart of the Danube, Margaret Island is a serene oasis offering medieval ruins and lush gardens. It's a popular spot for recreation, with activities like renting pedal carts or rowboats. The island appeals to nature lovers, families, and those seeking outdoor leisure. travellers can enjoy a peaceful respite and explore the island's natural beauty through its musical fountain and sprawling lawns overlooking the river.

Free

Lukacs Baths

Lukacs Baths is a renowned thermal bathhouse in Budapest, Hungary, offering both indoor and outdoor pools along with additional amenities such as special saunas and massage services. While it may not be as grandiose as some of the other popular spas in the city, it is favored by locals for its serene atmosphere and affordability. The baths have a rich history dating back to the 12th century when the hot spring on site was used for healing purposes.

Free

Fővám Square

Fővám Square is a delightful spot nestled at the Pest end of Liberty Bridge, right next to the Grand Central Market. This vibrant square not only showcases the market hall, which has garnered attention as one of Europe’s markets, but also offers a picturesque park that stretches towards the riverfront. Here, travellers can soak in views of Gellert Hill and the serene Danube River.

Free

Liszt Ferenc tér

Liszt Ferenc tér is a charming square in Budapest, named after the renowned Hungarian composer. The square features a memorial statue, open-air eateries, and bars where locals and tourists gather to chat and people-watch at pavement tables. The cobbled main square exudes an atmospheric vibe with its Baroque facades and the Esernyos sculpture by Imre Varga.
