Destination Guide — Brașov

Explore Brașov

A Brief History

Brașov was founded by the Teutonic Knights in the early thirteenth century and subsequently settled by Transylvanian Saxons, who developed it into a heavily fortified and prosperous commercial hub at the crossroads of the Ottoman Empire and Western Europe. The city maintained significant autonomy and wealth, defending itself against numerous invasions from the south. The Saxon mercantile history and medieval defenses remain largely intact. Today, visitors explore the vast Black Church, the Council Square, and the preserved watchtowers of the medieval walls.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

The Council Square

The Council Square in Brasov is a former market square featuring the town hall, fountains, shops, cafes, and museums. Surrounding the square are baroque buildings and cafes to explore. Nearby attractions include the Gothic Black Church, hiking trails up Mount Tampa for views, and Rope Street, known as Europe's narrowest street. Visitors can also take day trips to Bran Castle and Rasnov Citadel.

RON 25

Black Church

The Black Church is a Gothic church with a rich history dating back to the 1600s. It earned its name from a fire that blackened its walls, and today it is adorned with striking murals and Ottoman rugs. The church is an important landmark in the Saxon town of Brasov, offering visitors a glimpse into its storied past.

Free

Turnul Negru

Turnul Negru, also known as the Black Tower, is a restored 15th-century tower with a glass roof that now is a weapon exhibit. Despite its name, the tower is actually white and got its name from the blackened soot of a fire in 1599. Originally built as a lookout tower outside of the town's defensive walls, it stands at 11 meters tall on Warthe Hill.

RON 20

The White Tower

The White Tower is a restored 15th-century watchtower in Brasov, Romania. It offers a museum experience with photos and pictures of the city's history. The tower has an open semicircular plan shaped like a horseshoe and was once the most interesting defensive bastion of the Fortress of Brasov. Visitors can also explore nearby attractions such as the Black Church, Republicii Street with its Renaissance, Classic and Baroque architecture, and other historical buildings in the area.

Free

Catherine Gate

Catherine Gate, also known as eCatherinas Gate, is a historic landmark in Brasov, Romania. It is one of the oldest gates in the city and is situated under a tower that was once part of a larger structure. During the Saxon rule, this gate served as the only entrance for Romanians into the fortified city, albeit at specific times and for a toll. Nearby, visitors can find an orthodox church and the first Romanian-language school which became an important cultural center.

Free

Saint Nicholas Cathedral

Biserica Sfântul Nicolae, also known as St. Nicholas Church, is a historic Romanian Orthodox church located in the Schei district of Brasov, Romania. Dating back to the late 13th century, this Gothic-style church was later rebuilt incorporating Baroque architecture. It holds great significance as the first Orthodox church in Transylvania and is part of a complex that includes an Orthodox Cathedral and a museum.

Free (cable car RON 25)

Tâmpa

Tâmpa, located in Brasov, Romania, offers a experience for visitors. Taking the cable car to the top of Mount Tâmpa provides a quick and convenient way to enjoy views of the entire valley with Brasov. At an altitude of about 900 meters, visitors can also admire the Brasov sign reminiscent of the Hollywood sign.

Free

Piața Sfântul Ioan

Piața Sfântul Ioan is a visitor site in Brașov, Romania. Piața Sfântul Ioan is a active hub in Brașov, known for hosting the main stage of the Brasov Jazz & Blues Festival. Located behind the AroPalace Hotel, it's not just a venue but a must-visit spot that captures the essence of local life. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Free

The Fortress on the Watchtower

The Fortress-on-Straja, originally constructed in the 15th century, served various purposes such as a lookout point, prison, and city archives. Although currently closed to the public, it remains fully intact on the inside with its original furniture. Despite its temporary closure, visitors can still enjoy a scenic walk to the fortress and admire panoramic views of Brasov from atop Straja Hill.

Free

Solomon's Rocks

Solomon's Rocks, located just an hour away from Brasov by car, train, or bus, is a fantastic destination for hiking in the Piatra Craiului Mountains. The area has limestone rocks, glacial lakes, and mountain towns. Accessible by foot through villages or by city bus, it offers refreshing forest air and scenic trails. While the trails are poorly marked, reaching the mountaintop rewards hikers with views and woodlands.

Free

Belvedere

Located on a hillside just 2 km from the Brasov, the Belvedere Hotel is a gem that offers views over the city and its historic Old Town. This enchanting destination is not only known for its panoramas but also for its dining experience, where an award-winning kitchen team crafts dishes that tantalize visitor taste buds. The attentive waitstaff enhances a visit with their impeccable service.

RON 80

Aquatic Paradise

Aquatic Paradise is a visitor site in Brașov, Romania. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest. Local maps identify Aquatic Paradise within Brașov, Romania. Posted signs mark approaches and opening information for Aquatic Paradise.

RON 25

Fortified Evangelical Church Harman

The Fortified Evangelical Church Harman, located in the village of Harman, Transylvania, is a medieval church dating back to the 13th century. Built by Saxons, it is surrounded by strong walls and seven imposing towers, giving it a castle-like appearance. The church served as a fortification for defense against enemy attacks. Visitors can explore its history and enjoy the village surroundings easily accessible by train or bus.

RON 20

Seven Ladders Canyon

Seven Ladders Canyon is a visitor site in Brașov, Romania. Seven Ladders Canyon, part of the Masivul Piatra Mare protected area near the 3 Stejari domain, is a natural attraction in Romania. Despite its small length of only 230m and a difference in level between 55-60m, it offers views thanks to the cascading waterfalls. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

RON 35

Zoo Brașov

Zoo Brașov is a visitor site in Brașov, Romania. Known for its well-developed infrastructure and diverse animal population, this Instagram-worthy destination offers an up-close view of various mammals including tigers, lions, jaguars, brown bears, and snow leopards. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

RON 100

Adventure Park Brasov

Adventure Park Brasov is a public park in Brașov, Romania. Adventure Park Brasov, situated near the Zoo and Lake Noua, is a thrilling outdoor park spanning 2.5 hectares of forest. The longest zip line crosses the lake over a distance of 307 meters. Paths, lawns, and shaded areas make up the main public facilities.

RON 25

Valea Cetatii Cave

Discover the enchanting underground world of Valea Cetatii Cave, a natural formation shaped by water over time. Located a protected area, this cave has the Cathedral Hall, accessible through an ancient forest. Inside, marvel at the 30,000 cubic meter space adorned with calcite formations and hanging white arches reflected in small pools of water. While reaching the cave involves a steep climb from the parking lot, it's manageable for most visitors.
