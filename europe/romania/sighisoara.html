Sighișoara

Sighișoara was founded by Saxon craftsmen and merchants invited to Transylvania in the twelfth century to defend Hungarian royal frontiers and mine nearby hills. Its hilltop citadel, covered wooden stairway to the Church on the Hill, and clock tower with animated figures express guild organisation on the Târnava Mare. Vlad Dracula was born here in 1431 in a burgher house now marked for visitors, while festivals recall how this Saxon town preserved German-language civic life into modern Romania.

The Clock Tower

Sighisoara's Clock Tower guarded the principal entrance to the upper citadel and housed civic functions from the medieval period onward; the tower formed one part of a larger defensive system, while nearby houses remain separate attractions. A clock mechanism, moving figures, museum rooms, and an upper gallery occupy the tall structure overlooking tiled roofs and surrounding hills.

The Ironsmiths' Tower

Turnul Fierarilor, the Ironsmiths' Tower, guards the upper citadel edge in Sighisoara near the monastery church it was meant to cover during sieges. Guild members stored lances, muskets, pistols, and arquebuses inside for civic defense. Thick masonry, narrow openings, and a compact footprint show its role as a working fortification rather than a ceremonial landmark on the circuit.

House of Guilds

Casa Breslelor, the Guild House in Sighișoara, displays Saxon craft tools and workshop gear from roughly a century ago. Owner-led visits often add period music and recorded craft sounds in the rooms. The small museum sits inside the citadel's tourist circuit.

04 AdmissionRON 10

Biserica din Deal

The Church on the Hill crowns Sighisoara's School Hill as a late Gothic Lutheran church reached by the covered stairway, and museum-style entry may operate outside services, while the building's value comes from preserved art and setting. Interior murals, altarpieces, choir furnishings, crypts, and a cemetery reflect the city's Saxon religious history.

Sighișoara Citadel

Sighisoara Citadel remains a lived medieval upper town enclosed by walls and surviving guild towers. Houses and churches stand along steep lanes with schools, museums, shops, lodging and restaurants. Protected status covers the broader historic center shaped by Saxon settlement and later reconstruction; individual hotels and commercial properties occupy only parts of that ensemble.

46.2190° N / 24.7924° E

06 AdmissionFree

The Covered Stairway

Built in the seventeenth century, Sighisoara's Covered Stairway shelters a steep wooden ascent from the citadel streets to the Church on the Hill and old school. Its roof protected pupils and worshippers from snow and rain. Repairs have altered the number and form of the steps over time, but the passage continues to link the upper and lower parts of the citadel.

MYstical transylvania

Five indoor rooms use light and sound to place the Dracula legend beside documented episodes from Sighișoara's citadel past. The exhibit stays focused on local history rather than a full fortress tour. Compact galleries suit a short, story-led visit.
