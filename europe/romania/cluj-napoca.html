Cluj Napoca

Cluj-Napoca (Kolozsvár) was a major city of Transylvania under Hungarian, Habsburg, and Romanian rule, with a Gothic St. Michael's Church and a university tradition dating to the 16th–19th centuries. Roman Napoca lies beneath parts of the centre. Interwar and communist expansion added boulevards beyond the old core. Today cafes, civic squares, and minority cultural institutions reflect a layered Central European university city.

Unirii Square

Unirii Square, also known as Piata Unirii, is a active and historically rich destination in Cluj-Napoca. It is the largest square in the city and is surrounded by numerous attractions such as the Teleki and Banffy Palaces, a Franciscan Monastery, and Old St. Michaels Church with its 15th-century murals. The square sits at the center of Cluj-Napoca between the medieval fortress walls and the historical city center.

Alexandru Borza Botanical Garden

The Alexandru Borza Botanical Garden, part of Babes-Bolyai University in Cluj-Napoca, is a large 14-hectare masterpiece founded in 1920 by Professor Alexandru Borza. This historical monument houses over 10,000 plant species from around the world, organized into distinct sectors like ornamental, phytogeographical, systematic, economic, and medicinal. The garden's rich history and architectural allure make it an enchanting oasis for relaxation and exploration.

Wind Mill

The Wind Mill (Moara de Vânt) is a historic, traditional wooden windmill preserved within the open-air Romulus Vuia Ethnographic Park in Cluj-Napoca. It is a prominent example of the region's pre-industrial agricultural technology and rural architecture. The windmill is part of a larger collection of authentic peasant homes and workshops relocated to the park.
