Explore Sinaia

A Brief History

Sinaia originated around a seventeenth-century monastery named after Mount Sinai, but its modern history began when King Carol I of Romania chose it as his summer residence in the late nineteenth century. The construction of Peleș Castle transformed the secluded mountain village into a fashionable resort town for the Romanian aristocracy and European elites. Its elegant Alpine architecture reflects this royal patronage. Visitors experience this regal heritage by touring the opulent rooms of Peleș Castle, Pelișor Castle, and the historic Sinaia Casino.

Top Sights & Activities

RON 100

Peleș Castle

Peleș Castle is a visitor site in Sinaia. A Neo-Renaissance castle set in the Carpathian Mountains, known for its architecture and rich history as a former royal residence. Peleș Castle is a fortified historic structure associated with the defensive and administrative past of Sinaia, Romania.

RON 30

"Pelișor" Castle

"Pelișor" Castle is a visitor site in Sinaia. A smaller castle near Peleș, showing Art Nouveau style and housing royal family art collections. "Pelișor" Castle is a fortified historic structure associated with the defensive and administrative past of Sinaia, Romania.

RON 20

Ialomiței Cave

Ialomiței Cave is a visitor site in Sinaia, Romania. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest. Ialomiței Cave remains part of the public visitor circuit in Sinaia. Local maps identify Ialomiței Cave within Sinaia, Romania.

Free

Caraiman Monastery

Caraiman Monastery, established in 1998 by priest Gherontie Puiu, is a Orthodox monastery located amidst the Caraiman and Costila peaks in Busteni. The founder was inspired to build the monastery after a dream of the Virgin Mary instructing him to do so at a specific location. The monastery has well-maintained garden grounds with walking paths and offers mountain views.

RON 80

Cantacuzino Castle

Cantacuzino Castle is a museum in Sinaia, Romania. Construction and early use date to around 1911. Cantacuzino Castle, a Neo-Romanian castle built around 1911, offers guided tours of art exhibits and has a scenic terrace restaurant. The castle's exterior is crafted from carved stone while its interior is made of brick, with a concrete foundation. Galleries and public rooms are open on posted hours in Sinaia.

Free

Heroes' Cross on Caraiman Peak

The Heroes' Cross is a notable attraction located on the peak of Mount Caraiman in the Bucegi Mountains. It is a memorial for those who died during World War I and can be visited under good weather conditions. Climbing up a slope on the opposite side of the cross provides an arduous but experience. There's also an easy trail leading to Costila peak, with views at almost 2500 meters above sea level.

RON 20

Sinaia Monastery

Sinaia Monastery, also known as The Carpathians Cathedral, is a revered complex with origins dating back to the 15th century. It is a significant landmark in Sinaia, a popular tourist destination known for its royal residences and monuments. The monastery, the first construction in Sinaia, reflects the town's historical identity and houses valuable religious artifacts and an collection of icons.

Free

Dimitrie Ghica Park

Dimitrie Ghica Park is a lush retreat that invites both locals and travelers to bask in the tranquility of nature. Located this landscape is the Sinaia Casino, an notable building commissioned by King Carol in 1913. This Art Nouveau structure has transitioned from a active gambling hub to a sophisticated conference center, infusing the park with its regal history.

RON 40

The Casino of Sinaia

The Casino of Sinaia, originally built as a casino but now serving as a congress center, is an architectural gem and cultural landmark in the Dimitrie Ghica Park. Constructed in just one year from 1912 to 1913 at the request of King Carol, it was inspired by the opulence of Monte Carlo. The casino has historical significance, with many legends surrounding its past as a place where fortunes were made and lost.

Free

Stâncile Franz Joseph

Stâncile Franz Joseph is a visitor site in Sinaia. It remains a notable local point of interest situated in Sinaia, Romania. Visitors can observe the surrounding area and its connection to the city's broader historical landscape. Local guides sometimes include it on walking routes through the centre.
