Sinaia

Sinaia originated around a seventeenth-century monastery named after Mount Sinai, but its modern history began when King Carol I of Romania chose it as his summer residence in the late nineteenth century. The construction of Peleș Castle transformed the secluded mountain village into a fashionable resort town for the Romanian aristocracy and European elites. Its elegant Alpine architecture reflects this royal patronage. Visitors experience this regal heritage by touring the opulent rooms of Peleș Castle, Pelișor Castle, and the historic Sinaia Casino.

Peleș Castle

Peles Castle climbs a forested slope in the Carpathians above Sinaia as a Neo-Renaissance royal residence built for Romania's Hohenzollern kings. Timber framing, carved wood interiors, and terraced gardens surround a central keep-like mass with towers and steep roofs. Reception rooms, armor halls, and private apartments recount court life from the late nineteenth century through the monarchy's later decades.

45.3600° N / 25.5426° E

02 AdmissionRON 30

"Pelișor" Castle

Pelisor Castle stands a short walk from larger Peles in Sinaia, a smaller royal house finished in Art Nouveau lines with lighter timber and soft-colored interiors. Rooms display furniture, paintings, and decorative arts collected by the Romanian royal family. Compared with Peles' heavier Neo-Renaissance mass, Pelisor feels more domestic, with intimate salons and garden terraces facing the same mountain woods.

45.3605° N / 25.5392° E

03 AdmissionRON 20

Ialomiței Cave

Ialomita Cave opens in the Bucegi Mountains beside a small Orthodox monastery, well beyond central Sinaia. A developed route follows the underground stream through cool limestone chambers, pools and rock formations. The cave's position in a steep mountain valley connects natural geology with the religious landscape around the monastery and with hiking routes across the Bucegi plateau.

45.3930° N / 25.4369° E

Caraiman Monastery

Caraiman Monastery stands at the foot of the Bucegi Mountains beneath the steep slopes of Caraiman Peak. Founded around the turn of the twenty-first century, the Orthodox complex contains churches and monastic buildings. Gardens and open spaces frame views toward the mountain. Devotional stories about its establishment form part of the monastery's identity, while worship and community life remain central to the site.

45.4067° N / 25.5281° E

05 AdmissionRON 80

Cantacuzino Castle

Built around 1911 in Neo-Romanian style, Cantacuzino Castle shows carved stone outside and brick rooms over a concrete foundation, with guided art tours and a terrace restaurant. Galleries open on posted hours. Mountain-town views frame the terrace side.

45.4138° N / 25.5427° E

06 AdmissionFree

Heroes' Cross on Caraiman Peak

A large steel cross stands high on Caraiman Mountain as a memorial to Romanian soldiers killed in World War I. Built in the 1920s, the monument occupies exposed alpine terrain above the Prahova Valley. Routes across the Bucegi plateau provide approaches to the site. Its height and remote position make weather, trail conditions and mountain transport important parts of the memorial's setting.

45.4160° N / 25.4976° E

Sinaia Monastery

Sinaia Monastery was founded at the end of the seventeenth century and later gave the resort town its name. Old and new courtyards contain several churches and monastic cells. A bell tower, frescoes and icons reveal repeated phases of rebuilding. Tombs and a museum of religious objects add historical context to an active Orthodox community.

45.3553° N / 25.5491° E

08 AdmissionFree

Dimitrie Ghica Park

Dimitrie Ghica Park forms a landscaped center in Sinaia around paths, mature trees, fountains, hotels and the early twentieth-century casino. The former casino now hosts cultural and conference uses, while the park connects the railway area with the monastery and resort streets. Seasonal planting and public events add activity to the formal grounds.

45.3543° N / 25.5521° E

09 AdmissionRON 40

The Casino of Sinaia

King Carol ordered the Sinaia casino built in a single year from 1912 to 1913, modeled on Monte Carlo glamour; it now works as a congress center in Dimitrie Ghica Park. Legends of fortunes won and lost still cling to the rooms. Park paths lead to the ornate facade.

45.3552° N / 25.5534° E

Stâncile Franz Joseph

Stancile Franz Joseph are named rock outcrops on walking routes above Sinaia, where stone shelves and viewpoints look over the Prahova corridor and forested slopes. Paths from the resort town reach the formation without technical climbing, so the site appears on local walking loops rather than as a separate ticketed monument. The rocks themselves are the landmark; the draw is the overlook and the pine cover around them.

45.3682° N / 25.5254° E
