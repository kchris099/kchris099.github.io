Sibiu

Transylvanian Saxon settlers founded Hermannstadt with guild halls and, later, the Brukenthal Palace under Habsburg rule. Late-twentieth-century Saxon emigration left Romanian and Hungarian majorities in the same fortified plan. European Capital of Culture status in 2007 accelerated restoration of the linked squares and 'eye' attic windows. The Passage of Stairs links upper and lower town within the old fortification logic.

The Bridge of Lies

The Bridge of Lies, a small iron pedestrian bridge constructed in 1859, is steeped in legend and history. It is located in Sibiu, Romania, amidst a backdrop of medieval buildings that exude historic charm and romantic allure. The city itself has significant museums and historical sites such as the Brukenthal Museum, Astra Museum of Folkloric Traditional Civilization, Holy Trinity Cathedral, Evangelic Church, Council Tour of Sibiu, and old fortress tours.

“Saint Mary” Evangelical Cathedral

A fourteenth-century Gothic church in Sibiu's Upper Town, Saint Mary Evangelical Cathedral opens its exterior and main interior outside service times. Tall vaults and stone detailing mark the Lutheran landmark. The Upper Town setting keeps it within walking reach of the main squares.

03 AdmissionFree

The Large Square

Also called Piața Mare, Sibiu's main square has held trade, public events, and celebrations since the fourteenth century. Colorful medieval and Baroque houses line the broad paving as protected monuments. The open room still works as the Old Town's primary gathering ground.

45.7965° N / 24.1518° E

04 AdmissionFree

Holy Trinity Roman Catholic Church

Holy Trinity Roman Catholic Church stands on Sibiu's Large Square as a Baroque church with elaborate altars, frescoes, and stained glass. Mitropoliei Street nearby holds other denominations' buildings that show the town's mixed confessional past. The facade faces the open square used for markets and events.

45.7970° N / 24.1513° E

05 AdmissionRON 2

The Council's Tower

The Council Tower stands between Sibiu's Piata Mare and Piata Mica, a medieval gateway rebuilt over centuries after its thirteenth-century origins in the city walls. Seven floors of stairs lead to an upper platform overlooking tiled roofs and guild houses that once lined both squares. Ground-level arches still mark the old passage between the two plazas at the heart of the fortified center.

45.7976° N / 24.1521° E

06 AdmissionRON 20

Sibiu Zoological Garden

Smaller than major metropolitan zoos, Sibiu Zoo keeps animals in clean yards that travellers praise for care rather than sheer size. Paths and shaded areas organize the walking loop. The compact scale suits a shorter visit than a full-day safari park.

45.7629° N / 24.1241° E

07 AdmissionFree

Dumbrava Sibiului Natural Park

Dumbrava Sibiului Natural Park protects woodland, meadows, streams and wildlife southwest of Sibiu. The ASTRA open-air museum occupies part of the broader landscape but remains a separately managed cultural institution with traditional buildings and exhibits. Trails, roads and recreation areas connect the park, while describing museum and nature reserve as one attraction obscures their distinct purposes.

45.7498° N / 24.1086° E

08 AdmissionFree

Citadel Park

Citadel Park follows a surviving line of Sibiu's medieval walls and defensive towers near the historic center, and lighting and events change the atmosphere, while the park's value comes from its relationship to the wall. Trees, benches, paths, monuments, and the city's Walk of Fame occupy the narrow green strip beside fortifications and cultural venues.

45.7934° N / 24.1523° E

09 AdmissionFree

The Thick Tower, Sibiu State Philharmonic, Thalia Hall.

Sibiu's Thick Tower formed part of the city's defensive ring and was later integrated with Thalia Hall, a concert venue used by the State Philharmonic. Heavy masonry contrasts with the reconstructed performance interior, linking military architecture to modern cultural use. Concert schedules govern access.

45.7941° N / 24.1540° E

10 AdmissionFree

Cibin Market

Cibin Market operates near the Cibin River in Sibiu as a working food and household market. Stalls sell produce, meat, cheese, bread and regional ingredients used in Transylvanian cooking, while vendors and supply change through the week. Mici sold nearby are grilled minced-meat rolls, reflecting the market's practical role in local food life. Early hours bring the widest produce selection.

45.7990° N / 24.1445° E
