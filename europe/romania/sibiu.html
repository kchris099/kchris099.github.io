Sibiu

Transylvanian Saxon settlers founded Hermannstadt with guild halls and, later, the Brukenthal Palace under Habsburg rule. Late-twentieth-century Saxon emigration left Romanian and Hungarian majorities in the same fortified plan. European Capital of Culture status in 2007 accelerated restoration of the linked squares and 'eye' attic windows. The Passage of Stairs links upper and lower town within the old fortification logic.

The Large Square

The Large Square, also known as Piata Mare in Romanian, is a grand plaza located in Sibiu's Old Town. It has a rich history dating back to the 14th century and is the main town square, offering a active hub for trade, commerce, public events, and celebrations. This expansive square is lined with colorful medieval and baroque-era buildings that have been preserved as architectural monuments.

The Council's Tower

The Council's Tower is a visitor site in Sibiu. The Council Tower, dating back to the 13th century, is a symbol of Sibiu between Grand and Lesser Squares. Originally a gateway in the city's fortifications, it now offers panoramic views of the town for a small fee. The tower is located near Piata Mare and Piata Mica, where historic guild buildings once housed craftsmen. Travellers can explore this area and then climb the tower's seven stories to enjoy city vistas.
