Destination Guide — Sibiu

Explore Sibiu

A Brief History

Established as a regional settlement beyond an older fortified core, the city developed through industrialization in the late 19th century, post-war reconstruction, and modern civic expansion that reshaped its public spaces, markets, and surrounding landscape. Restored religious buildings, civic buildings, and natural features now define the contemporary visitor experience.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

The Bridge of Lies

The Bridge of Lies, a small iron pedestrian bridge constructed in 1859, is steeped in legend and history. It is located in Sibiu, Romania, amidst a backdrop of medieval buildings that exude historic charm and romantic allure. The city itself boasts significant museums and historical sites such as the Brukenthal Museum, Astra Museum of Folkloric Traditional Civilization, Holy Trinity Cathedral, Evangelic Church, Council Tour of Sibiu, and old fortress tours.

Free

“Saint Mary” Evangelical Cathedral

The “Saint Mary” Evangelical Cathedral is a 14th-century Gothic-style church located in the Upper Town of Sibiu. It features a towering steeple that offers panoramic views of the city and its surroundings from the bell tower. Inside, travellers can admire intricate stained glass windows, a beautiful altar, and one of Romania's largest historic pipe organs. Climbing to the top provides an exhilarating experience with sweeping vistas of Sibiu's cityscape.

Free

The Large Square

The Large Square, also known as Piata Mare in Romanian, is a grand plaza located in Sibiu's Old Town. It boasts a rich history dating back to the 14th century and serves as the main town square, offering a vibrant hub for trade, commerce, public events, and celebrations. This expansive square is lined with colorful medieval and baroque-era buildings that have been preserved as architectural monuments.

Free

Holy Trinity Roman Catholic Church

Located in the Large Square, Holy Trinity Roman Catholic Church is a prominent baroque church in Transylvania. Its interior is renowned for its elaborate altars, frescoes, and intricate stained glass windows that leave travellers in awe. The church stands out amidst other religious buildings on Mitropoliei street, showcasing Sibiu's cultural and ecumenical essence.

Free

The Council's Tower

The Council Tower, dating back to the 13th century, stands as a symbol of Sibiu between Grand and Lesser Squares. Originally a gateway in the city's fortifications, it now offers panoramic views of the town for a small fee. The tower is located near Piata Mare and Piata Mica, where historic guild buildings once housed craftsmen. travellers can explore this area and then climb the tower's seven stories to enjoy city vistas.

Free

Sibiu Zoological Garden

Nestled within the picturesque Astra National Museum Complex, Sibiu Zoological Garden offers a charming retreat for families and animal enthusiasts alike. This compact zoo features a delightful array of wildlife, from majestic bears to graceful zebras, all set against the backdrop of serene lakes formed by the Trinkbach brook. While it may not rival larger zoos in scale, Sibiu Zoo captivates travellers with its well-cared-for animals and clean environment.

Free

Dumbrava Sibiului Natural Park

Dumbrava Sibiului Natural Park, situated just 4 kilometers from the city in Dumbrava Forest, is a serene and green space that offers a change of scenery. This protected site features three lakes, diverse wildlife, and lush vegetation. The park also houses the largest open-air museum in Romania, the Astra Museum of Folkloric Traditional Civilization.

Free

Citadel Park

Citadel Park is a picturesque park situated near the former medieval fortifications in Sibiu. The park boasts an impressive Walk of Fame dedicated to prominent residents of Sibiu, along with well-maintained paths that are illuminated at night. travellers can admire the 600-year-old towers and enjoy ample seating options on numerous benches throughout the park.

Free

The Thick Tower, Sibiu State Philharmonic, Thalia Hall.

Sibiu, a historic city in Transylvania, is home to the Thick Tower, a fortress with thick walls that has been renovated and connected to the Thalia Concert Hall. The Thalia Hall, located in Upper Town, is an impressive theatre and concert venue that now houses the State Philharmonic of Sibiu. The Philharmonic puts on weekly concerts featuring various genres of music.

Free

Cibin Market

The Cibin market is a popular destination for locals and tourists in Bucharest, Romania. Here, shoppers can find fresh produce and meat, as well as traditional Romanian foods like mici (a type of pastry) and Romanian beer. The market is also a great place to learn about Transilvanian cuisine, as many of the ingredients used in traditional recipes are found here.
