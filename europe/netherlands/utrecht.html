Destination Guide — Utrecht

Explore Utrecht

A Brief History

Originating as a Roman fortification on the Rhine, Utrecht was the undisputed religious center of the medieval Netherlands. Its innovative multi-level canal wharves and monumental Gothic cathedral tower reflect centuries of extensive ecclesiastical wealth and maritime innovation. The remarkable survival of these cultural and architectural milestones provides a powerful window into the region's storied past.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

De Haar Castle

De Haar Castle (Kasteel de Haar) is the largest and most luxurious castle in the Netherlands, located near Utrecht. Originally a medieval fortress, it was extensively rebuilt in the late 19th century in a grandiose neo-Gothic style by architect Pierre Cuypers. The castle features opulent interiors and is surrounded by magnificent, expansive landscaped gardens.

Free

Hoog Catharijne

Hoog Catharijne is a remarkable shopping destination that stands out in Utrecht, being the largest indoor mall in the Netherlands. With over 125 shops, eateries, and services, it attracts around 30 million visitors annually. Conveniently located next to the train station, this modern complex offers everything from boutiques to grocery stores and even a cinema and hotel. What makes Hoog Catharijne particularly unique is its accessibility via canal—an unusual feature for a shopping mall!.

Free

De Inktpot

De Inktpot, also known as the Inkwell, stands as the largest brick building in the Netherlands. Constructed between 1919 and 1921 for Nederlandse Spoorwegen (NS), the government-operated railway company, it now serves as headquarters for ProRail. The building's most distinctive feature is a UFO perched on its front, originally created for Utrechts Panorama 2000 art exhibition.

Free

Domkerk

St Martin's Cathedral, also known as the Dom Church (Domkerk), is a storied Gothic cathedral located in the heart of Utrecht. It was originally built in 1254 and is considered important churches in The Netherlands. The cathedral features a landmark tower, choir, and transept, along with two chapels. Once the largest cathedral in the country, it suffered damage when a storm separated its tower in the 17th century.

Free

Pandhof van de Dom

Pandhof van de Dom, located between the Domkerk and the Academiegebouw, is a serene garden with medicinal herb beds and a covered cloister featuring beautiful window arches. This historical gem once connected the Domkerk and the Groot Kapittelhuis. Dating back to the late 14th and early 15th century, it boasts a neo-Gothic entrance gate from 1857.

Free

Dom Tower

The Dom Tower, also known as Domtoren in Dutch, is a Gothic church tower standing at 95 meters high with a 17th-century carillon and offers panoramic views. It is the tallest church tower in the Netherlands at 112.32 meters high and was originally part of the city's cathedral, which was never completed. Visitors can take guided tours to climb its 465 steps, explore giant bells, and learn about its history.

Free

Broese Booksellers

Broese Booksellers, established in 1974 and now located in a new building at Post Utrecht, offers an extensive collection of books for both adults and children. The bookstore features a diverse range of genres, including novels and travel guides. Additionally, visitors can explore CDs and vinyl records. The three-story space provides a library-like atmosphere with the added convenience of a café where patrons can relax.

Free

Jaarbeurs

Jaarbeurs is a popular exhibition and convention center situated in Utrecht, Netherlands. It has played host to a number of notable events such as Space '86, Hobby Computer Club Days, and the Mega Record & CD Fair in the past. The venue is known for its excellent organization and planning that makes it an ideal location for various conventions and exhibitions. However, some attendees have suggested adding an auditorium-like room for better visibility during presentations.

Free

Molen de Ster

Molen de Ster, also known as Mill the Star, is an 18th-century wind-driven sawmill located just west of Utrecht's train station. It offers guided tours and demonstrations on Saturdays, allowing visitors to witness traditional Dutch milling techniques in action. The picturesque setting beside a canal adds to its timeless charm, making it a quintessentially Dutch sight. In addition to the mill itself, there is a cozy café open on weekends and occasional events like markets or live music.

Free

Fort Rhijnauwen

Fort Rhijnauwen is the largest fort in the New Dutch Waterline, a historic military defense system. Located near Utrecht, the massive 19th-century fortification is surrounded by a wide moat and dense woodland. Today, it serves as a protected nature reserve and a historical monument, offering guided tours that explore its military architecture and local ecology.
