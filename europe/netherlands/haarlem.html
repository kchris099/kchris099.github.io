Explore Haarlem

A Brief History

Haarlem is in Netherlands. Established as a regional settlement beyond an older fortified core, the city developed through industrialization in the late 19th century, post-war reconstruction, and modern civic expansion that reshaped its public spaces, markets, and surrounding landscape. Museums, markets, and street plans in Haarlem still show layers from those periods.

MASCOT Circuit Zandvoort

MASCOT Circuit Zandvoort is a visitor site in Haarlem, Netherlands. Circuit Zandvoort is a known motorsport circuit that hosts various car and motorcycle races throughout the year. However, Zandvoort offers more than just racing excitement. The town has a beach stretching for miles along the coast, perfect for sunbathing, swimming, and water sports. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

€22

Keukenhof

Keukenhof, a 19th-century park located near Lisse in the Netherlands, is a must-visit destination for flower enthusiasts. Open from March to May, this expansive garden showcases an incredible array of Dutch spring flowers, including seven million active tulip bulbs. The name "Keukenhof," which translates to "kitchen garden," reflects its historical roots as land used for hunting and herb cultivation by Jacqueline, the Countess of Hainaut in the 15th century.

Free

Kasteel Keukenhof

Kasteel Keukenhof is a 17th-century mansion located on a country estate in Lisse, the Netherlands. The word 'Keukenhof' means 'kitchen', as the castle's parks were once used as an herb garden by Jacqueline, Countess of Hainaut. The castle has a rich 400-year history and offers guided tours through its interior, showing portraits, furniture, and monuments that bring its history to life.

€4

The St. Bavo Church in Haarlem

Bavo Church, also known as the Grote Kerk, is a Gothic masterpiece that dominates Haarlem's market square. With its 80-meter wooden tower and detailed architecture, this church is a sight to behold from the outside. Inside, travellers can marvel at the Muller organ, known for its grandeur and historical significance; it was once played by both Handel and a young Mozart.

Free

Amsterdamse Poort, Haarlem

Amsterdamse Poort in Haarlem is a and historic corner, surrounded by some of the city's oldest and most buildings. It served as a protection fortification for Haarlem during the 17th century and has undergone extensive renovation to restore its former glory. This 14th-century gate was part of the city wall and is the only one that survived over many years.

Free

Municipal Theater Haarlem

Municipal Theater Haarlem, located in a former church, offers a unique experience with its large stained-glass windows and soaring ceilings. Travellers can enjoy a variety of performances in this and spacious venue, with every seat offering a good view of the stage. The theater's intimate setting adds to the overall experience, making it an ideal place for an enjoyable evening out.

Free

Elswout

Elswout is a visitor site in Haarlem, Netherlands. The estate offers two walking routes of 2.5 and 4 km, providing ample opportunities to explore its well-maintained grounds and admire its structures. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Free

De Cruquius

The De Cruquius museum in Haarlem is devoted to the history of drainage and engine technology. The exhibits explore how machines and innovative engineering have helped reclaim land from the Haarlemmermeer, lakes in Holland. The museum is well-presented and interesting for all ages, with facilities including a tearoom.

Free

Brederode Castle

The ruins of a 13th-century brick castle built by William I van Brederode & surrounded by a moat is found in this town near the city of Haarlem. It is a and peaceful place, very child friendly, and everyone who was working at the Castel was extremely friendly. Really enjoyed time.

Caprera Open Air Theatre

Caprera Open Air Theatre is a unique outdoor stage located in Bloemendaal, located between dunes and a forest. It operates from March to September and is known for hosting theatre, cabaret, as well as local pop, rock, and alternative music performances. The venue's size is and offers food and drinks at the top before finding a spot to enjoy the show. The sound quality is excellent throughout the venue.

Free

Nationaal Park Zuid-Kennemerland

Nationaal Park Zuid-Kennemerland is a public park in Haarlem, Netherlands. It is an important defense against the sea and is home to native vegetation and designated nature reserves. Paths, lawns, and shaded areas make up the main public facilities. Nationaal Park Zuid-Kennemerland remains part of the public visitor circuit in Haarlem.

Free

Buitenplaats Beeckestijn

Buitenplaats Beeckestijn is a estate that gives visitors a quiet shoreline into nature, featuring beautifully maintained gardens and landscapes. The mansion itself is an elegant structure, showing the charm of historical architecture. While renovations limit access to the upper floors, guests can still explore the ground floor and enjoy a fascinating film about its rich history.
