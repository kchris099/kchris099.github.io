Haarlem

Medieval cloth and bleaching fields made Haarlem rich; Frans Hals and other Golden Age painters worked in the same civic culture. The Grote Kerk and hofjes almshouses along the Spaarne kept a distinct Holland identity a short distance from Amsterdam. The Teylers Museum, founded in 1784, claims status as the country's oldest public museum. Frans Hals Museum galleries occupy a former almshouse complex.

MASCOT Circuit Zandvoort

Circuit Zandvoort is a coastal motorsport track near Haarlem that hosts car and motorcycle races through the year, including Formula One events in recent seasons. Dunes separate the circuit from a long North Sea beach used for swimming and walking. Race weekends draw crowds to both the grandstands and the shore.

Keukenhof

Each spring, Keukenhof displays millions of bulbs planted in changing designs across a landscaped estate near Lisse. Pavilions, woodland paths, ponds, flower shows, and outdoor beds emphasize Dutch horticulture during a limited season. The park's name recalls earlier kitchen-garden use connected with the nearby estate, while exact bulb counts and bloom timing vary by year and weather.

Kasteel Keukenhof

Successive owners altered the house and interiors, which open through scheduled tours or events; the estate is historically related to, but operationally distinct from, the seasonal Keukenhof flower exhibition on neighboring grounds. Keukenhof Castle stands on a seventeenth-century country estate near Lisse, surrounded by formal gardens, woodland, farm buildings, and sculpture.

The St. Bavo Church in Haarlem

Haarlem's Grote Kerk rises over the market square with a late Gothic nave, timber crossing tower, choir, chapels, grave slabs and the monumental Muller organ. Handel and the young Mozart are associated with performances on the instrument. Protestant worship, concerts and heritage visits share the building, whose patron is Saint Bavo despite its Reformed use.

05 AdmissionFree

Amsterdamse Poort, Haarlem

A double-towered brick gate crosses the old route from Haarlem toward Amsterdam, surviving from the city's medieval defenses. Built around the fourteenth century and altered later, Amsterdamse Poort remained after most walls and entrances were demolished. Roads and canals now surround the structure, which is primarily viewed from public streets. Its twin towers remain a clear eastern landmark.

Municipal Theater Haarlem

Stadsschouwburg Haarlem occupies a purpose-built early twentieth-century theater on Wilsonsplein. Behind its formal facade, the auditorium hosts drama, dance, music, comedy and touring productions. Foyers, bars and stage facilities support a working performance house whose role in Haarlem's cultural life comes from live programming and the survival of its original theatrical setting.

Elswout

Elswout developed as a country estate on the wooded dune edge west of Haarlem. Paths pass lawns, water, bridges, follies, service buildings and a never-completed nineteenth-century manor, revealing several layers of landscape design. Protected woodland surrounds the architecture, while events or filming may temporarily limit access to parts of the grounds. Woodland gives the estate a secluded character.

De Cruquius

De Cruquius occupies a nineteenth-century pumping station built to drain the Haarlemmermeer. A large surviving beam engine anchors displays on Dutch water management, land reclamation and steam engineering, with machinery and demonstrations explaining how the system worked. The museum's modern interpretation remains closely tied to the preserved industrial building.

52.3381° N / 4.6381° E

09 AdmissionFree

Brederode Castle

Moated brick ruins near Santpoort preserve Brederode Castle, founded in the thirteenth century and damaged through warfare, flooding and abandonment; seasonal opening and volunteer management affect access, while the site lies outside Haarlem in a dune-edge landscape. Towers, walls, cellars, gate remains and archaeological traces reveal the seat of the Brederode family.

Caprera Open Air Theatre

Between dunes and forest in Bloemendaal, Caprera runs from March to September with theatre, cabaret, and local pop, rock, and alternative music. Food and drinks are sold at the top before seating for the show. Sound carries cleanly across the outdoor bowl.

Nationaal Park Zuid-Kennemerland

Zuid-Kennemerland National Park protects dunes, forests, and beaches west of Haarlem that buffer the North Sea. Marked trails cross habitats for deer and native coastal plants within designated reserves. Visitor centres and cycle paths link the dune ridges with seaside entrances.

Buitenplaats Beeckestijn

Amsterdam merchant families developed Beeckestijn as an eighteenth-century summer estate near Velsen, shaping its mansion and grounds across several generations. Formal gardens, woodland, fountains, pools and later landscape additions record changing ideas of country-house design, while the gardens offer the clearest continuous view of an estate whose interior use follows exhibitions and restoration work.
