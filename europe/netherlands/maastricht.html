Maastricht

Maastricht grew at a Meuse river crossing held by Romans, medieval prince-bishops, and later Dutch and French garrisons. Fortifications, underground marl caves, and Vrijthof square mark that frontier role. The 1992 treaty that created the euro bears the city's name. Basilicas, cobbled shopping streets, and a bilingual edge with Belgium show a Limburg capital shaped by borders and European diplomacy.

Sint Pieter Fort

Fort Sint Pieter was built in the early eighteenth century on a hill south of Maastricht to defend approaches from the Meuse valley. Bastions, casemates, gun positions, and underground connections are interpreted through guided tours. The neighboring limestone quarry passages form a separate tour system, while exterior earthworks and hill paths remain part of the wider landscape.

North Caves Maastricht Underground

Explore the intriguing North Caves Maastricht Underground, a historical network of tunnels and caves that offer a glimpse into the city's past. These subterranean passageways were used for various purposes over many years, from mining to wartime shelter. The Sint Pietersberg is home to some of the most caves in the Netherlands, including North Caves and Zonneberg Caves.

Book Store Dominicanen

Boekhandel Dominicanen occupies a thirteenth-century Dominican church in Maastricht, with bookshelves set under Gothic vaults. Dutch and English titles fill the nave and side aisles, and a café sits among the stacks. The conversion keeps the medieval structure while operating as a large independent bookstore.

Basilica of Saint Servatius

On Vrijthof square, the Romanesque Basilica of Saint Servatius remains a pilgrimage church with a treasury of relics of St. Servatius and other early pieces. Exterior and main interiors are usually viewable outside service times. Stone nave and towers dominate the square's western side.

Sint Servaasbrug

Stone arches carry Sint Servaasbrug across the Meuse between Maastricht's historic center and Wyck; a medieval bridge replaced earlier crossings, while war damage and repeated reconstruction altered much of the present fabric. Pedestrians and cyclists share the deck with controlled traffic, and a movable section allows navigation on the river.

Stadspark Maastricht

Maastricht's Stadspark follows former fortifications and the Jeker near the southern edge of the old city. Connected green sections contain lawns, ponds and walking paths. Defensive walls and monuments preserve the history of the reused military ground, while playgrounds and small animal enclosures support daily recreation within the city.

Speeltuin Fort Willem

Speeltuin Fort Willem is a staffed children's playground on Maastricht's northern side. Seasonal facilities include climbing structures, slides, sand and water play, small rides and a cafe, with special activities added at selected times. The enclosed recreation area is designed for family play and has no permanent zoological collection.

Valkenburg Castle Ruins

Ruins of a major hilltop castle in the Netherlands overlook Valkenburg and the Geul valley. Medieval lords expanded the fortress before French forces destroyed it in the seventeenth century, leaving walls, towers, passages, and foundations above the town. Paths are steep and exposed, while interpretation links the ruins with nearby quarry tunnels and urban defenses.

09 AdmissionFree

Gemeentegrot

Gemeentegrot, also known as Municipal Cave or Cauberg Cavern, is a historic marl quarry dating back to Roman times. It served various purposes over time, including as a WWII hideaway and nuclear fallout shelter. Travellers can explore its underground passages on foot or by taking a slow train tour. The cave is filled with art and hosts unique Christmas markets alongside informative exhibits about marlstone and caves at Mergelrijk.

Rodelbaan Valkenburg

Two alpine-coaster tracks descend the slope below Valkenburg's Wilhelmina Tower under private operation. Riders reach the starting area by chairlift or hillside access before controlling their speed along the winding route. The modern recreation facility combines a short thrill ride with seasonal views across the Limburg landscape. The hillside setting shapes both speed and views.
