Destination Guide — Pula

Explore Pula

A Brief History

Dominated by its magnificent first-century Roman amphitheater, Pula served as a crucial imperial port before passing through Venetian and Austro-Hungarian hands. Its layered architecture reflects a long history as a strategic naval base shaping the Istrian peninsula. This rich historical tapestry established a strong foundation that continues to shape the city's dynamic contemporary identity.

Top Sights & Activities

€10

Pula Arena

Pula Arena, a massive Roman amphitheater in Pula, is a major tourist attraction. Built in 27 BC, it boasts well-preserved four side towers and three architectural orders. Visitors can explore the viewing seats where gladiator fights were once watched and then venture into the underground passages used by gladiators. The arena could accommodate up to 20,000 spectators and is one of the six largest and -preserved Roman arenas still in existence today.

€7

Zerostrasse

Zerostrasse, also known as the underground tunnels, is a historic network of subterranean passages and galleries located in Pula. Initially constructed as war shelters in the early 1900s, these tunnels were designed to protect the city's inhabitants during times of conflict. Extending approximately 400 meters beneath the city center, this intricate system was capable of accommodating thousands of people.

€7

Pula Citadel

Pula Citadel, also known as Pula Castle or Kastel, is a historic fortress constructed by the Venetians in the 1600s. Positioned atop a hill overlooking Pula's Old Town, it offers panoramic views of the city and its bay. The citadel features a star-shaped fortress design, providing strategic advantages for defense and surveillance.

Free

Mosaic The Punishment of Dirce

Located in an isolated area, the well-preserved and finely crafted floor mosaic of 'The Punishment of Dirce' is a remarkable historical landmark. Situated within the Aquarium Pula, this intricate tile work dates back to ancient Roman times. Despite being hidden beneath the ground for years, it was revealed after the aerial bombings during World War II.

€2

Temple of Augustus

The Temple of Augustus, located in the heart of Pula's historic center, is a remarkable ancient Roman temple dedicated to Emperor Augustus. Originally built between 27 BC and AD 14, it stands as a prime example of Roman architecture with its regular square stone blocks. The temple has endured various transformations over the centuries, serving as a church in Byzantine times and later as a granary before becoming a museum of stone monuments.

Free

Arch of the Sergii

The Arch of the Sergii is an ancient Roman triumphal arch located in Pula, Croatia. Built between 29-27 BC, it originally served as a city gate and was dedicated to the powerful Sergii family who held influence over Pula for centuries. This well-preserved arch features intricate stone carvings and is larger than the Twin Gate.

€24

Aquarium Pula

Aquarium Pula is uniquely situated within the historic Austro-Hungarian fortress Verudela, located on a peninsula near Pula. The aquarium showcases a diverse array of marine life from the Adriatic Sea, the Mediterranean, and tropical waters. It also operates a sea turtle rescue center, combining marine education with the preservation of military heritage.

Free

Verudela Canyon

Verudela Canyon is a striking natural formation located on the Verudela Peninsula near Pula. The steep, rocky gorge was carved by the sea into the limestone cliffs, creating a dramatic coastal landscape. It is a popular spot for adventurous cliff jumping, swimming in the clear waters, and exploring the rugged coastal environment.

Free

Church of St. Fosca

The Church of St. Fosca, located near Vodnjan in Istria, is a captivating spiritual destination surrounded by ancient olive trees. Local legends speak of miraculous events associated with this small church, adding to its mystical allure. Visitors have reported experiencing a strong energy field connecting the Brijuni islands to the church. While part of a larger bike or walking tour, the church can also be reached by car despite limited parking.

Free

Chiavalon Extra Virgin Olive Oil

Chiavalon Extra Virgin Olive Oil is produced by the Chiavalon family, who have turned their 100-tree memorial into a thriving business with 9,300 trees over 35 hectares. Their state-of-the-art mill produces a portfolio of six extra virgin olive oils that are enjoyed on tables in Istria and exported to over 23 countries.
