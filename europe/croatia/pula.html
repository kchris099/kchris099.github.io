Pula

Rome built a stone amphitheatre here when Pula was a colony on the Istrian coast. Venetian and Austro-Hungarian navies later used the same harbour. The arena and the naval arsenal, more than any medieval commune hall, explain why Pula mattered as a military port rather than a merchant republic. Film festivals now fill the amphitheatre that once staged Roman games.

Pula Arena

Pula Arena, a massive Roman amphitheater in Pula, is a major tourist attraction. Built in 27 BC, it has well-preserved four side towers and three architectural orders. Visitors can explore the viewing seats where gladiator fights were once watched and then venture into the underground passages used by gladiators. The arena could accommodate up to 20,000 spectators and is one of the six largest and -preserved Roman arenas still in existence today.

Zerostrasse

Zerostrasse is a visitor site in Pula, Croatia. Zerostrasse, also known as the underground tunnels, is a historic network of subterranean passages and galleries located in Pula. Initially constructed as war shelters in the early 1900s, these tunnels were designed to protect the city's inhabitants during times of conflict. The main structures and grounds are the principal points of interest.

Pula Citadel, also known as Pula Castle or Kastel, is a fortified historic site in Pula, Croatia. Pula Citadel, also known as Pula Castle or Kastel, is a historic fortress constructed by the Venetians in the 1600s. Mosaic The Punishment of Dirce is a visitor site in Pula, Croatia. Situated within the Aquarium Pula, this detailed tile work dates back to ancient Roman times. Despite being hidden beneath the ground for years, it was revealed after the aerial bombings during World War II. The main structures and grounds are the principal points of interest.

Temple of Augustus

Temple of Augustus is a religious building in Pula, Croatia. Originally built between 27 BC and AD 14, it is a prime example of Roman architecture with its regular square stone blocks. The temple has endured various transformations over many years, serving as a church in Byzantine times and later as a granary before becoming a museum of stone monuments. The exterior and main interior spaces are usually viewable outside service times.

Aquarium Pula is uniquely situated within the historic Austro-Hungarian fortress Verudela, located on a peninsula near Pula. The aquarium showcases a diverse array of marine life from the Adriatic Sea, the Mediterranean, and tropical waters. Verudela Canyon

Verudela Canyon is a striking natural formation located on the Verudela Peninsula near Pula. The steep, rocky gorge was carved by the sea into the limestone cliffs, creating a dramatic coastal landscape. It is a popular spot for adventurous cliff jumping, swimming in the clear waters, and exploring the rugged coastal environment.

Church of St. Fosca

Fosca, located near Vodnjan in Istria, is a spiritual destination surrounded by ancient olive trees. Local legends speak of miraculous events associated with this small church, adding to its mystical allure. Visitors have reported experiencing a strong energy field connecting the Brijuni islands to the church. While part of a larger bike or walking tour, the church can also be reached by car despite limited parking.
