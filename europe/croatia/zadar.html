Explore Zadar

A Brief History

Established as a regional settlement beyond an older fortified core, the city developed through industrialization in the late 19th century, post-war reconstruction, and modern civic expansion that reshaped its public spaces, markets, and surrounding landscape. Restored religious buildings, civic buildings, and natural features now define the contemporary visitor experience.

Narodni trg

Narodni trg, also known as People's Square, is an ancient square in Zadar surrounded by historic buildings and a popular 11th-century church. It is a central hub for major celebrations and day-to-day life in the city. The square is enclosed by official buildings and features the 16th century City Guard and City Loggia. Nearby attractions include Trg pet bunara (Five Wells Square) and Siroka ulica, also known as Kalelarga.

Free

The Five Wells Square

The Five Wells Square, located near the Land gate, is a historic public square dating back to the 16th century. It is renowned for its five ornate stone wells that were part of a water supply system designed by the Venetians. The square is surrounded by medieval structures, including the Captain's tower embedded in the city walls. travellers can climb to the top of this tower for a panoramic view of the Old Town.

Free

Sea Organ

Nestled along the coastline of Zadar, the Sea Organ is a remarkable fusion of art and nature that captivates travellers with its enchanting melodies. This large experimental musical instrument is ingeniously designed beneath a staircase leading to the sea, where waves interact with a hidden system of pipes and whistles. As the tides ebb and flow, they create hauntingly beautiful sounds that are both unpredictable and mesmerizing.

Free

The Greeting to the Sun

The Greeting to the Sun is a modern artistic installation located in Zadar, consisting of 300 multi-layered glass plates arranged in a circle on the waterfront. Beneath these glass plates are solar modules that create a symbolic connection with nature through light. At sunset, the lighting elements within the circle illuminate and produce a mesmerizing light show synchronized with the waves and sounds of the nearby Sea Organs.

Church of St. Donatus

Saint Donatus’ Church is a large Catholic church with a circular shape that was constructed in the 9th century. It is located near the Roman Forum and St. Anastasia's Cathedral in Zadar, Croatia. The church has a pre-Romanesque style and is considered important landmarks in Dalmatia from that time period. Named after Bishop Donat, it features clean lines, a circular ground plan, and fortress-like architecture.

Grgur Ninski statue

The Grgur Ninski statue in Nin is a significant landmark, featuring a striking bronze sculpture of Bishop Gregory of Nin, created by renowned Croatian sculptor Ivan Strovic. This 10th-century bishop opposed the Pope and advocated for the use of the Croatian language in religious ceremonies. The statue is believed to bring good luck when its big toe is rubbed. While not as famous as its counterpart in Split, this original statue holds historical and cultural importance.

Church of the Holy Cross

In the historic town, consider include a visit to the Church of the Holy Cross, also referred to as the Church of St. Anselm. This ancient religious site, dating back to the 9th century, boasts a striking pre-Romanesque architecture adorned with intricate stone carvings and well-preserved frescoes inside. Despite its small size, it exudes a captivating ambiance that leaves travellers in awe.

Church of St. Nicholas

The Church of St. Nicholas in Nin is a famous and beautiful sight located in the idyllic countryside just outside the town. It has become a landmark thanks to its unique construction and historical significance, as it is said that seven kings were crowned in Nin and each visited the church after their coronation. The church, dating back around 900 years, sits on a hill that appears to be an ancient burial ground and features high stone walls.
