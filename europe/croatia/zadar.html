Zadar

Zadar began as a Liburnian and later Roman settlement on a Adriatic peninsula, with a forum and street grid still readable in the old town. Medieval Croatian and Venetian rule left churches, walls, and a fortified harbour. Austrian administration in the 19th century added civic buildings before Yugoslav and Croatian statehood. World War II bombing damaged much of the centre; reconstruction, the Sea Organ, and ferry links now frame a compact coastal city.
