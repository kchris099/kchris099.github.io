Explore Trogir

A Brief History

Trogir occupies a small island between mainland and Čiovo where Greek colonists founded Tragurion and where Romanesque and Venetian builders later carved the cathedral of St Lawrence and a compact ducal palace. Venetian control from 1420 to 1797 left loggias, city gates, and a waterfront riva on a site prized for shipbuilding and salt trade. Modern bridges link the old centre to wider suburbs, but the island core preserves one of the Adriatic's densest medieval street grids.

Kamerlengo Castle

Kamerlengo Castle, located in Trogir, Croatia, is a small 15th-century fortress that offers views of the city and harbor. Built by the republic of Venice, it began to deteriorate in the late 19th century but was later renewed after World War II. The castle is part of a rich tapestry of historical sites in Trogir, including the impressive Cathedral of St. Lawrence with its Romanesque entrance and the grand Cipiko Palace.

Free

Trogir Old Town

Trogir Old Town is a small medieval town located on an island off the Central Dalmatian coast of Croatia. It boasts impressive architectural landmarks such as St. Lawrence's Cathedral and the Garagnin-Fanfogna palace, which houses the Trogir Town Museum. The town offers a blend of cobblestone streets in the old quarter and beaches along the seaside promenades.

Free

Green Market

Green Market, located near the bridge to Trogir, offers a delightful experience of local life. travellers can explore an array of fresh produce, flowers, honey, spices, wine and cheese. The market is conveniently situated in the center with easy access to restaurants, bus stations, supermarkets and more. It's a favorite spot for its scenic views by the water.

Free

Trogir Promenade

Trogir Promenade is a must-visit attraction in the historic center, featuring the impressive 15th-century Kamerlengo Fortress and Sveti Marko tower. The waterfront promenade, known as Riva, offers a setting with palm trees, spacious walkways, and numerous bars and restaurants. It provides sunset views, opportunities for boat watching, and access to notable landmarks like Kamerlengo Castle and Marmonts Gloriette.

Free

St. Lawrence's Cathedral

Saint Lawrence's Cathedral, located in Trogir, is a prominent 13th-century cathedral with a mix of Romanesque and Gothic architecture. The construction of the cathedral began in the 13th century and was completed in the 17th century, resulting in a blend of different architectural styles. One of its main highlights is the exterior portal created by Croatian sculptor Radovan, featuring intricate designs with exotic animals and fantastical creatures.

Free

Chapel of St. Sebastian

The Chapel of St. Sebastian, constructed in 1477 by Nicholas of Florence to protect the city from the plague, cleverly combines a church and a tower with a city clock in a small space. The facade features statues of St. Sebastian and Christ giving blessings. travellers can climb the narrow spiral staircase to reach the top of the bell tower for panoramic views of the old town, which is over 2000 years old and under UNESCO's protection.

Free

Okrug Gornji Beach

Okrug Gornji Beach, also known as Copacabana Beach, is a 2-km long stretch of pebbled shoreline located in the municipality of Okrug Gornji on Ciovo Island. It's a popular spot for swimming and snorkeling, with amenities such as lifeguards, showers, and chair rentals available. Accessible from Trogir Old Town via a bridge, the beach offers clear waters and opportunities for sunbathing and people-watching.
