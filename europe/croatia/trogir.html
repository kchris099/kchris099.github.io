Trogir

Greek settlers called the island Tragurion; Romanesque and Venetian masons later carved St Lawrence Cathedral and a compact ducal palace inside the walls. From 1420 to 1797 Venice ran shipyards and salt trade from the riva. The island core's street grid is among the densest surviving medieval fabrics on the Adriatic. Kamerlengo fortress closes the western tip of that same fortified island.

Kamerlengo Castle

Kamerlengo Castle, located in Trogir, Croatia, is a small 15th-century fortress that offers views of the city and harbor. Built by the republic of Venice, it began to deteriorate in the late 19th century but was later renewed after World War II. The castle is part of a rich history of historical sites in Trogir, including the Cathedral of St. Lawrence with its Romanesque entrance and the grand Cipiko Palace.

Trogir Old Town

Trogir Old Town is a small medieval town located on an island off the Central Dalmatian coast of Croatia. It has architectural landmarks such as St. Lawrence's Cathedral and the Garagnin-Fanfogna palace, which houses the Trogir Town Museum. The town offers a blend of cobblestone streets in the old quarter and beaches along the seaside promenades.

Green Market

Trogir's Green Market spreads beside the bus station and bridge approach to the old town. Farmers and traders sell seasonal fruit, vegetables and herbs, joined by flowers, cheese and regional pantry goods. Household items and souvenir stands reflect the heavy visitor traffic, but the roadside market still provides a practical view of local food commerce beyond the medieval lanes.

Trogir Promenade

Trogir's Riva runs as a palm-lined waterfront with bars and restaurants facing the channel. Walkers watch boats at sunset and reach Kamerlengo Castle and Marmont's Gloriette along the same shore. The fifteenth-century fortress and Sveti Marko tower sit within short walking distance of the promenade.

St. Lawrence's Cathedral

Construction of Trogir's Cathedral of St Lawrence began in the thirteenth century and continued through several architectural periods. Master Radovan's carved west portal introduces biblical scenes, animals, and local figures, while later Gothic and Renaissance work appears in the Chapel of St John and the bell tower. The cathedral anchors the main square beside the loggia and civic palace.

Chapel of St. Sebastian

The Chapel of St Sebastian in Trogir was built in 1477 by Niccolò di Giovanni Fiorentino as a plague votive that also carries a city clock in its tower. Church and tower share one tight footprint on the square. The small interior opens beside the cathedral lane.

Blue Lagoon Croatia

The Blue Lagoon name commonly refers to the shallow water around the Krknjasi islets off Drvenik Veli, reached on boat excursions from Trogir and nearby ports. Rocky and pebbled edges border clear water used for swimming and snorkeling. Boat traffic, wind and the absence of a single developed beach shape the experience around the small islands.

43.4413° N / 16.1754° E

Okrug Gornji Beach

A long pebbled shoreline curves around Okrug Gornji on Ciovo Island, where dense holiday development reflects the area's role as a resort linked to Trogir by bridge. Often promoted as Copacabana Beach, its most developed section has cafes, restaurants, showers and seasonal water activities, while the shallow approach attracts families during summer.
