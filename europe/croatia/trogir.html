Explore Trogir

A Brief History

Trogir occupies a small island between mainland and Čiovo where Greek colonists founded Tragurion and where Romanesque and Venetian builders later carved the cathedral of St Lawrence and a compact ducal palace. Venetian control from 1420 to 1797 left loggias, city gates, and a waterfront riva on a site prized for shipbuilding and salt trade. Modern bridges link the old centre to wider suburbs, but the island core preserves one of the Adriatic's densest medieval street grids.

Kamerlengo Castle

Kamerlengo Castle, located in Trogir, Croatia, is a small 15th-century fortress that offers views of the city and harbor. Built by the republic of Venice, it began to deteriorate in the late 19th century but was later renewed after World War II. The castle is part of a rich history of historical sites in Trogir, including the Cathedral of St. Lawrence with its Romanesque entrance and the grand Cipiko Palace.

Free

Trogir Old Town

Trogir Old Town is a small medieval town located on an island off the Central Dalmatian coast of Croatia. It has architectural landmarks such as St. Lawrence's Cathedral and the Garagnin-Fanfogna palace, which houses the Trogir Town Museum. The town offers a blend of cobblestone streets in the old quarter and beaches along the seaside promenades.

Free

Green Market

Green Market, located near the bridge to Trogir, offers a experience of local life. Travellers can explore an array of fresh produce, flowers, honey, spices, wine and cheese. The market is conveniently situated in the center with easy access to restaurants, bus stations, supermarkets and more. It's a favorite spot for its scenic views by the water.

Free

Trogir Promenade

Trogir Promenade is a must-visit attraction in the historic center, featuring the 15th-century Kamerlengo Fortress and Sveti Marko tower. The waterfront promenade, known as Riva, offers a setting with palm trees, spacious walkways, and numerous bars and restaurants. It provides sunset views, opportunities for boat watching, and access to notable landmarks like Kamerlengo Castle and Marmonts Gloriette.

Free

St. Lawrence's Cathedral

St. Lawrence's Cathedral is a church in Trogir, Croatia. Saint Lawrence's Cathedral, located in Trogir, is a prominent 13th-century cathedral with a mix of Romanesque and Gothic architecture. The construction of the cathedral began in the 13th century and was completed in the 17th century, resulting in a blend of different architectural styles. The exterior and main interior spaces are usually viewable outside service times.

Free

Chapel of St. Sebastian

Chapel of St. Sebastian is a religious building in Trogir, Croatia. Construction and early use date to around 1477. Sebastian, constructed in 1477 by Nicholas of Florence to protect the city from the plague, cleverly combines a church and a tower with a city clock in a small space.

Okrug Gornji Beach

Okrug Gornji Beach, also known as Copacabana Beach, is a 2-km long stretch of pebbled shoreline located in the municipality of Okrug Gornji on Ciovo Island. It's a popular spot for swimming and snorkeling, with amenities such as lifeguards, showers, and chair rentals available. Accessible from Trogir Old Town via a bridge, the beach offers clear waters and opportunities for sunbathing and people-watching.
