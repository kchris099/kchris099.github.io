A Brief History

Unlike other Dalmatian coastal cities founded by Greeks or Romans, Šibenik was established by Croats in the ninth century. It spent nearly four centuries under the Venetian Republic, which fortified the city against Ottoman incursions and heavily influenced its Renaissance and Gothic architectural development. The city's wealth peaked in the fifteenth and sixteenth centuries through maritime trade and salt production. This Venetian-Dalmatian golden age is spectacularly preserved in the Cathedral of St. James and the city's four defensive fortresses.

St. Michael's Fortress

St. Michael's Fortress is a fortified historic site in Šibenik, Croatia. Records associated with the site include 1000. Michael's Fortress, located in the city center, is a restored medieval fort that offers guided tours and hosts various exhibits. It has an open-air summer stage with just over 1000 seats, making it an exclusive venue for cultural events and performances. Walls, courtyards, or towers form the main visitor route when the grounds are open.

Cathedral of St. James

Cathedral of St. James is a religious building in Šibenik, Croatia. The cathedral has over 70 sculpted heads on its facade and is considered one of Croatia's greatest architectural masterpieces. Built entirely of stone without any binding material, it showcases a blend of Gothic, Renaissance, and Gothic-Renaissance styles. The exterior and main interior spaces are usually viewable outside service times.

Barone Fortress

Barone Fortress, a 17th-century stronghold perched on a hill above Sibenik, was constructed in just two months to protect the city from an impending Ottoman invasion. Following extensive restoration in 2016, the fortress now has a visitor's center and interactive exhibits using augmented reality technology to immerse visitors in its historical significance.

€25

St. Nicholas’ Fortress

St. Nicholas Fortress is a 16th-century fortress located on the small island of Ljuljevac, at the entrance to St. Anthony's Channel near Sibenik. It is part of the UNESCO World Heritage Site and was built as a defense system against Turkish attacks from the sea. The fortress offers an intriguing glimpse into its historical significance and architectural design by Venetian builder Hyeronimus di San Michaela.

Free

St. Anthony’s Canal Hiking Trail

St. Anthony’s Canal Hiking Trail in Šibenik offers a 2.5 km paved coastal path along an inlet, providing views of the city and opportunities for family hikes. The trail is rich in natural beauty, featuring Aleppo pine forests and over 600 plant species. Along the peninsula leading to St Nicholas Fortress, hikers can explore pristine coves, clear blue waters, and shaded pine forests.

€7

Skradinski Buk waterfall

Skradinski Buk waterfall is a natural attraction located in the Krka National Park. Visitors can access the waterfall by taking a short cruise up the emerald Krka river, passing through a limestone gorge. The area offers trails, viewing spots, and even a roped-off swimming area where visitors can cool off in the clear waters. The surroundings are filled with lush woodland and tumbling pools that provide a chance to observe fish and dragonflies.

Free

Dalmatian Ethno Village

Dalmatian Ethno Village is a visitor site in Šibenik, Croatia. Dalmatian Ethno Village, located within Amadria Park Resort, offers a wide range of facilities including a wellness center, water park, shops, bars, and restaurants. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

€10

Falconry center

Sokolarski Centar in Dubrava, a short drive from Sibenik, is a falconry school and rescue centre for injured birds of prey founded by Emilio Mendjus. Visitors hear talks on falconry history and bird biology, then watch trained falcons and owls during handling demonstrations. The site combines conservation work with public education rather than a free-flight spectacle alone.

Free

Dolac

Dolac is a visitor site in Šibenik. It remains a notable local point of interest situated in Šibenik, Croatia. Visitors can observe the surrounding area and its connection to the city's broader historical landscape. Local guides sometimes include it on walking routes through the centre.
