Explore Split

A Brief History

Split grew within the palace Emperor Diocletian built for his retirement after 305 CE, whose corridors and mausoleum later became the medieval cathedral and civic core. Byzantine, Venetian, and Austrian rulers adapted the Roman shell into houses, markets, and fortifications facing the Adriatic. The waterfront Riva and palace cellars still organise the city, while ferries to the islands extend a role Split has played for seventeen centuries as Dalmatia's main harbour.

Klis Fortress

Perched on a hilltop with commanding views, Fortress Klis is a centuries-old stronghold near the village of Klis, just 9 km from Split. Its strategic position at an altitude of 360m made it a key defensive point throughout history. The fortress consists of three parts, each separated by gates, and features armories, barracks, towers, the duke's residence, and a church.

Free

Church of Our Lady

The Church of Our Lady, completed in 1937, stands as a example of Modernist architecture with its sleek and simple lines. next to a friary established in 1723, this church is not only an important religious site for the people of Dalmatia and Croatia but also offers a serene public space. The surrounding park features well-maintained lawns and immaculate gardens, making it used for family outings or leisurely strolls.

Free (substructures €10)

Diocletian's Palace

Diocletian's Palace, located in Split, Croatia, was built for the Roman emperor Diocletian in the 4th century AD. Although referred to as a palace, it is more like a massive fortress with half of it serving as Diocletian's residence and the rest housing a military garrison. Today, this ancient site is part of Split's historic core and is listed as a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Free

Jupiter's Temple

The Temple of Jupiter, located within the grounds of Diocletian's Palace in Split, Croatia, is a significant ancient landmark dating back to the 3rd century. Dedicated to Jupiter and featuring an Egyptian sphinx sculpted from black granite, this well-preserved Roman temple is a attraction. The temple was once supported by massive columns, with only one remaining today.

€10

Diocletian's Cellars

When visiting Split, Croatia, a attraction is Diocletian's Cellars located within the old town. These ancient cellars are part of Diocletian's Palace and offer a glimpse into Roman history and architecture. Accessible by stairs, the cellars may be a bit slick and steep but are worth exploring. While some areas require an entrance fee, the free section leading to the Riva provides an impressive experience.

Free

Split Riva

Split Riva is a lively waterfront area with a wide boardwalk, various dining options, and picturesque vistas. The majority of the attractions are situated within the old town, including Diocletian's palace walls and gates, Peristyle square, St. Domnius Cathedral & Bell Tower, as well as cultural events like Christmas markets and summer concerts.

Free

Split Old Town Hall

Old Town Hall, dating back to 1443, features Gothic windows and a loggia. It now serves as a venue for temporary exhibitions. Situated between the Old Town and historical center of Split, this UNESCO World Heritage site offers an array of activities such as exploring independent shops and cafes, admiring the architecture, and joining guided walking tours to delve into the city's rich history. Golden Gate

The Golden Gate is a 4th-century Roman stone entryway located at the north end of Diocletian's Palace in Split. This ornate gate, adorned with arches and statues, served as the portal to Salona and features a guardhouse housing the 9th-century Church of St. Martin.

Free

Green Market

Green Market, located near the east wall of Diocletian's Palace in Split Old Town, is a bustling outdoor farmers' market offering a wide variety of fresh produce and local products. The market features stalls selling fruits, vegetables, and a large selection of meats sourced from local farms. It is considered an essential activity when visiting Split, seamlessly blending into the tapestry of attractions in the area.

Free

Poljud Stadium

Poljud Stadium, built for the 1979 Mediterranean Games, is a top sporting arena in Split with seating for around 34,000. It's also a great place to stay if 're in town for a game or any of the city's events and cultural attractions like music festivals. Located just outside of Old Town, it offers easy access to the city center without the noise and crowds. The neighborhood surrounding Poljud Stadium also features Park Mladosti, home of the popular Ultra Festival.
