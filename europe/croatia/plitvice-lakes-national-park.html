Plitvice Lakes National Park

Plitvice Lakes grows out of a karst valley on the Korana river system, where natural travertine barriers have built sixteen terraced lakes over the past 10,000 years of calcium carbonate deposition in the Dinaric Alps. The site was protected in 1949 under the Socialist Republic of Croatia and UNESCO inscribed it on the World Heritage list in 1979, drawing the present visitor circuit along wooden walkways spanning the upper and lower lake terraces. The surrounding 295-square-kilometre reserve includes beech and fir forests extended under the UNESCO Ancient and Primeval Beech Forests serial nomination in 2017.

Plitvice Lakes National Park (adult April-October)

Plitvice Lakes National Park protects sixteen terraced travertine lakes linked by falls along the upper Korana in the Dinaric Alps, inscribed by UNESCO in 1979. The 295-square-kilometre karst reserve includes upper lakes such as Prošćansko and Ciginovac, reached by boat shuttle, and lower lakes linked by wooden boardwalks ending near Sastavci waterfall.

Veliki Slap (highest waterfall)

Veliki Slap is the 78-metre waterfall at the foot of the Lower Lakes cascade in Plitvice Lakes National Park, where the Plitvica stream drops off a vertical cliff into a moss-backed gorge below the principal boardwalk circuit. The fall is reached via a side trail branching west from the Sastavci confluence, with the descent equipped with wooden staircases upgraded during the 1990s. Visitors emerge onto a wet-rock viewing platform at the foot of the fall, included in the EUR40 Lower Lakes ticket.

Veliko Jezero

Veliko Jezero is the largest lake in the Lower Lakes system of Plitvice Lakes National Park, into which four cascading streams converge into a basin roughly 2.5 hectares wide at the eastern end of the lower boardwalk circuit. The lake is enclosed by the Milanovac and Gavanovac cascades and is the principal ferry staging point connecting the lower lakes with the Prošćansko lake above. Travellers reach it on the Lower Lakes walking trail, included in the EUR40 ticket.

44.8530° N / 15.6140° E
