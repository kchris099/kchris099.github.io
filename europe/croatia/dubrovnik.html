Explore Dubrovnik

A Brief History

Dubrovnik grew as the maritime republic of Ragusa, trading across the Adriatic and Mediterranean from the Middle Ages until the 19th century. Massive stone walls, completed largely by the 15th century, enclosed churches, palaces, and a public loggia. The city endured siege damage in the 1990s and underwent restoration of its limestone streets and roofs. UNESCO inscribed the Old City as a World Heritage site in 1979.

Lovrijenac Fortress

Standing on a cliff west of the old town walls, this fortress is nicknamed 'Dubrovnik's Gibraltar.' First recorded in 1301, it guarded the sea approach against Venetian pressure. Today it hosts summer performances of Hamlet and other plays within its stone chambers overlooking the Adriatic.

Free

Pile Gate

Main western entrance to Dubrovnik's walled old town, rebuilt in 1537 with a stone bridge and former drawbridge. Guards once raised the wooden span each night to seal the Republic of Ragusa. The gate leads directly onto Stradun, the principal pedestrian street through the historic center.

Free

Stradun

Stradun is a visitor site in Dubrovnik. Limestone-paved main street through Dubrovnik's old town, also called Placa. Shaped since the 13th century, it took its uniform baroque appearance after the 1667 earthquake. Shops, cafes, and churches line the pedestrian corridor between Pile and Ploce gates.

€30

Dubrovnik Cable Car

Opened in 1969 to link the old town with Mount Srđ, this cable car rises above the fortifications in minutes. Damaged during the 1990s conflict, it was rebuilt and reopened in 2010. The upper station offers wide views over the walled city, bay, and Elafiti islands.

Bokar Tower

Fort Bokar, a massive fortress built in the 15th century, stands proudly by the ocean with its distinctive cylindrical architecture. This historic site has been featured in popular culture, notably in Game of Thrones. It is part of Dubrovnik's formidable defensive system and was frequently showcased in the series as characters walked along the city walls.

Free

Fortress Imperial

Fort Imperial, also known as Emperors Fort, is a historic fortress located in Dubrovnik. Built between 1806 and 1812 during Napoleon's rule, it was later expanded by the Austrians for defense against the Ottoman Empire. During the Homeland War from 1991 to 1995, the fort played a strategic role in defending Dubrovnik.

Free

The Cathedral of the Assumption of the Virgin Mary

The Cathedral of the Assumption of the Virgin Mary, situated in Dubrovnik's Old Town, is a example of Baroque architecture. It was constructed in 1673 to replace the original 12th-century cathedral that was destroyed in an earthquake. The cathedral has an opulent interior adorned with a painting of the Assumption of Mary by known Italian artist Titian.

Free

Old Port of Dubrovnik

The old port of Dubrovnik, once a busy trading and maritime center, now is a gateway for island exploration and coastal excursions. Guarded by ancient fortresses like St. John, and Revelin, the port is surrounded by well-preserved Renaissance, Gothic, and Baroque structures that showcase the city's prosperous history.

Free

Porat Dubrovnik

Porat Dubrovnik is the historic Old Port located on the eastern side of Dubrovnik's walled city. For centuries, it served as the maritime hub of the Republic of Ragusa. Today, the harbor is filled with small fishing boats and excursion vessels, offering a setting for a stroll and providing access to nearby islands like Lokrum.

Sponza Palace, a 16th-century Gothic-Renaissance structure in Dubrovnik, has a rich history. It was originally a customs office and later served as a mint, state treasury, bank, and meeting place for the city's intellectuals. Walls, courtyards, or towers form the main visitor route when the grounds are open.

Saint Blaise’s Church

Saint Blaise's Church is a religious building in Dubrovnik, Croatia. Construction and early use date to around 1715. Saint Blaise's Church is a Venetian baroque-style church that was constructed in 1715. The exterior and main interior spaces are usually viewable outside service times.

Free

Jesuit Stairs

Jesuit Stairs, also known as the Historic Stairs, gained fame as the setting for Cersei's Walk of Shame in Game of Thrones. Located in Dubrovnik's old town, these steps are a must-visit. Nearby restaurants use pulleys to transport food and drinks to their terraces above, adding to the charm of the area. During filming, neighbors requested compensation from HBO to keep their blinds closed and maintain quiet surroundings.

Free

Buza Gate

Buza Gate is an elevated entryway to the walls of Dubrovnik, offering views of the city and access through a stepped alley. It leads to a popular open-air bar with tables arranged on different levels on the rocks, providing a sublime view of the sea and sky. Travellers can even climb down to the water for a swim. Many history-themed free tours in Dubrovnik start at Buza Gate or nearby landmarks like Amerling fountain or Nautika restaurant.

Free

Fort Revelin

Located Dubrovnik, Tvrdava Revelin is a 15th-century fortress that has transformed into a active nightclub and event space. With its architecture dating back to 1463, this historic site offers views of the port and old town, making it an ideal spot for both day and night adventures. Travellers can explore its archaeological museum or enjoy a meal at nearby restaurants while soaking in the scenery.

Free

Fort Royal

Fort Royal is a 19th-century fortress located on the island of Lokrum. The steep trail leading to the ruins offers views of the surrounding sea and landscape. Travellers can explore various attractions such as the Iron Throne from Game of Thrones, FKK Rocks Beach, and the salty Dead Sea swimming hole. The fort itself provides a historical perspective, with its construction dating back to Napoleon's occupation of Croatia.
