Croatia

Croatia's Adriatic coast followed a different history from its inland plains, beginning with Roman centers such as Pula and Diocletian's Palace in Split. Venetian authority shaped many ports, while Dubrovnik prospered as the independent Republic of Ragusa and the Habsburgs governed much of the interior. After Yugoslav rule and the war that followed independence in 1991, Zagreb and Vukovar came to hold very different memories of the modern state.
