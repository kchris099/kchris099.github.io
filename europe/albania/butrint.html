Butrint

Originally a Greek colony and later a prosperous Roman city, Butrint occupied a strategic position on the Straits of Corfu. It expanded significantly under the Roman Empire with the construction of an aqueduct, a forum, and public baths before becoming an important bishopric in the Byzantine era. After centuries of decline and Venetian control, the site was eventually abandoned due to marshland expansion. The diverse archaeological ruins, including the Roman theater, early Christian baptistery, and Venetian castle, now offer visitors a condensed view of Mediterranean history.

Lëkurësi Castle

Kalaja e Lëkurësit, or Lekuresi Castle, crowns a hill above Saranda with round Ottoman towers from the sixteenth century. Stone curtains look out over the Strait of Corfu and the Butrint lagoon. A later restaurant building stands on part of the ruin, while the surviving bastions and courtyard remain the main historic fabric of the site.

Saranda Embankment

Saranda's seafront promenade follows the curve of the bay between the passenger port and the city's central beaches, and the embankment is the city's main public waterfront and an evening gathering place for residents. Restaurants, cafes, benches, and small public squares line the pedestrian route, while ferries and excursion boats remain visible across the water.

39.8735° N / 20.0104° E

03 AdmissionFree

Port of Saranda

Passenger ferries linking Saranda with Corfu use this compact harbour on the city's central waterfront. The terminal handles immigration and customs beside berths for excursion boats and local vessels. Shops, exchange offices, restaurants, and the seafront promenade stand immediately outside, making the port both a transport gateway and part of daily activity around the bay.

Butrint Ancient Theatre

The Ancient Theatre, dating back to 200 BC, offers a fascinating glimpse into history and entertainment. From gladiator fights in Roman times to classical music concerts during summer festivals, it has been a versatile venue. The nearby family-owned Gladiator Restaurant provides views of the theatre from its balcony. Visitors can also explore the area around the theatre, but should be mindful of bees and mosquitoes.

39.7456° N / 20.0205° E

Butrint Museum

Opened in 1938, Butrint Museum occupies the reconstructed Venetian tower near the entrance to the archaeological site; the collection connects the ruins outside with the Greek, Roman, Byzantine and Venetian communities that occupied Butrint. Displays bring together sculpture, inscriptions, pottery, coins and smaller objects recovered during excavations.

39.7463° N / 20.0200° E

06 AdmissionALL 1,000

Butrint National Archaeological Park

Iron Age layers open the record at Butrint National Archaeological Park, where ruins spread through wetland and woodland south of Sarande. Paths thread among walls, theatres, and baptisteries without cordoning every stone. Sheltered galleries keep regular hours for labelled displays beside the open-air circuit.

39.7440° N / 20.0195° E

07 AdmissionFree

Plazhi Ksamilit

Ksamil Beach stretches along Albania's Ionian shore south of Saranda, a short run from the ruins at Butrint. White pebbles and shallow turquoise water meet small rocky coves, with cafes and rental chairs lining the sand. Ferry traffic and day boats from the port keep the bay busy in summer, while quieter mornings show clearer water and longer views toward Corfu.

39.7730° N / 20.0003° E

Lori Beach

Lori Beach occupies a small sheltered cove south of Ksamil, with a narrow sandy shore and shallow water close to the beach. Sunbeds and umbrellas cover much of the developed section, and a seasonal restaurant serves the bathing area, and the compact setting suits a short swim, though facilities and available space depend on summer operations.

09 AdmissionFree

The Last Bay

The Last Bay is a small pebble beach about a 10-15 minute walk from central Ksamil. It has clearer water and fewer vendors than the busier main beaches nearby, where music and denser crowds are common. Visitors use it for swimming and quiet shoreline walks rather than nightlife.

39.7680° N / 19.9910° E

10 AdmissionFree

Pulëbardha Beach

Turquoise water meets a pebble shore at Pulebardha Beach near Sarande, with cliffs framing the cove. Sunbathers and swimmers use the strand between dips, while the wider coast also holds cooler inland pools such as Syri Kalter. Rock and clear water, not a built-up resort strip, set the character here.

39.8001° N / 20.0007° E
