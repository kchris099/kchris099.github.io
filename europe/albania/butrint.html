Explore Butrint

A Brief History

Originally a Greek colony and later a prosperous Roman city, Butrint occupied a strategic position on the Straits of Corfu. It expanded significantly under the Roman Empire with the construction of an aqueduct, a forum, and public baths before becoming an important bishopric in the Byzantine era. After centuries of decline and Venetian control, the site was eventually abandoned due to marshland expansion. The diverse archaeological ruins, including the Roman theater, early Christian baptistery, and Venetian castle, now offer visitors a condensed view of Mediterranean history.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

Lëkurësi Castle

Kalaja e Lëkurësit, also known as the Castle of Lekuresi, is a hilltop castle ruin in Vlore with imposing round towers and panoramic views. The site includes a spacious eatery where visitors can enjoy drinks and watch the sunset. It is situated near natural attractions like the Lagoon of Narta, just a short drive away. The modern building constructed on top of ancient Ottoman castle ruins from the 1500s offers free entry and ample parking options.

Free

Saranda Embankment

Saranda Embankment, also known as the promenade, offers a modern and active atmosphere with numerous restaurants, coffee shops, and bars along the shoreline. The area is well-maintained with trash bins and recycling points to keep the surroundings clean. While there may be some presence of beggars or mischievous children, efforts are being made to improve the area. Visitors can enjoy ample seating and a clean environment while taking in the scenic views.

Free

Port of Saranda

Port of Saranda is a well-organized and convenient port located in the city. The staff are helpful and efficient, making it easy for travelers to navigate through the port. The surrounding area offers attractions such as clear waters, restaurants, souvenir shops, and bars. Visitors can also find currency exchange services nearby, there are small rock beaches where one can relax and enjoy the views.

ALL 1,000

Butrint Ancient Theatre

The Ancient Theatre, dating back to 200 BC, offers a fascinating glimpse into history and entertainment. From gladiator fights in Roman times to classical music concerts during summer festivals, it has been a versatile venue. The nearby family-owned Gladiator Restaurant provides views of the theatre from its balcony. Visitors can also explore the area around the theatre, but should be mindful of bees and mosquitoes.

ALL 1,000

Butrint Museum

The Butrint Museum, established in 1938, is a treasure trove for history enthusiasts. It showcases an collection of Graeco-Roman artifacts and items unearthed from excavations within Butrint. Visitors can immerse themselves in the ancient history of this heavily fortified settlement and former trading center.

Butrint National Archaeological Park

Butrint National Archaeological Park is a museum in Butrint, Albania. The park's archaeological significance dates back to the Iron Age and offers an authentic experience with unrestricted access to the ruins. Galleries and public rooms are open on posted hours in Butrint.

The staff are helpful and efficient, making it easy for travelers to navigate through the port. The surrounding area offers attractions such as clear waters, restaurants, souvenir shops, and bars. Visitors can also find currency exchange services nearby, there are small rock beaches where one can relax and enjoy the views.

Free

Lori Beach

Lori Beach is a small cove that has of a sandy beach, shallow waters and sun lounges for rent. Visitors can also enjoy snacks at the restaurant. Hammocks and sun umbrellas can be rented for 1,000 Lekes for the entire day. Private boat trips are available from local men in little boats, costing around 3,000 Lekes.

Free

The Last Bay

The Last Bay is a small pebble beach about a 10-15 minute walk from central Ksamil. It has clearer water and fewer vendors than the busier main beaches nearby, where music and denser crowds are common. Visitors use it for swimming and quiet shoreline walks rather than nightlife.

Free

Pulëbardha Beach

Pulëbardha Beach is a coastal destination situated in Sarande, Albania, known for its alluring turquoise water and striking cliffs that frame the pebble beach. It's an ideal spot for sunbathing and lounging while enjoying the scenery. Besides Pulëbardha Beach, there are other gorgeous beaches to explore in Sarande, including the known Blue Eye (Syri Kalter), which offers frigid waters unique to Albania.
