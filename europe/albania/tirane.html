Tiranë

Tirana became Albania's capital in 1920 after Ottoman rule and a brief period of independence following the First Balkan War. Italianate boulevards, communist-era housing blocks, and post-1990 painted façades record monarchy, Enver Hoxha's isolationist regime, and democratic reforms. Skanderbeg Square anchors ministries, museums, and mosques built across the 20th century.

Tirana Castle

Tirana Castle, located in the historic center of Tirana, is a medieval fortress with an Ottoman-era wall that now houses handicraft stores, eateries, art galleries, and a hotel. The castle represents a field fortification with a rectangular plan and is thought to belong to the fortifications built by Emperor Justinian in the 4th-6th century.

Mosque of Namazgah

Namazgah Mosque, also called the Great Mosque of Tirana, is the largest mosque in the Balkans. Construction began after the end of communist rule to give the city a central congregational mosque. Its exterior combines Ottoman-inspired forms with contemporary detailing, and the complex includes prayer halls and a large plaza.

Tanners' Bridge

Tanners' Bridge is a restored 18th-century stone bridge in Tirana, offering a glimpse into the city's Ottoman history. Originally part of the Saint George Road connecting Tirana with the eastern highlands, it served as the main route for farmers bringing their livestock to the butchery and leatherworking district.

Air Albania Stadium (Arena Kombetare) seats about 22,500 and opened in 2019 in Tirana. It is the home ground of the Albanian national football team and also hosts other sports events. The bowl sits in a dense urban block with modern stands and match-day facilities around the pitch.

05 AdmissionFree

Pyramid of Tirana

The Pyramid of Tirana, originally known as the Enver Hoxha Museum, is a distinctive pyramidal structure in the city. It was built in 1988 to honor Enver Hoxha, the former leader of Communist Albania. Designed by Hoxha's daughter and her husband, it was intended to be a museum showing his legacy. However, after his death, it was repurposed as a conference center.

Postbllok

Postbllok is a small memorial park near the former residence of communist leader Enver Hoxha in Tirana. It preserves one of the regime's concrete bunkers and the Memorial to Communist Isolation, which commemorates political prisoners. Open plaques and outdoor exhibits explain surveillance and detention practices from the Hoxha years.

41.3210° N / 19.8201° E

Et'hem Bej Mosque

In the city center, find the Et'hem Bej Mosque, an 18th-century historical place of worship that stands out for its colorful frescoes. This mosque is a significant site in Albania's history as it miraculously survived destruction during the communist era due to its detailed nature motifs and murals, which were valued for their cultural and artistic significance.

Orthodox Cathedral of Resurrection

The Resurrection of Christ Orthodox Cathedral, completed in 2012, is a prominent landmark in Tirana. It stands out with its modern marble facade and a 46-meter bell tower that adds to its unique appearance. The cathedral complex includes the main cathedral, residence of the Holy Synod, Chapel of the Nativity, cultural center, small chapel, and library.
