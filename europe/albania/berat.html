Explore Berat

A Brief History

Berat developed on the Osum gorge where Byzantine churches, Ottoman mosques, and timber-balconied houses climb the castle hill called the city of a thousand windows. Illyrian, Roman, and Angevin rulers each fortified the site before it became a centre of Bektashi Sufi learning and regional administration under Ottoman rule. Communist-era industry on the plain contrasted with the preserved Mangalem and Gorica quarters, which now embody Albania's UNESCO-listed model of hillside urban continuity.

Berat Castle

Berat Castle, also known as Kala, is a historic fortress located in the city of Berat. Dating back to the 4th century BC, it has been continuously inhabited and holds a rich history influenced by Byzantines, Venetians, and Ottomans. The castle neighborhood offers convenient parking options for travellers. It's to visit during summer when the weather is pleasant to enjoy views of the river and city from its walls.

Free

Old Town Berat

Old Town Berat, a UNESCO World Heritage Site in Albania, is a haven for history enthusiasts and culture lovers. The area's rich history is evident in its alleys and buildings, offering easy access to historical sites, artisan shops, and traditional eateries. Known as 'the town of a thousand windows,' the picturesque houses on the steep hill provide bird's eye views.

Free

Berat Viewing Platform

When visiting Berat, make sure to stop by the Berat Viewing Platform for a panoramic view of the Gorica Bridge, Osum River, and the Gorica Quarter. This vantage point offers vistas of the old city and is easily accessible by walking or driving. While there may be some litter along the way, reaching this free attraction is well worth it for its unparalleled views.

Free

Gorica Bridge

Gorica Bridge, located in Berat, Albania, is a renowned 18th-century Ottoman stone bridge that connects the neighborhoods of Mangalem and Gorica over the Osum River. This structure offers panoramic views of Berat's old town and the river below. With its seven arches and strategic location at the foot of a mountain, it has become a popular spot for photographers and tourists alike.

Free

River Side Camping

During my recent adventure in Berat, I had the pleasure of staying at River Side Camping, a spot that truly feels like home. conveniently close to the city center—just a 15-minute stroll away—this campsite boasts immaculate pitches and well-maintained facilities. The friendly family owners are fluent in Italian and go above and beyond to ensure guests feel welcome, even offering fresh oranges as a delightful surprise upon arrival.
