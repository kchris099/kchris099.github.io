Berat

Berat developed on the Osum gorge where Byzantine churches, Ottoman mosques, and timber-balconied houses climb the castle hill called the city of a thousand windows. Illyrian, Roman, and Angevin rulers each fortified the site before it became a centre of Bektashi Sufi learning and regional administration under Ottoman rule. Communist-era industry on the plain contrasted with the preserved Mangalem and Gorica quarters, which now embody Albania's UNESCO-listed model of hillside urban continuity.

Berat Castle

Also called Kala, Berat Castle rises above the Osum on foundations that reach the 4th century BC. Continuous settlement left Byzantine, Venetian, and Ottoman layers in the walls and in houses still lived in inside the enclosure. From the ramparts the river bend and the white houses of the old town sit in clear view on fair summer days.

Onufri National Iconographic Museum

Housed in the Church of the Dormition of Saint Mary inside Berat Castle, the Onufri National Iconographic Museum displays Albanian Orthodox art from the post-Byzantine period. Its galleries include icons attributed to Onufri and other painters, while the church interior retains a carved and gilded iconostasis and fragments of wall painting.

40.7091° N / 19.9454° E

03 AdmissionFree

Old Town Berat

White Ottoman-era houses climb the slopes above the Osum River in Berat's Mangalem and Gorica quarters; narrow lanes connect stone homes with mosques, churches and the inhabited castle above the town. Together, these neighborhoods form the historic centers shared by Berat and Gjirokastra and help explain Berat's familiar 'thousand windows' nickname.

40.7058° N / 19.9499° E

04 AdmissionFree

Berat Viewing Platform

From a hillside above Berat, this public viewing area brings the Osum River, Gorica Bridge and the white Ottoman-era houses of Mangalem and Gorica into a single panorama. Roads and footpaths climb from the old town to the open terrace. The viewpoint is most useful for understanding how the castle ridge, river valley and historic neighborhoods fit together.

40.7057° N / 19.9473° E

05 AdmissionFree

Gorica Bridge

Seven stone arches carry Gorica Bridge across the Osum River between Berat's Mangalem and Gorica quarters. The present bridge dates mainly from the eighteenth century and replaced an earlier wooden crossing. Its slightly uneven deck, stone parapets, and position below the castle make the structure an important part of the city's historic riverfront.

40.7032° N / 19.9446° E

River Side Camping

River Side Camping provides tent and camper pitches beside the Osum River within a short walk of central Berat. The family-run site has showers, toilets, electricity; water points, Wi-Fi and a shared kitchen for guests arriving by bicycle, motorcycle, car or campervan. It is a practical base for reaching the old town and castle.
