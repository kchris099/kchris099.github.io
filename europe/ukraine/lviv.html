Church of the Most Holy Apostles Peter and Paul

The Church of the Most Holy Apostles Peter and Paul in Lviv is a example of Baroque architecture from the late 17th century. It boasts ornate colonnades, a marble and gilt nave, and frescoed side chapels. The church's interior is adorned with intricate frescoes, sculptures, and other works of art. Notably, it houses a historic organ considered one of Europe's.

Lviv High Castle

Lviv High Castle, perched atop a historic hill, is a must-visit for anyone exploring the enchanting city of Lviv. This site not only offers panoramic views of the city and its picturesque surroundings but also serves as a reminder of its storied past. Once home to an impressive castle that dates back to the Middle Ages, this location has long been recognized as a strategic lookout point.

Free

Italian Courtyard

The Italian Courtyard is a charming and romantic spot located near Market Square in Lviv. This palace courtyard boasts beautiful colonnaded balconies, offering a serene escape from the bustling city center. Visitors can enjoy al fresco dining and live music performances in this picturesque setting. The architectural design reflects the Renaissance era, with stone houses limited to three windows on their facades according to historical laws.

Citadel Inn

Nestled atop a hill, the Citadel Inn Hotel & Resort is a charming blend of history and luxury, housed in a former castle that dates back to the 19th century. This genteel hotel offers guests an opportunity to immerse themselves in Lviv's rich past while enjoying modern amenities like free Wi-Fi and breakfast. With its unique architecture and views from the balconies, visitors can soak in the beauty of their surroundings.

Lviv National Opera

The Lviv National Opera, a grand theater established in 1900, boasts an elaborate facade with columns and hosts captivating opera and ballet productions. Situated near Rynok Square, the opera house is surrounded by notable landmarks such as the Armenian Church, St. George's Cathedral, and the High Castle Mountain.

Armenian Cathedral of Lviv

The Armenian Cathedral of Lviv, dedicated to the Armenian Apostolic Church, is a significant religious landmark in the city. Dating back to the 14th century, it stands as one of the few remaining examples of Armenian architecture in Ukraine. The cathedral has undergone various renovations over time and today showcases well-preserved medieval architecture.

Church of the Transfiguration in Lviv

The Church of the Transfiguration in Lviv is a Greek Catholic church with a rich history. It was founded in 1731 and later reconstructed, featuring a striking copper dome and ornate interior. The church, constructed between 1875-1898, replaced the former Catholic church and monastery of the Trinitarian Order. Situated just west of the Armenian Cathedral, this hidden gem boasts classic Orthodox grandeur and is often overlooked by tourists despite its beauty.

Lviv Circus stands out as a beloved destination for family outings in the heart of Lviv. Visitors can expect thrilling acts featuring acrobats and animals that evoke cherished childhood memories.

Lviv National Philharmonic

The Lviv National Philharmonic, established in the late 19th century, stands as one of the country's oldest and most esteemed orchestras. Renowned for its top-notch performances, it holds a significant place in Lviv's cultural scene. The orchestra showcases a diverse repertoire spanning from baroque to contemporary pieces and is celebrated for delivering concerts of exceptional artistic value.

Stryiskyi Park

Stryiskyi Park is a sprawling, lush green space featuring a picturesque swan pond and scenic walking trails. The park also houses a tropical greenhouse showcasing an array of cacti. Situated in an upscale neighborhood, the park boasts elegant Belle Epoche architecture and various architectural gems like villas, old houses, and Neo-Moorish style buildings nearby. It's a perfect spot for leisurely strolls amidst its hills, green lawns, and snowy fields during winter.

Free

Храм Свв. Ольги і Єлизавети УГКЦ

The Church of Saints Olga and Elizabeth, a neo-Gothic masterpiece located in Lviv, is often referred to as a monument to imperial love. Constructed in 1911, this architectural gem stands tall with three impressive towers reaching 86 meters high, making it the second tallest structure in the city. Named after Empress Elizabeth of Bavaria—affectionately known as Sisi—the church serves as a poignant tribute to her legacy following her tragic assassination.
