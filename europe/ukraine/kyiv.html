Kyiv

Kyiv was founded in the fifth century and grew to become the powerful capital of Kievan Rus', the cradle of East Slavic civilization. It suffered near-total destruction during the Mongol invasion of 1240 and subsequently fell under Lithuanian, Polish, and Russian rule. In the twentieth century, the city endured the devastation of World War II and the Soviet era before becoming the capital of an independent Ukraine in 1991. Today, Kyiv is defined by its resilient spirit, golden-domed cathedrals, and ongoing struggle for democratic sovereignty.

St. Andrew's Church

Saint Andrew's Church stands above Kyiv's Podil district at the end of Andriivskyi Descent. Designed in the eighteenth century under Bartolomeo Rastrelli, its green dome, smaller cupolas, ornate facade, gilded iconostasis, and hilltop terrace express imperial Baroque design. Landslide risk and restoration have repeatedly affected access, while the church retains religious and museum functions.
