Destination Guide — Kyiv

Explore Kyiv

A Brief History

Kyiv was founded in the fifth century and grew to become the powerful capital of Kievan Rus', the cradle of East Slavic civilization. It suffered near-total destruction during the Mongol invasion of 1240 and subsequently fell under Lithuanian, Polish, and Russian rule. In the twentieth century, the city endured the devastation of World War II and the Soviet era before becoming the capital of an independent Ukraine in 1991. Today, Kyiv is defined by its resilient spirit, golden-domed cathedrals, and ongoing struggle for democratic sovereignty.

Top Sights & Activities

St. Andrew's Church

St. Andrew's Church, also known as the Cathedral of St. Andrew, is an opulent 18th-century baroque church located on a hill that offers views of one of Kiev's oldest neighborhoods. The church overlooks the ancient Podil district and is a prominent landmark in the city with its vibrant architecture featuring a large dome and five smaller cupolas.

Free

Arka Svobody Ukrayinsʹkoho Narodu

The Peoples Friendship Arch is a large, rainbow-shaped arch built in 1982 to honor Russia and the Ukraine. The arch spans 50 meters and is located on a viewing deck overlooking the River Dnieper. It was designed by architect N Ivanov and is one of Kyiv's most popular tourist attractions.

Free

Glass Bridge

The Glass Bridge in Kyiv is a architectural marvel that connects Khreshchaty Park to Vladimirskaya Hill, offering views of the Dnipro River and the cityscape. Opened in 2019, this pedestrian and cyclist-friendly bridge features unique glass floor panels that allow visitors to gaze down at their feet while enjoying panoramic vistas.

Free

Mariyinsky Palace

Nestled on the picturesque hilly banks of the Dnipro River, Mariyinsky Palace stands as a example of Baroque architecture and a significant historical landmark in Kyiv. Completed in 1752, this magnificent structure was designed by the renowned architect Bartolomeo Rastrelli at the behest of Empress Elizaveta Petrovna. Originally serving as the official residence for Kyiv's governor, it later became a key site for Ukrainian governance during Soviet times.

350 UAH

Kiev Zoo

Kiev Zoo, established in 1909, is a remarkable destination for animal lovers and families alike. Spanning over 40 hectares, it stands as zoos in Europe and showcases an impressive array of around 368 species from across the globe. Visitors can marvel at majestic tigers and lions while also exploring diverse collections of amphibians, reptiles, birds, and even invertebrates.

Free

Sophia Square

Sophia Square (Sofiyska Square) is one of the most historic and significant public squares in Kyiv. It is dominated by the magnificent bell tower of St. Sophia's Cathedral and the prominent equestrian monument to Bohdan Khmelnytsky, a 17th-century Cossack leader. The square frequently hosts major national celebrations, cultural events, and the city's main Christmas tree.

St. Michael's Vydubytsky Men's Monastery

Nestled on a serene hill overlooking the Dnieper River, St. Michael's Vydubytsky Men's Monastery is a captivating gem in Kyiv that embodies the essence of Orthodoxy. Founded by Prince Vsevolod, son of Yaroslav the Wise, this monastery offers visitors a tranquil escape from the bustling city life.

150 UAH

Kyiv National Botanical Garden

Kyiv National Botanical Garden is a vast and extensive botanical garden that boasts a remarkable variety of flora, including exotic plants housed in glasshouses. It's not just a seasonal attraction; visitors flock to this UNESCO-protected site throughout the year to enjoy romantic walks amidst beautiful trees and flowers. The garden's sprawling green slopes provide the perfect setting for relaxation and contemplation, making it an ideal spot for couples to unwind with nature.

Free

Gorodetsky House

Nestled in the historic Lypsky neighborhood, the Gorodetsky House, also known as the House of Chimaeras, is a example of Art Nouveau architecture that captivates with its intricate facade. This landmark building was designed by the eccentric architect Horodetsky in the early 20th century and has served various purposes over time, including a hospital during Ukraine's independence. Today, it stands as an official residence for presidential meetings.

400 UAH

Insomnia Кімнати Страху

If you're looking for an exhilarating evening activity in Kyiv, "Insomnia Кімнати Страху" is a must-visit. This immersive experience will have you on the edge of your seat as you navigate through dark rooms filled with suspense and surprises. While it may seem like it's geared towards younger audiences, adults will find themselves just as captivated by the thrilling atmosphere and convincing actors who stay in character throughout.

Free

TSUM

When exploring the vibrant heart of Kyiv, a visit to TSUM (Central Department Store) is an must. Nestled at the bustling intersection of Khreshchatyk Street and Bogdan Khmelnitsky Avenue, this shopping haven offers a unique blend of history and modernity. Renovated in December 2016, its exterior retains a classic Soviet architectural style that harmonizes with the city's aesthetic, while inside discover a contemporary retail paradise.

Free

St. Nicholas Cathedral

Nestled in the heart of Kyiv, St. Nicholas Cathedral is a example of neo-Gothic architecture that has captivated visitors since its construction between 1896 and 1911. Originally built to cater to the city's burgeoning Polish community, this magnificent cathedral is not only the largest church in Kyiv but also a symbol of elegance with its sharp spires and intricate decorative elements.

150 UAH

Kyiv National Academic Operetta's Theatre

Kyiv National Academic Operetta's Theatre is a delightful destination for anyone seeking an enjoyable cultural experience. With performances that often feature beloved classics, you don't need to be fluent in Ukrainian or Russian to appreciate the music and storytelling. It's particularly family-friendly during the festive season, with special events led by Father Christmas that include fun games and charming gifts for children.

Free

Obolonsʹka Naberezhna

Obolonska Naberezhna is a scenic waterfront promenade stretching along the Dnieper River in the Obolon district of Kyiv. Developed primarily in the early 2000s, it features pedestrian walkways, landscaped parks, and modern residential architecture. The embankment is a popular recreational area for locals, offering views of the river, public art installations, and access to nearby beaches and cafes, reflecting the city's modern urban development.

Free

Park Natalka

Park Natalka, located in the southern part of the Obolonska embankment in Kyiv, has undergone extensive reconstruction to enhance its facilities and aesthetics. The park now features leafy riverside paths, picnic lawns, a paved maze, and sporting facilities including a soccer field. New additions such as observation platforms, arbors along the Dnieper bank, playgrounds, and mini-football fields have been incorporated.

Free

Park Bridge

Park Bridge in Kyiv is a delightful escape from the city's hustle and bustle, offering a serene walking experience with views of the Dnipro River and the surrounding cityscape. This pedestrian-friendly bridge features a dedicated bike lane, making it popular among cyclists and joggers alike. Visitors can enjoy leisurely strolls while soaking in panoramas during both day and night, as sunlight dances on the river or city lights reflect off its surface.

Free

A.V. Fomin Botanical Garden

A.V. Fomin Botanical Garden, one of Ukraine's oldest botanical institutions, is home to around 9,000 native and non-native plant species. Located in the city center of Kiev, this small park is a popular walking spot and offers diverse terrain for photographers to capture unique angles.

130 UAH

National Kyiv-Pechersk Historical and Cultural Reserve

The Kyiv-Pechersk Lavra, or Monastery of the Caves, is a massive, historic Orthodox Christian complex and a UNESCO World Heritage site. Founded in 1051, it features impressive gold-domed churches, a prominent bell tower, and an extensive network of narrow underground catacombs containing the mummified remains of revered monks. It is a profound spiritual center of Ukraine.

130 UAH

Kyiv-Pechersk Lavra

Kyiv-Pechersk Lavra, also known as the Lavra, is a UNESCO World Heritage-listed Orthodox Christian monastery complex in Kyiv. It features ornate religious buildings such as the Dormition Church, refectory, and bell tower. One of its main attractions is the network of underground catacombs where mummified remains of former monks are laid to rest.

200 UAH

Mystetskyi Arsenal National Art and Culture Museum Complex

Nestled in the heart of Kyiv, the Mystetskyi Arsenal National Art and Culture Museum Complex is a vibrant hub for contemporary art and culture. Housed in a historic arsenal, this expansive museum hosts an array of exhibitions, performances, and festivals throughout the year. It’s not just a place to admire art; it also serves as a venue for events like Ukrainian Fashion Week, attracting fashion enthusiasts from all over.

Free

Svyato-Vvedenskyi Monastery

Nestled in the serene landscape, Svyato-Vvedenskyi Monastery is a abbey dating back to the late 1800s. This architectural gem boasts a church adorned with intricate icons, including the revered Mother of God. Visitors often describe it as an oasis of tranquility and spiritual energy, making it a monument that beautifully encapsulates history and peace.

150 UAH

National Museum of the History of Ukraine in the Second World War

This war memorial consists of a massive statue of a Motherland holding a shield in one hand and an olive branch in the other, overlooking the city below. The museum which is located on the banks of the Dnieper River has exhibits about Ukraine's role in World War II from uniforms and letters to larger pieces of history like airplanes and artillery.

200 UAH

National Opera of Ukraine

The National Opera of Ukraine, also known as the Taras Shevchenko National Opera of Ukraine, is a lavish opera house in Kiev. Built in 1901 in the neo-Renaissance style, it has been a cultural mainstay since 1867. The impressive structure was rebuilt after a fire in the 1980s and now offers modern amenities.

Free

House Baron Steingel

Nestled in the heart of Kyiv, near the Golden Gate, House Baron Steingel is a architectural gem that captivates visitors with its historical charm. Often misattributed to Baron Steingel and various other figures, this building actually belonged to Mykhailo Pidhorsky, a wealthy nobleman and agricultural tycoon who acquired the property in 1892.

Free

Park Landscape Alley

Landscape Alley (Peizazhna Alley) is a whimsical, uniquely designed public park located in the historic Upper Town of Kyiv. The park is famous for its vibrant, surreal mosaic sculptures, including a massive, smiling blue cat and various Alice in Wonderland-themed installations. It offers a playful, creative atmosphere and excellent views of the historic Podil neighborhood.

Free

Children Landscape Park

Children Landscape Park, also known as the Song Festival Field, is a 30-hectare green space nestled in the Pechersk Hills of Kiev. Established in 1981 alongside the National Museum of Ukrainian History in World War II, this park serves as a vibrant hub for cultural events and festivities. The central area features an expansive open field where concerts and festivals come to life, making it a lively spot for both locals and visitors alike.

Free

Holosiivskyi National Natural Park

Holosiivskyi National Natural Park is a vast expanse of forest steppe landscapes, offering an escape from the hustle and bustle of modern city life in Kiev. Just a short metro ride away from the city center, this park provides an area for outdoor activities such as hiking along trails, exploring ponds, and enjoying playgrounds and eateries.

Free

Expo Center of Ukraine

The Expocenter of Ukraine, also known as VDNG, is a captivating cultural hub nestled in Kyiv that spans an impressive 286 hectares. This expansive exhibition complex, established in 1958 during the Soviet era, offers a delightful blend of history and modernity. Visitors can explore its Stalinist Empire-style architecture while enjoying various events such as trade shows and cultural festivals.

200 UAH

Kyiv River Port

Kyiv River Port is a vibrant hub for electronic music, featuring techno, house, and ethno genres. The port offers boat tours along the Dnipro river with options for longer cruises to Odessa. Visitors can enjoy upscale food stands and seating along the promenade in the summer. The port also hosts large cruise ships and has a bustling square with a fountain and waterfront bars.

8 UAH

Kiev Funicular

The Kyiv Funicular is a historic cable railway connecting the historic Uppertown (Podil) with the lower commercial neighborhood of Podil. Opened in 1905, it was designed to provide a reliable transport link up the steep Volodymyr Hill. The funicular offers panoramic views of the Dnieper River and the surrounding cityscape. It remains a functional part of Kyiv's public transport system and a notable example of early twentieth-century urban engineering.

Free

Володимирська Гірка

Nestled atop a hill, Володимирська Гірка is a captivating park that offers visitors panoramic views of the Dnipro River and its bridges. This enchanting space is perfect for leisurely strolls or simply soaking in nature's beauty, with vibrant blossoming trees providing an ideal backdrop for memorable photos. The park features both lively areas filled with street musicians and artists, as well as quieter spots where one can unwind amidst the greenery.

Free

St. Michael's Golden-Domed Monastery

St. Michael's Golden-Domed Monastery is a historic site in Kiev, Ukraine, featuring a cathedral, bell tower, refectory, and gates all built in the Ukrainian Baroque style. The monastery was founded in 1108 by Sviatopolk Iziaslavovych and is dedicated to Michael the Archangel. It is renowned for being the first cathedral in Kyiv with gilded domes made of solid gold leaf.

Free

Hydropark

Hydropark is a vibrant urban oasis nestled along the banks of the Dnieper River, offering an array of recreational activities for everyone. This expansive park, developed during the Soviet era, features sandy beaches perfect for sunbathing and swimming, alongside facilities like pools, boat rentals, and tennis courts. For those seeking a lively atmosphere, Hydropark boasts beach clubs with DJ entertainment and bars on nearby islands such as Dolobetsky Island.

Free

Taras Shevchenko Park

Nestled in the heart of Kyiv, Taras Shevchenko Park is a vibrant urban oasis that beckons both locals and visitors with its lush greenery and inviting atmosphere. Named after the esteemed Ukrainian poet, this park boasts beautifully landscaped walking paths perfect for leisurely strolls. As you wander through, encounter charming benches and intriguing pathways that enhance your experience.

150 UAH

Kyiv Academic Theatre of Drama and Comedy

Kyiv Academic Theatre of Drama and Comedy, also known as the Ukraine Drama Theatre, is a cultural gem located on Nikolaevsky Square. Named after Ivan Franko, this historic theater offers performances that blend classic and modern plays. The cozy venue features two stages and a lovely interior, making it an ideal choice for a cultural evening on Dnipro's left bank.

Free

Парк Перемога

Nestled on the left bank of the Dnipro River, Парк Перемога is a lush and expansive green oasis that beckons both locals and tourists alike. This vibrant park features winding paved pathways perfect for leisurely strolls, biking, or jogging. Families will find plenty of playgrounds to keep children entertained, while adults can enjoy serene moments on benches scattered throughout the grounds.

Free

Castle Hill

Castle Hill is a hidden gem nestled in the heart of Kyiv, offering an enchanting escape from the urban hustle. This historic site, named after a 14th-century castle that once stood there, features a long staircase leading to its summit. As you ascend from Andriivs'kyi descent through lush forest paths, be rewarded with views over Vozdvyzhenka Barrio and the vibrant buildings along Vozdvyzhenska Street.
