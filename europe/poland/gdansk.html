Destination Guide — Northern Poland

Explore Gdansk

A Brief History

Gdańsk grew as a Hanseatic port on the Baltic whose shipyards and merchants linked Poland to Scandinavian and Dutch trade. The medieval crane, Royal Route, and rebuilt Main Town Hall record Teutonic, Polish, and Prussian rule before 1945 border changes. Solidarity strikes at the Lenin Shipyard in 1980 began the movement that weakened communist rule across Eastern Europe.

Westerplatte

Westerplatte is a historic site in Poland that holds great significance as the location of the first World War II invasion. The area features a grand stone monument and ruins of barracks, including Guardhouse No. 1, which has been transformed into a museum. This peninsula at the mouth of the Port Canal was where Polish soldiers valiantly defended the Military Transit Depot against overwhelming German forces in September 1939.

Free

Neptune's Fountain

Neptune's Fountain is a 17th-century bronze statue of the sea god located in the Main Town (Gowne Miasto) area. The surrounding historic houses and cafes create a strikingly beautiful and colorful main street, with attractions like the Golden Gate and Green Gate at either end. The area was largely rebuilt after World War II, but still retains its charm with bas-reliefs, colorful frescos, musicians, and street performers.

Free

Długi Targ

Długi Targ, also known as the Long Market, is a historic public square in Gdansk that offers a charming blend of period architecture, restaurants, and souvenir shops. This 500-meter strip dates back to the 13th century and is lined with colorful traditional houses and notable landmarks such as St. Mary's Church, the Neptune Fountain, main town hall, Artus Court, and the Golden Tenement House.

Free

St. Mary's Church

St. Mary's Church, officially known as the Basilica of St. Mary of the Assumption of the Blessed Virgin Mary, is a Gothic Roman Catholic church located in Gdańsk's historic Old Town. Completed in 1502, this architectural marvel is renowned for being the largest brick church globally and can accommodate up to 20,000 worshippers at once. Visitors are encouraged to climb its impressive tower—405 steps await those who seek panoramic views of Gdańsk and beyond.

Free

Oliwski Park

Nestled in the charming Oliwa district of Gdansk, Poland, Oliwski Park is a 10-hectare oasis that beautifully marries history with nature. Originally established as a monastery garden by Cistercian monks in the 12th century, this park has evolved into a serene retreat featuring picturesque paths and exquisite French-style gardens.

Free

Wieża widokowa na wzgórzu Pachołek

Pachołek Hill Observation Deck, located in Gdansk, offers a 360-degree panoramic view of the city and the bay. Situated on the topmost peak in the Oliwa district, this viewing tower stands at an elevation of 100 meters above sea level. The tower is surrounded by dense woods and can be reached after a 15-minute climb up the stairs.

zł30

ZOO GDAŃSK

ZOO GDAŃSK, located in Oliwa and established in 1954, offers a diverse range of animal species from around the world. The expansive 126-hectare area is home to 181 different animal species, including majestic big cats like lions, leopards, and tigers as well as gentle creatures such as red-necked kangaroos and various turtle species. Visitors can also encounter native animals like the European bison.

Free

ERGO ARENA

ERGO ARENA is a cutting-edge indoor arena located on the border of Sopot and Gdansk, offering a capacity for 15,000 spectators. It serves as a venue for major sporting events and cultural performances. The modern design allows for hosting various sports competitions. Adjacent to the Tri-City Landscape Park, it provides easy access to bicycle paths and skating routes.

Free

Monument to the Fallen Shipyard Workers of 1970

The Monument to the Fallen Shipyard Workers of 1970, located in Gdansk, is a significant sight commemorating the workers who died in the anti-communist riots. Erected in 1980, it stands as a Symbol of Freedom and houses a small museum with fascinating exhibits. This monument pays tribute to the workers killed during the December 1970 demonstrations at the Gdansk Shipyards, which played a pivotal role in history.

Free

Góra Gradowa

Góra Gradowa, a 19th-century hilltop fort in Gdansk, offers city views and is easily accessible from the main bus and train station. Despite its modest height of 46 meters, the hill provides panoramic vistas of Gdansk. Visitors can explore the remains of the fortress and discover hidden bunkers with small exhibitions inside.

Free

Highland Gate

High Gate, also known as Wyzynna Gate, is a historic Renaissance city gate in Gdansk. It was the main entrance to the city and is adorned with symbols of Gdansk, Prussia, and Poland. This 16th-century structure forms part of Gdansk's fortifications and now houses the Gdansk Tourist Information Office. The area surrounding High Gate exudes a unique historic vibe with its magnificent old-fashioned German-style buildings.

Free

Golden Gate

The Golden Gate in Gdansk, a prominent Dutch Mannerist-style gate completed in 1614, is a strikingly beautiful landmark at the western end of the Long Market. The gate was rebuilt after World War II and features bas-reliefs and colorful frescos on surrounding buildings. Musicians and street performers add to the lively atmosphere. Adorned with antiques and symbolic figures, the Latin inscription on the gate reflects its historical significance.

Free

The Great Armoury

The Great Armoury in Gdansk, Poland is a example of Renaissance architecture, built in the early 1600s to store weapons and military gear. Designed by Flemish architect Anthonis van Obbergen, its ornate facade reflects the city's wealth during its golden age. Today, it hosts rotating art exhibits from the Gdansk Academy of Fine Arts. The building's intricate stonework and decorative gables make it a favorite among tourists exploring central Gdansk.

Free

Długa

Długa, also known as Long Street and Long Market, is a historic thoroughfare in Gdansk Old Town, Europe. It has been a bustling commercial hub for centuries and is flanked by impressive historic buildings that once belonged to wealthy merchants. The street is adorned with the Neptune fountain and the town clock at the top of the town hall. Surrounding attractions include St. Marys Basilica, the Old Town Hall, Gothic town gates, and Zuraw Crane within the Historical Center.

zł15

Shakespeare Theatre

Gdańsk Shakespeare Theater is a must-visit destination in the city, drawing multilingual crowds of all ages to its historical and popular Main Town. The city itself is steeped in over 1000 years of tradition and history, known for being the birthplace of the Solidarity Movement. With its proximity to the Old Town and main train station, it offers a fantastic experience within a stylish and modern building.

Free

Pier in Sopot

Molo w Sopocie, also known as Sopot Pier, is a renowned events venue featuring a long wooden pier extending into the Gulf of Gdansk. The area includes a public square with a concert shell, a historic lighthouse with panoramic views, and various food outlets. Situated near the Sopot Pier, visitors can enjoy vistas of the Baltic Sea and easy access to local attractions.

Sopot Lighthouse

Nestled in the picturesque seaside town of Sopot, the Sopot Lighthouse is a historic gem that dates back to the early 1900s. This structure, originally designed as an observation tower for a balneological institute, stands tall at 30 meters and offers visitors panoramic views of the Baltic Sea. As you stroll along the charming wooden pier leading to this landmark, be captivated by its intricate stained glass and sculptures that add to its allure.

Forest Opera

Forest Opera, also known as Opera Lesna, is an amphitheatre located in Sopot that offers a unique experience for opera and music lovers. The venue has excellent acoustics and hosts performances by both Polish and international artists. With a capacity of 4,500 spectators and a stage for 110 musicians, it serves as the main center for mass cultural events in the area.

Bastion Żubr

The Żubr Bastion, also known as the Bison Bastion, is one of the 14 bastions located in the southern part of Gdansk's old town. This grassy mound offers a nice view of the old moat and city defense system. While it may benefit from some cleaning and maintenance, visitors appreciate its open spaces for photography and its peaceful atmosphere away from the crowds.

Swan Tower

The Small Mill, constructed in the 1400s above Raduni River, is now home to the Polish Fishing Association. This charming and picturesque granary captivates visitors with its remarkable beauty. The mill's unique architecture and historical significance make it a popular tourist attraction. With its tranquil surroundings and serene atmosphere, it offers an exquisite experience for all who visit.

Straganiarska Gate

Straganiarska Gate is a Gothic water gate in Gdansk, Poland, built in the 15th century. It stands out with its well-preserved Gothic architecture and features two turrets and a stone-carved heraldic triad displaying the coat of arms of Prussia, Poland, and Gdansk. The gate also offers accommodation as a rare opportunity to spend a night in an ancient building due to the city's serious preservation efforts.

Free

St. Mary's Gate

St. Mary's Gate is a red-brick structure with origins dating back to the 15th century, reconstructed after World War II and adorned with the Gdańsk coat of arms. It serves as one of the main entrances to Gdansk and provides access to the picturesque cobbled Mariacka street. The gate is viewed from Granary Island across the river, where visitors can admire its Gothic style and restored polychrome featuring two lions holding the city's coat of arms.

Free

Mariacka

Mariacka is a charming cobblestone pedestrian promenade in Gdansk, lined with historic buildings featuring terraces and regal portals. This delightful street, often used as a backdrop for period movies, had to be reconstructed after suffering extensive damage during World War II. The entrances to the narrow merchant houses are on small terraces above the street level, where restaurants also offer seating with ornamental reliefs on low walls.

Free

St. Catherine's Church

St. Catherine's Church in Gdansk is a historic landmark dating back to the 1300s, making it the oldest church in the city. The Gothic architecture, including chapels and vaults, reflects its rich history and cultural significance. Over time, the church has undergone several renovations and expansions. Visitors can climb its imposing bell tower for panoramic views of the city and beyond, offering a unique perspective of Gdansk's Old Town and Raduni Canal.
