Zakopane

Artists and intellectuals in the late nineteenth century remade a Goral pastoral village into Poland's mountain cultural capital. The Zakopane Style of timber architecture and Tatra tourism defined the town more than any medieval charter. Kasprowy Wierch cable cars later tied the cultural town to high-Tatra skiing. Krupówki Street concentrates the wooden resort commerce that followed the artists.

Wodogrzmoty Mickiewicza

Wodogrzmoty Mickiewicza are cascading falls on a Tatra stream along hiking routes near Zakopane, named for the poet Adam Mickiewicz. Wooden walkways and trail junctions give access to the multi-step cascades. The falls are a standard stop on approaches into the higher valleys.

Morskie Oko

Morskie Oko is a glacial lake deep within Tatra National Park and reached by the main mountain road from Palenica Bialczanska. A long paved route climbs to the lakeshore beneath steep peaks, with a historic shelter nearby. Heavy crowds, snow, avalanche conditions and protected-area rules shape access, while the lake's setting reflects the high mountain landscape beyond the built-up center of Zakopane.

49.1971° N / 20.0701° E

Kasprowy Wierch Cable Car - Ground Station

The Kasprowy Wierch cable-car ground station at Kuźnice, a few kilometers from Zakopane, begins the lift into the High Tatras. Cabins rise from the valley station toward the summit ridge. Hikers and skiers queue here for access to alpine trails and winter slopes.

49.2693° N / 19.9792° E

04 AdmissionFree

Zapora Pod Nosalem

Zapora pod Nosalem is a small stone and concrete weir on the Bystra stream near Zakopane's Nosal district. Built for water control, it creates a modest cascade beside paths and roads leading toward Kuznice. The structure is a functional part of the local waterway, with its appearance changing according to rainfall, snowmelt and maintenance. The cascade is modest even after rain.

49.2780° N / 19.9823° E

05 AdmissionFree

Kasprowy Wierch

Kasprowy Wierch rises on the Polish-Slovak border in the High Tatras and is reached from Kuznice by cable car or mountain trails; winter routes require alpine judgment, while lift operation, snow cover and trail restrictions vary by season. The summit area contains meteorological facilities, ski infrastructure, ridgelines and broad views that change rapidly with cloud and wind.

49.2322° N / 19.9818° E

06 AdmissionFree

Sarnia Skała

A rocky limestone crest gives Sarnia Skala open views toward Giewont, Zakopane and surrounding Tatra valleys, and the summit is modest by Tatra standards but can become crowded and its visibility is never guaranteed by a fixed itinerary. Forest trails commonly approach through Dolina Strazyska or neighboring routes, with exposed roots, snow and changing weather affecting difficulty.

49.2647° N / 19.9417° E

07 AdmissionFree

Polana Strążyska

Polana Strazyska opens as a meadow below Giewont at the head of the forested Strazyska Valley. A mountain hut, wooden buildings, stream and trail junction make it a common stopping point before routes toward Siklawica waterfall or Sarnia Skala. The clearing belongs to Tatra National Park, where weather, mud, wildlife protection and seasonal trail conditions matter more than generic heritage wording.

49.2622° N / 19.9300° E

08 AdmissionFree

Siklawica Waterfall

Siklawica is a, 23-meter-high waterfall located at the upper end of the Strążyska Valley in the Tatra Mountains near Zakopane. The waterfall cascades down two nearly vertical rock faces in a peaceful, forested setting. It is a popular and relatively easy hiking destination, offering a refreshing natural retreat.

49.2594° N / 19.9302° E

09 AdmissionFree

Giewont

Giewont's limestone massif rises directly above Zakopane, with a large metal cross marking a high western summit commonly reached by hikers; other valleys and Morskie Oko are separate Tatra destinations. Steep trails, chains, exposure, congestion, lightning, and rapid weather changes make the final ascent serious despite its popularity.

49.2510° N / 19.9340° E

10 AdmissionFree

Dolina ku Dziurze

Dolina ku Dziurze follows a short wooded ravine from the edge of Zakopane to the entrance of Dziura cave. The shaded trail runs beside a small stream through Tatra National Park. Its final chamber is undeveloped and can be wet, cold or icy, so lighting, footwear and seasonal park rules shape the visit.

49.2745° N / 19.9411° E

Dolina Białego

In the Polish Tatras above Zakopane, Dolina Białego offers gentle forest trails past streams and waterfalls. Hikers often start near Strażyska Valley and follow the yellow trail toward Sarnia Skała. Wet rock can make footing tricky, but the valley scenery rewards a steady climb.

49.2750° N / 19.9570° E

Sarni Wodospad

Roe Deer Waterfall drops as a small cascade on the Strażyska stream inside the Polish Tatras. Popular walking paths in Strażyska Valley pass beside the water step on routes that continue into the mountain park. Hikers often pause at the fall for photos before climbing farther toward higher viewpoints.

13 AdmissionFree

Gubałówka

Gubałówka is a ridge above Zakopane reached by footpaths or a cable railway, with open views toward the High Tatras. Summit stalls and walkways serve day visitors who ride up for the panorama. The long hill forms the northern backdrop to the resort town below.

49.3074° N / 19.9370° E

14 AdmissionFree

Krupówki

Zakopane's main cobbled promenade packs shops, restaurants, and horse-drawn carriage traffic along a continuous pedestrian strip. National media often film on the street, and a large exotic parrot zoo sits nearby on Droga do Olczy. Away from the loudest blocks, quieter side streets offer parking and the bird collection.

49.2939° N / 19.9539° E

15 AdmissionFree

Świecący napis Zakopane

Large illuminated letters spelling Zakopane stand on Rownia Krupowa as a modern city photo marker. The open meadow can frame Giewont and other Tatra peaks when weather is clear, while evening lighting changes the appearance after dark. The large, glowing letters provide a typical souvenir backdrop, especially in the evening, with the dramatic peaks of the Tatra Mountains, including Giewont, visible in the distance.

49.2967° N / 19.9560° E

16 AdmissionFree

Aqua Park Zakopane

Under the Tatra peaks, Aqua Park Zakopane runs adventure tubes, a wild-river ride, waterfalls, thermal baths, jacuzzis, and lane pools for recreational swimming. Indoor halls keep the program usable in any season. Families split time between thrill slides and quieter soak areas.

49.2951° N / 19.9656° E

17 AdmissionFree

Willa Koliba

Willa Koliba on Kościeliska Street is an early Zakopane-style wooden house designed with Stanisław Witkiewicz's highland vocabulary. It operates as a museum of Podhale architecture and interiors from the turn of the twentieth century. Carved timber rooms show how the style shaped the resort town's identity.

49.2943° N / 19.9428° E

18 AdmissionFree

Kopieniec Wielki

Wielki Kopieniec rises above forest and meadow northeast of central Zakopane, reached by marked trails through Tatra National Park. The summit opens views toward Giewont, the High Tatras and surrounding foothills, while nearby Polana Kopieniec preserves seasonal shepherding landscape; gubalowka is a separate ridge and route conditions vary with snow, mud, wind and forestry work.

49.2716° N / 20.0162° E
