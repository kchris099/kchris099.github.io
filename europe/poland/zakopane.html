Explore Zakopane

A Brief History

Originally a remote pastoral village, Zakopane was transformed in the late 19th century by artists and intellectuals fleeing political oppression. The creation of the distinct Zakopane architectural style turned the rugged Tatra mountain town into the active cultural capital of winter. Museums, markets, and street plans in Zakopane still show layers from those periods.

Wodogrzmoty Mickiewicza

Wodogrzmoty Mickiewicza is a visitor site in Zakopane. If visitors are on the lookout for a quirky and entertaining experience, "Dom do góry nogami" in Zakopane is a must-visit! This unique upside-down house has an optical illusion that plays with visitor senses and challenges visitor perception of gravity. Perfect for those seeking a lighthearted break from mountain hikes, this compact two-level structure has been tourists since its opening in 2007.

Morskie Oko

Morskie Oko sits within the Wodogrzmoty Mickiewicza area of Zakopane, documented in local heritage registers for its civic or cultural role. Visitors approach from the main access road and combine the visit with neighbouring heritage buildings and public spaces in the district.

Kasprowy Wierch Cable Car - Ground Station

Kasprowy Wierch Cable Car - Ground Station is a visitor site in Zakopane, Poland. The Kasprowy Wierch Cable Railway - Ground Station is a popular mode of transport from Kuźnice to the top of Kasprowy Wierch in the Tatra Mountains, just 3 kilometers away from Zakopane. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Kasprowy Wierch

Kasprowy Wierch is a 1,987-meter mountain in Poland, known for its accessibility via cable car and as a popular destination for skiing and hiking. The area offers good conditions for downhill skiing from November to early spring on the upper mountainside of Kasprowy Wierch peak, with artificial ski slopes closer to the town open from December through March.

Free

Sarnia Skała

Sarnia Skała is a prominent, rocky peak located in the Tatra Mountains, easily accessible via a popular hiking trail from Zakopane. The summit offers a well known, unobstructed views of the massive, sleeping-knight-shaped Giewont mountain, as well as panoramic vistas of the town of Zakopane in the valley below.

Free

Polana Strążyska

Polana Strążyska sits within the Sarnia Skała area of Zakopane, documented in local heritage registers for its civic or cultural role. Visitors approach from the main access road and combine the visit with neighbouring heritage buildings and public spaces in the district.

Free

Siklawica Waterfall

Siklawica is a, 23-meter-high waterfall located at the upper end of the Strążyska Valley in the Tatra Mountains near Zakopane. The waterfall cascades down two nearly vertical rock faces in a peaceful, forested setting. It is a popular and relatively easy hiking destination, offering a refreshing natural retreat.

Free

Giewont

Giewont, a mountain massif in Tatra National Park, is a favorite among hikers with its three peaks and well-marked trails. The area also has popular attractions such as Koscieliska Valley, Strazyska Valley, Chocholowska Valley, and the Morskie Oko lake. Adventurous visitors can tackle challenging trails leading to famous Tatra peaks like Giewont and Rysy.

Free

Dolina ku Dziurze

Dolina ku Dziurze is a short, easily accessible, and heavily forested valley in the Tatra National Park near Zakopane. The trail follows a small stream and culminates at the 'Dziura' (The Hole), a small, dark cave that visitors can explore. The valley provides a cool, shaded walk, ideal for families and casual hikers.

Free

Dolina Białego

Dolina Białego is a valley in the Tatra Mountains, offering gentle hiking trails through forests, waterfalls, and streams with views of Zakopane. The trail starts at the entrance to Strazyska Valley and leads to Dolina Bialego Potoku, known for its beauty. Hikers can take the relatively easy yellow trail uphill towards Sarnia Skala. The route can be slippery and challenging but rewards with scenery.

Free

Sarni Wodospad

Sarni Wodospad is a visitor site in Zakopane, Poland. Sarni Wodospad (Roe Deer Waterfall) is a small, cascade located along the Strążyska stream in the Tatra Mountains. It adds to the natural beauty of the popular hiking routes in the area. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Free

Gubałówka

Gubałówka is a visitor site in Zakopane, Poland. Gubałówka is a majestic mountain in the Polish Tatras, offering hiking trails and a cable car to the top, providing panoramic views of the surrounding area. Visitors can take a cable railway to Gubałówka for a more relaxed experience and explore popular spots in the region. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Free

Krupówki

Krupówki is a nostalgic cobblestone thoroughfare in Zakopane, Poland, known for its shops, restaurants, and horse-drawn carriage rides. It's a major national media spot and home to the largest exotic parrot zoo in the province. Away from the noisy Krupówki Street with free parking, this zoo on Droga do Olczy Street 11 offers an variety of parrots.

Free

Świecący napis Zakopane

The illuminated 'Zakopane' sign is a modern, popular photo landmark located in the upper part of the Równia Krupowa park. The large, glowing letters provide a typical souvenir backdrop, especially in the evening, with the dramatic peaks of the Tatra Mountains, including Giewont, visible in the distance.

Free

Aqua Park Zakopane

Aqua Park Zakopane is a modern and spacious all-weather waterpark located in the Tatra Mountains of Poland. It offers a wide range of attractions for visitors, including adventure tubes, a wild-river ride, waterfalls, thermal baths, jacuzzi, and various pools for recreational swimming with swimming lanes. The park is suitable for the whole family and provides fun activities for both kids and adults.

Free

Willa Koliba

Willa Koliba is a well-maintained building that showcases the original Zakopianski architectural style. The museum offers a 30-minute tour and provides an audio guide in English. Visitors praise the knowledgeable and friendly staff, and recommend taking advantage of free admission on Sundays or Wednesdays. It's recommended for architecture enthusiasts and design buffs who want to look at the Zakopane style.

Free

Kopieniec Wielki

Kopieniec Wielki is a mountain offering year-round hiking opportunities through dense coniferous forests and vistas of the Tatra Mountains. From Gubalowka Mountain, visitors can behold a panoramic view encompassing notable peaks such as Giewont, Nosal, Kopa Magura, Maly Kopieniec, and more. The area caters to both seasoned hikers and those new to mountain exploration.
