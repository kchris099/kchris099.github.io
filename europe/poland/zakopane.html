Zakopane

Artists and intellectuals in the late nineteenth century remade a Goral pastoral village into Poland's mountain cultural capital. The Zakopane Style of timber architecture and Tatra tourism defined the town more than any medieval charter. Kasprowy Wierch cable cars later tied the cultural town to high-Tatra skiing. Krupówki Street concentrates the wooden resort commerce that followed the artists.

Willa Koliba on Kościeliska Street is an early Zakopane-style wooden house designed with Stanisław Witkiewicz's highland vocabulary.
