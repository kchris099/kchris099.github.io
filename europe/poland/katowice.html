Explore Katowice

A Brief History

Katowice grew from rural hamlets into the capital of the Silesian industrial district after coal and zinc mining accelerated in the nineteenth century under Prussian then Polish administration. Miners' colonies, neo-Gothic churches, and the Spodek arena express successive waves of German, Polish, and Silesian identity. Post-1989 investment turned the conurbation toward services and culture while headframes and workers' estates still record Upper Silesia's role in European heavy industry.

Market Square in Katowice

Market Square in Katowice is a market in Katowice, Poland. The Market Square in Katowice, Poland is a blend of historic and modern architecture, surrounded by a wide contemporary plaza with tram lines crisscrossing through it. Stalls sell food and goods during regular market hours.

Free

Silesian Theatre

In Katowice, the Silesian Theatre is a cultural gem that beautifully marries history with contemporary flair. Established in 1907, this neoclassical venue stands proudly at Market Square alongside 19th-century tenements and modern establishments. As Upper Silesia's largest drama stage, it showcases an repertoire that spans from classic literature to innovative contemporary works.

Free

Nikiszowiec Historic Mining District

Nikiszowiec Historic Mining District is a visitor site in Katowice, Poland. Construction and early use date to around 1908. Nikiszowiec Historic Mining District, built in 1908 for miners working in the nearby coal mine, exudes a unique charm. Located on the Industrial Monuments Route of the Silesian Voivodeship, it offers a chance to admire its architecture and experience local atmosphere. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Free

Silesian Stadium

Śląski Stadium, located in Chorzow, Poland, is a 1950s-built all-seater stadium with a maximum capacity of 55,211. It is the home ground for the Polish national football team and hosts various sports events and music concerts. Travellers can take guided tours of the stadium at affordable prices. The venue has been praised for its efficient handling of large crowds during events, although some have noted that the catering can be expensive and of low quality.

Free

Silesia Park

Silesia Park is a public park in Katowice, Poland. Between Chorzow and Katowice, Silesia Park is a large 620-acre nature haven that offers an abundance of activities for travellers. This expansive green space is nearly double the size of New York's Central Park, making it a must-visit destination in the Upper-Silesian Metropolis. Paths, lawns, and shaded areas make up the main public facilities.

zł40

Silesian Zoological Park

Silesian Zoological Park is a public park in Katowice, Poland. Silesian Zoological Park, located within the Silesian Culture and Recreation Park, is a large sanctuary for over 2500 animals. The park itself spans 2 km east-west and 3 km north-south, offering free access around the clock and welcoming dogs. Paths, lawns, and shaded areas make up the main public facilities.

zł139

Legendia Silesian Amusement Park

Legendia Silesian Amusement Park, the oldest amusement park in Poland, offers around 38 attractions including rollercoasters and rides suitable for younger children. The park provides a well-balanced experience with minimal queues during weekdays, making it convenient to explore all it has to offer in one day. Travellers can enjoy on-site restaurants and themed areas like Aladdin's Magicland.

Free

Silesia City Center

Silesia City Center is a visitor site in Katowice, Poland. The luxurious interior adds to the overall appeal, attracting travellers of all ages and backgrounds. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest. Silesia City Center remains part of the public visitor circuit in Katowice.

Teatr Rozrywki

Teatr Rozrywki is a visitor site in Katowice, Poland. The theater hall provides a comfortable viewing experience with its cozy armchairs. The staff exudes professionalism and creates a positive atmosphere throughout the establishment. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Free

Arena Spodek

Arena Spodek, often likened to a UFO due to its flying saucer-shaped design, is a known indoor arena in Katowice. Situated near Mariacka Street and the Silesian Museum, it stands out as one of the city's most distinctive spaces with its futuristic and minimalist vibe. Once an operational coal mine, it now hosts various cultural and sporting events, including rock concerts and basketball games.

Free

Katowice International Conference Centre

The Katowice International Conference Centre, designed by the known architectural studio Jems Architekci, is situated in the former industrial areas and pays homage to mining traditions. As part of the Culture Zone in Katowice, it offers a direct connection to Spodek and is known for its spacious and flexible rooms. The center has received praise for its cleanliness, ample restroom facilities, and free parking.

Free

National Polish Radio Symphony Orchestra

The National Polish Radio combination Orchestra, located in Katowice, is a known orchestra that was established in 1945. It has gained recognition for its diverse repertoire, ranging from Baroque to contemporary music. The orchestra frequently tours internationally and has performed at prestigious venues worldwide. The NOSPR's new seat, operational since 2014, is an notable building designed by Tomasz Konior with a cost of USD80 million.

Free

Silesian Insurgents' Monument

The Silesian Insurgents' Monument, completed in 1957, is a striking memorial dedicated to the participants of the Silesian Uprisings that took place between 1919 and 1921. Situated near the Culture Zone, this colossal structure consists of three bronze wings, each representing one of the uprisings. The monument was unveiled more than 50 years ago and stands on the former site of a cemetery.

Free

Archcathedral of Christ the King, Katowice

Archcathedral of Christ the King, Katowice is a religious building in Katowice, Poland. Construction and early use date to around 1927. The Archcathedral of Christ the King in Katowice is the largest cathedral in Poland, with construction spanning from 1927 to 1955. The structure is modern, built with reinforced concrete and clad with dolomite from nearby quarries. The exterior and main interior spaces are usually viewable outside service times.

Free

Teatr Korez

Teatr Korez is a contemporary drama theatre in Katowice presenting Polish and touring stage productions. The auditorium hosts a rotating season of plays under the theatre's own company and guest artists. Tickets are sold for scheduled evening and weekend performances.

Free

Kinoteatr Rialto

In Katowice, Kinoteatr Rialto is a artist-run venue that offers a mix of independent films and live music. This eclectic cinema stands out for its carefully curated repertoire, showing both alternative and mainstream movies that won't find in typical commercial theaters.

Free

Gryfnie

If find oneself wandering the streets of Katowice and are on the hunt for that perfect gift, look no further than Gryfnie. This shop is a treasure trove of unique items that celebrate Silesian culture, offering everything from quirky T-shirts and sweatshirts adorned with humorous local phrases to an array of coal products, books, calendars, and magnets.

Free

Immaculate Conception of the Blessed Virgin Mary

Immaculate Conception of the Blessed Virgin Mary sits within the Immaculate Conception of the Blessed Virgin Mary area of Katowice, documented in local heritage registers for its civic or cultural role. Visitors approach from the main access road and combine the visit with neighbouring heritage buildings and public spaces in the district.
