Explore Zamość

A Brief History

Zamość was founded in 1580 by the Polish nobleman Jan Zamoyski, who commissioned the Italian architect Bernardo Morando to design an ideal Renaissance city. Positioned on the trade route linking the Black Sea to northern Europe, the fortress town attracted Armenian, Greek, and Jewish merchants. Miraculously surviving the widespread destruction of World War II, Zamość remains a cohesive example of late Renaissance urban planning. Visitors can walk the original grid layout, exploring the Great Market Square and the surviving bastions of the fortress.

Ratusz w Zamościu

Ratusz w Zamościu, also known as Zamosc Town Hall, is a Renaissance building that is the centerpiece of the Market Square. This UNESCO World Heritage Site has an facade and ornate chambers adorned with murals and frescoes. Visitors can explore the town hall's interior, climb its grand staircase for panoramic views of the square, and even catch live music performances at certain times.

Rotunda Zamojska

Rotunda Zamojska is a significant historical site in Zamosc, offering visitors a chance to look at the city's past during World War II. The museum is housed within the old cells of the Rotunda, where prisoners were held and executed, adding a layer to the experience. With no entry fees and information plaques in multiple languages, it's an accessible attraction for anyone visiting Zamosc.
