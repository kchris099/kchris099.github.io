Destination Guide — Central Poland

Explore Warsaw

A Brief History

Warsaw became the capital of Poland in 1596 and expanded along the Vistula River. The royal castle and old town were largely destroyed in 1944 after the Warsaw Uprising. Postwar reconstruction followed historic documents and paintings, an effort UNESCO recognized when inscribing the rebuilt old town in 1980. Praga district across the river retains 19th-century buildings. The city is Poland's political and economic center.

Top Sights & Activities

PLN 60

The Royal Castle in Warsaw

The Royal Castle in Warsaw is a state museum and a national historical monument, which formerly served as the official royal residence of several Polish monarchs. The personal offices of the king and the administrative offices of the royal court were located in the Castle from the 16th century until the final partition of Poland in 1795. Situated in the Castle Square, at the entrance to the Old Town, the Royal Castle holds a significant collection of Polish and European art.

Free (cathedral museum PLN 5)

Archcathedral Basilica of St. John the Baptist

St John's Archcathedral is a Catholic church within the Old Town precinct in Warsaw, Poland. The Brick Gothic structure stands on Świętojańska Street, adjacent to the Jesuit Church. St John's is one of three major cathedrals in the city, but it is the only temple that also possesses the title of an archcathedral.

PLN 30

Palace of Culture and Science

The Palace of Culture and Science is a notable high-rise building in central Warsaw, Poland. With a total height of 237 metres, it is the second tallest building in both Warsaw and Poland, the eighth tallest building in the European Union and one of the tallest on the European continent.

PLN 25

National Museum in Warsaw

The National Museum in Warsaw is a national museum in Warsaw, one of the largest museums in Poland and the largest in the capital. It comprises a collection of ancient art, counting about 11,000 objects, an extensive gallery of Polish painting since the 16th century and a collection of foreign painting including Italian, French, Flemish, Dutch, German and Russian works.

Free

Tomb of the Unknown Soldier

The Tomb of the Unknown Soldier is a monument in Warsaw, Poland, dedicated to the unknown soldiers who have given their lives for Poland. It is one of many such national tombs of unknowns that were erected after World War I, and the most important such monument in Poland. The monument, located at Piłsudski Square, is the only surviving part of the Saxon Palace that occupied the spot until World War II.

Free

Warsaw University Library Garden

The University of Warsaw Library is a library of the University of Warsaw, Poland. Established in 1816 following the formation of the Royal Warsaw University, it was led by the first director Samuel Linde, a linguist and educator. By 1831, the library housed over 134,000 volumes.

Free

Złote Tarasy

Złote Tarasy is a modern shopping mall In Warsaw, near the Central Railway Station and the Palace of Culture and Science. This retail complex boasts over 200 fashion and gadget stores, as well as a large multi-screen cinema and numerous dining options, including the famous Hard Rock Cafe Warsaw. The mall's curved glass roof houses a variety of specialty shops and casual eateries, making it a popular destination for both locals and tourists.

Free

Ghetto Memorial Plaques

The Warsaw Ghetto boundary markers are significant historical landmarks in the city, commemorating the tragic fate of the Polish Jews during World War II. Located in the suburbs of Muranow and Mirow, these markers indicate the former boundaries of the Ghetto and serve as poignant reminders of this dark period in history. One such marker on Swietojerska Street shows the exact location of the former Ghetto wall.

Free

Warsaw Barbican

The Warsaw Barbican is a reconstructed 16th-century castle defense wall with towers that once encompassed the entire city. It stands as a historical landmark where travellers can wander along the walls and grassy slopes, offering picturesque views of the Old and New Towns meeting. This spot serves as a busy thoroughfare in the summer, linking some of the prettiest streets in the city together.

Free

Mały Powstaniec

Maly Powstaniec, also known as Little Insurgent, is a bronze sculpture unveiled in 1983 that serves as a poignant memorial to the child soldiers of World War II. The statue depicts a young boy wearing an oversized helmet and holding a submachine gun, symbolizing the brave children who fought and died during the Warsaw Uprising of 1944.

Free

Presidential Palace in Warsaw

The Presidential Palace in Warsaw is an 18th-century classical-style building that serves as the residence and workplace of Poland's president. Situated on the historic Royal Route, it offers guided tours through its elegant halls. The neoclassical palace, with its white glow, has been used by various noblemen and Polish presidents since the 17th century.

Free

Hala Koszyki Warszawa

Hala Koszyki is a bustling and renovated market hall in Warsaw, offering a trendy mix of restaurants, boutiques, street food options, and cultural events. This unique concept brings together 18 eateries inside a revitalized century-old art nouveau market hall. From independent restaurants to Poland's largest chains, the variety of dining options caters to every taste and budget. Additionally, the street food bazaar provides an array of culinary choices.

Free

City Zoological Garden in Warsaw

The City Zoological Garden in Warsaw is home to over 5,000 animals from 500 different species, including lions, hippos, otters, brown bears, African elephants, Rothschild giraffes, Indian rhinoceroses, gibbons and various birds. The zoo is In Warsaw and offers a diverse collection of wildlife for families to enjoy. Despite its challenging history during World War II, the zoo has thrived and remains a popular destination for nature enthusiasts.

Free

Vistula Boulevards

Vistula Boulevards is a picturesque waterside walkway along the Vistula River, offering a range of activities and attractions. travellers can enjoy cycling, jogging, or simply taking a leisurely stroll while admiring the river views. The boulevard features bars on boats, terrace cafes, and even summer beaches for relaxation. Additionally, it is home to notable landmarks such as the Museum of Modern Art, Copernicus Science Centre, Multimedia Fountain Park, and the Mermaids Statue.

Free

Nicolaus Copernicus Monument

The Nicolaus Copernicus Monument, located in front of the Staszic Palace in Warsaw, is a striking bronze statue honoring the renowned Polish astronomer. Designed by Bertel Thorvaldsen and completed in 1830, this landmark pays tribute to the founder of modern astronomy. The monument stands amidst historic surroundings and features a bronze representation of Copernicus's solar system.

Free

Church of the Holy Cross

The Holy Cross Church, situated in the heart of Warsaw, Poland, is a significant Catholic landmark with a rich history dating back to the 17th century. Renowned for its Baroque architecture and extensive renovations, this church holds special significance as it houses urns containing the hearts of esteemed Poles, including the legendary composer Frederic Chopin. The church's association with Chopin adds to its cultural importance and draws travellers from around the world.

Free

Fryderyk Chopin Monument

Nestled within the serene Lazienki Royal Gardens in Warsaw, the Frédéric Chopin Monument is a striking tribute to the legendary composer and pianist. This modernist bronze sculpture depicts Chopin seated gracefully beneath a stylized willow tree, creating an atmosphere of peace and reflection. Since its unveiling in 1926, despite facing destruction during World War II, it has been lovingly restored and remains representations of Chopin worldwide.

Free

Royal Łazienki - The Royal Theatre and Old Orangery

Royal Łazienki, located in Warsaw, is a grand complex surrounded by lush gardens and notable structures like the Myslewicki Palace and the White House. The Royal Theatre and Old Orangery are standout features within this historical landmark. The Royal Theatre showcases neoclassical design and hosts impressive stage productions, while the Old Orangery is a haven for rare plants. This place holds romantic tales of the last king of Poland arranging meetings by the lake with his beloved.

Free

Palace on the Isle

Lazienki Palace, a 18th-century summer residence, is nestled in the heart of Lazienki Park. The palace boasts baroque-style architecture and is situated on an artificial island surrounded by lush greenery. travellers can explore the expansive park, adorned with statues and peacocks, making it a picturesque setting for leisurely strolls and photo opportunities.

Free

Warsaw Uprising Monument

The Warsaw Uprising Monument is a striking 33-ft tall bronze sculpture that honors the courageous Polish resistance fighters who sacrificed their lives during the anti-Nazi uprising in 1944. This landmark symbolizes the bravery of those who fought against Nazi occupation and the looming threat of communism. Situated on Krasinski Square, near the modern Supreme Court, this monument portrays larger-than-life figures emerging from the ground, depicting Polish troops engaged in combat amidst ruins.

Free

Krasiński Garden

Krasiński Garden, located in the garden of a Baroque palace, is a popular public park in Warsaw. It features lawns, play areas, ancient trees, and a war monument. The garden dates back to the 17th century and was opened to residents in 1766. Today, it is a favorite walking spot for locals and students from the nearby university. travellers can enjoy lunch or sunbathing while kids can play at the playgrounds or feed ducks at the ponds.

Free

Pole Mokotowskie

Pole Mokotowskie, also known as Jozef Pilsudski Park, is a large park in Warsaw situated on a former airfield. It holds historical significance and was previously used for horse racing. The park offers various attractions such as lakes, sculptures, walking paths, biking trails, and roller-skating paths. It is a popular spot for locals and tourists alike due to its central location and numerous activities.

Free

Monument to the Ghetto Heroes

In Warsaw, the Monument to the Ghetto Heroes stands as a solemn tribute to those who perished in the unsuccessful Warsaw Ghetto Uprising of 1943. This 11-meter high structure, built in 1946, serves as a powerful symbol of war and resilience. Designed by Natan Rapaport and located near the POLIN Museum, it is a poignant reminder of the city's heroic history.

Free

Umschlagplatz Monument

The Umschlagplatz Monument in Warsaw is a solemn reminder of the tragic events that took place during the Holocaust. It serves as a departure point for Jews who were transported to Treblinka, with a memorial honoring the more than 300,000 individuals who lost their lives. This historical site holds great significance and offers travellers an opportunity to learn about the history and heritage of Warsaw's Jewish community.

Free

Saxon Garden

Saxon Garden, also known as Ogrod Saski, is a beautifully landscaped public park in Warsaw. It features Baroque statues, a landmark fountain, monuments, and tree-lined walkways. The park is part of a collection of gardens and parks that showcase the different visions of landscape architects throughout history. Saxon Garden offers a serene space for relaxation away from the city's hustle and bustle.

Free

Teatr Komedia

Nestled in the charming Zoliborz district of Warsaw, Teatr Komedia has been a beloved hub for light-hearted entertainment since its inception in 1957. This delightful venue, with a capacity of 485 seats, specializes in comedies, musicals, and whimsical shows that cater to both children and adults alike. Audiences can expect fast-paced performances brimming with witty dialogue and unexpected twists that promise plenty of laughter.

Free

Powsin Culture Park

The Powsin Culture Park (Park Kultury w Powsinie) is a large recreational area located on the southern outskirts of Warsaw, adjacent to the Kabaty Woods. The park offers extensive sports facilities, an outdoor swimming pool, picnic areas, and an amphitheater. It is a very popular weekend destination for Varsovians seeking outdoor activities and relaxation.

Free

Warsaw Royal Castle Gardens

Warsaw Royal Castle Gardens is a and well-preserved area located near the Royal Castle Warszawa. It's a beautiful spot with colorful flowers, making it especially enchanting during spring and summer. The entrance to the gardens is free, offering travellers plenty of places to relax and enjoy the picturesque surroundings. Situated close to the Vistula River, it's an ideal place for a leisurely stroll or to take in some views.

Free

Multimedialny Park Fontann

The Multimedia Fountain Park is located near the Old Town and the Vistula River in Warsaw. During the summer months, it hosts dramatic evening shows that combine synchronized water jets, lasers, and music, often projecting animated stories onto a screen of water. It is a major, vibrant attraction for both locals and tourists.

Free

Polish Army Museum

The Polish Army Museum, also known as Muzeum Wojska Polskiego, offers a captivating journey through Poland's military history. From ancient weaponry like shields and spears to modern aircraft and armored vehicles, the museum showcases a wide range of exhibits dating back to the 10th century. History enthusiasts will appreciate the extensive collection of artifacts, including weapons, armor, and uniforms from various periods.

Free

Bazar Olimpia market

Bazar Olimpia is a popular weekly outdoor market in Warsaw, offering a wide range of items from food and furniture to apparel and gifts. It has become a tradition for the city's residents since its establishment in the 1990s, growing significantly in size and popularity. travellers can immerse themselves in the local culture by exploring this expansive flea market, where they can find unique souvenirs and unexpected treasures.

Free

Skaryszewski Park named after Ignacy Jan Paderewski

Nestled in the vibrant Praga-Poludnie district, Skaryszewski Park in the name of Ignacy Jan Paderewski is a urban oasis that invites travellers to escape the city's hustle and bustle. This picturesque park boasts scenic walking trails winding through lush landscapes, serene ponds, and even kayak rentals for those looking to explore its tranquil waters.

Free

Sigismund's Column

Sigismund's Column, originally erected in 1644, is located at Castle Square, Warsaw, Poland and is one of Warsaw's most famous landmarks as well as the first secular monument in the form of a column in modern history. The column and statue commemorate King Sigismund III Vasa, who in 1596 had moved Poland's capital from Kraków to Warsaw. It is part of the Historic Centre of Warsaw, which was designated a UNESCO World Heritage Site on September 1-5, 1980.

Free

Mermaid's Statue

The Mermaid's Statue in Warsaw is a symbol of the city, representing its protective spirit and connection to the Vistula River. Erected in 1939 by sculptor Ludwika Nitschowa, it stands as an landmark and popular meeting point for locals and tourists. The statue depicts a mermaid with a sword and shield, known for her bravery in defending the city against harm.

Free

Observation Terrace on the bell tower of St. Anne's Church

Taras Widokowy na dzwonnicy kościoła św. Anny is a must-visit attraction in Warsaw's Old Town, offering panoramic views of the city and the Vistula River. The bell tower, originally built in the 16th century and reconstructed after being destroyed in 1945, requires climbing 150 stone steps to reach the viewing terrace.

PLN 70

Museum of King Jan III's Palace at Wilanów

Wilanów Palace is a former royal palace located in the Wilanów district of Warsaw, Poland. It was built between 1677 and 1696 for the King of Poland and Grand Duke of Lithuania John III Sobieski according to a design by architect Augustyn Wincenty Locci. Wilanów Palace survived Poland's partitions and both World Wars, and so serves as one of the most prominent examples of Baroque architecture in the country.
