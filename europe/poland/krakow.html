Kraków

Kraków served as the capital of Poland until 1596 and houses Wawel Castle and cathedral above the Vistula River. The medieval market square and St. Mary's Basilica survived wartime destruction that devastated Warsaw. German occupation from 1939 to 1945 included persecution in the Jewish quarter of Kazimierz. The historic center was inscribed as a UNESCO World Heritage site in 1978.
