Destination Guide — Central Poland

Explore Poznan

A Brief History

A pivotal early center of the Polish state, Poznan flourished as an essential medieval trade hub along the Warta River. Rebuilt meticulously after severe destruction in 1945, its vibrant Renaissance town hall and historic island cathedral symbolize enduring national resilience. The remarkable survival of these cultural and architectural milestones provides a powerful window into the region's storied past.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

Citadel Park

Park Cytadela in Poznan is not just a simple park, but also an open-air gallery with sculptures by various artists and part of the fortifications from the Prussian era. It's a valuable place for nature and biodiversity, making it popular for picnics and leisurely strolls. The park houses the historic Cytadela Fort from the 19th century and remnants of the former military fortress.

zł19

Palmiarnia Poznańska

Palmiarnia Poznańska is a century-old greenhouse located in the Art Nouveau part of the Lazarz district. It consists of 10 pavilions showcasing diverse ecosystems, from subtropical to savanna. Visitors can embark on an exciting journey through different climate zones and continents, surrounded by lush vegetation and the sound of rushing water.

Free

Old Zoo

Nestled within the historic Poznan Citadel, the Old Zoo, also affectionately known as the Zoo on the Citadel, is a charming destination that beautifully marries nature with history. This quaint zoo has roots dating back to 1874 and offers visitors a peaceful retreat filled with well-kept paths and lush greenery.

Free

Avenida Poznań

Avenida Poznań is a bustling shopping center located in the heart of Wielkopolska's capital. Spanning three floors with a total area of 60,000 square meters, it houses over 200 stores and 30 dining options. The center offers convenient parking with 1,500 spaces for motorists and free parking for cyclists. Additionally, it provides direct access to the Poznań railway and bus stations. Visitors praise its cleanliness and diverse shopping and dining choices.

Free

National Museum of Agriculture in Szreniawa

The National Museum of Agriculture in Szreniawa is a cultural gem that offers a fascinating insight into the history of food production and its impact on modern farming. The museum boasts an extensive collection showcasing folk culture, land reclamation, food processing, fertilization, and more. Visitors can explore vintage plows, tractors, bikes, and even interact with farm animals in the petting zoo.

Free

Posnania

Posnania is a massive contemporary mall in Poznan, offering over 250 shops and 40 cafes and restaurants. The bright, modern interior houses 260 stores, complemented by entertainment options like a cinema, bowling alley, and fitness club with a swimming pool. Additionally, it provides services such as the Concierge or Hands Free Shopping for stress-free shopping. The center's architecture features designer furniture and installations by European artists.

Free

Lake Malta

Lake Malta is a picturesque man-made lake that offers a variety of activities such as running and cycling. Adjacent to the lake, visitors can explore attractions like Jezyce with its street art and vibrant cafe scene, Kornik Castle where the legend of the 'White Lady' lingers, and the charming 'Vine Bridge' restaurant. In June, the Midsummer Night festival illuminates the sky with thousands of lanterns.

zł45

New Zoo

New Zoo is a sprawling 120-hectare zoological garden, making it the second largest in the country. The zoo is renowned for its modern naturalistic habitats and diverse array of animals, including African elephants, giraffes, zebras, Siberian tigers, and European bison. One of its highlights is the aviaries housing a variety of owls and participating in breeding programs for golden eagles.

Free

Stary Browar

Stary Browar is a unique 19th-century brick brewery transformed into a vibrant shopping and cultural center in Poznan. With over 200 stores and restaurants, it offers a diverse range of shopping experiences, from boutique shops to supermarkets. Visitors can explore an array of products, including fashion items, antiques, and rare vodkas at bargain prices. The complex also features the colorful Wielkopolski Square market, offering homemade goods and insights into Polish daily life.

Free

Basilica of Our Lady of Perpetual Help, Mary Magdalene and St. Stanislaus

The Basilica in Poznan is a monumental, historic church known as the Fara Poznańska. It is one of the most notable examples of Baroque architecture in Poland, featuring a impressive, richly decorated pink and white facade. The interior is equally lavish, with massive Corinthian columns, detailed stucco work, and a magnificent 19th-century organ.

Free

Museum of the History of Poznań

The Museum of the History of Poznań is a beautiful institution with rich history that is on display for tourists in the town hall. The museum has many interesting exhibits that cover a wide range of Poznan's history, from prehistoric times to present day. The interior of the building is impressively decorated with colorful paintings and intricate carved woodwork, and features a magnificent neo-Gothic clock tower.

Free

Fontanna Wolności

Dreamscape - Escape Room offers an exhilarating adventure for both children and adults, featuring four uniquely themed rooms that cater to varying levels of difficulty. One standout experience is the 'Dead Man's Revenge,' where participants are immersed in a captivating storyline involving a notorious art thief who has stolen the original Mona Lisa.

Free

The Imperial Castle in Poznań

The Imperial Castle, constructed between 1905 and 1910, is a prominent cultural center with bars, eateries, a museum, and tours. It's a favorite spot for photography enthusiasts due to its picturesque features like the Fountain of the Lions in the garden area. The castle's facade is adorned with intricate bas-reliefs depicting scenes from folklore and symbolic figures. Unlike most European cities that have one imperial castle, Poznan boasts two: the Royal Castle and the Imperial Castle.
