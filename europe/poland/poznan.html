Poznan

An early centre of the Polish state and a medieval fair town on the Warta, Poznań saw its island cathedral and Renaissance town hall rebuilt after 1945 destruction. Trade-fair grounds later added a modern economic layer to that Piast and mercantile core. St. Martin's croissant tradition and the June festival calendar grow from that fair-town culture.

Citadel Park

Citadel Park (Park Cytadela) spreads across nineteenth-century Prussian fortress grounds in Poznań, mixing lawns, sculpture displays, and surviving fort brickwork. Paths cross former military earthworks now used for picnics and walks. Museums and memorials occupy parts of the old citadel precinct.

Palmiarnia Poznańska

Poznan's Palm House fills a series of glass pavilions in Wilson Park with plants arranged by climate; renovations and environmental controls have changed the buildings over time, while the surrounding park remains a separate public landscape. Tropical trees, succulents, aquatic species, useful crops, and aquarium displays create a dense indoor collection whose roots reach the early twentieth century.

03 AdmissionFree

Old Zoo

Founded in the nineteenth century, Poznan's Old Zoo occupies compact grounds near the city center with mature trees, historic structures and a reduced collection of animals. Many large species moved to the New Zoo, leaving smaller exhibits, educational areas and park-like paths. The site provides the history of urban zoology, though current species and welfare arrangements need institutional verification.

04 AdmissionFree

Avenida Poznań

Three floors covering about 60,000 square meters hold more than 200 stores and roughly thirty dining spots beside Poznań's railway and bus stations. About 1,500 car spaces and free bicycle parking support day-long visits. Direct station access makes the mall a transit-linked retail stop.

National Museum of Agriculture in Szreniawa

The National Museum of Agriculture at Szreniawa occupies a former estate southwest of Poznan; tractors, tools, steam machinery and vehicles appear beside workshops, farm buildings and displays on livestock. Exhibitions and demonstrations trace cultivation, food processing, rural crafts and agricultural science, while seasonal programs change through the year.

Posnania

Roughly 250 to 260 stores plus dozens of cafés and restaurants fill Posnania's retail floors in Poznań. A cinema, bowling alley, and fitness club with a pool sit among the shops. Concierge-style services support longer shopping visits without leaving the complex.

07 AdmissionFree

Lake Malta

Lake Malta is a man-made lake that offers a variety of activities such as running and cycling. Adjacent to the lake, visitors can explore attractions like Jezyce with its street art and active cafe scene, Kornik Castle where the legend of the 'White Lady' lingers, and the 'Vine Bridge' restaurant. In June, the Midsummer Night festival illuminates the sky with thousands of lanterns.

New Zoo

Poznan's New Zoo spreads across wooded and lake-filled grounds on the eastern edge of the city. Large enclosures hold elephants, big cats, hoofed animals, birds and native species, with a miniature railway or shuttle sometimes assisting movement across the site. Collection size and breeding programs change, so the zoo's landscape, conservation work and division from the Old Zoo are more stable points of context.

09 AdmissionFree

Stary Browar

Stary Browar transformed the former Hugger brewery into a combined retail, art, office and event complex in central Poznan. Restored brick halls, new construction, courtyards, installations, shops and restaurants deliberately mix industrial fabric with contemporary design; it remains a privately operated commercial center, while exhibitions and performances change more often than the architecture.

10 AdmissionFree

Basilica of Our Lady of Perpetual Help, Mary Magdalene and St. Stanislaus

The Basilica in Poznan is a monumental, historic church known as the Fara Poznańska. It is a notable examples of Baroque architecture in Poland, featuring a, richly decorated pink and white facade. The interior is equally lavish, with massive Corinthian columns, detailed stucco work, and a 19th-century organ.

11 AdmissionFree

Museum of the History of Poznań

Inside Poznań's town hall, galleries trace the city's story from prehistoric finds to present-day civic life. Permanent rooms and rotating displays occupy historic chambers rather than a standalone modern wing. Posted hours govern entry to the public galleries.

12 AdmissionFree

Fontanna Wolności

Named for freedom, Fontanna Wolności sets water jets and basins at the center of an open Poznań plaza used for gathering and photos. The fountain belongs to the city's outdoor monumental furniture rather than an indoor museum. Steps and paving around the basins form the usual viewing ring.

52.4080° N / 16.9282° E

13 AdmissionFree

The Imperial Castle in Poznań

Constructed between 1905 and 1910, the Imperial Castle now holds bars, eateries, a museum, and guided tours behind a facade carved with folklore scenes and symbolic figures. Walls and courtyards organize the visitor route when the grounds are open. The complex works as a cultural center more than a private residence.
