Explore Lublin

A Brief History

As an important cultural and political bridge between Poland and Lithuania, Lublin flourished during the medieval and Renaissance periods. Despite profound 20th-century tragedies, its notably preserved old town reflects a long legacy of religious and intellectual diversity. Visitors today can clearly trace the detailed layers of this unique heritage throughout the preserved streets and monuments.

Botanical Garden UMCS

Botanical Garden UMCS is a public park in Lublin, Poland. The Ogród Botaniczny UMCS is a and botanical garden that offers an extensive range of plants and colorful flowers. The garden is perfect for families or for those seeking a peaceful afternoon surrounded by nature. Paths, lawns, and shaded areas make up the main public facilities.

Free

Multimedia Fountain

The Multimedia Fountain in Lublin, located in the renovated Litewski Square, is a blend of light, water, and sound. This modern attraction offers enjoyable views on summer evenings with colorful illuminations and choreographed water displays synchronized with various musical genres, promotional spots about Lublin are broadcast on the water curtain at certain times.

Free

Teatr im. Juliusza Osterwy

Teatr im. Juliusza Osterwy is a visitor site in Lublin, Poland. Juliusza Osterwy is a visitor site in Lublin. Housed in a beautifully reconstructed manor, visitors can explore an intimate exhibition featuring personal artifacts, manuscripts, maps, and 19th-century publications that vividly reflect the era's daily life. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Free

Plac Po Farze

Plac Po Farze, located between the Rynek and the Grodzka Gate in Lublin's Old Town, is a storied plaza with a rich historical significance. It was once home to the 13th-century Parish Church of St. Michael the Archangel, which was later demolished due to its deteriorating condition. However, remnants of this medieval church have been preserved and are now on display in the square.

Free

Dominican Abbey

The Dominican Abbey, also known as the Dominican Basilica and Monastery, is a attraction in Lublin. This grand building, located at the end of an alley in the Old Town, is one of the oldest structures in the city. Although it was initially founded in the 14th century and later ruined in 1575, extensive renovations have transformed it into a example of Baroque architecture.

Free

Archcathedral of St. John the Baptist and St. John the Evangelist

Archcathedral of St. John the Baptist and St. John the Evangelist is a religious building in Lublin, Poland. The Archcathedral in Lublin is a Baroque church and the largest in the city. It is a masterpiece of religious art and architecture. The exterior and main interior spaces are usually viewable outside service times.

Free

Brama Krakowska

The Cracow Gate, also known as Brama Krakowska, is a significant Gothic-style landmark in Lublin, Poland. Constructed in the 14th century, it is a symbol of the city and connects the City Centre with the Old Town. This historic gate was part of the royal route from Cracow to Vilnius and has served various purposes over time, including defense, fire observation tower, and residence for the clock-master.

zł40

Lublin Castle

Lublin Castle is a fortified historic site in Lublin, Poland. Originally built in the 14th century by King Kazimierz the Great, this medieval castle has undergone significant transformations over the years. The Donjon Tower remains a highlight, being one of the oldest structures on site. Walls, courtyards, or towers form the main visitor route when the grounds are open.

Free

Lublin Castle Fields

The Lublin Castle Fields (Błonia pod Zamkiem) is a large, open green space situated at the foot of the historic Lublin Castle. The area provides excellent, unobstructed views of the castle complex and the Old Town skyline. It is a popular recreational area for residents, frequently hosting outdoor concerts, festivals, and community events.

Free

Centre for the Meeting of Cultures in Lublin

Centre for the Meeting of Cultures in Lublin is a visitor site in Lublin, Poland. The Centre for the Meeting of Cultures in Lublin is a active cultural hub housed in an old monastery, now home to various galleries, a bookshop, and a cafe. The center hosts concerts, cinema screenings, and performances by the Lublin Dance Theatre. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.
