Explore Łodz

A Brief History

Established as a regional settlement beyond an older fortified core, the city developed through industrialization in the late 19th century, post-war reconstruction, and modern civic expansion that reshaped its public spaces, markets, and surrounding landscape. Restored religious buildings, civic buildings, and natural features now define the contemporary visitor experience.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

The Rose Passage

Nestled at 3 Piotrkowska Street, Pasaż Róży is a captivating courtyard that has undergone a transformation thanks to the artistic vision of Joanna Rajkowska. Once an uninviting space, it now dazzles with walls adorned in countless fragments of broken mirror glass, creating a mesmerizing mosaic that sparkles under the sun.

Free

Manufaktura

Manufaktura, located in Lodz, is a former 19th-century cotton factory that has been transformed into a vibrant shopping mall and entertainment complex. The project's success has revitalized the city's profile and demonstrated its resilience. travellers can explore the site's history at The Museum of the Factory and appreciate contemporary art at MS2 gallery.

Free

Priest's Mill

Priest's Mill, or Księży Młyn, is a captivating district in Łódź that beautifully marries its rich industrial past with contemporary charm. Once home to one of the city's largest textile factory complexes, this area has transformed into a vibrant cultural hub. As you stroll through its historic streets, discover an array of inviting cafes and restaurants like Winoteka for wine lovers and various eateries offering diverse cuisines.

Free

Palm House

Nestled within the historic Zrodliska Park, the Palm House stands as a example of modern architecture and botanical beauty. This greenhouse is home to an impressive collection of palms, some over 130 years old, reaching heights of up to 18 meters! travellers can wander through vibrant flowerbeds and a serene Japanese-style garden while enjoying views of exotic plants like bananas and cacti.

Free

Plastuś Monument

Park Ocalałych is a modern oasis in Lodz that serves as both a serene escape and a poignant memorial. This park honors the resilience of Holocaust survivors, featuring hundreds of trees planted by them, alongside significant WWII monuments. With its spacious grassy areas, it’s perfect for jogging or leisurely dog walks, making it an ideal spot for outdoor activities without the worry of obstacles like dense tree cover.

Free

Alexander Nevsky Cathedral

The Alexander Nevsky Cathedral in Łódź is a historical Orthodox church that was consecrated in 1884. This neo-Byzantine structure with its ornate interior and stained-glass windows is beautiful landmarks in the city. The cathedral's blue domes and valuable iconostasis from Saint Petersburg make it a for travellers. Built as a philanthropic project, it stands as a reminder of Łódź's history under the Russian Empire.

Free

Piotrkowska

Piotrkowska is a vibrant 4.2 km-long street in Lodz, known for its bustling atmosphere, diverse shopping options, artistic flair, and a wide array of restaurants and beer gardens. The northern half of the street is particularly appealing to travellers, offering an eclectic mix of shops, bars, cafes, and dining establishments. Meanwhile, the southern half provides a more tranquil experience with its straight cycle path and the nearby Reymonta Park.

Free

White Factory

The White Factory, or Biala Fabryka in Polish, is a restored textile factory that has been transformed into a museum complex. Its unique white plaster exterior distinguishes it from other brick factories in the area, and it has become a well-known historical landmark. The factory now houses two distinct exhibitions, offering travellers the opportunity to delve into Lodz's rich history.

Free

Atlas Arena

Atlas Arena is a prominent sports and entertainment venue in Poland, boasting state-of-the-art infrastructure that meets international standards. It has hosted renowned music concerts featuring global stars like Sting, Eric Clapton, Iron Maiden, and Depeche Mode. The arena offers full-service facilities for sports events as well as stage performances. travellers have praised the helpful staff who went above and beyond to assist them during events.

zł80

Orientarium ZOO Łódź

Łódź Zoo, also known as the Municipal Zoological Garden, is a popular attraction in the city of Łódź, Poland. Established in 1938, it is one of the oldest zoos in the country and houses over 350 species from around the world. travellers can enjoy feeding times and petting enclosures while exploring the diverse range of animal species.

zł209

Mandoria

Mandoria is an indoor theme park designed to resemble a 16th-century trading city, offering year-round entertainment with over 20 attractions. It caters to all ages but is particularly appealing to children aged 5-15, although adults can also enjoy the rides and activities. The park features two roller coasters, a Venetian carousel, and a six-track slide. Its historical decor reminiscent of Vienna's 17th/18th century adds to its charm.

Free

Arturówek

Arturówek, situated on the northern edge of Lodz within the Lagiewniki Forest, offers fantastic recreational opportunities for travellers. The area is perfect for leisurely walks, sunbathing, and active family recreation. travellers can explore Arturówek lake by pedalo or rowboat, relax on the beach, or experience an adrenaline rush at the rope course.
