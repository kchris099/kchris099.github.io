Łodz

Nineteenth-century textile mills under Russian imperial rule turned Łódź into a brick industrial metropolis along Piotrkowska Street. Multi-ethnic manufacturers and workers filled the city before war and nationalisation. After 1989, complexes such as Manufaktura reused those factories as museums and apartments. Piotrkowska's length made it a parade ground for industrial display and later nightlife.

The Rose Passage

The Rose Passage is a visitor site in Łodz. Located at 3 Piotrkowska Street, Pasaż Róży is a courtyard that has undergone a change thanks to the artistic vision of Joanna Rajkowska. Priest's Mill

Priest's Mill, or Księży Młyn, is a district in Łódź that beautifully marries its rich industrial past with contemporary charm. Once home to one of the city's largest textile factory complexes, this area has transformed into a active cultural hub. As visitors stroll through its historic streets, discover an array of inviting cafes and restaurants like Winoteka for wine lovers and various eateries offering diverse cuisines.

Plastuś Monument

Park Ocalałych is a modern oasis in Lodz that is both a quiet shoreline and a memorial. This park honors the resilience of Holocaust survivors, featuring hundreds of trees planted by them, alongside significant WWII monuments. With its spacious grassy areas, it's perfect for jogging or leisurely dog walks, making it an ideal spot for outdoor activities without the worry of obstacles like dense tree cover.

Alexander Nevsky Cathedral

The Alexander Nevsky Cathedral in Łódź is a historical Orthodox church that was consecrated in 1884. This neo-Byzantine structure with its ornate interior and stained-glass windows is landmarks in the city. The cathedral's blue domes and valuable iconostasis from Saint Petersburg make it a for travellers. Built as a philanthropic project, it is a reminder of Łódź's history under the Russian Empire.
