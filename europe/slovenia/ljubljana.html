Explore Ljubljana

A Brief History

Ljubljana developed at a crossroads of Alpine and Adriatic trade routes, ruled successively by Habsburg emperors and, after 1918, the Yugoslav kingdom. Earthquakes in 1511 and 1895 prompted rebuilding in Renaissance and Art Nouveau styles along the Ljubljanica River. Jože Plečnik's bridges, markets, and river embankments shaped the capital of independent Slovenia after 1991.

Ljubljana Zoo

Ljubljana Zoo is a compact city zoo located on the southern slope of the Roznik Hill, surrounded by woods and meadows. It's an ideal destination for a family outing, offering a children's playground, special events, and an on-site eatery. The zoo houses a diverse range of animals including birds, wild animals, and predatory species. Despite its smaller size, it offers pleasant surroundings for leisurely walks amidst rich flora and fauna.

Prešernov trg

Prešernov trg is a visitor site in Ljubljana, Slovenia. It holds historical significance as it was once the meeting point for several roads and an old entrance gate to the city. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest. Prešernov trg remains part of the public visitor circuit in Ljubljana.

Franciscan Church of the Annunciation

The Franciscan Church of the Annunciation, a 17th-century landmark in Ljubljana, is known for its striking pink facade and ornate interior adorned with ceiling frescoes. Situated on Preseren Square near the Ljubljanica river, this grand Baroque church stands out with its salmon-pink exterior and two bell towers. It competes for attention with other sights like Zmajski Most and the Triple Bridge.

Slovenian Philharmonic Building

The Slovenian Philharmonic Building is a significant landmark in Ljubljana, known for its graceful combination of various architectural styles including Baroque and Vienna Secession. It is located near the enchanting old town pedestrian zone and the Ljubljanica River, adding to the city's allure and cultural significance. The building houses a lovely medium-sized philharmonic orchestra that impresses with its technical ability and well-organized performances.

Saint Nicholas' Cathedral

Saint Nicholas's Cathedral, also known as Ljubljana Cathedral or the Church of St. Nicholas, is a Roman Catholic cathedral with a baroque interior featuring gilded columns and ceiling frescoes. The cathedral is an part of Ljubljana's skyline and has an ornate bronze side door that depicts centuries of Slovenian and Ljubljana's Catholic history.

Ljubljana Castle Funicular

The Ljubljana Castle Funicular, in operation for over 12 years, provides a popular and convenient way to access the medieval palace situated on the hill. With its lower station at Kreka Square, the one-minute journey offers panoramic views of Ljubljana from a considerable height. Visitors can enjoy capturing the sights of the city while seated by the glass in the booth.

Ljubljana Castle

Ljubljana Castle, a medieval fortress towering over the townhouses below, offers a journey through history and culture. Visitors can ascend to the castle via a glass cube and explore its first-rate museums, including the Virtual Castle showing its development, an exhibition on Slovene history with insights into socialist Yugoslavia and the Ten-Day War, and the Museum of Puppetry. The stocky Clock Tower provides unparalleled views of the old town and distant Kamniske Alps.

Sts. Cyril and Methodius Church

Cyril and Methodius Church, also known as the Orthodox Church, is a Serbian Orthodox Church located in Ljubljana. The church was constructed between 1932 and 1936 with neo-Byzantine architecture that sets it apart from other churches in the city. Designed by architect Momir Korunovic, the interior has active colors and distinctive iconography, creating a peaceful and reflective atmosphere for visitors to enjoy.

National Gallery of Slovenia

The National Gallery of Slovenia, established in 1918, is the main art museum in the country and houses an extensive collection of fine art spanning from the 13th century to the 20th century. Housed in a Neo-Renaissance palace with a modern glass facade, the gallery's permanent exhibition showcases nearly 600 works by Slovenian and European artists.

Trg republike

Trg republike is a visitor site in Ljubljana, Slovenia. Construction and early use date to around 1975. The Trg Republike is a public square in the center of Ljubljana, Slovenia. The square has several monuments, including the Monument to Revolution from 1975 and the Slovenian Parliament from 1992. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Narodni muzej Slovenije

Narodni muzej Slovenije, housed in a Renaissance palace, is the country's oldest museum and a must-visit for history enthusiasts. It shares its building with the Slovenian Museum of Natural History and has an extensive collection of archaeological artifacts, old coins, banknotes, and applied arts displays. The main building is a Neo-Renaissance gem built between 1883 and 1885. Visitors can explore exhibits illustrating the country's history, including maritime history and ancient artifacts.

Tivoli Park

Tivoli Park is a public park in Ljubljana, Slovenia. Tivoli Park is Ljubljana's largest park, covering 5 square kilometers of formal gardens, paved paths, and grassy picnic areas. Paths, lawns, and shaded areas make up the main public facilities. Tivoli Park remains part of the public visitor circuit in Ljubljana.
