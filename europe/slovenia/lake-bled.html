Lake Bled

Medieval bishops of Brixen held the cliff castle above the glacial lake; pilgrimage to the island church followed. Nineteenth-century resorts then sold Bled as an Alpine cure landscape. Castle, island, and lakeside villas form a single pilgrimage-to-spa sequence. Traditional pletna boats remain the regulated way to reach the island church. Cream cake (kremšnita) became a local culinary emblem of the resort decades.

Soteska Vintgar

Soteska Vintgar, discovered in 1891, is a 1.6-km gorge with a 500-m span of bridges and galleries. It's a popular spot that can get crowded with tourists, but it's worth the visit. Even bike there from Bled in about 25 minutes. The wooden walkway crosses over the rapids of the Radovna river for almost a mile, offering views in all weather conditions.

Waterfall Šum

Waterfall Šum is a, 13-meter-high cascade located at the end of the dramatic Vintgar Gorge, near Bled. It is the largest river waterfall in Slovenia. Visitors reach the falls by walking along the wooden boardwalks suspended over the Radovna River, culminating in a viewing platform that offers excellent perspectives of the powerful, rushing water.

46.4007° N / 14.0992° E

Lake Bled

Lake Bled is a destination, surrounded by mountains and forests, with a island that houses a historic church. The high season runs from May to October, attracting tourists from various countries. The local cuisine offers modern and diverse dishes, including homemade pasta, soups, and smoked trout specialties. Visitors can enjoy tasting menus featuring light and fresh dishes made from natural ingredients.

04 AdmissionFree

Church of the Assumption of the Blessed Virgin Mary

The Church of the Assumption of the Blessed Virgin Mary is a historic and church located on a tiny island in Lake Bled, Slovenia. Accessible by boat, this church stands on the site of an eighth-century Slavic pagan temple and is a popular spot for weddings. Tradition dictates that grooms carry their brides up the 99 steps leading to the church door, adding to its romantic allure.

46.3626° N / 14.0902° E

05 AdmissionFree

Ojstrica

Ojstrica, a mountain in the Kamnik-Savinja Alps, has a distinctive pyramid-shaped peak and offers climbing routes. Hiking to Ojstrica Hill provides a bird's eye view of Lake Bled and its church on Bled Island. For a more secluded experience, there are two other popular locations further up the hill. Visitors are encouraged to began on an early morning hike to witness the sunrise reflecting off the lake from this vantage point.

46.3596° N / 14.0786° E

06 AdmissionFree

Mala Osojnica

Mala Osojnica is a hilltop viewpoint that offers panoramic views of Lake Bled and its surroundings. Situated at an elevation of 670 meters, reaching this viewpoint involves a steep and challenging hike, but the effort is rewarded with vistas. From this vantage point, visitors can enjoy a broad perspective of the lake with the mountains in the background, including notable landmarks such as Bled Island, Bled Castle, Mt. Stol, and Vrtaca.

46.3569° N / 14.0824° E

07 AdmissionFree

The Heart of Bled

"The Bled" is a wooden deck overlooking the lake, offering views of Bled Castle and featuring a heart-shaped frame that's popular for photos. Designed by gardeners Matevz and Joza Cop, it was influenced by Swedish landscaper Swenson. The area around Vila Pongratz used to be a cultural center with a music pavilion, tennis courts, and sweet stalls.

46.3685° N / 14.1083° E

08 AdmissionFree

Zipline Dolinka Bled

Zipline Dolinka offers an exhilarating eco-adventure near Bled, featuring a series of long ziplines that cross the protected valley of the Sava Dolinka river. The guided tour provides thrilling aerial views of the lush forest canopy, the river below, and the surrounding Julian Alps, combining high-speed excitement with environmental awareness and natural scenery.

46.3709° N / 14.1078° E

09 AdmissionFree

Bled Castle

Bled Castle clings to a cliff above Lake Bled, with origins recorded from 1004 when Emperor Henry II granted the estate; Bishops of Brixen held it for centuries. Medieval and later buildings include a chapel, printing workshop, and museum rooms on lake life. Terraces look straight down to the island church.

46.3699° N / 14.1006° E
