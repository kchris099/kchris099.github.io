Piran

Salt pans and Venetian protection enriched Piran as a compact Adriatic town. Tartini Square and Gothic-Venetian houses face the harbour that tied the peninsula to the Serenissima. Slovenian Piran remains one of the clearest Venetian urban footprints on the eastern Adriatic. Giuseppe Tartini's birthplace museum ties the violinist to the square that bears his name.

Tartinijev trg

Tartini Square centers on a monument to composer and violinist Giuseppe Tartini, with the Church of St. Peter and Tartini House, his birthplace, among the surrounding historic buildings. Narrow Istrian streets lead off the oval paving. Views continue toward nearby church towers above the harbor town.

Akvarij Piran

Akvarij Piran displays Adriatic fish and other coastal species in tanks along the Slovenian shore town. A low ledge helps children view the waterline while adults read panels on local marine habitats. The compact aquarium focuses on regional fauna rather than exotic oceanaria, fitting Piran's fishing-port setting beside the Venetian-era old town.

Church of St. George

Piran's Church of St George, rebuilt in Baroque form in the early seventeenth century on a site recorded from 1344, crowns the ridge above the Tartini Square harbour. A parish museum opens treasury objects and crypt spaces beyond the grille view of the nave. The terrace looks over terracotta roofs to the sea.

Walls of Piran

The Walls of Piran are an ancient and fortified stone town wall with several notable gates, which have been renovated since the 7th century. The walls have undergone changes over time due to the city's expansion, resulting in the preservation of three different walls and seven city gates. While some areas have deteriorated, much of the original construction from between the 7th and 15th centuries remains intact.

45.5274° N / 13.5716° E

Piranski svetilnik

Piran Lighthouse occupies the seaward tip of the old town beside the Church of St. Clement and a former fortified round tower. Maritime equipment, stone walls, and views across the Adriatic define the compact site, though interior and tower access are not always available.

06 AdmissionFree

Mesečev Zaliv

Mesecov Zaliv, Moon Bay, is a rocky cove on the Slovenian coast near Piran where swimmers enter clear water from stone shelves and small beaches. Pine shade and cliff edges frame a quieter alternative to busier Portoroz strands nearby. Access paths drop from the coastal road, and the bay stays popular for summer swimming rather than built resort amenities on the sand.

45.5375° N / 13.6099° E

Da Ponte Fountain

The Da Ponte Fountain in the coastal old town, traditionally linked to Venetian architect Antonio da Ponte and dated to 1666, combines Baroque ornament with a working water basin. Carved details and a central structure mark a civic square stop among narrow lanes. Though sometimes confused with Koper listings, the fountain belongs to the Venetian-influenced streetscape visitors walk in Piran.

45.5455° N / 13.7298° E

09 AdmissionFree

Taverna

Taverna, located in Carpaccio square, was originally a salt warehouse built in the late 16th century. As salt panning declined in the early 20th century, the building found new purpose hosting various events and concerts. The area around Taverna offers ocean views and is dotted with popular cafes and restaurants. Travellers can also enjoy international cuisine festivals here.

45.5479° N / 13.7256° E

10 AdmissionFree

Naravni rezervat Škocjanski zatok

Skocjan Inlet Nature Reserve protects a restored brackish wetland shaped by freshwater, tidal influence and former industrial development. Circular paths and observation hides pass reed beds and shallow lagoons. Grazing areas and open water support many bird species. The reserve demonstrates rehabilitation of a degraded coastal edge, while its flat trails provide close views of habitat management and seasonal wildlife.

45.5507° N / 13.7603° E
