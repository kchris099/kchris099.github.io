Destination Guide — Dinant

Explore Dinant

A Brief History

Dinant grew in the Middle Ages around the strategic crossing of the Meuse River, developing a highly successful brass and copper working industry known as dinanderie. The town suffered frequent sieges and destruction due to its border position between the Prince-Bishopric of Liège and the County of Namur, culminating in the devastating sack of 1466 by Charles the Bold. It also endured heavy combat during World War I. Today, the town's resilient history is embodied in the striking Collegiate Church of Notre Dame and the imposing Citadel towering above the river.

Top Sights & Activities

Vêves Castle

Vêves Castle, a 15th-century fortress located in the village of Celles, stands on a rocky spur that has been fortified since the 7th century. The castle features six towers and an impressive galleried courtyard with an 18th-century Louis XV style facade. Although its outward appearance remains largely unchanged, the interior has undergone several renovations over the years.

€12

Castle of Freÿr

Located on the left bank of the Meuse River, the Castle of Freÿr is a Renaissance castle with opulent period furnishings and formal gardens featuring hedge mazes. Once a defensive stronghold, it was later expanded in Renaissance and Baroque styles. Visitors can take self-guided tours to explore its rich history and admire its well-maintained terraced garden.

Closed

Walzin Castle

Nestled in the picturesque Meuse Valley, Walzin Castle is a historical gem located near Dinant, Belgium. This majestic 13th-century fortress sits atop cliffs overlooking the serene River Lesse, making it an ideal spot for photography enthusiasts seeking to capture its enchanting beauty amidst lush nature. As one of Belgium's largest castles, it offers visitors a glimpse into the country's rich history and architectural grandeur.

€8

Furfooz Nature Reserve

Furfooz Nature Reserve offers a 4 km trail through forests, meadows, and caves with remnants of early human and Roman presence. The route is suitable for all ages and provides opportunities to observe birds like falcons. Accessible by car or bike, the trail features a river-view restaurant for a pit stop. Visitors can expect well-maintained paths with informative boards along the way.

€15

The Citadel of Dinant

Dinant Citadel is a medieval stronghold and museum that can be accessed via 408 steps or a cable car. It offers panoramic views of the valley. Admission fee of 11 is required, regardless of where one parks. A ticket counter can be found at both the top and bottom entrances, with limited access. Visitors may choose between taking the stairs or cable car, but must still wait in line and pay the fee.

Free

The house of Mister Sax

Maison Adolphe Sax is a small museum dedicated to the life and inventions of Adolphe Sax, the creator of the saxophone. The museum showcases various forms and shapes of his invention, along with documentation about his life and achievements. Despite its small size, it's well-kept and offers lots of information for visitors. Entrance is free, making it a worthwhile stop for those interested in jazz or musical history.

Free

Charles de Gaulle Bridge

Pont Charles de Gaulle, also known as the Charles de Gaulle Bridge, holds a significant place in history due to its connection to the Battle of Dinant during World War I. The bridge is a popular tourist attraction located near the Citadel of Dinant and offers views of the Meuse River. It is adorned with multiple saxophones representing different countries, serving as a tribute to the European Union.

€10

Grotte de Dinant La Merveilleuse

Grotte de Dinant La Merveilleuse is a captivating cave located on the left bank of the Meuse, discovered in 1904. It boasts concretions in various colors such as pink, brown, blue, and bright white, along with subterranean waterfalls. Visitors can explore this show-cave through a guided tour and climb down a light stairway of 120 steps to witness its natural wonders.

Free

Rocher Bayard

Rocher Bayard is a striking 40-meter-tall rock formation located on the right bank of the Meuse between Dinant and Anseremme. It is part of an impressive limestone ridge and can be accessed by driving along Rue Defoin. According to local folklore, Rocher Bayard broke away due to the hooves of the legendary warhorse Bayard carrying four knights.

Free

Castle of Crevecoeur

Ruins of a medieval castle located on a hilltop, offering panoramic views and a glimpse into the region's history. It remains a notable local point of interest situated in Dinant, Belgium. Visitors can observe the surrounding area and its connection to the city's broader historical landscape. Local guides sometimes include it on walking routes through the centre.
