Bosnia and Herzegovina

Bosnia and Herzegovina emerged from the Ottoman empire after centuries of contact between Christian, Muslim, and Orthodox communities along the Dinaric Alps. Austro-Hungarian administration added secular schools, Latin-script printing, and Bahnhof-style civic architecture to Sarajevo, while Mostar's medieval stonework and Ottoman bridges — destroyed in the 1990s war and rebuilt since — anchor the country's tangible heritage as a southeastern European crossroads.
