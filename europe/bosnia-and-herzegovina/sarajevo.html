Sarajevo

Sarajevo rose as an Ottoman provincial capital in the 15th century, with mosques, bazaars, and the Gazi Husrev-beg complex shaping its Baščaršija core. Habsburg rule after 1878 added tram lines, civic buildings, and a multi-confessional street plan. The 1914 assassination of Archduke Franz Ferdinand marked the city in world history. Siege damage from the 1990s war is still visible beside rebuilt markets, museums, and hillside neighbourhoods.
