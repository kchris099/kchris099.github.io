Destination Guide — Sarajevo

Explore Sarajevo

A Brief History

Established as a regional settlement beyond an older fortified core, the city developed through industrialization in the late 19th century, post-war reconstruction, and modern civic expansion that reshaped its public spaces, markets, and surrounding landscape. Restored religious buildings, civic buildings, and natural features now define the contemporary visitor experience.

Top Sights & Activities

Free (observation deck BAM 5)

Avaz Twist Tower

Avaz Twist Tower is a must-visit attraction located just outside the city center of Sarajevo, Bosnia and Herzegovina. This skyscraper houses various businesses and features a 35th-floor coffee bar as well as a 36th-floor observation deck offering panoramic views of the city and its surrounding landscapes, including the Dinaric Alps. The tower's unique architectural design, characterized by its twisted structure, adds to its charm and allure.

Free

Baščaršija

Baščaršija is a historic market in Sarajevo's Old Town, dating back to the 1400s. It was originally built as a caravanserai and has since evolved into a bustling Oriental bazaar with mosques, hammams, and cobbled alleyways. The district became a thriving trading hub in the 17th century, housing numerous workshops practicing various crafts.

5 BAM

Sevdah Art House

Sevdah Art House is a cultural oasis in the heart of Sarajevo, dedicated to preserving and promoting the traditional Bosnian sevdalinka music. Nestled in an authentic environment, it offers an intimate and warm atmosphere for travellers to immerse themselves in the soul-stirring performances of sevdalinka rooted in Bosnian tradition. The museum showcases famous singers who have interpreted these urban love songs.

BAM 3

Gazi Husrev-beg Mosque

Nestled in the vibrant Bascarsija Bazaar, Gazi Husrev-beg Mosque stands as a testament to the rich Ottoman heritage of Bosnia and Herzegovina. Completed in 1532, this magnificent mosque is not only the largest in the country but also significant architectural marvels in the Balkans.

Free

Tašlihan

Tašlihan is a large historical landmark in Sarajevo, Bosnia and Herzegovina. It used to be one of three caravanserais in the city (along with Morica Han and Kolobara Han), which served both travelers and horses. Tašlihan is now a popular tourist spot, with good visibility as well as being a great spot for photos.

Free

Latin Bridge

Latin Bridge, an Ottoman-era stone bridge in Sarajevo, Bosnia and Herzegovina, is a significant historical site. It gained notoriety as the location of the assassination of Archduke Franz Ferdinand of Austria in 1914, an event that triggered World War I. The bridge spans the Miljacka River and serves as a poignant reminder of the city's tumultuous past.

Free

Emperor's Mosque

The Emperor's Mosque, also known as Careva Dzamija, is Sarajevo's oldest place of worship, dating back to 1457-1565. Situated on the south bank of the River Milittska, it was commissioned by Sarajevo Governor Issa Aahakovic and has undergone numerous restoration and expansion projects throughout its history. With a capacity for about five hundred worshippers, it remains a significant mosque in Bosnia.

10 BAM

Gradska vijećnica Sarajevo

Sarajevo City Hall, also known as the Sarajevo City Council building, is a historic landmark in the city. Originally built in 1896, it served as the headquarters of ruling authorities and later as a national library until it suffered significant damage during the Serbian shelling of the city in 1992. After extensive restoration, it reopened to travellers in 2014.

Free

Yellow Fortress

Nestled atop a hill, the Yellow Fortress, or Zuta Tabija, is an 18th-century bastion that offers some of the most views of Sarajevo and its surrounding mountains. This historic site has become a beloved destination for both locals and travelers seeking to witness sunsets that bathe the city in golden hues.

BAM 5

White Fortress

White Fortress, also known as Bijela Tabija, is an ancient stone fortress located on the southeastern slopes of Sarajevo. The ruins offer panoramic views of the city and the Miljacka River. The fortress has a rich history dating back to medieval times and has been shaped by various periods including Ottoman and Austro-Hungarian rule. Despite being marred by graffiti, it remains a popular spot for travellers seeking vistas of Sarajevo valley.

Free

Pijaca Markale food market

Pijaca Markale food market is housed in a neoclassical building and offers a variety of local produce, meats, and dairy products. After exploring the market, travellers can step outside into the square and relax at one of the nearby cafes. The market's history is marked by significant events, including bombings in 1994 and 1995 that had far-reaching consequences.

Free

Sacred Heart Cathedral

Sacred Heart Cathedral, In Sarajevo, is a 19th-century Gothic-style Catholic church. The cathedral's twin towers and rose window dominate the city's central square, showcasing its grandeur. Designed by Austro-Hungarian architect Josip Vancas, the cathedral boasts neogothic architecture that harmonizes with the surrounding historic buildings.

BAM 2

Orthodox Church of the Nativity of the Theotokos

Nestled in the heart of Sarajevo, the Orthodox Church of the Nativity of the Theotokos stands as a magnificent testament to 19th-century architecture. Constructed in 1874 with contributions from local citizens and merchants across several cities, this grand church features an intricate interior adorned with portraits and stained glass.

Free

Vječna vatra

The Eternal Flame memorial in Sarajevo, Bosnia and Herzegovina is a beautiful tribute to those who lost their lives in the Second World War. The memorial with an eternal flame is In the city and is a popular tourist destination. However, some people have criticized the monument because it's often crowded and difficult to find parking nearby.

BAM 15

Sarajevo National Theatre

The Sarajevo National Theatre, situated in the heart of the city, is a prestigious cultural institution with a rich tradition and a significant role in promoting theater art. Established to foster and enhance theater art, it has evolved into a symbol of the city's cultural scene. The building itself is an impressive structure dating back to the 1920s, showcasing architecture and offering travellers an experience.

Free (cable car BAM 30)

Olympic bobsleigh and luge track Trebević

Nestled in the mountains above Sarajevo, the Olympic bobsleigh and luge track Trebević stands as a haunting yet captivating remnant of the 1984 Winter Olympics. Once bustling with athletes and spectators, this now-abandoned track has become a canvas for nature's reclamation and vibrant street art. As you stroll along its winding concrete paths, you're enveloped by fresh mountain air and surrounded by lush greenery, creating an enchanting atmosphere that contrasts sharply with its storied past.

Free

SCC - Sarajevo City Center

SCC - Sarajevo City Center is the ultimate destination for modern shopping in Sarajevo, offering over 150 shops, an amusement zone, bars, cafes and international restaurants. It's a hub of contemporary spirit where travellers can explore a wide range of stores featuring famous brands and cutting-edge technology. The center also boasts exclusive cafes and restaurants that offer exquisite gastronomic experiences.

Free

Ali Pasha’s mosque

Nestled in the heart of Sarajevo, Ali Pasha's Mosque is a representation of 16th-century Ottoman architecture. Constructed in 1561 under the patronage of Ali Pasha, the governor of Bosnia, this mosque features a striking dome and an elegant minaret that reaches towards the sky. Surrounded by a tranquil courtyard adorned with lush gardens and a cemetery, it offers visitors a serene escape from the bustling city life.
