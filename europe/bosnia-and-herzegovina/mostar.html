Destination Guide — Mostar

Explore Mostar

A Brief History

Developing around a 16th-century Ottoman bridge, Mostar became a thriving multicultural trading hub connecting the Adriatic with the Bosnian interior. Despite severe destruction during the 1990s conflict, its restored historic core stands as a powerful symbol of regional resilience. This rich historical tapestry established a strong foundation that continues to shape the city's dynamic contemporary identity.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

Old Bridge Mostar

The Old Bridge Mostar, also known as Stari Most, is a reconstructed Ottoman-era arched bridge over the Neretva River. Originally built in 1566 and destroyed during the Bosnian War in 1993, it has been restored to its former glory and is now a UNESCO World Heritage Site. The bridge holds historical significance as a symbol of connection between Christianity and Islam. Visitors can witness locals and adventure seekers jumping off the nearly 30-meter high bridge into the river below.

Free

Koski Mehmed Pasha Mosque

The Koski Mehmed Pasha Mosque, dating back to the 17th century, is a significant symbol of Mostar's diverse religious heritage. This Ottoman structure features a classic dome and a lower minaret, showcasing meticulous craftsmanship in its design. Founded by Mehmed Koskija and completed by his brother Mahmud, the mosque offers striking views of the river and the town.

Free

Neretva

Neretva is a small town known for the emerald green waters of the Neretva river, which is said to be one of the coldest rivers in the world. The town exudes historical and cultural significance, with every corner along its riverbank holding a unique story. Mostar's tradition includes boys leaping off the 25m tall Stari Most into the cold and fast-flowing Neretva River below.

Free

Millennium Cross

The Millennium Cross, also known as the Cross on the Hum, is a 100-foot high Roman Catholic memorial monument located on a hilltop in Bosnia & Herzegovina. It is considered the tallest cross in the country and holds significance as a pilgrimage site. Visitors can reach the site by taking a taxi or hiking up using suggested routes or hiking trails. While offering a fantastic view of the city, it's important to note that Old Town may not be visible from this vantage point.

Free

Roman Catholic Cathedral of Mary, Mother of the Church

The ideal way to enjoy your final evening in Mostar, following two days of active sightseeing, is to explore the vibrant nightlife at bars such as Black Dog, Shanklys, or the Brew Pub. Alternatively, return to the old town and relish the last moments of your visit by securing a table with a scenic view in one of Bosnia's most captivating destinations. The Catholic cathedral known as Cathedral of Mary, Mother of the Church stands out as an impressive architectural masterpiece.

Free

Spanish square

Španski Trg, located in the heart of Mostar, is a serene parkway offering a peaceful retreat from the city's hustle and bustle. Lined with lush greenery and vibrant flower beds, the picturesque square features plenty of benches for visitors to relax and enjoy the tranquil surroundings. Flanked by historic buildings and a memorial, it holds emotional significance amidst its beautiful architecture.

Free

Park Zrinjevac

Park Zrinjevac is a charming city park with well-maintained walking paths, playgrounds for children, and plenty of shaded benches. Visitors can enjoy Bosnian-style coffee and local treats from vendors selling fresh fruit, preserves, and nuts. The park offers a peaceful retreat in the heart of Mostar center, with opportunities for relaxation and leisurely strolls.

Free

Karađoz Beg Mosque

The Karađoz Beg Mosque, also known as the Karadoz-beg Mosque, is a significant 16th-century Ottoman mosque situated in Mostar, Bosnia and Herzegovina. It stands as a symbol of the region's Ottoman history and architecture. Despite being heavily damaged during various conflicts, including the Bosnian War and World War II, it was meticulously restored in the early 2000s.

Free

Biscevic House

Nestled along the picturesque east bank of the Neretva River, Biscevic House, or Biscevica Kuca, stands as a testament to Ottoman-era architecture in Mostar. This 17th-century Turkish house museum invites visitors to step back in time and experience the rich cultural heritage of the region.

Free

Mostar Peace Bell Tower

The Mostar Peace Bell Tower is a prominent modern structure located adjacent to the Franciscan Church of St. Peter and Paul in Mostar. Standing over 100 meters tall, it offers visitors an elevator ride to an observation deck with panoramic views of the city and the surrounding Neretva River valley. The tower serves as a symbol of post-war reconstruction and reconciliation.
