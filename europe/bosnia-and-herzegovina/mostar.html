Destination Guide — Mostar

Explore Mostar

A Brief History

Developing around a 16th-century Ottoman bridge, Mostar became a multicultural trading hub connecting the Adriatic with the Bosnian interior. Despite severe destruction during the 1990s conflict, its restored historic core a powerful symbol of regional resilience. Museums, markets, and street plans in Mostar still show layers from those periods. Administrative and transport changes in later centuries reshaped the town plan of Mostar.

Top Sights & Activities

Free (museum BAM 10)

Old Bridge Mostar

The Old Bridge Mostar, also known as Stari Most, is a reconstructed Ottoman-era arched bridge over the Neretva River. Originally built in 1566 and destroyed during the Bosnian War in 1993, it has been restored to its former glory and is now a UNESCO World Heritage Site. The bridge holds historical significance as a symbol of connection between Christianity and Islam. Visitors can witness locals and adventure seekers jumping off the nearly 30-meter high bridge into the river below.

BAM 12

Koski Mehmed Pasha Mosque

Koski Mehmed Pasha Mosque is a religious building in Mostar, Bosnia and Herzegovina. The Koski Mehmed Pasha Mosque, dating back to the 17th century, is a significant symbol of Mostar's diverse religious heritage. Founded by Mehmed Koskija and completed by his brother Mahmud, the mosque offers striking views of the river and the town. The exterior and main interior spaces are usually viewable outside service times.

Free

Neretva

Neretva is a small town known for the emerald green waters of the Neretva river, which is said to be one of the coldest rivers in the world. The town exudes historical and cultural significance, with every corner along its riverbank holding a unique story. Mostar's tradition includes boys leaping off the 25m tall Stari Most into the cold and fast-flowing Neretva River below.

Free

Millennium Cross

The Millennium Cross, also known as the Cross on the Hum, is a 100-foot high Roman Catholic memorial monument located on a hilltop in Bosnia & Herzegovina. It is considered the tallest cross in the country and holds significance as a pilgrimage site. Visitors can reach the site by taking a taxi or hiking up using suggested routes or hiking trails. While offering a fantastic view of the city, it's important to note that Old Town may not be visible from this vantage point.

Free

Roman Catholic Cathedral of Mary, Mother of the Church

The ideal way to enjoy visitor final evening in Mostar, following two days of active sightseeing, is to explore the active nightlife at bars such as Black Dog, Shanklys, or the Brew Pub. Alternatively, return to the old town and relish the last moments of a visit by securing a table with a scenic view in one of Bosnia's most destinations. The Catholic cathedral known as Cathedral of Mary, Mother of the Church stands out as an architectural masterpiece.

Free

Spanish square

Spanish square is a public square in Mostar, Bosnia and Herzegovina. Flanked by historic buildings and a memorial, it holds emotional significance amidst its architecture. Open pavement and surrounding facades define the visitor setting. Spanish square remains part of the public visitor circuit in Mostar.

Free

Park Zrinjevac

Park Zrinjevac is a city park with well-maintained walking paths, playgrounds for children, and plenty of shaded benches. Visitors can enjoy Bosnian-style coffee and local treats from vendors selling fresh fruit, preserves, and nuts. The park offers a peaceful retreat in Mostar center, with opportunities for relaxation and leisurely strolls.

Free

Karađoz Beg Mosque

The Karađoz Beg Mosque, also known as the Karadoz-beg Mosque, is a significant 16th-century Ottoman mosque situated in Mostar, Bosnia and Herzegovina. It is a symbol of the region's Ottoman history and architecture. Despite being heavily damaged during various conflicts, including the Bosnian War and World War II, it was careful restored in the early 2000s.

BAM 4

Biscevic House

Located along the east bank of the Neretva River, Biscevic House, or Biscevica Kuca, is a evidence of Ottoman-era architecture in Mostar. This 17th-century Turkish house museum invites visitors to step back in time and experience the local history of the region.

BAM 6

Mostar Peace Bell Tower

The Mostar Peace Bell Tower is a prominent modern structure located adjacent to the Franciscan Church of St. Peter and Paul in Mostar. Standing over 100 meters tall, it has an elevator ride to an observation deck with panoramic views of the city and the surrounding Neretva River valley. The tower is a symbol of post-war reconstruction and reconciliation.
