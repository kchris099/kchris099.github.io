Jajce

Jajce served as the last capital of the independent Kingdom of Bosnia before falling to the Ottoman Empire in 1528. Its strategic location at the confluence of the Pliva and Vrbas rivers made it a heavily fortified stronghold that changed hands between Ottoman and Austro-Hungarian forces over many years. During World War II, the town hosted the important 1943 AVNOJ conference that established the foundation of socialist Yugoslavia. Visitors can explore this varied past through the medieval fortress, the catacombs, and the Ottoman-era wooden watermills.
