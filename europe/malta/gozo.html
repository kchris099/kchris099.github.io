Explore Gozo

A Brief History

Gozo is the second-largest island of the Maltese archipelago and was settled in prehistoric times, with the Ggantija temples dating from around 3600 BCE. Phoenician, Roman, and Arab rulers left few standing structures, while the Knights of St. John built churches and the Citadel in Victoria. Fishing and agriculture long supported the population. Ggantija is part of Malta's UNESCO World Heritage temple sites.

Bazilika tal-Madonna Ta'Pinu

The Basilica of the National Shrine of the Blessed Virgin of Ta' Pinu is a Roman Catholic minor basilica and national shrine located some 700 metres from the village of Għarb on the island of Gozo, the sister island of Malta. Pope Pius XI granted a Pontifical decree of coronation, Marianum exstat Sanctuarium towards its venerated Marian image, the Blessed Virgin of Ta' Pinu on 24 May 1935.

€3

Rotunda St. John Baptist Church

The Rotunda of St. John the Baptist is a historic, circular Roman Catholic church located in the town of Xewkija, Malta. Completed in the 1970s, it boasts one of the largest unsupported domes in the world, dominating the skyline of Gozo. The impressive limestone structure can accommodate the entire population of the village and offers panoramic views from its roof.

€5

Cathedral of the Assumption

The Cathedral of the Assumption of the Blessed Virgin Mary into Heaven is a Roman Catholic cathedral in the Cittadella of Victoria in Gozo, Malta. The cathedral is dedicated to the Assumption of Mary, and has been the seat of the Roman Catholic Diocese of Gozo since the formation of the diocese in 1864.

Free (heritage museum €5)

Cittadella

The Cittadella, also known as the Castello, is the citadel of Victoria on the island of Gozo, Malta. The area has been inhabited since the Bronze Age, and the site now occupied by the Cittadella is believed to have been the acropolis of the Punic-Roman city of Gaulos or Glauconis Civitas. During the medieval period, the acropolis was converted into a castle which served as a refuge for Gozo's population.
