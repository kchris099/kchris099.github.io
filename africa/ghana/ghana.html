Ghana

Ghana emerged as the first sub-Saharan African country to gain independence on 6 March 1957 from British colonial administration that produced the Gold Coast colony. The modern state consolidated centuries of Akan, Ewe, and Ga polities, while the slave-fortress belt along the country's Gulf of Guinea seaboard and the 14th-century Asante Kingdom's kente-cloth craft anchor a documented Ancestral heritage. Travellers read Ghana's multilayered record at the coastal Cape Coast Castle, the inland Ashanti royal palaces at Kumasi, and the central markets at Accra.
