Destination Guide — Accra

Explore Accra

A Brief History

Growing from coastal Ga settlements around 17th-century European trade forts, Accra became the capital of the British Gold Coast in 1877. Its modern urban sprawl traces the nation's journey from colonial administration to leading the charge for African independence in 1957. Visitors today can clearly trace the intricate layers of this unique heritage throughout the preserved streets and monuments.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

Accra Mall

Accra Mall, situated in the heart of Accra, Ghana, is a modern and vibrant shopping center that offers a wide array of retail outlets. The mall boasts an impressive selection of clothing boutiques, global sports brands, and high-end jewelry stores on its ground floor. Visitors can explore glamorous African-inspired fashions at KIKI Clothing and browse beautiful prints at Woodin and Vlisco. Additionally, the mall features a well-equipped play area for children to enjoy while parents shop.

20 GHS

W.E.B Du Bois Memorial Centre for Panafrican Culture - Ghana

The W.E.B Du Bois Memorial Centre for Panafrican Culture in Ghana is a historical site and tourist attraction located on the former estate of W.E.B. Du Bois, an American civil rights activist and writer. The center pays homage to Du Bois' dedication to fighting for the rights of black people and his contributions to Pan-Africanism. It features a library with historical literature on black rights, a small museum displaying personal effects, and serves as a guest house.

Reservation required

Accra International Conference Centre

The Accra International Conference Centre is a premier venue that has welcomed countless dignitaries from around the globe. With a capacity of 1,600, it serves as the backdrop for many of Ghana's significant conferences and events. This modern facility boasts beautifully decorated spaces and offers an array of amenities suitable for various gatherings, from trade affairs to cultural showcases.

Free

Independence Arch

Independence Arch, also known as the Black Star Gate, is a significant symbol of Ghana's fight for independence from British rule. Built in 1961 by Dr. Kwame Nkrumah, it stands as a tribute to fallen soldiers and a beacon of freedom. The arch is located at Accra's Independence Square and serves as the focal point for national events such as presidential inaugurations, Independence Day celebrations, military parades, and more.

Reservation required

National Theatre

Nestled in the bustling heart of Accra, the National Theatre is a architectural gem that serves as a cornerstone of Ghana's dynamic performing arts landscape. Since its opening in 1992, this venue has been a stage for an array of performances that celebrate the country's rich cultural heritage and artistic prowess. Its design, reminiscent of a ship sailing gracefully through the skyline, makes it an sight.

100 GHS

Kwame Nkrumah Memorial Park

The Kwame Nkrumah Memorial Park & Mausoleum in Accra, Ghana, is a significant landmark honoring the country's first president. The mausoleum and museum pay tribute to Kwame Nkrumah, who led Ghana to independence from British colonial rule in 1957. The impressive marble mausoleum features a black star symbolizing African unity and is surrounded by water and statues of African freedom fighters.

Free

Centre for National Culture

The Centre for National Culture in Accra, often referred to as the Arts Centre, is a bustling market and cultural hub. It features numerous stalls selling traditional Ghanaian crafts, textiles, wood carvings, and jewelry. The center also hosts cultural performances and provides a space for local artisans to showcase and sell their work directly to visitors.

Free

Makola Market

Makola Market is a vibrant and bustling open-air market in Accra, Ghana. It offers a wide variety of local delicacies, traditional foodstuffs, and products such as fabric, household supplies, and groceries. The market is known for its lively atmosphere with warm locals dedicated to providing the service. Navigating through the narrow roads filled with merchants adds to the exciting experience.

50 GHS

Fort James

Fort James, also known as Jamestown, is a historic British colonial trading post in Accra. Built in 1673, it served as a prison until 2008 and has since been transformed into a museum. Visitors can explore the oldest city in Accra and experience the welcoming local culture. The area offers views of boats and beaches, showcasing the livelihoods of the fishing community.

Free

Abossey Okai Central Mosque

Nestled in the vibrant suburb of Maamobi, the Abossey Okai Central Mosque stands as a testament to architectural beauty and cultural significance. This grand mosque, in Ghana, showcases intricate designs with a touch of Turkish influence that captivates both worshippers and visitors alike. Spanning an acre, its striking white walls are complemented by green pillars, creating a visually appealing contrast.
