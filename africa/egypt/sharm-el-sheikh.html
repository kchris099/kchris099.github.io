Destination Guide — Sharm El Sheikh

Explore Sharm El Sheikh

A Brief History

Originally a modest Bedouin fishing village on the Sinai Peninsula, Sharm El Sheikh gained strategic military importance in the mid-20th century. Following peace agreements in the 1980s, targeted development rapidly transformed its coastline into a global diving resort. The remarkable survival of these cultural and architectural milestones provides a powerful window into the region's storied past.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

Mustafa Mosque

When exploring Sharm El Sheikh, a visit to the magnificent Al Mustafa Mosque is an must. This architectural gem, completed in 2008, beautifully mirrors the Fatimid style of Cairo's renowned Al-Azhar Mosque and shines even more brilliantly when illuminated at night. The mosque’s grand dome and exquisite marble details create a serene atmosphere perfect for reflection or simply appreciating its beauty.

Free

The Heavenly Cathedral (Coptic)

The Heavenly Cathedral is a contemporary Coptic place of worship located near the Old Market in Sharm El-Sheikh. This landmark, built in 2010, features innovative stained glass windows and creative drawings on its ceilings and walls depicting biblical scenes. It is revered among Coptic visitors and is considered one of the 10 most beautiful churches in the world.

Free

Soho Square Dancing Fountain

Soho Square Dancing Fountain is a mesmerizing sight with its gigantic light and water display. Visitors can sit in front of it, enjoy a drink, and lose track of time. It's a great way to soak in the culture of this Egyptian city, especially in the evening when the fountain comes alive with music and lights. The square also offers shopping opportunities, restaurants, and entertainment like animation shows at the amphitheater.

Free

Peace Square

Peace Square sits within the Soho Square Dancing Fountain area of Sharm El Sheikh, documented in local heritage registers for its civic or cultural role. Visitors approach from the main access road and combine the visit with neighbouring heritage buildings and public spaces in the district.

Free

Old Market Sharm el Sheikh

The Old Market in Sharm el Sheikh, also known as Sharm al-Maya, is a vibrant and modernized souk located around the Al-Sahaba Mosque. This bustling area is filled with traditional Arabic lanterns, shisha pipes, and intricately carved woodwork. Visitors can explore a variety of shops offering spices, perfumes, clothing, and authentic street food. The market comes alive in the evening with lively restaurants and cafes where visitors can immerse themselves in the local culture.

Free

Al-Sahaba Mosque

If you're looking to explore a different side of Sharm el Sheikh beyond its beaches and resorts, Al Sahaba Mosque at the Old Market is a must-visit. This mosque, reminiscent of tales from One Thousand and One Nights, welcomes visitors but has a dress code for women. While the city is renowned for its beaches, it also boasts cultural and historical sites like Al Sahaba Mosque. Built by the Egyptian Army, this mosque showcases impressive architecture and design details both inside and out.

Reservation required

Royal Seascope submarine Sharm elsheikh

The Royal Seascope is a semi-submarine attraction in Sharm El-Sheikh that allows visitors to explore the vibrant underwater world of the Red Sea without getting wet. Passengers sit in a submerged observation deck with large viewing windows, offering clear views of the colorful coral reefs and diverse marine life that make the region a premier diving destination.

Free

Pedestrian Rd Neama Bay

Pedestrian Rd Neama Bay is a documented coastal feature in Sharm El Sheikh, Egypt, recognised in regional geographic listings for its natural formation and public access. The shoreline is open to visitors throughout the year and connects to adjacent beachfront districts. Travellers approach from the coastal road or boardwalk and combine the visit with nearby harbour areas and seaside markets.

Free

Sinai Grand Casino

Nestled in the vibrant Naama Bay, Sinai Grand Casino offers an exhilarating blend of gaming and entertainment reminiscent of Las Vegas. With over 200 slot machines and a variety of table games including poker, roulette, and blackjack, it’s a paradise for gambling enthusiasts. The casino also hosts regular poker tournaments that keep the excitement alive.

Reservation required

El Sherif Safari

El Sherif Safari is a prominent tour operator in Sharm El-Sheikh, specializing in desert adventure excursions. They offer guided quad biking (ATV) tours, camel rides, and traditional Bedouin dinner experiences in the Sinai Desert. The tours provide an exhilarating way to explore the rugged desert landscape and experience local Bedouin culture.
