Siwa Oasis

Siwa Oasis has long been a Berber-speaking settlement in Egypt's Western Desert, watered by springs and famous in antiquity for the Oracle of Amun consulted by Alexander the Great. Mud-brick fortresses such as Shali dominated the oasis until rains and modern building changed the skyline. Date and olive farming remain central. Salt lakes, desert tracks to the Great Sand Sea, and restored dwellings now draw visitors to a community still distinct from the Nile Valley.

Amon Temple

Amon Temple, also known as the Temple of Amun, is a religious building in Siwa Oasis, Egypt. Dating back to the twenty-sixth Dynasty and growing through Greek and Roman times, this temple sits atop mountains and offers views of Siwa. It is believed to have been built in honor of Ham, son of Noah, by Danaus the Egyptian or linked to the Greek god Dionysus. The exterior and main interior spaces are usually viewable outside service times.

Cleopatra Spring

Cleopatra Spring is a known natural spring located in the Siwa Oasis, known for its clear and constantly moving water. It is a popular spot with several cafes serving refreshing drinks. The spring offers a relaxing experience, especially after visiting the nearby salt lakes. Travellers can take a dip in the deep hot spring to cleanse their bodies of salt before enjoying fresh juices from Siwa.

29.1969° N / 25.5502° E

Fatnas Island

Located on the edge of the oasis, Fatnas Island is a destination located just 6 kilometers from the Siwa. This enchanting spot is known for its inviting hot springs, where travellers can immerse themselves in therapeutic waters while enjoying cafes and restaurants nearby. As day turns to night, the island offers sunset views over Lake Siwa that are simply.

al-Mawta mountain

Al-Mawta mountain, also known as the Mountain of the Dead, is a landmark in Siwa Oasis. It's easier to climb than Gabal Dakrur and offers dozens of rock tombs with ancient wall paintings. The mountain is shrouded in mystery and was believed to be inhabited by spirits of the deceased. Hikers and adventurers are drawn to its challenging summit climb for views across the Great Sand Sea.

29.2114° N / 25.5251° E
