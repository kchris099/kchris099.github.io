Explore Siwa Oasis

A Brief History

Established as a regional settlement beyond an older fortified core, the city developed through industrialization in the late 19th century, post-war reconstruction, and modern civic expansion that reshaped its public spaces, markets, and surrounding landscape. Restored religious buildings, civic buildings, and natural features now define the contemporary visitor experience.

Top Sights & Activities

EGP 100

Amon Temple

Amon Temple, also known as the Temple of Amun, is a remarkable ancient site located in Siwa Oasis, Egypt. Dating back to the twenty-sixth Dynasty and thriving through Greek and Roman times, this temple sits atop mountains and offers views of Siwa. It is believed to have been built in honor of Ham, son of Noah, by Danaus the Egyptian or linked to the Greek god Dionysus. The ruins are accessible via a well-marked path up the rock.

Free

Cleopatra Spring

Cleopatra Spring is a renowned natural spring located in the Siwa Oasis, known for its crystal-clear and constantly moving water. It is a popular spot with several cafes serving refreshing drinks. The spring offers a relaxing experience, especially after visiting the nearby salt lakes. travellers can take a dip in the deep hot spring to cleanse their bodies of salt before enjoying fresh juices from Siwa.

Free

Fatnas Island

Nestled on the edge of the oasis, Fatnas Island is a destination located just 6 kilometers from the heart of Siwa. This enchanting spot is renowned for its inviting hot springs, where travellers can immerse themselves in therapeutic waters while enjoying delightful cafes and restaurants nearby. As day turns to night, the island offers sunset views over Lake Siwa that are simply.

EGP 120

al-Mawta mountain

Al-Mawta mountain, also known as the Mountain of the Dead, is a captivating landmark in Siwa Oasis. It's easier to climb than Gabal Dakrur and offers dozens of rock tombs with ancient wall paintings. The mountain is shrouded in mystery and was believed to be inhabited by spirits of the deceased. Hikers and adventurers are drawn to its challenging summit climb for views across the Great Sand Sea.
