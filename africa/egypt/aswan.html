Destination Guide — Aswan

Explore Aswan

A Brief History

Guarding the traditional southern frontier of ancient Egypt, Aswan was a vital garrison and trade hub for Nubian gold and ivory. The modern construction of the High Dam fundamentally altered its landscape, preserving monumental temples while reshaping the local ecology. The remarkable survival of these cultural and architectural milestones provides a powerful window into the region's storied past.

Top Sights & Activities

EGP 540

Philae Temple

Philae Temple, dedicated to the goddess Isis, is a complex located on an island in the middle of the Nile River. Accessible only by boat, it offers a unique and picturesque experience. The temple boasts well-preserved patterns on its walls and columns, creating a poetic and impressive scenery surrounded by the river. Visitors are advised to come with licensed guides as those at the location may provide false information.

220 EGP

Unfinished Obelisk

The Unfinished Obelisk in Aswan is a remarkable ancient archaeological site that showcases an abandoned obelisk carved from bedrock amidst granite quarries. This monumental structure, attributed to Queen Hatshepsut of the 18th dynasty, provides valuable insights into the stone-carving techniques of ancient Egyptians over 3,000 years ago.

Free

Coptic Orthodox Cathedral of the Archangel Michael

The Coptic Orthodox Cathedral of the Archangel Michael in Aswan is a serene and historically rich place to visit. Located near the Nubian Museum, it offers a peaceful atmosphere for visitors to explore. The cathedral's exterior is majestic, while its interior holds an unexpected and mystical charm. Visitors are greeted by warm and friendly locals who are happy to provide insights into the Coptic church and its unique features.

200 EGP

The High Dam

The Aswan High Dam is a monumental engineering achievement constructed across the Nile River in the 1960s. It created Lake Nasser, one of the world's largest artificial lakes, and plays a crucial role in controlling floods, providing irrigation, and generating hydroelectric power for Egypt. The dam offers impressive views of the massive reservoir and the river below.

200 EGP

Kalabsha Temple

The Kalabsha Temple, also known as the Temple of Kakabshah or Mandulis, is a revered ancient Nubian temple dedicated to the sun god. It was built in 30 B.C. during the reign of Roman Emperor Augustus and was later relocated due to construction of the Aswan High Dam. Visitors can admire exquisite reliefs throughout the site and witness an elephant depicted on one stone block outside.

Free

Aswan Old Souks

Aswan Old Souks, also known as Sharia as-Souq, is a vibrant bazaar offering a wide variety of goods and local products. From freshly cooked dishes to traditional Nubian outfits and Sudanese trinkets, the market is a bustling hub for shopping. Visitors can find everything from spices and perfumes to souvenirs, toys, and even car parts. The market's proximity to the Nile makes it easily accessible from anywhere in town.

Free

Sharia as Souq Market

Sharia as Souq Market in Aswan is a must-visit for souvenir hunters. The market offers a wide range of Egyptian items, from perfumes and spices to basketry and scarves, all sold by local vendors. The opening and closing times are flexible, adding to the authentic experience.

200 EGP

Tombs of the Nobles in Aswan

In the captivating city of Aswan, Egypt, visitors can immerse themselves in a rich tapestry of history and culture. The Tombs of the Nobles offer a glimpse into ancient Egyptian life, with necropolises dating back to the Old, Middle, and New Kingdoms. Perched on a hilltop overlooking the Nile River, these tombs belong to officials and nobles from antiquity. The site is still an active excavation area, promising potential discoveries in the future.

200 EGP

Dome of Abu Al-Hawa

Dome of Abu Al-Hawa, also known as Qubbet el-Hawa, is a small dome-like structure located on top of a sand hill housing noble tombs. Although there's not much to see at the tomb itself, the hike up offers panoramic views of Aswan City, the Nile River, and nearby Nubian Village. The area is well-maintained with manicured gardens and ample seating for visitors to relax and take in the scenery.

80 EGP

Aswan Botanical Garden

Aswan Botanical Garden, located on Kitchener's Island in the Nile, is a lush oasis named after Lord Horatio Kitchener. Covering 17 acres, it boasts nearly 750 plant species from tropical Africa, India, and Brazil. The well-maintained pathways offer a serene escape with towering palms and fragrant blooms. However, some areas are occupied by market stalls selling typical tourist items.
