Explore Dakar

A Brief History

Dakar was originally settled by the Lebou people before the Portuguese arrived in the fifteenth century. It grew significantly under French colonial rule, becoming the capital of French West Africa in 1902 and a major port for the peanut trade. The nearby Goree Island served as a center for the Atlantic slave trade. Today, Dakar is the active capital of independent Senegal, known for its busy markets, rich musical heritage, and strategic position on the Cap-Vert peninsula.

African Renaissance Monument

The African Renaissance Monument, completed in 2010, is a massive 49-meter bronze statue that portrays a man holding his child and rescuing his wife. Standing at 52 meters tall, it is the tallest statue in Africa and commemorates the end of slavery and the resurgence of the continent. Despite being built by a North Korean company, this monument has become a controversial yet symbolic landmark.

Free

Phare des Mamelles

Phare des Mamelles is a visitor site in Dakar, Senegal. Phare des Mamelles is a 19th-century lighthouse located on the Mamelles hills in Dakar, Senegal. It offers guided tours and panoramic views of the city's coastline. The stylish restaurant-bar at the base of the lighthouse provides a shuttle service to its open-air veranda, where various events are hosted. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Free

Mosque of the Divinity

The Mosque of the Divinity, situated in the Ouakam district near Dakar, Senegal, is a example of modern Islamic architecture. Designed by architect Cheikh Ngom and completed in 1997, it has two towering minarets that provide views of the Atlantic Ocean. The interior is adorned with ornate domes and colorful mosaics. This mosque has become an important pilgrimage site for Muslims and a attraction for travelers.

Free

Place du Souvenir Africain

Place du Souvenir Africain is a visitor site in Dakar, Senegal. The Place du Souvenir Africain is a significant cultural monument located on the Corniche in Dakar. It is dedicated to the memory, history, and prominent figures of the African continent and its diaspora. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Free

Grand Mosque of Dakar

Grand Mosque of Dakar, also known as the Grande Mosquee de Dakar or the Mosque of the Divinity, is a religious building in Dakar, Senegal. Construction and early use date to around 1906. The Grand Mosque of Dakar, also known as the Grande Mosquee de Dakar or the Mosque of the Divinity, is a significant religious monument in Senegal. The exterior and main interior spaces are usually viewable outside service times.

Free

Grand Théâtre National de Dakar

The Grand Théâtre National de Dakar is a symbol of Senegal's progress, resembling a classic European opera house with its luxurious red velveteen seating and grand stands. This venue, opened in 2011, holds the distinction of being the largest theater in Western Africa, accommodating up to 1800 guests on its main stage, it offers workshop rooms and break-out spaces for various events.

Free

The Museum of Black Civilisations

The Museum of Black Civilisations is a museum in Dakar, Senegal. The Museum of Black Civilisations in Dakar is a vast and modern museum that focuses on African culture and customs, with an emphasis on Senegalese and West African cultures. It also showcases other African cultures. While the signage may be challenging for non-French speakers, it offers a decent general cultural experience. Galleries and public rooms are open on posted hours in Dakar.

Free

Cathedral of Our Lady of Victories

Cathedral of Our Lady of Victories is a religious building in Dakar, Senegal. Construction and early use date to around 1936. The Cathedral of Our Lady of Victories is a grand place of worship located in the Dakar plateau. It was consecrated in 1936 and has since become a central pillar of Catholicism in Senegal. The exterior and main interior spaces are usually viewable outside service times.

Free

Leopold Sedar Senghor French Institute

Leopold Sedar Senghor French Institute is a visitor site in Dakar, Senegal. The Leopold Sedar Senghor French Institute, located near the Centre Culturel Francais, offers a space to relax and enjoy cultural events. It's known for its casual and active atmosphere, making it a good place for both adults and kids, visitors can enjoy food and music at the venue. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

XOF 1,000

House of Slaves

The House of Slaves, also known as Maison des Esclaves, is a museum and memorial located on Ile Goree, an island off the southern tip of Dakar. It was built in the 1700s and is the last remaining slave house out of approximately 30 on the island. The museum documents the history of the Atlantic slave trade and is a powerful reminder of the brutal barbarism of this dark period in history.
