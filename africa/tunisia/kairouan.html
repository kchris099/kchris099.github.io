Explore Kairouan

A Brief History

Kairouan was founded in 670 CE as a garrison town that became a centre of Sunni scholarship in North Africa. The Great Mosque's Aghlabid minaret and columns from earlier sites embody early Islamic urban planning. Medina workshops and reservoirs record how the city linked trans-Saharan trade to coastal Tunisia. Aghlabid reservoirs and carpet souks extend the medina's early Islamic street grid into surrounding oases.

Top Sights & Activities

Great Mosque of Kairouan

The Great Mosque of Kairouan, also known as the Mosque of Uqba, is a architectural marvel that dates back to the 600s. With its imposing fortress-like walls and distinctive square minaret, this mosque stands as one of North Africa's largest and most significant monuments.

Free

Bir Barrouta

Bir Barrouta is a captivating gem nestled in Kairouan, showcasing an ancient water-lifting mechanism powered by a camel. This unique site has been cherished for generations, with local beliefs linking it to the sacred Zamzam well in Mecca. Visitors are often enchanted by the kind staff who share insights about this historical marvel without any pressure for payment—donations are welcome instead.

Free

Zaouia of Sidi Sahabi

Nestled within the historic city of Kairouan, the Zaouia of Sidi Sahabi stands as a mausoleum dedicated to Abu Zama el-Belaoui, a revered companion of Prophet Muhammad. Known affectionately as "the barber" for his cherished possession of three hairs from the Prophet's beard, this white-domed structure is not only an architectural gem but also a significant spiritual site.

Free

Sidi Sahbi Mausoleum

Barbier Mausoleum is a significant site in the Islamic world, housing the tomb of Abu Zamaa el-Balaoui, a companion of Prophet Muhammad. The walled mosque features tiled patterns and intricate Arabic designs on white plaster. While non-Muslims cannot enter the tomb area, many visit to admire its beautiful architecture and seek blessings. The mausoleum's rooms are adorned with exquisite tile work, leading to the room containing the sarcophagus.
