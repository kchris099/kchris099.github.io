Kairouan

Kairouan was founded in 670 CE as a garrison town that became a centre of Sunni scholarship in North Africa. The Great Mosque's Aghlabid minaret and columns from earlier sites embody early Islamic urban planning. Medina workshops and reservoirs record how the city linked trans-Saharan trade to coastal Tunisia. Aghlabid reservoirs and carpet souks extend the medina's early Islamic street grid into surrounding oases.

Great Mosque of Kairouan

The Great Mosque of Kairouan developed from a foundation traditionally dated to the seventh century and took much of its present form under Aghlabid rule in the ninth. A vast courtyard, hypostyle prayer hall, square minaret, ancient columns, carved wood, tile and water systems influenced later western Islamic architecture. It is an active sacred site, with access governed by religious practice.

Bir Barrouta

Bir Barrouta is a gem located Kairouan, showing an ancient water-lifting mechanism powered by a camel. This unique site has been cherished for generations, with local beliefs linking it to the sacred Zamzam well in Mecca. Visitors are often enchanted by the kind staff who share insights about this historical marvel without any pressure for payment, donations are welcome instead.

Zaouia of Sidi Sahabi

The Zaouia of Sidi Sahabi in Kairouan centers on a white-domed complex tied to Abu Zama'a al-Balawi, a Companion of the Prophet nicknamed the Barber for relics of three hairs. Courtyards, tilework, and prayer spaces make it one of the city's major religious monuments. Non-Muslim access is limited in some areas, but the exterior architecture and courtyards still draw respectful visitors.

35.6825° N / 10.0907° E

Sidi Sahbi Mausoleum

Also known as the Barber's Mausoleum, this Kairouan shrine holds the tomb of Abu Zama'a al-Balawi within a multi-court religious complex of domes and tiled walls. Pilgrims visit for blessings tied to the Companion's story; non-Muslims typically view outer courts rather than the burial chamber itself. The site ranks among the Islamic world's better-known Companion shrines outside the Hijaz.

35.6821° N / 10.0900° E
