El Jem

El Jem is an ancient town in Tunisia that was built on the former Punic settlement of Thysdrus. Under Roman rule, the city grew wealthy through the cultivation and export of olive oil. The prosperity of Thysdrus led to the construction of a massive amphitheater in the third century, which remains the town's primary landmark. Despite facing invasions and political shifts throughout the centuries, the archaeological remains provide a clear record of Roman urban planning and architecture in North Africa.

Amphitheatre of El Jem

El Jem's amphitheater was built in the third century in Roman Africa, its massive oval walls rising above the modern town. Corridors, stairs, seating tiers, arena entrances and underground chambers reveal how large public spectacles were organized. Quarrying, conflict and later reuse damaged parts of the structure, but substantial walls and internal passages survive, allowing the building's scale and engineering to be read directly.

El Jem Archaeological Museum

El Jem Archaeological Museum displays mosaics, sculpture, ceramics, and domestic material from Roman Thysdrus and surrounding villas. Reconstructed house plans and nearby archaeological remains help place the amphitheater within a wealthy agricultural town.
