Sossusvlei

The Sossusvlei basin lies at the end of the Tsauchab river course in the central Namib, where shifting sand dunes have blocked the river from reaching the Atlantic for the past 50,000 years and have isolated the clay pan behind a final line of dunes 80 metres tall. The region was administered as part of the Namib Desert Park proclaimed in 1907 by German colonial authorities and expanded in 1979 into the present Namib-Naukluft National Park, the largest conservation area in Africa at 49,768 square kilometres. The Sesriem access road was upgraded in 1986 and the lodge infrastructure grew with Namibian independence and the 1990 Sossusvlei Lodge opening.

Sesriem Gate Entry

Sesriem Gate is the southern vehicular entrance to the Namib-Naukluft National Park, set at the end of the sealed C14 road from Solitaire and operating as the only authorised public access point to the Sossusvlei dunes. The gate is staffed by Namibia Wildlife Resorts from sunrise to sunset and records daily visitor entries. Travellers pay NAD150 per adult international visitor at the gate office, then drive the 60-kilometre road to the Sossusvlei parking area.

Sossusvlei Dunes

The Sossusvlei Dunes are a series of star-shaped sand dunes surrounding the Sossusvlei clay pan at the terminus of the Tsauchab river in the central Namib, including Dune 45, Big Daddy (325 metres), and Big Mama. The dunes are composed of iron-oxide-coated sand grains carried inland from the Orange River mouth over the past five million years. Travellers park at the 2WD lot and walk the final kilometre to the pan.

03 AdmissionNAD 150

Deadvlei

Deadvlei is a white clay pan in the central Namib located about 1.2 kilometres east of Sossusvlei, holding roughly 500 dead camelthorn trees that have been naturally desiccated for approximately 600 to 900 years due to the surrounding aridity. The pan is enclosed by the Big Daddy and Elim dunes and is reached on foot across the 1.1-kilometre dune track. Travellers pay NAD150 per adult international visitor at the Sossusvlei entrance.
