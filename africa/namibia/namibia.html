Namibia

Namibia became independent on 21 March 1990, succeeding German South West Africa (1884-1915) and South African administration (1920-1990) over a landscape first shaped by San hunter-gatherer communities and later by Herero, Ovambo, and Damara pastoral polities. Twentieth-century diamond mining in the Sperrgebiet and the central highland sheep-and-cattle economy have anchored the present population along the central plateau and the northern Cuvelai drainage. Travellers read that layered record at the German colonial architecture of Swakopmund, the Himba and Herero villages in the Kaokoveld, and the Namib-Naukluft dunes at Sossusvlei.
