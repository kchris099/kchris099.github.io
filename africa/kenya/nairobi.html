Destination Guide — Nairobi

Explore Nairobi

A Brief History

Founded in 1899 as a simple supply depot on the Uganda Railway, Nairobi rapidly replaced Mombasa as the capital of the British East Africa Protectorate. Its dramatic expansion from a swampy frontier camp into a modern metropolis reflects a complex colonial and post-colonial legacy. Visitors today can clearly trace the detailed layers of this unique heritage throughout the preserved streets and monuments.

Two Rivers Mall

Two Rivers Mall, situated on Limuru Road in Ruaka-Nairobi, is the largest shopping center in East Africa and now the biggest mall in sub-Saharan Africa. This contemporary landmark has over 200 stores, various dining options, and an amusement park. Located affluent neighborhoods like Runda and Muthaiga, the mall offers a diverse range of high-end local and international brands.

Free

The Hub Karen

The Hub Karen is a visitor site in Nairobi, Kenya. The Hub Karen is a spacious and upscale shopping and entertainment complex located in the active suburb of Karen in Nairobi. It offers a diverse range of retail outlets, from well-known international brands to local artisans, providing a distinctive shopping experience for fashion enthusiasts and bargain hunters. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Free

The Junction Mall

The Junction Mall is a visitor site in Nairobi, Kenya. Records associated with the site include 2004. The Junction Mall, situated at the intersection of Ngong Road, Naivasha Road, and Kingara Road in Nairobi, has been a popular shopping destination since its establishment in 2004. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Free

Yaya Centre

Yaya Centre is a visitor site in Nairobi, Kenya. Yaya Centre, situated in Nairobi's upscale Kilimani neighborhood, is a well-established shopping and entertainment complex. Since its founding in the early 80s, it has become one of the city's premier shopping destinations. With over 100 shops, offices, supermarkets, and restaurants offering diverse local and international brands, Yaya Centre provides a modern and convenient shopping experience for both locals and visitors.

Free

Uhuru Gardens

Uhuru Gardens is a public park in Nairobi, Kenya. Records associated with the site include 1963. Uhuru Gardens, located in Langata just 15 minutes from Nairobi city center, is a historic site and national monument large across 68 acres. Managed by the Kenya Defence Forces, it has walking paths and monuments commemorating the first raising of Kenya's flag in 1963. Paths, lawns, and shaded areas make up the main public facilities.

Free

Galleria Mall

Galleria Mall is a visitor site in Nairobi, Kenya. Galleria Mall, located in the Karen neighborhood along Langata Road, offers a full shopping, dining, and lifestyle experience. Its proximity to Nairobi National Park adds a unique touch to the shopping experience. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

$25

Nairobi Safari Walk

Nairobi Safari Walk is a visitor site in Nairobi, Kenya. Nairobi Safari Walk is a experience that offers a unique glimpse into Kenya's diverse wildlife. Along the way, observe a wide variety of wildlife, including lions, rhinos, cheetahs, leopards, and rare species such as the white Rhino and Albino Zebra. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

$15

Giraffe Centre

Located on the outskirts of Nairobi National Park along Langata Road, Giraffe Centre is a non-profit organization dedicated to the conservation of endangered Rothschild giraffes. The center, situated within the grounds of a luxurious guesthouse called Giraffe Manor, aims to protect and preserve these majestic creatures while providing educational opportunities for both children and visitors.

$20

Sheldrick Wildlife Trust Nursery

The Elephant Nursery, located within Nairobi National Park, is a rescue and rehabilitation center for orphaned elephants. Established in 1977 by the Sheldrick Trust, it has the chance to witness young elephants being fed and nurtured. The trust, founded by David and Daphne Sheldrick, pioneers techniques for raising orphaned black rhinos and elephants before reintroducing them into the wild.

Free

Westgate Shopping Mall

Located in the Westlands neighborhood, Westgate Shopping Mall is an upscale destination offering a wide array of international brands, dining options, and 24/7 security. This premier shopping center in East Africa provides a luxurious and modern environment for both local residents and international visitors. Despite facing challenges after a terror attack in 2013, it has continued to thrive as an engaging hub for shopping, leisure activities, and family-friendly events.

Free

Karura Forest- Sigiria

Karura Forest- Sigiria is a public park in Nairobi, Kenya. It offers extensive, well-maintained trails popular with runners, cyclists, and dog walkers. The dense, indigenous woodland provides a peaceful natural sanctuary within the city, featuring small waterfalls, diverse birdlife, and a relaxing cafe at the entrance. Paths, lawns, and shaded areas make up the main public facilities.

KES 350

National Museum of Kenya

National Museum of Kenya is a museum in Nairobi, Kenya. The National Museum of Kenya is a must-visit destination in Nairobi for those interested in delving into the rich heritage and history of East Africa. Galleries and public rooms are open on posted hours in Nairobi.

Free

Jamia Mosque Nairobi

Jamia Mosque Nairobi is a significant religious structure in Kenya, known for its traditional Arabic design with twin minarets and three silver domes. Situated in the central business district, it attracts worshippers from all over the country. The mosque's architecture combines Islamic and Swahili styles, featuring marble interiors adorned with Quranic inscriptions. Its imposing presence amid the busy cityscape makes it a prominent landmark in Nairobi.

Free

Maasai Market

Maasai Market is a market in Nairobi, Kenya. The Maasai Market is a active open-air marketplace that offers a diverse selection of traditional African crafts. Originally started by Maasai women to sell directly to tourists, the market has become a popular shopping extravaganza rotating between different locations throughout the week. Stalls sell food and goods during regular market hours.

Free

Kenyatta International Convention Centre

Kenyatta International Convention Centre, also known as KICC, is a visitor site in Nairobi, Kenya. The Kenyatta International Convention Centre, also known as KICC, is a prominent landmark in Nairobi. Offering 360-degree views of the city from its rooftop deck, KICC is a popular venue for conferences, seminars, and exhibitions. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Free

All Saints’ Cathedral Nairobi

All Saints' Cathedral Nairobi is a religious building in Nairobi, Kenya. Records associated with the site include 1917, 1952. All Saints' Cathedral Nairobi is a evidence of the city's rich history and cultural blend. Established in 1917, this Anglican church was elevated to cathedral status in 1952, making it one of the oldest and largest churches in Nairobi. The exterior and main interior spaces are usually viewable outside service times.

$80

Nairobi National Park

Nairobi National Park is a unique conservation center and wildlife sanctuary located just 10 kilometers from the city center of Nairobi, making it the only national park in Africa situated within a capital city. Covering an area of 117 square kilometers, this park is home to a diverse range of Kenya's wildlife, including lions, buffaloes, cheetahs, giraffes, and a rhino sanctuary. It also has over 400 bird species.
