Explore Mombasa

A Brief History

Mombasa is one of Kenya's oldest cities, with roots as a prominent Swahili trading port dating back to the twelfth century. It was fiercely contested by the Portuguese, Omani Arabs, and British due to its strategic location on the Indian Ocean. The Portuguese built Fort Jesus in the sixteenth century to secure their dominance. Today, Mombasa is a active coastal city that reflects a rich blend of African, Arab, and European cultural influences.

Top Sights & Activities

KES 550

Fort Jesus Museum

Fort Jesus Museum sits within the Fort Jesus Museum area of Mombasa, documented in local heritage registers for its civic or cultural role. Visitors approach from the main access road and combine the visit with neighbouring heritage buildings and public spaces in the district.

Free

Swahilipot Hub

Swahilipot Hub is a dynamic community space and technology incubator located near Fort Jesus in Mombasa. It is a collaborative environment for local youth, artists, and tech enthusiasts, fostering innovation and cultural expression. The hub frequently hosts workshops, art exhibitions, and networking events, playing a vital role in the contemporary cultural life of the city.

Free

Mombasa Spice Market

Mombasa Market, also known as the Spice Market, is a active and busy place where visitors can immerse themselves in the local culture. The market offers a wide variety of products including spices like turmeric, curry leaf powder, cloves, and cardamom. In addition to spices, visitors can find clothing, meat, bags, and fruits. The market is known for its friendly atmosphere and it's recommended to bargain with the vendors.

Free

Pembe Za Ndovu - Mombasa

The Pembe Za Ndovu, or Mombasa Tusks, is the most well known landmark in Mombasa. The massive aluminum structures, shaped like intersecting elephant tusks, form a distinctive archway over Moi Avenue. They were originally erected in 1952 to commemorate the visit of Queen Elizabeth II and have since become the defining symbol of the city.

Free

Nyali Cinemax

Located 3 km away from the city center of Mombasa, Nyali Cinemax is the country's first digital cinema hall and the sole one in the city. It comprises two movie halls with spacious seating, modern sound systems, and large screens. The theater offers excellent amenities such as delicious popcorn, friendly customer service (although some items on the menu may not be available), and comfortable seats.

Free

Wild Waters

Wild Waters is a well-known water park situated in Mombasa, Kenya, offering an array of water-based activities and attractions suitable for families, groups, and individuals seeking excitement. The park has various thrilling water slides such as the Boomerango, Freefall, and Kamikaze slides, visitors can enjoy a wave pool, lazy river, children's play area, and food options within the park.

Free

Mamba Village Mombasa

Mamba Village Mombasa is a visitor site in Mombasa, Kenya. The on-site restaurant serves an adventurous menu featuring game meat such as crocodile, zebra, and ostrich. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest. Mamba Village Mombasa remains part of the public visitor circuit in Mombasa.

Free

Nyali Beach

Nyali Beach is a stretch of white sand that attracts visitors looking to swim, sunbathe, picnic, and unwind. It offers a quiet shoreline close to the city with pristine water quality. The beach is popular during weekends, drawing families and friends seeking relaxation amidst palm trees and soft sands.

Free

Mama Ngina Waterfront

Mama Ngina Waterfront is a waterfront area in Mombasa, Kenya. Located just 3 km away from the city center, Mama Ngina Waterfront in Mombasa County is a public park that offers panoramic views of the Indian Ocean and the Kilindini Channel. This destination is surrounded by lush greenery, making it an ideal spot for spending quality time with friends and family. Quays and embankment paths form the main walking route.

KES 2,200

Haller Park

Haller Park, formerly known as Bamburi Nature Trail, is a seaside natural reserve that offers trails and diverse wildlife. The park is home to various animals such as tortoises, giraffes, hippos, zebras, waterbucks, and over 160 bird species including cranes and pelicans. It was originally an inspiration project in 1971 and has since been transformed into a wildlife park by Dr. Haller.
