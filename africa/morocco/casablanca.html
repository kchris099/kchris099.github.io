Casablanca

Casablanca expanded from a small Atlantic port after the French protectorate made it Morocco's economic capital in the early 20th century. Art Deco and Mauresque civic buildings, the Hassan II Mosque completed in 1993, and the historic medina reflect colonial planning and post-independence growth. As the country's largest city and main port, Casablanca links Atlantic trade with inland Moroccan markets and industry.

Morocco Mall

Morocco Mall occupies a large Atlantic-front complex west of central Casablanca with retail galleries, department stores, restaurants, entertainment, and an aquarium installation. Tenants and attractions change, while the site functions primarily as a commercial destination.

Mohammed V Square

Laid out in 1916 under French Protectorate planning by Lyautey with architects Henri Prost and Joseph Marrast, Mohammed V Square gathers Casablanca's administrative core. Prefecture, regional delegation, French Consulate, post office, State Bank, and Palace of Justice face the plaza. Formal axes still organize civic movement.

33.5913° N / 7.6183° W

03 AdmissionFree

Medina Market

Casablanca's medina market mixes produce, fish, meat, and clothing shops among older residential lanes, with a more touristed Old Medina Market section. Conditions vary block to block, from worn housing to active stall rows. Trade hours keep food and goods moving through the same alleys.

Hassan II Mosque

A tall minaret, cedar ceilings, carved plaster, marble, mosaics, and a retractable roof display Moroccan craftsmanship at monumental scale. Hassan II Mosque projects over Casablanca's Atlantic shore on a vast platform completed in 1993; non-Muslim interior visits generally occur through authorized tours outside prayer, while exterior terraces remain part of an active religious complex.

33.6082° N / 7.6327° W

05 AdmissionFree

Mohammed V Stadium

Mohammed V Stadium hosts major football matches for Casablanca clubs and national competitions, along with occasional concerts and public events. Renovations and tournament preparations have repeatedly changed capacity, seating, entrances, and availability.

33.5825° N / 7.6470° W

06 AdmissionFree

Royal Palace

High walls, formal gates, guards, and landscaped approaches mark Casablanca's Royal Palace in the Habous district. The compound remains a working royal residence and administrative property closed to ordinary public entry; exterior viewing must respect security boundaries, while nearby Mahkama du Pacha and market streets are separate sites with their own access.

33.5805° N / 7.6056° W

07 AdmissionFree

Pâtisserie Bennis Habous

Pâtisserie Bennis Habous is a known family-owned bakery that has been serving up delectable Moroccan treats since 1930. Located in the active Quartier Habous, this 1930s shop has a colorful tiled exterior and is known for handcrafted sweet and savory pastries. The bakery, now operated by the fourth generation of the Bennis family, is celebrated for its signature cornes de gazelle - almond paste cookies infused with orange blossom water.

08 AdmissionFree

Church of Notre Dame of Lourdes

Gabriel Loire's extensive stained glass fills Notre-Dame de Lourdes with red, blue, and green light across a modern concrete nave. Completed in the 1950s, the Catholic church contrasts a relatively plain exterior with dramatic interior walls and a large grotto-like Marian shrine; mass and parish activity continue, making respectful timing more important than nearby cafes or tram stops.

33.5828° N / 7.6157° W

09 AdmissionFree

El Hank Lighthouse

El Hank Lighthouse rises on Casablanca's western seafront beyond the main port. Built in the early twentieth century, the tall white masonry tower marks a coastal point facing Atlantic shipping routes and the entrance to the harbor. Its lantern remains part of the city's navigational infrastructure, while the surrounding headland provides a clear view of the tower against the ocean and expanding urban shoreline.

33.6100° N / 7.6548° W

10 AdmissionFree

Bouskoura forest.

Bouskoura Forest south of Casablanca is a large planted woodland used for walking, cycling, and weekend sports. Bench-lined paths and clearings sit among eucalyptus and pine stands outside the dense city. Families and runners share the trails that radiate from parking approaches.

33.4642° N / 7.5789° W
