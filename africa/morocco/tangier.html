Destination Guide — Tangier

Explore Tangier

A Brief History

Commanding the Strait of Gibraltar, Tangier absorbed Carthaginian, Roman, and Arab influences before serving as an International Zone in the mid-20th century. This unique diplomatic status fostered a legendary bohemian era that still defines its cosmopolitan, cross-cultural allure. Visitors today can clearly trace the intricate layers of this unique heritage throughout the preserved streets and monuments.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

Grand Socco

Grand Socco, also known as Place du 9 Avril 1947, is a vibrant market square that serves as a connection between the medina and Ville Nouvelle in Tangier. Renamed in honor of Mohammed V's independence speech, it comes alive in the evenings with vendors displaying an array of second-hand goods. The colorful market attracts peasant women selling fruits and fowl near the Anglican Church of St Andrew.

Free

Bab Kasbah

Bab Kasbah is a vibrant marketplace located in the oldest and most historic part of the city. It offers a wide array of eateries, shops, and lively vendors. The area is known for its typical Moroccan architecture and views of the harbor and European mainland. Visitors can explore the Sultan's Palace, now transformed into a museum showcasing Moroccan art and historical artifacts.

Free

Bab Al Bahr

Bab Al Bahr, also known as the Kasbah, offers an unparalleled view of the Mediterranean and the southern coast of Spain. Despite occasional misinformation from vendors outside its gate, this spot is a must-visit. The panoramic vista includes the port of Tangier and neighboring Spain. With its overlook of the Atlantic and Mediterranean seas, Bab Al Bahr provides a beautiful experience for visitors.

Free

Phoenician tombs

Nestled between Cafe Hafa and the upper entrance of the ancient Kasbah, the Phoenician tombs offer a unique glimpse into history while treating visitors to vistas. This 4th-century necropolis features around 50 rock-carved graves that are now hollow and often filled with rainwater. While some litter may detract from the experience, it’s worth braving for views of the turquoise Mediterranean Sea and even glimpses of Spain on clear days.

Free

Marshan Royal Palace

Marshan Palace, a architectural gem in Tangier, has a rich history that dates back to the early 1950s when it was originally built for the Tangier International Zone Legislative Assembly. Following Morocco's independence in 1956, it transitioned into a royal property and underwent renovations by King Mohammed VI in the 2010s to host diplomatic events.

Free

Tanja Marina Bay

Tanja Marina Bay is a vibrant and family-friendly destination located in the heart of Tangier, Morocco. It boasts a large urban marina with rows of luxurious boats and a variety of cafes and restaurants to choose from. At night, the pedestrian boulevard comes alive with cool strobe lights, creating an entertaining atmosphere for families. The marina offers a safe environment for evening strolls and is bustling with activities.

Free

Villa Harris Park

Villa Harris Park in Tangier, Morocco is a must-visit for nature enthusiasts. The park offers free access to its expansive natural setting with forest and ocean views. While there's an entrance fee to access the villa, visitors can still enjoy the lush greenery and beautiful surroundings without entering. Conveniently located near Mohammed V Mosque, it's easily accessible by grand taxi for a nominal fee.

Free

Rmilat Park

Rmilat Park, also known as Parc Perdicaris, is a wooded park in Tangier that offers panoramic views of the Al Boran Sea. It's a popular spot for picnics and exercising, with hilly hiking trails providing an opportunity to take in the scenery. The park features lush greenery and ample seating areas, making it perfect for a relaxing day out with friends or family.

Free

Cap Spartel

Cap Spartel is a dramatic promontory located near Tangier, marking the extreme northwestern point of Africa where the Atlantic Ocean meets the Mediterranean Sea. The site features a historic, picturesque lighthouse built in 1864. The surrounding area offers expansive ocean views, scenic coastal drives, and access to the nearby Caves of Hercules.

Free

Cape Spartel

Cape Spartel is a captivating site located in northern Morocco, where the Atlantic Ocean meets the Mediterranean Sea at the Strait of Gibraltar. It is known for its 300m high lighthouse and the legendary Caves of Hercules, where it's said that Hercules rested before his twelfth labor. The area features rocky coastlines, beautiful beaches like Ba Kassem beach, and offers a perfect day-trip from Tangier for families.
