Destination Guide — Tangier

Explore Tangier

A Brief History

Commanding the Strait of Gibraltar, Tangier absorbed Carthaginian, Roman, and Arab influences before serving as an International Zone in the mid-20th century. This unique diplomatic status fostered a legendary bohemian era that still defines its cosmopolitan, cross-cultural allure. Visitors today can clearly trace the detailed layers of this unique heritage throughout the preserved streets and monuments.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

Grand Socco

Grand Socco, also known as Place du 9 Avril 1947, is a active market square that is a connection between the medina and Ville Nouvelle in Tangier. Renamed in honor of Mohammed V's independence speech, it comes alive in the evenings with vendors displaying an array of second-hand goods. The colorful market attracts peasant women selling fruits and fowl near the Anglican Church of St Andrew.

Free

Bab Kasbah

Bab Kasbah is a visitor site in Tangier, Morocco. Bab Kasbah is a active marketplace located in the oldest and most historic part of the city. The area is known for its typical Moroccan architecture and views of the harbor and European mainland. Visitors can explore the Sultan's Palace, now transformed into a museum showing Moroccan art and historical artifacts. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Free

Bab Al Bahr

Bab Al Bahr, also known as the Kasbah, offers an unparalleled view of the Mediterranean and the southern coast of Spain. Despite occasional misinformation from vendors outside its gate, this spot is a must-visit. The panoramic vista includes the port of Tangier and neighboring Spain. With its overlook of the Atlantic and Mediterranean seas, Bab Al Bahr provides a experience for visitors.

Free

Phoenician tombs

Phoenician tombs is a visitor site in Tangier, Morocco. Located between Cafe Hafa and the upper entrance of the ancient Kasbah, the Phoenician tombs offer a unique glimpse into history while treating visitors to vistas. While some litter may detract from the experience, it's worth braving for views of the turquoise Mediterranean Sea and even glimpses of Spain on clear days. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Free

Marshan Royal Palace

Marshan Palace, a architectural gem in Tangier, has a rich history that dates back to the early 1950s when it was originally built for the Tangier International Zone Legislative Assembly. Following Morocco's independence in 1956, it transitioned into a royal property and underwent renovations by King Mohammed VI in the 2010s to host diplomatic events.

Free

Tanja Marina Bay

Tanja Marina Bay is a active and family-friendly destination located in Tangier, Morocco. It has a large urban marina with rows of luxurious boats and a variety of cafes and restaurants to choose from. At night, the pedestrian boulevard comes alive with cool strobe lights, creating an entertaining atmosphere for families. The marina offers a safe environment for evening strolls and is busy with activities.

Free

Villa Harris Park

Villa Harris Park in Tangier, Morocco is a must-visit for nature enthusiasts. The park offers free access to its expansive natural setting with forest and ocean views. While there's an entrance fee to access the villa, visitors can still enjoy the lush greenery and surroundings without entering. Conveniently located near Mohammed V Mosque, it's easily accessible by grand taxi for a nominal fee.

Free

Rmilat Park

Rmilat Park, also known as Parc Perdicaris, is a public park in Tangier, Morocco. Rmilat Park, also known as Parc Perdicaris, is a wooded park in Tangier that offers panoramic views of the Al Boran Sea. It's a popular spot for picnics and exercising, with hilly hiking trails providing a chance to take in the scenery. Paths, lawns, and shaded areas make up the main public facilities.

Cap Spartel

Cap Spartel is a visitor site in Tangier, Morocco. Construction and early use date to around 1864. Cap Spartel is a dramatic promontory located near Tangier, marking the extreme northwestern point of Africa where the Atlantic Ocean meets the Mediterranean Sea. The site has a historic, lighthouse built in 1864. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Free

Cape Spartel

Cape Spartel is a visitor site in Tangier, Morocco. Cape Spartel is a site located in northern Morocco, where the Atlantic Ocean meets the Mediterranean Sea at the Strait of Gibraltar. It is known for its 300m high lighthouse and the legendary Caves of Hercules, where it's said that Hercules rested before his twelfth labor. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.
