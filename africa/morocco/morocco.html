Morocco

Fez became an early center of Islamic rule and learning under the Idrisids, while later courts founded imperial capitals at Marrakesh and Meknes. Trade across the Sahara linked the interior with West Africa, and migration from al-Andalus influenced the architecture of northern cities. French and Spanish protectorates began in 1912, but independence in 1956 restored a monarchy that continued to rule from Rabat while regional Amazigh identities remained strong.
