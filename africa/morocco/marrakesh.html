Marrakesh

Marrakesh was founded in 1070 by the Almoravid ruler Yusuf ibn Tashfin and later served as capital for the Almohad caliphs who built the Kutubiyya mosque and city walls. Saadian tombs, Bahia Palace, and the Jemaa el-Fna square record how trans-Saharan trade, Andalusian artisans, and French colonial planning turned the red-walled city into a court centre linking the Atlas foothills to Atlantic ports.

Bahia Palace

Bahia Palace is a castle in Marrakesh, Morocco. Bahia Palace, also known as the "Palace of the Brilliant," is a vast and detailed designed late 19th-century palace located in Marrakech. The complex spans over 8 hectares, including a garden that offers a peaceful escape from the city's hustle and bustle. Walls, courtyards, or towers form the usual visitor route when open.

Bab Agnaou

Bab Agnaou, situated in the southwest corner of Marrakech, is a significant historical site as one of the 19 grand gates that once protected the city. It stands out with its detailed decorations reflecting 12th-century design. Originally an entrance to the El Badi Palace, it now is a decorative landmark. The gate is surrounded by busy shops and vendors offering various products, but visitors should be prepared to haggle for reasonable prices.
