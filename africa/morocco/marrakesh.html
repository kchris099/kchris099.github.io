Destination Guide — Marrakesh

Explore Marrakesh

A Brief History

Marrakesh was founded in 1070 by the Almoravid ruler Yusuf ibn Tashfin and later served as capital for the Almohad caliphs who built the Kutubiyya mosque and city walls. Saadian tombs, Bahia Palace, and the Jemaa el-Fna square record how trans-Saharan trade, Andalusian artisans, and French colonial planning turned the red-walled city into a court centre linking the Atlas foothills to Atlantic ports.

Top Sights & Activities

100 MAD

Bahia Palace

Bahia Palace, also known as the "Palace of the Brilliant," is a vast and intricately designed late 19th-century palace located in Marrakech. The complex spans over 8 hectares, including a beautiful garden that offers a peaceful escape from the city's hustle and bustle. With its colorful tile work, mosaics, colored glass, carvings, and elaborate ceilings, the palace dazzles visitors with its intricate design.

Free

Marrakech train station

Marrakech train station, a rail terminus built in 2008, showcases traditional Arabic architecture with its tiled facade and marble interior. While it may seem unusual to include a train station in a list of attractions, this central station is a for lovers of Moroccan architecture. Although there isn't much to do inside besides catching a train or grabbing a drink from the juice bar, visitors can explore nearby free attractions like the opera house and parks.

Free

Jemaa el-Fnaa

Jemaa el-Fnaa is a bustling square in Marrakech, known as the heart of the city's carnival. During the day, it hosts market stalls and various performers such as snake charmers, henna artists, and musicians. As night falls, the square transforms into a lively hub with food stands offering grilled meats and fresh orange juice vendors.

Free

Souk Semmarine

When visiting Marrakech, the bustling Souk Semmarine is a in the famous Souk District. This central channel offers a array of stalls and shops selling everything from colorful clothing, fabrics, antiques, food to spices. As you stroll through this lively market, encounter specialized shops offering pottery, patisseries, textiles, carpets, jewelry and crafts.

50 MAD

Madrasa Ben Youssef

Madrasa Ben Youssef is a 16th-century college building in Marrakech, known for its Islamic architecture. The intricate geometric patterns, ornate tile work, carved stucco, and cedar woodwork adorn the prayer hall and courtyards. Originally founded as a madrasa for religious and legal studies in the 14th century, it has a rich history as one of North Africa's largest theological colleges.

Free

Koutoubia

Koutoubia is a 12th-century mosque In Marrakech's city center, near Djemaa el-Fna square. The area offers a bustling atmosphere with narrow alleys that lead to the traditional souk, where visitors can find an array of handcrafted goods and aromatic spices. The mosque is a prominent landmark visible from various points in Marrakech.

Free

Park Arsat Moulay Abdesalam

Cyber Park Arsat Moulay Abdesalam is a serene escape from the bustling streets of Marrakech. Dating back to the 18th century, it boasts ornamental gardens with fountains, lush foliage, and picturesque walkways. This free attraction is perfect for families and offers a peaceful retreat near the city center. With an abundance of trees providing ample shade, visitors can relax and enjoy the park's tranquil ambiance.

Free

Ensemble Artisanal

Ensemble Artisanal Marrakech is a newly-built shopping complex located just a 10-minute walk from Djemaa el-Fna. The center features cozy Andalusian courtyards and Islamic architecture, creating a relaxing atmosphere for visitors. Here, explore various souvenir and traditional craftsman shops offering handmade leather goods, kitchenware, housewares, clothing, perfume, and more. Unlike the bustling souks, prices here are fixed so there's no need to haggle.

170 MAD

Jardin Majorelle

Jardin Majorelle, located in Marrakesh, is a captivating botanical garden designed by the French artist Jacques Majorelle. This serene oasis features an impressive variety of exotic plants, colors, and peaceful water features that provide a tranquil retreat from the city's hustle and bustle. The garden also houses a 1930s cubist villa and an on-site cafe where visitors can relax amidst lush greenery.

Free

Bab Agnaou

Bab Agnaou, situated in the southwest corner of Marrakech, is a significant historical site as one of the 19 grand gates that once protected the city. It stands out with its intricate decorations reflecting 12th-century design. Originally an entrance to the El Badi Palace, it now serves as a decorative landmark. The gate is surrounded by bustling shops and vendors offering various products, but visitors should be prepared to haggle for reasonable prices.
