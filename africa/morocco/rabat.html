Rabat

Rabat developed as a fortified ribat under the Almohads and became Morocco's administrative capital under French protectorate rule from 1912. The Kasbah of the Udayas, Hassan Tower, Chellah necropolis, and twentieth-century civic quarters mark Atlantic trade, Marinid patronage, and modern state planning in the kingdom's present government seat on the Bou Regreg estuary.

Hassan Tower

Hassan Tower is a visitor site in Rabat, Morocco. It was intended to be the world's largest minaret but remained unfinished after the death of its commissioner, Almohad emperor Yacoub al-Mansour. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Mausoleum of Mohammed V

The Mausoleum of Mohammed V is a significant site in Morocco, featuring the final resting place of King Mohammed V and the tombs of King Hassan II and Prince Abdallah. The mausoleum showcases elegant Alaouite architecture with a striking green dome set against white marble.

Chellah Castle

Chellah is a historic site located on the grounds of an ancient citadel, featuring Roman ruins and royal tombstones. The area covers about 3.5 km2 and includes the ruins of a walled Roman/Arab city, a medieval citadel overlooking the estuary, and the Ville Nouvelle developed under French Protectorate government. The site offers a glimpse into history with its ancient Roman and medieval ruins surrounded by lush gardens.

Kasbah Oudaya

Kasbah des Oudayas is a historic citadel with striking blue walls that overlook the Atlantic. Its origins can be traced back to the Almohad Caliphate, showing centuries of history. The Andalusian Garden within the kasbah is a oasis filled with blooming plants, fruit trees, and palms, originally designed by the French in the 20th century.

The National Zoo Rabat Morocco is a zoological park that houses around 130 different species of animals in simulated habitats such as mountains, deserts, savannas, and rainforests. Royal Palace

Royal Palace is a fortified historic site in Rabat, Morocco. Construction and early use date to around 1864. Visitors can start their journey at the Mausoleum of Mohammed V, which pays tribute to past kings before gazing upon the palace's architecture from outside. Although entry is restricted, its grandeur draws all who pass by. Walls, courtyards, or towers form the main visitor route when the grounds are open.

Assounna Mosque

Assounna Mosque is a religious building in Rabat, Morocco. Construction and early use date to around 1785. Constructed during the reign of Sultan Sidi Mohammed Ben Abdellah around 1785, this mosque has dimensions and a strikingly simple design. Its prayer hall is spaciously divided into three expansive naves that align with the Qibla wall. The exterior and main interior spaces are usually viewable outside service times.

St. Peter's Cathedral

Peter's Cathedral is an art deco Roman Catholic church located in Rabat, Morocco. It is the seat of the Archdiocese of Rabat and is known for its unique architecture and glass works. The choir performs exceptionally well during masses held in French, giving a sense of community to all attendees. Despite being situated in a predominantly Muslim country, the cathedral attracts visitors due to its beauty and proximity to restaurants and cafes near the tram stop.
