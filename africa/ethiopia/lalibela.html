A Brief History

Lalibela's rock-hewn churches were carved in the twelfth and thirteenth centuries under the Zagwe dynasty, which made the town a pilgrimage centre rivalling Jerusalem for Ethiopian Orthodox Christians. Monolithic sanctuaries such as Bete Giyorgis were cut from living tuff and linked by tunnels and courtyards, preserving a distinctive tradition of highland church architecture.

Church of St. George

Church of St. George is a religious building in Lalibela, Ethiopia. Records associated with the site include 1187. It is part of a group of churches connected by underground passageways and surrounded by a deep trench. Lalibela itself is considered one of the holiest cities in Ethiopia and was designed to be a New Jerusalem after the capture of Jerusalem in 1187. The exterior and main interior spaces are usually viewable outside service times.
