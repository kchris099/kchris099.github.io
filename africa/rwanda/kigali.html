Destination Guide — Kigali

Explore Kigali

A Brief History

Established as a regional settlement beyond an older fortified core, the city developed through industrialization in the late 19th century, post-war reconstruction, and modern civic expansion that reshaped its public spaces, markets, and surrounding landscape. Restored religious buildings, civic buildings, and natural features now define the contemporary visitor experience.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

Amahoro National Stadium

Amahoro National Stadium, located in the Remera area of Kigali, Rwanda, is the largest soccer stadium in the country. With a current capacity of 30,000 seats and plans to expand to 45,000 seats by June 2024, it offers an electrifying atmosphere for sports enthusiasts.

Free

Kigali Convention Centre

The Kigali Convention Centre has become a prominent feature of the city's skyline, with its unique beehive-like design and striking illuminated exterior. It serves as a venue for various conferences and events, drawing attention not only for its architectural marvel but also for the vibrant atmosphere it creates at night. The surrounding area reflects the country's commitment to cleanliness and community involvement, making it a pleasant place to visit.

Free

Inema Arts Centre

Nestled in the heart of Kigali, the Inema Arts Centre is a vibrant cultural hub that has been championing Rwandan artistry since its inception in 2012. Founded by two brothers dedicated to nurturing emerging talent, this contemporary gallery showcases an impressive array of works from both local and international artists. With ten resident artists creating across various mediums, travellers can immerse themselves in a rich tapestry of creativity.

Free

Caplaki Craft Village

Caplaki Craft Village is a must-visit spot in Kigali, located on the 3rd floor of the Masterpiece Mall. With over 30 local vendors offering a wide variety of souvenirs and crafts, it's a great place to find locally made goods. The market provides a calmer shopping experience compared to other markets in the area. Vendors are generally friendly and colorful, creating a fun atmosphere for bargaining with craftspeople and artists selling African souvenirs.

Free

Niyo Arts Center

Nestled in the vibrant Kacyiru neighborhood, the Niyo Arts Center stands as a beacon of creativity and social impact. Founded by Rwandan orphan Pacifique Niyonsenga, this colorful gallery and cultural hub not only showcases the talents of its resident artists but also uplifts local children living in poverty through art education.

Free

St Famille Church

St Famille Church is a pleasant and accommodating location suitable for people of all ages, including children, the elderly, and those with physical disabilities. It offers ample parking space and is particularly well-suited for weddings and religious events. The church itself is large, clean, and affiliated with the Catholic faith. Additionally, there are guest houses available as well as a clinic and pharmacy conveniently located nearby.

Free

Belgian Peacekeepers Memorial

The Belgian Peacekeepers Memorial, established by the Belgian government in 2000, commemorates the 10 Belgian UN Peacekeepers who were tragically killed during the Rwandan Genocide. This memorial serves as a poignant reminder of the events that unfolded in 1994 when Hutu extremists targeted these peacekeepers to compel the withdrawal of UN forces from Rwanda, ultimately leading to a full-scale genocide.

Free

Nyamirambo Women's Center (NWC)

In the vibrant Nyamirambo district of Kigali, find the Nyamirambo Women's Center (NWC), a non-profit organization dedicated to empowering Rwandan women through education and training. The center's skilled artisans create beautiful children's clothing, accessories, and home decor from traditional kitenge fabrics, which are sold to support community programs. travellers can also join walking tours to explore the neighborhood's culture and history.

Free

Kigali Genocide Memorial

The Kigali Genocide Memorial is a significant tribute to the victims of the 1994 Rwandan genocide. It offers travellers a chance to gain insight into the tragic events through various exhibits, including audio messages from victims and video recordings of the perpetrators. Despite being emotionally demanding, it provides a foundation for understanding Rwanda's complex history.

RWF 20000

Nyandungu Eco-Park

Nyandungu Urban Wetland Eco-tourism Park, located in Kigali City, spans 121.7 hectares of restored wetland and forest. The park offers a 10km trail for walking and cycling, allowing travellers to immerse themselves in the diverse habitats it encompasses. Bird enthusiasts can indulge in a full day of birdwatching along the designated trail. The park features well-paved routes suitable for cyclists and walkers, with shaded areas provided by bamboo tree canopies.
