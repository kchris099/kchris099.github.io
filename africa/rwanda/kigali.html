Kigali

Kigali was a modest colonial administrative post that became Rwanda's capital at independence in 1962. Hills define its neighbourhoods and road pattern. The 1994 genocide devastated the country; memorials such as the Kigali Genocide Memorial document that history. Reconstruction since then produced ordered streets, a growing services economy, and strict urban rules. Markets, crafts centres, and hilltop views frame a capital rebuilt with deliberate civic planning.

Amahoro National Stadium

Amahoro National Stadium, located in the Remera area of Kigali, Rwanda, is the largest soccer stadium in the country. With a current capacity of 30,000 seats and plans to expand to 45,000 seats by June 2024, it offers an electrifying atmosphere for sports enthusiasts.

Inema Arts Centre

Located Kigali, the Inema Arts Centre is a active cultural hub that has been championing Rwandan artistry since its inception in 2012. Founded by two brothers dedicated to nurturing emerging talent, this contemporary gallery showcases an array of works from both local and international artists. With ten resident artists creating across various mediums, travellers can immerse themselves in a rich history of creativity.

Caplaki Craft Village

Caplaki Craft Village is a must-visit spot in Kigali, located on the 3rd floor of the Masterpiece Mall. With over 30 local vendors offering a wide variety of souvenirs and crafts, it's a great place to find locally made goods. The market provides a calmer shopping experience compared to other markets in the area. Vendors are generally friendly and colorful, creating a fun atmosphere for bargaining with craftspeople and artists selling African souvenirs.

Belgian Peacekeepers Memorial

The Belgian Peacekeepers Memorial, established by the Belgian government in 2000, commemorates the 10 Belgian UN Peacekeepers who were tragically killed during the Rwandan Genocide. This memorial is a reminder of the events that unfolded in 1994 when Hutu extremists targeted these peacekeepers to compel the withdrawal of UN forces from Rwanda, ultimately leading to a full-scale genocide.

Nyamirambo Women's Center (NWC)

In the active Nyamirambo district of Kigali, find the Nyamirambo Women's Center (NWC), a non-profit organization dedicated to empowering Rwandan women through education and training. The center's skilled artisans create children's clothing, accessories, and home decor from traditional kitenge fabrics, which are sold to support community programs. Travellers can also join walking tours to explore the neighborhood's culture and history.

Kigali Genocide Memorial

The Kigali Genocide Memorial is a significant tribute to the victims of the 1994 Rwandan genocide. It gives visitors a chance to gain insight into the tragic events through various exhibits, including audio messages from victims and video recordings of the perpetrators. Despite being emotionally demanding, it provides a foundation for understanding Rwanda's complex history.

Nyandungu Eco-Park

Nyandungu Eco-Park is a public park in Kigali, Rwanda. Nyandungu Urban Wetland Eco-tourism Park, located in Kigali City, spans 121.7 hectares of restored wetland and forest. The park offers a 10km trail for walking and cycling, allowing travellers to immerse themselves in the diverse habitats it encompasses. Paths, lawns, and shaded areas make up the main public facilities.
