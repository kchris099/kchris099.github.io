Cape Town

Cape Town began as a Dutch East India Company refreshment station in 1652 and grew into a strategic port at the Cape of Good Hope. British rule, emancipation, and apartheid shaped its harbour, Company Gardens, and Table Mountain backdrop. Today the city links Atlantic trade routes to South Africa's legislative capital. Victoria & Alfred Waterfront and Bo-Kaap quarters extend harbour heritage beneath Table Mountain.

Castle of Good Hope

The Castle of Good Hope was built by the Dutch East India Company in the seventeenth century as a pentagonal fort, administrative center, storehouse and military base. Bastions, courtyards, cells, museums and ceremonial spaces reflect colonial power supported by enslaved and coerced labor. Shoreline reclamation moved the sea away, while the site is an active military property as well as a museum.
