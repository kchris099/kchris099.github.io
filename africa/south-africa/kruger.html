A Brief History

The Kruger lowveld was traversed by Voortrekker parties in the 1830s, with the first European settlement at White River in 1865 and the gold rush town of Pilgrim's Rest established in 1873. Paul Kruger proclaimed the Sabi Game Reserve in 1898 to protect wildlife from overhunting, and it was renamed Kruger National Park in 1926 under the National Parks Act. The 19,485-square-kilometre reserve was opened to the public with the Skukuza rest camp in 1929, and SANParks assumed management from the National Parks Board in 1994, retaining the lowveld's mosaic of acacia savanna, mopane woodland, and Lebombo basalt ridge.

Top Sights & Activities

R602

Kruger National Park (international adult day entry)

Kruger National Park is South Africa's flagship 19,485-square-kilometre national park, spanning the lowveld between the Crocodile River in the south and the Limpopo River frontier with Mozambique. The park protects the Big Five alongside 147 mammal species, 517 bird species, and the documented archaeological record of Iron Age settlements. Travellers pay ZAR602 per international adult day visitor and drive the self-guided road network.

Free

Skukuza Rest Camp

Skukuza Rest Camp is the largest rest camp and the administrative headquarters of Kruger National Park, set on the Sabi River 11 kilometres from the Paul Kruger Gate and operating as the central hub of the park's rest-camp network. The camp accommodates 500 visitors across bungalows, family cottages, and a tented camp. Travellers access the site without separate entry beyond the Kruger day permit.

R107

Bourke’s Luck Potholes

Bourke's Luck Potholes is a geological rock-pool formation at the confluence of the Blyde and Treur rivers in the Mpumalanga lowveld, on the western access route to Kruger National Park. The cylindrical potholes were carved by sand-laden eddies at the river junction over a documented 100,000-year period, with the largest pools roughly 6 metres deep. Travellers pay ZAR107 per adult and walk the 1-kilometre bridge-and-boardwalk circuit.
