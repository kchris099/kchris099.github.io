Johannesburg

Johannesburg was founded in 1886 after gold was discovered on the Witwatersrand, drawing mining capital and migrant labour from across southern Africa. Victorian public buildings, Apartheid Museum narratives, and Soweto townships record industrial boom, segregation, and democratic transition in the highveld metropolis. Constitution Hill precincts and Maboneng creative districts extend mining-era skylines across the Witwatersrand.

Sandton City

Sandton City is a luxurious shopping destination that has been around since 1973. It has a wide array of global luxury brands, gourmet restaurants, a cinema, and a kids' play area. The mall is home to prestigious designer boutiques and specialist shops, making it an ideal location for shoppers with diverse tastes.

Sandton Convention Centre

In northern Johannesburg's business district, Sandton Convention Centre stages exhibitions, conferences, and multi-day events amid surrounding malls, hotels, and entertainment hubs. Contemporary African decor and modern technical systems support large programs. Delegates rarely need to leave the Sandton core between sessions.

Rosebank Mall

Rosebank Mall occupies a central block beside the Gautrain station in northern Johannesburg. Enclosed floors combine fashion and household shops with restaurants, a cinema and the African Craft Market, where independent traders sell textiles, carvings, jewelry and other goods. It is a transport-linked commercial center within the larger Rosebank business and residential district.

DP World Wanderers Stadium

Also known as Imperial Wanderers, the Johannesburg cricket ground seats about 34,000 with 182 suites leased for events and hospitality. International and domestic matches fill the stands on fixture days. Gate and ticket procedures follow posted event rules at the perimeter.

FNB Stadium

Shaped like a calabash, FNB Stadium dominates the southern Johannesburg skyline beside Soweto. The rebuilt arena hosted the opening and final matches of the 2010 FIFA World Cup and remains the home venue for major football fixtures, concerts, and public ceremonies. Its earlier history also includes the 1990 gathering at which Nelson Mandela addressed a large crowd after his release.

Gandhi Square Precinct

Redeveloped and renamed for Mohandas Gandhi, Gandhi Square opens paved ground and surrounding facades in central Johannesburg. The civic plaza works as a transit and meeting space for office workers and shoppers. Open paving defines the setting more than landscaped gardens or monuments alone.

Located on the outskirts of Johannesburg CBD, Oriental Plaza is a busy indoor bazaar offering a wide array of goods at bargain prices. With 360 individually-owned stores, visitors can find everything from clothing and homeware to spices and designer brands. The unique open-air atmosphere and nonfixed prices make it a popular shopping destination for locals and visitors alike.

Constitution Hill Human Rights Precinct

Constitution Hill Human Rights Precinct in Johannesburg is a historically significant site that offers a deep and moving look into South Africa's journey to democracy and human rights. It comprises the Old Fort, Number Four Jail, Women's Jail, and the Awaiting Trial Block, which have been transformed into museums and heritage sites. The precinct sheds light on the country's struggles against apartheid while promoting reconciliation and justice.

Johannesburg Zoo

The Johannesburg Zoo, spanning 55 hectares, is home to a diverse array of rare African species and hosts a successful white lion breeding program. It offers a chance to encounter South Africa's wildlife within the city limits. Visitors can explore the zoo's 200 acres and encounter the Big Five, endangered species like the white rhino and wattled crane, as well as record-holding animals such as the cheetah and Andean condor.

Shoppers find cultural goods and souvenirs in one dense trading ground. The market offers a direct look at healer practices still in use.

Mandela's House

At 8115 Vilakazi Street in Soweto, the small brick house associated with Nelson Mandela preserves the scale of the family home he used before imprisonment and after his release. Photographs, furnishings, documents, and damage from political violence place his private life within the wider struggle against apartheid. The museum sits on a residential street shared with local businesses and homes.

Hector Pieterson Memorial

A photograph of dying schoolboy Hector Pieterson became one of the defining records of the Soweto uprising on 16 June 1976. The memorial and nearby museum trace the student protest against compulsory Afrikaans instruction, the police response and the movement's national consequences through testimony, images, objects and a route linked to the original march. The site commemorates many victims, not Pieterson.

Johannesburg Botanical Gardens

Formerly part of a sports and recreation estate, Johannesburg Botanical Garden spreads around the western shore of Emmarentia Dam; the dam supports canoeing and sailing clubs, while the garden functions primarily as an urban public landscape. Specialized areas include rose beds, herb and succulent collections, tree-lined lawns, and paths used for walking and birdwatching.

15 AdmissionFree

Klipriviersberg Nature Reserve

Grassland ridges, rocky koppies, river valleys and traces of earlier settlement make Klipriviersberg Nature Reserve an unusual natural area within metropolitan Johannesburg. Marked routes cross the roughly 600-hectare reserve, where zebra, blesbok, wildebeest, small mammals and many bird species may be seen. Conditions are exposed and facilities limited.

16 AdmissionFree

Klipriviersberg Municipal Nature Reserve

Klipriviersberg Municipal Nature Reserve protects grassland, wooded slopes and ridges south of central Johannesburg. Hiking paths cross archaeological remains and former farm landscapes, while zebra, wildebeest and other free-ranging herbivores may be encountered within the urban reserve. Its municipal status and proximity to residential suburbs make it an accessible example of Highveld habitat inside the metropolitan area.
