Kgalagadi

San hunter-gatherers used the arid Kgalagadi for millennia before colonial borders split the dune country. The 1999 Kgalagadi Transfrontier Park joined South African and Botswanan conservation areas. Wildlife migrations, not civic monuments, are the historical subject here. Red dune streets and dry riverbeds structure game drives more than any built civic core.

The Big Red Barn

The Big Red Barn is a popular, family-oriented outdoor recreation venue located on a large farm near Centurion. It offers a wide variety of activities, including extensive mountain biking and trail running tracks, a treetop zipline course, and a relaxed cafe. The rustic, open-air environment makes it a favorite weekend destination for active families.

Modderfontein Reserve

In Johannesburg's eastern suburbs, privately managed Modderfontein Reserve keeps hiking and cycling trails plus picnic areas inside a large conservation tract. Paths and shade give an urban natural retreat. The reserve sits within the metro sprawl rather than a remote wilderness.

03 AdmissionFree

Castle Kyalami - Church of Scientology in South Africa

Castle Kyalami is a turreted complex built in the 1990s as a private residence and later used for hotel and conference functions. The Church of Scientology acquired the property for its African headquarters, religious training and administrative activities. Large halls, towers and landscaped grounds create a deliberately castle-like design. The property now functions as an institutional campus with controlled public access.

04 AdmissionFree

Beaulieu Bird Sanctuary

Beaulieu Bird Sanctuary is a small protected wetland inside the Beaulieu equestrian estate at Midrand, used by local birdwatchers and photographers. Reed beds and open water draw waterfowl and other species within a suburban setting far from the Kgalagadi dunes that share the destination label. Paths and quiet observation points keep the focus on habitat rather than large visitor centers.

25.9804° S / 28.0697° E

05 AdmissionFree

Lory Park Zoo

Lory Park Animal and Owl Sanctuary is a compact private facility that keeps birds, reptiles, primates and several carnivores in managed enclosures. Education programs address animal care and conservation, while close-range viewing shapes much of the visitor experience. The sanctuary's controlled setting and smaller scale differ from open wildlife reserves, and its collection depends on ongoing rescue, breeding and husbandry decisions.

Waterfall City Park

Inside Midrand's Waterfall City development, this modern urban park supplies lawns and event space for surrounding commercial and residential blocks. Outdoor programs often use the open ground on weekends. Paths and shade make it a local recreation hub rather than a remote nature reserve.

Madhya Kailash Temple

Dedicated to Lord Shiva, Madhya Kailash Temple in Midrand hosts regular ceremonies and festivals for the local Hindu community. Exterior courts and main interior spaces stay viewable outside service times. The temple functions as both worship hall and cultural center.

08 AdmissionFree

Liliesleaf

Liliesleaf Farm sheltered leaders of the African National Congress and allied organizations during the early 1960s. Police raided the property in July 1963, arresting figures whose prosecution led to the Rivonia Trial. Surviving and reconstructed rooms explain clandestine meetings, communications and the farm's cover operation, connecting the quiet suburban setting with a decisive episode in the struggle against apartheid.

26.0437° S / 28.0536° E

09 AdmissionFree

Makhulong Park

In Tembisa near Johannesburg, Makhulong Park supplies sports fields, children's play areas, and open lawns for local soccer, gatherings, and weekend rest. Green space is scarce in the surrounding township fabric. Community use defines the park more than tourist programming.

Ivory Regional Park

In Ivory Park near Midrand, this large municipal park offers open ground, sports facilities, and picnic areas for a dense surrounding community. Outdoor activities and social events fill the lawns on weekends. The park works as essential public recreation rather than a ticketed attraction.
