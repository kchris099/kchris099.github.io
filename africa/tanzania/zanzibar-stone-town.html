Destination Guide — Zanzibar Stone Town

Explore Zanzibar Stone Town

A Brief History

Zanzibar Stone Town developed as a major Swahili trading port, flourishing under Omani Arab rule in the nineteenth century as a center for the spice and slave trades. Its architecture reflects a unique fusion of Arab, Persian, Indian, and European elements, characterized by narrow alleys, coral stone buildings, and intricately carved wooden doors. The town was the site of the shortest war in history in 1896. Today, it is a UNESCO World Heritage site, preserving its complex multicultural legacy.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

Emerson on Hurumzi

Emerson on Hurumzi is a chic hotel located in an 1800s villa, offering vibrant quarters with some rooms providing ocean views. The rooftop restaurant provides unique panoramic sights of the Indian Ocean and surrounding mosques, making it locations in town to enjoy the exotic view of Stone Town. The hotel has a rich history, with legends linking its name to the freedom of black slaves.

Free

Emerson Spice

Emerson Spice is a charming hotel located in Stone Town, Zanzibar, offering elegant rooms and suites with views of the Zanzibar Channel. Housed in a former residence of the island's Swahili rulers, this hotel provides a unique experience steeped in history. Each of its 10 rooms is individually styled to honor important figures from Zanzibar's past and features vintage furnishings reflecting indigenous design.

Free

Old Fort

Old Fort is a 17th-century structure in Stone Town, built by Omani Arab rulers as a defense against the Portuguese. The fort features striking ramparts and an open-air theatre where nightly entertainment and occasional festivals take place. Visitors can explore market stalls, art exhibitions, and nearby attractions such as the House of Wonders and the Old Dispensary.

Free

The Forodhani of Zanzibar

Forodhani Gardens is a vibrant public park located along the waterfront in Stone Town, Zanzibar. During the day, it offers a relaxing space with views of the Indian Ocean and historic buildings. In the evening, it transforms into a bustling, open-air night market famous for its wide array of Swahili and Zanzibari street food, particularly fresh seafood.

Free

Cheetah's Rock

Cheetah's Rock is a renowned wildlife conservation center on Zanzibar Island, offering educational tours that provide up-close interactions with various rescued animals. Visitors can learn about animal behaviors and the importance of preserving endangered species while enjoying wildlife tours to observe and take photos with animals such as cheetahs, zebras, lions, striped hyenas, turtles, golden tigers, and ring-tailed lemurs.

Free

Zanzibar Spice Community Shop

The Zanzibar Spice Community Shop is a vibrant local market situated in Stone Town, Zanzibar. It offers a diverse array of products including flavored tea, organic soaps, and an assortment of local spices such as cloves, cinnamon, and nutmeg that are cultivated on the island. Additionally, visitors can find locally made items like essential oils and traditional handicrafts.

5000 TZS

Christ Church Cathedral

Christ Church Cathedral in Zanzibar, located in Stone Town, is a historic Anglican church built on the site of the largest slave market in East Africa. Constructed between 1873 and 1880, it stands as a poignant symbol commemorating the end of the slave trade in Zanzibar. The cathedral also houses a Slave Trade Exhibition, serving as a reminder of the island's dark history and its role in the global abolition of slavery.

5000 TZS

East Africa Slave Trade Exhibition (Slave Chambers - Cathedoral Church Of Christ)

The East Africa Slave Trade Exhibition is located on the site of the former slave market in Stone Town. The poignant exhibit includes the original, cramped underground chambers where enslaved people were held before being sold. The adjacent Christ Church Cathedral, built to commemorate the end of the trade, features a powerful monument to the victims of slavery.

Free

Tortoise Sanctuary

Tortoise Sanctuary, located on Prison Island (Changu Island) in Tanzania, offers a unique experience to witness and feed the ancient Aldabra tortoises. Once a prison for rebellious slaves, the island now serves as a sanctuary for these rare creatures. Visitors can interact with around 100 magnificent tortoises and even feed them lettuce while exploring the remnants of the old prison. The sanctuary also features a large cage housing baby tortoises.

Free

Nakupenda Beach

Nakupenda Beach, which translates to "love you" in Swahili, is a stretch of white sand set against the turquoise waters of Zanzibar. During low tide, the beautiful sand becomes visible and offers an ideal setting for romantic walks or sunbathing. One of the main attractions here is the opportunity to swim with dolphins or turtles and capture aerial shots of this experience.
