Zanzibar Stone Town

Under nineteenth-century Omani rule, Stone Town became Zanzibar's Swahili port for cloves and the slave trade. Coral-stone houses, narrow alleys, and carved wooden doors mix Arab, Persian, Indian, and European building habits. The 1896 Anglo-Zanzibar conflict lasted under an hour yet fixed British dominance. UNESCO listing now covers that multicultural harbour fabric.

Christ Church Cathedral

Christ Church Cathedral in Zanzibar, located in Stone Town, is a historic Anglican church built on the site of the largest slave market in East Africa. Constructed between 1873 and 1880, it is a symbol commemorating the end of the slave trade in Zanzibar. The cathedral also houses a Slave Trade Exhibition, serving as a reminder of the island's dark history and its role in the global abolition of slavery.
