Algiers

Algiers grew from the Phoenician port of Icosium into a major Ottoman and French colonial capital overlooking the Bay of Algiers. The Casbah's whitewashed lanes, Ottoman palaces, and Ketchaoua Mosque record centuries of Berber, Arab, and European rule, while Haussmann-era boulevards and Martyrs Memorial reflect twentieth-century independence struggles and modern Algerian statehood.

Martyrs Memorial

The Martyrs Memorial, also known as Maqam Echahid, is a significant concrete monument in Algiers built in 1982 to honor the 20th anniversary of Algeria's independence. It pays tribute to those who lost their lives in the struggle for independence from colonial France. The monument consists of three stylized palms meeting at mid-height, entirely made of concrete.
