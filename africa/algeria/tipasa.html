Tipasa

Tipasa preserves a Roman colony founded in the first century CE on Algeria's Mediterranean coast, with forums, villas, and basilicas facing the sea. The nearby Royal Mausoleum of Mauretania, built for King Juba II, links the town to Numidian and Roman dynastic politics. Together they illustrate how North African ports connected Rome to Atlantic trade routes.

Royal Mausoleum of Mauretania

A circular stone drum rises above a square base at the Royal Mausoleum of Mauretania, an ancient funerary monument on the hills between Algiers and Tipasa. Concentric masonry courses form a stepped cone, while four false doors mark the cardinal sides. The monument is generally associated with the Numidian and Mauretanian royal tradition before Roman rule.
