Explore Victoria Falls

A Brief History

Known to local tribes as 'The Smoke That Thunders', the massive falls were introduced to the global stage by David Livingstone in 1855. The arrival of the railway bridge in 1905 catalyzed rapid colonial development, establishing a premier African tourism hub alongside raw natural power. This continuous historical development established a lasting framework that strongly informs the area's modern cultural evolution.

Victoria Falls

Victoria Falls, locally known as Mosi-oa-Tunya ('The Smoke That Thunders'), is one of the largest and most famous waterfalls in the world. Located on the Zambezi River at the border between Zambia and Zimbabwe, the falls feature a massive, continuous sheet of cascading water that creates a towering column of spray. The site offers dramatic viewpoints and is a major hub for adventure tourism.

Free

The Victoria Falls World Heritage Site

The Victoria Falls World Heritage Site encompasses the dramatic waterfall and the surrounding riverine environment on both the Zambian and Zimbabwean sides. The protected area preserves the unique geological formation of the basalt gorges and the diverse ecosystem supported by the constant spray from the falls. It includes a network of trails that provide various vantage points of the cascading Zambezi River.

Free

Devil's Cataract

Devil's Cataract is one of the five waterfalls that make up Victoria Falls, located in a country that boasts two-thirds of the falls and most of the viewing points. The site offers various attractions such as the David Livingstone statue, The Boiling Pot, Danger Point, and more. Visitors can experience the waterfall year-round from both wet and dry seasons. However, some have noted high entrance fees and occasional disturbances from helicopters.

Free

Mosi-oa-Tunya National Park

Mosi-oa-Tunya National Park, located on the Zambezi River, is a natural reserve that boasts the famous Victoria Falls and a diverse array of wildlife. Spanning 23 square kilometers, this park is home to an impressive population of white rhinos, as well as elephants, giraffes, zebras, buffalos, and various antelope species. Visitors can enjoy activities such as game drives and the popular Rhino Walk to observe these magnificent creatures in their natural habitat.

Free

Batonka Guest Lodge

Nestled in lush gardens, Batonka Guest Lodge is a tranquil retreat just a short walk from the main attractions. The upscale lodge boasts 27 breezy quarters with modern decor, whirlpool tubs, and private balconies or patios. Guests can unwind in the outdoor pool or dine at the on-site restaurant. Despite a minor reservation mix-up, the lodge's exceptional service and quick accommodation impressed visitors.

Free

Bayete Guest Lodge

Nestled in a serene setting, Bayete Guest Lodge is a family-run eco-certified lodge with 30 down-to-earth rooms surrounded by lush tropical gardens. The central features include a large teardrop swimming pool, a Dining Boma, and Bar. Guests can take advantage of the outdoor pool, terrace, and garden while enjoying complimentary wireless internet access and concierge services.

Free

Victoria Falls National Park

Victoria Falls National Park sits within the Victoria Falls National Park area of Victoria Falls, documented in local heritage registers for its civic or cultural role. Visitors approach from the main access road and combine the visit with neighbouring heritage buildings and public spaces in the district.

Free

Victoria Falls Crocodile Park

The Victoria Falls Crocodile Park is a conservation and educational facility located near the Zambezi River. It houses numerous Nile crocodiles of various sizes, from hatchlings to massive adults. The park offers guided tours that educate visitors about the biology, behavior, and ecological role of these apex predators, providing a safe environment to observe them up close.
