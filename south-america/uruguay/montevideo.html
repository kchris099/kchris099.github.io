Montevideo

Montevideo grew from a Spanish citadel founded in 1726 around a natural harbour on the Río de la Plata. As capital of independent Uruguay from 1830, it developed as a commercial and administrative centre between Argentina and Brazil. Ciudad Vieja churches, Art Deco towers along Avenida 18 de Julio, and the Mercado del Puerto reflect port wealth, European immigration, and 20th-century civic expansion on the eastern riverbank.
