Explore Colonia del Sacramento

A Brief History

Colonia del Sacramento was founded by the Portuguese in 1680 to smuggle goods across the Río de la Plata into the Spanish-controlled port of Buenos Aires. The town's strategic position caused it to violently change hands between the Spanish and Portuguese empires several times over a century, resulting in a unique architectural fusion of Portuguese stone houses and Spanish brick masonry. The town's irregular, winding street plan reflects this turbulent colonial past. Visitors walk these contested cobblestone streets among the historic lighthouse, the city gate, and the ruins of the San Francisco convent.

Colonia del Sacramento Lighthouse

Colonia del Sacramento Lighthouse, dating back to the 1850s, offers a historical journey with its staircase leading to the top and displays showcasing its past. The city's Plaza Mayor features various unique monuments such as the City Gate with an impressive drawbridge, the Street of Sighs offering a view of the sea, and the oldest church in Uruguay - Iglesia Matriz. Visitors are encouraged to allocate at least 4-5 hours to explore this charming city before catching their ferry.

Free

San Miguel Bastion

A historic military fortification that offers a glimpse into the colonial past and views of the surrounding area. It remains a notable local point of interest situated in Colonia del Sacramento, Uruguay. Visitors can observe the surrounding area and its connection to the city's broader historical landscape. Local guides sometimes include it on walking routes through the centre.

Free

Ciudad Vieja

Ciudad Vieja, also known as the Old City of Colonia del Sacramento, is a popular tourist destination located just 50 km from Buenos Aires. This historic neighborhood was declared a World Heritage Site by UNESCO in 1995 due to its successful fusion of Portuguese and Spanish architectural styles. The area's charming streets are explored on foot, allowing visitors to admire the colonial architecture that reflects the city's rich history of Portuguese and Spanish colonization.

Free

Colonia Del Sacramento

Colonia Del Sacramento is a charming destination that offers a delightful escape from Buenos Aires, just a short ferry ride away. This UNESCO World Heritage site boasts picturesque cobblestone streets lined with colonial buildings reflecting its rich Spanish and Portuguese heritage. As you wander through the historic quarter, discover an array of boutiques and cozy restaurants perfect for leisurely exploration.

UYU 2,000 (ferry ticket)

Colonia Express

Colonia Express is one of the three ferry operators that offer daily trips from Buenos Aires to Colonia. Unlike Buquebus and Seacat, Colonia Express departs from a different terminal, so it's important not to confuse them if you want to catch your ferry on time. The sailing time from Buenos Aires to Colonia is about 1 hour and 15 minutes, with early morning and early afternoon departures available.

Free

Colonia Del Sacramento Boulevard

Colonia Del Sacramento Boulevard, also known as Rambla, is a picturesque waterfront promenade offering views. It's perfect for leisurely walks during the day and becomes an ideal spot to witness sunsets. The well-maintained sidewalk is wide and safe, with street lights illuminating the path at night. The area boasts numerous restaurants within walking distance of the UNESCO protected old city, making it family-friendly.

UYU 150

Plaza de toros Real de San Carlos

Un viaje a Colonia no estaría completo sin una visita a la impresionante Plaza de Toros, un monumento que destaca tanto por su belleza arquitectónica como por su rica historia. Inaugurada en 1910 por el empresario argentino Nicolás Mihanovich, este ambicioso proyecto buscaba transformar la ciudad en un centro de entretenimiento regional con un complejo que incluía una plaza de toros, frontón y casino.
