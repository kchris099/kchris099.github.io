Colonia del Sacramento

Colonia del Sacramento was founded by the Portuguese in 1680 to smuggle goods across the Río de la Plata into the Spanish-controlled port of Buenos Aires. The town's strategic position caused it to violently change hands between the Spanish and Portuguese empires several times over a century, resulting in a unique architectural fusion of Portuguese stone houses and Spanish brick masonry. The town's irregular, winding street plan reflects this turbulent colonial past. Visitors walk these contested cobblestone streets among the historic lighthouse, the city gate, and the ruins of the San Francisco convent.

Colonia del Sacramento Lighthouse

Colonia del Sacramento Lighthouse rises beside the ruins of the seventeenth-century Convent of San Francisco within the historic quarter. The nineteenth-century masonry tower looks across tiled roofs, defensive remains and the Rio de la Plata, with a narrow stair leading upward when open. Wind, maintenance, capacity and seasonal hours affect climbing access.

San Miguel Bastion

San Miguel Bastion preserves Portuguese and Spanish defensive works at Colonia del Sacramento, with stone walls and gun positions facing Rio de la Plata approaches. Walkers reach the bastion from historic-quarter riverside lanes without leaving the old town grid. The fortification remains part of the colonial street plan.

Ciudad Vieja

Ciudad Vieja, also known as the Old City of Colonia del Sacramento, is a popular tourist destination located just 50 km from Buenos Aires. This historic neighborhood was declared a World Heritage Site by UNESCO in 1995 due to its successful fusion of Portuguese and Spanish architectural styles. The area's streets are explored on foot, allowing visitors to admire the colonial architecture that reflects the city's rich history of Portuguese and Spanish colonization.

Colonia Del Sacramento

Colonia del Sacramento preserves a riverside historic quarter shaped by repeated Portuguese and Spanish control. Cobblestone lanes, low houses, plazas, gates, walls, churches and waterfront remains reveal an urban pattern distinct from later colonial planning in the region. Tourism and restoration have changed many interiors, while the designation applies to the historic district.

Muelle De Colonia Del Sacramento

The old pier at Colonia del Sacramento extends into the Rio de la Plata near the historic quarter and yacht harbor; timber or masonry elements, moored boats, river views and changing light make it a simple waterfront stopping point. Wind, water level, maintenance, vessel activity and access controls affect how far the structure can be used.

Colonia Express

Colonia Express is one of the three ferry operators that offer daily trips from Buenos Aires to Colonia. Unlike Buquebus and Seacat, Colonia Express departs from a different terminal, so it's important not to confuse them For travellers who want to catch visitor ferry on time. The sailing time from Buenos Aires to Colonia is about 1 hour and 15 minutes, with early morning and early afternoon departures available.

Plaza de toros Real de San Carlos

Un viaje a Colonia no estaría completo sin una visita a la impresionante Plaza de Toros, un monumento que destaca tanto por su belleza arquitectónica como por su rica historia. Inaugurada en 1910 por el empresario argentino Nicolás Mihanovich, este ambicioso proyecto buscaba transformar la ciudad en un centro de entretenimiento regional con un complejo que incluía una plaza de toros, frontón y casino.

Granja Colonia

Granja Colonia, also known as Granja Arenas or Museo de las Colecciones, is a rural visitor property on Route 1 outside Colonia del Sacramento. The farm combines native animals, regional products and displays of accumulated objects, including large collections of pencils and other everyday items. Its setting links a private museum with the dairy and agricultural landscape of the Colonia department.
