Jericoacoara

For generations an isolated fishing hamlet hidden behind massive sand dunes, Jericoacoara operated without electricity until the late 20th century. Its designation as an environmental protection area preserved its rustic coastal identity even as it became a global kite-surfing hub. Visitors today can clearly trace the detailed layers of this unique heritage throughout the preserved streets and monuments.

Tree Sloth

Tree-dwelling sloths appear in the dunes and village edges around Jericoacoara, often pointed out by local guides. Seeing one usually means a slow scan of leafy branches near pousadas and dune paths. The animals have become a soft wildlife marker for the Ceara beach town beyond its sand and wind. Quiet observation beats approaching too closely along pousada fences.

Lagoa do Paraíso

Lagoa do Paraiso is a freshwater lagoon east of Jericoacoara reached by dune buggy or truck transfer. Shallow warm water, sandbars, and thatched bars fill the shore on sunny days. Many visitors combine the lagoon with dune rides before returning to the village for sunset. Sandbars create natural wading pools far from the Atlantic swell.

2.8576° S / 40.4506° W

03 AdmissionFree (beach club R$50)

Paradise Lagoon

Paradise Lagoon is the English name travelers use for Lagoa do Paraiso near Jericoacoara. Buggy routes cross dunes to reach calm swimming water away from the Atlantic surf. Hammocks and shallow wading areas make it a long midday stop on typical circuit tours. Palm-thatched kiosks rent chairs along the sandbars when the sun is high.

2.8754° S / 40.4556° W

04 AdmissionFree

Pedra Furada

Wave action opened a large arch through a coastal rock slab below Morro do Serrote near Jericoacoara. Pedra Furada is reached on foot along the shore at low tide or by an inland trail over exposed ground; seasonal sun alignment draws attention in July, but tide, heat, and uneven rocks are more important practical factors throughout the year.

2.7846° S / 40.5008° W

05 AdmissionFree

Praça de Jericoacoara

Praça de Jericoacoara, also called Pracinha de Jeri, opened around 2012 as the village's main gathering square. Shops, restaurants, and nightlife spill onto the edges after dark. During the day the open paving is a meeting point before dune and beach excursions. Dune-tour trucks pick up and drop passengers along the square's sandy edges.

2.7956° S / 40.5175° W

06 AdmissionFree

Dune Sunset Jericoacoara

Duna do Pôr do Sol rises beside Jericoacoara village where sand once formed a higher ridge above the sea; people gather on the slope in late afternoon to watch the sun set toward the Atlantic and then return across the sand after dark. Wind and coastal movement continually reshape the dune, which has changed noticeably in height and extent over time.

2.7987° S / 40.5212° W

07 AdmissionFree

Praia da Malhada

Praia da Malhada sits within walking distance of Jericoacoara village, with rocky shelves exposed at low tide. Tide pools and small caves appear among the stones when the water drops. The beach offers a quieter alternative to the main sunset dune farther west. Fishermen still use parts of the rock shelf when swell and tide allow.

2.7906° S / 40.5179° W

08 AdmissionFree (tourism tax R$42)

Jericoacoara National Park

Jericoacoara National Park protects a diverse coastal network in Ceará, encompassing massive sand dunes, pristine beaches, and clear freshwater lagoons. Established in 2002, the park preserves the unique natural environment and restricts vehicle access to maintain its ecological integrity. Visitors can explore the landscape via dune buggies, horseback, or hiking trails.

2.8181° S / 40.4970° W

09 AdmissionFree

Lagoa do Amâncio

Lagoa do Amancio is another freshwater lagoon in the Jericoacoara dune field, quieter than Paraiso on many days. Buggy tours reach its shores for swimming and short walks on pale sand. Vegetation around the water softens the surrounding wind-shaped dunes. Wind shapes fresh ripples across the water while buggies wait on the dune crest.

2.8326° S / 40.4890° W

10 AdmissionFree

Praia do Preá

Praia do Preá is a long, expansive beach located near Jericoacoara, known internationally as a premier destination for kitesurfing. The consistent and strong trade winds create ideal conditions for the sport, attracting enthusiasts of all skill levels. The beach maintains a relaxed, rustic atmosphere, with traditional fishing boats and small beachfront pousadas.

2.8130° S / 40.4122° W
