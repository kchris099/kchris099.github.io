Jericoacoara

For generations an isolated fishing hamlet hidden behind massive sand dunes, Jericoacoara operated without electricity until the late 20th century. Its designation as an environmental protection area preserved its rustic coastal identity even as it became a global kite-surfing hub. Visitors today can clearly trace the detailed layers of this unique heritage throughout the preserved streets and monuments.

Pedra Furada is a arch-shaped rock formation located on the beaches of Jericoacoara, Brazil. Jericoacoara National Park

Jericoacoara National Park protects a diverse coastal network in Ceará, encompassing massive sand dunes, pristine beaches, and clear freshwater lagoons. Established in 2002, the park preserves the unique natural environment and restricts vehicle access to maintain its ecological integrity. Visitors can explore the landscape via dune buggies, horseback, or hiking trails.
