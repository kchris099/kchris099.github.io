Brasília

Brasília was inaugurated in 1960 as a planned federal capital designed by Lúcio Costa and Oscar Niemeyer on the central plateau. Monumental axes, cathedral curves, and residential superquadras record mid-twentieth-century modernist nation-building. Pilotis ministries and lake districts spread Niemeyer's curves across the cerrado capital's planned sectors.

ParkShopping

ParkShopping opened in 1983 beside the southern approach to Brasília and grew into a large enclosed retail complex. Department stores, fashion and service shops, restaurants, cinemas, and family entertainment occupy several levels connected to parking and public transport; the mall reflects the capital's suburban commercial expansion, though it has limited relevance as a historical or cultural attraction.

Zoológico de Brasília

Zoológico de Brasília, also known as Brasilia Zoo, has been open since 1957 and is home to over 800 animals from 185 different species. The zoo covers a vast area of 139.75 hectares and offers various attractions such as the Natural Sciences Museum, a Butterfly Zoo, picnic areas, camping grounds, playgrounds, artificial lakes, and snack bars. Visitors can enjoy observing a variety of birds, monkeys, capybaras roaming freely around the zoo.

Catedral Metropolitana Nossa Senhora Aparecida

Brasilia's cathedral, designed by Oscar Niemeyer and consecrated in the early 1970s, is a circular hyperboloid of concrete ribs. Stained glass fills the gaps between the sixteen arms around a sunken nave. The structure sits between ministry blocks on the Monumental Axis as one of the capital's defining churches. Four large sculptures of the Evangelists stand outside near the entrance ramp.

Praça do Cruzeiro

A wooden cross marks Praça do Cruzeiro, an elevated site associated with the planning and construction of Brasília. The area served as an early viewpoint over the future capital and hosted an important outdoor Mass in 1957. Oscar Niemeyer later designed the circular setting around the monument; the cross standing there is a replica, while the original is kept at Brasília Cathedral.
