Explore Florianopolis

A Brief History

Florianopolis was founded by the Portuguese in 1673 as a strategic port and military outpost. In the eighteenth century, Azorean immigrants arrived, significantly shaping the island's culture, architecture, and fishing traditions. The city grew as a commercial hub and was renamed in 1894 to honor President Floriano Peixoto. Today, it is known for its historic center, diverse beaches, and high quality of life.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

Mirante do Morro da Lagoa da Conceição

Mirante do Morro da Lagoa da Conceição is a must-visit lookout point that offers a postcard-worthy view of both sides of the lagoon, the dunes of Joaquina, and beyond. In the early morning, visitors can observe fishermen heading out in their boats while waiters prepare for the day ahead. The area offers shade and sometimes even a cart selling fresh coconut water.

Free

Beiramar Shopping

Beiramar Shopping is a well-established complex located in the heart of Florianopolis, offering a wide range of retail shops, diverse dining options, and a movie theater. It is renowned for its beautiful structure and houses numerous international and national brands, ensuring a vast variety of products to cater to different tastes and styles. With 210 stores, this popular shopping destination provides convenience and practicality with covered parking facilities.

Free

Praça Getúlio Vargas

Praça Getúlio Vargas, also known as Firemen Square, is a peaceful and pleasant park in the heart of the city. It features a monument honoring the Heroes of Armed Struggle, particularly those involved in the Federalist Revolution. The square has been completely restored to its former glory and is now an ideal spot for leisurely strolls or relaxation under the shade of its many trees.

Free

Catedral Metropolitana de Florianópolis Nossa Senhora Do Desterro e Santa Catarina De Alexandria

The Catedral Metropolitana de Florianópolis Nossa Senhora Do Desterro e Santa Catarina De Alexandria is a Catholic church with a rich history dating back to the 18th century. Situated on the site of an older chapel from 1679, this cathedral boasts historic architecture, including twin towers and intricate stained-glass windows.

Free

Praça XV de Novembro

Praça XV de Novembro is a charming city oasis in Florianopolis, featuring mature trees and park benches for relaxation. The square is surrounded by significant landmarks such as the Cruz and Sousa Palace, which dates back to the 18th century. Nearby, authentic Luso-Brazilian architecture townhouses can be found on Fernando Machado street. This central location offers access to various amenities including dining, drinking, and entertainment options.

Free

Mercado Público de Florianópolis

The Mercado Público de Florianópolis is a historic public market situated in the city center. Constructed in the late nineteenth century, the vibrant market is a central hub for local commerce, offering fresh seafood, regional produce, and traditional Azorean crafts. It is also a popular social destination, featuring numerous bars and restaurants serving local delicacies.

Free

Mirante Morro das Pedras

Mirante Morro das Pedras, located in southern Florianopolis, offers a panoramic view of Armacao beach and Lagoa do Peri. Easily accessible with ample parking, it is adjacent to the Casa de Retiros Vila Fatima, a catholic retreat. Visitors can enjoy delicious local snacks like "pasteis" and sugar cane juice while taking in the scenery.

Free

Floripa Shopping

Floripa Shopping is a modern indoor mall located in a prime area with a contemporary architectural design and plenty of natural light. It offers a wide range of stores, including apparel, groceries, and accessories, as well as a diverse food court and entertainment options such as the popular Cinemark cinema. The shopping center is known for its pet-friendly policy and even provides free pet carts for visitors.

Free

Waterfall Costa da Lagoa

The Costa da Lagoa Waterfall is a scenic natural attraction located near the Conceição Lagoon in Florianópolis. Accessible primarily by boat or a scenic hiking trail, the waterfall is surrounded by dense Atlantic Forest vegetation. The site offers a refreshing swimming hole and a peaceful environment, making it a popular destination for eco-tourism and nature walks.

Free

Ponte Hercílio Luz

Ponte Hercílio Luz, a traffic bridge that was opened in 1982, serves as a significant link between the island of Santa Catarina and the mainland. This suspension bridge, completed in 1926, holds historical importance as it was the first to connect these two points. With its central span stretching around 340 meters (1,115 feet), it ranked among the world's top 100 largest bridges of its kind.

Free

Lookout Square Hercílio Luz

Lookout Square Hercílio Luz sits within the Ponte Hercílio Luz area of Florianopolis, documented in local heritage registers for its civic or cultural role. Visitors approach from the main access road and combine the visit with neighbouring heritage buildings and public spaces in the district.

Free

Parque da Luz

Parque da Luz is a delightful open space that offers a refreshing escape filled with lush trees and ample opportunities for outdoor fun, making it an ideal spot for families and their furry friends. This vibrant park features playgrounds, sports fields, and beautifully landscaped gardens. Visitors can enjoy various activities such as soccer games, leisurely strolls along the pedestrian paths or cycling on designated lanes.

Free

Trapiche Beira Mar Norte Avenue

Trapiche Beira Mar Norte Avenue sits within the Ponte Hercílio Luz area of Florianopolis, documented in local heritage registers for its civic or cultural role. Visitors approach from the main access road and combine the visit with neighbouring heritage buildings and public spaces in the district.

R$80

Ademir Rosa Theater

The city boasts two prominent theaters, each offering a unique experience. The Teatro Alvaro de Carvalho, near the Catedral Metropolitana, has a rich history dating back to the 1870s and features a spacious concert hall accommodating 470 people. On the other hand, the more modern and larger Ademir Rosa Theater is situated near the coastline on Avenida Governador Irineu Bornhausen.

Free

Caminho da Costa da Lagoa ao Canto dos Araçás

The "Caminho da Costa da Lagoa ao Canto dos Araçás" offers an enchanting two-hour hike that immerses you in the lush beauty of nature. Starting from Rua Joao Henrique Goncalves, this trail winds along the picturesque lagoon and leads you through charming fishing villages that are only reachable by foot or boat. Along the way, encounter historical gems like the Sobrado da Dona Loquinha, a building with a poignant past dating back to 1780.

R$16

Fortaleza de Santo Antonio de Ratones

Nestled in the landscape of Santa Catarina, the Fortaleza de Santo Antonio de Ratones stands as a remarkable historical site that dates back to the early 18th century. Originally built for military purposes and later used as a quarantine station, this elevated fortress offers views of the surrounding area. Visitors can explore its impressive features, including two batteries of cannons, a drawbridge, and an aqueduct that evoke a sense of medieval charm.

Free

Praia Barra da Lagoa

Praia Barra da Lagoa is a picturesque beach located on the east side of Florianopolis, offering calm waters that make it ideal for families. The area is known for its landscapes and offers a two-hour hike to the mystic rocks, where visitors can enjoy unique views of Lagoa da Conceicao district and neighboring beaches.

Free

Small beach of Barra

Small beach of Barra is a documented market or commercial centre in Florianopolis, Brazil, listed in local civic guides for its role in regional trade and daily commerce. The site operates during standard market hours and is open to visitors without charge for browsing. Travellers enter from the surrounding streets and combine the visit with nearby food vendors and craft stalls.

Free

Joaquina Beach

Joaquina Beach is a popular spot for local surfers and offers a picturesque setting with white sand and food vendors. It's known for its perfect waves that attract surfers, as well as its high dunes at the back of the beach where visitors can try sandboarding. The area also features other beaches catering to different preferences, such as Campeche Beach, which is ideal for a dream surf vacation and offers views of Campeche Island.

Free

Praia da Joaquina

Praia da Joaquina is one of the most famous beaches in Florianópolis, renowned for its excellent surfing conditions and expansive sand dunes. The beach has hosted numerous international surfing competitions and attracts water sports enthusiasts from around the world. The adjacent dunes are popular for sandboarding, providing a unique recreational experience on the island.

Free

Trilha Praia do Gravatá

Trilha Praia do Gravatá is a beloved hiking trail in Florianopolis, offering a relatively short and easy 1.5-2 hour trek with views throughout. The trail leads to a beautiful private beach nestled between Praia Mole and Praia da Joaquina, known for otter-spotting and paragliding. Covered by spikey gravata plants, the trail winds through the Atlantic Rainforest, featuring some steep sections and rough terrain.

Free (sandboard R$30 extra)

Dunas da Joaquina

Dunas da Joaquina is a must-visit destination for adventure seekers and nature enthusiasts. Visitors can enjoy the thrill of sandboarding on the impressive dunes, offering a unique and exciting experience. For those who prefer leisurely activities, taking a long walk across the sandy landscape to reach the beach is an option.

Temporarily closed

Morro do Lampião

Morro Lampião offers a short but steep 20-minute trail that leads to panoramic views of Florianopolis. The hike requires intermediate physical condition due to the steep incline and rock climbing at the top, but no equipment is necessary. The trail may get muddy after rain, so it's to visit when it's dry. Once at the top, hikers can enjoy a 360-degree view from Pantano do Sul to Barra da Lagoa.

R$15 (transport extra)

Campeche

Campeche is a destination known for its laid-back surf culture, vibrant bar scene, and a protected stretch of sand that offers views of Campeche Island. The beach boasts 5 kilometers of pristine white sand and crystal-clear waters with emerald hues. Over the years, it has seen significant development with the addition of fine homes and buildings, making it sought-after beaches in Floriano.

Free

Church of Our Lady of Needs

The Church of Our Lady of Needs is a beautiful and welcoming church located in the Santo Antonio neighborhood of Florianopolis, Brazil. The area is known for its abundant natural beauty, offering a variety of beaches with strong waves, calm waters, and natural pools. Praia da Joaquina is popular beaches on the island, attracting surfers with its strong winds.

Free

Mirante do Morro da Cruz

Mirante do Morro da Cruz, also known as Morro da Cruz Viewpoint, offers panoramic views of downtown Florianópolis and the surrounding bay. Perched atop a hill of the same name, this lookout provides an awe-inspiring vantage point. While the journey up the narrow and steep road may be challenging, it adds to the overall experience.

Free

Av.beira Mar Continental Florianopolis

Avenida Beira Mar Continental is a scenic waterfront avenue located on the mainland side of Florianópolis. The promenade offers expansive views of the island, the iconic Hercílio Luz Bridge, and the surrounding bays. It features wide walking and cycling paths, making it a popular destination for outdoor recreation, exercise, and enjoying the coastal scenery.

Free

UFSC

UFSC, also known as the state university along with UDESC, boasts a large and beautiful campus. While the infrastructure may not be perfect, it offers a serene environment surrounded by trees. Finding parking can be challenging, so plan accordingly. The campus features a library for studying and relaxation, a bookstore for essentials, and affordable dining options at the RU.

Free

Trilha do Poção Córrego Grande

Trilha do Poção Córrego Grande is a hidden gem nestled in the lush greenery of Corrego Grande, just a short drive from the bustling city center of Florianopolis. This charming trail leads adventurers to waterfalls and natural swimming pools, making it an ideal escape during Brazil's hot summer months.

Free

Parque de Coqueiros

Parque de Coqueiros is a bayfront park located at the entrance of the Coqueiros neighborhood, offering various recreational activities in its approximately 50,000 m2 area. Visitors can enjoy walking and biking tracks, soccer courts, volleyball court, gym facilities, playgrounds, and a lake. The park is free to access and provides parking. Additionally, the surrounding neighborhood of Coqueiros has become known for its culinary scene with lively bars and dining options.
