Destination Guide — North & Amazon

Explore Manaus

A Brief History

Manaus was established in 1669 as the Fort of Sao Jose do Rio Negro to secure Portuguese control over the Amazon basin. It experienced a massive economic boom during the late nineteenth-century rubber trade, resulting in opulent European-style architecture, including the famous Amazon Theatre. Following the collapse of the rubber market, the city declined until a free trade zone was established in 1967, revitalizing Manaus as a major industrial and ecological hub.

Top Sights & Activities

R$20

Amazonas Theater

The Amazon Theatre, a renowned Renaissance theater constructed during Manaus's rubber boom in 1896, is a landmark influenced by European design and materials. The opulent venue hosts various events, including an opera festival in April and May. Visitors can take guided tours to admire the theater's grand construction. Situated at Largo de Sao Sebastiao, it is surrounded by attractions like the central church and a lively square for a complete experience of Manaus's charm.

Free

Largo de São Sebastião

Largo de São Sebastião, also known as São Sebastião Square, is a major plaza in Manaus that boasts a mix of European and Middle-Eastern architectural styles. The square is surrounded by historical buildings and trees, with a grand monument at its center. It has become a focal point for visitors, offering pavement bars and cafes, as well as hosting live musical and cultural events.

Free

Paróquia de São Sebastião

Nestled in the vibrant heart of Manaus, the Paróquia de São Sebastião is a captivating gem that beautifully marries history and artistry. Celebrating its centennial with a renovation, this church showcases an exquisite interior adorned with European-style stained glass windows and dramatic altar paintings that depict saints in heroic battles. While it may not be the largest or oldest church in the city, its beauty makes it a must-visit attraction.

Free

Praça da Saudade

Praça da Saudade is a garden square located in downtown Manaos, built on a former burial ground. It features a memorial to poet Tenreiro Aranha and a charming pergola. The park is popular among the city's youth, especially at night when pubs and informal snack shops are open. It also serves as a meeting place for LGBT and other alternative groups.

Free

Parque Senador Jefferson Péres

Parque Senador Jefferson Péres is a well-maintained urban park located in the heart of the city. It features lush greenery, walking and biking tracks, and a casual laid-back ambiance. The park offers a quiet and beautiful setting for morning or afternoon walks, with plenty of benches and exercise equipment available. Despite being close to the busy city center, it provides a relaxing atmosphere away from the noise.

R$50

Museu da Amazônia - MUSA

The Museu da Amazônia (MUSA) is an expansive open-air museum and botanical garden located within the Adolpho Ducke Forest Reserve in Manaus. It offers visitors an immersive experience in the Amazon rainforest, featuring nature trails, a butterfly house, and a 42-meter observation tower that provides panoramic views of the forest canopy. The museum focuses on environmental education and research.

R$30

Arena da Amazônia

Arena da Amazônia, situated in the heart of Manaus, was a key venue for the 2014 World Cup in Brazil. Originally built in 1970, it underwent a transformation to become a modern stadium with sustainable features like rainwater harvesting and solar energy utilization. Despite concerns about its long-term viability due to the local club's modest attendance, the arena can accommodate over 40,000 fans.

Free

Sambódromo de Manaus

The Sambódromo de Manaus is a vibrant hub of celebration and culture, especially known for its lively festivals throughout the year. If you're planning a visit, consider timing your trip to coincide with the annual Jazz Festival in July or the Boi Manaus festival in October, which coincides with the city's birthday on October 24. These events feature an array of performances from local artists and tribal singers that truly showcase the region's rich musical heritage.

R$20

Zoológico Do CIGS 🐯

If you're exploring Manaus, a visit to the Zoológico Do CIGS is an must! Nestled within the Centro de Instrução de Guerra na Selva, this charming zoo showcases over 200 species of local wildlife, including the majestic jaguar and elusive black panther. The atmosphere is delightful, with well-maintained habitats that reflect the beauty of the Amazon rainforest. Managed by the military, this small yet captivating zoo offers a unique glimpse into Amazonian fauna.

Free (access fee R$8 for pedestrians)

Port of Manaus

The Port of Manaus, situated on the Amazon River, is a bustling harbor filled with cargo and cruise ships, as well as vibrant vendors and food stalls. It is strategically located between Sao Vicente beach and the Adolpho Lisboa Municipal Market, providing convenient access to customs facilities, warehouses, and container ramps. This important port serves the northern region of Brazil, covering almost the entire state of Amazonas along with parts of Roraima and Rondonia.

Free

Catedral Metropolitana Nossa Senhora da Conceição

Catedral Nossa Senhora da Conceição, also known as the Cathedral of Lady, is a historic church originally constructed in 1695 and later rebuilt in 1878 following a fire. Unlike many European cathedrals, it boasts a simple design with minimal major artwork. The interior is adorned with vibrant stained glass windows that create a colorful and luminous atmosphere.

Free

Museu da Cidade de Manaus - MUMA (Paço da Liberdade)

The Museu da Cidade de Manaus, housed in the historic Paço da Liberdade, traces the cultural and historical evolution of the city. The museum utilizes interactive and multimedia exhibits to explore the indigenous heritage, the rubber boom era, and the diverse communities that shaped Manaus. The building itself is a significant example of nineteenth-century neoclassical architecture.

Free

Mercado Municipal Adolpho Lisboa

When exploring Manaus, a visit to the Mercado Municipal Adolpho Lisboa is an must. This vibrant market, inspired by the original Les Halles in Paris, serves as a lively gathering spot for both locals and tourists. Here, indulge in unique Amazonian delicacies while browsing through an array of fresh produce, exotic meats, fish, tropical fruits, and colorful handicrafts that make perfect souvenirs.

Free

Ponta Negra Beach

Ponta Negra Beach is a picturesque coastal destination with a wide sandy beach, sunset views, and a variety of parks and eateries. Visitors can explore the city through guided tours that include the charming Ponta Negra boardwalk, the bustling city market offering regional products, and the Teatro Amazonas. The beach is popular among sightseers for swimming and immersing in local culture.
