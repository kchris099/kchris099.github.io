Ouro Preto

Ouro Preto boomed after gold discoveries in the 1690s Minas Gerais rush. Hillside churches by Aleijadinho, mine owner mansions, and cobblestone squares record baroque patronage funded by colonial Brazil's richest gold district. Mine tours and Aleijadinho church trails extend baroque hill towns across Minas Gerais gold country. Walking routes through the historic centre help visitors relate these monuments to wider patterns of settlement and civic life in Brazil.

Basílica Nossa Senhora do Pilar

Basilica Nossa Senhora do Pilar in Ouro Preto is a major colonial Catholic church with a richly gilded interior. Soapstone and gold leaf reflect the mining wealth that funded Minas Gerais baroque. The church remains a parish focus on the steep streets of the historic center. Heavy gold leaf on the altarpieces reflects candlelight during evening Mass.

Church of Santa Efigenia

The Church of Santa Efigênia crowns a hill above Ouro Preto, an eighteenth-century parish with a churchyard looking over the colonial roofs below. Local tradition credits enslaved builders, including the figure later called Chico-Rei, with raising the church. Gilded altarpieces and painted ceilings reward a climb from the lower streets.

Mina Du Veloso

Mina Du Veloso is a former gold mine featuring a 400-meter underground chamber supported by pillars. Visitors are guided through the mine to learn about Brazil's gold mining history, the techniques used by enslaved Africans, and the miners' daily lives and customs. Tours are available in Portuguese and English. The experience offers an insightful look into the reality of working in an old gold mine, with guides providing historical and geological information.

Praça Gomes Freire

Praça Gomes Freire opens a flatter civic space among Ouro Preto's hillside streets. Trees, benches, and surrounding low buildings create a pause between steeper climbs to churches. Residents use the square for everyday meetings away from the densest tourist lanes. Surrounding low buildings keep the plaza open to sky despite the hills.

Pelourinho - Mariana MG

The stone pillory in Mariana's historic center recalls the judicial authority exercised in colonial mining towns. Set near major civic and religious buildings, it forms part of a square shaped by churches, former government institutions and eighteenth-century houses. Its presence makes that violence visible in the civic center.

Igreja de Nossa Senhora do Rosário

Igreja de Nossa Senhora do Rosario in Ouro Preto is a colonial church long associated with Black Catholic brotherhoods. Curved facade lines and hillside siting distinguish it among the town's baroque churches. Interior altars and feast-day processions keep the parish active beyond sightseeing hours. Curved facade lines catch afternoon light on the climb toward the church.

Church of Saint Francis of Assisi

The Church of Saint Francis of Assisi is an 18th-century Roman Catholic church known for its design and lavishly painted interior. Constructed in 1768 by the celebrated Baroque artist Aleijadinho, it is one of Ouro Preto's most architectural landmarks.

Tiradentes' Statue

A bronze statue of Joaquim José da Silva Xavier, known as Tiradentes, stands at the center of Ouro Preto's principal square. The monument faces institutions linked to colonial government and the Inconfidência Mineira, including the former town hall and prison; its elevated position turns the executed independence conspirator into the organizing figure of the surrounding civic landscape.

Igreja de Nossa Senhora do Carmo

Igreja de Nossa Senhora do Carmo, completed around 1776, includes late work linked to sculptor Aleijadinho. Soapstone ornament and a hillside facade face one of Ouro Preto's upper streets. The Carmelite church ranks among the town's essential colonial religious buildings. Soapstone pulpits and altar details repay a slow walk through the nave.

Steep streets climb Morro Sao Sebastiao above Ouro Preto for views over Tiradentes Square and the Antonio Dias neighborhood. The ascent rewards walkers willing to take the grade through the hillside. Panoramas of baroque rooftops are the lookout's clear payoff.

Ponto 5 - Casa Aleijadinho

A modest historic house at Praca Antonio Dias is promoted locally as Casa Aleijadinho, close to streets and churches associated with Ouro Preto's baroque craft tradition. The property forms part of a neighborhood shaped by workshops, religious commissions and colonial building trades. Its small scale offers a domestic counterpoint to the city's richly decorated public monuments.

Mina Chico Rei

Narrow damp passages at Mina Chico Rei show how colonial gold ore moved through Ouro Preto's hillside, with guides explaining enslaved labor behind Minas church wealth. Low ceilings and old workings keep the tour underground. The mine ties mineral extraction to the town's sacred skyline.
