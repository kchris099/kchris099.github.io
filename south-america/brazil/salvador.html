Explore Salvador

A Brief History

Salvador was Brazil's first colonial capital from 1549, channelling sugar wealth through Pelourinho's baroque churches and port warehouses. African diaspora culture in Candomblé and cuisine records how the Bahian coast shaped Atlantic slavery and Brazilian identity. Elevador Lacerda viewpoints and candomblé terreiros extend Pelourinho heritage above Todos os Santos Bay.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

Fonte Nova Arena

Itaipava Fonte Nova Arena, originally built for the Confederations Cup in 2013, is now the home ground for Bahia and a venue for concerts. The stadium, with a capacity of 50,000 people, was completely rebuilt by German architects and hosted key matches in the 2014 FIFA World Cup. It offers guided tours that provide insights into its history and architecture.

Free

Monumento as Meninas do Brasil (Gordinhas)

The tourist attraction Monumento As Meninas do Brasil, also known as Gordinhas, consists of three sculptures created by Eliana Kertez in Bahia, Brazil. These artworks symbolize the different races present in the Brazilian population: the black, white, and indigenous peoples. Situated in Ondina neighborhood on Adhemar de Barros avenue 19, this intriguing sculpture collection showcases the diverse cultural heritage of Brazil.

R$1

Elevador Lacerda

The Elevador Lacerda is a historic art deco elevator that provides scenic views while connecting the upper and lower parts of Salvador. It offers a convenient and affordable way to explore the old city center, with taxis, buses, and foot travel also being viable options. This elevator was erected in the mid to late 1800s and showcases impressive Art Deco design from additional work done in the early 20th century.

Free

Mercado Modelo

Mercado Modelo is a historic public market located in the Comércio district of Salvador, near the Lacerda Elevator. Housed in a neoclassical building from 1912, it is a vibrant center for Bahian culture, offering a vast array of traditional handicrafts, local art, and souvenirs. The market is also known for its lively atmosphere, often featuring spontaneous capoeira performances.

Free

Fallen Cross Monument

The Fallen Cross Monument is a stainless steel sculpture honoring a 1500s cathedral that was demolished to make way for train tracks, which have since been removed. It offers a great vantage point to watch the sunset and overlooks the lower town with panoramic views of the port, Lacerda elevator, and Mercado. The monument marks the beginning of the historic city center and features a huge cross that makes a bold statement.

Free

Cathedral of Salvador

The Cathedral of Salvador, also known as the Cathedral Basilica of Salvador, is a Mannerist-style cathedral built in the 17th century. Situated on the Terreiro de Jesus next to Praca da Se, it was originally part of a Jesuit monastic and educational complex. The facade is adorned with stone brought from Portugal, while the interior boasts beautiful Baroque decorations dating back to the 18th century.

Free

Basílica do Senhor do Bonfim

The Basílica do Senhor do Bonfim, an impressive 18th-century church situated on Sacred Hill, is a significant stop on Bahia's religious tourism route. Each year, the renowned Lavagem do Bonfim ceremony unites Catholics and Candomblé practitioners in devotion. The neoclassical interior showcases remarkable works of art such as Francisco Velasco's ceiling panel and Jose Rufino Capinam's paintings at the entrance.

Free

Forte de Nossa Senhora de Monte Serrat

Forte de Nossa Senhora de Monte Serrat is a 16th-century hexagon-shaped fortress with an Italian-inspired design that offers views of Salvador's port. It features a large lawn and overlooks the picturesque Monte Serrat Beach, where visitors can witness sunsets against the backdrop of the Bay of All Saints. The site also includes the Church and Monastery of Lady of Mount Cerra, a notable collection of colonial architecture from the 17th century.

Free

Ponta do Humaitá

Ponta do Humaitá is quickly becoming a must-visit destination in Salvador, renowned for its sunset views and welcoming vibe. Nestled on the Itapagipe peninsula, this picturesque spot offers panoramas of the Baia de Todos os Santos. Visitors can explore charming 19th-century architecture, including a historic church adorned with beautiful tiles from the 17th century, alongside a quaint convent and an old lighthouse that adds to its charm.

Temporarily closed

Church and Convent of San Francisco

The Church and Convent of San Francisco, also known as the Golden Church, is an 18th-century Catholic church located in Salvador. Its exterior features intricate carvings, while the interior is covered in pure gold leaf, making it a example of Portuguese Baroque architecture in the Americas. Legend has it that builders used a thousand kilos of gold powder for its construction.

Free

Morro do Cristo

Morro do Cristo, located in the Barra neighborhood of Salvador, is a grassy knoll by the ocean featuring a prominent statue of Jesus Christ. The area offers sweeping views of Baia de Todos os Santos and the Farol da Barra lighthouse. Visitors can enjoy a leisurely walk along Farol da Barra beach to the lighthouse and explore the Bahia Nautica Museum inside.

R$20

Lighthouse Barra's beach

Lighthouse Barra's beach is a lively coastal stretch perfect for swimming and sunbathing, featuring a paved promenade lined with bars and restaurants. The area also boasts colonial secular architecture, such as the Barra lighthouse at the Atlantic tip of the peninsula and several 17th-century forts. Visitors can explore notable institutions like the Federal University of Bahia and the Catholic University of Salvador, along with various museums showcasing sacred art.

Free

Church of Our Lady of the Rosary of the Black People

The Church of Our Lady of the Rosary of the Black People (Igreja de Nossa Senhora do Rosário dos Pretos) is a historic Catholic church located in the Pelourinho district of Salvador. Built in the eighteenth century by enslaved and freed Africans, the church blends traditional Catholic liturgy with African cultural elements, serving as a profound symbol of Afro-Brazilian heritage and resilience.

R$20

Foundation Casa de Jorge Amado

The Foundation Casa de Jorge Amado is a cultural institution located in the historic Pelourinho district of Salvador. It is dedicated to preserving the legacy and works of Jorge Amado, one of Brazil's most celebrated modernist writers. The museum houses extensive archives, manuscripts, and personal artifacts, offering insights into his literature and his deep connection to Bahian culture.

R$20

Pierre Verger Space of Bahia Photography - Santa Maria Fort

Pierre Verger Space of Bahia Photography - Santa Maria Fort is a historic 17th-century fort located next to Porto da Barra beach. The fort offers great views and features interesting exhibitions, making it an atmospheric spot for a cultural walk. Visitors can also enjoy sunset views from this location, adding to the overall experience of exploring this site.

Free

Porto da Barra Beach

Porto da Barra Beach is a picturesque gem nestled in Salvador, known for its tranquil waters that beckon both swimmers and sunbathers alike. This beach not only offers a perfect spot to soak up the sun but also serves as a historical landmark, being the site of Bahia's original European settlement. The surrounding neighborhood buzzes with life, attracting both locals and visitors who come to enjoy refreshing drinks while watching sunsets over the bay.

R$20

Carybé Arts Space - São Diogo Fort

The Carybé Arts Space is a cultural center located within the historic São Diogo Fort in Salvador. It is dedicated to the works of Hector Julio Páride Bernabó, known as Carybé, a prominent artist who captured the essence of Bahian culture. The space features multimedia exhibitions and offers impressive views of the Bay of All Saints from its fortified coastal position.

Free

Teatro Castro Alves

Teatro Castro Alves is a renowned performing arts venue in Salvador, home to the Bahian Symphony Orchestra and the Bale de Castro Alves. Despite facing a major fire before its inauguration in 1958, it finally opened to the public in 1967. The theater boasts excellent audio-visual technology and features original artwork based on Castro Alves's famous poem about the horrors of the slave trade.

Free

Square Two July

Square Two July (Praça Dois de Julho), also known as Campo Grande, is a significant public square in Salvador commemorating the independence of Bahia from Portuguese rule on July 2, 1823. The square features a towering monument dedicated to the heroes of the independence struggle. It is a central gathering place and the traditional starting point for the annual Independence Day parade.

Free

Bahia Marina Pier

Nestled along Brazil's northeastern coast, Bahia Marina Pier is a gateway to the city of Salvador, often celebrated as the birthplace of modern Brazil. This UNESCO World Heritage site features a unique charm with its colorful colonial architecture and lively streets filled with Afro-Brazilian culture. The local saying, "Sem pressa, olha para o ceu, fala com Deus, voce ta na Bahia," captures the laid-back spirit that permeates this region.

Free

Feira de São Joaquim

During my week-long adventure in Salvador, one of the standout experiences was visiting the Feira de São Joaquim. This market is a treasure trove of sights and sounds, showcasing an eclectic mix of fresh fruits, meats, and unique items like medicinal herbs and infused cachaças. The atmosphere is lively yet rustic, with half-renovated structures adding to its charm.

Free

Teatro Sesc Casa do Comércio

Teatro Sesc Casa do Comércio is a cultural hub located in Salvador, offering an array of artistic experiences. This state-run center features a cinema, theatre, and restaurant, making it a perfect spot for both entertainment and dining. The architectural design is strikingly modern, characterized by its bold red and black interlocking sections that create a unique visual appeal.

Free

Teatro Jorge Amado

Teatro Jorge Amado is a cultural center located in Salvador, Bahia, Brazil. It has a capacity of 1,300 spectators with seating distributed across the audience, cabins, friezes and balcony. The theater features an Italian stage with movable rods and a walkway over 10m. There are 418 seats on two levels: ground floor and balcony. The foyer hosts visual art exhibitions by Calasans Neto.
