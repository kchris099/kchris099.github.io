Paraty

Gold from Minas Gerais left Brazil through Paraty's harbour in the colonial period. When railways bypassed the town, its cobbled streets and whitewashed churches escaped nineteenth-century rebuilds. That accidental survival, not imperial patronage, made Paraty a coastal heritage centre on the Costa Verde. Carnival and literary festivals later reused the same colonial streets without widening them.

Paraty

Paraty is a beautifully preserved colonial town located on the Costa Verde of Rio de Janeiro state. Established in the sixteenth century, it served as a major port for the export of gold from Minas Gerais. The town is known for its cobblestone streets, whitewashed buildings with colorful borders, and its backdrop of lush Atlantic Forest mountains and pristine coastal islands.
