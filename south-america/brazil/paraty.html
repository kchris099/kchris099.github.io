Explore Paraty

A Brief History

Established in the 16th century, Paraty flourished as the primary colonial port for exporting gold from Minas Gerais to Portugal. Bypassed by later railways, its pristine cobblestone streets and baroque architecture were preserved, cementing its status as a historical jewel. This rich historical tapestry established a strong foundation that continues to shape the city's dynamic contemporary identity.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

Cachoeira da Pedra Branca

Located in Paraty, Rio de Janeiro, Brazil, Cachoeira da Pedra Branca is a waterfall consisting of two natural cascades that crash into a large pool surrounded by rocks. Accessible via the road to Fazenda Murycana, it's a popular spot for swimming and relaxation. Visitors can enjoy the refreshing cool waters and even climb the stone wall for more adventurous activities.

Free

Cachoeira do Tobogã

Cachoeira do Tobogã, located a short drive away from Paraty, is a unique waterfall where the water flows over a stone to form a natural slide and pool. To reach this attraction, visitors can take a bus from Rodoviaria de Paraty to the neighborhood of Penha. The highlight of the place is the surfing stone that adds an element of entertainment for both locals and tourists.

Free

Trinity Beach Paraty

Trinity Beach Paraty is a documented coastal feature in Paraty, Brazil, recognised in regional geographic listings for its natural formation and public access. The shoreline is open to visitors throughout the year and connects to adjacent beachfront districts. Travellers approach from the coastal road or boardwalk and combine the visit with nearby harbour areas and seaside markets.

Free

Cepilho beach

Cepilho Beach, also known as Praia de Fora or Praia dos Ranchos, is a secluded spot with calm waters, making it perfect for a relaxing family day out. It's located near Trindade village and can be reached by an easily accessible trail. The beach offers beautiful sands and is ideal for body surfing or swimming in slightly rougher waters. While the town is dedicated to beaches, it can get crowded during summer and weekends.

Free

Pedra que Engole

Located in the protected reserve of Cairucu, Pedra que Engole offers an adventurous experience for visitors. This natural rock formation features a hole through which bathers can slide into a pool on the other side. The trail to reach this hidden gem is indicated by signs and takes you through a tropical forest along a river. The experience includes sliding through the water and enjoying a natural pool nestled between rocks.

Free

Lula Beach

Lula Beach, also known as Praia da Lulu or Beach of the Squid, is a secluded gem located on a small uninhabited island near Paraty. Accessible only by sea, this beautiful sandy beach boasts crystal clear waters and pristine white sands. The surrounding ocean is warm and inviting, perfect for swimming and relaxation. Visitors can enjoy the tranquil atmosphere and natural beauty of the inland forest that extends to the edge of the shore.

Free

Paraty

Paraty is a beautifully preserved colonial town located on the Costa Verde of Rio de Janeiro state. Established in the sixteenth century, it served as a major port for the export of gold from Minas Gerais. The town is renowned for its cobblestone streets, whitewashed buildings with colorful borders, and its backdrop of lush Atlantic Forest mountains and pristine coastal islands.

Free

Igreja de Nossa Senhora do Rosário e São Benedito

Located in Paraty, Rio de Janeiro, Brazil, the Igreja de Nossa Senhora do Rosário e São Benedito is a historical church with a rustic and simple architectural style. Built by African slaves in the early 18th century for their own use, it features gilded wooden altars and a grand chandelier inside. The church hosts lively celebrations of Sao Benedito at the beginning of December each year.

Free

Centro Histórico de Paraty

The Centro Histórico of Paraty is a very intact colonial district, characterized by its pedestrian-only cobblestone streets and distinctive Portuguese architecture. The area is prone to tidal flooding, a deliberate design feature intended to clean the streets. It is home to numerous historic churches, artisan shops, and cultural festivals, including the renowned FLIP literary festival.

Free

Praia do Pontal

Praia do Pontal is a bustling tree-lined stretch of coastline with numerous food and drink vendors. It's the closest beach to the historical center of Paraty, offering a laid-back atmosphere where visitors can relax with a cold beer or traditional Brazilian caipirinha from beach bars. The beach also provides opportunities for sea kayaking, paddle-boarding, and boat rentals.
