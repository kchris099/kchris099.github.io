Explore Belo Horizonte

A Brief History

Inaugurated in 1897 as Brazil's first planned modern city, Belo Horizonte was designed to replace the colonial capital of Ouro Preto. Its orderly grid and later modernist architecture reflect a deliberate push toward industrialization and progress in the republican era. The survival of these cultural and architectural milestones provides a powerful window into the region's storied past. Museums, markets, and street plans in Belo Horizonte still show layers from those periods.

Top Sights & Activities

Mercado central

Located behind the striking exterior of Mercado Central in Belo Horizonte lies a active maze of food and drink stalls that beckon to be explored. This busy market is a treasure trove of flavors, showing an array of products from across Brazil, but it's the local delicacies from Minas Gerais that truly steal the spotlight.

Free

Minas Shopping

Minas Shopping is a large and spacious mall with an extensive range of retail stores, a food court, cafes, and a movie theater. It is conveniently located near the Minas Shopping Station, making it easily accessible for visitors staying at the Ouro Minas Palace Hotel.

Free

Diamond Mall

Diamond Mall is a visitor site in Belo Horizonte, Brazil. The upscale enclosed retail shopping plaza known for its high quality shops is located in one of the sophisticated districts of Belo Horizonte. There is also a food court and a movie theater. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Free

Shopping Del Rey

Shopping Del Rey is a busy shopping center that offers a wide array of stores and dining options. With its diverse range of shops, extensive food court, and cinema, it provides an ideal destination for both shoppers and tourists alike, the mall stands out with its unique bowling court, which sets it apart from other malls in the area.

Free

Mercado Central de Belo Horizonte

The Mercado Central is a active and historic indoor market in Belo Horizonte. Since 1929, it has been a busy hub offering a vast array of regional products, including artisanal cheeses, cachaça, fresh produce, and traditional handicrafts. The market is also a popular social destination, featuring numerous small bars and eateries serving classic Minas Gerais cuisine.

Free

Shopping Cidade

Shopping mall Cidade is located in the capital city of Minas Gerais, Brazil. The mall has four different levels that house hundreds of stores from all different retail categories, such as clothing, beauty and cosmetics, electronics, pharmacies, restaurants and other lifestyle choices. In addition to cultural events like dance shows and musical performances, the mall also hosts workshops and training sessions for residents.

Free

Santuário São José

The shrine of Santuário São José is a church located in Belo Horizonte. It is open to everyone and its biblical murals are worth seeing. The inside of the church is decorated with angels and other holy images, making St. Joseph stand out prominently. This place is a oasis in the middle of the busy city and it can be recommended for people who want to rest, pray and admire some artwork.

Free

Praça Sete de Setembro

Praça Sete de Setembro is a busy public square located in the center of Belo Horizonte. It is marked by a prominent obelisk, known locally as the 'Pirulito,' which commemorates the centenary of Brazilian independence. The square is a major intersection for the city's main avenues and is a popular gathering place for cultural events and political demonstrations.

Free

Mercado Novo

Mercado Novo is a visitor site in Belo Horizonte, Brazil. Records associated with the site include 1962. In 1962, Belo Horizonte introduced Mercado Novo as a modern central market to reflect the city's contemporary vibe and cater to its growing educated population. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Free

Igreja Batista da Lagoinha - Matriz

Igreja Batista da Lagoinha, also known as the Baptist Church of the Pond, may appear massive in online visuals, but it is actually relatively small with three circular floors. Visitors often express a deep connection to the presence of Jesus Christ within its walls and describe it as an incredible place to seek God's voice and worship. The church has garnered high praise for its spiritual atmosphere and is regarded by those who have experienced it firsthand.

Free

Mirante do Mangabeiras

Mirante do Mangabeiras, located in Belo Horizonte, is a popular observation point that offers panoramic views of the city and its surroundings. Initially established in 1976, it has evolved into a significant tourist attraction over the years. Accessible by both driving and hiking, this elevated site provides an ideal vantage point for capturing sunset vistas and urban landscapes.

Free

Praça Governador Israel Pinheiro (Praça do Papa)

Praça do Papa, officially named Praça Governador Israel Pinheiro, is a scenic public square situated in the Mangabeiras neighborhood of Belo Horizonte. It gained its popular name after Pope John Paul II held a mass there in 1980. The elevated location provides sweeping panoramic views of the city skyline and the surrounding Curral del Rey mountain range.

Free

Juscelino Kubitschek Municipal Park

Juscelino Kubitschek Municipal Park, formerly known as Parque do Acaba Mundo, is a popular urban park established in 1990 and officially named in 1995. Spanning approximately 28,000 square meters, the park offers various amenities such as walking paths, playgrounds, outdoor gym equipment, seating areas with benches and tables, and a fenced and lit sports court for football.

R$30

Arena Independência

Arena Independência is a visitor site in Belo Horizonte. Casa do Baile, a circular building designed by architects Oscar Niemeyer and Roberto Burle Marx, is located on a man-made island in Pampulha Lagoon. Originally a dance hall built in 1943, it was renovated and reopened in 2002 to house various art exhibits and host multimedia events. Visitors can also enjoy the popular cafe/restaurant with waterfront views.

Free

Pátio Savassi

Pátio Savassi is a visitor site in Belo Horizonte, Brazil. Pátio Savassi is a high-end shopping complex located in the Funcionários neighborhood, over 200 stores spread across three levels. It offers a diverse range of shops, including fashion outlets, pharmacies, and even a supermarket, providing visitors with full solutions for their everyday needs. The main structures and grounds are the principal points of interest.

Free

Savassi Square

Savassi Square is the Belo Horizonte and a popular tourist attraction due to its active city square surrounded by restaurants, bars, and shops. The square is close to some of the city's top attractions such as the Bank of Brazil Cultural Center, Parque Municipal Americo Renne Giannetti, and the Christ the Redeemer statue.

Free

Praça da Liberdade

Praça da Liberdade, also known as Liberty Square, is a active cultural hub and popular green space in Belo Horizonte. It is surrounded by museums, historic buildings, and government offices that have been converted into the Praça da Liberdade Cultural Complex. This complex houses nine museums and galleries including the Metal Museum, Memorial Minas Gerais Vale, Mines Museum, Bank of Brazil Cultural Centre, and the Niemeyer Building.

Free

Memorial Minas Gerais Vale

Located in Belo Horizonte, Memorial Minas Gerais Vale is an innovative museum that offers interactive exhibits showing the rich mining history and culture of the state of Minas Gerais. Housed in a grand building at Liberty Square Circuit, the museum presents a full journey through the cultural richness of Minas Gerais from the 18th century to contemporary times.

Free

São Gonçalo parish

São Gonçalo parish is a visitor site in Belo Horizonte, Brazil. Parque Sarandi is a active hub for both sports enthusiasts and families alike. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest. São Gonçalo parish remains part of the public visitor circuit in Belo Horizonte.

R$25

Cineart Shopping Contagem

Cineart Shopping Contagem is a haven for film enthusiasts, showing the latest blockbusters alongside Brazilian cinema. This contemporary cinema complex has plush seating and modern sound systems, creating an immersive viewing experience that keeps visitors engaged from start to finish. With refreshing cold drinks and warm popcorn available, it's the perfect spot to unwind and enjoy visitor favorite films in style!

R$40

Estádio Governador Magalhães Pinto

Mineirão Stadium, situated in Belo Horizonte, Brazil, is a for sports enthusiasts and football aficionados. This colossal stadium was constructed in 1965 and has become one of the country's largest sporting venues. With a seating capacity exceeding 60,000 spectators, it is the home ground for known football clubs Atletico Mineiro and Cruzeiro Esporte Clube.

R$5

Parque Guanabara

Parque Guanabara is a visitor site in Belo Horizonte, Brazil. It's a great spot to spend half a day, offering more thrilling rides than typical fairs. However, some visitors have noted that certain major attractions were out of order during their visit. Despite this, the park provides entertainment for the whole family with diverse ride options. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Free

Casa do Baile

Casa do Baile is a visitor site in Belo Horizonte, Brazil. Records associated with the site include 1943. Designed by Oscar Niemeyer in 1943, the building has a distinctive undulating canopy that follows the curve of the lake. Originally intended as a restaurant and dance hall, it now functions as an architectural center and exhibition space. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Free

Praça da Estação

Praça da Estação is a historic square located in downtown Belo Horizonte. During the day, it bustles with activity and is a great place to soak up the local atmosphere. However, it's important to be cautious at night as the area can become unsafe after dark. The square often hosts entertaining shows, adding to its active energy during the day.

Free

Condomínio do Edifício Arcângelo Maletta

Condomínio do Edifício Arcângelo Maletta, also known as Malleta Building, is a historic mall on Augusto de Lima Avenue that has long been a hub for artists and intellectuals. During the military dictatorship, it served as a meeting place for leftists to openly discuss their views. The building houses various bars, restaurants, and used-book shops on its upper levels.

Free

Américo Renné Giannetti City Park

The City Park Américo Renné Giannetti is a large park in the center of Belo Horizonte that offers a variety of activities and landmarks. Among these are lakes with boat rentals, a playground, and birdwatching opportunities. The park also has a tropical feel, with palm trees and flowering plants adding to its natural beauty.

Free

Palácio das Artes

Palácio das Artes is a striking modern performing arts complex located in Belo Horizonte. Designed by Oscar Niemeyer, it houses the Fundacao Clovis Salgado and offers a range of artistic experiences. The complex includes three theaters hosting concerts, dance performances, theater, and operas, visitors can explore three art galleries showing various exhibitions.

Free

Mercado do Cruzeiro

Mercado do Cruzeiro is a known establishment in Belo Horizonte, offering a wide array of food, drinks, and entertainment options. Located in the Cruzeiro neighborhood, this market hosts various establishments including bars serving cold beers, special cocktails, and delicious snacks. Visitors can explore a diverse range of products such as fruits, vegetables, meats, cheeses, sweets, and olive oil.

Free

Sebrae Minas - Belo Horizonte (Sede Administrativa)

Sebrae Minas - Belo Horizonte (Sede Administrativa) is a active hub for creativity and innovation, located the city. This dynamic space is part of a larger cultural landscape that includes twelve notable institutions such as the Minas Gerais Public Archive and the Fiat House of Culture. Visitors can explore diverse offerings from art to history at places like the Bank of Brazil Cultural Center and MM Gerdau - Museum of Mines and Metal.
