Belo Horizonte

Inaugurated in 1897 to replace colonial Ouro Preto, Belo Horizonte was Brazil's first purpose-built republican capital. A geometric plan and later modernist civic buildings expressed Minas Gerais' industrial ambitions. Pampulha's Niemeyer ensemble and the central grid grew from that planned-city experiment, financed by surrounding iron-ore country.

Mercado central

Belo Horizonte's Mercado Central fills a large indoor block with narrow aisles and hundreds of independent stalls. Founded in 1929 and later placed under private management, it remains both a working food center and a social meeting place. Regional cheese, cachaça and spices sit beside produce and meat, while craft shops, flower sellers and small bars reflect everyday trade in Minas Gerais.
