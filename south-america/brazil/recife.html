Explore Recife

A Brief History

Recife was founded by the Portuguese in 1537 and served as a major port for the export of sugar. During the seventeenth century, it was briefly controlled by the Dutch, who influenced its urban planning and infrastructure. The city played a central role in the Pernambucan Revolt of 1817 and the broader Brazilian independence movement. Today, Recife is known for its historic colonial center, active carnival traditions, and extensive waterways.

National Historical Park of Guararapes

The National Historical Park of Guararapes is a popular destination known for its scenic off-road biking trails that wind through the wooded park, offering viewpoints overlooking the city and sea. The park also hosts the annual traditional festival of Nossa Senhora dos Prazeres, locally referred to as Festa da Pitomba, after the region's native fruit. This celebration takes place from March 16th to March 24th in the park's courtyard.

Catedral da Sé São Salvador do Mundo

The Catedral da Sé (São Salvador do Mundo) in Olinda was founded in 1540 as the Matriz de Sao Salvador, serving as the oldest religious temple in the city and a symbol of Portuguese presence. Despite being destroyed in 1631, it was reconstructed in 1656 and reopened in 1677. Over centuries, it underwent various restoration and modification processes but still holds significant importance for preserving Olinda's historical-cultural architecture.

Free

Centro Histórico de Olinda

The Centro Histórico de Olinda is a historic town center with well-preserved Portuguese-style Baroque buildings. Known as Cidade Alta, it has steep hillsides adorned with colorful houses, historic churches, and local craft shops perfect for finding souvenirs. During the day, visitors can explore museums and historical buildings, while at night they can enjoy cozy restaurants, coffee shops, and bars.

Basilica of Our Lady of Mount Carmel

Basilica of Our Lady of Mount Carmel is a 17th-century notable building with a striking black and white facade, adorned with opulent gilded altarpieces and side chapels. The basilica's history dates back to the institutional reform in the Carmelite order, known as the Touraine reform. It was built on the site of an old palace after consensus was reached to establish a church dedicated to Lady of Desterro.

Free

Casa da Cultura de Pernambuco

Casa da Cultura de Pernambuco is a active cultural center in Recife, offering local crafts, cultural performances, and a rich historical experience. The building was originally a prison but has been transformed into a hub of art and culture with 150 craft shops. Visitors can explore the three cell galleries interconnected by steep staircases, each housing diverse cultural offerings.

Capela Dourada

Located Recife, the Capela Dourada, or Golden Chapel, is a evidence of Baroque architecture and religious devotion. Established on May 13, 1696, by Captain General Caetano de Melo Castro and constructed under the skilled hands of master mason Antonio Fernandes de Matos, this chapel was completed quickly. By September 15, 1697, just sixteen months later, it opened its doors to worshippers with great ceremony.

Free

Parque da Jaqueira

Jaqueira Park is a large, green space in Recife that is perfect for enjoying a jog or bike ride, playing on the playground, or taking a peaceful picnic lunch. The park has plenty of benches and shady spots for resting and offers easy access for people with disabilities.
