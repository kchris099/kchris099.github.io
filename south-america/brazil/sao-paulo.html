Explore São Paulo

A Brief History

São Paulo began as a Jesuit mission village in 1554 and remained modest until 19th-century coffee exports and later industrialisation transformed it into Brazil's largest metropolis. Italian, Japanese, and northeastern Brazilian migration filled its grid of avenues, parks, and skyscrapers. Avenida Paulista, the historic Sé district, and Ibirapuera Park illustrate how the city became the country's financial engine and a major centre of art and architecture.

Conjunto Nacional

Conjunto Nacional is a historic shopping complex located on Paulista Avenue in Sao Paulo. Designed by architect David Libeskind, it offers a variety of amenities including dining options, a movie theater, and a gym. The main attraction is the local branch of Livraria Cultura, the largest bookstore in Latin America. Visitors can enjoy comfortable seating areas and even find sections for children.

Mirante 9 de Julho

Mirante 9 de Julho is a cultural center housed in a classic mansion built in 1928 by known architect Ramos de Azevedo. The venue, originally a tunnel constructed in 1936, now hosts various cultural events such as art exhibitions, music performances, and food fairs. It's known for its Brazilian coffee and brunch options. While there's no car park available, it's easily accessible by subway.

Zoológico de São Paulo is a large zoo located a lush forested area, housing over 3,000 animals and invertebrates. Martinelli Building

The Martinelli Building, Brazil's first skyscraper, is a historic landmark in Sao Paulo. Erected in 1929, it played a important role in the city's change into a busy metropolis. This structure has a distinctive mansard roof and an inviting 26th-floor terrace offering panoramic views of the city. While primarily serving as office space today, it welcomes visitors to explore its architectural beauty and enjoy the vistas from its rooftop.
