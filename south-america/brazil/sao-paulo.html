São Paulo

São Paulo began as a Jesuit mission village in 1554 and remained modest until 19th-century coffee exports and later industrialisation transformed it into Brazil's largest metropolis. Italian, Japanese, and northeastern Brazilian migration filled its grid of avenues, parks, and skyscrapers. Avenida Paulista, the historic Sé district, and Ibirapuera Park illustrate how the city became the country's financial engine and a major centre of art and architecture.

Mercado Municipal de São Paulo

Sao Paulo's Mercado Municipal, nicknamed the Mercadao, occupies a historic hall in the old Centro. Produce stalls downstairs and mezzanine restaurants serve the city's densest market crowds. Stained-glass windows and high roofs date from the early twentieth-century municipal design. Mortadella sandwiches at the mezzanine are a long-running lunch habit.

Early twentieth-century neo-Gothic towers of Sé Metropolitan Cathedral can hold about 8,000 people above São Paulo's central square. Exterior and main interior spaces remain viewable outside Mass. The cathedral dominates the downtown skyline around Praça da Sé.

Paroquia Nossa Senhora do Brasil, built around 1940 in a baroque-revival style, honors Our Lady of Brazil. Conjunto Nacional

Conjunto Nacional opened in the 1950s as a mixed-use modernist block on Avenida Paulista. David Libeskind's design combines shops, offices, apartments, cultural spaces and internal circulation beneath a broad horizontal volume. Bookstores, cinemas, restaurants and services have changed over time, but the complex remains important to the avenue's shift from residential district to commercial center.

Ibirapuera Park is Sao Paulo's major urban park, combining lakes, lawns, and Oscar Niemeyer buildings for museums and events. Martinelli Building

Completed in 1929, the Martinelli Building announced São Paulo's early vertical growth with offices, shops, apartments, and a villa-like roof structure. Italian immigrant Giuseppe Martinelli financed and enlarged the tower during construction to prove the safety of high-rise building. Restoration has preserved its ornate facades and central role in the old downtown skyline.
