Bonito

Bonito developed as a ranching outpost in Mato Grosso do Sul before clear rivers and limestone caves drew scientific attention in the late 20th century. Local government and landowners introduced visitor caps, licensed guides, and trail fees that turned the plateau into Brazil's leading freshwater ecotourism district. Snorkelling on the Rio da Prata, cave tours at Gruta do Lago Azul, and farm-to-trail lodges now organise travel around conservation rules.

Gruta do Lago Azul Natural Monument is a destination in Bonito, known for its cavernous space adorned with stalactites and stalagmites.
