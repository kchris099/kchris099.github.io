Bonito

Bonito developed as a ranching outpost in Mato Grosso do Sul before clear rivers and limestone caves drew scientific attention in the late 20th century. Local government and landowners introduced visitor caps, licensed guides, and trail fees that turned the plateau into Brazil's leading freshwater ecotourism district. Snorkelling on the Rio da Prata, cave tours at Gruta do Lago Azul, and farm-to-trail lodges now organise travel around conservation rules.

Gruta do Lago Azul Natural Monument

Stalactites and stalagmites ring a transparent blue lake at the floor of Gruta do Lago Azul Natural Monument near Bonito. The cavern lake is the clear highlight of the visit. Surrounding karst country also holds rivers, pools, and clear-water swimming elsewhere in the district.

Grutas de São Miguel Dia e Noite

Grutas de Sao Miguel are among Bonito's protected limestone caves, visited on guided routes with day and night options. Formations and underground chambers sit within a conservation area that also includes other noted grottoes nearby. Helmets and paths keep groups on marked circuits through the cooler subterranean air. Night entries use lighting that picks out formations without full daylight glare.

Balneario Municipal, Bonito

Bonito's municipal balneario is a public river swimming area used by residents and travelers. Shallow clear water, lawns, and simple facilities support half-day visits without leaving town. Families treat it as a low-key alternative to ticketed snorkel rivers farther out. Changing rooms and lawn chairs support full afternoons in the water.

Natural Aquarium

A visit to Aquario Natural follows the clear spring water of Baia Bonita near Bonito. After a short forest walk, participants use masks, snorkels, flotation vests and wetsuits to drift above freshwater fish and submerged plants along a guided route of roughly 900 metres. A support boat accompanies the float, and the surrounding reserve remains part of the experience.

Liberty Square

Open pavement and surrounding facades frame Liberty Square in Bonito as a compact town gathering floor. Green edges and simple plantings soften the civic block without borrowed overseas landmarks. Locals use the square as a pause between commercial streets.

Bonito's aquarium

Bonito's aquarium displays regional freshwater species, including piranha, in indoor tanks. Guided explanations cover the clear rivers and caves that make the plateau famous. The compact facility suits a short stop when weather or timing keeps visitors in town. Labels name fish that snorkelers later spot in the region's clear rivers.

Projeto Jibóia

Established in 2005, Projeto Jibóia is a center dedicated to educating visitors about non-venomous snakes and their behaviors. Founded by Henry Naufal, the project aims to promote knowledge about the region's snake species in a fun and engaging way, dispelling negative perceptions and combating illegal hunting.

Bio Park

Bio Park is an open wildlife shelter on restored land outside Bonito. Animals that cannot return to the wild receive veterinary care in species-appropriate enclosures, including native mammals, birds, reptiles and rescued individuals affected by trafficking, injury or habitat loss. A walking route and educational interpretation focus on welfare and conservation.

09 AdmissionR$312

Parque das Cachoeiras

Parque das Cachoeiras near Bonito offers an ecological trail across roughly 400 hectares, much of it preserved forest. Waterfalls and swimming holes appear along the walking circuit through humid vegetation. Guides manage pacing so groups reach multiple falls within a single visit. Swim stops at successive falls structure the pace of the forest walk.

Praia da Figueira

Located about 19 kilometers from the Bonito, MS, Praia da Figueira is a lakeside resort that has an expansive stretch of white sandy beach alongside a lagoon measuring 60,000 square meters. This destination invites visitors to unwind in nature while indulging in various activities such as kayaking, pedal boating, and even zip-lining. The clear waters are perfect for swimming or simply relaxing by the shore.
