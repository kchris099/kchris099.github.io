Explore Buzios

A Brief History

Armacao dos Buzios originated as a small fishing village and a base for pirates and slave traders in the sixteenth century. It remained relatively obscure until the 1960s when French actress Brigitte Bardot visited, bringing international attention to its scenic beaches. This exposure transformed Buzios into a popular upscale resort destination. Today, it retains its Mediterranean charm with cobblestone streets, boutique shops, and active nightlife.

Orla Brigitte Bardot

Orla Brigitte Bardot is a visitor site in Buzios, Brazil. Records associated with the site include 1964. It offers views of the coast and connects Armacao Beach with Rua das Pedras. The walkway has a bronze statue of Brigitte Bardot, who visited the town in 1964 and helped bring attention to it. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Juscelino Kubitschek statue

Juscelino Kubitschek statue is a monument in Buzios, Brazil. The Juscelino Kubitschek statue is located in the city of Santos, Brazil. The statue was created by artist Hildebrando Lima and is a gift from Paulo Soares Penido. It depicts Juscelino Kubitschek, who was the 36th President of Brazil. The memorial stands in an open plaza or roadside setting.

Orla Bardot

Orla Bardot is a visitor site in Buzios, Brazil. The scenic walkway is lined with colonial-style houses, boutique shops, and seafood restaurants. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest. Orla Bardot remains part of the public visitor circuit in Buzios.

Praia Azeda

Praia Azeda is a snug and popular beach known for its sweeping views and clear, calm waters, making it perfect for swimming and snorkeling. It is located on the west side with white sands framed by rocks and lush vegetation. The beach is a favorite among both locals and tourists. Nearby infrastructures at Joao Fernandes make it popular beaches due to its proximity to the city center, luxury hotels, and restaurants.

Chez Michou Búzios

Chez Michou Búzios is a visitor site in Buzios, Brazil. Records associated with the site include 1983. Chez Michou is a popular Belgian-owned creperie in Buzios that attracts both locals and tourists. During the day, it's a family-friendly spot, while at night, it becomes a hip hangout with street-side tables and live DJ sets. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Mirante do Forno

Mirante do Forno is a visitor site in Buzios, Brazil. Located just a kilometer away from the beach and easily accessible on foot, this overlook provides an vantage point to admire the beauty of the surrounding beaches. Visitors can relax and soak in the scenery while enjoying the calm sea views. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Free

Ferradura Beach

Ferradura Beach is a beach in Buzios, Brazil. Visitors can enjoy renting chairs, umbrellas, and kayaks while indulging in snacks from the nearby kiosks. The beach is part of a popular route that includes other beaches like Geriba, Ferradurinha, and Forno. Access is typically on foot from nearby roads or beachfront paths.

Free

Geriba Beach

Geriba Beach is a long and wide sandy beach, popular for beach and water sports with umbrellas and facilities available. It draws surfers, windsurfers, and boogie-boarders to its long stretch of coastline. Nearby Ferradura Beach is ideal for snorkeling while Joao Fernandes and Joao Fernandinho are the busiest beaches with cafes, bars, and restaurants lining the shores. Praia da Tartaruga is another coveted spot for sunbathing in the area.

Free

Ferradurinha Beach

Ferradurinha Beach is a beach in Buzios, Brazil. Ferradurinha Beach is a local and visitor favorite in Buzios, surrounded by rugged mountains and cliffs that protect the calm bay below. Access is typically on foot from nearby roads or beachfront paths.

Free (guided tour R$100)

Mirante do Pai Vitório - (Búzios)

Mirante do Pai Vitório - (Búzios) is a visitor site in Buzios, Brazil. It is particularly popular for its expansive sunset views and the chance to observe local marine life, including sea turtles. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.
