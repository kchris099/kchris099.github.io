Explore Buzios

A Brief History

Armacao dos Buzios originated as a small fishing village and a base for pirates and slave traders in the sixteenth century. It remained relatively obscure until the 1960s when French actress Brigitte Bardot visited, bringing international attention to its scenic beaches. This exposure transformed Buzios into a popular upscale resort destination. Today, it retains its Mediterranean charm with cobblestone streets, boutique shops, and vibrant nightlife.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

Orla Brigitte Bardot

Orla Brigitte Bardot, named after the famous French actress, is a picturesque seafront walk in Buzios, Brazil. It offers views of the coast and connects Armacao Beach with Rua das Pedras. The walkway features a bronze statue of Brigitte Bardot, who visited the town in 1964 and helped bring attention to it. Created by sculptor Christina Motta, the statue is a popular spot for taking pictures.

Free

Juscelino Kubitschek statue

The Juscelino Kubitschek statue is located in the city of Santos, Brazil. The statue was created by artist Hildebrando Lima and is a gift from Paulo Soares Penido. It depicts Juscelino Kubitschek, who was the 36th President of Brazil. This historical place is perfect for a morning walk and features beautiful seaside surroundings.

Free

Orla Bardot

Orla Bardot is a picturesque waterfront promenade in Armação dos Búzios, named in honor of the French actress Brigitte Bardot, who popularized the town in the 1960s. The scenic walkway is lined with colonial-style houses, boutique shops, and seafood restaurants. It features a famous bronze statue of Bardot and offers beautiful views of the bay and its colorful fishing boats.

Free

Praia Azeda

Praia Azeda is a snug and popular beach known for its sweeping views and clear, calm waters, making it perfect for swimming and snorkeling. It is located on the west side with white sands framed by rocks and lush vegetation. The beach is a favorite among both locals and tourists. Nearby infrastructures at Joao Fernandes make it popular beaches due to its proximity to the city center, luxury hotels, and restaurants.

Free

Chez Michou Búzios

Chez Michou is a popular Belgian-owned creperie in Buzios that attracts both locals and tourists. During the day, it's a family-friendly spot, while at night, it becomes a hip hangout with street-side tables and live DJ sets. The menu offers over 50 savory and sweet fillings for their famous crepes. The restaurant has been a staple on Rua das Pedras since 1983, known for its lively atmosphere and delicious crepes.

Free

Mirante do Forno

Mirante do Forno is a must-visit spot in Buzios, offering panoramic views of Praia do Forno beach and the tranquil blue waters of the Atlantic Ocean. Located just a kilometer away from the beach and easily accessible on foot, this overlook provides an amazing vantage point to admire the beauty of the surrounding beaches. Visitors can relax and soak in the scenery while enjoying the calm sea views.

Free

Ferradura Beach

Ferradura Beach is a picturesque horseshoe-shaped beach known for its calm waters and various amenities. Visitors can enjoy renting chairs, umbrellas, and kayaks while indulging in snacks from the nearby kiosks. The beach is part of a popular route that includes other beaches like Geriba, Ferradurinha, and Forno. Families favor Ferradura for its tranquil and cold waters, making it ideal for children.

Free

Geriba Beach

Geriba Beach is a long and wide sandy beach, popular for beach and water sports with umbrellas and facilities available. It draws surfers, windsurfers, and boogie-boarders to its long stretch of coastline. Nearby Ferradura Beach is ideal for snorkeling while Joao Fernandes and Joao Fernandinho are the busiest beaches with cafes, bars, and restaurants lining the shores. Praia da Tartaruga is another coveted spot for sunbathing in the area.

Free

Ferradurinha Beach

Ferradurinha Beach is a local and visitor favorite in Buzios, surrounded by rugged mountains and cliffs that protect the calm bay below. This picturesque spot boasts white sand and turquoise waters, making it ideal for those seeking a tranquil beach experience. While smaller than other nearby beaches, Ferradurinha offers a more intimate setting with clear and tranquil waters perfect for activities like kayaking and romantic beach picnics.

Free

Mirante do Pai Vitório - (Búzios)

Mirante do Pai Vitório is a impressive coastal viewpoint located in Búzios, offering dramatic vistas of the Atlantic Ocean and the rugged shoreline. Accessible via a short hike, the overlook provides a tranquil escape from the busier beaches. It is particularly popular for its expansive sunset views and the opportunity to observe local marine life, including sea turtles.
