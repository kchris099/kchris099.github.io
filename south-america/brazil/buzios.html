Buzios

Armacao dos Buzios originated as a small fishing village and a base for pirates and slave traders in the sixteenth century. It remained relatively obscure until the 1960s when French actress Brigitte Bardot visited, bringing international attention to its scenic beaches. This exposure transformed Buzios into a popular upscale resort destination. Today, it retains its Mediterranean charm with cobblestone streets, boutique shops, and active nightlife.

Orla Brigitte Bardot

Orla Brigitte Bardot links Armacao Beach with Rua das Pedras along Buzios' waterfront. A bronze statue recalls Bardot's 1964 visit that helped popularize the peninsula. Walkers follow the paved edge past boats, bars, and views across the harbor inlet. Fishing boats and schooners idle in the sheltered water just offshore.

Orla Bardot

Orla Bardot follows the shore between Armação and the center of Búzios, passing fishing boats, low houses, restaurants, and small beaches. A bronze figure of Brigitte Bardot recalls the actress's 1960s visits, which helped draw international attention to the former fishing settlement. The promenade connects directly with Rua das Pedras and becomes busiest in the evening.

Praia Azeda

Praia Azeda is a snug and popular beach known for its sweeping views and clear, calm waters, making it perfect for swimming and snorkeling. It is located on the west side with white sands framed by rocks and lush vegetation. The beach is a favorite among both locals and tourists. Nearby infrastructures at Joao Fernandes make it popular beaches due to its proximity to the city center, luxury hotels, and restaurants.

Chez Michou Búzios

Belgian-owned Chez Michou serves sweet and savory Breton-style crepes on Búzios' Orla, with daytime family tables and livelier queues later as the waterfront fills. Local fruit toppings share the menu with buckwheat savory options. Batter and crepe technique remain the kitchen's draw.

06 AdmissionFree

Mirante do Forno

Mirante do Forno sits about a kilometer inland from Forno Beach, reachable on foot by a short climb. The lookout faces nested coves and headlands typical of the Buzios peninsula. Late afternoon light picks out the beaches below before the sun drops behind the hills. A short scramble over rock rewards walkers who leave the beach sand.

Ferradura Beach

Chair, umbrella, and kayak rentals line Ferradura Beach in Buzios, with snack kiosks behind the sand. The cove sits on a popular beach circuit with Geriba, Ferradurinha, and Forno. Access runs on foot from roads and beachfront paths along the sheltered bay.

08 AdmissionFree

Geriba Beach

Geriba Beach is a long and wide sandy beach, popular for beach and water sports with umbrellas and facilities available. It draws surfers, windsurfers, and boogie-boarders to its long stretch of coastline. Nearby Ferradura Beach is ideal for snorkeling while Joao Fernandes and Joao Fernandinho are the busiest beaches with cafes, bars, and restaurants lining the shores. Praia da Tartaruga is another coveted spot for sunbathing in the area.

Ferradurinha Beach

Cliffs and rugged hills shelter the calm bay at Ferradurinha Beach in Buzios, a smaller cove favored by locals and visitors seeking quieter water. Foot access from nearby roads reaches the sand. Protection from open swell keeps the water quieter than exposed strands.

Mirante do Pai Vitório - (Búzios)

Mirante do Pai Vitorio overlooks open water on the Buzios coast, favored for sunset color on the horizon. From the rocks, visitors sometimes spot turtles in the clearer shallows below. A short access path keeps the viewpoint popular at the end of beach-hopping days. Photographers linger for the last color band on the Atlantic horizon.
