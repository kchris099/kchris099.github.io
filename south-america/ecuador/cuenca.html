Explore Cuenca

A Brief History

Cuenca was established by the Spanish in 1557 on the ruins of the prominent Inca city of Tomebamba, incorporating indigenous stonework into its early colonial foundations. The city flourished in the nineteenth century through the export of quinine bark and the booming production of Panama hats, funding the construction of elegant French-republican and neoclassical architecture. Cuenca's relative geographic isolation helped preserve its cohesive historic center. Visitors explore this prosperous blend of colonial and republican history at the blue-domed New Cathedral, the flower markets, and the Tomebamba riverfront.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

Puente Roto

Puente Roto, also known as Broken Bridge, is a popular riverside walkway in the historical center of Cuenca. It is lined with lawns, restaurants, and cultural sites such as the Pumapungo Museum and the Museum of Aboriginal Cultures. The bridge itself is an icon of Cuenca's history and offers a perfect spot for taking pictures. Despite being partially destroyed by river flooding, it has become a top attraction in the city.

Free

Ruinas de Pumapungo

The Ruinas de Pumapungo are ancient Incan ruins that offer a glimpse into the past with impressive stonework and beautiful gardens. It remains a notable local point of interest situated in Cuenca, Ecuador. Visitors can observe the surrounding area and its connection to the city's broader historical landscape. Local guides sometimes include it on walking routes through the centre.

Free

Turi Viewpoint

Mirador de Turi, situated around four kilometers from the city center of Cuenca, offers a panoramic view of the colonial buildings and houses. Visitors can enjoy a leisurely walk to reach this lookout point and are rewarded with views, especially during the late afternoon when they can witness the sunset against the backdrop of mountains. While there are shops and restaurants nearby, it's advisable to bring along a map of Cuenca for convenience.

Free

Catedral de la Inmaculada Concepción

The Catedral de la Inmaculada Concepción, also known as the Cuenca Cathedral, is a remarkable landmark in Cuenca, Ecuador. This grand Catholic church was built over 80 years starting in 1886 and can accommodate more than 9,000 worshippers. With its Romanesque-revival architecture featuring three blue and white domes, the cathedral stands out in the historic city center.

Free

Calderon Park

Calderon Park, located in the heart of Cuenca, is a small landscaped square surrounded by government buildings and two churches. The park features a monument to Abdon Calderon, a hero of the city's independence, as well as eight tall pines brought from Chile and planted by President Luis Cordero. Visitors can take a few minutes to rest and admire the city's majestic architecture while enjoying the shade of large trees.

$2

Old Cathedral of Cuenca

The Old Cathedral of Cuenca, also known as El Sagrario, is a historic Catholic temple dating back to the 16th century. Originally built in 1557, the same year the city was founded, it was later elevated to cathedral status in 1787. Today, it serves as a museum showcasing religious artwork. The cathedral holds significant historical importance, having been visited by the French Geodesic Mission in the 1700s for scientific exploration.

Free

Plaza de las Flores

Nestled in the heart of Cuenca's Historic Center, Plaza de las Flores is a picturesque square renowned for its vibrant flower market. This charming space showcases a array of flowers, including giant roses, orchids, jasmines, lilies, and carnations. The friendly lady vendors add to the authentic atmosphere of the plaza.

Free

Plaza San Francisco Cuenca Ecuador

Plaza San Francisco in Cuenca, Ecuador has a rich history dating back to 1558 when it became municipal property. Over the centuries, it has served various purposes such as being the city's first market and a hub for public transportation. Today, the plaza is surrounded by a diverse array of local crafts and regional goods.

Free

Mercado 9 de Octubre

Nestled in the heart of Cuenca, Mercado 9 de Octubre is a vibrant and lively market that captures the essence of local culture. Open daily, this bustling hub offers an impressive array of fresh produce, meats, and cheeses alongside tantalizing street food options like slow-roasted pork. The atmosphere is electric with the sounds and sights of everyday life, making it a perfect spot for photography enthusiasts.

$10

Amaru Zoológico Bioparque

Amaru Zoológico Bioparque is a unique open-air zoo nestled in rugged, mountainside terrain. The zoo serves as a private, non-profit conservation organization established to educate the community about protecting biodiversity and natural resources. Amaru Zoológico Bioparque is catalogued as a zoo within the historic quarters of Cuenca.
