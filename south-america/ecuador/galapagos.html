Galápagos

The Galápagos archipelago's volcanic islands inspired Darwin's observations on species variation during the 1835 Beagle voyage. Giant tortoises, marine iguanas, and strict park regulations preserve endemic ecosystems that anchor Ecuador's Pacific conservation identity. Visitor landing sites on Santa Cruz and Isabela balance scientific research stations with regulated wildlife trails.

Fishing piers

If visitors are looking for a experience in the Galapagos, a stroll along the Muelle de Los Pescadores, or the local fishing pier in Puerto Ayora, is an must. This spot offers views and an abundance of wildlife that will leave visitors mesmerized. As visitors walk down this quieter pier, encounter Sally lightfoot crabs scuttling about and adorable baby marine iguanas basking in the sun.

Charles Darwin Research Station

The Charles Darwin Research Station, operated by the Fundacion Charles Darwin, is a significant biological research facility located just east of Puerto Ayora on the southern coast of Santa Cruz. It houses an extensive collection of preserved specimens of Galápagos plant life and is a vital center for learning about the unique wildlife inhabiting the Galapagos Islands.
