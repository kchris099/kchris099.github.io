Explore Galápagos

A Brief History

The Galápagos archipelago's volcanic islands inspired Darwin's observations on species variation during the 1835 Beagle voyage. Giant tortoises, marine iguanas, and strict park regulations preserve endemic ecosystems that anchor Ecuador's Pacific conservation identity. Visitor landing sites on Santa Cruz and Isabela balance scientific research stations with regulated wildlife trails.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

Fishing piers

If you're looking for a delightful experience in the Galapagos, a stroll along the Muelle de Los Pescadores, or the local fishing pier in Puerto Ayora, is an must. This charming spot offers views and an abundance of wildlife that will leave you mesmerized. As you walk down this quieter pier, encounter Sally lightfoot crabs scuttling about and adorable baby marine iguanas basking in the sun.

Free (tortoise tour $10)

Charles Darwin Research Station

The Charles Darwin Research Station, operated by the Fundacion Charles Darwin, is a significant biological research facility located just east of Puerto Ayora on the southern coast of Santa Cruz. It houses an extensive collection of preserved specimens of Galápagos plant life and serves as a vital center for learning about the unique wildlife inhabiting the Galapagos Islands.
