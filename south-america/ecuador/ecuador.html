Ecuador

Ecuador's highlands and coast supported older societies before Inca expansion reached the region late in the fifteenth century. Spanish Quito grew after conquest through churches and workshops, while Ingapirca remained tied to the brief Inca presence. Independence was secured at Pichincha in 1822, after which the republic developed around competing coastal and Andean economies while Indigenous communities kept a strong role in markets and national politics.
