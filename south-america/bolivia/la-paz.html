Explore La Paz

A Brief History

Founded by the Spanish in 1548 in an Andean canyon to evade harsh winds, La Paz grew as a vital commercial link between Potosí's silver mines and the coast. Its steep topography shaped a stratified society, eventually evolving into Bolivia's bustling administrative capital. Visitors today can clearly trace the intricate layers of this unique heritage throughout the preserved streets and monuments.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

Plaza Murillo

Plaza Murillo, located in the heart of La Paz's old town, is a grand square that serves as the epicenter of Bolivia's political life. Surrounded by colonial architecture, this historic plaza is home to significant landmarks such as the Presidential Palace and the National Congress. The intricate facades and historical significance of these buildings offer visitors a glimpse into Bolivia's rich political and cultural heritage.

Free (museo san francisco BOB 20)

Plaza Mayor de San Francisco

Plaza Mayor de San Francisco is a bustling public square in the heart of La Paz, Bolivia. It is dominated by a colonial-era cathedral and convent and features vibrant street art and regular fairs. The surrounding area offers diverse experiences, from the tree-lined Prado to the upscale neighborhood of Miraflores.

Free

The Witches Market

The Witches Market is a documented market or commercial centre in La Paz, Bolivia, listed in local civic guides for its role in regional trade and daily commerce. The site operates during standard market hours and is open to visitors without charge for browsing. Travellers enter from the surrounding streets and combine the visit with nearby food vendors and craft stalls.

$3

Red Cap City Walking Tours - La Paz

Red Cap City Walking Tours offers engaging, guided explorations of La Paz, providing visitors with deep insights into the city's history, culture, and daily life. The tours typically cover major landmarks such as the Witches' Market, San Pedro Prison, and Plaza Murillo. Led by knowledgeable local guides, the experience highlights the unique traditions and complex social fabric of the Bolivian capital.

Free

Mirador Killi Killi

Mirador Killi Killi is a popular hilltop destination in La Paz, Bolivia, offering panoramic views of the city and its surroundings. It is known for its dramatic vistas and scenic gardens, making it a favorite spot for locals to unwind or enjoy romantic moments. The viewpoint holds historical significance as it served as the headquarters during an indigenous siege in 1781.

Free

Montículo

Montículo is a charming park located in the tranquil neighborhood of Sopocachi, offering views of the city and surrounding mountains. The park features a Neptune fountain and is known for its romantic ambiance, making it a popular spot for couples. The Inmaculada Concepcion del Monticulo church within the park is a favored wedding venue due to local beliefs about its luck-bringing properties. This off-the-beaten-path destination also attracts poets and writers seeking inspiration.

Free

Plaza España

Plaza España is a well-maintained public square located in the Sopocachi neighborhood of La Paz. The plaza features landscaped gardens, seating areas, and a central monument honoring the Spanish author Miguel de Cervantes. It serves as a peaceful gathering place for locals and provides a quiet contrast to the bustling commercial districts of the city.

BOB 15

Valle de la Luna

Valle de la Luna, also known as Valley of the Moon, is a unique area with trails and peculiar rock formations shaped by mountain erosion. The landscape is characterized by its lunar-like appearance, created by a combination of heavy precipitation over the years and soft volcanic soil. Situated near La Paz, this dramatic badland features natural amphitheaters and eroded rock walls that crumble under your fingertips.

Free

Auquisamaña

Auquisamaña is a protected ecological reserve located in the southern zone of La Paz. The area features unique geological formations, native Andean vegetation, and a network of hiking trails that offer impressive views of the city and the surrounding mountains. It provides an accessible natural retreat for residents and visitors seeking outdoor recreation and wildlife observation.

BOB 50

Diverland Park

Diverland Park is a spacious indoor playground perfect for families with kids. The park offers various attractions such as slides, ball ponds, and climbing frames to keep the little ones entertained. Additionally, there's a bowling alley for some family-friendly competition. While the play area may seem a bit dimly lit, it provides ample space for kids to interact and have fun. Parents can relax at designated table areas while keeping an eye on their children.
