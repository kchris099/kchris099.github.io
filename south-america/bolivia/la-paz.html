La Paz

Founded by the Spanish in 1548 in an Andean canyon to evade harsh winds, La Paz grew as a vital commercial link between Potosí's silver mines and the coast. Its steep topography shaped a stratified society, eventually evolving into Bolivia's busy administrative capital. Visitors today can clearly trace the detailed layers of this unique heritage throughout the preserved streets and monuments.
