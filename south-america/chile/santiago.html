Santiago

Santiago was refounded by Pedro de Valdivia in 1541 in a valley beneath the Andes, serving as capital of the Captaincy General and later the Republic of Chile. Earthquakes repeatedly reshaped its colonial churches and neoclassical centre before 19th-century nitrate wealth funded boulevards and public buildings. Plaza de Armas, Cerro San Cristóbal, and the Bellavista quarter show how the city became Chile's political, financial, and cultural hub.
