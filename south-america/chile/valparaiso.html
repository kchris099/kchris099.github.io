Explore Valparaíso

A Brief History

Valparaíso's steep hills served as Chile's principal Pacific port before the Panama Canal, with funiculars linking colourful timber houses to harbour quays. Naval heritage, poet Pablo Neruda's houses, and warehouse districts record nineteenth-century maritime trade. Cerro Alegre stairways and port warehouse districts extend funicular-linked hills above the Pacific harbour.

Sotomayor Square

Sotomayor Square is a public square in Valparaíso, Chile. The surrounding area includes the commercial quarter with its port works, warehouses, banks, and shopping center. Adjacent to the bay are administrative buildings grouped around the plaza. Open pavement and surrounding facades define the visitor setting.

Monument to the Heroes of Iquique

In Sotomayor Square in Valparaiso, Chile stands the Monumento a Los Heroes de Iquique, a tribute to fallen naval heroes from the battle of Iquique during the war in the Pacific. The monument is well-maintained and offers guided walking tours daily at noon for visitors to better understand its significance. It commemorates the bravery of Chilean commander Pratt and his naval operations against a Peruvian warship.

Vergara Pier

Vergara Pier is a visitor site in Valparaíso, Chile. Construction and early use date to around 2016. Originally constructed in the late 19th century and later remodeled in 2016, this pedestrian pier has transformed from an industrial hub into a beloved recreational spot. Visitors flock here to soak up views of the Pacific Ocean while enjoying activities like fishing for crabs and jaibas. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Plaza De La Victoria

Plaza De La Victoria is a historic public square in Valparaíso, known for its elegant landscaping, classical statues, and the striking wrought-iron fountain at its center. It is a popular gathering place for locals and a starting point for exploring the city's active street art and funicular railways.

Viña del Mar National Botanical Garden

Viña del Mar National Botanical Garden, spanning 370 acres, was established in 1917 to preserve Chilean plant species. The garden houses around 800 native plant species, with half of them at risk of extinction. Notably, it has a collection of 280 tree species and is home to the rare Sophora Toromiro tree from Easter Island.

Free

Wulff Castle

Wulff Castle, also known as Castillo Wulff, is a historic mansion built in the early 20th century by German industrialist Gustavo Adolfo Wulff. Perched on the edge of a cliff overlooking the Pacific Ocean, this castle offers ocean views and is a cultural center with exhibits showing its rich history. Although it has been closed to visitors for some time, it remains an landmark in Vina del Mar.

Free

Palace of Cerro Castillo

Palace of Cerro Castillo is a fortified historic site in Valparaíso, Chile. Constructed entirely from bricks imported from France, this enchanting castle offers a glimpse into the past. While photography inside is restricted to preserve its beauty, guided tours are available for those eager to learn more about its fascinating history. Walls, courtyards, or towers form the main visitor route when the grounds are open.

Estadio Sausalito

Estadio Sausalito is the home ground for the local football team Everton de Vina. The stadium has a passionate fan base, and matches here generally have fewer reported issues compared to larger clubs like Colo-Colo and Universidad de Chile. Ticket prices can range from 3,500 pesos or more for matches against bigger teams. The stadium is known for being organized, clean, and tidy with excellent volunteers during events like the Pan American Games 2023.

Ascensor Reina Victoria

Ascensor Reina Victoria, also known as Queen Victoria Funicular, is an hillside transportation system in Valparaiso. Dating back to 1902, it offers panoramic views of the city. Connecting Elias Street to Paseo Dimalow, this funicular provides a quick and affordable way to ascend the steep hillside. At the top, visitors can find various restaurants and cafes where they can relax and enjoy the scenery.

Free

Cerro Concepcion

Cerro Concepcion, a historic hill in Valparaiso, has the Ascensor Concepcion funicular dating back to 1883. This popular attraction climbs 69 meters at a 45-degree gradient from Carreno to Paseo Gervasoni. The area is home to Museo El Mirador de Lukas and two significant churches: Iglesia Anglicana San Pablo and Iglesia Luterana.

Free (ascensores CLP 1,000)

Cerro Alegre

Cerro Alegre is a visitor site in Valparaíso, Chile. Cerro Alegre and Cerro Concepcion are bohemian neighborhoods in Valparaiso, Chile, offering a relaxed atmosphere for daytime strolls and sunsets. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Concepción Funicular

Ascensor Concepcion, the oldest funicular in Valparaiso dating back to 1883, is a popular tourist attraction as well as a convenient mode of transport for locals. The rickety yet thrilling ride swiftly takes passengers up the steep hillside to Paseo Gervasoni, offering views of the city and port. Visitors can also opt for a ride on Ascensor Reina Victoria, built in 1902.

Playa Las Torpederas is a popular city beach in Chile, perfect for swimming and water sports. It offers snack bars and a lookout platform with views of the coast. The beach is ideal for family outings or peaceful walks. Visitors can also explore the Los Angeles Lighthouse, which was the first lighthouse built in Chile. Located at Punta Angeles, this pristine public beach provides an excellent venue for relaxation and outdoor activities.

National Maritime Museum

National Maritime Museum is a visitor site in Valparaíso. The Museo Naval Y Maritimo, located at Paseo 21 de Mayo in Valparaiso, Chile, is a museum that pays homage to the country's rich naval history. Visitors can explore a diverse array of ship artifacts, models, illustrations, and old cannons lining the entrance. The exhibition rooms are filled with intriguing items such as oil paintings, maps, models, and statues.

Free

Paseo 21 de Mayo de Valparaíso

Paseo 21 de Mayo is a scenic promenade located on Cerro Artillería in Valparaíso. It offers some of the most well known panoramic views of the city's colorful hillsides, the busy port, and the Pacific Ocean. The walkway is lined with artisan stalls selling local crafts and souvenirs, making it a favorite spot for visitors.
