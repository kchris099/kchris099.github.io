Valparaíso

Valparaíso's steep hills served as Chile's principal Pacific port before the Panama Canal, with funiculars linking colourful timber houses to harbour quays. Naval heritage, poet Pablo Neruda's houses, and warehouse districts record nineteenth-century maritime trade. Cerro Alegre stairways and port warehouse districts extend funicular-linked hills above the Pacific harbour.
