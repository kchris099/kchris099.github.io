Destination Guide — Valparaíso

Explore Valparaíso

A Brief History

Valparaíso's steep hills served as Chile's principal Pacific port before the Panama Canal, with funiculars linking colourful timber houses to harbour quays. Naval heritage, poet Pablo Neruda's houses, and warehouse districts record nineteenth-century maritime trade. Cerro Alegre stairways and port warehouse districts extend funicular-linked hills above the Pacific harbour.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

Playa Caleta Portales

Playa Caleta Portales is a popular urban beach and traditional fishing cove located in Valparaíso. The area is renowned for its lively seafood market, where visitors can purchase fresh catches directly from local fishermen. The beach offers a relaxed coastal atmosphere, numerous seafood restaurants, and a glimpse into the authentic maritime culture of the historic port city.

Free

Flower clock

The Flower Clock, located in Vina del Mar, Chile, is a garden clock adorned with flowers. Built to commemorate the 1962 World Cup hosted by Vina del Mar, this fully functional timepiece features a face made entirely of flowers meticulously maintained to not obstruct its white metal hands. Every 15 minutes, the clock chimes and plays seasonal music. A popular spot for visitors seeking picturesque photo opportunities, it stands as a testament to Vina's nickname 'The Garden City.'.

Free

Playa Caleta Abarca

Playa Caleta Abarca is a charming sandy beach nestled in a picturesque bay, just north of Valparaiso and close to the city of Vina del Mar. This inviting stretch of golden sand, though modest in size, serves as a lively gathering spot for sunbathers and beach enthusiasts alike. The promenade that runs alongside it is lined with hotels and open-air restaurants, creating an ideal atmosphere for relaxation and socializing.

Free

Sotomayor Square

Plaza Sotomayor is a bustling square in Valparaiso, flanked by the Chilean navy headquarters and a central memorial honoring naval heroes. The surrounding area includes the commercial quarter with its port works, warehouses, banks, and shopping center. Adjacent to the bay are administrative buildings grouped around the plaza. The lower part of the city also features the cathedral, parks, boulevards, theaters, cafes, and colonial buildings like the church of La Matriz.

Free

Monument to the Heroes of Iquique

In the heart of Sotomayor Square in Valparaiso, Chile stands the Monumento a Los Heroes de Iquique, a tribute to fallen naval heroes from the battle of Iquique during the war in the Pacific. The monument is well-maintained and offers guided walking tours daily at noon for visitors to better understand its significance. It commemorates the bravery of Chilean commander Pratt and his naval operations against a Peruvian warship.

Free

Prat Pier

Muelle Prat is a waterfront promenade at the heart of Valparaiso's port area. It offers a lively atmosphere with fishermen bringing in their catch, street vendors selling Chilean snacks, and artists capturing scenic bay views. Visitors can take boat tours from the pier to see the city's colorful hillside constructions and historic buildings.

Free

Vergara Pier

Nestled in the coastal city of Vina del Mar, Chile, Vergara Pier is a charming destination that beautifully marries history with leisure. Originally constructed in the late 19th century and later remodeled in 2016, this pedestrian pier has transformed from an industrial hub into a beloved recreational spot. Visitors flock here to soak up views of the Pacific Ocean while enjoying activities like fishing for crabs and jaibas.

Free

Viña del Mar

Viña del Mar, a coastal resort city in Chile, is famous for its gardens, beaches, and modern buildings. The Quinta Vergara Park houses the Quinta Vergara Amphitheater and the Artequin Museum with replicas of major artworks. The Museum of Archaeology and History Francisco Fonck displays stone moai sculptures from Easter Island and shrunken heads.

Free

Concon Dunes

Nestled along the Chilean coastline, just north of Valparaiso, the Concon Dunes are a must-visit destination for adventure seekers and nature lovers alike. These impressive dunes rise dramatically from the shore, offering views of both the ocean and the cityscape. The town of Concon itself is a lively resort area known for its beautiful beaches and bustling nightlife, but it’s these towering sand formations that truly steal the show.

Free

Plaza De La Victoria

Plaza De La Victoria is a historic public square in Valparaíso, known for its elegant landscaping, classical statues, and the striking wrought-iron fountain at its center. It serves as a popular gathering place for locals and a starting point for exploring the city's vibrant street art and funicular railways.

CLP 3000

Viña del Mar National Botanical Garden

Viña del Mar National Botanical Garden, spanning 370 acres, was established in 1917 to preserve Chilean plant species. The garden houses around 800 native plant species, with half of them at risk of extinction. Notably, it boasts a collection of 280 tree species and is home to the rare Sophora Toromiro tree from Easter Island.

Free

Wulff Castle

Wulff Castle, also known as Castillo Wulff, is a historic mansion built in the early 20th century by German industrialist Gustavo Adolfo Wulff. Perched on the edge of a cliff overlooking the Pacific Ocean, this castle offers ocean views and serves as a cultural center with exhibits showcasing its rich history. Although it has been closed to visitors for some time, it remains an landmark in Vina del Mar.

Free

Palace of Cerro Castillo

Nestled in the Cerro Castillo, the Palace of Cerro Castillo is a captivating historical gem that draws visitors with its rich heritage. Constructed entirely from bricks imported from France, this enchanting castle offers a glimpse into the past. While photography inside is restricted to preserve its beauty, guided tours are available for those eager to learn more about its fascinating history.

Free

Casino de Viña del Mar

Located on Calle San Martin, the Casino de Viña del Mar is a beachfront casino offering a variety of entertainment options. The casino features slot machines, card tables, bingo, and even a dance club with live shows. Its imposing Roman-Greek-style construction and elegant rooms create an inviting atmosphere for guests to enjoy table games and chance machines while earning points in the Enjoy Club.

Free

Estadio Sausalito

Estadio Sausalito is the home ground for the local football team Everton de Vina. The stadium has a passionate fan base, and matches here generally have fewer reported issues compared to larger clubs like Colo-Colo and Universidad de Chile. Ticket prices can range from 3,500 pesos or more for matches against bigger teams. The stadium is known for being organized, clean, and tidy with excellent volunteers during events like the Pan American Games 2023.

CLP 300

Ascensor Reina Victoria

Ascensor Reina Victoria, also known as Queen Victoria Funicular, is an hillside transportation system in Valparaiso. Dating back to 1902, it offers panoramic views of the city. Connecting Elias Street to Paseo Dimalow, this funicular provides a quick and affordable way to ascend the steep hillside. At the top, visitors can find various restaurants and cafes where they can relax and enjoy the scenery.

Free

Cerro Concepcion

Cerro Concepcion, a historic hill in Valparaiso, boasts the Ascensor Concepcion funicular dating back to 1883. This popular attraction climbs 69 meters at a 45-degree gradient from Carreno to Paseo Gervasoni. The area is home to Museo El Mirador de Lukas and two significant churches: Iglesia Anglicana San Pablo and Iglesia Luterana.

Free

Cerro Alegre

Cerro Alegre and Cerro Concepcion are bohemian neighborhoods in Valparaiso, Chile, offering a relaxed atmosphere for daytime strolls and sunsets. The area is filled with lively cafes, public squares, and historic cobbled streets that blend timeless charms with contemporary creativity.

CLP 300

Concepción Funicular

Ascensor Concepcion, the oldest funicular in Valparaiso dating back to 1883, is a popular tourist attraction as well as a convenient mode of transport for locals. The rickety yet thrilling ride swiftly takes passengers up the steep hillside to Paseo Gervasoni, offering views of the city and port. Visitors can also opt for a ride on Ascensor Reina Victoria, built in 1902.

Free

Playa Las Torpederas

Playa Las Torpederas is a popular city beach in Chile, perfect for swimming and water sports. It offers snack bars and a lookout platform with views of the coast. The beach is ideal for family outings or peaceful walks. Visitors can also explore the Los Angeles Lighthouse, which was the first lighthouse built in Chile. Located at Punta Angeles, this pristine public beach provides an excellent venue for relaxation and outdoor activities.

CLP 2000

National Maritime Museum

The Museo Naval Y Maritimo, located at Paseo 21 de Mayo in Valparaiso, Chile, is a captivating museum that pays homage to the country's rich naval history. Visitors can explore a diverse array of ship artifacts, models, illustrations, and old cannons lining the entrance. The exhibition rooms are filled with intriguing items such as oil paintings, maps, models, and statues.

Free

Paseo 21 de Mayo de Valparaíso

Paseo 21 de Mayo is a scenic promenade located on Cerro Artillería in Valparaíso. It offers some of the most iconic panoramic views of the city's colorful hillsides, the bustling port, and the Pacific Ocean. The walkway is lined with artisan stalls selling local crafts and souvenirs, making it a favorite spot for visitors.
