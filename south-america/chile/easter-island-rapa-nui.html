Explore Easter Island Rapa Nui

A Brief History

Easter Island Rapa Nui lies in Chile. Settled by Polynesians who carved the well known moai statues, Rapa Nui experienced ecological and social collapse before European contact in 1722. Subsequent colonization and land annexation significantly shaped the long running resilience of the indigenous Rapa Nui culture. The survival of these cultural and architectural milestones provides a powerful window into the region's storied past. Museums, markets, and street plans in Easter Island Rapa Nui still show layers from those periods.

Ahu Tahai

Ahu Tahai is a visitor site in Easter Island Rapa Nui, Chile. The main attractions are the moai statues, with one fully restored moai adorned with a reddish pukao topknot and coral eyes believed to hold supernatural power. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Ahu Vinapu

Ahu Vinapu is a visitor site in Easter Island Rapa Nui, Chile. Unlike most Moai and Ahu on the island, Ahu Vinapu is home to one of the few examples of a female Moai made of red scoria rock. The details of this feminine figure have eroded over time, but it is believed to be distinctively feminine in nature. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

CLP 70,000

Te Ra'ai

Te Ra'ai is a visitor site in Easter Island Rapa Nui, Chile. Here, visitors can experience the authentic curanto dish and witness its preparation. The restaurant also presents cultural shows, music performances, and theatrical plays with elaborate costumes and make-up. It's a sought-after destination, particularly during peak times, so it's advisable to make reservations in advance. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

$100

Puna Pau

Puna Pau is a historic rock quarry known for red scoria, which was used to carve the decorative elements for the moai statues on Easter Island. This site is where the moai headdresses, known as pukao, were crafted from the soft red volcanic stone. The quarry offers a glimpse into the origin of these unique headdresses and their significance in the island's history.

CLP 40,000

Ahu Akivi

Ahu Akivi is an intriguing archaeological site on Rapa Nui featuring seven moai statues that are positioned to face the setting sun during the Spring Equinox. Unlike most other ahu temples, these moai statues face out to sea, adding to their mystery and beauty. According to local legend, these seven moai represent the scouts sent by King Hotu Matua in ancient times and were strategically placed for astronomical purposes.

CLP 40,000

Ahu Tongariki

Ahu Tongariki is a visitor site in Easter Island Rapa Nui, Chile. Ahu Tongariki is the largest and most visually striking ceremonial platform (ahu) on Easter Island (Rapa Nui). The site was careful restored in the 1990s after being devastated by a tsunami, and it is particularly known for dramatic sunrise views. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Pu o Hiro

Pu o Hiro is a visitor site in Easter Island Rapa Nui. Mataveri International Airport (IPC) is a small airport located on the island, offering a quaint and arrival experience. Despite its size, the airport is the main gateway to the island with limited flight schedules.

Easter Island

Easter Island sits within the Easter Island area of Easter Island Rapa Nui, documented in local heritage registers for its civic or cultural role. Visitors approach from the main access road and combine the visit with neighbouring heritage buildings and public spaces in the district.

$100

Rapa Nui National Park

Rapa Nui National Park include a significant portion of Easter Island, protecting its unique archaeological and local history. The UNESCO World Heritage site includes nearly 900 monumental moai statues, ceremonial platforms, and the Rano Raraku quarry where the statues were carved. The park preserves the profound legacy of the Polynesian society that settled the isolated island.
