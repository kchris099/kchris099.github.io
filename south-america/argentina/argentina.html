Argentina

Argentina declared independence from Spain in 1816 and grew wealthy through cattle ranching, grain exports, and European immigration in the decades that followed. Buenos Aires boulevards, Cordoba Jesuit colleges, and Patagonian railway towns still show how republican politics and port trade built the southern cone's largest nation.
