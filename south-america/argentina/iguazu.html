Explore Iguazú

A Brief History

The Iguazu region was originally inhabited by the Guarani people before Spanish explorer Alvar Nunez Cabeza de Vaca recorded the falls in 1541. The area remained largely isolated until the creation of Iguazu National Park in 1934, which protected the surrounding subtropical rainforest. Today, the falls are recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage site, drawing visitors to their massive cascades and diverse wildlife.

Top Sights & Activities

ARS 60,000

Iguazu Falls

Iguazu Falls is a massive waterfall system on the Iguazu River, straddling the border between Argentina and Brazil. Comprising over 270 individual cascades, it is one of the largest waterfall systems in the world. The falls are surrounded by lush subtropical rainforest, which is protected within national parks on both sides of the border. Visitors can explore a network of walkways and viewing platforms that offer close-up perspectives of the cascading water.

Free (park entry ARS 60,000)

Garganta Del Diablo

Garganta Del Diablo, or the Devil's Throat, is the largest and most powerful section of the Iguazu Falls. This U-shaped chasm concentrates a massive volume of water, creating a deafening roar and a constant cloud of mist. A dedicated walkway extends across the upper river, allowing visitors to stand directly over the precipice and witness the sheer force of the cascading water. It is the centerpiece of the Iguazu National Park experience.

Free (park entry ARS 60,000)

Lookout Devil's Throat

The Lookout Devil's Throat provides a primary vantage point for observing the power of the Garganta Del Diablo. Positioned at the end of a long walkway over the upper Iguazu River, the observation deck places visitors directly at the edge of the horseshoe-shaped falls. The constant spray and thunderous sound of the water create an immersive environment, highlighting the raw natural forces that shape the surrounding landscape.

ARS 95,000

Paseos de Luna Llena en las Cataratas del Iguazú

Paseos de Luna Llena en las Cataratas del Iguazú offers a unique nighttime experience of the falls during the full moon. This guided tour allows visitors to walk the park's trails and view the Garganta Del Diablo illuminated by moonlight. The nocturnal setting provides a different perspective on the falls, emphasizing the sounds of the jungle and the rushing water. The experience highlights the natural environment of the park outside of regular visiting hours.

ARS 60,000

Iguazú National Park

Iguazú National Park is a public park in Iguazú, Argentina. Records associated with the site include 1934. Iguazú National Park was established in 1934 to protect the area surrounding the Iguazu Falls and the associated subtropical rainforest network. The park include a diverse range of flora and fauna, including jaguars, toucans, and numerous bird species. Paths, lawns, and shaded areas make up the main public facilities.

ARS 1,500

La Aripuca

La Aripuca is an ecological and cultural theme park located in Puerto Iguazú, designed to raise awareness about the preservation of the Atlantic Forest. The main structure is a massive replica of a traditional Guaraní trap, constructed from giant rescued tree trunks. Visitors can explore the grounds, learn about local indigenous cultures, and purchase regional artisanal products.

ARS 15,000

La Casa de Botellas

La Casa de Botellas is a unique eco-friendly attraction in Puerto Iguazú, constructed entirely from recycled plastic bottles and other repurposed materials. The project highlights sustainable building practices and environmental conservation. Visitors can tour the house and its surrounding structures, learning about the innovative techniques used to create functional living spaces from waste.

ARS 30,000

Güirá Oga

Güirá Oga is a wildlife refuge and rehabilitation center located near Puerto Iguazú. The facility focuses on rescuing, treating, and releasing injured or orphaned animals found in the surrounding rainforest, including birds, mammals, and reptiles. Visitors can take guided tours through the forested grounds to learn about the region's biodiversity and the center's conservation efforts. The refuge educates the public about the environmental challenges facing the local network.

Free

Saltos del Arroyo Mbocay

Saltos del Arroyo Mbocay is a series of small waterfalls located in a forested area near Puerto Iguazú. The site offers a quieter, more secluded natural environment compared to the main Iguazu Falls complex. Visitors can hike along trails that follow the Mbocay stream, passing through local vegetation and arriving at the cascades. It provides an accessible natural retreat within close proximity to the town center.
