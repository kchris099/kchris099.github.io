Destination Guide — El Calafate

Explore El Calafate

A Brief History

Originating in the early 20th century as a wool trading outpost, El Calafate was formally established in 1927. The creation of Los Glaciares National Park transformed this remote Patagonian settlement into a global hub for glaciology and exploration of the southern ice fields. Decades of careful preservation have ensured that this profound historical narrative remains a vital part of the modern experience.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

SouthRoad-Patagonia Extrema

SouthRoad-Patagonia Extrema offers specialized outdoor excursions and adventure tours in the rugged landscapes of Patagonia. Based in El Calafate, the operator provides guided treks, off-road vehicle tours, and wildlife observation experiences. The tours are designed to explore the remote and extreme environments of the southern Andes, including glaciers and steppe regions.

Free

Intendencia Parque Nacional Los Glaciares

Los Glaciares National Park, located in the Santa Cruz province of Argentina, is a must-visit destination for nature enthusiasts. The park is famous for the awe-inspiring Perito Moreno Glacier, which stretches 30km in length and 3km in width, with some parts reaching a height of 60m. Visitors can witness the dramatic spectacle of ice sheets breaking off and plunging into the water below from walkways and viewing platforms on Peninsula de Magallanes.

Free

Cerro Frías

Cerro Frías is a prominent hill located near El Calafate, offering panoramic views of the surrounding Patagonian landscape, including Lake Argentino and the Fitz Roy massif. Visitors can explore the area through guided horseback rides, 4x4 tours, or hiking trails. The site provides an accessible vantage point for observing the region's diverse flora and fauna.

Free

Plaza de Los Pioneros

Plaza de Los Pioneros is a central public square in El Calafate, dedicated to the early settlers and pioneers of the region. The plaza features landscaped gardens, seating areas, and historical monuments that commemorate the town's founding. It serves as a focal point for community gatherings and provides a relaxed environment for visitors exploring the town center.

Free

Estancia 25 de Mayo

Estancia 25 de Mayo offers a unique experience in the El Calafate area. It is not just a Patagonian farm, but also functions as a hotel or Airbnb. The main draw of this estancia is its surroundings, making it an ideal choice for visitors seeking beautiful scenery. Guests can enjoy activities such as sheep shearing and learn about the traditional ways of life on the estancia.

Free

Mirador Lago Argentino

Mirador Lago Argentino is a scenic viewpoint offering expansive vistas of Lake Argentino, the largest freshwater lake in Argentina. Located near El Calafate, the observation area allows visitors to appreciate the lake's striking turquoise waters and the distant peaks of the Andes. It is a popular stop for photography and taking in the vast Patagonian landscape.

Free

Reserva Laguna Nimez

Reserva Laguna Nimez is a peaceful natural reserve located near downtown El Calafate, offering a sanctuary for various indigenous bird species. The area is marshy and sits on the shore of Lago Argentino, providing a home to birds like flamingos, swans, ibises, and lapwings. Despite threats from nearby construction and urban expansion, it remains a haven for bird-watchers and offers a relaxing stroll amidst grazing horses and flocks of birds.

Free

Plaza de Niños Ausentes El Calafate

The spacious Plaza de los Niños Ausentes El Calafate playground is situated alongside a body of water, offering ample room for children to engage in physical activity and tire themselves out. The open area provides unobstructed vistas and excellent visibility. Adjacent to the playground are secure trails suitable for walking, biking, and fitness activities. In the mornings, it serves as an ideal location for running while enjoying views of the lake and mountains.

Free

Calafate Night

Calafate Night provides evening entertainment and cultural experiences in the town of El Calafate. The venue features traditional Argentine dining, including Patagonian lamb, accompanied by live music and folklore performances. It offers visitors an opportunity to experience local culture and hospitality after a day of exploring the nearby glaciers and national parks.

Free

Bahía Redonda

Bahía Redonda, a section of Lago Argentino in El Calafate, transforms into the largest natural ice-skating rink in South America during the winter. The annual El Calafate Ice Festival is held here, featuring activities like skating, ice hockey, sledding, and Chueca. Additionally, visitors can explore a bird reserve at the bay's eastern edge. The waterfront offers views of Lake Argentino and grazing horses.
