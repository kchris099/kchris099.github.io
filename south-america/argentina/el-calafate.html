El Calafate

Wool traders opened a settlement here in the early twentieth century; the town was formally laid out in 1927 on the shore of Lago Argentino. Creation of Los Glaciares National Park redirected the local economy from ranching toward ice-field research and expedition logistics. The Perito Moreno glacier front, not a colonial plaza, is what made El Calafate internationally known.

Intendencia Parque Nacional Los Glaciares

Los Glaciares National Park, located in the Santa Cruz province of Argentina, is a must-visit destination for nature enthusiasts. The park is famous for the awe-inspiring Perito Moreno Glacier, which stretches 30km in length and 3km in width, with some parts reaching a height of 60m. Visitors can witness the dramatic spectacle of ice sheets breaking off and plunging into the water below from walkways and viewing platforms on Peninsula de Magallanes.

Reserva Laguna Nimez

Reserva Laguna Nimez is a peaceful natural reserve located near downtown El Calafate, offering a sanctuary for various indigenous bird species. The area is marshy and sits on the shore of Lago Argentino, providing a home to birds like flamingos, swans, ibises, and lapwings. Despite threats from nearby construction and urban expansion, it remains a haven for bird-watchers and offers a relaxing stroll amidst grazing horses and flocks of birds.

Bahía Redonda

Bahía Redonda, a section of Lago Argentino in El Calafate, transforms into the largest natural ice-skating rink in South America during the winter. The annual El Calafate Ice Festival is held here, featuring activities like skating, ice hockey, sledding, and Chueca, visitors can explore a bird reserve at the bay's eastern edge. The waterfront offers views of Lake Argentino and grazing horses.
