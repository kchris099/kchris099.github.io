Salta

Founded in 1582 as a strategic outpost between the Viceroyalty of Peru and the Río de la Plata, Salta grew prosperous by supplying mules and goods to the silver mines of Potosí. During the Argentine War of Independence, local gaucho militias successfully defended the region against Royalist forces. The city retained more of its colonial architecture than most Argentine cities, blending Spanish colonial design with Andean cultural influences. Visitors encounter this distinct heritage at the pink-hued Salta Cathedral, the historic Cabildo, and the High Altitude Archaeological Museum.

July 9th Square

A large central fountain and assorted monuments organize July 9th Square in Salta for picnics and casual walks. Paths, lawns, and shade form the main public layout. The square functions as an everyday civic green rather than a sealed memorial precinct.

02 AdmissionFree

Catedral Basílica de Salta

Salta's pink-fronted cathedral basilica faces the main plaza with twin towers and a gilded interior. Named a National Historic Monument in 1941, it also holds the remains of independence leader Martin Miguel de Guemes. Sunday Mass and feast days fill the nave; quieter hours reveal the painted ceilings and side chapels.

03 AdmissionFree

Plaza Belgrano

Benches and low plantings at Plaza Belgrano in Salta support everyday rest and neighborhood gatherings. Surrounding streets link toward the historic center, so walking routes between churches, markets, and older houses often cross the plaza. Gravel paths and low fencing keep the plan simple to cross.

04 AdmissionFree

Plaza Gral. Don Martin Miguel de Guemes

Named for independence leader Martin Miguel de Guemes, this Salta plaza mixes lawn, paths, and a commemorative setting near July 9th Square. Residents use it for short walks and shade breaks between downtown blocks. The Guemes dedication ties the green space to northwestern Argentina's independence memory rather than a generic park label.

24.7831° S / 65.4111° W

05 AdmissionFree

Monument General Martin Miguel de Guemes

The Monument General Martin Miguel de Guemes, located at the base of San Bernardo Hill in a neighborhood of Salta, pays tribute to an Argentine independence hero and former governor. Erected in 1931 on the spot where he fell in battle, it offers a historical and setting. The surrounding park is popular with locals and provides a great view of the city, especially at sunset.

06 AdmissionFree

Walk Cerro San Bernardo entrance

Stone steps and paved paths begin the pedestrian ascent of Cerro San Bernardo near central Salta, and the route climbs through dry hillside vegetation to the summit gardens and viewpoints, with rest landings along the way. It provides an alternative to the cable car and gives progressively wider views over the city, Lerma Valley, and surrounding mountains.

07 AdmissionFree

San Bernardo's Hill

More than a thousand steps or a cable car from Parque San Martin climb Cerro San Bernardo above Salta. The summit restaurant and gardens sit above a wide city view. Outdoor walkers and cable-car riders share the hill as Salta's main elevated overlook.

Teleférico San Bernardo

Cabins of the Teleferico San Bernardo rise from Parque San Martin to the summit of Cerro San Bernardo above Salta. The upper station opens onto landscaped terraces, fountains, paths, and viewpoints over the city and Lerma Valley; the cable car can be combined with the long stairway on the hillside, allowing different routes for the ascent and descent.

09 AdmissionFree

20 de Febrero Monument

An equestrian figure and surrounding bronze groups commemorate the Battle of Salta at the Monumento 20 de Febrero. The monument stands in a landscaped square near the site associated with the 1813 victory of Manuel Belgrano's army; reliefs, allegorical figures, and inscriptions frame the central pedestal and connect the public space with the province's independence history.

24.7745° S / 65.4150° W

10 AdmissionFree

San Lorenzo Ravine

Quebrada de San Lorenzo is a dramatic forest reserve located just 10 kilometers from the city center, offering a perfect escape into nature. The reserve has lush yungas vegetation with abundant ferns, lichens, orchids, and other epiphytes. Visitors can enjoy bird watching while walking the well-marked trails through the exuberant forest. The area is known for its special magic that attracts all who visit it, with the San Lorenzo River adding to its charm.

24.7227° S / 65.5048° W

11 AdmissionFree

Municipal Reserve Yungas De San Lorenzo

Yungas forest rises along the streams and slopes of the Reserva Municipal Yungas de San Lorenzo west of Salta. Marked trails pass dense vegetation, bird habitat, and viewpoints over the Lerma Valley, with humidity and shade increasing inside the ravines; the reserve protects a transition between urban San Lorenzo and the cloud-forest environment of the eastern Andes.
