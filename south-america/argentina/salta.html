Explore Salta

A Brief History

Founded in 1582 as a strategic outpost between the Viceroyalty of Peru and the Río de la Plata, Salta grew prosperous by supplying mules and goods to the silver mines of Potosí. During the Argentine War of Independence, local gaucho militias successfully defended the region against Royalist forces. The city retained more of its colonial architecture than most Argentine cities, blending Spanish colonial design with Andean cultural influences. Visitors encounter this distinct heritage at the pink-hued Salta Cathedral, the historic Cabildo, and the High Altitude Archaeological Museum.

July 9th Square

July 9th Square is a public park in Salta, Argentina. With its large central fountain and various monuments, the park offers opportunities for activities such as picnicking, hiking, and bird watching. Paths, lawns, and shaded areas make up the main public facilities.

Free

Catedral Basílica de Salta

Catedral Basílica de Salta is a visitor site in Salta, Argentina. Catedral Basílica de Salta is a Roman Catholic cathedral with a striking pink facade and an opulent gilded interior. It was designated as a National Historic Monument in 1941 and holds great significance as the resting place of General Guemes, a national hero who played an important role in Argentina's fight for independence. The main structures and grounds are the principal points of interest.

Free

Plaza Belgrano

Plaza Belgrano is a visitor site in Salta. It remains a notable local point of interest situated in Salta, Argentina. Visitors can observe the surrounding area and its connection to the city's broader historical landscape. Local guides sometimes include it on walking routes through the centre.

Plaza Gral. Don Martin Miguel de Guemes

Plaza Gral. Don Martin Miguel de Guemes is a public park in Salta, Argentina. A leisurely stroll from Plaza Gral. Paths, lawns, and shaded areas make up the main public facilities. Plaza Gral. Don Martin Miguel de Guemes remains part of the public visitor circuit in Salta.

Monument General Martin Miguel de Guemes

The Monument General Martin Miguel de Guemes, located at the base of San Bernardo Hill in a neighborhood of Salta, pays tribute to an Argentine independence hero and former governor. Erected in 1931 on the spot where he fell in battle, it offers a historical and setting. The surrounding park is popular with locals and provides a great view of the city, especially at sunset.

San Bernardo's Hill

San Bernardo's Hill, also known as Cerro San Bernardo, is a popular spot in Salta for outdoor activities and relaxation. It offers panoramic views of the city from its summit, which can be reached by climbing over 1,000 steps or taking a cable car from Parque San Martin. At the top, visitors can enjoy a restaurant with gourmet dining options and explore gardens.

ARS 25,000

Teleférico San Bernardo

The Teleférico San Bernardo is a cable car that takes visitors to the summit of San Bernardo mountain, offering panoramic views of the city. The landscape includes landscaped fountains and forest trails. Visitors have multiple options for their ascent and descent, including walking up the stairs or taking the cable car both ways. At the top, there are gardens and local vendors selling souvenirs from Salta.

Free

20 de Febrero Monument

The Monumento 20 de Febrero is a significant stop on the city tour, paying tribute to the battle of Salta in Plaza Armada Argentina. Visitors can take a half-hour break at the nearby handicraft market, offering an array of trinkets, textiles, and Andean souvenirs. It's a family-friendly spot with a good playground for kids to enjoy.

Free

San Lorenzo Ravine

Quebrada de San Lorenzo is a dramatic forest reserve located just 10 kilometers from the city center, offering a perfect escape into nature. The reserve has lush yungas vegetation with abundant ferns, lichens, orchids, and other epiphytes. Visitors can enjoy bird watching while walking the well-marked trails through the exuberant forest. The area is known for its special magic that attracts all who visit it, with the San Lorenzo River adding to its charm.

Free

Municipal Reserve Yungas De San Lorenzo

Municipal Reserve Yungas De San Lorenzo is a 66-hectare sanctuary located just 12 km from downtown Salta, in the area of San Lorenzo. This reserve is home to the lush mountain jungle or yungas and offers excellent opportunities for bird watching and observing local fauna. One of its popular trails, Sendero de los Miradores, provides an hour-long hike with varying landscapes of the yungas as visitors ascend.
