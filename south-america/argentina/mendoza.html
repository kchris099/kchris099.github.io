Destination Guide — Mendoza

Explore Mendoza

A Brief History

Founded by Spanish settlers in 1561, Mendoza thrived as an agricultural oasis before being leveled by an earthquake in 1861. Rebuilt with broad avenues and extensive irrigation canals, it transformed into Argentina's viticulture capital, anchored by its deep rooted wine heritage. Today, this complex historical evolution remains significantly embedded in the civic architecture and vibrant daily life of the community.

Top Sights & Activities

Monument to the Army of the Andes

The impressive Army of the Andes Monument is a in Mendoza. It's well worth the visit for its impressive scale and variety of statues and memorials. However, there are no signs explaining the different parts of the monument, so be prepared to do a little bit of research before you go.

Free

Plaza Pedro del Castillo

Plaza Pedro del Castillo, the oldest square in Mendoza, is a captivating blend of history and culture. Established in 1562 as Plaza de Armas, it served as the heart of the colonial city and witnessed significant events like General San Martin's proclamation in 1817. The square features a striking monument dedicated to its founder, Pedro del Castillo, and is adjacent to the Foundational Area Museum, which narrates Mendoza's rich origins dating back to 1561.

Free

Square Chacras de Coria

Square Chacras de Coria is a charming destination that captures the essence of Mendoza's wine country. This delightful town square features a vibrant Sunday market, where visitors can explore an array of unique handicrafts and local products. Surrounding the plaza are numerous excellent restaurants offering diverse dining options, making it easy to grab a bite after browsing the fair.

Free

Square San Martin

Nestled in the bustling heart of the city's financial district, Plaza San Martin is a verdant oasis that invites visitors to relax amidst its beautifully landscaped gardens and shaded benches. The centerpiece of this charming square is an impressive equestrian statue of General San Martín, who played a pivotal role in Argentina's fight for independence.

Free

Peatonal Sarmiento

Peatonal Sarmiento is a bustling pedestrian street in the heart of Mendoza, lined with cafes, restaurants, and boutique shops. The tree-shaded avenue offers a relaxed atmosphere for dining and socializing, featuring outdoor seating and frequent street performances. It serves as a central artery connecting several of the city's main plazas and commercial districts.

Free

Plaza España

Plaza España is a well-maintained public square located in the Sopocachi neighborhood of La Paz. The plaza features landscaped gardens, seating areas, and a central monument honoring the Spanish author Miguel de Cervantes. It serves as a peaceful gathering place for locals and provides a quiet contrast to the bustling commercial districts of the city.

Free

Plazoleta Pellegrini

The park Plazoleta Pellegrini is a captivating and enchanting destination that unfortunately has been closed for safety reasons, preventing access to the once majestic hot springs. However, there is potential for significant improvements to boost tourism in the area. The presence of excessive craft shops creates an unappealing atmosphere, as their offerings lack quality and creativity.

Free

Plaza Independencia

Plaza Independencia in Mendoza is a vibrant and lively hangout spot, featuring lush greenery, a semicircular fountain, and various vendors. This main square covers 55,000 square meters of green space and boasts rare trees and plants. The centerpiece is the striking Frieze of Independence monument with depictions of significant scenes. Surrounding the plaza are shady pergolas that provide a perfect place to relax and take in the surroundings.

Teatro Independencia

Teatro Independencia, also known as Independence Theatre, stands as the main performing arts venue in Mendoza. It was established as part of a provincial initiative to boost tourism around Independence Square and has become an architectural gem of the city with its neoclassical facade and stylish interior. The theater hosts various cultural and artistic events, making it a must-visit for enthusiasts of architectural photography and performing arts.

Free

Plaza Chile

Plaza Chile is a beautifully landscaped square that serves as a serene oasis in the bustling city. This charming space features a delightful fountain, shade trees, and a statue symbolizing the friendship between Argentina and Chile. It's an ideal spot for families, with children enjoying the nearby playground while older visitors relax with their newspapers under the gentle sun.

Free

Fountain of the Continents

The Fountain of the Continents is a captivating sight where water gracefully dances in harmony with representations of the continents. Originally acquired in Paris and installed in 1911, it symbolizes the four continents known at that time: America, Asia, Europe, and Africa. During harvest season, the water is colored to resemble wine.

Free

Parque General San Martín

Parque General San Martín, the largest urban park in Mendoza, is a sprawling green sanctuary spanning over 400 hectares. It offers visitors a peaceful escape from the city with its lush landscapes, serene lakes, and charming rose gardens. The park is perfect for nature enthusiasts, outdoor lovers, and families looking to immerse themselves in natural beauty.

Free

Portones del Parque General San Martín

San Martin Park is a beautiful place to visit with wrought iron gates, lamps and a flying condor at the entrance. The park is well taken care of and clean. There are many trails to explore within the park and it's a great place for hikers, trekkers and nature lovers.

Free

Cerro Arco Mirador

Cerro Arco Mirador is a popular viewpoint located in the foothills of the Andes near Mendoza. Accessible via a challenging hike or a rough vehicle track, the summit provides sweeping panoramic views of the city of Mendoza and the surrounding vineyards. It is a favored destination for outdoor enthusiasts, paragliders, and those seeking a vantage point over the region.

Free

Parque Central

Parque Central is a vibrant recreational area featuring a lake, an art museum, and a plaza with a large sundial. The park offers various activities such as picnicking, running paths, and sports facilities. Visitors can enjoy rose gardens, kayaking, mountain views, food trucks, and people engaging in different activities. It's well-maintained with people taking care to keep it clean.
