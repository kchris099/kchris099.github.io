Explore Mendoza

A Brief History

Founded by Spanish settlers in 1561, Mendoza grew as an irrigated farm town until an 1861 earthquake flattened much of it. Reconstruction brought wide avenues and canal networks that still shape the grid. From the late nineteenth century the surrounding valleys turned the city into Argentina's main wine center, and bodega culture remains visible in civic buildings, plazas, and day trips into the foothills.

Monument to the Army of the Andes

The Army of the Andes Monument is a in Mendoza. It's well worth the visit for its scale and variety of statues and memorials. However, there are no signs explaining the different parts of the monument, so be prepared to do a little bit of research Before visitors go.

Plaza Pedro del Castillo

Plaza Pedro del Castillo is a public square in Mendoza, Argentina. Records associated with the site include 1562, 1817, 1561. Plaza Pedro del Castillo, the oldest square in Mendoza, is a blend of history and culture. Established in 1562 as Plaza de Armas, it served as the the colonial city and witnessed significant events like General San Martin's proclamation in 1817. Open pavement and surrounding facades define the visitor setting.

Free

Square Chacras de Coria

Square Chacras de Coria is a public square in Mendoza, Argentina. Square Chacras de Coria is a destination that captures the essence of Mendoza's wine country. Surrounding the plaza are numerous excellent restaurants offering diverse dining options, making it easy to grab a bite after browsing the fair. Open pavement and surrounding facades define the visitor setting.

Square San Martin

Square San Martin is a public square in Mendoza, Argentina. The centerpiece of this square is an equestrian statue of General San Martín, who played a important role in Argentina's fight for independence. Open pavement and surrounding facades define the visitor setting.

Free

Peatonal Sarmiento

Peatonal Sarmiento is a busy pedestrian street in Mendoza, lined with cafes, restaurants, and boutique shops. The tree-shaded avenue offers a relaxed atmosphere for dining and socializing, featuring outdoor seating and frequent street performances. It is a central artery connecting several of the city's main plazas and commercial districts.

Free

Plaza España

Plaza España is a public square in Mendoza, Argentina. Plaza España is a well-maintained public square located in the Sopocachi neighborhood of La Paz. It is a peaceful gathering place for locals and provides a quiet contrast to the busy commercial districts of the city. Open pavement and surrounding facades define the visitor setting.

Plazoleta Pellegrini

The park Plazoleta Pellegrini is a and enchanting destination that unfortunately has been closed for safety reasons, preventing access to the once majestic hot springs. However, there is potential for significant improvements to boost tourism in the area. Plaza Independencia

Plaza Independencia is a public square in Mendoza, Argentina. This main square covers 55,000 square meters of green space and has rare trees and plants. The centerpiece is the striking Frieze of Independence monument with depictions of significant scenes. Surrounding the plaza are shady pergolas that provide a place to relax and take in the surroundings. Open pavement and surrounding facades define the visitor setting.

Teatro Independencia

Teatro Independencia, also known as Independence Theatre, is the main performing arts venue in Mendoza. It was established as part of a provincial initiative to boost tourism around Independence Square and has become an architectural gem of the city with its neoclassical facade and stylish interior. The theater hosts various cultural and artistic events, making it a must-visit for enthusiasts of architectural photography and performing arts.

Free

Plaza Chile

Plaza Chile is a public square in Mendoza, Argentina. It's an ideal spot for families, with children enjoying the nearby playground while older visitors relax with their newspapers under the gentle sun. Open pavement and surrounding facades define the visitor setting.

Free

Fountain of the Continents

The Fountain of the Continents is a sight where water gracefully dances in harmony with representations of the continents. Originally acquired in Paris and installed in 1911, it symbolizes the four continents known at that time: America, Asia, Europe, and Africa. During harvest season, the water is colored to resemble wine.

Free

Parque General San Martín

Parque General San Martín is a visitor site in Mendoza, Argentina. Parque General San Martín, the largest urban park in Mendoza, is a large green sanctuary spanning over 400 hectares. The park is perfect for nature enthusiasts, outdoor lovers, and families looking to immerse themselves in natural beauty. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Free

Portones del Parque General San Martín

Portones del Parque General San Martín is a harbour in Mendoza, Argentina. San Martin Park is a place to visit with wrought iron gates, lamps and a flying condor at the entrance. The park is well taken care of and clean. There are many trails to explore within the park and it's a great place for hikers, trekkers and nature lovers. Quay and embankment paths form the main walking line.

Free

Cerro Arco Mirador

Cerro Arco Mirador is a popular viewpoint located in the foothills of the Andes near Mendoza. Accessible via a challenging hike or a rough vehicle track, the summit provides sweeping panoramic views of the city of Mendoza and the surrounding vineyards. It is a favored destination for outdoor enthusiasts, paragliders, and those seeking a vantage point over the region.

Free

Parque Central

Parque Central is a active recreational area featuring a lake, an art museum, and a plaza with a large sundial. The park offers various activities such as picnicking, running paths, and sports facilities. Visitors can enjoy rose gardens, kayaking, mountain views, food trucks, and people engaging in different activities. It's well-maintained with people taking care to keep it clean.
