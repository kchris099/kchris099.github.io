Destination Guide — Bariloche

Explore Bariloche

A Brief History

San Carlos de Bariloche was founded in 1902 on the shores of Nahuel Huapi Lake. It developed rapidly after the arrival of the railway in 1934 and the establishment of Nahuel Huapi National Park. European immigrants, particularly from Switzerland and Germany, heavily influenced its alpine architecture and chocolate industry. Today, it is a premier destination for skiing and outdoor recreation in Patagonia.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

Centro Cívico Bariloche

Centro Cívico Bariloche is the focal point of the town, featuring Alpine-inspired architecture and a museum that showcases the region's history. The buildings, constructed from timber and local stone, were designed in 1939 by Ernesto de Estrada in collaboration with renowned architect Alejandro Bustillo. At the main plaza, an equestrian statue of General Roca stands amidst graffiti art.

Free

Plaza Italia

Plaza Italia is a vibrant public square located in the Palermo neighborhood of Buenos Aires. It serves as a major transportation hub and is surrounded by notable attractions such as the Buenos Aires Zoo, the Botanical Garden, and the Rural Society exhibition grounds. The plaza features a central monument dedicated to Giuseppe Garibaldi, reflecting the city's Italian heritage.

Free

Cathedral Our Lady of Nahuel Huapi

The Cathedral Our Lady of Nahuel Huapi, also known as Nuestra Senora de Nahuel Huapi, is a monumental neo-gothic building located in Bariloche next to the lake. Designed by architect Alejandro Bustillo and completed in 1947, this cathedral boasts impressive walls, a gigantic dome, and stained-glass windows depicting biblical stories and historical figures from the area.

5000 ARS

Bariloche Paleontological Museum

The Bariloche Paleontological Museum is a fascinating destination for those interested in the region's natural history. The museum showcases a variety of regional fossils, including dinosaur skeletons that are hundreds of millions of years old. Visitors can explore exhibits featuring ancient artifacts and even a dinosaur egg. Although the museum is small, it offers well-organized displays and friendly hosts who provide insightful explanations about the exhibits.

Free

Plaza Belgrano

Plaza Belgrano sits within the Centro Cívico Bariloche area of Bariloche, documented in local heritage registers for its civic or cultural role. Visitors approach from the main access road and combine the visit with neighbouring heritage buildings and public spaces in the district.

Free

Cascada de los Duendes

Cascada de Los Duendes is a picturesque trail in the Gutierrez Lake Sector of Nahuel Huapi National Park. The route combines two short trails, leading to the beautiful double waterfall hidden in the forest. After enjoying the cascading waters, hikers can continue along a slightly sloping path to reach Mirador del Lago Gutierrez, offering panoramic views of the area. This easily accessible walk is suitable for families and can be enjoyed year-round with minimal difficulty.

Free

Mirador Lago Gutierrez

Mirador Lago Gutierrez is a family-friendly hike that takes you through forests, past rivers and waterfalls, and up to a rocky bluff with panoramic views of Lake Gutierrez and the surrounding mountains. The easy walking path takes about 20-30 minutes at a regular pace, making it accessible for most visitors. It's recommended to bring along a small picnic or beverage to enjoy while taking in the scenery.

60000 ARS

Teleférico Cerro Otto

Teleférico Cerro Otto offers a thrilling cable car ride to a peak with views of the lake. At the top, visitors can enjoy a rotating restaurant, dance club, and suspension bridge. The area is covered in snow with various levels of difficulty for sled tracks and snowshoe hikes through the forest. Expert guides provide insights into the flora, fauna, and geography. There are special tracks for children as well.

Free (meal 40000 ARS)

Experiencia Patacon by Lucho Remiro

Experiencia Patacon by Lucho Remiro is a must-visit for those who want to immerse themselves in culture and enjoy delicious food. The place offers great atmosphere, friendly people, and amazing gourmet experiences. The portions are generous, the homemade liquors are a delightful surprise, and the staff members like Lauty and Pablo provide excellent service. Visitors can expect delicious food with unexpected but pleasant surprises, all served with warmth and kindness.

Free (cable car 60000 ARS)

Cerro Otto

Cerro Otto, located within Nahuel Huapi National Park, is a mountain that offers panoramic views of Lago Nahuel Huapi and the Andes from its 1,405-meter summit. Accessible by car, foot, or cable car, it provides hiking and biking trails in addition to seasonal cross-country ski trails. While the cable cars may undergo maintenance at times, visitors can still enjoy the scenery and outdoor activities such as skiing during winter.
