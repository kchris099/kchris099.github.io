Ushuaia

Argentina founded a penal colony at Ushuaia in 1884 to stake a claim on Tierra del Fuego. Naval yards and, later, Antarctic cruise traffic replaced the prison economy. The former jail museum and the Beagle Channel waterfront tell that shift from exile outpost to southern embarkation port. Cruise ships now use the same channel the convicts once faced.

Plaza Islands Malvinas

Plaza Islands Malvinas, also known as Las Islas Malvinas, is a public square in Ushuaia, Argentina. Plaza Islands Malvinas is a striking memorial dedicated to the fallen soldiers of the Falklands War, also known as Las Islas Malvinas. The Heroes de Malvinas memorial stands at the plaza, honoring the 649 Argentine servicemen who lost their lives in the conflict. Open pavement and surrounding facades define the visitor setting.

Cartel Ushuaia

The Cartel Ushuaia is a prominent sign located along the waterfront of Ushuaia, marking the city as the 'End of the World' (Fin del Mundo). The colorful sign is a popular photo opportunity for visitors, symbolizing their arrival at the southernmost city in the world. It stands against the backdrop of the Beagle Channel and the surrounding snow-capped mountains.

Parque Nacional Tierra del Fuego

Parque Nacional Tierra del Fuego protects a diverse subantarctic network of forests, mountains, and coastal areas at the southern tip of Argentina. Established in 1960, the park offers numerous hiking trails, scenic viewpoints, and opportunities to observe local wildlife such as the Andean fox and various bird species. It is the only Argentine national park with a maritime coastline.

Glaciar Vinciguerra

Glaciar Vinciguerra lies in the Martial range above Ushuaia and feeds meltwater toward the Beagle Channel. Marked trails from the Martial Glacier area lead to viewpoints of the ice tongue and surrounding peaks. Weather changes quickly at altitude, so sturdy footwear and layers are standard for day hikes. The glacier is a clear example of Tierra del Fuego's coastal Andean icefields within reach of town.

Bridal Veil waterfall Ushuaia is a waterfall in Ushuaia, Argentina. The Bridal Veil waterfall (Cascada Velo de Novia) is a cascade located near Ushuaia, accessible via a scenic hiking trail through the dense Fuegian forest. The waterfall is formed by glacial meltwater tumbling over a rocky cliff, creating a delicate, veil-like appearance. Marked tracks lead to viewing platforms or overlooks.
