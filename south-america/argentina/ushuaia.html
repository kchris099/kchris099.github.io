Destination Guide — Patagonia

Explore Ushuaia

A Brief History

Established in 1884 as a remote penal colony, Ushuaia's harsh landscape served as an Argentine sovereign outpost in Tierra del Fuego. The mid-20th century brought naval expansion and tourism, reshaping the world's southernmost city into a bustling Antarctic expedition gateway. Decades of careful preservation have ensured that this profound historical narrative remains a vital part of the modern experience.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

Plaza Islands Malvinas

Plaza Islands Malvinas is a striking memorial dedicated to the fallen soldiers of the Falklands War, also known as Las Islas Malvinas. The Heroes de Malvinas memorial stands at the plaza, honoring the 649 Argentine servicemen who lost their lives in the conflict. The site features a Wall of Remembrance with engraved names of the fallen and an eternal flame. Visitors describe it as a beautiful and memorable place, carefully maintained for reflection and remembrance.

Free

Cartel Ushuaia

The Cartel Ushuaia is a prominent sign located along the waterfront of Ushuaia, marking the city as the 'End of the World' (Fin del Mundo). The colorful sign is a popular photo opportunity for visitors, symbolizing their arrival at the southernmost city in the world. It stands against the backdrop of the Beagle Channel and the surrounding snow-capped mountains.

65000 ARS

Southern Fuegian Railway

Southern Fuegian Railway sits within the Southern Fuegian Railway area of Ushuaia, documented in local heritage registers for its civic or cultural role. Visitors approach from the main access road and combine the visit with neighbouring heritage buildings and public spaces in the district.

40000 ARS

Parque Nacional Tierra del Fuego

Parque Nacional Tierra del Fuego protects a diverse subantarctic ecosystem of forests, mountains, and coastal areas at the southern tip of Argentina. Established in 1960, the park offers numerous hiking trails, scenic viewpoints, and opportunities to observe local wildlife such as the Andean fox and various bird species. It is the only Argentine national park with a maritime coastline.

44000 ARS

Museo Marítimo y del Presidio de Ushuaia

The Museo Marítimo y del Presidio de Ushuaia is a historic 19th-century prison building that now houses multiple museums. Located on the grounds of Ushuaia's naval base, it features the Maritime Museum, which showcases indigenous canoe-making skills, navigational history of Tierra del Fuego and Cape Horn, Antarctic exploration, and even displays on famous jails worldwide.

Free

Muelle Turístico

The Muelle Turístico is the main tourist pier in Ushuaia, serving as the departure point for boat tours exploring the Beagle Channel. From here, visitors embark on excursions to see the Les Eclaireurs Lighthouse, sea lion colonies, and penguin rookeries. The bustling pier area also features ticket offices, souvenir shops, and views of the harbor and surrounding mountains.

Free

Glaciar Vinciguerra

Long Beach in Ushuaia is a rocky and windy beach that offers amazing mountain views and fabulous vegetation. It's not very big, but it's a nice place for a walk, with easy access and ideal for a peaceful picnic. Visitors can park their car nearby and take a leisurely stroll along the beach. The area is considered one of the high points of a Patagonia trip, offering amazing views of Ushuaia and the Beagle Channel.

Free

Steep Beacon

Steep Beacon sits within the Steep Beacon area of Ushuaia, documented in local heritage registers for its civic or cultural role. Visitors approach from the main access road and combine the visit with neighbouring heritage buildings and public spaces in the district.

Free

Bridal Veil waterfall Ushuaia

The Bridal Veil waterfall (Cascada Velo de Novia) is a picturesque cascade located near Ushuaia, accessible via a scenic hiking trail through the dense Fuegian forest. The waterfall is formed by glacial meltwater tumbling over a rocky cliff, creating a delicate, veil-like appearance. The surrounding area offers a tranquil natural setting for hiking and photography.

Free

Laguna Turquesa

Laguna Turquesa is a striking alpine lake situated in the mountains near Ushuaia, known for its vibrant turquoise waters. The lake is reached via a steep and challenging hike through native forests and rocky terrain. The effort is rewarded with impressive views of the pristine glacial lake surrounded by dramatic peaks, offering a remote and serene wilderness experience.
