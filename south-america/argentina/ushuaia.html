Ushuaia

Argentina founded a penal colony at Ushuaia in 1884 to stake a claim on Tierra del Fuego. Naval yards and, later, Antarctic cruise traffic replaced the prison economy. The former jail museum and the Beagle Channel waterfront tell that shift from exile outpost to southern embarkation port. Cruise ships now use the same channel the convicts once faced.

Plaza Islands Malvinas

The Heroes de Malvinas memorial at Plaza Islands Malvinas in Ushuaia honors the 649 Argentine servicemen killed in the Falklands War. Open pavement and surrounding facades frame the commemorative setting. The plaza reads as a war memorial floor at the southern city's edge.

02 AdmissionFree

Cartel Ushuaia

The Cartel Ushuaia is a prominent sign located along the waterfront of Ushuaia, marking the city as the 'End of the World' (Fin del Mundo). The colorful sign is a popular photo opportunity for visitors, symbolizing their arrival at the southernmost city in the world. It stands against the backdrop of the Beagle Channel and the surrounding snow-capped mountains.

Southern Fuegian Railway

Steam locomotives follow a narrow-gauge line from the outskirts of Ushuaia into Tierra del Fuego National Park. The route revives part of the railway once used by prisoners to haul timber from the forests, with commentary and stops along the Pipo River valley. Known as the Train at the End of the World, the service combines transport history with views of peatland, woodland, and low mountains.

54.8334° S / 68.4230° W

Parque Nacional Tierra del Fuego

Forests, peat bogs, mountains, lakes and channels of the Beagle coast meet within Tierra del Fuego National Park west of Ushuaia. Roads and trails reach Lapataia Bay, Ensenada Zaratiegui and inland valleys shaped by glaciers. Established in 1960, the park protects a rare Argentine landscape where subantarctic woodland extends directly to the sea.

Museo Marítimo y del Presidio de Ushuaia

Ushuaia's former prison, a nineteenth-century compound, now houses maritime and penitentiary museums under one roof. Cell blocks, ship models, and Antarctic expedition displays share the restored wings. The waterfront setting recalls how the jail once marked the southern edge of Argentina's penal system. Model ships and cell reconstructions share corridors in the old wings.

06 AdmissionFree

Muelle Turístico

The Muelle Turistico is Ushuaia's main tourist pier for Beagle Channel boat departures. Excursions leave here for Les Eclaireurs Lighthouse, sea lion rocks, and seasonal penguin colonies. Cruise and catamaran traffic keep the quay busy, with ticket booths and waiting shelters along the boards.

07 AdmissionFree

Glaciar Vinciguerra

Glaciar Vinciguerra lies in the Martial range above Ushuaia and feeds meltwater toward the Beagle Channel. Marked trails from the Martial Glacier area lead to viewpoints of the ice tongue and surrounding peaks. Weather changes quickly at altitude, so sturdy footwear and layers are standard for day hikes. The glacier is a clear example of Tierra del Fuego's coastal Andean icefields within reach of town.

54.7265° S / 68.3363° W

Steep Beacon

Baliza Escarpados stands above the Beagle Channel at the end of the Playa Larga road, where a coastal trail continues toward Estancia Tunel and Rio Encajonado. The modest navigation marker is better known for its remote shoreline setting than for its height. Forested slopes, exposed coast and views across the channel define this eastern edge of Ushuaia's trail network.

54.8182° S / 68.1872° W

Bridal Veil waterfall Ushuaia

Cascada Velo de Novia near Ushuaia drops glacial meltwater over rock in a veil-like fall reached through dense Fuegian forest. Marked trails lead to viewing platforms along the approach. The cascade sits outside the town core as a short forest hike destination.

54.7711° S / 68.1996° W

10 AdmissionFree

Laguna Turquesa

Laguna Turquesa is a high alpine lake inland from Ushuaia, reached by a steep hike through lenga forest and scree. The water's blue-green color comes from glacial silt suspended in the basin. Most walkers treat the lagoon as a day destination, turning around after photographs from the shore rocks. Weather shifts fast; cloud can erase the turquoise color within minutes.

54.7339° S / 68.1439° W
