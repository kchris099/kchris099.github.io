Trujillo

Trujillo was founded by Spanish conquistador Diego de Almagro in 1534 and named after Francisco Pizarro's birthplace. Before Spanish arrival, the region was the center of the Moche and Chimu cultures, home to the massive adobe city of Chan Chan and the Huacas del Sol y de la Luna. During the colonial era, Trujillo became a wealthy administrative center, known for its elegant mansions and churches. Today, it is celebrated for its rich archaeological heritage and active marinera dance traditions.

PLAZA DE ARMAS

Plaza de Armas is a historic gem that marks the birthplace of the city, established by Spanish colonizers in 1534. This active square also holds significant historical importance as the site where Trujillo declared its independence on December 29, 1820. At its heart stands the striking Monumento a La Libertad, crafted by sculptor Edmund Moeller.

Plazuela El Recreo

Plazuela El Recreo is a visitor site in Trujillo, Peru. Plazuela El Recreo is a pedestrian zone in Trujillo, surrounded by active buildings and adorned with fountains. It's home to the first fountain of Trujillo, crafted from Italian Baroque marble. This well-maintained area is encircled by colonial buildings and offers a glimpse into the city's history, including an intriguing underground system from past centuries. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Trujillo Cathedral Basilica

Trujillo Cathedral Basilica is a religious building in Trujillo, Peru. Construction and early use date to around 1646. The Trujillo Cathedral Basilica, a example of Churrigueresque architecture, was originally constructed in 1646 and has an eye-catching yellow facade that draws visitors in. The exterior and main interior spaces are usually viewable outside service times.

Plaza de Armas of Trujillo

The Plaza de Armas of Trujillo is one of the largest and most elegant main squares in Peru. It is surrounded by brightly colored colonial mansions featuring distinctive wrought-iron window grilles, and the yellow facade of the Trujillo Cathedral. The center of the plaza is dominated by the Freedom Monument, celebrating the city's independence.

Trujillo Botanical Garden

The Jardín Botánico de Trujillo is an 2,500 square meter botanical garden that showcases a diverse collection of wildlife such as pigeons, peacocks, parrots, rabbits, turtles, small fish, and geese, it has approximately 400 distinct plant species. Situated roughly 2 km away from the Plaza de Armas, this garden provides an ideal retreat for nature enthusiasts to unwind.

La Huaca de la Luna is a large pyramid in Trujillo, Peru, built by the Moche civilization as a site for worship and human sacrifice. The town was once the capital of both the Moche and Chimu civilizations, with each leaving incredible legacies showing their advancements.

La Huaca del Sol

La Huaca del Sol is a visitor site in Trujillo, Peru. La Huaca del Sol is a significant archaeological site in the region, built by the Moche civilization. It served as a royal residence, ceremony site, and burial chamber. The complex includes the Temples of the Sun and the Moon, with the latter featuring phenomenal multicolored friezes. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Huaca "El Dragon", Complejo Arqueológico Chan-Chan

Huaca "El Dragon", Complejo Arqueológico Chan-Chan is a visitor site in Trujillo, Peru. Huaca El Dragón, also known as Huaca Arco Iris, is a well-preserved adobe pyramid located near the vast Chan Chan archaeological complex. Built by the Chimú culture, the structure is notable for its detailed friezes depicting stylized dragons or rainbows, which are believed to be associated with fertility and water rituals. The main structures and grounds are the principal points of interest.

Chan Chan

Chan Chan is a significant pre-Columbian archaeological site in America, known for its vast adobe complex adorned with art and carvings. Located just 10 minutes from Huanchaco, it attracts numerous tourists daily. The ruins are part of a larger adventure that includes nearby sites like the Huacas del Sol y de la Luna and Huaca de Bruja. For surfers, the proximity to Huanchaco's known surf makes it an appealing destination.
