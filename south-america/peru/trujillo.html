Destination Guide — Trujillo

Explore Trujillo

A Brief History

Trujillo was founded by Spanish conquistador Diego de Almagro in 1534 and named after Francisco Pizarro's birthplace. Before Spanish arrival, the region was the center of the Moche and Chimu cultures, home to the massive adobe city of Chan Chan and the Huacas del Sol y de la Luna. During the colonial era, Trujillo became a wealthy administrative center, known for its elegant mansions and churches. Today, it is celebrated for its rich archaeological heritage and vibrant marinera dance traditions.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

PLAZA DE ARMAS

Plaza de Armas is a historic gem that marks the birthplace of the city, established by Spanish colonizers in 1534. This vibrant square also holds significant historical importance as the site where Trujillo declared its independence on December 29, 1820. At its heart stands the striking Monumento a La Libertad, crafted by sculptor Edmund Moeller.

Free

Plazuela El Recreo

Plazuela El Recreo is a charming pedestrian zone in Trujillo, surrounded by vibrant buildings and adorned with fountains. It's home to the first fountain of Trujillo, crafted from Italian Baroque marble. The square also features the imposing doorway that leads to the mountains and an old pool that once graced Plaza Mayor. This well-maintained area is encircled by colonial buildings and offers a glimpse into the city's history, including an intriguing underground system from past centuries.

Free

Trujillo Cathedral Basilica

The Trujillo Cathedral Basilica, a example of Churrigueresque architecture, was originally constructed in 1646 and features an eye-catching yellow facade that draws visitors in. As you explore this historic site, be guided through its rich history and artistic significance, including works by the renowned German artist Edmund Moeller. The cathedral is not just a place of worship; it also houses a museum showcasing religious artwork that adds depth to your visit.

Free

Plaza de Armas of Trujillo

The Plaza de Armas of Trujillo is one of the largest and most elegant main squares in Peru. It is surrounded by brightly colored colonial mansions featuring distinctive wrought-iron window grilles, and the impressive yellow facade of the Trujillo Cathedral. The center of the plaza is dominated by the Freedom Monument, celebrating the city's independence.

Free

Real Plaza Trujillo

Real Plaza Trujillo stands out as a vibrant shopping destination in Peru, boasting over 30 diverse establishments that cater to various tastes and needs. With its extensive range of stores, including well-known brands and a large grocery store, visitors can enjoy a fulfilling shopping experience. The food court offers an array of dining options, making it perfect for families or friends looking to grab a bite after shopping.

Free

Trujillo Botanical Garden

The Jardín Botánico de Trujillo is an impressive 2,500 square meter botanical garden that showcases a diverse collection of wildlife such as pigeons, peacocks, parrots, rabbits, turtles, small fish, and geese. Additionally, it boasts approximately 400 distinct plant species. Situated roughly 2 km away from the Plaza de Armas, this garden provides an ideal retreat for nature enthusiasts to unwind.

Free

La Huaca de la Luna

La Huaca de la Luna is a large pyramid in Trujillo, Peru, built by the Moche civilization as a site for worship and human sacrifice. The town was once the capital of both the Moche and Chimu civilizations, with each leaving incredible legacies showcasing their advancements.

Free

La Huaca del Sol

La Huaca del Sol is a significant archaeological site in the region, built by the Moche civilization. It served as a royal residence, ceremony site, and burial chamber. The complex includes the Temples of the Sun and the Moon, with the latter featuring phenomenal multicolored friezes. While the larger Huaca del Sol is closed to visitors, La Huaca de la Luna offers well-preserved structures and remarkable polychrome murals on its multi-story wall.

Free

Huaca "El Dragon", Complejo Arqueológico Chan-Chan

Huaca El Dragón, also known as Huaca Arco Iris, is a well-preserved adobe pyramid located near the vast Chan Chan archaeological complex. Built by the Chimú culture, the structure is notable for its intricate friezes depicting stylized dragons or rainbows, which are believed to be associated with fertility and water rituals. The site offers valuable insights into the religious and artistic practices of the pre-Inca civilization.

Free

Chan Chan

Chan Chan is a significant pre-Columbian archaeological site in America, known for its vast adobe complex adorned with art and carvings. Located just 10 minutes from Huanchaco, it attracts numerous tourists daily. The ruins are part of a larger adventure that includes nearby sites like the Huacas del Sol y de la Luna and Huaca de Bruja. For surfers, the proximity to Huanchaco's renowned surf makes it an appealing destination.
