Destination Guide — Arequipa

Explore Arequipa

A Brief History

Built from brilliant white volcanic stone, Arequipa was established by the Spanish in 1540 in a fertile Andean valley. Its formidable colonial monasteries and distinct mestizo baroque architecture emerged as resilient responses to the region's frequent and devastating seismic activity. The remarkable survival of these cultural and architectural milestones provides a powerful window into the region's storied past.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

Plaza de Armas Arequipa

Plaza de Armas Arequipa is a monumental square featuring the city's cathedral and colonial buildings that house various restaurants and cafes. The square offers views of the surrounding area, including a volcano rising behind it. Visitors can enjoy dining at second-floor restaurants with galleries offering picturesque views of the square. Additionally, nearby establishments offer delightful local treats such as ice cream.

Free

Basilica Cathedral of Arequipa

The Basilica Cathedral of Arequipa is a massive Catholic cathedral in Peru, known for its on-site museum displaying religious objects and artwork. Despite being damaged and rebuilt multiple times due to earthquakes over its 500-year history, it remains beautiful Roman Catholic churches in the country. Situated in a popular tourist area near landmarks like the Santa Cantalina monastery and Colca Canyon, it attracts many visitors.

Free

Church of the Company of Jesus

Church of the Company, also known as Iglesia de la Compania, is a small baroque church located in the historic center of Arequipa. This monument is considered splendid creations of Peruvian Baroque and a starting point for the regional art. The elaborate carved facade features reliefs of animals, plants, and Christian figures. Inside, visitors can admire an interior chapel adorned with jungle motifs and an exaggerated baroque altar.

Free

Mundo Alpaca

Mundo Alpaca is a must-visit museum and boutique in Arequipa, offering a unique experience with llamas and alpacas. Visitors can explore the habitat of these fascinating creatures and learn about their significance through detailed explanations. The museum also features textile exhibits showcasing items made from alpaca wool, providing an opportunity to purchase souvenirs.

Free

Plaza de Yanahuara

Yanahuara Plaza is a large, peaceful park with palm and flower-lined paths. It's a great place to relax and take in the view of El Misti volcano. Mario Vargas Llosa Palace is located nearby, making it an excellent location for business meetings or photo shoots.

Free

Sabandía mill

The Sabandía Mill (Molino de Sabandía) is a beautifully restored, 17th-century water mill located in the lush countryside just outside Arequipa. Constructed from the region's characteristic white sillar stone, the mill is surrounded by weeping willows and traditional agricultural terraces. It offers a glimpse into the area's colonial agricultural history and a peaceful rural retreat.

Free

Santa Catalina Monastery

Nestled in the heart of Arequipa, Santa Catalina Monastery is a 16th-century complex that offers visitors a glimpse into Peru's rich history. Founded in 1580 by a wealthy Spanish widow, this vibrant site was once an exclusive nunnery and has since transformed into a captivating tourist attraction. Surrounded by imposing stone walls reminiscent of a fortress, it features charming streets adorned with colorful flowers and colonial architecture that transport you back in time.

Free

Free Tour Downtown Arequipa

Discover the charm of Arequipa with the Free Tour Downtown Arequipa, led by knowledgeable and entertaining guides. Explore the city's highlights, including free lookout points for sunsets and a taste of Peruvian chocolate and drinks. The guides are passionate about showcasing their city and culture, providing valuable recommendations for further exploration.

Free

Museum of Virreinal Art Santa Teresa

The Museum of Virreinal Art Santa Teresa is a colonial-era convent and museum located in Arequipa, Peru. It houses over 300 works of art, including paintings, porcelain, and liturgical items from the 16th and 17th centuries. The museum also features peaceful gardens, an ornate sanctuary, and offers guided tours for a better understanding of its history.

Free

Mercado San Camilo

Arequipa's Mercado San Camilo is a must-visit, steeped in tradition and history as a Monumental Historical Heritage site. Nestled in the historic center, it's beloved by locals and travelers alike for its labyrinthine passageways offering everything from exotic fruits to local culinary delights. The market boasts an authentic and budget-friendly dining experience, making it an essential stop for those seeking local cuisine.

Free

Templo de San Agustín

Iglesia de San Agustín, situated on a street of the same name, is a remarkable example of Baroque architecture dating back to 1575. Despite enduring significant damage from the powerful earthquake in 1868, the church underwent extensive restoration in the 19th century. This included the addition of Neoclassical embellishments to both its interior and facade. The church is admired for its grandeur and cleanliness, making it a beloved attraction in Arequipa.
