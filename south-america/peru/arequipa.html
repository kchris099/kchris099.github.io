Arequipa

Spanish founders in 1540 built with white sillar volcanic stone in an Andean valley prone to quakes. Monasteries such as Santa Catalina and mestizo baroque façades were rebuilt after successive tremors. Seismic resilience, not coastal trade, shaped the White City's fabric. El Misti volcano's silhouette explains both the sillar quarries and the seismic threat.
