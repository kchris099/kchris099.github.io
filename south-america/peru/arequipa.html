Explore Arequipa

A Brief History

Built from brilliant white volcanic stone, Arequipa was established by the Spanish in 1540 in a fertile Andean valley. Its formidable colonial monasteries and distinct mestizo baroque architecture emerged as resilient responses to the region's frequent and devastating seismic activity. The survival of these cultural and architectural milestones provides a powerful window into the region's storied past. Museums, markets, and street plans in Arequipa still show layers from those periods.

Plaza de Armas Arequipa

Plaza de Armas Arequipa is a monumental square featuring the city's cathedral and colonial buildings that house various restaurants and cafes. The square offers views of the surrounding area, including a volcano rising behind it. Visitors can enjoy dining at second-floor restaurants with galleries offering views of the square, nearby establishments offer local treats such as ice cream.

Basilica Cathedral of Arequipa

The Basilica Cathedral of Arequipa is a massive Catholic cathedral in Peru, known for its on-site museum displaying religious objects and artwork. Despite being damaged and rebuilt multiple times due to earthquakes over its 500-year history, it remains Roman Catholic churches in the country. Situated in a popular tourist area near landmarks like the Santa Cantalina monastery and Colca Canyon, it attracts many visitors.

Church of the Company of Jesus

Church of the Company of Jesus, also known as Iglesia de la Compania, is a religious building in Arequipa, Peru. Church of the Company, also known as Iglesia de la Compania, is a small baroque church located in the historic center of Arequipa. This monument is considered splendid creations of Peruvian Baroque and a starting point for the regional art. The exterior and main interior spaces are usually viewable outside service times.

Mundo Alpaca is a visitor site in Arequipa, Peru. Mundo Alpaca is a must-visit museum and boutique in Arequipa, offering a unique experience with llamas and alpacas. Plaza de Yanahuara

Yanahuara Plaza is a large, peaceful park with palm and flower-lined paths. It's a great place to relax and take in the view of El Misti volcano. Mario Vargas Llosa Palace is located nearby, making it an excellent location for business meetings or photo shoots.

Free

Sabandía mill

The Sabandía Mill (Molino de Sabandía) is a beautifully restored, 17th-century water mill located in the lush countryside just outside Arequipa. Constructed from the region's characteristic white sillar stone, the mill is surrounded by weeping willows and traditional agricultural terraces. It offers a glimpse into the area's colonial agricultural history and a peaceful rural retreat.

Santa Catalina Monastery

Santa Catalina Monastery is a visitor site in Arequipa, Peru. Construction and early use date to around 1580. Located Arequipa, Santa Catalina Monastery is a 16th-century complex that has a glimpse into Peru's rich history. Founded in 1580 by a wealthy Spanish widow, this active site was once an exclusive nunnery and has since transformed into a tourist attraction. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.
