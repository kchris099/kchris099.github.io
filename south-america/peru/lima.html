Explore Lima

A Brief History

Lima was founded by Francisco Pizarro in 1535 on the Rimac Valley coast as capital of Spanish Peru. Colonial churches, balconied mansions, and the Plaza Mayor anchored viceregal power before independence in 1821. Earthquakes and republican growth reshaped the centre while suburbs spread along the Pacific. Museums, convents, and coastal archaeological sites trace Lima's role as Peru's administrative and cultural capital for nearly five centuries.

Top Sights & Activities

Lima Main Square

Lima Main Square, also known as Plaza de Armas, is the historic heart of Lima and a must-visit for anyone exploring this vibrant city. Established in 1535, it serves as the birthplace of Lima and is surrounded by architectural landmarks like the Government Palace, Lima Cathedral, and Archbishop's Palace.

Free

Palacio Municipal de Lima

The Municipal Palace of Lima, also known as the City Hall, is a neoclassical building that serves as the headquarters of the Metropolitan Municipality of Lima. It houses important government officials and is a place where significant decisions are made. The palace features various rooms, including the Atahualpa Room, which holds historical paintings and artifacts. Situated in Lima's Plaza de Armas, it stands as a historical and architectural monument.

Free

Government Palace of Peru

The Government Palace of Peru, also known as the House of Pizarro, is a historic building located on the northern side of the Plaza de Armas. Originally the residence of Francisco Pizarro, it has served as the headquarters for various Peruvian governors, Viceroys, and Presidents. The palace features well-preserved rooms and hosts daily Changing of the Guard ceremonies in its courtyard at 11:45 am.

S / 40

Casa de Aliaga

Casa de Aliaga is a historic mansion in Lima, Peru, dating back to 1535 and considered the oldest dwelling in the Americas. It has been continuously inhabited by the same noble family since its founding, showcasing a mix of architectural styles from the 16th to the 19th century. The house reflects the maximalist sensibilities of Peruvian aristocracy and features Baroque, Moorish revival, Art Nouveau, Neo-Baroque, and Beaux-Arts elements.

Free

Alameda Chabuca Granda

Alameda Chabuca Granda is a bustling park in Lima's historic center, named after the renowned Peruvian singer and songwriter Chabuca Granda. Visitors can stroll along the Rimac River boardwalk and enjoy various fairs, festivals, and performances including dance, comedy, and concerts. The park offers a unique viewpoint with a red sculpture at its center, providing an overview of the city.

Basilica and Convent of Santo Domingo, Lima

The Basilica and Convent of Santo Domingo in Lima is a 16th-century religious site that showcases the rich history and architectural beauty of colonial Peru. Nestled just a block from the Plaza de Armas, this convent has been home to notable figures like Santa Rosa de Lima and Fray Martin de Porres. Visitors can wander through its verdant inner courtyards adorned with Spanish tiles, admire exquisite religious art, and delve into its impressive library filled with rare manuscripts.

Archdiocesan Shrine of the Heart of Jesus - San Pedro

The Archdiocesan Shrine of the Heart of Jesus - San Pedro is a 17th-century church located in the heart of Lima. Its baroque-style interior features intricate altars and gilded details, making it a picturesque spot for visitors exploring downtown. Built by the Jesuits and painted in a charming pale yellow and white, this colonial church is flanked by two tall bell towers.

Free

San Martin Square

St Martin Square, located in Lima's UNESCO-listed Historic Centre, is a significant urban gathering space featuring a central monument of José de San Martín on horseback. Known as the Protector of Peru for his role in South America's fight for independence from Spain, the square holds historical significance. Illuminated in the evenings, it offers a beautiful ambiance. The square was built early in the 20th century and is considered Cultural Heritage of Humanity.

Free

Iglesia Las Nazarenas

Iglesia Las Nazarenas, or the Church of the Nazarene, is a remarkable historical site nestled in Lima, Peru. Established in 1771 under Viceroy Amat's guidance, this church stands on land once inhabited by freed Angolan slaves. A captivating mural of Christ painted by one of these former slaves remains a focal point within its walls, having survived a devastating earthquake in 1655.

Free

Quinta Heeren

Quinta Heeren is a 19th-century mansion in Lima's Barranco district, built for German merchant Otto Heeren and later used as a cultural centre. The wooden balconies, garden patios, and period rooms recall Barranco's era as a seaside resort for Lima's elite. Exhibitions and concerts now fill the house beside the Bajada de Baños promenade.

Free

Bridge of Sighs

The Bridge of Sighs, or Puente de los Suspiros, is a wooden structure built in 1876 that has become a beloved landmark in Lima's Barranco district. This picturesque bridge offers not only views of the city but also serves as a perfect spot for people-watching and capturing memorable photos.

Free

Barranco Municipal Park

Municipal de Barranco Park, located in the upscale Barranco district of Lima, Peru, is a charming tree-lined park that offers a variety of attractions. The park features elegant statuary, paved paths for leisurely strolls, and a colonnaded stage where weekend events take place.

Free

Larcomar

Nestled atop the cliffs of Miraflores, Larcomar is a vibrant outdoor shopping mall that seamlessly blends luxury with views of the Pacific Ocean. This upscale destination boasts an array of eateries, from casual dining to fine restaurants, alongside chic boutiques and gift shops. Families can enjoy a playground area for kids while movie enthusiasts can catch the latest films in its impressive cinema complex.

Free

Love Park

LOVE Park, officially named John F. Kennedy Plaza, is a prominent public space located near Philadelphia City Hall. It is famous worldwide for Robert Indiana's iconic 'LOVE' sculpture, which serves as a very popular photo backdrop. The park features a large central fountain, paved walkways, and is a frequent site for food trucks and civic events.

Free

Navy Lighthouse

Marine Lighthouse is a cliffside beacon featuring a 72-ft. iron tower that emits light visible for 18 nautical miles. Situated in Miraflores, it offers ocean and cliff views, surrounded by a well-maintained park perfect for leisurely strolls or cycling. The area also boasts cultural events and nearby dining options ranging from casual to upscale, making it an ideal spot for a family outing or a romantic walk.

Free

Maria Reiche Park

Nestled along the Malecon de la Marina in Miraflores, Maria Reiche Park is a serene oasis that pays homage to the renowned German archaeologist who devoted her life to studying the Nazca Lines. This expansive park features vibrant floral totemic figures reminiscent of those ancient geoglyphs and offers views of the Pacific Ocean.

S / 12

Park of the Legends Zoo

Park of the Legends Zoo, located in Pueblo Libre, Peru, is a unique attraction that combines archaeology and biodiversity. The zoo features animals from around the world and is built on the ruins of an ancient city complex. Divided into three sections representing different landscapes of Peru, it showcases over 215 species including mammals, birds, reptiles, amphibians, fish and insects. Visitors can explore a botanical garden and a museum while also admiring ancient ruins within the park.

S / 5

Magic Water Circuit of the Reserve Park

The Magic Water Circuit of the Reserve Park, situated in Lima's Parque de la Reserva, is a captivating attraction featuring 13 fountains that come alive with colorful lights and laser beams at night. This mesmerizing display of water, light, and music offers an enchanting experience for families, art enthusiasts, and anyone seeking visually entertainment. Visitors can enjoy nightly performances showcasing choreographed light displays amidst the dancing waters.

Free

Park of the Exposition

The Parque de la Exposicion or Gran Parque de Lima is a tranquil oasis of peace and tranquility in Lima, amidst the city's noisy and hectic hustle and bustle. The park features gardens, lakes, an amphitheater, as well as buildings such as an art museum. It is adjacent to downtown Lima. Rooms at Miraflores Park Hotel offer either panoramic ocean or city views.

S / 60

Gran Teatro Nacional del Perú

The Gran Teatro Nacional del Perú, inaugurated in 2011, is the largest theater in Peru and a testament to the country's dedication to the arts. It serves as an international platform for showcasing Peruvian talent and hosting productions. The venue has hosted remarkable performances by renowned artists such as Placido Domingo and Erin Wall.

S / 35

Callao Monumental turismo histórico

Callao Monumental turismo histórico is a vibrant and creative zone in the district of Callao, offering a mix of contemporary art galleries, studios, and restaurants. This area has transformed into a popular tourist spot filled with art, music, and color. One of its main attractions is Casa Fugaz, featuring independent restaurants, artisanal fabric boutiques, galleries, and studio spaces for resident artists.

S / 20

Fortress of the real felipe

Real Felipe Fortress, a storied 18th-century fort, stands as a testament to Peru's rich history. Built over 250 years ago to safeguard the Callao harbor and South American gold shipments to Spain, it is the largest fortress constructed by the Spanish Crown outside of Spain. This historical site played a crucial role in protecting the capital from pirate attacks and privateers during colonial times.

S / 350

Condor Xtreme - Paragliding Miraflores

Condor Xtreme - Paragliding Miraflores offers an exhilarating paragliding experience over Lima, suitable for anyone without special skills. The flight is supervised by certified instructors and provides an adrenaline rush while also offering a calming sensation as you soar above the ocean and take in the city landscape and mountains. The setup process is efficient, with equipment provided, including noise-canceling headphones and a GoPro camera to capture the entire experience.

Pucllana Site Museum

Huaca Pucllana Site Museum is an ancient pre-Inca archaeological site situated in Miraflores, offering a captivating journey into Lima's pre-Columbian history. The clay and adobe pyramid complex provides valuable insights into the indigenous cultures that once thrived in the region. Guided tours are available to enhance the understanding of the site's historical significance, making it an educational destination for history enthusiasts, archaeology buffs, and families with older children.

Free

Friendship Park

Nestled in the heart of Lima, Friendship Park, or Parque de la Amistad, is a delightful oasis that promises moments for families and friends alike. This picturesque park boasts a Moorish-inspired arch adorned with vibrant tile mosaics, serving as its centerpiece. Spanning nearly thirty thousand square meters, it features winding walking paths that meander through lush greenery and tranquil ponds.

Free

John F. Kennedy park

Kennedy Park, located in the heart of Miraflores, is a delightful city park honoring President John F. Kennedy. It is renowned as parks in Lima and offers views, pleasant green spaces, and leafy trees for visitors to enjoy while strolling through its winding paths. The park is also home to over 100 stray cats protected by the Municipality of Miraflores and has its own organization dedicated to their well-being.

Free

7th of June Park

7th of June Park, also known as Kennedy Park, is a charming triangular urban oasis in Lima. It pays tribute to President John F. Kennedy and his involvement in the Alliance for Progress movement. The park boasts lush greenery, walking paths, an amphitheater, and a playground. What sets this park apart is its reputation for being home to numerous friendly cats that roam the grounds and provide company to visitors.

Free

Mercado 1 de Surquillo

Mercado No 1 de Surquillo is a traditional neighbourhood market in Lima's Surquillo district, supplying households and restaurants with Peruvian produce. Stalls display Andean potatoes, tropical fruit, fresh seafood, and butcher cuts at local prices. The covered aisles offer an everyday glimpse of Lima's food culture away from tourist-focused historic markets.
