Destination Guide — Chan Chan

Explore Chan Chan

A Brief History

Chan Chan was the vast capital of the Chimú Kingdom, flourishing from the ninth to the fifteenth century before falling to the expanding Inca Empire. As the largest earthen city in the pre-Columbian Americas, it housed an detailed society of artisans, administrators, and kings within a network of massive walled citadels. The city's prosperity relied on an advanced irrigation system that transformed the desert valley into a fertile oasis. Today, visitors navigate the large adobe walls, detailed geometric reliefs, and ceremonial plazas of the Nik An complex.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

PLAZA DE ARMAS

Plaza de Armas is a historic gem that marks the birthplace of the city, established by Spanish colonizers in 1534. This active square also holds significant historical importance as the site where Trujillo declared its independence on December 29, 1820. At its heart stands the striking Monumento a La Libertad, crafted by sculptor Edmund Moeller.

Free

Plazuela El Recreo

Plazuela El Recreo is a public park in Chan Chan, Peru. It's home to the first fountain of Trujillo, crafted from Italian Baroque marble. This well-maintained area is encircled by colonial buildings and offers a glimpse into the city's history, including an intriguing underground system from past centuries. Paths, lawns, and shaded areas make up the main public facilities.

PEN 10

Trujillo Cathedral Basilica

Trujillo Cathedral Basilica is a religious building in Chan Chan, Peru. Construction and early use date to around 1646. The Trujillo Cathedral Basilica, a example of Churrigueresque architecture, was originally constructed in 1646 and has an eye-catching yellow facade that draws visitors in. The exterior and main interior spaces are usually viewable outside service times.

Free

Real Plaza Trujillo

Real Plaza Trujillo is a public square in Chan Chan, Peru. With its extensive range of stores, including well-known brands and a large grocery store, visitors can enjoy a fulfilling shopping experience. The food court offers an array of dining options, making it perfect for families or friends looking to grab a bite after shopping. Open pavement and surrounding facades define the visitor setting.

PEN 5

Trujillo Botanical Garden

The Jardín Botánico de Trujillo is an 2,500 square meter botanical garden that showcases a diverse collection of wildlife such as pigeons, peacocks, parrots, rabbits, turtles, small fish, and geese, it has approximately 400 distinct plant species. Situated roughly 2 km away from the Plaza de Armas, this garden provides an ideal retreat for nature enthusiasts to unwind.

PEN 10

La Huaca de la Luna

La Huaca de la Luna is a large pyramid in Trujillo, Peru, built by the Moche civilization as a site for worship and human sacrifice. The town was once the capital of both the Moche and Chimu civilizations, with each leaving incredible legacies showing their advancements.

PEN 10

La Huaca del Sol

La Huaca del Sol is a visitor site in Chan Chan, Peru. La Huaca del Sol is a significant archaeological site in the region, built by the Moche civilization. It served as a royal residence, ceremony site, and burial chamber. The complex includes the Temples of the Sun and the Moon, with the latter featuring phenomenal multicolored friezes. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

PEN 10

Museo Huacas de Moche

Museo Huacas de Moche is a museum in Chan Chan, Peru. These temples are significant structures in pre-Hispanic Peru, offering insight into ancient civilizations. Galleries and public rooms are open on posted hours in Chan Chan. Museo Huacas de Moche remains part of the public visitor circuit in Chan Chan.

PEN 10

Chan Chan

Chan Chan is a significant pre-Columbian archaeological site in America, known for its vast adobe complex adorned with art and carvings. Located just 10 minutes from Huanchaco, it attracts numerous tourists daily. The ruins are part of a larger adventure that includes nearby sites like the Huacas del Sol y de la Luna and Huaca de Bruja. For surfers, the proximity to Huanchaco's known surf makes it an appealing destination.

PEN 10

Huaca Esmeralda

Located Trujillo, Huaca Esmeralda is an archaeological gem that offers a glimpse into the rich history of the Chimu culture. Although its original name remains a mystery, this site was named after the hacienda that once occupied its grounds. Huaca Esmeralda is a visitor site in Chan Chan, Peru with public access during posted hours.
