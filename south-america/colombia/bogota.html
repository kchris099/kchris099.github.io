Bogotá

Bogotá was founded in 1538 on a plateau of the Andes and became the capital of Gran Colombia and later the Republic of Colombia. La Candelaria's colonial houses, the Gold Museum, and Monserrate hill record Spanish administration, independence under Bolívar, and 20th-century urban growth as a regional financial centre. Municipal archives, parish records, and surviving architecture help explain how the city connected to wider regional trade routes, court patronage, and modern civic rebuilding after industrial and wartime change.
