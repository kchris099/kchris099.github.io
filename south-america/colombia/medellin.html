Destination Guide — Medellín

Explore Medellín

A Brief History

Medellín was founded in the Aburrá Valley in the seventeenth century, but its major expansion occurred during the late nineteenth-century coffee boom and subsequent textile industrialization. Following a period of severe violence linked to the drug cartels in the 1980s and 90s, the city underwent a remarkable urban transformation fueled by innovative public transit and social architecture. This trajectory from industrial hub to crisis and rebirth defines the modern city. Visitors witness this urban resilience through the Metrocable network, the revitalized Comuna 13, and the expansive Plaza Botero.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

Tour Comuna 13

Comuna 13 in Medellin is a prime example of the city's progressive and innovative approach to its underserved areas. This neighborhood, once plagued by crime, has been transformed with the installation of public escalators and colorful street murals adorning its staircases. The area now boasts bars, souvenir shops, and vibrant urban artwork that brightens up the previously dilapidated buildings.

Free

Plaza botero

Plaza Botero is a vibrant public space that showcases an impressive collection of twenty-three sculptures by the renowned artist Fernando Botero. This lively plaza not only serves as a cultural hub but also invites visitors to immerse themselves in the unique charm of Botero's distinctive style. Just a stone's throw away, explore the fascinating exhibits at the nearby Antioquia Museum, making this area a must-visit for art lovers and tourists alike.

COP 50,000 (event ticket)

Atanasio Girardot Stadium

Atanasio Girardot Stadium in Medellin is more than just a 45,000-seat soccer venue. It's a massive complex with various sports facilities and arenas where locals gather to exercise, play sports, and enjoy outdoor activities. The stadium, inaugurated in 1953, is the third largest in Colombia and hosts games for Medellin's teams: Nacional and Medellin.

Free

Plaza Minorista José María Villa

Plaza Minorista José María Villa is a bustling and spacious indoor marketplace located in downtown Medellin. It offers a vibrant atmosphere with various vendors selling produce, meat, seafood, and other goods. The market caters to both wholesale and direct-to-consumer customers, creating a lively hustle and bustle as people navigate through the stalls in search of the products.

Free

Plaza Botero - Medellín, Antioquia

Home to 23 sculptures by the renowned Colombian artist Fernando Botero, this lively plaza is a cultural highlight where enjoy art in the open air. It remains a notable local point of interest situated in Medellín, Colombia. Visitors can observe the surrounding area and its connection to the city's broader historical landscape. Local guides sometimes include it on walking routes through the centre.

Free

Parroquia La Catedral Metropolitana de Medellín

The Catedral Metropolitana de Medellín, also known as the Metropolitan Cathedral of Medellin, is a significant landmark in the city. Constructed with over 1,120,000 adobe bricks, it stands as baked clay structures globally. The cathedral boasts impressive Romanesque architecture and is adorned with beautiful artwork inside. As the principal church of the Archdiocese of Medellin, it holds great cultural and historical importance in the region.

COP 30,000 (guided tour)

El Palacio egipcio

The Egyptian Palace, located in the Prado Centro area north of downtown Medellin, has a rich history dating back to the early 1900s when it was known as the most expensive sector of the city. Despite falling into decay with industrialization and associated insecurity, this small but intriguing building holds a variety of interesting stories. While not well-known to tourists and somewhat deteriorated, it offers excellent views and is worth visiting for some nice photos.

COP 20,000 (performance ticket)

Pequeño Teatro de Medellín

Pequeño Teatro de Medellín is a renowned theater in the heart of Medellin, offering a permanent season of performing arts. Despite its name, it's beautiful theaters in the city and was created by professional actors aiming to provide cultural experiences through theatrical reflections. The intimate setting allows for an engaging experience, with diverse shows ranging from those featuring criollo music to others rooted in local culture and accent.

Free

Plaza Cisneros

Plaza Cisneros, also known as the Park of Lights, is a historic square in downtown Medellin that has undergone significant transformation. Renovated in 2005, it now boasts 300 dramatic light poles that create an artificial forest of lights. Named after Francisco Javier Cisneros, the Cuban engineer who constructed the Antioquia Railway, this plaza has become a popular tourist attraction.

Free

Parques del Río

Parques del Río is a modern linear park in Colombia designed to connect the city with the river, offering a blend of nature, sports, leisure, and gastronomy. Located between the Conquistadores and Industriales sectors near prominent landmarks like the EPM Intelligent Building and Plaza Mayor, this manicured riverside corridor features trees, flowers, public artworks, and cycling routes.

COP 65,000

Parque Norte

Parque Norte is a family-friendly theme park located in the northern center of the city. The 60,000-acre park underwent a major renovation less than a decade ago and is divided into five themed plazas: expedition, fantasy, adventure, events, and extreme. Each plaza offers different attractions including theme park-style rides surrounded by expansive green spaces.

Free

El Tesoro Parque Comercial

El Tesoro Parque Comercial is a modern and bustling shopping mall located in Medellin. It boasts an impressive array of global brands alongside local fashion favorites, making it a shopaholic's paradise. The mall also offers diverse dining options, from upscale restaurants to popular sweet treats. Situated amidst lush green hills, the location provides views of the city, adding to its allure.

COP 25,000

El Castillo Museum and Gardens

El Castillo Museum and Gardens is a grand castle-style home-turned-museum located in El Poblado, Medellin. It was once a private residence of a local textile baron and is now known for its impressive architecture inspired by the European monarchies' golden age. The museum boasts the largest collection of decorative arts in Medellin and offers temporary art exhibits.

COP 20,000

Casa Museo Otraparte

Nestled in the charming town of Envigado, Casa Museo Otraparte is a delightful museum that pays tribute to the life and works of Colombian philosopher and writer Fernando González. This quaint establishment, housed in a beautifully preserved colonial-style villa, features lush gardens adorned with large trees and a serene fountain at its heart.

Free

Parque de El Poblado

Parque de El Poblado is a vibrant green square and a popular meeting spot in Medellin. It features food vendors, artisan craft stalls, and is surrounded by a lively nightlife. Additionally, it is conveniently close to Mercados del Rio, offering a variety of dining options just a short walk away. The park also provides ample lush trees, wide green spaces, and pleasant walking paths for visitors to enjoy.

COP 25,000

Parque de la Conservación

Parque de la Conservación, formerly known as Santa Fe Zoo, has transformed into a nature reserve focused on wildlife conservation and rehabilitation. It is a popular destination for nature enthusiasts and tourists due to its diverse native flora and fauna. The park aims to provide a safe haven for animals that have been victims of exploitation or exotic species trafficking. Visitors can explore different animal habitats while learning about wildlife from around the world.

Free

Parque Arví

Parque Arví is a sprawling ecotourism park located to the east of Medellin, accessible via the renowned Medellin Metrocable cable-car system. It boasts an extensive area with numerous walking and hiking trails, a picturesque lake, and diverse exhibits showcasing its remarkable biodiversity. Visitors can explore nearly 15 miles of winding trails surrounded by lush vegetation and floral displays.

COP 19,000 (cable car)

Estación Metrocable Arví

Estación Metrocable Arví is a gondola system that provides views of Medellín's steep hills and the city below. It transports visitors to Parque Arvi, an ecological park offering serene green landscapes and lush forests. The final station is located deep within the wilderness, providing an ideal escape from the city.

COP 40,000

Arvi Comfama Park

Arvi Comfama Park is a must-visit destination in Medellin, offering panoramic views accessible via the Metro cable. Upon arrival at Jose Maria Cordoba airport, visitors can opt for VIP transfer services for a guided tour of the mountains. The park boasts thrilling activities such as zip lining and tree climbing with safety harnesses suitable for varying skill levels.

Free

Mirador el Cielo Medellin

Mirador el Cielo Medellin offers sweeping city views from a rustic hilltop spot with a grassy patch. Located on the outskirts of the city, it provides great panoramic views and fun activities like music, good food, and amusement park attractions. The viewpoint stands at about 5,500 feet and offers vistas of Cerro de Tres Los Cruces. Visitors can enjoy rides such as zip lines and a mega swing for two people.
