Explore Warsaw

A Brief History

Warsaw is an incredibly dynamic, deeply resilient capital city that was spectacularly, masterfully rebuilt from the ashes of World War II. Today, its remarkably picturesque Old Town perfectly blends with a highly energetic, rapidly modernizing downtown filled with striking skyscrapers and deeply fascinating museums. The city beautifully showcases an incredibly proud, highly vibrant Polish spirit.

Top Sights & Activities

€10

The Royal Castle in Warsaw

Originally the residence of the Polish monarchs, it was completely destroyed in WWII and faithfully reconstructed in the 1970s as a symbol of national resilience.

Free

Sigismund's Column

Erected in 1644, this Corinthian column commemorates King Sigismund III Vasa, who transferred the capital of Poland from Kraków to Warsaw.

Free

Archcathedral Basilica of St. John the Baptist

One of Warsaw's oldest churches, serving as the coronation and burial site for numerous Masovian dukes, and rebuilt in a Masovian Gothic style following WWII.

€10

Palace of Culture and Science

A towering high-rise building gifted by the Soviet Union in 1955, originally controversial but now housing various cultural institutions, theaters, and a university.

€10

National Museum in Warsaw

Established in 1862, it is one of the largest museums in Poland, housing a vast collection of ancient art, Polish painting, and foreign masterpieces.

Free

Tomb of the Unknown Soldier

A monument dedicated to the unknown soldiers who gave their lives for Poland, situated beneath the only surviving fragment of the Saxon Palace, which was destroyed in 1944.

Free

Mermaid's Statue

A bronze statue located in Warsaw's Old Town Square, depicting the city's legendary protector and serving as its official coat of arms since the 14th century.

€10

Warsaw University Library Garden

A modern rooftop botanical garden opened in 2002, integrating extensive green space and ecological design with the university's academic infrastructure.

€10

Museum of King Jan III's Palace at Wilanów

A Baroque royal palace that survived Poland's partitions and world wars intact, representing the height of Polish monarchial architecture in the late 17th century.
