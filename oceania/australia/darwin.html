Explore Darwin

A Brief History

Settled in 1869 as an isolated northern outpost, Darwin endured near-total destruction by Japanese bombing in 1942 and Cyclone Tracy in 1974. Continually rebuilt, the resilient port city emerged as Australia's multicultural gateway to Southeast Asia and the Top End. Visitors today can clearly trace the detailed layers of this unique heritage throughout the preserved streets and monuments.

Top Sights & Activities

A$20

Darwin Aviation Museum

The Darwin Aviation Museum is a treasure trove for aviation aficionados and history lovers, showing an extensive array of vintage military aircraft and fascinating artifacts. This museum highlights Darwin's significant contributions during World War II, featuring a striking B-52 bomber on loan from the U.S. Air Force as its centerpiece.

Free (Wave Lagoon A$8)

Darwin Waterfront Precinct

The Darwin Waterfront Precinct is a active bayside development featuring an array of restaurants, bars, and recreational activities. It offers a wave pool and sandy beach for swimming, making it a safe and enjoyable destination for tourists and locals alike. The area also presents unique photo opportunities with industrial-looking walls lining walkways against the backdrop of the bay.

A$42

Crocosaurus Cove

Crocosaurus Cove, located in downtown Darwin, is a wildlife haven showing the world's largest display of Australian reptiles, including the infamous saltwater crocodile. Visitors can witness these creatures from 3 months to 80 years old in purpose-designed displays that offer 360-degree viewing on all three levels. The Cove offers various activities such as the Big Croc Feed Show and Fishing for Crocs platform where visitors can feed and interact with these fascinating animals.

Free

Mindil Beach Sunset Market

Mindil Beach Sunset Market is a active hub that draws both locals and tourists alike, offering an eclectic mix of over 300 food stalls featuring global cuisines. From delectable Hong Kong dishes to Greek souvlaki, paella, burgers, and desserts like ice cream and sorbet, there's something for every palate.

Free

George Brown Darwin Botanic Gardens

George Brown Darwin Botanic Gardens is a public park in Darwin, Australia. Records associated with the site include 1886. The George Brown Darwin Botanic Gardens cover 42 hectares and showcase the diverse flora of northern Australia and other tropical environments. Established in 1886, the gardens has extensive collections of palms, cycads, orchids, and baobabs. Paths, lawns, and shaded areas make up the main public facilities.

Free

Museum and Art Gallery of the Northern Territory

Museum and Art Gallery of the Northern Territory is a museum in Darwin, Australia. Located on Larrakia Land overlooking Fannie Bay, the Museum and Art Gallery of the Northern Territory (MAGNT) is a top attraction in Darwin. The museum offers engaging public programs such as creative workshops, artist talks, and guided tours of its acclaimed exhibitions. Galleries and public rooms are open on posted hours in Darwin.

Free

Parap Village Markets

Parap Village Markets is a popular weekend market featuring a variety of vendors selling clothing, crafts, and food. The market offers an array of tropical fruits, Asian greens, and fresh produce from Filipino, Tamil, Thai, Vietnamese and Chinese growers. It's known for its busy atmosphere with live music and food stalls offering diverse options such as Cambodian rice balls, laksa, donuts, lemonade, and satay squid.

Free

Cullen Bay Marina

Cullen Bay Marina is a modern and active marina located just a short distance from Darwin's city centre. It offers a relaxing atmosphere with views of the bay filled with boats. The area has top seafood restaurants where visitors can enjoy an eclectic blend of flavors or savor fresh local fish and chips, it is an affluent area with exclusive homes and provides opportunities for leisurely walks, dining at various eateries, and enjoying sunset views.

Free

Nightcliff Jetty

Nightcliff Jetty, located in the neighborhood of Nightcliff in the north of Darwin, is a beloved spot for both locals and tourists. It offers uninterrupted views of the famous Darwin sunsets that light up the sky with a glorious orange glow, making it a popular location for wedding photos during the sunset. The old wooden jetty is a favorite among locals for fishing, while others prefer to relax on the rocks nearby to take in the sunset views.

Free

Rapid Creek Markets

Rapid Creek Markets is a market in Darwin, Australia. Rapid Creek Markets in Darwin is a busy and active market that offers a wide variety of tropical fruits, vegetables, and Asian delicacies. It primarily is a farmers market where locals gather to purchase their weekly supply of fresh produce and indulge in delectable curries and samosas. Stalls sell food and goods during regular market hours.
