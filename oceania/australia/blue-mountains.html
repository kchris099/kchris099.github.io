Explore Blue Mountains

A Brief History

The Blue Mountains were a formidable barrier to early European expansion from the Sydney colony until a navigable route was crossed in 1813. The region is the traditional country of the Darug and Gundungurra peoples, whose rock art remains scattered throughout the sandstone gorges. In the late nineteenth century, the mountains developed into a fashionable retreat for wealthy Sydneysiders and a center for coal and shale mining. Today, visitors experience this mix of ancient geology and Victorian tourism via the historic Scenic Railway, the Three Sisters rock formation, and the dense eucalyptus forests.

Wentworth Falls

Wentworth Falls is a visitor site in Blue Mountains. A majestic waterfall with several walking tracks and lookouts, providing views and photo opportunities. Wentworth Falls is a waterfall site near Blue Mountains, Australia reached by marked walking tracks.

Lincoln's Rock

Lincoln's Rock, also known as Flat Rock, is a popular cliff-edge overlook in the Blue Mountains offering views of the Jamison Valley and surrounding forested valleys. This sandstone creation on the Kings Tableland plateau is a favorite spot for family photography and weddings due to its vistas, especially at sunset. Despite its popularity, it provides ample space for visitors to enjoy picnics amidst the panoramic scenery.

Free

Grand Canyon Walking Track

The Grand Canyon Walking Track is a 6-km loop that provides views of sandstone cliffs, waterfalls, and rainforest. Established in 1907, it's popular walks in the Blue Mountains. The trail takes hikers through diverse ecosystems, from dry sclerophyll forest to cool-temperate rainforests. Guided tours are available for a more immersive experience.

Free

Evans Lookout

Evans Lookout is a spot near Blackheath in the Blue Mountains, offering views of mountains and cliffs. It's easily accessible with nearby parking, making it a convenient stop for travelers. The exposed orange cliffs create light effects during sunrise and sunset. This lookout is the starting point for serious hikes and is located at the beginning of the popular Grand Canyon Track.

Govetts Leap lookout

Govetts Leap lookout is a viewpoint in Blue Mountains, Australia. Named after William Govett, the first European to admire this view, the lookout is well-known to several Aboriginal nations. Approach paths are usually short walks from a road or parking area.

Anvil Rock Lookout

Anvil Rock Lookout is a viewpoint in Blue Mountains, Australia. The lookout's namesake comes from its rock formation resembling a blacksmith's anvil. A short 10-minute walk leads to panoramic vistas, making it a perfect retreat away from crowds. Approach paths are usually short walks from a road or parking area.

Gordon Falls Picnic Area

Gordon Falls picnic area is a spot near the junction of Lone Pine Avenue and Olympian Parade. The 900m return walk from the picnic area to the lookout offers views of the valley and falls. Visitors can enjoy a leisurely picnic at the top before began on longer bush walks or taking a brisk walk to the base of the falls for a different perspective.

Elysian Rock Lookout

Elysian Rock Lookout is one of the must-visit attractions in Leura, offering panoramic views of the Blue Mountains. This lookout provides sights of rugged mountain ranges, ancient rock formations, deep valleys, and vast eucalyptus forests. It's a perfect spot to catch a sunset and admire the natural beauty of the region.
