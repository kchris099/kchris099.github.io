Brisbane

Brisbane began as a penal settlement in 1824 and opened to free settlers in 1842. During World War II it was a major Allied headquarters in the Southwest Pacific. Postwar growth turned the colonial river port into Queensland's capital, with surviving nineteenth-century street grids along the Brisbane River and later civic precincts inland from the waterfront.

Mount Coot-Tha Summit Lookout

Mount Coot-Tha Summit Lookout offers panoramic views of the city and its surroundings. It is a popular spot to watch the sunset, with active colors painting the sky as twilight approaches. The lookout is located near Brisbane Botanic Gardens Mount Coot-tha, a 138-acre subtropical conservatory featuring diverse native and exotic plants, including the largest collection of Australian rainforest trees in the world.

Brisbane Botanic Gardens Mt Coot-tha

Located at the base of Brisbane's tallest mountain, Mt. Coot-Tha, Brisbane Botanic Gardens Mt Coot-tha spans over 56 hectares and is known as Queensland's premier subtropical botanic gardens. The gardens boast a diverse collection of native and exotic plants, including the largest assortment of Australian rainforest trees globally. Visitors can explore various themed spaces such as the Japanese Garden, Lagoon and Bamboo Grove, Fern House, and Fragrant Plants and Herb Garden.

Lone Pine Koala Sanctuary

Lone Pine Koala Sanctuary, located just north of Brisbane's CBD, is the world's largest koala sanctuary and a haven for Australian native wildlife. Visitors can watch koalas lounging in gumtrees, hand-feed kangaroos, and observe endangered species like the cassowary. The sanctuary offers keeper talks throughout the day and opportunities to learn about various Aussie wildlife while exploring at visitor own pace.

St John's Cathedral

St John's Cathedral in Brisbane, Australia is a local history site with its Gothic Revival architecture and detailed wood carvings. This Anglican church, listed on the Queensland Heritage Register, has a unique stone vaulted ceiling, making it the only building of its kind in the southern hemisphere. Designed by architect John Loughborough Pearson and constructed over a century in stages, this cathedral was finally completed in 2009.

Newstead House

Newstead House, located in Brisbane, Queensland, is the oldest surviving residence in the area, dating back to 1846. Initially a colonial Georgian cottage for Patrick Leslie, it has evolved over the years and now is a museum showing historical collections. The house overlooks the Brisbane River from a lush park and has a glimpse into Brisbane's history with its late Victorian period style interior.

Mount Gravatt Lookout

Located just 10 kilometers south of Brisbane's busy CBD, Mount Gravatt Outlook is a destination that has an experience. Perched atop a prominent hill at 187 meters above sea level, this lookout provides sweeping panoramic views of the city and beyond. On clear days, feast visitor eyes on the vistas that include the Moreton Bay islands, D'Aguilar Ranges, and Glasshouse Mountains.
