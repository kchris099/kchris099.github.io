Explore Melbourne

A Brief History

Melbourne prospered after an 1850s gold rush with Victorian arcades, tramlines, and laneway culture. Federation Square, Royal Exhibition Building, and Yarra embankments record how Victoria's capital became a finance and arts hub in southeast Australia. Royal Botanic Gardens and Yarra riverside precincts extend gold-era boulevards through Victoria's capital grid.

South Melbourne Market

South Melbourne Market is a market in Melbourne, Australia. Records associated with the site include 1867. South Melbourne Market is a indoor market that has been used by locals and tourists since 1867. While it may not be as large as Queen Victoria Market, it offers a diverse range of stalls selling fresh produce, meat, seafood, artisanal products, and vintage items. Stalls sell food and goods during regular market hours.

Shrine of Remembrance

The Shrine of Remembrance in Melbourne is the largest war memorial in Victoria, serving as a tribute to Australian soldiers who sacrificed their lives during the World Wars. The shrine hosts over 120 ceremonies and commemorative services annually, making it an active venue for honoring heroes. Its location offers views of Melbourne city and the bay area. The solemn atmosphere and architectural design resembling ancient Greece create a experience for visitors.

Free

Royal Botanic Gardens Victoria - Melbourne Gardens

Royal Botanic Gardens Victoria - Melbourne Gardens is a public park in Melbourne, Australia. Records associated with the site include 1846. Royal Botanic Gardens Victoria - Melbourne Gardens, established in 1846, covers 36 hectares and has Australian species as well as non-native gardens. Similar to New York's Central Park, it occupies a significant portion of the city and offers various attractions such as the Melbourne Observatory, Guilfoyles Volcano, and Fern Gully.

Royal Exhibition Building

The Royal Exhibition Building, constructed in 1880, is a significant 19th-century exhibition center with a striking dome. It is situated amidst beautifully landscaped gardens and holds UNESCO World Heritage status. This venue hosts various major events and exhibitions throughout the year, such as the Melbourne International Flower & Garden Show and GABS (the Great Australasian Beer SpecTAPular). Notably, it was also the location of Australia's first Parliament in 1901.

Free

Carlton Gardens

Carlton Gardens, located in Carlton near Melbourne's CBD, is a beautifully landscaped Victorian-era park that houses the Royal Exhibition Building and Melbourne Museum. This World Heritage-listed site offers a setting for picnics and leisurely strolls. Visitors can grab delicious takeaway treats from nearby delis, trattorias, or cafes on Lygon or Brunswick Streets before finding a cozy spot on the grassy patches within the gardens.

DFO South Wharf

DFO South Wharf is a shopping haven for both local and international brands, with over 180 stores offering a wide range of products from fashion to household items. Popular brands like Adidas, Armani, Calvin Klein, and Nike can be found here. After a day of shopping, visitors can relax at the waterfront cafes, restaurants, and bars along the South Wharf Promenade.

Brighton Bathing Boxes

The Brighton Bathing Boxes, located about 12km south of Melbourne city center at Brighton Beach, are a collection of 82 brightly colored wooden beach huts dating back to the early 1900s. Originally used for changing into swimwear during the Victorian era, these and much-photographed structures have become a staple of the beachfront. Each hut has its own unique design and character, making them a popular spot for tourists and locals alike to capture memorable photos.

Melbourne Cricket Ground

The Melbourne Cricket Ground (MCG) is an sports venue with a rich history dating back to 1853. It has been the main stadium for the 1956 Summer Olympics and is known as the home of Australian football. With a capacity of 100,000 people, it's one of Melbourne's top tourist destinations. The MCG offers daily tours that provide visitors with insights into its storied past and significance in Australian sports history.
