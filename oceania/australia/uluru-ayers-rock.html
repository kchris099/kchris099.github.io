Explore Uluru Ayers Rock

A Brief History

Uluru Ayers Rock is in Australia. For millennia, Uluru has been a sacred site for the Anangu people, central to their Tjukurpa creation stories. European exploration in the 1870s introduced pastoralism, but the 1985 return of the land to its traditional owners marked a historic shift toward cultural preservation. Museums, markets, and street plans in Uluru Ayers Rock still show layers from those periods.

Top Sights & Activities

A$38

Uluru

Uluru, also known as Ayers Rock, is a massive sandstone monolith in the Northern Territory's arid Red Centre. It is a site of deep cultural and spiritual significance to the indigenous Anangu people. The formation is known for its striking red hue, which changes dramatically at sunrise and sunset. Visitors can explore the base via guided walks to learn about its geology and ancient rock art.

A$50

Field of Light (pre-booked access only)

Field of Light Uluru is an outdoor art installation by Bruce Munro set on desert ground near Uluru. About 50,000 solar-powered stems glow in shifting colours across an area larger than several football fields. Pre-booked evening visits follow marked paths through the light field after sunset. Uluru remains the visual anchor beyond the installation, so the work is timed and ticketed separately from park day entry.

Free

Emu Walk Apartments

Emu Walk Apartments, situated in Yulara, Northern Territory, offers spacious and well-equipped apartments with amenities like air conditioning, TV, private toilets, parking, cots and microwaves. The property also provides conveniences such as bath essentials and a self-laundry service. Guests can enjoy the on-site restaurant that serves a variety of meals. The location is close to Ayers Rock Resort Shopping Centre.

Free

Imalung Lookout

Imalung Lookout, located in Yulara, offers a panoramic view of Uluru and the surrounding desert landscape. A short walk from most hotels in the area, this sand dune lookout provides an ideal vantage point to witness sunrises and sunsets over Uluru, visitors can also enjoy stargazing at Ewing Lookout for a mesmerizing experience under the night sky.
