Kakadu

Kakadu has been inhabited by Aboriginal peoples of the Alligator Rivers region for at least 65,000 years, with rock shelters at Ubirr, Nourlangie, and Nanguluwurr holding art that ranges from roughly 20,000-year-old Mimi spirit narratives to 1,500-year-old contact-period bark paintings reflecting Macassan trepang traders from Sulawesi. The Commonwealth gazetted Kakadu National Park in stages from 1979 in joint management with Traditional Owners under the Aboriginal Land Rights (Northern Territory) Act 1976, and the southern estate was returned to Aboriginal freehold at the end of 2024. Today the documented record is read across Arnhem Land escarpment country, Ramsar-listed floodplains, and operational ranger precincts at Bowali and Cooinda.

Kakadu National Park (park pass)

Kakadu National Park covers nearly 20,000 square kilometres of the Top End, jointly managed by Traditional Owners and Parks Australia. Gazetted from 1979 and Ramsar-listed in 1981, it holds Yellow Water billabong, coastal mangroves, and sandstone shelters such as Nourlangie. Bowali Visitor Centre and Cooinda Lodge sit on the main touring circuit through floodplain and escarpment country.

Jim Jim Falls

Jim Jim Falls is a roughly 200-metre twin waterfall cutting through the Arnhem Land escarpment within Kakadu National Park, draining a sandstone-plateau catchment into a sandy plunge pool. The site is reached from the Pine Creek-Cooinda arterial via a 4WD-only gravel road that closes in the Wet season roughly November to May. Visitors walk the final kilometre across the rock platform and may swim at the plunge pool in settled dry-season conditions.

Ubirr Rock Art Site

Ubirr is an Aboriginal rock-shelter gallery on the edge of the Nadab floodplain in northern Kakadu National Park, with panel sequences in the X-ray, contact, and dynamic-figures traditions spanning roughly 20,000 years. The main shelter holds hunting scenes, Mimi spirit-beings, and contact-era depictions of Macassan vessels. A 1-kilometre circuit climbs the sandstone outcrop to the principal shelter and a sunset overlook facing east into Arnhem Land.
