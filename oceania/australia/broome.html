Explore Broome

A Brief History

Founded in the 1880s as a pearling port, Broome drew Japanese, Malay, and Aboriginal divers, creating a unique multicultural society. Despite wartime attacks and the decline of the pearl shell industry, its heritage and coastal isolation shaped a distinct outback identity. The survival of these cultural and architectural milestones provides a powerful window into the region's storied past. Museums, markets, and street plans in Broome still show layers from those periods.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

Gantheaume Point

Gantheaume Point is a scenic promontory located at the southern end of Cable Beach in Broome. It is known for its striking red sandstone cliffs that contrast dramatically with the turquoise waters of the Indian Ocean. At low tide, visitors can observe ancient dinosaur footprints preserved in the reef rock, making it a site of significant geological and historical interest.

A$21

Sun Pictures

Sun Pictures in Broome is a historic outdoor cinema that has been running since 1913, making it the world's longest-running outdoor cinema. The setting allows visitors to enjoy new movie releases under pleasant blue skies, warm weather, and balmy evenings during the long dry season. This unique experience of watching films outdoors adds to the appeal of Broome as a popular getaway destination.

Free

Broome Visitor Centre

Broome Visitor Centre sits within the Sun Pictures area of Broome, documented in local heritage registers for its civic or cultural role. Visitors approach from the main access road and combine the visit with neighbouring heritage buildings and public spaces in the district.

Free

Broome Courthouse Markets

Broome Courthouse Markets is a market in Broome, Australia. Visitors can explore 130-million-year-old dinosaur footprints at Gantheaume Point, encounter Australia's largest crocodiles and other wildlife at Malcolm Douglas Crocodile Park and Animal Refuge, and admire local art at Broome Art Galleries. Stalls sell food and goods during regular market hours.

A$90

Broome Camel Safaris

Broome Camel Safaris offers well known camel rides along the expansive white sands of Cable Beach. Operating primarily during sunrise and sunset, the guided tours provide a unique and relaxed way to experience the coastal scenery. The sight of a camel train silhouetted against the active colors of the setting sun has become a defining image of Broome.

A$50

Malcolm Douglas Crocodile Park

The Malcolm Douglas Crocodile Park is a wildlife conservation facility located near Broome, founded by the known Australian documentary filmmaker. The park houses some of the largest crocodiles in Australia, along with alligators, kangaroos, and various bird species. Visitors can observe daily feeding tours and learn about the biology and conservation of these apex predators.

Free

Town Beach

Town Beach is a must-visit destination in Broome, offering a rich history of history and natural beauty. The area is steeped in stories, from Lord McAlpine's epic tales to the pearling industry and wartime legacies. Visitors can explore Gantheaume Point with its dinosaur tracks, Entrance Point, Chinatown, and the Japanese Cemetery.

A$12

Broome Historical Museum

Broome Historical Museum is a museum in Broome, Australia. Broome Historical Museum is a living slice of the town's history, showing a full visual display of its past. Visitors can also explore nearby attractions such as Gantheaume Point with 130-million-year-old dinosaur footprints, the Malcolm Douglas Crocodile Park and Animal Refuge, Broome Art Galleries, and the laid-back Broome Brewery for craft beer and meals. Galleries and public rooms are open on posted hours in Broome.
