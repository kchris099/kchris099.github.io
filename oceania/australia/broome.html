Broome

Pearling from the 1880s pulled Japanese, Malay, Chinese, and Aboriginal divers into a single Kimberley port. Air raids in World War II and the collapse of shell exports ended the boom, but Chinatown streets and Cable Beach remain tied to that maritime labour history rather than to inland pastoral Australia. Shinju Matsuri festival culture still nods to that pearling mix without freezing the town in nostalgia.

Gantheaume Point

Gantheaume Point is a scenic promontory located at the southern end of Cable Beach in Broome. It is known for its striking red sandstone cliffs that contrast dramatically with the turquoise waters of the Indian Ocean. At low tide, visitors can observe ancient dinosaur footprints preserved in the reef rock, making it a site of significant geological and historical interest.

Sun Pictures

Sun Pictures in Broome is a historic outdoor cinema that has been running since 1913, making it the world's longest-running outdoor cinema. The setting allows visitors to enjoy new movie releases under pleasant blue skies, warm weather, and balmy evenings during the long dry season. This unique experience of watching films outdoors adds to the appeal of Broome as a popular getaway destination.

Broome Camel Safaris

Broome Camel Safaris offers well known camel rides along the expansive white sands of Cable Beach. Operating primarily during sunrise and sunset, the guided tours provide a unique and relaxed way to experience the coastal scenery. The sight of a camel train silhouetted against the active colors of the setting sun has become a defining image of Broome.

Malcolm Douglas Crocodile Park

The Malcolm Douglas Crocodile Park is a wildlife conservation facility located near Broome, founded by the known Australian documentary filmmaker. The park houses some of the largest crocodiles in Australia, along with alligators, kangaroos, and various bird species. Visitors can observe daily feeding tours and learn about the biology and conservation of these apex predators.

Town Beach is a must-visit destination in Broome, offering a rich history of history and natural beauty. Visitors can explore Gantheaume Point with its dinosaur tracks, Entrance Point, Chinatown, and the Japanese Cemetery.
