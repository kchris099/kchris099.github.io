Destination Guide — Western Australia

Explore Perth

A Brief History

Established in 1829 by the Swan River Colony, Perth struggled until convict labor and the 1890s gold rush drove rapid expansion. Isolated on the western coast, it leveraged post-war mining booms to grow from a quiet colonial outpost into a modern resource powerhouse. This rich historical tapestry established a strong foundation that continues to shape the city's dynamic contemporary identity.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

Optus Stadium

Optus Stadium, located in Perth, has been recognized as the Most Beautiful Sports Facility in the World. It hosts a variety of major sporting events throughout the year, including AFL games with enthusiastic crowds and international matches for soccer, rugby, and cricket. The stadium also welcomes musical performances by renowned artists like Ed Sheeran and Guns N' Roses. In addition to its sports and entertainment offerings, Optus Stadium provides a venue for weddings.

Free

Blue Boat House

The Blue Boat House, located at Matilda Bay Reserve near Kings Park in Perth, is a charming and spot and brides for photo shoots. Built in the 1930s, this cute blue boatshed sits at the end of a wooden jetty on the Swan River and offers limitless Instagram-worthy shots.

Free

Elizabeth Quay

Elizabeth Quay is a vibrant waterfront destination located on the edge of the Swan River in Perth. It offers a variety of attractions including shopping, cafes, boat tours, art installations, and museums. The area hosts numerous cultural festivals and events and features stylish bars, children's play areas, and diverse dining options. Visitors can enjoy scenic views of the river throughout the day and into the evening.

Free

The Bell Tower

The Bell Tower, located near Elizabeth Quay in Barrack Square, is a modern landmark housing historic 16th-century church bells gifted from London. These bells have rung out to commemorate significant events throughout history. The tower also features a collection of rare artifacts and offers interactive tours where visitors can learn the art of chiming a bell. Additionally, it boasts an observation deck providing 360-degree views of the city.

Free

State Buildings

State Buildings is a luxurious destination in Perth, offering a variety of upscale experiences including dining, retail, and more. Visitors can explore venues like Long Chim courtyard, Wildflower's rooftop terrace with river views, Petition bar, and the elegant Postal Hall which serves as a high-end wedding venue. The buildings also provide picturesque backdrops for photography enthusiasts.

Free

Yagan Square

Yagan Square, named after the Aboriginal Noongar leader Yagan, is a vibrant public space in Perth that opened in March 2018 as part of the Perth City Link urban renewal project. The square features a market hall, gardens, and sculptures, including the illuminated Perth Digital Tower. It offers a playscape for children on the upper level of the Market Hall building with natural forms and various play areas suitable for different ages and abilities.

Free

RAC Arena

RAC Arena in Perth is a cutting-edge venue that can accommodate 15,000 people for indoor sports events and live performances by top artists. The arena hosts a prestigious tennis tournament featuring high-ranking male players from around the world, offering substantial prize money and ATP rankings. Additionally, it is renowned for its exceptional culinary offerings during events. The well-designed layout includes stage entrance doors, ample restrooms, and beverage vendors.

Free

Perth Mosque

Perth Mosque, despite being an old establishment, is in need of renovations for its bathroom and wudhu area. The toilets have handheld water wipes with minimal flow, making them impractical to use. Despite this, the mosque's location is ideal and it hosts active congregations for Jumaa prayers. Visitors appreciate the peaceful ambiance on the outskirts of the city and find it a convenient place to unwind.

Free

Saint Mary's Cathedral

Saint Mary's Cathedral, located in the heart of Perth, is a Gothic cathedral with a rich history. The exterior boasts impressive towers reaching towards the sky, while the interior atrium is filled with natural light and intricate details. Originally built in 1865, the cathedral has undergone additions and renovations over the years, seamlessly blending classic and modern architectural styles.

Free

Perth Zoo

Perth Zoo is a charming and historic destination that offers a wide variety of animal exhibits, behind-the-scenes tours, picnic areas, and cafes. The zoo is home to over 1200 animals from 164 different species originating from Asia, Africa, Australia, and South America. Visitors can encounter African Lions, Asian Elephants, Giraffes, Penguins, Numbats, Meerkats, Rhinoceroses and many more fascinating creatures.

Free

Kings Park and Botanic Garden

Kings Park and Botanic Garden in Perth, Western Australia is a beautifully landscaped park that offers views of the Swan River. It features honor avenues, war memorials, and historic landmarks. Visitors can take a free city bus to explore nearby attractions such as the Perth Cultural Centre, the Perth Mint, The Swan Bell Tower, and the new Elizabeth Quay.

Free

State War Memorial

The State War Memorial in Kings Park is a solemn and beautiful tribute to the Australian soldiers who made the ultimate sacrifice for their country. The memorial precinct includes the Cenotaph, Court of Contemplation, Flame of Remembrance, and Pool of Reflection, each honoring different aspects of Australia's wartime history. Visitors can also admire 62,000 knitted poppy flowers representing fallen soldiers from World War I.

Free

Aviation Heritage Museum

The Aviation Heritage Museum, situated in Bull Creek, is a captivating destination that showcases the rich aviation history of Western Australia. Boasting an impressive collection of 30 aircraft and numerous artifacts, the museum offers a unique experience for enthusiasts of civilian and military aviation. Visitors can explore displays that commemorate the achievements and sacrifices of Air Force personnel, while also gaining insight into Australia's extensive aviation legacy.

Free

Fremantle War Memorial

The Fremantle War Memorial, situated on Monument Hill in Fremantle, Western Australia, is a significant site honoring those who lost their lives during World War I. The memorial features a large obelisk known as the Fallen Soldiers and Sailors Memorial, surrounded by eight smaller memorials. Set amidst beautifully maintained rose gardens and lawns, the memorial offers panoramic views of the harbor and the surrounding area.

Free

Lake Monger Reserve

Lake Monger Reserve is a significant urban wetland located in Perth, renowned for its large population of iconic black swans. The reserve features a 3.5-kilometer paved walking and cycling path that circles the lake, offering scenic views of the water and the city skyline. It provides a peaceful natural sanctuary for birdwatching and recreation within the metropolitan area.
