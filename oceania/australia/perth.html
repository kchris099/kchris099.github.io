Perth

The Swan River Colony of 1829 nearly failed until convict labour and the 1890s gold rushes filled the western settlement. Distance from the eastern capitals kept Perth oriented toward Indian Ocean shipping and later mining headquarters. King's Park and the Swan estuary frame a city built in bursts of resource wealth. Fremantle's convict-built harbour worked as Perth's ocean door through those same booms.

Optus Stadium

Optus Stadium, located in Perth, has been recognized as the Most Sports Facility in the World. It hosts a variety of major sporting events throughout the year, including AFL games with enthusiastic crowds and international matches for soccer, rugby, and cricket. The stadium also welcomes musical performances by known artists like Ed Sheeran and Guns N' Roses. In addition to its sports and entertainment offerings, Optus Stadium provides a venue for weddings.

Blue Boat House

A narrow wooden jetty leads to the blue-painted Crawley Edge Boatshed on the Swan River in Perth. Built in the 1930s and later restored, the small structure has no public interior and is viewed from the foreshore path; its isolated position over calm water and the city-facing river backdrop explain its frequent use in photographs.

Elizabeth Quay

Elizabeth Quay reshaped Perth's Swan River foreshore into a harbor inlet lined with promenades and public art. Cafes, boat tours, and the nearby Bell Tower cluster around the inlet's bridges and boardwalks. Evening lights reflect on the water while office towers rise immediately behind the quay. Public art pieces and a pedestrian bridge define the inlet's geometry.

The Bell Tower

The Bell Tower at Barrack Square near Elizabeth Quay houses historic bells gifted from a London church. A glass and copper shaft displays the bells and offers elevated views over the Swan River. Interpretive panels explain the bells' English origins and their installation in Perth. Change-ringing demonstrations occasionally sound across Barrack Square.

State Buildings

Dining and retail rooms fill Perth's State Buildings precinct, from Long Chim courtyard and Wildflower's river-view terrace to Petition bar and Postal Hall. The restored civic fabric now hosts weddings and photography as well as meals. Courtyards stitch the heritage blocks into one hospitality cluster.

Yagan Square

Named for Noongar leader Yagan, Yagan Square opened in March 2018 as part of Perth City Link renewal. A playscape on the upper Market Hall level uses natural forms for mixed ages and abilities. Open pavement and surrounding facades frame the new civic floor between rail and city blocks.

RAC Arena

RAC Arena in Perth is a recent venue that can accommodate 15,000 people for indoor sports events and live performances by top artists. The arena hosts a prestigious tennis tournament featuring high-ranking male players from around the world, offering substantial prize money and ATP rankings, it is known for its culinary offerings during events. The well-designed layout includes stage entrance doors, ample restrooms, and beverage vendors.

Friday congregations fill Perth Mosque for Jumaa prayers in a long-established city mosque whose bathrooms and wudhu area still need renovation. Exterior and main prayer spaces remain viewable outside service times. The location keeps worship central even while fabric upgrades lag.

Saint Mary's Cathedral

Saint Mary's Cathedral is Perth's Catholic cathedral, with construction and early use dating from around 1865. Stone facades and a long nave face the city center near Elizabeth Quay approaches. Outside Mass, visitors typically see the exterior and main interior from the public seating areas.

Perth Zoo

Perth Zoo has occupied its South Perth site since 1898, with animal habitats arranged among mature gardens close to the Swan River. Australian wildlife, African savanna species, Asian animals, primates, reptiles and birds appear across distinct precincts. The zoo also supports breeding, veterinary care, field conservation and education programs alongside daily public viewing.

Kings Park and Botanic Garden

Landscaped slopes at Kings Park and Botanic Garden overlook the Swan River above Perth. Paths and lawns open wide plantings and memorials across the ridge. City buses link the park toward the Cultural Centre, Perth Mint, Swan Bell Tower, and Elizabeth Quay for combined days.

State War Memorial

The State War Memorial in Kings Park is a solemn and tribute to the Australian soldiers who made the ultimate sacrifice for their country. The memorial precinct includes the Cenotaph, Court of Contemplation, Flame of Remembrance, and Pool of Reflection, each honoring different aspects of Australia's wartime history. Visitors can also admire 62,000 knitted poppy flowers representing fallen soldiers from World War I.

Air Force service and Australian aviation history fill galleries at the Aviation Heritage Museum in Perth. Displays commemorate personnel achievements and sacrifices beside aircraft exhibits. Rooms open on posted hours for labelled collections rather than outdoor airfield walks.

Fremantle War Memorial

Fremantle War Memorial crowns Monument Hill above the harbor, set among rose beds and maintained lawns. From the terrace, views open across Fremantle port and the Indian Ocean approaches. The memorial honors local war dead and remains a civic gathering point on commemorative dates such as Anzac Day.

Lake Monger Reserve

Lake Monger Reserve wraps a freshwater lake west of Perth's CBD with a perimeter path popular for walking and birdwatching. Black swans and other waterbirds use the reeds while cyclists and joggers share the lakeside track. Lawns and shade trees support family picnics without turning the reserve into a formal botanic garden.
