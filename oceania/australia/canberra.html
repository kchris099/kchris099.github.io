Explore Canberra

A Brief History

Canberra was selected as the site for Australia's capital in 1908 as a compromise between rivals Sydney and Melbourne. American architect Walter Burley Griffin designed the city with a focus on geometric motifs and integration with the natural landscape. Construction began in 1913, and it officially became the seat of government in 1927. Today, Canberra houses national monuments, museums, and institutions that reflect Australia's political and cultural heritage.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

Australian War Memorial

The Australian War Memorial in Canberra is a profound national monument and museum dedicated to honoring the members of the Australian armed forces who have participated or died in wars. The expansive complex features the commemorative Roll of Honour, the Hall of Memory, and extensive exhibitions detailing Australia's military history from the colonial period to modern conflicts.

Free

Mount Ainslie Lookout

The Mount Ainslie Lookout in Canberra offers a paved observation deck with panoramic vistas and information plaques detailing distant landmarks. From this viewpoint, visitors can enjoy wonderful views of the capital city and surrounding areas, including main landmarks such as the War Memorial, ANZAC Parade, Old Parliament House, and the striking architecture of Parliament House. While there are no public toilets available at the site, it is just a one-minute walk from the carpark.

Free

Old Parliament House

Old Parliament House served as the seat of the Parliament of Australia from 1927 to 1988. Located in Canberra, the historic building now functions as the Museum of Australian Democracy, offering exhibitions on the nation's political history and democratic heritage. Visitors can explore the preserved legislative chambers, prime minister's office, and historic press gallery.

A$75

Canberra Theatre Centre

Located in the heart of Canberra, the Canberra Theatre Centre offers a modern building with three venues catering to various audience sizes. From large and historic to intimate, this premier performing arts venue hosts a wide range of entertainment options, from classic plays by Bell Shakespeare and performances by the Australian Ballet to contemporary shows and concerts. It's a must-visit for those looking for live entertainment experiences in Australia's creative capital.

Free

Australian National Botanic Gardens

The Australian National Botanic Gardens, situated in Canberra, is a serene and diverse botanical haven dedicated to the conservation and exploration of native Australian plants. Encompassing approximately 90 hectares, the gardens boast over 4000 species of flora from various Australian ecosystems. Visitors can meander through themed gardens such as a rainforest gully, eucalypt lawn, and rock garden to gain insight into the country's rich botanical heritage.

Free

National Arboretum Canberra

The National Arboretum Canberra is a vast botanical garden featuring 94 forests of rare, endangered, and symbolic trees from around the world. Established following the devastating 2003 bushfires, the site covers 250 hectares and offers extensive walking and cycling trails. The Village Centre provides panoramic views of Canberra and educational exhibits on tree conservation.

A$62

National Zoo & Aquarium

The National Zoo and Aquarium in Canberra is a combined facility offering visitors the chance to see a wide variety of native Australian animals and exotic species from around the world. It features extensive enclosures and interactive encounters, including opportunities to feed bears and meet cheetahs. The aquarium section houses diverse marine life, from sharks to colorful reef fish, providing a comprehensive wildlife experience.

Free

Royal Australian Mint

Royal Australian Mint sits within the National Arboretum Canberra area of Canberra, documented in local heritage registers for its civic or cultural role. Visitors approach from the main access road and combine the visit with neighbouring heritage buildings and public spaces in the district.

Free

Stromlo Forest Park

Stromlo Forest Park is a true gem nestled in the heart of Canberra, offering an array of outdoor activities for enthusiasts of all kinds. With its well-designed cycling circuit and grass running track, it caters to both runners and cyclists alike. The park features extensive equestrian paths and mountain bike trails that accommodate varying skill levels, making it a favorite among mountain biking aficionados.

A$26

Cockington Green Gardens

Cockington Green Gardens is a unique attraction in Canberra featuring meticulously crafted miniature buildings and landscapes. The displays represent classic British villages and international landmarks, set within beautifully maintained gardens. Visitors can explore the intricate models, ride a miniature steam train, and enjoy the detailed craftsmanship that has made it a popular family destination.
