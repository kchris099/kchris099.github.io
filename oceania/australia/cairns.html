Explore Cairns

A Brief History

Surveyed in 1876 as a port for inland goldfields, Cairns later became a sugar and railway hub on Queensland's tropical coast. Mid-twentieth-century shipping and air links then made it the main mainland base for Great Barrier Reef trips. Colonial-era street plans and harbor works sit beside modern reef-tourism docks and hotels along the present waterfront.

Top Sights & Activities

Skyrail Rainforest Cableway

The Skyrail Rainforest Cableway offers a unique perspective on the Wet Tropics World Heritage Area near Cairns. The scenic cable car ride glides over the canopy of the ancient tropical rainforest, with stops at Red Peak and Barron Falls for guided boardwalk tours. It provides an immersive educational experience focused on the biodiversity and ecology of the rainforest environment.

Free

Barron Falls

Barron Falls is a 250-meter waterfall located in Barron Gorge National Park, North Queensland. Visitors can take a scenic trail to reach lookout points offering views of the cascading waters from the Barron River. The area is part of the oldest tropical rainforest in the world, providing an enchanting experience for nature enthusiasts.

Free

Barron Gorge National Park

Barron Gorge National Park is a public park in Cairns, Australia. Barron Gorge National Park, located near Cairns, is a riverside park featuring lush tropical rainforests and trails that lead to waterfall and gorge views. Visitors can enjoy high-adrenaline activities with provided safety equipment and guides for a thrilling experience amidst the natural beauty of the park. Paths, lawns, and shaded areas make up the main public facilities.

A$71

Rainforestation Nature Park

Rainforestation Nature Park is an expansive nature reserve located in the World Heritage Rainforest, offering a variety of distinctive experiences for guests. Visitors can enjoy traditional dance performances showing indigenous culture, began on Army Duck Rainforest Tours, and explore walkways to observe Australian wildlife within the park and its surrounding rainforest.

Free

Crystal Cascades

Crystal Cascades is a waterfall in Cairns, Australia. This series of cascading waterfalls flows into natural swimming pools, creating a picture-perfect scene surrounded by lush greenery and the sounds of nature. The area offers paths and trails for exploration, as well as picnic areas for relaxation. Marked tracks lead to viewing platforms or overlooks.

A$61

Cairns Aquarium

Cairns Aquarium is a visitor site in Cairns, Australia. It opened or was completed around 2017. Cairns Aquarium, which opened in 2017, is a unique attraction dedicated to showing the diverse habitats and species of Tropical North Queensland. With over 16,000 sea creatures from 10 ecosystems and 71 habitats, it offers an immersive experience for visitors. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Free

Cairns Waterfalls

Cairns Waterfalls sits within the Cairns Aquarium area of Cairns, documented in local heritage registers for its civic or cultural role. Visitors approach from the main access road and combine the visit with neighbouring heritage buildings and public spaces in the district.

Free

Cairns Esplanade Lagoon

Cairns Esplanade Lagoon is a visitor site in Cairns, Australia. Cairns Esplanade Lagoon is a man-made saltwater pool located in Cairns' CBD, offering a safe and jellyfish-free swimming experience. Lifeguards patrol the lagoon during opening hours, ensuring safety for all visitors. The adjacent foreshore boardwalk provides picnic spots, birdwatching areas, free barbecues, and fitness equipment. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Free

Rusty's Markets

Rusty's Markets is a market in Cairns, Australia. Records associated with the site include 1975. Open every Friday, Saturday, and Sunday from 5 am, this enclosed market has been a weekend staple since 1975. With over 180 stalls offering a wide variety of goods including fresh produce, apparel, gifts, and flowers, it's a great stop for budget-conscious travelers. Stalls sell food and goods during regular market hours.

Free

Cairns Botanic Gardens

Cairns Botanic Gardens is a public park in Cairns, Australia. The garden provides numerous spots for family photos, capturing genuine emotions against the backdrop of lush greenery. Paths, lawns, and shaded areas make up the main public facilities. Cairns Botanic Gardens remains part of the public visitor circuit in Cairns.
