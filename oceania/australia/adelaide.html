Explore Adelaide

A Brief History

Adelaide was established in 1836 as the planned capital of a freely settled British province in Australia. Designed by Colonel William Light, the city features a grid layout surrounded by extensive parklands. It became known for its religious freedom and progressive political reforms in the nineteenth century. Today, Adelaide is celebrated for its well-preserved colonial architecture, vibrant arts festivals, and proximity to renowned wine regions.

Top Sights & Activities

A$29

Adelaide Oval

Adelaide Oval is a modernized sports and entertainment venue with a capacity of 53,500. The stadium's distinctive horseshoe shape and unique roof design make it an landmark. Following restoration, the stadium now hosts a variety of national and international sports events, as well as concerts and functions. The restoration aimed to preserve heritage elements while incorporating modern materials that complement the city's architecture.

A$48

Adelaide Zoo

Adelaide Zoo, nestled in the heart of Adelaide, is a vibrant oasis boasting over 2,500 animals from around the world. Visitors can interact with the wildlife and enjoy unique experiences such as hand-feeding giraffes and observing Giant Pandas Wang Wang and Fu Ni. The zoo offers educational opportunities for all ages and is renowned for its captivating animal collection. As one of Adelaide's top family-friendly attractions, it provides an day out for animal lovers.

Free

Adelaide Botanic Garden

Adelaide Botanic Garden is a sprawling 125-acre oasis featuring rare plants such as the Wollemi pine and various sculptures, greenhouses, and cafes. The garden offers a range of activities for kids, including meandering pathways, diverse landscapes to explore, birdwatching opportunities, and historic buildings to visit.

Free (hosted tour A$52)

National Wine Centre

Nestled in the heart of Adelaide, the National Wine Centre of Australia is a captivating destination for anyone passionate about wine. This modern facility not only showcases the rich viticultural heritage of Australia but also offers interactive displays that delve into the art and science of winemaking. Visitors can explore various wine varieties through engaging exhibits, while tasting rooms provide an opportunity to savor exquisite wines from South Australia's renowned regions.

Free

Rundle Mall

Rundle Mall is a bustling car-free shopping destination in the heart of the city, featuring an impressive array of over 1000 retailers and service providers. It houses major department stores like David Jones and Myer, as well as flagship outlets for renowned global brands such as Apple, H&M, Sephora, and more. The mall also boasts exclusive fashion labels and unique retailers within its various shopping arcades and centers.

Free

Victoria Square / Tarntanyangga

Victoria Square, also known by its Kaurna name Tarntanyangga, is the central public square of Adelaide. It serves as a major civic and cultural hub, hosting festivals, markets, and public events throughout the year. The square is surrounded by significant historic buildings, including the Supreme Court and the General Post Office, and features a prominent central fountain.

Free

Saint Francis Xavier's Cathedral

St Francis Xavier's Cathedral, located in Adelaide, is a Roman Catholic cathedral that has been under construction for approximately 145 years. The most recent addition to the cathedral is its tower, which was completed in 1996. This Gothic Revival building in the Early English style features an imposing structure with a 36-meter high tower and dimensions of 56.5 meters lengthwise and 29.5 meters horizontally.

Free

Chinatown Adelaide South Australia

Chinatown Adelaide South, located at the south-east end of Adelaide's CBD, is a vibrant and internationally famous area known for its diverse culinary offerings. This compact district features bubble-tea shops, grocery stores, and restaurants serving potsticker dumplings. The main street is adorned with two large Paifang marking the entrances at either end, stretching from Grote Street to Gouger Street.

Free

Mount Lofty Summit

Mount Lofty Summit, located just a short drive from Crafers township, is the highest peak in Adelaide. It offers panoramic views of the city skyline and surrounding hills. Visitors can also enjoy The Summit Restaurant and Cafe, a Visitor Information Outlet, and a gift shop at the summit.

Free

Mount Lofty Botanic Garden

Nestled between Crafers and Piccadilly, Mount Lofty Botanic Garden spans 97 acres of sloping terrain, featuring a picturesque lake, extensive walking trails, and themed gullies adorned with exotic plants. The garden showcases a diverse array of flora from various regions including South America, China, East Africa, New Zealand, South East Asia, North America as well as Australian natives.

Free

Glenelg Beach

Glenelg Beach is a popular sandy shore with several cafes and a grassy playground. It also features a jetty, perfect for sunset views and fishing. The beach can be reached within half an hour by tram service from Adelaide City. Despite the high footfall, the place maintains its calm ambiance throughout the day. Visitors can enjoy walking on warm sand while feeling refreshing breezes on their cheeks. Good restaurants are available in close proximity to satisfy one's hunger cravings.

Free

Brighton Jetty

Brighton Jetty is a historic coastal landmark located in the seaside suburb of Brighton, south of Adelaide. Originally built in 1882, the jetty extends into the Gulf St Vincent, offering excellent spots for fishing, walking, and enjoying sunsets. The surrounding esplanade features cafes, restaurants, and a relaxed beachside atmosphere popular with locals and visitors.

Free

Semaphore Foreshore

The Semaphore Foreshore is a vibrant coastal area in Adelaide, featuring a wide sandy beach, a historic jetty, and a lively esplanade. The area is known for its well-preserved Art Deco architecture, vintage carousel, and the Semaphore Summer Carnival. It provides a family-friendly environment with extensive parklands, walking paths, and a variety of dining options.
