Explore Hobart

A Brief History

Founded in 1804 as a brutal penal colony, Hobart grew into a prosperous whaling and shipbuilding port on the Derwent River. Its preserved Georgian sandstone architecture reflects a dark colonial past that eventually gave way to a thriving maritime and artistic culture. Decades of careful preservation have ensured that this profound historical narrative remains a vital part of the modern experience.

Top Sights & Activities

MONA

MONA (Museum of Old and New Art) is an innovative and provocative art museum located on the Berriedale peninsula in Hobart. Founded by David Walsh, it features a subterranean architecture that houses a diverse collection ranging from ancient antiquities to contemporary installations. The museum's unconventional approach and striking design have made it a major cultural destination, significantly impacting Tasmania's tourism and arts scene.

Free

Salamanca Market

Salamanca Market is a bustling Saturday market in Hobart, Tasmania, known for its diverse offerings. With over 300 vendors, the market features an array of products including food, produce, art, clothing and Tasmanian timber items. The vibrant atmosphere along the waterfront showcases locally crafted goods such as unique clothing and jewelry, artisan cheeses, fresh produce and more.

Free

Tasmanian Travel and Information Centre

The Tasmanian Travel & Information Centre in Hobart is a great starting point for exploring Tasmania. The staff, including Helena, Rashini, Chon, and Giovanna, are helpful and knowledgeable. They go above and beyond to provide assistance with recommendations, bookings, and travel advice. The center also offers tours of the Hobart district that are informative and well-managed.

Free

Constitution Dock

Constitution Dock, located on the Derwent River in Hobart, is a bustling area with converted colonial buildings that house galleries, seafood restaurants, and shops. It holds historical significance as the location where Hobart's founding document was signed. The dock is also famous for being the finish line of the Sydney to Hobart Yacht Race and features private yachts, fishing boats, historic buildings, and ships from past commercial activities.

Free

Farm Gate Market

Farm Gate Market is a lively and bustling Sunday market located in central Hobart, Tasmania. It offers an abundance of fresh, locally sourced produce including seasonal fruits and vegetables, artisanal cheeses, homemade breads and pastas, free-range meats and eggs, freshly caught oysters, and handcrafted gins from local distilleries. The market also features a variety of vendors selling pickles, relishes, jams as well as breakfast and lunch options such as oversized cookies and gourmet toasties.

A$10

Doone Kennedy Hobart Aquatic Centre

The Doone Kennedy Hobart Aquatic Centre is a popular spot for families, offering a kids pool and water slides that children love. The facility includes a 50m pool and well-maintained change rooms. While the parking can be challenging during busy times, the friendly staff and overall cleanliness make it a pleasant experience. Despite some minor drawbacks, it remains a great option in Hobart.

Free

Royal Tasmanian Botanical Gardens

The Royal Tasmanian Botanical Gardens, established in 1818, cover a 14-hectare site on Hobart's waterfront along the River Derwent. It is a popular attraction for families with plenty of trails and green space for kids to enjoy. The gardens feature historic plants, a Japanese garden, an Antarctic plant house, a fuchsia house, a cactus collection, and a traditional herb garden.

Free

Rosny Hill Lookout

Puddleduck Vineyard is a charming, family-friendly winery with ample space for kids to play. Visitors can enjoy a delightful wine flight featuring the crisp and creamy 2021 Bubbleduck, the lovely dry 2023 riesling, and the easy-drinking Fume Blanc 2023. The cellar door also offers delicious treats like brownies and coffee in a friendly atmosphere. Additionally, guests can purchase Puddleduck paraphernalia if they're fond of ducks.

A$25

Ninja Stadium

Blundstone Arena, also known as Bellerive Oval, is a family-friendly venue with a cycling track that offers scenic views of Howrah Beach. It provides great viewing experiences for cricket matches and has wide walkways for easy access. The hill area is perfect for families with children to enjoy the grassy space. While the food options are limited, visitors can bring their own snacks. The venue also offers good security measures and friendly staff.

A$18

Putters Adventure Golf

Moorilla Winery is located at the corner of MONA and offers a relaxed atmosphere with views of its own vineyard. The winery provides an incredible experience with knowledgeable and hospitable staff, such as Winnie, who shares insights about Moorilla's wines and its sister winery, Domaine A. Visitors can enjoy engaging wine tastings at the Cellar Door hosted by friendly staff like Jesse, who also shares interesting stories.
