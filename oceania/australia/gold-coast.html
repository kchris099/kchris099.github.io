Gold Coast

The Gold Coast grew from 19th-century timber and farming settlements along Queensland's southern beaches into a string of surf towns after World War II. State highway links and postwar holiday camps drew southern visitors, while Surfers Paradise hotels and later theme parks reshaped the dunes. Indigenous Yugambeh Country underlies the coastal strip; today boardwalks, canal estates, and the Springbrook and Lamington hinterland frame a metro built on sun, sand, and service industries.

More than 240 outlet stores at Harbour Town Premium Outlets on the Gold Coast discount brands such as Coach, Ralph Lauren, Victoria's Secret, Hugo Boss, Calvin Klein, and Nike. Casual eateries and a 14-screen cinema share the complex. Quayside walking lines link the retail blocks.

SkyPoint Observation Deck occupies levels 77 and 78 of the Q1 tower on the Gold Coast. Floor-to-ceiling glass faces the beach strip, hinterland ranges, and Broadwater channels. Surfers Paradise Beachfront Markets

Surfers Paradise Beachfront Markets set up along the foreshore paths of the Gold Coast's main beach strip. Stalls sell crafts, souvenirs, and casual food when the market operates, drawing walkers from the esplanade and nearby hotels. Access is on foot from beachfront roads; trade hours follow the posted market schedule rather than a permanent shop row.

Sea World on the Gold Coast is a marine theme park with animal exhibits and rides beside the Broadwater. Polar Bear Shores, a large shark lagoon, and Ray Reef are among the named animal areas. Shows and walk-through habitats sit between amusement rides on the same ticketed campus.

Hinze Dam

Hinze Dam in the Gold Coast hinterland holds Advancetown Lake behind a broad embankment and spillway. Lookouts face the water, forested ridges, and the engineered wall itself. Picnic lawns and walking paths make the reservoir a day trip inland from the coastal strip. Birdlife works the lake edge while kayakers launch from designated points.

Burleigh Head National Park

Ocean views, rainforest trails, rocky foreshore, volcanic columns, and mangroves meet at Burleigh Head National Park on the Gold Coast. Picnic and barbecue clearings sit among pines, with whale sightings possible in spring. The headland gives a short coastal escape beside Burleigh Heads.
