Explore Gold Coast

A Brief History

Gold Coast is in Australia. Established as a regional settlement beyond an older fortified core, the city developed through industrialization in the late 19th century, post-war reconstruction, and modern civic expansion that reshaped its public spaces, markets, and surrounding landscape. Museums, markets, and street plans in Gold Coast still show layers from those periods.

Harbour Town Premium Outlets

Harbour Town Premium Outlets is a waterfront area in Gold Coast, Australia. It has over 240 stores offering up to 70% off on big-name brands like Coach, Ralph Lauren, Victoria's Secret, Hugo Boss, Calvin Klein, Nike and more. Travellers can also enjoy casual eateries and a 14-screen cinema. Quays and embankment paths form the main walking route.

SkyPoint Observation Deck

SkyPoint Observation Deck is a visitor site in Gold Coast, Australia. Located on the 77th and 78th levels of the Q1 building, SkyPoint Observation Deck offers panoramic views of the Gold Coast and its hinterland. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Pacific Fair Shopping Centre

Pacific Fair Shopping Centre is a visitor site in Gold Coast, Australia. Pacific Fair Shopping Centre is a massive retail destination In the Gold Coast, near Jupiter's Hotel and Casino. The center also includes Australia's first purpose-built outlet shopping precinct within a major regional shopping center. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

GC Aqua Park

GC Aqua Park is a public park in Gold Coast, Australia. Situated in the scenic Broadwater Parklands on the Gold Coast, GC Aqua Park is an expansive inflatable water park offering endless excitement for travellers of all ages. Open year-round, it provides an accessible and budget-friendly option for creating cherished memories with loved ones. Paths, lawns, and shaded areas make up the main public facilities.

A$105

Sea World

Sea World is a visitor site in Gold Coast, Australia. Sea World is an oceanographic theme park that offers a variety of attractions for travellers of all ages. Guests can watch polar bears at Polar Bear Shores, observe tropical fish and sharks in the world's largest man-made shark lagoon, and even touch rays at Ray Reef. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Free

Hinze Dam

Hinze Dam is a visitor site in Gold Coast, Australia. Hinze Dam, located in the hinterland of the Gold Coast, is a massive structure offering views of Advancetown Lake, surrounding mountains, and the spillway. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

A$74

Wet'n'Wild Gold Coast

Wet'n'Wild Gold Coast is an expansive water park located just 1km from Movie World theme park, making it convenient to visit both attractions in one day. The park offers a variety of thrill rides, a wave pool, concessions, and a surf shop. It's perfect for families or groups of friends looking for fun activities.

A$110

Australian Outback Spectacular

Australian Outback is a bush-themed dinner show set in a high-tech arena that offers an immersive experience into the heartland of Australia. The show follows the story of struggling farmers Reg and Marge, showing the challenges and resilience of outback life. Guests are treated to a three-course meal supporting local farmers while being entertained by horseback riding, cattle mustering, music, and special effects.

Free

Burleigh Head National Park

Burleigh Head National Park is a public park in Gold Coast, Australia. Burleigh Head National Park is a surprising gem in Burleigh Heads, offering ocean views, rainforest trails, and wildlife encounters. The coastal headland area provides an easy escape into nature with its rocky foreshore, ancient volcanic columns, and lush mangroves. Travellers can enjoy picnics or barbecues amidst fragrant pine trees while spotting whales during spring.
