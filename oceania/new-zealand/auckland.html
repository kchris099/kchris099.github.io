Destination Guide — Auckland

Explore Auckland

A Brief History

Purchased from the local Maori in 1840 to serve as New Zealand's first colonial capital, Auckland grew rapidly around its two massive harbors. Though it eventually lost capital status to Wellington, its extensive maritime and commercial expansion cemented it as the nation's largest city. The survival of these cultural and architectural milestones provides a powerful window into the region's storied past. Museums, markets, and street plans in Auckland still show layers from those periods.

Sky Tower

The Sky Tower is a 60-storey landmark in Auckland, New Zealand, offering panoramic views of the city. It stands at 328 meters high, making it the tallest of its kind in the Southern Hemisphere. Visitors can reach the observation deck via a glass elevator and capture city panoramas that extend up to 80 kilometers on clear days.

Free

Saint Patrick's Cathedral

St Patrick's Cathedral, officially known as the Cathedral of St Patrick & St Joseph, is a evidence of Auckland's rich Catholic heritage. Established in 1848 on land originally granted by the British Crown to New Zealand's first Catholic Bishop, Jean Baptiste Pompallier, this Gothic-style cathedral has undergone significant transformations over the years.

Free

Aotea Square

Aotea Square is a active public space located in Auckland, adjacent to Queen Street. It is a versatile venue for various events such as rallies, outdoor concerts, and markets. During Christmas, it transforms into a festive wonderland with live music, circus performances, visual arts, an outdoor cinema, and even a visit from Santa. The square also hosts regular markets featuring New Zealand fashion labels, retro items, food, fabric, jewelry and furniture.

Free

Sweet Axe Throwing Co.

Sweet Axe Throwing Co. Is an indoor recreational facility in Auckland offering the unique and increasingly popular activity of urban axe throwing. Trained instructors guide visitors through safety protocols and throwing techniques before they compete in games and tournaments. It provides a dynamic, unconventional entertainment experience for groups and events.

Free

Spark Arena

Spark Arena is a large, well-known venue that has hosted some of the biggest names in live music. It's perfect for staged events, as it has great views of the whole arena. The stadium is well designed and there are plenty of public areas for food shops and easy access to clean rest rooms. The parking building is nearby, making it easy to get to the event.

NZ$39

Auckland Zoo

Auckland Zoo, New Zealand's largest zoo, is home to over 130 species and nearly 900 animals across a variety of exhibits. Situated conveniently in the city, it has the chance to began on a global tour through diverse habitats in just a few hours. Celebrating its centenary, the zoo has recently undergone an change and welcomed new residents such as Sumatran tigers and Sunda gharial crocodiles.

Free

Michael Joseph Savage Memorial Park

Michael Joseph Savage Memorial Park is a public park in Auckland, New Zealand. Michael Joseph Savage Memorial Park, located at Bastion Point in Auckland, is a beautifully maintained park paying tribute to New Zealand's first Labour Prime Minister. The memorial offers panoramic views of Rangatira reserve and Waitemata Harbor. Paths, lawns, and shaded areas make up the main public facilities.

Free

Bastion Point

Bastion Point is a visitor site in Auckland, New Zealand. Bastion Point in Auckland is a beloved spot for photographers, offering views of the Hauraki Gulf, Waitemata Harbour, and the city skyline. It's an ideal location to capture sunset silhouettes of Auckland City against the backdrop of the sea. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

NZ$34

SEA LIFE Kelly Tarlton's Aquarium

SEA LIFE Kelly Tarlton's Aquarium is a visitor site in Auckland, New Zealand. It opened or was completed around 1985. SEA LIFE Kelly Tarlton's Aquarium is a known Auckland attraction that offers an immersive experience into aquatic life. Opened in 1985 by New Zealand underwater adventurer Kelly Tarlton, the aquarium has unique curved tunnels and conveyor belts for visitors to explore marine habitats. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Free

Achilles Point

Achilles Point is a visitor site in Auckland, New Zealand. Records associated with the site include 1939. Achilles Point is a popular rocky viewpoint in Auckland, offering panoramic views of the bay and surrounding islands. It commemorates the 1939 battle of the River Plate and has traditional Maori carvings honoring historical chiefs. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.
