Queenstown

The 1862 Shotover gold rush created a boom camp that emptied when claims played out. Alpine scenery and later adventure sports rebuilt the town around Lake Wakatipu. Goldfields relics and the lake steamer heritage sit behind the modern resort economy. Arrowtown's Chinese mining settlement nearby preserves another face of the same rush. TSS Earnslaw steamers continue a lake transport tradition from the pastoral era.

Skyline Queenstown

A gondola climbs from Queenstown to Bob's Peak, where Skyline operates viewing terraces, luge tracks, dining, walking access and other commercial activities above Lake Wakatipu. The Remarkables, Cecil Peak and town grid appear in clear weather. Redevelopment and package changes affect facilities.

iFLY Indoor Skydiving Queenstown

A vertical wind tunnel at iFLY Queenstown creates controlled airflow for supervised indoor body flight, and the commercial activity simulates aspects of freefall but is not equivalent to an aircraft skydive or suitable for every person. Shotover Jet Queenstown

Purpose-built jet boats run through the narrow lower Shotover canyons under Shotover Jet's commercial operation. Skilled drivers perform rapid turns and close canyon maneuvers along a route shaped by river level and weather. Safety briefings, clothing, age limits, cancellations, and photography follow operator rules.

Tiki Trail- Top Station

Began on an exhilarating adventure along the Tiki Trail, a challenging yet rewarding hiking path that leads visitors from Queenstown to Bob's Peak. This scenic route winds through lush Douglas fir and beech forests, offering views of Lake Wakatipu and the Remarkables mountain range. The trail is steep, providing a solid workout that typically takes 1 to 2 hours to conquer.

TSS Earnslaw - RealNZ

The TSS Earnslaw is a historic, twin-screw coal-fired steamboat that has been operating on Lake Wakatipu since 1912. Known as the 'Lady of the Lake,' it offers scenic cruises from Queenstown, providing passengers with a nostalgic journey and dramatic alpine views. Visitors can also view the engine room and the stokers at work.

Queenstown Hill Walking Track

The Queenstown Hill Walking Track is a popular hiking trail that offers panoramic views of the lake and mountains as it traverses hills and forests. Also known as the Time Walk or Queenstown Millennium Time Walk, this moderately challenging hike takes approximately 2 to 3 hours to complete. Along the way, hikers can learn about the area's history through information plaques.
