Destination Guide — Wellington

Explore Wellington

A Brief History

Selected as the national capital in 1865 due to its central geographic position, Wellington was shaped by severe early earthquakes that prompted its distinct timber architecture. Its deep natural harbor and hillside geography have cultivated a vibrant, resilient administrative center. Decades of careful preservation have ensured that this profound historical narrative remains a vital part of the modern experience.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

Mount Victoria Lookout

Mount Victoria Lookout is a must-visit spot in Wellington, offering a tranquil picnic area with panoramic views of the city, harbor, and surrounding landscapes. Rising over 640 feet above sea level, this lookout can be reached by driving or hiking and promises an awe-inspiring vista that captures the essence of Wellington. The gentle half-hour walk from the city center to the grassy glade at the summit makes it easily accessible for visitors.

Free

Embassy Theatre

The Embassy Theatre in Wellington is a grand and extravagant cinema that has become an building for locals. It boasts screens in the Southern Hemisphere and has a classical design that harks back to the 1920s era when it was built. The marble staircase, wooden features, and tiling all contribute to its timeless charm.

Free

Wellington Waterfront Walk

The Wellington Waterfront Walk is a vibrant, pedestrian-friendly promenade that curves along the edge of the city's natural harbor. Lined with public art, historic wharves, cafes, and major cultural institutions like Te Papa, it is a central hub for recreation and civic events, offering beautiful views of the harbor and surrounding hills.

Free

TSB Arena

The TSB Arena is a popular venue for live shows and events, located on Queens Wharf. It is the largest indoor venue in the city and offers comfortable seating with adequate legroom. The upper floor provides plenty of well-maintained toilets, although prices for snacks and drinks can be a bit high. The arena hosts a variety of events and has decent acoustics, making it a great choice for entertainment.

Free

Wellington Cable Car

The Wellington Cable Car is a historic funicular railway that has been operating since 1902. It provides a scenic, five-minute journey from the bustling central business district up to the suburb of Kelburn. The upper terminal offers panoramic views of the city and harbor, and provides direct access to the Wellington Botanic Garden and the Cable Car Museum.

Free

Beehive

Nestled in the heart of Wellington, The Beehive stands as a striking testament to modern architecture and political history. This building, designed by British architect Sir Basil Spence and constructed between 1964 and 1979, is renowned for its unique beehive shape that sparks both admiration and debate among locals and visitors alike. As the home of New Zealand's Prime Ministerial offices, it offers a fascinating glimpse into the workings of government.

Free

Old St Paul's

Old St Paul's is a Gothic revival cathedral constructed in the 1860s by the Anglican Church. It is renowned as one of New Zealand's greatest heritage sites and a prime example of timber Gothic architecture. Despite no longer being an active parish, it remains popular for weddings, funerals, and religious services. The cathedral survived demolition in the 1960s when a new St Paul's was built nearby.

Free

Hnry Stadium

Sky Stadium, also known as the Cake Tin, is a 34,500-capacity sports stadium in Wellington that has been hosting rugby, cricket, soccer matches and big-name concerts since its opening in 2000. The stadium offers a great viewer experience and hosts a variety of events including concerts, sports events, and exhibitions. It is praised for its large capacity, ample parking space, clean facilities and diverse food selection.

Free

Zealandia Te Māra a Tāne

Zealandia Te Māra a Tāne is an extraordinary wildlife sanctuary located just a short drive from Wellington's bustling city center. This eco-park spans 225 hectares of lush, regenerating forest and serves as a haven for native species, including rare birds, lizards, frogs, and unique plant life.

Free

Wellington Botanic Garden

Wellington Botanic Garden, established in 1868, spans 61 acres and offers a variety of attractions for visitors. It features native bush, a tropical greenhouse, seasonal beds, and colorful floral displays. The garden provides numerous walking routes suitable for families or solo explorers seeking a peaceful retreat from the city.

Free

Wellington Wind Turbine

Wellington Wind Turbine, located atop Brooklyn Hill, offers panoramic views of Wellington city and harbor, Cook Strait, and even the South Island in the distance. As New Zealand's first commercial wind turbine, it has been generating electricity since 1993. The site is not only historic but also serves as a popular tourist attraction with its strong and consistent winds making it an ideal location for a wind farm.

Free

Wellington Zoo

Wellington Zoo, the country's first zoo, is a popular attraction offering animal activities and programs for visitors of all ages. It is located in the green belt of Wellington, New Zealand and was established by former Prime Minister Richard Seddon after being gifted with a cub. The zoo features a diverse range of wildlife from around the world, making it an exciting destination for animal lovers.

Free

Wētā Cave

The Wētā Cave in Wellington is the public gateway to Wētā Workshop, the renowned special effects and prop company behind major films like 'The Lord of the Rings' and 'Avatar.' The facility features a mini-museum with authentic props, a documentary screening, and guided tours that provide a fascinating behind-the-scenes look at the movie-making process.

Free

Weta Workshop

Nestled in the heart of Wellington, Wētā Workshop stands as a beacon of creativity and innovation in the realm of special effects and prop creation. Renowned for its groundbreaking work on films like The Lord of the Rings Trilogy, Avatar, and King Kong, this studio has captivated audiences with its visual storytelling.
