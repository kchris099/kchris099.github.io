Wellington

Chosen as capital in 1865 for its central location, Wellington rebuilt in timber after early earthquakes. The deep harbour and hillside suburbs shaped a compact administrative city. Parliament and the waterfront warehouses express that capital function more than any gold-rush myth. Cable cars and wooden villas on the hills answer the steep topography of the harbour bowl.

Old St Paul's is a Gothic revival cathedral constructed in the 1860s by the Anglican Church.
