Tongariro

Tongariro National Park, gazetted in 1887, protects volcanic peaks sacred to Ngāti Tūwharetoa on the North Island central plateau. Dual World Heritage status recognises both Māori cultural values and active geothermal landscapes around Ruapehu, Ngauruhoe, and Tongariro. Alpine crossing trails and ski fields extend dual cultural and volcanic heritage across the central plateau.

Tawhai Falls (Gollum's Pool)

Taranaki Falls

Taranaki Falls is a waterfall in Tongariro, New Zealand. The area offers an array of day walks and hikes suitable for all levels, from family-friendly strolls to challenging treks. Mount Ruapehu

Mount Ruapehu, the largest active volcano in New Zealand, is a destination with three peaks and two ski fields. The area offers romantic horseback riding experiences at places like Ruapehu Homestead Horsetrekking and Mellonsfolly Ranch/Old West Town. The summit of Mount Ruapehu is snow-covered for much of the year and showcases evidence of past glaciers.

Tongariro National Park Visitor Centre

The Tongariro National Park Visitor Centre is the perfect starting point for visitor adventure in the park. Located near Whakapapa Ski Field, it provides up-to-date information on hiking trails and wildlife viewing spots. The knowledgeable staff offer valuable advice, such as weather conditions and trail closures, ensuring a safe and enjoyable experience.
