Destination Guide — Rotorua

Explore Rotorua

A Brief History

Long recognized by the indigenous Te Arawa people for its powerful geothermal activity, Rotorua became a very sought-after Victorian spa destination in the 19th century. Its history remains significantly intertwined with active Maori culture and the enduring legacy of early wellness tourism. This continuous historical development established a lasting framework that strongly informs the area's modern cultural evolution.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

Whakarewarewa - The Living Maori Village

Whakarewarewa - The Living Maori Village is a cultural attraction located in the heart of Rotorua, New Zealand. It is the only living Maori village in the country and offers visitors a chance to experience the unique way of life of the Tuhourangi Ngati Wahiao people. The village provides guided tours, traditional Maori dance performances, and a feast featuring authentic Maori food.

Free

Redwoods Treewalk

The Redwoods Treewalk is a unique experience located in the Whakarewarewa Forest, featuring 28 suspension bridges that take you through redwood trees over 120 years old. By day, it offers an extraordinary recreational walk, while at night, it becomes a magical setting for photography. The treewalk provides an opportunity to explore one of New Zealand's oldest exotic forests from a new perspective.

Free

Redwoods – Whakarewarewa Forest

The Whakarewarewa Forest, commonly known as The Redwoods, is a vast recreational area near Rotorua. It is famous for its towering Californian Coast Redwoods, planted in the early 1900s. The forest features an extensive network of walking tracks, excellent mountain biking trails, and a unique suspended tree-walk that offers a different perspective of the canopy.

Free

Polynesian Spa

Nestled on the serene shores of Lake Rotorua, Polynesian Spa is a renowned natural mineral bathing and luxury spa retreat. With 28 mineral pools fed by two natural springs, it offers a rejuvenating experience that nourishes the skin and soothes tired muscles. The spa specializes in health and wellness treatments, catering to both relaxation seekers and athletes with hydrotherapy and reflexology walk options.

Free

Government Gardens

Government Gardens is a picturesque waterfront park in Rotorua, featuring a geothermal bathhouse and museum with Tudor-style architecture. Visitors can explore the 50-acre parkland, which includes historic buildings, geothermal features, fountains, and rose gardens. The area in front of the Rotorua Museum offers activities like bowls, petanque, and croquet.

Free

Hell's Gate Geothermal Reserve and Mud Spa

When visiting the North Island, consider include a trip to Hell's Gate Geothermal Reserve and Mud Spa in your itinerary. This hot-springs resort offers a unique experience with its boiling hot pools and erupting waters. The 50-acre reserve features some of the most active geothermal activity in the Southern Hemisphere, including the scorching Kakahi Falls and the steamy Inferno area with its bubbling mud reaching temperatures of up to 100 degrees Celsius.

Free

Rotorua Canopy Tours

Rotorua Canopy Tours provides an immersive eco-adventure through an ancient, native New Zealand forest. The guided experience features a network of ziplines, swing bridges, and treetop platforms. The tours focus heavily on conservation and education, detailing the ongoing efforts to restore the forest ecosystem and protect native bird species.

Free

ZORB™ Rotorua

ZORB Rotorua is the original site of the iconic New Zealand adventure activity: rolling down a hill inside a giant, inflatable, water-filled ball. Located in a picturesque valley, the facility offers several different track options, including straight drops and winding courses, providing a unique, exhilarating, and very entertaining experience.

Free

Skyswing Rotorua

The Skyswing is a thrilling amusement ride located at the top of Mount Ngongotaha, accessible via the Skyline Gondola. Riders are hoisted 50 meters into the air before pulling a release cord, swinging out over the Rotorua landscape at speeds of up to 150 km/h. It offers an adrenaline rush combined with expansive views of the lake and city.

Free

Paradise Valley Springs

Paradise Valley Springs is a wildlife park located just 10 minutes from Rotorua, offering an up-close encounter with New Zealand's native animals, birdlife, and African Lions. Visitors can hand-feed sociable farm animals like alpacas, emus, and sheep throughout the day. The park features cascading waterfalls and a 20-ft.-high treetop boardwalk for views.
