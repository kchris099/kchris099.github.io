Destination Guide — Christchurch

Explore Christchurch

A Brief History

Christchurch was planned as an Anglican settlement on the Canterbury Plains from 1850, with Gothic Revival cathedral and grid boulevards. Post-2011 earthquake rebuilding, Botanic Gardens, and Antarctic exploration heritage record South Island civic life. Tram loops and rebuilt civic precincts connect Canterbury Plains settlement history to Antarctic gateway museums.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

Bridge of Remembrance

The Bridge of Remembrance is a striking stone archway located in the pedestrian precinct around Cashel Street in Christchurch, New Zealand. It was built in the 1920s as a memorial to soldiers from Canterbury who lost their lives in World War I. The bridge also honors those who served in World War II and other conflicts. Following damage from the 2011 earthquake, extensive repair work was undertaken.

Free

Riverside Market

Riverside Market is a indoor market featuring a variety of retailers offering locally sourced food, along with restaurants, cafes, and bars. Originally established as a temporary solution after the 2011 earthquake, it has evolved into a popular destination for food enthusiasts and shoppers. The market offers an array of freshly baked goods, organic produce, seafood, coffee beans, and other delectable treats from local farmers and artisans.

Free

Canterbury Earthquake National Memorial

The Canterbury Earthquake National Memorial is located on both sides of the Avon River, downstream of the Montreal Street Bridge. It was created as a place for people to reflect and remember the 185 individuals who lost their lives in the 2011 Christchurch Earthquake. The memorial also honors those who aided in rescue and recovery efforts.

Free

Mona Vale Garden Park

Nestled alongside the serene Avon River, Mona Vale Garden Park is a captivating blend of history and natural beauty. This early 1900s homestead boasts meticulously designed formal gardens, lush fernery, and scenic riverside walks that invite visitors to immerse themselves in nature's splendor. With an impressive collection of perennial and rare trees, the park offers a unique opportunity to explore diverse plant species.

Free

The Margaret Mahy Family Playground

The Margaret Mahy Family Playground is a popular destination in Christchurch, built as part of the city's recovery efforts after the 2011 earthquake. Named after beloved children's author Margaret Mahy, this expansive playground spans 2.5 hectares and offers attractions for all ages. From towering slides and climbing nets to flying foxes and a splash pad water play area in summer, there's something for everyone here.

Free

Isaac Theatre Royal

The Isaac Theatre Royal, In Christchurch, is a beautifully restored 1908 theater with a rich history. Originally known as the Canterbury Music Hall, it has retained much of its original ornate interior and architecture. Following restoration work in the late 1990s to strengthen it against earthquakes, the theater survived significant seismic events in 2010 and 2011.

Free

Cardboard Cathedral

The Cardboard Cathedral, also known as the Transitional Cathedral, was built as an interim replacement for a church damaged in a 2011 earthquake. Located near the severely-damaged Christ Church Cathedral, it has become a symbol of Christchurch's resilience and rebuilding efforts. Designed by architect Shigeru Ban, this innovative structure is made of cardboard tubes, polycarbon roofing, and shipping container walls.

Free

Christchurch Botanic Gardens

The Christchurch Botanic Gardens, established in 1863, are located in a 21-hectare space next to the Avon River. The gardens feature native and exotic plants outdoors and in several conservatories. Throughout the year, visitors can enjoy different blooms such as cherry blossoms in September, roses in November, colorful hydrangeas in March, and orchids in June.

Free

Hagley Park North

Hagley Park North is a sprawling urban park in Christchurch, established in the 1850s for the enjoyment of its residents. It spans 165 hectares and features botanical gardens, a small lake, tennis courts, and other sports facilities. Divided into North and South Hagley Park by Riccarton Avenue, it offers ample space for various activities such as sports games, jogging tracks, bike lanes, and picnics.

NZ$45

Christchurch Gondola

The Christchurch Gondola offers a scenic cable car ride to the summit of Mount Cavendish in the Port Hills. The journey provides dramatic, 360-degree panoramic views of the city of Christchurch, the Canterbury Plains, the Southern Alps, and the dramatic coastline of the Banks Peninsula. The summit complex features a cafe, walking trails, and a historical ride.

Free

Bridle Path Walk

The Bridle Path Walk is a popular 2.4km trail that leads from the Port Hills to Lyttelton, offering views over Lyttelton Harbour. It's a classic route for both walkers and cyclists, with a steep incline but rewarding scenery. The walk provides fantastic views of the eastern part of Christchurch and is perfect for cooler weather due to ample sunlight. While there are only a few benches along the way, the panoramic vistas make it worthwhile.

NZ$74

International Antarctic Centre

The International Antarctic Centre in Christchurch is a major educational and entertainment facility dedicated to the exploration and science of Antarctica. Visitors can experience a simulated Antarctic storm, ride in an all-terrain Hägglund vehicle, and observe Little Blue Penguins. It serves as the primary visitor center for the city's role as a major Antarctic gateway.

NZ$14

He Puna Taimoana Hot Pools

He Puna Taimoana Hot Pools offers a range of amenities including spacious changing rooms, showers, paid and unpaid lockers, and an attached cafe. The pool overlooks the sea and is hot but can get crowded. There's also a good-sized exercise pool for proper swimming and a hydrotherapy pool with warm water and lots of jets. The facility provides easy access for disabled individuals with low steps or ramps into the pools.
