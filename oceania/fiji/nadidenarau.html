Nadidenarau

Denarau Island, off Viti Levu near Nadi, was largely mangrove and farmland until late-20th-century reclamation created a planned resort peninsula. Developers linked hotels, a marina, and a golf course to Nadi International Airport, making it Fiji's main gateway strip for long-haul visitors. Indigenous land agreements and coastal engineering shaped the layout. Today shuttle boats, marina berths, and resort villages organise most stays around Denarau.

South Sea Cruises

South Sea Cruises is a major maritime transport operator based at Port Denarau, Fiji. They provide essential ferry services and day cruises to the idyllic Mamanuca and Yasawa Island groups. The modern catamarans offer comfortable travel and access to pristine beaches, snorkeling sites, and exclusive island resorts.

Big Bula Waterpark

On Denarau Island, Big Bula Waterpark runs year-round as a family entertainment complex in Fiji's tropical climate. Slides and pools offer an alternative to beach days on the same island. Paths and shade around the attractions support longer stays between rides.

Sri Siva Subramaniya Swami Temple

Colorful carved towers rise above the Sri Siva Subramaniya Swami Temple at the southern end of Nadi; the present complex was completed in the 1990s in a South Indian Dravidian style and supports worship for Fiji's Indo-Fijian Hindu community. Shrines, painted ceilings, ceremonial spaces, and religious festivals give the temple an active spiritual role, with dress and photography rules applying inside.

04 AdmissionFJD 30

Sabeto Hot Spring Mud Pool and Tours

Sabeto's community-run mud pool and hot springs lie in the foothills between Nadi and Lautoka. A typical visit moves from cool mineral mud to drying areas and a sequence of warm pools in a simple rural setting. The experience is commercial and social as well as geothermal, with changing facilities, local guides, massage offers, and village management shaping access.

17.7182° S / 177.4844° E

05 AdmissionFJD 35

Garden of the Sleeping Giant

Raymond Burr established the Garden of the Sleeping Giant in the Sabeto valley as a private orchid collection in the late twentieth century. Covered walkways pass orchids, tropical plants, lily ponds, forest, and landscaped lawns beneath the mountain profile that gives the garden its name; it is a separate attraction from the nearby mud pools and hot springs, though tours often combine the two.
