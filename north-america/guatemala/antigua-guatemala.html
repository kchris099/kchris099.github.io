Destination Guide — Antigua Guatemala

Explore Antigua Guatemala

A Brief History

Antigua served as Spanish Guatemala's capital until 1773 earthquakes led to relocation. Baroque churches, convents, and arcaded plazas beneath volcan Agua record colonial administration and monastic patronage in the central highlands. Volcano vistas and restored convent ruins now anchor heritage walks through the former viceregal capital. Walking routes through the historic centre help visitors relate these monuments to wider patterns of settlement and civic life in Guatemala.
