Antigua Guatemala

Antigua served as Spanish Guatemala's capital until 1773 earthquakes led to relocation. Baroque churches, convents, and arcaded plazas beneath volcan Agua record colonial administration and monastic patronage in the central highlands. Volcano vistas and restored convent ruins now anchor heritage walks through the former viceregal capital. Walking routes through the historic centre help visitors relate these monuments to wider patterns of settlement and civic life in Guatemala.

Arco de Santa Catalina

The Santa Catalina Arch spans a cobbled street in Antigua Guatemala's historic center, a yellow colonial passageway once linking convent buildings on either side. From the correct approach, the arch frames Volcán de Agua beyond the rooftops. Clock tower and pedestrian traffic make it one of the city's most photographed street crossings.

Iglesia de la Merced

Iglesia de la Merced anchors a plaza on Antigua's north side with a Baroque yellow facade dense with stucco ornament. The Mercedarian church and adjoining cloister ruins show how religious orders shaped the colonial capital. Interior nave and courtyard walls remain in use for worship and quiet walking among volcanic-stone paving.

14.5612° N / 90.7348° W

03 AdmissionFree

Cerro de La Cruz

Cerro de La Cruz is a hilltop cross lookout above Antigua Guatemala, reached by a short climb or road from the town grid. From the platform the colonial rooftops, plaza grid, and Volcán de Agua line up in a single view. Families and walkers use the clearing as an orientation stop rather than a long hike.

14.5643° N / 90.7318° W

Convento Capuchinas

Convento Capuchinas preserves the ruined Capuchin convent in Antigua, including a circular tower of nuns' cells around a courtyard. Earthquake damage left thick walls, stairways, and vaulted rooms open to the sky. The complex illustrates colonial convent planning more clearly than many of Antigua's partially rebuilt churches.

14.5598° N / 90.7312° W

05 AdmissionFree

Santuario de San Felipe de Jesús

Santuario de San Felipe de Jesús stands in Antigua Guatemala as a Catholic sanctuary dedicated to the local devotion of San Felipe. The church facade and nave belong to the town's colonial religious fabric, with processions and feast-day crowds marking the calendar. Interior altars and side chapels remain active for parish worship.

14.5732° N / 90.7348° W

06 AdmissionFree

Parque Central de Antigua Guatemala

Parque Central fills Antigua Guatemala's main plaza with trees, benches, and a central fountain between the cathedral and municipal palace. Cobblestone streets and arcades ring the square, which still works as the town's civic meeting ground. Street vendors, shoe-shiners, and evening strollers keep the plaza in daily use.

14.5569° N / 90.7337° W

ChocoMuseo

ChocoMuseo in Antigua Guatemala is a commercial chocolate workshop and retail venue near the historic center. Demonstrations, classes, cacao products, tastings and displays introduce parts of chocolate production, with depth depending on the booked activity.

08 AdmissionFree

Tanque La Unión

A long colonial stone laundry tank once let residents wash clothes at arched stations along the water channel. The open utility recalls Spanish-era civic infrastructure in Antigua. It sits as everyday historic fabric rather than a church or palace ruin on the tourist map.

14.5552° N / 90.7312° W

Convento Santa Clara

Convento Santa Clara holds the ruined Clarissan convent in Antigua, with tall Baroque church walls and cloister arches open after earthquakes. Carved facade details and roofless nave spaces show the scale of colonial women's religious houses. Visitors walk the courtyard and church floor among surviving stone ornament.

14.5551° N / 90.7307° W

Parque Nacional Naciones Unidas

Parque Nacional Naciones Unidas covers forested slopes near Antigua Guatemala with trails, picnic clearings, and viewpoint overlooks toward the volcanic valley. Paths range from short family walks to longer loops under pine and oak. The park is a green escape from the colonial grid, not an archaeological ruin field.

14.4959° N / 90.6148° W
