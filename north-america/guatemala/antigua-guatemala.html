Destination Guide — Antigua Guatemala

Explore Antigua Guatemala

A Brief History

Antigua served as Spanish Guatemala's capital until 1773 earthquakes led to relocation. Baroque churches, convents, and arcaded plazas beneath volcan Agua record colonial administration and monastic patronage in the central highlands. Volcano vistas and restored convent ruins now anchor heritage walks through the former viceregal capital. Walking routes through the historic centre help visitors relate these monuments to wider patterns of settlement and civic life in Guatemala.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

Arco de Santa Catalina

The Arco de Santa Catalina is an iconic 17th-century archway spanning a cobblestone street in Antigua Guatemala. Originally built to allow nuns to cross the street unseen, it features a distinctive yellow facade and a clock tower added in the 19th century. The arch frames a picturesque view of the Agua Volcano, making it the most photographed landmark in the city.

Free

Iglesia de la Merced

The Iglesia de la Merced is a striking Baroque church in Antigua Guatemala, renowned for its intricate, bright yellow facade adorned with white stucco decorations. Rebuilt after the devastating 1773 earthquake, the church features a low, robust design to withstand seismic activity. The adjacent convent ruins contain a massive, historic stone fountain.

Free

Cerro de La Cruz

Cerro de La Cruz is a prominent hill located just north of Antigua Guatemala, offering the most famous panoramic viewpoint of the city. A short hike or drive leads to the summit, which features a large stone cross. From here, visitors can enjoy sweeping views of Antigua's colonial grid and the imposing Agua Volcano dominating the skyline.

GTQ 40

Convento Capuchinas

The Convento de las Capuchinas is a well-preserved 18th-century convent ruin in Antigua Guatemala. Designed by architect Diego de Porres, it features unique architectural elements, including a circular building containing the nuns' cells (the Tower of Retreat). The site offers insight into the austere daily life of the Capuchin order and the city's colonial history.

Free

Santuario de San Felipe de Jesús

The Santuario de San Felipe de Jesús is a significant pilgrimage church located in a village just outside Antigua Guatemala. It houses a venerated image of Christ that draws thousands of devotees, particularly during Holy Week. The church features a distinctive Gothic-inspired facade and a vibrant surrounding plaza filled with vendors selling traditional crafts and food.

Free

Parque Central de Antigua Guatemala

Parque Central is the historic main square and social heart of Antigua Guatemala. It is surrounded by significant colonial buildings, including the Captain General's Palace, the City Hall, and the San José Cathedral. The tree-shaded park features a central fountain, known as the Fountain of the Sirens, and is a lively gathering place for locals and visitors.

Free

ChocoMuseo

ChocoMuseo sits within the Parque Central de Antigua Guatemala area of Antigua Guatemala, documented in local heritage registers for its civic or cultural role. Visitors approach from the main access road and combine the visit with neighbouring heritage buildings and public spaces in the district.

Free

Tanque La Unión

Tanque La Unión is a historic public washhouse located in Antigua Guatemala, built in 1853. The structure features a series of stone basins under a neoclassical pavilion, where local women traditionally gathered to wash clothes and socialize. Today, it remains a picturesque community landmark surrounded by a small park and views of the nearby volcanoes.

GTQ 40

Convento Santa Clara

The Convento de Santa Clara is a large, ruined colonial convent in Antigua Guatemala, founded in the early 18th century. The complex features an expansive central courtyard, a beautiful two-story cloister with arched corridors, and a richly decorated church facade hidden within the grounds. The ruins provide a serene and evocative setting for exploring the city's architectural heritage.

GTQ 20

Parque Nacional Naciones Unidas

Parque Nacional Naciones Unidas is a protected natural area located near Lake Amatitlán, south of Guatemala City. The park offers forested hiking trails, picnic areas, and panoramic viewpoints overlooking the lake and the active Pacaya Volcano. It also features a unique plaza with miniature replicas of famous global landmarks, serving as an educational and recreational space.
