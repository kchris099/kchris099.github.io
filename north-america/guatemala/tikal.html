Explore Tikal

A Brief History

Tikal was one of the most powerful and populous cities of the ancient Maya civilization, dominating the political and military landscape of Mesoamerica from the fourth to the ninth century AD. Its rulers constructed monumental temples, palaces, and public plazas that reflected its vast regional influence and complex alliances with centers like Teotihuacan. The city was mysteriously abandoned by the tenth century and swallowed by the dense jungle. Today, visitors explore this classical Maya pinnacle through the towering Temple of the Great Jaguar, the Great Plaza, and the Lost World Pyramid.

Top Sights & Activities

GTQ 150

Templo IV, Tikal

Templo IV in Tikal, Guatemala is a must-visit for travelers interested in Mayan history and views. This ancient pyramid, built around 741 CE, stands at 65 meters tall, making it the tallest of its kind in the region. Climbing Templo IV offers visitors sunrise views and a unique perspective of the surrounding jungle. Guided tours bring the ruins to life with insights into Maya history and the diverse wildlife that inhabits the area.

GTQ 150

Templo del Gran Jaguar

The Great Jaguar Temple in Tikal is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and an structure that holds great architectural and archaeological significance. This fully restored funerary pyramid of King Jasaw Chan K'awil stands at 154 feet, offering views from various vantage points within the city. Visitors can explore the site on guided tours, learning about the wildlife, flora, fauna, and Mayan history along paved roads connecting different areas.

GTQ 150

Tikal National Park

A UNESCO World Heritage Site, Tikal National Park is an ancient Mayan city surrounded by lush rainforest, featuring impressive pyramids, temples, and diverse wildlife. It remains a notable local point of interest situated in Tikal, Guatemala. Visitors can observe the surrounding area and its connection to the city's broader historical landscape. Local guides sometimes include it on walking routes through the centre.

GTQ 150

The Lost World

The Lost World is an ancient Mayan complex dating back to CE 250, featuring 38 structures aligned astronomically. It served as a solar observatory and includes the impressive Great Pyramid, also known as the Lost World Pyramid, which predates other temples in the area by at least 400 years. The complex offers views of the surrounding jungle and wildlife. Visitors can take a sunrise tour to witness the jungle come alive as the sun rises from atop the tallest pyramid.
