Destination Guide — Panama City

Explore Panama City

A Brief History

Panama City was founded by the Spanish in 1519 as the first European settlement on the Pacific coast of the Americas. It served as an important transit point for gold and silver from the Inca Empire before being destroyed by pirate Henry Morgan in 1671 and rebuilt at its current location. The completion of the Panama Canal in 1914 transformed the city into a global maritime and financial hub. Today, Panama City contrasts its historic Casco Viejo with a modern skyline of towering skyscrapers.

Albrook Mall

Albrook Mall is the largest shopping center in Panama, conveniently located near the Albrook Gran Terminal. It offers a wide range of amenities including a hotel, casino, bars, supermarket, and various dining options. The mall has spacious passages and stadium-type cinemas with a capacity for 2,500 people. With completely air-conditioned department and brand stores, it also provides 5,000 parking spaces with strict security measures in place.

Free

Causeway Amador

Causeway Amador sits within the Causeway Amador area of Panama City, documented in local heritage registers for its civic or cultural role. Visitors approach from the main access road and combine the visit with neighbouring heritage buildings and public spaces in the district.

$17

Miraflores Visitor Center

Miraflores Visitor Center is a visitor site in Panama City, Panama. The Miraflores Visitor's Center is a must-visit attraction near the Panama Canal, offering a fascinating experience for visitors. Situated close to popular tourist spots like Casco Antiguo and the Causeway, it also has being in proximity to one of the world's largest malls. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Free

Miraflores Locks

Miraflores Locks is a visitor site in Panama City, Panama. The visitor center provides an up-close view of massive cargo ships being raised and lowered as they transit between the Pacific Ocean and Gatun Lake. The facility includes interactive exhibits and a documentary detailing the canal's history and operation. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Free

Pacific Lookout

The Pacific Lookout is a scenic vantage point located near the Amador Causeway in Panama City. It offers sweeping, panoramic views of the modern city skyline, the historic Casco Viejo district, and the entrance to the Panama Canal. It is a popular spot for photography and observing the constant flow of maritime traffic entering the canal.

Free

Photo Parador - Cinta Costera

The Photo Parador on the Cinta Costera is a very popular landmark featuring large, colorful letters spelling out 'PANAMA.' Located along the scenic waterfront promenade, it provides a typical photo opportunity with the dramatic skyline of Panama City serving as the backdrop. The area is a active hub for walking, cycling, and socializing.

Free

Metropolitan Cathedral Basilica of Santa Maria the Ancient

The Metropolitan Cathedral Basilica of Santa Maria the Ancient, completed in 1796 after a century of construction, is a stone church with imposing twin bell towers. Situated in Panama City's Old Town on Independence Square, this historic edifice began construction in 1688 and was consecrated in 1796. Despite surviving an earthquake that destroyed neighboring buildings in the late 19th century, it has been recently restored and is one of Central America's largest churches.

Free

Independence Square

Independence Square is a public square in Panama City, Panama. Independence Square, located in Casco Viejo, is a popular tourist attraction known for its historical significance and active atmosphere. The square is home to the Iglesia Catedral and Teatro Nacional, where many theatrical events take place. Open pavement and surrounding facades define the visitor setting.

Free

Plaza Simón Bolívar

Plaza Simón Bolívar, a historic square in Panama City's Casco Viejo, is a spot filled with cafes and restaurants. Renamed in 1883 to honor the independence hero Simon Bolivar, it offers a bohemian nightlife with live music and dancing at venues like La Casona and Relic Bar. The plaza is surrounded by historic buildings and outdoor restaurants, making it an ideal place for people-watching.

Free

Arco Chato

Arco Chato is a visitor site in Panama City, Panama. This stone archway stretches an 15 meters without any central supports, showing incredible engineering from its time. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest. Arco Chato remains part of the public visitor circuit in Panama City.

Free

Plaza de Francia

Plaza de Francia is a public square in Panama City, Panama. Plaza de Francia is a tourist attraction that pays tribute to France's contribution in constructing the Panama Canal. During the day, there isn't much to see but it's still worth taking a quick stroll around. The area has many Mexican restaurants, fedora hats, and rooftop bars. Open pavement and surrounding facades define the visitor setting.

Free

Church of the Mercy

Church of the Mercy is a religious building in Panama City, Panama. Records associated with the site include 1680. The Church of Merced, located in Casco Viejo, is a historical and religious structure with a baroque facade dating back to 1680. It showcases the rich history of the city and attracts tourists who are interested in exploring its diverse range of cathedrals, museums, shops, and dining options. The exterior and main interior spaces are usually viewable outside service times.

Casco Viejo

Casco Viejo is a visitor site in Panama City, Panama. Casco Viejo, located in Panama City, is a district that showcases the country's diverse architectural history. Visitors can explore various architectural styles, from traditional Spanish colonial to contemporary designs incorporating sustainable materials and recent technology. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Free

Plaza V Centenario

Plaza V Centenario is a public square in Panama City, Panama. Plaza V Centenario is a modern public square located in the historic Casco Viejo district of Panama City. It was built to commemorate the 500th anniversary of the discovery of the Pacific Ocean by Vasco Núñez de Balboa. Open pavement and surrounding facades define the visitor setting.

Free

Panamá Viejo

Panamá Viejo is the archaeological site of the original Panama City, founded in 1519 and destroyed by the pirate Henry Morgan in 1671. The ruins include the well known stone tower of the old cathedral, remnants of convents, and colonial houses. The site provides a profound look into the early Spanish colonial history of the Americas.

Free

Parque Omar

Parque Omar is a visitor site in Panama City, Panama. Parque Omar is a former golf course transformed into a large green space, offering a natural retreat for city dwellers. The park also houses the national library with computers and internet access and has restaurants and juice bars, it hosts regular activities such as free concerts and art shows during summertime. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Free

Cerro Ancon Reserve

Cerro Ancón is a prominent, 654-foot hill overlooking Panama City, covered by a protected tropical rainforest reserve. It is a popular destination for hiking and nature observation, offering the chance to see sloths, toucans, and monkeys within the city limits. The summit provides unparalleled, 360-degree views of the modern skyline, Casco Viejo, and the Panama Canal.

Free

Metropolitan Natural Park

Metropolitan Natural Park, established in 1988, is an urban wildlife refuge located just outside the main Panama City area. It offers a variety of easy-to-moderate trails, picnic areas, and dedicated lookout points. As Central America's only protected tropical forest within city limits, it attracts nature enthusiasts and wildlife photographers seeking to capture the diverse flora and fauna.
