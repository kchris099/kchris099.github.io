Quebec City

Quebec City was founded by Champlain in 1608 as a French fur-trading post on the St. Lawrence narrows. Ramparts, the citadel, and Lower Town stone warehouses record New France, British conquest, and lasting francophone civic life in North America. Plains of Abraham parkland and St Lawrence promenades extend the French ramparts above the river, and the historic centre remains one of Canada's densest colonial cores.
