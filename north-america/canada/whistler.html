Whistler

The Whistler area was historically inhabited by the Squamish and Lil'wat First Nations. European settlement began in the early twentieth century, primarily focused on logging and trapping. The region's change into a resort destination started in the 1960s with the development of Whistler Mountain for skiing. It gained global prominence after co-hosting the 2010 Winter Olympics, solidifying its status as a premier year-round alpine resort.

Whistler Blackcomb

Whistler and Blackcomb mountains form a linked resort above Whistler Village with extensive ski terrain, gondolas, alpine restaurants and the PEAK 2 PEAK connection. Summer operations shift toward hiking, sightseeing and mountain biking on selected lifts and trails. Weather, snow, wildfire conditions and maintenance can alter access on either mountain even when the village remains open.

Squamish Lil'wat Cultural Centre

Jointly operated by the Squamish and Lil'wat Nations, this Whistler cultural centre draws architectural ideas from a Squamish longhouse and a Lil'wat earthen dwelling. Exhibitions, films, carving, weaving, food and guided interpretation present the two Nations' distinct languages, histories and artistic traditions. Performances and workshops keep the focus on living cultures as well as historical collections.

Whistler Olympic Plaza

Whistler Olympic Plaza gathers open pavement for festivals, markets, and winter skating under lights in the village core. The seasonal rink becomes the main draw when snow settles around the surrounding facades. Between events the square works as a meeting ground linking village paths and nearby shops.

Whistler Village Centre

Whistler Village Centre is the pedestrian core of the resort town, with plazas, shops, and lodging clustered at the base of the mountain gondolas. Festivals and outdoor concerts use the car-free streets through the year, and ski or bike traffic funnels through the same squares to the lifts.

Whistler Sliding Centre

Built for the 2010 Winter Olympics, the Whistler Sliding Centre follows a steep refrigerated track on Blackcomb Mountain. Bobsleigh, skeleton and luge athletes continue to train and compete there, while scheduled public programs may offer guided visits or controlled passenger experiences. Availability depends on season, ice preparation, athlete use, weather and current safety rules.

Scandinave Spa Whistler

Located at the edge of the forest near Lost Lake in Whistler, Scandinave Spa offers a Nordic-inspired wellness experience. The spa encourages guests to indulge in a rejuvenating regimen that includes alternating between hot and cold pools, steam rooms, and invigorating waterfall rinses. Visitors can also enjoy eucalyptus steam baths, followed by a series of hot baths and refreshing plunges under chilly cascades.

Whistler Mountain Peak Chair

Peak Express Chair climbs the upper slopes of Whistler Mountain toward high alpine viewpoints and the Cloudraker area. In summer 2026 it operates on a weather-dependent schedule, carrying sightseers above snowfields, rock and hiking routes; winter use supports expert ski terrain. Wind, lightning, visibility and snow conditions can suspend service with little notice.

Vallea Lumina

Vallea Lumina offers an enchanting evening adventure in the old forest of Cougar Mountain, just a 20-minute drive from Whistler. This magical night walk uses video, lighting, sound, and special effects to bring the natural landscape to life as visitors search for a hidden valley along a brightly lit pathway. The experience is suitable for all ages and changes with the seasons, making it a great activity for families.

Train Wreck

Train Wreck is a popular local favorite hike near Whistler Village, starting at Cheakamus Crossing. The trail follows the scenic Cheakamus River and leads to abandoned train cars transformed into an art installation. It's a relatively easy hike suitable for families and beginners, with the option to connect to a larger network of trails for those seeking more challenge.

Whistler Bungee Bridge

Whistler Bungee Bridge offers an exhilarating bungee jumping experience in a natural setting. Located amidst Basalt Column Cliffs and Old Growth Forest, with the majestic Black Tusk peak as a backdrop, it is considered scenic bungee jumping sites globally. The thrill of freefalling towards the rivers and forests before being safely caught by the bungee creates an moment of weightlessness.
