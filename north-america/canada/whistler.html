Explore Whistler

A Brief History

The Whistler area was historically inhabited by the Squamish and Lil'wat First Nations. European settlement began in the early twentieth century, primarily focused on logging and trapping. The region's transformation into a resort destination started in the 1960s with the development of Whistler Mountain for skiing. It gained global prominence after co-hosting the 2010 Winter Olympics, solidifying its status as a premier year-round alpine resort.

Top Sights & Activities

C$149

Whistler Blackcomb

Whistler Blackcomb is a world-renowned alpine resort located in British Columbia, consistently ranked among the top ski destinations globally. It features two massive mountains connected by the engineering marvel of the Peak 2 Peak Gondola. The resort offers over 8,000 acres of skiable terrain in the winter and transitions into a premier destination for mountain biking and hiking in the summer.

25 CAD

Squamish Lil'wat Cultural Centre

The Squamish Lil'wat Cultural Centre in Whistler is a collaborative facility dedicated to sharing the history, art, and culture of the local Squamish and Lil'wat First Nations. The striking building, designed to reflect traditional architectural styles, houses extensive exhibits, a theater, and a cafe. Visitors can participate in guided tours, craft workshops, and cultural performances that offer deep insights into indigenous heritage.

Free

Whistler Olympic Plaza

Whistler Olympic Plaza is a vibrant hub of activity in Whistler, offering something for everyone. The plaza features an inclusive playground with structures designed to resemble a coastal forest, providing endless entertainment for kids. During the winter, visitors can enjoy skating on the exclusive rink surrounded by twinkling lights. The plaza also hosts free outdoor concerts and performances in the summer, making it a lively destination year-round.

Free

Whistler Village Centre

Whistler Village Centre is the vibrant, pedestrian-only heart of the Whistler resort community. Designed with a distinct alpine aesthetic, the village is lined with boutique shops, diverse restaurants, lively bars, and art galleries. It serves as the central hub for visitors, providing direct access to the mountain gondolas and hosting numerous festivals and events throughout the year.

Free

Whistler Sliding Centre

The Whistler Sliding Centre, operated by Whistler Sport Legacies, is a cutting-edge facility that hosted the bobsleigh, skeleton, and luge events during the 2010 Winter Olympics. It boasts the world's fastest ice track at nearly 1.5 kilometers in length. Visitors can tour the state-of-the-art venue or partake in thrilling passenger bobsleigh and skeleton rides, reaching speeds of up to 78mph (125kph).

C$118

Scandinave Spa Whistler

Nestled at the edge of the forest near Lost Lake in beautiful Whistler, Scandinave Spa offers a Nordic-inspired wellness experience. The spa encourages guests to indulge in a rejuvenating regimen that includes alternating between hot and cold pools, steam rooms, and invigorating waterfall rinses. Visitors can also enjoy eucalyptus steam baths, followed by a series of hot baths and refreshing plunges under chilly cascades.

C$110

Whistler Mountain Peak Chair

The Whistler Mountain Peak Chair is a must-visit destination for both winter sports enthusiasts and summer adventurers. In the colder months, it transports skiers and snowboarders to the mountain's highest point, where views of Black Tusk await. When summer rolls around, visitors can either ride the Peak Chair or embark on a moderately challenging 3.8-kilometer hike through landscapes adorned with towering snow walls.

C$50

Vallea Lumina

Vallea Lumina offers an enchanting evening adventure in the old forest of Cougar Mountain, just a 20-minute drive from Whistler. This magical night walk uses video, lighting, sound, and special effects to bring the natural landscape to life as visitors search for a hidden valley along a brightly lit pathway. The experience is suitable for all ages and changes with the seasons, making it a great activity for families.

Free

Train Wreck

Train Wreck is a popular local favorite hike near Whistler Village, starting at Cheakamus Crossing. The trail follows the scenic Cheakamus River and leads to abandoned train cars transformed into an art installation. It's a relatively easy hike suitable for families and beginners, with the option to connect to a larger network of trails for those seeking more challenge.

C$141

Whistler Bungee Bridge

Whistler Bungee Bridge offers an exhilarating bungee jumping experience in a natural setting. Nestled amidst Basalt Column Cliffs and Old Growth Forest, with the majestic Black Tusk peak as a backdrop, it is considered scenic bungee jumping sites globally. The thrill of freefalling towards the picturesque rivers and forests before being safely caught by the bungee creates an moment of weightlessness.
