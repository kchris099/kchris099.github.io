Explore Niagara Falls Ontario

A Brief History

A site of early colonial conflict and industrial harnessing, Niagara Falls evolved rapidly in the 19th century as railways brought the first mass tourism. Its history is defined by the tension between monumental hydroelectric development and global spectacle. This rich historical tapestry established a strong foundation that continues to shape the city's dynamic contemporary identity.

Top Sights & Activities

Free (parking $10)

Niagara Falls State Park

Niagara Falls State Park is a renowned destination featuring the waterfalls, as well as an aquarium and a variety of attractions such as Maid of the Mist boat trips, Journey Behind the Falls, Cave of the Winds, and Whirlpool Aero Cars. Visitors can also explore Niagara Falls Botanical Gardens & Butterfly Conservatory, Bird Kingdom, and Herschell Carrousel Factory Museum. The area offers diverse dining options and accommodations for different budgets.

$1

Niagara Falls Observation Tower

The Niagara Falls Observation Tower is a must-visit destination for anyone looking to soak in the beauty of this natural wonder. Perched conveniently near the Maid of the Mist elevator, it offers panoramic views that are simply unmatched. From this vantage point, visitors can enjoy unobstructed sights of the falls and watch as boats navigate through the rushing waters below.

Free

Niagara Falls

Niagara Falls is a world-renowned natural wonder consisting of three massive waterfalls straddling the border between Ontario, Canada, and New York, USA. The Horseshoe Falls, the largest of the three, is primarily on the Canadian side and is famous for its massive volume and power. The falls are a major source of hydroelectric power and a premier global tourist destination.

$23

Cave of the Winds

Cave of the Winds is a attraction near Prospect Point, offering an up-close and wet view of Bridal Veil Falls. Visitors are provided with ponchos and wooden walkways that take them within twenty feet of the thunderous falls on the hurricane deck. While it's guaranteed to get soaked, this experience provides some of the close-up views of the falls in town.

Free

Fallsview Tourist Area

The Fallsview Tourist Area is the primary commercial and entertainment district in Niagara Falls, Ontario, situated on an escarpment overlooking the Horseshoe Falls. The area features high-rise hotels, casinos, restaurants, and observation towers like the Skylon Tower. It provides visitors with dramatic panoramic views of the falls and a wide array of leisure and nightlife options.

C$18

Skylon Tower

The Skylon Tower is a must-visit attraction in Niagara Falls, offering panoramic views of the falls and the surrounding area. At 775 feet above the ground, visitors can enjoy sweeping vistas from both indoor and outdoor observation decks. The tower also features a unique revolving restaurant where guests can savor continental cuisine while taking in the sights. Additionally, there's an arcade and the opportunity to watch the 2D/4D film 'Legends of Niagara Falls.'.

C$33

Journey Behind the Falls

Experience the natural wonder of Horseshoe Falls from a unique perspective at Journey Behind the Falls. This year-round attraction offers visitors the chance to view the falls from below and behind through tunnels and observation decks. To reach this vantage point, guests descend 45 meters in an elevator before traveling through a 50-meter tunnel that leads to two observation platforms located at the bottom of the waterfall.

Free (pedestrian to us C$1)

Rainbow International Bridge

The Rainbow International Bridge is a pedestrian-friendly bridge that provides views of Niagara Falls, often adorned with rainbows. This structure was completed in 1941 after the collapse of its predecessor, the Honeymoon Bridge. Renowned for its architectural beauty and outstanding vistas, the bridge earned its name from an engraving of a passage from the Book of Genesis referencing the bow in the cloud.

C$22

Bird Kingdom

Bird Kingdom is a must-visit venue in Niagara Falls, offering an experience with its diverse collection of birds and reptiles. It boasts the title of the largest free-flying indoor aviary globally, providing a unique opportunity to witness over 400 birds from 80 different species in a multi-level rainforest habitat. This award-winning attraction allows visitors to observe beautiful tropical birds, many of which are endangered in the wild, flying freely within their enclosure.

C$21

White Water Walk

Experience the power and beauty of the largest rapids in North America at White Water Walk, located just four kilometers downstream of Niagara Falls. After a quick elevator ride, visitors can stroll along a wooden boardwalk that runs alongside the river through the narrow Niagara Gorge, offering views of rock layers dating back over 400 million years.

C$25

Whirlpool Aero Car

The Whirlpool Aero Car is a historic antique cable car that transports passengers across the Niagara River Gorge, directly above the mesmerizing Niagara Whirlpool. Operating since 1916, the suspended car offers thrilling, unobstructed views of the swirling Class 6 whitewater rapids below. The attraction provides a unique perspective on the powerful geological forces shaping the gorge.

Free

Queenston Heights Park

Queenston Heights Park is a beautifully landscaped city park that features the Brock and Laura Secord Monuments, picnic pavilions, and a playground. It is part of the Niagara gardens which include various attractions such as the Parks Floral Showhouse, Centennial Lilac Garden, Floral Clock, Dufferin Islands, and Oakes Garden Theatre.

C$5

Brock's Monument

Brock's Monument, located in Queenston Heights Park along Niagara Parkway, is a towering Romanesque pillar memorializing a Canadian hero of the War of 1812. The monument offers self-guided tours and rewards visitors with magnificent views of Lake Ontario to the north and Niagara Falls to the south from its top. It stands as one of Canada's oldest war memorials and was once the second tallest tower in the world.

Free

Floral Clock

The Floral Clock is a popular and well-established destination featuring a large clock face created with vibrant flowerbeds. Situated near the Centennial Lilac Gardens, this impressive display boasts 16,000 bedding plants that are changed twice a year. The clock's mechanism can be viewed by visitors when the tower door is open, along with historical photos showcasing its designs dating back to the 1950s.

C$25

Butterfly Conservatory

The Butterfly Conservatory is a captivating venue where witness over 2,000 butterflies fluttering in a lush rainforest setting. It's one of the many attractions at Niagara Falls State Park, offering an immersive experience with nature. The conservatory provides an opportunity to observe these beautiful creatures up close and learn about their habitat. It's a perfect family-friendly destination that offers an educational and enchanting experience for visitors of all ages.

Free

Niagara Parks Botanical Gardens

Established in 1936, the Niagara Parks Botanical Gardens is a sprawling European-style garden spanning 40 hectares. It showcases an array of plants from around the globe, including allees, roses, herbs, and vegetables. The site also houses one of Canada's largest conservatories where visitors can observe butterflies up close and partake in educational programs focused on horticulture.

C$48

Niagara City Cruises

Niagara City Cruises offers iconic boat tours that take visitors into the heart of the Niagara Gorge, right to the base of the Horseshoe Falls. The immersive experience brings passengers very close to the thundering water and dense mist, providing a visceral understanding of the falls' massive power. It is one of the most popular and enduring attractions in the region.

C$15

Niagara SkyWheel

The Niagara SkyWheel is Canada's largest observation wheel, offering panoramic views of the Horseshoe and American Falls, the Niagara River, and other landmarks. The glass-enclosed gondolas reach heights of 175 feet, providing a comfortable vantage point for visitors to enjoy the scenery in any season. With heating and air conditioning in each gondola, guests can take in the beauty of Niagara Falls year-round.

C$14

Niagara Speedway

Niagara Speedway is a thrilling amusement ride that merges the excitement of go-karts with a roller coaster-style track, featuring spiral ramps and hairpin turns. Located in Clifton Hill, it's part of an array of attractions including the Niagara Skywheel and Hornblower Niagara Cruises. The Speedway offers a multi-level experience where visitors can test their driving skills on North America's largest elevated go-kart track.

Free

Casino Niagara

Located in Niagara Falls, Casino Niagara is a popular gambling facility that has been drawing visitors since its opening in 1996. It boasts over 1,300 slot machines and 40 gaming tables, offering games like Blackjack and sports betting. The venue also features a lively sports bar for guests to enjoy.
