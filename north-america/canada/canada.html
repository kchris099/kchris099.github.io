Canada Guide

Canada grew from French fur-trade enclaves and British Loyalist settlements into a self-governing federation in 1867. Confederation linked Quebec's fortified river towns, Ontario lake ports, and prairie railway cities to the Pacific, leaving parliament buildings, harbour forts, and civic cores that reflect French, British, and Indigenous negotiations across a vast northern country.
