Edmonton

Edmonton served as a fur-trade fort on the North Saskatchewan River before Alberta's provincial capital moved here in 1905. Legislature grounds, High Level Bridge views, and West Edmonton Mall-era growth document how Hudson's Bay Company posts, Ukrainian homestead immigration, and Leduc oil strikes of 1947 transformed a prairie administrative centre into a northern logistics hub.

West Edmonton Mall

West Edmonton Mall combines shopping with an indoor water park, ice rink, Galaxyland rides, and marine exhibits. Retail corridors link amusement zones under one vast roof on the city's west side. Visitors often spend a full day moving between stores and ticketed leisure attractions. Hotel rooms attached to the complex let some guests stay inside the mall footprint.

Rogers Place

Rogers Place is Edmonton's downtown arena for NHL hockey and large concerts. Glass facades and Ice District towers surround the bowl used by the Oilers. Event nights fill nearby restaurants; on quiet days the plaza still draws passersby through the district. Winter ice and summer concert rigging swap through the same bowl calendar.

ICE District

Edmonton's ICE District surrounds Rogers Place within a recent mixed-use redevelopment of the downtown core, and the arena draws hockey crowds and concert audiences, while nearby blocks contain offices, residences and hotels. Restaurants, shops and public plazas connect the development with the city's transit network, and event nights bring its heaviest activity.

Citadel Theatre

Five performance spaces at Edmonton's Citadel Theatre stage musicals, plays, live music, and classes under evolving leadership and programming. Patrons enter for ticketed shows rather than fortress tours. The complex works as a major regional theatre campus.

Winspear Centre

Located in the arts district of Sir Winston Churchill Square, the Winspear Centre is a contemporary venue that hosts concerts, choir performances, and major cultural organizations like the Edmonton combination and the Edmonton Opera. Francis G. Winspear, this known performing arts space is home to the beloved Edmonton combination Orchestra known for its diverse music genres and collaborations with various artists.

Commonwealth Stadium

Commonwealth Stadium, home to the Edmonton Eskimos and now known as the Edmonton Elks, is a 60,000-seat venue that hosts Canadian football games along with various other sporting events and concerts. The stadium's atmosphere and lush green lawn make it an ideal location for events. In addition to football, Commonwealth Stadium also accommodates soccer, rugby matches, and large-scale musical performances throughout the year.

Italian Centre Shop Ltd.

The Italian Centre Shop Ltd. Is a beloved gem in Edmonton, with multiple locations that bring the essence of Italy right to visitor neighborhood. This family-owned establishment has been serving the community since the 1950s, offering an authentic Italian atmosphere where indulge in freshly baked pizzas made from high-quality ingredients.

Alberta Legislature Building

Alberta's legislature has met in the domed Beaux-Arts building above the North Saskatchewan River since 1913. Sandstone, marble, a ceremonial rotunda, chamber galleries, portraits and provincial symbols shape the interior. Landscaped grounds, fountains and public paths surround the working seat of government, where tour access must accommodate legislative sessions and security procedures.

Old Strathcona Farmers' Market

Vendors gather inside the former bus garage that has housed Old Strathcona Farmers' Market since 1983; the building's location near Whyte Avenue connects the market with the district's theaters, shops, restaurants and early streetcar-era commercial fabric. Produce, meat, baking, prepared food, crafts and locally made goods fill the indoor aisles on market days.

Castle Downs Park

Castle Downs Park covers a large recreation area in northwest Edmonton. Sports fields, ball diamonds, playgrounds, a spray park, skate facilities, picnic areas and seasonal skating sit near arenas and community buildings. Residents use the grounds throughout the year, although weather and maintenance affect which amenities are available at a given time.

Servus Credit Union Place

Servus Credit Union Place is a municipal sports and recreation complex in St. Albert, north of Edmonton. Aquatic facilities and ice surfaces share the building with indoor fields, courts and fitness areas. Children's spaces and multipurpose rooms support lessons, leagues and community events, while bookings and maintenance control access to individual areas. The complex is used throughout the year.
