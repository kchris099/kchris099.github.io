Explore Victoria

A Brief History

Established in 1843 as a Hudson's Bay Company trading post, Victoria flourished as the main port for the 1858 Fraser Canyon Gold Rush. Its well-preserved Edwardian architecture and maritime heritage reflect its long standing role as British Columbia's administrative capital. This rich historical tapestry established a strong foundation that continues to shape the city's dynamic contemporary identity.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

Cadboro-Gyro Park

Nestled in the picturesque district of Saanich, Cadboro-Gyro Park is a delightful oceanfront destination that offers something for everyone. With its inviting sandy beach and calm waters, it's perfect for swimming and sunbathing. Families can enjoy the expansive playground featuring whimsical climbing sculptures like sea monsters and a giant octopus, while grassy areas provide ample space for games and relaxation.

Free

Legislative Assembly of British Columbia

Parliament House is a attraction visited in the morning to capture its beauty in natural light. The building is set against a backdrop of lush greenery and the ocean, making it perfect for photography. Whether you're into history, architecture, or seeking a peaceful retreat with views, Parliament House is not to be missed. The surrounding area exudes charm with its picturesque parks, fountains, statues, and colorful flowers that transport visitors back in time.

$25

Black Ball Ferry Line

The Black Ball Ferry Line operates the MV Coho, providing a vital and scenic maritime link between Victoria, British Columbia, and Port Angeles, Washington. The 90-minute crossing traverses the Strait of Juan de Fuca, offering passengers dramatic views of the Olympic Peninsula and the rugged coastline of Vancouver Island. It has been a reliable transportation service since 1959.

C$18

Victoria Bug Zoo

The Victoria Bug Zoo is a unique, interactive educational facility located in downtown Victoria. It houses a diverse collection of live tropical bugs, insects, and arachnids from around the world, including giant walking sticks, tarantulas, and glow-in-the-dark scorpions. Knowledgeable guides provide hands-on experiences, allowing visitors to learn about and safely handle many of the fascinating creatures.

Free

Christ Church Cathedral

The Christ Church Cathedral is a large Gothic-revival cathedral in downtown Victoria, BC. The cathedral has a beautiful stone facade and features a pipe organ and a labyrinth. The cathedral is open for tours every day, and services are held seven days a week, including on Sundays at 8:00 am, 9:15 am, 11:00 am and 4:00 pm.

Free

Mile Zero Monument

The Mile Zero Monument in Victoria marks the western terminus of the Trans-Canada Highway, which stretches over 8,000 kilometers across the country to St. John's, Newfoundland. Located in Beacon Hill Park overlooking the Strait of Juan de Fuca, the monument is a popular photo spot and a symbol of the vast geographic expanse of the Canadian nation.

Free

Beacon Hill Children's Farm

Beacon Hill Children's Farm is a documented natural feature in Victoria, Canada, listed in regional geographic and conservation records for its geological formation and protected status. The site is accessible via marked trails and is open to visitors during daylight hours. Travellers follow the principal path from the trailhead and combine the visit with nearby viewpoints and forest walks.

Free (children's farm C$7)

Beacon Hill Park

Beacon Hill Park is a charming 74-hectare Victorian park in Victoria, B.C. It features beautifully landscaped gardens, ponds, and majestic trees. Visitors can enjoy the sight of peacocks roaming freely and relax by the bandshell. The park offers a serene escape for nature lovers and provides a picturesque setting for leisurely strolls or picnics. With its tranquil ambiance and natural beauty, Beacon Hill Park is a must-visit destination in Victoria.

Free

Ogden Point Sundial

The Ogden Point Sundial is a prominent public art piece and functional timepiece located at the Ogden Point Breakwater in Victoria. Set against the backdrop of the busy cruise ship terminal and the Strait of Juan de Fuca, the sundial is a popular point of interest for walkers enjoying the scenic breakwater path, offering a blend of maritime history and coastal beauty.

C$24

Craigdarroch Castle

Craigdarroch Castle is a grand Victorian mansion constructed by coal baron Robert Dunsmuir in the late 19th century. It's open for tours and is one of the notable attractions in Victoria, British Columbia. The region offers a variety of activities including visits to the Royal BC Museum, Cathedral Grove, Pacific Rim National Park Reserve, Rathtrevor Beach Provincial Park, and Mount Washington Alpine Resort.
