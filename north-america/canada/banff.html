Explore Banff

A Brief History

Established in the 1880s following the discovery of hot springs by railway workers, Banff became Canada's first national park. The arrival of the Canadian Pacific Railway drove early tourism, transforming a rugged wilderness camp into an internationally known alpine destination. Visitors today can clearly trace the detailed layers of this unique heritage throughout the preserved streets and monuments.

Top Sights & Activities

C$10

Cave and Basin National Historic Site

Cave and Basin National Historic Site is a significant landmark in Canada, as it was the birthplace of the country's national parks system. Discovered by railway workers in 1883, this site offers interactive exhibits that showcase Canada's network of protected places. Visitors can take guided tours of the original cave to learn about its history and culture, including lantern tours for an after-dark experience.

C$55

Banff Gondola

The Banff Gondola offers a scenic eight-minute ride to the summit of Sulphur Mountain, providing unparalleled views of the town of Banff, the Bow Valley, and six surrounding mountain ranges. At the summit, visitors can explore the observation deck, dine at the mountaintop restaurants, and walk the boardwalk to the historic Sanson's Peak meteorological station.

C$20

Banff Upper Hot Springs

Banff Upper Hot Springs is a historic thermal mineral springs located in Banff National Park, Canada. The hot springs were discovered in the late 1880s and have since become a popular destination for visitors seeking relaxation and rejuvenation. Situated at an elevation of over 5,000ft in the Rocky Mountains, the natural pool offers panoramic views of the surrounding landscape.

Free

Bow Falls Viewpoint

Bow Falls Viewpoint is a popular spot in Banff, known for its scenic waterfall that has been featured in 1950s films. The trail alongside the Bow River offers an easy stroll with views of the river and surrounding mountains. Starting at the south base of the pedestrian bridge, the trail passes through a forested area with benches for taking in the scenery.

Free

Surprise Corner Viewpoint

Surprise Corner Viewpoint is a popular spot in Banff that offers views of the 1888 Fairmont Banff Springs Hotel and Sulphur Mountain. The name 'Surprise Corner' comes from the unexpected and vistas that greet visitors as they round the corner. It's a budget-friendly option for travelers, as it's one of the many free things to do in Banff.

C$89

Mount Norquay

Mount Norquay is a popular ski resort and year-round alpine destination located just minutes from the town of Banff. Established in 1926, it is one of the oldest ski areas in Canada. In the summer, it offers scenic chairlift rides, hiking trails, and a via ferrata climbing experience, providing dramatic views of Mount Rundle and the Bow Valley.

Free

Cascade of Time Garden

Cascade of Time Garden is a public park in Banff, Canada. Records associated with the site include 1930. Just a short walk from downtown Banff, it provides a perfect vantage point for admiring the surrounding mountains and blooming flowers during the warmer months. Paths, lawns, and shaded areas make up the main public facilities.

Free

Banff Visitor Centre

When exploring the landscapes of Banff National Park, a visit to the Banff Visitor Centre is an essential stop. Located Banff village on Banff Avenue, this hub is staffed by knowledgeable representatives from various local organizations, including Parks Canada and Friends of Banff. They are eager to assist visitors with valuable insights about regional hikes, parks, and other activities that will enhance visitor adventure.

Free

Banff Town Sign

The Banff Town Sign is a popular, rustic wooden landmark located at the entrance to the town of Banff. Featuring the town's name carved in a distinctive, park-style font, it is a welcoming photo opportunity for visitors entering the national park. The sign is set against a backdrop of dense pine forests and the dramatic peaks of the Canadian Rockies.

Free

Hoodoos Viewpoint

Hoodoos Viewpoint offers a vantage point of the Bow River Valley and Mount Rundle, with the added bonus of potentially spotting wildlife like elk crossing the river below. The Surprise Corner to Hoodoos Trail is an easy hike that leads to this viewpoint, passing by the Banff Springs Hotel along the way. There are two scenic trails leading to the Hoodoos, which are unique rock formations offering views of the surrounding area.
