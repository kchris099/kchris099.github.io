Explore Ottawa

A Brief History

Ottawa was chosen as Canada's capital by Queen Victoria in 1857 for its location between English-speaking Ontario and French-speaking Quebec along the Ottawa River. Parliament Hill Gothic revival, Rideau Canal locks, and ByWard Market sheds record how lumber rafting, canal defence planning after the War of 1812, and federal civil-service growth shaped a bilingual government city.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

Parliament Hill

Parliament Hill stands as a majestic neo-Gothic complex that serves as the heart of Canada's legislature, surrounded by beautifully landscaped grounds and adorned with captivating artworks. This site is not only a hub for political activity but also holds significant historical importance, having been the backdrop for pivotal moments in LGBTQ+ rights in Canada, including the first gay protest in 1971 and landmark legal battles.

Free

Centennial Flame

The Centennial Flame, located on Parliament Hill in Ottawa, is a significant landmark dedicated in 1967 to commemorate Canada's 100th anniversary of confederation. It has become a popular photo spot for visitors, with the Centre Block and Peace Tower providing a picturesque backdrop. While it often draws crowds, lucky visitors may find moments to capture the flame without too many strangers in the frame.

Free

The National War Memorial

The National War Memorial, located in Confederation Square, is a significant tribute to the Canadian Armed Forces. The memorial features a 70-ft arch and bronze statues, serving as a poignant reminder of the sacrifices made by servicemen and women. Originally built in 1939 to honor those who died in World War I, it has since been rededicated to commemorate all Canadians lost in conflicts past and future.

Free (Lockage fees apply outside June 19 - Sept 7, 2026)

Rideau Canal, Locks 1 - 8 - Ottawa

Rideau Canal, Locks 1 - 8 in Ottawa is a historic site with manually operated locks dating back to the 19th century. The canal is popular for kayaking, cycling tours, and camping. The Bytown Museum near the last lock tells fascinating stories about Ottawa's history and the construction of the canal supervised by Colonel John By. A Celtic cross commemorates the workers who lost their lives during its construction.

Free

Ottawa Sign, ByWard Market

The Ottawa sign in the ByWard Market district marks a pedestrian plaza beside historic produce halls and weekend vendor stalls. The market has operated since 1826 and now mixes farm goods, maple products, and restaurants in converted warehouse blocks east of Parliament Hill. The illuminated sign is a common photo stop for visitors exploring Lower Town.

Free (guided tours C$10)

Notre Dame Cathedral Basilica

Notre Dame Cathedral Basilica, dating back to the 19th century, is a significant landmark in Ottawa. It is the oldest and largest church in the city, featuring striking Neo-Gothic architecture with skyline-dominating spires. The austere neoclassical exterior gives way to a colorful and elaborate interior adorned with magnificent stained-glass windows, intricate carvings, and hundreds of sculptures of religious figures.

Free

CF Rideau Centre

CF Rideau Centre is a downtown Ottawa shopping complex connected by indoor walkways to the Rideau Canal and major hotels. Opened in 1983 and expanded several times, it houses national retailers, a food court, and seasonal displays near the canal locks. The mall sits on former canal wharf land between Sussex Drive and Colonel By Drive.

Free (Lockage fees apply outside June 19 - Sept 7, 2026)

Rideau Canal National Historic Site

The Rideau Canal National Historic Site is a captivating 202-kilometre waterway that links the Ottawa River to Lake Ontario, making it cherished attractions in Canada’s capital. This UNESCO World Heritage Site offers a variety of activities year-round, from leisurely summer strolls and bike rides along its scenic trails to witnessing the impressive lock system operated by Parks Canada. In winter, it transforms into the world’s largest ice-skating rink, stretching an incredible 7.

Free

Rideau Hall

Nestled in the heart of Ottawa, Rideau Hall is a magnificent historic residence dating back to the 1830s and serves as the official home of Canada's Governor General. This grand structure, reminiscent of Buckingham Palace, boasts an impressive 175 rooms spread over 9,500 square meters and is set within beautifully landscaped grounds that span 88 acres.

Free

Casino du Lac-Leamy

Casino du Lac-Leamy in Gatineau is a popular destination known for its atmosphere and top-notch amenities. The casino offers an impressive array of over 1,800 slot machines and 64 table games, catering to various preferences and budgets. Visitors can also enjoy sophisticated dining options, entertainment lineup, and a relaxing spa. Nearby accommodations range from luxurious hotels like Hilton Lac-Leamy to cozy apartments such as Kialaa Studio.

Free (parking C$16)

Mackenzie King Estate

The estate of Canada's longest-serving prime minister, William Lyon Mackenzie King, is a lakeside property that includes cottages open to the public. The main house contains artifacts from the prime minister's life and offers afternoon tea or a meal in the dining room.

Free

Gatineau Park Visitor Centre

The Gatineau Park Visitor Centre is a sprawling conservation park located just a short trip across the river from Ottawa. It covers over 361 square miles and is the second most visited national park in Canada. The park offers diverse activities such as hiking, biking, skiing, and nature spotting amidst its woods, lakes, and waterfalls.

Free

Mer Bleue Bog

Mer Bleue Bog, located just east of Ottawa, is a 7,700-year-old natural bog and in southern Ontario. It offers an interpretive boardwalk trail that winds through pristine wetlands, providing opportunities for birdwatching and photography. The area boasts diverse plant life including rare orchids and carnivorous plants. Visitors can marvel at the unique northern boreal landscape with its unique wildlife and flora reminiscent of the Arctic.

Free

Dominion Arboretum

The Dominion Arboretum, established in 1889, is Canada's oldest arboretum spanning 64 acres of rolling land and showcasing an extensive collection of 10,000 plants including 4,000 species. With its diverse landscape featuring 1,000 tree species and several microclimates, it offers fantastic panoramic views. This picturesque park is a favorite among photographers and nature enthusiasts alike.

Free

Central Experimental Farm

The Central Experimental Farm, located in Ottawa, Ontario, is a sprawling working farm and research facility that also serves as a national historic site. It features gardens, historical buildings, and a museum within a large rural landscape. The farm is home to an arboretum, specimen plantings, ornamental gardens, wildlife garden, heritage buildings and greenhouses.

Free

Parc des Cèdres

Parc des Cèdres is a National Capital Commission green space on the Ottawa River near the Portage Bridge with lawns, shade trees, and views toward Gatineau. The park forms part of the capital pathway network used by cyclists and pedestrians linking downtown Ottawa to LeBreton Flats and the riverfront promenade.
