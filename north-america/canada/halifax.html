Halifax

Britain founded Halifax in 1749 on Mi'kma'ki territory as a naval counterweight in the North Atlantic. The 1917 harbour explosion flattened the north end and forced a wholesale rebuild. Citadel Hill, convoy-era piers, and the Maritime Museum document a city whose importance was military and maritime long before it was provincial capital alone. Pier 21 processed immigrants through the same harbour the navy once convoyed.
