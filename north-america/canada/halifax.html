Explore Halifax

A Brief History

Established as a regional settlement beyond an older fortified core, the city developed through industrialization in the late 19th century, post-war reconstruction, and modern civic expansion that reshaped its public spaces, markets, and surrounding landscape. Restored religious buildings, civic buildings, and natural features now define the contemporary visitor experience.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

Halifax Public Gardens

The Halifax Public Gardens, established in 1836 by the Nova Scotia Horticultural Society and designated as a National Historic Site in 1984, are a well-preserved example of Victorian gardens. Spanning 16 acres, this popular attraction offers travellers free admission to explore its winding paths, vibrant floral displays, charming fountains, and various garden features.

Free

Halifax Waterfront

Halifax Waterfront is a bustling hub with a working port and one of the world's longest downtown boardwalks, stretching approximately 3km. The wooden boardwalk offers picturesque views of the city's historic buildings, shops, and restaurants as well as its bustling harbor activity. Popular dining spots include The Bicycle Thief and Garrison Brewing Company. travellers can soak up the atmosphere while enjoying some nice sculpture/art along the waterfront.

Free

York Redoubt National Historic Site

Located just 12 km from downtown Halifax, York Redoubt National Historic Site is a historic fortress dating back to 1793. This defensive structure offers educational displays and water vistas, making it a perfect destination for history enthusiasts and nature lovers alike. The site provides forested trails with interpretive panels, fortifications, and rifled muzzle loading guns that offer views of the ocean.

C$22

Halifax Citadel National Historic Site

The Halifax Citadel National Historic Site, established in 1749, is a star-shaped hilltop fort that offers guided tours and features a coffee bar and gift shop. Located in Nova Scotia, Canada, the site is visited National Historic Sites in the country.

C$50

Scotiabank Centre

Scotiabank Centre is a versatile venue that hosts a variety of events, from concerts like The Doobie Brothers' 50th Anniversary Tour and The Glorious Sons' North American tour to sports events such as the Halifax Thunderbirds games. travellers have praised the facilities, noting comfortable seating and decent sound quality. While some experienced congestion in certain areas, others were impressed by the accommodating staff who ensured an enjoyable experience.

Free

Grand Parade

The Grand Parade is the ultimate destination for ringing in the New Year, hosting the largest celebration in Halifax on December 31, 2024. This vibrant gathering spot is not only a hub for festivities but also offers views of City Hall and historical landmarks like the Old Town Clock. With its rich history and peaceful ambiance, it serves as a poignant reminder of Canada's war heroes from various conflicts.

Free

Saint Mary's Cathedral Basilica

Saint Mary's Cathedral Basilica, located in downtown Halifax, is a example of neo-Gothic architecture. Originally designed as a smaller Georgian church in 1820, it was later transformed into a grand Victorian Gothic style during its construction in the 1860s and 1870s. The embellished facade was designed by renowned architect Patrick Charles Keely. The cathedral features intricate stained glass windows and a magnificent vaulted ceiling.

C$40

Neptune Theatre

Neptune Theatre, In Halifax, is a renowned performing arts venue with two stages that host a variety of plays and musicals. It is considered the cultural hub of the community and aims to connect and transform through live theatre and music. The theatre features some of the region's artists and musical theatre professionals, presenting a diverse playbill that amplifies creative voices.

Free

Halifax Central Library

Halifax Central Library is a modern architectural gem In Halifax, Nova Scotia. Its contemporary design, featuring a mix of glass, steel, and wood elements, makes it an ideal backdrop for indoor photoshoots. The library's open-plan layout and ample natural light create a bright and airy atmosphere that's perfect for capturing photographs.

Free

Fort Needham Memorial Park

Fort Needham Memorial Park, located in Dartmouth, is a tribute to the Halifax Explosion of 1917. It was established in the 1950s as part of the neighborhood's rebuilding efforts after the devastation caused by the explosion. The park features a memorial Bell Tower and hosts an annual memorial service on December 6th, commemorating the day of the explosion.
