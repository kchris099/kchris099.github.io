Winnipeg

When the Canadian Pacific Railway crossed the Red River in 1881, Winnipeg became the prairie gateway between Lake Winnipeg trade and eastern markets. Exchange District warehouses, The Forks market grounds, and St. Boniface's French-Canadian parish core grew from Métis river commerce, grain elevators, and immigration waves at the continent's geographic centre.

Assiniboine Park Zoo

Assiniboine Park Zoo occupies wooded grounds in Winnipeg's large Assiniboine Park. Habitats include northern species, grassland animals, tropical exhibits and the Journey to Churchill complex, where underwater viewing focuses on polar bears and seals. Conservation, research, veterinary care and education support the collection, whose animals and access routes change over time.

Canada Life Centre

Canada Life Centre is Winnipeg's downtown arena for NHL hockey and large concerts, seating crowds for the Jets and touring shows. Concourses and gates open onto the Exchange District streets, so game nights spill into nearby restaurants and the Forks corridor a short walk away.

The Forks Market

Located at the meeting point of the Assiniboine and Red rivers in Winnipeg, The Forks Market is a community hub offering an array of shopping, dining, and recreational activities. This historic site has evolved into a trendy destination featuring The Common, a gourmet food hall with communal tables and diverse culinary offerings by regional chefs.

The Forks National Historic Site

The Forks National Historic Site marks the junction of the Red and Assiniboine rivers, a gathering place for Indigenous peoples for thousands of years and later a trade hub. Today paths, the market buildings, and year-round events share the riverbank park between downtown bridges.

Saint Boniface Cathedral

The modern Saint Boniface Cathedral stands within the stone facade and open walls of its predecessor, destroyed by fire in 1968; the new 1970s church creates a smaller worship space behind the surviving Romanesque Revival ruins. Cemetery grounds contain graves linked to Manitoba's francophone, Métis, and Catholic history, including that of Louis Riel.

Upper Fort Garry

Upper Fort Garry Provincial Park marks the site of a key fur-trading post with garden beds and an engraved weathering-steel wall. Sculpture and interpretive technology retell why the fort mattered to Manitoba's early trade routes. When grounds are open, routes move through the landscaped remains rather than a fully intact stockade.

Blue Cross Park

Blue Cross Park is Winnipeg's downtown baseball stadium near The Forks and the Red River. The compact field hosts the Winnipeg Goldeyes and other events, with grandstands, suites, concessions and a concourse arranged close to the diamond. Its urban setting connects game days with nearby museums, paths, transit.

Centennial Concert Hall

The Centennial Concert Hall, located at 555 Main St. In Winnipeg, has been a prominent venue for the performing arts since its establishment in 1968. It hosts a variety of local and international artists, showing music, dance performances, and theater productions. Home to esteemed organizations such as the Royal Winnipeg Ballet, the Winnipeg combination Orchestra, and the Manitoba Opera, this hall is an integral part of Winnipeg's cultural scene.

Manitoba Legislative Building

Manitoba's Legislative Building opened in 1920 as a monumental seat for the provincial assembly; the Golden Boy crowns the exterior, while guided interpretation explains both public government and the building's dense architectural imagery. Tyndall stone, a central dome, grand staircases, legislative chambers, memorial rooms, sculpture and symbolic decoration fill the Beaux-Arts structure.

Fort Gibraltar Weddings and Events

Fort Gibraltar is a modern reconstruction of a North West Company trading post established near the Red and Assiniboine rivers in the early nineteenth century. Timber palisades, workshops, a warehouse, and interpretation support the annual Festival du Voyageur and private events. The current Whittier Park site is not the exact original fort and is a historical recreation.
