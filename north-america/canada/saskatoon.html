Explore Saskatoon

A Brief History

Founded in 1882 by the Temperance Colonization Society, Saskatoon grew quickly as a railway hub across the South Saskatchewan River. Driven by agricultural expansion and the establishment of the provincial university, it developed into a resilient prairie metropolis. Museums, markets, and street plans in Saskatoon still show layers from those periods.

Western Development Museum - Saskatoon

Western Development Museum - Saskatoon is a museum in Saskatoon, Canada. The Western Development Museum (WDM) in Saskatoon is a top attraction, offering a journey through Saskatchewan's history. Galleries and public rooms are open on posted hours in Saskatoon.

C$16

Wanuskewin

Wanuskewin is a cultural complex dedicated to honoring the history and artwork of First Nations people. It is built around 21 Northern Plains peoples' pre-contact sites, with a 6,000-year-old archaeological park that is on track to become a UNESCO World Heritage Site in the next few years. The site offers educational trails, ongoing archaeological digs, an amphitheater for First Nations performances, and free workshops for traditional crafts like drum-making and tipi-building.

C$17

Saskatoon Forestry Farm Park & Zoo

The Saskatoon Forestry Farm Park & Zoo is a major recreational and educational facility in Saskatchewan. Originally established in 1913 as a tree nursery, it was transformed into a park and zoo in the 1960s. The zoo houses a variety of native and exotic animals, while the expansive parklands offer heritage buildings, gardens, and extensive walking trails for family outings.

C$40

SaskTel Centre

SaskTel Centre, located in Saskatoon, is a versatile arena that hosts a wide range of events throughout the year. It is the home venue for the Saskatoon Blades and the Saskatchewan Rush, offering exciting junior ice hockey and professional lacrosse games. In addition to sports events, visitors can enjoy concerts, shows, rodeos, and more featuring both local and international talent. The venue provides comfortable seating for over 2,000 attendees with free parking available.

Free

River Landing

River Landing is a documented body of water in Saskatoon, Canada, recognised in regional geographic listings for its ecological and recreational role. The shoreline is accessible from the surrounding district and supports walking paths and boat launches. Travellers approach from the adjacent promenade and combine the visit with nearby parks and waterfront districts.

TCU Place

TCU Place is a visitor site in Saskatoon, Canada. TCU Place, located in Saskatoon, is a versatile convention center and theater that hosts a variety of shows, exhibits, and concerts. In addition to hosting Broadway musicals like Priscilla, Queen of the Desert, TCU Place also accommodates art galleries and theaters for comedy shows, plays, and dance recitals. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Kinsmen Park

Kinsmen Park in Saskatoon is a active green space that beautifully combines nature with art and recreation. This park is a favorite among families, featuring an playground known as Nutrien Playland, which has thrilling attractions like the longest double cable-track zipline and unique play structures such as a Dutch Disc spinning wheel and balance beams. Kinsmen Park also is a gateway to the scenic Meewasin Valley Trail, perfect for hiking or cross-country skiing.

Shaw Centre is a visitor site in Saskatoon, Canada. Shaw Centre is a popular recreational facility in Saskatoon that offers a variety of activities for visitors of all ages. Visitors can also enjoy the hot tub that accommodates up to 25 people. The facility is well-maintained, with clean pools and friendly staff providing helpful service. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Cranberry Flats Conservation Area

Cranberry Flats Conservation Area is a visitor site in Saskatoon, Canada. Cranberry Flats Conservation Area, located along the South Saskatchewan River, offers a peaceful escape from urban life. The secluded location also provides a chance to witness auroras away from urban lights. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.
