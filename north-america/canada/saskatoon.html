Explore Saskatoon

A Brief History

Founded in 1882 by the Temperance Colonization Society, Saskatoon grew quickly as a railway hub across the South Saskatchewan River. Driven by agricultural expansion and the establishment of the provincial university, it developed into a resilient prairie metropolis. This rich historical tapestry established a strong foundation that continues to shape the city's dynamic contemporary identity.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

Western Development Museum - Saskatoon

The Western Development Museum (WDM) in Saskatoon is a top attraction, offering a journey through Saskatchewan's history. The museum features various exhibits such as Boomtown, showcasing the province's economic activity at the turn of the century, and the Transportation Gallery with diverse modes of transportation. Additionally, visitors can explore galleries on topics like Whitecap Dakota First Nation and 100 Years of Saskatchewan History.

Free

Wanuskewin

Wanuskewin is a cultural complex dedicated to honoring the history and artwork of First Nations people. It is built around 21 Northern Plains peoples' pre-contact sites, with a 6,000-year-old archaeological park that is on track to become a UNESCO World Heritage Site in the next few years. The site offers educational trails, ongoing archaeological digs, an amphitheater for First Nations performances, and free workshops for traditional crafts like drum-making and tipi-building.

Free

Saskatoon Forestry Farm Park & Zoo

The Saskatoon Forestry Farm Park & Zoo is a major recreational and educational facility in Saskatchewan. Originally established in 1913 as a tree nursery, it was transformed into a park and zoo in the 1960s. The zoo houses a variety of native and exotic animals, while the expansive parklands offer heritage buildings, beautiful gardens, and extensive walking trails for family outings.

Free

SaskTel Centre

SaskTel Centre, located in Saskatoon, is a versatile arena that hosts a wide range of events throughout the year. It serves as the home venue for the Saskatoon Blades and the Saskatchewan Rush, offering exciting junior ice hockey and professional lacrosse games. In addition to sports events, visitors can enjoy concerts, shows, rodeos, and more featuring both local and international talent. The venue provides comfortable seating for over 2,000 attendees with free parking available.

Free

River Landing

River Landing is a documented body of water in Saskatoon, Canada, recognised in regional geographic listings for its ecological and recreational role. The shoreline is accessible from the surrounding district and supports walking paths and boat launches. Travellers approach from the adjacent promenade and combine the visit with nearby parks and waterfront districts.

Free

TCU Place

TCU Place, located in Saskatoon, is a versatile convention center and theater that hosts a variety of shows, exhibits, and concerts. The 2,000-seat theater regularly features performances by the Saskatoon Symphony during its September-to-April season. In addition to hosting Broadway musicals like Priscilla, Queen of the Desert, TCU Place also accommodates art galleries and theaters for comedy shows, plays, and dance recitals.

Free

Free

Kinsmen Park

Kinsmen Park in Saskatoon is a vibrant green space that beautifully combines nature with art and recreation. This park is a favorite among families, featuring an impressive playground known as Nutrien Playland, which boasts thrilling attractions like the longest double cable-track zipline and unique play structures such as a Dutch Disc spinning wheel and balance beams. Kinsmen Park also serves as a gateway to the scenic Meewasin Valley Trail, perfect for hiking or cross-country skiing.

Free

Shaw Centre

Shaw Centre is a popular recreational facility in Saskatoon that offers a variety of activities for visitors of all ages. The center features three pools, including a 25-meter Warm Up Pool and a 50-meter pool with springboards and diving boards. Visitors can also enjoy the hot tub that accommodates up to 25 people. The facility is well-maintained, with clean pools and friendly staff providing helpful service.

Free

Cranberry Flats Conservation Area

Cranberry Flats Conservation Area, located along the South Saskatchewan River, offers a peaceful escape from urban life. The area features walking trails suitable for families and beginners, leading to a valley lookout and sandy beaches. Visitors can enjoy serene views of the river and the Saskatchewan Prairies. The secluded location also provides an opportunity to witness auroras away from urban lights.
