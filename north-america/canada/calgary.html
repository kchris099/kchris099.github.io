Calgary

Calgary developed from a North-West Mounted Police post at the Bow and Elbow rivers into Alberta's oil-industry capital after Turner Valley discoveries in 1914. Sandstone city-hall blocks, Stampede grounds, and Glenbow Museum collections record how ranching culture, railway promotion, and twentieth-century hydrocarbon wealth built a prairie metropolis below the Rocky Mountain front.

Calgary Tower

The Calgary Tower is a towering landmark offering panoramic views from its glass-floored observation deck and revolving restaurant. In downtown Calgary, it provides vistas of the city's rivers, parks, and surrounding areas. The observation deck offers a 360-degree view of the busy city, the majestic Rocky Mountains, foothills, and prairies.

Olympic Plaza

Olympic Plaza, a former venue for the 1988 Olympic Winter Games in Calgary, has been transformed into a park with a pond and an amphitheater. The plaza is particularly popular for its seasonal ice skating rink, which becomes enchanting at night when illuminated, especially during the holiday season with a Christmas tree on display.

Calgary Public Library - Central

Calgary's Central Library in East Village opened in late 2018 as a multi-level public library with a soaring wood-lined atrium. Reading terraces, event rooms, and an arched entry face the new downtown blocks. The building quickly became a civic landmark for study, browsing, and community programs. Study terraces overlook the East Village streets through irregular window bays.

Simmons Building

The Simmons Building in Calgary's East Village is a reused industrial structure now filled with food vendors. Phil & Sebastian coffee, Charbar, and Sidewalk Citizen Bakery are among the tenants sharing the brick shell. Long tables and the river pathway outside make it a daytime gathering spot. Shared tables fill at brunch when the river pathway outside is busiest.

Peace Bridge

Santiago Calatrava designed Calgary's Peace Bridge as a red tubular crossing for pedestrians and cyclists over the Bow River. Opened in 2012, the low enclosed truss avoids supporting piers in the water and links the downtown river path with communities to the north. Its repeating steel and glass pattern frames views toward the skyline, riverbanks, and nearby park system.

Southern Alberta Jubilee Auditorium

The Southern Alberta Jubilee Auditorium, located in Calgary, Alberta, is a cultural hub that includes entertainment options for visitors. From opera and ballet to theater performances and live music concerts, this venue caters to diverse interests. Built in 1957 to commemorate the 50th anniversary of Alberta, it continues to be a popular destination for locals and tourists alike.

Scotiabank Saddledome

The saddle-shaped roof of Calgary's Scotiabank Saddledome has marked Stampede Park since the arena opened in 1983. The building is closely associated with Calgary Flames hockey, while its adaptable floor and seating also accommodate concerts, indoor sports and large civic events. Its distinctive profile reflects the ambition of the city's early 1980s Olympic development.

Calgary Stampede

The Calgary Stampede is a ten-day annual event centered on rodeo, chuckwagon racing, agricultural exhibitions, Indigenous programming, concerts, midway rides and food at Stampede Park. Its organization also operates year-round venues and community programs. The event draws on regional ranching culture but is a modern large-scale festival.

The Sentry Box

The Sentry Box at 1835 10 Ave SW is a long-running Calgary shop for games, fantasy, and science-fiction titles. Shelves of board games and miniatures fill the floor, with free play sessions on the calendar. Hobbyists treat it as a community clubhouse as much as a retail stop. Staff demos and open-play nights keep the hobby community returning weekly.

Nose Hill Park

Nose Hill Park preserves a large grassland and coulee plateau on Calgary's north side with extensive walking and cycling trails. Native prairie vegetation and city views replace manicured lawns across the reserve. Hikers and runners use the ridge paths year-round as an urban natural area rather than a playground park.
