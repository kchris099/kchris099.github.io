Explore Calgary

A Brief History

Calgary developed from a North-West Mounted Police post at the Bow and Elbow rivers into Alberta's oil-industry capital after Turner Valley discoveries in 1914. Sandstone city-hall blocks, Stampede grounds, and Glenbow Museum collections record how ranching culture, railway promotion, and twentieth-century hydrocarbon wealth built a prairie metropolis below the Rocky Mountain front.

Top Sights & Activities

C$19

Calgary Tower

The Calgary Tower is a towering landmark offering panoramic views from its glass-floored observation deck and revolving restaurant. In downtown Calgary, it provides vistas of the city's rivers, parks, and surrounding areas. The observation deck offers a 360-degree view of the bustling city, the majestic Rocky Mountains, foothills, and prairies.

Free (Closed for construction until 2028)

Olympic Plaza

Olympic Plaza, a former venue for the 1988 Olympic Winter Games in Calgary, has been transformed into a park with a pond and an amphitheater. The plaza is particularly popular for its seasonal ice skating rink, which becomes enchanting at night when illuminated, especially during the holiday season with a Christmas tree on display.

Free

Calgary Public Library - Central

The Central Library, located in the East Village of Calgary, is a architectural masterpiece that opened in late 2018. Designed by Snohetta and DIALOG architects, it boasts vaulted ceilings, abundant natural light, and unique features such as a steel beam time capsule and a mosaic made entirely of books. The building's facade is an ever-changing display of geometric shapes in various shades.

Free

Simmons Building

Located in Calgary, the Simmons Building is a historic site that used to be a mattress factory and now serves as a hub for creativity and culinary delights. The building's original features such as faded signage, brick walls, and solid pine elements make it an ideal spot for photography enthusiasts. Visitors can indulge in delectable treats from Phil & Sebastian, Charbar, and Sidewalk Citizen Bakery within the premises.

Free

Peace Bridge

The Peace Bridge in Calgary is a modern, striking red pedestrian and bike bridge designed by renowned Spanish architect Santiago Calatrava. It has become an symbol of the city's urban landscape and is a popular spot for both locals and tourists. The bridge offers engaging views of downtown's modern architecture, Princes Island, and the new St. Patrick's Island.

C$40

Southern Alberta Jubilee Auditorium

The Southern Alberta Jubilee Auditorium, located in Calgary, Alberta, is a cultural hub that includes entertainment options for visitors. From opera and ballet to theater performances and live music concerts, this venue caters to diverse interests. Built in 1957 to commemorate the 50th anniversary of Alberta, it continues to be a popular destination for locals and tourists alike.

C$50

Scotiabank Saddledome

In Calgary, Scotiabank Saddledome is a versatile arena that serves as the home for various sports teams including the Calgary Flames, Hitmen, and Roughnecks. Since its establishment in 1983, it has not only been a hub for thrilling hockey games but has also hosted an array of entertainment events such as concerts, conventions, and circuses.

C$28

Calgary Stampede

The Calgary Stampede is a renowned annual rodeo festival that takes place in a festival space hosting various shows, live music, entertainment, exhibits, and food stalls. This non-profit organization also offers year-round events like exhibitions, galas, concerts, conferences and rodeos. The festival aims to preserve western heritage and culture while engaging the community through youth initiatives and programs.

Free

BMO Centre

The BMO Centre is a popular choice for hosting large events, with the capacity to accommodate thousands of attendees. The venue's staff are known for their professionalism and dedication to ensuring the success of each event. Recent events held at the center include a car show featuring a diverse range of vehicles and informative exhibits, as well as an international women's show with celebrity appearances and unique fashion displays.

Free

The Sentry Box

The Sentry Box, located at 1835 10 Ave SW, is a haven for adventure, gaming, science fiction, and fantasy enthusiasts. Boasting an extensive inventory of over 100,000 products including board games, miniatures for painting, and Dungeons & Dragons books and dice. The store also hosts free board game sessions on Mondays and various events throughout the week such as D&D sessions and miniature painting introductions. Additionally, rooms can be rented for a small fee.

Free

Nose Hill Park

Nose Hill Park, located on the outskirts of the city, is a spacious and tranquil natural park known for its abundant wildlife and archeological finds. It offers a variety of recreational activities such as walking, hiking, and cycling along numerous trails. The park boasts diverse ecosystems including Rough Fescue grassland and native Canadian flora.
