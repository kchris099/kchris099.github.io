Havana

Havana's harbour attracted Spanish galleons from the sixteenth century, enclosing plazas, fortresses, and baroque churches within colonial walls. Malecón seafront avenues and twentieth-century hotels record republican prosperity and revolutionary-era change. Morro fortress views and Capitolio boulevards connect harbour fortifications to republican civic architecture.

Castle of the Three Kings of Morro

The Castle of the Three Kings of Morro, located in Havana's Old Town, is a historic fort dating back to the late 16th century. It is part of the Morro Cabana Military Historical Park and is a significant defensive structure against pirates and enemy forces. The castle offers panoramic views of the city and is considered one of Spain's most formidable defensive complexes from its colonial era.

Revolution Square

Revolution Square opens as a vast plaza in Havana used for presidential addresses and large state gatherings. Artwork around the square honours Cubans tied to the revolution, and government branches once clustered here. Open pavement and surrounding civic facades define the scale of the political ground.

José Martí Memorial.

The José Martí Memorial stands proudly in Havana's Plaza de la Revolución, a tribute to the revered writer and independence advocate. This star-shaped tower, completed in 1958, reaches a height of 109 meters and is adorned with elegant grey-marble tiles.

National Capitol of Cuba

Cuba's El Capitolio opened in 1929 as the home of the national legislature and later housed scientific and cultural institutions after the revolution. A monumental stair, classical portico, dome, halls, sculpture, and richly finished chambers express the ambitions of the republican era.

23.1350° N / 82.3596° W

05 AdmissionCUP 200

Fort Of San Carlos Of The Cabin

The Fort of San Carlos of the Cabin, an 18th-century fortress complex located in Havana, Cuba, is a significant historical site with a rich past. Built between 1763 and 1774 to strengthen the city's defenses after the British captured Havana in 1762, it is a symbol of resilience and strategic importance.

23.1471° N / 82.3496° W

06 AdmissionFree

Christ of Havana

The Christ of Havana is a monumental marble statue of Jesus, standing at 23 meters high on a hilltop plaza in Casa Blanca since 1958. Crafted from Carrara marble in Italy and assembled in Cuba, the sculpture offers panoramic views of the city and the bay. The artist intentionally left out the eyes to create an impression of watching over everything.

Plaza de la Catedral

In Old Havana near the bay, the baroque facade of Havana Cathedral dominates a paved square of cafes and colonial mansions. The court once formed an early civic center beside the harbor approaches. Musicians gather on the stones between cathedral services and evening cafe trade.

08 AdmissionFree

La Bodeguita Del Medio

Signatures, photographs, music memorabilia and handwritten messages cover the walls of La Bodeguita del Medio in Old Havana, and its fame now draws heavy tour traffic, but the narrow rooms and street frontage still show the scale of the original neighborhood business. The small restaurant and bar became associated with mojitos, Cuban food, musicians, writers and visiting celebrities during the twentieth century.

23.1408° N / 82.3522° W

09 AdmissionFree

Plaza de Armas

Plaza de Armas developed beside Havana's harbor as the city's principal military and administrative square. The former Palace of the Captains General, El Templete, the Castillo de la Real Fuerza and shaded gardens frame its present form. Booksellers and public seating have long animated the plaza, whose layout connects colonial government, the founding tradition of Havana and daily movement through the old city.

23.1402° N / 82.3495° W

10 AdmissionFree

Old Town Square

Plaza Vieja, listed as Old Town Square, dates from 1559 as a residential plaza in Old Havana. Colonial arcades and restored facades surround a paved center once used for markets and public punishments. Cafes and galleries now occupy the ground floors while the square hosts cultural events under the open sky.

23.1360° N / 82.3500° W

Saint Francis of Assisi Plaza

Before the former convent of Saint Francis on Havana's harbor edge, this square joins the Old Havana route with Plaza Vieja, Plaza de Armas, and the cathedral plaza. A lion fountain and cruise-terminal approaches give the court a waterfront civic character. Arcaded buildings around the paving recall the mercantile port functions of the site.
