A Brief History

Trinidad prospered from nineteenth-century sugar and slave plantation wealth in central Cuba. Cobblestone Plaza Mayor, pastel mansions, and nearby Valley of the Sugar Mills record colonial mercantile architecture after the decline of the local sugar boom. Topes de Collantes escarpments and sugar-mill ruins extend colonial plazas into the central Cuban highlands.

Plaza Mayor

Located Trinidad, Plaza Mayor is a city square that is the perfect starting point for a visit of this historic town. Surrounded by an array of beautifully preserved buildings, including the honey-hued La Parroquial Mayor church and the pastel-colored Palacio Brunet housing Museo Romantico, it's a feast for the eyes. The turquoise Sanchez Iznaga mansion adds to its charm with its architectural elegance.
