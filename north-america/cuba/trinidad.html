Trinidad

Trinidad prospered from nineteenth-century sugar and slave plantation wealth in central Cuba. Cobblestone Plaza Mayor, pastel mansions, and nearby Valley of the Sugar Mills record colonial mercantile architecture after the decline of the local sugar boom. Topes de Collantes escarpments and sugar-mill ruins extend colonial plazas into the central Cuban highlands.

Plaza Mayor

Cobblestones and fenced gardens define Plaza Mayor at the center of Trinidad's colonial core. The Church of the Holy Trinity, Palacio Brunet, former merchant houses, museums, and pastel facades surround a square shaped by the wealth of nearby sugar estates. Raised paths and steps cross the sloping space, which remains a civic and architectural focus.
