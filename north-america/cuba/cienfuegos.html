Explore Cienfuegos

A Brief History

Cienfuegos was laid out in 1819 as a neoclassical port on Cuba's southern coast. José Martí Park, Palacio del Valle, and seafront boulevards record French settler influence and nineteenth-century Caribbean commerce outside Havana. Punta Gorda mansions and yacht harbours extend the nineteenth-century port grid along the southern bay. Walking routes through the historic centre help visitors relate these monuments to wider patterns of settlement and civic life in Cuba.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

José Martí Park

José Martí Park is a paved plaza surrounded by 19th-century buildings, featuring a memorial to José Martí and a bandstand. The park pays homage to José Martí, an influential figure in Cuban history known for his role in the country's liberation and independence movement. The area also includes a bustling pedestrian shopping area that leads into the main city park, forming the historic center of Cienfuegos.

CUP 200

Palacio del Valle

Nestled in the heart of Cienfuegos, often referred to as "La Perla del Sur," the Palacio de Valle is a must-visit for anyone exploring this charming city. This Spanish-Moorish architectural gem not only serves as a seafood restaurant but also houses fascinating historical exhibits and boasts a rooftop bar with views. As you navigate through the bay, be treated to picturesque sights of Cienfuegos' landmarks, including Palacio de Valle itself.
