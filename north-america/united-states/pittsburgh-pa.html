Destination Guide — Northeast

Explore Pittsburgh, PA

A Brief History

Pittsburgh sits at the confluence of the Allegheny and Monongahela rivers forming the Ohio, a site long used for French and British fortifications. Carnegie steel mills, Duquesne Incline slopes, and Point State Park outlines record how bituminous coal, Bessemer furnaces, and river-barge commerce made western Pennsylvania the workshop of American heavy industry before postwar redevelopment.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

PNC Park

PNC Park is a picturesque baseball stadium located on the riverside, hosting Pittsburgh Pirates games. The classic architecture of the park mimics America's traditional baseball fields and has hosted all-star games and playoffs. The area around the park offers various attractions such as restaurants, retail shops, and museums like the National Aviary and the Andy Warhol Museum.

Free

Stage AE

Stage AE is a versatile concert venue situated on the North Shore, nestled between PNC Park and Acrisure Stadium. Boasting a spacious interior with a balcony, it also offers outdoor seating and an indoor club. Known for attracting top musical talent from various genres, the venue's outdoor stage is particularly popular due to its excellent sound quality and scenic views from the gently sloped lawn.

Free

Acrisure Stadium

Acrisure Stadium, formerly known as Heinz Field, is a 68,000-seat steel stadium located in Pittsburgh. It serves as the home ground for the Pittsburgh Steelers and the University of Pittsburgh Panthers football teams. Whether you're a dedicated fan or just there for the atmosphere and classic concession snacks, attending a game at this venue is an experience not to be missed.

Free

Market Square

Market Square, established in 1764, has been revitalized into a bustling hub in downtown Pittsburgh. It offers a variety of dining options such as upscale Italian and Creole cuisine, making it a popular spot for food enthusiasts. The square's lively atmosphere and regular events like the Farmers Market and Night Markets make it spots in Pittsburgh. Additionally, Market Square hosts Yoga sessions during the summer months.

Free

Heinz Hall for the Performing Arts

Heinz Hall for the Performing Arts, located at 600 Penn Ave in Pittsburgh, is a historic venue that serves as the home of the prestigious Pittsburgh Symphony Orchestra. The hall boasts opulent decor including red velvet, Italian marble, and Viennese crystal chandeliers. Nearby venues like Benedum Center host performances by the Pittsburgh Ballet, Opera, and musicals by Pittsburgh CLO.

Free

Benedum Center for the Performing Arts

The Benedum Center for the Performing Arts is a renowned 2,800-seat venue located in downtown Pittsburgh. It hosts a variety of performances including local ballet, theater, and opera companies as well as touring shows and musicians. The center features productions from Pittsburgh CLO, Opera, and nationally touring Broadway shows. Additionally, it occasionally presents concerts by notable performers such as Mannheim Steamroller and Straight No Chaser.

Free

David L. Lawrence Convention Center

The David L. Lawrence Convention Center in Pittsburgh is a massive and structure that spans over 1.5 million square feet, offering views of the Allegheny River and the city's skyline. It hosts a wide range of events, including conventions on various topics, concerts, trade shows, and more. Visitors can enjoy views from outdoor patios near the main halls as well as from higher floors accessed through aerial walkways.

Free

Point State Park

Point State Park, located at the confluence of the Allegheny, Monongahela, and Ohio Rivers in downtown Pittsburgh, spans 36 acres and offers a mix of historical landmarks and recreational opportunities. The park features the Fort Pitt Museum and the Fort Pitt Blockhouse, which provide insight into Pittsburgh's founding and its role in the American Revolutionary War. Visitors can enjoy boating, hiking, biking, or simply relax while taking in picturesque views of the city and rivers.

$36

Pittsburgh Zoo & Aquarium

The Pittsburgh Zoo and PPG Aquarium occupies 77 acres in Highland Park with African savanna, Asian forest, and tropical rainforest habitats. Exhibits include elephants, big cats, red pandas, and a two-level aquarium with coral reef and Pennsylvania watershed galleries. The zoo opened in 1898 and participates in Species Survival Plan breeding programs.

$21

National Aviary

The National Aviary, located in Pittsburgh's historic North Side, is a renowned bird sanctuary boasting over 500 birds from 150 different species worldwide. Many of these avian residents are considered threatened or endangered in their natural habitats. The aviary offers interactive exhibits where visitors can walk through and intimately interact with the free-flying birds. Additionally, guests have the unique opportunity to hand-feed and meet rare species during their visit.

$20

Phipps Conservatory and Botanical Gardens

Phipps Conservatory and Botanical Gardens, located in Schenley Park, is a horticultural paradise featuring a Victorian greenhouse and various botanical gardens. The complex has been recognized for its sustainable efforts, with three of the greenest buildings in the world on its campus. These include the Center for Sustainable Landscapes (CSL), Nature Lab at Phipps, and Exhibit Staging Center (ESC).

$25

Carnegie Museum of Natural History

The Carnegie Museum of Natural History in Pittsburgh is housed in a grand building and features an impressive collection of dinosaur fossils, sea creature fossils, and minerals. The museum is one of the four Carnegie Museums in the city and offers extraordinary exhibits. Visitors can also explore the adjacent Carnegie Museum of Art, The Andy Warhol Museum, and the Carnegie Science Center for a diverse cultural experience.

Free

St. Paul Cathedral

St. Paul Cathedral, a Gothic Revival structure completed in 1906, is located near the University of Pittsburgh and serves as the mother church of the Roman Catholic Diocese of Pittsburgh. Situated in the intellectual center of Pittsburgh's Oakland neighborhood, this impressive cathedral stands at 247 feet tall and features a statue of St. Paul atop its grand facade.

Free

Grandview Overlook

Grandview Overlook, situated atop the 450-foot hill of Mt. Washington, offers a romantic and picturesque setting with its fenced lookout along Grandview Ave. Visitors can reach this revered viewpoint by climbing up the steep slopes or taking a scenic ride on the historic Duquesne Incline or Monongahela Incline.

$25

Gateway Clipper Fleet

Gateway Clipper Fleet operates riverboat dining and sightseeing cruises on the Allegheny, Monongahela, and Ohio rivers from Station Square. Narrated one-hour tours pass the Point, stadiums, and industrial shorelines, while evening dinner cruises feature live entertainment. The fleet has served Pittsburgh visitors since 1958 with climate-controlled indoor decks.

Free

F.N.B. Stadium

Highmark Stadium is a 3,500-seat venue located in Pittsburgh, offering a picturesque setting along the Monongahela River. It serves as the home ground for the Riverhounds soccer team and hosts various professional and college sports events. The stadium provides views of the city skyline and the Fort Pitt Bridge, making it an ideal spot to enjoy a soccer match or other sporting events.

$5

Duquesne Incline

The Duquesne Incline in Pittsburgh offers a unique way to experience the city's views. This historic funicular was once the primary mode of transportation for locals navigating the steep hills, and now it provides visitors with an journey to the top of Mt. Washington. The upper station house features a gift shop offering Pittsburgh-themed souvenirs, maps, and photos of the city.

Free

Points of View

Nestled on the picturesque hills of Mount Washington, Point of View is a must-visit park that boasts not only a bronze sculpture of George Washington but also panoramic views of Pittsburgh. This remarkable statue commemorates the historic meeting between Washington and Seneca leader Guyasuta, making it a significant cultural landmark.

$50

Kennywood

Kennywood is a historic amusement park located just a short drive from downtown Pittsburgh. It offers a mix of classic and modern rides, including famous wooden roller coasters and attractions for visitors of all ages. The park spans 150 acres along the Monongahela River and features over 45 rides, such as the thrilling Sky Rocket coaster and water rides like Pittsburg Plunge.

Free

Pennsylvania Macaroni Co.

Pennsylvania Macaroni Co., affectionately known as Penn Mac by locals, is a long-standing Italian grocery store in the Strip District. It includes Italian groceries, including cheese and pasta, along with a deli counter. The shop is part of the city's immigrant history and provides an authentic experience with its hand-painted signs and bustling atmosphere.

Free

Mr. Smalls Theatre

Mr. Smalls Theatre, located in the Millvale neighborhood, is a versatile venue housed in a former Catholic church. The main theater space can accommodate around 800 people and hosts national acts, offering top-notch acoustics for an eclectic range of performances. Additionally, The Funhouse within the complex provides a more intimate setting for up to 175 attendees and is perfect for local band bills.
