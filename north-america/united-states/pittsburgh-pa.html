Pittsburgh, PA

Situated at the confluence of the Allegheny and Monongahela rivers, Pittsburgh began as a strategic frontier fort during the French and Indian War. In the nineteenth century, abundant local coal and river transport fueled its rise as the center of the American steel industry, attracting waves of European immigrants. Following the decline of heavy manufacturing in the late twentieth century, Pittsburgh successfully transitioned to an economy driven by healthcare, education, and technology. The city's dramatic topography and numerous bridges reflect its industrial past and modern resilience.
