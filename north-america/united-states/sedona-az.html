Sedona, AZ

Sedona sat as a small ranching and orchard community among red-rock canyons until mid-20th-century Western films and later New Age tourism expanded lodging and galleries. Indigenous peoples including the Yavapai and Apache have deeper ties to the Verde Valley. Oak Creek and surrounding national forest land limit sprawl. Chapel architecture, trailheads, and vortex lore now organise a desert town built on scenery and seasonal visitors.

Chapel of the Holy Cross

Located atop the red rock buttes of Sedona, the Chapel of the Holy Cross is a modern notable building that dates back to 1956. Perched about 250 feet high on a twin pinnacled spur, this chapel offers panoramic views of the surrounding landscape. While it is an important site for the Catholic Church Diocese, it warmly welcomes visitors daily (except on major holidays) to explore its unique design and artwork.

Sedona Airport Scenic Lookout

The Sedona Airport Scenic Lookout offers views of the town and rugged red sandstone hills, especially at sunset. Situated atop a large hill in West Sedona, visitors can drive up to the parking area for a small fee and enjoy panoramic vistas of West Sedona, Coffee Pot Mountain, Thunder Mountain (Capitol Butte), and Coffeepot Rock. The lookout is also popular for watching the sunset or sunrise over the landscape.

Airport Mesa

Airport Mesa offers a diverse hiking experience suitable for all skill levels. The 3-mile loop trail around the Sedona Airport provides panoramic views of the red rocks and passes by a vortex. The trail is fairly even with moderate length, making it an easy hike for most visitors, there are basalt boulders and red rocks along the way, creating a setting for hikers to explore.

Amitabha Stupa and Peace Park

Amitabha Stupa and Peace Park is a public park in Sedona, AZ, United States. This Tibetan Buddhist shrine, adorned with prayer flags, stands at 36 feet tall and is a place of pilgrimage for devout Buddhists who leave offerings at its base. Visitors are welcome to explore the park from dawn to dusk, where they can also find the Tara Stupa and a mahogany statue of Shakyamuni Buddha. Paths, lawns, and shaded areas make up the main public facilities.

Boynton Canyon Trail

The Boynton Canyon Trail is a very popular, scenic hiking route in Sedona, Arizona. The trail winds through a dramatic red rock box canyon, featuring towering sandstone walls and dense, diverse vegetation. It is also widely recognized as the site of one of Sedona's major 'vortexes,' drawing visitors seeking spiritual energy and meditation spots.
