Destination Guide — West

Explore Sedona, AZ

A Brief History

Settled by ranchers and homesteaders in the early 20th century, Sedona's dramatic red rock canyons remained a quiet agricultural outpost. The mid-century rise of Western filmmaking and subsequent spiritual tourism fundamentally transformed the rugged landscape into a celebrated desert retreat. The remarkable survival of these cultural and architectural milestones provides a powerful window into the region's storied past.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

Chapel of the Holy Cross

Nestled atop the red rock buttes of Sedona, the Chapel of the Holy Cross is a modern architectural marvel that dates back to 1956. Perched about 250 feet high on a twin pinnacled spur, this chapel offers panoramic views of the surrounding landscape. While it serves as an important site for the Catholic Church Diocese, it warmly welcomes visitors daily (except on major holidays) to explore its unique design and impressive artwork.

Free

Sedona Airport Scenic Lookout

The Sedona Airport Scenic Lookout offers views of the town and rugged red sandstone hills, especially at sunset. Situated atop a large hill in West Sedona, visitors can drive up to the parking area for a small fee and enjoy panoramic vistas of West Sedona, Coffee Pot Mountain, Thunder Mountain (Capitol Butte), and Coffeepot Rock. The lookout is also popular for watching the sunset or sunrise over the landscape.

Free

Airport Mesa

Airport Mesa offers a diverse hiking experience suitable for all skill levels. The 3-mile loop trail around the Sedona Airport provides panoramic views of the red rocks and passes by a vortex. The trail is fairly even with moderate length, making it an easy hike for most visitors. Additionally, there are basalt boulders and red rocks along the way, creating a picturesque setting for hikers to explore.

Free

Amitabha Stupa and Peace Park

Nestled in the serene landscape of Sedona, Amitabha Stupa and Peace Park is a spiritual oasis spread across 14 acres. This Tibetan Buddhist shrine, adorned with prayer flags, stands at 36 feet tall and is a place of pilgrimage for devout Buddhists who leave offerings at its base. Visitors are welcome to explore the park from dawn to dusk, where they can also find the Tara Stupa and a mahogany statue of Shakyamuni Buddha.

Free

Soldier Pass Trailhead

The Soldier Pass Trailhead provides access to one of Sedona's most diverse and visually striking hiking areas. The trail leads to significant geological features, including the Devil's Kitchen sinkhole and the Seven Sacred Pools. The route offers excellent views of the surrounding red rock formations and connects to a larger network of wilderness trails.

Free

Red Rock Ranger District Visitor Center

The Red Rock Ranger District Visitor Center is the primary information hub for the Coconino National Forest surrounding Sedona. It offers comprehensive maps, trail conditions, and educational exhibits on the region's unique geology, flora, and fauna. The center also provides essential guidance on purchasing the required Red Rock passes for parking at trailheads.

Free

Boynton Canyon Trail

The Boynton Canyon Trail is a very popular, scenic hiking route in Sedona, Arizona. The trail winds through a dramatic red rock box canyon, featuring towering sandstone walls and dense, diverse vegetation. It is also widely recognized as the site of one of Sedona's major 'vortexes,' drawing visitors seeking spiritual energy and meditation spots.

Free

Devil's Bridge Trailhead

Devil's Bridge Trailhead is a documented piece of civic infrastructure in Sedona, United States, recognised in local engineering and heritage listings for its construction period and continued public use. The structure is accessible from the surrounding streets and is visible from adjacent viewpoints. Travellers cross or view the site while moving between nearby districts and transit connections.
