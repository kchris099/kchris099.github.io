Joshua Tree, CA

Joshua Tree sits where the Mojave and Colorado deserts meet, named for the yucca trees that early Mormon travellers likened to biblical figures. Miners and ranchers left scattered workings; artists and climbers later claimed the granite outcrops. The area became a national monument in 1936 and a national park in 1994. Boulder piles, fan-palm oases, and night-sky views now structure visits across the high desert.

Joshua Tree Visitor Center

The Joshua Tree National Park travellers Center is a small facility located just off Highway 62, offering maps, exhibits, books, souvenirs, and information on guided hikes. Travellers can chat with park rangers to learn about their favorite hikes and pick up field guides on desert wildflowers. The center opens at 7:30 am and is a convenient starting point for morning adventures in the park.

World Famous Crochet Museum

The Crochet Museum, located next to Art Queen in Joshua Tree, is a and free attraction featuring an array of crocheted animals and creations housed in a converted drive-thru photo stand. Travellers can step inside the museum and take selfies at the selfie stand set up outside. While nothing is for sale inside the museum, donations are welcome. Nearby, travellers can support a small business by purchasing crochet novelties from the Art Queen store.

Noah Purifoy Outdoor Desert Art Museum

The Noah Purifoy Outdoor Desert Art Museum is a unique open-air exhibit featuring an unconventional collection of folk-art assemblages. The sculptures and installations, referred to as 'Junk Dada', are created by famed African American artist Noah Purifoy and have been showcased in some of the world's top museums. However, travellers can view some of his works for free at his former outdoor desert studio located north of Joshua Tree.

Desert Christ Park

Desert Christ Park, established between 1951 and 1961 by Frank Antone Martin, is a 3.5-acre sculpture garden showing over 35 religious statues set against the Mojave Desert backdrop. Travellers can experience a unique and offbeat nighttime adventure as the illuminated sculptures create a surreal atmosphere under the starry desert sky. Despite its small size, it offers an interesting stroll that can be completed in less than half an hour.
