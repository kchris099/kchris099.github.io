A Brief History

The United States traces its origins to thirteen British colonies along the Atlantic seaboard whose Declaration of Independence in 1776 established a federal union that gradually expanded westward through Louisiana, the Mexican Cession, and Alaska. Nineteenth-century industrialisation transformed the country into a global manufacturing power while twentieth-century immigration, suburban growth, and the civil-rights movement reshaped its social fabric. Today the United States preserves a layered regional heritage through its National Park system, surviving colonial districts in Charleston and Boston, Spanish mission corridors in California, and contemporary civic landmarks in Manhattan and Washington, each anchoring a national narrative of continuity and renewal.

Top Sights & Activities

$15

Joshua Tree Visitor Center

The Joshua Tree National Park Visitor Center is a small facility located just off Highway 62, offering maps, exhibits, books, souvenirs, and information on guided hikes. Visitors can chat with park rangers to learn about their favorite hikes and pick up field guides on desert wildflowers. The center opens at 7:30 am and serves as a convenient starting point for morning adventures in the park.

$15

World Famous Crochet Museum

The Crochet Museum, located next to Art Queen in Joshua Tree, is a charming and free attraction featuring an array of crocheted animals and creations housed in a converted drive-thru photo stand. Visitors can step inside the museum and take selfies at the selfie stand set up outside. While nothing is for sale inside the museum, donations are welcome. Nearby, visitors can support a small business by purchasing crochet novelties from the Art Queen store.

$15

‘Transmission’ Sculpture by Daniel Popper

If you're venturing near Joshua Tree, notable the "Transmission" sculpture by Daniel Popper, located about 20 minutes north of the park. This remarkable installation stands out among the desert's artistic offerings with its intricate design and beauty. While 't get too close due to fencing around it, viewing from a distance is still rewarding—especially under a full moon!.

$15

Noah Purifoy Outdoor Desert Art Museum

The Noah Purifoy Outdoor Desert Art Museum is a unique open-air exhibit featuring an unconventional collection of folk-art assemblages. The sculptures and installations, referred to as 'Junk Dada', are created by famed African American artist Noah Purifoy and have been showcased in some of the world's top museums. However, visitors can view some of his works for free at his former outdoor desert studio located north of Joshua Tree.

$15

Desert Christ Park

Desert Christ Park, established between 1951 and 1961 by Frank Antone Martin, is a 3.5-acre sculpture garden showcasing over 35 religious statues set against the Mojave Desert backdrop. Visitors can experience a unique and offbeat nighttime adventure as the illuminated sculptures create a surreal atmosphere under the starry desert sky. Despite its small size, it offers an interesting stroll that can be completed in less than half an hour.
