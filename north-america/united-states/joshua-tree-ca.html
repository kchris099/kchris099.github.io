Joshua Tree, CA

Joshua Tree sits where the Mojave and Colorado deserts meet, named for the yucca trees that early Mormon travellers likened to biblical figures. Miners and ranchers left scattered workings; artists and climbers later claimed the granite outcrops. The area became a national monument in 1936 and a national park in 1994. Boulder piles, fan-palm oases, and night-sky views now structure visits across the high desert.

Joshua Tree Visitor Center

Joshua Tree Visitor Center stands in the town of Joshua Tree outside the national park boundary. Exhibits, maps and ranger information introduce desert ecology, cultural history and the long drives between the park's western approaches. The center is an orientation point before travelers enter a landscape of Joshua trees, granite formations and widely separated trailheads. The town setting separates it from the park's desert interior.

World Famous Crochet Museum

The Crochet Museum is a tiny roadside display housed in a converted photo booth in Joshua Tree. Rotating crocheted animals and objects fill the compact space beside other small art businesses, creating an informal folk-art stop. Its playful name is promotional, while hours and access depend on the surrounding private property. The converted booth limits the display to a few steps.

‘Transmission’ Sculpture by Daniel Popper

Daniel Popper's Transmission sculpture stands about twenty minutes north of Joshua Tree National Park behind a perimeter fence. The oversized human figure is clearest from the public roadside rather than approached closely. Night visits under a full moon are common among desert photographers who stay outside the barrier.

04 Admission$15

Noah Purifoy Outdoor Desert Art Museum

Noah Purifoy Outdoor Desert Art Museum preserves an open-air assemblage environment created by artist Noah Purifoy near Joshua Tree; large sculptures made from discarded materials address race, violence, labor, consumer culture and desert life across an exposed site. Sun, wind, uneven ground and fragile artworks require care.

05 AdmissionFree

Desert Christ Park

Desert Christ Park, established between 1951 and 1961 by Frank Antone Martin, is a 3.5-acre sculpture garden showing over 35 religious statues set against the Mojave Desert backdrop. Visitors can experience a unique and offbeat nighttime adventure as the illuminated sculptures create a surreal atmosphere under the starry desert sky. Despite its small size, it offers an interesting stroll that can be completed in less than half an hour.
