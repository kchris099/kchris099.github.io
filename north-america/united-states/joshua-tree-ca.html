Explore Joshua Tree, CA

A Brief History

Established as a regional settlement beyond an older fortified core, the city developed through industrialization in the late 19th century, post-war reconstruction, and modern civic expansion that reshaped its public spaces, markets, and surrounding landscape. Restored religious buildings, civic buildings, and natural features now define the contemporary visitor experience.

Top Sights & Activities

$15

Joshua Tree Visitor Center

The Joshua Tree National Park travellers Center is a small facility located just off Highway 62, offering maps, exhibits, books, souvenirs, and information on guided hikes. travellers can chat with park rangers to learn about their favorite hikes and pick up field guides on desert wildflowers. The center opens at 7:30 am and serves as a convenient starting point for morning adventures in the park.

Free

World Famous Crochet Museum

The Crochet Museum, located next to Art Queen in Joshua Tree, is a charming and free attraction featuring an array of crocheted animals and creations housed in a converted drive-thru photo stand. travellers can step inside the museum and take selfies at the selfie stand set up outside. While nothing is for sale inside the museum, donations are welcome. Nearby, travellers can support a small business by purchasing crochet novelties from the Art Queen store.

Free

'Transmission' Sculpture by Daniel Popper

If you're venturing near Joshua Tree, includes the "Transmission" sculpture by Daniel Popper, located about 20 minutes north of the park. This remarkable installation stands out among the desert's artistic offerings with its intricate design and beauty. While 't get too close due to fencing around it, viewing from a distance is still rewarding—especially under a full moon!.

$15

Noah Purifoy Outdoor Desert Art Museum

The Noah Purifoy Outdoor Desert Art Museum is a unique open-air exhibit featuring an unconventional collection of folk-art assemblages. The sculptures and installations, referred to as 'Junk Dada', are created by famed African American artist Noah Purifoy and have been showcased in some of the world's top museums. However, travellers can view some of his works for free at his former outdoor desert studio located north of Joshua Tree.

Free

Desert Christ Park

Desert Christ Park, established between 1951 and 1961 by Frank Antone Martin, is a 3.5-acre sculpture garden showcasing over 35 religious statues set against the Mojave Desert backdrop. travellers can experience a unique and offbeat nighttime adventure as the illuminated sculptures create a surreal atmosphere under the starry desert sky. Despite its small size, it offers an interesting stroll that can be completed in less than half an hour.
