Denver, CO

Denver was founded in 1858 as a gold-rush camp where the South Platte River meets Cherry Creek, then became the Rocky Mountain region's rail and smelting hub. Civic Center neoclassical blocks, Larimer Square warehouses, and Red Rocks amphitheatre record how silver booms, Colorado statehood in 1876, and postwar suburban growth built a mile-high capital between prairie and peaks.

Denver Zoo

Denver Zoo began in 1896 with an orphaned black bear cub named Billy Ryan and now sprawls across City Park habitats. Bear Mountain and Predator Ridge place lions, hyenas, and other species in yards designed to echo wild terrain. Path networks link primate, elephant, and bird areas for a full-day circuit inside the city.
