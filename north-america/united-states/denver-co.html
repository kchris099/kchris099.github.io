Explore Denver, CO

A Brief History

Denver was founded in 1858 as a gold-rush camp where the South Platte River meets Cherry Creek, then became the Rocky Mountain region's rail and smelting hub. Civic Center neoclassical blocks, Larimer Square warehouses, and Red Rocks amphitheatre record how silver booms, Colorado statehood in 1876, and postwar suburban growth built a mile-high capital between prairie and peaks.

Denver Zoo

Denver Zoo is a visitor site in Denver, CO, United States. Records associated with the site include 1896. Established in 1896 with an orphaned black bear cub named Billy Ryan, this beloved attraction has evolved into a hub for wildlife enthusiasts. Visitors can explore diverse habitats designed to mimic natural environments, including Bear Mountain and Predator Ridge, where majestic lions and hyenas roam freely. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Free

Coors Field

Coors Field is a 76-acre stadium in Denver, home to the Colorado Rockies. Some seats offer views of the Rocky Mountains, adding to the allure of this ballpark. The stadium provides a nostalgic feel of a classic ballpark while incorporating modern technology. Visitors can take 'Behind the Seams' tours for a behind-the-scenes look at one of Major League Baseball's premier ballparks.

Free

Denver Union Station

Denver Union Station, a stately train station dating back to 1881, has been revitalized into a hub that offers an array of experiences. From classic bars and eateries with communal seating areas and patios to architecture, local transportation, art, food, lodging, and shopping - it's a place where history meets modernity.

Free

Empower Field at Mile High

Allow Field at Mile High is the horseshoe-shaped football stadium that is the home ground for the NFL's Denver Broncos. It also doubles as a major concert venue, hosting known artists like Guns N' Roses, Metallica, and Taylor Swift. The stadium offers an viewing experience with massive screens ensuring that even those in the nosebleed seats can enjoy a close-up view of the performances.

Free

Larimer Square

Larimer Square is a public square in Denver, CO, United States. Larimer Square, located in Denver's Lower Downtown district, is a historic area where the city was founded. This spot is home to independent shops, bars, restaurants, and boutiques. It offers a mix of modern city life and historical charm. Open pavement and surrounding facades define the visitor setting.

Free

Denver Performing Arts Complex

The Denver Performing Arts Complex (DPAC) is a large center with 10 performance spaces, including theaters for plays, concerts, and other events. It also houses several restaurants. The complex is part of Denver's arts and culture scene, which includes six museums in the city center.

Free

Colorado Convention Center

Colorado Convention Center, also known as the big blue bear, is a visitor site in Denver, CO, United States. The Colorado Convention Center is a modern and spacious venue in Denver, featuring meeting and expo facilities as well as an abundance of public art. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Free

Ball Arena

Ball Arena is a stadium in Denver, CO, United States. Ball Arena, In Lower Downtown Denver, is a versatile venue that hosts major sporting events and big-name music performances. Home to the Denver Nuggets, Colorado Avalanche, and Colorado Mammoth, it offers an array of entertainment options throughout the year. Event access follows posted ticket and gate procedures.

$27

Downtown Aquarium

Located in downtown Denver, the Downtown Aquarium is a large complex that offers both entertainment and dining experiences. The main highlight of this attraction is its modern aquarium, which houses over 500 species of marine and land creatures within more than a million gallons of underwater exhibits.

Free

Stanley Marketplace

Stanley Marketplace is a spacious and inviting warehouse space that houses a variety of food vendors, retailers, and businesses. Located in Aurora but within walking distance from Denver's Stapleton neighborhood, it offers an array of independent boutiques, bookstores, markets, breweries, and wine-and-spirit shops.

Free

Cathedral Basilica of the Immaculate Conception

Located North Capitol Hill, the Cathedral Basilica of the Immaculate Conception is a evidence of 19th-century architecture. With its striking twin spires reaching 210 feet into the sky, this Gothic masterpiece is impossible to overlook. As visitors step through its grand brass doors, visitors are greeted by an interior adorned with stained glass windows crafted by F.X.

Colorado State Capitol

Colorado State Capitol is a visitor site in Denver, CO, United States. It opened or was completed around 1894. The Colorado State Capitol, a grand Corinthian-style building with a striking gold dome, stands proudly at an elevation of 1 mile above sea level. Designed by architect Elijah E. Myers and opened in 1894, it mirrors the style of the United States Capitol. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Free

Ogden Theatre

Ogden Theatre is a theatre in Denver, CO, United States. Records associated with the site include 1919. Published seating capacity is about 1,600,. The Ogden Theatre, a historic concert venue in Denver since 1919, offers an intimate setting for touring bands. Public access is usually through ticketed performances or daytime visits.

Free

Paramount Theatre

The most important Theatre, a historic Denver landmark dating back to 1930, is an elegant art deco performance space that hosts a variety of live entertainment. Originally designed for silent movies, it now showcases over 100 performances annually including concerts, stand-up comedy shows, lectures and more. The theater's slim art deco facade houses a grand old movie house with comfy seats and solid sightlines from the balcony.

Free

Trinity United Methodist Church

Located Denver, Trinity United Methodist Church is a evidence of Gothic Revival architecture. Designed by the known Robert Roeschlaub in 1887, this historic church showcases locally sourced rhyolite from Castle Rock, giving it a unique and striking appearance. Its spire reaches over 180 feet into the sky, making it one of the tallest stone towers in America at the time of its completion.

$8

Four Mile Historic Park

Four Mile Historic Park is a public park in Denver, CO, United States. Four Mile Historic Park is a unique outdoor living history museum located on 12 acres of land. Visitors can explore an 1800s log cabin, a barn with farm animals, and take guided tours to learn about the area's rich history. Paths, lawns, and shaded areas make up the main public facilities.
