Explore Santa Fe, NM

A Brief History

Established by the Spanish in 1610, Santa Fe is the oldest state capital in the United States. Its profound blend of Native American, Spanish colonial, and Anglo pioneer history is rigidly preserved through its iconic adobe architecture and deep-rooted commitment to the regional arts. Decades of careful preservation have ensured that this profound historical narrative remains a vital part of the modern experience.

Top Sights & Activities

$5

Santa Fe Plaza

Santa Fe Plaza is a historic city center that reflects traditional Spanish Colonial cities. Resembling destinations in Mexico and Central America, it features ornate historic buildings surrounding a large plaza where people relax and soak in the special atmosphere. Since its establishment in 1610, the plaza has been central to daily life, hosting centuries of festivities, events, and fairs.

$10

The Cathedral Basilica of St. Francis of Assisi

Nestled at the end of East San Francisco Street, the Cathedral Basilica of St. Francis of Assisi is a architectural gem that dates back to the late 19th century. Commissioned by Bishop Jean-Baptiste Lamy, this grand cathedral took 15 years to complete and reflects his vision to elevate Santa Fe's religious landscape beyond its traditional adobe roots.

$25

Lensic Performing Arts Center

The Lensic Performing Arts Center is a venue in Spanish Renaissance style that hosts live shows and film screenings. It is part of the vibrant performing arts scene in New Mexico, alongside other major venues like The Outpost Performance Space in Albuquerque and the Santa Fe Opera. The Lensic offers a diverse range of performances, from classical music to flamenco exhibitions, contemporary jazz, theater presentations, films, and lectures.

Free

Cross of the Martyrs

The Cross of the Martyrs is a large, white cross situated on a prominent hill overlooking downtown Santa Fe. It commemorates the Franciscan friars and Spanish colonists who died during the 1680 Pueblo Revolt. A short, paved walkway leads to the monument, providing historical plaques along the route and offering panoramic views of the city and the Jemez Mountains.

$2

San Miguel Chapel

The San Miguel Chapel is a 17th century adobe church located in Santa Fe, New Mexico. It was originally built by Franciscan friars and Tlaxcalan Indians, and features restored paintings and art. The chapel is a moving tribute to the faith of its congregation, which dates back to the early days of American exploration.

Free

Kakawa Chocolate House

Kakawa Chocolate House in Santa Fe is a unique specialty chocolate shop that offers authentic and historic drinking chocolates. Their hot chocolate drinks are small but intensely rich, focusing on traditional Pre-Columbian Mesoamerican Mayan Aztec and 1600's European drinking chocolates. The Marie Antoinette hot chocolate, made with chocolate, almond milk, orange blossom, cinnamon, and vanilla, is recommended.

$30

Meow Wolf Santa Fe's House of Eternal Return

Meow Wolf Santa Fe's House of Eternal Return is a 22,000 square foot immersive art experience filled with multimedia elements and a mysterious narrative. Housed in a former bowling alley owned by George R.R. Martin, this fantasy world created by 150 artists offers captivating layers of creative chaos and interactivity without the gore of Game of Thrones.

Free

Santa Fe Farmers Market

The Santa Fe Farmers Market is a popular spot that offers more than just fresh produce. It's located near the Second Street Brewery at the Railyard, where visitors can enjoy local music and delicious food while taking in the vibrant atmosphere. The area also hosts outdoor concerts, movies, festivals, and art fairs throughout the summer and into fall.

$40

The Santa Fe Opera

The Santa Fe Opera is a renowned opera festival held in a picturesque hilltop setting, offering classic and contemporary performances. The 64th season, running from July 3 to August 29, features 37 shows including world premieres and company premieres. Visitors can enjoy tailgate dinners before the performances. Santa Fe itself is a vibrant city with a rich history, diverse culture, and strong artistic influence.

Free

Fort Marcy Park

Fort Marcy Park is a historic site with remnants of a military fort, offering an innovative scavenger hunt experience through the Operation City Quest app. The park's location near Lincoln Avenue and the Plaza provides access to various shops and boutiques for shopping enthusiasts. Visitors can also enjoy events like the Burning of Zozobra and night views with a peaceful atmosphere.
