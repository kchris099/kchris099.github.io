Arches, UT

Arches National Park protects more than two thousand sandstone arches formed in Entrada sandstone above the Colorado Plateau near Moab. Ute and earlier peoples knew the area long before ranchers and prospectors arrived. The park was proclaimed a national monument in 1929 and a national park in 1971. Delicate Arch and Devils Garden trails now organise visits across a desert of fins, balanced rocks, and red-rock windows.

Delicate Arch

Delicate Arch is the main attraction of Arches National Park, and it can be viewed from an overlook or reached via a trail. The hike to the arch is labeled as strenuous but is not overly difficult, although it is quite long. It's recommended to arrive early due to limited shade and crowded trails later in the day. For a memorable experience, consider hiking to Delicate Arch for a sunset view.

Upper Delicate Arch Viewpoint

Upper Delicate Arch Viewpoint provides a distant view of Utah's Delicate Arch from across a canyon without following the full trail to the formation. A short but uneven climb reaches the higher overlook, where scale, heat, glare, and weather affect visibility. The viewpoint does not lead to the arch itself, and visitors must remain on marked routes to protect desert soil and safety.

03 Admission$15

Lower Delicate Arch Viewpoint

Lower Delicate Arch Viewpoint offers a relatively short walk to a distant view of Delicate Arch across a slickrock bowl in Arches National Park. The arch appears as a freestanding fin on the skyline rather than at close range. Travellers who skip the longer upper hike still see the park's signature formation from this overlook.

38.7342° N / 109.5001° W

04 Admission$15

Double Arch

Two large sandstone arches rise from one foundation at Double Arch, reached by an easy walk from the parking area in Arches National Park. Photographers gather at the base for framed desert sky. Nearby park stops include Balanced Rock, The Windows, and Park Avenue.

38.6916° N / 109.5404° W

05 Admission$15

Turret Arch

Turret Arch stands in the Windows section of Arches National Park, where a short trail reaches a broad sandstone opening beside a smaller adjacent arch. Erosion along fractures created the formation over geologic time, while nearby fins and windows show the same process at different stages. Heat, slickrock, crowds, and fragile desert surfaces shape access.

38.6843° N / 109.5349° W

06 Admission$15

Arches National Park Visitor Center

Arches National Park Visitor Center stands near the park entrance with exhibits on geology, desert ecology and Indigenous history. Maps and ranger information help explain the road network, trailheads and fragile sandstone landscape before travelers continue into the park. The building also introduces heat, water needs and low-impact practices suited to the high desert environment. A desert garden surrounds parts of the building.

38.6166° N / 109.6198° W

07 Admission$15

La Sal Mountains Viewpoint

From La Sal Mountains Viewpoint, park roads look toward the La Sal range and formations such as Three Gossips and Courthouse Towers. Short walks from parking reach the rail and interpretive signs. The stop works as an early orientation pullout inside Arches National Park.

38.6264° N / 109.5912° W

08 Admission$15

Sand Dune Arch

Sand Dune Arch hides between fin walls where a soft red-sand path leads to a shaded, ground-level opening. The short walk suits heat-sensitive travellers seeking cooler footing than open slickrock. Longer linking trails continue toward Broken Arch and other formations for those extending the hike.

38.7643° N / 109.5811° W

Devil's Garden Trail

Devil's Garden Trailhead opens routes to Landscape Arch and onward spurs toward Navajo and Partition Arches among other spans. Hikers choose short out-and-backs or longer desert loops from the same start. Sandstone fins and open slickrock define the walking surface.

38.7829° N / 109.5951° W

10 Admission$15

Landscape Arch

Rockfalls have altered the formation and led to protective closures beneath it, showing that arches remain active geologic formations. Landscape Arch spans a thin ribbon of sandstone in Devils Garden, reached by a relatively level trail that continues toward more difficult routes. Heat, limited shade, sand, and fragile soil require planning, while climbing on the arch is prohibited.

38.7905° N / 109.6071° W
