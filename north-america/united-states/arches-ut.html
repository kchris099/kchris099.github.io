Explore Arches, UT

A Brief History

Established as a regional settlement beyond an older fortified core, the city developed through industrialization in the late 19th century, post-war reconstruction, and modern civic expansion that reshaped its public spaces, markets, and surrounding landscape. Restored religious buildings, civic buildings, and natural features now define the contemporary visitor experience.

Delicate Arch

Delicate Arch is the main attraction of Arches National Park, and it can be viewed from an overlook or reached via a trail. The hike to the arch is labeled as strenuous but is not overly difficult, although it is quite long. It's recommended to arrive early due to limited shade and crowded trails later in the day. For a memorable experience, consider hiking to Delicate Arch for a sunset view.

$15

Upper Delicate Arch Viewpoint

The Three Gossips is a fascinating rock formation located in the Courthouse Towers area of Arches National Park. Resembling three figures engaged in conversation, these stone structures are a sight to behold. travellers can easily access this unique formation by driving 3.5 miles from the Arches travellers Center and following the sign for the Courthouse Towers Viewpoint.

Double Arch

Double Arch is a sandstone formation in Arches National Park, featuring two large arches rising from the same foundation. It's a popular spot for photography and offers an easy trail from the parking lot to reach the magnificent sight. The park also boasts other highlights such as Balanced Rock, The Windows, and Park Avenue viewpoint. travellers can embark on hikes like the Delicate Arch trail and explore rock art in the area.

$15

Turret Arch

Turret Arch is a unique sandstone formation located in the Windows section of Arches National Park. It is characterized by its castle-like appearance and features large and small openings, with a taller, turret-like rock pillar to the side. The arch is the smallest of three on the 'Windows Loop Trail', which is 1.2 miles long with 150 feet of elevation gain and suitable for all skill levels.

$15

Arches National Park Visitor Center

The Arches National Park travellers Center is a hub of information and resources for travellers. It provides trail maps, geology exhibits, and knowledgeable rangers who offer guidance on the park's landmarks. The center showcases the forces of nature that have shaped the landscape, including the highest concentration of natural arches in the world. travellers can learn about millions of years of geological events through rock layers and gain insights into ancient culture and natural history.

$30

La Sal Mountains Viewpoint

The La Sal Mountains Viewpoint is a spot in Arches National Park, offering views of the La Sal Mountains and famous rock formations like Three Gossips and Courthouse Towers. The viewpoint provides 360-degree views with different natural features in every direction, making it an ideal location to witness sunrises and sunsets.

$15

Landscape Arch

Landscape Arch is a natural wonder located in Arches National Park, with a span of 290 feet, making it the longest sandstone arch in the park. It can be accessed via the Devils Garden trail, which offers hikers the option to explore seven different arches along a 7.9-mile counterclockwise loop. For those seeking a shorter hike, a 1.9-mile trek leads directly to Landscape Arch.
