Grand Canyon, AZ

The Grand Canyon was carved by the Colorado River through nearly two billion years of rock, long known to Native nations including the Havasupai, Hopi, and Navajo. John Wesley Powell's 1869 river trip mapped much of the gorge for science. National park status came in 1919 after earlier monument protection. Rim villages, mule trails, and river corridors organise travel along one of North America's deepest exposed geologic sections.

Hopi House

Hopi House is a historic Native American-style building located across from El Tovar. It was opened in 1905 as the first curio shop at the Grand Canyon and was designed by architect Mary Elizabeth Jane Colter to reflect the adobe pueblo style of the Hopi Indians. The house houses a small gift shop showing native arts and crafts, and over the years, it has been careful preserved as an original simulacrum with a rich history.
