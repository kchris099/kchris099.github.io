Explore Grand Canyon, AZ

A Brief History

Inhabited by ancestral Puebloans for millennia, the vast canyon remained largely unmapped by Europeans until the mid-19th century. The arrival of the Santa Fe Railway sparked pioneering conservation efforts, transforming the remote wonder into a monumental symbol of the American West. Decades of careful preservation have ensured that this profound historical narrative remains a vital part of the modern experience.

Top Sights & Activities

Mather Point

Mather Point is a popular scenic viewpoint located along the South Rim of the Grand Canyon. It offers canyon vistas and is conveniently close to the visitor center. Yavapai Point, situated west of Mather Point, provides an equally view of the Grand Canyon Village area. From Yavapai Point, visitors can admire various landmarks such as the Bright Angel Trail, Indian Gardens, Phantom Ranch, and even catch a glimpse of the Colorado River and its suspension bridge.

$35

Grand Canyon Visitor Center

Nestled in the heart of the magnificent Grand Canyon National Park, the Grand Canyon Visitor Center is more than just a place for gathering information. It serves as a bustling hub offering various services and experiences. Visitors can find everything from bike rentals to coffee, exhibits showcasing historical artifacts, and even a gift shop. This vibrant center is not just a pit stop but rather a comprehensive starting point for exploring the wonders of the Grand Canyon.

$20

Grandview Point

Nestled along the South Rim of the Grand Canyon, Grandview Point is a must-visit destination that offers views of vibrant canyons and the winding Colorado River. Located just 12 miles from Grand Canyon Village, this scenic overlook provides an impressive 270-degree panorama that captures the essence of this natural wonder.

$20

Hermit's Rest

Hermit's Rest, a historic structure designed in the National Park rustic style by architect Mary Colter in 1914, serves as a rest stop along Hermit Road. This road offers some of the most viewpoints in Grand Canyon National Park and is closed to private vehicles from March through November. Visitors can enjoy canyon viewing and easy hiking without traffic or parking issues, thanks to the free shuttle buses that stop at eight overlooks along the route.

$20

Hopi Point

Hopi Point, located on the South Rim of the Grand Canyon, offers an unparalleled view of the canyon's vistas. This popular overlook provides unobstructed views and is a prime spot for witnessing sunrises and sunsets. Situated along Hermit Road, it is part of a scenic route that includes other famous viewpoints like Mohave Point. Additionally, visitors can explore the Trail of Time, which features informative signs detailing the geological history of the area.

$20

Powell Point

Powell Point is a scenic overlook at the Grand Canyon with sweeping views and a commemorative memorial to exploratory crews. It's recommended for photography enthusiasts, but caution is advised during monsoon season due to lightning risks. Accessible by shuttle buses, Powell Point offers geological and historical insights from drivers en route to Hermits Rest.

$20

Mohave Point

Mohave Point is a popular South Rim overlook along the Hermit Road, offering vistas of terraced cliffs, sandstone mesas, and the curving Colorado River. It's part of the Trail of Time, where each step represents a million years of geological history. The area is known for its famous sunrise and sunset viewpoints, including The Abyss, a one-mile vertical indentation into the canyon wall. Artists often find inspiration in the unique way sunlight interacts with the rock formations here.

$20

Grand Canyon

The Grand Canyon, a world-renowned marvel located in Northern Arizona, is a testament to nature's artistry. Carved by the Colorado River through the desert landscape, this national park showcases rock formations in vibrant reds and oranges. The South Rim is particularly popular for its expansive viewpoints—nearly two dozen spots where visitors can gaze deep into the canyon’s heart.

$20

Pipe Creek Vista

Pipe Creek Vista is a charming overlook at the Grand Canyon that offers views of the canyon's dramatic landscape. Located along Desert View Drive, it serves as the first viewpoint on this scenic route, making it a popular stop for visitors eager to soak in the vistas. While access Pipe Creek Vista by personal vehicle or via the free Kaibab Rim Shuttle Bus from the Grand Canyon Visitor Center, parking can be quite limited due to its popularity.

$20

Yaki Point

Yaki Point is a remote lookout and shuttle stop that offers views of the Grand Canyon, making it a popular spot for watching both sunrise and sunset. Visitors can also enjoy evening park ranger programs and dark-sky star-gazing. During the day, there are plenty of activities to do such as hiking on Bright Angel or South Kaibab Trails, attending free ranger talks, and exploring the visitor centers.

$20

Grand Canyon Depot

Experience the magic of a bygone era with a journey on the Grand Canyon Railway, a historic train that takes passengers on a scenic ride to the South Rim of the Grand Canyon. This heritage railroad, which dates back to 1901, offers travelers an opportunity to witness Arizona's landscapes while reliving the romance of classic rail travel. Departing from Williams, Arizona, the train travels 64 miles through picturesque terrain before reaching its destination at Grand Canyon National Park.

$20

Hopi House

Hopi House is a historic Native American-style building located across from El Tovar. It was opened in 1905 as the first curio shop at the Grand Canyon and was designed by architect Mary Elizabeth Jane Colter to reflect the adobe pueblo style of the Hopi Indians. The house houses a small gift shop showcasing native arts and crafts, and over the years, it has been meticulously preserved as an original simulacrum with a rich history.

$20

Bright Angel Trailhead

Bright Angel Trailhead serves as the starting point for a challenging 7.9-mile hike, known as the park's 'superhighway,' which connects the Colorado River and South Rim. To embark on this one-way journey, it is recommended to park at the Backcountry Information Center at the South Rim and take the Trans Canyon Shuttle to the North Rim. The shuttle arrives at a convenient time for beginning the hike in shade.

$20

Bright Angel Lodge

Bright Angel Lodge is a historic accommodation located in Grand Canyon Village, offering rustic rooms with modern amenities such as TVs and private baths. The lodge also features surrounding cabins and historically significant structures available as guest accommodations. Conveniently situated near popular attractions like Bright Angel Trailhead and Grand Canyon Railway, the lodge provides easy access to the natural wonders of the area.

$20

Grand Canyon South Rim

The Grand Canyon South Rim, located within the Grand Canyon National Park, is a popular destination offering a range of visitor services and accommodations. Open year-round, it provides convenient access to shuttle bus services, concierge and food options, as well as various hotels near the park entrance. Visitors can enjoy free Ranger Talks, museums, shops, geological and historical information, and the thrilling Grand Canyon IMAX Experience.

$20

North Rim Visitor Center

The North Rim Visitor Center is the primary information and orientation hub for the less-visited, higher-elevation northern side of the Grand Canyon. It provides educational exhibits, ranger programs, and essential services for visitors exploring the remote, forested rim, which offers distinct, expansive views compared to the more crowded South Rim.

$14

Grand Canyon Visitor Center IMAX

The Grand Canyon Visitor Center, located at the South Rim, serves as an informative hub for visitors to the landmark. Here, guests can obtain park entrance tickets, maps, and brochures while receiving guidance from knowledgeable local representatives on tours and special events in the area. The center also features an IMAX theater showcasing 'Grand Canyon: The Hidden Secrets,' offering a thrilling virtual experience of soaring over the canyon and rafting the Colorado River.

$20

South Rim Trail

The South Rim Trail is a thrilling hiking path that offers Grand Canyon views and convenient shuttle service. This trail is a nature lover's paradise, with each step revealing vistas of the rugged beauty of the canyon. Along the way, hikers can explore an outdoor interpretative display that traces the canyon's geological history over 2.1 billion years in just over a mile.
