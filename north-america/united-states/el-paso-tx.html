El Paso, TX

El Paso sits where the Rio Grande exits mountain canyons onto the Chihuahuan Desert, a crossing used by Spanish expeditions and Butterfield Overland Mail stages. Mission Ysleta, Magoffin Home adobe, and Franklin Mountains trails record how Mexican Revolution refugee flows, Fort Bliss garrison growth, and maquiladora trade linked Texas to Ciudad Juárez.

San Jacinto Plaza

San Jacinto Plaza is El Paso's central downtown square, refreshed in a 2014 redesign with lawns, shade trees, and seating. Ping-pong tables and chess boards sit among sixteen historic markers that outline local history. Paved walks and surrounding building fronts keep the plaza tied to the business district.

The Plaza Theatre

Built in the 1930s and restored in 2006, El Paso's Plaza Theatre shows Spanish Colonial Revival detail, mosaic floors, antique furnishings, and a painted night-sky courtyard ceiling. Free Tuesday tours present the National Historic Building when scheduled. Downtown streets reach the marquee directly.

03 AdmissionFree

St. Patrick Cathedral

St. Patrick Cathedral is the Roman Catholic cathedral church of El Paso, rising with twin towers above downtown streets. The parish has long been a focal point for the city's Catholic community. Outside scheduled liturgies the exterior and main nave are usually available for quiet viewing of the sanctuary.

04 AdmissionFree

Cathedral of Ciudad Juarez

Across the Rio Grande from El Paso, the Cathedral of Ciudad Juárez (Our Lady of Guadalupe) grew from a mission founded in 1681 by Fray García de San Francisco. The present church remains a principal Catholic landmark of Juárez. Outside Mass the exterior and main interior spaces are often open for respectful visits from the border city.

El Paso Zoo and Botanical Gardens

El Paso Zoo and Botanical Gardens occupies a municipal site with animal enclosures, planted areas, education and family facilities. Species from several regions appear in changing exhibits, while the botanical element is integrated into zoo landscaping. Heat, construction, collection changes and animal welfare influence the experience.

Sun Bowl

Sun Bowl Stadium seats about 51,000 for University of Texas at El Paso football on the city's west side. The same field hosts the Hyundai Sun Bowl, among the older college bowl games still played each winter. Mountain views rise beyond the stands, giving night games a clear desert backdrop.

Magoffin Home State Historic Site

Magoffin Home preserves an adobe residence built in the nineteenth century for a prominent El Paso family involved in trade, politics and regional settlement. Furnished rooms, thick walls, courtyards and family objects interpret life along the United States-Mexico border. Guided access and restoration reveal several additions, while the story also requires context about power and cultural exchange.

Mount Cristo Rey

Mount Cristo Rey rises in New Mexico near the Texas and Mexico borders, crowned by a large limestone statue of Christ. A pilgrimage trail climbs through exposed desert terrain with views across three jurisdictions, while the monument reflects Catholic devotion and borderland history.

09 Admission$15

Jungle Reef

Jungle Reef is an indoor interactive aquarium at The Shoppes at Solana in El Paso. Fish, eels and sharks occupy the main display tanks. Other supervised areas contain stingrays and koi, along with reptiles, birds and small mammals. Hands-on encounters and children's activities make the venue a commercial animal attraction where keeper oversight and posted care rules shape contact with the animals.

Bob-O's Family Fun Center

Bob-O's packs laser tag, go-karts, bumper boats, batting cages, and an arcade into one El Paso amusement yard. Age-grouped activities keep younger children and teens on different tracks without splitting the site. Birthday parties and evening play fill the lot on weekends when desert heat eases.
