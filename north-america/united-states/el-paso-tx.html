Explore El Paso, TX

A Brief History

El Paso sits where the Rio Grande exits mountain canyons onto the Chihuahuan Desert, a crossing used by Spanish expeditions and Butterfield Overland Mail stages. Mission Ysleta, Magoffin Home adobe, and Franklin Mountains trails record how Mexican Revolution refugee flows, Fort Bliss garrison growth, and maquiladora trade linked Texas to Ciudad Juárez.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

San Jacinto Plaza

San Jacinto Plaza is a serene urban oasis that beautifully blends history with modern charm. This town square, revitalized in 2014, features lush green spaces, inviting park benches, and shady trees perfect for relaxation. Visitors can engage in friendly games of ping pong or chess while exploring the plaza's rich heritage through its 16 historic markers. A highlight of San Jacinto Plaza is the striking sculpture 'Los Lagartos,' which pays homage to the alligators once found in its pond.

Free

The Plaza Theatre

The Plaza Theatre, originally constructed in the 1930s and restored to its former glory in 2006, is a historic gem located in downtown El Paso. This National Historic Building of Significance offers free tours every Tuesday, allowing visitors to admire its Spanish Colonial Revival architecture, mosaic-tiled floors, antique furniture, and a design that creates the illusion of an outdoor courtyard under a starry painted ceiling.

Free

St. Patrick Cathedral

St. Patrick Cathedral stands as a historical landmark in El Paso, with its construction spanning from 1725 to 1826. This majestic structure graces the Plaza de Armas and features an impressive baroque facade that captivates visitors. Inside, the cathedral boasts beautiful windows and a serene altar, making it a cherished place for worshippers seeking solace amidst life's chaos.

Free

Cathedral of Ciudad Juarez

The Cathedral of Ciudad Juarez, also known as Lady of Guadalupe Mission, is a historic site established in 1681 by Fray Garcia de San Francisco. It is part of the El Paso Mission Trail and is considered the first mission in the area. The cathedral features beautiful wood beams and woodwork throughout its well-preserved structure. Despite its traditional exterior, the interior presents a nontraditional appearance.

$16

El Paso Zoo and Botanical Gardens

The El Paso Zoo and Botanical Gardens is a 35-acre park that houses over 220 African, American, and Asian species in natural habitat replicas. Families can enjoy exploring exhibits such as Animals of Africa, Animals of Asia, and Animals of the Americas. The zoo offers Zoo Adventure Programs for both children and adults, along with daily animal encounters featuring sea lions, eagles, monkeys, and more.

$20

Sun Bowl

The Sun Bowl Stadium in El Paso, Texas is a 51,000-seat outdoor football arena and the home field of the University of Texas at El Paso's football team. It also hosts the Hyundai Sun Bowl, one of the oldest college bowl games in the country. Nestled in the mountains near UTEP's campus, it offers a fun family experience and is known for its exciting atmosphere during college football games. In addition to sporting events, the stadium also hosts concerts and other entertainment.

$7

Magoffin Home State Historic Site

The Magoffin Home State Historic Site in downtown El Paso is a well-preserved adobe home built in 1875. It was the residence of Joseph and Octavia Magoffin, a multicultural family involved in U.S. expansion, military service, and Civil War events. The house showcases Territorial-style architecture and original furniture, including a grand piano.

Free

Mount Cristo Rey

Mount Cristo Rey, located near downtown El Paso in New Mexico, is home to a towering 29-foot statue and a scenic hiking trail offering picturesque views of the border. The 5-mile trail is wide enough for groups to pass each other and leads to the summit where visitors can enjoy vistas. Despite some litter along the way, the peaceful atmosphere and scenery make it a must-visit for nature enthusiasts looking for an outdoor experience.

$15

Jungle Reef

Jungle Reef offers a family-friendly experience with various animals and activities. Visitors enjoyed interacting with the friendly animals and found the prices reasonable. The opportunity to feed stingrays and koi fish was a highlight, as well as the chance to be a tree trunk for parakeets. The indoor setting allows for enjoyment regardless of weather conditions, and staff members like Ivan and Bella were praised for their friendliness and knowledge.

Free

Bob-O's Family Fun Center

Bob-O's Family Fun Center is a thrilling amusement venue that includes activities for the entire family. From laser tag and go-karts to bumper boats, batting cages, and an arcade, there's something for everyone. The center features attractions like kiddie rides, mini-golf, and delicious pizza at the dining area. With a focus on providing safe and wholesome entertainment for all ages, Bob-O's ensures that each family member has a special experience.
