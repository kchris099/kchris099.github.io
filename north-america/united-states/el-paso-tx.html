El Paso, TX

El Paso sits where the Rio Grande exits mountain canyons onto the Chihuahuan Desert, a crossing used by Spanish expeditions and Butterfield Overland Mail stages. Mission Ysleta, Magoffin Home adobe, and Franklin Mountains trails record how Mexican Revolution refugee flows, Fort Bliss garrison growth, and maquiladora trade linked Texas to Ciudad Juárez.

Magoffin Home State Historic Site

The Magoffin Home State Historic Site in downtown El Paso is a well-preserved adobe home built in 1875. It was the residence of Joseph and Octavia Magoffin, a multicultural family involved in U.S. Expansion, military service, and Civil War events. The house showcases Territorial-style architecture and original furniture, including a grand piano.
