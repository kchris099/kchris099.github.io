Explore Portland, ME

A Brief History

Portland, ME is in United States. Established as a regional settlement beyond an older fortified core, the city developed through industrialization in the late 19th century, post-war reconstruction, and modern civic expansion that reshaped its public spaces, markets, and surrounding landscape. Museums, markets, and street plans in Portland, ME still show layers from those periods.

Fort Williams Park

Fort Williams Park is a fortified historic site in Portland, ME, United States. Fort Williams Park, situated in Cape Elizabeth just off Route 77, is a town-operated state park offering free access with some paid parking. The park also provides ample picnic areas and panoramic vistas. Walls, courtyards, or towers form the main visitor route when the grounds are open.

Free (Museum $2)

Portland Head Light

Portland Head Light is a historic lighthouse that has been in operation since 1791 and now is a navigation museum. It is located on the rocky and coastline within Ft. Travellers can enjoy scenic views of the surrounding area and explore the lighthouse's rich history, nearby attractions such as Two Lights State Park offer further opportunities for exploration and enjoyment.

Free

Fort Allen Park

Fort Allen Park is a 10-acre urban park with a rich historical background, offering water views of Casco Bay and Fort Gorges. Originally built as a fort during the Revolution in 1775, it now provides seating areas, a gazebo, and various historical monuments. Travellers can enjoy walking trails, picnics, and summer concerts at the bandstand.

Free

Bug Light Park

Bug Light Park, also known as Portland Breakwater Light, is a public park in Portland, ME, United States. Records associated with the site include 1875. Bug Light Park, also known as Portland Breakwater Light, is a 9-acre green space offering views of the harbor and city skyline. The park has a quaint lighthouse dating back to 1875, affectionately nicknamed Bug Light due to its small size. Paths, lawns, and shaded areas make up the main public facilities.

Free

Spring Point Ledge Lighthouse

Spring Point Ledge Lighthouse is a historic caisson-style lighthouse that was built in 1897 on a rocky breakwater. It is open year-round and offers a unique experience as the only caisson-style lighthouse in America that walk to, connected to land via a breakwater on the campus of Southern Maine Community College.

Free

Thompson's Point

Thompson's Point is a visitor site in Portland, ME, United States. Thompson's Point is a versatile riverside venue that offers a blend of nature and culture. During summer, it collaborates with the State Theatre of Portland to host major concerts and Makers Market events, while in winter, it transforms into an ice skating rink. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Free

Casco Bay Ferry

Casco Bay Ferry is a beach in Portland, ME, United States. Began on a scenic journey with the Casco Bay Ferry, offering access to Portland's local islands. For seeking hidden coves, historic forts, or coastal vistas, this ferry provides an easy escape. During off-peak seasons, driving onto the ferry is hassle-free as it accommodates 20 cars. Access is typically on foot from nearby roads or beachfront paths.

$19

Victoria Mansion

Victoria Mansion, also known as the Morse-Libby House or Morse-Libby Mansion, is a 19th-century Italian villa-style home located in downtown Portland. Built in 1860 as a summer retreat for a wealthy hotelier, it was designed by architect Henry Austin and has an asymmetrical structure with a four-story tower, overhanging eaves, inviting verandas, and ornate windows.

$8

Crescent Beach State Park

Crescent Beach State Park in Cape Elizabeth, Maine is a oceanside park featuring a mile-long crescent-shaped beach. It's known for its fine sand, ideal for sunbathing and leisurely walks along the shore. The park offers various amenities such as picnic areas, grills, a snack bar, and a children's playground making it perfect for families and friends to enjoy a day out.

Free

Kettle Cove State Park

Kettle Cove State Park is a public park in Portland, ME, United States. During low tide, kids can explore the tide pools while adults can enjoy the scenery. In addition to the beach, there are walking trails and a small grassy area suitable for picnics. Paths, lawns, and shaded areas make up the main public facilities.
