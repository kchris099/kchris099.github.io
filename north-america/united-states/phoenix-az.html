Phoenix, AZ

Phoenix grew from Hohokam canal agriculture revived by nineteenth-century irrigation companies in the Salt River Valley. Territorial capitol buildings, Papago Park red buttes, and postwar subdivision grids document how reclamation law, air-conditioning adoption, and Sun Belt migration transformed a desert farming town into Arizona's largest metropolitan area.

Hole in the Rock

Hole in the Rock is a famous geological formation located in Papago Park, featuring multiple cave-like openings formed by natural erosion in red sandstone. The short and easy Hole-in-the-Rock Trail provides visitors with a chance to explore this landmark and offers panoramic views of Downtown Phoenix from its summit. Believed to have been used by the ancient Hohokam civilization for tracking the sun's position, it now is a spot for sunrise or sunset viewings.
