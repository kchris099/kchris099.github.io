Explore Phoenix, AZ

A Brief History

Phoenix grew from Hohokam canal agriculture revived by nineteenth-century irrigation companies in the Salt River Valley. Territorial capitol buildings, Papago Park red buttes, and postwar subdivision grids document how reclamation law, air-conditioning adoption, and Sun Belt migration transformed a desert farming town into Arizona's largest metropolitan area.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

Hole in the Rock

Hole in the Rock is a famous geological formation located in Papago Park, featuring multiple cave-like openings formed by natural erosion in red sandstone. The short and easy Hole-in-the-Rock Trail provides visitors with an opportunity to explore this landmark and offers panoramic views of Downtown Phoenix from its summit. Believed to have been used by the ancient Hohokam civilization for tracking the sun's position, it now serves as a picturesque spot for sunrise or sunset viewings.

$40

Phoenix Zoo

The Phoenix Zoo covers 125 acres and offers a variety of animal exhibits, including a children's area and petting zoo. Visitors can also enjoy a narrated tram ride around the zoo. anticipated events at the Phoenix Zoo is Zoo Lights, where millions of lights are strung on trees and displayed as animal shapes along pathways.

Free

ASU Gammage

ASU Gammage, a historic theater located on the Arizona State University campus, is renowned for hosting various arts performances. Designed by the famous architect Frank Lloyd Wright in the 1950s, it was originally intended to be an opera house for the King of Iraq but was repurposed as an auditorium for ASU after political events led to the cancellation of the original project.

$22

SEA LIFE Arizona Aquarium

Nestled within the Arizona Mills in Tempe, SEA LIFE Arizona Aquarium is a captivating 26,000-square-foot interactive experience that invites families to dive into the wonders of marine life. This aquarium showcases an impressive array of aquatic creatures across diverse habitats, making it a perfect destination for all ages. Visitors can marvel at the 360-degree ocean tunnel where majestic sharks, graceful rays, and playful turtles swim overhead.

Free

Chase Field

Chase Field, located in downtown Phoenix near various entertainment venues, is a versatile stadium that serves as the home of the Arizona Diamondbacks and hosts a variety of sporting events and concerts. The venue features a retractable dome roof equipped with an advanced cooling system, making it comfortable for visitors even during hot days. It also offers ADA accessibility throughout the building and is conveniently accessible via public transportation.

Free

Symphony Hall

Symphony Hall in downtown Phoenix is a versatile performing arts venue that hosts various renowned organizations, including the Phoenix Symphony, Arizona Opera, and Ballet Arizona. The hall features over 2,300 seats with wood bases for improved acoustics.

Free

St. Mary's Roman Catholic Basilica

St. Mary's Roman Catholic Basilica, also known as The Church of the Immaculate Conception of the Blessed Virgin, is a grand adobe church that was completed in 1915. It holds a unique place in the history of Phoenix and the Diocese of Phoenix, being the oldest Catholic parish in the city and playing an important role for its founders over a century ago.

Free

Orpheum Theatre Phoenix

The Orpheum Theatre Phoenix, a beautifully renovated beaux arts-style venue, has been a cultural hub in downtown Phoenix since 1929. This historic gem is part of the National Register of Historic Places and features an intimate stage with Baroque/Spanish Revival ornamentation. It serves as the home for the Phoenix Opera and hosts a variety of events including Broadway musicals, concerts, comedy shows, and Ballet Arizona performances.

Free

The Van Buren

The Van Buren, located in downtown Phoenix, has a rich history as it was originally an automotive showroom for the Phoenix Motor Company back in 1939. However, today it stands as a music venue that can accommodate around 1,800 concertgoers. The interior is adorned with chandeliers and desert murals on the walls, creating a unique and inviting atmosphere for attendees.

Free

Wesley Bolin Memorial Plaza

Wesley Bolin Memorial Plaza is a serene gathering spot nestled just east of the historic Arizona State Capitol, where history unfolds through its impressive array of monuments and memorials. This public park not only honors significant figures like Barry Goldwater but also pays tribute to various wars and events that shaped the nation.

Free

The Phoenix Theatre Company

The Phoenix Theatre Company is a historic arts organization in Arizona that has been showcasing performances and premieres for over a century. Situated near the Phoenix Art Museum and Heard Museum, it offers a diverse lineup of classic and new productions each season. The theater provides an entertainment experience that caters to all generations, featuring Broadway-style shows accompanied by live pre-performance music, food, and drinks.

$10

West Wind Drive-In

The West Wind Glendale 9 Drive-In is a unique destination in Phoenix that offers a diverse experience. During the day, visitors can explore a market selling various items such as produce and clothes. As evening falls, the venue transforms into a drive-in cinema, providing an atmosphere of relaxed family fun with a mix of old-school charm and modern technology. With nine digital projectors, it showcases first-run movies, often featuring double features.

Free

Phoenix Mountains Preserve

The Phoenix Mountains Preserve is a collection of parks offering desert hiking trails with an abundance of cacti and native wildlife. In Phoenix, this vast open space provides a perfect opportunity to immerse yourself in the natural beauty of the desert landscape.

Free

Echo Canyon Recreation Area

Echo Canyon Recreation Area is a popular spot in the valley, known for its Camelback Mountain and three hiking trails. The area is characterized by layered sandstone formations and offers a chance to encounter various wildlife such as rabbits, snakes, lizards, squirrels, and diverse bird species. The Echo Canyon trail at Camelback Mountain is particularly renowned for its challenging ascent to the summit.

Free

Geordie's at Wrigley Mansion

Geordie's at Wrigley Mansion is an opulent Italianate mansion constructed by William Wrigley, Jr. in 1929. The restaurant offers a luxurious dining experience with panoramic views of the Valley below through floor-to-ceiling windows. Guests can indulge in an eight-course extravaganza featuring Chef Christopher Gross's signature surprises, including dishes like Spanish red prawns with caviar and ravioli of porcini mushroom in a perigot black truffle sauce.

Free

Dobbins Lookout

Dobbins Lookout is a destination in Phoenix, Arizona. Situated at an elevation of 2,330 feet atop South Mountain Park and Preserve, it offers unparalleled panoramic views of the city skyline, metropolitan area, and the surrounding mountains. Accessible by both driving and hiking, visitors can embark on a scenic drive through the mountains or opt for a more adventurous hike to reach this viewpoint.
