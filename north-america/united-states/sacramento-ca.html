Sacramento, CA

Sacramento became California's capital in 1854 after Gold Rush river trade made it a supply point between San Francisco Bay and Sierra mines. Sutter's Fort, State Capitol grounds, and Old Sacramento levees record how steamboat commerce, Central Pacific Railroad lobbying, and Central Valley agriculture turned a flood-prone embarcadero into the seat of the nation's most populous state.
