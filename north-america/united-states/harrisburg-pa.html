Explore Harrisburg, PA

A Brief History

Harrisburg lies on the Susquehanna River and became Pennsylvania's capital in 1812, with a capitol complex, bridges, and rail corridors that grew with 19th-century canal and iron industries. The city served as a Union transport hub during the Civil War and later developed riverfront parks and government offices. Nearby Hershey emerged around chocolate manufacturing and amusement tourism in the 20th century.

Pennsylvania State Capitol

Pennsylvania State Capitol is a visitor site in Harrisburg, PA, United States. Records associated with the site include 1906. The Pennsylvania State Capitol was dedicated in 1906 after designs by Joseph Huston in American Renaissance style. Its dome, rotunda, and Senate and House chambers contain murals, marble, and stained glass documenting state history. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

$50

Hersheypark

Hersheypark is a major family amusement park located in Hershey, Pennsylvania. Originally created in 1906 as a leisure park for the employees of the Hershey Chocolate Company, it has expanded into a massive entertainment complex featuring numerous roller coasters, a large water park, and a zoo, all maintaining a distinct chocolate theme.
