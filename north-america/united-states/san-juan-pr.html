Destination Guide — Puerto Rico

Explore San Juan, PR

A Brief History

San Juan is the oldest European-founded city in the United States territories, established by Spanish colonists in 1521. As the first major port of call for galleons arriving in the Americas, it became a vital and heavily fortified military stronghold defending the Spanish Empire from English, Dutch, and pirate attacks. The city expanded beyond its walled islet in the twentieth century as Puerto Rico transitioned to an American territory. This extensive maritime and colonial history is preserved in the massive fortifications of El Morro and the cobblestone streets of Old San Juan.

San Juan National Historic Site

San Juan National Historic Site is a visitor site in San Juan, PR, United States. San Juan National Historic Site, established in 1949, is a combination of El Morro and San Cristobal forts operated by the National Park Service. The site played an important role in Spanish defense efforts and control of the Caribbean route for over 250 years. The main structures and grounds are the principal points of interest.

$10

Castillo San Felipe del Morro

Castillo San Felipe del Morro is a visitor site in San Juan, PR, United States. Castillo San Felipe del Morro, also known as El Morro, is a 16th-century Spanish fortification located on a promontory in Old San Juan. Visitors can enjoy views of the shore and explore the expansive grassy lawn surrounding the fort. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

$10

San Cristóbal Castle

Begin a visit of Old San Juan at the Castillo San Cristóbal, a heavily fortified citadel constructed between 1765 and 1785. This historic fort, which is the largest among the Spanish-built forts in the Americas, played a significant role in Puerto Rico's history. It offers panoramic views of the coastline, Condado, and Old San Juan.

Free

Plaza Colón

Plaza Colón is a public square in San Juan, PR, United States. Construction and early use date to around 1971. The plaza also includes Plazuela de la Rogativa, built in 1971, which showcases a modern sculpture depicting a religious procession that commemorates an event from 1797 when British ships attempted to take control of the colony. Open pavement and surrounding facades define the visitor setting.

Free

Plaza Las Américas

Plaza Las Américas is a modern indoor shopping plaza in San Juan, offering a variety of stores and restaurants for visitors to enjoy. With its arched mezzanines and seasonal decor, the mall provides a perfect setting for some retail therapy or to pick up any forgotten items. While it may not offer traditional Puerto Rican souvenirs, it has over three hundred stores including chain outlets, the latest fashion trends, American dining options, movie theaters, and unique shops.

Free

Puerta de San Juan

Puerta de San Juan, a large gate dating back to around 1635, served as the main entrance to the walled city during its Spanish colonial era. Located along Clara Lair Street, this pink-red exterior gate stands out amidst the surrounding brick buildings. An inscription in Latin that translates to 'Blessed is he that comes in name of the Lord' adorns the top of the door.

Free

Fuente Raíces

The Raices Fountain is a and well-known historical landmark in Puerto Rico. It commemorates the influence of Spanish, African, and Taino heritages on the founding of the Americas. The fountain was constructed in 1992 as part of celebrations marking 500 years since European colonization of the islands.

Free

Parque de las Palomas

Parque de las Palomas, also known as Pigeon Park, is a small outdoor area located at the edge of the fortification walls in Old San Juan. It offers scenic views and park benches where visitors can relax and enjoy the company of friendly pigeons. The park is a popular spot for families, with kids having fun chasing after the pigeons that call this place home.

Free

Paseo de la Princesa

Paseo de la Princesa, also known as the Princess Promenade, is a active pedestrian pathway located just outside the walls of Old San Juan. It offers an array of colorful artisan displays and street vendors showing their wares under the warm Puerto Rican sun. The promenade provides views of San Juan Bay and the Atlantic Ocean, creating a truly magical and romantic atmosphere.

Free

Plaza de Armas

The Plaza de Armas is a historic square featuring a fountain with 4 statues from the 19th century. The plaza is in Old San Juan and is a great place to relax and take in the sights and sounds of this active city. Plaza de Armas is catalogued as a sights & landmarks within the historic quarters of San Juan, PR.

Free

Cuartel de Ballajá

Located Old San Juan, the Cuartel de Ballaja is a stunningly restored historic building that captures the essence of Puerto Rico's colonial past. With its active pastel arches and expansive courtyard, this architectural gem offers an ideal backdrop for photography enthusiasts eager to showcase the island's charm.

Free

Plaza del Quinto Centenario

Plaza del Quinto Centenario, also known as Quincentennial Plaza, is a modern space in Old San Juan that pays homage to Columbus' first voyage. Situated at the highest point in the city, this plaza offers views of the Atlantic. The centerpiece of the plaza is a 40-foot tall sculpture made of black granite and ceramics, created by known artist Jaime Suarez. This monumental artwork symbolizes the clay roots of American history.

Free

DISTRITO T-Mobile

DISTRITO T-Mobile, also known as the T-Mobile District, is a popular open-air events and entertainment venue located in the Convention Center District in San Juan. Since its opening in 2021, it has become a favorite destination for both locals and visitors. The five-acre complex offers a active atmosphere with a variety of restaurants and bars to choose from, catering to different tastes.

Free

Puerto Rico Convention Center

The Puerto Rico Convention Center, located in the Convention Center / Miramar area, is a modern facility that hosts year-round events for both locals and international businesses. The area is also home to the Pan American Cruise Pier and Isla Grande Airport, making it a hub for business travelers, the harbor is undergoing redevelopment and a new luxury hotel by Starwood has been recently constructed in the vicinity.

Free

Laguna del Condado Profesor Jaime Benítez Rexach National Park

Laguna del Condado Profesor Jaime Benítez Rexach National Park, also known as Parque Nacional Laguna del Condado San Juan, is a 2010 park located along the scenic Condado Lagoon in Puerto Rico. This urban oasis offers a shoreline boardwalk and a ramp for launching kayaks and paddleboards.

Free

Balneario El Escambrón

Balneario El Escambrón is a visitor site in San Juan, PR, United States. Balneario El Escambrón is a public beach located just outside Old San Juan in Puerto Rico. The beach is ideal for families and swimmers of all ages, with lifeguards on duty and restrooms available for convenience, it offers opportunities for snorkeling and scuba diving around its coral reef. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.
