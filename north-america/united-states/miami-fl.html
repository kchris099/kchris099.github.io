Explore Miami, FL

A Brief History

Incorporated in 1896 following the extension of Henry Flagler's Florida East Coast Railway, Miami rapidly transformed from a small trading post into a major resort destination. The 1920s land boom spurred extensive development, leaving a legacy of Mediterranean Revival and Art Deco architecture. In the late twentieth century, significant immigration from Cuba and Latin America fundamentally reshaped Miami's cultural and economic landscape. Today, the city serves as a dynamic international gateway, defined by its diverse communities, tropical climate, and vibrant arts scene.

Holocaust Memorial Miami Beach

The Holocaust Memorial Miami Beach serves as a profound tribute to the millions of Jewish lives lost during World War II. Designed by artist-architect Kenneth Treister, this outdoor museum features an impressive array of over 100 sculptures, with the centerpiece being the Sculpture of Love and Anguish—a striking bronze arm reaching skyward, symbolizing both despair and hope.

Free

Española Way

Española Way is a delightful pedestrian area located in Miami Beach, featuring Spanish colonial architecture and Mediterranean Revival style. This picturesque village offers open-air streets lined with charming restaurants and cafes, perfect for leisurely strolls, people-watching, or enjoying a cup of coffee or glass of wine. The area's romantic ambiance is enhanced by twinkling cafe lights at night.

Free

Lummus Park

Lummus Park is a vibrant and spot in Miami, offering a mix of beach activities, outdoor fitness options, and family-friendly amenities. The park spans 74 acres and features a beachfront path that showcases South Beach's famous landmarks and architecture. Visitors can enjoy various activities such as swimming, biking, roller skating, and working out with the outdoor gym equipment. Additionally, the park hosts pop-up markets and art shows like Scope Miami Beach.

Free

Art Deco Welcome Center

The Art Deco Welcome Center is a must-visit for architecture enthusiasts in Miami. Situated in the vibrant South Beach neighborhood, this area boasts over 800 historic buildings with the distinctive Art Deco style of the 1920s and 1930s. The center provides information on tours and opportunities to explore the colorful streets, galleries, shops, and restaurants that define this energetic district.

Bayside Marketplace

Bayside Marketplace is a vibrant waterfront shopping center located in downtown Miami, offering a diverse mix of familiar retail stores, local artisan stalls, and unique tourist shops. This two-level outdoor plaza features over 150 shops, including popular national retailers like Guess, GAP, and Victoria's Secret. Visitors can also explore an international food court and enjoy impeccable international cuisine while taking in waterfront views of Biscayne Bay.

Bayfront Park

Bayfront Park is a vibrant waterfront green space in the city, featuring an amphitheater and a landmark fountain. The park hosts numerous community events and offers various attractions, including the renowned Bayside Marketplace with its diverse range of vendors and dining options. Visitors can explore memorials dedicated to historical figures and enjoy free outdoor yoga classes by the bay.

Free

South Pointe Park

South Pointe Park is a beautiful waterfront park located in the upscale South of Fifth neighborhood at the southernmost tip of South Beach. It offers beach and pier access, a playground with interactive water features, and a dog park for off-leash fun. Visitors can enjoy views of Biscayne Bay meeting the Atlantic Ocean, as well as the downtown Miami skyline and Fisher Island. The park also features walking paths, picnic areas, barbecue pits, and a mini splash zone for kids.

Free

Ocean Dr

Ocean Drive is a renowned coastal street located in the heart of South Beach, Miami. It is home to over 900 Art Deco and 1930s/50s-style buildings, including landmarks like the Colony Hotel. The area offers a unique Miami experience with its historical architecture, upscale hotels, vibrant nightlife, and trendy restaurants. Visitors can enjoy people-watching, take photos, and soak up the lively atmosphere of Miami.

Free

South Pointe Park Pier

South Pointe Park Pier is a 450-ft-long pier located at the southern end of Miami Beach. It's a popular spot for fishing, offering viewing stations and fish-cleaning facilities. The park itself provides panoramic views of the Atlantic Ocean, Port of Miami, and city skyline. Visitors can enjoy various recreational activities such as beach access, lush landscaping, picnic areas, walking trails, water playground, and a boardwalk. Additionally, there's a parking lot available for convenience.

Free

Adrienne Arsht Center for the Performing Arts of Miami-Dade County

The Adrienne Arsht Center for the Performing Arts of Miami-Dade County, located at 1300 Biscayne Blvd, is a prominent arts complex that opened in 2006 after significant investment. Renamed after a generous donation by philanthropist Adrienne Arsht, it houses the Sanford and Dolores Ziff Ballet Opera House and the Knight Concert Hall.

Free

Gesù Church

Gesu Church is a historic Roman Catholic church located in downtown Miami. The church was originally constructed with wood frame architecture in 1897 and underwent a major renovation in 1924, where it received a Mediterranean Revival style architecture. The Gesu Church is an important part of Miami's history and is still active today.

Curtiss Mansion

Curtiss Mansion, located in Miami, is a Spanish-influenced venue set amidst tropical gardens and palm trees. Originally built for aviator Glenn Curtiss, the mansion now serves as a unique setting for weddings and events. The nonprofit organization maintaining the grounds ensures a picturesque backdrop for special occasions. With its rich history and beautiful surroundings, Curtiss Mansion offers an experience for couples tying the knot.

Venetian Pool

Venetian Pool is a historic and spring-water-filled pool located in Coral Gables, Florida. Built in 1923, it was originally a rock quarry pit used to mine oolitic limestone for the area's early buildings. Designed by Denman Fink and Phineas Paist, it was once dubbed the world's most beautiful swimming hole.

Coral Gables Congregational United Church of Christ

Coral Gables Congregational United Church of Christ, established in 1923, is the city's first church and showcases Spanish colonial revival architecture. The Mediterranean Revival style building was constructed in 1924 by Merrick for his father and features a Baroque belfry as its most prominent feature. Designed by architects Kiehnel and Elliott, it was intended to replicate a church in Costa Rica.

Watsco Center

The Watsco Center, located in Coral Gables, Florida, is a versatile arena with 8,000 seats that hosts live basketball games and various events. It's known for its cozy atmosphere and great views of the action. The venue offers accessible seating and amenities like food, beverages, and merchandise. Visitors have praised the helpful staff and excellent facilities such as clean bathrooms. Additionally, it has been used for concerts featuring DJs during Miami Music Week.

Crandon Park

Crandon Park, situated on Key Biscayne, is a popular island park featuring a protected beach and various recreational facilities. The park's Crandon Park Beach offers crystal-clear waters and an offshore sandbar for sunbathing. Visitors can enjoy eco-tours, picnic areas, and concession stands. Additionally, the Family Amusement Center features a restored carousel and beachfront playground.

Free

Lummus Park Historic District

Lummus Park Historic District, affectionately known as Lummus Park, is a picturesque beachfront oasis that has been captivating visitors since its establishment in 1909. This historic gem not only boasts lush lawns and swaying palm trees but also features a charming promenade perfect for leisurely strolls. As one of Miami's earliest green spaces, it holds a significant place on the U.S. National Register of Historic Places, highlighting its cultural importance.
