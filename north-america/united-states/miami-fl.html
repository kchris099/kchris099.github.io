Miami, FL

Miami rose from a late-19th-century railroad terminus into a winter resort and, after the 1959 Cuban Revolution, a major centre of Latin American exile and trade. Art Deco hotels on South Beach, the port, and causeways across Biscayne Bay define its form. Boom-and-bust real estate cycles reshaped skyline and neighbourhoods. Calle Ocho, beaches, and downtown towers show a subtropical metro built on tourism, migration, and shipping.
