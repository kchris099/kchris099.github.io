Columbus, OH

Columbus was planned in 1812 as Ohio's state capital at the Scioto and Olentangy river fork, chosen for central location rather than existing size. Ohio State University campus, German Village brick, and Short North galleries record how canal and railway traffic, manufacturing investment, and state government employment shaped a prairie capital in the Midwest.

Southern Theatre

The Southern Theatre, dating back to the late 1800s, is a restored 19th-century theater and one of the oldest surviving theaters in central Ohio. It was one of the first landmarks in Columbus to use electricity. The venue hosts live musical performances, independent films, Broadway-style shows, and offers backstage tours throughout the year. Operated by the Columbus Association for the Performing Arts (CAPA), it continues to bring entertainment experiences to its audience.
