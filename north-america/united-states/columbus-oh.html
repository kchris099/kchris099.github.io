Columbus, OH

Columbus was planned in 1812 as Ohio's state capital at the Scioto and Olentangy river fork, chosen for central location rather than existing size. Ohio State University campus, German Village brick, and Short North galleries record how canal and railway traffic, manufacturing investment, and state government employment shaped a prairie capital in the Midwest.

Nationwide Arena

Nationwide Arena opened in downtown Columbus in 2000 as home ice for the NHL's Columbus Blue Jackets, with published capacity near 20,000. Concerts, skating shows, and hockey fill the calendar. Event entry follows posted ticket and gate procedures around the Arena District.

Ohio Stadium

Ohio Stadium, affectionately referred to as "The Horseshoe," is a historic gem located Columbus, Ohio. This venue is the proud home of the Ohio State Buckeyes football team and has become synonymous with college football culture. Designed by Howard Dwight Smith, it stands out as one of the earliest concrete stadiums, showing its unique horseshoe shape that fans adore.

The Schottenstein Center

The Schottenstein Center, also known as the Value City Arena at The Jerome Schottenstein Center, is a prominent venue located on the Ohio State University campus. It is the home for various men's and women's sports events and concerts. Hosting over 200 events annually, including concerts featuring known artists like Lady Gaga and Justin Timberlake, it has become an arena with a rich history of entertainment.

Ohio Theatre

Just south of the Ohio Statehouse in Columbus, the Ohio Theatre opened in 1928 as a Spanish Baroque movie palace later saved as a performing-arts house. Gilded plaster and a large auditorium frame concerts, ballet, and touring shows. Public entry is usually for ticketed performances.

Southern Theatre

The Southern Theatre opened in the nineteenth century in downtown Columbus and survives as an intimate performance venue with balconies, decorative plaster and a restored auditorium. Music, theater, dance, talks and community events use the space, while modern systems support current productions. Interior access normally follows a performance or organized tour.

The Book Loft of German Village

Thirty-two rooms of new and used titles fill a converted house complex in Columbus's German Village. Narrow staircases link themed rooms, and a gift shop sells shirts and small souvenirs beside the stacks. Brick streets and cottages outside make the bookstore feel woven into the neighborhood rather than a mall annex.

Franklin Park Conservatory and Botanical Gardens

Franklin Park Conservatory and Botanical Gardens combines Victorian glasshouse architecture with modern plant displays, outdoor gardens, art, education and seasonal exhibitions in Columbus. The institution blends horticulture with public programming.
