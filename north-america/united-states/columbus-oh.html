Destination Guide — Midwest

Explore Columbus, OH

A Brief History

Columbus was planned in 1812 as Ohio's state capital at the Scioto and Olentangy river fork, chosen for central location rather than existing size. Ohio State University campus, German Village brick, and Short North galleries record how canal and railway traffic, manufacturing investment, and state government employment shaped a prairie capital in the Midwest.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

Nationwide Arena

Nationwide Arena is a versatile venue located in the lively Arena District of Columbus, Ohio. It boasts an impressive 685,000 square feet and a seating capacity of 20,000. The arena's elegant design features terrazzo floors, brick pillars, and marble details. It has hosted various events including big-name bands, orchestra performances, and NHL games since its opening in 2000. As the home of the Columbus Blue Jackets, it offers an immersive fan experience for hockey enthusiasts.

Free

North Market Downtown

North Market Downtown is a covered farmers' market offering a wide array of products such as flowers, produce, and handicrafts. It's also a hub for global dining options with influences from various countries like Somalia, El Salvador, Mexico, Yemen, and Vietnam. The market features over 30 vendors including butchers, bakers, grocers, and restaurants. Visitors can enjoy farm-to-table fare and innovative dishes created by talented chefs.

$30

Ohio Stadium

Ohio Stadium, affectionately referred to as "The Horseshoe," is a historic gem nestled in the heart of Columbus, Ohio. This venue serves as the proud home of the Ohio State Buckeyes football team and has become synonymous with college football culture. Designed by Howard Dwight Smith, it stands out as one of the earliest concrete stadiums, showcasing its unique horseshoe shape that fans adore.

Free

The Schottenstein Center

The Schottenstein Center, also known as the Value City Arena at The Jerome Schottenstein Center, is a prominent venue located on the Ohio State University campus. It serves as the home for various men's and women's sports events and concerts. Hosting over 200 events annually, including concerts featuring renowned artists like Lady Gaga and Justin Timberlake, it has become an arena with a rich history of entertainment.

Free

Ohio Theatre

Nestled just south of the Ohio Statehouse, the Ohio Theatre is a true gem of Spanish-Baroque architecture that has captivated audiences since its grand opening in 1928. Originally designed as a movie palace and variety show venue, this majestic theater features an impressive 21-foot chandelier and intricate details that transport visitors to another era.

Free

Southern Theatre

The Southern Theatre, dating back to the late 1800s, is a restored 19th-century theater and one of the oldest surviving theaters in central Ohio. It was one of the first landmarks in Columbus to use electricity. The venue hosts live musical performances, independent films, Broadway-style shows, and offers backstage tours throughout the year. Operated by the Columbus Association for the Performing Arts (CAPA), it continues to bring captivating entertainment experiences to its audience.

Free

Katzinger's Delicatessen

Katzinger's Delicatessen is a long-standing establishment that has been serving up classic deli favorites since the late 1980s. Located in Columbus, Ohio, this New York-style deli prides itself on preparing almost everything from scratch, with the exception of their daily-delivered bread from Pittsburgh. The menu offers a wide variety of sandwiches, including options for gluten-free bread.

Free

The Book Loft of German Village

Nestled in the charming German Village, The Book Loft is a renowned independent bookstore that occupies a historic Pre-Civil War building. Boasting an impressive 32 rooms filled with over 750,000 books, it offers an extensive selection for all literary tastes. Beyond books, visitors can also find an array of gifts and t-shirts to peruse. This picturesque setting provides ample opportunities for bookish photo ops and leisurely browsing through its vast collection.

Free

Shadowbox Live

Shadowbox Live is a renowned performance-arts venue and cafe in Columbus, known for staging rock musicals, sketch comedies, and original works year-round. Established in 1988 as an all-volunteer ensemble, this award-winning nonprofit troupe has found its home in the Brewery District since 2011. The venue includes a main stage, Backstage Bistro for visiting performers, and is operated by troupe members.

$25

Franklin Park Conservatory and Botanical Gardens

Franklin Park Conservatory and Botanical Gardens is a sprawling 88-acre destination featuring both indoor and outdoor art and nature exhibits, as well as lush greenhouses and beautifully landscaped grounds. The conservatory offers seasonal displays, short-term art exhibitions, and permanent indoor biomes showcasing over 400 plant species from around the globe. Notably, it houses the largest private botanical garden collection of glass works by renowned artist Dale Chihuly.
