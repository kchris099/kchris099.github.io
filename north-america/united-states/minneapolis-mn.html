Minneapolis, MN

Minneapolis rose at Saint Anthony Falls, the Mississippi River's only natural cataract, powering flour mills that fed national markets by the 1880s. Stone Arch Bridge, Mill City ruins, and Chain of Lakes parkways record how lumber rafting, Pillsbury and Washburn fortunes, and Scandinavian immigration built the Upper Midwest's commercial twin to Saint Paul.

Lake Harriet Regional Park

Lake Harriet Bandshell Park is a setting located Minneapolis, offering biking and walking trails, water activities, and a castle-inspired band shell. The bandshell has been hosting concerts since 1883 and is a popular spot for professional portraits due to its large soft box-like structure. The park's natural beauty makes it a sought-after location for engagement and family photos, with lakeside paths, docks, blooming gardens, and sunset lighting.

Basilica of Saint Mary

Beaux-Arts planning, a large dome, stained glass, chapels, and an ornate sanctuary reflect its ceremonial role, while later recognition made it an early basilica designated in the United States. The Basilica of Saint Mary in Minneapolis was completed in the early twentieth century as a major Roman Catholic church near downtown. Worship and parish events remain primary, with restoration sometimes affecting access.

Spoonbridge and Cherry

Spoonbridge and Cherry, created by Claes Oldenburg and Coosje van Bruggen, has stood in the Minneapolis Sculpture Garden since 1985. A giant spoon spans a pond while a red cherry balances on its tip. Paths around the Walker Art Center campus give clear views of the sculpture from several angles.

Historic Stone Arch Bridge

Minneapolis's Stone Arch Bridge carries pedestrians and cyclists across the Mississippi River beside Saint Anthony Falls; built in the 1880s for railroad traffic, its masonry arches linked flour-milling districts and later became part of the riverfront trail system. Rehabilitation projects can close sections for extended periods, while views include locks, mills, falls, and the downtown skyline.

The Minnesota State Fair operates for a limited period in late summer on permanent grounds between Minneapolis and Saint Paul. Minnehaha Falls

Minnehaha Falls drops over a limestone ledge into a gorge within one of Minneapolis's historic public parks. Paths, bridges, overlooks, historic structures and connections toward the Mississippi surround the waterfall, whose appearance changes sharply with rain, drought and winter ice. Cliff edges, slippery stairs, seasonal closures and creek conditions require attention beyond the landscaped upper park.

Saint Paul's Union Depot reopened after restoration as a multimodal transport and civic complex. The 1920s headhouse and vast waiting room recall the city's passenger-rail era, while current spaces support intercity trains, buses, local transit, events, and exhibitions.
