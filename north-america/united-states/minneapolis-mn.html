Explore Minneapolis, MN

A Brief History

Minneapolis rose at Saint Anthony Falls, the Mississippi River's only natural cataract, powering flour mills that fed national markets by the 1880s. Stone Arch Bridge, Mill City ruins, and Chain of Lakes parkways record how lumber rafting, Pillsbury and Washburn fortunes, and Scandinavian immigration built the Upper Midwest's commercial twin to Saint Paul.

Lake Harriet Regional Park

Lake Harriet Bandshell Park is a picturesque setting nestled in the heart of Minneapolis, offering biking and walking trails, water activities, and a castle-inspired band shell. The bandshell has been hosting concerts since 1883 and is a popular spot for professional portraits due to its large soft box-like structure. The park's natural beauty makes it a sought-after location for engagement and family photos, with charming lakeside paths, docks, blooming gardens, and sunset lighting.

Free

Basilica of Saint Mary

The Basilica of Saint Mary, located in downtown Minneapolis, is a historic Roman Catholic minor basilica and the first basilica established in America. This architectural masterpiece boasts stained-glass windows, shrines, and an art gallery. Constructed in 1915, it is a prime example of Beaux Arts architecture and has been on the National Register of Historic Places since 1975.

Free

Spoonbridge and Cherry

Spoonbridge and Cherry is a renowned fountain located in downtown Minneapolis, Minnesota. This sculpture features a 52-foot-long spoon with a 1,200-pound cherry perched on top. Created by sculptors Claes Oldenburg and Coosje van Bruggen, it has become a symbol of the city since its installation in 1985 at the Minneapolis Sculpture Garden.

Free

Stone Arch Bridge, located in downtown Minneapolis at the intersection of West River Road and Portland Ave, is a historic former railroad bridge that offers panoramic views of St. Anthony Falls. Guthrie Theater

The Guthrie Theater, a renowned venue in Minneapolis, offers a diverse range of plays across its three stages. The theater also features full-service dining, multiple bars, and scenic river views. Established in 2006 to replace the original Guthrie Theater near the Walker Art Center, the new facility includes various amenities such as costume and set shops, storage spaces, classrooms, and a gift shop offering unique merchandise.

$18

Minnesota State Fair

The Minnesota State Fair is a late-summer extravaganza that spans 322 acres and includes attractions. From livestock competitions to car shows, concerts, and games, the fair has something for everyone. Visitors can indulge in an assortment of foods on sticks, ride the giant slide, admire seed art, people-watch, enjoy refreshing lemonade, and experience thrilling rides like the Sky Glider.

Free

Allianz Field

Allianz Field, located in St. Paul's University neighborhood, is the impressive home of Minnesota United FC and a venue for various events like concerts and boxing matches. Open since March 2019, it offers convenient and secure reservable parking. The stadium provides great views and comfortable temperatures, making it an enjoyable experience for visitors. The facility boasts top-notch amenities including private box areas with fantastic food options.

Free

Target Field

Target Field is the home stadium of the Minnesota Twins and offers both group and private behind-the-scenes tours. The stadium's sleek and modern design, featuring a large Minnie and Paul logo in center field, opened in 2010. Even if you're not there during baseball season, still visit the plaza and amphitheater without buying a ticket. Additionally, various restaurants have created attractive patio spaces from old warehouses around the stadium.

Free

Target Center

Located in downtown Minneapolis, Target Center is a versatile arena that underwent a $140 million renovation in 2017. It is home to the NBA's Minnesota Timberwolves and the WNBA's Minnesota Lynx, both of which have achieved significant success. The venue hosts over 1,000,000 guests annually and attracts renowned touring artists, family shows, and major sporting events.

Free

Grand Casino Arena

Xcel Energy Center, located in downtown St. Paul, is a state-of-the-art arena that serves as the home of the Minnesota Wild hockey team and hosts various concerts and events. The venue, which opened in 2000, offers an exciting atmosphere for fast-paced NHL games as well as entertaining shows on ice and regular concerts. Adjacent to the center is the Science Museum of Minnesota, where visitors can explore STEM-focused themes and enjoy films at the Omnitheater.

Free

Saint Paul RiverCentre

Saint Paul RiverCentre is a spacious and versatile venue that hosts a variety of events, from dog shows to fundraising galas and concerts. The facility offers ample room for vendors, multiple event areas to keep guests entertained, and convenient parking with easy access to the arena. Visitors appreciate the friendly staff, cleanliness, and well-organized facilities.

Ordway Center for the Performing Arts

The Ordway Center for the Performing Arts in St. Paul is a contemporary venue that hosts theater, dance, and music productions as well as arts-education programs. The LGBTQ community in St. Paul is spread out across the city, with many great neighborhoods to call home. The city includes condos and single-family homes at varying prices and styles.

Rice Park

Rice Park is a central gathering place located in downtown Saint Paul, offering a variety of attractions and activities for visitors. The park features a central fountain and hosts a popular winter festival and ice rink. Surrounded by landmarks such as the Landmark Center, Ordway Center for the Performing Arts, and George Latimer Central Library, Rice Park exudes history dating back to its dedication in 1849.

Free

Palace Theatre

Nestled in the heart of St. Paul, the Palace Theatre stands as a testament to over a century of cultural significance since its inception in 1916 as a vaudeville venue. This beautifully restored theater showcases Art Deco design elements, from its marquee to intricate ceiling details, making it an architectural gem. The Palace Theatre is not just about history; it pulsates with life through an array of live music performances and events that draw crowds year-round.

Free

Cathedral of Saint Paul

The Cathedral of Saint Paul, also known as the National Shrine of the Apostle Paul, is a magnificent beaux arts cathedral located in Saint Paul, Minnesota. This grand church was built between 1904 and 1915 and boasts French-inspired architecture with a giant 23-meter wide copper dome and spire. Situated on an elevated hilltop, it offers views of the downtown district.

$10

Fort Snelling

Nestled in Hennepin County, Minnesota, Fort Snelling is a captivating unorganized territory that boasts the historic Fort Snelling itself. This impressive structure, which dates back to the early 1800s, stands proudly on the southern banks of the Mississippi River at the confluence of the Twin Cities.

Free

Hidden Falls Regional Park

Hidden Falls Regional Park is a charming and well-established park that offers visitors the chance to explore waterfalls, scenic picnic areas, and trails. The park is part of Fort Snelling State Park and features a 7-mile stretch of river from Hidden Falls to Harriet Island in St. Paul. This area is popular among locals for its picturesque surroundings, with opportunities to see dogs swimming alongside paddlers, barges passing by, and paddleboats on the river.

Free

Riverview Theater

Riverview Theater is a classic 1940s movie theater located in the Howe neighborhood of Minneapolis. It offers a nostalgic movie experience with its 1950s style, featuring high-back rocker seats and digital sound. The theater showcases a variety of films, from Hollywood blockbusters to niche art films, in its 700-seater auditorium. With affordable ticket prices for children and special deals on matinees and Tuesdays, it's a great place for family outings.

Free

Minnehaha Falls

Minnehaha Falls anchors a familiar sightseeing stop in Minneapolis, MN. Visitors encounter it along the city’s central routes through United States, often combining the visit with nearby parks, museums, and historic streets on the same itinerary. Street-level access and clear signage make it easy to reach on foot or by public transit from downtown districts.

Union Depot, located in St. Paul, MN, is a historic train station that offers a unique experience in the heart of the city. Visitors can start their Saturday with free yoga and then explore the farmers market, which runs through October in the summer and has a smaller version during winter. The depot also houses eateries like Salty Tart and Saint Dinette where savor delicious treats.

CHS Field

CHS Field is a 7,000-capacity baseball stadium located in the historic Lowertown District of St. Paul. It serves as the home ground for the city's St. Paul Saints and was built on brownfield land, opening its doors in 2015. The park features the City of Baseball Museum, showcasing a reverence for baseball history amidst a fun and lighthearted atmosphere.
