Monument Valley, AZ/UT, UT

Monument Valley's buttes rise on Navajo Nation land along the Arizona–Utah line, long part of Diné sacred geography. Hollywood Westerns from the 1930s onward made the silhouettes globally familiar. Tribal parks manage scenic drives and guided access rather than a U.S. national park unit. Trading posts and overlooks organise visits across red sandstone mesas still inhabited and interpreted by Navajo communities.

Monument Valley Tribal Park Visitor Center

The Monument Valley Tribal Park Visitor Center sits on the rim above the Mittens and Merrick Butte, within the Navajo Nation park. Exhibits and viewing decks introduce geology, film history, and Navajo cultural context for the valley. The 17-mile scenic drive starts from the same complex.

02 Admission$10

Wildcat Trail

Wildcat Trail is a 3-mile loop located in the Monument Valley Tribal Park. The trail starts near the Navajo Tribal Park visitor center and offers views of West Mitten Butte, East Mitten Butte, Merrick Butte, and Sentinel Mesa. Wildcat Trail is catalogued as a hiking area within the historic quarters of Monument Valley, AZ/UT.

36.9853° N / 110.1133° W

03 Admission$45 (tent site)

Monument Valley KOA Journey

Situated just across from the Tribal Park, Monument Valley KOA is a typical campground offering amenities like restrooms, a dog park, and hookups. Basic WiFi is available along with guided photography and Jeep tours. Visitors can enjoy vistas and even began on a scenic drive through Monument Valley. The campground also offers equestrian tours of the park, meeting conveniently on-site.

37.0170° N / 110.1535° W

04 Admission$10

John Ford Point

John Ford Point, named after the known film director who used Monument Valley as a backdrop for many of his Western movies, has sweeping vistas of the rugged buttes and desert landscape. Perched on a plateau at the edge of Monument Valley, this spot provides a panoramic view that includes recognizable formations such as The Mittens - East Mittens, West Mittens, and Merrick Butte.

36.9544° N / 110.0874° W

05 Admission$10

Artist's Point

Artist's Point is a overlook that offers views of West Mitten Butte, Merrick Butte, and East Mitten Butte. Visitors describe it as a dream come true and spots on the drive. The area is virtually empty, making it a peaceful and special place to take in the natural beauty. Due to limited capacity during the pandemic, there are no crowds, allowing visitors to fully appreciate the scenery.

36.9573° N / 110.0592° W
