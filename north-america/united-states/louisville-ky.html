Explore Louisville, KY

A Brief History

Louisville was founded in 1778 at the Falls of the Ohio, a river barrier that forced cargo portage and attracted early settlement. Churchill Downs racetrack, Main Street cast-iron storefronts, and bourbon distillery warehouses record how river commerce, antebellum steamboat manufacturing, and nineteenth-century whiskey maturation cellars shaped Kentucky's largest metropolitan centre.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

Louisville Palace Theatre

The Louisville Palace Theatre is a grand, historic venue that opened in 1928 and continues to be a hub for live entertainment. Its opulent Baroque design, adorned with intricate plasterwork and ornate ceilings featuring over 140 carvings, creates an enchanting atmosphere. The theater hosts a diverse array of events including live music concerts, ballet performances, stand-up comedy shows, and movie screenings.

Free

Fourth Street Live!

In the heart of Louisville, Fourth Street Live! is a entertainment complex that offers an array of nightlife experiences. This bustling venue features an assortment of nightclubs, bars, and restaurants serving top-notch cuisine. Hosting regular concerts and shows, it's the ultimate place to let loose and have a great time in the late hours.

Free

The Kentucky Center for the Performing Arts

Nestled near the downtown waterfront, The Kentucky Center for the Performing Arts stands as a cultural beacon in Louisville, KY. This expansive venue is dedicated to showcasing an array of artistic expressions through its educational programs and diverse performances that include music, dance, and theater.

Free

KFC Yum! Center

The KFC Yum! Center is a massive indoor arena located in downtown Louisville, serving as the stomping ground for the University of Louisville's Cardinals basketball team. With a seating capacity of 22,000, it hosts an array of events including big-name concerts featuring artists like Billie Eilish and Cher, family-friendly shows such as Disney on Ice, comedy performances by renowned comedians like Kevin Hart and Dave Chappelle, wrestling matches, and other special events.

Free

Louisville Slugger Field

Louisville Slugger Field is the home stadium of the Louisville Bats, a minor-league baseball team. The field has a unique design, incorporating a former train shed into its structure. Named after the famous Louisville Slugger baseball bat, it offers views of the Ohio River and Indiana. Visitors can also enjoy attractions like the nearby Louisville Slugger Museum & Factory.

Free

Big Four Bridge

The Big Four Bridge is a transformation of a historic railroad bridge that now serves as a pedestrian and bicycle pathway, connecting the Louisville Waterfront Park to Jeffersonville, Indiana. Spanning an impressive 2,525 feet over the Ohio River, this structure offers views of both city skylines and riverfront landscapes. Since its conversion in 1969, it has become a beloved destination for walkers, runners, and cyclists alike.

$45

Derby Dinner Playhouse

Derby Dinner Playhouse is a popular dinner theater located in Clarksville, IN, attracting residents from Louisville and Southern Indiana. The playhouse offers a variety of performances, including Broadway favorites, cheerful comedies, and shows for kids. With fancy costumes, a live orchestra, and talented actors, the theater provides professional-quality entertainment. In addition to the performances, Derby Dinner Playhouse also hosts kids' acting workshops.

Free

Central Park

Central Park is the massive, iconic 843-acre public park located in the center of Manhattan, New York City. Designed by Frederick Law Olmsted and Calvert Vaux, it features extensive landscaped lawns, wooded areas, artificial lakes, and numerous architectural landmarks. It serves as the primary recreational and cultural green space for millions of residents and visitors.

Free

Against the Grain Brewing

Nestled next to the Louisville Slugger Field, Against the Grain Brewery & Public House is a must-visit for beer enthusiasts and BBQ lovers alike. This industrial brewpub boasts an impressive lineup of craft beers brewed on-site, with new varieties making their debut almost weekly. With four locations throughout Louisville, including a lively downtown spot featuring a Sandwich Emporium and Flamingo Lounge, there's something for everyone.

$20

Churchill Downs

Churchill Downs is a historic and thoroughbred horse-racing track located in Louisville, Kentucky. It is most famous for hosting the annual Kentucky Derby, a prestigious event in the world of horse racing. The site also features a museum that offers tours to visitors year-round.

$18

Kentucky Derby Museum

The Kentucky Derby Museum stands beside Churchill Downs and traces Thoroughbred racing history from the first Derby in 1875. Interactive galleries cover jockey silks, breeding farms, and the twin spires grandstand, and guided walking tours visit the paddock and luxury suites on non-race days. The museum operates year-round except major race weeks.

$16

Louisville Zoo

Louisville Zoo, nestled in the Poplar Level neighborhood, is a beloved attraction featuring over 1,700 animals from around the globe. With eight major exhibits like Glacier Run and Gorilla Forest, visitors can witness diverse species and enjoy educational shows and animal feedings. The zoo also offers unique wedding venues where couples can exchange vows surrounded by their favorite animals.

$30

Louisville Mega Cavern

Louisville Mega Cavern is a former limestone mine beneath the city offering tram tours, walking routes, zip lines, and seasonal light displays in constant 58-degree conditions. The 100-acre underground complex was mined through the 1970s and now hosts educational exhibits on geology, recycling, and civil-defense history. Tram tours run about 70 minutes.

$12

Locust Grove

Locust Grove, a circa 1792 mansion and former home of Louisville founder George Rogers Clark, is a National Historic Landmark steeped in history. The house has hosted presidents like James Monroe and Andrew Jackson, explorers such as Lewis and Clark, and famous figures like Cassius Clay. This 55-acre historic site showcases Federal-style architecture and offers tours, exhibits, events, and educational programs to the public.
