Explore Las Vegas, NV

A Brief History

Las Vegas originated in 1905 as a small railroad town situated near a rare desert oasis. Following the legalization of gambling in Nevada in 1931 and the subsequent construction of the Hoover Dam, it rapidly transformed into a major hub for gaming and entertainment. Its urban landscape is distinctive, characterized by massive, thematic resort architecture that continuously reinvents itself to reflect the evolving nature of American commercial tourism.

Top Sights & Activities

The Sphere

Completed in 2023 on the Strip, this spherical venue hosts concerts and immersive shows. Populous designed the steel frame wrapped in LED panels that display programmable images on the exterior. Inside, a 16K wraparound screen surrounds the audience for concert films and residencies.

Free

Bellagio Conservatory & Botanical Gardens

Inside the Bellagio resort, this conservatory changes floral displays five times a year for seasons and holidays. Horticulturists and engineers install themed trees, ponds, and sculptures under the glass dome. Admission is free, and crowds gather especially for Chinese New Year and Christmas installations.

Free

Wynn Esplanade

Opened with Wynn Las Vegas in 2005, this open-air shopping lane connects the resort tower to Encore. Boutiques, cafes, and art installations line a curving path beside pools and landscaping. It reflects Steve Wynn's emphasis on upscale retail as part of the Strip resort experience.

Free

The Venetian Las Vegas

Built in 1999 where the Sands once stood, this resort recreates Venetian landmarks including campaniles, canals with gondolas, and the Rialto Bridge facade. Shops and casinos fill the themed interior. The design helped launch a wave of destination resorts that mix gambling with family-oriented attractions.

Free

The Forum Shops at Caesars Palace

Linked to Caesars Palace since 1992, this mall brought Roman-themed statues, fountains, and a painted sky ceiling to the Strip. Luxury brands line curving arcades above a casino level. Its success encouraged other resorts to combine themed architecture with high-end retail under one roof.

Free

Las Vegas Strip

Las Vegas Boulevard South, commonly called the Strip, runs south from Stratosphere to Mandalay Bay with major resorts on both sides. Early casinos opened outside city limits to skirt taxes; El Rancho Vegas in 1941 began the modern resort corridor. Today it forms the city's primary tourism and convention zone.

Free

Paris Las Vegas

Paris Las Vegas opened in 1999 with a half-scale Eiffel Tower, Arc de Triomphe facade, and cobblestone-style promenades along the central Strip. The resort combines French-themed architecture with shops, restaurants, and casino space. Visitors can walk the public areas and streetscapes without charge.

Free

Fountains of Bellagio

The Fountains of Bellagio opened with the resort in 1998 on an 8.5-acre lake designed by WET. Choreographed water, light, and music performances run throughout the day and evening along the Strip frontage. Viewing areas on the sidewalk and bridge draw large crowds for the timed shows.

Free

Statue of Liberty Replica

A 150-foot replica of the Statue of Liberty stands at the New York-New York Hotel and Casino, which opened in 1997 on the Las Vegas Strip. The figure rises above a Brooklyn Bridge reproduction and Manhattan-themed facades. The outdoor monument is visible from the public sidewalk and resort forecourt.

Free

Fremont Street Experience

Fremont Street Experience is a five-block pedestrian mall in downtown Las Vegas that opened in 1995 with designer Jon Jerde's vaulted LED canopy. Nightly Viva Vision light shows and free concerts on outdoor stages fill the former Glitter Gulch casino corridor east of the Strip.

Free

Resorts World Las Vegas

Resorts World Las Vegas opened in June 2021 on the north Strip as a Genting Group resort with three hotel brands and a 117,000-square-foot casino. A 5.5-acre pool deck, 5,000-seat theatre, and restaurants sit within an East Asian–themed complex near the Convention Center.

Free

The Aquarium at Silverton Casino

The Aquarium at Silverton Casino is a 117,000-gallon saltwater reef tank in the hotel lobby southwest of the Strip. Thousands of tropical fish, sharks, and stingrays share the habitat, with scheduled mermaid swims and interactive diver-led feeding sessions at set times.
