Explore Las Vegas, NV

A Brief History

Las Vegas originated in 1905 as a small railroad town situated near a rare desert oasis. Following the legalization of gambling in Nevada in 1931 and the subsequent construction of the Hoover Dam, it rapidly transformed into a major hub for gaming and entertainment. Its urban landscape is profoundly unique, characterized by massive, thematic resort architecture that continuously reinvents itself to reflect the evolving nature of American commercial tourism.

The Sphere

A spherical music and entertainment arena completed in 2023. Designed by Populous, it is the world's largest spherical structure, equipped with a 16K resolution wraparound interior LED screen and exterior display.

Free

Bellagio Conservatory & Botanical Gardens

A 14,000-square-foot indoor garden located within the Bellagio resort, opened in 1998. It features elaborate seasonal floral displays crafted by a large team of horticulturalists and engineers.

Free

Wynn Esplanade

A luxury retail promenade located within the Wynn Las Vegas casino resort, which opened in 2005. It was part of developer Steve Wynn's shift toward high-end destination retail on the Las Vegas Strip.

Free

The Venetian Las Vegas

A luxury hotel and casino resort opened in 1999 on the site of the former Sands Hotel. Its design utilizes architectural replicas of various Venetian landmarks, including the Palazzo Ducale and the Rialto Bridge.

Free

The Forum Shops at Caesars Palace

A high-end shopping mall connected to Caesars Palace, opened in 1992. It pioneered the concept of themed retail environments in Las Vegas, featuring Roman statues and an artificial painted sky.

Free

Las Vegas Strip

A stretch of Las Vegas Boulevard South known for its concentration of resort hotels and casinos. Originally outside city limits to avoid gambling restrictions, it began rapid development with the El Rancho Vegas in 1941.
