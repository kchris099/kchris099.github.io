Explore Taos, NM

A Brief History

Centered around a continuously inhabited ancient Pueblo, Taos served as a crucial, frequently rebellious colonial trading post on the Spanish frontier. The early 20th-century arrival of bohemian painters cemented its enduring reputation as a fierce, independent nexus of southwestern art. Today, this complex historical evolution remains significantly embedded in the civic architecture and vibrant daily life of the community.

Top Sights & Activities

$25

Taos Pueblo

Taos Pueblo is an ancient, continuously inhabited Native American community located in northern New Mexico. The multi-storied adobe buildings, constructed over 1,000 years ago, are a UNESCO World Heritage site and a National Historic Landmark. The pueblo offers a profound, living connection to the traditional culture and architecture of the Tiwa-speaking people.

Free

Red Willow Park

Kit Carson Park, named after the famous frontiersman, is a 19-acre public park and historic cemetery in Taos. It offers a variety of activities for visitors of all ages. The park features walking and jogging trails around its perimeter, making it an ideal spot for outdoor exercise. Sports enthusiasts can enjoy tennis courts, basketball courts, and baseball fields as well as open areas for various sports activities.

$20

Taos Art Museum at Fechin House

The Taos Art Museum at Fechin House is located in the former residence of Russian artist Nicolai Fechin and now showcases works from the Taos Society of Artists. The adobe building, constructed in 1933, features captivating landscapes and portraits by artists who contributed to Taos' reputation as a creative hub. Visitors can also admire the intricate wooden carvings adorning the columns, doors, and furniture, which reflect Fechin's Russian heritage combined with New Mexican influences.

$3

San Francisco de Asís Catholic Mission Church

San Francisco de Asís Catholic Mission Church, situated in Ranchos de Taos just south of Taos, is a remarkable example of Spanish Colonial architecture. Constructed in the late 18th century entirely from adobe, it features striking sculptural buttresses and two front-facing bell towers. The interplay of light and shadow on its dramatic shapes has made it a beloved subject for artists like Georgia O'Keeffe and Ansel Adams.

Free

Taos Valley RV Park

Nestled just a short 10-minute drive from the vibrant downtown plaza, Taos Valley RV Park offers an idyllic escape for camping and RV enthusiasts. This serene park is perfect for setting up a mobile fishing camp, with easy access to the renowned Taos Fly Shop nearby. Guests can enjoy full hookups, a convenient dump station, and amenities such as a refreshing swimming pool and laundry facilities.

Free

Taos Plaza

Taos Plaza is a charming public square and community focal point known for its craft shops, cafes, and laid-back vibe. It holds historical significance as the earliest residents of the Taos-Tiwa tribe have inhabited the valley for over a millennium. The Spanish settlers also left their mark in the area, particularly in the village of Ranchos de Taos with its San Francisco de Asis Church.

Free

Downtown Taos Historic District

The Downtown Taos Historic District encompasses the central plaza and surrounding streets of Taos, New Mexico. The area is characterized by its well-preserved, traditional Pueblo Revival adobe architecture. It serves as the cultural and commercial heart of the town, featuring numerous art galleries, boutique shops, historic inns, and museums.

Free

Kit Carson House & Museum

The Kit Carson Home and Museum is a historic adobe house located near the Taos Plaza. Built in 1825, it was the residence of the famous American frontiersman, trapper, and scout, Kit Carson, and his family. The museum preserves the home's 19th-century appearance and exhibits artifacts related to Carson's complex life and the history of the American West.

Free

Fred Baca Park

Fred Baca Park is a lovely public green space in Taos that offers various amenities for visitors to enjoy. The park features a playground, spacious field, basketball courts, and leafy paths that provide shade and a refreshing escape from the desert surroundings. It's an ideal spot for leisurely walks under the morning sun while observing the diverse bird and butterfly species. Families frequent the park to make use of its clean facilities, shaded play area, and walking loop for exercise.

$20

Millicent Rogers Museum

The Millicent Rogers Museum in Taos houses a vast, premier collection of Native American and Hispanic art from the Southwest. Established by the Standard Oil heiress, the museum features exquisite turquoise and silver jewelry, traditional pottery, Navajo textiles, and historic Hispanic religious art (santos), providing a comprehensive overview of the region's artistic heritage.
