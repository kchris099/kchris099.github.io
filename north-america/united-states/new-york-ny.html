Explore New York, NY

A Brief History

New York City grew from the Dutch settlement of New Amsterdam at the Hudson estuary into a major port after British takeover in 1664 and rapid expansion in the 19th century through immigration, finance, and industry. Manhattan's grid, bridges, parks, tenements, and skyscrapers record waves of arrival from Europe, the Americas, and Asia, along with labor movements and cultural institutions. The 20th century cemented its role as a media and financial capital.

Central Park

Central Park is the massive, iconic 843-acre public park located in the center of Manhattan, New York City. Designed by Frederick Law Olmsted and Calvert Vaux, it features extensive landscaped lawns, wooded areas, artificial lakes, and numerous architectural landmarks. It serves as the primary recreational and cultural green space for millions of residents and visitors.

Free

Bethesda Terrace

Bethesda Terrace is a central, architectural masterpiece within Central Park, overlooking the Lake. It features a grand, sweeping staircase and the famous Bethesda Fountain, topped by the 'Angel of the Waters' sculpture. The lower terrace is renowned for its magnificent Minton tile ceiling and excellent acoustics, frequently hosting street musicians.

Free

Belvedere Castle

Belvedere Castle is a striking, miniature Gothic-style castle located on Vista Rock, the highest natural point in Central Park. Originally built in 1869 as a purely decorative folly, it now serves as a visitor center and houses the official weather station for Central Park. Its terraces offer excellent panoramic views of the Great Lawn and the Ramble.

Free

Times Square

Times Square is the bustling, neon-lit commercial and entertainment hub of Midtown Manhattan. Known as 'The Crossroads of the World,' it is famous for its massive, towering digital billboards, dense crowds, and proximity to the Broadway theater district. It is a major center for global tourism and the site of the annual New Year's Eve ball drop.

Charging Bull

The Charging Bull is a massive, iconic bronze sculpture located in the Financial District of Lower Manhattan. Created by artist Arturo Di Modica, the aggressive, dynamic statue symbolizes the aggressive financial optimism and prosperity of Wall Street. It is a very popular tourist attraction and a widely recognized symbol of American capitalism.

Rockefeller Center

Rockefeller Center is a massive, historic complex of commercial buildings in Midtown Manhattan, renowned for its Art Deco architecture and public art. It is home to the Top of the Rock observation deck, Radio City Music Hall, and the studios of NBC. The central plaza is famous worldwide for its seasonal ice-skating rink and the annual Christmas tree lighting.

Empire State Building

The Empire State Building is a legendary, 102-story Art Deco skyscraper in Midtown Manhattan. For nearly 40 years, it was the tallest building in the world. Its iconic, spire-topped silhouette is a defining feature of the New York skyline, and its open-air observation decks offer some of the most famous and expansive views of the city.

Flatiron Building

The Flatiron Building is a historic, distinctive triangular skyscraper located at the intersection of Fifth Avenue and Broadway in Manhattan. Completed in 1902, its unique, wedge-shaped design makes it one of the most recognizable and photographed buildings in New York City, standing as a pioneering example of early steel-framed skyscraper construction.

Grand Central Station

Grand Central Terminal is a monumental, historic commuter rail terminal located in Midtown Manhattan. Renowned for its magnificent Beaux-Arts architecture, the main concourse features a massive, celestial-themed painted ceiling and the iconic four-faced brass clock atop the information booth. It is both a vital transportation hub and a major architectural landmark.

The High Line

The High Line is an innovative, 1.45-mile-long elevated linear park built on a historic, abandoned New York Central Railroad spur on the West Side of Manhattan. The park features contemporary landscaping, public art installations, and unique, elevated views of the city streets and the Hudson River, serving as a model for urban repurposing.

Vessel

Vessel is a striking, honeycomb-like structure and visitor attraction located in the Hudson Yards development in Manhattan. Designed by Thomas Heatherwick, the interactive artwork consists of 154 intricately interconnecting flights of stairs and 80 landings. It offers visitors unique, shifting perspectives of the surrounding modern architecture and the Hudson River.

Madison Square Garden

Madison Square Garden, often referred to simply as 'The Garden,' is a world-famous multi-purpose indoor arena located in Midtown Manhattan, situated directly above Penn Station. It is the premier venue in New York City for major professional sports, including basketball and ice hockey, as well as high-profile international concerts and entertainment events.

Top of The Rock

Top of The Rock is an observatory located on top of Rockefeller Center, featuring an ocean-liner style design and providing city views. travellers can explore Rockefeller Center, known for hosting The Today Show, shopping opportunities, and dining options before taking the elevator to the Top of The Rock for a bird's-eye view of the city. Unlike the Empire State Building, this vantage point allows capturing photos with the building in them.

Free

Radio City Music Hall

Nestled in the heart of Rockefeller Center, Radio City Music Hall is a Art Deco theater that has been captivating audiences since its grand opening in 1932. Known as the "Showplace of the Nation," this venue boasts a seating capacity of nearly 6,000 and has hosted an array of legendary events, from concerts to award ceremonies and live television broadcasts. Its striking exterior features a long marquee sign on Sixth Avenue that beckons travellers to capture its beauty.

Free

St. Patrick's Cathedral

St. Patrick's Cathedral, a towering Neo-Gothic church built in 1879, is a historic landmark and symbol of the Roman Catholic Church in New York City. Situated across from Rockefeller Center on Fifth Avenue, it is the largest Catholic cathedral in the United States and can accommodate around 5,000 worshippers every Sunday.

Yankee Stadium

Yankee Stadium, home of the New York Yankees, is a renowned cultural icon in The Bronx and New York City. Although it was built in 2009 to replace the original stadium from 1923, it incorporates design elements from its predecessor. travellers can enjoy classic tours that include visits to the New York Yankees Museum, Monument Park, and even have lunch at the adjacent Hard Rock Cafe. Group ticket rates are available with special benefits for groups of 10 or more.

American Dream

American Dream is a massive indoor complex located in East Rutherford, New Jersey. It offers a wide range of entertainment options including shopping, dining, a theme park, water park and even a ski slope. The development faced some delays in construction but eventually opened its doors to the public. One of its main attractions is Nickelodeon Universe, the largest indoor theme park in North America with 30 rides and attractions for all ages.

Chelsea Market

Chelsea Market is a popular indoor marketplace in New York City that has been attracting both locals and tourists for the past 25 years. It offers a wide variety of high-quality grocers, shops, and eateries. travellers can find everything from fresh produce, artisanal cheeses, and imported ingredients to unique fashion, art, and vintage items. The market also features a diverse selection of eateries serving Indian cuisine, tacos, Mediterranean street food, and pasta.

Free

MetLife Stadium

MetLife Stadium, formerly known as Giants Stadium, is a renowned football stadium that serves as the home ground for both the New York Giants and the New York Jets. In addition to hosting football games, it also accommodates college basketball events, major concerts, and other headline-grabbing occasions. Notably, it was the venue for Super Bowl XLVIII in 2014, making East Rutherford the smallest city to ever host a Super Bowl.

One World Trade Center

One World Trade Center, also known as The Freedom Tower, is a remarkable symbol of resilience and strength. Standing at 1,776 feet tall with its spire, it is the tallest building in the United States and the sixth tallest in the world. This 94-floor skyscraper took eight years to build and was completed in 2014. Its unique design and full-glass facade make it stand out against the New York City skyline.

Built in 1766, it is the oldest surviving religious building in Manhattan and a prime example of late Georgian church architecture. The Woolworth Building

The Woolworth Building, a neo-Gothic skyscraper completed in 1913, once held the title of the tallest building in the world until 1930. This architectural gem, designed by renowned architect Cass Gilbert and financed by five-and-dime mogul Frank Woolworth, stands proudly at 792 feet in Manhattan's Financial District. Often overshadowed by more modern structures, it remains an landmark that draws travellers for its intricate design reminiscent of European cathedrals.

Free

African Burial Ground National Monument

African Burial Ground National Monument is a significant historical site in New York City, honoring the 15,000 Africans buried there from the late 17th to 18th centuries. It is part of the National Park of New York Harbor, which includes other notable landmarks like Statue of Liberty National Monument and Ellis Island. The monument features an outdoor memorial and an exhibition that delves into the African diaspora and slavery in New York City.

Free

Trinity Church

Nestled at the bustling intersection of Wall Street and Broadway in Lower Manhattan, Trinity Church stands as a magnificent testament to Gothic Revival architecture. This historic Episcopal church is not just a place of worship; it’s also home to an adjacent cemetery where notable figures like Alexander Hamilton and his wife, Elizabeth Schuyler, rest eternally. The church's rich history includes visits from George Washington himself after his inauguration in 1789.

Free

New York Stock Exchange

The New York Stock Exchange, located on Wall Street, is a renowned landmark and houses one of the world's top stock exchanges. The building itself boasts impressive Greek Revival architecture, featuring six towering Corinthian columns and ornamental figures symbolizing American capitalism. Notably, a 3.5-ton bronze sculpture called 'Integrity' once stood in front of the exchange before being relocated to nearby Bowling Green due to its mysterious overnight appearance by the artist.

Free

The Battery

The Battery, a 23-acre historic park located at the southern tip of Manhattan, offers views of Ellis Island and the Statue of Liberty. It features well-manicured green spaces, picturesque promenades, art installations, a kid-friendly Playscape and ShowBox amphitheater for improv performances. The park also includes monuments honoring soldiers, explorers, and immigrants.

Free

Marsha P. Johnson State Park

Marsha P. Johnson State Park, also known as East River State Park, is a 7-acre riverside park located in Williamsburg, Brooklyn. It was named after Marsha P. Johnson, a prominent transgender woman of color and LGBTQ rights activist. The park offers picnic sites with skyline views of Manhattan and a gentle breeze from the East River. travellers can enjoy the lush lawn, waterfront views, and even a small pebbly beach where they can dip their toes into the river.

Free

Brooklyn Heights Promenade

The Brooklyn Heights Promenade is a picturesque tree-lined walkway that offers views of the Statue of Liberty, Brooklyn Bridge, and the Manhattan skyline. This hidden gem provides ample walking space and benches for travellers to relax and take in the scenery. The promenade also features historic mansions on one side, adding to its charm. From this vantage point, travellers can enjoy panoramic views of Lower Manhattan, including the World Trade Center.

$38

Edge NYC

Edge NYC is a 100-story observation deck located at 30 Hudson Yards, New York City. Opened in 2020, it offers 360-degree views of the city, including unique perspectives with glass floor panels. As the highest outdoor sky deck in the Western Hemisphere, it provides an unmatched open-air experience suspended in mid-air. travellers can enjoy views of landmarks such as the Empire State Building and the Hudson River while feeling like they are floating in the sky.

Free

Chrysler Building

The Chrysler Building, a example of art deco architecture, was completed in 1930 and once held the title of the world's tallest building. Its spire and design make it recognizable buildings in New York City. While it doesn't have an observation deck, travellers can still admire its beauty from outside or from nearby observation decks like the Empire State Building's. It's recommended to purchase tickets in advance to avoid long wait times.

Free

Bryant Park

Bryant Park, In Midtown Manhattan, is a popular 4-acre green space behind the NY Public Library's main branch. It offers a relaxing break from the city with lush gardens, benches, and shady spots. The park hosts various events throughout the year including summer movie nights, fitness classes, live concerts, and art installations. In winter, travellers can enjoy ice skating and browse through Christmas markets while sipping on mulled wine.

United Nations Headquarters

The United Nations Headquarters, located on an 18-acre complex along the East River, serves as a symbol of unity and international diplomacy. travellers can take guided tours of the flagship building to learn about the organization's work, explore art and architecture, and possibly observe the Security Council in session. The headquarters also features a bookshop, educational exhibits on peace and human rights, and opportunities to send postcards with UN stamps.

$36

Intrepid Museum

The Intrepid Museum, located on an aircraft carrier, offers a fascinating journey through history and technology. It showcases significant innovations such as the world's first space shuttle, the Concorde, a nuclear-weapons-carrying submarine, and various military aircraft. The museum provides an immersive experience that celebrates the people and advancements that have shaped our world.
