Tampa, FL

Tampa grew from a U.S. Army post at Fort Brooke on Tampa Bay into a cigar-manufacturing centre after Vicente Martinez Ybor moved factories from Cuba in 1885. Ybor City brick cigar halls, Bayshore boulevard mansions, and port cruise terminals record how Spanish and Italian immigration, phosphate shipping, and tourism reshaped a Gulf Coast harbour. Walking routes through the historic centre help visitors relate these monuments to wider patterns of settlement and civic life in United States.

Centro Asturiano de Tampa, established in 1902, is a historic Spanish social club and events venue listed on the National Register of Historic Places.
