Cleveland, OH

Cleveland was founded in 1796 where the Cuyahoga River meets Lake Erie, then boomed as an iron-ore and coal transshipment port after the Ohio and Erie Canal opened in 1832. Terminal Tower art-deco, West Side Market halls, and Rock and Roll Hall of Fame lakefront record how Standard Oil fortunes, steel mills, and Great Lakes shipping built northeast Ohio's industrial capital.

Heinen's Grocery Store

Heinen's Grocery Store is a supermarket chain located in the historic Cleveland Trust Co. Building in Downtown Cleveland. The building, dating back to 1907, has a mix of Beaux-Arts, Neoclassical, and Renaissance Revival architectural styles. Visitors can admire the glass dome in the Rotunda and enjoy affordable wine and beer tasting on the second floor. The store offers departments for meat, produce, deli, bakery items, wellness products, and more.

West Side Market, established in 1912, is a and historic public market offering a wide array of goods such as meat, produce, bread, and hot food.
