Explore Albuquerque, NM

A Brief History

Albuquerque developed around a Spanish villa founded in 1706 along the Rio Grande near Pueblo trading routes. Old Town adobe around the San Felipe de Neri church, Route 66 motels, and Sandia Peak tramways document how Mexican land grants, Atchison Topeka and Santa Fe Railway promotion, and federal research laboratories shaped New Mexico's largest city.

Top Sights & Activities

Petroglyph National Monument

Petroglyph National Monument, located west of the Rio Grande, is a vast desert park adorned with over 23,000 ancient petroglyphs dating back to 1000 BCAD 1700. The site showcases prehistoric Native American drawings on stone walls, with some carvings added by early Spanish settlers. Visitors can explore several trails such as Boca Negra Canyon, Piedras Marcadas, and Rinconada Canyon to observe these historical rock carvings.

$20

ABQ BioPark Zoo

ABQ BioPark Zoo is a public park in Albuquerque, NM, United States. The ABQ BioPark Zoo is part of a larger complex that includes an aquarium, botanic garden, and Tingley Beach. Visitors can get up close to their favorite animals while learning about them. Situated along the Rio Grande near Old Town, this expansive park offers a variety of outdoor activities for families to enjoy. Paths, lawns, and shaded areas make up the main public facilities.

Free

Old Town Plaza

Old Town Plaza is a public square in Albuquerque, NM, United States. Records associated with the site include 1706. Old Town Plaza is a historical gem that marks the birthplace of Albuquerque, established by Spanish Governor Cuervo y Valdes in 1706. This square was once part of El Camino Real de Tierra Adentro, a vital route connecting Mexico City to New Mexico's early capitals. Open pavement and surrounding facades define the visitor setting.

Free

San Felipe de Neri Catholic Church

San Felipe de Neri Catholic Church, an adobe church founded in 1706 and rebuilt in 1793, is one of the oldest buildings in the city. Located just north of Central Avenue at the west end of Route 66's downtown stretch, this historic site offers a glimpse into New Mexico's Spanish colonial past. The church has retained its original Southwestern style despite undergoing renovations over the years. Adjacent to the church is a museum showing furnishings and art from its history.

$20

ABQ BioPark Aquarium

ABQ BioPark Aquarium is a public park in Albuquerque, NM, United States. The ABQ BioPark Aquarium is part of the Albuquerque Biopark, which includes a zoo and botanical gardens connected by a narrow-gauge railroad. It offers a diverse range of aquatic life from the Rio Grande to the Gulf of Mexico, including colorful fish, eels, and more. Paths, lawns, and shaded areas make up the main public facilities.

$20

ABQ BioPark - Botanic Garden

ABQ BioPark - Botanic Garden is a public park in Albuquerque, NM, United States. ABQ BioPark - Botanic Garden is a vast expanse of gardens with walking paths, offering seasonal classes, tours, and events. It's an ideal spot for a romantic picnic amidst blooming flowers and natural beauty. Paths, lawns, and shaded areas make up the main public facilities.

Free

Isleta Amphitheater

Isleta Amphitheater is an outdoor concert venue located in Albuquerque, New Mexico. It is often used as a venue for various concerts throughout the summer season. The amphitheater can seat up to 15,000 people and has food and drink vendors available.

Free

Albuquerque New Mexico Temple

Albuquerque New Mexico Temple is a religious building in Albuquerque, NM, United States. The church in Albuquerque, New Mexico, is regarded as a beloved structure within the state. Its profound spiritual energy can be sensed even from outside its doors. Driving through the entrance gate evokes a distinctive sensation. The exterior and main interior spaces are usually viewable outside service times.

$12

Expo New Mexico

Expo New Mexico, located in the Uptown area of Albuquerque, is a versatile venue that hosts a wide range of events throughout the year. It is home to the known New Mexico State Fair and is a popular location for sporting events, festivals, concerts, and markets like the Flea Market. The fairgrounds offer ample space and are well-maintained, making it an ideal destination for families and friends to explore.

Free

Popejoy Hall

Popejoy Hall, located on the University of New Mexico campus, is a known performing arts theater that hosts a variety of national acts, including Broadway shows and dance companies. With a seating capacity of up to 1,985 patrons, it is the largest multi-purpose venue in New Mexico.
