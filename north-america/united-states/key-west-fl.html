Explore Key West, FL

A Brief History

Operating as a notoriously lucrative center for shipwreck salvaging in the 19th century, Key West became one of Florida's wealthiest cities. Its geographic isolation and naval importance fostered a fiercely independent, bohemian culture characterized by unique, storm-resilient timber architecture. Visitors today can clearly trace the intricate layers of this unique heritage throughout the preserved streets and monuments.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

Fort Zachary Taylor Historic State Park

Fort Zachary Taylor is a Florida State Park centered around a Civil War-era coastal fortress in Key West. The park houses the largest cache of Civil War armament in the world. In addition to its historical significance, the park features one of the top natural beaches in Key West, popular for swimming, snorkeling, and sunset viewing.

Free

Southernmost Point of the Continental U.S.A.

The Southernmost Point buoy is a large, brightly painted concrete marker in Key West, famously designating the lowest latitude land of the contiguous North American states. It is one of the most photographed landmarks in the Florida Keys, symbolizing the island's unique geographic location, just 90 miles from Cuba.

Free

The Key West Butterfly and Nature Conservatory

The Key West Butterfly and Nature Conservatory is a climate-controlled, glass-enclosed habitat located on Duval Street. It houses hundreds of free-flying, colorful butterflies and exotic birds amidst a lush, tropical botanical garden. The peaceful environment offers a serene, educational contrast to the bustling commercial activity of the surrounding area.

Free

Key West Lighthouse

The Key West Lighthouse is a historic maritime navigational aid that operated from 1848 until 1969. Visitors can climb the 88 iron steps to the observation deck at the top, which provides excellent, panoramic views of the island and the surrounding ocean. The adjacent Keeper's Quarters museum details the lives of the lighthouse keepers.

Free

Yankee Freedom Dry Tortugas Ferry

Yankee Freedom Dry Tortugas Ferry sits within the Yankee Freedom Dry Tortugas Ferry area of Key West, documented in local heritage registers for its civic or cultural role. Visitors approach from the main access road and combine the visit with neighbouring heritage buildings and public spaces in the district.

Truman Little White House

Truman Little White House sits within the Truman Little White House area of Key West, documented in local heritage registers for its civic or cultural role. Visitors approach from the main access road and combine the visit with neighbouring heritage buildings and public spaces in the district.

Free

Key West Aquarium

The Key West Aquarium is one of the oldest aquariums in Florida, operating since 1934. Located in Mallory Square, it focuses on the marine life native to the Florida Keys, featuring exhibits on sharks, sea turtles, and local reef fish. The facility is known for its interactive touch tanks and educational feeding presentations.

Free

Sunset Celebration at Mallory Square

The Sunset Celebration at Mallory Square is a daily, vibrant street festival in Key West. As the sun sets over the Gulf of Mexico, the waterfront square fills with a diverse array of street performers, musicians, local artisans, and food vendors. It is a quintessential Key West tradition, drawing large crowds to celebrate the evening.

Free

Smathers Beach

Smathers Beach is a documented coastal feature in Key West, United States, recognised in regional geographic listings for its natural formation and public access. The shoreline is open to visitors throughout the year and connects to adjacent beachfront districts. Travellers approach from the coastal road or boardwalk and combine the visit with nearby harbour areas and seaside markets.

Free

Edward B. Knight Pier

The Edward B. Knight Pier, formerly known as White Street Pier, is a long, wide concrete pier extending far into the Atlantic Ocean on the southern side of Key West. It is a popular, tranquil spot for fishing, walking, and observing marine life, offering expansive, unobstructed views of the water and the distant horizon.
