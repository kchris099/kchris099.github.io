Orlando, FL

Orlando developed as a citrus and cattle town after the Second Seminole War, then expanded rapidly when Walt Disney World opened southwest of the city in 1971. Lake Eola park, Church Street warehouses, and theme-park resort corridors record how railway promotion, aerospace employment, and international tourism transformed central Florida's inland hub.
