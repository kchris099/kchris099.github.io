Explore Orlando, FL

A Brief History

Orlando developed as a citrus and cattle town after the Second Seminole War, then expanded rapidly when Walt Disney World opened southwest of the city in 1971. Lake Eola park, Church Street warehouses, and theme-park resort corridors record how railway promotion, aerospace employment, and international tourism transformed central Florida's inland hub.

Top Sights & Activities

The Florida Mall

The Florida Mall in Orlando is a massive one-story shopping center, boasting over 250 dining and retail outlets. It's conveniently located just 15 minutes from the airport, making it a convenient stop for travelers. The mall offers upscale stores, a variety of dining options, and entertainment experiences. Families can enjoy the interactive Play Park for kids and an expansive Dining Pavilion that goes beyond the typical mall food court.

Free

Universal CityWalk Orlando

Universal CityWalk Orlando is a attraction within the Universal Orlando Resort, offering an array of entertainment options for visitors. The complex features a diverse range of restaurants, shops, and family-friendly shows. From themed games like Great Movie Escape to clubs, cinemas, and mini-golf, there's something for everyone. Indulge in outrageous milkshakes and desserts at The Toothsome Chocolate Emporium or savor sushi-burger fusion at The Cowfish.

$134

Universal Islands of Adventure

Universal Islands of Adventure is a top destination for thrill-seekers and fans of the Wizarding World of Harry Potter. The park features attractions like Hogwarts Castle in the Wizarding World's Hogsmeade, family-friendly areas like Seuss Landing, and classic rides such as Jurassic Park. Visitors can experience innovative thrills on Spiderman and Skull Island: Reign of Kong.

$134

The Wizarding World of Harry Potter - Hogsmeade

The Wizarding World of Harry Potter - Hogsmeade anchors a familiar sightseeing stop in Orlando, FL. Visitors encounter it along the city’s central routes through United States, often combining the visit with nearby parks, museums, and historic streets on the same itinerary. Street-level access and clear signage make it easy to reach on foot or by public transit from downtown districts.

$134

Universal Studios Florida

Universal Studios Florida is a sprawling amusement park in Orlando that offers an immersive experience into the world of Hollywood entertainment. The park features movie-themed rides, attractions, and entertainment that cater to visitors of all ages. From family-friendly adventures like Despicable Minion Mayhem to high-speed coasters like Hollywood Rip Ride Rockit and Revenge of the Mummy, there's something for everyone.

$134

The Wizarding World of Harry Potter - Diagon Alley

The Wizarding World of Harry Potter - Diagon Alley is a magical recreation of the famous street from the Harry Potter series, featuring a wand shop and a thrilling ride based on Gringotts Bank. This enchanting area at Universal Studios Florida offers an immersive experience for fans of the beloved books and films. Visitors can explore intricately detailed shops, such as Zonkos Joke Shop and Honeydukes Sweetshop, before indulging in themed treats and drinks like butterbeer.

$134

The Simpsons Ride

The Simpsons Ride anchors a familiar sightseeing stop in Orlando, FL. Visitors encounter it along the city’s central routes through United States, often combining the visit with nearby parks, museums, and historic streets on the same itinerary. Street-level access and clear signage make it easy to reach on foot or by public transit from downtown districts.

Free

ICON Park

ICON Park is a large entertainment complex featuring an observation wheel, an aquarium, and a wax museum. The Orlando Eye, a 400-foot observation wheel located at ICON Park, offers panoramic views of Orlando and its surroundings. The ride takes about 18 minutes and provides vistas of natural lakes like Big Sandlake and Little Sandlake, as well as landmarks such as Walt Disney World and Universal Orlando.

$30

The Orlando Eye

The Orlando Eye is a 400-foot tall observation wheel located in ICON Park, offering enclosed glass capsules with air conditioning and panoramic views of downtown Orlando. Operated by the same organization behind London Eye, this attraction provides a 20-minute ride in climate-controlled comfort. It's a must-visit for families, offering Wi-Fi-enabled capsules and access to over 50 restaurants, shops, and other on-site attractions at ICON Park.

$30

SEA LIFE Orlando Aquarium

If you're looking for an family adventure, SEA LIFE Orlando Aquarium is a must-visit destination in the heart of Florida. This captivating aquarium features a variety of marine life, including fish and other ocean creatures that will leave you in awe. One of its standout attractions is Florida's only 360-degree tunnel, which offers visitors an immersive experience as they walk through and observe sea life from all angles.

$15

Pirate's Cove Adventure Golf

Pirate's Cove Adventure Golf in Orlando, Florida is a must-visit for golf enthusiasts. The venue offers two 18-hole courses with unique challenges and lush tropical vegetation to shield players from the sun. The affordably priced attraction features cascading waterfalls, dungeons, tropical flora, and even an actual pirate ship to putt through. Visitors rave about the beautifully designed amenities, including mermaids and numerous water features throughout the intricately themed mini-golf course.

$38

WonderWorks Orlando

WonderWorks Orlando is an amusement park for the mind located in the city's International Drive entertainment district. Housed in a unique upside-down building, it offers over 100 hands-on science exhibits across six wonder zones, including Extreme Weather, Space Discovery, and Physical Challenges. Visitors can also enjoy attractions like laser tag, a ropes course, and a 6D motion ride. The venue provides an engaging and educational experience suitable for all ages.

$80

Universal Volcano Bay

Universal Volcano Bay is a thrilling water park located in the southernmost state of Florida. It features high-thrill slides and rides, as well as low-key pools for relaxation. The park's centerpiece is the impressive 200-foot-tall Krakatau volcano, offering a day of fun-filled family activities.

Free

Kia Center

Located in Downtown Orlando, Kia Center is a versatile venue that hosts various events, including NBA and NHL games, live music concerts, and the Venardos Circus. The arena offers a atmosphere with enthusiastic fans cheering for their teams. Visitors can enjoy a wide selection of food vendors and top-notch restaurants within the center. With strict security measures in place, the staff is known for being helpful and accommodating to guests' needs.

Free

Inter&Co Stadium

Inter & Co Stadium, also known as Exploria Stadium, is a modern sports venue located in downtown's Parramore District. It serves as the home ground for both the Orlando City Soccer Club and the Orlando Pride. The stadium's unique design includes a horseshoe-shaped roof that covers the stands while leaving the field open to the elements. This creates an electric atmosphere during games, with passionate soccer fans adding to the lively ambiance.

Free

St. James Catholic Cathedral

St. James Catholic Cathedral in Orlando holds a significant place in the city's history and serves as a spiritual haven for both locals and tourists. The cathedral boasts a beautiful blend of traditional and modern architecture, with meticulously crafted details that greet visitors upon arrival. Inside, the atmosphere is serene, adorned with exquisite stained glass windows and intricate woodwork, creating an ideal setting for prayer and reflection.

Free

Lake Eola Park

Lake Eola Park is a city centerpiece offering a variety of activities for both visitors and locals. From leisurely walks or jogs around the lake to feeding the diverse waterfowl, there's something for everyone. The park also hosts a Sunday farmer's market and special events, making it an ideal spot for relaxation and entertainment. Additionally, visitors can rent swan-shaped paddleboats to explore the lake.

Free

Dr. Phillips Center for the Performing Arts

The Dr. Phillips Center for the Performing Arts is a grand, multilevel venue that includes performances including Broadway plays, concerts, and dance shows. Upon entering the center, visitors are greeted by a magnificent staircase and ambient lighting that sets the stage for an immersive arts experience.

Free

Avalon Exchange

Avalon Exchange is a popular thrift shop in Winter Park, Orlando, known for its clean and organized space offering budget-friendly retro and popular brands. The store features a unique winged crystal chair at the entrance, perfect for snapping selfies. Additionally, visitors can explore the well-curated selection at Loving Thyself Rocks and Writers Block bookstore located nearby.

$60

Fun Spot America

Fun Spot America Theme Parks in Orlando offers a break from the typical big-name theme parks, providing a more old-fashioned and budget-friendly amusement park experience. With two convenient locations, it features thrilling rides like the White Lightning wooden roller coaster and Mine Blower indoor steel coaster. Families can enjoy bumper cars, go-karts, and a Ferris wheel. The park's free admission makes it an attractive option for those seeking affordable excitement in Orlando.

$25

K1 Speed Orlando

K1 Speed in Orlando offers electric go-karting for all skill levels, along with a variety of other activities. While Orlando is known for its bustling theme parks and attractions, there are also opportunities to enjoy quieter pursuits such as kayaking or visiting museums. For those seeking entertainment, options range from traditional theme parks to unique experiences like cabaret-style music at Judsons Live or sipping Champagne at Flutes Champagne Bar.

$30

Dezerland Park Orlando

Dezerland Park Orlando is an expansive indoor entertainment center that includes activities to keep visitors entertained all day long. With over 800,000 square feet of space, the park features go-kart racing, bowling, axe-throwing, trampoline jumping, arcade games, bumper cars, and glow-in-the-dark mini-golf. Additionally, it houses Orlando's largest museum - The Auto Museum.
