Bryce Canyon, UT

Bryce Canyon's amphitheatres of hoodoos formed through frost and stream erosion on the Paunsaugunt Plateau. Paiute people told stories of the rock figures; Mormon settler Ebenezer Bryce ranching nearby lent the site its English name. It became a national monument in 1923 and a national park in 1928. Rim walks and high-elevation viewpoints now show layered Claron Formation colour above southern Utah's plateaus.

Bryce Canyon National Park Sunset Point

Bryce Canyon National Park's Sunset Point is a must-visit for its views of the colorful rock formations and landscapes. The Rim Trail, stretching from Bryce Point to Fairyland Point, offers relatively easy hikes with views of the park's popular areas. Travellers can also enjoy the scenic drive that leads to Rainbow Point and Yovimpa Point at the very end of Bryce Canyon.

Bryce Canyon National Park Sunrise Point

Sunrise Point in Bryce Canyon National Park is a overlook that showcases the park's amphitheater and its striking russet rock hoodoo formations. Perched at an elevation of around 8,000 feet, this viewpoint is particularly popular during sunrise and sunset when the first light casts active colors across the landscape.

Bryce Canyon National Park Visitor Center

Bryce Canyon National Park Visitor Center is a public park in Bryce Canyon, UT, United States. The Bryce Canyon National Park travellers Center is a full facility offering ranger assistance, a bookstore, exhibits, and an award-winning film. The center provides multimedia exhibits, Wi-Fi access, maps, and backcountry camping permits for sale, there's a gift shop supporting park programs and conservation efforts. Bryce Point

Bryce Point is a popular vista point in Bryce Canyon offering views of the amphitheater, making it a prime location for watching both sunrise and sunset. Accessible by car or through a scenic hike along the Rim Trail, travellers can enjoy panoramic views throughout their journey. For more adventurous explorers, the 22.9-mile Under-the-Rim Trail provides a chance to experience Bryce Canyon's forested backcountry and wilderness areas with fewer crowds.

Lower Inspiration Point

Lower Inspiration Point is a vista located atop steep cliffs, providing travellers with, unobstructed views of Bryce Canyon's main amphitheater. While this viewpoint offers a fantastic perspective of the canyon, many travelers recommend taking a short hike to Upper Inspiration Point for an even more view. The area is rich in wildlife; during my visit, I spotted chipmunks and deer roaming about.

Fairyland Point

Fairyland Point is a viewpoint located just outside the main entrance of Bryce Canyon National Park. From here, travellers can enjoy views of Fairyland Canyon, home to the youngest hoodoos in the park. This point also is the starting point for the incredible 8-mile Fairyland Loop Trail. Despite being relatively small compared to other national parks in the United States, Bryce Canyon impresses with its beauty and diverse offerings.

Dark Ranger Telescope Tours

If visitors are looking for an stargazing experience, consider joining the Dark Ranger Telescope Tours in Bryce Canyon. Located just a short drive from town, this family-owned business offers an entertaining and educational night of cosmic exploration. With modern equipment and passionate guides like Sawyer, Liam, and Thomas, have the chance to view celestial wonders such as Venus, Mars, Vega, Mizar, and the Milky Way through their massive telescopes.
