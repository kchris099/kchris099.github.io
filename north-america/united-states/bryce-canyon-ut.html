Explore Bryce Canyon, UT

A Brief History

Established as a regional settlement beyond an older fortified core, the city developed through industrialization in the late 19th century, post-war reconstruction, and modern civic expansion that reshaped its public spaces, markets, and surrounding landscape. Restored religious buildings, civic buildings, and natural features now define the contemporary visitor experience.

Top Sights & Activities

Free (requires park admission $20)

Bryce Canyon National Park Sunset Point

Bryce Canyon National Park's Sunset Point is a must-visit for its views of the colorful rock formations and landscapes. The Rim Trail, stretching from Bryce Point to Fairyland Point, offers relatively easy hikes with views of the park's popular areas. travellers can also enjoy the scenic drive that leads to Rainbow Point and Yovimpa Point at the very end of Bryce Canyon.

Free (requires park admission $20)

Bryce Canyon National Park Sunrise Point

Sunrise Point in Bryce Canyon National Park is a overlook that showcases the park's amphitheater and its striking russet rock hoodoo formations. Perched at an elevation of around 8,000 feet, this viewpoint is particularly popular during sunrise and sunset when the first light casts vibrant colors across the landscape.

$20

Bryce Canyon National Park Visitor Center

The Bryce Canyon National Park travellers Center is a comprehensive facility offering ranger assistance, a bookstore, exhibits, and an award-winning film. It's the perfect starting point for travellers eager to explore the park's unique features. The center provides multimedia exhibits, Wi-Fi access, maps, and backcountry camping permits for sale. Additionally, there's a gift shop supporting park programs and conservation efforts.

$20

Bryce Point

Bryce Point is a popular vista point in Bryce Canyon offering views of the amphitheater, making it a prime location for watching both sunrise and sunset. Accessible by car or through a scenic hike along the Rim Trail, travellers can enjoy panoramic views throughout their journey. For more adventurous explorers, the 22.9-mile Under-the-Rim Trail provides an opportunity to experience Bryce Canyon's forested backcountry and wilderness areas with fewer crowds.

$20

Lower Inspiration Point

Lower Inspiration Point is a vista located atop steep cliffs, providing travellers with , unobstructed views of Bryce Canyon's main amphitheater. While this viewpoint offers a fantastic perspective of the canyon, many travelers recommend taking a short hike to Upper Inspiration Point for an even more view. The area is rich in wildlife; during my visit, I spotted chipmunks and deer roaming about.

$20

Fairyland Point

Fairyland Point is a viewpoint located just outside the main entrance of Bryce Canyon National Park. From here, travellers can enjoy views of Fairyland Canyon, home to the youngest hoodoos in the park. This point also serves as the starting point for the incredible 8-mile Fairyland Loop Trail. Despite being relatively small compared to other national parks in the United States, Bryce Canyon impresses with its extraordinary beauty and diverse offerings.

Free (requires park admission $20)

Natural Bridge

Bryce Amphitheater is a natural wonder with rock formations and cliff trails. The Bryce Amphitheater Traverse Trail offers a 3-4 hour hike starting at Bryce Point and ending at Sunrise Point, providing a unique perspective of the area. travellers can stop by the Bryce Canyon travellers Center to learn about the park's geography and pick up brochures before exploring the amphitheater.

$50

Dark Ranger Telescope Tours

If you're looking for an stargazing experience, consider joining the Dark Ranger Telescope Tours in Bryce Canyon. Located just a short drive from town, this family-owned business offers an entertaining and educational night of cosmic exploration. With state-of-the-art equipment and passionate guides like Sawyer, Liam, and Thomas, have the opportunity to view celestial wonders such as Venus, Mars, Vega, Mizar, and the Milky Way through their massive telescopes.

$12

Bryce Wildlife Adventure

Bryce Wildlife Adventure is a 14,000 square foot facility featuring realistic wildlife displays. The collection includes hundreds of animals from various species, showcasing impressive taxidermy quality. travellers can expect to encounter animals they may have never seen before and learn interesting facts about familiar ones. Additionally, the adventure offers the unique opportunity to feed deer, making it an enjoyable experience for families.
