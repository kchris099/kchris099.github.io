Explore San Francisco, CA

A Brief History

San Francisco grew from a Spanish mission and presidio at the Golden Gate into a major Pacific port after the 1849 gold rush drew migrants from across the Americas, Europe, and Asia. Cable cars, Victorian neighborhoods, and harbor expansion followed 19th-century wealth, while the 1906 earthquake and fire reshaped much of the center. Shipyards, counterculture movements, and later technology industries added new layers.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

Golden Gate Bridge

The Golden Gate Bridge opened in 1937 as a suspension span linking San Francisco to Marin County across the strait. Its Art Deco towers and International Orange paint became symbols of the city. Pedestrian walkways on the east side offer views of the bay, Alcatraz, and the Pacific entrance.

Free

Palace of Fine Arts

The Palace of Fine Arts was built for the 1915 Panama-Pacific International Exposition in the Marina District. Bernard Maybeck designed the colonnade and rotunda beside a lagoon. The structure was rebuilt in the 1960s and remains a public monument to the fair's Beaux-Arts architecture.

Free

The Painted Ladies

The Painted Ladies are a row of Victorian houses on Steiner Street facing Alamo Square. Built in the 1890s, they illustrate post-fire residential architecture on the city's western hills. The grassy square provides a common viewpoint toward downtown over the colorful facades.

$10

Coit Tower

Coit Tower stands on Telegraph Hill and was completed in 1933 with funds bequeathed by Lillie Hitchcock Coit. Its murals depict Depression-era California life inside the cylindrical shaft. An elevator rises to an observation deck with views over the bay and downtown skyline.

$15

San Francisco Maritime National Historical Park

San Francisco Maritime National Historical Park preserves historic vessels and waterfront buildings at Hyde Street Pier. Exhibits cover Pacific Coast seafaring, fishing, and port labor. Boarding passes allow access to selected ships and the visitor center on the Fisherman's Wharf waterfront.

Free

Ghirardelli Square

Ghirardelli Square occupies a former chocolate factory near Fisherman's Wharf. Converted in the 1960s into shops and restaurants, the brick complex preserves industrial architecture tied to Domingo Ghirardelli's 19th-century confectionery business and later waterfront tourism.

Free

Lombard St

Lombard Street between Hyde and Leavenworth is known for eight sharp hairpin turns on a steep Russian Hill block. The brick-lined curves were designed in 1922 to reduce the grade for vehicles. Flower beds and hedges line the one-way descent used by motorists and pedestrians.

Free

Ferry Building

The Ferry Building opened in 1898 as a terminal for bay ferries before bridge construction reduced water traffic. Its clock tower faces the Embarcadero along the waterfront. The restored hall now houses a public market with regional food vendors and views toward the Bay Bridge.

$20

de Young Museum

The de Young Museum in Golden Gate Park houses collections of American art, textiles, and works from Africa, Oceania, and the Americas. A copper-clad building by Herzog and de Meuron replaced earlier structures damaged by earthquake. Galleries and a tower overlook the park and city beyond.

Free (ballpark tour $46)

Oracle Park

Oracle Park, home to the San Francisco Giants, is a must-visit for sports enthusiasts and baseball fans. Located in the vibrant SoMa district, it offers more than just baseball. Daily public tours provide behind-the-scenes access to areas like the travellers Clubhouse and batting cages while showcasing the team's history and achievements. The park also features a Fan Lot with activities for kids.

Free

PIER 39

PIER 39 is a lively waterfront destination in San Francisco, offering views of the Golden Gate Bridge, Alcatraz Island, and the city skyline. Nestled at the edge of Fisherman's Wharf near North Beach, it boasts over 50 unique stores selling a variety of items from sports gear to fine jewelry and San Francisco souvenirs. travellers can explore attractions like Houdini's Magic Shop and Lefty's San Francisco.

$28

Aquarium of the Bay

Located at Pier 39 in San Francisco, the Aquarium of the Bay is a popular attraction showcasing over 20,000 local marine animals. travellers can walk through two impressive 91-meter-long acrylic tunnels to observe creatures like sharks, rays, and octopuses up close. The aquarium also offers interactive experiences such as touching rays and leopard sharks.

Free

Sea Lions

Sea Lions have made the docks off Pier 39 in San Francisco their favorite hangout since 1990, becoming the city's beloved mascots. California law mandates boats to make way for these marine mammals, with as many as 1300 sea lions taking over valuable slips between January and July. The Sea Lion Center adjacent to Pier 39 educates the public about these wild creatures and the environmental threats they face.

Free

Union Square

Union Square is a vibrant and dynamic neighborhood nestled in the heart of downtown San Francisco, just a few blocks north of Market Street and southwest of the Financial District. This lively area is cherished by both locals and travellers for its prime location and electric atmosphere. Surrounded by towering buildings adorned with eye-catching advertisements, Union Square Park offers a unique respite where one can soak in the city's energy without worrying about traffic.

Free

The San Francisco Mint

The San Francisco Mint is an 1874 classical revival building that is open for events and can be used for different types of gatherings. It has ballrooms and vault rooms, which make it a unique historical landmark. The site forms part of The San Francisco Mint's documented civic and cultural landscape within United States, with archives and visitor information available from municipal or regional heritage authorities.

Free

Fort Baker

Fort Baker, a former U.S. Army post, is nestled beneath the Golden Gate Bridge and offers views of the San Francisco Bay. Originally part of the fortifications protecting the Golden Gate, it now forms part of the Golden Gate National Recreation Area. travellers can explore historic buildings and scenic trails while enjoying panoramic vistas of San Francisco, Alcatraz Island, and the famous bridge.

Free

Battery Spencer

Battery Spencer is a 19th-century concrete battery with panoramic views of Golden Gate and San Francisco. The observation deck provides the spot to view the golden bridge, even on cloudy days. Access to this location is easy by means of driving or biking, followed by a short walk to the top. As it sits almost 500 feet above the bay, it was once an ideal location for large coastal defense guns during its time under army control.

Free

Sutro Baths

Sutro Baths, a historic site dating back to 1896, was once an expansive public bathhouse featuring seven saltwater swimming pools. Today, it lies in ruins but remains a popular destination for hikers and photographers. The surrounding Lands End area offers a picturesque 3.4-mile trail through cypress groves and dog-friendly beaches, culminating at the Sutro Baths ruins. This location is also favored for engagement photo sessions due to its cliffs and ocean views.

Free

Lands End Lookout

Lands End Lookout is a popular spot for a scenic walk, offering waterfront hiking with views of the Pacific Ocean. The trail winds along rocky cliffs and features wildflowers, shipwreck views, and access to the ruins of Sutro Baths. Dogs are allowed off-leash year-round, making it a great destination for dog owners. The area also includes turn-of-the-century gun emplacements and a hidden beach called Mile Rock.

Free

Point Bonita Lighthouse

Point Bonita Lighthouse, the third lighthouse built in the West, is a historic active site accessible via a half-mile trail. The lighthouse offers ocean views and is located near Tomales Bay State Park, known for its diverse landscapes. From Point Bonita, travellers can enjoy distant views of the Golden Gate Bridge and San Francisco skyline. The lighthouse also offers free monthly sunset and full-moon tours with advance reservations.

Free

Golden Gate Park

Golden Gate Park is a sprawling 1,017-acre oasis in the heart of San Francisco, attracting hikers, bikers, art enthusiasts, and music lovers. The park is home to the impressive de Young Museum, housed in a modern architectural marvel designed to blend with its natural surroundings. Inside the museum, travellers can explore an extensive collection of American art spanning from the 17th to the 20th centuries as well as textile arts and diverse cultural artworks.

$14

Japanese Tea Garden

The Japanese Tea Garden, located in Golden Gate Park, San Francisco, is a serene 5-acre escape featuring a traditional teahouse and meandering paths that lead past koi ponds, pagodas, and a picturesque bridge. Originally created as part of an exhibit for the 1894 California Midwinter International Exposition, this garden has become one of the city's most beloved attractions.

$11

San Francisco Botanical Garden

San Francisco Botanical Garden, located within Golden Gate Park, is a 55-acre urban oasis and living museum. It features diverse plants from around the world, with over 9,000 different species on display. The garden offers free admission to San Francisco residents with valid ID and hosts various free days throughout the year. Additionally, it includes a Children's Garden that provides hands-on exploration for kids through sensory activities and interactive exhibits.

$19

Conservatory of Flowers

The Conservatory of Flowers, located in Golden Gate Park, is a Victorian-era glass greenhouse that houses over 2,000 species of plants and flowers. Dating back to 1879, it is the oldest existing conservatory of its kind in the Western Hemisphere. This architectural gem showcases rare and exotic plants from more than 50 countries across five galleries. travellers can admire a orchid collection and other unusual flora while enjoying a tranquil escape from city life.

Free

16th Avenue Tiled Steps

The 16th Avenue Tiled Steps in San Francisco's Golden Gate Heights neighborhood is a remarkable community art project that transformed a 163-step stairway into a mosaic masterpiece. Completed in 2005 through collaboration between locals, artists, and sponsors, the steps feature vibrant mosaic tiles depicting a sea-to-sky theme. Inspired by Brazil's Escadaria Selaron, the stairs offer a shimmering path from the sea to the sky and provide an excellent photo opportunity.

$30

San Francisco Zoo

San Francisco Zoo is a popular destination for families, offering a variety of attractions such as more than 2,000 animals, a kids' zoo, steam train, carousel and special exhibits. The Mother's Building within the zoo holds historical significance and features impressive tile mosaics and murals created by female artists during the WPA era. Situated on 99 acres near the Pacific Ocean, the zoo provides carefully designed habitats that mimic natural environments for its diverse range of creatures.

Free

Fort Point National Historic Site

Fort Point National Historic Site is a well-preserved Civil War-era masonry fort located beneath the Golden Gate Bridge in San Francisco. It was built in 1853 to defend the city from potential enemy threats and has since been recognized as a California Historical Landmark. The fort offers cannon drills, tours, and a rich history dating back to the mid-19th century.

Free

Golden Gate Overlook

Golden Gate Overlook, located in the Presidio of San Francisco, is renowned for its views of the Golden Gate Bridge and the Pacific coast. The observation deck offers panoramic vistas of the bridge, with cypress trees framing the scene. travellers can witness moments such as morning fog enveloping the bridge or the warm glow of a sunset over the Pacific.

Free

Presidio Officers' Club

Nestled within the historic Presidio, the Presidio Officers' Club is a captivating destination that seamlessly blends history and culture. Originally an 18th-century officers' mess for Spanish soldiers, this remarkable venue has undergone thoughtful renovations, preserving its adobe brick walls while enhancing its structural integrity. Today, it serves as a free museum and cultural center where travellers can delve into military history and local heritage.

Free

San Francisco City Hall

San Francisco City Hall, a grand Beaux-Arts building constructed in 1915, serves as the official seat of government for the city and county. Designed by architect Arthur Brown, Jr., it is renowned for its 307-foot dome, which surpasses the United States Capitol Building's dome in height. The site has witnessed significant political events and is a popular wedding venue due to its timeless elegance and opulent Beaux-Arts design.

Free

San Francisco Symphony

The San Francisco Symphony is a must-visit for classical music enthusiasts, boasting a 2,700-plus-seat concert hall that hosts mesmerizing performances. Led by conductor Michael Tilson Thomas, the talented musicians deliver awe-inspiring shows that are unparalleled. Families can also enjoy the Music for Families Series, offering kids aged 5-12 an opportunity to experience live symphony performances. Additionally, travellers can explore nearby cafes and bars before or after attending a.

Free

Washington Square

Washington Square is a historic park In North Beach, San Francisco's Italian neighborhood. This three-acre grassy plot hosts various festivals, concerts, and free movie nights throughout the year. Surrounded by lively cafes, shops, and restaurants, travellers can enjoy delicious food from nearby eateries like Marios Bohemian Cigar Store or Mama's restaurant. The park also offers benches for relaxation and is adjacent to the Saints Peter and Paul Cathedral.

Free

Golden Gate Fortune Cookie Factory

Nestled in San Francisco's Chinatown, the Golden Gate Fortune Cookie Factory has been a local gem since 1962. Tucked away in Ross Alley, this unassuming bakery is known for its handmade fortune cookies, which are made right before your eyes in a tiny open kitchen. Despite its small size, the factory produces an impressive 20,000 cookies daily and offers tours for travellers to witness the mesmerizing process.

Free

Transamerica Pyramid

The Transamerica Pyramid is a distinctive 850-ft-tall building located in San Francisco's Financial District. Designed by architect William Pereira and completed in 1972, it features a unique narrow pyramid shape with a light-colored facade coated in crushed quartz and an aluminum-paneled spire. Originally serving as the headquarters of the Transamerica Corporation, it was once the city's tallest building at 853 feet until 2018.

Free

Grace Cathedral

Grace Cathedral is a massive and awe-inspiring house of prayer located in San Francisco's Nob Hill. The cathedral boasts soaring towers, stained glass windows, and beautiful mosaics by renowned artist Jan Henryk de Rosen. It is the cathedral church of the Episcopal Diocese of California and features French Gothic architecture reminiscent of famous European cathedrals.

Free

The Wave Organ

The Wave Organ is a fascinating modern art sculpture situated along the San Francisco Bay near the Exploratorium. Created in 1986 by artist Peter Richards, this unique installation features over 20 PVC and concrete pipes that are activated by the waves, producing mesmerizing low tones reminiscent of listening through a conch shell. The sculpture offers an interactive experience for travellers to enjoy the natural sounds of the bay in a creative and innovative way.

Free

The Cathedral of Christ the Light

The Cathedral of Christ the Light is a modern Catholic church in Oakland, known for its avant-garde architecture that combines wood and glass. Designed by Craig W. Hartman of Skidmore, Owings & Merrill, this structure conveys an inclusive statement of welcome and openness with its airy and brightly lit interior. It replaced the heavily damaged Cathedral of Saint Francis de Sales in 2008 and has become a standout feature on Lake Merritt.

Free

Paramount Theatre Oakland

The Paramount Theatre Oakland, an Art Deco theater that first opened its doors in 1931, is a must-visit destination for lovers of performing arts and classic movies. This National Historic Landmark hosts a variety of events including theatre shows, musical acts, and even family-friendly performances like Baby Shark Live! travellers can also enjoy ballet and symphony performances by the Oakland Ballet and Symphony.

Free

Grand Lake Theatre

Grand Lake Theatre, located in the Grand Lake neighborhood at 3200 Grand Avenue, is a beautifully restored 1926 movie palace that originally served as a vaudeville and silent movie theater. It now screens first-run films and offers vintage ticket prices for matinees and Tuesday showings. The main auditorium features the Mighty Wurlitzer Organ played by the Northern California Theater Organ Society on Friday and Saturday evenings.

Free

Alamo Square Park

Alamo Square is a picturesque park in San Francisco, offering green space, a playground, and a tennis court. The park is famous for its views of the city's painted Victorian houses known as the 'Painted Ladies.' These colorful mansions have a rich history dating back to 1857 and have been home to various notable figures. The southeastern edge of the park features the well-known 'Postcard Row,' while the northwestern end boasts even more ornate and vibrant Victorian houses.
