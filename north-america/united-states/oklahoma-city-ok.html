Oklahoma City, OK

Oklahoma City sprang up in a single day during the Land Run of 1889 when settlers claimed homesteads in former Unassigned Lands. State capitol oil wells, Bricktown warehouse districts, and Memorial Museum exhibits record how prairie town-site auctions, Dust Bowl migration, and 1995 federal-building bombing remembrance shaped a Great Plains government and energy centre.

Oklahoma City Zoo

The Oklahoma City Zoo is a large 119-acre attraction that houses over 1,900 animals from various habitats. Visitors can enjoy up-close encounters with creatures like sea lions, rhinos, and brown bears on behind-the-scenes tours. For those seeking less intimidating interactions, there are interactive habitats such as the Flamingo Mingle and the Herpetarium Wetlands Walkway.

Will Rogers Gardens

Will Rogers Gardens occupies planted grounds in Oklahoma City with beds, lawns, and walking paths used for quiet outings among the city's named garden parks. Shade trees and open grass give room for casual walks near surrounding streets. The site stays a horticultural park rather than an amusement midway.

Paycom Center

Paycom Center is Oklahoma City's principal indoor arena and the home court of the NBA's Thunder, and with a seating capacity of 18,203, it hosts thrilling NBA games that draw large crowds. Concerts, family shows and other large events also use its seating bowl and support spaces, which have changed through renovations and sponsorship.

Myriad Botanical Gardens cover about 15 acres downtown with outdoor lawns and the Crystal Bridge Tropical Conservatory. Paths loop lakes and seasonal plantings used by office workers and weekend visitors. The conservatory's arched greenhouse holds tropical species year-round.

Oklahoma City's Civic Center Music Hall occupies a renovated Art Deco house in the downtown civic district. The Oklahoma City Philharmonic and Ballet share the calendar with touring artists. Annual series bring national names into the same hall used by local companies throughout the season.

Overholser Park

Overholser Park in Oklahoma City takes its name from the city's early mayor and the nearby lake developed in the 1910s. Lawns, shade trees, and walking paths form a neighborhood green used for picnics and casual recreation. The park dates to the civic expansion years around 1919 along the west side.

Centennial Land Run Monument

Bronze figures at the Centennial Land Run Monument recall the 1889 Land Run that opened Oklahoma Territory to settlement. The group stands in an open plaza setting along the Oklahoma River trails. Walkers read the scene from surrounding pavement without entering a separate enclosed gallery.

Chickasaw Bricktown Ballpark

Chickasaw Bricktown Ballpark opened in 1998 as the home of Oklahoma City's minor-league baseball team. Brick facades, statues, concourses and nearby canals connect the stadium with the redevelopment of the Bricktown district; the Dodgers, a minor league team based at the ballpark, have four league titles, two conference titles and thirteen division titles.

Pie Junkie

Pie Junkie, located in the Plaza District of OKC, is a small, locally-owned bakery that offers a wide variety of traditional and inventive pie fillings. Whether visitors visit in the morning or late afternoon, indulging in a slice of their delicious pie is always appropriate. Known for producing over 20,000 pies annually, Pie Junkie has become Oklahoma City's go-to spot for high-quality sweet and savory pies.

Wheeler Ferris Wheel at Wheeler District

A former Santa Monica Pier Ferris wheel was relocated to the Wheeler District beside the Oklahoma River, where it now is a neighborhood attraction; the ride overlooks downtown, the river corridor and surrounding redevelopment from enclosed or open gondolas depending on equipment. Weather, wind, maintenance, private events and seasonal hours affect operation, while the district remains an evolving mixed-use area.
