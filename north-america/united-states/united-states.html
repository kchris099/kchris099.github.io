United States

Indigenous nations built cities and trade systems across North America long before European colonies formed on the Atlantic coast. Independence in 1776 created a republic that expanded westward through purchase and war, bringing settler growth with Native dispossession before slavery led to civil war and emancipation. Industrialization and immigration later remade cities, while movements for labor and civil rights tested the promises associated with Independence Hall and the Constitution.
