Explore Santa Barbara, CA

A Brief History

Santa Barbara was established around a Spanish presidio and a Franciscan mission in the late eighteenth century, built on lands long inhabited by the Chumash people. Following a devastating earthquake in 1925, city leaders mandated a cohesive reconstruction in the Spanish Colonial Revival style, permanently shaping the city's architectural identity. The city later became a hub for the early environmental movement following a major 1969 oil spill. Visitors experience this curated Mediterranean atmosphere at the Old Mission Santa Barbara, the historic County Courthouse, and the red-tiled downtown arcades.

Stearns Wharf

Stearns Wharf is a visitor site in Santa Barbara, CA, United States. Stearns Wharf is a must-visit destination in Santa Barbara, showing the charm of California's oldest wooden wharf still in operation. As visitors stroll along the wharf, be treated to views of the harbor and sandy beaches framed by the majestic Santa Ynez Mountains. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Free

Moreton Bay Fig Tree

The Moreton Bay Fig Tree in Santa Barbara is a must-visit for nature enthusiasts and history buffs alike. Dating back to 1876, this tree spans over 170 feet, making it of its kind in the country. Its large roots and enchanting canopy create a peaceful oasis just minutes from downtown Santa Barbara's State Street. With historical significance dating back to its planting in 1877, this 120-year-old tree is designated as a notable historic interest.

Free

Santa Barbara Harbor

Santa Barbara Harbor is a waterfront area in Santa Barbara, CA, United States. Visitors can explore the wharf, spot seals and pelicans, visit the Sea Center aquarium, or enjoy fresh seafood from local fishermen. Quays and embankment paths form the main walking route.

Free

Santa Barbara County Courthouse Gardens

The Santa Barbara County Courthouse Gardens, a Spanish Colonial Revival building constructed in 1929, is a attraction in downtown Santa Barbara. The interior has murals, colorful tiles, and elegant furniture that pay homage to the style of ancient missions. Visitors can enjoy free guided tours and climb to the top of the clock tower, El Mirador, for views of the city.

Free

The Superior Court of California County of Santa Barbara, also known as the Santa Barbara County Courthouse, is a historic 1929 tile-roof complex known for its Spanish Colonial Revival architecture. Santa Barbara Botanic Garden

Santa Barbara Botanic Garden is a public park in Santa Barbara, CA, United States. Santa Barbara Botanic Garden is a known living museum and member of the American Alliance of Museums, dedicated to preserving and showing California native plants. It stands out as more than just a space with pretty flowers, offering collections-based research and educational opportunities for visitors. Paths, lawns, and shaded areas make up the main public facilities.

$30

Santa Barbara Zoo

Santa Barbara Zoo is a and easily accessible 30-acre zoo with lush gardens that offer views of the ocean, mountains, and Santa Barbara. Despite its small size, it houses an collection of 500 animals from 146 different species. Visitors can enjoy a miniature train ride around the property to see all the animals on offer.

$17

Old Mission Santa Barbara

Old Mission Santa Barbara, founded in 1786, is a historic Spanish mission located on the California coast. The church, built in 1820, is known for its double-bell facade and holds religious services. Visitors can explore a museum showing artifacts and objects from the mission's history. The site also includes lush gardens with ocean views, a cemetery, mausoleum, and aqueduct ruins.

Free

Arroyo Burro Beach County Park

Arroyo Burro Beach County Park, also known as Hendry's Beach, is a popular oceanfront destination in Santa Barbara. It's beloved by locals for its dog-friendly policy and amenities like dog-washing stations. The park offers a stretch of sand where visitors can sunbathe, swim, surf, and even whale watch. With grassy areas for picnicking and on-site grills available, it's an ideal spot for outdoor gatherings with family and friends.

Free

Shoreline Park

Shoreline Park is a public park in Santa Barbara, CA, United States. Shoreline Park is a and popular park in California, offering views of the coastline, the city of Santa Barbara, and the Santa Ynez Mountains. Visitors can hike down a wooden staircase to discover a secluded tidal pool and private beach area for quiet relaxation. Paths, lawns, and shaded areas make up the main public facilities.
