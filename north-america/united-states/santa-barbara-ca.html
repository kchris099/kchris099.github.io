Santa Barbara, CA

Santa Barbara was established around a Spanish presidio and a Franciscan mission in the late eighteenth century, built on lands long inhabited by the Chumash people. Following a devastating earthquake in 1925, city leaders mandated a cohesive reconstruction in the Spanish Colonial Revival style, permanently shaping the city's architectural identity. The city later became a hub for the early environmental movement following a major 1969 oil spill. Visitors experience this curated Mediterranean atmosphere at the Old Mission Santa Barbara, the historic County Courthouse, and the red-tiled downtown arcades.

Stearns Wharf

Stearns Wharf extends from Santa Barbara's waterfront on a wooden pier first completed in the nineteenth century. Shops, restaurants, fishing areas, marine facilities and views toward the harbor and Santa Ynez Mountains occupy a structure repeatedly repaired after storms and fires. Vehicle access, parking, waves, maintenance and private businesses affect movement along the working wharf.

Moreton Bay Fig Tree

Santa Barbara's Moreton Bay fig spreads a broad canopy beside the former railroad district, supported by massive surface roots and multiple heavy limbs, and planted in the late nineteenth century, it became a protected city landmark as streets and development grew around it. Soil compaction, climbing, drought, pruning, and traffic threaten the living tree.

Santa Barbara Harbor

Santa Barbara Harbor concentrates wharf walking, seal and pelican watching, the Sea Center aquarium, and fresh seafood from local boats along the working waterfront. Quays and embankment paths form the main circuit. Fishermen's stalls and visitor piers share the same harbor edge.

Santa Barbara County Courthouse Gardens

Santa Barbara's County Courthouse, finished in 1929 in Spanish Colonial Revival style, opens garden courtyards and an interior of murals, tiles, and period furnishings. Free guided tours explain the building, and El Mirador clock tower climbs to city views. Sunken gardens host concerts and ceremonies beside the Anacapa Street facade.

Superior Court Of California County Of Santa Barbara

The 1929 Santa Barbara County Courthouse is a Spanish Colonial Revival complex of white walls and red tile roofs. Sunken gardens and an open clock tower draw visitors even when court is not in session. Murals and hand-painted ceilings detail the interior public halls.

Santa Barbara Botanic Garden

A member of the American Alliance of Museums, Santa Barbara Botanic Garden focuses on California native plants through living collections, research, and education rather than ornamental flower beds alone. Paths cross the canyon plantings. Interpretive signs explain species adapted to the local climate.

Santa Barbara Zoo

Santa Barbara Zoo occupies a compact hillside site overlooking the Pacific, with gardens and animal habitats arranged across former estate land. Mammals, birds, reptiles, education areas and conservation programs fill a relatively walkable layout shaped by slopes and mature planting.

Old Mission Santa Barbara

Old Mission Santa Barbara, founded in 1786, is a historic Spanish mission located on the California coast. The church, built in 1820, is known for its double-bell facade and holds religious services. Visitors can explore a museum showing artifacts and objects from the mission's history. The site also includes lush gardens with ocean views, a cemetery, mausoleum, and aqueduct ruins.

34.4383° N / 119.7141° W

09 AdmissionFree

Arroyo Burro Beach County Park

Also called Hendry's Beach, Arroyo Burro Beach County Park allows dogs, with washing stations on site, plus sand for swimming, surfing, and seasonal whale watching. Grassy picnic areas and grills support group gatherings. Locals treat the cove as a regular oceanfront outing spot west of downtown.

34.4028° N / 119.7432° W

Shoreline Park

Shoreline Park overlooks the Santa Barbara coast, city grid, and Santa Ynez Mountains, with a wooden staircase down to a tidal pool and quieter beach pocket. Paths and lawns cover the bluff top. Walkers use the stair for low-tide exploring below the main lawn.
