Explore Tucson, AZ

A Brief History

Tucson preserves one of North America's longest continuously inhabited settlements, with a Spanish presidio founded in 1775 on Tohono O'odham lands. Mission San Xavier del Bac, adobe barrios, and University of Arizona campuses record how Apache frontier trade, Gadsden Purchase boundaries, and twentieth-century astronomy and defence research shaped southern Arizona's desert city.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

San Xavier del Bac Mission

San Xavier del Bac is a Spanish Colonial Catholic mission built in 1783 that is now a National Historic Landmark. This beautiful church features baroque architecture and painted artwork and it is still used for religious purposes, making it a great place to visit if you are interested in history or religion.

Free

Gaslight Theatre

Gaslight Theatre is a long-standing establishment in Tucson, offering a Western-themed setting and entertaining shows six nights a week. Since the 1970s, this theater has been delighting audiences with its family-friendly musical comedies, sci-fi spoofs, and superhero dramas. The venue also provides casual food options like pizza and popcorn as well as beverages including beer and wine.

$13

Reid Park Zoo

Reid Park Zoo, located in Tucson, Arizona, was established in 1965 by Gene Reid. The zoo spans 24 acres and is home to over 30 different species of animals from South America, Asia, and Africa. It is a city-owned non-profit organization funded by state tax dollars and conservation societies. Visitors can see a wide variety of animals such as lemurs, flamingos, otters, bears, tortoises, and anteaters.

$19

Tucson Botanical Gardens

The Tucson Botanical Gardens, situated on the historic Porter Family property, spans over 5 acres and offers a variety of educational activities for visitors. The gardens feature diverse areas such as the Cactus & Succulent Garden, Herb Garden, Barrio Garden, and Cox Butterfly and Orchid Pavilion where tropical butterflies can be seen from October to May.

Free

St. Augustine Cathedral

St. Augustine Cathedral, the oldest Catholic institution in Tucson, is a architectural marvel that captivates visitors with its Spanish-Colonial-Revival design and two spires. The cathedral's construction was completed in 1968, accommodating up to 1,250 people. Its late-19th-century Spanish-style building draws inspiration from the Cathedral of Queretaro in Mexico while incorporating local desert scenes into its intricate carvings.

Free

Tucson Convention Center

Tucson Convention Center (TCC) is a popular venue for various public shows and events, including the Pueblo Show and Arizona Mineral & Fossil Show. The center hosts unique vendors from around the world offering mineral specimens, crystal points, and other goods. Additionally, it is conveniently located near several top destinations such as the University of Arizona, The Fox Theater, Rialto Theater, Centennial Hall, and Tucson Music Hall.

Free

Fox Tucson Theatre

Located in downtown Tucson, the Fox Tucson Theatre is a historic 1930s-era building that serves as a performance venue for live music, revues, and theater. The theater's Southwest Art Deco architecture and unique decor make it a standout attraction with eye-catching neon lights and vintage signage. Recently renamed the Linda Ronstadt Music Hall, it pays homage to the city's cultural legacy.

Free

Rialto Theatre

The Rialto Theatre is a historic vaudeville theater that dates back to 1919 and has been restored to its former glory. It now serves as a venue for nationally touring bands and indie acts, making it a must-visit spot for music lovers. Located in downtown Tucson, the area has seen revitalization with new restaurants, apartments, and art projects like the mural project.

Free

Tumamoc Hill Hiking Trail

Tumamoc Hill Hiking Trail is a serene path for walking and running, offering expansive views of an ecological reserve. The three-mile paved trail features moderate elevation gain and is adorned with wildflowers in the spring. Open from 4 am to 10 pm, it sits on University of Arizona property. Visitors are advised to park along Anklam Road rather than at nearby medical facilities.
