Tucson, AZ

Tucson preserves one of North America's longest continuously inhabited settlements, with a Spanish presidio founded in 1775 on Tohono O'odham lands. Mission San Xavier del Bac, adobe barrios, and University of Arizona campuses record how Apache frontier trade, Gadsden Purchase boundaries, and twentieth-century astronomy and defence research shaped southern Arizona's desert city.

San Xavier del Bac Mission

San Xavier del Bac, a Spanish Colonial Catholic mission completed around 1783, is a National Historic Landmark south of Tucson. White twin towers and a carved portal face the desert approach. The active parish still holds services inside the ornate interior.

Gaslight Theatre

Since the 1970s, Tucson's Gaslight Theatre has staged Western-themed musical comedies, sci-fi spoofs, and superhero dramas six nights a week in a family-oriented house. Pizza, popcorn, beer, and wine are available with the shows. The long-running format keeps melodrama and audience participation central.

Reid Park Zoo

Reid Park Zoo, located in Tucson, Arizona, was established in 1965 by Gene Reid. The zoo spans 24 acres and is home to over 30 different species of animals from South America, Asia, and Africa. It is a city-owned non-profit organization funded by state tax dollars and conservation societies. Visitors can see a wide variety of animals such as lemurs, flamingos, otters, bears, tortoises, and anteaters.

Tucson Botanical Gardens

On the historic Porter family property, Tucson Botanical Gardens covers more than five acres with plant collections and educational programs for visitors. Paths and shade structure the desert and specialty gardens. Classes and self-guided walks share the same grounds.

05 AdmissionFree

St. Augustine Cathedral

Saint Augustine Cathedral in Tucson developed from a nineteenth-century Catholic parish, while the present Spanish Colonial Revival building reflects major twentieth-century reconstruction. A twin-towered facade, domed sanctuary, stained glass and religious art support its role as the seat of the local diocese. Masses, funerals, parish work and preservation determine access to the active church.

Tucson Convention Center

Tucson Convention Center (TCC) is a popular venue for various public shows and events, including the Pueblo Show and Arizona Mineral & Fossil Show. The center hosts unique vendors from around the world offering mineral specimens, crystal points, and other goods, it is conveniently located near several top destinations such as the University of Arizona, The Fox Theater, Rialto Theater, Centennial Hall, and Tucson Music Hall.

Fox Tucson Theatre

Located in downtown Tucson, the Fox Tucson Theatre is a historic 1930s-era building that is a performance venue for live music, revues, and theater. The theater's Southwest Art Deco architecture and unique decor make it a standout attraction with eye-catching neon lights and vintage signage. Recently renamed the Linda Ronstadt Music Hall, it pays homage to the city's cultural legacy.

Rialto Theatre

The Rialto Theatre is a historic vaudeville theater that dates back to 1919 and has been restored to its former glory. It now is a venue for nationally touring bands and indie acts, making it a must-visit spot for music lovers. Located in downtown Tucson, the area has seen revitalization with new restaurants, apartments, and art projects like the mural project.

09 AdmissionFree

Purple Tree

Purple Tree sits near Tumamoc Hill, an 860-acre ecological preserve and National Historic Landmark west of downtown Tucson. The Desert Laboratory has worked the hill since 1903, and the land holds meaning for Sobaipuri and Tohono O'odham communities. Nearby cafes and shops serve hikers finishing the paved climb.

Tumamoc Hill Hiking Trail

Tumamoc Hill's paved trail on University of Arizona land climbs a steep road grade open roughly 4 a.m. to 10 p.m. Parking is directed to Anklam Road rather than hospital lots beside the trail base. Summit views take in Tucson's basin, surrounding ranges, and city lights after dusk.
