Atlanta, GA

Atlanta was founded in 1837 as a Western and Atlantic Railroad terminus called Terminus, then rebuilt after Civil War fires into a New South commercial centre. Martin Luther King Jr. Birth home, Centennial Olympic Park, and Peachtree Street office towers record how rail junctions, cotton brokerage, and twentieth-century civil-rights leadership reshaped Georgia's Piedmont hub.

Fox Theatre

Atlanta's Fox Theatre opened in the 1920s from a design originally connected with the Shriners, combining elaborate Islamic, Egyptian and theatrical motifs; the building survives as an active performance venue, so access and atmosphere depend on scheduled events. A starry auditorium, lounges, decorative plaster and historic stage equipment support concerts, films, musicals and tours.
