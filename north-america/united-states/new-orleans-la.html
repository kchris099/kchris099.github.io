Explore New Orleans, LA

A Brief History

New Orleans was founded by the French in 1718 on a Mississippi River crescent above delta swamps, then shaped by Spanish rule, Haitian immigration, and American purchase in 1803. French Quarter balconies, St. Louis Cathedral, and Garden District mansions record how Caribbean trade, Creole culture, and port commerce built a jazz and festival city on filled alluvial land.

Jackson Square

Jackson Square is a historic park in New Orleans, dating back to 1721 and surrounded by significant buildings. It's where the Louisiana Purchase was finalized and home to landmarks like The St Louis Cathedral, The Cabildo, and The Presbytere. Artists, musicians, and fortune tellers gather here, displaying their original artwork along the fence and sidewalks. While not an official market, the art on display rivals gallery pieces.

Free

St. Louis Cathedral

St. Louis Cathedral is a grand church dating back to the 1700s, featuring stained glass and a painted ceiling. In the mid-1800s, Baroness Micaela Almonester de Pontalba oversaw the rebuilding of the cathedral and initiated the construction of Pontalba Buildings in an effort to counteract development across Canal Street. The cathedral's white facade and sleek steeples dominate the square, flanked by historic structures like Cabildo and Presbytere.

Free

Marie Laveau's House Of Voodoo

Marie Laveau's House of Voodoo is a must-visit spot in New Orleans, located on the Bourbon Street in the historic French Quarter. This unique museum and store is situated at the former home of Marie Laveau, the known Voodoo Queen of New Orleans. Since its opening in 1988, it has become a popular destination for both locals and tourists alike.

Free

House of Blues New Orleans

House of Blues New Orleans is a visitor site in New Orleans, LA, United States. The House of Blues Restaurant & Bar in New Orleans is a rock- and blues-themed establishment that offers Southern-inspired dishes and live music performances. Located in the French Quarter, this historic venue provides standing room viewing of a stage with original Mississippi mud beneath it, giving performers a true taste of the South. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Bourbon St

Bourbon St is a visitor site in New Orleans, LA, United States. The street has historical significance, as it was frequented by famous writers like Truman Capote and Tennessee Williams. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Musical Legends Park

Musical Legends Park in New Orleans is a cozy park adorned with statues of jazz legends and hosts live local bands daily. The park, maintained by the New Musical Legends Foundations, Inc, aims to showcase the and musical heritage of New Orleans. It also is an educational venue for students and contributes to economic development and tourism in the area.

Free

Saenger Theatre

Saenger Theatre is a historic landmark known for hosting live musical acts and touring theatrical productions. The theater, which opened in 1927, has a grand Italian courtyard-inspired interior with decorative moldings and an overhead lighting design resembling a starry constellation. Despite its capacity being reduced from 4,000 to 2,600 after restoration following Hurricane Katrina in 2005, it remains theaters in town.

Free

Congo Square

Congo Square, located within Louis Armstrong Park at 828 North Rampart Street in the French Quarter of New Orleans, holds significant historical and cultural importance. In the 18th and 19th centuries, it served as a gathering place for African slaves who held rituals every Sunday evening. This was made possible by Napoleonic law which granted slaves Sundays off and provided them with a space to gather.

Free

Central Grocery and Deli

Central Grocery and Deli is a small Italian market in the French Quarter known for its specialty foods and famous muffuletta sandwiches. The founder, Lupo Salvatore, invented the sandwich in 1906. Despite facing challenges during Hurricane Ida in 2021, the market's legacy lives on through its known muffulettas, which can still be enjoyed at nearby locations.

Free

French Market: Shops of the Colonnade

The Shops of the Colonnade is a, old-fashioned shopping area that showcases local vendors offering a diverse range of products such as candy, clothing, gifts, and souvenirs. In the French Quarter, it provides easy access to nearby attractions like Jackson Square and the French Market. The French Market itself has a rich history dating back to the late 18th century and spans five blocks, featuring an array of local food, art, and retail shopping.

$8

New Orleans Jazz Museum

The New Orleans Jazz Museum, housed in a historic mint dating back to 1835, offers a journey through the city's rich musical legacy. The museum showcases the profound influence of jazz on New Orleans through interactive exhibits and live music performances in its courtyard and balcony. Visitors can explore an extensive collection of jazz instruments and ephemera, immersing themselves in the history of this musical genre.

$45

Lafayette Square

Lafayette Square is a park located in downtown New Orleans, surrounded by various neighborhoods and industries. The square is a popular venue for a variety of events including concert series, inaugurations, weddings, and Mardi Gras parade watching. It also hosts the annual Crescent City Blues and BBQ Festival organized by the New Orleans Jazz and Heritage Festival Foundation.

Free

New Orleans City Park

New Orleans City Park is a public park in New Orleans, LA, United States. Visitors can enjoy activities such as renting swan boats on Big Lake, exploring Storyland with the kids, and experiencing the whimsical Carousel Gardens Amusement Park. Paths, lawns, and shaded areas make up the main public facilities.

Free

Chalmette Battlefield

Chalmette Battlefield is a historic landmark park that offers a glimpse into the 1815 battlefield, with guided tours and annual reenactments. The visitor center provides insights into early 19th-century life through living history volunteers. Visitors can walk in the footsteps of soldiers and climb to the top of the Chalmette Monument for panoramic views of the battleground and Mississippi River.

$30

Audubon Zoo

Located in New Orleans, Audubon Zoo is a known destination that offers a diverse range of animal exhibits designed to replicate their natural habitats. Visitors can observe Amur leopards, orangutans, and rare white alligators among other fascinating creatures. The zoo's Louisiana Swamp exhibit provides an immersive experience with alligators, nutrias, and catfish.
