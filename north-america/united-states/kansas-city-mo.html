Kansas City, MO

The Hannibal Bridge of 1869 locked Kansas City into rail and stockyard primacy at the Missouri bend. Jazz around 18th and Vine, the Liberty Memorial, and Country Club Plaza later layered music, war memory, and planned shopping onto that livestock-and-freight base. Negro Leagues Baseball Museum anchors the same 18th and Vine cultural corridor. American Jazz Museum shares that Vine Street block with the baseball museum.
