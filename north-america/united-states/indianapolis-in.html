Indianapolis, IN

Founded in 1821 as the planned capital of Indiana, Indianapolis grew rapidly due to its central location and the arrival of the railroad in the 1840s. The city became a major manufacturing and transportation hub during the late nineteenth and early twentieth centuries, particularly in the emerging automobile industry. This industrial heritage culminated in the creation of the Indianapolis Motor Speedway in 1909. Today, Indianapolis blends its historic civic architecture and monuments with a modern economy, serving as a cultural and commercial center for the Midwest.

The USS Indianapolis National Memorial honors the crew of the naval ship that was sunk in 1945 by torpedoes.
