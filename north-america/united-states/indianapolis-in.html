Explore Indianapolis, IN

A Brief History

Founded in 1821 as the planned capital of Indiana, Indianapolis grew rapidly due to its central location and the arrival of the railroad in the 1840s. The city became a major manufacturing and transportation hub during the late nineteenth and early twentieth centuries, particularly in the emerging automobile industry. This industrial heritage culminated in the creation of the Indianapolis Motor Speedway in 1909. Today, Indianapolis blends its historic civic architecture and monuments with a modern economy, serving as a cultural and commercial center for the Midwest.

Top Sights & Activities

$25

Indianapolis Motor Speedway

The Indianapolis Motor Speedway, also known as the Brickyard, is a renowned speedway that hosts the Indy 500 race and various other racing events. Visitors can explore the on-site museum, take tours, and even enjoy a round of golf. Additionally, the park is home to Victory Field, where the Indianapolis Indians play during warmer months. Sports enthusiasts can also partake in year-round tours of nearby Lucas Oil Stadium.

$20

Lucas Oil Stadium

Lucas Oil Stadium is a state-of-the-art, retractable-roof stadium located in Indianapolis. It serves as the home of the NFL's Indianapolis Colts and hosts various events including concerts. The stadium has a seating capacity of 67,000 and offers views of the downtown skyline. Visitors can take year-round tours to explore this impressive venue that opened in 2008 and hosted the 2012 Super Bowl.

Free

Victory Field

Victory Field is the home of the Indianapolis Indians, a minor league baseball team affiliated with the Pittsburgh Pirates. The field can host over 14,000 visitors and offers views of the Indianapolis skyline. It features various amenities such as an arcade, bar & grill, picnic area, and fireworks displays during warmer months. Even non-baseball fans are encouraged to visit for family-friendly experiences like the Knot Hole Kids Club and kids programs.

Free

White River State Park

White River State Park, located in the heart of downtown, is a sprawling urban park along the banks of the White River. It features a diverse range of attractions including a zoo, amphitheater, and cultural venues such as museums and theaters. Visitors can enjoy activities like strolling along the riverside trail or exploring the canal walk with options for gondola rides and paddleboat rentals. The park also houses Victory Field, home to minor league baseball's Indianapolis Indians.

$23

Indiana State Museum

The Indiana State Museum is a must-visit attraction in Indianapolis, offering a diverse range of exhibits that cover everything from fossils to pop culture. Located within the scenic White River State Park, the museum is surrounded by other notable attractions such as the NCAA Hall of Champions sports museum and the Eiteljorg Museum. Visitors can also explore nearby landmarks like Victory Field and Lucas Oil Stadium.

$33

Indianapolis Zoo

The Indianapolis Zoo, located in White River State Park, is a popular destination for both locals and tourists. Spanning 64 acres, it houses over 3,800 animals from various species. Visitors can explore a wide range of exhibits featuring rare and endangered creatures such as white rhinos, Amur tigers, Guinea baboons, red pandas, and Komodo dragons. The zoo offers interactive experiences including live feedings and trainer shows that appeal to all ages.

Free (observation elevator $2)

Soldiers & Sailors Monument

Soldiers & Sailors Monument is recorded in the destination register for Indianapolis, United States, alongside publicly accessible spaces, parks, and halls that the city promotes through its visitor programme. The location typically pairs with markets, civic squares, and cultural venues in published tour loops; recurring visitors cite it as a regular inclusion on regional itineraries..

$10

Scottish Rite Cathedral

The Scottish Rite Cathedral in downtown Indianapolis is a architectural marvel that has been an landmark since its completion in 1929. Although not a church, it offers an impressive event space spanning 116,000 square feet and can accommodate up to 2,000 people for cocktail service or seat 650 for dinner. The cathedral's intricate stonework, stained glass windows, and ornate decor make it a popular choice for weddings and special events.

Free

Indianapolis Public Library - Central Library

Nestled just a short walk from the Monument Circle, the Indianapolis Public Library - Central Library is a architectural marvel that beautifully blends history and modernity. Opened in 1917 and designed by renowned architect Paul Philippe Cret, this Beaux-Arts-style building features striking Greek Doric elements, complete with grand staircases and intricate details that radiate elegance.

Free

USS Indianapolis National Memorial

The USS Indianapolis National Memorial honors the crew of the naval ship that was sunk in 1945 by torpedoes. The three hundred seventeen survivors of the crew visit the memorial to remember their comrades who lost their lives. The memorial is built in the shape of a cruise ship and is made out of grey and black marble.
