Boise, ID

Oregon Trail traffic paused at a tree-lined river crossing that French-speakers called Boisé. Capital status after 1890, Basque boarding houses, and Boise River irrigation projects shaped a high-desert administrative city between Rockies and Snake River plain. The Basque Block restaurants and museum recognise a diaspora drawn by sheep work. The Idaho State Capitol's sandstone dome anchors the downtown mall axis.

Idaho Anne Frank Human Rights Memorial

Quotations and contemplative space at the Idaho Anne Frank Human Rights Memorial in Boise honor Anne Frank and wider human-rights struggles. Panels invite reflection on personal responsibility. The open plaza setting keeps the memorial accessible from downtown sidewalks.

Morrison Center

Located on the banks of the Boise River near downtown Boise, the Morrison Center is a well equipped performing arts facility that greatly enriches the cultural scene of Boise and Idaho. It houses a 2,000-seat performance hall use by various groups including Boise State departments, touring acts, and local organizations like the Boise Opera and the Boise Philharmonic.

Zoo Boise

Zoo Boise houses more than 200 animals from about 83 species inside Julia Davis Park. Exhibits include an African boat ride area, a kids' farm, and a seasonal butterfly space supported by Friends of Zoo Boise. Programs lean toward conservation education rather than thrill rides, with keepers often on hand during weekend events.

Julia Davis Park

Julia Davis Park extends along the Boise River on land donated by the Davis family. Lawns and mature trees provide the main recreational landscape, with paths connecting rose gardens and memorials. Several museums and the city zoo occupy distinct sites inside the park. Festivals and river access add further uses, linking everyday recreation with Boise's civic and cultural institutions.

Albertsons Stadium

Albertsons Stadium on the Boise State University campus is a football bowl with published seating near 36,000. Blue artificial turf and the surrounding foothills give the ground a distinctive look for Broncos games and occasional other events. Entry follows posted ticket and gate procedures on event days.

MK Nature Center - Idaho Fish and Game

The MK Nature Center follows a stream and wetland corridor beside the Boise River, operated by Idaho Fish and Game for environmental education. Underwater windows, interpretive paths, native plants, fish habitat, and wildlife observation introduce local ecosystems in a compact setting; seasonal water levels and animal movement change what is visible, while the center is distinct from a zoo or hatchery attraction.

Table Rock

Table Rock rises above Boise on a sandstone plateau crossed by trails and roads. Communication structures and a large illuminated cross occupy the summit, which opens broad views across the city and Snake River Plain. The approach passes exposed foothill habitat with limited shade, while heat, dust, erosion and wildfire closures influence route conditions.

Old Idaho Penitentiary Site

Stone cellblocks of the Old Idaho Penitentiary held inmates from 1872 until the prison closed in 1973. Among those housed here were Harry Orchard and Lyda Southard, names still tied to Idaho criminal history. Self-guided routes now pass yards, solitary cells, and museum rooms that document daily prison life without soft-pedaling the site's severity.

Aquarium of Boise

More than 250 aquatic species fill tanks at the Aquarium of Boise on the city's west side. A shark and ray pool is a frequent stop, with scheduled feedings that draw families to the glass. Galleries mix freshwater Idaho species with tropical saltwater displays in a compact, one-building layout.

Boise Idaho Temple

Near Boise's interstate corridor, the Boise Idaho Temple is an active place of worship for the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints, with light-colored walls, a tower, and landscaped grounds. After dedication, interior access is reserved for eligible church members except during rare open houses.

Hyde Park

Historic storefronts now contain restaurants, cafes, shops, and services surrounded by residential streets and access toward Camel's Back Park. Hyde Park forms a small commercial district along North 13th Street in Boise's North End. Businesses and events change frequently, so the area is better described as a neighborhood center than a fixed attraction with a guaranteed tenant list.

The Peregrine Fund's World Center For Birds of Prey

The Peregrine Fund's World Center for Birds of Prey combines conservation offices, breeding work, archives and a public interpretive center south of Boise. Exhibits and scheduled presentations explain raptors and international recovery programs, while some birds may be seen in managed settings. Research needs, animal welfare, weather and program schedules determine each visit.

Idaho Shakespeare Festival

Since 1977 the Idaho Shakespeare Festival has staged outdoor productions on Boise's east edge. Seasons mix classic plays and musicals with newer works, including pieces by BIPOC playwrights. The amphitheater setting puts audiences under open sky, and training programs feed aspiring actors into the company.
