Spokane, WA

Spokane prospered after the Northern Pacific Railway arrived in 1881, turning Spokane Falls lumber mills and mines into an Inland Empire wholesale centre. Riverfront Park expo grounds, Davenport Hotel brick, and Mission Revival churches document how Washington's eastern wheat belt, hydroelectric power, and Expo 74 revitalization reshaped a Palouse trade city.

Avista Stadium

Avista Stadium hosts Spokane's minor-league baseball team in a mid-twentieth-century ballpark east of the city center. A covered grandstand, open seating, field, concessions and event facilities support seasonal games and community programs. Team affiliations, sponsorship, promotions, security and public access change with the schedule, while the grounds are not ordinarily open as a daytime attraction.

Garland Theater

The Garland Theater, a historic movie house that opened in 1945, continues to entertain the community with its classic and modern film screenings. Over the years, it has undergone various upgrades to maintain a modern image, including the introduction of widescreen technology and new seating. Despite changing ownership and facing competition from larger cineplexes, the theater remains a beloved venue for families looking to enjoy affordable movie nights.

03 AdmissionFree

The Fox Theater

Spokane's Fox Theater opened in 1931 as an Art Deco movie palace and later reopened after restoration as the Martin Woldson Theater at the Fox. Murals, decorative plaster, balconies and a renewed auditorium preserve its theatrical character. The building now hosts orchestral concerts, touring performers and community events, while regular film screenings no longer define its program.

47.6570° N / 117.4270° W

04 AdmissionFree

Bing Crosby Theater

The Bing Crosby Theater, originally built in 1915 as a silent film movie house, has evolved into an elegant venue with a retro flair. Renamed after Bing Crosby's performance there in the 1920s, it now hosts a rich variety of live music, comedy shows, and other performances. With three renovations over its 102-year history, this 744-seat theater offers an immersive experience for attendees.

05 AdmissionFree

Cathedral of Our Lady of Lourdes

The Cathedral of Our Lady of Lourdes stands in downtown Spokane as the seat of the local Roman Catholic diocese. The present early twentieth-century church uses Romanesque Revival forms, twin towers, stained glass and a long sanctuary shaped by later renovations. Worship, funerals, diocesan ceremonies and parish work guide public access to the active cathedral.

06 AdmissionFree

Manito Park

Opened in 1904 on about 78 acres (with related grounds often cited near 90), Manito Park in Spokane holds an arboretum, conservatory, and botanical gardens; the name draws from Algonquin manitou, meaning a life force. Paths and lawns organize the formal and informal sections. Horticultural displays define the park's identity.

47.6394° N / 117.4094° W

Duncan Garden

Duncan Garden's European Renaissance layout sits inside Manito Park among four other distinct garden rooms that together support Spokane's horticultural reputation. Formal beds and paths fill the Renaissance section. Seasonal plantings change the color blocks through the year.

Nishinomiya Tsutakawa Japanese Garden

The Nishinomiya Tsutakawa Japanese Garden inside Spokane's Manito Park honors the city's sister-city link with Nishinomiya, Japan. Stone lanterns, a pond, and carefully pruned plantings occupy a quiet corner of the larger park. Paths loop the water garden for a short, enclosed walk.

First Interstate Center for the Arts

Beside Spokane's riverfront convention district, First Interstate Center for the Arts stages touring theater, music, comedy, dance and civic programs. Its auditorium, stage, lobby and technical spaces support productions of different scales within an event-based venue.

Riverfront Park

Riverfront Park occupies about one hundred acres on the Spokane River where Expo '74, the 1974 World's Fair, once stood. Falls overlooks, a skyride, and open lawns mix natural river gorge with downtown recreation. Paths follow the islands and shore for picnics and everyday walking.
