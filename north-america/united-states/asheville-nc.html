Asheville, NC

Asheville grew as a railroad and resort town in the Blue Ridge after the late 19th century, drawing tuberculosis patients and wealthy seasonal residents. The Biltmore Estate, completed in 1895 for the Vanderbilt family, set a lasting pattern of architecture and forestry patronage. Art Deco downtown buildings and later craft industries followed. Mountain views, brewery districts, and the estate still frame western North Carolina's largest city.

Biltmore

Biltmore, an French Renaissance château built by George W. Vanderbilt, has 250 rooms and lavish gardens that replicate a working French estate. The estate is a popular attraction, drawing in around 3.8 million visitors annually including well-known figures like Edith Wharton and several U.S. Presidents.

Biltmore Estate

Biltmore Estate was created in the late nineteenth century for George Vanderbilt, with a large chateau-style house, gardens by Frederick Law Olmsted, working estate land and extensive collections. Furnished rooms, service areas, architecture and landscape reveal great wealth and labor. The privately operated property now includes hospitality and retail businesses, with timed entry and separate activities.

Western North Carolina Nature Center

Western North Carolina Nature Center focuses on animals and habitats of the Southern Appalachian region. Wooded paths connect enclosures for native or formerly native mammals, birds and reptiles. Education and conservation programs support interpretation of the collection. Animal welfare and changing species shape the facility, while surrounding mountain habitats provide its wider ecological context.

More than 60 acres of cultivated gardens at the North Carolina Arboretum include major bonsai holdings and ericaceous plantings suited to acid mountain soils. Blue Ridge slopes surround the Asheville-area grounds. Paths weave between themed garden rooms across the campus.

Mast General Store Asheville

Located Asheville, Mast General Store is a historic retailer that has been providing a wide array of goods since 1883. With its exterior and extensive selection, it has become a popular tourist destination. The store offers an range of products, including food, apparel, outdoor gear, and sustainable items from local makers like Eagle Nest Outfitters (ENO). Visitors can find everything from retro toys to hand-crafted rocking chairs and barrels of candy.

The Orange Peel

The Orange Peel is an Asheville music hall with a mid-century past as a Black soul and R&B club, reopened in 2002 as a major live venue seating more than a thousand. Local and national acts fill a standing-room floor and balcony. The club anchors the city's contemporary music reputation in a downtown warehouse district.

Basilica of Saint Lawrence, Deacon and Martyr

The Basilica of Saint Lawrence was completed in the early twentieth century for Asheville's Catholic community, designed by Rafael Guastavino using tile-vault construction. A broad dome, brick exterior, stained glass, religious art, and a compact nave distinguish the building. It is an active parish and minor basilica, so services and conservation needs guide public access.
