Explore Asheville, NC

A Brief History

Asheville, NC is in United States. Surrounded by the Blue Ridge Mountains, Asheville grew rapidly in the late 19th century following the arrival of the railroad. The massive construction of the Biltmore Estate established an long-running legacy of opulent architecture and arts patronage that still defines the city. The survival of these cultural and architectural milestones provides a powerful window into the region's storied past.

Top Sights & Activities

$80

Biltmore

Biltmore, an French Renaissance château built by George W. Vanderbilt, has 250 rooms and lavish gardens that replicate a working French estate. The estate is a popular attraction, drawing in around 3.8 million visitors annually including well-known figures like Edith Wharton and several U.S. Presidents.

$80

Biltmore Estate

The Biltmore Estate in Asheville is the largest privately owned home in the United States, built by George Washington Vanderbilt II in the late 19th century. The massive, 250-room French Renaissance chateau is surrounded by 8,000 acres of careful landscaped gardens designed by Frederick Law Olmsted, offering a glimpse into Gilded Age opulence.

Free

WNC Farmers Market

The WNC Farmers Market is a year-round, daily market featuring indoor and covered stalls that showcase local produce, crafts, and plants. The market operates from 8 AM to 5 PM with seasonal variations in hours. Located in Truck Shed No. 1, it is dedicated to farmers who sell only what they grow.

$14

Western North Carolina Nature Center

Western North Carolina Nature Center is a visitor site in Asheville, NC, United States. This zoological park invites visitors to immerse themselves in the rich biodiversity of the area, featuring fascinating creatures like black bears, red wolves, river otters, and even red pandas. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Free

Blue Ridge Parkway Visitor Center

Blue Ridge Parkway Visitor Center is a public park in Asheville, NC, United States. The Blue Ridge Parkway Visitor Center is a must-visit destination for those exploring the scenic byway. Located in Asheville, it offers a chance to look at the history and natural beauty of the parkway. Paths, lawns, and shaded areas make up the main public facilities.

$25

The North Carolina Arboretum

The North Carolina Arboretum is a public garden located in the Blue Ridge Mountains near the Western North Carolina town of Asheville. The arboretum contains more than 60 acres of carefully cultivated gardens, including extensive bonsai collections in the United States. The gardens also showcase a variety of flora, including extensive ericaceous (acid soil-loving) plant collections in the United States.

Free

Pack Square Park

Pack Square Park is a public park in Asheville, NC, United States. Pack Square Park is a active community green space in Asheville, popular for hosting festivals and events. One of the highlights is the free outdoor concert series called Shindig on the Green, Splashville, a fountain area that doubles as a splash pad for kids, provides entertainment for families during live music events. Paths, lawns, and shaded areas make up the main public facilities.

Free

French Broad Chocolate Lounge

French Broad Chocolate Lounge is known for its bean to bar chocolate, ensuring high-quality and flavorful ingredients sourced ethically from farmers. The lounge offers a variety of treats including cakes, cookies, and ice cream made with grass-fed milk and cage-free eggs. Its new location on Pack Square provides a comfortable space to enjoy premium chocolates, ice cream, hot and cold chocolate drinks, as well as specialty coffees and teas.

Free

Mast General Store Asheville

Located Asheville, Mast General Store is a historic retailer that has been providing a wide array of goods since 1883. With its exterior and extensive selection, it has become a popular tourist destination. The store offers an range of products, including food, apparel, outdoor gear, and sustainable items from local makers like Eagle Nest Outfitters (ENO). Visitors can find everything from retro toys to hand-crafted rocking chairs and barrels of candy.

$25

LaZoom Tours

LaZoom Tours is a visitor site in Asheville, NC, United States. LaZoom Tours offers very popular, comedic bus tours of Asheville, North Carolina. The tours are a staple of Asheville's active, eccentric entertainment scene. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Free

The Orange Peel

The Orange Peel is a visitor site in Asheville, NC, United States. Records associated with the site include 2002. It has a rich history, once serving as a famous black soul and RnB club in the 60s and later revitalized in 2002 to become a vital part of the city's musical heritage. With a capacity for over 1,000 people, it hosts various acts from local favorites to national performers. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Free

Basilica of Saint Lawrence, Deacon and Martyr

Basilica of Saint Lawrence, Deacon and Martyr is a religious building in Asheville, NC, United States. It opened or was completed around 1905. The Basilica of Saint Lawrence, Deacon and Martyr is a must-visit in downtown Asheville, North Carolina. This minor basilica showcases and unique architecture designed by a Spanish architect. The exterior and main interior spaces are usually viewable outside service times.

Free

Hole Doughnuts

Hole Doughnuts is a popular spot known for its freshly made donuts using local ingredients, along with coffee and orange juice. Despite its small size, the shop offers a variety of creative flavors such as Saffron Almond, Salt and Peppercorn Maple, and Bourbon Milk Punch. The menu is simple, featuring four doughnut flavors including a weekly seasonal option.
