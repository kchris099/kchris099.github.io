Austin, TX

Austin became the Republic of Texas capital in 1839 on a Colorado River site chosen for central location and hilltop views. Limestone capitol dome, university campus oak groves, and Congress Avenue warehouse districts record how state government, University of Texas expansion, and late-twentieth-century technology firms shaped a Hill Country administrative city.

Zilker Botanical Garden

Zilker Botanical Garden occupies a varied hillside within Austin's Zilker Metropolitan Park, and heat, drought, construction and events influence the landscape, while the garden's collections reflect civic horticulture. Themed areas include native and adapted plants, ponds, paths, a Japanese garden, a prehistoric-themed section and seasonal displays.

Zilker Metropolitan Park

Land donor naming marks 351-acre Zilker Metropolitan Park, with soccer fields, a nine-hole golf course, and wide lawns along Austin's Lady Bird Lake edge. Sports and informal recreation share the municipal green. Paths link the active fields to quieter grass areas.

Barton Springs Pool

Within Zilker Park, Barton Springs Pool draws water from the Edwards Aquifer into a spring-fed municipal swimming area. Stone edges, lawns, diving areas and a long natural-bottom pool support recreation alongside habitat for protected salamanders; it is located within Zilker Metropolitan Park, which also houses other attractions like the Zilker Botanical Gardens and the UMLAUF Sculpture Garden & Museum.

Texas Capitol

Granite and marble walls enclose the Texas Capitol, finished in 1888 with a dome and rotunda above House and Senate chambers. The working legislature still meets here, so galleries and corridors stay open around session schedules. Across the street, the Bullock Texas State History Museum adds galleries on state politics and settlement for anyone continuing from the grounds.

Saint Mary Catholic Cathedral

Once called St. Patrick's, Saint Mary Catholic Cathedral grew into the Gothic Revival seat of the Diocese of Austin, with large stained-glass windows lighting the nave. The parish anchors Catholic life for a wide regional congregation. Outside Mass the exterior and main interior are usually viewable.

The Paramount Theatre

Austin's Paramount Theatre opened in 1915 and remains a downtown venue for films, concerts, comedy, talks and live performance. An ornate auditorium, balcony, stage, marquee and restored decorative details reflect its vaudeville and cinema history; interior access normally follows a scheduled event or tour, while programming changes across festivals and seasons.

UT Tower

Completed in 1937, the UT Tower rises 307 feet over the University of Texas at Austin Main Mall. Its limestone shaft and clock faces mark the campus from blocks away and still frame graduation photos each spring. Public observation openings are limited, yet the tower remains the clearest vertical landmark on the Forty Acres.

BookPeople

BookPeople is Austin's large independent bookstore, with regular author readings, a coffee bar, and a crowded gift section. A kids' zone includes a small amphitheater used for story times and summer contests. Shelves mix new releases with Texas titles, and the store has long ranked as a local favorite among capital-city readers.

Austin Central Library, Austin Public Library

Austin Central Library overlooks Shoal Creek near Lady Bird Lake in a contemporary building designed for public learning and civic use. Reading rooms, collections, technology spaces, children's areas, art and terraces support more than book borrowing, while a rooftop garden opens the upper level to the city. The open interior and environmental systems respond to Austin's climate and encourage movement between study, events and public gathering.

Stevie Ray Vaughan Statue

The Stevie Ray Vaughan Statue in Austin, Texas is a larger-than-life bronze sculpture by Ralph Helmick, depicting the legendary guitarist in his hat and poncho, holding a guitar. The statue was commissioned by the Stevie Ray Vaughan Memorial Fund and installed at Vic Mathias Shores in 1993, where the musician had performed numerous concerts. It is a tribute to Vaughan's musical legacy and his impact on Austin's cultural scene.

Peter Pan Mini-Golf

Family-run since 1948, Peter Pan Mini-Golf occupies a South Austin lot packed with oversized figures. Courses wind past Peter Pan, Tinkerbell, a T-Rex, and a giant Converse-style boot painted in bright colors. Evening play under string lights is common, and the kitschy sculptures matter as much as the putt-putt score.

Mt Bonnell

Mount Bonnell is a limestone overlook above the Colorado River in west Austin, reached by a short staircase from the roadside. The ridge opens views toward Lake Austin, hills, homes and the city, while paths extend over uneven rock. Heat, limited parking, steep edges, erosion and crowding matter.

The Boardwalk at Lady Bird Lake

The Boardwalk at Lady Bird Lake is a 7,250-ft. Boardwalk that offers views of the shoreline, city skyline, and even turtles. It's part of the 10-mile Ann and Roy Butler Hike-and-Bike Trail, also known as the Lady Bird Lake trail, which is popular among locals and visitors for walking, running, and cycling.

Allens Boots

On South Congress since the 1970s, Allens Boots stocks more than 10,000 pairs from a dozen makers inside about 8,000 square feet. Liberty Black fringe styles, embroidered Old Gringo boots, and Texas-made Lucchese classics share the shelves. The store remains a South Congress retail fixture.
