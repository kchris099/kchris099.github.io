Explore Austin, TX

A Brief History

Austin became the Republic of Texas capital in 1839 on a Colorado River site chosen for central location and hilltop views. limestone capitol dome, university campus oak groves, and Congress Avenue warehouse districts record how state government, University of Texas expansion, and late-twentieth-century technology firms shaped a Hill Country administrative city.

Top Sights & Activities

$8

Zilker Botanical Garden

Zilker Botanical Garden is a 31-acre oasis within Zilker Park, offering a variety of attractions such as ponds, trails, and educational programs for kids. The garden features a herb garden, an Oriental Garden, and a butterfly garden that is visited from April to October to see migrating butterflies. Additionally, the Hartman Prehistoric Garden showcases plants from the Cretaceous period along with dinosaur tracks and a bronze sculpture of an Ornithomimus.

Free

Zilker Metropolitan Park

Zilker Metropolitan Park, a 351-acre urban park in Austin, Texas, is a popular destination named after the generous donor of its land. The park features the Barton Springs Pool and bathhouse, as well as attractions like the Zilker Botanical Gardens and Umlauf Sculpture Garden and Museum. It offers various sports facilities such as soccer fields and a nine-hole golf course.

$5

Barton Springs Pool

Barton Springs Municipal Pool is a renowned natural spring area and a popular hangout spot in Austin. It is located within Zilker Metropolitan Park, which also houses other attractions like the Zilker Botanical Gardens and the UMLAUF Sculpture Garden & Museum. The pool, with its 68-degree waters, attracts locals and tourists alike for swimming and sunbathing.

Free

Texas Capitol

The Texas Capitol, a 1888 granite and marble structure, boasts a remarkable rotunda and dome along with House and Senate chambers. Adjacent to this historic site is the Bullock Texas State History Museum, named after Lieutenant Governor Bob Bullock. The museum offers interactive exhibits across three main themes: the founding of Texas, the state's history and name origins, and opportunities within the state.

Free

Saint Mary Catholic Cathedral

Saint Mary Catholic Cathedral is a 19th-century church in Austin, Texas. Originally named St. Patrick's, it was later renamed Saint Mary's and underwent significant construction to become the magnificent cathedral it is today. The Gothic Revival architecture and large stained glass windows make it a sight to behold. As the cathedral parish of the Catholic Diocese of Austin, it serves over 450,000 worshippers in the area.

Free

The Paramount Theatre

The Paramount Theatre in downtown Austin is a historic venue that hosts a variety of events including film festivals, comedy shows, drama performances, dance recitals, concerts and parties. The theater has welcomed numerous renowned artists over the past century and continues to embrace its vaudeville roots by regularly featuring stand-up comedians.

$6

UT Tower

The UT Tower, a historic landmark on the University of Texas at Austin campus, stands tall at 307 feet and was completed in 1937. It serves as a symbol of academic excellence and personal opportunity for the university. The tower features various lighting configurations that reflect different accomplishments and events throughout the school year.

Free

BookPeople

BookPeople is a large, locally owned bookstore in Texas that offers regular readings, a coffee bar, and quirky gift items for sale. The three-floor store features a cozy cafe and unique Keep-Austin-Weird decor. It has been consistently voted as the bookstore in the capital city. The kid's area is a highlight with its colorful mini-amphitheater for story times and summer competitions.

Free

Austin Central Library, Austin Public Library

The Austin Central Library, also known as the Austin Public Library, is a modern architectural marvel designed by Lake Flato. This six-floor library offers a community-oriented environment with gravity-defying bridges and staircases connecting its various levels. Visitors can enjoy reading rooms, an outdoor rooftop lounge, and a dedicated kids' wing featuring a wide range of books and interactive activities.

Free

Stevie Ray Vaughan Statue

The Stevie Ray Vaughan Statue in Austin, Texas is a larger-than-life bronze sculpture by Ralph Helmick, depicting the legendary guitarist in his hat and poncho, holding a guitar. The statue was commissioned by the Stevie Ray Vaughan Memorial Fund and installed at Vic Mathias Shores in 1993, where the musician had performed numerous concerts. It stands as a tribute to Vaughan's musical legacy and his impact on Austin's cultural scene.

$10

Peter Pan Mini-Golf

Peter Pan Mini-Golf is a family-owned establishment that has been operating since 1948 in South Austin. The two 18-hole courses feature challenges such as the Giant Tyrannosaurus Rex, the Whale, the Old Mill, and more. The place is known for its quirky and almost psychedelic models of Peter Pan, T-Rex, Tinkerbell, a giant Converse-style boot, and other whimsical characters.

Free

Mt Bonnell

Mt Bonnell anchors a familiar sightseeing stop in Austin, TX. Visitors encounter it along the city’s central routes through United States, often combining the visit with nearby parks, museums, and historic streets on the same itinerary. Street-level access and clear signage make it easy to reach on foot or by public transit from downtown districts.

Free

The Boardwalk at Lady Bird Lake

The Boardwalk at Lady Bird Lake is a 7,250-ft. boardwalk that offers views of the shoreline, city skyline, and even turtles. It's part of the 10-mile Ann and Roy Butler Hike-and-Bike Trail, also known as the Lady Bird Lake trail, which is popular among locals and visitors for walking, running, and cycling.

Free

Allens Boots

Allens Boots is a must-visit landmark in Austin, Texas, located on South Congress since the 1970s. This store offers the largest selection of cowboy boots in the country, with over 10,000 pairs from 12 different manufacturers. The 8,000-square-foot space showcases a variety of styles including vintage-style Liberty Blacks with fringe, intricately embroidered Old Gringos, and classic Luccheses made in Texas since 1883.
