Explore Great Smoky Mountains, NC

A Brief History

The Great Smoky Mountains were home to the Cherokee for millennia before European-American settlers arrived in the late eighteenth century, establishing isolated Appalachian farming and logging communities. Intensive commercial logging in the early twentieth century devastated the forests, sparking a grassroots conservation movement that culminated in the establishment of the national park in 1934. The park preserved both the recovering biodiversity and the region's pioneer heritage. Visitors experience this Appalachian history at the historic cabins, gristmills, and churches in Cades Cove and Cataloochee.

Top Sights & Activities

Free (parking tag $5)

Great Smoky Mountains National Park

A national park known for its biodiversity, scenic mountain views, hiking trails, and historic buildings. It remains a notable local point of interest situated in Great Smoky Mountains, NC, United States. Visitors can observe the surrounding area and its connection to the city's broader historical landscape. Local guides sometimes include it on walking routes through the centre.

$45

Ripley's Aquarium of the Smokies

Ripley's Aquarium of the Smokies is a vast marine facility in Gatlinburg, open 365 days a year. It houses over 10,000 marine creatures, including sharks, sea turtles, stingrays, and penguins. The aquarium features interactive exhibits like the Discovery Center for hands-on experiences with animals. Visitors can also learn about the aquarium's Systems Control area that manages millions of gallons of saltwater. Additionally, it offers a glass-bottom boat ride experience.

$40

Anakeesta

Anakeesta is a popular outdoor adventure destination that offers various exciting activities such as zip lining, gondola rides, and a treetop play area. It stands out among other nearby mountain attractions with its polished and flashy features. Visitors can choose between an enclosed gondola or a chairlift to reach the ridgetop village, which boasts hanging bridges, mountain coasters, dueling zip lines, and an observation tower at the summit.

Free

Ben Morton Overlook

Ben Morton Overlook, located in the Great Smoky Mountains National Park, is a popular roadside stop along Route 441 that offers sunset views. The westward-facing vantage point provides panoramas of the forested canyon and skyline ridges. This spot is particularly renowned for its picturesque sunset views during late spring through high summer. It's an ideal location for capturing sunset photography and offers a less congested alternative to other nearby pullouts.

Free

Roaring Fork Motor Nature Trail

The Roaring Fork Motor Nature Trail is a must-visit scenic drive nestled within the Great Smoky Mountains National Park. This enchanting 5.5-mile loop takes you through lush old-growth forests, where towering trees and vibrant wildlife abound. As you navigate this picturesque route, encounter charming log cabins that whisper tales of the past, alongside the soothing sounds of the Roaring Fork stream cascading nearby.

Free

Grotto Falls

Nestled in the Great Smoky Mountains, Grotto Falls is a must-visit destination for nature lovers and photography enthusiasts alike. This enchanting 25-foot waterfall cascades down a rocky ledge into a serene pool, creating an idyllic backdrop for photos. The journey to Grotto Falls involves a moderate 3-mile roundtrip hike along the Trillium Gap Trail, which meanders through lush forests and offers glimpses of the natural beauty surrounding you.

Free

Great Smoky Mountains

The Great Smoky Mountains, spanning North Carolina and Tennessee, are a renowned national park with 187,000 acres of forested peaks. This UNESCO World Heritage Site is home to diverse flora and fauna, including black bears and salamanders. With over 850 miles of trails and waterfalls, it's a popular destination for families and nature enthusiasts. Gatlinburg, nestled in the mountains, offers outdoor activities year-round from hiking in May to skiing in winter.

Free

Gatlinburg Scenic Overlook

The Gatlinburg Scenic Overlook is a must-visit spot on the Gatlinburg Bypass, offering panoramic views of the city and the surrounding Great Smoky Mountains. It's a popular destination for photographers and nature enthusiasts, providing opportunities to capture sunsets and potentially spot wildlife like bears and wild turkeys. Visitors can take advantage of this pull-over location to snap selfies with the majestic mountain backdrop or simply soak in the picturesque scenery.

Free

Kuwohi Observation Deck

Clingmans Dome is the highest peak in the Great Smoky Mountains, standing at 6,643 feet. At its summit sits an observation tower built in 1959 and recently updated by the National Park Service. On clear days, visitors can enjoy views stretching up to 100 miles, although haze often limits visibility to around 20 miles. This landmark offers a relatively easy walk to reach the top without muddy hiking involved.

$40

Gatlinburg SkyPark

Gatlinburg SkyPark offers an open-air chairlift ride to an 1,800-ft. overlook with views of the Smoky Mountains. It features the longest pedestrian cable bridge in North America, known as the Gatlinburg SkyBridge, offering views of downtown Gatlinburg and the Great Smoky Mountains. The park also includes the newly opened Gatlinburg SkyTrail with Tulip Tower, providing ADA accessibility along its Boardwalk section.

$18

Gatlinburg Space Needle

Ascend to the top of the Gatlinburg Space Needle, a 407-ft. observation tower that has been an part of the city since 1969. The two glass elevators provide a 360-degree view as you ride up to the outdoor viewing deck, where take in panoramic vistas of Gatlinburg and the surrounding Smoky Mountains. In addition to its views, visitors can also enjoy a theater and an arcade at the top.

$30

Mysterious Mansion

Mysterious Mansion is a Victorian-style haunted house in Gatlinburg that offers self-guided tours and spooky surprises all year round. This classic turn-of-the-century mansion has been terrifying tourists for over 40 years, with regular shows in October and special Halloween performances on weekends. Visitors can explore secret passageways, dark corridors, winding staircases, and chilling chambers filled with zombies and other frights.

$20

Hillbilly Golf

Hillbilly Golf is a unique and miniature golf destination in Gatlinburg, offering two Appalachian-themed 18-hole courses situated on the side of a mountain. Visitors begin their adventure with an incline trolley ride 300 feet up to the top, where they can choose from the two courses. As players make their way down the mountainside, they encounter amusing obstacles such as moonshine stills, outhouses, covered wagons, and other elements reflecting mountain folk life.

$20

Moonshine Mountain Coaster

Nestled in the forest terrain, Moonshine Mountain Coaster offers an exhilarating experience with individual cars equipped with brakes and the option for a night ride. Located conveniently on the Parkway, it's just a short distance from downtown Gatlinburg. Formerly known as the Gatlinburg Mountain Coaster, this attraction was renamed in 2021 after being purchased. The coaster provides rain shields to keep riders dry even during rainy weather.

Free

Gatlinburg Trail Trailhead

Nestled in the heart of the Smoky Mountains, the Gatlinburg Trail Trailhead offers an enchanting escape for nature lovers and their furry companions. This scenic 3.8-mile roundtrip path meanders along the West Prong of the Little River, providing river views and a chance to cross one of the park's longest footbridges. The trail is well-maintained with minimal elevation gain, making it accessible for all hikers.

Free

Sugarlands Visitor Center

The Sugarlands Visitor Center serves as the main entry point to the Great Smoky Mountains National Park and is located just outside Gatlinburg, TN. It provides access to the park, along with valuable information, maps, campsite reservations, a wildlife museum, and a video about the park's creation. Additionally, visitors can interact with Park Rangers and participate in a program designed to engage kids in various activities that culminate in earning a junior Ranger badge.

Free

Cataract Falls

Nestled within the landscape of a mountainous national park, Cataract Falls is a 25-foot waterfall that beckons nature lovers and casual hikers alike. Just under two miles from downtown Gatlinburg, the Cataract Falls Trail offers an easy roundtrip hike of about 3/4 miles, making it perfect for families with children or those who prefer shorter walks.
