Great Smoky Mountains, NC

The Great Smoky Mountains were home to the Cherokee for millennia before European-American settlers arrived in the late eighteenth century, establishing isolated Appalachian farming and logging communities. Intensive commercial logging in the early twentieth century devastated the forests, sparking a grassroots conservation movement that culminated in the establishment of the national park in 1934. The park preserved both the recovering biodiversity and the region's pioneer heritage. Visitors experience this Appalachian history at the historic cabins, gristmills, and churches in Cades Cove and Cataloochee.
