Explore Charleston, SC

A Brief History

Charleston, SC lies in United States. Founded in 1670, Charleston amassed extensive colonial wealth through plantation agriculture fueled by enslaved labor. Surviving sieges, the Civil War, and severe earthquakes, its careful preserved antebellum architecture reflects a complex, significantly layered history of the American South. Visitors today can clearly trace the detailed layers of this unique heritage throughout the preserved streets and monuments.

Top Sights & Activities

Charleston City Market

Charleston City Market is a historic 4-block market that has been around for over 200 years. It's a hub for local artisans selling sweetgrass baskets, leather goods, art, and more. The market also offers a variety of food options and fresh farm-to-table produce. The architectural significance of the buildings adds to the charm of this cultural hotspot. Visitors can explore the area and indulge in local cuisine at nearby eateries like Butcher and Bee or Home Team BBQ.

$39

Bulldog Tours

Bulldog Tours is a prominent tour operator in Charleston, South Carolina, specializing in walking tours that explore the city's rich history, culinary scene, and famous ghost stories. Their guided experiences often include exclusive access to historic sites, such as the Old City Jail and pre-Revolutionary dungeons, providing an immersive look at Charleston's past.

Free

Rainbow Row

Rainbow Row is a collection of 13 active rowhouses in Charleston's Historic District, dating back to the 18th and 19th centuries. These pastel-painted Georgian-style buildings are a popular tourist attraction, offering a glimpse into the city's rich history. Visitors can join guided tours that explore the LGBTQ+ history of the area, with proceeds supporting the College of Charleston's SC LGBTQ Archive. Alternatively, self-guided tours using an online map are also available.

Free

Pineapple Fountain

Pineapple Fountain is a visitor site in Charleston, SC, United States. The Pineapple Fountain is the well known centerpiece of the Waterfront Park in Charleston. It is a very photographed landmark, offering a scenic spot for visitors to cool off and enjoy views of the Charleston Harbor. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Free

Waterfront Park

Waterfront Park, also known as Joe Riley Waterfront Park, is a public park in Charleston, SC, United States. Waterfront Park, also known as Joe Riley Waterfront Park, is a popular 12-acre open space in Charleston offering views of the harbor and the Cooper River. It is known for Pineapple Fountain and is a gathering place for residents and visitors to enjoy the scenery of Charleston Harbor and the Arthur Ravenel Jr. Paths, lawns, and shaded areas make up the main public facilities.

$20

McLeod Plantation Historic Site

Located on Johns Island, the McLeod Plantation Historic Site is a well-preserved historical plantation featuring a Georgian-style mansion, slave cabins, and interpretive tours. Established in 1851 with funds from sea island cotton and the labor of enslaved individuals, it now is a 37-acre Gullah/Geechee heritage site. Visitors can explore the McLeod family home, slave dwellings, and learn about the plantation's significance during the Civil War.

Free

Fort Sumter and Fort Moultrie National Historical Park

Fort Sumter and Fort Moultrie National Historical Park is a pair of historic military sites with origins dating back to 1776. The park offers views of Charleston Harbor. At Fort Moultrie, visitors can see a model of the original palmetto log and sand fort that defended Charleston from British invasion in 1776.

Free

Sullivan's Island Lighthouse

Sullivan's Island Lighthouse, located in the Fort Moultrie unit of Fort Sumter National Monument, is a historic landmark that has been guiding mariners into Charleston Harbor since its construction in 1862. This 85-foot tower, with a unique triangular shape resembling a tapered cone, is an assurance and symbol of guidance for generations. Although it does not offer indoor tours or visits, the lighthouse's distinctive architectural design makes it resistant to gales and hurricanes.

Free (Ferry $43)

Fort Sumter National Monument

Fort Sumter National Monument is a fortified historic site in Charleston, SC, United States. Records associated with the site include 1861. Fort Sumter National Monument is a must-visit destination for history enthusiasts, as it marks the location where the first shots of the Civil War were fired in April 1861. Walls, courtyards, or towers form the main visitor route when the grounds are open.

$35

South Carolina Aquarium

The South Carolina Aquarium is a large marine attraction featuring interactive displays and a diverse array of native and exotic sea creatures. Visitors can explore the state's varied ecosystems, from salt marshes to open ocean habitats, through engaging exhibits such as a two-story aquarium tank and touch tanks. The aquarium also offers educational tours and programs against the backdrop of views of Charleston harbor and the Ravenel Bridge.

Free

Mount Pleasant Pier

Mount Pleasant Pier is a visitor site in Charleston, SC, United States. Mount Pleasant Pier is a 1,250-foot-long modern pier located in the town of Mount Pleasant, South Carolina. It is part of the Memorial Waterfront Park complex and offers views of Charleston Harbor and the Arthur Ravenel Jr. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Free

Arthur Ravenel Jr. Bridge

The Arthur Ravenel Jr. Bridge is a notable building that gracefully spans the Cooper River, linking Downtown Charleston to Mount Pleasant. Completed in 2005, this cable-stayed bridge replaced older structures and has one of the longest main spans in the Western Hemisphere at an 471 meters. Its striking triangular arches are beautifully illuminated at night, creating a scene for evening strolls or bike rides along its dedicated lanes.

$45

Adventure Sightseeing

Adventure Sightseeing is a visitor site in Charleston, SC, United States. The climate-controlled tours cover the city's major architectural landmarks, historic neighborhoods, and the Battery, providing a comfortable and informative overview of Charleston's extensive colonial and Civil War history for visitors. The main structures and grounds are the principal points of interest.

Free

Charleston Visitor Center

The Charleston Visitor Center, located on Meeting Street, is the official starting point for many guided tours in the area. Even if visitors are not part of a tour group, it's worth stopping by to gather free tourist information and watch Forever Charleston, an orientation film that helps prioritize visitor itinerary, visitors can purchase the Charleston Heritage Passport at the center for discounted access to various attractions.

Free

Charleston Music Hall

The Charleston Music Hall is housed in a 19th-century Gothic Revival building and is a versatile venue for concerts, dance performances, theater productions, and comedy shows. It is known for its intimate atmosphere despite being able to accommodate nearly a thousand guests in its two-story main auditorium. The venue hosts a diverse range of events featuring both nationally-known and locally-grown music acts and comedians, offering an eclectic lineup that caters to various tastes.

Free

Marion Square

Marion Square is a public square in Charleston, SC, United States. This historic park is not just a spot to unwind on the grass or enjoy a leisurely picnic; it also buzzes with activity during events like farmers markets and festivals. Open pavement and surrounding facades define the visitor setting.

Free

Charleston Gaillard Center

The Charleston Gaillard Center is a historic and elegant performing arts venue that has been recently renovated to become a performance hall. It originally started in the 1960s as an auditorium and exhibit hall, but now it hosts big-name concerts, Broadway shows, dance performances, and classical music events.

Free

Boone Hall Plantation & Gardens

Boone Hall Plantation in Mount Pleasant is one of America's oldest working plantations, known for its dramatic 'Avenue of Oaks', a nearly mile-long driveway lined with massive, moss-draped live oak trees. The site includes original slave cabins and offers educational presentations on Gullah culture and the history of enslaved people in the Lowcountry.

Free

Charles Pinckney National Historic Site

Located in Mount Pleasant, South Carolina, the Charles Pinckney National Historic Site is a 28-acre remnant of Charles Pinckney's original 18th-century plantation known as Snee Farm. The site offers indoor and outdoor exhibits that look at the life and times of Charles Pinckney, who was a signer of the U.S. Constitution. Visitors can explore archaeological findings that tell the story of both Pinckney and the enslaved people who lived on the plantation.

Free

Unitarian Church in Charleston

The Unitarian Church in Charleston, located on Archdale Street, is the oldest of its kind in the South and holds a significant place in South Carolina's history. Constructed between 1772 and 1787, it replaced the original building destroyed by the Great Fire of 1740. The church's design, credited to Francis Lee and influenced by the Adam style, sets it apart from traditional ecclesiastical architecture with its boxy shape and absence of a steeple.

Free

Cathedral of Saint John the Baptist

Cathedral of Saint John the Baptist is a religious building in Charleston, SC, United States. The Cathedral of Saint John the Baptist, located in Charleston, South Carolina, is a historic brownstone cathedral known for its architecture and art. It was built on the site of Vauxhall Gardens, an entertainment venue from the post-Revolutionary era. The exterior and main interior spaces are usually viewable outside service times.

Free

Charleston Area Convention Center

Charleston Area Convention Center is a visitor site in Charleston, SC, United States. North Charleston Wannamaker County Park is an expansive natural space, abundant with forests and marshes. This park is perfect for various outdoor activities like running, biking, camping, hiking, and simply appreciating nature's beauty. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Free

The Battery

The Battery, located at the intersection of Murray Boulevard and East Battery Street in Charleston, was once a defensive seawall but is now known for historic homes and proximity to White Point Garden and Battery Park. Visitors can enjoy a leisurely walk along the well-maintained path by the bay, admiring the beautifully landscaped homes and historical mansions on one side while taking in views of the water on the other.
