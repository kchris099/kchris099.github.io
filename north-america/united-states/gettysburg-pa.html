Gettysburg, PA

Gettysburg is a south-central Pennsylvania town where Union and Confederate armies clashed for three days in July 1863, producing casualties that made it the bloodiest engagement of the American Civil War. The battlefield, national cemetery, and thousands of monuments preserve troop positions across ridges, fields, and town streets. President Abraham Lincoln's Gettysburg Address of November 1863 framed the war as a test of national unity and equal citizenship.

Gettysburg National Military Park

Gettysburg National Military Park is a public park in Gettysburg, PA, United States. Records associated with the site include 1863. Gettysburg National Military Park protects the 1863 battlefield across ridges, fields, and town edges. Auto and walking routes connect Union and Confederate positions, with a travellers center museum interpreting the campaign and its aftermath. Paths, lawns, and shaded areas make up the main public facilities.

State of Pennsylvania Monument

The State of Pennsylvania Monument is the largest and most prominent memorial on the Gettysburg battlefield. Dedicated in 1910, the massive granite and bronze structure honors the 34,530 Pennsylvanian soldiers who fought in the battle. Visitors can climb to the observation deck for a commanding, panoramic view of the surrounding historic landscape.

Little Round Top

Little Round Top is a prominent, rocky hill on the Gettysburg battlefield that served as a important defensive position for the Union Army on the second day of the battle. The successful defense of this high ground is considered a important moments of the engagement. The site offers expansive views of the surrounding battlefield.
