Explore Anchorage, AK

A Brief History

Anchorage was planned in 1914 as a federal railroad construction camp on Cook Inlet and became Alaska's commercial hub after statehood in 1959. Earthquake rebuilding in 1964, oil-pipeline headquarters, and Dena'ina Athabascan place names document how Arctic logistics, federal land grants, and resource booms shaped America's northernmost large city.

Top Sights & Activities

Earthquake Park

Earthquake Park is a public park in Anchorage, AK, United States. Records associated with the site include 1964. Earthquake Park in Anchorage, Alaska, is a 134-acre area that bears witness to the devastating 1964 earthquake. The park offers trails and an interpretive display that educates visitors about the impact of the powerful earthquake. Paths, lawns, and shaded areas make up the main public facilities.

Free

Point Woronzof Park

Point Woronzof Park is a 191.7-acre natural oasis located just southwest of Anchorage, right by the coast and near the Ted Stevens International Airport. This park is a haven for outdoor enthusiasts, offering trails perfect for hiking, running, biking, and even cross-country skiing during the winter months.

$25

Alaska Zoo

The Alaska Zoo, located in Anchorage, AK, offers a unique chance to experience a variety of native Alaskan mammals and birds, as well as exotic animals like tigers and camels. The zoo is committed to wildlife conservation and provides a safe environment for families to observe and learn about different species. Situated in the Hillside neighborhood, the facility participates in rescuing injured or abandoned animals from around the state.

Free

Delaney Park

Delaney Park, also known as the Delaney Park Strip, is a spacious park located in Anchorage. Named after an ex-mayor of the city, this block-wide area offers various recreational facilities such as sports fields, tennis courts, and horseshoe pits. Originally established as a firebreak between the forest and the growing city in the 1910s, it has transformed over time from a golf course to an airstrip before becoming a public park post-World War II.

Free

Alaska Center for the Performing Arts

Alaska Center for the Performing Arts is a visitor site in Anchorage, AK, United States. The building itself is a striking blend of brick and glass, adorned with colorful rings of light that add to its charm. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Free

Town Square Park

Town Square Park, situated between 5th and 6th Avenues and E and F Streets, is a urban oasis offering various attractions throughout the year. In the summer, visitors can enjoy floral displays and live concerts. When winter arrives, the park transforms with an ice-skating rink and ice sculptures.

Free

Visit Anchorage Log Cabin Visitor Information Center

The Visit Anchorage Log Cabin Visitor Information Center is a unique urban visitor center located in downtown Anchorage. Housed in a sod-roofed log cabin, the center offers local exhibits and travel guidance for visitors. While the cabin itself is not the main place for information, visitors can find a spacious area at the back with plenty of brochures from around the state and helpful staff.

Free

Alaska Wild Berry Products

Alaska Wild Berry Products, located at 5225 Juneau St, is a must-visit destination offering an extensive selection of tourist T-shirts, souvenir items, candies, jams, salmon, and sausage gift packs. The store has the world's largest chocolate fountain as one of its main attractions. Visitors can also find unique Alaskan fashion items at Once in a Blue Moose, CAKE, Bottom's Boutique and Circular Boutique nearby.

Free

Kincaid Park

Kincaid Park, located near the southern end of the Tony Knowles Coastal Trail in Anchorage, is a natural retreat named after former Alaska Governor Bob Kincaid. Spanning over 1,500 acres along the rugged coastline, this park offers a mix of recreational facilities and untamed wilderness. The extensive trail network covers over 40 miles and provides opportunities for activities such as hiking, trail running, mountain biking, cross-country skiing, and dog mushing during winter.

$14

Alaska Botanical Garden

The Alaska Botanical Garden is a public horticultural institution located in the Campbell Creek valley east of Anchorage, established in 1992 to study wild Alaskan plants and subarctic horticulture. The Garden holds a one-mile Lowe-Briggs trail, a formal perennial garden, an alpine rock garden, and a peatland boardwalk illustrating native Boreal species.
