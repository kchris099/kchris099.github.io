Destination Guide — Midwest

Explore Chicago, IL

A Brief History

Chicago grew from a frontier trading post at the Chicago River mouth into a rail and grain hub after the 1871 fire destroyed much of the centre. Skyscrapers, parkland by Frederick Law Olmsted, and lakefront industry defined the 20th-century city. Migration from the South and Eastern Europe fed labour for stockyards, steel, and later finance and universities along Lake Michigan.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

Wrigley Field

Wrigley Field, the second-oldest baseball stadium in the US after Fenway Park, exudes a nostalgic charm with its ivy-clad walls and manual scoreboard. Despite modern additions like a hotel and retail spaces, the historic venue maintains its old-time friendly atmosphere for Chicago Cubs fans. The stadium's renovations have focused on structural improvements and creating club levels and suites.

$40

Shedd Aquarium

Located on the southern end of Grant Park along Lake Michigan's shoreline, Shedd Aquarium is a renowned indoor aquarium in Chicago. It houses an impressive 32,000 animals representing diverse aquatic habitats from around the world. Visitors can marvel at sea otters, seahorses, beluga whales, reef sharks, and Giant Pacific octopus among other fascinating creatures. The aquarium offers educational experiences and emphasizes conservation efforts.

Free

The Museum Campus

Nestled along the shores of Lake Michigan, Museum Campus is a sprawling 57-acre oasis that houses three cultural institutions: the Adler Planetarium, Shedd Aquarium, and Field Museum. This picturesque setting is perfect for leisurely strolls through lush parks and gardens while soaking in views of both the skyline and the lake.

Free

Cloud Gate

Cloud Gate, also known as "The Bean," is a renowned outdoor sculpture in Chicago that attracts both locals and tourists. Created by Anish Kapoor, this metallic structure is made of 168 welded stainless steel plates and stands at 33 feet tall. Its reflective surface offers unique views of the surrounding cityscape, making it a hotspot for photography enthusiasts.

Free

Millennium Park

Millennium Park is a 24.5-acre urban oasis that has become a must-visit destination in the Midwest. This modern park beautifully blends art, nature, and performance spaces, making it an ideal spot for both relaxation and entertainment. At its heart lies the Frank Gehry-designed Jay Pritzker Pavilion, where visitors can enjoy free concerts and movie screenings during the summer months.

Free

Crown Fountain

Crown Fountain, located in Millennium Park, is a captivating public sculpture created by Catalan artist Jaume Plensa. The 50-foot LED towers project images of Chicagoans onto glass blocks and feature spouting mouths that resemble gargoyles. Surrounding the towers is a shallow wading pool where visitors, especially kids, enjoy splashing around. Despite initial controversy, the fountain has become one of Chicago's most beloved landmarks and a popular spot for Instagram-worthy photos.

Free

Chicago Cultural Center

The Chicago Cultural Center, housed in an 1897 Beaux-Arts building, was originally the city's central public library. It features vaulted lobby, mosaics, and two stained-glass domes. The building is a blend of history and art with intricate Favrile glass mosaics and rich hardwoods. Today, it hosts free art exhibits, music performances, dance shows, theater productions, panel discussions, films screenings, lectures and family events.

Free

The Chicago Theatre

The Chicago Theatre, a French Baroque-style auditorium with a seating capacity of 3,600, has been entertaining audiences since 1921. Located in the Loop district on North State Street, it is a popular venue for national musical and comedy acts. The grand facade of the theatre features Romanesque Revival details and a striking neon sign. Inside, visitors can admire the opulent decor with gilded ceilings and marble flooring.

Free

United Center

The United Center in Chicago is a massive arena that hosts over 200 events annually, including Bulls and Hawks games, as well as concerts featuring a wide range of musical acts. With a capacity of nearly 20,000 spectators, it is the largest indoor arena in the U.S. The venue also features statues of sports legends like Michael Jordan and has welcomed numerous superstar music acts since its opening in 1994.

$29

360 CHICAGO

Situated on the 94th floor of the John Hancock Building, 360 CHICAGO offers panoramic views of Chicago and its surroundings. This observation deck provides visitors with an opportunity to admire sweeping vistas of Lake Michigan and four neighboring states. Equipped with high-powered telescopes, guests can marvel at the cityscape from a height of 1000 feet, including a view of the Willis Tower.

Free

Chicago Riverwalk

Riverwalk is a bustling pedestrian path that runs along the Chicago River, offering city views and a variety of dining options. Spanning 1.25 miles from Lake Michigan to Lake Street, this vibrant area in Downtown Chicago is filled with shops, eateries, and recreational activities. Visitors can explore cozy cafes, unique boutiques, and lively bars while taking in the picturesque waterfront scenery.

Free

The Wrigley Building

The Wrigley Building, a beloved addition to Chicago's skyline since 1924, is architecturally significant as the first major skyscraper on the Magnificent Mile. Its clock tower rises 425 feet above street level and is adorned with elegant Art Deco details. The building's innovative use of six different shades of white terracotta tiles makes it a standout structure in downtown Chicago.

Free

Tribune Tower

Nestled among the impressive skyline of Michigan Avenue, Tribune Tower stands as a testament to architectural grandeur and historical significance. This Neo-Gothic skyscraper, completed in 1925 for The Chicago Tribune newspaper, showcases an extraordinary façade adorned with stones sourced from landmarks around the globe, such as St. Peter's Cathedral and the Taj Mahal.

Free

Skydeck Chicago

Located on the 103rd floor of the former Sears Tower, Skydeck Chicago provides visitors with views of four states. The unique viewing platform The Ledge lies 4ft outside the building dimensions and offers brave visitors unrivaled views over the city. With museum-quality exhibits and a 60-second ride up to the 103rd floor, Skydeck Chicago is a attraction in Chicago.

Free

The Rookery Building

The Rookery Building, a example of Romanesque architecture, stands as Chicago's oldest high-rise, completed in 1888. This remarkable structure was quickly erected following the Great Chicago Fire and symbolizes the city's resilience and hope. Designed by renowned architects Daniel Burnham and John Root, it features an enchanting lobby transformed by Frank Lloyd Wright into a masterpiece of light and design.

Free

Chicago Board of Trade Building

The Chicago Board of Trade Building is a example of Art Deco architecture, once the tallest building in Chicago. Designed by Holabird & Root and completed in 1930, it features strong vertical lines, set-backs, and geometric ornamentation characteristic of the style. At its peak stands a 30-foot tall statue of Ceres, the Roman goddess of agriculture, symbolizing the trading activities within.

Free

Lyric Opera of Chicago

The Lyric Opera of Chicago stands as a beacon of artistic excellence, captivating audiences with its opulent performances in the magnificent Civic Opera House. This venue, an Art Deco gem that has graced the city since 1929, boasts a striking marble exterior and an expansive interior that can accommodate over 3,500 guests. Renowned for its exceptional acoustics and grand scale, it ranks as North America's second-largest opera house.

Free

Garfield Park Conservatory

Garfield Park Conservatory is a horticultural center located within the expansive Garfield Park. This enchanting oasis spans about 12 acres and features an impressive variety of plants from around the world. Visitors can explore towering palm trees, fragrant orchids, seasonal blooms, and a traditional Moroccan fountain. The conservatory also houses the Desert House, showcasing a diverse collection of cacti and succulent plants.

Free

The 606

The 606, a 2.7-mile elevated trail, was once an abandoned rail line that has been transformed into a vibrant urban park and recreational space. Connecting the neighborhoods of Logan Square, Humboldt Park, Wicker Park, and Bucktown, this former railway corridor now serves as a popular route for runners and bikers. The trail features public art installations and offers convenient access to nearby amenities such as coffee shops and stores.

Free

Clarence F. Buckingham Memorial Fountain

Located in Grant Park, Chicago, the Clarence F. Buckingham Memorial Fountain is a 3-level rococo fountain that has been standing proud for almost a century. Built in 1927 to honor philanthropist Clarence Buckingham, this landmark offers impressive 20-minute water shows every hour on the hour from May through October.

Free

Auditorium Theatre

The Auditorium Theatre, a National Historic Landmark, is an architectural gem designed by Louis Sullivan and Dankmar Adler. This 1889 theater offers excellent acoustics and sight lines for a variety of shows including dance performances, music concerts, and speaker series. The building also features historic tours to showcase its design and rich history.

Free

Dearborn Station

Dearborn Station, the oldest of Chicago's downtown train stations, offers a unique blend of history and modern amenities. Located in Printer's Row, it features a shopping mall with retail and entertainment spaces. The building's architecture is modeled after a train station, adding to its charm and providing an opportunity for children to explore. While it has historical significance and some interesting businesses inside, there is potential for more diverse offerings.

Free

Lincoln Park Zoo

Lincoln Park Zoo is a small, free zoo located near Lake Michigan in Chicago. It offers a diverse range of animal exhibits, including lions, primates, reptiles, and a children's area with an interactive farm exhibit. Visitors can observe snow monkeys relaxing in the hot springs of the Regenstein Macaque Forest and marvel at gorillas and chimpanzees in the expansive Regenstein Center for African Apes. The Pepper Family Wildlife Center features big cats separated by glass windows for safe viewing.

Free

Lincoln Park Conservatory

The Lincoln Park Conservatory, a greenhouse in Chicago's North Side, has been attracting nature enthusiasts since the 1870s. This 1890 glass structure is home to a diverse array of plant species from around the world. With four distinct display houses showcasing ancient ferns, towering palms, blooming orchids, and an extensive collection of cacti and succulents, it offers a serene retreat for plant lovers and those seeking relaxation.

Free

Navy Pier

Navy Pier, a former Navy training center turned Chicago landmark, is a beloved destination for families and tourists alike. This 3,000-foot pier offers a vibrant atmosphere with carnival rides, diverse dining options, bars, and free entertainment such as live music and street performers. Visitors can also enjoy the IMAX Theater, the enchanting indoor Crystal Gardens filled with soaring palm trees, the Children's Museum, and views of Lake Michigan.
