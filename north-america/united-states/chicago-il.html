Explore Chicago, IL

A Brief History

Chicago grew from a frontier trading post at the Chicago River mouth into a rail and grain hub after the 1871 fire destroyed much of the centre. Skyscrapers, parkland by Frederick Law Olmsted, and lakefront industry defined the 20th-century city. Migration from the South and Eastern Europe fed labour for stockyards, steel, and later finance and universities along Lake Michigan.

Wrigley Field

Wrigley Field, the second-oldest baseball stadium in the US after Fenway Park, exudes a nostalgic charm with its ivy-clad walls and manual scoreboard. Despite modern additions like a hotel and retail spaces, the historic venue maintains its old-time friendly atmosphere for Chicago Cubs fans. The stadium's renovations have focused on structural improvements and creating club levels and suites.

$40

Shedd Aquarium

Located on the southern end of Grant Park along Lake Michigan's shoreline, Shedd Aquarium is a known indoor aquarium in Chicago. It houses an 32,000 animals representing diverse aquatic habitats from around the world. Visitors can marvel at sea otters, seahorses, beluga whales, reef sharks, and Giant Pacific octopus among other fascinating creatures. The aquarium offers educational experiences and emphasizes conservation efforts.

Free

The Museum Campus

Located along the shores of Lake Michigan, Museum Campus is a large 57-acre oasis that houses three cultural institutions: the Adler Planetarium, Shedd Aquarium, and Field Museum. This setting is perfect for leisurely strolls through lush parks and gardens while soaking in views of both the skyline and the lake.

Free

Cloud Gate

Cloud Gate, also known as "The Bean," is a known outdoor sculpture in Chicago that attracts both locals and tourists. Created by Anish Kapoor, this metallic structure is made of 168 welded stainless steel plates and stands at 33 feet tall. Its reflective surface offers unique views of the surrounding cityscape, making it a hotspot for photography enthusiasts.

Navy Pier

Navy Pier, a former Navy training center turned Chicago landmark, is a beloved destination for families and tourists alike.
