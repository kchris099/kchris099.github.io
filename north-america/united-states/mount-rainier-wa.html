Mount Rainier, WA

Mount Rainier is an active stratovolcano long sacred to Coast Salish peoples and later a focus of climbing and scientific study. Congress created Mount Rainier National Park in 1899, among the earliest in the system. Paradise and Sunrise visitor areas, glaciers, and old-growth forests structure access. Volcanic risk, heavy snowfall, and wildflower meadows define seasons on Washington's highest peak.
