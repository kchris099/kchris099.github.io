Big Sur, CA

Big Sur remained sparsely settled Esselen and later ranching country until Highway 1 opened the Santa Lucia coast in 1937. Writers and artists followed the road; state parks and the Monterey County coast preserved much of the cliff line from dense building. Landslides periodically close the highway. Redwood canyons, bridge overlooks, and scattered lodges define a coastal stretch shaped by geology and limited access.

Partington Cove

Partington Cove is a beach in Big Sur, CA, United States. The area offers two hiking options: one leading to the rocky mouth of Partington Creek and a small beach, while the other takes visitors through an historic 60-ft tunnel to a cove with rusty ruins of an old ship-loading apparatus from the tanoak-logging industry. Access is typically on foot from nearby roads or beachfront paths.

Julia Pfeiffer Burns State Park

Julia Pfeiffer Burns State Park is a four-square-mile park in Central California known for its ocean views, including the Bixby Creek Bridge and the McWay Falls. The park offers a short hike to a waterfall, a redwood grove, and coastal vistas. Named after Julia Pfeiffer Burns, a prominent Big Sur resident, this coastal destination is popular among hikers seeking panoramic views.

Pfeiffer Big Sur State Park

Pfeiffer Big Sur State Park is a public park in Big Sur, CA, United States. Pfeiffer Big Sur State Park, located 26 miles south of Carmel, is a 1,000-acre gem offering a variety of outdoor activities amidst surroundings. Travellers can enjoy camping, swimming in the Big Sur River, and exploring the historic homesteading roots of the park. Paths, lawns, and shaded areas make up the main public facilities.
