A Brief History

The United States traces its origins to thirteen British colonies along the Atlantic seaboard whose Declaration of Independence in 1776 established a federal union that gradually expanded westward through Louisiana, the Mexican Cession, and Alaska. Nineteenth-century industrialisation transformed the country into a global manufacturing power while twentieth-century immigration, suburban growth, and the civil-rights movement reshaped its social fabric. Today the United States preserves a layered regional heritage through its National Park system, surviving colonial districts in Charleston and Boston, Spanish mission corridors in California, and contemporary civic landmarks in Manhattan and Washington, each anchoring a national narrative of continuity and renewal.

Top Sights & Activities

$10

Partington Cove

Partington Cove is a hidden gem along the Big Sur coast, accessible by a dirt trail, wooden bridge, and tunnel. The area offers two hiking options: one leading to the rocky mouth of Partington Creek and a small beach, while the other takes you through an historic 60-ft tunnel to a cove with rusty ruins of an old ship-loading apparatus from the tanoak-logging industry.

$10

McWay Falls

Nestled in the Julia Pfeiffer Burns State Park along California's Central Coast, McWay Falls is a 80-foot waterfall that cascades directly into the Pacific Ocean. This natural wonder offers a picturesque sight, with its unique feature of flowing onto a small beach. The journey to this hidden gem involves a short hike through the unspoiled charm of Big Sur, making it an adventure for nature enthusiasts.

$10

Julia Pfeiffer Burns State Park

Julia Pfeiffer Burns State Park is a four-square-mile park in Central California known for its ocean views, including the Bixby Creek Bridge and the McWay Falls. The park offers a short hike to a waterfall, a redwood grove, and coastal vistas. Named after Julia Pfeiffer Burns, a prominent Big Sur resident, this coastal destination is popular among hikers seeking panoramic views.

$10

Pfeiffer Big Sur State Park

Pfeiffer Big Sur State Park, located 26 miles south of Carmel, is a 1,000-acre gem offering a variety of outdoor activities amidst surroundings. The park features hiking trails that lead through the impressive redwood forest and offer views of the Big Sur coastline. Visitors can enjoy camping, swimming in the Big Sur River, and exploring the historic homesteading roots of the park.
