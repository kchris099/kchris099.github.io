Savannah, GA

James Oglethorpe's 1733 ward-and-square plan organised Savannah as a geometric colonial experiment. Rice and cotton wealth lined the squares with mansions; the Civil War largely spared the grid. Twentieth-century preservation kept Forsyth Park and the river warehouses as a planned port town rather than a rebuilt industrial strip. Bonaventure Cemetery's Victorian plots became a literary landmark after Midnight in the Garden of Good and Evil.
