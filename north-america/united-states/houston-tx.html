Explore Houston, TX

A Brief History

Houston was founded in 1836 by New York land promoters near Buffalo Bayou's port to the Gulf of Mexico. Ship Channel refineries, Museum District institutions, and NASA Johnson Space Center campuses record how cotton export, Spindletop oil discoveries, and postwar petrochemical investment built America's fourth-largest city on a humid coastal plain.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

The Galleria

Nestled in the heart of Houston, The Galleria is a sprawling shopping haven that boasts over a million square feet of retail space. With nearly 500 boutiques and restaurants, it’s a paradise for shoppers seeking luxury brands like Chanel, Gucci, and Louis Vuitton alongside contemporary favorites such as H&M and Zara. One standout feature is its ice rink beneath a glass roof, where visitors can enjoy ice skating while sipping on crafted drinks served in unique ice glasses.

Free

Houston Arboretum & Nature Center

The Houston Arboretum and Nature Center occupies 155 acres on Memorial Park's western edge, rooted in 1951 parkland set aside for nature education and opened as a children's botanical hall in 1967. Five miles of trails pass prairie, wetland, woodland, and ravine habitats restored after Hurricane Ike. Field stations and a nature center explain native plants and wildlife.

Free

Central Market

Central Market is a hub for food lovers, offering an impressive selection of gourmet vegetables, meats, and wines. This expansive space also features an inviting in-house café where visitors can enjoy delicious bites while soaking in the lively atmosphere filled with food events and live music. Recognized by Bon Appétit as one of the top regional supermarket chains, it’s a favorite among Houstonians who appreciate quality ingredients and community connections.

Free

Buffalo Bayou Park

Buffalo Bayou Park is a sprawling 160-acre green space that offers views of the Houston skyline. The park features a variety of attractions, including hike and bike trails along the bayou, captivating art installations, and outdoor activity spaces such as Eleanor Tinsley Park and the Lee and Joe Jamail Skatepark. Visitors can also enjoy delicious food and drinks at The Dunlavy, a chandelier-lit kitchen and bar within the park.

Free

Sam Houston Park

Sam Houston Park is a historically rich park in Houston, featuring guided tours of old structures dating back to the 1800s. The Heritage Society Museum within the park was founded in 1954 and has since saved and restored ten historic buildings, along with a Museum Gallery showcasing over 23,000 artifacts documenting life in Houston from the early 1800s to the mid-1900s. Visitors can explore various historic houses within the park, each representing different architectural styles and periods.

Free

The Hobby Center for the Performing Arts

Nestled in downtown Houston, the Hobby Center for the Performing Arts is a modern complex featuring two theaters and a grand three-story lobby with French-inspired design offering views of the city skyline. The center is renowned as the prime entertainment hub in the Houston Theater District, housing the impressive 2,650-seat Sarofim Hall, the intimate 500-seat Zilkha Theatre, and the Humphreys School of Musical Theater.

$25

Downtown Aquarium

Downtown Aquarium occupies a six-acre Bagby Street complex built around Fire Station No. 1 and the Central Waterworks Building, adapted by Landry's and opened in 2003. Eight themed galleries hold more than 200 aquatic species in 500,000 gallons of tanks, alongside restaurants, a white-tiger exhibit, and the Shark Voyage electric train through a 200,000-gallon habitat.

Free

Daikin Park

Minute Maid Park, the home of Major League Baseball's Houston Astros, is a must-visit destination in downtown Houston. This 45,000-seater stadium boasts a retractable roof, ensuring an enjoyable experience regardless of the weather. The park features a replica 19th-century locomotive that runs along the west side and lights up and makes sounds during key moments in the game. With over 40,000 seats and air-conditioned comfort, it provides an intimate and lively atmosphere for fans.

Free

Discovery Green

Discovery Green is a 12-acre park located in downtown Houston, just across from the George R. Brown Convention Center. The park offers a variety of attractions and activities, including live music, public art installations, restaurants, a playground, and a pond where visitors can go kayaking. Additionally, the park hosts free community programming such as writing workshops and exercise classes.

Free

Houston Legendary Graffiti Building

The Houston Legendary Graffiti Building at 2011 Leeland Street in East Downtown was an outdoor mural site also called Graffiti Park. Local artists including Daniel Anguilu maintained permission walls with portraits of Selena, George Floyd, and Houston hip-hop figures for more than a decade. The warehouse was demolished in June 2025 during Interstate 45 widening.

$25

Houston Zoo

The Houston Zoo sprawls across 55 acres and is home to over 6,000 animals showcased in natural habitat-style exhibits. Visitors can observe a diverse range of creatures including tigers, cheetahs, elephants, gorillas, orangutans, warthogs, and sea lions. The zoo offers engaging experiences such as daily feedings and the opportunity to interact with giraffes.

Free

Hermann Park

Hermann Park, located in Houston, Texas, is a sprawling 445-acre urban park known for its diverse attractions and activities. Established in 1914, it is and most beautiful parks in the city. The park features a golf course, Japanese garden, natural science museum, kiddie train rides, and the Sam Houston monument. Visitors can explore the renowned Houston Zoo within the park's grounds as well. Other highlights include Miller Outdoor Theatre and McGovern Centennial Garden.

Free

Miller Outdoor Theatre

Miller Outdoor Theatre, situated within Houston's Hermann Park, is an outdoor performing arts venue offering free live shows from March to November. Originally designed as an amphitheater in 1992, it underwent significant refurbishment in 1998. With seating for over 1,700 visitors and lawn space for an additional 4,500 patrons, the theater hosts a variety of performances including dance, music, theater and symphony presentations by the Houston Symphony.

Free

McGovern Centennial Gardens

McGovern Centennial Gardens, located in Hermann Park's northeast end, spans 8 acres and offers a variety of themed gardens, a sculpture promenade, and a 30-foot mount providing city views. The site was established in 2014 to commemorate the park's centennial anniversary and features distinct areas such as the rose garden, arid garden, woodlands garden, and community garden producing seasonal vegetables and fruit trees. Visitors can also enjoy the spacious Centennial Green lawn.
