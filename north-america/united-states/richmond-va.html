Explore Richmond, VA

A Brief History

Richmond served as the capital of the Commonwealth of Virginia and later became the capital of the Confederate States of America during the Civil War, making it a primary target for Union forces. The city was strongly shaped by the domestic slave trade before the war and became a major center of African American commerce and culture in the Jackson Ward neighborhood afterward. Industrial tobacco and iron manufacturing fueled its later expansion. Visitors explore this complex, layered history at the Virginia State Capitol, the American Civil War Museum, and the historic Tredegar Iron Works.

Altria Theater

Altria Theater, situated on the Virginia Commonwealth University campus in Richmond, is a historic landmark that has been a prominent venue for various events since its opening in 1927. Originally known as The Mosque Theater, it has become popular stages for theatrical and musical performances in the city. The theater hosts a wide range of events including Broadway productions, concerts, comedy shows, children's theater, lectures, school commencements and fashion shows.

Free

Virginia War Memorial

The Virginia War Memorial, established in 1956, is a significant monument and education center dedicated to honoring the brave men and women from Virginia who have served in the military. It serves as a Center of Excellence for educating Virginians about the nation's wartime experiences. The memorial features two shrines that list the names of military heroes who made the ultimate sacrifice.

$40 (event ticket)

Richmond Raceway

Richmond Raceway is a captivating D-shaped racetrack located on the northern edge of Richmond, Virginia. Since its inception in 1946 as a half-mile dirt track, it has evolved into a premier venue for thrilling NASCAR Cup Series and Xfinity Series events. The unique design of the track allows drivers to reach impressive speeds while adding an element of excitement with potential close encounters on the asphalt.

Free

Virginia State Capitol

The Virginia State Capitol, a grand colonial-style structure built in 1785, is the oldest legislative body in the nation. Designed by Thomas Jefferson and influenced by the Roman Maison Carre in Nimes, France, it remains Virginia's seat of government. White House of the Confederacy

The White House of the Confederacy, built in 1818, served as the executive residence of Confederate President Jefferson Davis and his family from 1861 to 1865. Restored to its wartime appearance, the house is now furnished with period furniture and offers guided tours showcasing its historical significance.

Free (mansion tour $10)

Maymont

Maymont, located in Richmond, Virginia, is a 19th-century estate that offers tours of its Gilded Age mansion, gardens, and wildlife center. Originally a private estate, it was generously donated to the city of Richmond and now spans over 100 acres. Visitors can start their tour at the Robins Nature & Visitor Center to see various fish, reptiles, and mammals from the James River and Chesapeake Bay.

$8

The Robins Nature Center at Maymont

The Robins Nature Center at Maymont is a wildlife center dedicated to conserving aquatic animals, fish, and reptiles. Visitors can start their tour at the nature and visitor center, where they can observe dozens of fish, reptiles, and mammals found in the James River and Virginia's Chesapeake Bay. The otter habitat is a highlight of the indoor tour.

$8 (film ticket)

The Byrd Theatre

The Byrd Theatre, a historic movie palace established in 1928 and located in Richmond's Carytown district, is a beloved institution offering a nostalgic cinematic experience. With its opulent interior featuring crystal chandeliers and a Wurlitzer organ, the theatre screens second-run films, classic movies, and special events at affordable prices.

$15 (guided tour)

Historic St. John's Church

Historic St. John's Church, a 1741 Episcopal church in Richmond, Virginia, holds great significance in American history. It was here on March 23, 1775, that Patrick Henry delivered his famous "Give me liberty or give me death!" speech during the Second Virginia Convention, persuading them to arm a Virginia militia.

Free

Libby Hill Park

Located in historic Church Hill, Libby Hill Park offers scenic views of Richmond's skyline and a variety of activities such as benches, tall trees, and a bike trail. The park is perfect for a picnic or for taking in the sights from atop one of Richmond's hills.
