San Diego, CA

San Diego preserves the site of California's first Spanish mission, founded in 1769 on Kumeyaay homelands above a deep natural harbour. Balboa Park exposition buildings, Gaslamp Quarter revival, and naval-base growth record how Mexican secularization, transcontinental rail competition with Los Angeles, and Cold War shipyards built a border-region city facing the Pacific and Tijuana.
