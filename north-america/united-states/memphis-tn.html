Memphis, TN

Memphis was platted in 1819 on Chickasaw bluffs above a Mississippi River crossing that linked cotton plantations to global markets. Beale Street music clubs, National Civil Rights Museum exhibits, and riverfront cotton-exchange façades record how riverboat commerce, Yellow Fever epidemics, and twentieth-century recording studios shaped Tennessee's western metropolis.

National Civil Rights Museum

The National Civil Rights Museum incorporates the Lorraine Motel, where Martin Luther King was assassinated in 1968, together with neighboring buildings connected to the crime and investigation. Galleries trace slavery, segregation, protest and legal change through artifacts and testimony. Preserved motel rooms and the exterior wreath anchor these broader histories in a specific site of national remembrance.

Orpheum Theatre

The Orpheum Theatre, a historic and opulent former opera hall in downtown Memphis, is a known venue hosting a variety of performances. From touring theater troupes to music acts, opera, ballet, and Broadway shows, the theater offers diverse entertainment options. It is also home to community events such as Memphis in May and special Elvis Week events. With its rich history and architectural significance, the Orpheum Theatre holds a place on the National Register of Historic Places.

Beale Street Landing

Beale Street Landing lines the Mississippi with walking paths, picnic lawns, and rental bikes, and hosts wedding groups on the riverfront. Connected Tom Lee Park extends the same embankment feel with further river views. The landing works as a civic waterfront park rather than a closed marina club.

FedExForum

FedExForum is downtown Memphis's major indoor arena, hosting the NBA's Grizzlies, college basketball, concerts and touring events. Its design and displays reference the city's music and sports traditions, while nearby Beale Street shapes event-day movement.

AutoZone Park

AutoZone Park, located in downtown Memphis, is a popular sports venue that hosts minor league baseball games for the Memphis Redbirds and soccer matches for the Memphis 901 FC. The stadium offers inventive concessions and lawn seating, creating a atmosphere for sports enthusiasts. Recognized as the minor league ballpark in America in 2009, AutoZone Park provides an intimate setting where fans can get up close to the action and enjoy nightly fireworks shows during the season.

Tennessee Welcome Center

The Tennessee Welcome Center on Riverside Drive provides highway and visitor information from a bluff above the Mississippi River. Maps, brochures, rest facilities, and an outdoor overlook support trip planning, while statues and nearby paths connect the stop with Memphis's riverfront; it functions primarily as a public service facility.

Bass Pro Shops at the Pyramid

Bass Pro Shops at the Pyramid is a known outdoor retailer with stores across North America, offering a wide range of hunting, fishing, and outdoor gear. The 535,000-square-foot megastore in Memphis has an array of attractions including a cypress swamp, alligator pools, duck aviaries, and 10 aquariums with over 1,800 fish.

Memphis Zoo

Memphis Zoo occupies part of Overton Park and has developed from an early twentieth-century menagerie into a modern zoological institution. Habitats, gardens, conservation programs and education spaces present species from several climates across a large walking site.

Graceland Mansion

Graceland Mansion preserves the Memphis home where Elvis Presley lived from 1957 until his death in 1977. Guided circulation passes selected rooms, family areas, grounds, and the Meditation Garden, while furnishings reflect different periods of Presley's occupancy; the mansion is one component of the larger Graceland visitor operation, and upstairs private rooms remain closed to the public.
