Seattle, WA

Seattle rose as a Puget Sound timber and shipping entrepôt after the Great Northern Railway reached Elliott Bay in 1893, then hosted the 1962 World's Fair that left the Space Needle. Pioneer Square brick, Pike Place Market stalls, and Boeing-era suburbs record how Pacific Northwest resource extraction, Japanese immigration, and tech industry expansion reshaped a rain-soaked harbour city.
