Explore Nashville, TN

A Brief History

Nashville became Tennessee's permanent capital in 1843 on the Cumberland River, a position strengthened by railway networks after the Civil War. Parthenon replica, Ryman Auditorium stages, and capitol hill limestone record how printing, higher education, and country-music recording industries turned a regional political centre into a national cultural capital.

Top Sights & Activities

Grand Ole Opry

Nashville, also known as the capital of country music, is a city with a rich history and diverse entertainment scene. Visitors can explore various historical sites such as Fort Nashborough, Fort Negley, the Parthenon, Tennessee State Museum, and the Hermitage. The city offers sophisticated dining options and a lively atmosphere for all interests. One of its main attractions is the Grand Ole Opry, where both classic and contemporary country acts perform in weekly live radio shows.

Free

Opry Mills

Opry Mills is a sprawling shopping destination with over 200 stores, offering a wide range of upscale retailers and outlet stores. Visitors can explore popular outlets such as Bass Pro Shops and the Lego Store, along with an array of other well-known brands like Adidas, Kate Spade, Nike, and Victoria's Secret. The mall also boasts diverse dining options including the Aquarium, Bavarian Bierhaus, and Rainforest Cafe.

$30

Nashville Zoo at Grassmere

Nashville Zoo at Grassmere, located just 6 miles from downtown Nashville, is a zoological garden and historic plantation farmhouse. It features multiple habitats with a diverse range of animals and popular attractions like the Adventure 4D Theater, Soaring Eagle Zipline, and Wild Animal Carousel.

Free

Bridgestone Arena

Bridgestone Arena, In downtown Nashville, is a versatile and spacious venue that hosts a variety of events. While it serves as the home to the NHL's Nashville Predators and has been a hub for sports excitement, it was primarily designed for concerts. The arena boasts exceptional acoustics and can accommodate up to 20,000 attendees for center-stage concerts. Over the years, it has become a hotspot for big-name artists touring nationally when not occupied by sporting events.

Free

Ryman Auditorium

The Ryman Auditorium, known as the "Mother Church of Country Music," is a historic venue in Nashville that hosts diverse concerts and daily tours. It is an symbol of the city's musical roots, with intricate details and storied walls that make it visually captivating and historically significant. Alongside other famous entertainment destinations like the Grand Ole Opry House, Belcourt Theatre, and honky tonk bars, the Ryman Auditorium stands at the heart of Nashville's rich history.

Free

Nashville Symphony Schermerhorn Symphony Center

Nestled in the heart of Nashville, the Schermerhorn Symphony Center is a neoclassical gem that serves as a hub for music enthusiasts. Opened in 2006 and designed by architect David M. Schwarz, this architectural marvel boasts an acoustically perfect concert hall with 800 seats, making it ideal for classical, pop, and jazz performances alike.

Free

Music City Center

Nashville's downtown area, known as "the District," is a hub with Lower Broadway, Printers Alley, and Second Avenue offering shops, restaurants, historic music landmarks, nightclubs, and honky tonk bars. The Gulch neighborhood to the west is also trendy with bars and upscale restaurants. Music City Center is a massive 1.2 million square feet convention and exhibition complex in downtown Nashville designed by Tvsdesign.

Free

John Seigenthaler Pedestrian Bridge

The John Seigenthaler Pedestrian Bridge is a 3,150-ft. truss bridge exclusively for pedestrians, spanning the Cumberland River and offering views of downtown Nashville. Known for its appearances in the TV show Nashville, it serves as a convenient route to Nissan Stadium on the East Bank for events. The bridge provides an impressive vantage point of the city skyline against the tranquil river flow, making it a popular spot for photographers capturing various lighting conditions.

Free

Nashville Farmers' Market

Nashville Farmers' Market is a daily market offering a diverse range of local foods, produce, crafts, classes, and chef demos. Established in 1955 as an open-air market and later updated with an enclosed market house and covered farm sheds in 1995, it has become an part of the city. With over 150 farmers and food artisans participating throughout the year, visitors can explore a wide selection of fresh produce every weekend.

Free

First Horizon Park

First Horizon Park is a contemporary minor-league baseball stadium and events venue that serves as the home of the AAA baseball Nashville Sounds. The Band Box, located above the right field wall, offers a premium experience with elevated ballpark fare, premium beers, and frozen cocktails. Guests can enjoy lounging on couches, playing foosball, cornhole, putt putt, and table tennis while watching games. Special dishes by notable chefs are available during Thursday night home games.

$28

Musicians Hall of Fame and Museum

Nestled in the heart of downtown Nashville, the Musicians Hall of Fame and Museum is a treasure trove for music enthusiasts. This captivating museum pays tribute to the rich history of American recorded music, showcasing not only musicians but also the engineers and producers who played pivotal roles behind the scenes. Visitors can marvel at priceless artifacts, including Jimi Hendrix's guitar and instruments from legends like Garth Brooks and Johnny Cash.

Free

Tennessee Performing Arts Center

The Tennessee Performing Arts Center in Nashville is a hub for the city's creative spirit. With three separate stages, it hosts a diverse range of performances including dance, music, drama, and traveling shows. The center presents a variety of monthly performances such as the HCA Healthcare/TriStar Health Broadway series and special engagements by resident artistic companies like Nashville Ballet, Nashville Opera, and Nashville Repertory Theatre.

$20

Cheekwood

Cheekwood is a sprawling estate from the 1930s that features an art museum, picturesque botanical gardens, a cafe, and a gift shop. The museum houses works previously in the collection of Stanford Fine Art, now acquired by various art museums worldwide. Nashville's growth has led to the opening of Stanford Contemporary, a new gallery dedicated to modern and contemporary art within Cheekwood. The museum showcases American art through portraits, impressionist paintings, and contemporary works.

$10

The Parthenon

The Parthenon in Nashville is a full-scale replica of the Athenian Parthenon, built in 1897 to commemorate Tennessee's centennial and honor Nashville as 'The Athens of the South.' Located in Centennial Park, this impressive structure also houses American art galleries. The building was initially constructed out of plaster but was later rebuilt with sturdier materials. Visitors can explore the exterior for free or pay a nominal fee to see the 42-foot-tall statue of Athena inside.

$12

Belcourt Theatre

Belcourt Theatre is a beautifully restored cinema in Nashville that features two screens and a lobby bar. It showcases a diverse range of films, from mainstream to avant-garde, including independent releases, documentaries, foreign language films, and classics. The theater also hosts special engagements and screenings of new releases.

Free

Antique Archaeology Nashville

Antique Archaeology Nashville is a must-visit destination for fans of the popular TV show American Pickers, hosted by Mike Wolfe. This unique store showcases an eclectic collection of vintage treasures and distinctive home decor that Wolfe has unearthed during his adventures across America. While it may not be a traditional museum, visitors can explore an array of collectibles and memorabilia from the show itself.
