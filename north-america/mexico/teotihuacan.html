Explore Teotihuacán

A Brief History

Teotihuacán was the largest and most influential city in the pre-Columbian Americas, flourishing between the first and seventh centuries AD. At its peak, it housed over 100,000 residents within a organized urban grid, dominating cultural and trade networks as far away as the Maya region. The city was mysteriously burned and abandoned before the Aztecs arrived, who revered the ruins as the birthplace of the gods. Today, visitors trace this vast ancient metropolis along the Avenue of the Dead and atop the monumental Pyramids of the Sun and the Moon.
