Mexico

Mexico emerged as a federal republic in 1824, building on three centuries of Spanish colonial administration that had followed the 1521 fall of Tenochtitlan and the 16th-century Franciscan mendicant missions. The country's 3,000-year pre-Hispanic past survives in Olmec, Maya, Toltec, and Mexica cultural layers, while 19th-century reform and 1910 revolution defined the modern state. Travellers read that record at the Teotihuacan pyramids, the Zapotec capital of Monte Alban, the colonial centres of Mexico City and Oaxaca, and the 16th-century Dominican convents of the Morelos highlands.
