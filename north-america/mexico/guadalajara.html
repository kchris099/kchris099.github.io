Destination Guide — Central Mexico

Explore Guadalajara

A Brief History

Founded in 1542, Guadalajara flourished as a colonial administrative hub independent of Mexico City. Its deep historical wealth fostered traditional Mexican icons such as mariachi and tequila, while evolving into a massive modern center for technology and distinctive regional arts. Visitors today can clearly trace the intricate layers of this unique heritage throughout the preserved streets and monuments.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

Guadalajara's Cathedral

Guadalajara Cathedral, a grand 16th and 17th-century structure, houses the relics of St. Innocent. The cathedral is located in a neighborhood dominated by historical landmarks such as the Governor's Palace and the Hospicio Cabanas. The latter was designated a UNESCO World Heritage Site for its original chapel adorned with striking apocalyptic murals by artist Jose Clemente Orozco.

Free

Plaza de Armas

The Plaza de Armas is an attractive heritage museum in the heart of Guadalajara. The square is surrounded by beautiful neoclassical buildings, such as the Cathedral and the Palace of Justice. It's a great place to people watch and enjoy a cup of coffee or an ice cream from one of the many cafes and restaurants located around the square.

Free

Plaza de la Liberación

Plaza de la Liberación, located just behind the Cathedral in Guadalajara, is a spacious square filled with lively activities. On Sunday afternoons, visitors can enjoy watching magicians and mimes, listening to musicians, and browsing through street vendors while surrounded by fountains and tabachin trees.

Free

Teatro Degollado

Teatro Degollado stands as a example of neoclassical architecture in Guadalajara, embodying the rich cultural tapestry of Jalisco. Nestled within the historical center, this architectural gem showcases a blend of indigenous influences and European styles, particularly French and Italian.

Free

Plaza Fundadores

Plaza Fundadores (Founders' Square) is a historic public square located in the center of León, Guanajuato. It features the prominent Fountain of Lions, which commemorates the founding of the city in 1576. The plaza is a vibrant social hub, surrounded by colonial architecture, cafes, and shops, frequently hosting local cultural events.

Free

Rotonda de los Jaliscienses Ilustres

Nestled in the vibrant heart of Guadalajara, the Rotonda de los Jaliscienses Ilustres stands as a magnificent tribute to the remarkable figures who have shaped Jalisco's cultural and historical landscape. Originally known as the Rotonda de los Hombres Ilustres, this monument features 17 elegant fluted columns that support a quarry ring.

Free

Palacio Municipal de Guadalajara

The City Hall, also known as the Palacio Municipal de Guadalajara, is an impressive neoclassical building located in downtown Guadalajara. The Gabriel Flores murals depicting the foundation of Guadalajara are particularly vivid and make it a for tourists visiting the city. The palace houses Jalisco's city council offices and is free to walk in.

Free

Andares Shopping Mall

Andares Shopping Mall, located in the district of Zapopan, is a high-end open-air shopping destination that has been attracting visitors since 2008. The mall features water elements, bars, a food court, and a movie theater for entertainment. It is known for its collection of luxury brands and designer stores where indulge in high-end shopping. Additionally, the mall offers a variety of dining options ranging from famous international restaurants to local eateries at the VIP Avenue.

Free

Parroquia El Expiatorio Eucarístico

The Templo Expiatorio del Santísimo Sacramento is a remarkable neo-Gothic church in Mexico, known for its Italian mosaics, stained glass windows, and a clock tower. Designed by Adamo Boari, the construction of this historic church took 75 years to complete due to disruptions caused by the Mexican Revolution. Despite being a popular tourist spot, it offers visitors quiet corners for reflection and tranquility within its large premises.

Free

Los Dos Templos

Los Dos Templos, located in the Guadalajara Historic Centre, is a significant site featuring two temples: the Temple of Lady of Aranzazu and the Temple of San Francisco de Asis. The former was built between 1749 and 1752 as part of a larger complex, while the latter is known for its Gothic construction with stone blocks that have stood solid for centuries. These beautiful 400 to 500-year-old churches showcase Mexicans' deep devotion to the Catholic faith.

Free

Basílica de Nuestra Señora de Zapopan

The Basílica de Nuestra Señora de Zapopan is a Baroque-style complex located in the heart of Zapopan, Mexico. This historic structure dates back to the early 18th century and features classical Spanish-inspired architecture with ornate church sanctuary and an abbey.

Free

Plaza de las Americas (John Paul II)

Plaza de las Americas, also known as John Paul II, is a charming destination nestled in Zapopan, just a short distance from the historic heart of Guadalajara. This lovely plaza offers visitors a serene atmosphere with plenty of benches to relax and enjoy the surroundings. indulge in local delicacies from various food vendors while soaking up the vibrant ambiance filled with families and friends enjoying their day out.

Free

Glorieta de La Minerva

The Minerva Round roundabout is a beautiful, historical landmark in the city of Barcelona. It features a fountain and manicured greens, making it a popular meeting place to celebrate the victories of the city's soccer teams or national team. The only downside is that it's often filled with cars, but it's definitely worth visiting on a Sunday to enjoy Via Recreativa while avoiding the traffic.

Free

Glorieta Stampede

The Glorieta Stampede, crafted by the talented sculptor Jorge de la Pena, is a bronze sculpture that captures a dynamic group of galloping horses. Each horse is intricately detailed, exuding power and emotion as they appear to be elemental beings—water, shadow, and fire horses—all in motion. The artwork's beauty changes with different viewing angles, making it worthwhile to stroll around and appreciate its artistry from all perspectives.

Free

Art Garden of the Chapalita Roundabout

The Chapalita Roundabout Park is a large, beautiful park with a fountain in the center. On Sundays, local artists exhibit their work there, and there's always plenty of music and art to enjoy on this lovely day. It's perfect for a lazy afternoon or evening out, and it's also close to some great restaurants.

Free

Expo Guadalajara

Expo Guadalajara is the largest convention center in Mexico, offering over 300,000 square feet of customizable meeting space. It hosts renowned events like the International Book Fair and serves as a magnet for visitors. The surrounding Zona Expo area provides accommodation options for all budgets, with the hipster vibe of the Americana neighborhood being particularly appealing to younger visitors.

Free

Baseball Stadium Los Charros de Jalisco

Baseball Stadium Los Charros de Jalisco, located in Zapopan, is home to the Charros de Jalisco and the Mariachis de Guadalajara. The stadium was built for the Panamerican Games in 2012 and has garnered praise for its modern facilities. Despite some areas needing improvement such as seating and cleanliness, visitors have enjoyed a great experience with top-notch security, entertainment including mascots, and diverse food options.

Free

Auditorio Telmex

Nestled in the vibrant Metropolitan Area of Guadalajara, the Auditorio Telmex stands as a premier entertainment venue that showcases an impressive array of performances. With its sleek modern design and capacity to accommodate over 8,000 guests, this auditorium has become a cultural hub for nearly a decade. It hosts everything from pop concerts to classical orchestras and serves as the official site for the International Film Festival in Guadalajara.

Free

Plaza Tapatía

The Plaza Tapatía, located in the heart of Guadalajara, is a popular destination for tourists and locals alike. The square is adorned with a number of statues and fountains, making it an ideal place to relax or take in the sights and sounds of the city. In addition to its beauty, the plaza is also home to several shops and restaurants, making it an excellent choice for a day out.

Free

Mercado Libertad - San Juan de Dios

Mercado Libertad, also known as San Juan de Dios, is a massive indoor market in Guadalajara where find a wide variety of goods including food, clothing, handicrafts, and electronics. The market boasts a huge food court offering an array of local delicacies such as torta ahogada, birria, pozole, fish ceviche, and traditional Mexican breakfast dishes.

Free

Plaza de los Mariachis

Plaza de los Mariachis is a charming square located in the city center of Guadalajara, known for its bronze statue, fountain, and live mariachi music. The plaza embodies the spirit of Guadalajara's rich culture and serves as a hub for the Mariachi bands that serenade the city with their unique and legendary music. Every year, it hosts the International Mariachi Festival, attracting around 500 Mariachis from all over to celebrate this musical tradition.

Free

Acuario Michin Guadalajara

Acuario Michin in Guadalajara is a fantastic destination for families and marine life enthusiasts. With over 300 species and 9000 specimens, the aquarium offers an educational experience while promoting conservation efforts. Visitors can explore five pavilions showcasing diverse aquatic life, including crocodiles, neotropical otters, and exotic fish native to Jalisco's waters. The aquarium also features interactive experiences like feeding sessions and a petting zoo with lemurs.

Free

Zoologico Guadalajara

Zoologico Guadalajara is a popular zoo that offers a diverse range of animals, shows, train tours, and an aquarium. It's known as one of the nice parks in Guadalajara due to its wide variety of animal classifications, including wild animals, numerous bird species, and predatory animals.

Free

Selva Mágica

Selva Mágica is a prominent amusement park in Guadalajara, offering a wide range of attractions suitable for both kids and adults. From gentle carousels to thrilling roller coasters, the park caters to visitors of all ages and preferences. It's known for its exciting rides, including the adrenaline-pumping Bullet. Additionally, the park features various games and food options, making it an ideal destination for families.

Free

Barranca de Huentitán

Nestled in the heart of Guadalajara, Barranca de Huentitán National Park offers an exhilarating adventure for nature enthusiasts and fitness lovers alike. As you embark on this challenging hike, be greeted by views that showcase the canyons carved by the Santiago River. The trail features steep sections where old rail slats provide support as you navigate your way down to the serene waters of Rio Verde.

Free

Mirador Independencia Park

Mirador Independencia Park, also known as Parque Mirador Independencia, is a popular tourist attraction in Guadalajara. It offers a relaxed green space with a playground, outdoor theater, and a family-friendly restaurant. The park is renowned for its picturesque views of the 2,000-foot wide canyon and hosts regular events such as concerts and theatre shows. Surrounded by thick trees and appealing green spaces, it's an ideal spot for leisurely walks or spending quality time with family.

Free

Los Colomos Forest

Bosque Los Colomos is a popular destination in Guadalajara, offering a wide range of activities for visitors. This urban forest and natural park boasts walking paths, picnic areas, and striking landscaping. One of its main attractions is the well-maintained Japanese Garden, providing an authentic experience for those interested in the city's history. The park also features romantic bridges, a small dam, and various trails that are perfect for leisurely strolls or bike rides.

Free

Arena Coliseo de Guadalajara

Arena Coliseo de Guadalajara is a popular venue for lucha libre wrestling matches in Guadalajara. The Mexican form of theatrical freestyle wrestling features masked and colorfully costumed luchadores engaging in thrilling bouts to the cheers of an enthusiastic crowd. The atmosphere is filled with drama, choreography, dancing ring girls, and snack vendors offering a variety of treats.

Free

Guadalajara Metropolitan Park

Guadalajara Metropolitan Park sits within the Guadalajara Metropolitan Park area of Guadalajara, documented in local heritage registers for its civic or cultural role. Visitors approach from the main access road and combine the visit with neighbouring heritage buildings and public spaces in the district.

Free

Plaza de Artesanías

Plaza de Artesanías, located in Guerrero state, is a popular market in Zihuatanejo that attracts both tourists and locals. This covered market offers a variety of traditional handmade items such as pottery, sombreros, blankets, and other trinkets. Visitors can explore the quaint shops and restaurants while enjoying the opportunity to purchase unique treasures from the artisanal stores.

Free

Jardín Hidalgo

Nestled in the heart of Tlaquepaque, Jardin Hidalgo is a vibrant central square that serves as a tranquil retreat amidst the town's lively atmosphere. This beautifully landscaped garden features a striking statue of Miguel Hidalgo y Costilla and is adorned with benches and a charming fountain, making it an ideal spot to unwind under the shade of majestic trees. Surrounding this oasis are historic buildings, including the elegant San Pedro Tlaquepaque Parish, which adds to its picturesque allure.

Free

Parroquia San Pedro Apóstol

Parroquia San Pedro Apóstol is a Franciscan church in Tlaquepaque, completed in 1813 and featuring a mix of architectural styles from Byzantine to baroque. Situated on the main square in Centro, Jardin Hidalgo, this historic church offers visitors a glimpse into its rich history dating back to the 18th century.

Free

Mercado Benito Juárez

Nestled in the heart of Tlaquepaque, Mercado Benito Juárez is a vibrant local market that pays homage to one of Mexico's most revered presidents. Since its opening in 1979, this bustling hub has become a favorite among locals and visitors alike. As you stroll through its lively aisles, encounter an array of fresh produce artfully displayed alongside butchers skillfully preparing prime cuts right before your eyes.
