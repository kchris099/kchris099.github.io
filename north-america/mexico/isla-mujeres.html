Isla Mujeres

Isla Mujeres held Maya shrines linked to Ixchel, goddess of fertility and the moon, before Spanish sailors named the island for female statues they found. It later served fishers and occasional pirate traffic off the Yucatán coast. 20th-century ferry service from Cancún turned the narrow island into a day-trip and small-hotel destination. The town plaza, Caribbean shore, and Garrafón reefs sit on that Maya-to-resort sequence.
