Destination Guide — Yucatan & Caribbean

Explore Isla Mujeres

A Brief History

Revered in pre-Columbian times as a sanctuary for the Mayan goddess of childbirth, the island was later named by the Spanish for its abundant female statues. Shifting from a notorious pirate hideout to a quiet fishing village, it now thrives on its vibrant maritime heritage. This rich historical tapestry established a strong foundation that continues to shape the city's dynamic contemporary identity.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

La Isla Cancún

La Isla Cancún is a vibrant open-air shopping village boasting over 160 national and international stores, as well as a variety of dining and entertainment options. Situated in Cancun's Hotel Zone, this bustling complex features high-end retailers like Gucci, Louis Vuitton, Tiffany & Co., Rolex, and more. Its picturesque setting along the Nichupte Lagoon offers a charming atmosphere with Venetian-inspired design and scenic canals.

Free

Interactive Aquarium Cancún

The Interactive Aquarium Cancún is the only aquarium in Cancun, offering a unique and interactive experience for the whole family. With 13 enormous fish tanks, visitors can observe a wide variety of marine species including piranhas, eels, jellyfish, sea lions, dolphins, sharks and more. The Touch Area allows guests to interact with starfish, sea urchins and sea cucumbers.

Free

Plaza Las Americas

Plaza Las Americas is a bustling shopping center located in vibrant Cancun, offering a wide range of brand-name stores, a supermarket, food court, and cinema. It's the largest mall in Cancun and is popular among locals for its mix of international brands and traditional artisan shops. Visitors can enjoy souvenir shopping and experience an authentic local atmosphere while exploring the diverse retail options.

Free

Parque de las Palapas

Nestled in the heart of Cancun, Parque de las Palapas is a vibrant city park that offers a delightful escape into local culture. This lively space features playgrounds for children, shaded tables for relaxation, and an open-air stage that hosts various performances.

Free

Iglesia Cristo Rey

Iglesia Cristo Rey is a prominent Catholic church located in the center of Mazatlán. Built in the mid-20th century, the church features a distinctive, modern architectural style with a large, sweeping parabolic roof. It serves as an important spiritual center for the local community and stands out among the city's more traditional colonial buildings.

Free

Mercado 28

Mercado 28, also known as Market 28, is a bustling flea market in downtown Cancun that offers an array of authentic souvenirs and local goods. This long-running market spans an entire city block and features colorful stalls selling everything from leather goods to traditional Mexican clothing, pottery, lacquerware, and hand-made accessories. Visitors can immerse themselves in the vibrant local culture while enjoying street food delights at small eateries dotted throughout the market.

Free

Market 23

Market 23, also known as Mercado 23, is a bustling open-air market located in downtown Cancun. It offers an authentic local experience with a wide variety of fresh produce, meats, and handcrafted goods. While it does attract some tourists, the majority of its visitors are locals who come to shop for fresh ingredients and unique artisanal products.

Free

Parroquia de Cristo Resucitado

Parroquia de Cristo Resucitado is a contemporary Catholic church located in the Zona Hotelera of Cancun. The open-air design and lush greenery create a tranquil atmosphere for both English and Spanish masses. Construction began in 1995, transforming the original structure of four wooden poles and a metal roof into this central attraction. Visitors can also explore Parroquia de la Sagrada Familia and Parroquia de Corpus Christi nearby.

Free

Playa Langosta

Playa Langosta is a beautiful white-sand beach located at km 5 of the Hotel Zone in Cancun. It's known for its clear, shallow waters, making it perfect for families with children. The beach offers amenities such as a playground, public restrooms, and waterfront restaurants where enjoy delicious shrimp tacos. Additionally, there are play areas for kids including inflatables in the sea for an extra fee and a free children's play park near the entrance.

Free

Isla Mujeres

Isla Mujeres is a beautiful island located off the coast of Cancun, known for its beaches, vibrant coral reefs, and charming local atmosphere. Visitors can enjoy snorkeling, exploring the island's history at Punta Sur, or relaxing on Playa Norte, beaches in the region.

Free

Dolphin Discovery Isla Mujeres

Dolphin Discovery Isla Mujeres is a top-notch dolphin experience located just south of the island of Isla Mujeres, easily accessible via a 30-minute ferry ride from Cancun's Hotel Zone. The park offers an opportunity to swim and interact with dolphins, as well as encounter other captivating sea creatures like sea lions, turtles, and manatees.

Free

Tortugranja

Tortugranja, located on Isla Mujeres off the coast of Cancun's Hotel Zone, is a no-frills turtle farm and aquarium that offers an up-close look at various species of turtles. The small and simple facility features indoor and outdoor exhibits with tanks housing baby turtles as well as larger ones of different species. Visitors can also participate in turtle releases during nesting season from May to September. Additionally, there is a gift shop on-site for souvenirs.

Free

Zona Arqueológica de El Meco

Located just 30 minutes north of Cancun's main hotel zone, the Zona Arqueológica de El Meco is an ancient Mayan site that was once a thriving fishing village and port city. It features a pyramid called El Castillo, which stands at roughly 41 feet high and offers panoramic views of the Caribbean Sea and surrounding lagoons. The area is also inhabited by numerous iguanas, making it a unique experience for animal lovers.
