Explore Isla Mujeres

A Brief History

Isla Mujeres is in Mexico. Revered in pre-Columbian times as a sanctuary for the Mayan goddess of childbirth, the island was later named by the Spanish for its abundant female statues. Shifting from a notorious pirate hideout to a quiet fishing village, it now thrives on its active maritime heritage. Museums, markets, and street plans in Isla Mujeres still show layers from those periods.

La Isla Cancún

La Isla Cancún is a visitor site in Isla Mujeres, Mexico. Its setting along the Nichupte Lagoon offers a atmosphere with Venetian-inspired design and scenic canals. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest. La Isla Cancún remains part of the public visitor circuit in Isla Mujeres.

$16

Interactive Aquarium Cancún

The Interactive Aquarium Cancún is the only aquarium in Cancun, offering a unique and interactive experience for the whole family. With 13 enormous fish tanks, visitors can observe a wide variety of marine species including piranhas, eels, jellyfish, sea lions, dolphins, sharks and more. The Touch Area allows guests to interact with starfish, sea urchins and sea cucumbers.

Free

Plaza Las Americas

Plaza Las Americas is a busy shopping center located in active Cancun, offering a wide range of brand-name stores, a supermarket, food court, and cinema. It's the largest mall in Cancun and is popular among locals for its mix of international brands and traditional artisan shops. Visitors can enjoy souvenir shopping and experience an authentic local atmosphere while exploring the diverse retail options.

Free

Parque de las Palapas

Parque de las Palapas is a visitor site in Isla Mujeres, Mexico. Located Cancun, Parque de las Palapas is a active city park that offers a escape into local culture. Parque de las Palapas is a visitor site in Isla Mujeres, Mexico with public access during posted hours. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Free

Iglesia Cristo Rey

Iglesia Cristo Rey is a visitor site in Isla Mujeres, Mexico. Iglesia Cristo Rey is a prominent Catholic church located in the center of Mazatlán. It is an important spiritual center for the local community and stands out among the city's more traditional colonial buildings. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Free

Mercado 28

Mercado 28, also known as Market 28, is a visitor site in Isla Mujeres, Mexico. Mercado 28, also known as Market 28, is a busy flea market in downtown Cancun that offers an array of authentic souvenirs and local goods. Visitors can immerse themselves in the active local culture while enjoying street food delights at small eateries dotted throughout the market. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Free

Market 23

Market 23, also known as Mercado 23, is a busy open-air market located in downtown Cancun. It offers an authentic local experience with a wide variety of fresh produce, meats, and handcrafted goods. While it does attract some tourists, the majority of its visitors are locals who come to shop for fresh ingredients and unique artisanal products.

Free

Parroquia de Cristo Resucitado

Parroquia de Cristo Resucitado is a visitor site in Isla Mujeres, Mexico. Records associated with the site include 1995. Parroquia de Cristo Resucitado is a contemporary Catholic church located in the Zona Hotelera of Cancun. Construction began in 1995, transforming the original structure of four wooden poles and a metal roof into this central attraction. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Free

Playa Langosta

Playa Langosta is a white-sand beach located at km 5 of the Hotel Zone in Cancun. It's known for its clear, shallow waters, making it perfect for families with children. The beach offers amenities such as a playground, public restrooms, and waterfront restaurants where enjoy delicious shrimp tacos, there are play areas for kids including inflatables in the sea for an extra fee and a free children's play park near the entrance.

Free

Isla Mujeres

Isla Mujeres is a island located off the coast of Cancun, known for its beaches, active coral reefs, and local atmosphere. Visitors can enjoy snorkeling, exploring the island's history at Punta Sur, or relaxing on Playa Norte, beaches in the region.

$119

Dolphin Discovery Isla Mujeres

Dolphin Discovery Isla Mujeres is a well equipped dolphin experience located just south of the island of Isla Mujeres, easily accessible via a 30-minute ferry ride from Cancun's Hotel Zone. The park offers a chance to swim and interact with dolphins, as well as encounter other sea creatures like sea lions, turtles, and manatees.

Free

Tortugranja

Tortugranja is a visitor site in Isla Mujeres, Mexico. Tortugranja, located on Isla Mujeres off the coast of Cancun's Hotel Zone, is a no-frills turtle farm and aquarium that offers an up-close look at various species of turtles. Visitors can also participate in turtle releases during nesting season from May to September, there is a gift shop on-site for souvenirs. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

MX$75

Zona Arqueológica de El Meco

Zona Arqueológica de El Meco is a visitor site in Isla Mujeres, Mexico. Located just 30 minutes north of Cancun's main hotel zone, the Zona Arqueológica de El Meco is an ancient Mayan site that was once a growing fishing village and port city. The area is also inhabited by numerous iguanas, making it a unique experience for animal lovers. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.
