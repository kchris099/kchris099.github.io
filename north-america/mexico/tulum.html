Explore Tulum

A Brief History

Tulum was one of the last cities built and inhabited by the Maya, peaking between the thirteenth and fifteenth centuries. Uniquely situated on coastal cliffs overlooking the Caribbean Sea, it served as a major trading port for obsidian and jade. The city survived the initial Spanish conquest but was abandoned by the late sixteenth century. Today, Tulum is renowned for its well-preserved archaeological ruins and its transition into a popular eco-tourism destination.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

Tulum

Tulum is a world-renowned destination on the Riviera Maya, famous for its well-preserved Mayan ruins situated on a dramatic cliff overlooking the Caribbean Sea. The area has evolved into a major tourist hub, offering pristine white-sand beaches, boutique eco-resorts, and a vibrant dining and nightlife scene, while maintaining a bohemian, wellness-focused atmosphere.

Free

El Castillo

El Castillo (The Castle) is the largest and most prominent structure within the Tulum archaeological site. Perched on a 12-meter limestone cliff above the Caribbean Sea, it served as both a temple and a navigational lighthouse for Mayan trading canoes navigating the treacherous coastal reef system.

Free

Playa Pescadores

Playa Pescadores is a beautiful, public beach located just south of the Tulum ruins. It is characterized by its soft white sand, calm turquoise waters, and the presence of numerous small, colorful fishing boats. The beach offers a more relaxed, traditional atmosphere compared to the upscale beach clubs further south, and provides excellent views of the ruins.

Free

Laguna de Kaan Luum

Laguna Kaan Luum is a impressive, large cenote located south of Tulum, surrounded by dense jungle. The lagoon features a massive, deep blue sinkhole in its center, surrounded by shallow, light turquoise waters. It is a popular, tranquil spot for swimming, paddleboarding, and relaxing on the wooden piers, offering a quieter alternative to the coastal beaches.

Free

Casa Tortuga Tulum

Casa Tortuga is a popular nature park in Tulum featuring a network of four distinct cenotes (natural sinkholes). Visitors can take guided tours to swim and snorkel through the open-air and cavernous cenotes, exploring the clear, fresh water and impressive stalactite formations. The park provides a comprehensive and accessible cenote experience.

Free

Yax-Muul

Yax-Muul is an ecological park located near Tulum, offering guided tours of a pristine underground cenote system. Visitors can swim and snorkel in the crystal-clear waters of the subterranean caves, surrounded by dramatic limestone formations. The park emphasizes environmental conservation and provides a less crowded, immersive natural experience.

Free

Gran Cenote

Gran Cenote is one of the most famous and popular cenotes in the Tulum area. It features a large, open-air swimming area connected to a series of partially submerged caves adorned with stalactites and stalagmites. The clear, fresh water is ideal for snorkeling, allowing visitors to observe small fish and freshwater turtles in their natural habitat.

Free

Cenote Zacil-Ha

Cenote Zacil-Ha sits within the Gran Cenote area of Tulum, documented in local heritage registers for its civic or cultural role. Visitors approach from the main access road and combine the visit with neighbouring heritage buildings and public spaces in the district.

Free

Cenote Santa Cruz Tulum

Parque Dos Aguas is a delightful gem nestled in the heart of Tulum, offering an enchanting blend of relaxation and vibrant local culture. This park features expansive playgrounds that are part of a larger plaza, making it an ideal spot for families to enjoy various activities. As you wander through, discover food stalls serving up mouthwatering street snacks like elote lollipops and fried ice cream, especially lively in the evenings when vendors set up around 6 PM.

Free

Escultura Ven a la Luz

The 'Ven a la Luz' (Come into the Light) sculpture is a massive, iconic wooden artwork created by South African artist Daniel Popper. Originally part of an art festival, the sculpture depicts a woman opening her chest to reveal a lush, green interior. It has become one of the most photographed landmarks in Tulum's hotel zone.
