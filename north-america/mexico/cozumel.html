Explore Cozumel

A Brief History

A strongly sacred Mayan pilgrimage site dedicated to the moon goddess Ixchel, Cozumel was virtually abandoned following Spanish-introduced epidemics. The mid-20th-century arrival of global scuba divers resurrected the island, transforming it into a major Caribbean destination. Museums, markets, and street plans in Cozumel still show layers from those periods.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

Coral Reefs Monument

The Coral Reefs Monument is a prominent sculpture located on the waterfront promenade (malecón) of San Miguel de Cozumel. It depicts a pair of scuba divers exploring a coral reef, celebrating the island's global reputation as a premier diving destination. The monument is a popular meeting point and photo opportunity for visitors.

$11

Cozumel Musem

Cozumel Musem is a visitor site in Cozumel, Mexico. The Cozumel Museum (Museo de la Isla de Cozumel) provides a full overview of the island's natural environment, history, and culture. Located in a historic building on the waterfront, the exhibits cover the island's geological formation, its significance as a Mayan pilgrimage site, and its development into a modern tourist destination. The main structures and grounds are the principal points of interest.

Free

Atlantis Submarines Cozumel

Atlantis Submarines offers a unique underwater excursion in Cozumel, taking passengers up to 100 feet below the surface in a real, Coast Guard-certified submarine. The tour provides an accessible way for non-divers to experience the active marine life and coral formations of the Chankanaab reef system, including a sunken shipwreck.

Free

Dolphinaris Cozumel

Dolphinaris Cozumel sits within the Coral Reefs Monument area of Cozumel, documented in local heritage registers for its civic or cultural role. Visitors approach from the main access road and combine the visit with neighbouring heritage buildings and public spaces in the district.

MX$210

Zona Arqueológica San Gervasio

San Gervasio is the most significant Mayan archaeological site on the island of Cozumel. Historically, it was a major center for the worship of Ixchel, the goddess of fertility and childbirth, drawing pilgrims from across the Mayan world. Visitors can explore the ruins of several temples and plazas set within a dense jungle environment.

Free

Isla de la Pasión

Isla de la Pasión sits within the Zona Arqueológica San Gervasio area of Cozumel, documented in local heritage registers for its civic or cultural role. Visitors approach from the main access road and combine the visit with neighbouring heritage buildings and public spaces in the district.

Free

El Mirador

El Mirador is a scenic viewpoint located on the rugged, undeveloped eastern coast of Cozumel. The area is characterized by dramatic limestone rock formations carved by the powerful waves of the Caribbean Sea. It offers a stark, contrast to the calm, sandy beaches on the western side of the island.

Free

Paradise Beach Cozumel

Paradise Beach Cozumel is a beach in Cozumel, Mexico. Access is typically on foot from nearby roads or beachfront paths. Local maps identify Paradise Beach Cozumel within Cozumel, Mexico. Posted signs mark approaches and opening information for Paradise Beach Cozumel.

Free

Playa Uvas sits within the Playa Uvas area of Cozumel, documented in local heritage registers for its civic or cultural role. Visitors approach from the main access road and combine the visit with neighbouring heritage buildings and public spaces in the district.

$31

Chankanaab Beach Adventure Park

Chankanaab Beach Adventure Park is a major eco-park in Cozumel, located within the National Marine Park. It offers a wide variety of activities, including snorkeling in a protected lagoon, botanical gardens, a crocodile exhibition, and a replica Mayan village. The park provides a full, family-friendly introduction to the island's natural and local history.
