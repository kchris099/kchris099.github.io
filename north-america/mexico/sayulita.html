Destination Guide — Sayulita

Explore Sayulita

A Brief History

Established as a regional settlement beyond an older fortified core, the city developed through industrialization in the late 19th century, post-war reconstruction, and modern civic expansion that reshaped its public spaces, markets, and surrounding landscape. Restored religious buildings, civic buildings, and natural features now define the contemporary visitor experience.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

Sayulita Plaza

Sayulita Plaza is a vibrant and picturesque spot where immerse yourself in the local daily life. grab a 6-pack beer from a mini super tienda for just $2.50 and relax on the benches, taking in the colorful surroundings. During Halloween and Dia de Los Muertos, the plaza comes alive with holiday decorations, offering a unique experience to witness local traditions.

Free

Playa De Los Muertos

Playa De Los Muertos, also known as the Beach of the Dead, is located just south of Sayulita's main beach. The name comes from the cemetery that lies behind it, adorned with colorful graves decorated with surfboards. Despite its eerie name, it is a popular spot for Mexican families and offers a safer swimming experience compared to other beaches in the area.

Free

Mercado del Pueblo

Mercado del Pueblo, also known as Sayulita's Farmer's Market, has been showcasing the wonders of the region since 2009. This outdoor market operates from November through May and is a hub for local farmers and families offering high-quality natural products. travellers can explore a variety of stalls featuring organic produce, artisan goods, and even enjoy live music while sampling delicious treats.

Free

Sayulita Beach

Sayulita Beach is a popular destination with several sandy stretches to explore. The main beach, Playa Sayulita, is convenient but can get crowded. For a more peaceful experience, travellers can head to Playa de Los Muertos or venture to the neighboring village of San Pancho for Playa San Pancho. Mal Paso beach offers a secluded option about 20 minutes from the main beach.

Free

Playa Carrecitos

Playa Carricitos is a secluded beach near Sayulita, accessible by a semi-steep walk through the jungle. It offers a private and relaxing atmosphere, away from the crowds. The beach is not ideal for surfing in July but becomes great in November. travellers can enjoy sunsets and solitude at this natural beauty spot. Despite being a bit tricky to find, it's worth the effort as it provides an opportunity to unwind and appreciate the welcoming community around sunset fires.
