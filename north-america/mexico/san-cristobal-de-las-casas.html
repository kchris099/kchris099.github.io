San Cristobal De Las Casas

San Cristóbal de las Casas was founded in 1528 in the Chiapas highlands as a Spanish colonial city amid Tzotzil and Tzeltal Maya communities. Baroque churches, arcaded plazas, and a grid of coloured houses mark that era. It served as state capital at intervals and became a focus of indigenous politics and tourism after the late 20th century. Markets and nearby Maya villages still frame highland Chiapas life around the old core.

Templo de Nuestra Señora de Guadalupe

Templo de Nuestra Señora de Guadalupe sits above San Cristóbal de las Casas at the top of a stairway of seventy-nine steps. The hilltop church looks toward surrounding mountains, and evenings often draw people to the terrace for the sunset. The climb is shorter than other sanctuary approaches in town yet still lifts visitors above the grid.

Catedral de San Cristóbal de las Casas

Catedral de San Cristóbal de las Casas fronts the main plaza of the highland city, with origins traced to the sixteenth century and a foundation date often given as 1528. Painted Baroque details and a broad facade face the zócalo. The cathedral remains the civic-religious center of the colonial street plan.

Plaza de la Paz

Cathedral facades and civic buildings frame Plaza de la Paz at the center of San Cristobal de las Casas. The open space supports ceremonies, protests, performances, vendors and everyday movement between the cathedral and adjacent Zocalo; artisan stalls may appear during events.

04 AdmissionFree

Mercado de la Caridad y Santo Domingo

Around mid-sixteenth-century Santo Domingo church and its distinctive mortar facade, Mercado de la Caridad sells souvenirs, clothing, and handicrafts. Artisans from Mexico and Guatemala display work, sometimes making pieces on the spot. Market lanes press against the church compound walls.

16.7406° N / 92.6378° W

Mercado Viejo Zinacantan

If visitors are looking to immerse oneself in the active local culture of San Cristobal de las Casas, a visit to Mercado Viejo is an must. This busy market, also known as Jose Castillo Tielemans market, offers a array of fresh produce, meats, and spices that will inspire any home cook.

El Arcotete

A river has cut a natural arch through limestone at El Arcotete, a forest recreation area outside San Cristobal. Trails, caves, picnic or camping areas and privately operated activities may surround the formation. Water levels, equipment and access change with weather and management, while the stone arch and wooded valley remain the defining natural landmarks.

16.7260° N / 92.5843° W

Grutas Del Mamut

Grutas del Mamut form a natural cave system near San Cristóbal de las Casas, set inside a recreation park. Passages open onto chambers large enough for guided walks, while the surrounding grounds add ziplines and horseback routes. The site pairs underground geology with outdoor activity space above the entrances.

16.7432° N / 92.5861° W

Mercado de Dulces y Artesanías Ámbar

Near San Francisco Church, this indoor market gathers regional sweets, amber jewelry, textiles, clothing, crafts and souvenirs in central San Cristobal. The mix reflects both local production and wider tourist trade, so material origin and authenticity vary by stall.

09 AdmissionFree

Arco del Carmen

Arco del Carmen is an eighteenth-century gateway that once marked an entrance into San Cristóbal. Colorful papel picado often hangs across the arch through the year, thickening around Day of the Dead and Christmas. The structure still frames a busy approach into the historic streets beyond.

16.7339° N / 92.6383° W

10 AdmissionFree

Iglesia de San Cristóbalito

Iglesia de San Cristóbalito is a small red-and-white church located on top of a hill, offering panoramic views of the city. To reach the church, visitors must climb around 200 tree-lined steps. The area surrounding the church is home to several famous museums, including Centro de Textiles del Mundo Maya, Museo del Ambar, and Museo Mesoamericano del Jade. It's recommended to visit during the day due to safety concerns at night.

16.7343° N / 92.6418° W
