Puebla

Puebla was founded in 1531 as a Spanish grid city east of volcan Popocatépetl. Talavera tile facades, cathedral towers, and culinary traditions record viceregal trade between Veracruz port and Mexico City on the central plateau. Cholula pyramid views and Talavera workshops extend viceregal tile craft across the central volcanic belt. Walking routes through the historic centre help visitors relate these monuments to wider patterns of settlement and civic life in Mexico.
