Destination Guide — Central Mexico

Explore Puebla

A Brief History

Puebla was founded in 1531 as a Spanish grid city east of volcan Popocatépetl. Talavera tile facades, cathedral towers, and culinary traditions record viceregal trade between Veracruz port and Mexico City on the central plateau. Cholula pyramid views and Talavera workshops extend viceregal tile craft across the central volcanic belt. Walking routes through the historic centre help visitors relate these monuments to wider patterns of settlement and civic life in Mexico.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

Zócalo de Puebla

Zócalo de Puebla is a lively public square at the heart of the city, offering a atmosphere with its fountain, benches, and surrounding eateries and shops. It's a popular spot for people-watching, from students in academic gowns celebrating graduations to families enjoying leisurely strolls. The square is surrounded by historical buildings and green spaces, making it an ideal starting point for exploring the city.

Free

Catedral de Puebla

Catedral de Puebla is a Roman Catholic cathedral located in Mexico, known for its ornate interior featuring several chapels and an octagonal altar. The architecture is a blend of Herreresque-Renaissance and early baroque styles, with construction dating back to the 16th century. The cathedral's impressive exterior includes two towering structures that are the tallest in Mexico.

MX$458

Africam Safari

Africam Safari is a wildlife conservation zoo in Puebla, Mexico, spanning 500 acres of diverse habitats. Founded by Carlos Camacho Espiritu in the 1970s, it houses over 450 animal species from around the world. Visitors can drive through open-air enclosures or join guided tours to see elephants, giraffes, rhinos, tigers, and more. The park offers educational experiences like tracking animals and picnicking with giraffes.

MX$80

Estrella de Puebla

Estrella de Puebla is a massive observation wheel located in a linear park, offering panoramic views of the city's landmarks. Puebla itself is recognized by UNESCO as a World Heritage Site, showcasing its rich cultural and historical significance through its well-preserved colonial architecture and cobblestone streets. The attraction stands as a symbol of modernity within this historic setting, providing visitors with privileged views of the surrounding area.

MX$85

Chapel of the Rosary, St. Dominic’s Temple

Nestled within the historic Templo de Santo Domingo, the Capilla del Rosario is a 17th-century chapel that captivates visitors with its opulent interior adorned in 24k gold stucco. Often referred to as The Golden House, this exquisite space showcases intricate details crafted from Talavera and onyx, creating an atmosphere of sheer elegance.

MX$95

Museo Amparo

Museo Amparo, established in 1991, is a beautifully designed museum located in two colonial buildings combined to showcase an exceptional private collection of pre-Columbian and Colonial art. The museum features over 3,500 pieces of pre-Columbian, 19th-century viceroyal, and contemporary art.

Free

Alley of the frogs

The Alley of the Frogs, also known as Callejon de los Sapos, is a charming and street located in Puebla's Historic Center. It is famous for its antique shops, art galleries, and lively atmosphere. The cobblestone alleyway is adorned with colorful murals and sculptures, creating a unique and inviting ambiance. Visitors can explore the diverse selection of shops offering antiques, artisanal goods, and furniture.

MX$210

Zona Arqueológica de Cholula

The Zona Arqueológica de Cholula is a remarkable ancient Mesoamerican site featuring the impressive Piramide Tepanapa, which resembles a hill rather than a typical pyramid. The area includes extensive tunnels that run through the structure, offering visitors an eerie and fascinating experience. This archaeological zone encompasses excavated areas around the pyramid and its underground tunnels, accessible through a tunnel on the north side.

Free

Santuario de la Virgen de los Remedios

Perched atop the ancient Piramide Tepanapa, the Santuario de la Virgen de los Remedios is a church that offers views of Cholula. This structure, adorned with colorful tiles and built in the 16th century, stands as a fascinating symbol of cultural convergence. Interestingly, it was constructed before the Spanish fully recognized that they were building on a site that once housed a pagan temple.

MX$70

Regional Museum of Cholula

The Regional Museum of Cholula, located at the base of the Great Pyramid, is a cultural gem showcasing Mesoamerican art and history. Housed in a former psychiatric hospital, it offers a diverse collection of alebrijes and exhibits on the region's geography and volcanic landscape. Although some sections may be closed for renovation, visitors can still enjoy informative displays that blend indigenous histories with colorful depictions of alebrijes.

Free

Container City

Container City is a and innovative drinking destination in Puebla, Mexico. Comprised of interconnected shipping containers transformed into bars, restaurants, and shops, it's a popular spot for young locals. The area offers an array of experiences from themed cocktail bars to urban clothing stores. While it's known for its lively nightlife scene with clubs and hook-up spots, it also caters to families earlier in the evening.

Free

Parian market

The Parian Market, also known as the Antigua Plaza de San Roque, is a bustling marketplace located in the historic center of Puebla. With 112 stands offering a wide variety of traditional clothing, pottery, jewelry, and other local wares, it's the largest handicrafts market in the city. Visitors can explore an array of authentic Mexican snacks and immerse themselves in Puebla's traditional craftsmanship by browsing through stalls selling Talavera pottery.

MX$150

Teatro Principal

Teatro Principal is a gem nestled in the heart of Puebla, showcasing the city's rich cultural heritage. This 18th-century theater stands as a testament to exquisite antique architecture, making it one of Latin America's most beautiful venues. Visitors can immerse themselves in a variety of performances, from popular Mexican plays to concerts that resonate with history and artistry.

Free

Paseo San Francisco

Paseo San Francisco is a historic walkway in Puebla, Mexico, known for its traditional restaurants and beautiful gardens. It's believed to be the birthplace of chalupas, a delightful Mexican specialty. The area offers colonial architecture, art installations, and a leisurely stroll through Puebla's past and present. Additionally, it features a shopping center with various dining options and coffee shops alongside cinemas on the top floor.

MX$199

Acuario Michin Puebla

Acuario Michin Puebla is a delightful destination for families and marine life enthusiasts alike. Opened in December 2019, this modern aquarium features over 50 exhibits that house more than 20,000 animals, including sharks, rays, turtles, and even goats! Visitors can engage with interactive touch pools and enjoy educational displays that make learning about aquatic ecosystems fun for all ages.

Free

Templo de Santa María Tonantzintla

Templo de Santa María Tonantzintla is a in Puebla, with its beautifully preserved architecture and baroque decorations. The exterior features colorful tiles and primitive icons, while the interior is uniquely Mexican. This Catholic/Aztec church holds historical significance as the first where native Americans portrayed their interpretation of the gospel.

MX$150

Zoo Parque Loro Puebla

Zoo Parque Loro Puebla is a charming little zoo that has been delighting visitors since 1999. Nestled in a compact area, it offers an intimate experience with hundreds of birds, reptiles, and mammals, including some endangered species. Families will find it to be a perfect spot for a short outing; easily explore the park in about an hour. During your visit, encounter friendly staff who are dedicated to the well-being of the animals.

Free

Mercado de Sabores

Mercado de Sabores is a bustling public market where explore stalls filled with textiles, intricate Talavera pottery, and other handcrafted goods that showcase Puebla's cultural heritage. The market also offers a variety of local delicacies such as cemitas, tacos arabes, and artisanal cheeses that highlight the region's culinary traditions. Located near La Paz district, it provides an opportunity to discover designer boutiques and indigenous fashion pieces.

MX$100

Teleférico Puebla

The Puebla Cable Car (Teleférico) offers a scenic aerial ride connecting the historic Forts of Loreto and Guadalupe with the Zaragoza exhibition area. The modern gondolas provide passengers with expansive, panoramic views of the city of Puebla, the surrounding valley, and the distant, snow-capped Popocatépetl and Iztaccíhuatl volcanoes.

MX$60

Historic Zone of the Forts

The Historic Zone of the Forts in Puebla, Mexico is a captivating park district that showcases centuries-old fortifications and museums dedicated to military and civic history. This area offers a blend of natural beauty and historical significance, with lush gardens surrounding well-preserved forts and military installations. Visitors can immerse themselves in the region's cultural heritage while exploring the scenic landscapes and engaging in recreational activities.

Free

Mirador de Los Fuertes

Mirador de Los Fuertes is a location to visit with friends or a loved one, offering a view of the city of Puebla as the sun sets and the city lights up. From this hilltop spot, visitors can admire the historic Loreto fort, which now houses a museum providing insight into Mexico's history, including the Battle for independence fought against Napoleon III's French army in 1862.

MX$100

Teleferico Puebla

Teleferico Puebla sits within the Teleférico Puebla area of Puebla, documented in local heritage registers for its civic or cultural role. Visitors approach from the main access road and combine the visit with neighbouring heritage buildings and public spaces in the district.

Free

Mercado municipal San pedro cholula

The Mercado Municipal San Pedro Cholula is a hub that beautifully retains its traditional charm. Here, find women selling an array of seeds, flowers, and herbs right from the floor. On Wednesdays and Sundays, the market transforms into a lively tianguis with street vendors joining in, attracting locals from surrounding communities eager to buy and sell their goods. This market is renowned for its locally sourced products like fresh fruits, vegetables, and exquisite flowers.

Free

Centro San Pedro Cholula

The historic center of San Pedro Cholula is a vibrant district characterized by its colonial architecture, bustling markets, and the massive Great Pyramid of Cholula. The area features numerous historic churches, including the prominent Convento de San Gabriel, and offers a deep dive into the region's complex pre-Columbian and Spanish colonial history.

Free

Convento de San Gabriel Arcángel

Nestled in the heart of Cholula, known as the City of Churches, the Convento de San Gabriel Arcángel stands as a testament to the region's rich history and cultural fusion. Built in the mid-1500s atop ancient ruins dedicated to Quetzalcoatl, this expansive complex features not only a bustling church but also an impressive Franciscan library and a Royal Chapel surrounded by serene courtyards.

Free

Mercado Municipal La Acocota

Mercado Municipal La Acocota is a lively and colorful covered market that embodies the essence of Mexican culture. Here, find an array of vendors offering fresh produce, local ingredients, and delightful meals. One standout is Maria Ximello's booth, where she serves up traditional dishes like chunky lamb shank barbacoa in a rich guasmole de zancarron broth. The atmosphere is warm and inviting, perfect for enjoying a meal with friends or family.

MX$30

Parque Ecológico

Parque Ecológico is a sprawling 58-hectare city park located in the northeastern suburbs of Puebla. It offers a variety of activities for visitors, including bike trails, sports facilities, playgrounds, an aviary, and botanical gardens. The park features lakes and extensive woodlands providing plenty of shady spots to relax. Visitors can enjoy running circuits, basketball courts, and ramps for skateboarding.

Free

Cholula Parrish of Saint Andrew

The charming Cholula Parrish of Saint Andrew, although temporarily closed, is eagerly anticipated to reopen soon. Its magnificence is particularly striking during the radiant sunset. This historical site brims with rich traditions and cultural significance. The architectural beauty of the church and its surroundings adds to the appeal of this remarkable location.
