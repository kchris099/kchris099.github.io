Explore Palenque

A Brief History

Palenque was a major Maya city-state that flourished in the seventh century under the rule of K'inich Janaab' Pakal. It is renowned for its sophisticated architectural design and extensive hieroglyphic inscriptions, which have provided crucial insights into Maya history and mythology. The city was abandoned in the eighth century and reclaimed by the jungle until its excavation in the modern era. Today, Palenque is a UNESCO World Heritage site, celebrated for its elegant temples and intricate stucco reliefs.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

Zona Arqueológica Palenque

Zona Arqueológica Palenque is a renowned archaeological site and tourist attraction, featuring the ruins of an ancient Maya city-state. The site is set amidst dense jungle, with hundreds of ruined buildings spread over 15 sq km. The exquisite Maya architecture was built without metal tools, pack animals or the wheel. Nearby attractions include the famous Agua Azul Falls and a village-style resort with traditional decor, spa facilities, and a traditional Maya steam room.

Free

Palenque Site Museum "Alberto Ruz Lhuillier"

The Palenque Site Museum, named after the archaeologist who discovered the tomb of Pakal the Great, is located near the entrance of the Palenque archaeological zone. It houses a significant collection of Mayan artifacts excavated from the site, including intricate stucco panels, jade jewelry, and a replica of Pakal's sarcophagus. The museum provides essential historical and cultural context for understanding the ancient city.

Free

Parque Central Palenque

Nestled within the enchanting ruins of Palenque, the Templo de La Calavera captivates visitors with its intriguing architecture and rich history. This remarkable structure, part of a larger complex that includes the Temple of Inscriptions, is shrouded in mystery and evokes thoughts about its past functions. Although climbing to the top is no longer permitted, still admire its impressive skull carvings from ground level.

Free

Cabeza Maya

The Cabeza Maya is a prominent, large-scale sculpture of a Mayan head located at a major traffic roundabout in the modern town of Palenque. It serves as a welcoming landmark for visitors arriving to explore the nearby archaeological site, symbolizing the region's deep connection to its indigenous heritage and history.

Free

Aluxes Ecopark

Parroquia Santo Domingo de Guzmán is a historical church located in the town of Santo Domingo de Chiapas, Mexico. The church's origins date back to 1746 when Spanish priest Pedro Lorenzo de la Nada discovered mysterious stone structures with unfamiliar architecture in the area. Despite some neglect, the church remains well-located and serves as a place for various religious ceremonies including weddings and masses held from Wednesday to Sunday.
