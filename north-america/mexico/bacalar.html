Explore Bacalar

A Brief History

Inhabited by the Maya long before the arrival of Spanish conquistadors, Bacalar was heavily fortified in the 18th century to repel persistent pirate attacks. Its historic lakeside fortress remains a central monument in a town transitioning from a colonial frontier to a tranquil retreat. This continuous historical development established a lasting framework that strongly informs the area's modern cultural evolution.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

Canal de los Piratas

The Canal de los Piratas (Pirates' Channel) is a narrow, shallow waterway connecting the Bacalar Lagoon to the Hondo River. Historically used by pirates to access the town, the channel is now a popular tourist destination known for its striking, multi-toned blue waters and mineral-rich mud, which visitors often apply to their skin for its purported exfoliating properties.

Free

Balneario Ejidal Mágico Bacalar

Balneario Ejidal Mágico Bacalar is a popular, community-run public beach club situated on the shores of the Bacalar Lagoon. It offers accessible facilities, including wooden docks, water slides, and palapas for shade. The site provides an affordable and family-friendly environment for swimming and enjoying the dramatic, clear waters of the 'Lagoon of Seven Colors.'

Free

Fuerte de San Felipe de Bacalar

Fuerte de San Felipe de Bacalar is a sturdy stone fort built in 1729 to protect the town from Caribbean pirates. The fortress, located on the edge of Bacalar's tranquil lagoon, offers views over the shimmering turquoise waters. Constructed with volcanic and marine rocks as well as limestone, it stands as a testament to the town's history and resilience against Dutch, French, and English invaders.

Free

Letras Bacalar

The 'Letras Bacalar' is a large, colorful, freestanding sign spelling out the name of the town, located prominently in the central plaza. Decorated with vibrant motifs representing the local culture and natural environment, the sign serves as a quintessential photo opportunity for tourists visiting the Pueblo Mágico, symbolizing their arrival at the famous lagoon destination.

Free

Zócalo

The Zócalo, or Plaza Principal, is the historic central square of Bacalar. It is a tranquil, tree-shaded park surrounded by local shops, restaurants, and the imposing Fort of San Felipe. The plaza serves as the social heart of the town, frequently hosting local markets, cultural events, and providing a relaxed gathering space for residents and visitors.

Free

Laguna de los 7 colores

The Laguna de los 7 Colores (Lagoon of Seven Colors) is the defining natural feature of Bacalar, renowned for its striking, crystal-clear waters that display varying shades of blue. The distinct colors are created by the differing depths of the lagoon and its white limestone bottom. It is a premier destination for swimming, kayaking, and eco-tourism in the region.

Free

Balneario Municipal El Aserradero

Balneario Municipal El Aserradero is a public pier located in the town center, offering free access to the beautiful Bacalar lagoon. Visitors can relax on wooden docks and under straw palapas for shade. There are some amenities such as a pool for kids and shaded areas to sit and unwind. The pier leads to the bathing area, providing easy access to the warm waters of the lagoon. While it can get crowded on weekends, early mornings offer a more serene experience.

Free

DayTour Bacalar

DayTour Bacalar is a prominent local tour operator specializing in guided boat and pontoon excursions across the Bacalar Lagoon. Their tours typically cover the major highlights of the lagoon, including the Cenote Negro, the Pirates' Channel, and Bird Island. The guided experiences focus on the unique ecology and history of the region, providing a comprehensive overview of the natural wonder.

Free

Cenote Esmeralda

Cenote Esmeralda is one of the deepest cenotes located within the Bacalar Lagoon. Known for its intense, dark blue waters, it creates a striking contrast with the surrounding shallow, turquoise lagoon. The cenote is a popular stop for boat tours, offering visitors a unique opportunity to swim and observe the dramatic underwater drop-off.

Free

Cenote Azul

Cenote Azul is a natural wonder located in the Yucatan Peninsula, known for its crystal-clear waters and impressive depth of 90 meters. Visitors can enjoy swimming and snorkeling in this picturesque cenote, surrounded by lush greenery and serene beauty. The site also features a restaurant with regional specialties made from fresh ingredients, as well as two swings for a touch of adventure.
