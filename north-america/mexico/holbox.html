Holbox

A barrier-island fishing community occasionally sheltered pirates and refugees of the Caste War. Recent decades turned Holbox toward low-rise tourism under ecological limits. Whale-shark seasons and sand streets, not colonial plazas, define its historical turn from isolation to destination. Golf carts replaced cars under local rules meant to keep the sand streets quiet.

Hoyo negro Yalahau

The Yalahau Cenote (Hoyo Negro) is a natural freshwater spring located on the mainland near Isla Holbox. Historically used as a fresh water source for pirates and fishermen, it is now a popular tourist destination. Visitors can swim in the cool, clear water, which is surrounded by dense mangroves and local wildlife.

Holbox Ferry

The Holbox Ferry provides the primary transportation link between the mainland port of Chiquilá and Isla Holbox. The short, scenic crossing takes approximately 30 minutes, traversing the shallow, protected waters of the Yalahau Lagoon. The ferry service is essential for both locals and tourists accessing the car-free island.

Letras Holbox

The 'Letras Holbox' is a large, colorful sign spelling out the name of the island, located on the main beach near the town center. It is a typical photo spot for visitors, symbolizing their arrival at the laid-back, tropical destination. The sign is often surrounded by the island's characteristic shallow, turquoise waters.

Playa Holbox

Playa Holbox is a visitor site in Holbox, Mexico. Playa Holbox is the expansive main beach stretching along the northern coast of the island. It is known for its very shallow, calm, and warm waters, which extend far out into the Gulf of Mexico. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Isla Holbox

Isla Holbox is a visitor site in Holbox, Mexico. Isla Holbox is a small, car-free island located off the northern coast of the Yucatán Peninsula, separated from the mainland by a shallow lagoon. It is known for relaxed, bohemian atmosphere, sandy unpaved streets, and abundant wildlife, including flamingos and seasonal whale sharks. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Playa Punta Cocos

Playa Punta Cocos is a visitor site in Holbox, Mexico. Punta Cocos is a secluded, pristine beach located at the western tip of Isla Holbox. The area is largely undeveloped, offering a peaceful retreat for swimming, relaxing in hammocks, and observing the diverse birdlife of the surrounding nature reserve. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

07 AdmissionFree

Bioluminiscencia

Bioluminiscencia is a visitor site in Holbox. The bioluminescence phenomenon in the waters around Isla Holbox is a major natural attraction. During certain times of the year, particularly on dark, moonless nights, microscopic plankton in the water emit a bright blue glow when disturbed. Visitors can take guided nighttime boat or kayak tours to experience this mesmerizing natural light show.

21.5084° N / 87.3926° W

08 AdmissionFree

Punta Mosquito

Punta Mosquito is a remote, expansive sandbank located at the eastern end of Isla Holbox. Accessible by a long walk through shallow water during low tide, the area is a protected nature reserve. It is one of the top locations on the island for bird watching, particularly for observing large flocks of pink flamingos in their natural habitat.
