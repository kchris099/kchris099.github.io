Explore Holbox

A Brief History

A historically isolated fishing community built on a barrier island, Holbox was a sanctuary for pirates and later a refuge during the Caste War. Its modern transition into a fiercely protected ecological haven highlights a careful balance between natural preservation and tourism. Today, this complex historical evolution remains significantly embedded in the civic architecture and vibrant daily life of the community.

Top Sights & Activities

MX$200

Hoyo negro Yalahau

The Yalahau Cenote (Hoyo Negro) is a natural freshwater spring located on the mainland near Isla Holbox. Historically used as a fresh water source for pirates and fishermen, it is now a popular tourist destination. Visitors can swim in the cool, clear water, which is surrounded by dense mangroves and local wildlife.

MX$330

Holbox Ferry

The Holbox Ferry provides the primary transportation link between the mainland port of Chiquilá and Isla Holbox. The short, scenic crossing takes approximately 30 minutes, traversing the shallow, protected waters of the Yalahau Lagoon. The ferry service is essential for both locals and tourists accessing the car-free island.

Free

Letras Holbox

The 'Letras Holbox' is a large, colorful sign spelling out the name of the island, located on the main beach near the town center. It is a quintessential photo spot for visitors, symbolizing their arrival at the laid-back, tropical destination. The sign is often surrounded by the island's characteristic shallow, turquoise waters.

Free

Playa Holbox

Playa Holbox is the expansive main beach stretching along the northern coast of the island. It is renowned for its incredibly shallow, calm, and warm waters, which extend far out into the Gulf of Mexico. The beach features a relaxed atmosphere, with numerous rustic beach clubs, hammocks, and opportunities for kiteboarding.

Free

Isla Holbox

Isla Holbox is a small, car-free island located off the northern coast of the Yucatán Peninsula, separated from the mainland by a shallow lagoon. It is famous for its relaxed, bohemian atmosphere, sandy unpaved streets, and abundant wildlife, including flamingos and seasonal whale sharks. The island offers a tranquil alternative to the larger resort towns of the Riviera Maya.

Free

Playa Punta Cocos

Punta Cocos is a secluded, pristine beach located at the western tip of Isla Holbox. It is known for its tranquil environment, shallow waters, and excellent sunset views. The area is largely undeveloped, offering a peaceful retreat for swimming, relaxing in hammocks, and observing the diverse birdlife of the surrounding nature reserve.

Free

Bioluminiscencia

The bioluminescence phenomenon in the waters around Isla Holbox is a major natural attraction. During certain times of the year, particularly on dark, moonless nights, microscopic plankton in the water emit a bright blue glow when disturbed. Visitors can take guided nighttime boat or kayak tours to experience this mesmerizing natural light show.

Free

Punta Mosquito

Punta Mosquito is a remote, expansive sandbank located at the eastern end of Isla Holbox. Accessible by a long walk through shallow water during low tide, the area is a protected nature reserve. It is one of the top locations on the island for bird watching, particularly for observing large flocks of pink flamingos in their natural habitat.
