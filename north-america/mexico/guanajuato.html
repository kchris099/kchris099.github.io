Guanajuato

Eighteenth-century silver veins paid for baroque churches and a university in a steep ravine city. Independence conspiracies used those same alleys. Subterranean streets cut for flood control and the Cervantino festival venues belong to later phases of a mining capital that never sprawled onto a flat grid. The mummy museum and Callejón del Beso folklore are later tourist sideshows of the mining city.

Monumento Al Pipila

A massive stone figure of El Pipila stands above Guanajuato, commemorating the miner traditionally credited with helping insurgents breach the Alhondiga de Granaditas in 1810. The monument dates from the twentieth century and overlooks the city's dense valley. Stairs, roads, and the funicular reach the terrace, where historical memory and urban views share equal importance.

Funicular

Guanajuato's short funicular climbs from behind Teatro Juarez to the terrace near the Pipila monument. The steep track provides public transport between the historic center and hillside viewpoint, with rooftops and narrow alleys appearing during the ascent. Operating hours, queues and maintenance affect access, while clear weather improves the view from the upper station.

03 AdmissionFree

Teatro Principal

One of Guanajuato's historic theatrical sites, Teatro Principal has been rebuilt and adapted several times since its eighteenth-century origins. The present auditorium supports university productions, music, dance, festivals, and public events within the historic center.

21.0158° N / 101.2518° W

04 AdmissionFree

Basílica Colegiata de Nuestra Señora de Guanajuato

A yellow-and-red Baroque facade faces Guanajuato's Plaza de la Paz at the Collegiate Basilica of Our Lady of Guanajuato. Mining wealth supported seventeenth-century construction, and the interior houses a revered wooden image of the Virgin traditionally connected with Spain.

21.0165° N / 101.2533° W

05 AdmissionFree

Templo de la Compañía de Jesús Oratorio de San Felipe Neri

An elaborate pink-stone facade marks the former Jesuit church now known as Templo de la Compania or Oratorio de San Felipe Neri. Construction began in the eighteenth century, while a later dome changed the city skyline after the original collapsed. Altars, paintings, a broad nave, and university connections reflect Guanajuato's religious and educational history.

21.0174° N / 101.2525° W

06 AdmissionFree

Cjon. del Beso

Cjon. Del Beso, also known as the Alley of the Kiss, is a narrow alleyway located next to Plaza de los Angeles in Guanajuato, Mexico. This famous spot is steeped in a tragic love story that draws countless couples to visit and share a kiss on its third stair for guaranteed 7 years of happiness according to local legend. The close proximity of the walls and balconies creates an intimate atmosphere that adds to the allure of this magical place.

21.0163° N / 101.2564° W

07 AdmissionFree

Mercado Hidalgo

A 1910s cast-iron hall that began as a train station now holds Mercado Hidalgo's food stalls, regional specialties, straw baskets, and hats. Named for priest Miguel Hidalgo, the market leans on cooked dishes as much as dry goods. Crowds thicken around midday meal service.

21.0175° N / 101.2581° W

08 AdmissionMX$145

Alhóndiga de Granaditas Regional Museum

Built as a fortified grain store shortly before the independence uprising, the Alhondiga de Granaditas became the scene of a major insurgent assault in September 1810. The massive stone building now houses a regional museum covering archaeology, colonial society, independence, and Mexican history. Murals and memorial spaces interpret the event; the Pipila statue stands separately on the hill above.

21.0188° N / 101.2578° W

09 AdmissionFree

Shrine of Christ the King

The Shrine of Christ the King crowns a hill above Silao with a large statue of Christ visible across the Guanajuato plain. Approach roads climb to terraces overlooking Silao and distant Guanajuato city. Pilgrims and sightseeing drivers share the summit parking and chapel grounds.

21.0118° N / 101.3689° W

10 AdmissionFree

Bicentennial Park Silao

Created for Mexico's 2010 bicentennial commemorations, Guanajuato Bicentennial Park occupies a large site near Silao. Exhibition halls, plazas, monuments and cultural installations support temporary shows, state events and educational programs. The broad modern complex has changing uses, so the experience depends on which buildings and exhibitions are operating. Large distances separate some exhibition areas.
