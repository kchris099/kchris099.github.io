Explore Taxco

A Brief History

taxco grew as a regional centre beyond an older fortified core, shaped by nineteenth-century commerce and post-war reconstruction that reshaped its public spaces, religious buildings, and surrounding landscape. Restored civic halls, market streets, and natural features now define the contemporary visitor experience for travellers exploring Mexico today.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

Cristo Rey. Cristo Monumental.

Cristo Rey, also known as Cristo Monumental, is a must-visit spot in Taxco for city views. Perched on a hilltop, it offers a 360-degree panoramic view of the city. While some visitors hike up to reach this viewpoint, others opt for a short taxi ride. However, safety concerns have been raised due to some visitors standing on the fence for better pictures.

Free

Zocalo, Taxco

Nestled in the heart of Taxco, the Zocalo, officially known as Plaza Borda, serves as the vibrant centerpiece of this charming town. This picturesque main plaza is surrounded by an array of delightful restaurants, unique shops—especially silver boutiques—and historical landmarks. A must-visit is Casa Borda, a significant cultural hub that offers lessons in fine arts and languages.

Free

Santa Prisca de Taxco

Santa Prisca de Taxco stands as a testament to baroque architecture, captivating visitors with its facade and rich history. Built in 1758, this church is a highlight of the town, showcasing an impressive contrast between its ornate belfries and the elegantly simple nave. The rose-colored stone glows beautifully under sunlight, particularly around the oval bas-relief of Christ's baptism that adorns its entrance.

Free

Museum of Religious Art Taxco, Casa Humboldt

Nestled in the charming town of Taxco de Alarcon, the Museum of Religious Art Taxco, also known as Casa Humboldt, is a delightful blend of history and artistry. Housed in a beautifully restored baroque-style building that once served as the residence for renowned naturalist Alexander von Humboldt during his brief visit in 1803, this museum offers visitors an intriguing glimpse into 18th-century religious art and artifacts.

Free

Casa Spratling

Casa Spratling - S Caffecito is a charming restaurant located in the former residence of William Spratling, an American who once lived in Taxco, Mexico. The ambiance is described as homey and inviting, with friendly and accommodating staff. Guests recommend trying the French press coffee, huanzontle, Italian dishes, and other delectable offerings. The owner, Violante, is known for her warm hospitality and provides tips and recommendations to enhance the dining experience.

Free

Former convent of San Bernardino of Siena

The Former Convent of San Bernardino of Siena is a captivating historical site nestled in the heart of Taxco, a charming Pueblo Mágico. This convent played a significant role during the War of Independence, notably as the location where Iturbide drafted the Plan of Iguala, uniting royalist forces and insurgents. Visitors can enjoy free entry to this beautiful church, which is conveniently located just 300 meters from the city center and easily accessible for those with mobility challenges.

Free

Mercado Tetitlán

Mercado Tetitlán is a traditional market in Taxco that offers an authentic and undiluted Mexican experience. It's just a short walk from the center and provides an array of delicious, affordable food options. The market also features handcrafted items, particularly silver jewelry, in the old Spanish colonial style. Visitors can explore numerous small shops offering local products like vegetables, fruits, and meats.

Pre-Hispanic Mine of Taxco

Nestled beneath the Hotel Posada de la Mision in Taxco, Mina Prehispánica de Taxco is an old mining museum that offers a fascinating tour. Visitors are equipped with helmets and vests before descending into the depths of the mine to learn about the rich pre-colonial mining history and culture of the area. The knowledgeable guides provide insightful explanations, making it an educational experience for all.
