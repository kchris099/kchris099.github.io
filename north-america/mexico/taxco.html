Taxco

Silver discoveries in the sixteenth century created Taxco; José de la Borda's wealth in the eighteenth paid for Santa Prisca's Churrigueresque façade. Steep cobbled lanes and silversmith workshops kept the mountain town specialised in metalwork long after the great colonial mines slowed. William Spratling's twentieth-century workshop revival rebranded Taxco silver for modern markets.

Cristo Rey. Cristo Monumental.

Cristo Rey, also known as Cristo Monumental, is a must-visit spot in Taxco for city views. Perched on a hilltop, it offers a 360-degree panoramic view of the city. While some visitors hike up to reach this viewpoint, others opt for a short taxi ride. However, safety concerns have been raised due to some visitors standing on the fence for better pictures.

Zocalo, Taxco

Located Taxco, the Zocalo, officially known as Plaza Borda, is the active centerpiece of this town. This main plaza is surrounded by an array of restaurants, unique shops, especially silver boutiques, and historical landmarks. A must-visit is Casa Borda, a significant cultural hub that offers lessons in fine arts and languages.

Santa Prisca de Taxco

Santa Prisca de Taxco is a evidence of baroque architecture, visitors with its facade and rich history. Built in 1758, this church is a highlight of the town, showing an contrast between its ornate belfries and the elegantly simple nave. The rose-colored stone glows beautifully under sunlight, particularly around the oval bas-relief of Christ's baptism that adorns its entrance.

Museum of Religious Art Taxco, Casa Humboldt is a museum in Taxco, Mexico. Records associated with the site include 1803. Former convent of San Bernardino of Siena

The former Convento de San Bernardino de Siena in Taxco is a colonial church complex tied to the Independence era, when Agustín de Iturbide worked on the Plan of Iguala nearby. Stone arcades and the parish church face streets a few hundred metres from the silver town's centre. The building remains an active place of worship in the Pueblo Mágico.

Pre-Hispanic Mine of Taxco

Located beneath the Hotel Posada de la Mision in Taxco, Mina Prehispánica de Taxco is an old mining museum that offers a fascinating tour. Visitors are equipped with helmets and vests before descending into the depths of the mine to learn about the rich pre-colonial mining history and culture of the area. The knowledgeable guides provide insightful explanations, making it an educational experience for all.
