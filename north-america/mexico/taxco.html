Taxco

Silver discoveries in the sixteenth century created Taxco; José de la Borda's wealth in the eighteenth paid for Santa Prisca's Churrigueresque façade. Steep cobbled lanes and silversmith workshops kept the mountain town specialised in metalwork long after the great colonial mines slowed. William Spratling's twentieth-century workshop revival rebranded Taxco silver for modern markets.

Cristo Rey. Cristo Monumental.

Cristo Rey, or Cristo Monumental, stands on a ridge above Taxco with a full circle view of the white town and surrounding hills. Many visitors take a short taxi; others walk the climb. Railings frame photo stops; local advice warns against standing on fences for shots.

02 AdmissionFree

Zocalo, Taxco

Located Taxco, the Zocalo, officially known as Plaza Borda, is the active centerpiece of this town. This main plaza is surrounded by an array of restaurants, unique shops, especially silver boutiques, and historical landmarks. A must-visit is Casa Borda, a significant cultural hub that offers lessons in fine arts and languages.

03 AdmissionFree

Santa Prisca de Taxco

Silver magnate Jose de la Borda financed Santa Prisca in the 1750s, creating a compact Mexican Baroque church that dominates Taxco's steep center. Twin towers, carved pink-stone facades, gilded retablos, paintings, and a richly ornamented dome display the wealth of the mining era. The interior is not simple, and active worship continues alongside heritage visits.

Museum of Religious Art Taxco, Casa Humboldt

A Baroque house traditionally associated with Alexander von Humboldt's brief 1803 stay now contains Taxco's Museum of Viceregal Art. Rooms display religious paintings, sculpture, silverwork, furniture, and objects recovered during restoration. The Humboldt connection gives the building its popular name, while the collection focuses more broadly on colonial life and sacred art in the mining town.

Casa Spratling

The Guillermo Spratling Museum examines the designer's influence on Taxco's twentieth-century silverwork and the archaeological collection he assembled. Galleries include Mesoamerican objects and reproductions, Mezcala-style stonework and historical material about the city. Spratling's metal designs connect the collection with Taxco's modern craft revival. The museum opened in 1975.

18.5570° N / 99.6061° W

06 AdmissionFree

Former convent of San Bernardino of Siena

The former Convento de San Bernardino de Siena in Taxco is a colonial church complex tied to the Independence era, when Agustín de Iturbide worked on the Plan of Iguala nearby. Stone arcades and the parish church face streets a few hundred metres from the silver town's centre. The building remains an active place of worship in the Pueblo Mágico.

07 AdmissionFree

Templo de Chavarrieta en Taxco

Templo de Chavarrieta is a small Catholic church associated with its namesake neighborhood in Taxco. A white facade, bell openings and simple interior reflect the scale of a local parish, while Santa Prisca in the town center displays the city's more elaborate Baroque tradition. Continuing religious use and the steep residential setting give this church its local context.

08 AdmissionFree

Mercado Tetitlán

Mercado Tetitlan is a neighborhood market in Taxco, selling produce, meat, prepared meals and household necessities away from the town's most tourist-oriented silver shops. Stall mix and hours follow local demand. The site provides everyday food commerce.

18.5551° N / 99.6050° W

09 AdmissionFree

Vicente Guerrero Park

Vicente Guerrero Park is a neighborhood public space in Taxco named for the independence leader born in the surrounding region; everyday civic use and the commemorative name define the park, which contains no major historic building or formal museum collection. Trees, paths and informal seating provide a modest place for rest and local gatherings away from the town's busiest monumental streets.

18.5545° N / 99.6043° W

10 AdmissionFree

Pre-Hispanic Mine of Taxco

Located beneath the Hotel Posada de la Mision in Taxco, Mina Prehispánica de Taxco is an old mining museum that offers a fascinating tour. Visitors are equipped with helmets and vests before descending into the depths of the mine to learn about the rich pre-colonial mining history and culture of the area. The knowledgeable guides provide insightful explanations, making it an educational experience for all.
