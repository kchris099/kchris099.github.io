Muscat

Muscat's harbours linked Arabian Sea trade to interior Oman from the sixteenth century under Portuguese pressure and later Al Said dynastic rule. Forts, mosques, and corniche palaces mark maritime commerce and Ibadi Islamic governance in the Gulf sultanate's capital. Mutrah souq corniches and royal opera precincts still extend harbour forts along the Gulf of Oman shoreline.
