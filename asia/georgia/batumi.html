Batumi

= Batumi is located on the site of the ancient Greek colony in Colchis called "Bathus" or "Bathys", derived from (Ancient Greek: βαθύς λιμεν, bathus limen; or βαθύς λιμήν, bathys limēn; lit. The 'deep harbor'). Under Hadrian (c. 117-138 AD), it was converted into a fortified Roman port and later deserted for the fortress of Petra founded in the time of Justinian I (c. 527-565). Garrisoned by the Roman-Byzantine forces, it was formally a possession of the kingdom of Lazica until being occupied briefly by the Arabs, who did not hold it; In 780 Lazica fell to kingdom of Abkhazia via a dynastic union; the latter led the unification of the Georgian monarchy in the 11th century. From 1010, it was governed by the eristavi (ერისთავი, viceroy) of the king of Georgia.

Ali & Nino Statue

The Ali and Nino statue on Batumi's seaside promenade is a kinetic sculpture of two figures that move together and apart, inspired by the novel of the same name. Metal silhouettes rotate on the waterfront, becoming a familiar meeting point along the Black Sea boulevard.

Batumi Lighthouse

Batumi's octagonal stone lighthouse, completed in 1882, marks the old harbour edge on the Black Sea shore. The tower's silhouette remains easy to pick out from the boulevard walkways near the statue shore. Nearby paths look back toward the city front and out across open water beyond the breakwaters.

41.6559° N / 41.6410° E

Batumi Dolphinarium

Batumi Dolphinarium shows scheduled marine-animal shows in an open-air pool complex near 6 May Park. Performances emphasize trained dolphin behavior and audience entertainment, with separate swimming sessions sometimes offered under controlled conditions. Because the attraction keeps cetaceans in captivity, its educational claims and animal-welfare implications deserve consideration alongside practical details such as show times and seating exposure.

Argo Cable Car

Cabins of the Argo cable car rise from Batumi's waterfront district to Anuria Hill on the eastern side of the city. The journey crosses dense neighborhoods before opening toward the Black Sea, port, old center and surrounding foothills; a viewing terrace, restaurant and visitor facilities occupy the upper station, while wind, maintenance and seasonal demand can affect operation.

41.6475° N / 41.6455° E

05 AdmissionFree

Batumi Central Mosque

Orta Jame, Batumi's central mosque, keeps a modest brick and tile exterior near the old Muslim quarter of the Black Sea port. The prayer hall and courtyard receive worshippers daily; visitors may view the exterior and, when appropriate, the main interior outside prayer times. Minaret and dome mark the skyline a few blocks inland from the boulevard.

41.6486° N / 41.6438° E

06 AdmissionFree

Batumi Piazza Square

Italianate facades, arcades, mosaic paving and a tall clock tower enclose Batumi's Piazza Square. Developed in the twenty-first century, the pedestrian court contains hotels, restaurants, cafes and event spaces. Music and outdoor seating often bring activity after dark, while the architecture offers a modern interpretation of a European piazza. The mosaic pavement is a defining visual element.

41.6495° N / 41.6412° E

07 AdmissionFree

Holy Mother Virgin Nativity Cathedral

Pointed arches and twin towers give Batumi's Holy Mother Virgin Nativity Cathedral its neo-Gothic profile. Constructed around the turn of the twentieth century for the city's Roman Catholic community, it later passed into Georgian Orthodox use after the Soviet period. Stone carving, stained glass, icons, and a narrow basilican interior show how the building's religious identity changed while its architecture remained.

41.6463° N / 41.6387° E

08 AdmissionFree

Europe Square

Europe Square opens as a landscaped plaza in central Batumi with fountains, lawns, and facing civic buildings. Paths cross the planting beds toward surrounding cafés and hotels. The square functions as a formal gathering space rather than a market yard, with clear sightlines across the paving.

41.6510° N / 41.6361° E

09 AdmissionFree

Astronomical Clock

Batumi's Astronomical Clock is mounted high on an ornate corner building at Memed Abashidze Avenue and Konstantine Gamsakhurdia Street. Along with ordinary time, the mechanism indicates celestial information through small dials and moving symbols linked to the sun, moon and zodiac. The decorative facade gives the clock a strong civic presence. Most of its details are read from street level across the intersection.

41.6513° N / 41.6373° E

Lawns and shaded stretches break up the paved walk. Evening crowds gather around the fountain shows while runners and strollers share the shoreline route by day.

The upper Argo cable-car station occupies Anuria Hill above Batumi and functions mainly as a viewpoint and service complex. Terraces face the port, Black Sea coastline, city blocks and mountains, while dining and event spaces surround the arrival area.

Plane spotting point

Along the Black Sea shoreline near Batumi International Airport, an informal viewing point sits beneath the approach path used by arriving aircraft. Planes pass low over the coast before reaching the runway, with the sea, airport perimeter and changing light forming the main setting. No formal viewing infrastructure defines the place. Its appeal comes from the airport's unusual coastal position.

41.6183° N / 41.5868° E

Batumi Botanical Garden

Botanical collections cover steep coastal slopes at Batumi Botanical Garden north of the city. Founded in 1912 under botanist Andrey Krasnov, the garden arranges plants from East Asia, the Himalayas, the Americas, Australia, the Mediterranean and other climatic regions along long walking routes. Sea views and subtropical forest frame the collections, while gradients and distance make footwear and route planning important.

41.6944° N / 41.7075° E

Gonio Fortress

Gonio Fortress occupies a rectangular Roman and later Byzantine enclosure south of Batumi near the Turkish border. Surviving walls and corner towers enclose courtyards now used for archaeology displays and seasonal events. Walks along the ramparts overlook the Chorokhi river mouth and coastal plain.

41.5733° N / 41.5731° E
