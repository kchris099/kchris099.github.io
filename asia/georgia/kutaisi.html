Explore Kutaisi

A Brief History

Kutaisi is in Georgia. Established as a regional settlement beyond an older fortified core, the city developed through industrialization in the late 19th century, post-war reconstruction, and modern civic expansion that reshaped its public spaces, markets, and surrounding landscape. Museums, markets, and street plans in Kutaisi still show layers from those periods.

Top Sights & Activities

Bagrati Cathedral

Bagrati Cathedral, located in Kutaisi, Georgia, is a historic Orthodox cathedral originally built in the 11th century by King Bagrat III. Despite being damaged and rebuilt over many years, it remains a significant monument representing the Republic of Georgia. The cathedral offers views of the Kolkheti Lowlands and Teleti Range. Travellers can also explore the ruins of the inner citadel behind the cathedral.

Free

Gelati Monastery

Gelati Monastery, located on a wooded hillside 8km northeast of Kutaisi, is a known medieval monastery complex in Georgia. It was once a cultural and academic center during Georgia's medieval 'golden age', with notable scholars and rulers buried on its grounds. The main Cathedral of the Virgin has active frescoes, adding to the monastery's allure. Designated as a UNESCO World Heritage site, Gelati Monastery holds great historical and cultural significance for Georgia.

Free

Motsameta Monastery

Motsameta Monastery is a visitor site in Kutaisi, Georgia. It houses the sacred remains of Saints David and Konstantine Mkheidze, who were martyred during an Arab expedition to Georgia. The monastery offers views of the surrounding landscapes and can be reached via a short walking path through lush greenery. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Free

Colchis Fountain

Colchis Fountain is a modern fountain adorned with golden statues of animals and replicas of ancient Georgian figures. The region of Colchis, which includes areas like Egrisi, Lasika, and Odishi in Samegrelo, has a rich historical background dating back to the 8th century BC.

Free

Meskhishvili Theater

Meskhishvili Theatre in Kutaisi has a rich history dating back to 1861, when the first historical performance took place. The theatre is known for carrying on great traditions and hosting significant theatrical events. Located in the main city center, the building itself is quite attractive and eye-catching. Despite not understanding the Georgian language, travellers have enjoyed watching performances such as 'visitor Majesty.'.

Free

White Bridge

White Bridge is a bridge in Kutaisi, Georgia. In Kutaisi, the White Bridge is a attraction that offers a glimpse into local daily life. This French-designed bridge, along with the Red and Chain Bridges, provides views of the Rioni riverbed. A statue of a boy holding a hat pays homage to children who once played in the river below. Pedestrian decks and adjacent streets provide the usual viewing points.

Free

Bikentia's Kebabery

Bikentia's Kebabery is a unique dining spot in Kutaisi, designed in the old Soviet style with only six standing tables. The menu is simple, offering just one item: homemade beef kebab served with spicy tomato satsebeli sauce, fresh parsley, and onion. Guests can enjoy this dish along with a choice of beer or Georgian lemonade for a fixed price.

Free

Green Bazaar

When in Kutaisi, a visit to the Green Bazaar is a must for anyone interested in Georgian cuisine and local culture. This central marketplace offers an array of fresh produce, wine, bread, meats, cheeses, spices, and various goods. Despite its seemingly small size at first glance, the bazaar comprises numerous sections to explore.

Free

Besik Gabashvili Amusement Park

Besik Gabashvili Amusement Park is a public park in Kutaisi, Georgia. Besik Gabashvili Amusement Park in Kutaisi is a small but fun attraction located on the west bank of the Rioni River. Accessible by foot or cable car, it offers an enjoyable outing for families with young kids. The park also has nearby hotels and restaurants for convenience. Paths, lawns, and shaded areas make up the main public facilities.

GEL 20

Sataplia Nature Reserve

Sataplia Nature Reserve covers 330 hectares and has lush forests, karst caves, and dinosaur footprints. It's a year-round destination, but cave exploration requires a guided tour. The park entrance is free, with a nominal fee for tours. Travellers can enjoy the view from the glass bridge. The reserve offers an immersive experience in paleontological history and cave exploration accompanied by knowledgeable guides who speak Russian, Georgian, and English.
