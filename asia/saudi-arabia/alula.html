Explore Alula

A Brief History

A critical oasis on the ancient incense route, AlUla flourished under the Dadanite and Nabataean kingdoms, who carved monumental tombs into its sheer sandstone cliffs. Largely untouched for millennia, its impressive pre-Islamic ruins now emerge as a premier global heritage site. Today, this complex historical evolution remains significantly embedded in the civic architecture and vibrant daily life of the community.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

AlUla Fort Viewpoint

The AlUla Fort Viewpoint offers a commanding, panoramic perspective over the ancient mud-brick ruins of AlUla Old Town and the surrounding lush oasis valley. The elevated vantage point allows visitors to appreciate the strategic location of the historic settlement, nestled between dramatic sandstone mountains and the fertile palm groves that sustained it for centuries.

Free

Huna Al Ula

Huna Al Ula is a modern, vibrant community and co-working space located within the historic AlUla region. It serves as a creative hub for local artisans, entrepreneurs, and visitors, featuring a cafe, exhibition spaces, and areas for cultural workshops. The venue blends contemporary design with traditional elements, fostering local talent and cultural exchange.

Free

AlUla Tantora

The Tantora is a historic sundial located in the Old Town of AlUla. For centuries, it served as a crucial agricultural tool for the local community, marking the start of the winter planting season and regulating the distribution of water from the oasis springs. It remains a significant symbol of the region's traditional knowledge and agricultural heritage.

Free

AlUla Museum

The AlUla Museum provides a comprehensive overview of the region's extensive and complex history, spanning from prehistoric times through the Dadanite, Nabataean, and Islamic periods. The exhibits feature significant archaeological finds, including ancient inscriptions, ceramics, and statues, offering essential context for visitors exploring the numerous heritage sites scattered throughout the AlUla valley.
