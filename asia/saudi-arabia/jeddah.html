Jeddah

Jeddah served as the Red Sea gateway for Mecca pilgrims and Indian Ocean trade for centuries. Coral-stone houses with wooden roshan balconies in Al-Balad contrast with modern corniche development, recording Hejazi merchant architecture and contemporary Saudi urban growth. Corniche promenades and King Fahd Fountain extend Hejazi harbour life along the Red Sea shipping lanes.

Cenomi Mall of Arabia

Cenomi Mall of Arabia is a massive 261,000 square meter shopping destination located near the King Abdulaziz International Airport in Jeddah. It has an lineup of global fashion brands including Zara, Forever 21, Debenhams, DKNY, and Ted Baker. The mall also offers a variety of dining options ranging from fast food favorites like Burger King and Pizza Hut to fine restaurants such as Fridays and Smith & Wollensky.

Bab Makkah

Bab Makkah, the Makkah Gate, marks a historic entrance associated with the old walled town of Jeddah on the Red Sea. The gate name recalls the inland road toward Mecca that once passed this side of the settlement. Surrounding streets mix remaining coral-stone houses with later commercial blocks, so the gate reads as a threshold between the historic Balad fabric and wider modern Jeddah.

Jeddah Flagpole

A very tall flagpole rises from King Abdullah Square in Jeddah, carrying a large Saudi national flag above landscaped ceremonial grounds. Installed in 2014, the unsupported steel mast became a prominent modern marker on the city's skyline. The site is viewed mainly from surrounding roads and open spaces, where the scale of the pole can be understood in relation to the broad plaza.

King Fahad's Fountain

King Fahd's Fountain projects a powerful column of seawater above Jeddah's Red Sea coast. Installed in the 1980s, the offshore jet is illuminated after dark and can be seen from long stretches of the corniche. Wind alters the plume's shape and carries spray toward the waterfront, while the fountain's scale makes it a clear modern marker of the city's relationship with the sea.

Interpretation connects the displays with coral habitats and coastal ecology, giving the aquarium a public educational role in a city closely tied to the sea.
