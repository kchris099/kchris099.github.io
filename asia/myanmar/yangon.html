Destination Guide — Yangon

Explore Yangon

A Brief History

Established as a regional settlement beyond an older fortified core, the city developed through industrialization in the late 19th century, post-war reconstruction, and modern civic expansion that reshaped its public spaces, markets, and surrounding landscape. Restored religious buildings, civic buildings, and natural features now define the contemporary visitor experience.

Top Sights & Activities

20,000 MMK

Shwedagon Pagoda

Nestled in the heart of Yangon, the Shwedagon Pagoda stands as a testament to Myanmar's rich cultural heritage. Towering at 99 meters and adorned with an impressive 11 tons of pure gold, this historic Buddhist temple is not only the oldest pagoda in the country but also revered religious sites globally. Dating back over 2,600 years, it houses sacred relics from four past Buddhas, including strands of hair from Gautama Buddha himself.

Free

Chaukhtatgyi Buddha Temple

Nestled near the serene Kandawgyi Lake in Yangon, the Chaukhtatgyi Buddha Temple is a must-visit for anyone exploring Myanmar. This remarkable temple is home to an impressive 66-meter reclining Buddha, statues in the country. The intricate details of this magnificent figure, from its delicately crafted eyelashes to the beautifully inscribed feet, showcase exceptional artistry and devotion.

Free

The Secretariat Yangon

The Secretariat Yangon, also known as the Ministers' Building, is a Victorian-style brick complex in Myanmar. Constructed between 1889 and 1905 to house the British colonial government, it was designed by architect Henry Hoyne-Fox. Following independence, it served as the main Ministers Office until being abandoned when the government moved to Nay Pyi Taw in 2005.

Free

St Mary's Cathedral

St Mary's Cathedral, a example of Gothic Revival architecture, stands proudly in Myanmar as the largest Catholic cathedral in the country. Completed in 1909, this magnificent structure features a striking red brick facade adorned with soaring spires and intricate stained glass windows that evoke the grandeur of European cathedrals.

Free

Sule Pagoda

Nestled in the bustling heart of downtown Yangon, the Sule Pagoda stands as a testament to Myanmar's rich history and culture. This gilded Buddhist stupa, believed to be around 2,500 years old, is not only an architectural marvel with its ornate exterior but also a significant landmark in Burmese politics. It has served as a gathering point for pivotal pro-democracy movements throughout history.

Free

Bogyoke Aung San Market

Bogyoke Aung San Market is a bustling bazaar housed in a colonial building, offering a wide array of items such as antiques, jewelry, art, and food. It's situated near attractions like the 2,200-year-old Sule Pagoda and vibrant night markets. The market has almost a century of history and is a popular spot for tourists to purchase souvenirs like Burmese cloth, clothing, and jewelry.

300 MMK

Karaweik Palace

Karaweik Palace, situated on the picturesque Karaweik Lake in Yangon, is a must-visit destination for those seeking an authentic experience of Myanmar's cultural and culinary heritage. The palace is renowned for its traditional Pann Sel Myo and the Karaweik mythical bird design that adorns its structure.

Free

Thiri Mingala Kabar Aye Pagoda

Thiri Mingala Kabar Aye Pagoda, also known as the World Peace Pagoda, is a Buddhist temple in Yangon, Myanmar. Built in 1952 to commemorate 2500 years of Buddha's enlightenment, it features grand architecture and is surrounded by a circular platform with local vendors. The pagoda is famous for its peaceful courtyard that offers a serene escape from the bustling city streets.

Free

Swe Taw Myat Pagoda

Nestled in the vibrant city of Yangon, the Swe Taw Myat Pagoda stands out as a example of modern Buddhist architecture. Adorned with shimmering gold leaf and white stucco, it features beauty under the afternoon sun. The pagoda features intricate motifs that captivate travellers, inviting them to wander through its various entrances and explore its charming alleyways.

Free

Kyauk Taw Gyi (White Marble Buddha)

Located near Yangon Airport, Kyauk Taw Gyi is a famous pagoda featuring the impressive Lawkachantha Abayalarbamuni Buddha Image. This remarkable white marble statue stands at 37 feet high, 24 feet wide, and 11 feet deep, carved from a single piece of stone.
