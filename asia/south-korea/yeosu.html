Explore Yeosu

A Brief History

Yeosu has a long maritime history, serving as a crucial naval base during the Joseon Dynasty. It was the headquarters of Admiral Yi Sun-sin, who famously defended Korea against Japanese invasions in the sixteenth century using the innovative turtle ships. In the modern era, Yeosu developed into a major industrial port and hosted the 2012 World Expo, which revitalized its coastal infrastructure. Today, the city is known for its scenic archipelago, historic pavilions, and vibrant seafood culture.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

Yi Sun-Sin Square

Yi Sun-Sin Square in Yeosu, South Korea, is a popular tourist destination that pays tribute to the legendary naval commander Admiral Yi Sun-sin. The square features a statue of Yi Sun-sin and his famous Turtle Ship, offering visitors an opportunity to learn about his heroic achievements. Situated near the coast, the square provides views of the sea and serves as an ideal location to relax and appreciate the natural beauty of Yeosu.

Free

Yeosu Fish Market

Yeosu Fish Market holds historical significance as the birthplace of the famous 'Geobukseon' and offers an array of fresh seafood. Visitors can enjoy a variety of sashimi at lower prices compared to restaurants, accompanied by a refreshing sip of beer. The market allows patrons to select their seafood from the first floor and have it cooked at a restaurant on the second floor. While language barriers may exist, communication is facilitated through gestures, calculators, and translation tools.

Free

Yeosu Cable Car Dolsan Station

Dolsan Station is the southern terminal of the Yeosu Maritime Cable Car, the first of its kind in South Korea to connect an island to the mainland over the ocean. The station area features an observation deck and a park, providing dramatic, sweeping views of the Yeosu coastline, the Dolsan Bridge, and the bustling harbor below.

Free

Yeosu Handsome Cruise

Embark on an journey with the Yeosu Handsome Cruise, the largest coastal cruise in South Korea. This remarkable experience allows you to soak in the vistas of Yeosu from the water. Be prepared for a lively atmosphere, as this popular cruise attracts many visitors, even amidst ongoing health concerns.

Free

Hamel Lighthouse

Hamel Lighthouse sits within the Yeosu Cable Car Dolsan Station area of Yeosu, documented in local heritage registers for its civic or cultural role. Visitors approach from the main access road and combine the visit with neighbouring heritage buildings and public spaces in the district.

Free

Aqua Planet Yeosu

Aqua Planet Yeosu is a must-visit destination for marine life enthusiasts and families alike. Established by the Hanwha Group during the 2012 Yeosu World Expo, this impressive aquarium spans an area of 16,400 square meters and features a massive 6,000-ton tank, making it the largest aquarium in South Korea. Visitors can marvel at over 34,000 marine creatures from around 280 different species while enjoying captivating shows like beluga whale performances and interactive feeding demonstrations.

Free

Big-O Show

The Big-O Show in Yeosu is a event, especially for families with kids. The show features impressive water, music, and fire performances that create an enthusiastic atmosphere. With limited seating available for online reservations using local credit cards only, it's recommended to secure tickets early at 5 pm. Although some find the ticket price a bit expensive at 20,000, many visitors consider this water show as they've seen worldwide.

Free

Soho Dongdong Bridge

The Soho Dongdong Bridge is a scenic, wooden pedestrian walkway built over the ocean along the coastline of Yeosu. The bridge is particularly famous for its vibrant, color-changing LED illumination at night, creating a romantic and very photogenic atmosphere. It is a popular destination for evening strolls and enjoying the coastal breeze.

Free

U-World Luge Theme Park Yeosu

U-World Luge Theme Park in Yeosu is a comprehensive family entertainment complex featuring a popular, gravity-fueled luge track that winds down the mountainside. The park also offers a variety of other attractions, including an indoor dinosaur theme park, an amusement park with classic rides, and scenic views of the surrounding coastal landscape.

Free

Yeulmaru Art Center

Yeulmaru Art Center is a top-notch midsize concert hall located in the southern part of the Korean peninsula. It hosts concerts and musicals that are truly worth experiencing. The center also features an outdoor concert hall on the nearby Jangdo Island, offering views and a unique performance space. Visitors can enjoy art exhibitions, cultural events, and live music at this expansive venue.
