Yeosu

Yeosu has a long maritime history, serving as a important naval base during the Joseon Dynasty. It was the headquarters of Admiral Yi Sun-sin, who famously defended Korea against Japanese invasions in the sixteenth century using the innovative turtle ships. In the modern era, Yeosu developed into a major industrial port and hosted the 2012 World Expo, which revitalized its coastal infrastructure. Today, the city is known for its scenic archipelago, historic pavilions, and active seafood culture.

Yi Sun-Sin Square

Coastal paving at Yi Sun-Sin Square in Yeosu faces the sea and honors Admiral Yi Sun-sin, whose campaigns shaped Korea's naval story. Open pavement and flanking buildings define a simple civic room used for rest and waterfront views. The square sits close enough to the shore that sea air and harbor light stay part of the experience.

Yeosu Fish Market

Yeosu Fish Market concentrates tanks and counters of fish, shellfish, octopus and regional seafood near the old harbor. Some vendors arrange slicing or cooking through associated dining areas, but services, prices and preparation methods differ. The market has no credible claim as the birthplace of the turtle ship; that naval history belongs to the wider Yeosu and Jeolla Left Naval Headquarters landscape.

03 AdmissionFree

Yeosu Cable Car Dolsan Station

Dolsan Station anchors the island end of the Yeosu Maritime Cable Car near Dolsan Park. Cabins cross the harbor channel toward Jasan, passing above water, roads and port activity with views of Dolsandaegyo and the city shoreline. The terminal organizes boarding and circulation for standard and glass-floor cabins while connecting the ride with nearby park paths and viewpoints.

Yeosu Handsome Cruise

Yeosu Handsome Cruise operates large coastal excursion boats among the archipelago and bridges of Yeosu's waterfront. Decks and indoor cabins face island scenery, night lighting, and harbor industry along scheduled routes. The cruise functions as a sightseeing circuit on the water, complementary to cable-car and aquarium stops on shore.

05 AdmissionFree

Hamel Lighthouse

Painted red at the end of a short breakwater, Hamel Lighthouse marks the waterfront near the Yeosu Expo and Jongpo Marine Park. Its name recalls Hendrick Hamel, the Dutch sailor held in Korea during the seventeenth century, whose account introduced the country to many European readers.

34.7364° N / 127.7471° E

06 AdmissionFree

Aqua Planet Yeosu

Built for the 2012 Yeosu Expo precinct, Aqua Planet Yeosu interprets marine habitats through large tanks, tunnels and smaller displays. The aquarium occupies a substantial waterfront building associated with the former exposition grounds. Its permanent layout centers on ocean life and aquatic conservation, supported by education programs and scheduled presentations.

07 AdmissionFree

Big-O Show

The Big-O Show is an outdoor water and multimedia spectacle on Yeosu's Expo waterfront, using a large circular fountain structure left from the 2012 world's fair. Evening programmes combine jets, light, and music over the harbour. The venue sits beside the modern expo pier and aquarium district.

34.7481° N / 127.7494° E

08 AdmissionFree

Soho Dongdong Bridge

A lit pedestrian bridge and waterside path cross part of Soho Bay in western Yeosu, linking parks and residential waterfront areas. Color-changing illumination makes Soho Dongdong Bridge especially visible after dark, while the route offers views across the sheltered coast. Slippery surfaces, strong wind and rain can affect use of the modern recreation structure.

34.7447° N / 127.6529° E

09 AdmissionFree

U-World Luge Theme Park Yeosu

A gravity-powered luge track winds down a hillside at U-World Luge Theme Park in Yeosu, paired with an indoor dinosaur area and classic amusement rides. Coastal scenery surrounds the slopes, so rides often open onto views of the shore. Paths and lawns link the attractions for families moving between track, indoor halls, and outdoor play zones.

10 AdmissionFree

Yeulmaru Art Center

Concerts, musicals, and visual arts programs fill Yeulmaru Art Center in southern Yeosu, a midsize hall that also absorbs overflow from waterfront festivals. Auditorium sightlines and lobby gathering space guide the design more than outdoor park grounds. Regional audiences use the galleries and stage rooms as a steady cultural stop away from the harbor stages.
