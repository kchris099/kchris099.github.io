Explore Suwon

A Brief History

= Suwon has been inhabited since at the latest the early Bronze Age: artifacts such as pottery, sculptures, and arrowheads dating from the Bronze Age to the early Iron Age have been found in the area, including on Yeogisan, which is now a monument of Gyeonggi Province. During the Three Kingdoms of Korea period, the area was a territory of the statelet Mosuguk, part of the Mahan confederacy. It came under the influence of Goguryeo in the late 5th century CE before becoming part of Unified Silla (668–935). Suwon then fell to Goryeo after a military campaign led by King Taejo. In the 13th and 14th centuries, the area was promoted, demoted, merged, and made part of various administrative districts. It then became a part of Joseon upon the dynasty's founding, and in 1395 it was made an administrative centre of Gyeonggi Province.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

Paldalmun Gate

Paldalmun Gate, the grand southern entrance of Hwaseong Fortress, is a remarkable testament to Suwon's rich history. Constructed in the late 18th century, this two-story wooden pavilion showcases a blend of Chinese and Western architectural influences. Although it stands apart from the fortress walls due to urban development in the 1970s, Paldalmun remains an landmark surrounded by crescent-shaped walls.

Free

Suwon Hwaseong Fortress

Suwon Fortress Hwaseong is an 18th-century fortress with ancient city walls and original structures, providing access to the old city. It offers a glimpse into Korean history and culture. Nearby attractions include riding a railway bike in Uiwang for a sunset view near Seoul, exploring Gwangmyeong Cave for unique experiences in wine caves, and visiting Kunchiyan's Hwadam Forest to enjoy nature in summer.

Free

Hwaseomun Gate

Hwaseomun Gate, also known as the West Gate, is a captivating example of Korean architecture and one of the four main gates within the UNESCO World Heritage Site of Hwaseong Fortress. Illuminated by colorful lights at night, it offers a backdrop for photography and an opportunity to appreciate its intricate details. Adjacent to the gate stands the northwest watchtower Seobuk Gongsimdon, adding to its historical charm.

Free

Janganmun Gate

Janganmun Gate, an impressive fortress gate from the late 18th century, stands as a testament to Korea's architectural heritage. Unlike typical gates that face south, this unique structure is positioned to welcome the king arriving from Hanyang. Its striking curved-roofed tower rests on a robust stone base, making it not only visually captivating but also historically significant as the main north gate of Hwaseong and the largest of its kind in Korea.

Free

Hwahongmun Gate (Buksumun)

A water gate in Hwaseong Fortress designed to allow water flow while serving as a defensive structure. It remains a notable local point of interest situated in Suwon, South Korea. Visitors can observe the surrounding area and its connection to the city's broader historical landscape. Local guides sometimes include it on walking routes through the centre.

₩2000

Hwaseong Haenggung

Hwaseong Haenggung Palace, situated within a fortress in Suwon, is a meticulously restored palace dating back to 1789. The palace complex includes a visitor center, theater, and exhibition hall. It served as the temporary residence of the king and hosts various cultural experiences and activities. Visitors can enjoy the serene village scenery inside the city walls and witness a night view.

Free

Seojangdae (Western Command Post)

Seojangdae (Western Command Post) is located at the top of Paldal-san and offers a panoramic view of the city. Visitors can pay to ring the large Hyowon Bell and explore Seonodae, an octagonal tower used by crossbow archers. The area is well-maintained and illuminated at night, creating a sight.

₩2000

Suwon Hwaseong Museum

Suwon Hwaseong Museum is a dedicated space showcasing the rich history and culture of Hwaseong Fortress and Suwon city. It offers a comprehensive look into King Jeongjo's aspirations, the fortress construction process, and the daily lives during the Joseon Dynasty. The museum houses various exhibits including models, maps, original architectural plans, and multimedia displays that provide detailed insights into the fortress's construction and significance.

Free

Changnyongmun Gate

Changnyongmun Gate is a significant part of the Hwaseong Fortress in Suwon, offering a glimpse into history and architectural prowess. As one of the main gates, it played a crucial role in defending Suwon's heritage. While some may find it less impressive compared to other sections, its historical significance is undeniable. The surrounding landscape provides a picturesque backdrop for visitors to appreciate the recently constructed Joseon Dynasty castle and indulge in kite-flying activities.

Free (archery experience ₩3000)

Yeonmudae (Dongjangdae/Eastern Command Post)

A historic command post within Hwaseong Fortress used for military defense. It remains a notable local point of interest situated in Suwon, South Korea. Visitors can observe the surrounding area and its connection to the city's broader historical landscape. Local guides sometimes include it on walking routes through the centre.
