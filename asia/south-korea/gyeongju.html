Gyeongju

Gyeongju was capital of the Silla kingdom from the first millennium CE, unifying much of the Korean peninsula. Tumuli parks, Bulguksa temple, and Seokguram grotto record Buddhist patronage and aristocratic burial culture in the Kyongju basin of North Gyeongsang. Cheomseongdae observatory and royal tomb parks scatter Silla-era monuments across the Kyongju basin.

Bulguksa

Bulguksa on Mount Toham near Gyeongju was completed in 774 as a major Silla Buddhist temple with twin stone pagodas, stair bridges, and painted halls. Worshippers still hold services while visitors walk courtyards between Dabotap and Seokgatap. Despite steady tourism, monastic routines and mountain trees keep sections of the complex quieter than the crowded entrance paths suggest.

Seokguram Grotto

Seokguram Grotto on Mount Toham houses an eighth-century granite Buddha within a stone rotunda overlooking the East Sea approaches near Gyeongju. Relief bodhisattvas and disciples ring the circular chamber behind a viewing barrier that protects the sculpture. The grotto pairs with Bulguksa as a Silla Buddhist monument reached by mountain road and a short uphill walk.

Donggung Palace & Wolji Pond

Built in 674 during the Unified Silla period, the royal pond now called Wolji formed part of the secondary palace at Donggung, and reconstructed pavilions reflect in the water after dark, though most original palace buildings no longer survive above ground. Archaeological work recovered roof tiles, utensils, sculpture, and other objects that reveal court ceremonies and banquets around an irregular shoreline and three islands.

04 AdmissionFree

Cheomseongdae Observatory

Cheomseongdae is a seventh-century stone tower associated with astronomical observation at the Silla capital. Its carefully stacked masonry forms a tapered cylinder above a square base, with a central opening and upper platform reached internally. Interpretations of its exact method and numerical symbolism differ, so the structure is better described through its surviving form and courtly scientific context than through unproven calculations.

05 AdmissionFree

Woljeonggyo Bridge

The present Woljeonggyo Bridge recreates a Silla crossing documented in historical records and archaeological remains over the Namcheon stream, and interior passages and evening lighting emphasize traditional carpentry. Completed as a timber structure in the twenty-first century, it connects areas once associated with the royal palace and Mount Namsan.

35.8292° N / 129.2181° E

06 AdmissionFree

Gyeongju Gyochon Traditional Village

Gyeongju Gyochon Traditional Village reconstructs and maintains hanok streets rooted in settlement patterns that stretch back toward the Silla era. Timber houses, courtyards, and food stalls present everyday forms of historic Korean domestic architecture for walking visits. Cultural programs and craft shops operate among the lanes without turning the village into a sealed theme park.

07 Admission₩3,000

Cheonmachong

Inside Daereungwon, Cheonmachong allows entry into a reconstructed chamber within a large Silla burial mound excavated in 1973. The tomb takes its name from a painted birch-bark horse guard found among gold crowns, ornaments, weapons, and imported objects. Displays explain elite burial practice while replicas protect many originals, and the identity of the person interred during the fifth or sixth century remains uncertain.

35.8386° N / 129.2105° E

08 AdmissionFree

Bunhwangsa Temple and Stone Brick Pagoda

Bunhwangsa Temple, constructed in A.D. 634 during the Silla dynasty, has its original stone tower and adorned pillars that have withstood the test of time. Despite losing many buildings and relics to invasions in the late 16th century, it still holds the Mojeon Stone Tower as National Treasure No.30. The temple's old well, though no longer in use since the 1980s, adds to its historical charm.

35.8405° N / 129.2344° E

Gyeongju World

Beside Gyeongju's ancient sites, Gyeongju World runs the Draken dive coaster, family rides, and California Beach water park in summer. Modern thrill circuits contrast with the city's heritage tombs and temples. The park supplies a full-day amusement counterpoint to historic walking.

10 AdmissionFree

Gyeongju East Palace Garden

Near Bomun and Gyeongju's historic zone, East Palace Garden combines themed gardens, a bird park, exhibition areas, and family recreation in a contemporary public complex. Landscaped grounds draw on Gyeongju imagery without claiming to be an excavated royal palace.
