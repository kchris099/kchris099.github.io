Destination Guide — Gyeongsang

Explore Gyeongju

A Brief History

Gyeongju was capital of the Silla kingdom from the first millennium CE, unifying much of the Korean peninsula. Tumuli parks, Bulguksa temple, and Seokguram grotto record Buddhist patronage and aristocratic burial culture in the Kyongju basin of North Gyeongsang. Cheomseongdae observatory and royal tomb parks scatter Silla-era monuments across the Kyongju basin.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

Bulguksa

Constructed in 774, the Buddhist temple complex situated on Mount Toham boasts two pagodas. The location is with a serene maple-tree setting that enhances the beauty of its buildings designed with impressive roofing beams. Although it attracts many tourists, who flock to admire its architectural marvels and attend active prayers performed by worshippers, it still maintains a tranquil atmosphere.

Free

Seokguram Grotto

Seokguram Grotto is a remarkable historical hermitage that showcases exquisite Buddhist sculptures and offers views of the surrounding sea. Nestled atop a hill, reaching this national treasure requires a bit of effort, including a taxi ride followed by a scenic walk that may involve some stairs. While visitors are not permitted to take photos inside the grotto due to protective glass barriers, the intricate carvings and serene atmosphere make it an experience.

₩3000

Donggung Palace & Wolji Pond

Donggung Palace and Wolji Pond is a Silla-era royal secondary palace site at the eastern foot of Mount Namsan in Gyeongju. The pond, built in 674 AD and enlarged during the reign of King Gyeongbok, anchors a garden network of small islands, pavilions, and connecting causeways. The eastern palace buildings, banquet halls, and stone pagodas were excavated and reconstructed in the 1980s and now form an evening floodlit visitor route promoted by the city tourism office.

Free

Cheomseongdae Observatory

Cheomseongdae Observatory is a cylindrical structure made of 362 stones, symbolizing the solar year. It is located in Gyeongju, the ancient capital of the Silla kingdom in Korea. This city was once a bustling cultural and commercial hub with significant archaeological remains. The observatory is part of the UNESCO World Heritage Site and is just one of many historical treasures found in Gyeongju, including Daereungwon Tomb Complex and Bulguksa Temple.

Free

Woljeonggyo Bridge

Woljeonggyo Bridge is a reconstructed Silla Dynasty bridge that offers a picturesque sight, especially at night when it is illuminated and reflects on the water. It has historical significance as it was part of the love story of a Buddhist monk and a princess, as well as serving as a gateway connecting royal palaces. The original bridge was built over 1000 years ago but sadly burnt down; however, in 2018, the largest wooden bridge in Korea was reconstructed.

Free

Gyeongju Gyochon Traditional Village

Gyeongju Gyochon Traditional Village is a captivating tourist destination that offers a glimpse into the past with its traditional buildings, live demonstrations, and cultural events. This historic hanok village, originally established in the 7th century and reconstructed over time, provides visitors with an immersive experience of South Korean life from ancient times. The village features charming cafes, courtyards, and opportunities to dress in hanbok attire for memorable photos.

₩3000

Cheonmachong

Cheonmachong, also known as the Heavenly Horse Tomb, is a famous Silla burial mound dating back to the 5th or 6th century. It was unearthed in 1973 and is one of the few tombs open to the public. The tomb is believed to belong to a high-class nobleman, although the owner's identity remains unconfirmed.

Free

Bunhwangsa Temple and Stone Brick Pagoda

Bunhwangsa Temple, constructed in A.D. 634 during the Silla dynasty, boasts its original stone tower and adorned pillars that have withstood the test of time. Despite losing many buildings and relics to invasions in the late 16th century, it still holds the Mojeon Stone Tower as National Treasure No.30. The temple's old well, though no longer in use since the 1980s, adds to its historical charm.

₩54000

Gyeongju World

Gyeongju World is a major amusement park located in the historic city of Gyeongju. It offers a wide variety of attractions, including the 'Draken,' one of South Korea's most thrilling dive roller coasters, as well as family-friendly rides and a large water park (California Beach) in the summer. It provides modern entertainment contrasting with the city's ancient historical sites.

Free

Gyeongju East Palace Garden

Gyeongju East Palace Garden is recorded in the destination register for Gyeongju, South Korea, alongside publicly accessible spaces, parks, and halls that the city promotes through its visitor programme. The location typically pairs with markets, civic squares, and cultural venues in published tour loops; recurring visitors cite it as a regular inclusion on regional itineraries..
