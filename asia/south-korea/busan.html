Busan

Korea's main gateway toward Japan, Busan industrialised in the twentieth century and held out as a refugee capital during the Korean War. Jagalchi fish market and the harbour hills reflect that wartime influx and maritime economy on the southeast coast. Gamcheon Culture Village covers hillsides once settled by wartime refugees. UN Memorial Cemetery commemorates the wartime coalition that held the perimeter.

Songdo Bay Station (Songdo Cable Car)

If visitors are looking for an experience in Busan, the Songdo Bay Station, also known as the Busan Air Cruise, is a must-visit. This aerial tramway spans 1.62 kilometers and offers a ride that takes approximately 15 to 20 minutes. Starting from Songlim Park and gliding over the sparkling waters of Songdo Beach, be treated to views of landmarks like Yeongdodaegyo Bridge and Namhangdaegyo Bridge.

Songdo Cloud Trails

A curving walkway extends over the water at the eastern end of Songdo Beach, linking the shore with rocky islets and coastal viewpoints. Sections of transparent or grated flooring reveal the sea below, while sculptures recall the beach's long role in Busan tourism.

03 AdmissionFree

Gamcheon Culture Village

Steep lanes and tightly packed houses define Gamcheon, a hillside neighborhood shaped by families displaced during the Korean War. A later community art project introduced murals, installations, small galleries, and marked walking routes without erasing its residential character; rooftop views stretch toward Busan's port, but narrow passages still serve local homes, so the village is more than a colorful photo setting.

04 AdmissionFree

Bupyeong Kkangtong Market

Stalls at Bupyeong Kkangtong Market serve Korean favorites such as tteokbokki and bindaetteok (mung-bean pancakes) during regular trading hours. After eating, walkers often continue toward Songdo Beach or Yeongdo Island. The market works as a food hub before nearby coastal stops.

05 AdmissionFree

Gukje Market

Gukje Market developed after Korea's liberation and the Korean War, when refugees and traders exchanged imported and military-surplus goods in central Busan. Its covered lanes now hold clothing, tools, household supplies, food, and small workshops beside Bupyeong Kkangtong and BIFF Square; the market is an active commercial district, and product ranges depend on individual sections.

35.1014° N / 129.0282° E

06 AdmissionFree

BIFF Square

Movie theaters, festival plaques, and food stalls fill the pedestrian streets around BIFF Square in Nampo-dong. The area was reorganized in connection with the Busan International Film Festival, and handprints of actors and filmmakers mark the pavement near long-running cinemas. Street snacks and market traffic connect it closely with Gukje and Jagalchi, but it remains a public entertainment district.

Busan Tower

Rising from Yongdusan Park, Busan Tower provides an elevated view over Nampo-dong, the North Port, bridges, and surrounding hills. The present visitor experience combines an observation level with digital displays and changing commercial facilities inside a structure first opened in the 1970s.

LOTTE Department Store Gwangbok

At the edge of the old downtown and North Port, Lotte Department Store Gwangbok combines retail floors with dining, entertainment, and public viewing areas. A large indoor fountain occupies the central atrium, where scheduled water and light programs may operate, while rooftop spaces look across the harbor. Store tenants and show times change often.

09 AdmissionFree

Hwangnyeongsan Beacon

Stone beacon remains on Hwangnyeongsan recall the signal-fire network that once warned Busan and inland authorities of threats along the coast, and the open ridge is especially exposed to wind and changing visibility, while the historic beacon itself is an outdoor monument. Roads and walking paths now lead to summit viewpoints over the harbor, Gwangan Bridge, and the dense urban basin.

10 AdmissionFree

Bujeon Market

From dawn through evening, Bujeon Market displays fresh produce, seafood, meat, cooking ingredients, and affordable ginseng in fresh, dried, and persimmon forms. Squid, octopus, and fish stalls keep the wet aisles busy. Shoppers treat it as a full-day provisioning ground.

11 AdmissionFree

Taejongdae

Taejongdae Resort Park covers clifftop woodland on Busan's Yeongdo Island, with evergreen forest, rocky shores, caves, and lookouts over the Korea Strait. Walking roads and a coastal trail reach a lighthouse point and white-cliff edges pounded by open sea. The park mixes pine shade with abrupt drops to the water, staying breezy even when inland Busan feels humid.

Oryukdo Skywalk

Situated on Seungdumal, the Oryukdo Skywalk is a free-admission cantilever walkway that offers views of the sea and Oryukdo Island. This horseshoe-shaped bridge extends over a 35-meter high coastal cliff, providing visitors with an exhilarating experience as they walk on its glass floor and gaze at the ocean waves crashing both in the distance and beneath their feet.

13 AdmissionFree

Igidae

Igidae is a popular coastal walkway located near Gwangan Bridge and Gwangalli Beach. The nearly 5-kilometer trail offers views of the city and mountains, with dense forest on one side and sea cliffs on the other. Once a military path, it now attracts nature lovers seeking relaxation and panoramic vistas. Pedestrian bridges traverse the dense trees along the seaside trail, making it a favored spot for winter hikes.

14 AdmissionFree

Sajik Baseball Stadium

Sajik Baseball Stadium has seated about 27,500 fans as home of the Lotte Giants since 1985. Game days fill the stands with team chants and stadium food stalls selling Korean ballpark dishes. The ground sits in Busan's Sajik district with bus and metro links for match traffic. Non-game visits are limited; the main public draw is attending a Giants fixture in season.

Busan Children's Grand Park

Lakeside walks, an interactive science museum, a small zoo, and seasonal events fill Busan Children's Grand Park for family days outdoors. Paths and lawns link the activity zones around the water. The park targets children without excluding accompanying adults on the same circuits.

16 AdmissionFree

Nurimaru APEC House

Nurimaru APEC House is a significant structure located on Dongbaekseom Island, at the edge of Haeundae. It was constructed by the Korean government to host the 2005 APEC meeting, where leaders from 21 member nations gathered. The name "Nurimaru" is derived from the Korean words for "world" and "summit.".

SEALIFE Busan Aquarium, located just 5 minutes away from Haeundae Beach, is a top destination for exploring the fascinating world of marine life. With over 250 marine species and 10,000 kinds of fish on display, this family-friendly aquarium offers various themed zones showing different aquatic environments and creatures from around the globe.
