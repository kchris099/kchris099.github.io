Explore Busan

A Brief History

Long serving as the primary maritime gateway connecting the Korean peninsula with Japan, Busan rapidly industrialized in the 20th century. Remaining unconquered during the Korean War, it absorbed a massive influx of refugees, shaping its identity as a fiercely resilient, busy port. The survival of these cultural and architectural milestones provides a powerful window into the region's storied past. Museums, markets, and street plans in Busan still show layers from those periods.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

Songdo Bay Station (Songdo Cable Car)

If visitors are looking for an experience in Busan, the Songdo Bay Station, also known as the Busan Air Cruise, is a must-visit. This aerial tramway spans 1.62 kilometers and offers a ride that takes approximately 15 to 20 minutes. Starting from Songlim Park and gliding over the sparkling waters of Songdo Beach, be treated to views of landmarks like Yeongdodaegyo Bridge and Namhangdaegyo Bridge.

Free

Songdo Cloud Trails

The Songdo Cloud Trails (Songdo Skywalk) is a popular coastal attraction in Busan, featuring a curved, glass-bottomed walkway that extends out over the ocean. The elevated path has the thrilling sensation of walking on water, along with panoramic views of Songdo Beach, the surrounding coastline, and the distant city skyline.

Free

Gamcheon Culture Village

Located just a short distance from Nampo-dong, Gamcheon Culture Village is a active gem in Busan that draws visitors with its kaleidoscope of colorful houses and artistic murals. This hillside community, originally established by war refugees, has transformed into one of the city's most beloved attractions.

Free

Bupyeong Kkangtong Market

Bupyeong Kkangtong Market is a market in Busan, South Korea. This busy hub is the place to indulge in Korean favorites like ddeokbokki and bindaetteok (mung bean pancakes). After satisfying visitor cravings at this active market, easily venture to nearby attractions such as Songdo Beach or Yeongdo Island for views and cultural experiences. Stalls sell food and goods during regular market hours.

Free

Gukje Market

Gukje Market, also known as the International Market, is the largest traditional market in Busan. It gained its international moniker due to the multilingual abilities of its vendors. The market offers a wide variety of goods, from home items and souvenirs to delectable local foods like spicy vermicelli and pork feet. Situated near BuPyeong-KkangTong Market, Gukje Market's layout consists of separate areas for different products.

Free

BIFF Square

BIFF Square, located in Busan, is a active area known for its first-run movie theaters, street food stalls, and handprints of famous Korean actors. This district has been revitalized to support the growth of Korea's film industry. At night, the square comes alive with numerous street food tents offering popular Korean snacks like tteokbokki and dakkochi, it is conveniently situated near various markets and shopping outlets.

₩12,000

Busan Tower

Busan Tower, located in Yongdusan Park at the the city, stands at 118 meters tall and has an observation deck and a café. From this vantage point, visitors can take in sweeping views of Nampo-dong, Busan's old downtown area, and the entire North Port region. The tower also offers special light displays during the Starry Night Fiesta from July to October on Friday, Saturday, and Sunday nights.

Free

LOTTE Department Store Gwangbok

LOTTE Department Store Gwangbok is a visitor site in Busan, South Korea. LOTTE Department Store Gwangbok is an shopping and entertainment complex located on the Busan Port. One of its main attractions is the world's tallest indoor musical water fountain, which offers free shows for visitors to enjoy. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Bujeon Market

Bujeon Market is a market in Busan, South Korea. It offers a wide variety of fresh produce, seafood, meat, and cooking ingredients. The market is known for its affordable ginseng products, including fresh, dried, and persimmon ginseng. Visitors can immerse themselves in the local culture from dawn to dusk while exploring the active displays of colorful vegetables and seafood such as squids, octopus, and fish. Stalls sell food and goods during regular market hours.

Free

Taejongdae

Taejongdae is a visitor site in Busan, South Korea. Taejongdae Resort Park in Busan is a clifftop park area that offers views of the sea. It is located on Yeongdo Island and has dense evergreen woodland and rocky coasts with cliffs and caves. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Free

Oryukdo Skywalk

Situated on Seungdumal, the Oryukdo Skywalk is a free-admission cantilever walkway that offers views of the sea and Oryukdo Island. This horseshoe-shaped bridge extends over a 35-meter high coastal cliff, providing visitors with an exhilarating experience as they walk on its glass floor and gaze at the ocean waves crashing both in the distance and beneath their feet.

Free

Igidae

Igidae is a popular coastal walkway located near Gwangan Bridge and Gwangalli Beach. The nearly 5-kilometer trail offers views of the city and mountains, with dense forest on one side and sea cliffs on the other. Once a military path, it now attracts nature lovers seeking relaxation and panoramic vistas. Pedestrian bridges traverse the dense trees along the seaside trail, making it a favored spot for winter hikes.

Free

Sajik Baseball Stadium

Sajik Baseball Stadium has seated about 27,500 fans as home of the Lotte Giants since 1985. Game days fill the stands with team chants and stadium food stalls selling Korean ballpark dishes. The ground sits in Busan's Sajik district with bus and metro links for match traffic. Non-game visits are limited; the main public draw is attending a Giants fixture in season.

Free

Busan Children's Grand Park

Busan Children's Grand Park is a public park in Busan, South Korea. Busan Children's Grand Park is a spacious recreational area in Busan, South Korea, offering a variety of attractions for children and families. Visitors can enjoy lakeside walks, an interactive science museum, and a small zoo, the park hosts seasonal events that add extra excitement to the experience. Paths, lawns, and shaded areas make up the main public facilities.

Free

Nurimaru APEC House

Nurimaru APEC House is a significant structure located on Dongbaekseom Island, at the edge of Haeundae. It was constructed by the Korean government to host the 2005 APEC meeting, where leaders from 21 member nations gathered. The name "Nurimaru" is derived from the Korean words for "world" and "summit.".

₩29,000

SEALIFE Busan Aquarium

SEALIFE Busan Aquarium, located just 5 minutes away from Haeundae Beach, is a top destination for exploring the fascinating world of marine life. With over 250 marine species and 10,000 kinds of fish on display, this family-friendly aquarium offers various themed zones showing different aquatic environments and creatures from around the globe.

₩27,000

BUSAN X the SKY

Located in the Haeundae L City Landmark Tower, BUSAN X the SKY offers panoramic views of Busan's famous landmarks such as Gwangan Bridge and Marine City. The observatory provides a chance to enjoy the wide ocean view of Haeundae and the splendid cityscape of Busan. Visitors can also experience Sky Cruise, a high-speed elevator ride that offers a thrilling journey to the 100th floor with multimedia graphics entertainment.
