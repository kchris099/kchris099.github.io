Jeonju

Jeonju was the ancestral seat of the Joseon Yi clan and a major provincial capital under that dynasty in Jeolla. Hanji paper-making and bibimbap cooking traditions grew in the same administrative town. Hundreds of wooden houses in Jeonju Hanok Village form the main historic district visitors walk today for Joseon-era street fabric, crafts, and food markets.

Jeonju Hanok Village

Jeonju Hanok Village is a cultural village in South Korea featuring over 800 traditional wooden houses known as hanoks. The village is home to various guesthouses, restaurants, cafes, and traditional clothing rental shops. Visitors can experience the cobblestone lanes and unique architecture of the village while enjoying octopus grills at dusk. The attraction is easily accessible by taking bus 974 or 976 from Jeonju National Palace headquarters.

Located Jeonju, South Korea, Jeondong Cathedral is a evidence of both history and architecture. Pungnammun Gate

Pungnammun Gate, also known as the South Gate, is a 600-year-old landmark in Jeonju, South Korea. It is the last remaining gate of the four that were once part of Jeonju's fortress walls. This ancient structure has survived turbulent times, including damage during Japanese invasions and fires during King Yeongjo's reign.

Jeonju Hyanggyo is a well-preserved Confucian school complex from the Joseon era, featuring several historic buildings surrounded by majestic ginkgo trees. Jeonju National Museum

The Jeonju National Museum is a premier cultural institution dedicated to preserving and exhibiting the rich historical heritage of the Jeollabuk-do province. The museum houses extensive collections of artifacts from the prehistoric era through the Joseon Dynasty, including significant Buddhist art, traditional ceramics, and items related to the region's prominent scholarly traditions.
