Explore Jeonju

A Brief History

Jeonju served as the spiritual capital of the Joseon Dynasty, being the ancestral home of the royal Yi family. It flourished as a major administrative and cultural center, known for its production of high-quality hanji (traditional Korean paper) and its distinct culinary traditions, notably bibimbap. The city has carefully preserved its historic core, featuring hundreds of traditional wooden houses. Today, Jeonju Hanok Village is a major destination for experiencing classical Korean architecture and culture.

Top Sights & Activities

Jeonju Hanok Village

Jeonju Hanok Village is a cultural village in South Korea featuring over 800 traditional wooden houses known as hanoks. The village is home to various guesthouses, restaurants, cafes, and traditional clothing rental shops. Visitors can experience the cobblestone lanes and unique architecture of the village while enjoying octopus grills at dusk. The attraction is easily accessible by taking bus 974 or 976 from Jeonju National Palace headquarters.

Free

Jeondong Cathedral

Jeondong Cathedral is a religious building in Jeonju, South Korea. Construction and early use date to around 1908. Located Jeonju, South Korea, Jeondong Cathedral is a evidence of both history and architecture. Constructed between 1908 and 1914, this Romanesque church is adorned with detailed brickwork and elegant arches that captivate visitors from the moment they arrive. The exterior and main interior spaces are usually viewable outside service times.

Free

Pungnammun Gate

Pungnammun Gate, also known as the South Gate, is a 600-year-old landmark in Jeonju, South Korea. It is the last remaining gate of the four that were once part of Jeonju's fortress walls. This ancient structure has survived turbulent times, including damage during Japanese invasions and fires during King Yeongjo's reign.

Free

Gyeonggijeon Shrine

Gyeonggijeon Shrine is a visitor site in Jeonju, South Korea. Construction and early use date to around 1410. Gyeonggijeon Shrine, located in Jeonju, South Korea, is a significant cultural and historical site that holds the legacies of the Joseon dynasty. Built in 1410 to enshrine King Taejo, the founder of the dynasty, it has history exhibits and royal portraits. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Free

Royal Portrait Museum

Discover the rich history of Korea's monarchy at the Royal Portrait Museum in Jeonju. This recent addition to the city showcases painted portraits of various Korean monarchs, including King Sejong, King Yeongjo, and King Cheoljong. The museum is also home to the Gyeonggijeon Shrine, which holds a significant painting of King Tae-jo, the founder of the Joseon Dynasty.

Free

Cheongyeollu

Cheongyeollu is a visitor site in Jeonju, South Korea. During the day, visitors can enjoy leisurely strolls or bike rides along the river, while at night, the pavilion is beautifully illuminated, creating a nightscape. The intimate setting makes it ideal for couples seeking a romantic outing or groups looking to unwind amidst the soothing sounds of nature. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Free

Jeonju Hyanggyo

Jeonju Hyanggyo is a well-preserved Confucian school complex from the Joseon era, featuring several historic buildings surrounded by majestic ginkgo trees. Established in 1354 during the Koryo Dynasty, this educational institution has over 650 years of history and tradition. The site includes significant structures like Daeseongjeon, Gyeseongsa, Myungryoondang, Dongjae, and Seojae.

Free

Jeonju Zoo

Jeonju Zoo is a visitor site in Jeonju, South Korea. Records associated with the site include 1978. Jeonju Zoo, located just a kilometer away from Deokjin Park in Jeonju city, South Korea, has been open since 1978 and is home to over 670 animals including tigers, lions, giraffes, rhinos, camels, and hippos. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Free

Jeolla Gamyoung

Jeolla Gamyoung is a visitor site in Jeonju, South Korea. It opened or was completed around 2020. Jeolla Gamyoung is a reconstructed provincial government complex that once governed the historical province of Jeolla during the Joseon Dynasty. The original buildings were destroyed during the Korean War, but in 2020, a smaller replica was opened to the public. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Free

Jeonju National Museum

The Jeonju National Museum is a premier cultural institution dedicated to preserving and exhibiting the rich historical heritage of the Jeollabuk-do province. The museum houses extensive collections of artifacts from the prehistoric era through the Joseon Dynasty, including significant Buddhist art, traditional ceramics, and items related to the region's prominent scholarly traditions.
