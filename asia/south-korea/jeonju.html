Jeonju

Jeonju was the ancestral seat of the Joseon Yi clan and a major provincial capital under that dynasty in Jeolla. Hanji paper-making and bibimbap cooking traditions grew in the same administrative town. Hundreds of wooden houses in Jeonju Hanok Village form the main historic district visitors walk today for Joseon-era street fabric, crafts, and food markets.

Jeonju Hanok Village

More than 800 traditional wooden hanoks line cobblestone lanes with guesthouses, restaurants, cafes, and hanbok rental shops. Evening octopus grills scent the streets as visitors walk the timber fronts. Buses 974 and 976 link the village from Jeonju National Palace headquarters.

02 AdmissionFree

Jeondong Cathedral

Built between 1908 and 1914, Jeondong Cathedral shows Romanesque brickwork and arches that catch the eye from the street beside Jeonju's hanok district. The church sits within easy walking reach of the traditional village lanes. Exterior walls and the main interior stay viewable outside Mass.

35.8134° N / 127.1493° E

03 AdmissionFree

Pungnammun Gate

Also called the South Gate, Pungnammun is the last of four fortress gates that once ringed Jeonju, roughly six centuries old. Japanese invasions and fires in King Yeongjo's reign damaged the structure, yet it still stands. The gate marks the old wall line at street level.

35.8135° N / 127.1476° E

04 AdmissionFree

Gyeonggijeon Shrine

Gyeonggijeon Shrine in Jeonju was built in 1410 to enshrine King Taejo, founder of the Joseon dynasty, and still holds royal portrait rites and history exhibits. Gateways, ceremonial courtyards, and museum rooms sit beside Jeonju's hanok village fabric. The shrine explains dynastic legitimacy through architecture and portraits rather than through a palace throne hall.

35.8153° N / 127.1498° E

05 AdmissionFree

Royal Portrait Museum

Within the Gyeonggijeon precinct, the Royal Portrait Museum displays copies and surviving material related to official images of Joseon kings. King Taejo's portrait anchors the collection, supported by displays on court painters, ritual handling, copying, and preservation.

35.8162° N / 127.1494° E

06 AdmissionFree

Cheongyeollu

Wooden floors and open sides at Cheongyeollu catch river breezes for walkers and cyclists who pause by day and see the pavilion lit after dark. Evening reflections draw couples and small groups without amplified entertainment. The stop works as a local rest point rather than a ticketed museum.

35.8107° N / 127.1533° E

07 AdmissionFree

Jeonju Hyanggyo

Jeonju Hyanggyo is a well-preserved Confucian school complex from the Joseon era, featuring several historic buildings surrounded by majestic ginkgo trees. Established in 1354 during the Koryo Dynasty, this educational institution has over 650 years of history and tradition. The site includes significant structures like Daeseongjeon, Gyeseongsa, Myungryoondang, Dongjae, and Seojae.

35.8126° N / 127.1572° E

08 AdmissionFree

Jeonju Zoo

Jeonju Zoo, open since 1978 about a kilometer from Deokjin Park, keeps more than 670 animals including tigers, lions, giraffes, rhinos, camels, and hippos. Paths loop between outdoor yards and indoor houses on a compact city-edge campus. Families treat it as a half-day outing paired with nearby park lawns rather than a safari drive.

35.8551° N / 127.1444° E

09 AdmissionFree

Jeolla Gamyoung

Jeolla Gamyoung reconstructs the Joseon-era provincial government compound that once administered Jeolla. Original buildings were lost in the Korean War; a smaller replica opened to the public around 2020 with offices, gates, and courtyards reinterpreted for visitors. Exhibits explain local governance under the dynasty inside timber halls set for walking tours.

35.8160° N / 127.1460° E

10 AdmissionFree

Jeonju National Museum

The Jeonju National Museum is a premier cultural institution dedicated to preserving and exhibiting the rich historical heritage of the Jeollabuk-do province. The museum houses extensive collections of artifacts from the prehistoric era through the Joseon Dynasty, including significant Buddhist art, traditional ceramics, and items related to the region's prominent scholarly traditions.

35.8013° N / 127.0897° E
