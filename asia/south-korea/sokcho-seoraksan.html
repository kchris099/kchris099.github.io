Explore Sokcho Seoraksan

A Brief History

Sokcho Seoraksan is in South Korea. Established as a regional settlement beyond an older fortified core, the city developed through industrialization in the late 19th century, post-war reconstruction, and modern civic expansion that reshaped its public spaces, markets, and surrounding landscape. Museums, markets, and street plans in Sokcho Seoraksan still show layers from those periods.

Gwon Geum Seong Fortress

Gwongeumseong Fortress sits on a ridge above Seoraksan, reached by cable car to the upper station and a short, often slippery 10–15 minute walk to the ruins. Stone walls and open platforms look out over the coastal mountains toward Sokcho. Hiking boots help on wet rock; visitors who skip the final walk can still use the cable-car viewpoint. The site is inside Seoraksan National Park access rules.

Free

Seoraksan National Park Seorakdong District

Seoraksan National Park Seorakdong District is a public park in Sokcho Seoraksan, South Korea. Seoraksan National Park Seorakdong District is a visitor site in Sokcho Seoraksan. Abai Village is a unique and historically rich place with a special atmosphere. The residents and shop owners are former North Korean refugees, adding to the village's sense of history. Paths, lawns, and shaded areas make up the main public facilities.

Free

Seoraksan Sinheungsa Temple

Seoraksan Sinheungsa Temple is a religious building in Sokcho Seoraksan, South Korea. Seoraksan Sinheungsa Temple is a Buddhist temple located on a mountain slope in South Korea. It is a popular attraction, especially during the fall season when the surrounding area is adorned with active colors. The exterior and main interior spaces are usually viewable outside service times.

Free

Biseondae Peak

Biseondae Peak is a visitor site in Sokcho Seoraksan, South Korea. Teddy Bear Farm is a place to spend quality time with children, offering an entrance fee of 5,000 won for kids and 7,000 won for adults. Travellers can marvel at teddy bears adorned in various themes such as wedding parties, birthdays, and Christmas. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Seoraksan National Park

Seoraksan National Park is a public park in Sokcho Seoraksan, South Korea. The park offers awe-inspiring natural experiences with its vistas and landscapes. It is the highest mountain in the Taebaek range in Gangwon Province, South Korea. The area is rich in biodiversity and provides a peaceful retreat for nature enthusiasts and hikers. Paths, lawns, and shaded areas make up the main public facilities.

Free

Seoraksan

Seoraksan, a 5,604-ft. Mountain located near Sokcho, South Korea, is a popular destination for nature enthusiasts and hikers. The national park offers various hiking trails with views, waterfalls, lush plant life, and diverse wildlife. Travellers can began on a challenging full-day hike to the Daecheongbong Peak or opt for a more leisurely experience by taking a cable car ride to enjoy the scenery.

Free

Seorak Waterpia

Seorak Waterpia is a vast spa complex with indoor and outdoor saunas, hot mineral-water pools, water slides, and wave pools. It's a great spot for families with elementary school kids, offering pool parties in the evenings. However, some outdoor areas may close early. The park faced challenges due to a fire that damaged outside pools and rides but received praise for its helpful staff. While bad weather affected some attractions during one visit, the lazy river rapids were enjoyable.

Free

Oeongchi Bada Hyangiro Trail

Oeongchi Bada Hyangiro Trail is a visitor site in Sokcho Seoraksan, South Korea. Located just below the Lotte Resort near Sokcho Beach lies the enchanting Oeongchi Bada Hyangiro Trail, a wooden walkway that meanders along the coastline. As visitors stroll along, be captivated by views of the azure ocean and hear the soothing sounds of waves crashing against the shore. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Free

Sokcho Beach

Sokcho Beach is a beach in Sokcho Seoraksan, South Korea. Sokcho Beach is a popular destination known for its 2 km long white sand shoreline, offering opportunities for swimming, walking, and shell collecting. It is a favorite spot among locals, with parks, flower roads, and trails attracting Sokcho citizens. Access is typically on foot from nearby roads or beachfront paths.

Free

Sokcho Eye

Sokcho Eye is a ferris wheel that offers panoramic views of Sokcho. It stands tall, providing vistas of the East Sea, cityscape, and natural landscapes. The slow-moving ride allows ample time to admire the beauty of Sokcho both day and night. Whether for a romantic proposal or a fun outing with family and friends, each group can enjoy their own car for an intimate experience.
