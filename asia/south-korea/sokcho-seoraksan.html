Explore Sokcho Seoraksan

A Brief History

Established as a regional settlement beyond an older fortified core, the city developed through industrialization in the late 19th century, post-war reconstruction, and modern civic expansion that reshaped its public spaces, markets, and surrounding landscape. Restored religious buildings, civic buildings, and natural features now define the contemporary visitor experience.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

Gwon Geum Seong Fortress

Gwon Geum Seong Fortress, located in South Korea, offers panoramic views of the surrounding mountains. travellers can reach the fortress by taking a cable car to the mountain station and then embarking on a short but slippery 10-15 minute walk to the top. The area is known for its beautiful nature and is popular for hiking, although it may be challenging for some. For those who prefer an easier way to enjoy the scenery, taking the cable car provides a more accessible option.

Free

Seoraksan National Park Seorakdong District

Abai Village is a unique and historically rich place with a special atmosphere. The residents and shop owners are former North Korean refugees, adding to the village's sense of history. The area features a poignant mural trail that tells the story of the refugees' lives, creating an open-air museum experience. travellers can also enjoy North Korean specialty food at local restaurants. One must-try dish is the squid sundae, which is recommended by locals.

Free

Seoraksan Sinheungsa Temple

Seoraksan Sinheungsa Temple is a Buddhist temple located on a mountain slope in South Korea. It is a popular attraction, especially during the fall season when the surrounding area is adorned with vibrant colors. travellers can reach the temple by hiking or taking a cable car. The temple features a bronze Buddha statue and offers views of the serene winter scenery. Additionally, there are opportunities for challenging hikes to Ulsanbawi and visits to Geumganggul cave.

Free

Biseondae Peak

Teddy Bear Farm is a delightful place to spend quality time with children, offering an entrance fee of 5,000 won for kids and 7,000 won for adults. travellers can marvel at teddy bears adorned in various themes such as wedding parties, birthdays, and Christmas. Notably, there are displays depicting Korean history and traditional hanbok attire. The farm also features a playground with a slide for kids and offers drawing activities.

Free

Seoraksan National Park

Seoraksan National Park is a and special destination, boasting large mountains, waterfalls, diverse flora and fauna, and numerous hiking trails. The park offers awe-inspiring natural experiences with its vistas and beautiful landscapes. It is the highest mountain in the Taebaek range in Gangwon Province, South Korea. The area is rich in biodiversity and provides a peaceful retreat for nature enthusiasts and hikers.

Free

Seoraksan

Seoraksan, a 5,604-ft. mountain located near Sokcho, South Korea, is a popular destination for nature enthusiasts and hikers. The national park offers various hiking trails with views, waterfalls, lush plant life, and diverse wildlife. travellers can embark on a challenging full-day hike to the Daecheongbong Peak or opt for a more leisurely experience by taking a cable car ride to enjoy the scenery.

Free

Seorak Waterpia

Seorak Waterpia is a vast spa complex with indoor and outdoor saunas, hot mineral-water pools, water slides, and wave pools. It's a great spot for families with elementary school kids, offering pool parties in the evenings. However, some outdoor areas may close early. The park faced challenges due to a fire that damaged outside pools and rides but received praise for its helpful staff. While bad weather affected some attractions during one visit, the lazy river rapids were enjoyable.

Free

Oeongchi Bada Hyangiro Trail

Nestled just below the Lotte Resort near Sokcho Beach lies the enchanting Oeongchi Bada Hyangiro Trail, a picturesque wooden walkway that meanders along the coastline. Spanning 1.74 kilometers, this scenic path connects Sokcho Beach to Oeongchi Port and features a delightful mix of wooden decks and dirt trails. As you stroll along, be captivated by views of the azure ocean and hear the soothing sounds of waves crashing against the shore.

Free

Sokcho Beach

Sokcho Beach is a popular destination known for its 2 km long white sand shoreline, offering opportunities for swimming, walking, and shell collecting. The beach is surrounded by a pine forest and features resorts and eateries. It is a favorite spot among locals, with parks, flower roads, and trails attracting Sokcho citizens. Along with other nearby attractions like Seoraksan Mountain and hot springs, Sokcho Beach contributes to making the area a comprehensive tourist destination.

Free

Sokcho Eye

Sokcho Eye is a captivating ferris wheel that offers panoramic views of Sokcho. It stands tall, providing vistas of the East Sea, cityscape, and natural landscapes. The slow-moving ride allows ample time to admire the beauty of Sokcho both day and night. Whether for a romantic proposal or a fun outing with family and friends, each group can enjoy their own car for an intimate experience.
