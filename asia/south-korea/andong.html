Andong

Joseon Confucian scholars such as Yi Hwang made Andong a centre of yangban learning. Hahoe clan village, wooden academies, and the Hahoe mask dance tradition grew from that scholarly culture in North Gyeongsang. The town's reputation rests on Confucian heritage rather than on industrial ports. Soju distilling and heotjesabap dining customs are marketed as living Confucian hospitality.

Andong Hahoe Folk Village

Hahoe Folk Village is a Joseon-era clan settlement of the Pungsan Ryu family beside a bend of the Nakdonggang River west of Andong. Tile-roofed yangban houses, thatched commoner homes, and riverside fields remain in daily use. Inscribed with Yangdong Village as a UNESCO World Heritage site in 2010, it preserves Hahoe Byeolsingut mask-dance traditions and was designated a National Folklore local history in 1984.

Bongjeongsa Temple

Bongjeongsa Temple is a religious building in Andong, South Korea. It is the oldest wooden edifice in Korea and is part of UNESCO's world heritage sites alongside six other temples in South Korea. The temple has historical and cultural richness enveloped by nature. A walk along its path provides a peaceful retreat that doubles as an excellent hiking course for those looking for adventure.
