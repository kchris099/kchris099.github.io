Destination Guide — Andong

Explore Andong

A Brief History

andong grew as a regional centre beyond an older fortified core, shaped by nineteenth-century commerce and post-war reconstruction that reshaped its public spaces, religious buildings, and surrounding landscape. Restored civic halls, market streets, and natural features now define the contemporary visitor experience for travellers exploring South Korea today.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

Bongjeongsa Temple

Bongjeongsa Temple, a centuries-old wooden temple located in the serene mountains and lush forests, boasts of a magnificent stone pagoda. It is the oldest wooden edifice in Korea and is part of UNESCO's world heritage sites alongside six other temples in South Korea. The temple offers visitors historical and cultural richness enveloped by nature. A walk along its path provides a peaceful retreat that doubles as an excellent hiking course for those looking for adventure.

Free

Andong Imcheonggak

Andong Imcheonggak is a captivating destination nestled in Gyeongsangbuk-do, steeped in history and cultural significance. This serene site offers visitors a glimpse into Korea's rich past, with its beautifully preserved architecture and landscapes. The locals are welcoming, eager to share stories about the area’s heritage, including insights into historical figures like Seok Ju Lee Sang-ryong.

Free

Andong Maskdance Festival Park

The Andong Maskdance Festival, held in October, is a event that celebrates the world mask dance culture. The festival features a variety of mask dances including Songpa Sandae, Kwanno, Kangrung, Unyul, Pongsan, Suyoung Yaryu and more. Visitors can also participate in making their own masks and enjoy the lively atmosphere of the festival.

Free

Andong Old Market

Andong Old Market is a lively hub in downtown Andong, offering an array of local flavors and cultural treasures. It's a bustling haven for food enthusiasts and shoppers, where immerse yourself in the vibrant chaos, savor delectable street eats, and hunt for unique souvenirs. The market boasts around 30 jjimdag restaurants, each with its own distinct way of preparing this traditional dish. Visitors can indulge in the diverse tastes while experiencing the warm hospitality of the locals.

Free

Andong Gunja Village

Seongjingol Mural Village in Andong is a small, colorful town filled with surprises at every corner. The maze-like streets are adorned with vibrant murals, funny statues, and captivating paintings. Visitors can explore the area and discover unique artwork while enjoying the picturesque views of Andong. Art and design enthusiasts will appreciate the creative atmosphere of this charming village.

Free

Nakgang Waterway Park

Taesamyo Temple, located in Andong, is a significant shrine that houses the memorial tablets of Kim Seon-pyeong, Kwon Haeng, and Jang Jeong-pil. These individuals played crucial roles in the founding of Goryeo and are honored with an ancestral rite held annually in February and August according to the lunar calendar. The temple is a designated Gyeongsangbuk-do monument and offers free admission for visitors interested in exploring its historical significance.

Free

Andong Municipal Museum

The Andong Municipal Folk Museum, also known as the Andong Folk Museum, is a free museum located near the Wolyeongjong Bridge. It offers insights into the history of the area and showcases exhibits that provide a glimpse into life in old Korea. The museum is easily accessible by bus from downtown and features beautiful houses rescued when a dam was built in the 1970s.
