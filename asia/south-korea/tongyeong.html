Tongyeong

Admiral Yi Sun-sin based naval operations here during the Imjin War, tying Tongyeong to Joseon maritime defence. Later artists and writers used the archipelago setting as a cultural motif. Coastal fortification heritage and ferry-linked islands explain the city's historical identity. Chungmu gimbap and cable-car views over Hallyeohaesang are modern frames on that naval bay.

Tongyeong Cablecar Skywalk

A glass-floored platform near the upper terminal of the Tongyeong cable car extends the mountain viewpoint above the slopes of Mireuksan. From this small skywalk, the harbor, city and islands of Hallyeohaesang appear across multiple channels in clear weather. It is an accessory to the ropeway station.

02 AdmissionFree

Skyline Luge Tongyeong

Skyline Luge Tongyeong runs gravity carts on a twisting track about 1.5 kilometers long down Mireuksan slopes. Riders usually ascend by the Tongyeong Cable Car for a short lift with views over Hallyeo Haesang waters before steering carts downhill. Multiple runs and shared family lines make it a timed-activity stop rather than a scenic overlook alone.

03 AdmissionFree

Tongyeong Cable Car

The Tongyeong Cable Car climbs the northern slope of Mireuksan from a lower station outside the city center to a ridge below the summit, and enclosed cabins pass over forest with widening views of Tongyeong's harbor and the islands of Hallyeohaesang National Park. Walking paths continue from the upper station, while wind, inspection, and seasonal demand can interrupt service or alter queue times.

34.8266° N / 128.4251° E

04 AdmissionFree

Tongyeong Concert Hall

Tongyeong Concert Hall belongs to the Tongyeong International Music Foundation complex on the waterfront, built to support orchestral, chamber, and festival performances. Its main hall and smaller black-box venue host programs connected with composer Isang Yun as well as visiting musicians; the building's coastal setting is visible from public approaches, but its acoustic character is meaningful primarily during an actual performance.

34.8261° N / 128.4401° E

05 AdmissionFree

Yi Sun-sin Park

Yi Sun-Sin Park is a waterfront parkland featuring an observation deck, a cultural center, and a revered general's statue. The highlight of the park is the 17.3m-high bronze statue of Admiral Yi Sun-Sin, offering views of the coastline and the sea of Hansando from a nearby trail. Visitors can explore historic artifacts such as the Cheonja Chongtong cannon and displays of Admiral Yi Sun-Sin's turtle ships.

06 AdmissionFree

Dpirang Nammangsan Scupture Park

Nammangsan Sculpture Park, branded DPIRANG, spreads outdoor works from the Tongyeong International Sculpture Symposium along forested hillside paths. Pieces by invited artists sit among trees with views toward the harbour town. Walking loops take about an hour at an easy pace.

07 AdmissionFree

Hansan Battle Plaza

Between Tongyeong's harbor and ferry terminal, Hansan Battle Plaza commemorates Admiral Yi Sun-sin's victory at the Battle of Hansan. Paved open ground and surrounding facades create a civic clearing rather than a crowded shopping strip. The setting invites a pause on Korea's naval history while ferries and port traffic move nearby.

34.8403° N / 128.4245° E

08 AdmissionFree

Seoho Market

Seoho Market sits opposite the Tongyeong Ferry Terminal and sells fresh fish, vegetables, meat, and snacks from covered stalls and open aisles. Foot traffic is usually lighter than at the larger central Tongyeong market, though the stock is similar. Oysters, anchovies, and other seafood are sold by catch and season, with produce and prepared snacks along the same lanes.

09 AdmissionFree

Tongyeong Live Fish Market

Stalls around Tongyeong Jungang Market display live fish, shellfish, dried seafood, vegetables, prepared foods, and household goods close to the harbor. Buyers may select seafood for preparation by nearby restaurants, though arrangements and prices vary by vendor. The market remains part of the city's working fishing economy beneath Dongpirang.

34.8449° N / 128.4257° E

10 AdmissionFree

Sebyeonggwan Hall, Tongyeong

Sebyeonggwan was the main guest and command hall of the Three Provinces Naval Headquarters established at Tongyeong after the Imjin War. Its broad raised floor and large timber roof accommodated ceremonies and administration within the former naval camp. Rebuilt in the seventeenth century and designated a National Treasure, the hall survives among reconstructed gates and government buildings that explain the city's military origin.
