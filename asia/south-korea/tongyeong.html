Destination Guide — Gyeongsang

Explore Tongyeong

A Brief History

Historically the formidable headquarters of the Joseon naval fleet commanded by Admiral Yi Sun-sin, Tongyeong was instrumental in defending the peninsula. The enduring military heritage and vibrant coastal topography later fostered an outsized legacy of influential artists and writers. The remarkable survival of these cultural and architectural milestones provides a powerful window into the region's storied past.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

Tongyeong Cablecar Skywalk

The Tongyeong Cablecar Skywalk is an observation deck located at the upper terminal of the Hallyeosudo Viewing Ropeway on Mount Mireuksan. The glass-bottomed platform extends over the mountainside, offering visitors thrilling, unobstructed panoramic views of the picturesque Tongyeong harbor and the numerous islands scattered across the Hallyeohaesang National Park.

Free

Skyline Luge Tongyeong

Skyline Luge Tongyeong is a must-visit attraction in South Korea, offering an exhilarating hillside cart ride on a twisting 1.5km track suitable for all ages. Additionally, visitors can take the Tongyeong Cable Car for a nine-minute ride to the top of Mireuk mountain, providing panoramic views of Hallyeo Haesang National Marine Park. The peak features a skywalk, observatory, and photo zones for capturing memorable moments.

Free

Tongyeong Cable Car

Experience the scenery of Tongyeong with a thrilling ride on the Tongyeong Cable Car. This exciting cable car journey takes you up Mireuksan Mountain, offering views of the city, harbor, and surrounding landscape. As the longest tourist ropeway in Korea, spanning 1,975 meters with 47 cars, it provides a memorable experience that lasts around 9 minutes to reach the summit station.

Free

Tongyeong Concert Hall

Nestled along the waterfront of Tongyeong, the Tongyeong Concert Hall is a cultural gem that should not be missed. This architectural marvel, completed in 2011 and inspired by the traditional Korean Janggu drum, offers an exceptional venue for classical music performances. Even if you’re not attending a concert, the hall's striking design and beautiful surroundings make it worth a visit. The acoustics are impressive, enhancing every note played within its walls.

Free

Yi Sun-sin Park

Yi Sun-Sin Park is a waterfront parkland featuring an observation deck, a cultural center, and a revered general's statue. The highlight of the park is the 17.3m-high bronze statue of Admiral Yi Sun-Sin, offering views of the coastline and the sea of Hansando from a nearby trail. Visitors can explore historic artifacts such as the Cheonja Chongtong cannon and displays of Admiral Yi Sun-Sin's turtle ships.

Free

Dpirang Nammangsan Scupture Park

Nammangsan Sculpture Park, also known as DPIRANG, is a captivating attraction in Tongyeong, South Korea. The park showcases an array of intriguing sculptures created by renowned artists from around the world who participated in the Tongyeong International Sculpture Symposium. As you wander through the park's forested paths, encounter unique and unusual pieces of art while enjoying views of the city.

Free

Hansan Battle Plaza

Nestled between the harbor and ferry terminal, Hansan Battle Plaza stands as a poignant tribute to the historic Battle of Hansan, where Admiral Yi Sun-sin led Korean forces to victory against Japan. While it may not be a bustling tourist hotspot, this square offers visitors a chance to reflect on Korea's naval history. The plaza features impressive monuments and statues that depict the legendary turtle ship and various figures from the battle, providing insight into Admiral Yi's legacy.

Free

Seoho Market

Seoho Market, and oldest markets in South Korea, is situated opposite the Tongyeong Ferry Terminal. It offers a wide range of goods from fresh fish and vegetables to meats and snacks. While it may be less bustling than the central Tong-yeong market, it boasts a variety of products similar to its larger counterpart. Visitors can find an array of seafood such as oysters and anchovies at reasonable prices.

Free

Tongyeong Live Fish Market

Nestled behind the harbor and beneath Dongpirang, the Tongyeong Live Fish Market is a vibrant hub that comes alive as day turns to night. The market offers an enchanting blend of sights, sounds, and aromas that create an atmosphere. As you stroll through its bustling aisles, encounter not only a array of fresh seafood but also colorful vegetables, dried goods, and kimchi lining the outer edges.

Free

Sebyeonggwan Hall, Tongyeong

Sebyeonggwan Hall in Tongyeong is a historically significant building that stands out amidst the cityscape. It offers a glimpse into traditional Korean architecture with its beautiful Hanok and various historical houses. The entrance fee is around 3,000 won. Located within the naval control camp, it is known as the widest floor building from the Joseon Dynasty. Despite being a national treasure, visitors can take off their shoes and relax while enjoying the cool breeze.
