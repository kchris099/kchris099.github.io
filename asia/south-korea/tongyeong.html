Tongyeong

Admiral Yi Sun-sin based naval operations here during the Imjin War, tying Tongyeong to Joseon maritime defence. Later artists and writers used the archipelago setting as a cultural motif. Coastal fortification heritage and ferry-linked islands explain the city's historical identity. Chungmu gimbap and cable-car views over Hallyeohaesang are modern frames on that naval bay.

Tongyeong Cablecar Skywalk

The Tongyeong Cablecar Skywalk is an observation deck located at the upper terminal of the Hallyeosudo Viewing Ropeway on Mount Mireuksan. The glass-bottomed platform extends over the mountainside, offering visitors thrilling, unobstructed panoramic views of the Tongyeong harbor and the numerous islands scattered across the Hallyeohaesang National Park.

Tongyeong Cable Car

Experience the scenery of Tongyeong with a thrilling ride on the Tongyeong Cable Car. This exciting cable car journey takes visitors up Mireuksan Mountain, offering views of the city, harbor, and surrounding landscape. As the longest tourist ropeway in Korea, spanning 1,975 meters with 47 cars, it provides a memorable experience that lasts around 9 minutes to reach the summit station.

Tongyeong Concert Hall

Located along the waterfront of Tongyeong, the Tongyeong Concert Hall is a cultural gem that should not be missed. This notable building, completed in 2011 and inspired by the traditional Korean Janggu drum, offers an venue for classical music performances. Even if visitors are not attending a concert, the hall's striking design and surroundings make it worth a visit. The acoustics are, enhancing every note played within its walls.

Yi Sun-sin Park

Yi Sun-Sin Park is a waterfront parkland featuring an observation deck, a cultural center, and a revered general's statue. The highlight of the park is the 17.3m-high bronze statue of Admiral Yi Sun-Sin, offering views of the coastline and the sea of Hansando from a nearby trail. Visitors can explore historic artifacts such as the Cheonja Chongtong cannon and displays of Admiral Yi Sun-Sin's turtle ships.

Hansan Battle Plaza

Hansan Battle Plaza is a public square in Tongyeong, South Korea. Located between the harbor and ferry terminal, Hansan Battle Plaza is a tribute to the historic Battle of Hansan, where Admiral Yi Sun-sin led Korean forces to victory against Japan. While it may not be a busy tourist hotspot, this square has a chance to reflect on Korea's naval history. Open pavement and surrounding facades define the visitor setting.

Seoho Market

Seoho Market sits opposite the Tongyeong Ferry Terminal and sells fresh fish, vegetables, meat, and snacks from covered stalls and open aisles. Foot traffic is usually lighter than at the larger central Tongyeong market, though the stock is similar. Oysters, anchovies, and other seafood are sold by catch and season, with produce and prepared snacks along the same lanes.

10 AdmissionFree

Sebyeonggwan Hall, Tongyeong

Sebyeonggwan in Tongyeong is a Joseon naval hall within the historic control camp, noted for one of the widest wooden floor plans from that dynasty. National Treasure status reflects its role in coastal defence administration under commanders linked to the Yi Sun-sin era. Visitors remove shoes to enter the raised hall among surviving hanok structures.
