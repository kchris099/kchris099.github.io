Destination Guide — Seoul

Explore Seoul

A Brief History

Seoul has served as Korea's capital in different forms for more than six centuries, beginning with the Joseon dynasty's founding city of Hanyang in 1394. Royal palaces, gates, government avenues, and later occupation-era and postwar reconstruction shaped the urban core. Today's city combines restored historical districts with transport corridors, riverside public space, and high-rise development.

Top Sights & Activities

₩3000

Gyeongbokgung Palace

Gyeongbokgung was founded in 1395 as the main royal palace of the Joseon dynasty. It was burned during the Imjin War, rebuilt in the 19th century, and later altered under Japanese rule. Ongoing restoration has returned major halls, gates, and courtyards to public use.

Free

Gwanghwamun Gate

Gwanghwamun is the main south gate of Gyeongbokgung. First completed in the late 14th century, it was destroyed and rebuilt several times, including a modern restoration completed in 2010. The gate forms the ceremonial threshold between the palace and the central boulevard.

Free

Statue of King Sejong the Great

The statue of King Sejong stands in Gwanghwamun Square and commemorates the 15th-century ruler associated with the creation of Hangul and many scientific and administrative reforms. The monument and its underground exhibition spaces connect public history with one of Seoul's main civic axes.

Free

National Museum of Korea

The National Museum of Korea moved to Yongsan in 2005 and holds major collections of Korean archaeology, Buddhist art, painting, calligraphy, and royal objects. The institution traces its roots to the Korean Empire period and now serves as the country's main national museum.

Free (observation deck ₩26000)

N Seoul Tower

N Seoul Tower opened to the public in 1980 on Namsan and combines a communications tower with observation and dining levels. It became a city landmark for panoramic views over Seoul and for the public spaces and trails that connect the tower to Namsan Park.

₩31000

Lotte World Tower

Lotte World Tower opened in 2017 in Songpa District and rises 555 metres over the Jamsil area. The tower combines offices, hotel, retail, and observation levels, and its Seoul Sky observatory became a key viewpoint in the southeastern part of the capital.

Free

Seokchon Lake

Seokchon Lake is a divided urban lake beside the Lotte World complex in Jamsil. The waterside paths and seasonal cherry blossoms made it a popular public recreation area and event space in southeastern Seoul, especially after broader redevelopment of the district.

Free

Cheonggyecheon

Cheonggyecheon is a restored stream corridor through central Seoul reopened in 2005 after the removal of an elevated highway. The project reintroduced water, bridges, paths, and public art into a historic east-west route that had long been covered during rapid urban development.

Free

Changdeokgung

Changdeokgung Palace, a remnant of the Joseon dynasty, invites travellers to explore its rich history and beautiful gardens. As the second oldest of Seoul's five grand palaces, it served as the main royal residence from 1592 until 1868. This UNESCO World Heritage Site is renowned for its well-preserved structures dating back to the 1600s-1800s, offering a glimpse into Korea's architectural heritage before modern reconstructions took over.

Free

Deoksugung Palace

Deoksugung Palace, In Seoul, was once the residence of King Gojong of Joseon. The palace showcases a blend of Korean and Western architectural styles, reflecting a historical transition period. travellers can witness the Changing of the Royal Guard ceremony and explore notable halls like Jeonggwanheon Hall and Seokjojeon Hall, which now houses the Daehan Empire History Museum.

Free

Bukchon Hanok Village

Nestled in the heart of Seoul, Bukchon Hanok Village is a captivating destination that transports travellers back to the 14th century. This charming village features narrow streets adorned with beautifully restored traditional Korean homes, offering a unique glimpse into the country's rich architectural heritage. As you wander through its labyrinthine alleys, discover not only views but also an array of cultural experiences nearby.

Free

Gwanghwamun Square

Gwanghwamun Square, a vast public space established in 2009 as part of Seoul's eco-friendly redevelopment, is a key component of the city's plan to connect prominent attractions along Sejong-daero. This includes Gwanghwamun Square, Deoksugung Palace, Sungnyemun Gate, and Seoullo 7017. The area will be transformed into a pedestrian path symbolizing the Walking City policy.

Free

Gwangjang Market

Gwangjang Market is a vibrant hub of traditional Korean culture, where travellers can indulge in an impressive selection of street food and shop for unique clothing and souvenirs. After savoring the delicious offerings at this bustling market, take a leisurely stroll around the historic Dongdaemun Gate, which dates back to the late 1300s. This area not only showcases Seoul's rich history but also features the serene Cheonggyecheon River, perfect for a peaceful walk away from city traffic.

Free

Jogyesa Temple

Jogyesa Temple stands as the heart of Jogye Korean Buddhism, offering a serene escape amidst the bustling skyscrapers of Seoul. Unlike many temples nestled in mountainous regions, this vibrant sanctuary is conveniently located near Gyeongbokgung and Changdeokgung palaces. Adorned with brilliant jade, red, and gold hues alongside intricate frescoes, it boasts a magnificent seated Buddha statue that draws travellers in.

Free

Changgyeonggung Palace

Changgyeonggung Palace is one of Seoul's oldest palace complexes, dating back to the early 12th century. It has served as a recreational retreat for the royal family and features castle structures surrounded by a scenic park with gazebos and pagodas. The name "Changgyeonggung" translates to "unbridled revelry," reflecting its historical purpose.

Free

Heunginjimun Gate (Dongdaemun)

Heunginjimun Gate, also known as Dongdaemun Gate, is a restored part of the Fortress Wall of Seoul from the Joseon Dynasty. The surrounding neighborhood of Dongdaemun is a bustling shopping area with over 20 malls and 30,000 traditional shops and markets. It's a popular destination for international travellers and offers an array of attractions including the Dongdaemun Design Plaza (DDP) and Cheonggyecheon River.

Free

Myeongdong Cathedral

Myeongdong Cathedral, a 19th-century Gothic-style Catholic cathedral in Seoul, is a significant monument in Korean Catholicism. It was the first church in Korea to be built with brick and features a 45-meter tall bell tower. The interior boasts high vaulted ceilings and houses the remains of 9 saints and martyrs. This historical site attracts travellers who come to admire its architecture and learn about its religious significance.

Free

Ikseon-dong Hanok Village

Nestled in the heart of Seoul, Ikseon-dong Hanok Village is a trendy neighborhood that beautifully blends traditional charm with modern flair. This picturesque area features narrow alleys lined with hanok houses dating back to the 1920s, creating a rustic yet hip atmosphere. travellers can explore an array of unique boutiques and delightful restaurants, many of which are perfect for capturing Instagram-worthy moments.

Free

63 Building

Located in Yeouido, Seoul, the 63 Building, also known as 63 Square, is a towering modern structure that has become one of the city's most landmarks. Standing at 249 meters tall with 63 floors, it offers views of the Han River and the cityscape below. The building houses various attractions such as an IMAX cinema, a marine park, a wax museum, and several dining options.

Free

Blue House (Cheong Wa Dae)

The Blue House, also known as Cheong Wa Dae, serves as the executive office and official residence of South Korea's president. Nestled among the forested foothills of Bugaksan Mountain, this exquisite house is often referred to as the Korean version of the White House due to its significance. travellers can enjoy panoramic views of ancient and modern Seoul from the nearby Seoul City Wall.

Free

Changdeokgung Secret Garden

Changdeokgung Secret Garden is a picturesque and expansive area surrounding the Changdeokgung Palace in Seoul. This UNESCO World Heritage-listed site is renowned for its pavilions, serene ponds, and verdant trees, making it a favorite spot for photographers. To avoid crowds, it's to visit during the week, particularly in the late afternoon as the sun sets.

Free

Bongeunsa Temple

Bongeunsa is a traditional Korean Buddhist temple complex located in the upscale Gangnam district. Despite being surrounded by corporate high-rises, the temple's shrines and halls offer a serene retreat with its tree-filled hillside setting. Established in AD 794, the temple has undergone several reconstructions over the centuries. travellers enter through the Jinyeomun (Gate of Truth) guarded by four king guardians.

Free

Myeongdong Shopping Street

Myeongdong Shopping Street is a vibrant hub in Seoul, renowned for its bustling atmosphere and neon lights, especially at night. This lively area seamlessly blends major fashion brands with an array of Korean cosmetics stores and enticing street food stalls. As you stroll through the streets, encounter mouthwatering options like fried crab, skewers of various meats, refreshing fruit shakes, and even succulent lobster.

Free

Namsangol Hanok Village

Namsangol Hanok Village is a recreated Korean village featuring five restored hanoks, built between 1392 and 1910. Situated at the base of Mount Namsan, it offers a peaceful encounter with traditional Korean culture. The village showcases yangban houses from the Joseon era and includes a traditional theater, Seoul Namsan Gugakdang. Free guided tours in English are available on specific days.

Free

Namdaemun Market

Namdaemun Market, a vibrant hub that opened its doors in 1964, is a must-visit destination for anyone exploring Seoul. With over 10,000 stalls, this bustling market offers an impressive array of goods ranging from clothing and household items to unique local products. As you wander through the lively aisles, be tempted by the mouthwatering street food that showcases Korea's culinary delights.

Free

Seoullo 7017

Seoullo 7017 is a unique park that was opened in 2017 on a highway overpass from the 1970s, now transformed into a pedestrian walkway. Stretching for about 1km, it features various installations including architectural and artistic elements, offering views of the surrounding road system. At night, the entire walkway is illuminated with a light exhibition.

Free

Lotte World

Lotte World is a 3-in-1 adventure park in Seoul, easily accessible by subway. It's often compared to Disneyland and features a magic castle and two mascots, Lotty and Lorry. The park offers thrilling rides suitable for all ages, making it perfect for families. travellers can enjoy familiar characters, movie-themed attractions like a swinging viking ship and magic balloon ride, as well as river rapids and carousels at the indoor park.

Free

Dongdaemun Design Plaza (DDP)

Dongdaemun Design Plaza (DDP) is a futuristic architectural landmark and events venue in Seoul. Opened in 2014, it offers a unique space to dream, design, and play. The complex features the Design Museum showcasing innovative Korean designs and the Design Market where travellers can experience cutting-edge technologies. Designed by Zaha Hadid, DDP is located in the fashion hub of Dongdaemun and has revitalized the area into a trendy district with niche designer brands.

Free

Jamsil Baseball Stadium

Jamsil Baseball Stadium, located in the Songpa-gu district south of Seoul, is a versatile sports center that hosted events during the 1988 Olympic Games. It offers not only baseball games but also various other activities throughout the year. The area around Jamsil subway station is a convenient location for travelers to stay due to its excellent transportation links and concentration of activities.

Free

Jongmyo Shrine

Jongmyo Shrine is a significant Confucian shrine in South Korea, dedicated to the royals of the Joseon dynasty. It houses ancestral tablets of kings and queens, reflecting the kingdom's adherence to Confucian principles. Despite its lack of ornamentation compared to royal palaces, this simplicity was intentional to create an atmosphere of dignity and sanctity.

Free

Starfield Library

Nestled within the COEX Mall in Gangnam, Seoul, the Starfield Library is a futuristic cultural space that spans two levels and houses an impressive collection of 70,000 books. Its floor-to-ceiling bookshelves have become on social media platforms, attracting travellers from around the world. The library offers a unique experience for book lovers and Instagram enthusiasts alike, with its towering shelves providing the perfect backdrop for captivating photos.

Free

Jingwansa Temple

Nestled within the serene embrace of Bukhansan National Park, Jingwansa Temple is a centuries-old Buddhist sanctuary that offers travellers an authentic glimpse into monastic life. Just a short drive from downtown Seoul, this hidden gem invites travelers to immerse themselves in its tranquil surroundings and partake in unique experiences like the temple stay program.

Free

Gyeonghuigung Palace

Nestled in the heart of Seoul, Gyeonghuigung Palace is a hidden gem that dates back to the 17th century, specifically built in 1617 during the Joseon dynasty. Originally serving as the residence for crown princes, this palace is smaller than its more famous counterparts but boasts a rich history marked by periods of neglect and restoration. The Myeongjeongjeon Hall stands out as a prime example of traditional Joseon architecture and was once a hub for royal meetings.

Free

Seoul City Wall Trail

The Seoul City Wall Trail, also known as Hanyangdoseong, is a pedestrian path that runs alongside a 14th-century wall. The trail passes through a park, an observation deck, and landmark buildings. The city government has planned to promote walking culture by organizing the Seoul Walkathon festival along the pedestrian-friendly paths connecting royal palaces, major shopping areas, and historical sites in downtown Seoul.

Free

Myeongdong NANTA Theatre

The Myeongdong NANTA Theatre is a attraction in Seoul, offering the award-winning and long-running NANTA Show. This non-verbal comedy performance incorporates traditional Korean percussion music and tells the story of cooks preparing for a wedding through slapstick play. The 90-minute high-energy show takes place at a dedicated theatre in Myeong-dong and has been captivating audiences worldwide since 1997.

Free

Seolleung and Jeongneung Royal Tombs

Seolleung and Jeongneung are two significant cultural heritage sites in Seoul, both listed on the UNESCO World Heritage List. Seolleung is the resting place of King Seongjong and Queen Yun, while Jeongneung houses the tomb of King Jungjong. travellers can't access the tombs directly to preserve their historical significance, but they can admire the intricate sculptures from a distance. The surrounding park offers a pleasant setting for a leisurely walk, with clean public facilities available.

Free

LOTTE MART ZETTAPLEX Seoul Station

LOTTE MART ZETTAPLEX Seoul Station is a must-visit for anyone exploring the city. This unique supermarket offers an array of Korean products and groceries, from processed goods to cosmetics, electronics, fashion accessories, and more. It's a great place to experience local lifestyle and indulge in generous samples. The store is conveniently located next to Seoul Station and features eateries, Daiso, clothes, and other essentials.

Free

Myeongdong Night Market

Myeong Dong Night Market is not your typical traditional night market, but it's a popular spot for travelers due to its location in the bustling shopping district of Myeongdong. It features major stores like Lotte and Shinsegae, as well as various cosmetic brands. The market comes alive from 5:00 PM onwards with food stalls offering Korean delicacies such as gyerangbbang and dak-galbi.

Free

Sungnyemun Gate (Namdaemun)

Sungnyemun Gate, also known as Namdaemun, is a historic landmark in Seoul that is part of the city's pedestrian-friendly Sejong-daero space reorganization project. The gate holds significant cultural and historical value and was even set on fire by an arsonist in 2008 before being restored and reopened in 2013. travellers can now freely access the gate and enjoy traditional guard-changing performances.

Free

Seoul Children's Grand Park

Seoul Children's Grand Park is an expansive family-friendly destination featuring a zoo, botanical garden, and amusement park facilities. First opened in 1973, it provides a vast green retreat within the bustling city. Visitors can enjoy scenic walking trails, seasonal flower displays, and various outdoor recreational spaces.
