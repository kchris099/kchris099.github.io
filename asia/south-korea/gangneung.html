Destination Guide — Gangwon

Explore Gangneung

A Brief History

Isolated for centuries by the formidable Taebaek Mountains, Gangneung developed a unique cultural independence significantly tied to its coastal traditions. Its preserved Joseon-era estates and ancient festivals offer a rare, undisturbed glimpse into the historical fabric of the eastern seaboard. Visitors today can clearly trace the intricate layers of this unique heritage throughout the preserved streets and monuments.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

Seongyojang House

Nestled in the picturesque Gangwon-do Province, Seongyojang House is a example of 18th-century architecture that once belonged to an upper-class family during the Joseon Dynasty. This beautifully preserved traditional Korean house now serves as a museum, offering visitors a glimpse into the elegant lifestyle of Korea's aristocracy.

Free

Ojukheon

Ojukheon, located in Gangneung, is a historic site that holds great significance as the birthplace of the renowned Confucian scholar Yi Yulgok and his mother, Shin Saimdang. This complex features Munseongsa Temple, an ancestral shrine of Yulgok, Mongryongsil Chamber where he was born, Eoje Pavilion where his relics are kept, Yulgok Memorial Hall, and an exhibition hall of family relics.

Free

Gangneung Ojuk Hanok Village

Gangneung Okuk Hanok Village offers a unique experience where visitors can rent Korean-style hanoks and immerse themselves in the neighborhood's atmosphere. While not entirely traditional, these fairly new hanoks provide an authentic village stay experience. Additionally, the Seongyojang House within the village is a must-visit, offering a glimpse into the former upper-class residence of the Naebeon Lee noble family.

Free

Gangneung Wolhwa Street

Wolhwa Street in Gangneung is a revitalized pedestrian avenue built along a former railway line. The vibrant street is lined with cafes, boutique shops, and local food stalls, and features numerous art installations and murals. It has become a popular cultural and commercial hub, offering a relaxed environment for strolling and experiencing the local urban atmosphere.

Free

Anmok Beach

Anmok Beach in Gangneung is a popular destination known for its wide and relaxed sandy beach, bordering a road filled with several casual coffee shops. Perfect for coffee lovers, the Coffee Street at Anmok Beach boasts of unique themed cafes that provide an excellent view of the ocean. Despite cold weather conditions, Anmok Beach is still worth visiting because of its beautiful scenery and numerous cafes with their distinct ambiance and specialties.

Free

Gyeongpo Beach

Gyeongpo Beach is a popular summer destination boasting of a wide sandy shoreline, surrounded by pine trees and lined with various dining and accommodation options. The beach is known for being clean with staff dedicated to keeping it free of litter. The water is crystal clear and shimmering, making it an ideal spot for swimming. During off-seasons, the place becomes less crowded but still offers a friendly entertainment strip serving fresh seafood.

Free

Gyeongpo Lake Plaza

Gyeongpo Lake Plaza in Gangwon-do is a serene haven for nature enthusiasts and families seeking a peaceful retreat. This picturesque park offers views of the sun setting behind layered mountains, while diverse wildlife such as mallards, cranes, and egrets can be spotted around the lake. The vibrant blooms in spring add to its charm, making it an ideal spot for jogging or biking along the scenic 4.5 km path that encircles the lake.

Free

Gyeongpo Lake

Gyeongpo Lake is a documented body of water in Gangneung, South Korea, recognised in regional geographic listings for its ecological and recreational role. The shoreline is accessible from the surrounding district and supports walking paths and boat launches. Travellers approach from the adjacent promenade and combine the visit with nearby parks and waterfront districts.

Free

Gangmun Beach

Gangmun Beach is a coastal gem located in Gangneung, South Korea, where nature and tranquility come together beautifully. This picturesque beach features a serene seaside pine forest that stretches towards the livelier Anmok Beach to the south. The tall pines, planted nearly a century ago to protect locals from harsh winter winds, create a backdrop against the vibrant blue of the Pacific Ocean.

Free

Daegwallyeong Natural Recreation Forest

Daegwallyeong Natural Recreation Forest sits within the Daegwallyeong Natural Recreation Forest area of Gangneung, documented in local heritage registers for its civic or cultural role. Visitors approach from the main access road and combine the visit with neighbouring heritage buildings and public spaces in the district.
