Destination Guide — Gangwon

Explore Gangneung

A Brief History

Isolated for centuries by the formidable Taebaek Mountains, Gangneung developed a unique cultural independence significantly tied to its coastal traditions. Its preserved Joseon-era estates and ancient festivals offer a rare, undisturbed glimpse into the historical fabric of the eastern seaboard. Visitors today can clearly trace the detailed layers of this unique heritage throughout the preserved streets and monuments.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

Seongyojang House

Seongyojang House is a visitor site in Gangneung, South Korea. This beautifully preserved traditional Korean house now is a museum, offering visitors a glimpse into the elegant lifestyle of Korea's aristocracy. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Free

Ojukheon

Ojukheon is a visitor site in Gangneung, South Korea. Ojukheon, located in Gangneung, is a historic site that holds great significance as the birthplace of the known Confucian scholar Yi Yulgok and his mother, Shin Saimdang. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Free

Gangneung Ojuk Hanok Village

Gangneung Okuk Hanok Village offers a unique experience where visitors can rent Korean-style hanoks and immerse themselves in the neighborhood's atmosphere. While not entirely traditional, these fairly new hanoks provide an authentic village stay experience, the Seongyojang House within the village is a must-visit, offering a glimpse into the former upper-class residence of the Naebeon Lee noble family.

Free

Gangneung Wolhwa Street

Gangneung Wolhwa Street is a visitor site in Gangneung, South Korea. Wolhwa Street in Gangneung is a revitalized pedestrian avenue built along a former railway line. It has become a popular cultural and commercial hub, offering a relaxed environment for strolling and experiencing the local urban atmosphere. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Free

Anmok Beach

Anmok Beach is a beach in Gangneung, South Korea. Anmok Beach in Gangneung is a popular destination known for its wide and relaxed sandy beach, bordering a road filled with several casual coffee shops. Perfect for coffee lovers, the Coffee Street at Anmok Beach has of unique themed cafes that provide an excellent view of the ocean. Access is typically on foot from nearby roads or beachfront paths.

Free

Gyeongpo Beach

Gyeongpo Beach is a beach in Gangneung, South Korea. The beach is known for being clean with staff dedicated to keeping it free of litter. The water is clear and shimmering, making it an ideal spot for swimming. During off-seasons, the place becomes less crowded but still offers a friendly entertainment strip serving fresh seafood. Access is typically on foot from nearby roads or beachfront paths.

Free

Gyeongpo Lake Plaza

Gyeongpo Lake Plaza is a public square in Gangneung, South Korea. This park offers views of the sun setting behind layered mountains, while diverse wildlife such as mallards, cranes, and egrets can be spotted around the lake. The active blooms in spring add to its charm, making it an ideal spot for jogging or biking along the scenic 4.5 km path that encircles the lake. Open pavement and surrounding facades define the visitor setting.

Free

Gyeongpo Lake

Gyeongpo Lake is a documented body of water in Gangneung, South Korea, recognised in regional geographic listings for its ecological and recreational role. The shoreline is accessible from the surrounding district and supports walking paths and boat launches. Travellers approach from the adjacent promenade and combine the visit with nearby parks and waterfront districts.

Free

Gangmun Beach

Gangmun Beach is a beach in Gangneung, South Korea. Gangmun Beach is a coastal gem located in Gangneung, South Korea, where nature and tranquility come together beautifully. The tall pines, planted nearly a century ago to protect locals from harsh winter winds, create a backdrop against the active blue of the Pacific Ocean. Access is typically on foot from nearby roads or beachfront paths.

Free

Daegwallyeong Natural Recreation Forest

Daegwallyeong Natural Recreation Forest sits within the Daegwallyeong Natural Recreation Forest area of Gangneung, documented in local heritage registers for its civic or cultural role. Visitors approach from the main access road and combine the visit with neighbouring heritage buildings and public spaces in the district.
