Summer Palace gardens and Ming-era wall segments complement imperial monuments on the North China plain.

Temple of Heaven

The Temple of Heaven is a known temple complex dating back to 1420, known for its distinctive circular buildings set in a popular park. It's located in the Dongcheng district of Beijing, which is an ideal area for accommodations due to its proximity to historical sites like the Forbidden City and Tiananmen Square. The district also offers attractions such as Jingshan Park for nature lovers and the Wangfujing pedestrian mall for shopping enthusiasts.

Yuanmingyuan Park

Yuanmingyuan Park, located in northwestern Beijing, is home to the ruins of the Old Summer Palace. Once an imperial residence known for its architecture and gardens, it was looted and destroyed during the Second Opium War in 1860. The palace was known for its vast art and antiquities collection. Today, visitors can explore the ruins of Dashuifa Site, a reminder of the palace's history.

Meridian Gate

The Meridian Gate, also known as Wumen, is a grand and historic ceremonial entrance to the Forbidden City in Beijing. It served as the main entrance for Ming and Qing Emperors and is now landmarks within the Palace Museum. This imposing structure consists of five towers and gateways, overlooking Tiananmen Square. As the largest gate of the museum, it remains the sole entry point into the Forbidden City.
