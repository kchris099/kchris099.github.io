Luoyang

Luoyang was a founding capital of several early Chinese dynasties and a major Buddhist centre on the Central Plains. The Longmen Grottoes, White Horse Temple, and Tang-era palace ruins record how emperors, monks, and court poets projected imperial authority along the Yellow River corridor between the Han and Tang golden ages. Walking routes through the historic centre help visitors relate these monuments to wider patterns of settlement and civic life in China.

Longmen Grottoes

Limestone cliffs beside the Yi River contain thousands of niches and statues at the Longmen Grottoes. Carving began under the Northern Wei after the capital moved to Luoyang and continued through the Sui and Tang dynasties, producing works that range from tiny devotional figures to the large Vairocana Buddha at Fengxian Temple. Inscriptions and changing styles document several centuries of Buddhist patronage.
