Explore Xi'an

A Brief History

Xi'an, ancient Chang'an, served as capital for the Western Han, Sui, and Tang dynasties and terminus of the Silk Road. City walls, bell and drum towers, and the Great Mosque record how imperial ritual, Buddhist pilgrimage, and Muslim merchant quarters shaped northwest China's greatest medieval metropolis. Walking routes through the historic centre help visitors relate these monuments to wider patterns of settlement and civic life in China.

Top Sights & Activities

CNY15

Great Mosque of Xi'an

Great Mosque of Xi'an is a religious building in Xi'an, China. Surrounding the mosque, visitors can explore streets lined with various crafts and traditional Shaanxi handicrafts. Nearby, the Muslim Quarter offers an array of delectable Muslim cuisine, including roasted beef, fish, and pancakes at reasonable prices. The exterior and main interior spaces are usually viewable outside service times.

CNY30

Bell Tower of Xi'an

Bell Tower of Xi'an is a visitor site in Xi'an, China. Construction and early use date to around 1384. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest. Bell Tower of Xi'an remains part of the public visitor circuit in Xi'an. Local maps identify Bell Tower of Xi'an within Xi'an, China.

CNY54

Ancient City Wall

The Ancient City Wall of Xi'an is one of the oldest, largest, and -preserved Chinese city walls. Built during the Ming Dynasty, the massive fortification encircles the historic city center. Visitors can walk or cycle along the wide top of the wall, enjoying panoramic views of the ancient city and its modern surroundings.

¥54

Fortifications of Xi'an

Fortifications of Xi'an, also known as Xian City Wall, is a fortified historic site in Xi'an, China. Construction and early use date to around 1370. The Fortifications of Xi'an, also known as Xian City Wall, is a well-preserved ancient defensive system in China. Built in 1370 during the Ming dynasty, it spans 13.7 kilometers and is surrounded by a dry moat. Walls, courtyards, or towers form the main visitor route when the grounds are open.

¥25

Small Wild Goose Pagoda

Small Wild Goose Pagoda is a landmark in Xi'an, China. Dating back to the 8th century, it offers views from the top and is surrounded by an adjacent park. The pagoda is part of the historical Jianfu Temple complex, which has been rebuilt during the Ming and Qing dynasties.

CNY40

Giant Wild Goose Pagoda

The Giant Wild Goose Pagoda, also known as Big Wild Goose Pagoda, is a known attraction in Xi'an, China. Dating back to the 7th century during the Tang Dynasty, this brick pagoda was initially constructed with five stories to house Buddhist scriptures and figurines brought from India. Renovated during the Ming Dynasty, it now stands at a height of 64 meters and is a well-preserved religious site for Buddhists.

Free

Shaanxi History Museum

Located Xi'an, Shaanxi History Museum is a treasure trove for history enthusiasts and art lovers alike. Opened in 2009, this museum showcases an extensive collection of over 370,000 cultural relics that span from ancient times to the late Qing dynasty. The building itself is a example of Tang-style architecture, reminiscent of the grandeur of China's past.
