Explore Xi'an

A Brief History

Xi'an, ancient Chang'an, served as capital for the Western Han, Sui, and Tang dynasties and terminus of the Silk Road. City walls, bell and drum towers, and the Great Mosque record how imperial ritual, Buddhist pilgrimage, and Muslim merchant quarters shaped northwest China's greatest medieval metropolis. Walking routes through the historic centre help visitors relate these monuments to wider patterns of settlement and civic life in China.

Great Mosque of Xi'an

The Great Mosque of Xi'an is a architectural marvel built in 742, boasting and colorful designs along with serene gardens. The mosque stands as a symbol of the harmonious coexistence between Muslim and Han people in Xian. Surrounding the mosque, visitors can explore streets lined with various crafts and traditional Shaanxi handicrafts. Nearby, the Muslim Quarter offers an array of delectable Muslim cuisine, including roasted beef, fish, and pancakes at reasonable prices.

Bell Tower of Xi'an

The Bell Tower of Xi'an, an structure built in 1384 during the Ming dynasty, stands as a testament to the city's rich history. This remarkable tower has served various purposes over its 600-year existence, originally functioning as a timekeeper and now transformed into a museum showcasing cultural relics and traditional artworks. Visitors can enjoy captivating performances during the day and views at night from this historic landmark.

Fortifications of Xi'an

The Fortifications of Xi'an, also known as Xian City Wall, is a well-preserved ancient defensive system in China. Built in 1370 during the Ming dynasty, it spans 13.7 kilometers and is surrounded by a dry moat. The imposing walls feature imposing gates and offer a cycling path atop for visitors to explore. This historical site holds great significance for the Chinese nation and has been designated as a national cultural site.

Small Wild Goose Pagoda

Small Wild Goose Pagoda is a landmark in Xi'an, China. Dating back to the 8th century, it offers views from the top and is surrounded by an adjacent park. The pagoda is part of the historical Jianfu Temple complex, which has been rebuilt during the Ming and Qing dynasties.

¥40

Giant Wild Goose Pagoda

The Giant Wild Goose Pagoda, also known as Big Wild Goose Pagoda, is a renowned attraction in Xi'an, China. Dating back to the 7th century during the Tang Dynasty, this brick pagoda was initially constructed with five stories to house Buddhist scriptures and figurines brought from India. Renovated during the Ming Dynasty, it now stands at a height of 64 meters and serves as a well-preserved religious site for Buddhists.

Free

Shaanxi History Museum

Nestled in the heart of Xi'an, Shaanxi History Museum is a treasure trove for history enthusiasts and art lovers alike. Opened in 2009, this impressive museum showcases an extensive collection of over 370,000 cultural relics that span from ancient times to the late Qing dynasty. The building itself is a example of Tang-style architecture, reminiscent of the grandeur of China's past.
