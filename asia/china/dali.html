A Brief History

Dali is located in Yunnan province and has a long history as a political and cultural center in southwestern China. It was the capital of the Nanzhao Kingdom in the eighth century and later the Kingdom of Dali, which governed the region until the Mongol conquest in the thirteenth century. The area sits between the Cangshan Mountains and Erhai Lake, an environment that influenced its historical settlement patterns. Dali retains significant architectural heritage from the Ming and Qing dynasties.

Three Pagodas

Nestled in the serene landscape of Dali, Yunnan, the Three Pagodas stand as a testament to ancient architecture and Buddhist heritage. This remarkable trio of pagodas is set against a tranquil reflecting pond, creating an enchanting atmosphere that captivates visitors.

Free

Dali Ancient City

Dali Ancient City is a captivating destination that beautifully blends history and modernity. With its charming chessboard-like layout filled with artisanal shops, delightful eateries, and cozy bars, visitors can enjoy a laid-back atmosphere while exploring the vibrant streets. The South Gate, also known as Double Crane Tower or Cheng'en Tower, stands proudly as the oldest structure in Dali Old Town, boasting over 600 years of history.
