Hong Kong

Hong Kong Island was ceded to Britain in 1842 after the First Opium War; Kowloon and the New Territories followed under later treaties. The colony grew as a free port linking China and global shipping, with Victoria Harbour, tram lines, and steep hillside districts defining daily life. Japanese occupation in 1941–45 and postwar refugee influxes remade the city. The 1997 handover returned sovereignty to China while keeping a separate legal and commercial system.

Stanley Murray House

Stanley Murray House is a historic colonial-era building located in Stanley Bay, off the southern coast of Hong Kong Island. Originally built in 1844 as officers' quarters, it is one of the oldest surviving public buildings in Hong Kong. During World War II, it served as a headquarters for the Japanese military police. In 1982, it was dismantled and later reconstructed at its current location using pieces from the original structure.
