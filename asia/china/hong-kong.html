Hong Kong

Hong Kong Island was ceded to Britain in 1842 after the First Opium War; Kowloon and the New Territories followed under later treaties. The colony grew as a free port linking China and global shipping, with Victoria Harbour, tram lines, and steep hillside districts defining daily life. Japanese occupation in 1941–45 and postwar refugee influxes remade the city. The 1997 handover returned sovereignty to China while keeping a separate legal and commercial system.

The Peak Tower

The Peak Tower stands near the upper terminus of the Peak Tram as a commercial and viewing complex above Hong Kong Island, and the site looks across Central, Victoria Harbour, Kowloon and the island's wooded slopes when weather and haze allow. Its curved modern form contains shops, restaurants, entertainment spaces and the paid Sky Terrace, while public roads and nearby paths offer other outlooks.

Victoria Peak

Hong Kong Island reaches its high elevation around Victoria Peak, a wooded ridge above Central and the western harbor; dense skyline, harbor traffic, outlying hills and changing cloud conditions define the outlook. Residential roads, the Peak Tram, buses and walking paths lead to viewpoints around the Peak Tower and Lugard Road.

Hong Kong Disneyland

Opened in 2005 on reclaimed land at Lantau Island's Penny's Bay, Hong Kong Disneyland adapts the Disney resort format to a compact regional park. The Castle of Magical Dreams anchors themed areas with rides, stage shows, parades, restaurants, and character appearances. Attractions and entertainment change over time, so the park is better understood as an operating resort than as a fixed collection of seven original lands.

Hong Kong Palace Museum

Nine galleries occupy the Hong Kong Palace Museum at the western end of the West Kowloon Cultural District. Developed with Beijing's Palace Museum, the institution displays imperial Chinese art, decorative objects, calligraphy, clothing, and rotating loans alongside exhibitions from other collections. The contemporary building uses stacked volumes and a vertical central axis that echoes.

Sky 100 Hong Kong Observation Deck

Sky 100 occupies the 100th floor of the International Commerce Centre in West Kowloon, an indoor deck with floor-to-ceiling glass over Victoria Harbour and the skyline. Exhibits and telescopes explain districts visible below, and the height places the harbour crossing roughly at mid-level in the view.

Kowloon Walled City Park

Completed in 1995, Kowloon Walled City Park follows early Qing Jiangnan garden design across eight scenic zones. The ground once held a sixteenth-century garrison fort that later became the dense Kowloon Walled City. Paths, lawns, and shade now occupy the cleared site where the crowded settlement stood.

Sik Sik Yuen Wong Tai Sin Temple

Wong Tai Sin Temple brings Taoist, Buddhist and Confucian practices together within a large religious compound in Kowloon. The administering organization maintains halls, gardens, shrines and spaces associated with fortune telling and worship of the healer deity. Red columns, tiled roofs, incense courts and the Good Wish Garden create a ceremonial setting that remains active for devotees, especially around Lunar New Year.

Former Kowloon-Canton Railway Clock Tower

A 44-meter red-brick clock tower is all that remains of the demolished Kowloon-Canton Rail Terminus in Tsim Sha Tsui. The exterior cannot be entered, yet it marks the walk from the Star Ferry pier toward the promenade. Bay views open around the tower base where the former station once stood.

Victoria Harbour

Victoria Harbour is a active and waterfront area that is a backdrop for both day and night activities. This natural channel, which separates Hong Kong from the Kowloon Peninsula, has historical significance as one of the British Empire's major military and trading ports.

Lan Kwai Fong

Narrow lanes in Lan Kwai Fong pack restaurants, bars, and nightclubs into a compact Central district. Food ranges from updated Chinese bao shops to egg-waffle stands beside late-night venues. Office workers fill the alleys after hours, making the slope a regular nightlife corridor rather than a daytime market alone.

Tai Kwun

Tai Kwun reuses Hong Kong's former Central Police Station, magistracy, and Victoria Prison as a heritage and arts compound. Restored colonial blocks sit beside new galleries for exhibitions, shops, and dining, turning a long-vacant law-enforcement campus into a public cultural yard in Central.

Man Mo Temple

On Hollywood Road in Sheung Wan, Man Mo Temple dates to the 1800s and honours the gods of literature and war. Scholars once sought favour from King-Emperor Man and Holy King Emperor Kwan here during Ming and Qing eras. Incense coils hang in the halls, and the exterior and main interiors are usually viewable outside ritual times.

St John's Cathedral

Completed in 1849, St John's Cathedral stands in Central as the seat of Hong Kong's Anglican archbishop, and Japanese forces converted it to a clubhouse during the occupation, and postwar restoration returned the building to worship. The cruciform Gothic Revival building has a white exterior, pointed arches, stained glass, memorials, and a shaded garden amid government and commercial towers.

Hong Kong Heritage Museum

Sha Tin's Hong Kong Heritage Museum uses courtyards and tiled roofs to organize galleries on art, design, history and popular culture. Permanent displays include Cantonese opera material and the T.T. Tsui Gallery of Chinese Art, while major temporary exhibitions rotate through larger halls. A Bruce Lee exhibition has also interpreted the actor's career through film, personal objects and cultural influence.

Ten Thousand Buddhas Monastery

The Ten Thousand Buddhas Monastery is a large and ornate Buddhist temple accessed by a steep flight of steps. Despite its name, it actually houses just under 13,000 Buddha statues. Constructed in 1957, the path leading up to the temple is adorned with 500 life-sized golden Buddhas. At the top, travellers can explore a nine-tier pagoda and temple filled with even more Buddha statues.

Ocean Park

Ocean Park combines amusement rides with a marine and animal section on Hong Kong's southern shore. Enclosures include Adventures in Australia, Bird Theatre, Chinese Sturgeon Aquarium, Gator March, and Panda Village. Paths link thrill rides with animal habitats across the hillside park rather than separating them into distant sites.

Sai Wan Swimming Shed

Sai Wan Swimming Shed is a simple coastal bathing structure on Hong Kong Island's western shore, about a twenty-minute walk from Kennedy Town MTR. Wooden platforms and ladders give access to the water away from the denser harbourfront, so swimmers and sunset watchers share a quieter shoreline strip.

Stanley Murray House

Murray House began as officers' quarters in Central in 1844 and later housed Japanese military police during the occupation. The colonial block was dismantled in 1982, with numbered stone components stored before its reconstruction on the Stanley waterfront; its arcaded facades and verandas now frame restaurants and shops, so the building's present commercial use differs from its original military function.
