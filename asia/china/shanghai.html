Destination Guide — Shanghai

Explore Shanghai

A Brief History

Rising from a humble fishing village, Shanghai was transformed by the 1842 Treaty of Nanking into a global treaty port. The foreign concessions brought rapid industrialization and architectural fusion, turning the city into the undisputed economic powerhouse of modern China. This rich historical tapestry established a strong foundation that continues to shape the city's dynamic contemporary identity.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

The Bund

The Bund is a historic 1,500-meter-long waterfront promenade in Shanghai, featuring a mix of modern and colonial buildings. The area has undergone gentrification, attracting luxury brands like MCM, Cartier, Prada, and Ralph Lauren to set up shop in former colonial banking and trading headquarters along the Huangpu River.

Free

Dongfangmingzhu Pleasure Cruise Boat Wharf

The Dongfangmingzhu Pleasure Cruise Boat Wharf is a major departure point for sightseeing cruises along the Huangpu River in Shanghai. Located near the iconic Oriental Pearl Tower, the wharf offers visitors the opportunity to experience the striking contrast between the historic Bund architecture and the modern Pudong skyline from the water.

Free

Oriental Pearl TV Tower

The Oriental Pearl TV Tower is a futuristic 468-meter tall structure in Shanghai, offering panoramic city views, a history museum, and a revolving restaurant. It is located in the Lujiazui area and is part of the modern Shanghai skyline. The tower features metallic spheres and serves as an entertainment center with multimedia shows, VR roller coasters, and observation decks including a glass-bottomed observatory.

Free

Shanghai Tower

The Shanghai Tower, soaring to an impressive height of 632 meters, is a must-visit landmark in the heart of Shanghai. As you embark on your adventure, start by heading to Lujiazui where experience one of the fastest elevators in the world that whisks you up to the 118th-floor observation deck at 546 meters.

Free

Jin Mao Tower

Shanghai's Jin Mao Tower is an 88-story skyscraper that boasts a striking design and houses various amenities such as restaurants, offices, and the Grand Hyatt hotel. This architectural marvel stands at 420.5 meters tall and is known for its impressive Skywalk, an enclosed observation deck located on the 88th floor that can accommodate over 1,000 visitors at once.

Free

Jade Buddha Temple

The Jade Buddha Temple, located in the Anyuan Lu district of Shanghai, is a Buddhist temple known for its large and ornate jade Buddha sculptures. The temple was built between 1918 and 1928 to replace the original one from 1882. It houses two Shakyamuni statues brought from Burma by the monk Huigen.

Free

Jing'an Temple

Jing'an Temple is a historic Buddhist complex located in the bustling center of Shanghai. The temple, which comprises three main halls and houses a large sitting jade Buddha, offers visitors a unique experience against the backdrop of busy shopping malls and skyscrapers. The aroma of incense fills the air as you wander through the halls, where light incense bundles and make offerings at various shrines and statues.

Free

Tianzifang

Tianzifang is a vibrant arts and crafts enclave developed from a renovated traditional residential area in the French Concession of Shanghai. The labyrinthine alleyways are lined with boutique shops, art galleries, cafes, and bars, retaining the historic Shikumen architectural style while offering a lively, bohemian atmosphere.

Free

Yu Garden

Yu Garden, a 5-acre garden constructed in 1577, showcases Ming dynasty pavilions, ponds, rockeries, and arched bridges. The Deluxe Yu Garden View Rooms offer comfort and indulgence with views of the garden and old Shanghai town. These rooms feature king or twin beds, a sitting area, walk-in closet, large marble bathroom with luxury amenities like 'Bed of Reign,' digitally controlled guestroom system with iPad for TV control and room service orders.

Free

Nanjing Road Pedestrian Street

Nanjing Road Pedestrian Street is a renowned and bustling stretch of road in Shanghai, China. It has earned the reputation as 'the First Chinese Business Street' and is home to over 600 shops offering traditional, specialty, and famous brand stores. Visitors can explore souvenir shops, street vendors, buskers, and restaurants along this street. As the largest full-time shopping street in Shanghai and Asia at large, it offers a wide variety of unique features for shoppers to enjoy.
