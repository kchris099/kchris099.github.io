Explore Datong

A Brief History

Datong was founded as Pingcheng under the Han dynasty and elevated to Northern Wei capital in 398 CE, when monks led by Tan Yao began carving the Yungang Grottoes (460-525 CE). The Liao dynasty built the Shanhua Temple complex (1038 onward), and the Jin dynasty rebuilt Shanhua in 1148 and reconstructed Huayan in 1140; Ming garrisons then reset the city as a Great Wall frontier garrison. Twentieth-century coal seams along the Datong-Shuozhou line turned it into an industrial centre, and rail-freight decline from the 1990s cleared space for heritage tourism on the cave temples and reconstructed Ming walls.

Top Sights & Activities

CNY120

Yungang Grottoes

The Yungang Grottoes comprise a UNESCO World Heritage Buddhist cave-temple complex with over 51,000 carved statues along the northern Wuzhou River valley west of Datong. Excavated 460-525 CE under the Northern Wei, the site marks a peak of Buddhist art when Pingcheng was the imperial capital and the monk Tan Yao led the carving. Visitors walk an open-air cliff face to observe surviving Tang and Song polychrome paint and towering sandstone Buddhas.

CNY40

Shanhua Temple

Shanhua Temple is a 9th-century Buddhist temple in central Datong, originally laid out in the late Tang and rebuilt in its current form in 1148 under the Jin dynasty. The complex retains surviving Liao-era timber halls, two Tang-period stone pagodas, and a complete set of original sandstone statuary. Travellers enter through the mountain gate on Ren'ai Street, cross the Maitreya Hall, and reach the rear Hall of the Three Buddhas.

CNY40

Huayan Temple

Huayan Temple is an 11th-century Liao dynasty Buddhist compound laid out as twin western and eastern monastery buildings on the south side of central Datong. The brick-and-timber complex was reconstructed under the Jin dynasty in 1140 and again after Ming restorations in the 16th century, retaining Liao-period wooden statuary and a library pavilion housing Buddhist sutras. Visitors pass the bell and drum towers, then walk the central axis to a standing clay Amitabha Buddha.
