Destination Guide — South India

Explore Mysore

A Brief History

Serving as the capital of the Kingdom of Mysore for nearly six centuries, the city thrived under the Wodeyar dynasty's progressive rule. Its magnificent palaces and strong tradition of silk and sandalwood craftsmanship reflect a long era of sustained cultural patronage. The remarkable survival of these cultural and architectural milestones provides a powerful window into the region's storied past.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

St. Joseph's Cathederal (St. Philomena's shrine)

St. Philomena's Cathedral, located in Mysore, is a neo-gothic church with soaring twin spires and intricate stained glass windows depicting the Christ era. Built between 1933-1941, it was inspired by the Cologne Cathedral in Germany. The church houses a relic of Saint Philomena obtained in 1926 and features a candlelit prayer room that offers solace to visitors.

Free

Sri Ranganathaswamy Temple

Nestled along the serene banks of a river, the Shri Ranganathaswami Temple, also known as Pancharanga Kshetra, is a revered pilgrimage destination that boasts intricate carvings and historic architecture. This ancient temple dates back to 984 CE and stands as a architectural marvel near Mysore, close to the site of Tipu Sultan's demise. Dedicated to Lord Vishnu in his reclining form (Anantha Shayana), visitors are often captivated by the idol itself.

₹20

Daria Daulat Bagh

Daria Daulat Bagh, also known as Tipu's Summer Palace, is a historical gem located in Srirangapatna. Constructed in 1784, this Indo-Saracenic masterpiece was built from teak and rosewood and features lavish decorations covering its interiors. The palace is adorned with intricate floral designs on the ceilings and murals depicting courtly life and Tipu's campaigns against the British on the walls.

Free

Sri Nimishambha Temple

Sri Nimishambha Temple is a 17th-century Hindu temple located on the picturesque banks of the Kaveri River in Srirangapatna, Karnataka. Dedicated to Goddess Nimishambha, an incarnation of Parvati, this ornate temple boasts a colorful gopuram and exquisite carvings on its tower.

₹75

Ranganathittu Bird Sanctuary

Ranganathittu Bird Sanctuary is a haven for birds and other wildlife, spread across 6 islets on the Kaveri River. It's renowned for its diverse flora and fauna, making it the largest sanctuary in Karnataka. Named after the Hindu god Ranganathaswamy, this sanctuary offers guided boat tours along the river where visitors can spot crocodiles basking in the sun and discover secret spots.

Free

Balamuri Falls

GRS Fantasy Park, located just 15 minutes from Mysore in Karnataka, is a popular tourist spot known for its amusement and water park. Spanning across 40 acres of greenery, the park offers a range of attractions suitable for the entire family. From thrilling speed rides and free-fall waterslides to gentle carousels for younger kids, there's something for everyone.

₹350

Snow Palace Mysuru

Snow Palace Mysuru is a popular indoor entertainment facility offering a simulated winter environment in the tropical city of Mysore. The attraction features sub-zero temperatures, artificial snowfalls, ice sculptures, and snow slides. It provides a unique, family-friendly recreational experience, allowing visitors to enjoy winter activities year-round.

₹299

Lokaranjan Aqua World Underwater Zoo

Lokaranjan Aqua World Underwater Zoo is a popular attraction in Mysore, India, located near the Mysore Zoo. It is known as the largest underwater aquarium in the country and features an impressive "underwater glass tunnel" that provides visitors with a unique underwater experience. The zoo showcases a diverse range of fish species and offers an engaging environment for both children and adults to learn about marine life.

₹120

Sri Chamarajendra Zoological Gardens

Sri Chamarajendra Zoological Gardens, commonly known as Mysuru Zoo, is a well-established and popular zoo located near the palace in Mysore, India. It was established by Sri Chamarajendra Wadiyar Bahadur and is known for its cleanliness, conservation breeding of endangered species, and recreation of natural habitats for captive animals.

Free

Shree Chamundeshwari Devi Temple

Perched atop the Chamundi hills in Mysore, the Mysuru Shri Chamundeshwari Thayi Temple is a traditional Hindu temple dedicated to goddess Durga. It features statues of Nandi and Mahishasura, the demon, and offers panoramic views. The temple is steeped in history and legend, with its rituals closely tied to the story of goddess Chamundeshwari's triumph over the buffalo-headed demon Mahishasura.

Free

Chamundi Hill View Point

Chamundi Hill View Point is a documented natural feature in Mysore, India, listed in regional geographic and conservation records for its geological formation and protected status. The site is accessible via marked trails and is open to visitors during daylight hours. Travellers follow the principal path from the trailhead and combine the visit with nearby viewpoints and forest walks.

Mysuru Palace

Mysore Palace, a Indo-Saracenic architectural marvel completed in 1912, boasts a grand durbar hall and hosts weekly illuminations. The palace is famous for the traditional Dasara procession, Jamboo Savari, featuring a decorated elephant carrying the idol of Goddess Chamundeshwari during Vijaya Dashami. This centuries-old celebration was initiated by Raja Wodeyar in 1610 and has continued uninterrupted.

Free

Kote Sri Anjaneya Swamy Temple

Kote Sri Anjaneya Swami Gudi is one of the oldest and most prominent temples located near the northern gate of Mysuru Palace. Dedicated to Lord Hanuman, this powerful and peaceful temple is a popular destination for locals and tourists alike. The temple is known for its special poojas performed on amavasye days, as well as the tradition of blessing new vehicles.

₹1297

GRS Fantasy Park

GRS Fantasy Park is a major amusement and water park located on the outskirts of Mysore. The expansive facility offers a wide array of thrilling water slides, wave pools, and dry amusement rides suitable for all ages. It is a premier destination for family entertainment and recreation, providing relief from the summer heat.
