Darjeeling

= Darjeeling lies between the Mechi and Teesta rivers in the Eastern Himalayas. In the 18th century, it was part of a boundary region that had stirred ambitions and insecurities in several South Asian states. For the greater part of the century, the Chogyal-ruler of the northern Kingdom of Sikkim had asserted possession of this territory. In the closing decades, the Gurkha kingdom of Nepal expanded eastwards to bring Darjeeling into its territory. Its army stopped short of the Teesta, beyond which at the time lay the Kingdom of Bhutan. The English East India Company began to show an interest in the Darjeeling hills in the early 19th century. At the time Darjeeling's indigenous population largely consisted of the Lepcha and Limbu peoples. The Company's interference in territorial matters began in the aftermath of its army's victory over the Gurkhas in the Anglo-Nepalese War.

Dali Monastery

Druk Sangag Choling, commonly called Dali Monastery, was established in Darjeeling in the twentieth century within the Drukpa Kagyu tradition of Tibetan Buddhism. A large prayer hall, murals, statues, monastic quarters, and hillside courtyards support teaching and ritual. Monks' schedules and ceremonies guide access, and size comparisons with every monastery in West Bengal add little to its actual religious role.

02 AdmissionFree

Peace Pagoda, Darjeeling

White tiers rise above the Dali Monastery slopes, a Japanese-style peace pagoda overlooking Darjeeling's tea ridges and Himalayan haze. Worshippers circle the base and climb toward the Buddha image under open sky. Outside service hours the exterior and main halls usually stay open for quiet viewing, with prayer flags and mountain air framing the climb from town.

03 AdmissionFree

Darjeeling Mall

Darjeeling Mall, or Chowrasta, is the open hilltop plaza at the heart of Darjeeling where Ridge Road meets the town's main promenade. Benches, pony rides, and views toward Kanchenjunga on clear days define the square. Locals treat it as the daily meeting ground above the bazaar lanes.

27.0449° N / 88.2677° E

04 AdmissionFree

Shri Mahakal Temple, Darjeeling

Shri Mahakal Temple sits above Darjeeling's Chowrasta Mall as a hilltop shrine dedicated to Mahakal (Shiva). Prayer flags and stone steps approach the sanctum with views toward Kanchenjunga on clear days. The temple remains an active worship site; exteriors and courtyard spaces open around daily rituals.

27.0478° N / 88.2679° E

05 AdmissionFree

Observatory Hill View Point

Observatory Hill rises above Darjeeling's Mall as a shared sacred landscape for Hindu and Buddhist traditions, and cloud frequently obscures the mountains, while the hill's layered religious history matters more than treating it solely as a guaranteed panorama. Paths lined with prayer flags lead to Mahakal Temple, smaller shrines, bells, and wooded viewpoints that may reveal Kanchenjunga in clear weather.

27.0499° N / 88.2660° E

06 AdmissionFree

Darjeeling Clock Tower

Darjeeling's clock tower forms part of the municipal building near the central market and Mall area; alterations and repairs have changed the surrounding structure. Built during the colonial period, its masonry, clock faces, and hourly chimes remain a practical orientation point within the steep town center.

27.0408° N / 88.2661° E

Tiger Hill, Darjeeling

Tiger Hill reaches about 2,590 metres east of Darjeeling and is used mainly for early views toward Kanchenjunga and, under exceptional conditions, Everest. Road traffic and large dawn crowds gather at the summit observatory, while cloud, rain, and haze often prevent a clear sunrise.

26.9967° N / 88.2944° E

08 AdmissionFree

Ghoom Monastery

Ghoom Monastery (Yiga Choeling) on the ridge above Darjeeling houses Tibetan Buddhist halls and a manuscript collection that includes volumes of the Kangyur. A large Maitreya statue dominates the main shrine. The hill station setting places the gompa among tea slopes and the toy-train line.

27.0117° N / 88.2504° E

09 AdmissionFree

Ghum Railway Station

Ghum station opened in the nineteenth century on the Darjeeling Himalayan Railway at roughly 2,258 metres above sea level. Narrow-gauge tracks, a modest station building, signals, and a railway museum explain the engineering and operation of the historic mountain line; it is often described as India's high railway station, but newer lines at greater elevations make that claim outdated.

27.0083° N / 88.2538° E

10 Admission₹200 (factory tour)

Happy Valley Tea Estate

Happy Valley Tea Estate spreads across steep slopes below Darjeeling, where rows of tea bushes descend toward factory buildings and wooded ravines. Established in the nineteenth century, the estate illustrates the labor-intensive cultivation and processing behind the region's tea industry. Paths and viewpoints connect the planted hills with the modern town above. The steep terrain is central to the estate's character.

27.0513° N / 88.2587° E

11 Admission₹40

Tenzing Rock HMI

Tenzing Rock is a natural outcrop used by the Himalayan Mountaineering Institute for basic climbing instruction near Darjeeling, and its name honors Tenzing Norgay, who trained and taught in the area after the 1953 Everest ascent. Supervised practice may take place on designated faces, but equipment, instructor availability.

27.0578° N / 88.2567° E
