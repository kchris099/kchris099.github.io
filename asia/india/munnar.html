Destination Guide — South India

Explore Munnar

A Brief History

Developed in the late 19th century by British planters, Munnar was transformed from dense forest into sweeping tea estates. The colonial-era infrastructure and enduring plantation economy completely reshaped the landscape, establishing a lasting legacy in the Western Ghats. Visitors today can clearly trace the intricate layers of this unique heritage throughout the preserved streets and monuments.

Top Sights & Activities

₹10

Mattupetty Dam

Tucked away in the enchanting hills of Munnar, Kerala, Mattupetty Dam is a blend of engineering and nature. This concrete marvel not only serves as part of the Pallivasal hydro-electric power project but also offers visitors a chance to engage in thrilling water sports like speed boating and paddle boating, organized by the Idukki Tourist Promotion Council.

₹25

Ripple Waterfalls

Ripple Waterfalls is a picturesque cascade located near Munnar, set amidst dense forests and rolling tea plantations. The waterfall tumbles over a series of rocky steps, creating a soothing, rippling effect. The surrounding area offers a tranquil natural environment, making it a popular spot for nature photography and peaceful relaxation.

₹200

Eravikulam National Park

Eravikulam National Park, located in Munnar, Kerala, is a scenic natural area known for its lush greenery and views of the Western Ghats. The park is home to the endangered Nilgiri Tahr and offers well-maintained trails and guided tours for visitors to immerse themselves in the rich biodiversity of the region.

₹50

Lakkom Waterfalls

Lakkom Waterfalls is a impressive natural attraction located within the Eravikulam National Park near Munnar. The falls originate from the pristine Eravikulam stream and cascade down a steep, rocky incline into a clear, shallow pool. Visitors can enjoy a refreshing dip in the cool waters, surrounded by the dense, biodiverse flora of the Western Ghats.

₹1000

Dream Land Fun and Adventure

Dream Land Fun and Adventure is an outdoor amusement and adventure park situated in the scenic hills of Munnar. The park offers a wide variety of activities, including ziplining, ATV rides, camel safaris, and a suspension bridge. It provides an exciting, family-friendly alternative to the region's traditional sightseeing and nature walks.

Free

2nd Mile View Point

The 2nd Mile View Point is a dramatic roadside overlook located near Munnar, offering sweeping panoramic views of the surrounding tea estates and the Western Ghats. The elevated vantage point allows visitors to appreciate the vast, undulating landscape of manicured tea bushes and mist-covered mountains, making it a favorite stop for photography.

Free

Attukad Waterfalls

Nestled in the picturesque hills of Munnar, Attukad Waterfalls is a must-visit destination for nature lovers. The sight of multiple waterfalls cascading down amid lush greenery and rolling hills is truly . Located about 9 km from Munnar town, the journey to the falls offers scenic views of tea plantations, making it an adventure in itself.

Free

Pothamedu View Point

Pothamedu View Point is one of the most prominent scenic overlooks in Munnar, providing expansive views of the sprawling tea, coffee, and cardamom plantations. The elevated location offers a vast panorama of the lush, green valleys and the winding Muthirapuzha River. It is particularly popular for its impressive sunrise and sunset vistas.

Free

Photo point

Photo Point is a frequently popular, scenic stop located on the Mattupetty Road near Munnar. The area is characterized by gently rolling hills covered in dense, neatly manicured tea plantations, interspersed with tall silver oak trees. The striking contrast of colors and the orderly landscape make it an ideal location for capturing quintessential Munnar photographs.

₹60

Munnar Rose Garden

Nestled in the picturesque hills of Munnar, the Munnar Rose Garden is a delightful 2-acre haven that captivates visitors with its vibrant array of flora. This enchanting garden showcases not only rose varieties but also an assortment of spices and fruit trees, including cardamom, vanilla, strawberries, and litchis. The aromatic atmosphere created by these plants adds to the charm of this serene escape.

Free

Chinnakanel Waterfalls

CSI Christ Church, located in the vibrant town of Palayam in Trivandrum, is a gothic church that has contributed to the historical and architectural splendor of the area for over 19 decades. As the oldest church in the city, it was consecrated in 1859 and boasts a simple yet elegant Gothic style with beautiful stained glass windows depicting The Good Shepherd.
