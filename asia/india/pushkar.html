Explore Pushkar

A Brief History

Revered as one of India's oldest sacred cities, Pushkar centers around a holy lake linked to Hindu creation myths. For centuries, it has drawn millions of pilgrims and traders to its annual camel fair, maintaining a significantly traditional rhythm amidst modernizing Rajasthan. This continuous historical development established a lasting framework that strongly informs the area's modern cultural evolution.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

Shree Savitri Mata Mandir

Savitri Mata Temple, located in Pushkar, Rajasthan, is a captivating Hindu temple perched on a hilltop. Visitors can reach the temple via a cable car or by trekking up the ascent. The temple offers views of Pushkar Lake and the town below. Dedicated to Savitri, Lord Brahma's first wife, it holds historical and religious significance. Other notable temples in the area include Ramavaikunth Temple and Varaha Temple.

Free

Jagatpita Shri Brahma Mandir

Jagatpita Shri Brahma Mandir is a simple Hindu temple dedicated to the god Brahma, featuring colorful shrines and offering views of the surrounding lake. The temple is located near Pushkar Sarovar and other religious sites, making it a significant location in Hindu texts. Legend has it that Lord Brahma created springs at this site after defeating demon Vajranabha, and later performed a yagna here.

Reservation required

Maharaja Desert Camp Pushkar Safari

If you're looking for an desert experience, the Maharaja Desert Camp Pushkar Safari is a must-visit destination. Nestled in the landscapes of Pushkar, this camp offers both camel and jeep safaris that take you through sand dunes and provide views of sunrises and sunsets. As you traverse the golden sands, be treated to picturesque backdrops featuring the majestic Aravalli hills. The adventure doesn't stop there!.

Reservation required

Pushkar Desert Safari

Pushkar Desert Safari offers immersive excursions into the arid landscapes surrounding the holy town of Pushkar. Operators provide guided camel rides and jeep tours through the rolling sand dunes, often culminating in a traditional Rajasthani dinner and cultural performance at a desert camp. It is a quintessential experience of the Thar Desert environment.

Reservation required

Dharma camel safari

Dharma Camel Safari is a well-established tour operator in Pushkar, specializing in authentic desert experiences. They offer customized camel treks ranging from short sunset rides to multi-day camping expeditions in the Thar Desert. The tours provide an intimate look at the rugged landscape and the traditional lifestyle of the local desert communities.

Free

Pushkar Lake

Pushkar Lake, situated in the small town of Pushkar in Rajasthan, is a sacred Hindu pilgrimage site surrounded by bathing ghats and temples. It is a prominent spot for spiritual distinction and salvation, drawing thousands of devotees annually to take a dip in its holy waters. The lake is also famous for hosting the world-renowned Pushkar Fair, which showcases the vibrant culture of Rajasthan with colorful displays, traditional dishes, dance performances, and entertaining contests.

Free

Rangji Temple

Rangji Temple is a example of South Indian architecture and Mughal design, making it one of the top places to visit in Pushkar at night. This 18th-century temple is dedicated to Lord Vishnu's incarnation, Rangji, and features a monumental Gopuram and expansive entrance reflecting Dravidian architecture.

Free

Shri Rama Vaikunth Nath (Naya rang ji) Temple, Pushkar

Nestled just 10 kilometers northwest of Ajmer City, the Shri Rama Vaikunth Nath (Naya Rang Ji) Temple in Pushkar is a testament to Hindu devotion and architectural splendor. Built in 1922, this historic stone temple showcases intricate carvings and vibrant shrines that captivate visitors. Its South Indian-style architecture features towering spires adorned with 361 unique depictions of deities, making it significant temples in the area.

Free

Gurudwara Sahib - guru nanak dev ji Pushkar (Ajmer)

Gurudwara Sahib in Pushkar is a significant religious site that holds great importance for both Sikhs and Hindus. It is believed that Guru Nanak Dev and Guru Gobind Singh, two prominent Sikh Gurus, visited this place centuries ago. The gurudwara was built in the early 19th century to commemorate their visit. This serene temple offers a peaceful atmosphere where visitors can experience a sense of tranquility.

Reservation required

Pushkar Camel Safari

Pushkar Camel Safari provides classic guided tours through the dramatic sand dunes of Rajasthan. The experience allows visitors to ride traditional decorated camels, exploring the remote desert terrain and small rural villages. The safaris are particularly popular during the annual Pushkar Camel Fair, offering a deep dive into the region's nomadic heritage.
