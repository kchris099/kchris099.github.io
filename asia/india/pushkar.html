Pushkar

Hindu tradition ties Pushkar's lake to creation myths, and pilgrimage has long outweighed any courtly function. The annual camel fair layered livestock trade onto that sacred calendar. Brahma's temple and the ghats around the lake are the town's reason for existing. Only a few Brahma temples exist in India; Pushkar's is the pilgrimage magnet among them.

Shree Savitri Mata Mandir

Savitri Temple occupies Ratnagiri Hill above Pushkar and is reached by a long stair route or ropeway. The shrine honors Savitri within local traditions surrounding Brahma's sacrifice and the formation of Pushkar's sacred geography. Temple buildings are relatively modest, while lake, town, and aravalli views open from the summit; ropeway operation and hill access change with weather and maintenance.

02 AdmissionFree

Jagatpita Shri Brahma Mandir

Jagatpita Shri Brahma Mandir is a simple Hindu temple dedicated to the god Brahma, featuring colorful shrines and offering views of the surrounding lake. The temple is located near Pushkar Sarovar and other religious sites, making it a significant location in Hindu texts. Legend has it that Lord Brahma created springs at this site after defeating demon Vajranabha, and later performed a yagna here.

Maharaja Desert Camp Pushkar Safari

Maharaja Desert Camp outside Pushkar sets tents on sand toward the Aravalli foothills for overnight stays and camel outings. Folk music and camp meals follow dusk rides across the dunes. The site packages a desert night within reach of Pushkar's lake and temples.

Pushkar Desert Safari

Pushkar Desert Safari offers immersive excursions into the arid landscapes surrounding the holy town of Pushkar. Operators provide guided camel rides and jeep tours through the rolling sand dunes, often culminating in a traditional Rajasthani dinner and cultural performance at a desert camp. It is a typical experience of the Thar Desert environment.

Dharma camel safari

Dharma Camel Safari is a private Pushkar operator offering short camel rides and longer excursions through the dry countryside around town. Programs may combine sunset travel, village routes, meals or overnight camping according to the selected trip, placing the activity within Pushkar's tourism economy and camel traditions while route length, camp arrangements and animal handling vary by booking.

06 AdmissionFree

Pushkar Lake

Pushkar Lake forms the sacred center of the town, ringed by stone ghats, temples, lanes and dharamshalas. Hindu traditions connect its waters with Brahma and pilgrims bathe or conduct rites along designated steps. The annual fair brings livestock trading, performances and tourism beyond the lake precinct, while water quality, ritual rules and funeral activity deserve respectful factual treatment.

26.4872° N / 74.5540° E

07 AdmissionFree

Rangji Temple

South Indian temple forms meet Mughal-influenced detailing in this Pushkar shrine near the sacred lake. Exterior courts and main interiors usually remain viewable outside service times, including evening hours when lighting picks out the carved surfaces. The temple stands apart from simpler lakeside shrines by that mixed architectural vocabulary.

26.4903° N / 74.5554° E

08 AdmissionFree

Shri Rama Vaikunth Nath (Naya rang ji) Temple, Pushkar

Naya Rangji Temple, also called New Rangji, brings South Indian Vaishnava forms into Pushkar's nineteenth-century pilgrimage landscape, and construction around 1922 produced the Naya Rang Ji temple about ten kilometers northwest of Ajmer, within Pushkar's pilgrimage field. A high gateway, courtyards, shrines, colonnades, and a ceremonial chariot support worship of Ranganatha.

09 AdmissionFree

Gurudwara Sahib - guru nanak dev ji Pushkar (Ajmer)

Gurudwara Sahib in Pushkar is a Sikh shrine associated with visits by Guru Nanak and later Guru Gobind Singh. The prayer hall and courtyard sit within the pilgrimage town shared by Hindu lake temples. Langar and kirtan keep the gurdwara active for both Sikh and local visitors.

Pushkar Camel Safari operators lead guided rides on decorated camels across dune tracks and village edges outside the lake town. Routes vary from short sunset loops to longer desert circuits. The rides use the open Rajasthan fringe rather than Pushkar's bazaar lanes.
