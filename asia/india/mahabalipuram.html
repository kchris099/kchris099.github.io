Explore Mahabalipuram

A Brief History

Mahabalipuram is in India. Established as a regional settlement beyond an older fortified core, the city developed through industrialization in the late 19th century, post-war reconstruction, and modern civic expansion that reshaped its public spaces, markets, and surrounding landscape. Museums, markets, and street plans in Mahabalipuram still show layers from those periods.

Five Rathas (Pancha Rathas)

Five Rathas (Pancha Rathas) is a visitor site in Mahabalipuram, India. The Five Rathas, also known as Pancha Pandava Rathas, are a group of monolithic temples in Mahabalipuram. Each ratha is dedicated to one of the five Pandava brothers from the Hindu epic Mahabharata. The main structures and grounds are the principal points of interest.

Free

Tiger Cave

Tiger Cave is a visitor site in Mahabalipuram, India. Tiger Cave, located about 5 km from Mahabalipuram in the Saluvankuppam region, is a retreat for nature enthusiasts. It's a popular picnic spot where families can enjoy outdoor activities and even have a photoshoot. Travellers can explore the rock-cut temple and an old Shiva temple on the premises. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

₹40

Group of Monuments at Mahabalipuram

Group of Monuments at Mahabalipuram is a monument in Mahabalipuram, India. The Group of Monuments at Mahabalipuram, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, is a collection of ancient stone temples and structures dating back to the 7th-8th centuries. The memorial stands in an open plaza or roadside setting.

₹600

Mahabalipuram Shore Temple

Mahabalipuram Shore Temple is a religious building in Mahabalipuram, India. The Mahabalipuram Shore Temple is a historic Hindu temple located along the Coromandel coast in India. It is part of a group of sanctuaries that includes rathas, mandapas, and open-air rock reliefs. The modern city of Mahabalipuram was established nearby in the 19th century by the British Raj. The exterior and main interior spaces are usually viewable outside service times.

Free

Mahabalipuram Beach

Mahabalipuram Beach, located south of the city of Mahabalipuram, offers a setting with its white sand and green landscape. Travellers can explore an old ruined Dutch fort and cemetery nearby, showing fine stone work and tombstones dating back to the arrival of European powers in India. The beach is known for pure white sand and offers opportunities for swimming, surfing, and enjoying views of the 8th-century Shore Temple.

Free

Krishna's Butter Ball

Krishna's Butter Ball is a visitor site in Mahabalipuram, India. Krishna's Butter Ball is a unique and offbeat tourist attraction located in Mahabalipuram. The boulder is perched on a downhill slope in a truly mystical and mysterious manner, defying all laws of physics and reason. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Free

Varaha Cave

Varaha Cave is a visitor site in Mahabalipuram, India. Constructed in the 7th century in the rock-cut style, it showcases detailed life-sized sculptures, frescoes, and columns. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest. Varaha Cave remains part of the public visitor circuit in Mahabalipuram. The main structures and grounds are the principal points of interest.

Free

Descent of the Ganga River and Arjuna's Penance

Descent of the Ganga River and Arjuna's Penance is a visitor site in Mahabalipuram, India. The Descent of the Ganga River, also known as Arjuna's Penance, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site located in Mahabalipuram, India. This ancient rock relief was commissioned by the Pallava kings and depicts the descent of the sacred river Ganga to earth from the heavens. The main structures and grounds are the principal points of interest.

Free

Krishna Mandapam

Krishna Mandapam is a visitor site in Mahabalipuram, India. Krishna Mandapam is an ancient cave temple in Mahabalipuram, dedicated to Lord Krishna. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest. Krishna Mandapam remains part of the public visitor circuit in Mahabalipuram.

Free

Arulmigu Sri Sthala Sayana Perumal Temple

Located in Mahabalipuram, the Arulmigu Sri Sthala Sayana Perumal Temple is an ancient and revered shrine dedicated to Lord Vishnu. Constructed in the Dravidian style of architecture, it is known for its painted stone statues and shore location. Arulmigu Sri Sthala Sayana Perumal Temple remains an active place of worship and a documented historic building in Mahabalipuram, India.
