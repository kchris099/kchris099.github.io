Destination Guide — Mahabalipuram

Explore Mahabalipuram

A Brief History

Established as a regional settlement beyond an older fortified core, the city developed through industrialization in the late 19th century, post-war reconstruction, and modern civic expansion that reshaped its public spaces, markets, and surrounding landscape. Restored religious buildings, civic buildings, and natural features now define the contemporary visitor experience.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

Five Rathas (Pancha Rathas)

The Five Rathas, also known as Pancha Pandava Rathas, are a group of monolithic temples in Mahabalipuram. Each ratha is dedicated to one of the five Pandava brothers from the Hindu epic Mahabharata. Carved out of granite, these temples showcase distinctive architectural styles and intricate details. The individual rathas are named after Draupadi, Arjuna, Bhima, Sahadeva, and Dharmaraja.

Free

Tiger Cave

Tiger Cave, located about 5 km from Mahabalipuram in the Saluvankuppam region, is a picturesque retreat for nature enthusiasts. The site features an 8th-century Hindu temple with tiger-head carvings and a well-maintained lawn surrounded by palm and casuarina trees. It's a popular picnic spot where families can enjoy outdoor activities and even have a photoshoot. travellers can explore the rock-cut temple and an old Shiva temple on the premises.

Free

Group of Monuments at Mahabalipuram

The Group of Monuments at Mahabalipuram, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, is a collection of ancient stone temples and structures dating back to the 7th–8th centuries. Located on the Coramandel Coast in Tamil Nadu, India, this site features impressive Rathas (chariots), cave sanctuaries, and open-air reliefs such as the famous Arjuna Penance.

Free

Mahabalipuram Shore Temple

The Mahabalipuram Shore Temple is a historic Hindu temple located along the Coromandel coast in India. It is part of a group of sanctuaries that includes rathas, mandapas, and open-air rock reliefs. The temple complex features thousands of sculptures dedicated to Shiva and has been designated as a UNESCO World Heritage Site. The modern city of Mahabalipuram was established nearby in the 19th century by the British Raj.

Free

Mahabalipuram Beach

Mahabalipuram Beach, located south of the city of Mahabalipuram, offers a picturesque setting with its white sand and green landscape. travellers can explore an old ruined Dutch fort and cemetery nearby, showcasing fine stone work and tombstones dating back to the arrival of European powers in India. The beach is famous for its pure white sand and offers opportunities for swimming, surfing, and enjoying views of the 8th-century Shore Temple.

Free

Krishna's Butter Ball

Krishna's Butter Ball is a unique and offbeat tourist attraction located in Mahabalipuram. It features a massive granite boulder, measuring six meters in height and five meters in width, with an estimated weight of 250 tons. The boulder is perched on a downhill slope in a truly mystical and mysterious manner, defying all laws of physics and reason.

Free

Varaha Cave

The Varaha Cave, located near Mahabalipuram, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and a remarkable example of indigenous temple architecture. Constructed in the 7th century in the rock-cut style, it showcases intricate life-sized sculptures, frescoes, and columns. The cave temple is dedicated to Lord Vishnu and features detailed carvings depicting various Hindu gods such as Durga and Gajalakshmi.

Free

Descent of the Ganga River and Arjuna's Penance

The Descent of the Ganga River, also known as Arjuna's Penance, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site located in Mahabalipuram, India. This ancient rock relief was commissioned by the Pallava kings and depicts the descent of the sacred river Ganga to earth from the heavens. The relief showcases intricate carvings of gods, people, elephants, and scenes from Hindu mythology and South Indian life.

Free

Krishna Mandapam

Krishna Mandapam is an ancient cave temple in Mahabalipuram, dedicated to Lord Krishna. The temple features grand bas-relief sculptures illustrating timeless stories of Lord Krishna's benevolence and scenes from pastoral life in Govardhan. remarkable carvings depicts how Lord Krishna saved Braj and its people by lifting the entire Govardan Parvat on his little finger.

Free

Arulmigu Sri Sthala Sayana Perumal Temple

Located in Mahabalipuram, the Arulmigu Sri Sthala Sayana Perumal Temple is an ancient and revered shrine dedicated to Lord Vishnu. Constructed in the Dravidian style of architecture, it is known for its painted stone statues and picturesque shore location.
