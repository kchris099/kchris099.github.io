Destination Guide — South India

Explore Bangalore

A Brief History

Established as a mud fort in 1537, Bangalore evolved into a major British military cantonment due to its favorable climate. Post-independence investments in heavy industry and research laid the foundation for its explosive transformation into India's premier technology hub. Visitors today can clearly trace the intricate layers of this unique heritage throughout the preserved streets and monuments.

ISKCON Bangalore

The ISKCON temple in Bangalore, located in Rajaji Nagar, is a magnificent tribute to Lord Krishna. The temple was built on a 7-acre campus and took 6 years to complete. Designed by Sri Madhu Pandit Dasa, the architecture features a 17-meter gold-plated flag post and 8.5-meter gold Kalash Shikaras. Situated on Hare Krishna Hill, the temple's Neo-Classical style blends modern amenities with traditional design.

Free

Phoenix Marketcity

Step into the world of Phoenix Marketcity, a massive mall in Bangalore that offers an unparalleled shopping and leisure experience. With over 270 international brand-name stores spread across four levels, visitors can explore a wide range of products including electronics, clothes, beauty products, footwear, and fashion accessories. The mall also features a sizeable food court, dining outlets, a spa, and a gaming zone.

₹2,300

M. Chinnaswamy Stadium

M. Chinnaswamy Stadium, situated in the vibrant city of Bangalore, is a renowned cricket ground that serves as the home turf for the Indian Premier League team, Royal Challengers Bangalore. This 40-year-old stadium is centrally located and easily accessible via various modes of public transportation. Despite some obstructed views due to pillars, it has hosted numerous matches and boasts impressive infrastructure. The passionate crowd adds to the electric atmosphere during games.

Free

C.S.I St. Mark's Cathedral, located in Bangalore, is an 1808 church known for its monumental baroque architecture and English-language services. The cathedral has a rich history dating back to the early 19th century during the East India Company's rule in India. Shri Chāmarajendra (Cubbon) Park

Cubbon Park, officially named Shri Chamarajendra Park, is a major historic park covering over 300 acres in the central administrative area of Bangalore. Established in 1870, the park features abundant flora, shaded walking paths, and several prominent neo-classical buildings, including the State Central Library and the High Court of Karnataka.

Free

Sri Chamarajendra Park

Nestled within the lush greenery of Cubbon Park, the Bangalore Aquarium stands as a must-visit attraction for nature enthusiasts and families alike. As the largest aquarium in the city, it boasts an impressive array of exotic fish species that will captivate your imagination. From vibrant Siamese fighters to graceful angelfish and intriguing freshwater prawns, each tank offers a glimpse into the underwater world.

Free

UB City

UB City is a three-level luxury mall located in the Central Business District of Bengaluru, India. Sprawling over 16 lakh square feet, it consists of four towers housing offices, banks, retail stores, a five-star hotel, serviced apartments, restaurants, lounges, food courts, pubs, cafes and an art gallery. The mall offers high-end shopping with international retailers like Rolex and Louis Vuitton.

₹50

Vidhana Soudha

Vidhana Soudha is a massive and architecturally significant building that houses the state legislative chambers in Bangalore, Karnataka. It is a landmark structure constructed in a neo-Dravidian style, incorporating elements from various historical kingdoms. The building features imposing columns, a central dome topped with a structure resembling the state emblem, and watchtowers.

₹30

Indira Gandhi Musical Fountain Park

The Indira Gandhi Musical Fountain Park, situated in the heart of the city, boasts beautifully landscaped gardens with a variety of tree species and shrubs. The main highlight is the impressive musical fountain that showcases coordinated water, lights, and music performances twice nightly. This technologically advanced fountain is known for its captivating patterns projected on a large screen. Built in 1995 by B.N.A.

Free

St. Mary's Basilica, Bengaluru

St. Mary's Basilica, Bengaluru, is a Gothic-style Catholic church that was consecrated in 1882 and is located just 2 kilometers from the city center. It is the oldest church in Bangalore, with a history dating back to the 17th century. The church celebrates St. Mary's Feast Day every September, attracting large crowds and spreading an elegant festive atmosphere throughout the city.

Free

Samsung Opera House

The Experience Store - Samsung Opera House in Bangalore offers an immersive in-store experience with a wide range of Samsung products including smartphones, smartwatches, and more. The venue has a rich history, having been previously utilized for local businesses before being shut down for over 20 years due to disputes. After winning the case in 2008, the owner transformed it into the world's largest Samsung experience center.

Free

Maha Bodhi Society Bangalore

Maha Bodhi Society, located in Central Bangalore, is a serene and enriching destination for those seeking to explore Buddhism and meditation. The temple houses a vast collection of Buddhist texts and books, making it a hub for spiritual retreats and lectures. Visitors can participate in guided meditation sessions led by monks, providing an ideal environment for tranquility and mindfulness. The peaceful surroundings and welcoming atmosphere create a perfect retreat for those seeking peace.

Free

Infant Jesus Church and Shrine

Infant Jesus Church, located in Bangalore, is a Roman Catholic church dedicated to Infant Jesus of Prague. The church features a shrine that attracts numerous devotees seeking blessings and miracles. With the capacity to accommodate up to 2500 people at once, it hosts seven masses in different languages. Established in 1971, this relatively new shrine has gained popularity due to the reported miracles associated with it.

Free

Bugle Rock Park

Bugle Rock Park, situated in Basavanagudi behind the Bull Temple, is a serene and culturally rich park. It is named after a 3000-year-old rock called Bugle, which was once used for warning the people about intruders by a bugle call. The park features beautiful gardens, waterfalls, fountains, and three temples. One of its main attractions is the watchtower built by Kempe Gowda that offers panoramic views of the city.

Free

Shri Gavi Gangadhareshwara Swamy Temple

Shri Gavi Gangadhareshwara Swamy Devasthana, also known as Gavipuram Cave Temple, is a fascinating example of Indian rock-cut architecture. Dating back to the 16th century and dedicated to Lord Shiva, this temple is renowned for its monolithic sculptures and annual sun illumination phenomenon on Makara Sankranti. The temple's unique location within a natural cave adds to its mystical charm, making it a must-visit destination in Bengaluru.

₹70

HAL Heritage Centre and Aerospace Museum

The HAL Heritage Centre and Aerospace Museum, located in Bangalore, India, is a unique attraction that showcases the rich history of Indian aviation and the contributions of Hindustan Aeronautics Limited (HAL). Established in 2001, this museum features an extensive collection of aircraft and helicopter displays, aircraft engine models, flight simulators, and a mock Air Traffic Control Tower.

Free

Shivoham Shiva Temple

Shivoham Shiva Temple, located in Old Airport Road, Bangalore, is a sacred complex featuring impressive 65-feet tall Shiva Idol and Lord Ganapathi statues. The temple's artificial cave leading to the inner sanctum adds to its allure. It is a popular destination for worshippers and tourists alike, drawing visitors from all over the world.

Free

Ashoka Pillar Monument

The Ashoka Pillar Monument in Bangalore is a prominent historical landmark located in the Jayanagar neighborhood. Erected to commemorate the inauguration of the planned suburb, the tall stone pillar is a central focal point of the area. It stands in a landscaped traffic circle, surrounded by the bustling commercial and residential district.

₹30

Lalbagh Botanical Garden

Lalbagh Botanical Garden in Bangalore is a historic and expansive garden that originated as a private royal garden spanning 40 acres. Initially designed in Mughal style, it was further developed by Tipu Sultan and later expanded by British and Indian horticulturists. The garden is renowned for its support of agriculture, conservation of plant species, and contribution to the advancement of cultivation.

Free

Ragigudda Sri Prasanna Anjaneya Swamy Temple

Ragigudda Shri Prasanna Anjaneyaswami Temple, located in Jayanagar, Bangalore, is a historic Hindu temple situated on a hill. It is dedicated to Lord Hanuman and features colorful shrines and scenic views. The temple was built in 1969 and has a mythological legend behind its construction, with the hill being made of Ragi grain.

Free

Mini Forest

Mini Forest sits within the Ragigudda Sri Prasanna Anjaneya Swamy Temple area of Bangalore, documented in local heritage registers for its civic or cultural role. Visitors approach from the main access road and combine the visit with neighbouring heritage buildings and public spaces in the district.

Free

Ranga Shankara

Ranga Shankara, located in Bangalore, is a renowned theater dedicated to promoting performing arts. Established by Arundhati Nag in honor of her late husband, actor Shankar Nag, the theater offers a diverse calendar of theatrical events from India and around the world. The venue's intimate setting provides an immersive experience for audiences, showcasing traditional Indian dramas, contemporary plays, and experimental performances.

Free

Lulu Mall Bengaluru

Lulu Mall, situated on a sprawling 14-acre mixed-use property in Rajaji Nagar, Bengaluru, officially opened its doors in October 2021. The mall boasts an impressive 7.9 lakh square feet of space across four storeys and is home to over 140 national and international brands. Additionally, the property features 356 super luxury apartments and duplex penthouses, further elevating the overall premium shopping experience for visitors.

Free

Sri Someshwara Swamy Temple, Halasuru

Sri Someshwara Swami Temple, located in Bangalore, is an ancient Hindu temple dedicated to Lord Shiva. It was originally built during the Chola period and later renovated during the Vijayanagara Kingdom and Kempe Gowda II's reign. The temple features intricate carvings of various Hindu deities such as Arunachaleswara, Nanjundeshwara, Kamakshamma, Panchalingeshwara, and Bhimeswara.

Free

Ulsoor Lake

Ulsoor Lake, and oldest lakes in Bangalore, offers a peaceful retreat amidst lush greenery. Visitors can enjoy boating and leisurely walks along the well-maintained pathways that surround the lake. The picturesque setting with its striking reflections on the water makes it a perfect spot for capturing Instaworthy photos. Additionally, nearby attractions such as a war memorial and Lalbagh Botanical Gardens provide romantic backdrops for pre-wedding photographs.

₹130

PLaY Arena

PLaY Arena in Bangalore is a leisure complex that offers a wide range of activities to keep visitors entertained. From bowling and go-karting to paintball, laser tag, and climbing, there's something for everyone. The venue also features arcade games, table tennis, and archery. While it's located a bit outside the central part of Bangalore, it's worth the visit for both adults and children alike.

Free

Agara Lake

Agara Lake, located in Bangalore, is a picturesque spot known for its lush greenery and diverse birdlife including pelicans and coots. The almost 100-acre lake features a walking track along the tree-lined shoreline, making it an ideal destination for nature enthusiasts and joggers. Efforts to restore and convert the area into a biodiversity park are ongoing, with initiatives by citizens and organizations contributing to its preservation.

₹10

Turahalli Forest

Turahalli Forest, the city's only surviving forest, offers rugged trails for hiking, mountain biking, and rock climbing. The lush greenery and scenic views make it a popular spot for cyclists. Muthathi is surrounded by dense forest as part of the Cauvery Wildlife sanctuary with the Cauvery river flowing through it. It has mythological associations with Sita and Hanuman.

₹300

Bengaluru Palace

Bengaluru Palace, a modern royal residence inspired by Windsor Castle, is a must-visit destination in the city. Built in 1878 as the abode for Mysore King Chamarajendra Wadiyar X's son, this historical marvel boasts large courtyards, interior decor, fortified towers, and colorful gardens. The palace also houses an amusement park and offers tours to visitors.

₹650

Snow City Bengaluru

Snow City Bengaluru is an indoor amusement park within the Fun World Complex, offering a variety of snow-themed activities and attractions. This unique destination provides relief from the heat with its snow balling, mountain climbing, ice skating/skiing, and a captivating snow castle. Spanning approximately 12,000 square feet, it features ecological snow elements such as rock-strewn mountains and engaging activities like snow rafting and sliding.

Free

Chowdiah Memorial Hall

Chowdiah Memorial Hall, shaped like a violin, is a renowned cultural center in Bengaluru. It was constructed in 1961 as a tribute to the legendary violin maestro Tirumakudalu Chowdiah and is managed by the Academy of Music. The spacious auditorium hosts various events including concerts, plays, and other performances. While it's not exclusively dedicated to drama, many theatrical shows are featured here, offering visitors a different perspective on theatre.

₹10

Sankey Tank

Sankey Tank, located in the suburbs of Malleshwaram, is a man-made recreational lake that offers a serene escape for visitors. The area features park benches, gazebos, and a waterside promenade perfect for leisurely strolls. Originally constructed in 1882 to combat drought, the 35-acre lake is now a popular spot for couples seeking a romantic setting with its walking track, boating facilities, and lush greenery.

Free

Indian Institute of Science (Bengaluru)

The Indian Institute of Science is a world-renowned research institute located in Bangalore, India. It was founded by industrialist Jamsetji Tata in 1919 and features beautiful colonial-era buildings designed by architect C.F Steven. The institute is home to a variety of research facilities, including laboratories dedicated to physics, chemistry, biology, and nanoscience.

Free

Buddha Shanthi Kanive

Buddha Shanthi Kanive (Buddha Peace Valley) is a serene Buddhist meditation center and park located near Bangalore. The site features a large, golden statue of Lord Buddha, landscaped gardens, and quiet walking paths designed for contemplation and spiritual retreat. It offers a peaceful environment away from the noise of the city.
