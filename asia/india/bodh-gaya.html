Bodh Gaya

Bodh Gaya is identified as the place where Siddhartha Gautama attained enlightenment beneath a Bodhi tree. The Mahabodhi Temple complex, rebuilt and restored across centuries, became a major Buddhist pilgrimage centre. Ashokan and later pala patronage, colonial archaeology, and modern temples built by Asian Buddhist nations surround the site. Pilgrims, monasteries, and the temple's stone spire now define this Bihar town's identity.

Mahabodhi Temple

Mahabodhi Temple marks the place where Buddhist tradition locates Siddhartha Gautama's enlightenment beneath the Bodhi tree; its status recognizes the site's global religious importance, while security and worship shape movement through the complex. The tall brick sanctuary, railings, stupas, votive shrines, and monastic activity reflect many centuries of rebuilding and pilgrimage.

02 AdmissionFree

Wat Thai Buddhagaya Temple

Wat Thai Bodh Gaya was established in the twentieth century to represent Thailand near Buddhism's principal pilgrimage center. A steeply layered roof, gilded ornament, murals, Buddha images, monks' quarters, and a meditation hall follow Thai religious design.

24.6942° N / 84.9850° E

03 AdmissionFree

The Royal Bhutanese Monastery

Royal Bhutanese Monastery brings Bhutanese Buddhist craftsmanship to Bodh Gaya through painted walls, carved timber, decorative ceilings, prayer spaces and a large Buddha image. Built as an active monastic and pilgrimage center, it supports worship, study. Quiet conduct and scheduled religious activity shape the compound's use.

24.6923° N / 84.9844° E

04 AdmissionFree

The Great Buddha Statue

The Great Buddha Statue at Bodh Gaya depicts the seated Buddha in meditation on a high lotus pedestal, surrounded by standing figures of ten principal disciples. Unveiled in 1989, the stone-and-concrete monument rises about twenty-five metres including its base within a landscaped precinct. Its modern scale and international Buddhist sponsorship distinguish it from the excavated remains and medieval shrines nearby.

24.6904° N / 84.9819° E

05 AdmissionFree

Root Institute for Wisdom Culture

Root Institute for Wisdom Culture sits near the Mahabodhi Stupa in Bodh Gaya as a Buddhist study and meditation center. Courses, retreats, and a quiet garden give visitors structured practice time away from the main temple queues. The campus supports longer stays more than a quick photo stop.

24.6930° N / 84.9762° E

06 AdmissionFree

Metta Buddharam Temple

Pale surfaces, layered roofs and decorative relief identify Metta Buddharam as a modern Thai Buddhist compound in Bodh Gaya. Monks, pilgrims, meditation groups and ceremonies give the site an active religious purpose. Often called the Silver Temple, it uses a white metallic color scheme that distinguishes the shrine from many gold-toned monasteries nearby.

24.6994° N / 84.9810° E

SETH'S CHHAMA CHHAM THE WATERPARK [GAYA]

Seth's Chhama Chham Waterpark near Gaya offers pools, slides, and shaded resting areas as a recreation stop outside the Bodh Gaya pilgrimage core. Families use the water rides and lawns on hot days. The site is an amusement park rather than a religious or archaeological monument.

08 AdmissionFree

Maa Sarva Mangla Gauri Shakti Peeth Temple

Mangla Gauri Temple stands on a hill in Gaya and is counted in Hindu tradition among the Shakti Peethas. A long stair approach leads to the compact sanctuary, where ritual offerings and daily worship continue. Navratri brings larger gatherings, while the elevated site opens views over the surrounding city and distinguishes the temple from Bodh Gaya's Buddhist pilgrimage landscape.

24.7751° N / 85.0023° E

09 AdmissionFree

Pind Daan Gaya

Pind Daan Gaya is a revered site located on the banks of the Falgu River, where Hindu pilgrims partake in ancestral rituals to honor their departed ancestors. It is believed that conducting Pind Daan at this sacred location ensures the salvation of their souls. The customary practices involve offering pindas (ritual cakes) to the ancestors while reciting prayers and hymns.

24.7792° N / 85.0060° E

10 AdmissionFree

Shri Vishnupad Temple, Gaya Bihar

Vishnupad Temple rises beside the Falgu River in Gaya around a stone impression revered as Vishnu's footprint, and entry restrictions may apply to non-Hindus. Ahilyabai Holkar rebuilt the present dark-stone sanctuary in the eighteenth century, while priests conduct pind daan rites for ancestors throughout the year.

24.7774° N / 85.0092° E
