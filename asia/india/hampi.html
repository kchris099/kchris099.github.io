Explore Hampi

A Brief History

Serving as the imperial capital of the Vijayanagara Empire in the 14th century, Hampi was one of the wealthiest cities in the world. Destroyed by a coalition of sultanates in 1565, its monumental stone ruins now stand as a vast testament to an era of unparalleled prosperity. This rich historical tapestry established a strong foundation that continues to shape the city's dynamic contemporary identity.

Top Sights & Activities

₹40

The Stone Chariot (Hampi)

The Stone Chariot in Hampi is a renowned religious site located within the Vijaya Vitthala Temple. This UNESCO World Heritage site showcases incredible architecture and intricate carvings, reflecting advanced ancient culture and technology. Visitors can reach the chariot by walking or taking an e-buggy from the parking area. The short walk offers a pleasant experience, but it's important to stay hydrated as there are no water facilities near the chariot itself.

₹500

Shree Vijaya Vitthala Temple

The Shree Vijaya Vitthala Temple, dating back to the 15th century, is a massive complex showcasing the architectural brilliance of the Vijayanagara empire. Dedicated to Lord Vitthala, an incarnation of Lord Vishnu, it features various shrines and halls including the Maha Mantapa, Kalayana Mantapa, Devi Shrine, Utsav Mantapa and Ranga Mantapa. The stone chariot in its courtyard is a striking structure that was once movable.

Free

Virupaksha temple

Embarking on a journey to Hampi is incomplete without exploring the magnificent Virupaksha Temple. This ancient Hindu temple, dedicated to Lord Shiva in his form as Virupaksha, stands as a testament to the rich spiritual heritage of the region. Its intricate architecture and historical significance make it a must-visit site for travelers seeking to immerse themselves in India's cultural tapestry.

₹40

Group of Monuments at Hampi

The Group of Monuments at Hampi is a UNESCO World Heritage Site, showcasing the ancient ruins of a magnificent 14th-century city. The site is renowned for its fascinating temples, including the impressive Virupaksha temple. Beyond its historical significance, Hampi is also a top destination for bouldering enthusiasts from around the globe.

Free

Kadalekalu Ganesha

Kadalekalu Ganesha is a significant religious site in Hampi, known for its traditional stone architecture and historical importance. The temple houses a massive 15-foot monolithic statue of Lord Ganesha, carved entirely from granite. This impressive sculpture is believed to be in the world and is a testament to the skill and artistry of ancient craftsmen.

Free

Hemakuta Hill Temple

Hemakuta Hill Temple is a must-visit in Hampi, offering ancient temple structures, ruins of Vijayanagara, and panoramic views. The area is rich with UNESCO World Heritage sites and is popular among photographers. Visitors can also enjoy rock climbing and witness sunrises and sunsets from this vantage point. Additionally, the surrounding landscape adorned with temples and rocky hills provides a surreal backdrop for exploration.

Free

Saasivekaalu Ganesha

Saasivekaalu Ganesha, located in the Hampi region of Karnataka, India, is a historic monument and a beloved site for devotees. The eight feet tall monolithic statue of Lord Ganesha is carved from a single rock and is known for its intricate details and finely carved features. It holds significance as the remover of obstacles and bestower of wisdom in Hindu mythology.

Free

Hampi Shri Krishna Temple

Hampi Shri Krishna Swami Temple is a 16th-century temple complex with intricately carved stone pillars and towers, as well as a sacred water tank. The site includes various temple complexes such as Narasimha, Ganesa, Hemakuta group of temples, Achyutaraya temple complex, Vitthala temple complex, Pattabhirama temple complex, and Lotus Mahal complex.

Free

Hampi Shri Lakshmi Narasimha Temple

Shri Lakshmi Narasimha Temple is one of the must-visit attractions in Hampi, known for its 6.7-meter monolith statue of Lord Narasimha, an avatar of Lord Vishnu. This half-man half-lion statue showcases the exceptional craftsmanship of Vijayanagara artisans and continues to captivate travelers from all over the world.

Free

Hampi Bazaar

Hampi Bazaar, located in the town of Hampi, was once the bustling capital of the Vijayanagara empire. Today, it remains a vibrant local market where visitors can explore stalls offering various goods. The area is also home to the famous Virupaksha temple and other sacred sites in what is known as the Sacred Quarter. One notable attraction near the bazaar is the Monolith Bull, a large ancient structure situated in a pavilion opposite the Virupaksha Temple.

₹40

Lotus Mahal

Lotus Mahal, also known as Kamal Mahal or Chitragani Mahal, is a structure located within the Zenana Enclosure of the Vijayanagara Empire. This ancient stone palace boasts tranquil gardens, arches, and elaborate carvings that showcase a beautiful blend of Hindu and Islamic architecture. The two-storied elegant pavilion features massive pillars, exquisite stucco mouldings, and intricate carvings of artistic patterns and birds on its walls.

₹600

Elephant Stables

The Elephant Stables in Hampi, India, are a remarkable example of Indo-Islamic architecture and were once used to house the royal elephants of the Vijayanagara Empire. This well-preserved 16th-century structure boasts 11 intricately carved rooms set in grassy grounds. Despite the entry fee, visitors find it worth it for the chance to explore the cool stone buildings and relax on the expansive lawn.

Free

Hazara Raama Temple

Hazara Raama Temple is a 15th-century Hindu temple in Hampi, known for its intricate carvings depicting animals, deities, and people. The temple features a thousand frescoes portraying scenes from the Ramayana, leaving visitors speechless. It is dedicated to Lord Rama and also houses relics of Lord Krishna.

₹40

Royal Palace

The Royal Palace (Bangalore Palace) is a magnificent Tudor-style palace located in the heart of Bangalore. Built in the late 19th century by the Wadiyar dynasty, the architecture was inspired by Windsor Castle. The palace features fortified towers, elegant wood carvings, and extensive grounds that frequently host major concerts and public events.

Free

Underground Shiva Temple

The Underground Shiva Temple in Hampi is a fascinating 14th-century Hindu temple dedicated to Lord Shiva. Situated below ground level, the core areas of the temple are typically submerged, making access to the inner parts restricted. Visitors can experience the marvel of wide steps leading to the interior, where a massive hall with cubical pillars supporting the roof awaits.

Free

Sanapur Lake

Sanapur Lake is a documented body of water in Hampi, India, recognised in regional geographic listings for its ecological and recreational role. The shoreline is accessible from the surrounding district and supports walking paths and boat launches. Travellers approach from the adjacent promenade and combine the visit with nearby parks and waterfront districts.
