Hampi

As capital of Vijayanagara in the fourteenth and fifteenth centuries, Hampi ranked among the era's richest cities. A coalition of Deccan sultanates sacked it in 1565. The boulder-strewn ruins of temples and royal enclosures are what survive of that imperial Hindu metropolis on the Tungabhadra. Vitthala temple's stone chariot and musical pillars are among the most photographed imperial fragments.

The Stone Chariot (Hampi)

The Stone Chariot in Hampi is a known religious site located within the Vijaya Vitthala Temple.
