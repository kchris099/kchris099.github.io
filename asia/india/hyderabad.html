Destination Guide — South India

Explore Hyderabad

A Brief History

Founded in 1591 by the Qutb Shahi dynasty, Hyderabad flourished as a global center for the diamond trade. The subsequent rule of the Nizams endowed the city with magnificent Islamic architecture and a syncretic culture that significantly influenced the Deccan plateau. Decades of careful preservation have ensured that this profound historical narrative remains a vital part of the modern experience.

Top Sights & Activities

₹300

Golconda Fort

Golconda Fort, once the capital of a flourishing kingdom from the 14th to 16th century, boasts 87 semi-circular bastions. While Hyderabad is known for attractions like Charminar and Chowmahalla and Falaknuma Palaces, there are also hidden gems to explore. The city offers diverse experiences beyond its famous museums and delicious Hyderabadi biryani.

₹20

Qutub Shahi Tombs

The Qutub Shahi Tombs, constructed in the 16th and 17th centuries, are a collection of ornate domed tombs built for the rulers of the Qutub Shahi dynasty. The site is part of a large archaeological park undergoing renovation by the Aga Khan Development Network. With 40 mausoleums, 23 mosques, a hammam, and pavilions set amidst landscaped gardens, it's an ambitious heritage project.

₹300

Charminar

Charminar, a 16th-century mosque in Hyderabad, India, is an architectural and cultural symbol of the city. Built by Mohammed Quli Qutb Shah to commemorate the end of epidemics caused by water shortage, this UNESCO World Heritage site stands tall amidst the vibrant Laad Bazaar. The massive structure features four grand arches facing cardinal points and 48-meter-tall minarets atop each column.

Free

Makkah Masjid

Makka Masjid, also known as Mecca Masjid, is an impressive and ornate mosque in Hyderabad. It is one of the oldest and largest mosques in India, accommodating up to 10,000 people. Built between 1614 and 1694 by Muhammad Quli Qutub and his family, this architectural marvel reflects the opulent lifestyle of the Quli Dynasty.

₹400

Chowmahalla Palace

Chowmahalla Palace, also known as The Four Palaces, is a historical gem in Hyderabad that served as the official seat of the Asaf Jahi Dynasty. Built in the 18th century, this opulent palace boasts magnificent Persian-style architecture reminiscent of the Shah Palace in Tehran, Iran. Its lush grounds feature gardens, fountains, and smaller palace structures.

Free

Gulzar Houz

Gulzar Houz is a historic fountain located in the bustling old city of Hyderabad, near the iconic Charminar. Constructed during the Qutb Shahi dynasty, the octagonal fountain was originally designed to provide drinking water to the surrounding markets. Today, it remains a significant architectural landmark amidst the vibrant commercial district.

₹100

Nehru Zoological Park

Nehru Zoological Park, located in the charming neighborhood of Bahadurpura, Hyderabad, is a captivating destination for wildlife enthusiasts and families. Spanning 380 acres, the park features diverse species from around the world and immersive habitats. Visitors can enjoy safari rides and explore expansive grounds while observing animals such as tigers, rhinos, elephants, monkeys, and more.

Free

Birla Temple

Nestled atop the picturesque Kala Pahad, the Birla Temple in Hyderabad is a Hindu temple crafted from pristine white marble. This architectural marvel, completed in 1976 by Swami Renganathananda of the Ramakrishna Ashram and funded by the Birla Foundation, offers a tranquil escape from the hustle and bustle of city life. Visitors can immerse themselves in prayer and meditation within its serene atmosphere while admiring intricate carvings and towering shrines.

₹20

Lumbini Park

Lumbini Park is a popular destination in Hyderabad, offering free entry for families and travelers. Situated by the Hussain Sagar Lake, it boasts beautiful gardens, musical fountain shows, a large Buddha statue, and boat rides. The park provides ample green spaces with leafy trees and serene water ponds, making it an ideal spot for leisurely walks or quality time with loved ones without breaking the bank. It's definitely a must-visit for those seeking budget-friendly activities in Hyderabad.

Free

Hussain Sagar View Point

The Hussain Sagar View Point offers expansive vistas of the large, heart-shaped artificial lake in Hyderabad. From this vantage point along the Necklace Road, visitors can enjoy clear views of the massive monolithic statue of Gautama Buddha situated on an island in the center of the lake, particularly striking when illuminated at night.

Free

Buddha Statue

The Buddha Statue of Hyderabad is a monolithic structure located on an island in the middle of Hussain Sagar Lake. Accessible by boat, it is a prominent attraction in the heart of Hyderabad. The area surrounding the lake, known as Necklace Road, offers a vibrant mix of restaurants, recreational facilities, and parks. The statue itself is made entirely of white granite and stands as monuments in the city.

₹20

NTR Gardens.

NTR Gardens is a sprawling urban park spanning over 13 hectares, offering a range of family-friendly attractions, including a Japanese garden, fountains, and a restaurant. Situated overlooking the picturesque Hussain Sagar Lake in Hyderabad, it's an ideal spot for a budget-friendly day out with friends. Visitors can leisurely wander along the pathways adorned with water ponds and lush greenery or relax in the various seating areas scattered throughout the park.

Free

Love Hyderabad Sculpture

The 'Love Hyderabad' Sculpture is a popular typographic installation located along the Necklace Road bordering Hussain Sagar lake. The large, red-and-white letters provide a frequently photographed landmark for locals and tourists, symbolizing civic pride and offering a modern, vibrant contrast to the city's historic architecture.

Free

Hussain Sagar

Hussain Sagar Lake, located in Hyderabad and Secunderabad, is a popular tourist destination. This man-made lake was constructed in 1562 to meet the water and irrigation needs of the city. It is surrounded by Lumbini Park, Indira Park, and Sanjeeviah Park. The centerpiece of the lake is a large Buddha statue situated on a rock.

₹999

Thrill City

Located just 6 km from the city center, Thrill City is a futuristic virtual reality amusement park in Hyderabad. Situated near Sanjeeviah Park on Necklace Road, it offers an array of thrilling attractions such as flying and cricket simulators, bumping cars, a bowling alley, swimming pool, and splash coaster. This park promises an immersive experience that transports visitors to abstract and realistic simulations.

₹600

Snow World

Snow World is a family-friendly indoor park in Hyderabad, India, offering an artificial snow experience with slides, games, and play areas. It's the first snow-themed park in the country and attracts both locals and tourists. The park features various activities to cater to different interests. Visitors can experience a gradual decrease in temperature as they move through different zones, eventually reaching the subzero Cryo Zone.

₹50

Bansilalpet Stepwell

Bansilalpet Step Well, a recently restored ancient marvel in Hyderabad, showcases the city's rich architectural heritage. The stepwell features intricate craftsmanship and design, with well-preserved carvings and structural beauty that transport visitors to an ancient era as they descend its steps. The site also includes a mini museum displaying the history and restoration of the well.

Free

Shri Jagannatha Swami Temple

Shri Jagannatha Swami Temple, located in Banjara Hills, is a contemporary Hindu temple constructed by the local Oriya community. The intricately carved terracotta-colored walls depict vibrant carvings of gods and animals, replicating its original counterpart in Jagganath Puri. The temple is known for its peaceful ambiance and beautiful wooden architecture made from teak wood, adding a unique charm to the bustling city of Hyderabad.

Free

Lotus Pond

Lotus Pond is a tranquil urban park and small water body located in the Jubilee Hills neighborhood of Hyderabad. Surrounded by dense vegetation and walking trails, the pond is known for its blooming lotus flowers and diverse birdlife. It provides a quiet, natural sanctuary for morning walks and nature photography within the busy city.

₹40

KBR park

Kasu Brahmananda Reddy National Park (KBR) in Hyderabad is a sprawling sanctuary for nature enthusiasts. The park is home to a diverse range of plant and bird species, including the majestic peacock. It offers running paths and lush greenery, providing a welcome escape from urban life. With 600 plant species, 140 bird species, and 30 varieties of butterflies and reptiles, KBR National Park is a haven for wildlife lovers.

Free

Inorbit Mall

Inorbit Mall Cyberabad, situated on the outskirts of Cyberabad, is a shopper's haven offering a diverse range of shops and entertainment options. Whether you're into international fashion brands or local artisanal products, this versatile mall has something for everyone. browse through fashion boutiques, dine at various restaurants, or catch the latest movies at the multiplex cinema. The mall also hosts entertaining live events like the Pink Power Flea Market on weekends.

₹10

Durgam Cheruvu

Durgam Cheruvu, also known as "The Secret Lake," is an 83-acre lake located in Ranga Reddy district, Hyderabad. It offers various activities such as fishing, boating, and features a floating fountain. The newly constructed cable-stayed bridge adds to the city's attractions and will be illuminated with multi-colored lights each night.

₹50

Sri Kotla Vijayabhaskara Reddy Botanical Garden

Nestled in Madhapur near Hi-tech City, Botanical Gardens spans 120 acres and features 19 sectors or Vanams. This serene park offers extensive walkways, seating areas, and diverse flora. Conveniently located next to Sarath City Mall, it provides ample parking and easy access to nearby food options. The park boasts a well-designed 3 km walking track suitable for all ages, along with play areas for children and fitness facilities for adults.

Free

Sri Karmanghat Hanuman Devastanam

Sri Hanuman Devasthanam Karmanghat is an ancient temple in Hyderabad, founded by the Kakatiya King Prataparudra in the 12th century. The deity revered here is known as Sankat Mochana, meaning solver of challenges and difficulties, holding great significance in Hinduism. The temple also houses smaller shrines dedicated to various deities.

₹5

Priyadarshini Park

Priyadarshini Park, located alongside the picturesque Saroornagar Lake in Hyderabad, spans 20 acres and offers a range of recreational facilities. The park features exercise fields, an Olympic track, tennis courts, and a gymnasium. Families can enjoy children's rides and snacks from vendors while taking in the beautiful green landscape. As evening falls, the shimmering lake provides a backdrop to the cityscape.

Free

ISKCON-Sri Sri Radha Madanmohan Mandir

The ISKCON - Sri Sri Radha Madan Mohan Temple in Abids, Hyderabad is a renowned Hare Krishna temple featuring vibrant spiritual atmosphere, daily bhajans, and kirtans. The temple complex showcases a mix of modern and traditional South Indian architectural styles, creating a serene environment for devotees to seek blessings from the presiding deity.

Free

St. Joseph's Cathedral

St. Joseph's Cathedral, located in Hyderabad, India, is a 19th-century Roman Catholic cathedral known for its beautiful design and spacious sanctuary. The cathedral, which serves as the main church of the Archdiocese of Hyderabad, features two bell towers that play hymns and add to its grandeur.

₹5

Dr. G.S. Melkote Park

Dr. G.S. Melkote Park is a delightful urban oasis nestled in Bhawani Nagar, sprawling over seven acres of lush greenery and shaded paths. This well-maintained park caters to visitors of all ages, offering a variety of amenities including a dedicated walking track for joggers and walkers, benches for seniors to relax, and an engaging play area filled with swings and slides for children. The park's long skating rink is perfect for kids eager to learn how to skate.

Free

Shri Kanchi Kamakoti Peetham Shri Shyam Baba Mandir

Shri Kanchi Kamakoti Peetham Shri Shyam Baba Mandir, also known as the Shyam temple, is a Hindu temple located in Hyderabad. This ancient temple is dedicated to Lord Shyama, an incarnation of Krishna. The architecture and designs of the temple are truly captivating, featuring beautiful paintings and stone figures that attract devotees from far and wide.
