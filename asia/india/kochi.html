A Brief History

Muziris, a port somewhere north of Kochi (mostly identified with Kodungallur in Thrissur district), was the centre of Indian spice trade for many centuries, and was known to the Arabs, Yavanas (Greeks and Romans) as well as Jews, Syrians, and Chinese since ancient times. Kochi rose to significance as a trading centre after the port Muziris around Kodungallur (Cranganore) was destroyed by the massive flooding of Periyar in 1341. The earliest documented references to Kochi occur in books written by Chinese voyager Ma Huan during his visit to Kochi in the 15th century as part of Admiral Zheng He's treasure fleet. There are also references to Kochi in accounts written by Italian traveller Niccolò Da Conti, who visited Kochi in 1440.

National Shrine Basilica of Our Lady of Ransom - Vallarpadam

The National Shrine Basilica of Our Lady of Ransom, also known as Vallarpadam Basilica, is a known pilgrimage center with a rich history dating back to its construction by Portuguese Missionaries in 1524. Situated on the Vallarpadam Island in the Vembanad Lake Backwaters near Fort Kochi, this ancient church is distinguished by its twin towers and houses a famous picture of the Blessed Virgin Mary.

Free

LuLu International Shopping Mall

LuLu International Shopping Mall is a visitor site in Kochi, India. It opened or was completed around 2013. LuLu International Shopping Mall, opened in 2013, is a massive complex with over 300 outlets offering a wide range of shopping options. From high-end international brands to everyday household items, the mall has something for everyone. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Free

Thrikkakkara Vamana Moorthy Temple

Located just 11 kilometers from Ernakulam Junction, the Thrikkakkara Vamana Moorthy Temple is a unique homage to Vamana, the fifth avatar of Lord Vishnu. This temple is not only significant for its religious importance but also for its historical value, housing around 18 ancient stone inscriptions that date back to between the 10th and 13th centuries.

₹250 (match ticket)

Jawaharlal Nehru Stadium, Kochi

The Jawaharlal Nehru Stadium is and popular sporting venues in Kerala. It has played host to a number of important international matches, including cricket and football, since it was opened in 1996. The stadium is also popular for its extensive grounds which can be used for a variety of events such as exhibitions, cinema events and political rallies. The stadium's unique lighting towers offer illumination levels that are suitable for HD telecast.

Free

Chottanikkara Bhagavathy Temple

Chottanikkara Bhagavati Temple is a Hindu temple complex located near Chottanikkara, Kerala. The main deity is Raja Rajeshwari Devi, an incarnation of Goddess Durga. The temple is known for self-manifested deity with four arms covered in golden armor. It's a popular pilgrimage site where devotees worship the goddess in three different forms throughout the day.

Free

Sree Poornathrayeesa Temple,Tripunithura

Sree Poornathrayeesa Temple Thrippunithura is a revered Hindu temple dedicated to the deity Vishnu, known for its annual festivals and legends. The temple is believed to bless childless couples with children and hosts various festivals throughout the year, including the Vrishchikoltsawam festival in late November and early December. The historical temple was reconstructed after a major fire in the 19th century and is associated with Arjuna of Pandavas.

Free

Fort Kochi Beach

Fort Kochi Beach is a fortified historic site in Kochi, India. This area is known for its blend of Indo-European architecture seen in old churches and temples, making it a popular attraction for tourists exploring Cochin. Walls, courtyards, or towers form the main visitor route when the grounds are open.

Free

Fort Kochi View Point

A scenic viewpoint in Fort Kochi, offering panoramic views of the sea and the busy harbor. It remains a notable local point of interest situated in Kochi, India. Visitors can observe the surrounding area and its connection to the city's broader historical landscape. Local guides sometimes include it on walking routes through the centre.

Free

Vasco Da Gama Square

Vasco da Gama Square, located at Fort Kochi, is a narrow promenade along the beach that offers a glimpse into the city's past. Named after the known Portuguese explorer Vasco da Gama, this spot is home to the Chinese fishing nets on teak and bamboo poles. These nets were brought by traders from Kublai Khan's court and are still used by local fishermen today. The square provides an ideal vantage point to watch them in action.

Free

Chinese Fishing Nets

When visiting Fort Kochi in South India, be sure to take in the mesmerizing sight of the Chinese Fishing Nets. These nets, suspended from tall bamboo frames, have been a symbol of Kerala's colonial legacy for over 500 years. They are particularly against the backdrop of the setting sun and are a popular subject for photographs and postcards.

Free

St. Francis CSI Church

St. Francis CSI Church, located in Fort Kochi, Kerala, is a historically significant site as it is the first European church built in India. The church has witnessed the colonial struggles of different European powers and today attracts visitors from around the world. Despite its simple architecture, the church's stained glass windows and porticoes exude an old-world charm that draws admirers.

Free

Santa Cruz Roman Catholic Latin Cathedral Basilica, Kochi

Santa Cruz Cathedral Basilica in Fort Cochin is a reconstructed 1880s Catholic church with a rich history dating back to the 1500s when it was originally built by the Portuguese. This structure stands out in white against the lush greenery of Kochi and has two spires that serve as a landmark for both locals and tourists.

Free

Ernakulam Shiva Temple

Ernakulam Shiva Temple, also known as Ernakulathappan, is a religious building in Kochi, India. The temple holds great significance as it is the only pilgrimage site in South India where the deity's statue faces west. The exterior and main interior spaces are usually viewable outside service times.

Free

Durbar Hall Ground

Durbar Hall Ground is a visitor site in Kochi, India. Durbar Hall Ground is not just a venue for events, but also a family-friendly space. Children can enjoy various sports while parents take leisurely strolls along the well-kept pathways. The area offers refreshment stalls and ample parking, making it convenient for families to spend quality time together in a healthy environment. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Free

Subhash Bose Park

Subhash Bose Park, also known as Subhash Chandra Bose Park, is a urban park located in Kochi, Kerala. Situated adjacent to Marine Drive, it offers views of the Vembanad Lake backwaters and Kochi Harbor. Subhash Bose Park is catalogued as a park within the historic quarters of Kochi.

Free

Jew Town Fort Kochi

Jew Town Fort Kochi is a fortified historic site in Kochi, India. Jew Town in Fort Kochi is a must-visit for those seeking traditional and unique items. Visitors can find artistic souvenirs at reasonable prices by honing their bargaining skills. Walls, courtyards, or towers form the main visitor route when the grounds are open.

₹10

Paradesi Synagogue

The Paradesi Synagogue, located in Mattancherry, Kochi, is a historic site with a rich and communist period. Originally constructed in 1568, it suffered damage from the Portuguese in 1662 but was rebuilt by the Dutch shortly after. The synagogue has ornate brass bimah, elegant wooden benches, and detailed hand-painted floor tiles from Canton.

₹20

Mattancherry Palace

Mattancherry Palace, also known as Dutch Palace, is a example of Kerala-style architecture with a touch of colonial influence. Originally constructed by the Portuguese in 1555 and later renovated by the Dutch, this palace is located just 10 km from main Ernakulam. The interiors are adorned with 17th and 18th-century murals depicting scenes from the epics Ramayana and Mahabharata.

Free

St. George Orthodox Koonan Kurish Old Syrian Church

The St. George Orthodox Koonan Kurishu Old Syrian Church, located in Mattancherry, Kochi, is a significant pilgrimage site with deep historical and spiritual importance. It is known for being the location of the Coonan Cross Oath, a stand against colonialism in India. The church houses holy relics of St. George and is affiliated with the Malankara Orthodox Syrian Church.

Closed

Aspinwall House

Aspinwall House, originally the headquarters of Aspinwall & Company Ltd., is now a cultural hub in Fort Kochi. Named after English trader John H. Aspinwall, it overlooks the backwaters and hosts art exhibitions, including those for the Kochi-Muziris Biennale. The building's Victorian architecture adds to its charm, and visitors can explore contemporary art and crafts exhibitions at The David Hall Museum and Aspinwall House itself.

Free

Kadamakudy Lake View Point

Kadamakudy Lake View Point is a visitor site in Kochi, India. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest. Kadamakudy Lake View Point remains part of the public visitor circuit in Kochi. Local maps identify Kadamakudy Lake View Point within Kochi, India.
