Sanchi

Sanchi Stupa is a Buddhist complex, known for Great Stupa, on a hilltop at Sanchi Town in Raisen District of the State of Madhya Pradesh, India. It is located about 23 kilometres from Raisen town, the district headquarters and 46 kilometres (29 mi) northeast of Bhopal, capital of Madhya Pradesh. The Great Stupa at Sanchi is one of the oldest stone structures in India, and an important monument to the historical architecture of India. It was originally commissioned by the Mauryan emperor Ashoka in the 3rd century BCE.

Buddhist Monuments at Sanchi Raisen

Sanchi preserves Buddhist stupas, monasteries, temples, pillars, gateways and inscriptions developed from the Mauryan period through later centuries. The Great Stupa's carved toranas carry narratives and symbols around a solid relic mound, while surrounding remains document long monastic use. Paths cross an exposed hilltop archaeological park with monuments spread across several levels.

Sanchi Stupa

The Great Stupa at Sanchi is among India's earliest surviving stone Buddhist monuments, raised and enlarged from the Mauryan period onward. Torana gateways carry detailed reliefs of Jataka tales and symbols of the Buddha. The hilltop complex overlooks the surrounding Madhya Pradesh plain.

03 Admission₹250

Udaygiri Caves

Udayagiri Caves near Vidisha preserve Gupta-period Hindu and Jain rock-cut monuments from the early fifth century. Shrines, inscriptions and reliefs connect the site with royal patronage and religious practice. The monumental image of Vishnu as Varaha dominates the surviving carvings, while the hills and caves form a separate archaeological destination within the wider Sanchi region.

Sanchi Archaeological Museum

The Sanchi Archaeological Museum displays sculpture, reliquaries, inscriptions, capitals, pottery, and architectural fragments recovered from the hill and neighboring sites. A notable Ashokan lion capital and Buddhist images help explain objects removed for protection from the open monuments.
