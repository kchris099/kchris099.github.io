Explore Leh

A Brief History

Leh was established as the capital of the Himalayan kingdom of Ladakh in the seventeenth century by King Sengge Namgyal. It served as a crucial stop on the ancient trade routes connecting Tibet, Kashmir, and Central Asia. The city's architecture is dominated by the nine-story Leh Palace and numerous Buddhist monasteries. Today, Leh remains a vital center of Tibetan Buddhist culture and a gateway for high-altitude trekking and exploration.

Top Sights & Activities

₹250

Leh Palace

Perched majestically atop a mountain, the Leh Palace, or Lhachen Palkhar, is a historical landmark in the enchanting town of Leh, nestled within the landscapes of Kashmir. Constructed in the 17th century under King Sengge Namgyal's reign, this former royal residence boasts nine stories and was designed to mirror the grandeur of Tibet’s Potala Palace.

Free

Shanti Stupa

Shanti Stupa, located in the serene suburb of Changspa near Leh, is a remote place of worship featuring a large golden statue of Buddha. This peaceful sanctuary was inaugurated in 1991 by Japanese Buddhist Bhikshu Gyomo Nakamura and Ladakhi lama Kushok Bakula. Perched at an altitude of 4267 meters, it offers views and serves as a symbol of peace amidst global conflicts.

₹30

Ancient palace Shey

Nestled in the landscapes of Ladakh, the Ancient Palace Shey, also known as Shey Gompa or the Crystal Monastery, is a captivating 17th-century complex that offers visitors a glimpse into its rich history and spiritual significance. Once serving as the summer capital of Ladakh, this serene site features panoramic views from its hilltop location.

Free

Leh Main Market

Leh Main Market, also known as Kharyog, is the historic Old Town of Leh located below the seventeenth century Leh Palace. It used to be a walled fortification with only 120 houses and five gates in different directions. The area offers a glimpse into the traditional residential houses and community spaces of the region. Despite being a popular tourist destination, Ladakh presents pristine and untouched views of nature with its barren land and snow-covered mountains.

₹225

Zorawar Fort

Zorawar Fort, located just a 30-minute trek from Leh city, is a historical monument built in the 19th century by General Zorawar Singh. Constructed with marcullak, sun-dried bricks, stone, and wood, this fort covers around 27.17 acres of land and features a natural spring, a temple, and a mosque. It holds great significance in the region's military history and is popular among history enthusiasts.

Free

Offside cafe

Offside Cafe is a hidden gem located in Leh, offering views of the city and the airport. The cafe is situated near an ancient Buddhist monastery and a Kali Mata temple, providing a spiritual and peaceful atmosphere. Visitors can enjoy watching flights land and take off while taking in the mountain scenery. The cafe's warm and welcoming ambiance, along with friendly staff, creates a homely feel for guests.

Free

Khardungla Pass

Khardung La is a high mountain pass in the Ladakh region, widely recognized as one of the highest motorable roads in the world at an elevation of over 5,300 meters. It serves as the gateway to the Shyok and Nubra valleys. The pass offers expansive, rugged panoramic views of the Himalayas and is a major destination for adventure motorcyclists.

Free

Leh market

Leh Market is surrounded by a plethora of attractions that are sure to leave a lasting impression on any traveler. The market is located near a small lane leading to Chubi, which provides a shortcut to the back alley of Leh Palace. Adjacent to the market is Kashmiri Gali, where people from Kashmir have established their businesses. While it's difficult to narrow down the must-visit places in Leh, these attractions are likely to find a permanent place in any traveler's journal.

Reservation required

Mototour Ladakh / Himalayas Motorcycle Expedition in India

Mototour Ladakh offers specialized, guided motorcycle expeditions through the challenging and dramatic terrain of the Indian Himalayas. The tours typically cover high-altitude passes, remote Buddhist monasteries, and vast desert landscapes, providing an adventurous and immersive way to experience the unique culture and geography of the Ladakh region.

Free

Phyang Monastery

Phyang Monastery, also known as Phyang Gompa, is a Tibetan Buddhist monastery located in the serene village of Phyang near Leh in Ladakh, northern India. Established in 1515, this historic monument houses intricate and brightly colored frescoes, ancient scriptures, traditional weapons, and a museum. The monastery is home to around 150 monks and has its own school for children monks where they can learn various subjects.
