Leh

Leh was established as the capital of the Himalayan kingdom of Ladakh in the seventeenth century by King Sengge Namgyal. It served as an important stop on the ancient trade routes connecting Tibet, Kashmir, and Central Asia. The city's architecture is dominated by the nine-story Leh Palace and numerous Buddhist monasteries. Today, Leh remains a vital center of Tibetan Buddhist culture and a gateway for high-altitude trekking and exploration.
