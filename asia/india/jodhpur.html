Jodhpur

Rao Jodha of the Rathore clan founded Jodhpur in 1459 and raised Mehrangarh on a rock ridge above the Marwar plain. The indigo-washed old town and courtly palaces grew from that desert kingdom's trade toward Gujarat and Delhi. Stepwells and zenana courtyards are the domestic side of the same Rajput capital. Umaid Bhawan palace, finished in the twentieth century, extended Rathore display into modernity.

Umaid Bhawan Palace, Jodhpur

Located Jodhpur, Umaid Bhawan Palace is a evidence of opulence and architectural brilliance. This palace, constructed between 1928 and 1943 for Maharaja Umaid Singh, showcases Indo-Saracenic design with its grand yellow sandstone façade and large gardens that cover an 26 acres. Once built to provide employment during a drought, it now is both a luxurious hotel and museum, inviting travelers to immerse themselves in its rich history.

Umaid Bhawan Museum

Umaid Bhawan Museum, located in Jodhpur city, is a popular tourist spot and attractions in the area. The museum is housed within a portion of the majestic Umaid Bhawan Palace, with other parts serving as a luxurious hotel and residence for the royal family. It showcases an collection of antiques that belonged to the maharajas and displays detailed artwork, providing insight into the history of the Suryvanshi dynasty.

Mehrangarh Fort

Mehrangarh Fort, dating back to around 1460, is one of India's largest forts. It offers lift access and galleries, guided tours, and shops for visitors. The times to appreciate its architectural grandeur are at sunrise or sunset when the golden walls shimmer against the crimson sky. The fort stands tall at 400 feet amidst glittering blue homes and offers panoramic views of Jodhpur, it provides a chilling sense of history.

Machia Biological Park, Jodhpur

Machia Biological Park, situated near Kaylana Lake in Jodhpur, is a great place to appreciate nature's beauty and observe local wildlife. The park houses various wild animals such as wild cats, desert foxes, deer, monitor foxes, and monkeys. Visitors can take a leisurely walk along the pathways to spot these animals in their natural habitat or indulge in bird watching, the park offers an Elephant Safari for a unique aerial view of the landscapes.
