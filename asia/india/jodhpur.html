Explore Jodhpur

A Brief History

Jodhpur was founded in 1459 by Rao Jodha, a Rajput chief of the Rathore clan, who built the formidable Mehrangarh Fort on a rocky ridge. The city served as the capital of the Kingdom of Marwar and thrived as a vital trading post on the route connecting Delhi to Gujarat. It became known as the "Blue City" due to the indigo-painted houses in its old quarter. Today, Jodhpur is celebrated for its majestic palaces, historic stepwells, and vibrant Rajasthani culture.

Umaid Bhawan Palace, Jodhpur

Nestled in the heart of Jodhpur, Umaid Bhawan Palace stands as a testament to opulence and architectural brilliance. This palace, constructed between 1928 and 1943 for Maharaja Umaid Singh, showcases Indo-Saracenic design with its grand yellow sandstone façade and sprawling gardens that cover an impressive 26 acres. Once built to provide employment during a drought, it now serves as both a luxurious hotel and museum, inviting travelers to immerse themselves in its rich history.

₹30

Umaid Bhawan Museum

Umaid Bhawan Museum, located in Jodhpur city, is a popular tourist spot and attractions in the area. The museum is housed within a portion of the majestic Umaid Bhawan Palace, with other parts serving as a luxurious hotel and residence for the royal family. It showcases an impressive collection of antiques that belonged to the maharajas and displays intricate artwork, providing insight into the history of the Suryvanshi dynasty.

Free

Ganesh Temple Ratanada

The Ganesh Temple Ratanada is a Hindu temple located on a hill in the center of the city of Jodhpur. It is popular tourist destinations in Jodhpur and offers panoramic city views. The temple has been renovated over the years and now sees a massive rush on Wednesdays, which can make waiting in long queues an unpleasant experience.

Free

Toorji Ka Jhalra Bavdi

Nestled in the heart of Jodhpur's Old City, Toorji Ka Jhalra Bavdi is a beautifully restored 18th-century stepwell adorned with exquisite sandstone carvings. This ancient marvel offers a captivating challenge for photographers, with its intricate designs, arched niches, and symmetrical patterns providing an ideal backdrop for capturing images.

Free

Ghanta Ghar Jodhpur

Ghanta Ghar Jodhpur, also known as the Clock Tower, is a prominent landmark in the bustling Sardar Market. Built in 1911, it showcases Rajput architecture and intricate carvings. The tower serves as a navigational point and symbol of Jodhpur's rich heritage.

₹800

Mehrangarh Fort

Mehrangarh Fort, dating back to around 1460, is one of India's largest forts. It offers lift access and galleries, guided tours, and shops for visitors. The times to appreciate its architectural grandeur are at sunrise or sunset when the golden walls shimmer against the crimson sky. The fort stands tall at 400 feet amidst glittering blue homes and offers panoramic views of Jodhpur. Additionally, it provides a chilling sense of history.

₹100

Jaswant Thada

Jaswant Thada is an exquisite white marble memorial built in 1899 to honor Maharaja Jaswant Singh II, serving as the cremation ground for the royal family of Jodhpur. This architectural marvel, resembling a glowing structure when illuminated by sunlight, is located just 1km northeast of Mehrangarh Fort.

Free

Bheru Bagh Parshvnath Jain Shvetamber Teerth

Bheru Bagh Parshvnath Jain Shvetamber Teerth is a significant Jain temple complex located in Jodhpur. The temple is dedicated to Lord Parshvanath and is renowned for its intricate marble carvings, peaceful atmosphere, and detailed architectural design. It serves as an important spiritual center and pilgrimage site for the Jain community.

Free

Pragat Shri Santoshi Mata Mandir Dham

Nestled in the Mandore Garden, the Santoshi Mata Temple is a significant Hindu shrine dedicated to the revered goddess of contentment. This mountain temple attracts numerous devotees, particularly women, who seek blessings on Fridays, considered her special day. The temple's picturesque location atop a hill offers views of Jodhpur and its surroundings. Visitors can admire colorful altars and movie posters while immersing themselves in the spiritual ambiance.

₹30

Machia Biological Park, Jodhpur

Machia Biological Park, situated near Kaylana Lake in Jodhpur, is a great place to appreciate nature's beauty and observe local wildlife. The park houses various wild animals such as wild cats, desert foxes, deer, monitor foxes, and monkeys. Visitors can take a leisurely walk along the pathways to spot these animals in their natural habitat or indulge in bird watching. Additionally, the park offers an Elephant Safari for a unique aerial view of the picturesque landscapes.
