Fatehpur Sikri

Archaeological evidence points to settlement of the region since the Painted Grey Ware period. According to historian Syed Ali Nadeem Rezavi, the region flourished under Sunga rule and then under Sikarwar Rajputs, who built a fortress when they controlled the area from 7th to 16th century, until the Battle of Khanwa (1527). The area later came under the rule of the Delhi Sultanate and many mosques were built at the place which grew in size during the period of the Khalji dynasty. = At the site of Birchhabili Tila, located on the southeast bank of what used to be a large lake, excavation indicated the presence of a settlement as far back as the 6th century CE. The oldest layer, dating to the 6th through 9th centuries, contained various remains of buildings, including brick walls, building foundations, one dressed sandstone pillar, and a man-made pit also made of sandstone.

Fatehpur Sikri Fort

Fatehpur Sikri Fort, a landmark fortress and former capital of the Mughal Empire, has red palaces and elaborate monuments. Some popular attractions within the fort include Jodha Bai Palace, Jama Masjid, Buland Darwaza, and Panch Mahal. This fortified city near Agra was built in the late 16th century to serve as the Mughal Empire's capital.

Panch Mahal

Panch Mahal is a monument in Fatehpur Sikri, India. This five-story pavilion was primarily used for the entertainment of Emperor Akbar and stands on the western side of the Fatehpur Sikri complex. Unlike the grand palaces in Rajasthan, Panch Mahal is a Persian wind tower. The memorial stands in an open plaza or roadside setting.

Diwan-e-Khas

Diwan-e-Khas, also known as the Jewel House due to its significance in Akbar's court, is a visitor site in Fatehpur Sikri, India. This Persian-style structure was reserved for royal members to discuss private matters and was also called the Jewel House due to its significance in Akbar's court. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Buland Darwaza Fatehpur Sikri

Buland Darwaza is a grand sandstone gate built by the Mughal emperor Akbar in the early 17th century. It is one of the main attractions in Fatehpur Sikri, along with Jama Masjid, Sheikh Salim Chisti's Tomb, and other significant structures. The tomb of Salim Chishti, known for its Mughal architecture, is located near Buland Darwaza within the complex.

Jama Masjid

Jama Masjid, a example of Mughal architecture, was commissioned by Emperor Akbar in the 1570s and is a significant landmark in Fatehpur Sikri. This grand mosque is not only an notable building but also is a spiritual hub within the city. Its expansive courtyard can accommodate thousands of worshippers, making it mosques in India. The detailed design and majestic domes reflect the artistic brilliance of that era.
