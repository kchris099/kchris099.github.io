A Brief History

Archaeological evidence points to settlement of the region since the Painted Grey Ware period. According to historian Syed Ali Nadeem Rezavi, the region flourished under Sunga rule and then under Sikarwar Rajputs, who built a fortress when they controlled the area from 7th to 16th century, until the Battle of Khanwa (1527). The area later came under the rule of the Delhi Sultanate and many mosques were built at the place which grew in size during the period of the Khalji dynasty. = At the site of Birchhabili Tila, located on the southeast bank of what used to be a large lake, excavation indicated the presence of a settlement as far back as the 6th century CE. The oldest layer, dating to the 6th through 9th centuries, contained various remains of buildings, including brick walls, building foundations, one dressed sandstone pillar, and a man-made pit also made of sandstone.

Top Sights & Activities

₹550

Fatehpur Sikri Fort

Fatehpur Sikri Fort, a landmark fortress and former capital of the Mughal Empire, boasts red palaces and elaborate monuments. Some popular attractions within the fort include Jodha Bai Palace, Jama Masjid, Buland Darwaza, and Panch Mahal. This fortified city near Agra was built in the late 16th century to serve as the Mughal Empire's capital.

₹550

Panch Mahal

Panch Mahal, located in Fatehpur Sikri, is a remarkable multistoried palace famous for its columnated construction and intricately carved pillars. This five-story pavilion was primarily used for the entertainment of Emperor Akbar and stands on the western side of the Fatehpur Sikri complex. Unlike the grand palaces in Rajasthan, Panch Mahal is a Persian wind tower.

₹550

Diwan-e-Khas

Diwan-E-Khas in Fatehpur Sikri is a building that served as the private audience hall for Emperor Akbar. The central stone pillar, known as the Lotus Throne, features intricate carvings and was where the emperor met with guests and dignitaries. This Persian-style structure was reserved for royal members to discuss private matters and was also called the Jewel House due to its significance in Akbar's court.

₹550

Jodha Bai's Palace

Jodha Bai's Palace, a red sandstone structure built by the great Mughal emperor Akbar, is a must-visit gem nestled within the Fatehpur Sikri complex. This architectural marvel features a central courtyard and beautifully blends Hindu and Mughal design elements, showcasing the rich cultural tapestry of its time. As one of the key attractions in Fatehpur Sikri, it stands alongside other historical sites like Salim Chisti's tomb and Buland Darwaza.

Free

Buland Darwaza Fatehpur Sikri

Buland Darwaza is a grand sandstone gate built by the Mughal emperor Akbar in the early 17th century. It is one of the main attractions in Fatehpur Sikri, along with Jama Masjid, Sheikh Salim Chisti's Tomb, and other significant structures. The tomb of Salim Chishti, known for its exquisite Mughal architecture, is located near Buland Darwaza within the complex.

Free

Jama Masjid

Jama Masjid, a example of Mughal architecture, was commissioned by Emperor Akbar in the 1570s and stands as a significant landmark in Fatehpur Sikri. This grand mosque is not only an architectural marvel but also serves as a spiritual hub within the city. Its expansive courtyard can accommodate thousands of worshippers, making it mosques in India. The intricate design and majestic domes reflect the artistic brilliance of that era.
