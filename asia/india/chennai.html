Chennai

Chennai, formerly Madras, was established in 1639 when the British East India Company built Fort St. George, one of the first major English settlements in India. It grew into a major naval base and administrative center for the Madras Presidency. The city played an important role in the Carnatic Wars and the development of the Indian railway system. Today, Chennai is a major cultural and economic hub, known for its Dravidian architecture, classical music traditions, and expansive Marina Beach.
