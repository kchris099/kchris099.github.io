Pondicherry

France planted a Coromandel trading post in 1674; the enclave changed hands among European powers before settling again under French rule. A rectangular street grid, seaside promenade, and Tamil quarters inland record that dual colonial and local geography. Transfer to India in 1954 ended the French administration but not the bilingual street names.

Arulmigu Manakula Vinayagar Devasthanam

Manakula Vinayagar Temple predates much of French Pondicherry and remains a major urban shrine dedicated to Ganesha, and a colorful gopuram leads into a compact complex with painted ceilings, festival chariots and gold-covered sanctuary elements. Repeated rebuilding has altered the fabric, but active worship and public festivals continue to connect the temple with the surrounding former French quarter.

Sri Aurobindo Ashram

Sri Aurobindo Ashram in Puducherry occupies colonial-era houses around quiet courtyards where the philosopher and the Mother lived and taught. Flower offerings and a shared dining hall structure ashram life more than tourist spectacle. Visitors may view designated areas under ashram rules along shaded streets.

03 AdmissionFree

Indira Gandhi Statue

A statue of Indira Gandhi stands at a major road junction in Puducherry and commemorates India's former prime minister. The monument occupies a landscaped traffic island within the modern street network. It functions mainly as a civic landmark and reference point, with passing vehicles and surrounding roads defining the setting more than formal museum interpretation.

04 AdmissionFree

Sacred Heart Basilica

Sacred Heart of Jesus Basilica was built in the early twentieth century near Puducherry's railway station in a Gothic Revival style. Pointed arches, twin towers and a high nave frame stained-glass panels depicting biblical scenes and saints. Devotional images, Mass and parish ceremonies continue within a building that reflects the Catholic institutions of the former French colony. Red and white masonry gives the facade a distinct profile.

05 AdmissionFree

Sunday Market

The Sunday Market in Pondicherry is a active and busy open market that offers a fascinating glimpse into Indian and South Indian Sunday Bazaars. Located at the corner of Nehru Street and Gandhi Street junction, this market is filled with numerous merchants selling a wide array of items including silverware, gadgets, books, home goods, toys, fresh produce, flowers, groceries, and seafood.

06 AdmissionFree

Our Lady of Immaculate Conception Cathedral

Puducherry's Immaculate Conception Cathedral occupies a Catholic site rebuilt several times after warfare and demolition. The present church was completed in the late eighteenth century with a white facade, paired columns, curved gables, and a high altar shaped by French-period religious life.

07 AdmissionFree

Mahatma Gandhi Statue

Along Puducherry's seafront, a standing bronze of Mahatma Gandhi occupies an open pavilion framed by tall granite columns. D. P. Roy Choudhury designed the figure, which faces the broad pedestrian space of Goubert Avenue and the Bay of Bengal. The monument anchors civic ceremonies and public gatherings while forming part of a waterfront sequence that includes colonial buildings and other memorials.

08 AdmissionFree

Promenade Beach

A rocky urban shoreline about 1,500 meters long runs beside a promenade, war memorial, and nearby park near the Mahatma Gandhi and Monsieur Dupleix statues, the New pier, and Puducherry port. Locals and travellers walk the paved edge more than swim. The stretch anchors Pondicherry's waterfront civic strip between memorials and harbor works.

09 AdmissionFree

Notre Dame des Anges Church

In the French Quarter, this Catholic church is noted for limestone interiors polished with an eggshell mixture to resemble marble. Exterior and main halls usually remain viewable outside service times. The smooth pale walls give the nave an unusual finish among Pondicherry's colonial churches, and the building sits close to the seaside civic landmarks.

11.9301° N / 79.8344° E

10 AdmissionFree

Rock Beach

Also called Pondicherry Beach, Promenade Beach, and Mahatma Gandhi Beach, this 1.5 km coastal stretch runs from the War Memorial to Dupleix Park on Goubert Avenue. Access is typically on foot from nearby roads and beachfront paths. Rocky shore and promenade paving define the walk more than soft sand bathing beaches.

11 AdmissionFree

Puducherry Beach

Puducherry's central waterfront follows the rocky Promenade Beach along Goubert Avenue. A sea wall faces the Bay of Bengal, while statues, public buildings and colonial-era facades line the landward side. The pedestrian route supports walking and civic events in the heart of the city. Sandier bathing beaches occupy other stretches of coast farther from the center.

12 AdmissionFree

Sri Varadaraja Perumal Temple

Varadaraja Perumal Temple in Puducherry is a long-standing Vishnu shrine shaped by Chola-period origins and later regional patronage. A gopuram, pillared halls, painted ceilings, processional deities, and festival courtyards organize active worship within the Tamil quarter; architectural dates vary across sections.

13 AdmissionFree

Pondy Marinaa

Pondy Marina is a developed coastal promenade and leisure strip on Pondicherry's shore, with walkways, food stalls, and entertainment pitches facing the Bay of Bengal. Embankment paths draw evening crowds for sea air and snacks. The zone sits apart from the heritage French Quarter streets inland.

New Light House Cum DGNSS Station

Puducherry's modern lighthouse and DGNSS station stand south of the historic town. The tall concrete tower supports coastal navigation and complements the older nineteenth-century lighthouse near the promenade. Its technical function remains central, while public access to the interior depends on official arrangements and is less regular than access to the surrounding roads.

11.9166° N / 79.8308° E

15 Admission₹15

Chunnambar Boat House

Chunnambar Boat House is a government tourism terminal on the backwater south of Puducherry. Boats depart from its jetties for transfers toward Paradise Beach and for short trips along the river channel. Waiting areas, parking and food services support the operation, while schedules and routes depend on water conditions and the local tourism authority.
