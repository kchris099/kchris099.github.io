Pondicherry

France planted a Coromandel trading post in 1674; the enclave changed hands among European powers before settling again under French rule. A rectangular street grid, seaside promenade, and Tamil quarters inland record that dual colonial and local geography. Transfer to India in 1954 ended the French administration but not the bilingual street names.
