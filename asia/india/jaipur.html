Jaipur

Jaipur was planned in 1727 by Maharaja Sawai Jai Singh II as a fortified Rajput capital with a grid of bazaars and palaces painted pink for a later royal visit. Hawa Mahal, Jantar Mantar, and Amber Fort mark astronomy, court ritual, and desert trade in Rajasthan's principal city. City Palace courtyards and Nahargarh ridge forts still frame Rajput power above the Aravalli hills.

Hawa Mahal

Hawa Mahal, often referred to as the "Palace of Winds," is a architectural gem located in Jaipur, Rajasthan. Built in 1799 by Maharaja Sawai Pratap Singh, this five-story palace is known for its striking facade adorned with numerous small windows known as jharokhas. These intricately designed openings not only enhance the beauty of the structure but also serve a practical purpose by allowing cool breezes to flow through, making it an early form of natural air conditioning.
