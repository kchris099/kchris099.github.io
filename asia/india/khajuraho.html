Khajuraho

Flourishing between the 10th and 12th centuries under the Chandela dynasty, Khajuraho was a religious capital. Abandoned to the jungle and rediscovered in the 19th century, its surviving sandstone temples are globally known for their detailed, expressive sculptures. Visitors today can clearly trace the detailed layers of this unique heritage throughout the preserved streets and monuments.

Khajuraho Temple group

Sandstone temples from about 950 CE stand on raised platforms without enclosing walls, so open walkways circle each shrine. Detailed exterior carvings cover walls that remain readable from the circulating paths. Exterior and main interior spaces usually stay viewable outside service times across the clustered groups that define Khajuraho's monument field.

Vishwanath Temple

In the Western Group, this Shiva shrine centers on a marble lingam ringed by 101 smaller lingams, with images of Brahma and a large Nandi bull nearby. Exterior courts and principal interiors usually remain open outside service times. The temple sits among Khajuraho's densest sculpted cluster, where platform walks lead between neighboring Chandela shrines.

Shree Matangeshwar Temple Khajuraho

Chandra Dev of the Chandela line built this ninth-century Shiva temple, which still holds morning aarti for local worshippers. Exterior and main halls usually stay viewable outside service times. Unlike quieter ruined neighbors, Matangeshwar remains an active ritual focus inside the Khajuraho temple field, with living worship alongside the archaeological park.

04 Admission₹600

Kandariya Mahadeva Temple Khajuraho

Kandariya Mahadeva crowns Khajuraho's western group, built under the Chandela rulers in the early eleventh century and dedicated to Shiva. A soaring central tower rises among many clustered spires above a high platform. Sandstone bands of deities, guardians, musicians, couples, and daily life cover the exterior, while the interior leads through halls toward the sanctum.

05 Admission₹50

Raneh Water Falls

Raneh Falls is a dramatic natural waterfall located on the Ken River, near Khajuraho. The river has carved a deep, dramatic canyon through pure crystalline granite in varying shades of pink, red, and grey. During the monsoon season, the falls become a powerful torrent, creating a display of natural forces in the forested landscape.

24.8748° N / 80.0513° E

06 AdmissionFree

Chaturbhuj Temple, Khajuraho

Chaturbhuj Temple stands in Khajuraho's southern group and dates to the early twelfth century. Its name refers to the four-armed image of Vishnu in the sanctuary, a tall figure lit through the doorway. A raised platform, entrance hall, tower, and restrained sculpture distinguish it from the more densely carved western temples, while the absence of erotic imagery is often noted.

24.8251° N / 79.9311° E

Dulhadeva Shiva Temple

Also called Duladeo, this Shiva shrine in Khajuraho's Southern Group dates to around 1000 CE. Exterior platforms and main interiors usually remain viewable outside service times. The temple sits apart from the Western Group's denser cluster, so approaches feel quieter while still showing the Chandela sandstone vocabulary of tower and carved walls.

08 AdmissionFree

Bhagawan Parshwanath Digambar Jain Mandir

Parshvanath Temple is a large Jain monument in Khajuraho's eastern group, originally dedicated to Adinath and later associated with Parshvanath, and archaeological and living religious functions overlap without making the entire temple group a single mandir. Exterior walls carry finely carved celestial figures, guardians, musicians, and domestic scenes, while an active Digambar Jain community maintains worship nearby.

09 AdmissionFree

Vamana Temple

An eleventh-century Western Group shrine holds a five-foot image of Vamana, Vishnu's dwarf incarnation, with accompanying figures of other Vishnu forms and Brahma. Exterior and main interiors usually stay open outside service times. The compact temple sits among Khajuraho's monument walks, its iconography focused on the Vamana theme rather than the larger Shiva complexes nearby.

24.8516° N / 79.9350° E

Khajuraho Railway Station

Khajuraho Railway Station stands several kilometers from the main temple groups and provides the town's modern rail connection. Platforms and waiting rooms serve arriving passengers. Taxis and local transport continue from the terminal toward the monuments. Architectural details draw loosely on regional temple forms, placing travel infrastructure beside a destination known for medieval stone carving.
