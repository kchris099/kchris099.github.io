A Brief History

Bhubaneswar stands near the ruins of Sisupalgarh. Dhauli, near Bhubaneswar was the site of the Kalinga War (c. 262-261 BCE), in which the Mauryan emperor Ashoka invaded and annexed Kalinga. A complete edicts of the Mauryan Emperor, Ashoka, dating from between 272 and 236 BCE, remains carved in rock, 8 kilometres (5.0 mi) to the southwest of the modern city. After the decline of the Mauryan empire, the area came under the rule of Mahameghavahana dynasty, whose most well-known ruler is Kharavela. His Hathigumpha inscription is located at the Udayagiri and Khandagiri caves near Bhubaneswar.

Nandankanan Zoological Park

Nandankanan Zoological Park, located in Bhubaneswar, Odisha, is a large 437-hectare zoo and botanical garden established in 1960. It's home to over 1,500 animals including white tigers and pangolins. The park offers various attractions such as boating, safaris featuring white tigers, lions, bears and herbivores, a reptile park, zoo museum, orchid house and an aquarium.

Lingaraj Temple

Lingaraj Temple is a religious building in Bhubaneswar, India. It holds great historical and cultural significance and is known for its distinctive Kalinga-style architecture. According to ancient inscriptions and Sanskrit treatises, the temple is at the center of a sacred area surrounded by other significant temples and mango groves. The exterior and main interior spaces are usually viewable outside service times.

Udayagiri and Khandagiri Caves

Located just 6 kilometers from the active city of Bhubaneshwar, the Udayagiri and Khandagiri Caves are a treasure trove of ancient history, dating back to the 1st century BCE. These intricately carved cave dwellings served as homes for Jain ascetics and offer a unique glimpse into their simple yet profound lifestyle.

Shree Ram Temple, Bhubaneswar

Shri Ram Temple, located in Bhubaneswar, is a popular pilgrimage site dedicated to Lord Rama, Goddess Sita, and Lord Lakshmana. Brahmeswara Temple

The Brahmeswara Temple, constructed in the 9th century under King Udyotakesari of the Somvasi dynasty, is a known Hindu temple dedicated to Lord Shiva. Its elegant Kalinga-style architecture and ornate carvings make it a popular attraction for tourists and historians. The temple complex consists of a main shrine surrounded by four similar shrines, with doorways adorned with intricately carved figures and depictions of the nine planets in Indian astrology.
