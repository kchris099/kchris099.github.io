Jaisalmer

Rawal Jaisal established the desert fortress in 1156 on caravan routes across the Thar. Merchant havelis in golden sandstone were paid for by that overland trade. When maritime routes undercut the caravans, isolation left the fort city largely unmodernised inside its walls. Living families still occupy parts of the fort, unusual among Rajasthan's hill strongholds.

BADA BAGH

BADA BAGH, also known as Bara Bagh, is a visitor site in Jaisalmer, India. Bada Bagh, also known as Bara Bagh, is a spacious garden located just 6 kilometers from Jaisalmer. Jaisalmer Fort

Jaisalmer Fort is a massive, fully inhabited sandstone fort rising from the Thar Desert in Rajasthan. Built in 1156 AD, it is known as the 'Golden Fort' due to the yellow sandstone walls that glow brilliantly at sunset. The complex houses thousands of residents, ornate Jain temples, a royal palace, and numerous narrow, winding alleys.

Gadisar Lake

Gadisar Lake, constructed in the 14th century by Rawal Gadsi Singh, is a man-made rainwater lake surrounded by small temples. It is a water source for the people of Jaisalmer and is known for its scenic tranquility. The lake attracts tourists and offers a peaceful retreat for architecture lovers, culture enthusiasts, and bird watchers.

Patwon Ki Haveli is a cluster of five linked mansions built by Jain merchants in nineteenth-century Jaisalmer. Jaisalmer War Museum

Jaisalmer War Museum is a museum in Jaisalmer, India. Jaisalmer War Museum, located just 11 kilometers from the main city, is a patriotic museum dedicated to showing the history and information about the Indian Army and the wars it has fought. One of its highlights is an Honour wall engraved with the names of gallantry award winners of the Param Vir Chakra and Maha Vir Chakra. Galleries and public rooms are open on posted hours in Jaisalmer.
