Explore Jaisalmer

A Brief History

Established in 1156 as a secure fortress on the ancient camel trade routes, Jaisalmer drew extensive wealth from merchants traversing the Thar Desert. Bypassed by modern maritime trade, its intricately carved golden sandstone havelis were preserved in the arid isolation. Decades of careful preservation have ensured that this profound historical narrative remains a vital part of the modern experience.

BADA BAGH

Bada Bagh, also known as Bara Bagh, is a spacious garden located just 6 kilometers from Jaisalmer. This ancient site features ornate stone cenotaphs dating back to the 16th century, dedicated to the royal family of Jaisalmer. The beautifully carved images of late Maharajas and their families adorn these cenotaphs. It's a great spot for photography enthusiasts, with the windmills in the backdrop adding an extra charm.

Free (royal palace museum ₹250)

Jaisalmer Fort

Jaisalmer Fort is a massive, fully inhabited sandstone fort rising from the Thar Desert in Rajasthan. Built in 1156 AD, it is known as the 'Golden Fort' due to the yellow sandstone walls that glow brilliantly at sunset. The complex houses thousands of residents, ornate Jain temples, a royal palace, and numerous narrow, winding alleys.

Free

SHOPPING IN JAISALMER -- JAI SHANKAR HANDICRAFTS

When in Jaisalmer, a visit to Jai Shankar Handicrafts is a must for those seeking authentic Rajasthani handcrafts and textiles. Located near the fort entrance, this shop offers a wide range of products including handloom fabrics, home decor items, jewelry, and accessories. Visitors can also get custom-tailored dresses and even rent motorbikes for exploring the city. The friendly staff and vibrant atmosphere make shopping here an enjoyable experience.

Free

Burj Al Jaisalmer Luxury desert camp in Sam sand dunes

Nestled in the enchanting Thar Desert, Burj Al Jaisalmer Luxury Desert Camp in Sam Sand Dunes offers an extraordinary escape into nature's beauty while ensuring a lavish experience. Guests are welcomed with traditional hospitality and can embark on thrilling camel safaris across the golden dunes. As night falls, immerse yourself in the vibrant local culture with captivating folk performances and live music under a starlit sky.

Free

Gadisar Lake

Gadisar Lake, constructed in the 14th century by Rawal Gadsi Singh, is a man-made rainwater lake surrounded by small temples. It serves as a water source for the people of Jaisalmer and is known for its scenic tranquility. The lake attracts tourists and offers a peaceful retreat for architecture lovers, culture enthusiasts, and bird watchers.

Free

Gadisar Lake Jaisalmer

Gadisar Lake Jaisalmer is a documented body of water in Jaisalmer, India, recognised in regional geographic listings for its ecological and recreational role. The shoreline is accessible from the surrounding district and supports walking paths and boat launches. Travellers approach from the adjacent promenade and combine the visit with nearby parks and waterfront districts.

₹500

WelcomHeritage Mandir Palace

WelcomHeritage Mandir Palace is a stately palace hotel located in the heart of Jaisalmer, just a short walk from popular attractions like Jaisalmer Fort and Bhatia Market. Originally built for the royals in the 20th century, this historic hotel offers bright rooms and sophisticated suites with modern amenities. Guests can enjoy two restaurants, an outdoor pool, and onsite massages.

Free

Nathmal Ki Haveli

Nathmal Ki Haveli is a 19th-century privately owned mansion in Jaisalmer, known for its intricate exterior carvings and unique architecture. It was the former residence of Diwan Mohata Nathmal, the prime minister of Jaisalmer. Interestingly, it was designed by two Muslim jeweler brothers instead of traditional builders. Each brother constructed their respective side of the haveli to look similar but with subtle differences upon closer inspection.

₹20

Patwa Haveli Foundation - Jaisalmer

The Patwa Haveli Foundation in Jaisalmer is a must-visit for those interested in the exquisite architecture and rich history of traditional Indian mansions. This haveli, built by five brothers in the 19th century, boasts artwork and a complex structure that will leave you amazed. While the entry fee may seem steep to some, the well-maintained beauty and historical significance make it worthy of a visit.

Free

Jaisalmer War Museum

Jaisalmer War Museum, located just 11 kilometers from the main city, is a patriotic museum dedicated to showcasing the history and information about the Indian Army and the wars it has fought. The museum features a wide range of exhibits including tanks, guns, military vehicles, trophies, and vintage equipment. One of its highlights is an Honour wall engraved with the names of gallantry award winners of the Param Vir Chakra and Maha Vir Chakra.
