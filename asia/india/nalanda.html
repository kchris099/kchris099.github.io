A Brief History

= Archaeological excavations at sites near Nalanda, such as the Juafardih site about three kilometres away, have yielded black ware and other items. These have been carbon dated to about 1200 BCE. This suggests that the region around Nalanda in Magadha had a human settlement centuries before the birth of the Mahavira and the Buddha. Early Buddhist texts state that Buddha visited a town near Rajagriha (modern Rajgir – the capital of Magadha) called Nalanda on his peregrinations. He delivered lectures in a nearby mango grove named Pavarika and one of his two chief disciples, Shariputra, was born in the area and later attained nirvana there. These Buddhist texts were written down centuries after the death of the Buddha, are not consistent in either the name or the relative locations.

Top Sights & Activities

Free (ropeway ₹120)

Viswa Shanti Stupa Rajgir

Perched atop the Gridhakuta Hill at an altitude of 400m, Viswa Shanti Stupa Rajgir is a modern white stupa built by the Japanese as a symbol of world peace. It features four gilded Buddha statues representing significant events in Buddha's life. Accessible by cable cars, this site also includes a temple for prayers dedicated to universal peace.

Free (ropeway ₹120)

Griddhakuta (Vulture's Peak)

Gridhrakuta, also known as Vulture Peak or Vultures Peak, is a significant pilgrimage site for Buddhists located in the Nalanda district of Bihar. It is believed to be ancient sites where Buddha preached his Lotus Sermon and used to meditate. The peak resembles the shape of a vulture's head due to natural stone formations.

Free

Swarn Bhandar

Nestled in the historical town of Rajgir, the Swarn Bhandar caves, also referred to as Son Bhandar, are a fascinating glimpse into ancient architecture from the Maurya Empire. Carved from a single massive rock during the 3rd or 4th century CE by Jain sage Vairadeva, these two artificial caves served as meditation spaces and showcase intricate carvings and inscriptions that reflect their rich heritage.

Free

Saptaparni Cave

Saptaparni Cave, also known as Saptaparni Guha or Sattapanni Guha, is a significant historical and spiritual site located in Rajgir, Bihar. It is believed to be the place where the first Buddhist council was held after Lord Buddha's Parinirvana. The cave gets its name from the seven Saptaparni trees that once shaded its entrance.

₹10

Venu Van

Venu Van, also known as the Bamboo Forest, is a serene park located near Rajgir Hot Springs in India. It holds historical significance as it was the place where Lord Buddha meditated and spread his teachings after enlightenment. The park features a peaceful garden with a pond and various trees, creating a tranquil atmosphere for visitors.

₹5

Veerayatan

Veerayatan is a serene and peaceful destination with three locations in Bihar: Rajgir, Pawapuri, and Lachhuar. The premises are surrounded by lush greenery and offer ample space for leisure activities. Visitors can enjoy the experience of Vaibhavgiri mountain behind the campus. Meals are provided three times a day, following Jainism's non-violence beliefs, with no food served after sunset. Accommodation options are available for those looking to stay on-site.

Free

Makhdum Kund

Nestled in the historic town of Rajgir, Bihar, Makhdum Kund is a captivating blend of spirituality and natural beauty. This revered site features a hot spring and a shrine dedicated to the esteemed Sufi saint Makhdum Shah, who spent twelve years meditating in the nearby woods. The kund attracts both Hindu and Muslim pilgrims alike, drawn by its sacred waters believed to hold healing properties.

₹10

Xuan Zang ( Hsuen Tsang ) Memorial

Memorial dedicated to the famous Chinese Buddhist monk and traveler who studied at Nalanda University. It remains a notable local point of interest situated in Nalanda, India. Visitors can observe the surrounding area and its connection to the city's broader historical landscape. Local guides sometimes include it on walking routes through the centre.

₹5

Nalanda Archaeological Museum

Museum displaying artifacts excavated from Nalanda University, including sculptures, coins, and inscriptions. It remains a notable local point of interest situated in Nalanda, India. Visitors can observe the surrounding area and its connection to the city's broader historical landscape. Local guides sometimes include it on walking routes through the centre.

A Thai Buddhist temple offering cultural insights and a peaceful atmosphere for visitors. It remains a notable local point of interest situated in Nalanda, India.
