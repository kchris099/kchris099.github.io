Nalanda

= Archaeological excavations at sites near Nalanda, such as the Juafardih site about three kilometres away, have yielded black ware and other items. These have been carbon dated to about 1200 BCE. This suggests that the region around Nalanda in Magadha had a human settlement centuries before the birth of the Mahavira and the Buddha. Early Buddhist texts state that Buddha visited a town near Rajagriha (modern Rajgir - the capital of Magadha) called Nalanda on his peregrinations. He delivered lectures in a nearby mango grove named Pavarika and one of his two chief disciples, Shariputra, was born in the area and later attained nirvana there. These Buddhist texts were written down centuries after the death of the Buddha, are not consistent in either the name or the relative locations.

01 AdmissionFree (ropeway ₹120)

Viswa Shanti Stupa Rajgir

Japanese builders raised a white peace stupa atop Gridhakuta Hill at about 400 meters, reachable by cable car, with a prayer temple for universal peace beside it. Exterior spaces and main interiors usually stay viewable outside service times. The modern white dome crowns the Rajgir ridge, linking contemporary Buddhist peace architecture to the older pilgrimage landscape below.

25.0044° N / 85.4445° E

02 AdmissionFree (ropeway ₹120)

Griddhakuta (Vulture's Peak)

Gridhrakuta, or Vulture Peak, rises above Rajgir and is revered in Buddhist traditions as a place where the Buddha taught and meditated. A path and ropeway-area routes lead toward a rocky summit with shrines, caves, and views across the valley. Texts associate several discourses with the hill, but exact events belong to religious tradition.

25.0018° N / 85.4465° E

03 AdmissionFree

Swarn Bhandar

Swarn Bhandar near Rajgir refers to rock-cut Jain caves traditionally linked to sage Vairadeva in the early centuries CE. Chambers carved from a single mass of rock hold inscriptions and reliefs used for meditation. The hillside caves sit among Rajgir's other Jain and Buddhist remains.

25.0039° N / 85.4169° E

04 AdmissionFree

Saptaparni Cave

Buddhist tradition associates a rock shelter on Rajgir's Vaibhara Hill with an early council held after the Buddha's death. Stone steps and a hillside path lead to the austere formation above the ancient city. Religious texts and pilgrimage memory give Saptaparni Cave its importance. The site contains little built decoration, directing attention toward the natural setting and the long history of devotion attached to it.

25.0069° N / 85.4105° E

05 Admission₹10

Venu Van

Also known as the Bamboo Forest, Venu Van is remembered as a place where the Buddha meditated and taught after enlightenment. Paths, lawns, and shade form the public facilities among the bamboo. The park near Rajgir and Nalanda keeps that early Buddhist association while functioning as an open green for quiet walks.

25.0151° N / 85.4196° E

06 Admission₹5

Veerayatan

Veerayatan at Rajgir is a Jain spiritual campus with temples, gardens, and simple lodging that follows vegetarian and sunset meal customs. Teaching halls and green grounds support longer stays for practice. The center sits within reach of Nalanda and Rajgir's pilgrimage circuit.

25.0139° N / 85.4159° E

07 AdmissionFree

Makhdum Kund

Makhdum Kund near Rajgir is a sacred spring pool visited by both Hindu and Muslim pilgrims who regard the water as beneficial. Steps and shrines rim the kund in a shared ritual landscape. The stop pairs naturally with other Rajgir hot springs and hill shrines.

25.0166° N / 85.4233° E

08 Admission₹10

Xuan Zang ( Hsuen Tsang ) Memorial

The Xuanzang Memorial Hall near Nalanda commemorates the seventh-century Chinese monk who studied at the ancient university. Exhibition spaces and a commemorative structure sit close to the excavated monastery ruins. The memorial frames the Sino-Indian Buddhist link for visitors to the archaeological park.

25.1450° N / 85.4540° E

09 Admission₹5

Nalanda Archaeological Museum

Nalanda Archaeological Museum holds sculpture, bronzes, inscriptions, seals, pottery, coins and architectural fragments excavated from the nearby monastic university. Displays help identify Buddhist, Hindu and Jain objects that appear in fragmentary form among the ruins.

25.1353° N / 85.4484° E

10 AdmissionFree

Wat Thai Nalanda Temple Bihar

Wat Thai Nalanda is a modern Thai Buddhist temple and monastic center near the ancient university ruins; a Thai Buddhist temple offering cultural insights and a peaceful atmosphere for visitors. A prayer hall, Buddha images, layered roofs, monks' quarters and landscaped grounds support worship and international pilgrimage.

25.1281° N / 85.4432° E
