Udaipur

Udaipur was founded in 1559 by Maharana Udai Singh II as the new capital of the Mewar kingdom after the fall of Chittorgarh. The city was strategically built around a series of artificial lakes and protected by the Aravalli Mountains. It is known for its opulent Rajput-era palaces, particularly the City Palace and the Lake Palace on Lake Pichola. Today, Udaipur is celebrated for its romantic setting, detailed architecture, and rich Mewari heritage.

Jagmandir Island Palace

Also called Lake Garden Palace, the island complex on Lake Pichola now operates with hotel dining, spa facilities, and a museum among historic courts. Walls, courtyards, and towers form routes when grounds are open. Boat approaches reach the palace from the municipal ride points, and lake views define every terrace and gallery.

Lake Pichola

Lake Pichola, an artificial freshwater lake in Udaipur, Rajasthan, offers boat rides and is home to several islands with palaces. Built in 1362, it was initially intended to meet the city's water and irrigation needs. The lake has four islands including Arsi villa & Jag Niwas, which houses a small palace and a sanctuary for bird watching.

Lake Pichola Municipal Boat Ride Point

Municipal launches on Lake Pichola carry passengers toward island palaces and shoreline circuits from a marked waterfront stop. Walking paths and boat decks share the approach with nearby parks. Travellers use the ride point as the practical start of lake tours rather than a scenic overlook alone, with queues forming at posted departure times.

Manshapurn Karni mata Ropeway Udaipur

Manshapurna Karni Mata Ropeway climbs from Deendayal Upadhyay Park toward Machla Magra hill above Udaipur. Small cabins provide changing views of Lake Pichola, the old city, palaces and surrounding ridges before reaching the Karni Mata temple area. Operating hours, queueing, wind and maintenance vary.

Karni Mata Mandir, Udaipur

Karni Mata Mandir in Udaipur is a hillside shrine to the Rajput goddess Karni Mata, reached by a climb above the lake city. The temple serves local devotees with regular aarti and festival days. Views back toward the old town reward the walk as much as the sanctum itself.

City Palace

The City Palace in Udaipur, Rajasthan is a large complex that houses the palaces of 11 different rulers. It stands tall above Lake Pichola and offers lakeside views. The palace has a facade that stretches almost 250 meters in length and rises over 30 meters in height. Visitors can enter through the Baripol Gate or the Tripolia Gate, both dating back to the 17th and 18th centuries.

Jagdish Temple

This sixteenth-century Vishnu temple in Udaipur covers walls with carvings of Hindu legends, and Brahmins still conduct ceremonies inside. Exterior courts and main interiors usually remain viewable outside service times. The raised shrine sits above busy old-city lanes near the lake palaces, remaining an active worship site rather than a silent ruin.

Ghantaghar

Ghantaghar, Udaipur's clock tower, was built in 1887 under Maharana Sajjan Singh to give the city a public time signal before personal watches were common. The tower rises above jewelry and textile lanes in the old market. Shoppers still use it as a meeting landmark among the bazaars.

Ambrai Ghat

Ambrai Ghat lines Lake Pichola with stone steps and a waterfront walk facing City Palace and lake hotels. Evening breeze and mirrored palace lights draw strollers and photographers. The ghat stays more open and less ceremonial than many temple landings.

Nexus Celebration Mall, Udaipur

Nexus Celebration Mall is a large retail and entertainment complex on Udaipur's northern side. Shops, restaurants, a food court, cinema, event areas and parking occupy a building whose arches, domes and decorative surfaces borrow from regional palace architecture. The commercial interior changes with tenants and events, but the exterior design remains a clear example of how contemporary retail development adapts local visual references.

Sukhadia Circle Statue

A 21-meter three-tiered marble fountain lights the center of a northern Udaipur traffic roundabout, with a circular pond used for paddle boats. Gardens and street-food stalls ring the basin. Evening illumination turns the fountain into a local gathering point, and the circle functions as both traffic node and neighborhood recreation hub.

Saheliyon ki bari

Maharana Sangram Singh II laid out Saheliyon-ki-Bari in the eighteenth century as a garden associated with royal women and their attendants. Marble pavilions, lotus pools, fountains, kiosks, and shaded paths depend on an ingenious water system. Later repairs and additions shaped the present grounds, while the small museum and crowded tourism circuit differ from the garden's original courtly privacy.

Fateh Sagar Pal

Fateh Sagar Pal is the embankment and promenade along Fateh Sagar Lake in Udaipur. The road-edge public space connects boat jetties, food stalls and seating with views across the water toward its islands and surrounding hills. Traffic and several similarly named lakeside points can make orientation difficult, but the promenade itself runs along the lake outside Celebration Mall.

Fateh Sagar Lake

Fateh Sagar is an artificial lake rebuilt under Maharana Fateh Singh after an earlier dam failed. Embankments and boat routes cross a basin north of Lake Pichola, where Nehru Garden occupies one island and the Udaipur Solar Observatory another. Low surrounding hills and the long lakeside road frame a recreational landscape shaped by both water management and later public use.

Neemach Mata Mandir

Also called Mahakaleshwar Temple, this Shiva shrine on Fateh Sagar's banks has drawn devotees for nearly nine centuries. A roughly one-kilometer uphill walk reaches the temple with views back over the lake. Pilgrims treat the climb as part of the visit, and the hilltop compound remains a long-standing Udaipur goddess and Shiva address.

16 Admission₹110

Monsoon Palace

Sajjangarh, commonly called the Monsoon Palace, stands on a high Aravalli ridge above Udaipur. Maharana Sajjan Singh began the late nineteenth-century residence as an astronomical and seasonal retreat, though the plan remained incomplete. White stone rooms, terraces and distant lake views sit within a wildlife sanctuary, where road access, weather and haze influence the visit.

Sajjan Garh Biological Park

Opened in 2005 near the Monsoon Palace, this outdoor zoo holds tigers, lions, panthers, alligators, ostriches, and other species across walking, bicycle, and electric-cart routes. Dining facilities sit on the grounds for day visitors. The park occupies hillside land below Sajjan Garh, pairing wildlife enclosures with the palace ridge above.

Badi Lake

West of Udaipur's denser lakes, Badi's shoreline supports walking paths and boat launches reached from surrounding district roads. Travellers often combine the stop with nearby parks along the same approach. Quieter water and open banks distinguish it from the palace-lined Lake Pichola circuit closer to the old city.

Animal Aid Unlimited

Animal Aid Unlimited is a sanctuary that provides shelter, medical care, and training programs for rescued street animals. Visitors have expressed their amazement at the scale, organization, and compassion displayed at the facility. The staff members are passionate about their work and provide informative tours to visitors. The sanctuary is home to various animals including dogs, cows, donkeys, chickens, and horses.

Titanium Education of English Language Pvt. Ltd.

Classroom courses in English skills occupy ordinary teaching rooms in Udaipur, not a historic monument or public park.
