Explore Udaipur

A Brief History

Udaipur was founded in 1559 by Maharana Udai Singh II as the new capital of the Mewar kingdom after the fall of Chittorgarh. The city was strategically built around a series of artificial lakes and protected by the Aravalli Mountains. It is known for its opulent Rajput-era palaces, particularly the City Palace and the Lake Palace on Lake Pichola. Today, Udaipur is celebrated for its romantic setting, detailed architecture, and rich Mewari heritage.

Lake Pichola

Lake Pichola, an artificial freshwater lake in Udaipur, Rajasthan, offers boat rides and is home to several islands with palaces. Built in 1362, it was initially intended to meet the city's water and irrigation needs. The lake has four islands including Arsi villa & Jag Niwas, which houses a small palace and a sanctuary for bird watching.

City Palace

The City Palace in Udaipur, Rajasthan is a large complex that houses the palaces of 11 different rulers. It stands tall above Lake Pichola and offers lakeside views. The palace has a facade that stretches almost 250 meters in length and rises over 30 meters in height. Visitors can enter through the Baripol Gate or the Tripolia Gate, both dating back to the 17th and 18th centuries.

Free

Jagdish Temple

Jagdish Temple is a church in Udaipur, India. Jagdish Temple is a significant Hindu temple in Udaipur, dedicated to Lord Vishnu. The 16th-century structure showcases detailed carvings depicting ancient Hindu legends. Visitors can witness spiritual ceremonies conducted by Brahmins and devotees inside the temple. The exterior and main interior spaces are usually viewable outside service times.

Ghantaghar

Ghantaghar is a visitor site in Udaipur. Ghanta Ghar, or the clock tower, is a historical gem located in Udaipur. Constructed in 1887 by Maharana Sajjan Singh, this structure is a reminder of the city's rich heritage and community spirit. Originally built to help locals keep track of time before watches became common, it stands tall amidst active markets filled with an array of jewelry options, from imitation to silver and tribal pieces.

Fateh Sagar Lake

Fateh Sagar Lake, constructed in 1678 and later remodeled, is a known artificial lake in Udaipur. Spanning about 2.5 kilometers, it has three islands - one featuring water-jet fountains, another housing the Udaipur Solar Observatory, and the third hosting Nehru Park with a small zoo. The lake offers boat rides at affordable rates and is an important tourist attraction in Rajasthan's tourism landscape.

Free

Neemach Mata Mandir

Neemach Mata Mandir, also known as Mahakaleshwar Temple, is a revered Hindu temple in Udaipur dedicated to Lord Shiva. Situated along the banks of Fateh Sagar Lake, this ancient temple has been drawing devotees for nearly 900 years. The temple offers views and can be reached after a challenging yet rewarding 1 km hike.

Sajjan Garh Biological Park

Sajjan Garh Biological Park, located in Udaipur, Rajasthan, is a large outdoor zoo inaugurated in 2005. It is home to various species of birds and animals including tigers, lions, panthers, alligators, ostriches, and more. Visitors can explore the park on foot or rent a bicycle or take an electric cart ride. The park also offers dining facilities for visitors to enjoy.
