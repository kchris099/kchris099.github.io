Explore Rishikesh

A Brief History

Long celebrated as a sacred gateway to the Garhwal Himalayas, Rishikesh has hosted countless sages and pilgrims along the banks of the Ganges. In the 20th century, the arrival of global spiritual seekers cemented its modern identity as the yoga capital of the world. Visitors today can clearly trace the intricate layers of this unique heritage throughout the preserved streets and monuments.

Top Sights & Activities

The Beatles Ashram, Rishikesh

The Beatles Ashram, located about 3 km from Lakshman Jhula in Rishikesh, was once a quiet place for yoga and meditation under Maharishi Mahesh Yogi. However, it gained global attention in 1968 when The Beatles visited to learn about Transcendental Meditation. Today, the ashram features Beatles art and graffiti. The area is a mystical campus with architecture nestled in a forested area near the Ganges.

Free

Ram Jhula

Ram Jhula, also known as Shivananda Jhula, is a 450ft suspension bridge that spans the Ganges River and connects the banks with Shivananda and Swarga ashrams. This landmark in Rishikesh is a popular tourist spot offering mesmerizing views of the river and the lush greenery at the foothills of the Himalayas.

Free

The Divine Life Society (Sivananda Ashram)

The Divine Life Society, also known as Sivananda Ashram, is a vibrant and intricately designed spiritual retreat located on the banks of a river. It offers meditation sessions and yoga courses for visitors seeking a cultural and spiritual immersion. Situated in Rishikesh, India, this ashram is part of the renowned pilgrimage destination and provides a serene atmosphere where guests can learn about Hinduism and other spiritual traditions.

Free

Shree Neelkanth Mahadev Temple

Shree Neelkanth Mahadev Temple is a revered Hindu shrine dedicated to Lord Shiva, located in a scenic, elevated position near Udaipur. The temple is known for its peaceful atmosphere, intricate stone carvings, and a sacred lingam. It attracts numerous devotees and offers visitors a quiet spiritual retreat with views of the surrounding Aravalli hills.

₹50

Neer Waterfall

Neer Waterfall, situated in Rishikesh, offers a delightful trekking adventure with captivating views of the surrounding landscapes. The journey from Ram Jhula to the waterfall is a memorable experience, featuring several waterfalls and bathing pools along the way. There is an entry fee for access, but it's worth it for the opportunity to enjoy a beautiful bath at the waterfall and swim in its natural pool.

₹600

Rafting In Rishikesh

Rishikesh, known as the Adventure capital of India, offers a plethora of thrilling activities for adrenaline junkies. From river rafting and cliff jumping to camping and rappelling, there's no shortage of adventure here. The region's towering mountains also provide opportunities for rock climbing, trekking, and mountaineering. Rafting enthusiasts flock to Rishikesh from around the world to take on the rapids and soak in the scenery along the way.

Free

Parmarth Niketan Ghat,Rishikesh

Parmarth Niketan - Ganga Ghat is a serene and picturesque destination along the banks of the holy Ganges River in Rishikesh, India. It is renowned for its divine Ganga Aarti, attracting a large crowd to witness the calming ritual. The chanting of mantras by young monks creates an atmosphere of peace and tranquillity.

Free

Parmarth Niketan Ashram

Parmarth Niketan Ashram is a spacious retreat nestled in the Himalayas, located along the banks of River Ganga in Rishikesh. It aims to promote visitors' well-being through yoga, meditation, and Ayurveda courses. The ashram offers a spiritual journey with morning prayers, yoga classes, meditation sessions, satsang and lectures, kirtan, and the renowned Ganga Aarti at sunset. Additionally, it provides Nature Cure and Ayurvedic treatments.

Free

Lakshman Jhula

Lakshman Jhula is a famous suspension bridge in Rishikesh, connecting Tapovan to Jonk village. According to Hindu mythology, it is believed that Lord Laxman crossed the Ganges at this spot using jute ropes. The bridge was built in 1930 to honor this feat and has since become a significant tourist attraction.

Free

Triveni Ghat

Triveni Ghat in Rishikesh is a renowned spiritual site located on the banks of the Ganges River. It holds great significance as it is believed to be the confluence point of three major rivers - Ganga, Yamuna, and Saraswati. According to Hindu mythology, taking a dip in the holy waters here is said to cleanse one of all sins. The ghat offers a serene and tranquil atmosphere, perfect for experiencing the spiritual aura of Rishikesh.

Free

Patna Waterfall

Shri Bharat Mandir is a revered ancient temple located in the heart of Rishikesh, known for its rich history and intricate carvings. It is believed to be one of the oldest religious sites in India, with legends dating its existence back to the same time as Rishikesh itself. The temple is dedicated to Lord Hrishikesh Narayan and houses an idol of Lord Vishnu carved from a single Shaligram stone.
