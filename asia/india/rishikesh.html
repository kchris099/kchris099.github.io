Rishikesh

Long celebrated as a sacred gateway to the Garhwal Himalayas, Rishikesh has hosted countless sages and pilgrims along the banks of the Ganges. In the 20th century, the arrival of global spiritual seekers cemented its modern identity as the yoga capital of the world. Visitors today can clearly trace the detailed layers of this unique heritage throughout the preserved streets and monuments.

The Beatles Ashram, Rishikesh

The Beatles Ashram, about three kilometers from Lakshman Jhula in Rishikesh, was Maharishi Mahesh Yogi's meditation center where the band studied Transcendental Meditation in 1968. Abandoned dormitories and meditation pods now sit in forest reclaiming the campus. Graffiti-covered walls and river views define the ruin walk.

Ram Jhula

Ram Jhula spans the Ganges between ashram districts north of central Rishikesh, linking Sivananda Nagar with Swarg Ashram. The steel suspension bridge carries pedestrians and two-wheel traffic amid temples, shops and river views. Structural work, crowd controls or replacement projects can restrict use, so current crossing status matters more than fixed length figures or brochure language.

The Divine Life Society (Sivananda Ashram)

The Divine Life Society, or Sivananda Ashram, occupies riverside buildings in Rishikesh dedicated to Swami Sivananda's teaching. Yoga classes, meditation halls, and a publishing house structure daily ashram life. The Ganga-facing campus draws long-stay students as well as short visitors.

30.1238° N / 78.3124° E

04 AdmissionFree

Shree Neelkanth Mahadev Temple

Neelkanth Mahadev Temple lies in forested hills above Rishikesh, not near Udaipur's Aravalli range. Hindu tradition associates the shrine with Shiva drinking poison during the churning of the ocean, giving the deity the name Neelkanth. A colorful gateway, spring, sanctum and pilgrimage road support active worship, while landslides, traffic and seasonal crowds affect access.

30.0811° N / 78.3407° E

05 Admission₹50

Neer Waterfall

Neer Waterfall lies in the forested hills above Rishikesh, reached on foot from the Ram Jhula side of the Ganges. The trail passes smaller falls and rock pools before the main cascade and swimming hollow. Local trekkers use it as a short Himalayan foothill outing away from the riverside ashrams.

06 Admission₹600

Rafting In Rishikesh

Rafting trips from Rishikesh run on regulated stretches of the Ganges upstream from the city, with launch points and distances selected for different rapid grades. Commercial operators supply guides, helmets, buoyancy equipment and river transport. Water releases, monsoon closures and weather define the season, and the moving current with rocky banks keeps the activity under organized supervision.

Parmarth Niketan Ghat,Rishikesh

Parmarth Niketan Ghat fronts a large ashram on the Ganga in Rishikesh and is known for its evening aarti with young monks chanting on the steps. Lamps and synchronized prayer draw crowds along the river wall. The ghat links hostel stays with the public ritual shoreline.

Parmarth Niketan Ashram

Parmarth Niketan Ashram is a spacious retreat located the Himalayas, located along the banks of River Ganga in Rishikesh. It aims to promote visitors' well-being through yoga, meditation, and Ayurveda courses. The ashram offers a spiritual journey with morning prayers, yoga classes, meditation sessions, satsang and lectures, kirtan, and the known Ganga Aarti at sunset, it provides Nature Cure and Ayurvedic treatments.

09 AdmissionFree

Lakshman Jhula

A 1930 suspension bridge links Tapovan to Jonk across the Ganges, recalling the legend of Lakshman crossing on jute ropes at this bend. Pedestrians and two-wheelers share the span above the river. Ashrams and shops crowd both banks, and the bridge remains a fixed crossing point in Rishikesh's upper pilgrimage strip.

30.1254° N / 78.3307° E

10 AdmissionFree

Triveni Ghat

Triveni Ghat in Rishikesh is a major bathing and aarti ghat on the Ganga, traditionally linked to the meeting of Ganga, Yamuna, and Saraswati. Pilgrims take ritual dips from the stone steps. Evening lamps and crowded landings mark it as one of the town's primary sacred shores.

30.1037° N / 78.2993° E

Patna Waterfall

Patna Waterfall near Rishikesh is a forest cascade reached by a trail through hillside scrub and shade trees. Seasonal flow thickens after monsoon rains and thins in dry months. Day hikers treat the fall as a short nature outing away from the main ghats.
