Destination Guide — West India

Explore Mumbai

A Brief History

Mumbai grew from Koli fishing villages into a major port under the East India Company and British Raj. Victorian Gothic railway terminals, art deco seafront apartments, and Gateway of India arch record colonial trade, nationalist politics, and post-independence growth as India's financial capital. Marine Drive art deco and Elephanta ferry landings extend harbour commerce along the Arabian Sea rim.

Top Sights & Activities

₹600

Elephanta Caves

Elephanta Caves is a 7th-century cave temple located on an island near Mumbai. The city offers a plethora of tourist attractions, including Haji Ali Dargah, Girgaoum Chowpatty, and Siddhivinayak Temple. Visitors can find budget-friendly accommodations in the area.

Free

Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj Statue

The Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj Statue is a prominent monument located near the Gateway of India in Mumbai. The large equestrian statue honors the 17th-century warrior king and founder of the Maratha Empire. It stands as a powerful symbol of regional pride and history, serving as a focal point for civic gatherings and celebrations.

Free

Haji Ali Dargah

Haji Ali Dargah is a renowned Mughal-style island mausoleum in Mumbai, housing the tomb of Pir Haji Ali Shah Bukhari, a revered Muslim saint. The site offers exquisite grandeur and beauty, attracting tourists to explore its religious significance and architectural splendor. Situated on an island, the dargah provides a captivating view at sunset as sunlight reflects off the ocean and illuminates the coastal roads.

Free

Bandra Fort

Bandra Fort, also known as Castella de Aguada, is a captivating piece of history nestled in the Bandra area of Mumbai. Built by the Portuguese in 1640, this fort served as a watchtower overlooking Mahim Bay and offers views of the Arabian Sea and Worli islands. The rocky pathways lead you through its storied past while providing backdrops for photography enthusiasts.

Free

Band Stand

Bandra Bandstand is a popular coastal promenade located in the upscale Bandra suburb of Mumbai. The rocky seafront walkway offers sweeping views of the Arabian Sea and the Bandra-Worli Sea Link. It is a favored spot for evening strolls, featuring a 'Walk of the Stars' honoring Bollywood actors, and the historic remains of the Bandra Fort.

Free

Basilica of Our Lady of the Mount (Bandra, Mumbai)

Basilica of Our Lady of the Mount, also known as Mount Mary Basilica, is a 16th-century Catholic church located in Mumbai. The church holds the annual feast of the Blessed Virgin Mary on September 8, marking the start of the weeklong Bandra Fair. This event attracts numerous tourists and pilgrims who visit temporary shops surrounding the church to purchase candles for healing purposes.

Free

Worli Fort

Worli Fort, a 17th-century British-built fortress located in Mumbai, offers sea views from its walls. Situated near the Worli fishing village, this historic site was originally constructed to protect the coast around Mahim Bay and Worli. The fort's strategic location made it a hideout for pirates in the past. Today, it is surrounded by local children playing cricket and visited by tourists seeking a glimpse of history despite its somewhat inconvenient access.

Free

Bandra Worli Sea Link

If you're a fan of scenic drives, the Bandra-Worli Sea Link is a must-visit marvel of modern engineering in Mumbai. This steel bridge connects Bandra to Worli, cutting down travel time significantly. The bridge looks particularly at night when it's illuminated by golden lights. Driving on this bridge with the sea on both sides feels like something out of a car commercial and makes for an experience, especially after dark.

Free

ISKCON Temple, Juhu

The ISKCON Temple, Juhu, Mumbai, also known as Sri Radha Rasabihari ji Temple, is a Hindu temple located in the Juhu area of Mumbai. Spanning 4 acres near the beautiful Juhu Beach, it is dedicated to Lord Krishna and is -visited temples in Mumbai. The temple compound includes a monumental marble temple and a vegetarian buffet restaurant.

Free

Prithvi Theatre

Prithvi Theatre, established in 1975 as a tribute to the legendary Prithviraj Kapoor, is a revered cultural institution located in the neighborhood of Juhu. The permanent building was constructed by Shashi Kapoor in 1978, but its origins trace back to 1944 when Prithviraj Kapoor founded Prithvi Theatres. This cozy theater with a capacity of hardly 200 specializes in presenting Hindi works and offers a welcoming atmosphere for theater enthusiasts.

Free

जुहू चौपाटी

Juhu Chowpatty is one of the most famous and crowded beaches in Mumbai, located in the affluent Juhu suburb. The expansive sandy beach is renowned for its lively atmosphere and numerous food stalls serving local street food delicacies like pav bhaji and bhel puri. It is a popular destination for families, particularly during dramatic evening sunsets.

Free

Shree Siddhivinayak Temple

Shree Siddhivinayak Temple, located in Mumbai, is a grand Hindu temple dedicated to Lord Ganesha. It has gained popularity over the years and continues to attract tourists and devotees alike. The temple houses numerous statues of the elephant lord Ganesh and is known for its opulent gold-inlaid interior, making it one of the wealthiest temples in India. Devotees of Ganesha often make pilgrimages to this sacred site to seek blessings and offer prayers.

Free

Chaityabhoomi Dr. Babasaheb Ambedkar Smarak

Chaityabhoomi is a revered memorial located in Dadar, Mumbai, dedicated to Dr. B.R. Ambedkar, the chief architect of the Indian Constitution. The site marks his cremation place and features a stupa-like structure. It is a major pilgrimage center, drawing millions of followers annually on his death anniversary to pay their respects.

Free

Veer Baji Prabhu Udyan

Veer Baji Prabhu Udyan sits within the Shree Siddhivinayak Temple area of Mumbai, documented in local heritage registers for its civic or cultural role. Visitors approach from the main access road and combine the visit with neighbouring heritage buildings and public spaces in the district.

Free

Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj Park

Chatrapati Shivaji Maharaj Park, established in 1925, is the largest park in Mumbai. It spans 27 acres and is located in the central Dadar neighborhood. The park features cricket and soccer clubs, a pool, and walking paths. It holds historical significance as it has been the site of gatherings for freedom fighters and political rallies.

Free

Mancherji Joshi Five Gardens

The Mancherji Joshi Five Gardens is a historic, planned neighborhood and park complex in the Dadar Parsi Colony of Mumbai. Created in the 1920s, the area features five interconnected circular gardens surrounded by tree-lined avenues and heritage art deco buildings. It provides a rare, expansive green space for recreation and community events.

Free

BAPS Shri Swaminarayan Mandir, Mumbai

The BAPS Shri Swaminarayan Mandir in Mumbai is a magnificent traditional Hindu temple located in Dadar. Constructed entirely from intricately carved pink sandstone and marble, the temple features no structural steel. It serves as a vibrant center for spiritual and cultural activities, showcasing exquisite Indian craftsmanship and devotion.

Free

Sion Fort

Shiv Fort, built between 1669 and 1677, stands in ruins atop a hill offering panoramic views. Visitors are often amazed by the vistas from the fort. Despite being relatively unknown, it is a great spot for photography and a peaceful trip or romantic outing. The fort is open from 8 am to 2 pm and then again from 4 pm to 8 pm, making it accessible for most of the day.

₹500

Kanheri Caves

Nestled within the Sanjay Gandhi National Park in Mumbai, India, the Kanheri Caves are a remarkable testament to ancient Buddhist architecture and art. Carved by monks over 2,000 years ago, these 109 caves feature rock-hewn temples adorned with intricate sculptures, paintings of Buddha, and inscriptions dating from the 1st century BCE to the 10th century AD.

Free

Mahakavi Kalidas Natyamandir

Located in Mulund, Mumbai, the Mahakavi Kalidas Natyamandir is a prominent cultural center and one of the city's oldest theaters. Despite its grand inauguration by Shiv Sena chief Uddhav Thackeray, the venue has yet to host any shows due to a dispute between the BMC and BKLP. However, there are indications that this may soon change as efforts are being made to resolve the issue.

Free

Priyadarshini Indira Gandhi Sports Complex

Priyadarshi Indira Gandhi Sports Complex is the largest sports facility in Mumbai, offering a wide range of amenities such as swimming pool, tennis and badminton courts, and a well-equipped gym with cardio section. The complex also provides coaching classes for various sports including badminton, tennis, swimming, football, and karate. Additionally, it features open facilities that are occasionally used for events like weddings.

₹1400

KidZania Mumbai

KidZania Mumbai is an indoor amusement park located in R City Mall, Ghatkopar, designed to inspire and motivate children through role-play activities. Unlike traditional amusement parks, KidZania doesn't have automatic rides or roller coasters. Instead, it offers a child-size city where kids can explore various professions and engage in fun tasks. This unique blend of entertainment and learning allows children to develop skills while enjoying a safe and engaging environment.

Free

Powai Lake

Powai Lake, a 19th-century artificial lake in Mumbai, offers a serene escape with boating and fishing opportunities. Surrounded by lush greenery, the lake is home to migratory birds and crocodiles. Visitors can enjoy a long jogging track, a musical fountain, and children's play areas. This popular destination also provides budget-friendly accommodation options nearby.

₹900

Snow World

If you're looking for a unique escape from the sweltering heat of Mumbai, look no further than Snow World, an enchanting indoor snow theme park nestled in Phoenix Market City, Kurla. This family-friendly destination offers a delightful array of winter activities such as ice skating, sledding, and snowboarding. Imagine spending a day bundled up in cozy attire while enjoying thrilling snowball fights and experiencing the magic of realistic snowfall in the Snow Dance arena—all at a frosty -10°C!.

Free

Bandra Sea View

The Bandra Sea View offers expansive, unobstructed vistas of the Arabian Sea from the coastal promenades of the Bandra suburb in Mumbai. The area is characterized by its rocky shoreline, historic forts, and the modern architectural marvel of the Bandra-Worli Sea Link. It is a popular destination for residents seeking a refreshing sea breeze and scenic sunsets.

Free

Mahakali Caves

In Mumbai, India, the Mahakali Caves are an ancient archaeological site featuring 19 hillside caves carved out of solid black basalt between the 1st century BCE and 6th century CE. Also known as Kondivite Caves, these stone monastery caves once housed Buddhist monks and feature Buddha figures, Stupas, and carved idols of Lord Buddha.

Free

Jogeshwari Caves

Jogeshwari Caves sits within the Mahakali Caves area of Mumbai, documented in local heritage registers for its civic or cultural role. Visitors approach from the main access road and combine the visit with neighbouring heritage buildings and public spaces in the district.

Free

Aksa Beach

Aksa Beach, located in the western suburbs of Mumbai, is a popular destination for both locals and tourists. It offers a serene retreat from the bustling city life and boasts one of the longest coastlines in the area. Visitors can enjoy shallow waters, sunsets, and a variety of street food vendors offering local delicacies like bhelpuri, pav bhaji, and South Indian dishes.
