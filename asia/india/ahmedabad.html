Explore Ahmedabad

A Brief History

Ahmedabad was founded in 1411 on the Sabarmati River as the Gujarat Sultanate's capital. Pols, stepwells, and mosques with carved stone screens illustrate Indo-Islamic merchant architecture, while Gandhi's ashram links the city to India's independence movement and modern textile industry. Sabarmati riverfront walks and pol neighbourhoods preserve sultanate-era merchant lanes beside modern Gujarat industry.

Jama Masjid (Sunni)

Jama Masjid, a yellow-sandstone mosque, was built in 1424 by Sultan Ahmed Shah I in Ahmedabad. It is not only the city's but also the country's most popular and mosque. Jhulta Minar, Bibiji Masjid

Jhulta Minar, also known as the Shaking Minarets, is a fascinating architectural wonder located in Ahmedabad, Gujarat. Situated within the Sidi Bashir Mosque complex, these minarets are known for their unique shaking phenomenon. When one minaret is shaken, the other starts shaking on its own despite being connected by detailed arches and corridors. This perplexing optical illusion has puzzled visitors for centuries and remains an unsolved mystery to this day.

Ambapur Ni Vav Gandhinagar

Ambapur Ni Vav, a 15th-century stepwell, is an architectural gem located the village of Ambapur, just on the outskirts of Ahmedabad. Sabarmati Ashram

Sabarmati Ashram, also known as Gandhi Ashram or Satyagraha Ashram, is a significant site of Hindu spirituality located in the Sabarmati suburb of Ahmedabad, Gujarat. It served as the residence for Mahatma Gandhi and his wife Kasturba Gandhi. The ashram is known for being the starting point of Gandhi's famous Dandi March.
