Explore Amritsar

A Brief History

Founded in 1577 by the fourth Sikh Guru, Amritsar was designed around the sacred Golden Temple. Long running multiple historical sieges and the tragic 1919 Jallianwala Bagh massacre, the city remains the indomitable spiritual and cultural the global Sikh community. The survival of these cultural and architectural milestones provides a powerful window into the region's storied past. Museums, markets, and street plans in Amritsar still show layers from those periods.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

Sri Harmandir Sahib

Sri Harmandir Sahib, also known as the Golden Temple, is a central place of worship for Sikhs worldwide. The temple is made of white marble covered in gold leaf and is surrounded by the Amritsar Sarovar, with a causeway called the Guru's Bridge leading to its entrance. Inside, devotees can find the Guru Granth Sahib, the holy scripture of Sikhism.

Free

Gurudwara Ber Baba Budha Sahib

Gurudwara Ber Baba Budha Sahib, also known as Bir Sahib, is a significant religious site located on the Chabal-Amritsar road in Amritsar, Punjab. It holds great historical and spiritual importance as it was where Baba Budha Ji spent a considerable part of his life from 1506 to 1631.

Free

Sri Akal Takht Sahib

Akal Takht Sahib, also known as Akal Bunga, is a significant religious site for Sikhs. Established in 1606, it is one of the five takhts in Sikhism and is a place for seeking justice. Situated next to the Golden Temple in Amritsar, it houses ancient Sikh scriptures and books. There is no entry fee to visit this sacred place, which is open from 5 AM to 10 PM daily.

₹300

Gobindgarh Fort

Gobindgarh Fort, a historic fortress dating back to the 17th century, is a significant part of Amritsar's heritage. Spanning across 43 acres, this fort has an immersive experience through infotainment shows such as light and sound displays, a museum showing its history, and exhibitions. The fort also hosts cultural performances including bhangra and gatka shows.

Free

Golden Gate (Sri Amritsar City Gateway)

Golden Gate (Sri Amritsar City Gateway) is a visitor site in Amritsar, India. The Golden Gate, or Sri Amritsar City Gateway, is a landmark that greets visitors as they enter the active city of Amritsar. This structure resembles the entrance to a royal palace, with its architecture and grandeur. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Free

Jallianwala Bagh

Jallianwala Bagh is a visitor site in Amritsar, India. Records associated with the site include 1919. Jallianwala Bagh is a public garden in Amritsar, India, known for its memorial to the tragic massacre of 13 April 1919. It is part of the Heritage Mile along with the Golden Temple and the Partition Museum. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Free

Martyrs Well

Martyrs Well is a significant historical site located within the Jallianwala Bagh in Amritsar. It holds great importance as it commemorates the victims of the tragic Jallianwala Bagh massacre that took place in 1919 during British rule. The well and its surrounding landscaped gardens serve as a reminder of the sacrifice and pain endured by numerous men, women, and children who sought refuge from the brutal gunfire by British troops.

₹450

Hop on Hop Off Bus Tour

Hop on Hop Off Bus Tour sits within the Jallianwala Bagh area of Amritsar, documented in local heritage registers for its civic or cultural role. Visitors approach from the main access road and combine the visit with neighbouring heritage buildings and public spaces in the district.

₹700

Sadda Pind Amritsar

Sadda Pind Amritsar is a visitor site in Amritsar, India. Sadda Pind Amritsar is a unique destination that offers a glimpse into traditional Punjabi village life. It's like a living museum where visitors can immerse themselves in the cultural arts, architecture, and flavors of Punjab. Guests can participate in hands-on activities such as pottery and Phulkari embroidery led by skilled artisans. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Free

Gurudwara Chheharta Sahib

Gurudwara Chheharta Sahib is a visitor site in Amritsar, India. Gurudwara Chheharta Sahib is a significant religious site located 8 kilometers from Amritsar in the village of Chheharta Sahib. The gurdwara is known for its ancient well with medicinal properties, which was dug by Guru Arjan Dev ji to commemorate the birth of Guru Hargobind ji. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Free

Ahuja Lassi

Ahuja Lassi is a must-visit spot near Hindu College and Durgiana Temple in Amritsar. The secret ingredient in their lassi remains a mystery to many, making it stand out from others. It's recommended to start visitor day with a tall steel glass of their original lassi. The mix of thickness and sweetness makes it unparalleled. Despite being located away from the main market, the trip is worthwhile for this unique experience.

Free

I.D.H Market

I.D.H Market, located near Amritsar bus stand, is a wholesale market offering a wide range of products at budget-friendly prices. From stationery and cosmetics to home decor items and electronic accessories, this busy market has it all. While the area can be congested and challenging to navigate due to its size, it's a great place for shopping for basic necessities at reasonable rates.
