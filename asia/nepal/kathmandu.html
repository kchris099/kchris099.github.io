Kathmandu

Kathmandu Valley kingdoms built pagoda temples, palace squares, and Buddhist stupas from the Licchavi period through Malla rule. Durbar Squares in Kathmandu, Patan, and Bhaktapur preserve Newar woodcarving and tiered roof architecture beneath the Himalaya foothills. Swayambhunath stupa and Pashupatinath ghats extend valley temple geography beneath Himalayan gateways.

Buddha Stupa

Boudhanath centers on a massive white dome and square harmika painted with Buddha eyes, ringed by a clockwise pilgrimage path in eastern Kathmandu. Tibetan refugees and Himalayan communities established monasteries, shops and schools around the stupa during the twentieth century. Earthquake reconstruction restored the monument, while prayer wheels, lamps and repeated circumambulation keep the site active.

Guhyeshwari Shaktipeeth Temple

Located Kathmandu, Guhyeshwari Shaktipeeth Temple is a destination for spiritual seekers and cultural enthusiasts alike. This sacred site, dedicated to the goddess Guhyeshwari or Adi Shakti, has a rich history dating back to its renovation by King Pratap Malla in the 17th century.

03 AdmissionNPR 1,000

Pashupatinath Temple

Hindus from across the region treat Pashupatinath as a major Shiva shrine, with pilgrim numbers peaking at festivals such as Shivaratri. Exterior courts and main viewing areas are usually accessible outside peak ritual times. The Bagmati-side temples remain Kathmandu's primary Hindu pilgrimage focus.

27.7105° N / 85.3488° E

04 AdmissionNPR 2,000

Bhaktapur Durbar Square

Bhaktapur Durbar Square preserves palaces, temples, courtyards, gates and sculptures from the Malla royal city, linked with nearby Taumadhi and Dattatreya squares through brick lanes. The Golden Gate, Fifty-Five Window Palace, stone columns and carved timber demonstrate Newar craftsmanship. Earthquakes have destroyed and altered structures repeatedly, making restoration history part of the site's meaning.

27.6721° N / 85.4283° E

05 AdmissionNPR 2,000

Nyatapola Temple

Raised in 1702 for goddess Siddhi Lakshmi, five-tiered Nyatapola has survived earthquakes with balanced proportions and dense wood carving. Exterior tiers and approach stairs are usually viewable outside ritual times. The temple anchors Bhaktapur Durbar Square's southern skyline.

27.6714° N / 85.4293° E

06 AdmissionNPR 1,000

Patan Darbar Square

Patan Durbar Square preserves the former royal and religious center of Lalitpur's Malla rulers. Brick temples, carved wooden facades and stone shrines cluster around palace courtyards that now include the Patan Museum. Hindu and Buddhist practice continues within the ensemble, so ritual activity, local movement and historic architecture remain closely interwoven in the heart of the old city.

27.6727° N / 85.3253° E

07 AdmissionNPR 1,000

Krishna Mandir

King Siddhinarsingh Malla built the three-tiered stone Krishna Mandir in Patan Durbar Square in 1637. Carved reliefs across the facades tell Vaishnava narratives. The temple sits among neighbouring palace monuments, viewed from the surrounding paving.

27.6737° N / 85.3250° E

08 AdmissionNPR 100

Hiranya Varna Mahavihar

Gilded copper roofs and metal ornament give Hiranya Varna Mahavihar its English nickname, the Golden Temple. The Newar Buddhist monastery in Patan surrounds a compact courtyard filled with images, prayer spaces and carved architectural details. Resident ritual activity continues alongside visitor access. Repeated rebuilding and patronage account for much of the layered fabric, while founding traditions differ in date.

27.6752° N / 85.3245° E

09 AdmissionFree

Budhanilkantha Temple

A five-meter black-stone image of Vishnu reclining on the cosmic serpent lies in an open pool at Budhanilkantha beneath Shivapuri Hill; the sculpture probably dates from the Licchavi period and remains a major Hindu pilgrimage focus. Priests and devotees conduct offerings at water level, while non-Hindus may face restrictions on approaching the central image.

27.7781° N / 85.3623° E

10 AdmissionNPR 1,000

Kathmandu Durbar Square

Kathmandu Durbar Square links Hanuman Dhoka palace, courtyards, temples, statues and Kumari Ghar within the old royal center. Malla and Shah rulers reshaped the complex over centuries and the 2015 earthquake caused severe loss followed by prolonged reconstruction; active shrines, civic movement and museum spaces coexist, so the area cannot be treated as a frozen palace compound.

27.7043° N / 85.3075° E

11 AdmissionNPR 1,000

Kasthamandap

Kasthamandap on Hanuman Dhoka Durbar Square began as a seventh-century wooden pavilion and later served temple and shelter uses. Tradition says a single tree supplied its timber, and the building gave Kathmandu its name. Reconstruction after the 2015 earthquake returned a timber pavilion to the square.

12 AdmissionFree

Janabaha Dyo (Seto MachhindraNath Temple)

Carved struts, metalwork, courtyard shrines, and ritual processions connect the sanctuary with the annual chariot festival. Seto Machhindranath's image is kept at Jana Bahal, where a layered-roof temple serves both Buddhist and Hindu devotees in central Kathmandu, and legends and dates vary, while calling the deity a rain god narrows a complex shared religious tradition.

27.7067° N / 85.3102° E

13 AdmissionNPR 1,000

Dharahara

Dharahara, or Bhimsen Tower, was raised in 1832 by Prime Minister Bhimsen Thapa as a nine-storey landmark over Kathmandu. Quakes damaged and later destroyed successive towers; a rebuilt viewing tower now stands on the historic footprint. The site remains a vertical marker of the city's centre.

27.7004° N / 85.3122° E

14 AdmissionFree

Baber Mahal Revisited

Baber Mahal Revisited is a destination that combines history with modern charm. This trendy complex, set within the elegant restoration of an early 20th-century royal palace, has a experience filled with boutique shops and dining options. The careful restoration of the original structures transports guests to another era while providing access to high-quality crafts and clothing.

27.6946° N / 85.3225° E

15 AdmissionNPR 200

Swayambhu Mahachaitya

Swayambhu Mahachaitya, often called the Monkey Temple, crowns a hill west of Kathmandu as a sacred Buddhist complex also revered by Hindus. The white stupa and surrounding shrines look over the valley. UNESCO listed the site as a World Heritage property in 1979.

27.7149° N / 85.2904° E

16 AdmissionNPR 400

Garden of Dreams

Field Marshal Kaiser Shumsher created the Garden of Dreams in the early twentieth century as a private neoclassical retreat beside his palace, and modern cafes and events occupy parts of the site, while its quiet depends on visitor numbers and city noise. Restored pavilions, ponds, fountains, pergolas, paths, and formal planting now form a walled public garden near Thamel.

27.7141° N / 85.3146° E
