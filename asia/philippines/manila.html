Destination Guide — Manila

Explore Manila

A Brief History

Founded in 1571 on the ruins of a Muslim settlement, Manila became the indispensable hub of the Spanish galleon trade linking Asia to the Americas. Despite severe destruction during World War II, its resilient walled city of Intramuros remains a powerful monument to its colonial past. Decades of careful preservation have ensured that this profound historical narrative remains a vital part of the modern experience.

SM Megamall Building A is a massive, non-air-conditioned mall located in Ortigas, bustling with crowds seeking fake brands, jewelry, and colorful artwork. Quezon Memorial Circle

Nestled in the heart of Quezon City, the Quezon Memorial Circle, also known as "Circle," is a bustling urban park that pays tribute to former Philippine President Manuel L. Quezon. This national park boasts a 66-meter mausoleum housing the Quezon Memorial Shrine, offering an observation deck with panoramic city views. Visitors can explore various museums such as Museo ni Manuel L.

Free

SM City North EDSA

SM City North EDSA is a massive retail complex in Quezon City, established in 1985. It boasts over 1,000 stores and restaurants, as well as a cinema. The mall is less crowded than others and features an indoor playground suitable for young children. Additionally, it offers bridge access to two other malls and living residences.

Free

Parish of the Holy Sacrifice (Diocese of Cubao)

Located within the UP Diliman campus, the Parish of the Holy Sacrifice is a unique Catholic church with a domed, circular structure. Built in the 1950s through community contributions, it stands as an example of modern architecture and houses works by four National Artists. The church's central altar and priest's table are positioned at the center of the circle, deviating from traditional layouts. Surrounded by trees, it offers a serene atmosphere for prayer and reflection.

Free

SM Mall of Asia

SM Mall of Asia, situated in Pasay, Philippines, is a colossal bayfront shopping complex that draws both locals and tourists. Spanning 42 hectares, it offers an array of attractions including an amusement park, theater, restaurants, and a convention center. The mall boasts views of Manila Bay and houses various entertainment options such as an IMAX theater and an Olympic-sized ice skating rink. Additionally, visitors can enjoy the MOA Eye Ferris Wheel and a children's playground.

Free

SM MOA Eye

The SM MOA Eye is a large, 55-meter-tall Ferris wheel located at the SM Mall of Asia amusement park in Pasay City, Metro Manila. The air-conditioned gondolas provide passengers with expansive, panoramic views of Manila Bay, the sprawling mall complex, and the city skyline. It is particularly popular for sunset viewing and evening rides.

Free

Baclaran Church

The National Shrine of Our Mother of Perpetual Help, also known as Baclaran Church, is a example of Neo-Romanesque architecture that draws thousands of worshippers each week. This revered site features a magnificent 24-bell carillon and an rose window that adds to its charm.

Free

Fort Santiago

Fort Santiago is a historic defensive fortress located in Manila, Philippines. It was originally constructed in 1590 by Spanish conquistadors on the site of a pre-Hispanic king's palace. The fortress has been restored and now serves as a museum and public park within the Intramuros district. One of its main attractions is the Rizal Shrine, dedicated to national hero José Rizal who was imprisoned there before his execution in 1896.

Free

Plaza Roma

Plaza Roma, established in 1797, is a small yet historically significant park with a fountain and monument offering views of the grand Manila Cathedral. It is an integral part of the Walled City and features benches, bike racks, and a popular public book spot. On Sundays, the plaza comes alive with stalls selling local crafts and souvenirs while street performers entertain the bustling crowd comprising churchgoers, wedding parties, tourists, and vendors.

Free

Minor Basilica and Metropolitan Cathedral of the Immaculate Conception - The Manila Cathedral

The Minor Basilica and Metropolitan Cathedral of the Immaculate Conception, also known as Manila Cathedral, is a historic church with ornate architecture dating back to 1571. It has been visited by several popes and is considered the Mother of all churches in the Philippines. The cathedral's Neo-Romanesque design, solemn ambiance, priceless relics, and stained glass windows make it a must-visit for both locals and tourists.

₱75

Intramuros

Intramuros, also known as the Walled City, is a must-visit destination in Manila that offers a captivating journey through the city's rich history and culture. This 16th-century stone citadel is filled with landmarks such as the San Agustin Church, Manila Cathedral, Fort Santiago, and Baluarte de San Diego.

Free

Bonifacio and the Katipunan Revolution Monument

Bonifacio and the Katipunan Revolution Monument sits within the Fort Santiago area of Manila, documented in local heritage registers for its civic or cultural role. Visitors approach from the main access road and combine the visit with neighbouring heritage buildings and public spaces in the district.

Free

Venice Grand Canal Mall

The Venice Grand Canal Mall in Taguig City, part of the bustling Bonifacio Global City in Metro Manila, offers a taste of Italy with its gondoliers and Italian-inspired architecture. This open-air mall is a favorite spot for photographers and Instagrammers due to its intricate details, warm pastel colors, and picturesque ambiance. Beyond its visual appeal, the mall boasts upscale shops, fitness and beauty services, diverse dining options, live performances, and street acts.

SM Aura Premier

SM Aura Premier is a chic shopping destination that offers a blend of upscale retail, entertainment, and office spaces. The mall features a state-of-the-art movie theater, a rooftop sculpture garden, and a concert venue for live performances. Additionally, it provides serviced offices with fully furnished amenities and flexible rental terms. The name 'Aura' symbolizes luxury and elegance, reflecting the mall's sophisticated ambiance.

Market! Market!

Market! Market! is a vibrant shopping destination located in the fast-growing Bonifacio Global City in Taguig City, Philippines. Owned by Ayala Land, it offers a mix of brand-name stores, boutiques, eateries, and even a supermarket and cinema. This unique development combines traditional and non-traditional retailers within a mall setting. Opened in 2004, it has become a popular spot for weekend getaways and shopping sprees.

Free

The Fort Strip

The Fort Strip is a vibrant entertainment complex located in Bonifacio Global City, just across from the popular Bonifacio High Street. It offers a wide range of amenities including fitness gyms, spas, trendy clubs, and hip bars. The area is also home to a variety of great restaurants offering diverse cuisines.

Free

Minor Basilica of San Sebastian and Shrine of Our Lady of Mount Carmel (Archdiocese of Manila)

The Minor Basilica of San Sebastian and Archdiocesan Shrine of Our Lady of Mount Carmel in the Archdiocese of Manila is a Gothic church constructed in 1891. It features remarkable steel vaulting, beautiful stained-glass windows, and two impressive towers. The basilica is renowned for being the only church in Asia made entirely out of steel, making it a must-visit destination for those embarking on Visita Iglesia.

Free

Robinsons Place Manila

Robinsons Place Manila is a premier shopping destination in the Philippines, featuring seven storeys filled with premium and luxury brands in fashion, cosmetics, and more. The mall offers a wide range of entertainment options, retail stores, and gaming zones for visitors to enjoy. Additionally, guests can indulge in diverse dining options after a day of shopping. For those seeking unique experiences, the mall also provides bike lessons on its rooftop at The Tracks MNL by the NBO.

Free

Our Lady of Remedies Parish - Malate Church (Archdiocese of Manila)

Our Lady of Remedies Parish - Malate Church, also known as the Lady of Remedies Parish Church, is a historic 16th-century landmark in Manila. It boasts baroque architecture and is home to the Spanish statue of Our Lady of Remedies. The church's facade features unique twisted columns and Mexican Baroque-style architecture. Dedicated to the Lady of Los Remedios, it houses a revered statue brought from Spain in 1624.

Free

Minor Basilica and National Shrine of Saint Lorenzo Ruiz (Binondo Church)

Nestled in the heart of Manila's vibrant Chinatown, the Minor Basilica and National Shrine of Saint Lorenzo Ruiz, commonly known as Binondo Church, is a historic gem that dates back to 1596. This granite church features a striking 16th-century bell tower and offers regular masses in multiple languages including Filipino, Mandarin, Hokkien, and English.

Free

Archdiocesan Shrine of the Blessed Sacrament (Archdiocese of Manila)

The Archdiocesan Shrine of the Blessed Sacrament in Manila is a 17th-century Mission-style church adorned with baroque details and houses a statue of Our Lady of the Pillar. To reach this historic site, visitors can take a jeep bound for Recto or Blumentritt, alight at Plaza Lacson, and stroll through Chinatown's bustling streets.

Free

Glorietta

Glorietta is a bustling multi-level shopping mall in the renowned business district of Makati. Divided into four sections, it offers a wide array of retailers, dining options, and a cinema. Glorietta 5 stands alone and features restaurants, specialty shops, and an activity center for events. The mall is known for its vast selection of over 500 shops and restaurants spread across 250,000 square meters.

Free

Sto. Nino de Paz Greenbelt Chapel

Sto. Nino de Paz Greenbelt Chapel, also known as the Chapel of Santo Nino de Paz, is a modern Catholic church located in Manila. Surrounded by skyscrapers, this dome-shaped church stands out with its open sides and lush greenery. The architecture of the chapel is a major attraction for tourists, featuring a roof that resembles a soldier's cap and serene ponds and sculptures that create a peaceful atmosphere amidst the city's hustle and bustle.

Free

Ayala Triangle Gardens

Ayala Triangle Gardens is a hidden gem nestled in the vibrant heart of Makati's central business district. This tranquil, triangle-shaped park spans 2 hectares and serves as a peaceful retreat from the hustle and bustle of city life. Surrounded by towering skyscrapers, it features beautifully landscaped lawns, tree-lined paths perfect for jogging or leisurely strolls, and ample open spaces ideal for picnics or casual gatherings.

Free

Salcedo Weekend Market

The Salcedo Weekend Market, located in the heart of Makati's CBD, is a vibrant community market that offers a wide array of artisanal produce, fresh meats, and organic vegetables. Held every Saturday at the Salcedo Park, this market showcases an impressive selection of locally grown vegetables and free-range meats alongside international cuisines from around the world. The market also features handcrafted products such as coconut kefir and cane sugar drinks.

Free

Saint Andrew the Apostle Parish Church - Bel-Air, Makati City (Archdiocese of Manila)

Nestled in the chic neighborhood of Bel-Air, Makati City, the Saint Andrew the Apostle Parish Church stands as a testament to modern architecture. Designed by renowned Filipino architect and National Artist Leandro Locsin in 1968, this church features an unconventional butterfly floor plan that symbolizes St. Andrew's crucifixion on an X-shaped cross.

Free

Rockwell Center

Rockwell Center is a vibrant and dynamic neighborhood located in Makati, offering a fast-paced and lively community for residents. The area boasts luxurious condominium properties seamlessly integrated with architecturally business and leisure buildings. At night, the center comes alive with an array of lifestyle, retail, and wellness spots that attract night owls looking for entertainment or relaxation after work.

Free

Santuario de San Antonio Parish (Archdiocese of Manila)

Santuario de San Antonio Parish, located in Forbes Park, Makati, is a 1950s church known for its simple cream-and-white facade and gilded altarpiece. Built by Franciscan missionaries after World War II to replace the original Sanctuario in Intramuros, this Catholic church has become a popular venue for family religious gatherings, baptisms, and especially weddings for the affluent and influential.

Santo Domingo Church - National Shrine of Our Lady of the Most Holy Rosary (Diocese of Cubao)

Santo Domingo Church, also known as the National Shrine of Our Lady of the Holy Rosary of La Naval de Manila, is a grand Catholic church located in Quezon City. It is churches in Metro Manila and Asia, boasting a blend of Art Deco and Spanish Modern architectural styles designed by renowned architect Jose Maria Zaragoza.

Minor Basilica of the National Shrine of Our Lady of Mount Carmel (Diocese of Cubao)

The Lady of Mt. Carmel Shrine in Mariana, New Manila, Quezon City was declared a National Shrine in December 2015 and is set to become a Minor Basilica. Built by the Carmelites in 1954, this church honors the Marian statue of Lady of Mt. Carmel and was inaugurated on July 16, 1964. It is known for its refined beauty and solemnity.

Free

Smart Araneta Coliseum

The Smart Araneta Coliseum, also known as The Big Dome, is a renowned indoor arena located in Quezon City, Metro Manila, Philippines. It is recognized as indoor arenas in Asia and has been a host to various events such as concerts, sports competitions, and entertainment shows. With a seating capacity of 16,500 people, it offers an immersive experience for attendees.

Araneta City

Araneta City, previously known as Araneta Center, is a historic commercial complex that has transformed into a prominent entertainment and shopping hub. It houses the renowned Smart Araneta Coliseum, hosting major events and concerts. The city boasts Gateway Mall, Ali Mall, and Farmers Plaza, providing diverse shopping and dining experiences. Accessible via various transportation modes, it offers amenities, shops, government offices, and restaurants for relaxation.

Free

The Immaculate Conception Cathedral of Cubao

The Immaculate Conception Cathedral in Cubao is a Gothic-style cathedral with symmetrical pointed arches. It is the central church of Puerto Princesa and a significant tourist attraction in Palawan. This historic cathedral, known as the 'Blue Church', boasts a light blue vault that extends to the altar. It offers a serene and refreshing sacred space for contemplation and personal intentions.

Free

Real Monasterio ​de Santa Clara de Manila

The Real Monasterio de Santa Clara de Manila is a 20th-century nunnery designed in colonial-style, featuring modernist interiors. Visitors often leave eggs as offerings at the site, which serves as a place of solemn guidance for prayer and contemplation. Offering adequate parking space and public restroom facilities, the church also offers eggs for purchase to use in offerings. This beautiful Catholic Church dedicated to St. Claire of Assisi is ideal for prayer requests and offering purposes.

Free

José Rizal Monument

The José Rizal Monument in Manila is a striking bronze sculpture with an obelisk, honoring the Filipino nationalist who was executed. Guarded by sentries and fronted by a 46m flagpole, it holds José Rizal's remains and symbolizes Philippine nationhood. Visiting this monument is a must for any traveler to Manila, as it stands as a beautiful and inspiring tribute to the national hero.

Free

Rizal Park

Rizal Park, also known as Luneta Park, is a sprawling 57-hectare urban park located in the heart of Manila. It is a popular destination for locals and tourists alike, offering lush lawns, vibrant gardens, and outdoor event spaces. The park is an ideal spot for picnics with its shady trees and expansive grassy areas.

Free

Baluarte de San Diego

Baluarte de San Diego, a stone fort built in 1586 in the shape of an ace of spades, is a must-visit landmark within Intramuros, Manila. This historical site features Spanish-inspired architecture and cobbled streets that transport visitors back to the Spanish era. Once used as a drowning chamber during the Spanish era, it now houses a bonsai display and serves as a venue for special events.

Free

Manila Ocean Park

Manila Ocean Park is a must-visit aquatic theme park and educational facility in Manila. It offers hands-on activities, an oceanarium with over 14,000 aquatic creatures from 270 species, including sharks, stingrays, jellyfish, and the first penguin facility in the country. The park also features live shows, exhibits, and an amusement park area. It's a great place for families to explore and learn about marine life while enjoying entertaining experiences.

Free

San Felipe Neri Parish Church - Poblacion, Mandaluyong City (Archdiocese of Manila)

San Felipe Neri Parish Church in Poblacion, Mandaluyong City is a historical sanctuary amidst the city's modernity. It stands as a testament to the area's spiritual heritage and offers solace from urban hustle. Despite its location on busy streets, it remains a peaceful place of worship with a grotto devotion area for Jesus Christ near the entrance. The church is spacious, equipped with amenities like TV streaming, and has a large parking lot suitable for events like weddings.

Free

Mandaluyong City Hall

The Mandaluyong City Hall is a contemporary edifice covered in blue glass and featuring a distinct roaring tiger logo on its entrance. The building boasts of strict health safety protocols, manned cashier windows to facilitate faster transactions, and an overall clean and secure environment.
