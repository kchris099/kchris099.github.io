Cebu

Established as the first Spanish settlement in the Philippines in 1565, Cebu was the vital staging ground for the lucrative galleon trade. Its deep colonial roots and the long-running presence of Magellan's Cross highlight a foundational legacy in the archipelago's Catholic conversion. Visitors today can clearly trace the detailed layers of this unique heritage throughout the preserved streets and monuments.

Buwakan ni Alejandra

Terraced flower beds cover a hillside garden in Balamban, western Cebu, under the private attraction Buwakan ni Alejandra; plant displays change with weather and maintenance, while the site is a commercial ornamental garden. Seasonal blooms, paths, decorative structures, and mountain views are arranged primarily for walking and photography.

02 AdmissionFree

TOPS Cebu

High on Cebu's Busay ridge, TOPS Cebu looks across the metropolitan area, Mactan Channel and surrounding mountains from a redeveloped viewing complex. Terraces, sheltered seating, restaurants and evening lighting support long stays, but haze and cloud can obscure the skyline. The present attraction is a commercial lookout reached by a steep road.

Temple of Leah

A Cebu businessman began the Temple of Leah in 2012 as a memorial to his wife, Leah Villa Albino-Adarna. Monumental stairs, columns, statues, galleries, and a central bronze figure borrow from classical European architecture. The privately operated complex displays family memorabilia and overlooks the Busay hills, but it is neither an ancient temple nor a place of worship.

10.3689° N / 123.8733° E

04 AdmissionFree

Terrazas de Flores Botanical Garden

Rows of flowering plants descend a hillside at Terrazas de Flores in Cebu's Busay highlands. Stone paths, small shelters and viewpoints connect landscaped terraces planted for changing seasonal color. The privately run garden is primarily a leisure and photography venue, so bloom density depends on weather, maintenance and the planting cycle.

10.3791° N / 123.8687° E

05 AdmissionFree

Sachiko's Little Kyoto

Japanese-inspired gates, pavilions, lanterns and gardens shape Sachiko's Little Kyoto on a hillside in Cebu. Dining areas, costume rental and staged photography settings form a contemporary leisure complex with views across the surrounding uplands. The design draws selectively on familiar Japanese motifs, arranging them as a themed landscape for meals, gatherings and photographs. The hillside location broadens the view beyond the themed grounds.

10.3713° N / 123.8553° E

06 Admission₱100

Sirao Garden

Nicknamed Little Amsterdam of Cebu, the highland garden in Busay spreads fields of celosia among oversized photo props such as a giant hand and a windmill. The elevation keeps the air cooler than downtown and favors afternoon photography. Color blocks of flowers frame most of the walking paths.

10.4062° N / 123.8658° E

07 Admission₱100

Sirao Garden-Little Amsterdam

In the town of Sirao, flower beds and mixed plantings fill a compact garden that peaks in September when blooms are densest. Day visitors come for the color fields and the highland setting above Cebu. Paths weave between plantings rather than through formal museum galleries.
