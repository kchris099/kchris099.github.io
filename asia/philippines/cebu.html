Cebu

Established as the first Spanish settlement in the Philippines in 1565, Cebu was the vital staging ground for the lucrative galleon trade. Its deep colonial roots and the long-running presence of Magellan's Cross highlight a foundational legacy in the archipelago's Catholic conversion. Visitors today can clearly trace the detailed layers of this unique heritage throughout the preserved streets and monuments.

Buwakan ni Alejandra

Buwakan ni Alejandra, also known as The Flower Garden of Alejandra, is a visitor site in Cebu, Philippines. This careful curated garden showcases a harmonious arrangement of active flowers that create a mesmerizing history of colors. The name "Buwakan" translates to "flower" in the local Cebuano language, reflecting its status as a known flower farm in the region. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

TOPS Cebu

Tops Lookout, situated along Cebu Transcentral Highway, offers panoramic views of the city. For a nominal fee, visitors can enjoy this scenic spot until midnight, making it an ideal location for a quiet evening with friends or a romantic date. The lookout provides cold mountain air and various casual dining options, creating a perfect setting for picnics and relaxation. It is recommended to visit around 5:00 PM to witness the sunset and the city lights coming to life.

Temple of Leah

Located along Cebu Trans-central highway, the Temple of Leah is a grand Roman-style temple built in 2012 as a tribute to Leah Villa Albino-Adarna. Often referred to as Cebu's version of the Taj Mahal, it showcases Greek-inspired architecture and houses the treasures and memorabilia of Leah Albino-Adarna.

Terrazas de Flores Botanical Garden

Terrazas de Flores is a unique botanical garden located in the mountainous area of Busay, near Cebu City. It is the first flower terrace in the Philippines, featuring over a hundred species of active flowers arranged in neat, cascading rows on the hillside. The garden offers a cool, colorful retreat and panoramic views of the surrounding landscape.

Sachiko's Little Kyoto is a visitor site in Cebu, Philippines. The place provides fantastic views and numerous spots for moments. It offers an authentic taste of Japanese cuisine and the option to rent a kimono for photo opportunities. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Sirao Garden

Sirao Garden, often referred to as the 'Little Amsterdam of Cebu,' is a popular tourist attraction located in the highlands of Busay. It is known for vast, colorful fields of celosia flowers and numerous quirky, oversized photo props, such as a giant hand and a windmill. The elevated location provides a, active setting for photography.
