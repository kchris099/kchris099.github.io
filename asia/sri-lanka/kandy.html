Kandy

Kandy resisted Portuguese and Dutch encroachment as the last independent Sinhalese kingdom until British annexation in 1815. The Temple of the Tooth, lake embankment, and colonial quarters record how highland rulers guarded Buddhist relic traditions in central Sri Lanka. Royal Botanical Gardens and highland tea routes extend Kandyan court culture into Sri Lanka's central massif.

Embekka Dewalaya

Embekka Devalaya, southwest of Kandy, is a fourteenth-century shrine associated with the Gampola kingdom and the deity Kataragama. Its timber audience hall contains carved pillars, beams, brackets and motifs depicting dancers, wrestlers, animals, plants and everyday scenes. The active religious compound also includes ritual structures.

Sri Maha Bodhi Viharaya

Sri Maha Bodhi Viharaya in the Kandy region is a Buddhist temple centered on bodhi-tree veneration and image houses used by local devotees. Whitewashed shrines, bells, and offering tables structure daily worship. The site belongs to the living temple network around the lake city rather than to the ancient Anuradhapura bodhi precinct.

Sri Maha Kataragama Devalaya - Kandy

Kandy's Kataragama Devalaya is a Hindu-Buddhist shrine dedicated to the deity Kataragama, also associated with Murugan or Skanda. Courtyards, gateways, ritual halls, offerings, drumming, and festival observances connect it with the city's multi-religious sacred landscape near the Temple of the Tooth.

Kandy City Centre

Kandy City Centre is a modern enclosed shopping complex near Kandy Lake and the city's central transit area. Several floors contain retail shops, dining, entertainment, banking and everyday services within a building designed for a constrained urban site. Its commercial function contrasts with nearby sacred and colonial landmarks. The busy location makes it part of contemporary city life.

Arthur's Seat View Point, Kandy

Arthur's Seat is a hillside viewpoint above Kandy giving wide looks across the lake, grid of roofs, and surrounding green ridges. A short roadside stop serves taxis and walkers seeking orientation photos of the basin town. The clearing works in clearer morning or late light when haze lifts off the hills.

06 AdmissionFree

Kandy Lake

Kandy Lake, created in the early nineteenth century beside the Temple of the Tooth and former royal precinct, is a central ornamental sheet of water with a walkable perimeter. Island shrines and cloud-reflected hills define the city's core postcard view. Evening circuits mix devotees, vendors, and joggers under the temple lights.

7.2912° N / 80.6421° E

07 AdmissionLKR 3,000

Sri Dalada Maligawa

Located Kandy, Sri Dalada Maligawa, also known as the Temple of the Sacred Tooth Relic, is a landmark of spiritual significance and local history. This temple is revered by Buddhists worldwide for housing one of Buddhism's most sacred treasures, the canine tooth of Buddha. According to legend, this relic was smuggled from India on a princess's hair after Buddha's cremation in 483 BC.

7.2936° N / 80.6413° E

08 AdmissionFree

Pallekele International Cricket Stadium

Pallekele International Cricket Stadium stands east of Kandy in a suburban and hilly setting developed for major matches. Opened around the 2011 Cricket World Cup, it hosts international and domestic fixtures under changing sponsorship names; seating, transport, security, and public access depend on the event, while the venue lies well outside Kandy's compact historic center.

7.2807° N / 80.7225° E

09 AdmissionLKR 3,540

Royal Botanic Gardens, Peradeniya

Royal Botanic Gardens at Peradeniya developed from earlier royal and colonial plant collections beside a bend of the Mahaweli River. Long avenues lead through palms, orchids, medicinal plants, tropical trees and lawns containing many historic specimens. Research, conservation and public recreation share the grounds, while colonial-era plant exchanges and scientific networks remain visible in the organization and diversity of the collection.

7.2682° N / 80.5966° E

Ranawana Purana Rajamaha Viharaya

Ranawana Purana Rajamaha Viharaya north of Kandy is known for a large standing Buddha image and a monastic campus of shrine rooms, museums, and gardens. Pilgrims climb terraces for blessings while visitors photograph the statue against hill backdrops. The temple functions as an active religious center with tourist facilities at the edges.
