Melaka

Melaka rose as a Malay sultanate port before Portuguese, Dutch, and British control of the Strait of Malacca. Stadthuys, Cheng Hoon Teng temple, and hillside St. Paul's ruins record how Southeast Asian spice trade attracted successive European and Asian merchant communities. Jonker Street night markets and riverside quays extend straits-trade memory along the Malacca River mouth.

Menara Taming Sari

Experience the views of Melaka from the Taming Sari Tower (Malacca Tower), a modern structure with a rotating observation deck that rises to 80 meters. Whether visitors visit during the day or at night, be treated to a seven-minute ride offering panoramic vistas of the city. Paul's Hill to Melaka River and beyond.

A Famosa

Porta de Santiago is the surviving gate of A Famosa, the Portuguese fortress begun after Melaka's conquest in 1511. Stone masonry and coats of arms remain at the foot of St. Paul's Hill. The original keep no longer stands. Dutch alterations changed the gate, and British demolition in the early nineteenth century removed most walls and towers before the entrance was spared.

2.1918° N / 102.2504° E

03 AdmissionFree

Church of Saint Paul, Malacca

Ruined walls on St. Paul's Hill belong to a church founded by Portuguese nobleman Duarte Coelho in the 1520s and later enlarged by Jesuits. St. Francis Xavier's body rested here temporarily before transfer to Goa, while Dutch rule converted the church for Reformed worship and added tombstones. Loss of the roof now exposes the nave to weather above Melaka's historic center.

The Huskitory

The Huskitory is a privately run dog-interaction business in Melaka centered on scheduled indoor sessions with Siberian huskies and, at times, other breeds. Staff manage group size, handling, feeding and photography within a controlled commercial setting. The activity differs from a shelter or open public park because visits follow booked sessions and depend on the health, temperament and care routines of the animals.

Jonker Street Night Market

Weekend evenings transform Jonker Walk into a pedestrian night market through Melaka's historic Chinatown, and trade starts in the evening when the street closes to through traffic and temporary booths take the roadway. Temporary stalls sell snacks, drinks, clothing, crafts, toys and souvenirs between long-established shophouses, temples and clan buildings.

Dutch Square (Red Square) Melaka

Christ Church, the Stadthuys, a clock tower, and a central fountain give Dutch Square its familiar red-painted appearance. The buildings reflect Dutch and later British administration in Melaka, although their current color scheme is more recent. The square links the riverfront with St. Paul's Hill and surrounding museums. A Famosa and Chinatown are nearby, not structures within the plaza.

Church of St. Francis Xavier Melaka

French priest Pierre Favre oversaw construction of St. Francis Xavier Church in the mid-nineteenth century on a site associated with earlier Catholic worship. Twin Gothic Revival towers and a pale facade rise above Jalan Laksamana in central Melaka. The church remains active for Mass, and its design draws broadly on European Gothic forms.

Cheng Hoon Teng Temple

Dating to the 1640s and also called Merciful Cloud Temple, Cheng Hoon Teng honours the Goddess of Mercy while drawing Taoist, Buddhist, and Confucian worshippers in Melaka. Timber halls and courtyards sit in the historic Chinese street grid. Exterior and main interiors are usually viewable outside services.

Encore Melaka

Encore Melaka is a modern theater complex seating about 1,800 for shows, product launches, premieres, and corporate events. Lighting and sound systems support large-stage productions in a contemporary hall. The venue adds evening entertainment capacity beside Melaka's historic core.

The Shore Sky Tower

The Shore Sky Tower occupies the upper level of The Shore development beside the Melaka River. Indoor windows, open viewing areas and glass-floor sections look across the historic center, modern suburbs and the coastal plain. The attraction forms part of a mixed commercial complex, and weather, haze, private functions and operating schedules can affect the view and access to individual areas.

Melaka Straits Mosque

Masjid Selat Melaka sits on the Strait of Malacca shoreline with a gold dome and slender minarets against open water. Modernist forms make the mosque a landmark from both land and sea approaches. Exterior terraces and the main prayer hall are usually viewable outside service times.
