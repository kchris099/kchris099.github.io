Destination Guide — Kuala Lumpur

Explore Kuala Lumpur

A Brief History

Kuala Lumpur began in the 1850s at the meeting point of the Klang and Gombak rivers, where miners and traders served the Klang Valley tin fields. It became Selangor's capital in 1880, the capital of the Federated Malay States in 1896, and later Malaysia's national capital. Colonial institutions, mosques, rail links, and post-independence towers define the city today.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

Batu Caves

Batu Caves is a limestone hill complex north of central Kuala Lumpur. The site became a Tamil Hindu shrine in the late 19th century and is associated with Lord Murugan. Its main cave is reached by a long stairway beside the large Murugan statue added in 2006.

Free (observation deck RM127)

Petronas Twin Towers

The Petronas Twin Towers opened in 1998 and were designed by Cesar Pelli. At 452 metres, they were the world's tallest buildings until 2004. Their plan draws on Islamic geometric motifs, and the bridge and observation level became central landmarks in Kuala Lumpur's post-independence skyline.

Free

Bangunan Sultan Abdul Samad

Completed in 1897, Bangunan Sultan Abdul Samad was designed by A. C. Norman with later revisions by R. A. J. Bidwell and A. B. Hubback. Its clock tower, copper domes, and Indo-Saracenic style made it a colonial administrative landmark on what is now Jalan Raja.

Free

Merdeka Square

Merdeka Square, historically the Padang, was the civic field of colonial Kuala Lumpur. At midnight on 31 August 1957, the Union Jack was lowered here and the flag of independent Malaya was raised. The square remains tied to national ceremonies and public events.

Free

River of Life

River of Life is an urban riverfront redevelopment around the confluence of the Klang and Gombak rivers. The project was launched in the 2010s to improve water quality, public space, and lighting in the historic core where Kuala Lumpur first took shape.

Free

National Monument

Tugu Negara, also known as the National Monument, is a significant landmark and historical sculpture in Malaysia. It serves as a tribute to the 11,000 lives lost during the Emergency and those who fought for Malaya's independence. The monument is located within walking distance of Lake Gardens Kuala Lumpur and is surrounded by other popular attractions like Kuala Lumpur Bird Park, Deer Park, and Butterfly Park.

Free

Sunway Pyramid Shopping Mall

Sunway Pyramid is a massive mall with an Egyptian theme, offering a wide array of retail stores, dining options, and entertainment facilities. The facade of the mall is and it houses numerous attractions such as Sunway Pyramid Ice for ice skating, Galactic Laser for laser tag, Stars Archery, and Sunway Mega Lanes for bowling. Families can also enjoy The Parenthood, an indoor play center for children.

RM200

Sunway Lagoon

Sunway Lagoon, located just a 45-minute drive outside Kuala Lumpur, is a vast amusement park covering 88 acres. Originally known for its water park, it has expanded to include five additional zones: an Amusement Park with various rides, a Wildlife Park, Scream Park featuring a huge haunted house, Extreme Park offering activities like ATV rides and bungee jumping, and Nickelodeon's Lost Lagoon designed for younger travellers.

Free

National Mosque of Malaysia

The National Mosque of Malaysia, located in Kuala Lumpur, is a must-visit architectural marvel spread across 13 acres. It can accommodate up to 15,000 worshippers and features a main prayer hall adorned with intricate Quranic verses. The mosque's main dome represents Malaysia's states and the five pillars of Islam. Its 73m-high minaret, beautiful gardens, marble pools, fountains, and vibrant tiles add to its allure.

RM90

KL Bird Park

KL Bird Park is a 173-acre paradise in the heart of Kuala Lumpur, featuring thousands of colorful birds in free-roaming areas, walk-through aviaries, and traditional enclosures. It is part of the Perdana Botanical Gardens and offers various attractions such as the Orchid Garden, Deer Park, Butterfly Park, and Islamic Art Museum.

Free

Perdana Botanical Gardens

The Perdana Botanical Garden in Kuala Lumpur is a must-visit destination, known for its tropical plants, rare fruit trees, and various attractions. The Orchid Gardens cover 10,000 square meters and showcase 800 orchid varieties along with fountains and walking paths. The Hibiscus Garden is famous for Malaysia's national flower. Weekends are bustling with tourists engaging in activities, creating an enjoyable atmosphere.

RM80

Menara Kuala Lumpur

Menara Kuala Lumpur, one of the tallest freestanding towers globally and the highest in Southeast Asia, stands majestically at 421 meters. This landmark is not just a communications hub but also a fantastic viewpoint offering panoramic views of Kuala Lumpur. travellers can indulge in a unique dining experience at its revolving restaurant, which provides a 360-degree perspective of the city as they savor their meals.

Free

Jalan Alor Food Street

Jalan Alor Food Street in Kuala Lumpur is a must-visit destination, especially after 5:00pm when it comes alive with an array of hawker stalls offering delicious Malay and Chinese cuisine. The street becomes packed with locals and tourists alike, creating a vibrant atmosphere. Whether you're looking for food-on-the-go or prefer to sit at plastic tables enjoying heaping platters, there's something for everyone.

RM38

MinNature Malaysia

MinNature Malaysia, located in Sungei Wang Plaza, is a must-visit for families with kids. It's the largest indoor miniature and train exhibition in Asia, offering a magical and educational experience. travellers are impressed by the intricate miniatures, especially the street stall creations. The exhibition features interactive displays with moving parts activated by sensors and captivating light shows. Despite its large floor area, the entrance fee is considered a bargain.

RM75

Berjaya Times Square Theme Park

Berjaya Times Square Theme Park is a massive indoor theme park located in Malaysia, spanning across two floors within the Berjaya Time Square Kuala Lumpur. The park offers a diverse range of attractions suitable for both thrill-seekers and young children. With its Fantasy Garden catering to little kids and Galaxy Station designed for adrenaline junkies, the park promises an exciting experience for all travellers.

Free

Sultan Abdul Samad Jamek Mosque

Sultan Abdul Samad Jamek Mosque, built in the early 1900s, is one of the oldest mosques in Malaysia. It boasts a Moorish architectural style and sits at the confluence of the Klang and Gombak rivers, offering views. Once serving as the main mosque in Kuala Lumpur, it has a rich history with famous preachers and religious leaders having attended.

Free

Sin Sze Si Ya Temple

Sin Sze Si Ya Temple, established in 1864, is a Chinese Taoist temple located in the Chinatown area of Kuala Lumpur. It is the oldest Chinese temple in the city and was built under the direction of Kapitan Yap Ah Loy. The temple is dedicated to Sin Sze Ya and Si Sze Ya, two deities believed to have played a role in Yap's rise to Kapitan status.

Free

Central Market

The Central Market, also known as Pasar Seni, is a significant cultural site in Kuala Lumpur. It has evolved from a traditional wet market into a vibrant destination that reflects Malaysia's diverse cultural influences. The market features distinct sections representing Malay, Indian, and Chinese heritage where travellers can find local products like souvenirs and arts and crafts. Additionally, the upper floor houses a food court offering local delicacies.

Free

Taman Connaught Night Market (Wednesday)

Pasar Malam Taman Connaught, also known as Cheras Pasar Malam, is the longest night market in Kuala Lumpur, spanning a two-kilometer laneway with a total of 700 stalls. It's a bustling shopping complex featuring various famous stalls offering plushies, trendy fashion pieces, accessories, bags, crafts, and household items at reasonable prices.

Free

Guan Di Temple Chinatown Tun H.S Lee

Guan Di Temple, also known as Kwong Siew Free School, is a remarkable Taoist temple in Kuala Lumpur. Built in 1888, it is dedicated to Guan Di, the Chinese god of war and literature. The temple's bright orange exterior and a large statue of Guandi make it an impressive sight. Inside, travellers can see a 59kg copper weapon believed to possess special powers that protect those who touch it.

Free

Sri Maha Mariamman Temple

Sri Maha Mariamman Temple, the oldest Hindu temple in Kuala Lumpur, was constructed in the 1800s and is renowned for its monumental entrance tower adorned with intricate carvings. This vibrant and colorful temple showcases a range of Hindu deities depicting famous legends on its facade. While it serves as a place of worship for the local Tamil community, travellers can explore the grounds to admire its architecture and immerse themselves in its spiritual ambiance.

Free

Petaling Street Market

Petaling Street Market, located in the vibrant heart of Chinatown, is a must-visit destination for anyone looking to immerse themselves in the lively atmosphere of Kuala Lumpur. This bustling shopping district is alive with energy, featuring an array of vendors offering everything from trendy clothing to mouthwatering street food. The market's charming Oriental architecture adds to its allure, creating a unique backdrop for your shopping adventure.

Free

Kwai Chai Hong

Kwai Chai Hong, located in Kuala Lumpur, has quickly gained popularity as a must-visit destination for photography enthusiasts. This charming heritage lane is adorned with trendy cafes, hip bars, and vibrant street art. The permanent murals and regular pop-up art installations have become a local favorite. travellers can immerse themselves in the artwork, which includes interactive elements such as old wooden benches integrated into the murals.

Free

1 Utama Shopping Centre

1 Utama Shopping Centre, situated in Bandar Utama Township, Malaysia, is a colossal mall that boasts over 700 shops spread across 5 million square feet and 6 expansive retail levels. As the seventh-largest mall globally, it offers an opulent shopping experience with a wide array of high-end international chains, department stores, and dining options. Additionally, travellers can enjoy entertainment facilities such as cinemas and a rooftop garden.

Free

IKEA Damansara

IKEA Damansara is a popular Scandinavian furniture store offering ready-to-assemble furniture, home decor, textiles, and lighting. It's known for its affordable prices and Swedish food, including meatballs, chicken wings, salmon, and plant-based dishes like the rendang burger. The store also offers frozen versions of their popular dishes. Customers appreciate the convenient self-checkout and stock locating systems.

RM95

KidZania Kuala Lumpur

KidZania Kuala Lumpur is a vast children's theme park where kids can immerse themselves in various career role-plays, such as firefighting and surgery. The scaled-down cityscape allows children to experience professions like doctor, fashion designer, banker, journalist, and more in a fun and simplified manner. Kids can earn KidZania currency called kidZos by participating in activities like flying planes in mock cockpits or anchoring news programs.

Free

The Curve

The Curve is a stylish shopping destination located in Petaling Jaya, offering three floors of diverse retail outlets featuring clothing, home goods, and more. In addition to its numerous shops, the mall also boasts a wide array of outdoor dining options and recreational activities. What sets The Curve apart from other malls is its weekly flea market and non-retail events that add an extra layer of excitement to the shopping experience.

Free

Kota Damansara Community Forest Reserve

Kota Damansara Community Forest Reserve is an 800-acre lowland forest area managed by the community to preserve its natural beauty. Established in 2008, it has become a popular destination for trail riders and hikers. The reserve is home to a variety of bird species, making it a great spot for birdwatching around the main pond area.

Free

Istana Budaya

Istana Budaya, also known as The Palace of Culture, is a prominent theatre in Malaysia that was established in 1999. It serves as the focal point for various theatrical performances including musical theatre, operetta, classical concerts, and opera from around the globe. With a seating capacity of 1,412 people, it hosts events by both local and international artists. Istana Budaya is home to the National Theatre Company and the National Symphony Orchestra.

Free

Thean Hou Temple

Thean Hou Temple, also known as the Temple of the Goddess of Heaven, is a magnificent six-tiered Buddhist temple that opened in 1989. Located on a hill overlooking lush surroundings, it can be reached from KL Sentral Station by foot or taxi. This Chinese-style temple beautifully combines contemporary and traditional elements of Buddhism, Taoism, and Confucianism.

Free

Little India Brickfields

Little India Brickfields is a vibrant and bustling area in Kuala Lumpur, known for its colorful markets offering a wide array of clothing and jewelry. It's also a haven for South Asian cuisine, with numerous restaurants serving up delicious dishes. travellers can explore the market stalls filled with interesting goods, from handbags to designer sunglasses.

Free

GSC Mid Valley Megamall

GSC at IOI City Mall Phase 1 offers a diverse mix of entertainment and retail options, with attractions like District 21, Icescape Ice Rink, and a variety of fast-fashion and sports brands. The cinema provides a lovely movie rotation with different room sizes for various experiences, as well as simple ticketing options both in person and online.

Free

Istana Negara

The official residence of the King of Malaysia, Istana Negara is an impressive palace that is off-limits to the public but can be viewed from outside. The site forms part of Istana Negara's documented civic and cultural landscape within Malaysia, with archives and visitor information available from municipal or regional heritage authorities.

Free

National Palace Photo Point

An ideal spot to take photos of the National Palace, the official residence of the Malaysian monarch, from the outside. The site forms part of National Palace Photo Point's documented civic and cultural landscape within Malaysia, with archives and visitor information available from municipal or regional heritage authorities.

Free

Thai Buddhist Chetawan Pagoda

The Thai Buddhist Chetawan Pagoda is a large and intricately designed temple complex with traditional Thai architecture. It is a popular destination for both locals and tourists, offering a unique cultural experience. travellers often note the similarities between this pagoda in Kuala Lumpur and temples found in Thailand. The site hosts popular festivals and is recommended for families looking to enjoy a fun vacation.

Free

Paradigm Mall

Paradigm Mall is a modern shopping destination located in Petaling Jaya, featuring six floors of well-known stores, various dining options, and a movie theater. The mall also offers amenities such as free shuttle service and ample parking to ensure a pleasant experience for travellers. Additionally, it is conveniently situated near the Kelana Jaya LRT station and the city's busiest expressway.

Free

The Weekend Workshop

The Weekend Workshop in Petaling Jaya offers a range of crafting workshops, perfect for DIY enthusiasts. From resin jewelry making to macrame plant hangers, there's something for everyone. The relaxed atmosphere and patient instructors make it an ideal team-building activity or solo adventure. Additionally, the friendly staff and cozy ambience create a welcoming environment for participants.

Free

Suria KLCC

Suria KLCC, a 6-storey shopping hub that opened in 1998, is a popular destination for both locals and tourists of all ages. Situated next to the PETRONAS Twin Towers, the mall offers designer boutiques and international restaurants. travellers can explore indoor attractions like the Petrosains Discovery Center and Aquaria KLCC, or enjoy outdoor activities at the lush 50-acre KLCC Park.

Free

Symphony Lake Water Show

Situated in the heart of KLCC Park, Symphony Lake Water Show is a mesmerizing spectacle not to be missed. The show takes place every night at the man-made lake, featuring two musical fountains that put on a performance with programmed animations, colorful lights, and music. The 50-acre garden surrounding the lake offers a serene escape with tropical foliage and walking trails.

Free

KLCC Park

KLCC Park is a sprawling 50-acre urban oasis nestled around the Petronas Towers in Kuala Lumpur. The park features a manmade lake called Lake Symphony, where travellers can enjoy choreographed fountain displays and water shows. With over 1900 trees, including various species of palms and indigenous flora, the park was carefully designed to attract local and migratory birds while promoting biodiversity.
