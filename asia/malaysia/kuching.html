Explore Kuching

A Brief History

Kuching developed as the administrative capital of the Kingdom of Sarawak after James Brooke, an English adventurer, was appointed the White Rajah in 1841. The Brooke dynasty ruled for over a century, establishing a mix of indigenous, Chinese, and European colonial administration along the Sarawak River. The city survived Japanese occupation during World War II before becoming part of Malaysia in 1963. The legacy of the White Rajahs remains visible in the waterfront Astana palace, Fort Margherita, and the historic Chinese shophouses along Main Bazaar.

Top Sights & Activities

Malaysia-China Friendship Park

Malaysia-China Friendship Park, also known as Taman Sahabat, is a public park in Kuching, Malaysia. Located about six kilometers south of the waterfront in Kuching, this 2.8-hectare man-made park commemorates the 30th anniversary of diplomatic relations between Malaysia and China. Paths, lawns, and shaded areas make up the main public facilities.

Free

Cat Statue, Kuching, Sarawak.

The Cat Statue in Kuching, Sarawak is an landmark that celebrates the city's feline association. Erected in the 1990s, it has become a must-visit spot for cat enthusiasts and tourists alike. The statue is located in the city center and provides a perfect backdrop for photos and a leisurely stroll. Kuching, which means 'cat' in Bahasa Melayu, is known as 'Cat City,' with numerous cat statues scattered throughout.

Free

Tua Pek Kong Temple

Tua Pek Kong Temple, also known as Siew San Teng Temple, is a significant landmark in Kuching, Sarawak. It is the oldest Taoist temple in the city and has a rich history dating back to before 1839. The temple's ornate facade and colorful architecture make it a sight located on the foothill near Sarawak River.

Free

The Old Court House Kuching

The Old Court House Kuching is a monument in Kuching, Malaysia. The memorial stands in an open plaza or roadside setting. The Old Court House Kuching remains part of the public visitor circuit in Kuching. Local maps identify The Old Court House Kuching within Kuching, Malaysia.

Free

Kuching Waterfront

Stroll along this scenic promenade along the Sarawak River, lined with food stalls, shops, and historical landmarks. It remains a notable local point of interest situated in Kuching, Malaysia. Visitors can observe the surrounding area and its connection to the city's broader historical landscape. Local guides sometimes include it on walking routes through the centre.

Free

Darul Hana Bridge

Darul Hana Bridge, a unique pedestrian bridge in Kuching, is a must-visit for photography enthusiasts. Completed in 2017 at a cost of RM 35 million, this landmark connects the northern and southern areas of the city. Its dynamic twisting shape and colorful night lights make it an ideal spot for capturing personal memories while on vacation.

Free

Sunday Market (Medan Niaga Satok)

Sunday Market (Medan Niaga Satok) is a market in Kuching, Malaysia. The market is well-organized with different sections for specific items, making it easy to navigate. Previously located on Satok Road, it has now moved to Medan Niaga Satok and operates on Saturday and Sunday afternoons. Stalls sell food and goods during regular market hours.

Free

Sarawak State Library

Sarawak State Library is a visitor site in Kuching, Malaysia. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest. Sarawak State Library remains part of the public visitor circuit in Kuching. Local maps identify Sarawak State Library within Kuching, Malaysia.

RM65

Borneo Happy Farm

Borneo Happy Farm offers a refreshing escape from urban life, providing visitors with an immersive farm experience and the chance to reconnect with nature. The farm is home to a variety of domesticated animals such as chickens, horses, turkeys, goats, ducks, buffaloes, turtles, rabbits and guinea pigs. Visitors can also explore the Bif pond filled with arowana and other fish or take a tram to navigate the expansive grounds.
