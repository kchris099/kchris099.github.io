Kuching

Kuching developed as the administrative capital of the Kingdom of Sarawak after James Brooke, an English adventurer, was appointed the White Rajah in 1841. The Brooke dynasty ruled for over a century, establishing a mix of indigenous, Chinese, and European colonial administration along the Sarawak River. The city survived Japanese occupation during World War II before becoming part of Malaysia in 1963. The legacy of the White Rajahs remains visible in the waterfront Astana palace, Fort Margherita, and the historic Chinese shophouses along Main Bazaar.

Malaysia-China Friendship Park

Malaysia-China Friendship Park, also known as Taman Sahabat, is a public park in Kuching, Malaysia. Located about six kilometers south of the waterfront in Kuching, this 2.8-hectare man-made park commemorates the 30th anniversary of diplomatic relations between Malaysia and China. Paths, lawns, and shaded areas make up the main public facilities.
