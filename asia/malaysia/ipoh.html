Ipoh

Ipoh expanded rapidly in the late nineteenth century during a massive tin-mining boom in the Kinta Valley, attracting significant Hakka Chinese and Indian immigrant communities. Wealth from the mining industry funded the construction of elaborate colonial architecture and Sino-eclectic shophouses throughout the city center. Following the collapse of tin prices in the 1970s, the city preserved much of its heritage layout. Contemporary visitors can observe this mercantile past through the grand Ipoh Railway Station, the Concubine Lane shophouses, and the surrounding limestone cave temples.

Perak Cave Temple, also known as Perak Tong, is a Buddhist temple situated on a hill in Malaysia. Founded in 1926 by Chinese Buddhist immigrants, the temple has detailed murals, grand altars, and a towering golden Buddha statue. Visitors can climb over 400 steps to reach the hilltop and enjoy views. The cave system within the temple offers an intriguing exploration experience for travelers.
