Destination Guide — Ipoh

Explore Ipoh

A Brief History

Ipoh expanded rapidly in the late nineteenth century during a massive tin-mining boom in the Kinta Valley, attracting significant Hakka Chinese and Indian immigrant communities. Wealth from the mining industry funded the construction of elaborate colonial architecture and Sino-eclectic shophouses throughout the city center. Following the collapse of tin prices in the 1970s, the city preserved much of its heritage layout. Contemporary visitors can observe this mercantile past through the grand Ipoh Railway Station, the Concubine Lane shophouses, and the surrounding limestone cave temples.

Top Sights & Activities

RM28

Kellie's Castle

Kellie's Castle is a historical mansion built by a Scottish planter, offering a glimpse into colonial architecture and rumored to be haunted, making it an intriguing visit. It remains a notable local point of interest situated in Ipoh, Malaysia. Visitors can observe the surrounding area and its connection to the city's broader historical landscape. Local guides sometimes include it on walking routes through the centre.

Free

Birch Memorial Clock Tower

The Birch Memorial Clock Tower, erected in 1909 as a tribute to James W.W. Birch, the inaugural British Resident of Perak, stands as a significant historical landmark offering insights into the colonial history of Perak. The tower features four distinct panels depicting the evolution of civilization from the Stone Age to the Renaissance.

Free

Sultan Idris Shah II Mosque (State Mosque)

The Masjid Negeri Perak is popular tourist destinations in Ipoh because of its architecture and history. Completed in 1968, the mosque is located opposite the Ipoh Railway Station and is a great place to visit if you want to see some beautiful Islamic architecture. The two-storied building has a 38-metre high minaret and features orange domes that are a memorable sight.

Free

Padang Ipoh

Padang Ipoh, also known as Ipoh Green, is a significant landmark in the Old Town of Ipoh. It was built to commemorate Queen Victoria's Diamond Jubilee and has been a site for various events. Adjacent to the field is the Ipoh Royal Club, established by the British, where cricket was often played. The park offers a large lawn with green grass and is surrounded by impressive buildings. Visitors can also explore nearby cave temples with religious statues and murals.

Free

Mural Art's Lane

Mural Art's Lane in Ipoh New Town is a vibrant outdoor gallery that showcases murals painted on the back walls of historic heritage houses. Initiated by local art teacher Eric Lai, these captivating artworks celebrate the rich tapestry of Malaysian culture, featuring scenes like traditional dances and playful children at play.

Free

Ipoh Kallumalai Arulmigu Subramaniyar Temple

Ipoh Kallumalai Arulmigu Subramaniyar Temple, established in 1930, is a serene Hindu temple dedicated to Lord Murugan. Nestled near DR Seenivasagam Park and a limestone cliff, the temple offers a tranquil atmosphere for quiet reflection and appreciation of the natural surroundings. Visitors can capture views from the terrace against the cliffs.

Free

Perak Cave Temple

Perak Cave Temple, also known as Perak Tong, is a Buddhist temple situated on a picturesque hill in Malaysia. Founded in 1926 by Chinese Buddhist immigrants, the temple boasts intricate murals, grand altars, and a towering golden Buddha statue. Visitors can climb over 400 steps to reach the hilltop and enjoy views. The cave system within the temple offers an intriguing exploration experience for travelers.

RM130

Lost World of Tambun

Located in Perak, Malaysia, the Lost World of Tambun is a one-of-a-kind eco-adventure destination surrounded by tropical jungles and limestone cliffs. This 40-acre park features 10 theme parks with 88 attractions, including water rides, amusement park activities, petting zoos, and hot springs. It's an ideal escape for families seeking adventure and relaxation. Additionally, the park offers eco-tourism tours to nearby limestone caves.

Free

Sam Poh Tong Temple

Sam Poh Tong Temple is a renowned limestone cave temple that dates back to 1953. It sits on a 12-acre site and was used as a place of worship as early as 1920. The temple is located in a huge cave within the limestone outcrops behind Gunung Rapat and features a central altar with Buddhist figures and Chinese deities. The caves offer a unique garden view inside, and the surrounding landscape includes jogging paths, lakes, and reflexology footpaths.

Free

Kek Lok Tong Cave Temple

Gua Kek Look Tong is a limestone cave temple situated on 12 acres of land, featuring intricate Buddha sculptures and a serene garden with a reflexology footpath. Located in Gunung Rapat near other famous limestone caves like Sam Poh Tong, the area also offers traditional coconut shell-broiled Heong Peng biscuits with a delightful molten filling.
