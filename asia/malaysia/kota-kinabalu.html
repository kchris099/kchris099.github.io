Explore Kota Kinabalu

A Brief History

Kota Kinabalu grew from a small fishing settlement called Api-Api into the capital of British North Borneo and, after 1963, of Sabah state in Malaysia. Rebuilt after wartime bombing, the city now serves as the gateway to Kinabalu National Park and the coral islands of Tunku Abdul Rahman Marine Park while retaining markets and mosques along its waterfront.

Top Sights & Activities

RM5

Kota Kinabalu City Mosque

Kota Kinabalu City Mosque stands on Likas Bay with a blue dome and minaret reflected in a surrounding lagoon when tides allow. Completed in 2000, the contemporary design accommodates large Friday congregations and evening photography from the lakeside promenade.

Free

I Love KK

The I Love KK sign is a waterfront photo landmark on Kota Kinabalu's central promenade near the handicraft market. The letters frame harbour views toward Gaya Island and have become a common meeting point for visitors exploring the city centre on foot.

Free

Gaya Street Market

Gaya Street is Kota Kinabalu's Sunday market strip in the old town, trading produce, crafts, and antiques under awnings near the central business district. The weekly fair supplements permanent shops along a road named for the British North Borneo Company's early settlement. Collectors and tourists browse the stalls from early morning.

Free

Atkinson Clock Tower

The Atkinson Clock Tower is a wooden tower built in 1905 on a hill above Kota Kinabalu's old town, named for the first district officer of Jesselton. It survived wartime bombing and now stands among modern shops as one of the city's few colonial-era landmarks.

Free

Kota Kinabalu City Park

Kota Kinabalu City Park is a small urban green space near the waterfront with lawns and shaded paths for residents escaping the central market crowds. Benches and plantings provide a short rest stop between the harbour promenade and the old town shops. The park sits within walking distance of the I Love KK sign and handicraft market.

Free

Tanjung Aru Beach

Tanjung Aru Beach is Kota Kinabalu's main public beach west of the airport, lined with food stalls and shaded lawns. The name refers to the casuarina or aru trees that fringe the sand. Sunset views toward the Tunku Abdul Rahman islands draw evening crowds from the city.

Kokol Hill Elf

Kokol Hill is a highland viewpoint south of Kota Kinabalu reached by a winding road through villages above the city. Hill resorts and cafés use the elevation for cooler air and panoramic views over the coast and offshore islands at sunset. The Elf-themed viewpoint deck is a popular photo stop on the ridge.

Free

Sabah State Mosque

Sabah State Mosque is the principal Muslim place of worship in Kota Kinabalu's civic quarter, distinct from the larger floating city mosque on Likas Bay. Its dome and minaret anchor the government district near state offices and the central market. Worshippers gather for daily prayers and Friday sermons in the heart of the capital.

Free

Bukit Kopungit Hiking Trail starting point

Bukit Kopungit is a forested hill south of Kota Kinabalu with hiking trails starting from suburban roads above the airport. Local walkers climb the slopes for exercise and views toward the city and South China Sea on clear mornings. The trailhead serves as a gateway to lowland rainforest minutes from the city centre.
