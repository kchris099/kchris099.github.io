Kota Kinabalu

Kota Kinabalu replaced Jesselton after World War II as capital of Sabah on Borneo's northwest coast. The name honours Mount Kinabalu, sacred to Kadazan-Dusun communities. Waterfront mosques, the Atkinson Clock Tower, and island ferries reflect British North Borneo administration, Malaysian federation since 1963, and trade across the South China Sea.

Kota Kinabalu City Mosque

Masjid Bandaraya Kota Kinabalu is a grand mosque with a blue and gold dome, partially surrounded by a lagoon. It offers tours and is a highlight of city tours in Kota Kinabalu. The area also includes Signal Hill, State Government Building, Double Six Monument, Atkinson Tower, Jalan Gaya, and other attractions, visitors can enjoy the tranquility of the sea and the liveliness of the city.

Puh Toh Si Chinese Temple

Puh Toh Tse (Puh Toh Si) is a Chinese Buddhist temple complex on the hills above Kota Kinabalu with bright halls and guardian statues. Stairs and terraces climb between prayer rooms and outdoor courtyards. Views from the upper levels look toward the city and coastal plain.

Atkinson Clock Tower

The structure survived wartime destruction that erased much of the colonial town and remains on a small hillside above the center, and trees and later development partly limit views, while conservation work is essential to its continuing survival. A timber clock tower built in 1905 remembers Francis George Atkinson, an early district officer of Jesselton, now Kota Kinabalu.

04 AdmissionFree

Gaya Street Market

Every Sunday morning, Gaya Street closes to through traffic and fills with stalls selling produce, cooked food; plants, clothing, crafts, household items and souvenirs. The road was known as Bond Street during the colonial period and remains part of central Kota Kinabalu's commercial grid.

Kota Kinabalu City Park

Within Kota Kinabalu City Park, the floating City Mosque rises on stilts above a lagoon, with room for roughly 12,000 worshippers. Nearby greens such as Perdana Park and several war memorials extend the same shoreline civic belt. Paths and water views link religious architecture to everyday recreation along the coast.

06 AdmissionFree

I Love KK

The Love KK sign located on the public waterfront area of Kota Kinabalu is a popular tourist attraction for tourists who have come to see and photograph the city. It is one of the icons of Kota Kinabalu and is close to open area seafood Market and restaurant.

07 AdmissionFree

Sabah State Mosque

A striped blue-and-white dome and tall minaret distinguish Sabah State Mosque near central Kota Kinabalu. Completed in the 1970s, the building contains a large prayer hall, courtyards, and facilities serving the state's Muslim community; architectural comparisons and capacity figures vary across sources, while active worship and modern Sabah civic identity provide the clearest context.

5.9599° N / 116.0671° E

08 AdmissionFree

Tanjung Aru Beach

Long sand at Tanjung Aru faces open water west of Kota Kinabalu, drawing sunset crowds to food stalls and shoreline cafes. Swimmers and water-sport rentals share the same stretch on calmer days. Evening light across the islands remains the beach's main draw for residents and travellers alike.

5.9474° N / 116.0456° E

09 AdmissionRM10

Lok Kawi Wildlife Park

Opened by the Sabah Wildlife Department in 2007, Lok Kawi Wildlife Park combines zoological exhibits with a botanical section south of Kota Kinabalu. Paths pass enclosures for Bornean elephants, orangutans, proboscis monkeys, sun bears and other regional animals. The grounds introduce Sabah's wildlife and plants in one managed setting, with interpretation focused on local species and conservation work.

5.8495° N / 116.0693° E

10 AdmissionRM10

Kokol Hill Elf

Kokol Hill rises inland from Kota Kinabalu with cooler air and clear mornings that can reveal Mount Kinabalu's summit. Viewpoints and small stays such as Kokol Heights Villa face the ranges and distant coast. Sunset light over the South China Sea draws evening visitors to the ridge.
