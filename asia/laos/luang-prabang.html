Destination Guide — Luang Prabang

Explore Luang Prabang

A Brief History

Serving as the royal capital of the Lane Xang kingdom from the 14th century, Luang Prabang was a supreme center of Lao Buddhism. Its peaceful assimilation of French colonial architecture with ancient temple aesthetics created a uniquely preserved Southeast Asian heritage site. Museums, markets, and street plans in Luang Prabang still show layers from those periods.

Top Sights & Activities

LAK 30,000

Wat Xiengthong

Wat Xiengthong is a visitor site in Luang Prabang, Laos. Wat Xiengthong is a Buddhist temple complex in Luang Prabang, Laos, known for its ornate shrines, prayer halls, and gardens. Built in 1560 by King Setthathirath, the temple showcases traditional Lao architecture with sweeping two-tiered roofs and detailed mosaics. The main structures and grounds are the principal points of interest.

Free

Wat Sensoukharam

Wat Sensoukharam is a market in Luang Prabang, Laos. Wat Sensoukharam, also known as Wat Sen, is an 18th-century Buddhist temple located in Luang Prabang, Laos. It is named 'Temple of 100,000 treasures' due to being built with stones from the Mekong River. The main hall houses a collection of religious artifacts and murals depicting Buddhist teachings and local folklore. Stalls sell food and goods during regular market hours.

Free

Luang Prabang Night Market

Luang Prabang Night Market is a market in Luang Prabang, Laos. Visitors can immerse themselves in the history as they browse through finely woven textiles, detailed wooden creations, authentic pottery, delicate silver jewellery, and active silk scarves. The market also has a variety of delectable street foods and local specialties like the known Luang Prabang sausage. Stalls sell food and goods during regular market hours.

Free

Luang Prabang morning market

The Luang Prabang morning market is a active and busy place where local vendors sell an array of goods, including fresh fruits, vegetables, meats, and handmade textiles. Visitors can immerse themselves in the cultural richness by exploring traditional Lao garments, marveling at detailed Hmong embroidery, and admiring crafts from hill tribes.

Free

Wat Wisunarat

Wat Wisunarat is a visitor site in Luang Prabang, Laos. Records associated with the site include 1513. Located on the opposite side of Phu Si hill, Wat Wisunarat is a evidence of the rich history and architectural beauty of Luang Prabang. Dating back to 1513, this Buddhist temple is known for its striking watermelon-domed stupa and is considered the oldest operating temple in the area. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

LAK 30,000

Phousi Hill

Phousi Hill, located at the Luang Prabang, offers panoramic views and is a popular spot to watch the sunset. The hill is surrounded by the Mekong and Khan rivers and the Luang Prabang Mountain Range. Visitors can explore the Royal Palace Museum for a glimpse into Laotian history and enjoy vistas of Phousi Hill, mountains, and rivers.

Free

MandaLao Elephant Conservation

MandaLao Elephant Conservation, a short drive from Luang Prabang, shelters elephants rescued from logging and riding camps. Guests feed fruit and sugarcane under staff guidance, then walk with the animals for about two hours on forest paths. The programme bans riding and keeps group sizes limited. The focus is observation and care rather than performance.

Free

Phosy Market

Phosy Market, located on the outskirts of town, offers a glimpse into local life. This semi-outdoor market is a treasure trove of goods, from spices and animal parts to kitchenware and textiles. It's a place where find everything for visitor daily needs, including fresh produce and textiles. The market is not heavily frequented by Western tourists, making it an authentic experience.

Free

Ock Pop Tok Living Craft Centre

The Ock Pop Tok Living Craft Centre in Luang Prabang, Laos, offers an immersive experience into traditional Lao weaving. The award-winning center provides detailed explanations of the weaving process, including the extraction of natural dyes from leaves, bark, and flowers. Visitors can learn about different weaving techniques used by various ethnolinguistic groups in Laos.

Free

The Living Land Company

The Living Land Company is an organic community farm and educational enterprise located near Luang Prabang. It has a hands-on, immersive experience in traditional rice cultivation, allowing them to participate in every step of the process, from plowing with water buffalo to harvesting. The farm promotes sustainable agriculture and supports local community development programs.
