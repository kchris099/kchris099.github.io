Destination Guide — Vientiane

Explore Vientiane

A Brief History

Established as a capital in the 16th century to evade Burmese invasions, Vientiane was repeatedly sacked and later rebuilt under French colonial administration. Its broad boulevards and gilded stupas reflect a quiet resilience amidst centuries of regional conflict and foreign rule. Visitors today can clearly trace the detailed layers of this unique heritage throughout the preserved streets and monuments.

Top Sights & Activities

LAK 30,000

Pha That Luang Vientiane

Pha That Luang Vientiane is a historic 44-meter gold Buddhist stupa surrounded by a walled enclosure and grassy courtyard. Nearby, visitors can explore an air-conditioned shopping mall offering electronics, sports equipment, and jewelry. The original Morning Market is filled with mass-produced silks, T-shirts, and souvenirs. Along the Mekong River banks, locals and tourists enjoy walks, exercise, dining at restaurants, and shopping for clothes and souvenirs at night.

LAK 10,000

Patuxai

Patuxai, also known as the Victory Monument, is a visitor site in Vientiane, Laos. Patuxay, also known as the Victory Monument, is a striking war memorial in Vientiane. Resembling the Arc de Triomphe from afar, it stands at the city center and symbolizes Laos' liberation and independence. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Free

Patuxay Park

Patuxay Park, also known as the Patuxai Victory Gate, is a significant landmark in Vientiane, Laos. It is a war memorial and triumphal arch dedicated to those who fought for independence from France. The monument was constructed between 1957 and 1968 and holds historical and cultural importance for the Laotian people. Visitors can explore the inside of the monument, which offers interesting details, and climb to the top for a great view of Vientiane.

Free

Wat Si Muang Temple

Wat Si Muang is a active and historically significant Buddhist temple in Vientiane, known for its association with good luck and fortune. Legend has it that the temple was named after a young woman who sacrificed herself to appease angry spirits during its construction in 1563. Visitors flock to this colorful temple to receive blessings from monks and pray for good fortune.

Free

COPE Visitor Centre

COPE Visitor Centre is a visitor site in Vientiane, Laos. The COPE Visitor Centre in Vientiane is a profound educational facility detailing the devastating, ongoing impact of unexploded ordnance (UXO) left in Laos from the Secret War. It highlights the important work of the Cooperative Orthotic and Prosthetic Enterprise in providing rehabilitation services to UXO survivors. The main structures and grounds are the principal points of interest.

LAK 30,000

Wat Sisaket

Wat Sisaket is a visitor site in Vientiane, Laos. It was constructed by the last king of the Vientiane Kingdom and is one of the oldest temples in the city. The temple houses a museum and cloister wall that contains thousands of small Buddhas. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

LAK 30,000

Ho Phrakeo Museum

Ho Phrakeo Museum, originally a royal temple built in the 16th century to enshrine the Emerald Buddha, is now a museum and shop. The shrine no longer houses the Emerald Buddha, which was moved to Bangkok during the Siamese occupation of Laos. Instead, it showcases various Buddhist artifacts such as bronze and stone Buddhas, 6th-century sculptures and tablets, and other Laotian treasures.

Free

Chao Anouvong Park

Chao Anouvong Park, named after the last king of Laos, offers a riverside promenade, picnic spots, and a nightly market with souvenir vendors. The park used to have ramshackle riverside bars and restaurants offering sunset views and fresh grilled fish. However, the government transformed the area by building a flood wall for city protection and relocating eateries to create a more communal space.

Free

Wat Ong Teu Temple

Wat Ong Teu Mahawihan, also known as the 'Temple of the Heavy Buddha,' is a 16th-century Buddhist temple located along Vientiane's riverside. It was built by King Setthathirath and has undergone careful restoration in the 19th and 20th centuries after long running a devastating Siamese invasion.

Free

Ban Anou Night Market

Ban Anou Night Market is a must-visit spot in Vientiane, offering an array of street food and traditional local delicacies. The market opens every evening around 6 pm, featuring a variety of street snacks such as pho with hand-pulled noodles, grilled skewered meats, rice pancakes, and local beverages like coconut drinks and chai tea. In addition to the culinary delights, visitors can explore stalls selling clothing, accessories, and souvenirs.
