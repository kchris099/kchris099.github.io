Explore Singapore

A Brief History

Singapore grew around the Singapore River, where maritime trade linked Southeast Asia with China, India, and Europe. British colonial rule established a free port in 1819, followed by Japanese occupation from 1942 to 1945, self-government in 1959, and independence in 1965. The modern city retains civic buildings, waterfront infrastructure, religious districts, and food traditions from these periods.

Top Sights & Activities

Jewel Rain Vortex

The Rain Vortex opened with Jewel Changi Airport in 2019. Water falls 40 metres through the centre of the glass-and-steel roof into the Forest Valley below. Architect Moshe Safdie designed Jewel as a public complex connecting airport terminals with gardens, shops, dining, and transport links.

Free

Marina Bay Sands

Marina Bay Sands opened in stages during 2010 and 2011 on reclaimed land at Marina Bay. Moshe Safdie designed its three hotel towers and connecting SkyPark. The complex includes a hotel, convention centre, shops, theatres, casino, ArtScience Museum, and a public observation deck.

Free

Merlion

The 8.6-metre Merlion was built by local craftsman Lim Nang Seng and unveiled in 1972. Its fish body refers to Singapore's maritime past and the name Temasek, while its lion head refers to Singapura. The statue moved to its current Marina Bay site in 2002 after Esplanade Bridge restricted the former view.

Free

Fullerton Promenade

The promenade follows the bay beside One Fullerton and the mouth of the Singapore River. This waterfront was once part of the colonial port district near Fullerton Square, the General Post Office, and the Waterboat House. The present pedestrian route connects Merlion Park with Collyer Quay and Marina Bay.

Free (OCBC Skyway S$14)

Supertree Grove

Supertree Grove opened with Gardens by the Bay in 2012. Its tree-like structures rise between 25 and 50 metres and support vertical gardens. Some collect solar energy or rainwater for garden functions. The OCBC Skyway links two Supertrees 22 metres above the ground.

Free

Fort Canning Park

Fort Canning Park, In Singapore, is a historically significant site that has witnessed many milestones in the city-state's history. It has been home to 14th-century kings' palaces and played a role in the surrender of Singapore to the Japanese during World War II. travellers can explore remnants of a 19th-century fortress, a Spice Garden inspired by Sir Stamford Raffles' experiments with spices, and the ASEAN Sculpture Garden.

S$40

Singapore Flyer

The Singapore Flyer, the world's largest giant observation wheel, offers a unique experience for travellers to enjoy views of the city. With its 28 glass cabins slowly revolving at 165m above ground level, it provides panoramic vistas of Marina Bay and its architectural landmarks. The observation wheel also offers various packages including high tea and butler-serviced flights, allowing guests to be pampered while taking in the scenery.

Free

The Helix Bridge

The Helix Bridge, a steel footbridge that opened its doors in 2010, is an architectural marvel inspired by the double-helix structure of DNA. Spanning 280 meters, this pedestrian walkway connects Marina Centre to Marina South and serves as a vital link to the Marina Bay Sands resort.

S$23

SkyPark Observation Deck

The SkyPark Observation Deck, situated on the 57th floor of the Marina Bay Sands Hotel tower 3, offers panoramic views of Singapore's cityscape and Marina Bay. travellers can enjoy this experience for a fee, with discounted rates available for Sands Rewards LifeStyle Members. The deck is closed for a brief period on Wednesdays and Thursdays but remains open on public holidays.

Free

Marina Barrage

Marina Barrage, located at Marina South, is a multifunctional destination in Singapore. It serves as a reservoir and also offers recreational activities such as kayaking and dragon boating. The modern architectural gem features interactive exhibits on sustainability and provides panoramic views of the city skyline, including landmarks like the Singapore Flyer, Marina Bay Sands, and Gardens by the Bay.

S$20

Flower Dome

The world's largest glass greenhouse, showcasing exotic plants from Mediterranean and semi-arid regions. The site forms part of Flower Dome's documented civic and cultural landscape within Singapore, with archives and visitor information available from municipal or regional heritage authorities.

Cloud Forest

Step into the Cloud Forest, a captivating world shrouded in mist and surrounded by diverse vegetation. Located within Singapore's Gardens By The Bay, this modern greenhouse showcases lush greenery and hidden floral treasures, along with a 35-meter indoor waterfall cascading from a mountain of verdant foliage. travellers can also explore the adjacent Flower Dome, featuring plants from around the globe in a Mediterranean-like climate.

Free

Esplanade - Theatres on the Bay, Singapore

A premier arts center known for its unique architecture and diverse range of performances, from theater to concerts. The site forms part of Esplanade - Theatres on the Bay, Singapore's documented civic and cultural landscape within Singapore, with archives and visitor information available from municipal or regional heritage authorities.

Singapore Botanic Gardens

The Singapore Botanic Gardens, established in 1860 and listed as a UNESCO World Heritage site, spans 82 hectares of tropical paradise. It features lush lawns, ancient rainforests, tranquil lakes, and themed gardens. The National Orchid Garden within the premises showcases the world's largest collection of tropical orchids. For families, there's the Jacob Ballas Children's Garden with interactive features.

S$89

Universal Studios Singapore

Universal Studios Singapore, located on Sentosa Island, is a sprawling movie-themed amusement park that offers an array of attractions and entertainment for travellers of all ages. With seven distinct zones inspired by popular films and television shows, the park boasts thrilling rides such as Battlestar Galactica and Jurassic Park Rapids Adventure. Guests can also explore intricately designed streets resembling Paris and Hollywood, shop for souvenirs, or enjoy a quick meal between adventures.

S$50

Skyline Luge Singapore

An exciting adventure activity combining a thrilling luge ride and a scenic skyride. The site forms part of Skyline Luge Singapore's documented civic and cultural landscape within Singapore, with archives and visitor information available from municipal or regional heritage authorities.

Free

CHIJMES

CHIJMES, located on Victoria Street in the Downtown Core of Singapore, is a historical landmark that was once a Catholic convent and school. The 19th-century structure has been transformed into a vibrant lifestyle complex featuring restaurants, bars, event spaces, and shops. Its neoclassical architecture, sprawling lawns, courtyards, and marble waterfalls make it an enchanting destination for travellers.

Free

Cathedral of the Good Shepherd

The Cathedral of the Good Shepherd, constructed in 1847, is a significant complex featuring a crypt, adoration chapel, and heritage exhibit gallery. This Roman Catholic Church serves as the main Cathedral Church of the Archdiocese of Singapore. Its architectural style is influenced by Renaissance design and London churches such as St Paul's and St Martin-in-the-Fields.

S$15

Peranakan Museum

The Peranakan Museum, housed in a historic 1912 building, offers an immersive exploration of Peranakan culture through interactive and multimedia exhibits. It is also home to an authentic Straits cuisine restaurant led by Executive Chef Raymond Khoo, serving three generations of traditional Peranakan recipes. The museum showcases the rich heritage of the Peranakans with thematic galleries covering various aspects of their culture, from wedding ceremonies to crafts and feasting.

S$49

Singapore Zoo

Singapore Zoo is a lush rainforest zoo offering tram rides, walking trails, and viewing platforms to observe wildlife habitats and exhibits. The zoo provides an immersive experience for travellers to witness various animal species in naturalistic environments. Additionally, it hosts events such as urban lifestyle festivals featuring culinary delights, arts, entertainment, and live performances.

S$45

River Wonders

River Safari Singapore is a unique zoo and aquarium with a river theme, offering travellers the chance to see over 5,000 animals in an environment designed to resemble their natural habitats. The park features two main zones: Rivers of the World and Wild Amazonia, where guests can encounter animals from various rivers and the rainforest. One of the highlights is the world's largest freshwater aquarium, as well as a panda exhibit featuring Kai Kai and Jia Jia.

S$58

Night Safari

Night Safari, located in the Upper Seletar Reservoir, is the world's first nocturnal wildlife park. It offers tram rides and walking trails through a rainforest where travellers can spot creatures of the night such as elephants, tigers, and leopards. The park houses about 2500 animals that are observed after sunset. Guests can embark on guided walking tours or take a tram safari to get up close to these fascinating creatures in their natural habitat.

Free

Clarke Quay

A vibrant riverside quay known for its bustling nightlife, restaurants, and bars. Perfect for evening entertainment and dining. The site forms part of Clarke Quay's documented civic and cultural landscape within Singapore, with archives and visitor information available from municipal or regional heritage authorities.

Free

Old Hill Street Police Station

Old Hill Street Police Station, built in 1934, is a historic building with a vibrant facade featuring over 900 rainbow-hued louvered windows. Once a police barracks and now home to government ministries, the structure hosts various arts events in its main courtyard known as the ARTrium. The building's colorful exterior makes it an unmissable sight and a popular spot for travellers seeking unique photo opportunities.

Free

Lau Pa Sat

Lau Pa Sat, a beautifully restored Victorian hawker center located in Singapore's Central Business District, is an essential stop for anyone looking to indulge in the vibrant street food scene. This historical gem, originally built as a fish market in the early 19th century, showcases octagonal architecture and intricate cast-iron details that reflect its colonial past.

Free

Thian Hock Keng Temple

Thian Hock Keng Temple is a historical site that showcases classic Chinese architectural elements, including impressive columns and intricate dragon sculptures. This temple, dedicated to Mazu, the Goddess of the Sea, serves as a significant place of worship for various faiths in Singapore, blending Buddhism with Ancestral Worship and Confucianism. A highlight of your visit is the captivating mural by local artist Yip Yew Chong on its rear wall.

Free

Maxwell Food Centre

Famous for its diverse and delicious hawker food offerings, including the renowned Tian Tian Hainanese Chicken Rice. The site forms part of Maxwell Food Centre's documented civic and cultural landscape within Singapore, with archives and visitor information available from municipal or regional heritage authorities.

Free

Sri Mariamman Temple

Located in Chinatown, Singapore, Sri Mariamman Temple is the city's oldest Hindu temple, dating back to 1827. The temple is dedicated to the Hindu goddess Mariamman, known for curing illness and disease. Its ornate tower adorned with deities is a prominent feature of the temple. Recognized as a national monument, it offers travellers a glimpse into Singapore's rich cultural heritage and religious traditions.

Free

Sultan Mosque

Nestled in the vibrant Kampong Glam Malay Heritage District, the Sultan Mosque stands as a landmark and a significant Islamic house of worship. This architectural gem, originally established in 1825 with contributions from Raffles and the East India Company, showcases an enchanting Saracenic design crowned by a striking golden dome. The mosque's current structure was completed in 1928 under the guidance of an Irish architect, blending Indian and Islamic influences seamlessly.

Free

Church of Our Lady of Lourdes

The Church of Our Lady of Lourdes is a secular landmark in Singapore that welcomes people of all faiths. The church was built in tribute to the apparitions of the Virgin Mary at the French town of Lourdes, and features neo-Gothic architecture with lancet windows.

Free

Bugis Street

Bugis Street is a bustling shopping destination in Singapore, nestled between Little India and Marina Bay. With over 800 stalls, it's known as the cheapest market in the country for souvenirs, accessories, clothes, electronics, houseware, and cosmetics. Fashion enthusiasts will find an array of clothing, shoes, and accessories. The lively alleyways are filled with shops offering fashion jewelry, bags, and more.

S$35

Singapore Cable Car

The Singapore Cable Car is a popular attraction that has been drawing tourists since its opening in 1974. It connects Mount Faber with Sentosa Island, offering views of the city skyline, lush greenery, and sparkling waters along the almost 6-kilometer route. The ride provides an opportunity to admire the scenic beauty of Mount Faber, Sentosa Island, and Marina Bay from close to 100 meters above sea level.

Free

Southern Ridges

The Southern Ridges in Singapore is a picturesque 5.5-mile walking trail that connects several parks through a series of unique bridges, offering views of the city and the Southern Islands. The trail includes the impressive Henderson Waves pedestrian bridge and Alexandra Arch, as well as the lush greenery of Hort Park. Along the way, hikers can enjoy picnic areas, botanical gardens, and even a war museum.

Free

Gillman Barracks

Gillman Barracks, a former British Army base turned contemporary arts cluster, is a vibrant hub for art and culture in Singapore. The well-preserved colonial barracks now house international art galleries, eateries, and the NTU Centre for Contemporary Art. travellers can explore 13 galleries spread across five buildings on foot, taking in the blend of colonial architecture and modern art.

Free

Sri Veeramakaliamman Temple

Sri Veeramakaliamman Temple is a Hindu temple dedicated to the goddess and destroyer of evil. The elaborate architecture and colorful statues of deities make it a landmark in Little India, Singapore. travellers can explore the intricately detailed exterior and interior, but should remember to remove their shoes and dress respectfully. Surrounding the temple, there are specialty stores offering beautiful saris, spices, and accessories for those interested in shopping.

S$15

Indian Heritage Centre

The Singapore travellers Centre @Indian Heritage Centre is a must-visit destination for those interested in the rich Indian cultural heritage of Singapore. The center's four-story building seamlessly blends traditional Indian and modern architectural elements, creating a captivating visual experience. With five permanent galleries, a museum shop, and various programming spaces, travellers can explore the history and diversity of Singapore's Indian community.

Free

Fountain of Wealth

The Fountain of Wealth, located at the heart of Suntec City in Singapore, is a towering 13.8 meter-tall silicon bronze structure and one of the world's largest fountains. It offers nightly light shows with displays of lights. travellers can soak in its positive energy by walking around it three times clockwise, stretching their right hand over the water to make a wish. The fountain holds symbolic significance as a representation of life and wealth.

S$20

ION Sky

The ION Sky observation deck is located inside a skyscraper in Singapore and offers panoramic views of the city skyline. It's great for seeing all of the different areas of Singapore from one place, and it's also a great way to get some lunch with friends.

Free

Orchard Rd

Orchard Road in Singapore is a renowned 2.2-km stretch known for its abundance of shopping malls, boutiques, restaurants, and luxury hotels. Originally named after the fruit orchards and plantations that once occupied the area, it has evolved into a bustling shopping hub with over 5000 establishments. travellers can explore a wide range of options from newer arcades like ION Orchard and Somerset 313 to established favorites such as Tangs and Ngee Ann City.
