Explore Büyükada

A Brief History

Büyükada is the largest of the Princes' Islands in the Sea of Marmara near Istanbul and was known in antiquity as Prinkipo. Greek, Armenian, and Jewish communities built wooden villas and churches during Ottoman times. Motor vehicles are restricted; carriages and bicycles serve local transport. The islands served as places of exile for Byzantine royalty. Ferries connect the island to Istanbul's Kabataş and Kadıköy piers.

Greek Orthodox Monastery of St George Koudounas

Greek Orthodox Monastery of St George Koudounas is a visitor site in Büyükada, Turkey. The strenuous, rewarding climb rewards pilgrims with absolutely, panoramic views over the shimmering Sea of Marmara. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.
