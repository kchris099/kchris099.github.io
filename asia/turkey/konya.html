Explore Konya

A Brief History

Konya was the Seljuk capital in the twelfth and thirteenth centuries and home of the mystic poet Rumi. Alaeddin Mosque, Ince Minare Madrasa, and Mevlana Museum record how Persianate architecture and Sufi brotherhoods shaped central Anatolian urban life. Whirling dervish ceremonies and Seljuk tilework extend Rumi's legacy across central Anatolian bazaar streets.

Mevlana Museum

Mevlana Museum, also known as the Mausoleum of Rumi, is a museum in Konya, Turkey. The Mevlana Museum, also known as the Mausoleum of Rumi, is a significant tourist attraction in Konya. Visitors can explore ancient manuscripts, hand-written copies of the Quran, musical instruments from Rumi's time, and art pieces dating back to the Seljuk era. Galleries and public rooms are open on posted hours in Konya.

Free

Selimiye Mosque

The Selimiye Mosque is a evidence of Ottoman architectural brilliance and is a significant religious landmark in Konya. Constructed between 1558 and 1570 under the direction of Sultan Selim II, this mosque showcases the genius of known architect Mimar Sinan. Its dome, soaring minarets, and calligraphy reflect classic Ottoman design elements that draw visitors.

Free

Konya Tropical Butterfly Garden

The Konya Tropical Butterfly Garden is a destination featuring exotic plants, butterflies, and an insect museum. Situated adjacent to the Selcuklu Flower Garden and the Adventure Tower, this expansive urban park has a butterfly-shaped building with a glass roof covering 7,600 square meters.

Alaaddin Hill Park is a public park in Konya, Turkey. Visitors can explore the Alaaddin Mosque and remnants of an old palace while enjoying the lush surroundings. Paths, lawns, and shaded areas make up the main public facilities. Alaaddin Hill Park remains part of the public visitor circuit in Konya.

Alaaddin Keykubad Mosque

The Alaaddin Keykubad Mosque is a grand 13th-century mosque located in Konya's citadel, featuring dynastic tombs and an adjacent flower garden. The mosque has a ebony pulpit and delicate prayer-niche dating back to 1155, with the complex being completed in 1221. It offers a peaceful atmosphere and interesting history, situated in a park that is perfect for leisurely walks.

Iplikci Mosque

Iplikci Mosque is a religious building in Konya, Turkey. Iplikci Mosque, an ancient mosque dating back to the 13th century, underwent restoration around 50 years ago and continues to be a place of worship. The mosque is one of the oldest in Turkey, built during the time of the Seljuks who introduced Turkic culture and Islam to Anatolia. The exterior and main interior spaces are usually viewable outside service times.

Free

Sırçalı Medrese

Located Konya, Turkey, Sırçalı Medrese is a historical Islamic school that dates back to the 13th century. Constructed in 1242 under the Seljuk Sultan Kaykaus II, this architectural gem showcases glazed brick and tile decorations that reflect the artistry of its time. Originally founded by Bedreddin Muslih, it served as a center for learning Hanafi fiqh and tafsir during both Seljuk and Ottoman periods.

Free

The Atatürk Monument, erected in 1926, commemorates Turkey's first president and is situated on an stone platform. Its beauty and significance make it a cool and lovely attraction that shouldn't be missed. The memorial stands in an open plaza or roadside setting.

KONYA MERAM BAĞLARI is a visitor site in Konya, Turkey. Konya Meram Baglari is a top tourist spot in Konya, offering families a chance to enjoy the outdoors. Around the World in 80 Days Park is a public park in Konya, Turkey. It offers something for everyone of all ages, including miniature mosques, life-sized superhero figures like Hulk and Superman, and a cafeteria with affordable prices. At just 10TL (70 euro cents) for entrance fee, it's an excellent value for money. Paths, lawns, and shaded areas make up the main public facilities.
