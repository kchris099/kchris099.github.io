Konya

Konya was the Seljuk capital in the twelfth and thirteenth centuries and home of the mystic poet Rumi. Alaeddin Mosque, Ince Minare Madrasa, and Mevlana Museum mark Persianate architecture and Sufi brotherhoods in central Anatolian urban life. Whirling dervish ceremonies and Seljuk tilework still extend Rumi's legacy across central Anatolian bazaar streets.

Mevlana Museum

The Mevlana Museum occupies the former lodge of the Mevlevi order around the turquoise-tiled mausoleum of the poet and mystic Jalal al-Din Rumi; the compound remains a place of reverence as well as a state museum, especially during commemorative periods. Tombs, manuscripts, musical instruments, dervish cells, textiles, and ritual objects explain the community that developed after his death.

Selimiye Mosque

Konya's Selimiye Mosque was built between 1558 and 1570 under Sultan Selim II beside the Mevlana complex. A large dome, minarets, and calligraphy organize the classical Ottoman prayer hall. The mosque faces the same square as the Mevlana Museum for combined visits.

37.8705° N / 32.5040° E

03 AdmissionFree

Azizia Mosque

Azizia Mosque in Konya mixes Ottoman planning with later baroque detailing on its facade and interior fittings. Building work began in 1676, and the mosque still holds regular prayer. Outside service hours the courtyard and main hall are often open for quiet viewing of the prayer space and decoration.

37.8700° N / 32.5009° E

04 AdmissionFree

Kapu Mosque

Historic Kapu Mosque in Konya keeps exterior courts and main prayer halls usually viewable outside services, remaining part of the city's active mosque network near Aziziye. Worship continues in the older fabric rather than a sealed museum conversion. Stone and timber details reward a quiet look between prayer times.

37.8699° N / 32.4993° E

Konya Büyükşehir Arena

National and international football matches fill Konya Büyükşehir Arena, a medium-capacity stadium with parking, restaurants, and a gym overlooking the pitch. Posted ticket and gate procedures control event entry. Match days define the venue's public use.

Konya Tropical Butterfly Garden

The Konya Tropical Butterfly Garden is a destination featuring exotic plants, butterflies, and an insect museum. Situated adjacent to the Selcuklu Flower Garden and the Adventure Tower, this expansive urban park has a butterfly-shaped building with a glass roof covering 7,600 square meters.

07 AdmissionFree

Alaaddin Hill Park

Alaaddin Hill rises above central Konya as a landscaped park around the Seljuk-era Alaaddin Mosque and the remaining foundations of an old palace. Tree-lined paths and lawns climb the mound, giving views over the surrounding streets. The hill has long been a civic gathering ground as well as a place of worship.

37.8725° N / 32.4923° E

08 AdmissionFree

Alaaddin Keykubad Mosque

Alaeddin Mosque crowns Konya's central hill within the former Seljuk citadel. Construction and enlargement under several sultans produced a broad hypostyle prayer hall, carved wooden pulpit, reused columns, tilework, and dynastic tombs. The building's irregular form reflects successive campaigns.

37.8733° N / 32.4929° E

09 AdmissionFree

Iplikci Mosque

Thirteenth-century Seljuk builders raised İplikçi Mosque, restored about fifty years ago and still used for prayer among Anatolia's older mosques. Exterior and main interiors can usually be seen outside services. Seljuk brickwork marks early Turkic Islamic presence in Konya.

37.8719° N / 32.4967° E

10 AdmissionFree

Sırçalı Medrese

Sircali Medrese was founded in the thirteenth century as a Seljuk theological school, its name referring to the glazed tile decoration around the monumental entrance and interior. A courtyard, student cells, carved stone, inscriptions, and surviving blue tilework reveal the institution's educational function. Later restoration and museum use changed the rooms, but the portal remains its clearest architectural feature.

37.8700° N / 32.4940° E

11 AdmissionFree

Atatürk Monument

Konya's Ataturk Monument depicts Mustafa Kemal Ataturk in military dress on a high stone base within a civic square. Erected during the early republic, it formed part of a nationwide program that used public sculpture to establish new political symbols after the Ottoman period. Traffic and official ceremonies now surround the open memorial.

37.8676° N / 32.4853° E

12 Admission₺50

KONYA MERAM BAĞLARI

Meram Bağları is a municipal green belt of orchards, paths, and picnic grounds on Konya's edge, revived from neglected vineyard land into a family recreation area. Weekends bring denser crowds for walks and outdoor meals under trees. The setting favors slow outdoor time rather than a single monument or museum visit.

37.8527° N / 32.4202° E

13 AdmissionFree

Around the World in 80 Days Park

Around the World in 80 Days Park in Konya's Meram district is a themed public park with miniature landmark models, character statues, and shaded lawns. Paths wind past playful sets rather than historic monuments. Families use it as a local green space on the city's western side.
