Explore Ani

A Brief History

Ani is a ruined medieval city situated on the current border between Turkey and Armenia. During the tenth and eleventh centuries, it was the capital of the Bagratid Kingdom of Armenia and a major regional center for trade along the Silk Road. Known historically as the City of 1001 Churches, Ani experienced a period of great prosperity before entering a decline due to successive invasions by the Byzantines, Seljuks, and Mongols, as well as a devastating earthquake in the fourteenth century. The site was eventually abandoned entirely.
