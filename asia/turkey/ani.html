Ani

Ani is a ruined medieval city situated on the current border between Turkey and Armenia. During the tenth and eleventh centuries, it was the capital of the Bagratid Kingdom of Armenia and a major regional center for trade along the Silk Road. Known historically as the City of 1001 Churches, Ani experienced a period of great prosperity before entering a decline due to successive invasions by the Byzantines, Seljuks, and Mongols, as well as a devastating earthquake in the fourteenth century. The site was eventually abandoned entirely.

Ani Ruins

Across a windswept plateau beside the Armenian border, Ani preserves the remains of a medieval city that flourished under the Bagratid kingdom and later rulers. Churches, mosques, walls, gates, houses and caravan buildings stand amid grassland cut by deep river gorges. The ruins reveal several architectural traditions and repeated episodes of conquest, earthquake damage, abandonment and excavation.

Ani Cathedral

Ani Cathedral was commissioned by the Bagratid king Smbat II and completed in the early eleventh century under Queen Katranide, with the architect Trdat credited for its design. Tall pointed arches, clustered piers, and a long stone nave survive despite the collapse of the dome. Later use as a mosque and centuries of earthquake damage form part of the building's layered history.
