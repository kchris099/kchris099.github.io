Explore Istanbul

A Brief History

Istanbul is a spectacularly vibrant, deeply historic metropolis that uniquely spans two continents, beautifully straddling the sparkling Bosphorus Strait. Historically serving as the incredibly grand capital of both the Byzantine and Ottoman empires, the city is masterfully layered with breathtaking mosques, massive palaces, and incredibly energetic bazaars. It perfectly offers a deeply intoxicating, sensory-rich blend of Eastern and Western cultures.

Top Sights & Activities

The Blue Mosque

Constructed between 1609 and 1616 by Sultan Ahmed I, this imperial mosque is celebrated for its historic six minarets and intricate Iznik tile interior.

Hagia Sophia

Originally built as a Byzantine cathedral in 537 AD, later converted to a mosque and a museum, it remains a monument of immense architectural and religious history.

Free

Gulhane Park

Once the outer gardens of the Topkapi Palace, this historical urban park was opened to the public in 1912, retaining centuries-old trees and pathways.

1,500 TL

Topkapi Palace Museum

The primary residence and administrative headquarters of the Ottoman sultans from the 15th to the 19th centuries, now preserving imperial artifacts.

Free

Grand Bazaar

One of the oldest covered markets in the world, initiated by Mehmed the Conqueror in 1455 to stimulate the economic life of the newly conquered city.

Free

Süleymaniye Mosque

An Ottoman imperial mosque commissioned by Suleiman the Magnificent in 1550, blending Islamic and Byzantine architectural elements on the city's Third Hill.

Free

Egyptian Bazaar

Also known as the Spice Bazaar, it was built in 1660 with revenues from the Ottoman eyalet of Egypt, historically serving as the center of the spice trade.

Free

Galata Bridge

Spanning the Golden Horn, this bridge has historically connected the traditional city of Istanbul with the districts of Galata, serving as a vital urban artery.

Free

Salt Galata

Housed in the former 19th-century Imperial Ottoman Bank headquarters, this institution now functions as a modern cultural and research center.

Free

Kamondo Stairs

An elegant curvilinear staircase built in the 1870s by a prominent Sephardic Jewish banking family to connect the banking district with their residential neighborhood.

€30

Galata Tower

Originally built as a watchtower by the Genoese in 1348, this stone structure has functioned as a defense tower, prison, and fire observatory throughout its history.

Free

Taksim Mosque

A contemporary mosque completed in 2021, designed in an Art Deco style with Ottoman influences, situated prominently in the secular center of Taksim Square.

Free

Taksim Square

The modern heart of Istanbul, historically functioning as the point where the main water lines from the north were collected and branched off to other parts of the city.

2,000 TL

Dolmabahçe Palace

Built in the mid-19th century, this European-style palace served as the main administrative center of the Ottoman Empire during its final decades.

Free

Venerable Patriarchal Church of St. George

Since the 17th century, this neoclassical church has served as the seat of the Ecumenical Patriarchate of Constantinople, the center of the Eastern Orthodox Church.

Free

Saint Stephen's Bulgarian Orthodox Church

Erected in 1898, this church is notable for being constructed entirely of prefabricated cast iron shipped via the Danube from Vienna.

Free

Grand Çamlıca Mosque

Completed in 2019, it is the largest mosque in Turkey, combining Ottoman and Seljuk architectural styles as a modern symbol of the nation's Islamic heritage.

Free

Çamlıca Hill

One of the highest natural points in Istanbul, historically utilized for military observatories and now offering a vantage point of the Bosphorus and the city.
