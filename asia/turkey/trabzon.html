Trabzon

Trabzon was founded by Greek colonists in the eighth century BC and served as an important port on the Silk Road. It became the capital of the Empire of Trebizond after the Fourth Crusade, maintaining its Byzantine Greek identity until it fell to the Ottomans in 1461. The city's history is reflected in its medieval walls, the Hagia Sophia church, and the nearby Sumela Monastery. Today, Trabzon is a major cultural and commercial center on Turkey's Black Sea coast.

Forum Trabzon Shopping Center

Forum Trabzon Shopping Center is a modern retail hub offering a wide range of local and international brands, along with a food court and cinema for entertainment. It's an ideal spot to unwind after exploring the city's historical sites or natural wonders like Uzungol and various plateaus. The mall, which opened in 2008, is recommended for its diverse shopping options and leisure activities.

Trabzon Square Park

City-center lawns, benches, and landmark statues define Trabzon Square Park as a daily gathering green. Landscaped paths link shaded seating to surrounding cafes, restaurants, and bars on the adjoining blocks. Locals and travellers use the lawns for short rests between errands across the downtown grid.

Meydan

Trabzon's Meydan is the city's main civic square, a paved gathering space ringed by shops, civic buildings, and transit stops. Locals use it as a meeting point and evening promenade, while travellers pass through on the way to the harbor and hillside districts. The open center hosts seasonal events and everyday foot traffic alike.

Fatih Parkı

Fatih Park occupies a terraced slope near central Trabzon, with paths, trees, seating, tea facilities and views across surrounding neighborhoods. The landscaped public space offers a pause between busy roads and nearby historic sites. The park offers a peaceful, green retreat from the busy city streets, featuring shaded walking paths, seating areas, and a traditional tea garden.

07 AdmissionFree

Gulbahar Hatun Mosque

Gulbahar Hatun Mosque was built in the early sixteenth century as part of a charitable complex honoring the mother of Sultan Selim I, and much of the original complex has disappeared, but the mosque is an active religious landmark beside Trabzon's Atapark. Alternating light and dark stone, a domed prayer hall, portico, and nearby tomb distinguish the surviving buildings.

Boztepe Volkan Konak Viewing Terrace is a commercial lookout and hospitality venue on the heights above Trabzon. Seating, food service and framed platforms face the city, harbor and Black Sea, but the experience depends on business hours, weather and private management. It is distinct from the broader public viewpoints and tea gardens spread across Boztepe hill.

Boztepe Observation Deck

Boztepe's public observation areas occupy a ridge south of central Trabzon. Tea gardens, cafes and scattered terraces look across the harbor, coastline and Black Sea from slightly different angles. Roads, paths and a cable-car connection bring visitors to the elevated park, where weather and haze continually change the coastal panorama.

Trabzon Hagia Sophia Mosque

Hagia Sophia Mosque, located in the city of Trabzon, is a late-Byzantine mosque with a central dome and well-preserved frescoes that reflect its history as an Orthodox church. The structure dates back to the Byzantine era and has a glimpse into its rich past. The Ataturk Mansion, situated on the ridges of Soguksu amidst pine forests, provides insight into the life and legacy of Turkey's founding father.
