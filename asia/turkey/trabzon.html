Explore Trabzon

A Brief History

Trabzon was founded by Greek colonists in the eighth century BC and served as a crucial port on the Silk Road. It became the capital of the Empire of Trebizond after the Fourth Crusade, maintaining its Byzantine Greek identity until it fell to the Ottomans in 1461. The city's history is reflected in its medieval walls, the Hagia Sophia church, and the nearby Sumela Monastery. Today, Trabzon is a major cultural and commercial center on Turkey's Black Sea coast.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

Forum Trabzon Shopping Center

Forum Trabzon Shopping Center is a modern retail hub offering a wide range of local and international brands, along with a food court and cinema for entertainment. It's an ideal spot to unwind after exploring the city's historical sites or natural wonders like Uzungol and various plateaus. The mall, which opened in 2008, is recommended for its diverse shopping options and leisure activities.

Free

Trabzon Square Park

Trabzon Square Park, located in the heart of the city center, is a recreational green space featuring beautifully landscaped grounds, benches, and landmark statues. Visitors can enjoy the serene atmosphere and admire decorative water fountains while taking in the marvelous architecture of surrounding buildings. The park offers a peaceful setting for both tourists and locals to socialize, with plenty of cafes, restaurants, and bars nearby.

Free

Meydan

Meydan Trabzon (Atatürk Square) is the bustling, central public square of Trabzon. It is surrounded by numerous cafes, restaurants, and shops, and features a prominent statue of Mustafa Kemal Atatürk. The square serves as the primary social and commercial hub of the city, offering a lively atmosphere and a starting point for exploring the surrounding historic streets.

Free

Fatih Parkı

Fatih Park is a well-maintained, terraced public park located on a hillside in the center of Trabzon. The park offers a peaceful, green retreat from the busy city streets, featuring shaded walking paths, seating areas, and a traditional tea garden. Its elevated position provides pleasant, partial views of the city and the Black Sea.

Free

Trabzon Botanical Garden

Trabzon Botanical Garden is a well-maintained and safe spot showcasing a wide variety of plants, fruits, vegetables, and herbs. It offers vivid colors that catch the eye while strolling through the garden. The center features fresh flowers, small enclosures for deer, beautiful fountains, ponds, and even animals like fishes, rabbits, chickens, and deer. It's an amazing place to visit in spring to see tulip flowers and interact with various animals.

Free

Trabzon Aquarium

The Trabzon Aquarium is a modern marine attraction located in the Zağnos Valley. It features a diverse collection of aquatic life from the Black Sea and global oceans, housed in various themed tanks, including a large shark tunnel. The facility provides an engaging, educational experience for families and highlights regional marine biodiversity.

Free

Gulbahar Hatun Mosque

Gulbahar Hatun Mosque, located near Kanuni Evi in Atapark, is a historic site that was originally part of a complex but now only the mosque and mausoleum remain. The mosque is named after Gulbahar Hatun, the mother of Ottoman conqueror Yavuz Sultan Selim, and was built in 1514 AD. It is known for its unique design made from a combination of black and white stone.

Free

Boztepe Volkan Konak Seyir Terası

Boztepe Volkan Konak Seyir Terası sits within the Boztepe Volkan Konak Seyir Terası area of Trabzon, documented in local heritage registers for its civic or cultural role. Visitors approach from the main access road and combine the visit with neighbouring heritage buildings and public spaces in the district.

Free

Boztepe Observation Deck

The Boztepe Observation Deck is situated on a prominent hill overlooking Trabzon. Accessible by road or a scenic cable car, the elevated park offers sweeping, panoramic views of the entire city, the bustling harbor, and the expansive Black Sea. It is a very popular destination, especially at sunset, featuring numerous traditional tea gardens.

Free

Trabzon Hagia Sophia Mosque

Hagia Sophia Mosque, located in the city of Trabzon, is a late-Byzantine mosque with a central dome and well-preserved frescoes that reflect its history as an Orthodox church. The structure dates back to the Byzantine era and offers visitors a glimpse into its rich past. The Ataturk Mansion, situated on the ridges of Soguksu amidst pine forests, provides insight into the life and legacy of Turkey's founding father.
