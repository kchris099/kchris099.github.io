Ephesus

Ephesus was a major Ionian Greek city whose Artemis temple and later Roman harbour made it capital of Asia province, drawing colonnaded avenues, the Library of Celsus, and an amphitheatre that could host political assembly. Early Christian councils met here, and tradition places the Virgin Mary's house nearby on Bulbuldag. Silting of the Cayster River shifted settlement toward Selçuk; extensive ruins still record classical urban life on the Aegean coast.
