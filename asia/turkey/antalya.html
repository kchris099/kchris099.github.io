Explore Antalya

A Brief History

Founded in the Hellenistic period and later expanded by the Romans, Antalya served as a critical Mediterranean naval base for the Byzantines and Seljuks. The beautifully preserved Kaleiçi district highlights a long legacy of maritime dominance before the modern tourism boom. Museums, markets, and street plans in Antalya still show layers from those periods.

Hadrian's Gate

Hadrian's Gate is a significant historical site in Antalya, Turkey. It is a Roman triumphal arch built in 130 AD to commemorate Emperor Hadrian's visit to Attaleia. The gate consists of three identical marble, granite, and stone archways standing 8m tall and is considered the most in Pamphylia. It bears marks and additions from various civilizations that followed Hadrian. The two towers flanking the entrance are from different time periods.

Antalya Clock Tower

The Antalya Clock Tower is a historic stone landmark dating back to the 9th century, featuring a pentagonal base and a crenellated tower with four clock faces. It is the only remaining tower out of 80 columns that once formed Antalya's ancient stone wall. Built in 1909 AD by orders of the Ottoman Sultan Abdul Hamid II, this structure is a focal point in Antalya's Old Town.

Perge Ancient City

Perge Ancient City, located just 18km north of Antalya, is a significant site in ancient Pamphylia. It has a rich history dating back to the Hittites around 1500 BCE and was inhabited continuously until the Byzantine era. The city was ruled by various powers including the Persians, Athenians, Alexander the Great, and the Romans.

Duden Waterfalls

Duden Waterfalls is a natural formation located in a park, featuring upper waterfalls of a partly underground river that cascade in front of a cave. The site offers an opportunity for outdoor adventures and sightseeing, making it an ideal destination for nature enthusiasts and history buffs alike. With its surroundings and historical significance, Duden Waterfalls is a must-visit attraction on the Mediterranean coast of Turkey.

Lower Duden Waterfalls

Located on the eastern outskirts of Antalya, the Lower Duden Waterfalls are a natural formation that gracefully tumble into the Mediterranean Sea. Originating from the Duden River, which flows down from the Taurus Mountains, these waterfalls create a spectacle as they cascade over cliffs surrounded by lush greenery. This destination is particularly enchanting in spring when water flow is at its peak, but it remains a sight year-round.

Hidirlik Tower

Hidirlik Tower, a round stone structure dating back to the 2nd century CE, stands on the western edge of Antalya's old town amid the Karaalioglu park. Believed to have been part of the city's ancient fortifications, its original purpose remains a mystery, with theories ranging from a mausoleum to a watchtower or lighthouse for the busy port below.

Antalya Aquarium

Antalya Aquarium is a popular attraction near Konyaalti Beach, offering themed exhibits, a long tunnel tank, and tropical reptiles. It's known for being the largest aquarium tunnel in the world, making it a must-visit spot for families with children. Visitors can enjoy a leisurely stroll while marveling at an collection of marine life from various seas and oceans, including sharks and other aquatic animals like Sting Rays, Red Dragon Fish, and California Moray.
