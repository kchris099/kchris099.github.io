Antalya

Attalos II founded the harbour city; Rome, Byzantium, and the Seljuks each fortified the cliffs above the Mediterranean. Kaleiçi's tangled lanes are the pre-tourism core; modern resorts spread along the beaches beyond the old walls. Hadrian's Gate is the ceremonial Roman entry still used to walk into Kaleiçi. Seljuk-era Yivli Minare marks the medieval Islamic skyline above the harbour.

Hadrian's Gate

In 130 AD Romans raised Hadrian's Gate to mark the emperor's visit to Attaleia: three matching marble, granite, and stone arches about eight meters high, with later additions and flanking towers from different periods. The triple arch still opens into Kaleiçi. Layered repairs show later civilizations' marks.

02 AdmissionFree

Antalya Clock Tower

Standing beside the entrance to Kaleici, Antalya's Clock Tower incorporates an older stone bastion from the city's fortifications with a clock added during the late Ottoman period. Its square upper stage, crenellated masonry, and visible clock faces mark the transition between the modern commercial streets and the old town. Conflicting dates in popular accounts reflect the difference between the ancient base and later clock installation.

36.8869° N / 30.7059° E

03 AdmissionFree

Yivli Minare Mosque

The fluted brick minaret of Yivli Minare Mosque has marked Antalya's skyline since the Seljuk period. The surrounding religious complex was rebuilt and altered over several centuries, and the present multi-domed prayer hall does not preserve one untouched thirteenth-century phase. Courtyards, stonework, tile fragments, and nearby tombs connect the mosque to the city's medieval Muslim administration and continuing worship.

36.8866° N / 30.7045° E

Old Town Bazaar Antalya

Stalls and small shops fill the lanes between Antalya's old city gates, harbor streets and residential quarters, forming what is often marketed as the Old Town Bazaar. Textiles, spices, leather goods, ceramics, food and souvenirs vary by street and season. The area is a working commercial district within Kaleici.

Republic Square

Open pavement and surrounding facades define Antalya's Republic Square as a central civic clearing for rest and gathering near Kaleici approaches. The paved room belongs to this Mediterranean city's core rather than a park borrowed from another capital. Facades and paving set a simple public meeting ground.

Murat Pasha Mosque

Ottoman builders completed Murat Pasha Mosque around 1570 with three domes, calligraphy, ceiling tile work, and an orange tree in the courtyard. Exterior and main halls can usually be seen outside prayer times. Garden citrus and tiled interiors share the compound.

Perge Ancient City

About eighteen kilometers north of Antalya, Perge in Pamphylia holds remains from Hittite roots around 1500 BCE through continuous settlement into the Byzantine era under Persians, Athenians, Alexander, and Rome. Ruined streets and monuments spread across the archaeological field. Successive empires left layered stone.

Duden Waterfalls

Upper falls of a partly underground river drop before a cave at Düden Waterfalls inside a park setting on Turkey's Mediterranean coast. Paths support outdoor walking around the cascade. Water, cave mouth, and planted grounds form the visit rather than a dry overlook alone.

Mall of Antalya

Mall of Antalya stands near the airport as a large contemporary shopping center linked with retail stores, restaurants, a supermarket, cinemas and indoor entertainment. Broad enclosed corridors organize the complex around everyday shopping and leisure. Its location near major roads makes it a practical commercial stop for surrounding neighborhoods and travelers passing through the eastern side of the city.

10 AdmissionFree

Lower Duden Waterfalls

Located on the eastern outskirts of Antalya, the Lower Duden Waterfalls are a natural formation that gracefully tumble into the Mediterranean Sea. Originating from the Duden River, which flows down from the Taurus Mountains, these waterfalls create a spectacle as they cascade over cliffs surrounded by lush greenery. This destination is particularly enchanting in spring when water flow is at its peak, but it remains a sight year-round.

11 AdmissionFree

Hidirlik Tower

Hidirlik Tower stands where Antalya's old walls meet Karaalioglu Park above the Mediterranean cliffs. Built in the Roman period, the round upper structure rises from a square base, but its original purpose remains debated; proposals include a monumental tomb, lighthouse, or defensive lookout. Later repairs and its position over the harbor explain why the tower became a familiar city landmark.

36.8813° N / 30.7036° E

Karaalioglu Park

Sea views and Roman tower ruins share Karaalioglu Park, an urban green where Antalya locals seek cooler air on hot summer days along the cliff edge. Paths, lawns, and shade trees make up the main public facilities. Coastline outlook and archaeology sit together on the same park ground.

36.8796° N / 30.7049° E

Antalya Zoo

Antalya Zoo holds regional and exotic animals in hillside enclosures on the city's outskirts, with paths linking aviaries, mammal houses, and shaded rest areas. Educational signage and family-oriented routes structure a typical visit. The park sits away from the old harbor, so travellers usually plan a separate trip from Kaleiçi sightseeing.

Antalya Aquarium

Near Konyaalti Beach, Antalya Aquarium displays marine and freshwater species in themed tanks connected by a long underwater tunnel. Sharks, rays, reef fish and reptiles form the core collection, while other commercial attractions share the complex; the transparent passage beneath the main tanks remains its most recognizable architectural element.

Aktur Park

Pay-per-ride roller coasters, children's rides, carnival games, and go-karts fill Aktur Park in Antalya, with queues often short on many attractions. Food, drinks, and toilets are separate purchases from ride access. The amusement ground suits mixed ages for a full day of rides.

16 AdmissionFree

Konyaaltı Varyant Büyük Seyir Terası - Panaromic View

Konyaalti Varyant's broad observation terrace overlooks the curving pebble beach, Mediterranean water and the Beydaglari mountains west of Antalya; the platform sits beside the road descending from the city center to the coast, with railings and open paved space for short stops. Haze, traffic and afternoon light change the view, while the site functions primarily as a roadside public lookout.
