Hadrian's Gate

Hadrian's Gate is a significant historical site in Antalya, Turkey. It is a Roman triumphal arch built in 130 AD to commemorate Emperor Hadrian's visit to Attaleia. The gate consists of three identical marble, granite, and stone archways standing 8m tall and is considered the most in Pamphylia. It bears marks and additions from various civilizations that followed Hadrian. The two towers flanking the entrance are from different time periods.

Antalya Clock Tower

The Antalya Clock Tower is a historic stone landmark dating back to the 9th century, featuring a pentagonal base and a crenellated tower with four clock faces. It is the only remaining tower out of 80 columns that once formed Antalya's ancient stone wall. Built in 1909 AD by orders of the Ottoman Sultan Abdul Hamid II, this structure is a focal point in Antalya's Old Town.
