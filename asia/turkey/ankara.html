Explore Ankara

A Brief History

An ancient Galatian and Roman stronghold, Ankara was a quiet provincial town until selected by Mustafa Kemal Atatürk in 1920 to head the independence movement. Its sweeping modernist boulevards reflect its rapid, deliberate change into the administrative the Turkish Republic. Museums, markets, and street plans in Ankara still show layers from those periods.

Haci Bayram Mosque

Haci Bayram Mosque is a church in Ankara, Turkey. Haci Bayram Mosque is a religious building in Ankara, Turkey. The Haci Bayram Mosque, constructed in the 15th century by Architect Mehmet Bey, is a significant Ottoman-era landmark located in Ulus. Named after the prominent religious scholar Haci Bayram Veli, it reflects a blend of Seljuk and early Ottoman architectural styles with detailed tile work and ornate calligraphy.

Ataturk Statue

Ataturk Statue is a monument in Ankara, Turkey. Construction and early use date to around 1927. The Ataturk Statue, built in 1927 as a memorial for the Turkish War of Independence, has bronze figures including the prominent leader riding on horseback. The monument is a symbol of Turkey's struggle for independence and honors those who fought for their nation's freedom. The memorial stands in an open plaza or roadside setting.

Suluhan Bazaar

Suluhan Bazaar, also known as Hasan Pasa Han, is a market in Ankara, Turkey. Suluhan Bazaar, also known as Hasan Pasa Han, is a historical caravanserai dating back to the 16th century. Once a hub for coffee and yarn trade in the Ottoman period, it now houses cafes and souvenir shops. Located in Ulus, this traditional Turkish market offers a variety of fresh produce, meats, fish, and household items at reasonable prices. Stalls sell food and goods during regular market hours.

Anıtkabir

Anıtkabir is a visitor site in Ankara, Turkey. Anıtkabir in Ankara is a grand mausoleum and museum dedicated to Kemal Ataturk, the founding father and first president of the Republic of Turkey. It also is the final resting place for Ismet Inonu, Turkey's second president. The Ataturk Museum within Anıtkabir displays his personal items, medals, wardrobe, and gifts received during his lifetime. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Ankara Castle

Ankara Castle, an ancient fortress perched on a rocky hill, provides panoramic views of the city. With its strong fortifications and detailed architecture, this historic site has a glimpse into Turkey's past. The castle has been used by various civilizations for defense and settlement over its 4,000-year history and has undergone numerous repairs.

Kocatepe Mosque

Kocatepe Mosque is a religious building in Ankara, Turkey. It opened or was completed around 1967. Kocatepe Mosque, a example of neoclassical Ottoman architecture, is the largest mosque in Ankara and one of Turkey's most religious structures. METU Forest

The METU (Middle East Technical University) Forest is a vast, semi-wild woodland area located on the university's campus in Ankara. Planted largely by students and faculty over several decades, it is a important 'green lung' for the city. The forest offers extensive, unpaved trails popular with hikers, runners, and mountain bikers seeking a natural escape.

Korean War Memorial

Korean War Memorial is a monument in Ankara, Turkey. Records associated with the site include 1950, 1953, 1953. The Korean War Memorial in Ankara is a heartfelt tribute to the Turkish soldiers who lost their lives during the Korean War from 1950 to 1953. Established in 1953 and renovated in 2010, this modern architectural gem invites visitors to reflect on bravery and sacrifice. The memorial stands in an open plaza or roadside setting.
