Explore Ankara

A Brief History

An ancient Galatian and Roman stronghold, Ankara was a quiet provincial town until selected by Mustafa Kemal Atatürk in 1920 to head the independence movement. Its sweeping modernist boulevards reflect its rapid, deliberate transformation into the administrative heart of the Turkish Republic. Decades of careful preservation have ensured that this profound historical narrative remains a vital part of the modern experience.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

Haci Bayram Mosque

The Haci Bayram Mosque, constructed in the 15th century by Architect Mehmet Bey, is a significant Ottoman-era landmark located in Ulus. The mosque features brick outer walls on stone and is accompanied by the Haci Bayram-i Veli Tomb. Named after the prominent religious scholar Haci Bayram Veli, it reflects a blend of Seljuk and early Ottoman architectural styles with intricate tile work and ornate calligraphy.

Free

Ataturk Statue

The Ataturk Statue, built in 1927 as a memorial for the Turkish War of Independence, features bronze figures including the prominent leader riding on horseback. The monument serves as a symbol of Turkey's struggle for independence and honors those who fought for their nation's freedom. The statue is an impressive piece of art that stands tall and proud, representing the strength and resilience of the Turkish people during difficult times.

Free

Suluhan Bazaar

Suluhan Bazaar, also known as Hasan Pasa Han, is a historical caravanserai dating back to the 16th century. Once a hub for coffee and yarn trade in the Ottoman period, it now houses cafes and souvenir shops. Located in Ulus, this traditional Turkish market offers a variety of fresh produce, meats, fish, and household items at reasonable prices. The bustling marketplace features stores selling goods in a traditional manner with negotiable prices and no price tags.

Free

Anıtkabir

Anıtkabir in Ankara is a grand mausoleum and museum dedicated to Kemal Ataturk, the founding father and first president of the Republic of Turkey. It also serves as the final resting place for Ismet Inonu, Turkey's second president. The site features a marble hall, turrets, murals, and art galleries. The Ataturk Museum within Anıtkabir displays his personal items, medals, wardrobe, and gifts received during his lifetime.

Free

Ankara Castle

Ankara Castle, an ancient fortress perched on a rocky hill, provides panoramic views of the city. With its strong fortifications and intricate architecture, this historic site offers visitors a glimpse into Turkey's past. The castle has been used by various civilizations for defense and settlement over its 4,000-year history and has undergone numerous repairs.

Free

Aslanhane Mosque

The Aslanhane Mosque is an imposing, historical mosque in Ankara. It was built by the Seljuk Turks in the 12th century, and features inner architecture. The mosque is open to tourists 24 hours a day, and features a section for women prayers at the upper stage. The toilets are nicely clean, but you need to pay for using them. The carpets are very nice and coincide with the style of the mosque - worth visiting if you're ever in Ankara!.

Free

Kuğulu Park

Kuğulu Park, also known as Swan Park, is a popular urban oasis located in the heart of Ankara's bustling Tunali Hilmi shopping street. The park features picturesque walking paths, a children's playground, and a serene pond inhabited by swans, geese, and ducks. Visitors can relax on park benches and immerse themselves in the city's daily life before exploring nearby shops and cafes. At night, the park transforms with illuminated fountains and bridges creating a captivating ambiance.

Free

Seğmenler Parkı

Seğmenler Park is a large, popular public park located in the Çankaya district of Ankara. Situated in a small valley, the park features extensive green lawns, mature trees, walking paths, and a small amphitheater. It is a favored gathering place for young people, families, and dog walkers, offering a vibrant, relaxed atmosphere in the heart of the capital.

Free

Kocatepe Mosque

Kocatepe Mosque, a example of neoclassical Ottoman architecture, stands as the largest mosque in Ankara and one of Turkey's most impressive religious structures. Completed between 1967 and 1987, it boasts an expansive area of 4,500 square meters that can accommodate over 24,000 worshippers. Perched on Kocatepe Hill, this architectural marvel features four soaring minarets and a grand central dome that captures the eye from afar.

Free

Ahmet Hamdi Akseki Mosque

The Ahmet Hamdi Akseki Mosque, opened in 2013, is a contemporary marvel located in Ankara, Turkey. This mosque stands out with its modern architecture and impressive features. It boasts slender minarets and is adorned with elegant marble and intricate calligraphy. The neoclassical design incorporates ancient Islamic elements, creating a unique blend of tradition and modernity.

Free

Şehit Cuma Dağ Tabiat Tarihi Müzesi

Şehit Cuma Dağ Tabiat Tarihi Müzesi sits within the Ahmet Hamdi Akseki Mosque area of Ankara, documented in local heritage registers for its civic or cultural role. Visitors approach from the main access road and combine the visit with neighbouring heritage buildings and public spaces in the district.

Free

Altınköy Açık Hava Müzesi

The Altınköy Open Air Museum in Ankara is a meticulously reconstructed traditional Anatolian village. Covering a large area, the museum features authentic wooden houses, a working water mill, a mosque, and agricultural fields, all relocated from various parts of Turkey. It provides an immersive, educational experience regarding rural Turkish life in the 19th and early 20th centuries.

Free

Panora Alışveriş ve Yaşam Merkezi

Panora Alışveriş ve Yaşam Merkezi is a vibrant shopping destination that offers an extensive selection of stores, making it a favorite spot for both locals and visitors in Ankara. This commercial hub features a mix of international brands, from high-end names like Rolex to popular retailers such as Sephora and Zara. Beyond shopping, the mall provides various dining options and entertainment facilities, including an aquarium and game center, ensuring there's something for everyone.

Free

Lake Eymir

Lake Eymir, situated within the Middle East Technical University campus in Ankara, Turkey, offers a tranquil retreat for nature enthusiasts and visitors seeking a break from urban life. The picturesque lake is surrounded by pine trees and lush greenery, providing an ideal setting for activities such as cycling, jogging, and leisurely walks. It's a popular spot for picnics and birdwatching, with opportunities to observe various bird species.

Free

METU Forest

The METU (Middle East Technical University) Forest is a vast, semi-wild woodland area located on the university's campus in Ankara. Planted largely by students and faculty over several decades, it serves as a crucial 'green lung' for the city. The forest offers extensive, unpaved trails popular with hikers, runners, and mountain bikers seeking a natural escape.

Free

Aqua Vega Aquarium

Aqua Vega Aquarium, situated within the Nata Vega Outlet in Ankara, Turkey, offers a mesmerizing experience for visitors. The aquarium boasts an impressive underwater tunnel showcasing a diverse range of marine life including fish and sharks. It is not just a tourist attraction but also serves as an educational hub with a focus on conservation efforts. Families with kids can enjoy spending quality time here while observing various sea creatures from different parts of the world.

Free

Atatürk Bahçesi

Atatürk Park is a prominent, centrally located public park in Antalya, offering a peaceful green space amidst the bustling city. The park features well-maintained gardens, shaded seating areas, and a large monument dedicated to Mustafa Kemal Atatürk, the founder of the Turkish Republic. It serves as a popular resting spot for locals and visitors exploring the city center.

Free

Korean War Memorial

The Korean War Memorial in Ankara stands as a heartfelt tribute to the Turkish soldiers who lost their lives during the Korean War from 1950 to 1953. Established in 1953 and renovated in 2010, this modern architectural gem invites visitors to reflect on bravery and sacrifice. The memorial features soil brought from Korea, symbolizing martyrdom and connection between nations. It’s a serene spot for history enthusiasts, especially those with ties to South Korea.
