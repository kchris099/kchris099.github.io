Destination Guide — Ubud

Explore Ubud

A Brief History

Ubud became a cultural centre in upland Bali under Dutch colonial rule and twentieth-century royal patronage of the arts. Palace courtyards, Hindu temples, and rice-terrace landscapes record how Balinese dance, painting, and subak irrigation co-evolved in the Gianyar regency. Monkey forest sanctuaries and rice-terrace walks extend palace dance traditions through Gianyar uplands.

Penglipuran Village

Penglipuran Village, located in the East Bali region of Bangli, is a highland community known for its well-preserved traditional layout and culture. The village offers a glimpse into Balinese society's ancient Tri Hita Karana philosophy, with neatly trimmed gardens bordering stone-paved paths that wind through the neighborhood. Visitors can explore the main village area lined with old-fashioned thatched houses featuring arched gateways and manicured lawns.

Tegallalang Rice Terrace

Located just 30 minutes north of Ubud, Ceking Rice Terrace is a destination that showcases the beauty of terraced rice paddies surrounded by lush greenery. This landscape is not only a feast for the eyes but also an excellent example of traditional Balinese farming practices, particularly the subak irrigation system, which has been in use since the eighth century.

IDR 70,000

Alas Harum Bali

Alas Harum is a popular agrotourism destination located near Tegallalang, Bali. It has a full experience of Balinese agriculture, including Luwak coffee tasting and terraced rice field walks. The site is known for extreme jungle swings, glass-bottomed observation decks, and numerous creative photo spots set against the lush tropical valley.

Free (coffee tasting IDR 65,000)

Bali Pulina, Tegallang Gianyar

Bali Pulina is a large venue located near the Tegallalang Rice Terraces, offering lush tropical vegetation and coffee and tea tastings. Visitors can explore the traditional process of making Bali's famous Luwak coffee and enjoy the coffee plantation, outdoor activities such as ziplining through the foliage are available for adventure enthusiasts.

Pura Gunung Kawi Sebatu

Gunung Kawi Sebatu is an ancient 11th-century temple and funerary complex featuring 10 rock-cut shrines carved into cliff niches. Located near Ubud, it is surrounded by myths and theories that add to its allure, such as the legend of a warrior carving detailed reliefs with his fingernails. The temple covers an area of 5,000 square meters and is a place of worship for Lord Vishnu in the Hindu religion.

Sacred Monkey Forest Sanctuary

The Sacred Monkey Forest Sanctuary in Ubud is a dense, natural forest reserve that is a habitat for hundreds of long-tailed macaques. The sanctuary contains three ancient Hindu temples covered in moss and detailed stone carvings. It represents the Balinese principle of Tri Hita Karana, emphasizing harmony between humans, nature, and the divine.

Free (dance show IDR 100,000)

Peliatan Royal Palace

Peliatan Palace is a cultural center and the former home of royalty, located at the meeting point of two rivers with cascading streams. Since the 1930s, it has been a haven for local and international artists. The palace is now a hub for fine arts and cultural performances, drawing art enthusiasts, writers, musicians, dancers, and those interested in anthropology and alternative healing.
