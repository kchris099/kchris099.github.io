Explore Denpasar

A Brief History

Denpasar is the capital and the largest city of the province of Bali, Indonesia. Denpasar is the largest city in the Lesser Sunda Islands and the second largest city in Eastern Indonesia after Makassar. It is bordered by Badung Regency to its west and north, Gianyar Regency to its east, and the Indian Ocean and the Badung Strait to its south. The growth of the tourism industry on the island of Bali has pushed the city of Denpasar to become a center of business activities, and has positioned the city as an area with high per capita income and growth in Bali.

Top Sights & Activities

IDR 50000

Bajra Sandhi Monument

Bajra Sandhi Monument is a monumental pavilion in Renon Square commemorating the Balinese struggle for independence. Its bell-shaped shrine rises above dioramas tracing island history from prehistoric kingdoms to the puputan resistance. The civic park around the monument hosts weekend exercise groups and festival gatherings in central Denpasar.

Free

Badung Market

Pasar Badung is Denpasar's largest traditional market on the banks of the Badung River in the city centre. Multi-storey halls sell textiles, ceremonial supplies, and produce to residents from across Bali. The market moved to a modern building after fire destroyed the old structure, retaining its role as the island's wholesale hub.

Free

Monumen Tragedi Kemanusiaan

Monumen Tragedi Kemanusiaan marks the site of the 2002 Bali bombings on Jalan Legian in Kuta, a short drive from Denpasar. A marble memorial lists victims' names beside a reflecting pool where ceremonies are held each October. The quiet garden gives visitors space to remember the attack that targeted nightclubs in the resort district.

Free

Beachwalk Shopping Center

Beachwalk Shopping Center is an open-air mall on Kuta Beach with dining terraces overlooking the Indian Ocean. Retail floors mix international brands with Indonesian tenants above a landscaped promenade linking the sand to Legian Street. Evening visitors use the site as a gateway between the beach and southern Denpasar's commercial strips.

Free

Kuta Beach

Kuta Beach is Bali's -known surf beach west of Denpasar, with a long arc of sand facing southwest toward the Indian Ocean. Beginner waves, beach vendors, and sunset crowds define the resort strip that grew from a fishing village into Indonesia's busiest tourist coast. Lifeguard towers and rental boards line the shore near the Beachwalk mall.

Free

Patung Bayi, Sukawati, Gianyar

Patung Bayi is a roadside statue in Sukawati, Gianyar, depicting a baby figure that has become a local landmark on the route between Denpasar and Ubud. The sculpture stands near art markets and temple villages east of the capital. Travellers pause at the junction for photos beside the brightly painted figure.

IDR 30000

Tegenungan Waterfall

Tegenungan Waterfall drops into a forested gorge on the Petanu River south of Ubud, reached by steps from a car park on the Denpasar–Gianyar road. A viewing platform overlooks the cascade where swimmers use the pool at the base on calm days. Souvenir stalls and warungs line the approach through clove plantations.

IDR 30000

Pura Puseh Desa Batuan

Pura Puseh Desa Batuan is the village temple of Batuan, known for classical Balinese paintings and carved stone gateways. The 11th-century shrine complex anchors a hamlet of artists between Denpasar and Ubud. Visitors in sarongs walk the split gate to see meru towers and moss-covered statues in the inner courtyard.

IDR 395000

Bali Zoo

Bali Zoo in Singapadu village houses Sumatran elephants, orangutans, and Indonesian birds in shaded enclosures south of Ubud. Keeper talks and a waterplay area supplement standard walks through the compact park. The zoo promotes conservation education for families staying in the southern resort belt near Denpasar.

Free

Mal Bali Galeria

Mal Bali Galeria is a shopping mall on the Sunset Road corridor linking Denpasar to Kuta and Seminyak. Department stores and cinemas serve residents from the capital's western suburbs. The centre sits on one of the island's busiest retail arteries between the airport and city hall.

Free

Patung Dewa Ruci

Patung Dewa Ruci is a towering statue of the Hindu god Bhima at the Simpang Siur roundabout in Kuta, unveiled in 2013 above a traffic underpass. The warrior figure holds a club against a lotus backdrop visible from the Ngurah Rai airport road. The monument marks the gateway between the airport and the main resort coast.

Free

Pantai Batu Bolong

Pantai Batu Bolong is a temple-backed beach in Canggu where Pura Batu Bolong sits on a lava rock outcrop at the shore. Surfers and sunset viewers use the grey sand north of Seminyak, with views along the coast toward Tanah Lot. The temple name refers to the perforated rock formation beside the breakers.

Free

Pantai Berawa

Pantai Berawa is a Canggu beach between Batu Bolong and Echo Beach, popular with surfers and beach clubs west of Denpasar. Cafés and villas line the lane behind the sand facing consistent reef breaks. The stretch has grown from rice fields into one of Bali's trendiest coastal neighbourhoods.

Free

Discovery Mall Bali

Discovery Mall Bali fronts Kuta Beach with shops and restaurants above the sand at the end of Kartika Plaza. The compact centre serves hotel guests from the adjacent resort strip with souvenirs and dining terraces. Pedestrian access links the mall directly to the beach promenade.

IDR 610000

Waterbom Bali

Waterbom Bali is a landscaped water park in Kuta with slides, lazy rivers, and shaded lawns ranked among Asia's top aquatic attractions. Day passes include access to gravity rides and family pools on a terraced site near the airport road. The park draws resort visitors seeking a full-day break from the beaches west of Denpasar.

IDR 50000

Taman Ayun Temple

Pura Taman Ayun is a royal water temple in Mengwi, Badung, surrounded by moats and manicured gardens. Built in the 17th century for the Mengwi kingdom, its meru towers rise above a rectangular pond within a walled compound. The complex is a UNESCO-listed example of Balinese subak temple architecture northwest of Denpasar.

Free

Sukawati Art Market

Pasar Seni Sukawati is a long-standing art market in Gianyar where vendors sell paintings, carvings, and textiles to wholesale buyers and tourists. Two-storey halls line the main road east of Denpasar, with bargaining expected on batik and woodwork. Morning is busiest when coach groups stop on routes to Ubud.

IDR 300000

Hidden Canyon Beji Guwang

Hidden Canyon Beji Guwang is a river gorge near Sukawati where guided treks wade through narrow rock walls carved by the Oos River. Local operators provide safety equipment for the canyoning route through sculpted sandstone slots. The adventure site lies inland from the southern beaches in Gianyar regency.

IDR 50000

Hidden Mini Zoo Bali

Hidden Mini Zoo Bali is a small private zoo in Denpasar's suburbs exhibiting birds, reptiles, and farm animals in compact enclosures. Families visit for short interactive sessions and photo opportunities without travelling to the larger parks at Singapadu. The site targets local weekend outings within the capital's metropolitan area.

Free

Satya Dharma Temple

Satya Dharma Temple is a Chinese-Balinese temple in Denpasar honouring deities from both traditions within a single courtyard complex. Incense and offerings mark festivals attended by the city's Chinese Indonesian community. The shrine illustrates how migrant faiths merged with Balinese ritual life in the provincial capital.

Free

Double Six Beach

Double Six Beach is the northern section of Seminyak's sand strip named for the historic 66 Club site on the shore west of Denpasar. Beach bars and loungers line the coast where sunset crowds gather facing the Indian Ocean. The beach links Seminyak's boutique hotel quarter with the broader Kuta–Legian resort coast.
