Destination Guide — Bandung

Explore Bandung

A Brief History

Developed by Dutch colonialists in the 19th century as a high-altitude tea and coffee hub, Bandung's cool climate earned it the nickname 'Paris of Java'. Its extensive collection of tropical Art Deco architecture highlights a brief but intensely wealthy colonial golden age. Visitors today can clearly trace the intricate layers of this unique heritage throughout the preserved streets and monuments.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

Paris Van Java

Paris Van Java Mall, located on Jalan Sukajadi Nomor 137-139, is a vibrant shopping center with a variety of brand-name stores, dining options, and entertainment. The mall features an open-air concept with numerous restaurants and cafes for visitors to enjoy. Inside the spacious mall, visitors can explore department stores, boutique shops, a grocery store, a cinema, a bookstore, as well as restaurants and coffee shops.

Free

Rumah Mode Factory Outlet

Rumah Mode Factory Outlet is a bustling shopping destination in Bandung, offering a wide range of clothing, shoes, and bags. The outlet also features cozy eateries set in a charming garden. It's known for its popularity and can get quite crowded on weekends. The complex includes various shops like Mode Plus, Natural, Donatello on Jl Setiabudi, as well as others like Blossom, Glamour, Raffles City on Jl Dago and more.

35000 IDR

Floating Market Lembang

Floating Market Lembang is a renowned culinary destination offering a wide range of meal choices sold on floating boats in a designated lake. In addition to the market, visitors can explore miniature town replicas, enjoy a mini carnival, and experience the famous rainbow slide. The affordable ticket price includes complimentary drinks.

Free

Braga Citywalk

Braga Citywalk, located on the legendary Jl Braga in Bandung, is a mixed-use development that combines a mall, condominiums, and a hotel. Its proximity to tourist attractions such as the Mandala Wangsit museum and the Asia Africa Conference museum attracts a steady stream of visitors. Popular among young locals, this destination offers various amenities including shopping centers, entertainment venues, and star-rated hotels.

Free

Grand Mosque of Bandung

The Grand Mosque of Bandung, also known as Masjid Agung Bandung or Masjid Raya Bandung, is a significant Islamic landmark in the heart of Bandung, Indonesia. Built in the 19th century, this majestic mosque boasts an impressive dome and twin 81-meter minarets. It stands as a testament to the city's rich Islamic heritage and cultural significance. The mosque's design and surrounding gardens provide ample opportunities for capturing photographs.

Free

Alun-Alun Bandung

Alun-Alun Bandung is a vibrant city square that serves as the heart of West Java's capital, offering a lively atmosphere for both locals and visitors. This urban hub is surrounded by significant landmarks, including the Great Mosque, which provides panoramic views of the city from its tower. While exploring this historic area, also discover nearby attractions like the Asia Africa Monument and various museums that celebrate Bandung's rich cultural heritage.

Free

Ciwalk

Ciwalk, short for Cihampelas Walk, is a modern open-air mall located just off Cihampelas street in Bandung. It features a variety of local boutiques and international chains, as well as a food court and rides for kids. Over time, it has become more than just a shopping center, evolving into a popular hangout spot for both tourists and locals. The mall offers an outdoor 'street' of restaurants and cafes where visitors can enjoy dining while shopping.

35000 IDR

Ciwalk XXI

Nestled in the vibrant heart of Bandung, Ciwalk XXI, or Cihampelas Walk, is a modern open-air mall that blends shopping and leisure. This inviting space boasts lush green areas alongside an array of retail shops and dining venues, making it an ideal spot for both avid shoppers and those simply looking to unwind.

50000 IDR

Bandung Zoo (Kebun Binatang Bandung)

Bandung Zoological Garden, located in the lively city of Bandung, Indonesia, is a popular tourist attraction for families and wildlife enthusiasts. Spanning 14 hectares, this captivating zoo is home to a diverse collection of animals from Indonesia and around the world. Visitors can enjoy exploring well-maintained enclosures that house native Indonesian wildlife as well as species from overseas. The zoo offers an enjoyable and educational experience for visitors of all ages.

Free

Salman ITB Mosque

Nestled within the vibrant campus of Institut Teknologi Bandung, the Salman ITB Mosque is a remarkable spiritual haven that has been welcoming visitors since its opening in 1972. This mosque is particularly notable for its striking architectural design, featuring a unique concave roof that departs from traditional domed structures, showcasing a harmonious blend of modern aesthetics and Islamic principles.

Free

Babakan Siliwangi City Forest

Babakan Siliwangi City Forest, located in the Dago area of Bandung, is a renowned urban forest with raised viewing platforms and bridges that offer views of the lush tropical greenery. Recognized by the United Nations as one of the world's urban forests, it serves as an oasis in the midst of the bustling city.

200000 IDR

Trans Studio Bandung

Trans Studio Bandung is an indoor theme park located within the Trans Studio Bandung Mall, making it a popular entertainment destination for families visiting the city. Divided into three zones - Magic Corner, Lost City, and Studio Central - the park offers a wide range of thrilling rides and attractions suitable for both children and adults. With over 20 different games to enjoy, including electric and water-based activities, kids can immerse themselves in a world of fun.

3000 IDR

Monumen Perjuangan Rakyat Jawa Barat

Monumen Perjuangan Rakyat Jawa Barat stands as a powerful tribute to the struggle for independence, particularly highlighting the efforts of West Java's citizens during Indonesia's fight for freedom. Established in 1995, this impressive monument is designed to resemble "bambu runcing," or sharpened bamboo, which symbolizes the weaponry used by Indonesians against colonial forces.

Free

Gasibu Field

Gasibu Field is a popular spot in the heart of Bandung, West Java, located near the Gasibu Market and Gedung Sate. It's a versatile hub for various activities such as sports and cultural events. The field offers a clean jogging track and serves as an ideal place for leisurely walks. Visitors can engage in games like soccer, frisbee, or traditional Indonesian sports like sepak takraw.

5000 IDR

Gedung Sate

Gedung Sate, located in Bandung, Indonesia, is a grand Dutch colonial administration building that has been repurposed as the government house of West Java. This historic complex once served as the headquarters for the Dutch Indies Government Companies and now houses the offices of the governor of West Java and the provincial house of representatives. The architecture is a captivating blend of Italianate Renaissance, Spanish Moorish, Balinese, and Thai influences.

Free

Taman Lansia

Taman Lansia in Bandung is a peaceful and well-maintained park featuring lush greenery, picnic spots, jogging paths, and food vendors. The park boasts a large dinosaur sculpture and plans to create artificial lakes for flood control. Visitors can enjoy leisurely walks amidst various trees, ponds, and ample seating areas. Despite some issues with entry points and occasional rubbish in the river, the park offers a pleasant environment for relaxation and outdoor activities.

35000 IDR

Farmhouse Susu Lembang

Farmhouse Susu Lembang is a charming artificial village that offers a unique European atmosphere, complete with picturesque replicas of famous European buildings and landmarks. Visitors can immerse themselves in the experience by renting traditional Dutch costumes for memorable photos. The village also features a mini zoo where families can interact with various animals, including feeding calves, sheep, rabbits, and even exotic creatures like hedgehogs and iguanas.

50000 IDR

The Great Asia Africa

The Great Asia Africa sits within the Farmhouse Susu Lembang area of Bandung, documented in local heritage registers for its civic or cultural role. Visitors approach from the main access road and combine the visit with neighbouring heritage buildings and public spaces in the district.

20000 IDR

Jendela Alam

Jendela Alam is a nature-themed educational attraction located in the Komplek Graha Puspa, Bandung. It offers agricultural activities and experiences for children who love animals, such as pony rides, animal feeding, and fish catching. Visitors can immerse themselves in farm experiences while learning about the natural world. Although it is a small place that can be explored in 20-30 minutes, it provides an enjoyable experience for families with young children.

Free

Sudirman Street Day and Night Market

Sudirman Street Day and Night Market is a vibrant culinary hotspot in Bandung, located at Jalan Jendral Sudirman No. 107. This lively market operates daily from 10 AM to midnight, offering an extensive array of street food that caters to diverse tastes. Visitors can indulge in a delightful mix of Indonesian and Chinese dishes, along with Western options, ensuring there's something for everyone.

Free

Cibadak Night Culinary Festival

Located at Jalan Cibadak No. 50-155, Astana Anyar, the Cibadak Night Culinary Festival is like Bandung's version of 'Chinatown.' Operating from 7:00 AM to 2:00 AM daily, it offers a variety of delicious dishes inspired by Chinese culture, with most being non-halal.

50000 IDR

Fairy Garden

Fairy Garden, located in the Lembang area just 15 kilometers from Bandung City, is a newly opened family-friendly park with a fairy theme. The 2-hectare park features six different themed gardens and offers a variety of attractions for both children and adults. Visitors can enjoy a modest playground, fairy dance shows, and staff dressed in costumes.

50000 IDR

The Lodge Maribaya

Nestled in the highlands of Bandung, The Lodge Maribaya offers a trendy and exotic setting surrounded by pine trees, making it a picturesque spot for documenting your visit. Adventure seekers will find an array of thrilling activities such as the bike park, hot air balloon rides, sky swing, zip bike adventures, and more. The lodge's location near the pine forest provides a cool escape for visitors seeking respite from the city heat.

11000 IDR

Tebing Karaton

Tebing Karaton, also known as the jutting rock, is a well-known cliff located in Juanda forest, Ciburial village, Bandung. It offers views of the city of Bandung and has become popular for its picturesque forest scenery during sunrise and sunset. The area used to be called Cadas Jontor but gained attention around 2014 when travelers started sharing their selfies from this majestic cliff on social media.

35000 IDR

Curug Maribaya

Curug Maribaya is a documented coastal feature in Bandung, Indonesia, recognised in regional geographic listings for its natural formation and public access. The shoreline is open to visitors throughout the year and connects to adjacent beachfront districts. Travellers approach from the coastal road or boardwalk and combine the visit with nearby harbour areas and seaside markets.

Free

Kebun Begonia

Kebun Begonia is a popular small garden known for its colorful flowers and also offers the opportunity to pick fresh vegetables. Visitors can purchase fresh flowers and even enjoy delicious blueberries along the way. While it can get crowded on weekends, it's recommended to visit in the morning to fully appreciate the beauty of this place. The garden has two parts, with the first part being more developed and lovely, while the second part is still in progress.

Free

Gunung Putri Lembang

Gunung Putri Lembang is a popular destination for hikers and sunset enthusiasts. The mountain offers and mysterious natural scenery, making it an attractive hiking spot in Lembang. Visitors can camp at the peak and witness sunrise views. From the top, one can enjoy panoramic vistas of the surrounding neighborhoods and Bandung City. The hike to the viewpoints is relatively easy, taking around 15 minutes in total.

Free

Asia-Africa Conference Museum

The Asian African Conference Museum is a historical site dedicated to the 1955 Bandung Conference, which brought together newly-independent African and Asian states. This significant event marked a milestone in world history by uniting 29 countries from both continents during a turbulent period of the Cold War.

Free

Gedung Merdeka

Gedung Merdeka, also known as the Independence Building, is a significant historical landmark in Bandung, Indonesia. It holds great cultural and historical importance as it was the venue for the Asia Africa Conference in 1955. The building now houses a free museum that features a reconstruction of the signing of the Bandung Declaration, with figures representing prominent heads of state present at the conference.

Free

Plaza Cikapundung River Spot

Plaza Cikapundung River Spot is a unique urban park that takes advantage of its riverside location, offering a different vibe compared to other city parks. The landscape design incorporates the waterfront, making it an ideal place to relax and enjoy various activities. The park features vibrant red accents that contrast with the natural surroundings, creating a visually striking environment.

Free

Bandung Lautan Api Monument

The Bandung Lautan Api Monument stands proudly on Jalan BKR Ciateul in the Regol district, serving as a poignant tribute to the historic battle that symbolizes Indonesia's fight for independence. This monument not only honors the bravery of those who sacrificed their lives to defend their homeland but also reflects the deep devotion of Indonesians towards their country.

Free

Taman Hutan Raya Ir. H. Djuanda

Taman Hutan Raya Ir. H. Djuanda, a 590-hectare conservation area and botanical garden, is a must-visit destination for nature lovers exploring Bandung. This lush park features an array of walking trails that meander through coniferous forests, leading you to captivating waterfalls and intriguing caves.

Free

Gua Belanda

Gua Belanda (Dutch Cave) is a historical site located within the Djuanda Forest Park in Bandung, Indonesia. Originally built by the Dutch colonial military in the early 20th century as a hydroelectric tunnel, it was later used as a radio station and bunker during World War II. Visitors can explore the dark, damp tunnels to learn about the region's wartime history.

Free

Ade Irma Suryani Nasution Traffic Garden

Ade Irma Suryani Nasution Traffic Garden is a recreational area that provides an excellent outdoor experience for children. The park is equipped with a traffic playground that teaches children about safety, as well as a pool and several playgrounds for them to enjoy various activities such as biking, train riding, running, climbing and carousels. Families can gather here for picnics while the kids take advantage of the swimming pool.

Free

Dago Dreampark

Dago Dreampark is a popular adventure park located in a pine forest in Bandung. It has been visited places in the area for several years. The park, which opened in 2016, offers unique attractions that combine modernity, futuristic elements, and nature. Its design and architecture are inspired by the beauty of Bali's ecosystem while incorporating modern features. The park is known for activities such as ATV riding, paintballing, and imaginative props for taking selfies.

Free

Sudut Pandang Bandung

Sudut Pandang is a contemporary lifestyle and dining complex located in the Punclut area of Bandung. It is known for its unique, futuristic architecture, featuring glass domes and interactive art installations. The venue offers panoramic views of the Bandung basin and provides a very photogenic environment that blends modern design with natural scenery.
