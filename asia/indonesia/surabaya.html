Explore Surabaya

A Brief History

Surabaya has served as a major trading port on the northern coast of Java since the Majapahit Empire in the fourteenth century. It became a important commercial and naval base during the Dutch East Indies era. The city earned the title "City of Heroes" for its important role in the Battle of Surabaya in 1945, a defining moment in the Indonesian National Revolution. Today, Surabaya is Indonesia's second-largest city, blending colonial-era architecture with modern industrial development.

Tunjungan Plaza is a busy shopping center located in Surabaya, offering a wide array of retail outlets, dining options, and entertainment facilities. Gedung Negara Grahadi

Gedung Negara Grahadi is a visitor site in Surabaya, Indonesia. This late 18th-century building is the official residence for the Governor of East Java and is frequently use for exhibitions and formal ceremonies. With its grand entrance, elegant stone columns, and well-preserved facade, it exudes an air of opulence that draws visitors. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Monumen Bambu Runcing

Monumen Bambu Runcing, or the Pointed Bamboo Monument, is a striking landmark in Surabaya, characterized by its three towering bamboo spear-like pillars. This structure is not only visually but also is a symbol of Indonesia's struggle for independence. Surrounded by beautifully landscaped gardens and fountains, it transforms into an enchanting sight at night when illuminated by soft lights.

Suro and Boyo Statue

The Suro and Boyo Statue is a significant landmark in Surabaya, representing the city's identity. This 25-meter-high statue depicts a shark (sura) and a crocodile (boyo) engaged in a tussle amidst seaweed. Situated in Suroboyo Park, it is a symbol of the city's cultural and historical significance.

Surabaya Zoo

Surabaya Zoo, also known as AzissZoo Surabaya, is a popular destination for both local and foreign tourists. Established in 1918, this expansive 15-hectare zoo is located in Bandar Surabaya and is easily accessible. It's an ideal spot for family vacations with its diverse range of animals from lions and bears to komodo dragons.

The city park Monumen Perjuangan Polisi Republik Indonesia is a nice place to visit in Surabaya. It has a monument that commemorates the efforts of the Special Police troops during the freedom struggle and there are plaques that educate visitors about the happenings of November 1945. The park also has a small pond and is well-maintained.

Surabaya North Quay

Surabaya North Quay, managed by PT Pelindo II (Persero), is a popular destination for cruise ships from outside Indonesia. The views of the sea and sky make it a favorite spot for locals and tourists alike, especially in the evening. Visitors can enjoy live music, art performances, culinary markets, and local product exhibitions. This busy waterfront area offers a active atmosphere with diverse dining options and opportunities to explore Surabaya's maritime history.

Monumen Jalesveva Jayamahe

Monumen Jalesveva Jayamahe, located in the Tanjung Perak area of Surabaya, is a towering and statue that is a proud symbol of the Indonesian Navy's maritime prowess. Standing at 30.6 meters tall, it was inaugurated by President Soeharto in 1996 and represents Indonesia's historical maritime achievements dating back to the era of the Sriwijaya and Majapahit kingdoms.

Pasar Ampel

Pasar Ampel is a active, historic market located in the Arab Quarter of Surabaya. The narrow, busy streets are lined with stalls selling a wide variety of goods, including Middle Eastern dates, perfumes, textiles, and traditional Muslim attire. The market is situated near the Sunan Ampel Mosque, making it a major center for religious tourism and cultural exchange.

Monumen Tugu Pahlawan dan Museum Sepuluh Nopember Surabaya

Monumen Tugu Pahlawan dan Museum Sepuluh Nopember Surabaya is an memorial complex that commemorates the historic Battle of Surabaya in 1945. The Hero Monument, inaugurated by Soekarno, is a tribute to Indonesia's independence heroes. The Sepuluh November Museum, located within the complex, is the only museum in Indonesia dedicated to honoring the events of November 10 in Surabaya.

Free

Gereja Katolik Kelahiran Santa Perawan Maria

Gereja Katolik Kelahiran Santa Perawan Maria, also known as the Church of the Birth of Lady, is a Gothic-style Catholic church in Surabaya. Constructed in 1895 with imported red bricks from Europe, it has high ceilings and Gothic architecture reminiscent of classical European churches. Despite being damaged during the war of independence in 1945, it has been careful renovated and now is one of Surabaya's oldest cathedrals.

Free

Carnival Park Surabaya, located at 333 Ahmad Yani Street, is a active amusement park that has been visitors since its opening in 2014. al-Akbar National Mosque Surabaya

The al-Akbar National Mosque in Surabaya is a blend of spirituality and modern architecture. With its bright blue dome, herbal gardens, and a tower offering panoramic city views, the mosque is a symbol of the city's dedication to harmonizing religious devotion with contemporary design. While not typically considered a kid-friendly attraction, it provides a quiet shoreline from the urban bustle and invites families to immerse themselves in cultural learning.

IDR 10,000

Sanggar Agung Temple

Sanggar Agung Temple is a visitor site in Surabaya. Unique temple complex located on Kenjeran Beach, with a large statue of Guan Yin and statues of deities from many different cultures. The temple is popular among Buddhists, Taoists, and Kong Hu Cu followers for its quiet setting and views of the ocean.

Free (park entry IDR 15,000)

4 Brahma Statue Arts

4 Brahma Statue Arts is a monument in Surabaya, Indonesia. This site is home to the largest Buddhist statue in Indonesia and is a working temple where worshippers come to pray. The memorial stands in an open plaza or roadside setting. 4 Brahma Statue Arts remains part of the public visitor circuit in Surabaya.

Free

Suramadu View Point

Suramadu View Point sits within the Suramadu View Point area of Surabaya, documented in local heritage registers for its civic or cultural role. Visitors approach from the main access road and combine the visit with neighbouring heritage buildings and public spaces in the district.
