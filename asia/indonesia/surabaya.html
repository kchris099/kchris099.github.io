Surabaya

Surabaya has served as a major trading port on the northern coast of Java since the Majapahit Empire in the fourteenth century. It became a important commercial and naval base during the Dutch East Indies era. The city earned the title "City of Heroes" for its important role in the Battle of Surabaya in 1945, a defining moment in the Indonesian National Revolution. Today, Surabaya is Indonesia's second-largest city, blending colonial-era architecture with modern industrial development.

Monumen Jalesveva Jayamahe

Monumen Jalesveva Jayamahe, located in the Tanjung Perak area of Surabaya, is a towering and statue that is a proud symbol of the Indonesian Navy's maritime prowess. Standing at 30.6 meters tall, it was inaugurated by President Soeharto in 1996 and represents Indonesia's historical maritime achievements dating back to the era of the Sriwijaya and Majapahit kingdoms.

Monumen Tugu Pahlawan dan Museum Sepuluh Nopember Surabaya

Tugu Pahlawan rises in central Surabaya as the principal memorial to the November 1945 battle against returning colonial forces. The tapered monument stands above the underground Sepuluh Nopember Museum, where photographs, recordings, objects, and dioramas examine the conflict and its civilian cost. The complex links national commemoration with one city's specific wartime destruction and resistance.

The parish continues to hold regular worship in the restored interior.
