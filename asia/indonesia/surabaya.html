Destination Guide — Surabaya

Explore Surabaya

A Brief History

Surabaya has served as a major trading port on the northern coast of Java since the Majapahit Empire in the fourteenth century. It became a crucial commercial and naval base during the Dutch East Indies era. The city earned the title "City of Heroes" for its pivotal role in the Battle of Surabaya in 1945, a defining moment in the Indonesian National Revolution. Today, Surabaya is Indonesia's second-largest city, blending colonial-era architecture with modern industrial development.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

Tunjungan Plaza

Tunjungan Plaza is a bustling shopping center located in the heart of Surabaya, offering a wide array of retail outlets, dining options, and entertainment facilities. With its four interconnected buildings, including the impressive 52-story Tunjungan Plaza V, this mega mall provides visitors with an extensive selection of over 500 stores ranging from fashion brands to supermarkets.

Free

Gedung Negara Grahadi

Nestled in the vibrant city of Surabaya, Gedung Negara Grahadi stands as a testament to Dutch colonial architecture. This late 18th-century building serves as the official residence for the Governor of East Java and is frequently utilized for exhibitions and formal ceremonies. With its grand entrance, elegant stone columns, and well-preserved facade, it exudes an air of opulence that captivates visitors.

Free

Monumen Bambu Runcing

Monumen Bambu Runcing, or the Pointed Bamboo Monument, stands as a striking landmark in Surabaya, characterized by its three towering bamboo spear-like pillars. This impressive structure is not only visually captivating but also serves as a poignant symbol of Indonesia's struggle for independence. Surrounded by beautifully landscaped gardens and fountains, it transforms into an enchanting sight at night when illuminated by soft lights.

Free

Grand City Mall Surabaya

Grand City Mall Surabaya is a massive retail complex and event venue spanning 4.5 hectares with 8 floors. It features a diverse array of international and local brands, as well as an expansive food court offering various culinary delights. The modern building design exudes luxury, housing not only shops but also offices, apartments, and five-star hotels.

Free

Muhammad Cheng Hoo Mosque

The Muhammad Cheng Hoo Mosque, also known as the Cheng Hoo Mosque, is a compact yet striking religious site in Surabaya, Indonesia. Its design is inspired by Chinese architecture and features a tiered pagoda that pays homage to the 15th-century Chinese-Muslim admiral Zheng He. The mosque serves as a sacred monument celebrating the integration of Chinese culture into Surabaya's local community, reflecting the city's rich history of cultural acculturation.

Free

Pakuwon Mall Surabaya

Pakuwon Mall Surabaya, formerly known as Supermal Pakuwon Indah, is a massive contemporary retail center located in the southwest area of the city. It offers a wide range of shopping options from international brands to local outlets, diverse dining choices, and modern facilities such as CGV Blitz cinema. The mall's strategic location with easy access makes it a bustling hub for visitors.

Free

Lenmarc Mall Surabaya

Lenmarc Mall Surabaya is a modern six-level shopping complex that embraces the concept of a family mall, catering to all family needs in one place. Since its establishment in 2010, it has attracted numerous renowned local and international tenants, including Transmart Carrefour, J.CO Donuts & Coffee, Cinema XXI, ACE Hardware, Toys Kingdom, and many more. The mall features ample parking space for approximately 2500 cars.

Free

Pasar Atom

Pasar Atom is a long-standing market and shopping center in Surabaya, offering a wide array of goods from fashion and electronics to traditional snacks. It's a bustling place where locals and tourists come to shop and bargain for the deals. The market is famous for its diverse range of products, including Batik, a traditional Indonesian fabric. Visitors can explore different sections for unique finds and enjoy local street food while immersing themselves in the vibrant atmosphere.

Free

ITC Mega Grosir Surabaya

The ITC Surabaya Mega Grosir is a popular shopping mall located in east Java. It features a wide range of local clothing, footwear and accessories vendors, a supermarket and a food court. This low-key mall is perfect for those who love shopping, as it has a wide variety of collections that 't find anywhere else.

Free

Suro and Boyo Statue

The Suro and Boyo Statue is a significant landmark in Surabaya, representing the city's identity. This 25-meter-high statue depicts a shark (sura) and a crocodile (boyo) engaged in a tussle amidst seaweed. Situated in Suroboyo Park, it stands as a symbol of the city's cultural and historical significance.

15000 IDR

Surabaya Zoo

Surabaya Zoo, also known as AzissZoo Surabaya, is a popular destination for both local and foreign tourists. Established in 1918, this expansive 15-hectare zoo is located in Bandar Surabaya and is easily accessible. It's an ideal spot for family vacations with its diverse range of animals from lions and bears to komodo dragons.

Free

Bungkul Park

Bungkul Park, located in the heart of Surabaya, East Java Province, is a bustling green space that offers a variety of attractions. It features a skate park, play equipment for children, and plenty of shade trees. The park has gained recognition by winning the 2013 United Nations Asian Townscape Award for its contribution to landscape construction. Visitors can enjoy a peaceful atmosphere with green lawns and trees or indulge in local street food while relaxing in the shade.

Free

Monumen Perjuangan Polisi Republik Indonesia

The city park Monumen Perjuangan Polisi Republik Indonesia is a nice place to visit in Surabaya. It has a monument that commemorates the efforts of the Special Police troops during the freedom struggle and there are plaques that educate visitors about the happenings of November 1945. The park also has a small pond and is well-maintained.

Free

Royal Plaza

Royal Plaza is a lively, multi-level shopping haven that caters to budget-conscious shoppers in Surabaya. Open daily from 10 am to 9:30 pm, this bustling center features an array of local specialty shops alongside a food court and movie theater, making it the perfect spot for both shopping and snacking. The ground floor buzzes with activity as visitors explore various stalls offering mid-range to affordable brands.

10000 IDR

Surabaya North Quay

Surabaya North Quay, managed by PT Pelindo II (Persero), is a popular destination for cruise ships from outside Indonesia. The views of the sea and sky make it a favorite spot for locals and tourists alike, especially in the evening. Visitors can enjoy live music, art performances, culinary markets, and local product exhibitions. This bustling waterfront area offers a vibrant atmosphere with diverse dining options and opportunities to explore Surabaya's maritime history.

Free (Museum Pusat TNI AL Jalesveva Jayamahe charges 15000 IDR)

Monumen Jalesveva Jayamahe

Monumen Jalesveva Jayamahe, located in the Tanjung Perak area of Surabaya, is a towering and statue that serves as a proud symbol of the Indonesian Navy's maritime prowess. Standing at 30.6 meters tall, it was inaugurated by President Soeharto in 1996 and represents Indonesia's historical maritime achievements dating back to the era of the Sriwijaya and Majapahit kingdoms.

Free

Sunan Ampel Great Mosque

Sunan Ampel Great Mosque is one of the oldest mosques in East Java. It was built by the revered Sunan Ampel, who is credited with expanding Islam in Java. The mosque's architecture is a mix of Javanese and Arab styles, and it also features a beautiful tomb complex for Sunan Ampel.

Free

Pasar Ampel

Pasar Ampel is a vibrant, historic market located in the Arab Quarter of Surabaya. The narrow, bustling streets are lined with stalls selling a wide variety of goods, including Middle Eastern dates, perfumes, textiles, and traditional Muslim attire. The market is situated near the Sunan Ampel Mosque, making it a major center for religious tourism and cultural exchange.

5000 IDR

Monumen Tugu Pahlawan dan Museum Sepuluh Nopember Surabaya

Monumen Tugu Pahlawan dan Museum Sepuluh Nopember Surabaya is an memorial complex that commemorates the historic Battle of Surabaya in 1945. The Hero Monument, inaugurated by Soekarno, stands as a tribute to Indonesia's independence heroes. The Sepuluh November Museum, located within the complex, is the only museum in Indonesia dedicated to honoring the events of November 10 in Surabaya.

Free

Gereja Katolik Kelahiran Santa Perawan Maria

Gereja Katolik Kelahiran Santa Perawan Maria, also known as the Church of the Birth of Lady, is a Gothic-style Catholic church in Surabaya. Constructed in 1895 with imported red bricks from Europe, it boasts high ceilings and Gothic architecture reminiscent of classical European churches. Despite being damaged during the war of independence in 1945, it has been meticulously renovated and now stands as one of Surabaya's oldest cathedrals.

Free

City of Tomorrow Mall

City of Tomorrow Mall is a must-visit shopping destination in Surabaya, conveniently located near Juanda Airport. This expansive mall features multiple floors housing a wide range of retail stores, including big-name tenants like Hypermart and Matahari Department Store. Visitors can also enjoy diverse dining options at the food court, cafes, and restaurants within the mall. Additionally, families can take advantage of the children's recreation area and catch a movie at Cinema 21.

Closed

Carnival Park Surabaya

Carnival Park Surabaya, located at 333 Ahmad Yani Street, is a vibrant amusement park that has been captivating visitors since its opening in 2014. This popular tourist attraction offers a delightful escape within the City of Heroes and is perfect for families and couples seeking an enjoyable weekend outing. The park boasts an array of thrilling rides including roller coasters, Ferris wheels, and water games, ensuring an experience for all.

Free (minaret access 10000 IDR)

al-Akbar National Mosque Surabaya

The al-Akbar National Mosque in Surabaya is a magnificent blend of spirituality and modern architecture. With its bright blue dome, herbal gardens, and a tower offering panoramic city views, the mosque stands as a symbol of the city's dedication to harmonizing religious devotion with contemporary design. While not typically considered a kid-friendly attraction, it provides a serene escape from the urban bustle and invites families to immerse themselves in cultural learning.

10,000 IDR

Sanggar Agung Temple

Unique temple complex located on Kenjeran Beach, with a large statue of Guan Yin and statues of deities from many different cultures. The temple is popular among Buddhists, Taoists, and Kong Hu Cu followers for its tranquil atmosphere and views of the ocean.

Free (park entry 15,000 IDR)

4 Brahma Statue Arts

The 4 Brahma Statue Arts, situated in the Pantai Ria amusement park in Kenjeran, offers a serene and culturally rich environment for visitors. This site is home to the largest Buddhist statue in Indonesia and serves as a working temple where worshippers come to pray. The area features huge statues of Four Faces Brahma and Ganesha, creating a spiritual atmosphere that may feel eerie to those unfamiliar with the beliefs.

Free

Suramadu View Point

Suramadu View Point sits within the Suramadu View Point area of Surabaya, documented in local heritage registers for its civic or cultural role. Visitors approach from the main access road and combine the visit with neighbouring heritage buildings and public spaces in the district.
