Explore Yogyakarta

A Brief History

Yogyakarta served as the capital of the Mataram Sultanate and later the Yogyakarta Sultanate under Dutch indirect rule. Following independence, the city retained special status as a royal administrative region on Java. The kraton palace quarter, Malioboro commercial axis, and nearby Prambanan Hindu complex record Javanese court culture alongside Indonesia's modern republic.

Top Sights & Activities

IDR 25000

Taman Sari Tourist Village

Taman Sari was a royal garden retreat built in the mid-18th century west of the Yogyakarta kraton. Pools, tunnels, and a bathing complex served the sultan's household within a larger water palace. Restored sections now allow visitors to walk among the brick ruins and underground passages of the former pleasure gardens.

Free

Alun Alun Kidul

Alun Alun Kidul is the southern palace square of the Yogyakarta kraton, lined with banyan trees and evening food stalls. The open plaza south of the royal complex hosts local festivals and the famous blindfolded walk between twin banyans. Families gather here after dark for grilled snacks and pedal cars around the square.

Free

Ambarrukmo Plaza

Ambarrukmo Plaza is a large shopping and entertainment complex in southern Yogyakarta near the airport road. Department stores, cinemas, and dining courts serve residents from the Ambarrukmo and Catur Tunggal districts. The mall anchors a commercial node away from the historic Malioboro axis.

IDR 70000

Gembira Loka Zoo

Gembira Loka Zoo is a municipal zoo in Yogyakarta housing Indonesian and international species since the 1950s. Shaded paths connect aviaries, primate islands, and reptile houses on the banks of the Gajah Wong River east of the city centre. The zoo participates in breeding programmes for endangered regional wildlife.

IDR 5000

Hutan Pinus Pengger

Hutan Pinus Pengger is a pine forest recreation area on the hills south of Yogyakarta near Bantul. Managed paths and viewpoints overlook the city plain and Gunung Merapi on clear days. Local visitors use the cool hillside for photography platforms and weekend walks among the planted stands.

IDR 5000

Becici Peak

Becici Peak is a forest viewpoint on the hills south of Yogyakarta near Dlingo village. Wooden platforms among pine and hardwood trees frame panoramas toward the southern coast and volcanic ridges. The site became popular for sunrise photography after serving as a location for a presidential visit.

Free

Beringharjo Traditional Market

Pasar Beringharjo is Yogyakarta's main traditional market north of the kraton, trading batik, spices, and household goods since the colonial era. Narrow aisles under corrugated roofs connect wholesalers with batik workshops and souvenir vendors along Jalan Malioboro. Morning buyers supply restaurants and hotels across the city from its produce halls.

IDR 100000

Vredeburg Fort Museum

Fort Vredeburg Museum occupies a Dutch colonial fortress beside Malioboro Street in central Yogyakarta. Built in the 18th century and converted to a history museum after independence, it displays dioramas of Indonesia's struggle for freedom. The restored ramparts and barracks face the sultan's palace across the northern alun-alun.

Free

Jl. Malioboro

Jalan Malioboro is Yogyakarta's principal commercial street linking the railway station to the sultan's palace. Shops sell batik, silver, and street food along a corridor that mixes colonial storefronts with becak lanes and evening crowds. The axis forms the city's main tourist promenade and public transport spine.

IDR 25000

Keraton Ngayogyakarta Hadiningrat

The Keraton Ngayogyakarta Hadiningrat is the walled palace of the Sultan of Yogyakarta in the city centre. Built in the mid-18th century, it houses reception halls, a museum collection, and courtyards still used for court ceremonies. The complex anchors the cultural quarter south of the Tugu monument and Malioboro Street.

IDR 25000

Yogyakarta Royal Palace

The Yogyakarta Royal Palace quarter encompasses the kraton's reception halls and courtyards still used by the reigning sultan. Visitors enter through gates south of the Tugu monument to see Javanese court architecture, gamelan collections, and museum displays. The walled complex remains both a residence and a living centre of Javanese ceremonial life.

IDR 400000

Ramayana Ballet Prambanan

Prambanan is a 9th-century Hindu temple compound east of Yogyakarta, built by the Sanjaya dynasty and dedicated chiefly to Shiva. Its tall pointed shrines and narrative relief panels illustrate the Ramayana on the central Java plain. The restored temples stand in a landscaped archaeological park beside the Sewu Buddhist complex.

IDR 15000

Blue Lagoon Jogja

Blue Lagoon Jogja is a commercial swimming and photo attraction on the eastern fringe of Yogyakarta with turquoise pools and landscaped decks. Day visitors from the city use the site for family recreation away from the historic centre, combining shallow pools with garden backdrops for weekend outings.

IDR 20000

Obelix Village - Mini Zoo Jogja

Obelix Village is a family recreation park in Sleman regency north of central Yogyakarta with a mini zoo, flower garden, and riverside deck. Children feed goats and rabbits while parents use the landscaped paths and café terraces for weekend outings. The site opened in 2022 as a lowland complement to the city's hilltop pine viewpoints.

Free

Prawirotaman Traditional Market

Pasar Prawirotaman is a neighbourhood market in Yogyakarta's guesthouse district south of Malioboro. Stalls sell produce, snacks, and household goods to residents and budget travellers staying along the lane of small hotels and cafés. The market serves the student and backpacker quarter between the Code River and the southern suburbs.
