Explore Al Ain

A Brief History

Sustained for millennia by its complex falaj irrigation networks, the Al Ain oasis served as a vital agricultural anchor in the deep desert. Its formidable mud-brick forts and shaded palm groves preserve the ancient Bedouin heritage that fundamentally shaped the founding of the UAE. This rich historical tapestry established a strong foundation that continues to shape the city's dynamic contemporary identity.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

Mall of Al Ain

Al Ain Mall is a top shopping destination in Al Ain, offering a wide array of fashion and lifestyle stores, including popular brands like H&M, Mumus, and Max. In addition to shopping, the mall features a hypermarket for daily needs, an ice rink for recreational activities, and a 4-screen cinema for entertainment. Visitors can also enjoy dining options such as Burger King and Starbucks.

Free

Al Ain Zoo

Al Ain Zoo is a renowned conservation center and wildlife park in Al Ain, boasting an impressive collection of over 4,000 animals from 200 different species. The zoo offers engaging experiences for families, including feeding shows and opportunities to observe predatory animals, wild birds, and various wildlife. Visitors can explore spacious enclosures that house indigenous and exotic species such as Arabian oryx, Barbary sheep, rhinos, hippos, tigers, lions, giraffes, lemurs and more.

Free

Hazza bin Zayed Stadium

Hazza bin Zayed Stadium is a modern sports arena and structure in Al Ain, United Arab Emirates. Its contemporary design draws inspiration from the shape of a palm tree trunk. The stadium is part of a vibrant development that includes a variety of dining options and cafes, making it an ideal destination for both leisure and business travelers. Additionally, the area offers conveniences such as pharmacies and banks, creating a well-rounded experience for visitors.

Free

Al Ain Oasis

Al Ain Oasis is a sprawling 1200-hectare expanse featuring palm plantations, walking paths, and bike rentals. It offers a serene experience with its tranquil atmosphere and shaded pathways lined with various fruit trees. The oasis provides insights into ancient farming techniques using traditional irrigation systems to cultivate thousands of date palms and other fruits.

Free

Al Jahli Park

Al Jahili Park is a prominent, beautifully landscaped public park located in the center of Al Ain. It is situated adjacent to the historic Al Jahili Fort, providing a lush, green contrast to the traditional mud-brick architecture. The park features wide lawns, mature trees, and walking paths, making it a popular destination for family outings and relaxation.

Free

Al Jahili Fort

Al Jahili Fort, built in the 1890s, is a significant historic site in the United Arab Emirates. It stands as castles in Al Ain City and showcases local military architecture. The fort attracts tourists with its landscaped gardens, exhibitions, and a bookshop. Once a summer residence for royals, it now houses a visitor information center and galleries hosting temporary and permanent exhibitions.

Free

Bait Mohammed Bin Khalifa

Nestled in the heart of Al Ain, Bait Mohammed Bin Khalifa is a charming pastel-hued house that has become a vibrant cultural hub since its restoration and reopening in 2022. Originally built in 1958, this historic site offers visitors a glimpse into the transformative era of the UAE's history, particularly following the discovery of oil. The space not only showcases exhibits on its revival and UNESCO World Heritage sites but also provides creative spaces for local artists and an inviting café.

Free

AlRumaila Fort

Al Rumaila Fort, also known as the Muraijib Fort, is a historic defensive structure located in Al Ain. Built in the early 20th century, the fort features traditional mud-brick architecture, a central courtyard, and watchtowers. It provides insight into the region's historical military architecture and the traditional methods used to protect the vital oasis settlements.

Free

Al Ain Adventure

Nestled in the picturesque Ain Al Fayda region of Abu Dhabi, Al Ain Adventure is a must-visit for thrill-seekers and families alike. This remarkable aqua park features the Middle East's first man-made whitewater rafting facility, alongside exciting options for surfing and kayaking. With a family pool, kids' splash area, and an aerial obstacle course, there's something to keep everyone entertained throughout the day.

Free

Al Mubazzarah Park

Nestled on the slopes of Jebel Mountain, Al Mubazzarah Park offers a natural oasis with lush greenery and rocky landscapes. The park is particularly enchanting at sunset, creating a picturesque setting. Visitors can indulge in the hot springs for a rejuvenating experience to soothe body aches and fatigue. Families can enjoy spacious open areas with designated spots for barbecues and shaded seating.
