Al Ain

Falaj irrigation sustained oasis agriculture inland from the Gulf for millennia. Mud-brick forts and palm gardens made Al Ain a inland counterpart to coastal pearling towns and a home landscape for the Al Nahyan leadership that formed Abu Dhabi and the UAE. Jebel Hafeet tombs push the oasis story back into Bronze Age burial landscapes. Al Jahili Fort exhibitions interpret oasis defence and date agriculture together.
