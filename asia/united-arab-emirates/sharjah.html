Sharjah

Nineteenth-century pearling and trade made Sharjah the lower Gulf's leading port before Dubai's later rise. Late-twentieth-century museum and heritage-area policy repositioned the emirate as a cultural counterweight. The Heart of Sharjah lanes restore that mercantile street grain. UNESCO naming Sharjah as Cultural Capital of the Arab World reinforced that museum strategy.
