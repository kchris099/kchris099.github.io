Destination Guide — Sharjah

Explore Sharjah

A Brief History

Flourishing as a wealthy pearling and maritime trade hub in the 19th century, Sharjah was the most important port on the lower Gulf. Its deliberate late-20th-century focus on rigorous historical preservation established the emirate as the undisputed cultural and intellectual capital of the UAE. This continuous historical development established a lasting framework that strongly informs the area's modern cultural evolution.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

Al Noor Mosque

Al Noor Mosque, situated in Sharjah near Al Majaz Park and Al Noor Island, is a remarkable religious site inspired by the Ottoman-era Blue Mosque in Istanbul. This grand mosque boasts traditional Islamic architecture with a striking gold-trimmed dome and towering minarets. While it serves as a place of worship for all visitors, it also offers guided tours on specific days to provide insights into Emirati culture and history.

Free

City Centre Sharjah

City Centre Sharjah, situated in the heart of Sharjah on Al Wahda Street, has recently been revamped to offer an enhanced shopping and entertainment experience. With 143 international stores, diverse dining options, and the first 12-screen VOX Cinemas with GOLD by Rhodes, it caters to various needs. The mall also features a hypermarket, food court, and kids' entertainment center including Magic Planet.

Free

Sahara Centre

Sahara Centre in Sharjah is a vibrant mall that offers an extensive array of shopping, dining, and entertainment options. It houses a diverse selection of global brands including Levis, Aldo, Toys R Us, Samsung, and Adidas. The mall also features a food court with multi-cuisine restaurants, boutique spas for relaxation, and a luxurious cinema for movie enthusiasts. Additionally, Sahara Centre has received several prestigious awards for its outstanding services.

Free

Al Fahidi Historical Neighbourhood

Al Fahidi Historical Neighbourhood, located along the Dubai Creek, offers a glimpse into the rich heritage of the emirate. This living museum features traditional wind-tower architecture and maze-like alleyways that transport visitors to a bygone era. The area is home to art galleries, cafes, boutique hotels housed in restored heritage buildings, and the Dubai Museum which showcases exhibits on archaeology, Emirati culture and traditions.

89 AED

Dubai Dolphinarium

The Dubai Dolphinarium is a major indoor family entertainment facility located in Creek Park. It features a large, climate-controlled arena where visitors can watch very trained bottlenose dolphins and fur seals perform acrobatic shows and interactive displays. The facility also includes a tropical bird show and offers opportunities for close-up encounters with the animals.

25 AED

Sharjah Aquarium

Sharjah Aquarium is an educational attraction featuring 20 separate tanks spread over two floors, showcasing nearly 100 different species. Located in the center of the waterfront, Al Qasba offers a unique package for families and children to explore Arabian culture through various galleries, theaters, and activity centers. Additionally, entrepreneurs can conduct business around the Multaqa Al Qasba Conference Centre.

Free

Al Mamzar Beach Park

Al Mamzar Beach Park, located at the Dubai-Sharjah border, offers a vast expanse of landscaped greenery, four pristine beaches, and various recreational facilities such as swimming pools, children's games, and bike rentals. Visitors can enjoy paddleboarding in the calm waters while taking in scenic views of Sharjah. The park also features barbecue areas, volleyball pitches, basketball courts, and a small skate park.

Free

Al Mamzer Beach

Al Mamzer Beach, located in Dubai, is a popular destination for locals and tourists alike. The beach park offers a peaceful atmosphere with landscapes of blue waters and lush palm and coconut trees. Visitors can enjoy various activities such as BBQs, jet skiing, sunbathing, swimming, picnicking, and more on its 5 sandy stretches. Families often visit the beach for water sports like kayaking and jet skiing while children can partake in various water games.

Free

Al Qasba

Al Qasba is a premier cultural, entertainment, and leisure destination in Sharjah. The complex is centered around a picturesque, kilometer-long canal lined with numerous restaurants, cafes, and art galleries. It features the prominent Eye of the Emirates Ferris wheel and hosts various cultural festivals, making it a vibrant, family-friendly hub in the city.

Free

Souq AlShanasiyah

Souq AlShanasiyah is a historical market located in Sharjah, known for its remarkable facilities and diverse range of products. The market is organized with specific stores selling specific items, making it easy to navigate. As part of the Heart of Sharjah development project, excavations revealed the foundations of the old souq that used to link different areas. This covered street offers traditional items as souvenirs and features fans to keep shoppers cool in warm weather.

Free

Rolla Square Park

Rolla Square Park in Sharjah is a serene and well-maintained park that offers a peaceful escape from the city's hustle and bustle. Opened to the public in 2012, this modern park features playgrounds, fountains, pathways, and vast green spaces perfect for walking or jogging. The park's historical significance dates back to the 1800s when the first banyan tree was planted in Al Rolla square.

Free

King Faisal Mosque

Located in the city center of Sharjah, King Faisal Mosque is a monumental 1980s structure that embodies Islamic and Modern Architecture principles. The mosque features vast prayer halls and ample parking space. Its design, resembling a Jenga tower with rotated levels creating a multi-pointed star pattern, sets it apart as an landmark in Sharjah.

Free

Central Souk

Discover the vibrant and historic Blue Souk in Sharjah, also known as the central souk. This market is adorned with a striking blue tile design and boasts over 600 shops offering a wide array of goods, including gold, electronics, clothing, perfume, and regional specialties. Divided into sections, the upper level features arts, crafts, clothing, and souvenirs while the water-facing side showcases traditional Bedouin jewelry and stones.

Free

Sharjah Corniche

Sharjah Corniche is a waterfront promenade that runs along the Arabian Gulf, offering a delightful mix of natural beauty and urban charm. The time to visit is from November to March when the weather is perfect for outdoor activities like walking, jogging, cycling, and rollerblading. take in scenic views of the Gulf, lush gardens, and the Eye of the Emirates Ferris wheel by strolling or renting bikes.

Free

City Centre Ajman

City Centre Ajman is the largest mall in the emirate, boasting over 80 international and local brands, a 9-screen cinema hall, and an entertainment center. With almost 35,000 square meters of retail space, it attracts around 10.5 million visitors annually. The addition of a new wing in 2017 brought in 30 new stores, with plans for further redevelopment including more parking levels and energy-efficient design.

Free

UAE Pygmy Zoo

The UAE Pygmy Zoo, located in Ajman, is a unique wildlife attraction that offers a variety of bird, reptile, and mammal species. The zoo features a petting farm area with adorable miniature animals. It is situated in Al Zorah Marina 1 and provides family-friendly entertainment such as camel and horse rides, trampolines, slides, and stage activities for children. Visitors can also enjoy interacting with parrots, cats, dogs, ponies, and more.

Free

Ajman Marina

Ajman Marina is a modern, developing waterfront area located along the city's coastline. The wide, paved promenade is popular for walking, jogging, and cycling, offering pleasant views of the Arabian Gulf and the luxury yachts moored in the harbor. It provides a relaxed, scenic environment away from the busier commercial districts of the city.

Free

Marsa Ajman

Marsa Ajman is a prominent, upscale waterfront development featuring a mix of residential, commercial, and leisure facilities. The area includes a scenic marina, a wide promenade, and a variety of dining and retail options. It is designed to enhance the city's coastal lifestyle, offering a modern, vibrant environment for residents and tourists.

Free

Al Tallah Camel Race Course

Al Tallah Camel Race Course is a popular venue for camel racing, offering traditional tents and food stalls selling local specialties. It's a great spot for camping near the race course, especially during the cold season from November to December. Visitors can enjoy BBQ parties and night camping while taking in the incredible weather. However, it's important to maintain courtesy and respect for the environment by properly disposing of garbage.

Free

Sharjah National Park

Sharjah National Park is a sprawling green oasis that spans over 100 acres, making it the largest park in Sharjah, United Arab Emirates. This picturesque destination is perfect for families looking to enjoy quality time together amidst well-maintained lawns and lush greenery. With ample space for picnics, children can delight in the playgrounds while soccer fields invite some friendly competition. The park also features serene water ponds and shaded areas ideal for relaxation.
