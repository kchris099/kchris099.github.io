Sharjah

Nineteenth-century pearling and trade made Sharjah the lower Gulf's leading port before Dubai's later rise. Late-twentieth-century museum and heritage-area policy repositioned the emirate as a cultural counterweight. The Heart of Sharjah lanes restore that mercantile street grain. UNESCO naming Sharjah as Cultural Capital of the Arab World reinforced that museum strategy.

Al Noor Mosque

Gold trim on the dome and tall minarets mark Al Noor Mosque's traditional Islamic silhouette along Sharjah's waterfront. Worship continues daily, and guided tours on selected days explain Emirati religious practice and building details. Outside prayer, the exterior and main halls are usually viewable.

Al Fahidi Historical Neighbourhood

Al Fahidi Historical Neighbourhood along Dubai Creek preserves wind-tower houses now used as galleries, cafes, boutique hotels, and museum spaces interpreting Emirati archaeology and culture.
