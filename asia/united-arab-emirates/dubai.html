Dubai

Dubai grew from a fishing and pearling village on the Persian Gulf coast of the Trucial States. The Maktoum family has ruled since 1833. Oil discovered in 1966 funded infrastructure, though trade and aviation later dominated the economy. The creek district preserves merchants' houses and souqs. Federation with Abu Dhabi formed the United Arab Emirates in 1971. Modern harbor and airport expansion shaped the late 20th-century city.

Burj Khalifa

Burj Khalifa rises from Downtown Dubai as a mixed-use tower completed in 2010, with offices, residences, hotel space, restaurants and public observation levels. Its tapering form, stepped setbacks and reinforced concrete core were designed for extreme height and desert wind; timed elevators provide views across the city and coast, while access differs by deck and commercial package.

The Dubai Fountain

The Dubai Fountain stages choreographed water, light, and music shows on the Burj Khalifa Lake beside Dubai Mall, with jets rising in timed sequences across the basin. Crowds gather along the promenade and mall bridges for evening performances. The spectacle sits at the foot of the Burj Khalifa rather than inside the tower itself.

Dubai Mall Promenade - Fountain Show

The Dubai Fountain contains a choreographed fountain system located on the 12-hectare artificial Burj Khalifa Lake, at the center of the Downtown Dubai development in Dubai, United Arab Emirates. It was designed by WET Design, a California-based company also responsible for the fountains at the Bellagio Hotel Lake in Las Vegas.

Dubai Mall

Dubai Mall concentrates multi-level retail, dining terraces, and display atriums beside Downtown Dubai's street grid. Shoppers move between fashion floors and food courts during ordinary commercial hours. Plaza and roadway approaches connect the complex to neighboring towers and public spaces.

Dubai Gold Souk

Gold jewelry shops line the covered lanes of Dubai Gold Souk in Deira, where traders display necklaces, coins, and set pieces behind glass. Bargaining and window browsing fill the alleys during market hours. Nearby spice and textile streets make an easy combined Old Dubai walk.

Dubai Spice Souk

Sacks of saffron, dried limes, frankincense, and mixed spices crowd the stalls of Dubai Spice Souk. Aromatic shops sit close to the Gold Souk lanes in Deira's traditional trading quarter. Travellers browse during standard market hours and continue on foot to adjoining food vendors.

Al Fahidi Historical Neighbourhood

Al Fahidi Historical Neighbourhood preserves lanes and courtyard buildings beside Dubai Creek, many restored with gypsum, coral stone, timber and wind towers. Former residences now contain museums, galleries, cafes, cultural organizations and guesthouses. Restoration and adaptive reuse interpret part of Dubai's pre-oil mercantile history within a living urban district.

25.2642° N / 55.3005° E

Dubai Frame

Dubai Frame stands in Zabeel Park as a 150-meter observation structure shaped like an enormous rectangular frame. Elevators lead to a glass-floored sky bridge that contrasts older districts toward the creek with newer towers to the south; ground-level galleries present a simplified story of Dubai's development, while heat, haze, queues, and timed entry affect the visit.

25.2355° N / 55.3003° E
