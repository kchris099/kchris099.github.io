Abu Dhabi

Bani Yas pearling and fishing camps occupied the island until oil revenues from the 1960s funded a planned capital of corniches and cultural districts. Qasr Al Hosn recalls the pre-oil fort; the modern skyline is the state-building project that followed. Saadiyat Island museums extend that state cultural project beyond the original island core. The Founder's Memorial narrates the federation story beside the modern corniche.

Qasr Al Watan

Qasr Al Watan forms the public ceremonial portion of Abu Dhabi's presidential palace complex. Marble halls, domes, geometric decoration, diplomatic gifts, a library and exhibits on government and national institutions present an official account of the United Arab Emirates.

02 AdmissionFree

UAE Presidential Palace

Abu Dhabi's Presidential Palace complex includes Qasr Al Watan, the ceremonial building opened for public visits. Processional courts, a vast central hall, formal meeting rooms and displays on federal institutions occupy the visitor route, while other parts of the larger compound remain devoted to government work. The architecture uses stone, domes and geometric ornament to express the modern state's official identity.

Emirates Palace Marina

Emirates Palace Marina is an exclusive beach club located within one of the UAE's most luxurious hotels. Despite its exclusivity, the beach club welcomes day guests to enjoy its 1.3km private sandy beach, two elegant swimming pools, and a range of water sports activities such as windsurfing and kayaking. The hotel itself is known for its opulence and legendary levels of luxury.

Al Wahda Mall

Al Wahda Mall, opened in 2007 in Abu Dhabi, gathers fashion, jewelry, electronics, home goods, and a children's zone under one large retail roof. Dining and entertainment floors extend visits beyond shopping. The mall's central location and brand mix make it a high-traffic leisure hub for residents and travellers.

Etihad Towers

Five towers form the Etihad Towers skyline cluster, mixing offices, apartments, hotels, and The Avenue retail floors. Luxury labels such as Hermes, Cartier, and Rolex sit among restaurants and commercial suites. Postcard views of the modern towers draw walkers along the corniche approaches.

The Founder’s Memorial

The Founder's Memorial commemorates Sheikh Zayed bin Sultan Al Nahyan through gardens, interpretive displays and a suspended artwork called The Constellation. Thousands of geometric shapes align from selected viewpoints to form a three-dimensional portrait after dark. The open site near Abu Dhabi's Corniche emphasizes state memory, environmental themes.

Arabic-inspired glass architecture on Al Maryah Island holds more than 130 brand outlets, including about fifty firsts for Abu Dhabi. Designer floors list Jimmy Choo, Celine, and Alexander McQueen among other labels. The mall sits in the Central Business District street grid for easy walk-in access.

Abu Dhabi Mall

Abu Dhabi Mall in the downtown core holds more than two hundred stores, cinemas, and supervised play zones such as Prison Island and Xtreme Zone. Perfume counters and international fashion brands fill the atriums. Families often combine retail time with a film or kids' activity without leaving the complex.

Dhow Harbour

The Dhow Harbour in Abu Dhabi offers a glimpse into the city's maritime past, contrasting sharply with the modern skyline. The harbor is filled with traditional wooden dhows, historically used for pearl diving and trading. Visitors can observe fishermen maintaining their nets and boats, providing an authentic, traditional atmosphere along the busy Corniche.

Marina Mall Abu Dhabi is a premier shopping destination located near Heritage Village on the Breakwater in the southwest corner of Abu Dhabi city. This expansive complex has over 400 stores, including designer boutiques, and offers a diverse range of entertainment options such as a bowling alley, movie theater, ice rink, and amusement center with arcade games and rides.

Emirates Heritage Village

Near Abu Dhabi's breakwater, Emirates Heritage Village reconstructs selected aspects of pre-oil life through tents, palm structures, workshops and displays on irrigation, crafts and trade. The complex is an interpretive cultural attraction created in modern times. Demonstrations, shops and programs vary, while the setting offers views toward the city skyline across the water.

Corniche Beach

Eight kilometers of white sand and turquoise water line Abu Dhabi's Corniche Beach, which holds international clean-water recognition. Lifeguarded sections split family swimming from more active stretches along a landscaped boulevard. Foot access comes from corniche roads and continuous beachfront paths.

Family Park A

Along the Corniche, Family Park A spreads lawns and shade near the waterfront for outdoor play and quiet sitting. Residents use the green for evening walks and children's recreation beside the sea wall. Paths and planted beds organize the main public facilities facing the gulf.

Qasr Al Hosn

Dating from 1795, Qasr Al Hosn now combines the historic fort with the Cultural Foundation, National Consultative Council building, and House of Artisans. Museum galleries and festivals replaced decades of closed access. Exhibits include Al Sadu weaving traditions practiced by Bedouin women and recognized by UNESCO.
