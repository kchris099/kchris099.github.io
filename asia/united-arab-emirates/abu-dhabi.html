Destination Guide — Abu Dhabi

Explore Abu Dhabi

A Brief History

Historically a seasonal fishing and pearling settlement of the Bani Yas tribe, Abu Dhabi remained notably isolated until the mid-20th-century discovery of oil. The ensuing decades of hyper-development transformed the arid island into a monumental, meticulously planned global capital. This continuous historical development established a lasting framework that strongly informs the area's modern cultural evolution.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

Qasr Al Watan

Qasr Al Watan, located at one end of Abu Dhabi's main Corniche, is a grand presidential palace made of domed marble and limestone. Open to the public since 2019, it has quickly become a cherished cultural gem in the UAE. For an entry fee of Dhs60, visitors can explore its ornate interior featuring a mosaic-clad great hall, an extensive library with towering bookshelves, and exhibits that narrate the rich history of the nation.

Free

UAE Presidential Palace

Qasr Al Watan is the dramatic, working Presidential Palace in Abu Dhabi, recently opened to the public. The massive complex features expansive, intricate Arabian architecture, vast domes, and opulent interiors adorned with gold and marble. It offers visitors profound insights into the UAE's governance, cultural heritage, and traditional craftsmanship.

Free

Emirates Palace Marina

Emirates Palace Marina is an exclusive beach club located within one of the UAE's most luxurious hotels. Despite its exclusivity, the beach club welcomes day guests to enjoy its 1.3km private sandy beach, two elegant swimming pools, and a range of water sports activities such as windsurfing and kayaking. The hotel itself is renowned for its opulence and legendary levels of luxury.

Free

Al Wahda Mall

Al Wahda Mall, established in 2007, is a premier shopping destination in Abu Dhabi that attracts over 30 million visitors annually. Spanning across 3.3 million square feet, the mall features over 350 brands including global chain retailers, a grocery store, and more than 50 eateries. It boasts an impressive retail mix covering fashion, jewelry, electronics, home improvement, entertainment, health & beauty products and a dedicated kids' section with renowned high-street brands.

Free

Etihad Towers

Etihad Towers is a contemporary complex of five towers in Abu Dhabi, offering a mix of shopping, dining, luxury accommodations, and an observation deck. The Avenue at Etihad Towers is a high-end retail destination featuring luxury brands like Hermes, Cartier, and Rolex as well as exclusive restaurants and commercial spaces. Known for its modern architecture and often featured in postcards, the complex includes offices, apartments, and hotels.

Free

The Founder’s Memorial

Nestled just a few blocks away, The Founder’s Memorial stands as a tribute to Sheikh Zayed bin Sultan Al Nahyan, the first president of the UAE. This remarkable site features gardens ideal for leisurely walks and offers engaging multimedia presentations that delve into the life and legacy of this influential leader. A highlight is the Constellation, an eye-catching three-dimensional portrait of Sheikh Zayed that captures his essence beautifully.

Free

The Galleria Al Maryah Island

The Galleria Al Maryah Island is an elegant shopping destination located in the heart of Abu Dhabi's new Central Business District. Housed in a Arabic-style glass building, it offers over 130 world-renowned brand outlets, including 50 firsts for Abu Dhabi. Visitors can explore designer labels and high-end brands such as Jimmy Choo, Celine, and Alexander McQueen.

Free

Abu Dhabi Mall

Located in downtown Abu Dhabi, the expansive Abu Dhabi Mall offers a plethora of shopping and entertainment options. With over 200 retail stores, visitors can explore popular brands like Zara and shop for fragrant perfumes. The mall also features a Fitness First outlet for those looking to squeeze in a workout. Families can enjoy supervised play areas such as Prison Island and Xtreme Zone for older kids, while Vox Cinemas offers the latest movie releases.

Free

Dhow Harbour

The Dhow Harbour in Abu Dhabi offers a glimpse into the city's maritime past, contrasting sharply with the modern skyline. The harbor is filled with traditional wooden dhows, historically used for pearl diving and trading. Visitors can observe fishermen maintaining their nets and boats, providing an authentic, traditional atmosphere along the bustling Corniche.

Free

Marina Mall Abu Dhabi

Marina Mall Abu Dhabi is a premier shopping destination located near Heritage Village on the Breakwater in the southwest corner of Abu Dhabi city. This expansive complex boasts over 400 stores, including designer boutiques, and offers a diverse range of entertainment options such as a bowling alley, movie theater, ice rink, and amusement center with arcade games and rides.

Free

Emirates Heritage Village

Located near Marina Mall in Abu Dhabi, Emirates Heritage Village is a reconstructed Arab village run by the Emirates Heritage Club. It offers visitors a glimpse into traditional Emirati culture and lifestyle before the discovery of oil in the region. The village showcases the Bedouin way of life with traditional tents, coffee, and displays of the Falaj irrigation system used for palm grove irrigation.

Free

WaveBreaker

WaveBreaker is a popular, relaxed beach bar and restaurant located at the Hilton Dubai Jumeirah on the JBR Walk. Situated directly on the sand, it offers a casual dining experience with excellent views of the Arabian Gulf and the Ain Dubai observation wheel. It is a favored spot for enjoying sunset drinks and beachside leisure.

Free

Corniche Beach

Corniche Beach in Abu Dhabi is a popular city beach with international clean-water status. The main beachfront stretches for eight kilometers, offering white sand, turquoise water, and a beautifully landscaped boulevard. Divided into several sections with lifeguarded beaches, it caters to families and action-seekers alike. The chic promenade features food stalls, cafes, and sitting-out areas that create a tropical paradise ambiance.

Free

Family Park A

Family Park A is a large, well-maintained public park located along the Corniche in Abu Dhabi. Designed specifically for family recreation, the park features extensive shaded picnic areas, barbecue facilities, and numerous modern playgrounds. It provides a safe, green environment for residents to relax and enjoy outdoor activities near the waterfront.

Free

Qasr Al Hosn

Qasr Al Hosn is a historic palace and fort in Abu Dhabi, dating back to 1795. It was previously inaccessible to the public but has now been transformed into a museum with cultural programs and festivals. The site includes the historic fort, the Cultural Foundation, the National Consultative Council building, and the House of Artisans. Visitors can learn about the city's history and its important cultural tradition of Al Sadu weaving by Bedouin women, recognized by UNESCO.
