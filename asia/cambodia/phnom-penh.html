Destination Guide — Phnom Penh

Explore Phnom Penh

A Brief History

Phnom Penh is in Cambodia. Established as a regional settlement beyond an older fortified core, the city developed through industrialization in the late 19th century, post-war reconstruction, and modern civic expansion that reshaped its public spaces, markets, and surrounding landscape. Museums, markets, and street plans in Phnom Penh still show layers from those periods.

Top Sights & Activities

$1

Wat Phnom Daun Penh

Wat Phnom Daun Penh is a Buddhist temple that is the tallest religious structure in Phnom Penh, with its origins dating back to 1373. This site is steeped in legend, particularly the tale of a wealthy woman named Penh who discovered four bronze Buddha statues floating down the Mekong River. To honor these sacred relics, she constructed an artificial hill, Phnom, which ultimately gave the city its name.

$10

Royal Palace of Cambodia

The Royal Palace, a palace complex established between 1866 and 1870, is an essential stop for anyone visiting Cambodia. Located along the banks of the Chaktomuk River, this architectural gem showcases Khmer design with subtle French influences. As visitors wander through its expansive grounds, encounter significant structures like the active Throne Hall and the enchanting Moonlight Pavilion, where traditional Khmer dance performances draw travellers.

$10

Silver Pagoda

Silver Pagoda is a visitor site in Phnom Penh, Cambodia. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest. Silver Pagoda remains part of the public visitor circuit in Phnom Penh. Local maps identify Silver Pagoda within Phnom Penh, Cambodia.

Free

Wat Botum Park

Wat Botum Park is home to the ancient Khmer Buddhist pagoda, Wat Botum, also known as the Temple of Lotus Blossoms. Built in the 15th century by King Ponhea Yat, this colorful temple showcases photogenic saffron-colored robes and houses important relics and paintings. The park adjacent to the temple offers a playground for kids and open grassy areas for exercise, travellers can explore the Cambodia-Vietnam Friendship Monument nearby.

Free

Royal Palace Park

Royal Palace Park is a fortified historic site in Phnom Penh, Cambodia. The Royal Palace Park is a must-visit attraction in Phnom Penh, serving as the official royal residence for Cambodia's king. While certain parts of the extravagant palace complex are open to travellers, much of it consists of beautifully maintained gardens and golden temples. Walls, courtyards, or towers form the main visitor route when the grounds are open.

Free

Independence Monument

Independence Monument, a 20-meter tower located at the intersection of Norodom Blvd and Sihanouk Blvd, was unveiled in 1962 to commemorate Cambodia's independence from France. Shaped like a lotus flower, it is viewed from the park to the west. Adjacent to it is the Norodom Sihanouk Memorial, housing a bronze statue of King Norodom Sihanouk.

Free

National Olympic Stadium

National Olympic Stadium is a stadium in Phnom Penh, Cambodia. The National Olympic Stadium in Phnom Penh may not have hosted major international sporting events, but it remains a significant landmark. The 1960s architectural style is evident in its striking lines and sharp blocks. Event access follows posted ticket and gate procedures.

Free

Orussey Market

Orussey Market is a market in Phnom Penh, Cambodia. Orussey Market, located in Phnom Penh, is the city's largest traditional market spanning three floors. Stalls sell food and goods during regular market hours. Orussey Market remains part of the public visitor circuit in Phnom Penh.

$6

Choeung Ek Genocidal Center

Choeung Ek Genocidal Center is a memorial in Phnom Penh, serving as a memorial of the tragic history during the Khmer Rouge regime. This site, often referred to as the Killing Fields, is where countless victims were executed and buried. Travellers are met with an emotional experience as they explore mass graves and a striking glass-walled stupa filled with skulls, symbolizing the loss of life.

Free

Toul Tompong Market (Russian Market)

Toul Tompong Market (Russian Market) is a market in Phnom Penh, Cambodia. From refreshing fruit smoothies and aromatic Khmer coffee to hearty noodles, rice dishes, soups, and fresh salads, there's something to satisfy every palate. Stalls sell food and goods during regular market hours.
