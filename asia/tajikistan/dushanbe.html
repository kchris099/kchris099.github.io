Destination Guide — Dushanbe

Explore Dushanbe

A Brief History

Dushanbe developed from a small market village named for its Monday bazaar and became capital of Soviet Tajikistan in 1924. Wide Stalin-era avenues, parks, and neoclassical government buildings were expanded after independence in 1991. Museums, the flagpole square, and nearby Hissar Fortress illustrate how the city administers a mountainous republic at the crossroads of Persian and Central Asian cultural traditions.

Top Sights & Activities

Monument of Ismail Samani

The Monument of Ismail Samani is a significant landmark located in Dushanbe, Tajikistan. It is a tribute to the national hero and ancient king, Ismoil Somoni, celebrated across the country. The monument, towering over 25 meters high and adorned with gold decorations, was erected in 1999 to honor the 1100th anniversary of the state of the Samanids.

Free

National Library of Tajikistan

National Library of Tajikistan is a visitor site in Dushanbe, Tajikistan. It is recognized as the largest library in Central Asia and houses over three million books. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest. National Library of Tajikistan remains part of the public visitor circuit in Dushanbe.

Free

Rudaki Park

Rudaki Park, situated in Dushanbe, Tajikistan, is a careful designed public park surrounded by significant landmarks and structures. The park has fountains, sculptures, manicured lawns, and an array of active flowers. It is home to the giant flagpole and a well-photographed sculpture of Rudaki with views of the city's buildings. This makes it an ideal spot for a summer outing.

Free

Flagpole of Tajikistan

Dushanbe, the capital city of Tajikistan, is home to the Dushanbe Flagpole, one of the tallest free-standing flagpoles globally. Standing at a towering height of 165 meters, this monumental structure proudly displays Tajikistan's national flag, which measures 60 meters in length and weighs 420 kilograms.

Free

Hаji Ya'qub Mosque

Hаji Ya'qub Mosque is a religious building in Dushanbe, Tajikistan. The Haji Ya'qub Masjid in Dushanbe is a and significant religious site, as well as an architectural wonder. The exterior and main interior spaces are usually viewable outside service times.

Free

Victory Monument

Victory Monument, located in Dushanbe, Tajikistan, is a significant tribute to the Great Patriotic War. As of March 2024, it is undergoing relocation within the city. The tank and some commemorative signs have been removed, leaving behind two pillars adorned with stone engravings that capture various wartime moments, these are reminiscent of Egyptian art in their style.

TJS 30

Ayni Opera House

Ayni Opera House, named after a prominent Tajik poet, has glamorous and imposing architecture with tall white columns. Constructed in the 1940s, it is a cultural gem located in the center of Dushanbe city. The building hosts various opera shows, ballet performances, and classical concerts throughout the year. Even if visitors are not a fan of opera or ballet, visiting the exterior is rewarding.

TJS 5

Botanical Garden

Botanical Garden is a public park in Dushanbe, Tajikistan. Nature enthusiasts will find delight in exploring the diverse collection of flora on display here. Paths, lawns, and shaded areas make up the main public facilities. Botanical Garden remains part of the public visitor circuit in Dushanbe.

TJS 25

Navruz Palace

In Dushanbe, Tajikistan's capital city, lies the Navruz Palace. This cultural gem pays homage to the country's rich heritage and is a symbol of national identity, particularly celebrating the significant Navruz festival. Originally intended as a teahouse, it was transformed into a grand palace during construction due to its interior crafted by artisans from across Tajikistan.

Free

The Ismaili Centre, Dushanbe

The Ismaili Centre, Dushanbe is a visitor site in Dushanbe, Tajikistan. Guided tours offer insights into its unique design and rich history. Visitors praise the warm welcome from volunteers who provide informative tours and access to the center's library. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.
