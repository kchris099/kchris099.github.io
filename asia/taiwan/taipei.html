Destination Guide — Taipei

Explore Taipei

A Brief History

Taipei developed around Dutch and Qing-era settlements in the Taipei Basin and became the seat of the Republic of China government after 1949. Japanese colonial boulevards, Chiang Kai-shek Memorial Hall, and Taipei 101 reflect 20th-century political change and economic growth. Temples such as Longshan, night markets, and museums show how the city blends Fujianese folk religion, indigenous heritage, and modern Taiwanese identity.

National Palace Museum

The National Palace Museum is a active hub for anyone captivated by the rich history of Chinese history and culture. Home to one of the world's most extensive collections of Chinese art and artifacts, this museum showcases over 8,000 years of civilization. Visitors can marvel at an array of items, including prehistoric relics, jade pieces, detailed porcelain works, and historical documents that offer a glimpse into the lives during the imperial Ming and Qing dynasties.
