Explore Taipei

A Brief History

Taipei developed around Dutch and Qing-era settlements in the Taipei Basin and became the seat of the Republic of China government after 1949. Japanese colonial boulevards, Chiang Kai-shek Memorial Hall, and Taipei 101 reflect 20th-century political change and economic growth. Temples such as Longshan, night markets, and museums show how the city blends Fujianese folk religion, indigenous heritage, and modern Taiwanese identity.

Top Sights & Activities

National Palace Museum

The National Palace Museum is a vibrant hub for anyone captivated by the rich tapestry of Chinese history and culture. Home to one of the world's most extensive collections of Chinese art and artifacts, this museum showcases over 8,000 years of civilization. Visitors can marvel at an impressive array of items, including prehistoric relics, exquisite jade pieces, intricate porcelain works, and historical documents that offer a glimpse into the lives during the imperial Ming and Qing dynasties.

NT$100

Chiang Kai-Shek Shilin Residence

Chiang Kai-Shek Shilin Residence, the former home of Republic of China President Chiang Kai-shek, boasts gardens and scenic landscapes. The residence is divided into two parts: the garden and the main building. Mrs. Jiang's favorite rose garden, a Western-style garden popular for wedding photos, and traditional Chinese garden features like arch bridges and flowing water create an atmosphere reminiscent of ancient Chinese courtyards.

Free

Chiang Kai-shek Memorial Hall

Nestled in the vibrant heart of Taipei, the Chiang Kai-shek Memorial Hall stands as a significant national landmark dedicated to honoring the legacy of Chiang Kai-shek, Taiwan's former president. This impressive structure is surrounded by a sprawling park that features serene fish ponds and beautifully landscaped gardens, making it an ideal spot for relaxation and reflection.

Free

National Concert Hall

The National Concert Hall is a part of the Chiang Kai-shek Memorial Hall complex in Taipei, Taiwan. It's an landmark featuring a white and blue domed hall that pays tribute to the former dictator. The area also includes the classical Chinese National Theater. Nearby, explore Ximending, known as the Harajuku of Taipei, with its unique teashops and restaurants.

Free

Liberty Square

Liberty Square, also known as Freedom Square, is a significant public gathering place in Taipei. Covering 240,000 square meters, it has been a popular spot since the 1970s. The square is home to three major landmarks: the Chiang Kai-shek Memorial Hall, the National Concert Hall, and the National Theater. Visitors can explore beautifully landscaped gardens and traditional structures surrounding the memorial hall.

Free

National Theater

The National Theatre Hall, located in Taipei City, is part of the Chiang Kai-shek Memorial Hall complex. It features a white and blue domed hall dedicated to the former dictator and also houses the classical Chinese National Theater. The ornate National Concert Hall stands opposite to it, creating a picturesque scene for visitors.

Free

Taipei 101 Shopping center

The Taipei 101 Shopping Center is a must-visit destination located within the Taipei 101 tower, offering an array of shops and dining options. The lower floors house this impressive mall, which features seven levels filled with international brands like Chanel, Gucci, Louis Vuitton, and more. Visitors can also explore the gourmet food court on the B1 level and find a variety of luxury items at the gift shop.

NT$600

Taipei 101 Observatory

The Taipei 101 Observatory is a must-visit destination for anyone looking to experience the vibrant city of Taipei from above. Located on the 89th floor, this sleek observation deck offers 360-degree views that stretch across the urban landscape and beyond, with lush hills framing the horizon. Visitors can access both indoor and outdoor viewing areas via a high-speed elevator, making it an exhilarating journey to one of the tallest buildings in the world.

Free

Eslite Spectrum Songyan

Eslite Spectrum Songyan is a charming mall located in a bustling shopping district. The interior features low ceilings and wooden designs, creating a cozy and inviting atmosphere. Each floor of the four-story mall showcases independent designers offering an array of products, from books on the fifth floor to artisanal bags, wallets, and jewelry on other levels.

Free

Taipei Arena

Situated in Downtown Taipei, the Taipei Arena is a modern venue that hosts a variety of events including ice skating, basketball games, and concerts. This indoor sporting arena, constructed in 2005, serves as the main stadium facility in the city and can accommodate major international sports events such as ice hockey, gymnastics, handball, and more. Additionally, it offers facilities for activities like basketball and tennis.

Free

Shenkeng Old Street

Shenkeng Old Street is a vibrant historical destination that captures the essence of traditional Taiwanese culture. Known for its delectable tofu specialties, this charming street offers a delightful escape from the hustle and bustle of Taipei's markets. Visitors can savor an array of tofu dishes, ranging from sweet to savory, while exploring quaint shops housed in beautifully preserved buildings.

NT$100

Taipei Zoo

Nestled in a picturesque setting, Taipei Zoo is an expansive indoor-outdoor haven that promises a delightful experience for visitors of all ages. This well-maintained zoo features thoughtfully designed enclosures that allow guests to observe a diverse array of animals, from playful rabbits and majestic elephants to exotic reptiles and the beloved giant pandas. Families will appreciate the dedicated children's area, as well as interactive opportunities for kids to engage with some of the animals.

Free

Lungshan Temple

Lungshan Temple, a historic gem nestled in Taipei, dates back to the 1700s and serves as a spiritual haven for both Buddhist and Taoist worshippers. This captivating temple is renowned for its architecture, featuring intricately carved stone dragon pillars and beautifully decorated eaves that draw visitors from all walks of life.

Free

Bopiliao Historical Block

Bopiliao Historical Block, situated in Wanhua, Taipei's oldest neighborhood, offers a glimpse into the city's 18th-century architecture and history. The pedestrianized street is part of a museum showcasing traditional Taiwanese artifacts and restored old buildings. Visitors can explore local treasures like bicycle taxis and children's toys while enjoying the preserved shops.

Free

Bitan Tourist Attraction

Bitan is a popular tourist attraction in New Taipei, offering scenic cliffs, pedal boats, eateries, and a suspension bridge for pedestrians. It is a riverside park with various amenities such as restaurants and paddle boat rentals, providing a great way to relax away from the city. The 200-meter long suspension bridge lights up at night to create a beautiful art show.

NT$30

National Taiwan Museum

The National Taiwan Museum is a venerable institution that offers a variety of exhibits, covering both the country's history and natural history. Located in the historic and political core of Zhongzheng district, visitors can explore Taiwan's complex history by touring nearby landmarks such as the Presidential Building and Chiang Kai-shek Memorial Hall. Additionally, the museum provides insight into the country's indigenous peoples through its exhibits.

Free

228 Peace Park

228 Peace Memorial Park is a serene and picturesque park in Taipei, Taiwan. It features winding paths, charming pavilions, and various monuments dedicated to the 228 Massacre. The park also houses a museum that commemorates the tragic events of the White Terror Period in 1947 when thousands of people were executed for opposing the government.

NT$40

The Lin Family Mansion and Garden

"The Lin Family Mansion and Garden" is a serene 4.9-acre estate showcasing an exquisite mansion and traditional Chinese garden design. Visitors can immerse themselves in the rich historical ambiance while exploring the meticulously crafted gardens. Nearby attractions include the popular "Air Force San-Chong Village No.1," offering interactive experiences for families, and the expansive "New Taipei Metropolitan Park," featuring vast lawns and inclusive playground equipment.

Free

Jieyun Temple

Jieyun Temple, an urban sanctuary adorned with a remarkable intricately carved arch, courtyard and sculpture garden, is situated next to a bustling night market. This revered establishment holds significant cultural value as it embodies Taiwanese traditions and serves as a spiritual haven where devotees seek divine blessings. The temple exudes tranquility, complemented by the presence of a picturesque garden in its vicinity.

Free

Crescent Bridge

Crescent Bridge is a modern steel arch bridge designed for pedestrians and cyclists, featuring a glass-bottom trail that offers views of the Tamsui River. It connects bikeways along Dahan River, spanning across Banqiao District and Xinzhuang District. The night lighting enhances the scenic beauty of the river at night. Nearby attractions include Banqiao 435 Art Zone and Xinzhuang Old Street, making it an ideal spot for a weekend trip.

Free

Taipei North Gate (ChengEn Gate)

Taipei North Gate, also known as Cheng'en Gate, is a historic 2-story gatehouse that dates back to the Qing Dynasty in the 19th century. This awe-inspiring landmark played a crucial role in the city's defensive fortifications and remains an important symbol of Taipei's rich cultural heritage. The gate has undergone renovations over the years but still retains its original architectural style.

Free

The Red House

The Red House, a historic octagonal building established in 1908, is a must-visit destination in Taipei. It houses various attractions such as exhibits, a teahouse, markets, and theaters. Surrounding the area are artistic hubs like Huashan 1914 Creative Park and charming street fairs offering unique finds. The bustling streets around The Red House are always filled with crowds enjoying street performances, themed restaurants, and innovative retail stores.

Free

Nishi Honganji Square

Nishi Honganji Remains is a reconstructed Buddhist temple complex in Taipei, Taiwan. It features a bell tower, archive building, and red-brick facade. The area also includes Eighty-Eightea Rinbansyo, an original Japanese-era wooden house where visitors can enjoy a traditional Taiwanese tea experience. This historical site was once the largest Japanese Buddhist temple in Taiwan during the Japanese era.

Free

Lin An Tai Historical House and Museum

Lin An Tai Historical House and Museum is a well-preserved traditional mansion from the 1800s, now serving as a museum set within a spacious garden. Originally belonging to the prosperous Lin family, who relocated from Fujian County in China, this historical site showcases the architectural principles of fengshui that were believed to bring prosperity. The estate features a 30-room Fujian-style house with a central courtyard, swallowtail roof, and period furniture.

Free

Dajia Riverside Park

Dajia Riverside Park, located in Taipei's Zhongzheng district, is a sprawling metropolitan green space along the Keelung River. It offers a wide range of amenities for visitors of all ages, including soccer pitches, tennis courts, baseball fields, playgrounds, and even a racetrack for radio-controlled cars. Families can enjoy picnics or bike rides on the extensive network of paths while taking in views of Miramar's Ferris Wheel.

Free

Xingtian Temple

Xingtian Temple is a modern temple dedicated to the patron saint of businessmen. It features large red doors and dragon sculptures. Located in a beautiful neighborhood with Japanese-era wooden homes, it offers a serene and clean environment despite attracting over 10,000 visitors daily. Unlike other temples in the area, Xingtian Temple focuses more on spirituality rather than history or tourism-friendly events.

Free

Taipei Confucius Temple

Nestled in the vibrant Datong District of Taipei, the Taipei Confucius Temple is a homage to the teachings of Confucius and a must-visit for anyone exploring Taiwan's rich cultural heritage. Originally built in 1879 and reconstructed in 1930 after its destruction during Japanese rule, this temple beautifully marries traditional Minnan-style architecture with modern educational experiences.

Free

823 Memorial Park

823 Memorial Park, also known as No. 4 Park, is a delightful urban retreat nestled in New Taipei City. This lush green space offers a variety of amenities for visitors of all ages, including sports courts and picnic areas perfect for family gatherings. The park is home to the National Taiwan Library, where unwind with a good book after enjoying the outdoors. Nearby, Dlcservice Cafe tempts passersby with its inviting aroma of coffee and delicious Japanese-style meal boxes.

Free

Kishu An Forest of Literature

Kishu An Forest of Literature is a Japanese-style pavilion originally built in 1917, offering literary exhibitions, cultural events, and a teahouse. The wooden lodge was reconstructed after two fires in the 1990s. Visitors can relax in the old-style house or visit the nearby bookstore with a restaurant. Additionally, there's an option to rent a small conference room for activities like academic discussions. The park also offers a quaint spot for lunch with healthy and delicious meals.

Free

Dharma Drum Mountain Nung Chan Monastery

Dharma Drum Mountain Nung Chan Monastery is a minimalist Buddhist temple located in the lush landscapes of Beitou district. The architectural wonder, completed in 1975, spans 2.5 acres and reflects early Taiwanese practices. It stands as influential sites in Chinese Buddhism. The monastery features a central lotus pond, a meditation hall, and resident monks.

Free

Guandu Temple

Nestled against the backdrop of a mountain and overlooking the serene Tamsui River, Guandu Temple is a example of traditional architecture that dates back to 1661. This vibrant folk temple, adorned with colorful pillars and intricate stone carvings, serves as a sanctuary dedicated to Matsu, the revered goddess of the sea.

Free

Raohe Night Market

Raohe Street Night Market is a lively and historic night market in Taipei, offering a wide array of shops and street food vendors. The market is a nostalgic representation of the city's culture, with traditional gates at the entrances and the Songshan Ciyou Temple nearby. Visitors can wander through narrow lanes adorned with red lanterns and enjoy local treats such as black pepper buns, beef noodle soup, and scallion pancakes.

Free

Songshan Fengtian Temple

Nestled in the lush foothills of Taipei, Songshan Fengtian Temple is a 19th-century temple complex that captivates visitors with its intricate and colorful carvings. As you approach, pass through an impressive gate leading to the main courtyard, where the temple's striking architecture truly shines. Although it may not be ancient, its beauty and spiritual ambiance make it a worthwhile stop before embarking on nearby hiking trails.

Free

Dadaocheng

Dadaocheng Pier Plaza is a vibrant quayside area in Taipei, once the commercial hub of the city. Dihua Old Street, lined with wholesale shops selling various goods, now features a mix of old and new establishments. Traditional wholesale shops coexist with modern boutiques, trendy cafes, and upscale inns. The plaza is known for its lively atmosphere and nighttime illuminations along the river walkway.

Free

Taipei Xia Hai City God Temple

Taipei Xiahai Chenghuang Temple, also known as City God Temple, is a popular prayer site for young couples and singles alike. The temple is dedicated to The City God and numerous Buddhist deities, including the Chinese Cupid. It houses over 600 deity statues and is believed to ensure a smooth romantic future for those who pray to Cupid.

Free

Ningxia Night Market

Ningxia Night Market is a lively and bustling nighttime market located in Taipei, Taiwan. It offers a wide variety of traditional food options within covered outdoor surroundings. While it may be smaller in scale compared to other markets, it provides an ideal experience for families or first-time visitors due to its manageable size. The market features two rows of stalls along Ningxia road, creating a narrow walkway filled with an array of delicious food choices.

Free

Qingtiangang Grassland

Qingtiangang Grassland National Park is a vast, grassy lava plateau in the Taipei City of Taiwan. The park is home to wild buffalo, and is a popular hiking destination. The park is open 24 hours, and has facilities for camping, picnicking, and sports.

Free

Huashan 1914 Creative Park

Huashan 1914 Creative Park is a cultural hotspot housed in a former sake winery and train station. The park features an array of shops, local art exhibitions, film screenings, and craft events. The site has been transformed into a vibrant space with a playground designed around the themes of the old train station and distillery. Visitors can enjoy various recreational facilities such as swings, slides, and even a zip line.

Free

Taiwan Literature Base

Taiwan Literature Base is an arts and event space housed in a traditional Japanese colonial-style building, located on Qidong Street. This historical street played a significant role in trade and food distribution during the Japanese colonial era. The base features several well-preserved Japanese colonial-era houses and offers exhibitions about books, as well as a small library for children. Visitors can explore the unique architecture of the building while enjoying free admission to the site.

NT$150

Miramar Ferris Wheel

The Miramar Ferris Wheel is a prominent feature of the Miramar Entertainment Park in Taipei, standing at 70 meters (230 feet) tall. Positioned on the roof of the park, it provides elevated views of the city, including landmarks such as Taipei 101 and Yangming Mountain. For those seeking an extra thrill, there are glass-floor cabins available for a unique perspective.
