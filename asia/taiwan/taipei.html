Taipei

Taipei developed around Dutch and Qing-era settlements in the Taipei Basin and became the seat of the Republic of China government after 1949. Japanese colonial boulevards, Chiang Kai-shek Memorial Hall, and Taipei 101 reflect 20th-century political change and economic growth. Temples such as Longshan, night markets, and museums show how the city blends Fujianese folk religion, indigenous heritage, and modern Taiwanese identity.

National Palace Museum

Taipei's National Palace Museum holds a major collection of Chinese imperial art transferred from mainland institutions during the twentieth century. Bronzes, ceramics, painting, calligraphy and jade rotate through the galleries. Rare books and court objects add further evidence of imperial administration and taste. The museum's formation reflects war, state relocation and competing ideas of cultural inheritance across many dynasties.
