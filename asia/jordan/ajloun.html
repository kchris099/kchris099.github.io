Destination Guide — Ajloun

Explore Ajloun

A Brief History

Centered around a formidable 12th-century fortress built by Saladin's general to thwart the Crusaders, Ajloun controlled the vital iron-mining and trade routes of the Levant. The preserved castle and surrounding woodlands reflect its long role as a strategic regional guardian. Museums, markets, and street plans in Ajloun still show layers from those periods.

Ajloun Castle

Ajloun Castle is a fortified historic site in Ajloun, Jordan. Ajloun Castle is a formidable 12th-century Islamic fortress situated on a hilltop in northern Jordan. The elevated position provides sweeping, panoramic views of the Jordan Valley. Walls, courtyards, or towers form the main visitor route when the grounds are open.

Free

Ajloun Forest Reserve

The Ajloun Forest Reserve is a protected natural area in northern Jordan, characterized by dense woodlands of evergreen oak, pine, and wild pistachio trees. The reserve offers a network of scenic hiking trails and supports diverse wildlife, including the endangered Roe Deer. It provides a cool, green contrast to the more arid landscapes typical of the region.

Free

Arch of Hadrian

Arch of Hadrian is a visitor site in Ajloun, Jordan. This colossal arch, the largest known in the Roman Empire, was built during Hadrian's visit and is the entry point to the ancient city of Jerash. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Free

Archaeological Site of Jerash

Archaeological Site of Jerash is a archaeological site in Ajloun, Jordan. The Archaeological Site of Jerash is a destination that offers a journey back in time to the Roman era. The preserved ruins of this ancient city provide a chance to immerse oneself in its grandeur. The South Theatre is a evidence of the city's active past, where various performances once took place. Interpretive signs and open excavation areas guide a typical visit.

Free

Nymphaeum

Nymphaeum is a visitor site in Ajloun, Jordan. Nymphaeum is a well-preserved ancient Roman structure located in the city of Gerasa. This monumental fountain served as a gathering place and was dedicated to water nymphs, playing a important role in the city's water supply system. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Free

Oval Plaza

Oval Plaza, also known as Cardo Maximus, is a public square in Ajloun, Jordan. Oval Plaza, also known as Cardo Maximus, is a significant attraction in Jerash, Jordan. This ancient Roman site served as the social and economic center of the city during the Roman Empire. It once functioned as a marketplace and is adorned with two altars and a fountain added in the 7th century AD. Open pavement and surrounding facades define the visitor setting.

Free

Temple of Zeus

The Temple of Zeus in Jerash, built in 162/163 AD, is a towering structure located near the South Theatre. While some columns remain standing, others have fallen, showing the size of the building blocks. In the 5th century AD, the temple was dismantled and used as a quarry for constructing churches in the city. Visitors can gain insight into the excavation project by visiting the Jerash Archaeological Museum which houses numerous fascinating artifacts.

Free

Southern Theatre

Southern Theatre is a theatre in Ajloun, Jordan. With a seating capacity of over 3,000 spectators and acoustics, it continues to host performances today. The ornate stage and unparalleled architectural prowess of the Roman Empire make it a standout attraction in the southern part of the ancient city. Public access is usually through ticketed performances or daytime visits.

Free

Jerash Visitor Center

The Jerash Visitor Center, located just outside the main gate to the archaeological site of Jerash, Jordan, is a great starting point for exploring the ancient Roman city. Established in the early seventies and rehabilitated in 2015 through cooperation with USAID, this center offers information and guidance about the historic sites of Jerash.

Free

Al Ma'wa for Nature and Wildlife is a visitor site in Ajloun, Jordan. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.
