Destination Guide — Ajloun

Explore Ajloun

A Brief History

Centered around a formidable 12th-century fortress built by Saladin's general to thwart the Crusaders, Ajloun controlled the vital iron-mining and trade routes of the Levant. The preserved castle and surrounding woodlands reflect its long role as a strategic regional guardian. Decades of careful preservation have ensured that this profound historical narrative remains a vital part of the modern experience.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

Ajloun Castle

Ajloun Castle is a formidable 12th-century Islamic fortress situated on a hilltop in northern Jordan. Built by the Ayyubids to defend against Crusader incursions and control the local iron mines, the castle features thick stone walls, deep moats, and strategic defensive towers. The elevated position provides sweeping, panoramic views of the Jordan Valley.

Free

Ajloun Forest Reserve

The Ajloun Forest Reserve is a protected natural area in northern Jordan, characterized by dense woodlands of evergreen oak, pine, and wild pistachio trees. The reserve offers a network of scenic hiking trails and supports diverse wildlife, including the endangered Roe Deer. It provides a cool, green contrast to the more arid landscapes typical of the region.

Free

Arch of Hadrian

The Arch of Hadrian is a remarkable triple-bayed Roman arch constructed in 129 CE to honor Emperor Hadrian. It stands at 11 meters high and features intricate detailing on its three major archways. This colossal arch, the largest known in the Roman Empire, was built during Hadrian's visit and serves as the entry point to the ancient city of Jerash.

Free

Archaeological Site of Jerash

The Archaeological Site of Jerash is a captivating destination that offers a journey back in time to the Roman era. The preserved ruins of this ancient city provide an opportunity to immerse oneself in its grandeur. Visitors can wander through the historic streets, admire the impressive Hadrian's Arch, and absorb the ambiance of the Oval Plaza. The South Theatre stands as a testament to the city's vibrant past, where various performances once took place.

Free

Nymphaeum

Nymphaeum is a well-preserved ancient Roman structure located in the city of Gerasa. This monumental fountain served as a gathering place and was dedicated to water nymphs, playing a crucial role in the city's water supply system. The ornate semicircular design features intricately carved columns, facade, and niches, showcasing impressive Roman architecture and artistry.

Free

Oval Plaza

Oval Plaza, also known as Cardo Maximus, is a significant attraction in Jerash, Jordan. This ancient Roman site served as the social and economic center of the city during the Roman Empire. The plaza features an impressive oval shape surrounded by Ionic columns and measures 90mx80m. It once functioned as a marketplace and is adorned with two altars and a fountain added in the 7th century AD.

Free

Temple of Zeus

The Temple of Zeus in Jerash, built in 162/163 AD, is a towering structure located near the South Theatre. While some columns remain standing, others have fallen, showcasing the size of the building blocks. In the 5th century AD, the temple was dismantled and used as a quarry for constructing churches in the city. Visitors can gain insight into the excavation project by visiting the Jerash Archaeological Museum which houses numerous fascinating artifacts.

Free

Southern Theatre

The Southern Theatre, located in Jerash, Jordan, is a remarkable ancient Roman theater dating back to the 2nd century. With a seating capacity of over 3,000 spectators and impressive acoustics, it continues to host performances today. The ornate stage and unparalleled architectural prowess of the Roman Empire make it a standout attraction in the southern part of the ancient city.

Free

Jerash Visitor Center

The Jerash Visitor Center, located just outside the main gate to the archaeological site of Jerash, Jordan, is a great starting point for exploring the ancient Roman city. Established in the early seventies and rehabilitated in 2015 through cooperation with USAID, this center offers information and guidance about the historic sites of Jerash.

Free

Al Ma'wa for Nature and Wildlife

Al Ma'wa for Nature and Wildlife is a remarkable conservation center that offers visitors an experience with majestic animals such as lions, tigers, and bears. Nestled in the serene forests of northern Jordan at a high elevation, this sanctuary provides a safe haven for animals recovering from past hardships. The site features an easy one-hour guided walking tour through its picturesque trails, making it accessible to most guests.
