Destination Guide — Tel Aviv

Explore Tel Aviv

A Brief History

Founded in 1909 on the sand dunes north of the ancient port of Jaffa, Tel Aviv expanded rapidly with waves of European immigration. The 1930s arrival of Bauhaus architects created the distinct White City, shaping a fiercely modern, secular metropolis on the Mediterranean. Museums, markets, and street plans in Tel Aviv still show layers from those periods.

The Clock Tower

The Clock Tower in Jaffa, Tel Aviv is a limestone structure built in 1903 to honor one of the last sultans of the Ottoman Empire. It is a central point surrounded by historical buildings, a beach, and various shops, galleries, and restaurants. The tower was constructed by Sultan Abed-el-Hamid the 2nd and has been renovated over the years with new clocks and stained glass windows depicting Jaffa's history.

Free

Park HaTachana

Park HaTachana, also known as the Old Train Station, is a public park in Tel Aviv, Israel. Construction and early use date to around 1882. Park HaTachana, also known as the Old Train Station, is a historic complex in Jaffa that has been transformed into a active tourist destination. Originally built in 1882 to run trains from Jaffa to Jerusalem, the station now hosts an open market on weekends offering a variety of hand-crafted items and artisanal products.

Tel Aviv Port

Tel Aviv Port, also known as Namal, is a active entertainment district featuring trendy shops, restaurants, and clubs along a scenic seafront promenade. The area is easily accessible from centrally located hotels like Dan Tel Aviv and Dan Panorama Tel Aviv. Nearby attractions include Dizengoff Street, Jaffa's old town, the known Carmel Market, and Neve Tzedek neighborhood.

Azrieli Ayalon Mall

Located Ramat Gan, the Azrieli Ayalon Mall is a pioneering shopping destination in Israel, being the first large mall situated outside of a city center. Its strategic location near Tel Aviv and Bnei Brak attracts numerous visitors eager to explore its vast array of global brand-name shops and local favorites. Shoppers can indulge in an selection that includes international giants like Zara and Aldo alongside beloved Israeli brands such as Castro and Fox.

ANU Museum of the Jewish People

The ANU Museum of the Jewish People is a heritage museum tracing the history of the Jewish people from antiquity to the present day. The museum is situated in Ramat Aviv, a northern Tel Aviv suburb and is popular with Jews from all over the world. Permanent displays take visitors through history, introducing them to important Jewish figures. The museum also conducts educational programs to teach about Judaism.

Free

Old Jaffa

Old Jaffa, an ancient city that predates Jerusalem, is a must-visit in Tel Aviv. Once its own city, it has been absorbed into Tel Aviv but still retains its charm with old buildings and winding alleyways. Visitors can explore the flea market behind the old Ottoman clock tower and stroll through the oldest section of Jaffa. The area is steeped in legends like Jonah and the Whale, making it a fascinating historical site to visit.

St. Peter's Church

St. Peter's Church is a religious building in Tel Aviv, Israel. It opened or was completed around 1894. Peter's Church is a Franciscan Catholic brick church located in Old Jaffa, completed in 1894. It stands out with its tall brick facade and a towering bell tower, making it the largest and most distinctive building in the area. The exterior and main interior spaces are usually viewable outside service times.

Jaffa Light

When exploring the enchanting Old City of Jaffa, one cannot miss the Jaffa Light. This historic lighthouse, perched on the sea wall, offers views of the Mediterranean Sea and is a evidence of Jaffa's rich maritime heritage. Although it is no longer operational and not accessible for entry, its presence adds charm to this ancient port city. Visitors can stroll along the fortified sea wall that has protected Jaffa from invaders and storms over time.

HaBima Square

HaBima Square, named after Israel's National Theatre, is a active public space in Tel Aviv surrounded by cultural institutions like the Habima Theater and a sunken garden. It is a focal point in the city and hosts various activities and performances during Hanukkah celebrations. The square's design combines minimalism with functionality, offering visitors a place to relax amidst its modern surroundings.

Habima Theatre

Located Tel Aviv, Habima Theatre is a landmark of cultural expression and artistic exploration. Established in 1912 in Bialystok, Russia, this venue has evolved into Israel's national theater since 1958, showing a rich history of Hebrew performances that look at Jewish traditions and contemporary issues. The theater is equipped with translating and subtitling technology to ensure accessibility for all audiences.
