A Brief History

Shahrisabz was the birthplace of Timur (Tamerlane) in the fourteenth century and a Timurid patronage centre. Ak-Saray Palace ruins and Dorut Tilavat necropolis record how the conqueror commemorated his Kesh valley origins in present-day Kashkadarya. Timurid mausoleums and bazaar lanes extend Kesh valley patronage within Kashkadarya province. Walking routes through the historic centre help visitors relate these monuments to wider patterns of settlement and civic life in Uzbekistan.

Ak-Saray Palace

The Ak-Saray (White Palace) is the monumental, ruined summer palace of Timur (Tamerlane), located in his hometown of Shahrisabz, Uzbekistan. Built in the late 14th century, only the massive, 38-meter-high entrance portal remains today. The towering ruins are covered in dramatic, intricate blue, white, and gold tilework, reflecting the massive scale of Timurid architecture.
