Uzbekistan

Uzbekistan's oasis cities prospered where Silk Road routes crossed Central Asia. Bukhara became a major center of Islamic scholarship, while Samarkand drew wealth from trade and later served as Timur's capital. The Registan belongs to the monumental building programs of his successors. Russian conquest and Soviet rule then changed borders and agriculture, before independence in 1991 joined Uzbek national identity with older regional traditions.
