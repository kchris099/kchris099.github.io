Qatar

Qatar's coastal settlements depended on fishing and pearling long before petroleum wealth transformed the peninsula. Al Zubarah grew as an eighteenth-century port tied to merchants across the Gulf, while Ottoman and British influence shaped the rule of the Al Thani family. Independence came in 1971, and gas exports then financed Doha's rapid expansion while drawing a large migrant population without displacing the political importance of tribal ties and Islamic institutions.
