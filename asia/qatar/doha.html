Explore Doha

A Brief History

Doha grew from a fishing and pearling settlement on the Persian Gulf coast of the Qatar peninsula. The Al Thani family consolidated rule in the 19th century as Ottoman influence waned. Oil exports after 1949 financed rapid urban growth. The Museum of Islamic Art and restored Souq Waqif occupy the waterfront. Independence from Britain came in 1971. Modern towers rose along the Corniche in the late 20th century.

Top Sights & Activities

Souq Waqif

In Doha Qatar, Souq Waqif is respected as a landmark, being considered as a historical market and heritage centre. In general, it is described by its revitalized traditional establishments and a vast cultural and civic space within the city.

Free

MIA Park

A wonderfully sweeping, beautifully landscaped green space elegantly curving directly along the brilliant waterfront. It complements the modern architecture of the adjacent Museum of Islamic Art.

$50

Museum of Islamic Art

A, architecturally magnificent institution beautifully housing one of the, incredible collections of Islamic artifacts globally. It powerfully stands as a brilliant beacon of Middle Eastern cultural heritage.

Free

Flag Plaza

Hamad International Airport is an international airport in Qatar, and the home base of the national flag carrier airline, Qatar Airways. Located east of the capital, Doha, it replaced the nearby Doha International Airport as Qatar's principal and main national airport and one of the busiest airports in the Middle East. Formerly known as New Doha International Airport during construction, Hamad International Airport was originally scheduled to open in 2008.

Free

Villaggio Mall

Villaggio Mall is a shopping mall located in the Aspire Zone in the west end of Doha, the capital city of Qatar. It is located on Al Waab street between the Hyatt Plaza and Sports City and has over 200 stores, including many famous brands in the U.S., U.K., Italian and German markets. The mall has direct access to the Aspire Tower hotel, and has a Doha Metro station nearby.

Free

Galeries Lafayette

Galeries Lafayette is an upmarket French department store chain. The flagship store is located on Boulevard Haussmann in the 9th arrondissement of Paris. It is part of Groupe Galeries Lafayette and has been a member of the International Association of Department Stores since 1960.

Free

Staind Glasses at Snan Food Hall

A striking modern architectural installation featuring vibrant stained glass ceilings that illuminate an upscale dining hall. The colorful patterns reflect traditional geometric artistry blended with contemporary commercial design.

Free

Lightning Stairs

An artistic and architectural public staircase designed with sharp, angular forms resembling a lightning bolt. It serves as a modern urban focal point within the rapidly developing architectural landscape of Qatar.

Free

Lusail Marina Promenade

Lusail is the third-largest city in Qatar after Doha and Al-Rayyan, and serves as an important economic hub. Located on the coast in the southern part of the municipality of Al Daayen, it lies about 23 kilometres north of the Doha city centre, just north of the West Bay Lagoon, covering over 38 square kilometres.
