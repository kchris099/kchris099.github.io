Doha

Doha grew from a fishing and pearling settlement on the Persian Gulf coast of the Qatar peninsula. The Al Thani family consolidated rule in the 19th century as Ottoman influence waned. Oil exports after 1949 financed rapid urban growth. The Museum of Islamic Art and restored Souq Waqif occupy the waterfront. Independence from Britain came in 1971. Modern towers rose along the Corniche in the late 20th century.

Souq Waqif

Souq Waqif combines restored market lanes with shops selling spices, textiles, perfumes, household goods, crafts, animals and falconry equipment. Restaurants, courtyards, cultural events and evening crowds make it a major Doha gathering place. Much of the present fabric reflects modern reconstruction using traditional forms, while continuing trade and social use give the district more substance than its historic appearance alone.

MIA Park

Lawns and paths curve along Doha's waterfront beside the Museum of Islamic Art, matching the museum's modern silhouette with open green. Shade and shoreline walks define the park more than playground equipment. The bay edge supplies the main scenic line.

Museum of Islamic Art

The Museum of Islamic Art sits on a Doha waterfront peninsula in a limestone building designed by I. M. Pei. Galleries hold manuscripts, ceramics, metalwork, and textiles from across the Islamic world, with upper floors framing views of the Corniche and West Bay skyline across the harbor basin.

25.2952° N / 51.5393° E

04 AdmissionFree

Flag Plaza

Flag Plaza is a ceremonial square on Doha's Corniche where tall flagpoles display the Qatari national flag beside landscaped lawns and seafront paths. The open plaza was completed as part of wider Corniche upgrades and frames views toward the West Bay skyline. Evening lighting and Gulf breezes make it a regular stop on Corniche walks between the dhow harbour and museum district.

25.2958° N / 51.5413° E

05 AdmissionFree

Villaggio Mall

On Al Waab Street between Hyatt Plaza and Sports City in the Aspire Zone, Villaggio Mall holds more than 200 stores with brands from the U.S., U.K., Italy, and Germany. Direct links reach Aspire Tower hotel, and a Doha Metro station sits nearby. The mall anchors western Doha's retail strip.

25.2582° N / 51.4429° E

Galeries Lafayette

Galeries Lafayette is an upmarket French department store chain. The flagship store is located on Boulevard Haussmann in the 9th arrondissement of Paris. It is part of Groupe Galeries Lafayette and has been a member of the International Association of Department Stores since 1960.

Staind Glasses at Snan Food Hall

Stained-glass panels at Snan Food Hall in Doha color the light over dining counters inside a contemporary food hall. Glass artwork hangs above shared tables and vendor kitchens. The installation turns a casual eating floor into a marked visual stop within the hall.

08 AdmissionFree

Lightning Stairs

Lightning Stairs climb the landscaped artificial hill within Katara Cultural Village in Doha. The broad, angular stairway leads toward a restaurant and upper terrace, with views across Katara's buildings, waterfront and bay. Its geometric form is treated as a piece of contemporary public design, and a lift provides another route to the top when the exposed outdoor climb is uncomfortable in the heat.

25.3450° N / 51.5260° E

09 AdmissionFree

Lusail Marina Promenade

Lusail Marina Promenade follows a planned waterfront north of Doha with paths, landscaping, towers, marinas, restaurants and views across newly developed districts. Its character reflects rapid twenty-first-century urban construction. Lusail is the third-largest city in Qatar after Doha and Al-Rayyan, and is an important economic hub.

25.3626° N / 51.5332° E
