Destination Guide — Doha

Explore Doha

A Brief History

Doha grew from a fishing and pearling settlement on the Persian Gulf coast of the Qatar peninsula. The Al Thani family consolidated rule in the 19th century as Ottoman influence waned. Oil exports after 1949 financed rapid urban growth. The Museum of Islamic Art and restored Souq Waqif occupy the waterfront. Independence from Britain came in 1971. Modern towers rose along the Corniche in the late 20th century.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

Souq Waqif

Souq Waqif is the historic social and commercial heart of Doha, meticulously restored to reflect traditional Qatari architecture. The bustling market features a labyrinth of alleyways filled with shops selling spices, textiles, traditional garments, and souvenirs. It is also a major dining and entertainment hub, offering a wide array of regional cuisine and shisha cafes.

Free

MIA Park

MIA Park is a beautifully landscaped, crescent-shaped public park adjacent to the Museum of Islamic Art in Doha. It offers expansive green lawns, walking paths, and a cafe, providing a serene environment for families and visitors. The park's location on the Corniche offers some of the most dramatic, unobstructed views of the modern West Bay skyline.

QAR 50

Museum of Islamic Art

The Museum of Islamic Art in Doha is a premier cultural institution housing one of the world's most comprehensive collections of Islamic artifacts. Designed by I.M. Pei, the striking limestone building stands on an artificial island off the Corniche. The museum's extensive galleries showcase centuries of exquisite ceramics, textiles, metalwork, and manuscripts from across the Islamic world.

Free

Flag Plaza

Flag Plaza is a ceremonial square on Doha's Corniche where tall flagpoles display the Qatari national flag beside landscaped lawns and seafront paths. The open plaza was completed as part of wider Corniche upgrades and frames views toward the West Bay skyline. Evening lighting and Gulf breezes make it a regular stop on Corniche walks between the dhow harbour and museum district.

Free

Villaggio Mall

Villaggio Mall is a shopping mall located in the Aspire Zone in the west end of Doha, the capital city of Qatar. It is located on Al Waab street between the Hyatt Plaza and Sports City and has over 200 stores, including many famous brands in the U.S., U.K., Italian and German markets. The mall has direct access to the Aspire Tower hotel, and has a Doha Metro station nearby.

Free

Galeries Lafayette

Galeries Lafayette is an upmarket French department store chain. The flagship store is located on Boulevard Haussmann in the 9th arrondissement of Paris. It is part of Groupe Galeries Lafayette and has been a member of the International Association of Department Stores since 1960.

Free

Staind Glasses at Snan Food Hall

The Snan Food Hall in Msheireb Downtown Doha features a remarkable, large-scale stained glass installation that adds a vibrant, artistic element to the modern culinary space. The colorful glasswork creates dynamic light patterns throughout the day, enhancing the sophisticated atmosphere of the high-end food market and providing a popular subject for photography.

Free

Lightning Stairs

The Lightning Stairs (Treptele Fulger) are a unique and visually striking set of public stairs located in Cluj-Napoca. Recently renovated with dynamic, colorful lighting installations, the stairs provide a vibrant pedestrian connection between different levels of the city. They have quickly become a popular, modern landmark and a favorite spot for evening photography.

Free

Lusail Marina Promenade

Lusail is the third-largest city in Qatar after Doha and Al-Rayyan, and serves as an important economic hub. Located on the coast in the southern part of the municipality of Al Daayen, it lies about 23 kilometres north of the Doha city centre, just north of the West Bay Lagoon, covering over 38 square kilometres.
