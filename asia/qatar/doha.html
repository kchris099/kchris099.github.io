Destination Guide — Doha

Explore Doha

A Brief History

Doha grew from a fishing and pearling settlement on the Persian Gulf coast of the Qatar peninsula. The Al Thani family consolidated rule in the 19th century as Ottoman influence waned. Oil exports after 1949 financed rapid urban growth. The Museum of Islamic Art and restored Souq Waqif occupy the waterfront. Independence from Britain came in 1971. Modern towers rose along the Corniche in the late 20th century.

Souq Waqif

Souq Waqif is a visitor site in Doha, Qatar. In Doha Qatar, Souq Waqif is respected as a landmark, being considered as a historical market and heritage centre. In general, it is described by its revitalized traditional establishments and a vast cultural and civic space within the city. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Free

MIA Park

MIA Park is a public park in Doha, Qatar. A wonderfully sweeping, beautifully landscaped green space elegantly curving directly along the brilliant waterfront. It complements the modern architecture of the adjacent Museum of Islamic Art. Paths, lawns, and shaded areas make up the main public facilities.

QAR 50

Museum of Islamic Art

Museum of Islamic Art is a museum in Doha, Qatar. A, architecturally institution beautifully housing one of the, incredible collections of Islamic artifacts globally. Galleries and public rooms are open on posted hours. Local maps mark Museum of Islamic Art inside Doha.

Free

Flag Plaza

Flag Plaza is a ceremonial square on Doha's Corniche where tall flagpoles display the Qatari national flag beside landscaped lawns and seafront paths. The open plaza was completed as part of wider Corniche upgrades and frames views toward the West Bay skyline. Evening lighting and Gulf breezes make it a regular stop on Corniche walks between the dhow harbour and museum district.

Free

Villaggio Mall

Villaggio Mall is a shopping mall located in the Aspire Zone in the west end of Doha, the capital city of Qatar. It is located on Al Waab street between the Hyatt Plaza and Sports City and has over 200 stores, including many famous brands in the U.S., U.K., Italian and German markets. The mall has direct access to the Aspire Tower hotel, and has a Doha Metro station nearby.

Free

Galeries Lafayette

Galeries Lafayette is an upmarket French department store chain. The flagship store is located on Boulevard Haussmann in the 9th arrondissement of Paris. It is part of Groupe Galeries Lafayette and has been a member of the International Association of Department Stores since 1960.

Free

Staind Glasses at Snan Food Hall

Staind Glasses at Snan Food Hall is a visitor site in Doha, Qatar. The main structures and grounds are the principal points of interest. Local maps mark Staind Glasses at Snan Food Hall inside Doha. Approaches are signed from nearby streets in Doha, Qatar.

Free

Lightning Stairs

The Lightning Stairs (Treptele Fulger) are a unique and visually striking set of public stairs located in Cluj-Napoca. Recently renovated with dynamic, colorful lighting installations, the stairs provide a active pedestrian connection between different levels of the city. They have quickly become a popular, modern landmark and a favorite spot for evening photography.

Free

Lusail Marina Promenade

Lusail is the third-largest city in Qatar after Doha and Al-Rayyan, and is an important economic hub. Located on the coast in the southern part of the municipality of Al Daayen, it lies about 23 kilometres north of the Doha city centre, just north of the West Bay Lagoon, covering over 38 square kilometres.
