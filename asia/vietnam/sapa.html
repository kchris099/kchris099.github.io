Sapa

Sa Pa developed as a French colonial hill station in the early 20th century above terraced valleys farmed by Hmong, Yao, and other highland peoples. Cool climate and Fansipan’s peaks drew administrators and later trekkers. War and border tensions slowed growth; since the 1990s tourism and market town commerce expanded rapidly. Rice terraces, weekend ethnic markets, and mountain roads still organise life on this Lao Cai highland ridge.

Sa Pa Stone Church

Notre Dame Cathedral is a stone church with a belltower and Roman Gothic architecture, built by the French in 1895. It's located in Sapa, Vietnam, a beloved destination known for its troubled past and recovery. Travellers can explore nearby attractions like the Sapa Museum, Cat Cat Village, and Ham Rong Mountain within walking distance. The cathedral area offers an eerie atmosphere in the early morning fog, surrounded by Hmong ethnic people making a living.

Love Waterfall

Love Waterfall, located the Hoang Lien National Park, is a 100-meter cascade that glistens like silver in the sunlight as it plunges into the Gold Stream. The surrounding scenery is equally enchanting, with the stream winding through lush greenery and bamboo clusters. To reach this natural formation, travellers must began on a hike through bamboo forests and rhododendron-filled landscapes, offering views of the Hoang Lien mountain range.
