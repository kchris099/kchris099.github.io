Explore Sapa

A Brief History

Sapa is in Vietnam. Established as a regional settlement beyond an older fortified core, the city developed through industrialization in the late 19th century, post-war reconstruction, and modern civic expansion that reshaped its public spaces, markets, and surrounding landscape. Museums, markets, and street plans in Sapa still show layers from those periods.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

Sa Pa Stone Church

Notre Dame Cathedral is a stone church with a belltower and Roman Gothic architecture, built by the French in 1895. It's located in Sapa, Vietnam, a beloved destination known for its troubled past and recovery. Travellers can explore nearby attractions like the Sapa Museum, Cat Cat Village, and Ham Rong Mountain within walking distance. The cathedral area offers an eerie atmosphere in the early morning fog, surrounded by Hmong ethnic people making a living.

Free

Sapa Square

Sapa Center is the main hub for travellers to Sapa, offering a wide range of accommodation options and serving as a starting point for exploring the surrounding attractions. It provides a chance to immerse in the local culture and witness the daily lives of the residents. The town center is busy with activity, especially in the evenings, making it an ideal location for those who enjoy city life.

Free

Sun Plaza Sapa

Sun Plaza Sapa is a public square in Sapa, Vietnam. It has become a well-known check-in spot for young people and is just steps away from the Stone Church. Open pavement and surrounding facades define the visitor setting. Sun Plaza Sapa remains part of the public visitor circuit in Sapa.

₫70,000

Ham Rong Sa Pa Eco-Tourism Area

Khu Du Lịch Sinh Thái Hàm Rồng SaPa, also known as Ham Rong Mountain, is a prominent peak in Sapa town. Its dragon head-shaped summit offers views of the surrounding landscape. The hike to the 1900m-high summit is relatively easy, with steps leading up through lush vegetation and a flower garden featuring numerous orchid varieties.

Free

Sa Pa Night Market

Sapa Night Market is a busy center for goods and cultural exchange, where the Hmong, Dao, and Nung ethnic minorities gather. In Sapa on Luong Dinh Cua Street, this active market offers a wide array of hand-made products, pharmaceuticals, forestry items, and traditional brocade clothing. Travellers can explore an interesting collection of goods including chestnut stuffed pastries, local food stands, and electronics.

Free

Muong Hoa Station

Muong Hoa Station is a key stop for the Muong Hoa Mountain Train, offering a scenic five-minute ride over the valley with views of farms and hamlets. It's part of the journey from Sun Plaza to Fansipan, where travellers can enjoy spacious gardens, cultural villages, and themed areas. The station connects to Hoang Lien Station for the cable car ride up to Fansipan Peak.

Free

O Quy Ho Pass Viewpoint

O Quy Ho Pass Viewpoint is a viewpoint in Sapa, Vietnam. O Quy Ho Pass Viewpoint is a must-visit destination for nature lovers and adventure seekers alike. The experience begins with the mesmerizing silver waterfall at Tran Ton Pass, leading to the thrilling O Quy Ho Dragon Pass. Approach paths are usually short walks from a road or parking area.

₫70,000

Love Waterfall

Love Waterfall, located the Hoang Lien National Park, is a 100-meter cascade that glistens like silver in the sunlight as it plunges into the Gold Stream. The surrounding scenery is equally enchanting, with the stream winding through lush greenery and bamboo clusters. To reach this natural formation, travellers must began on a hike through bamboo forests and rhododendron-filled landscapes, offering views of the Hoang Lien mountain range.

Free

Ô Quy Hồ pass

Ô Quy Hồ Pass, also known as Heaven Gate or Tram Ton Pass, is a 50-kilometer-long mountain pass in Vietnam that offers views of valleys and forests. As the highest point in Ô Quy Hồ Pass at 1900 meters, Tram Ton provides a vantage point to admire the Hoang Lien mountain range and the snaking roads below.

Free

Silver Waterfall Sa Pa

Silver Waterfall is a natural beauty spot in Sapa, Vietnam. Located 14km from Sapa on the winding O Qui Ho Pass, it offers a view of the cascading waterfall running down from Lo Sui Tong mountain peak. The way to experience its beauty is by trekking up 300 steps to a bridge that provides a panoramic view of the waterfall and surrounding mountains.
