Huế

Huế was imperial capital of the Nguyen dynasty from 1802, with a walled citadel and tombs along the Perfume River. Royal pavilions, mandarin courts, and Buddhist pagodas record how Vietnamese rulers adapted Chinese court ritual in central Vietnam. Royal tomb avenues and Perfume River boat landings extend Nguyen imperial ritual into the central coast hinterland.

An Dinh Palace

An Dinh Palace, situated along the An Cuu Canal, served as the royal residence for Vietnam's last kings. Originally built as a private home for King Khai Dinh, it later became the dwelling place of Emperor Bao Dai and his family from 1945 to 1955. After undergoing careful restoration work adhering to UNESCO standards, this early 1900s palace now welcomes visitors eager to explore its period rooms and admire its art collection.
