Hanoi

Hanoi became Vietnam's capital under Ly and later dynasties on the Red River delta. Temple of Literature, French colonial opera house, and Ho Chi Minh Mausoleum record Confucian examination culture, colonial urbanism, and twentieth-century independence struggles. Hoan Kiem Lake promenades and Old Quarter guild streets extend dynastic and colonial layers around the Red River.

Ho Chi Minh’s Stilt House

Ho Chi Minh's Stilt House is a simple yet wooden stilt home that served as the residence for Communist leader Ho Chi Minh during the last 11 years of his life. Situated within the Presidential Palace complex, this architectural gem was inspired by the traditional houses of the Viet Bac ethnic minority. The two-story house, surrounded by lush gardens and a carp pond, has been preserved just as Ho left it in 1969.
