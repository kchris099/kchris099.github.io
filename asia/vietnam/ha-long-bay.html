Destination Guide — Ha Long Bay

Explore Ha Long Bay

A Brief History

Ha Long Bay's dramatic karst seascape formed over millions of years through tectonic and erosional processes, resulting in thousands of towering limestone pillars rising from the Gulf of Tonkin. Historically, the bay served as a strategic maritime gateway, famously witnessing naval battles against Mongol fleets in the thirteenth century. It has also supported floating fishing communities for generations. Today, travelers experience this natural and historical heritage by cruising among the limestone islands and exploring extensive cave systems like Sung Sot and Thien Cung.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

Clock Tower in a Roundabout

The Clock Tower in a Roundabout is an landmark designed by the known architect Salvador Perez Arroyo, who has also contributed to other notable structures in Ha Long City. This clock tower is a symbol of Quang Ninh's active growth while honoring its rich history and natural surroundings. The city aims to enhance its visual appeal with additional unique icons at key intersections, reflecting modern design intertwined with ecological themes.

₫380,000

Sun Wheel

When visiting Ha Long Bay, don't miss the Sun Wheel, a towering Ferris wheel perched atop Ba Deo mountain. With 64 cabins and a capacity of 1,200 passengers per hour, it offers unrivaled panoramic views of the World Heritage site below. Take the cable car to reach this attraction and be greeted by vistas both day and night.

Free

Bãi Biển Sun World Hạ Long

Bai Chay Beach is a man-made beach known for its long stretch of sand, making it a popular spot for swimmers. After exploring the heritage bay, visitors can continue their journey in Ha Long with various experiences such as admiring the 1000m long coast of Bai Chay Beach.

Alisa Premier Cruise- Best Cruise in Halong Bay

Alisa Premier Cruise is a 5-star cruise located at Tuan Chau Whaft, Dock No.16, Tuan Chau, Halong Bay, Quang Ninh, Vietnam. It offers a modern style with 22 elegant cabins featuring separate balconies and bathtubs. The cruise provides traditional itineraries to visit attractions like Surprise Cave, Pearl Farm, and Titov Island.

₫310,000

Hòn Trống Mái Hạ Long

Hòn Trống Mái Hạ Long, also known as Hòn Gà Chọi or Fighting Cocks Island, is a monument in Ha Long Bay, Vietnam. Another unique attraction is the cave within the island, which produces drumming-like echoes when the wind passes through its stalactites and stalagmites. The memorial stands in an open plaza or roadside setting.

₫310,000

Dau Go Cave

Dau Go Cave, also known as the Cave of Wonders, is a limestone cave in Halong Bay, Vietnam. It is the largest among many caves in the region and is located on Driftwood Island. The cave's name translates to 'Wooden Stakes' in Vietnamese due to its historical significance during the Mongol invasion of 1288.

₫120,000

Cat Ba National Park

Cat Ba National Park, located on the island of the same name, is a vast and diverse natural reserve. Encompassing lush forests, limestone peaks, mangroves, and marine waters, the park has an array of flora and fauna with over 1,500 species. Visitors can spot rare animals such as the endangered white-headed langur and enjoy birdwatching with 160 bird species in the area.

₫380,000

Sun World Halong Park

Sun World Halong Park is a public park in Ha Long Bay, Vietnam. Sun World Halong Park is a top entertainment complex in the coastal city of Ha Long, offering a variety of attractions for tourists. Visitors can also enjoy panoramic views of Ha Long Bay from the Sun Wheel, which stands at 215m tall. Paths, lawns, and shaded areas make up the main public facilities.

Free

Bai Chay beach

Bai Chay beach, the largest in Ha Long, is an expansion of the old Bai Chay beach, now stretching nearly 1km with clean artificial white sand. Accessible via Grab or a walk from Sun World entrance, it offers restroom facilities and views of Halong Bay. Frequented by locals and Vietnamese tourists, it's a great spot for cheap cold beers and relaxation.

₫310,000

Ti Top Island

Ti Top Island is a small but island in Halong Bay, known for its crescent-shaped beach at the foot of a mountain. It's a popular stop on many cruise itineraries, offering views from the central vantage point and a tourist-friendly beach below. While it can get crowded due to the influx of tourist boats, arriving early in the morning allows for a less crowded experience and a chance to hike up around 400 steps to reach the viewpoint gazebo.
