Ha Long Bay

Ha Long Bay's dramatic karst seascape formed over millions of years through tectonic and erosional processes, resulting in thousands of towering limestone pillars rising from the Gulf of Tonkin. Historically, the bay served as a strategic maritime gateway, famously witnessing naval battles against Mongol fleets in the thirteenth century. It has also supported floating fishing communities for generations. Today, travelers experience this natural and historical heritage by cruising among the limestone islands and exploring extensive cave systems like Sung Sot and Thien Cung.

Clock Tower in a Roundabout

A clock tower stands within a traffic roundabout in Ha Long City as a modern civic marker surrounded by roads and newer urban development. Its visible clock faces and vertical form help orient drivers within the expanding city. The monument is appreciated from surrounding sidewalks and vehicles, with limited space for extended pedestrian use at the roundabout itself.

Hòn Trống Mái Hạ Long

Hon Trong Mai consists of two limestone formations rising close together from Ha Long Bay, often compared with a pair of fighting or kissing chickens. Cruise routes provide the usual view, and the shapes appear different with angle, tide, haze and boat traffic. Erosion and marine conditions formed the natural landmark, while its names and symbolism come from local tradition.

Dau Go Cave

Dau Go Cave opens inside a limestone island in Ha Long Bay, with large chambers containing stalactites, stalagmites and formations highlighted by installed lighting. Steps and pathways guide visitors through a cave altered for tourism, while historical legends have become attached to its name. Boat schedules, humidity, slippery surfaces, crowds, conservation and weather affect access from the bay.

Cat Ba National Park

Cat Ba National Park, located on the island of the same name, is a vast and diverse natural reserve. Encompassing lush forests, limestone peaks, mangroves, and marine waters, the park has an array of flora and fauna with over 1,500 species. Visitors can spot rare animals such as the endangered white-headed langur and enjoy birdwatching with 160 bird species in the area.

Bai Chay beach

Bai Chay Beach stretches for roughly a kilometer along the developed mainland shore of Ha Long City opposite the limestone islands. Imported sand, a promenade, hotels, restaurants and recreation facilities create an urban resort setting. The shoreline has been expanded and managed over time, so its present form reflects tourism development as much as natural coastal processes.

Ti Top Island's crescent beach sits at the foot of a karst hill on many Ha Long cruise stops, with roughly 400 steps to a viewpoint gazebo above the sand. Early arrivals find quieter beach time before boat crowds build. The gazebo overlooks the surrounding limestone towers.
