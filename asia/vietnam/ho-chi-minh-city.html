Explore Ho Chi Minh City

A Brief History

Ho Chi Minh City, formerly Saigon, developed on the Saigon River as a Khmer port before Vietnamese settlement grew in the 17th century. French colonial rule from the mid-19th century left boulevards, Notre-Dame Cathedral, and the Central Post Office. The city was the capital of South Vietnam until 1975. Markets such as Ben Thanh and tunnels at Cu Chi nearby record wartime and commercial history.

Statue of Ho Chi Minh

Statue of Ho Chi Minh sits within the Ho Chi Minh City area of Ho Chi Minh City, documented in local heritage registers for its civic or cultural role. Visitors approach from the main access road and combine the visit with neighbouring heritage buildings and public spaces in the district.

Nguyen Hue

Nguyen Hue is a broad, bustling pedestrian promenade located in the heart of District 1 in Ho Chi Minh City. Stretching from the ornate City Hall to the Saigon River, the boulevard is lined with French colonial architecture, modern skyscrapers, and numerous cafes and boutiques. It serves as a central gathering place for locals and tourists, particularly lively during evening hours and public festivals.

Free

Saigon Central Post Office

A magnificent French colonial building constructed in the late 19th century, featuring a beautifully preserved vaulted interior. It remains an active post office and a testament to the city's architectural heritage and historical communications network.

₫80000

Independence Palace

A important historical landmark that served as the home and workplace of the President of South Vietnam during the Vietnam War. The site is famously remembered as the location where the war officially ended in 1975 when a tank crashed through its gates.

Free

Saigon Opera House

The Ho Chi Minh City Opera House, also known as the Municipal Theatre of Ho Chi Minh City, is a 19th-century French colonial building that serves as the home to the city's ballet and symphony. Designed by renowned architect Eugene Ferret, this ornate masterpiece was originally constructed in 1897 as a symbol of entertainment for French colonists. The opera house features an elegant facade reminiscent of the Petit Palais in Paris and is located at the beginning of Le Loi Avenue.

Ho Chi Minh Statue

A prominent statue of Ho Chi Minh located in a central square, a popular spot for photos and to learn about the city's namesake. The site forms part of Ho Chi Minh Statue's documented civic and cultural landscape within Vietnam, with archives and visitor information available from municipal or regional heritage authorities.

Notre Dame Cathedral of Saigon

Notre Dame Cathedral of Saigon, also known as Duc Ba Cathedral, is a 19th-century Catholic basilica In Ho Chi Minh City. Built between 1863 and 1880 by French colonists, this architectural marvel showcases unique French design with its red brick exterior and Romanesque bell towers reaching a height of 58 meters.

Free

Saigon Book Street

Ho Chi Minh City Book Street, also known as Nguyen Van Binh Book Street, is a bustling pedestrian-only street in District 1. Stretching over 100 meters along Dang Van Binh Street, it's a paradise for book lovers and families. The street features various kiosks, bookshops, stalls, and coffee shops offering books in different genres and languages such as English, Chinese, and French.

Free

Ben Nghe Street Food

Ben Nghe Street Food is a vibrant culinary hotspot that truly captures the essence of Vietnamese street food culture. Nestled conveniently near Ben Thanh Market, this lively market boasts over 20 stalls offering an array of delectable dishes. From savory noodle bowls and crispy fried snacks to mouthwatering banh mis and grilled seafood, there's something to satisfy every palate.

₫240000

Bitexco Financial Tower

Bitexco Financial Tower, also known as the "roof" of Ho Chi Minh City, is a 68-floor skyscraper In District 1. This building offers an observation deck with views of the city and the riverside. travellers can explore retail shops, dining options, and even a cineplex within its premises.

Free

Nhà Thờ Giáo Xứ Tân Định

Tân Định Parish Church, also referred to as the Church of the Sacred Heart of Jesus, is a gem nestled in Ho Chi Minh City. Dating back to the 1870s, this architectural marvel showcases a vibrant pink facade that instantly captures attention. Its design beautifully blends French colonial and Gothic elements, creating an enchanting atmosphere for travellers. As one of the oldest churches in the city, Tân Định offers a unique glimpse into Vietnam's rich cultural heritage and history.

Free

Tân Định Market

Tan Dinh Market, located in the lively district of Phu Nhuan, is a bustling hub known for its diverse range of goods including fabrics, clothing, and fresh produce. The market's location near the city center makes it a popular spot for locals and travellers alike to indulge in vibrant Vietnamese cuisine at the food stalls offering dishes like Singapore frog porridge, pork rib porridge, broken rice, sticky rice with chicken, crab soup and desserts.

Free

Vinh Nghiem Buddhist Temple

Vinh Nghiem Buddhist Temple, located in Ho Chi Minh City, is a sprawling complex that showcases traditional Vietnamese architecture with a modern Japanese-inspired touch. The temple features a 25-meter-tall tower and a vegetarian restaurant for travellers to enjoy. It is known for its unique design, incorporating elements from Khmer, Vietnamese, and Cham styles.

₫60000

Saigon Zoo & Botanical Garden

One of the oldest zoos in the world, featuring a diverse collection of animals and a beautiful botanical garden. Jade Emperor Pagoda

Located in Saigon, the Jade Emperor Pagoda, also known as Chua Phuoc Hai, is a must-visit temple with exquisite carved gilt woodwork and a variety of Taoist and Buddhist deities. The temple's roof is adorned with dragons, birds, and animals. Dedicated to the Jade Emperor, one of the highest deities in Taoism, this pagoda offers a serene atmosphere once you step into its compound.

Free

Đài tưởng niệm Bồ tát Thích Quảng Đức

The Venerable Thich Quảng Đức Monument is a serene and spacious park located just 10 km from the city center of Ho Chi Minh City. The monument pays tribute to Thich Quang Duc, a revered Buddhist monk who self-immolated in protest against religious discrimination in 1963. The centerpiece of the monument is a 12-ton statue depicting the monk engulfed in flames, along with a large relief that narrates his poignant story.

Free

Xa Loi Buddhist Temple

Xa Loi Buddhist Temple, also known as Giac Lam Pagoda, is a prominent and historic Buddhist pagoda located in Ho Chi Minh City. Built in 1956, it features unique architecture with a seven-story stupa in Khmer, Vietnamese, and Cham styles. The main building houses precious documents, cultural artifacts, and Buddhist artworks.

Free

Ho Thi Ky Flower Market

Ho Thi Ky Flower Market is a bustling outdoor market in District 10, offering a riot of colorful flowers from roses to chrysanthemums and orchids. Most flowers are locally grown, with some imported from Cambodia. The market opens early in the morning and gets crowded before Tet (Vietnamese New Year). In addition to flowers, travellers can find street food and traditional sweet soup at the Cambodian market within Ho Thi Ky.

₫220000

Thien Hau Pagoda

Nestled in the vibrant Cho Lon neighborhood, the Thu Thanh Assembly Hall is a 18th-century pagoda that pays tribute to Thien Hau, the revered Goddess of the Sea. This architectural gem showcases intricate Chinese design elements, adorned with colorful figurines and beautiful lanterns that create an enchanting atmosphere.

Free

Binh Tay Market

Binh Tay Market, also known as Cholon market, is a historic and bustling covered market located in the heart of Ho Chi Minh City's Chinatown. With over 2,300 stalls, this market offers a wide variety of goods including home items, produce, clothing, jewelry, and more.

₫180000

Suoi Tien Theme Park

Suoi Tien Theme Park is a one-of-a-kind entertainment complex in Ho Chi Minh City, inspired by Vietnamese folklore and Eastern philosophy. The park offers a variety of attractions, including thrilling rides like roller coasters and the Kamikaze, as well as water-based activities. travellers can explore an aquarium, aquatic circus performances, and spiritual sites such as pagodas and simulations of heaven and hell.
