Da Nang

Cham ports and later a French naval base occupied the bay; the American war brought heavy fighting and airfield use. Postwar reconstruction and beach tourism remade Da Nang into central Vietnam's largest coastal city. The Cham Museum and river harbours point back to that prewar and wartime sequence. Marble Mountains shrines and Non Nuoc stone carving sit just south of the modern beach strip.

The Marble Mountains

Five limestone hills known as the Marble Mountains rise south of Da Nang with caves, pagodas, and carved shrines. Stone stairs and paths climb between viewpoints over the coast and Marble Village workshops. Daylight visits combine temple stops with short forested segments between peaks.

Con Market

Con Market (Chợ Cồn) has been a central covered marketplace in Da Nang since about 1940. Vendors sell produce, household goods, clothing, and prepared food along crowded aisles. Regular market hours keep the hall busy for residents more than for souvenir browsing alone.

03 AdmissionFree

Da Nang Cathedral

Located Da Nang, the Da Nang Cathedral, affectionately known as the Pink Cathedral or 'Rooster Church,' is a example of Gothic architecture that dates back to 1923. Its striking pink facade and 70-meter bell tower make it a standout landmark in the city. Visitors are often captivated by its elegant interior adorned with stained glass windows that narrate biblical tales.

Da Nang Museum of Cham Sculpture

Da Nang Museum of Cham Sculpture preserves stone and terracotta works created by the Cham civilization across central Vietnam. Early twentieth-century galleries and later extensions organize altars, deities, architectural fragments, inscriptions, and decorative carving by archaeological site and period; the collection reflects both Cham religious traditions and colonial-era collecting, while conservation and gallery updates change individual displays.

Dragon Bridge

Dragon Bridge spans the Han River in Da Nang with a dragon-shaped steel form often photographed alongside Thuan Phuoc, Han River, and Tran Thi Ly bridges. New Year programs and traditional music events use Dragon Bridge East Park nearby. Pedestrian decks and adjacent streets supply viewing points.

CauTinhYeu

Da Nang's Love Bridge forms a short pedestrian deck on the Han River near the Dragon Bridge, and railings, heart-shaped lighting, locks left by couples and open views toward the skyline make it a contemporary public-space attraction. Evening crowds, weather, river events, maintenance and nearby development affect access and the appearance of the waterfront.

07 AdmissionFree

Han River Bridge

Han River Bridge opened near the turn of the twenty-first century as a symbol of Da Nang's urban development and a working link across the river. Its central section was designed to rotate for shipping, though opening schedules and operations have changed over time. Traffic, maintenance, lighting, and special events affect pedestrian access, while other city bridges remain separate landmarks along the same waterfront.

My Khe Beach

My Khe Beach stretches along Da Nang's coastal road, open year-round and linked to adjacent beachfront districts by boardwalk and street access. Walkers combine sand time with nearby harbor and seaside market stops. Foot paths from the coastal road reach the shoreline directly.

Sun Wheel

Inside Asia Park, the Sun Wheel rises about 115 meters with 64 cabins for city and river views, ranking among the world's taller observation wheels and often photographed with Da Nang's bridges. Other park rides and Asian-themed architecture share the grounds. Cabins rotate through full panoramic circuits.

Da Nang DownTown

South of the city center, the former Asia Park amusement complex now uses the Da Nang Downtown brand. A large observation wheel, rides, performance areas, themed architecture and commercial spaces operate within a privately managed entertainment venue.

Helio Night Market

Near Sun World Theme Park, Helio Night Market (Khu Am thuc dem Helio) fills evening hours with BBQ, seafood, beer, wine, and bubble tea stalls. Food vendors operate during the market's night schedule. Locals and travellers share picnic tables among the stalls.

Bac My An Market

Bac My An Market occupies a residential district of Da Nang with stalls selling produce, meat, seafood and household goods. Clothing, snacks and prepared dishes fill other sections of the narrow aisles, where vendors and inventory change during the day, and the market's compact scale and ordinary neighborhood trade distinguish it from larger tourist-focused markets.

Ban Co Peak

Ban Co Peak rises above Da Nang with trail access to ridge viewpoints over the city and bay. Marked paths suit daylight hikes that pair with nearby forest walks on the same massif. Clear mornings show the coastline bending past Son Tra toward the Marble Mountains to the south.
