Da Nang

Cham ports and later a French naval base occupied the bay; the American war brought heavy fighting and airfield use. Postwar reconstruction and beach tourism remade Da Nang into central Vietnam's largest coastal city. The Cham Museum and river harbours point back to that prewar and wartime sequence. Marble Mountains shrines and Non Nuoc stone carving sit just south of the modern beach strip.

Da Nang Cathedral

Located Da Nang, the Da Nang Cathedral, affectionately known as the Pink Cathedral or 'Rooster Church,' is a example of Gothic architecture that dates back to 1923. Its striking pink facade and 70-meter bell tower make it a standout landmark in the city. Visitors are often captivated by its elegant interior adorned with stained glass windows that narrate biblical tales.

The Da Nang Museum of Cham Sculpture is a destination for anyone interested in the local history of Vietnam.
