Destination Guide — Da Nang

Explore Da Nang

A Brief History

Serving as a major port for the Champa Kingdom and later a crucial French colonial naval base, Da Nang endured heavy bombardment during the Vietnam War. Its rapid post-war reconstruction transformed a shattered military hub into central Vietnam's booming, forward-looking coastal metropolis. This rich historical tapestry established a strong foundation that continues to shape the city's dynamic contemporary identity.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

The Marble Mountains

The Marble Mountains is a documented natural feature in Da Nang, Vietnam, listed in regional geographic and conservation records for its geological formation and protected status. The site is accessible via marked trails and is open to visitors during daylight hours. Travellers follow the principal path from the trailhead and combine the visit with nearby viewpoints and forest walks.

Free

Con Market

Nestled in the heart of Da Nang, Con Market is a vibrant and historic marketplace that has been captivating visitors since 1940. This sprawling hub spans an impressive 14,000 square meters and features thousands of vendors offering everything from fresh produce to unique clothing and household items.

Free

Da Nang Cathedral

Nestled in the heart of Da Nang, the Da Nang Cathedral, affectionately known as the Pink Cathedral or 'Rooster Church,' is a example of Gothic architecture that dates back to 1923. Its striking pink facade and impressive 70-meter bell tower make it a standout landmark in the city. Visitors are often captivated by its elegant interior adorned with beautiful stained glass windows that narrate biblical tales.

Free

Da Nang Museum of Cham Sculpture

The Da Nang Museum of Cham Sculpture is a captivating destination for anyone interested in the rich cultural heritage of Vietnam. Established in 1915 and later expanded with a modern facility completed in 2011, this museum boasts an impressive collection that includes over 300 pieces of Cham artifacts, making it the largest of its kind globally.

Free

Dragon Bridge

Dragon Bridge in Da Nang is a must-visit destination, offering a glamorous view of the city and showcasing four famous bridges: Thuan Phuoc Bridge, Han River Bridge, Dragon Bridge, and Tran Thi Ly Bridge. The 666-meter long bridge features a unique design with a mighty yellow dragon that breathes fire and water on weekends. Visitors can also enjoy special programs such as New Year's Eve art celebrations and traditional Vietnamese music performances at Dragon Bridge East Park.

Free

CauTinhYeu

Cầu Tình Yêu (The Bridge of Love) is a modern, pedestrian-only bridge located on the Han River in Da Nang, Vietnam. Inspired by similar bridges worldwide, it features numerous heart-shaped lantern posts where couples attach engraved padlocks to symbolize their enduring love. The bridge offers excellent views of the city skyline and the nearby Dragon Bridge.

Free

Han River Bridge

Han River Bridge is a modern structure that can swing aside to make way for passing ships and is beautifully illuminated at night. It's one of the four main bridges in Da Nang and a popular attraction for visitors. Walking along this bridge offers a view of the city, including other famous bridges like Thuan Phuoc Bridge, Dragon Bridge, and Tran Thi Ly Bridge.

Free

My Khe Beach

My Khe Beach is a documented coastal feature in Da Nang, Vietnam, recognised in regional geographic listings for its natural formation and public access. The shoreline is open to visitors throughout the year and connects to adjacent beachfront districts. Travellers approach from the coastal road or boardwalk and combine the visit with nearby harbour areas and seaside markets.

Free

Sun Wheel

Sun Wheel is a prominent attraction in Da Nang, Vietnam, located within the Asia Park amusement park. Standing at 115 meters tall with 64 cabins, it offers a panoramic view of the city and its surroundings. The wheel ranks among the top five highest rotations globally and is often photographed alongside many bridges. Visitors can also enjoy various rides, activities, and diverse Asian architectural elements within the park.

Free

Da Nang DownTown

When exploring Da Nang, Vietnam, a visit to Asia Park is an experience that shouldn't be overlooked. This modern amusement park boasts a Ferris wheel that offers views of the city skyline, especially enchanting at night. While it may not be the largest theme park in Southeast Asia, its charm lies in its unique themed areas inspired by various Asian countries and thrilling rides. Visitors often find themselves pleasantly surprised by the fun atmosphere and friendly staff.

Free

Helio Night Market

Located in Da Nang, Vietnam, Khu Ẩm thực đêm Helio is a vibrant and bustling nighttime market situated near the renowned Sun World Theme Park. Open from 5 PM to 10:30 PM daily, this market features over 150 stalls offering a wide variety of Asian street food and beverages at reasonable prices. From BBQ and seafood to beer, wine, and bubble milk tea, there's something for every palate.

Free

Bac My An Market

Bac My An Market is a bustling hub for meat, fish, and produce vendors, as well as a popular spot for avocado ice cream. Situated near Danang's University of Economics, this market offers budget-friendly prices that cater to both students and tourists. Originally a small local market, its proximity to the university and resort areas has transformed it into a large-scale market offering a wide range of goods such as fabrics, dried foods, livestock, spices, and vegetables at affordable prices.

Free

Ban Co Peak

Ban Co Peak is a documented natural feature in Da Nang, Vietnam, listed in regional geographic and conservation records for its geological formation and protected status. The site is accessible via marked trails and is open to visitors during daylight hours. Travellers follow the principal path from the trailhead and combine the visit with nearby viewpoints and forest walks.
