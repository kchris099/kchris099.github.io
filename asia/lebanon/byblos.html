Explore Byblos

A Brief History

Continuously inhabited since the Neolithic period, Byblos became a major Phoenician port city known for trading cedar wood and papyrus. It was the center of the cult of Adonis and played a key role in the development of the Phoenician alphabet. Successive layers of Egyptian, Assyrian, Persian, Greek, Roman, and Crusader occupation reshaped its urban layout over millennia. Today, visitors can trace this extensive historical continuity through the ruins of the Temple of the Obelisks, the Crusader Castle, and the medieval harbor.

Byblos Citadel

Byblos Citadel is a fortified historic site in Byblos, Lebanon. Built from local limestone and incorporating remnants of Roman architecture, this castle is layered with influences from various civilizations including Phoenician and Ottoman cultures. Walls, courtyards, or towers form the main visitor route when the grounds are open.

Free

The Old Souq

The Old Souq in Jbeil, Lebanon is a must-visit destination offering a variety of experiences. It's known for its family-owned shops where find souvenirs, spices, and even a fossil fish museum. The area comes alive at night with pubs and offers sea views. The old souks are filled with handcrafts, souvenir shops, restaurants serving Italian, Japanese, and Lebanese cuisine as well as seafood.

Free

Byblos Dock And Fishing Port

Byblos Dock And Fishing Port is a waterfront area in Byblos, Lebanon. The port area offers a glimpse into the ancient city's ruins and provides opportunities for boat rides to admire Byblos from the sea. Quays and embankment paths form the main walking route.

Free

Jbeil Public Park

Jbeil Public Park is a public park in Byblos, Lebanon. Jbeil Public Park, located in Byblos, is a top destination for families and tourists alike. Children can enjoy the playground despite some broken parts, while adults have access to outdoor exercise equipment. Paths, lawns, and shaded areas make up the main public facilities.
