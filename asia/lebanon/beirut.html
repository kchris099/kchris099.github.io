Explore Beirut

A Brief History

Beirut is an ancient city with a history spanning over 5,000 years, originally settled by the Canaanites and Phoenicians. It flourished as a major Roman colony, renowned for its prestigious law school. Following periods of Arab, Crusader, and Ottoman rule, Beirut emerged as a cosmopolitan cultural and intellectual center in the twentieth century. Despite the devastation of the Lebanese Civil War, the city has continually rebuilt itself, maintaining its status as a dynamic Mediterranean capital.

Top Sights & Activities

Beirut Souks Lebanon

Beirut Souks is a vibrant urban complex In the city, offering a wide array of specialty shops, dining options, and entertainment facilities. The area was once home to bustling traditional bazaars but has been transformed into a modern commercial district following its reconstruction by renowned architect Rafael Moneo. travellers can explore luxury brand stores alongside more traditional market stalls, offering unique items such as clothing, jewelry, furniture, and lamps.

Free

Martyrs' Square

Martyrs' Square is a historic public space in the center of Beirut, named in honor of Lebanese nationalists executed there in 1916. The square features a prominent bronze monument commemorating the martyrs and serves as a central gathering place for civic events. It is surrounded by significant architectural landmarks, including the Mohammad Al-Amin Mosque.

Free

Mohammad Al Amin Mosque

Mohammad Al Amin Mosque, an Ottoman-influenced Muslim mosque, boasts a striking 48-meter blue dome and was reopened in 2008 after extensive renovations. Situated in downtown Beirut on the corner of Martyrs Square, this Sunni mosque stands out with its vast and towering presence compared to the surrounding architecture.

Free

Beit Beirut - Museum and Urban Cultural Center

Beit Beirut, also known as the Barakat Building or Yellow House, is a unique museum and urban cultural center located in a restored 1920s Ottoman-style apartment block. This building holds significant historical value as it served as a strategic sniper nest and witnessed intense combat during the civil war in Beirut.

Free

Al Raouche Rocks

Al Raouche Rocks, also referred to as Pigeon Rocks, is a must-visit destination in Beirut that captivates travellers with its limestone formations set against the backdrop of the Mediterranean Sea. This natural wonder not only showcases views but also offers a glimpse into Lebanon's rich history, having been shaped by a significant earthquake in the 13th century.

Free

Beirut Lighthouse

Beirut Lighthouse stands as a striking blend of functionality and cultural significance, embodying the charm of Beirut's Mediterranean coastline and its rich historical tapestry. This modern marvel, located in the Raouche area along Manara Street, offers panoramic views of both the sea and cityscape from afar.

Free

Corniche El Manara

Corniche El Manara, Ain El Mraiseh, Beirut is a popular seaside promenade that stretches for about 5 kilometers from St. George Bay to Ramlet al-Bayda. It was designed during the French Mandate period and offers a glimpse into the city's lifestyle. travellers can spot affluent locals at upscale cafes and in luxurious cars against the backdrop of the Mediterranean Sea and Mount Lebanon. The area is known for its vibrant nightlife with clubs being popular attractions at night.

Free

Sanayeh Park

Sanayeh Park, also known as Rene Mouawad Garden, is a historic public park in Beirut's Sanayeh district. Established in 1907 during the Ottoman period, it offers playgrounds, paved trails, and restroom facilities. The park features lush green spaces, dense trees, and charming water ponds for leisurely strolls and relaxation. Named after former Lebanese president Rene Mouawad, this park provides a rare green oasis in the bustling city of Beirut.

Free

Horsh Beirut

Horsh Beirut, the largest green space in the city, has been neglected since its completion in 1995. The campaign aims to open it securely and permanently for the public to enjoy fresh air and nature. The area is mainly filled with pine and olive trees, along with various plants like Arbutus unedo and bougainvillea. Despite being a bit run down, efforts are being made to clean up and improve landscaping.

Free

Souk El Tayeb Farmers' Market

Located on Armenia Street in Mar Mikhael, Beirut, Souk El Tayeb is a bustling farmers' market that opens every Saturday. The market offers a wide variety of seasonal produce, dairy items, sweets, and crafts from around 100 small producers across Lebanon. Despite the damage caused by the Beirut Port Explosion, the market continues to attract the freshest produce from countryside farms.
