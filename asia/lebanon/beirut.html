Beirut

Beirut is an ancient city with a history spanning over 5,000 years, originally settled by the Canaanites and Phoenicians. It flourished as a major Roman colony, known for its prestigious law school. Following periods of Arab, Crusader, and Ottoman rule, Beirut emerged as a cosmopolitan cultural and intellectual center in the twentieth century. Despite the devastation of the Lebanese Civil War, the city has continually rebuilt itself, maintaining its status as a dynamic Mediterranean capital.

Beirut Souks Lebanon

Beirut Souks is a active urban complex In the city, offering a wide array of specialty shops, dining options, and entertainment facilities. The area was once home to busy traditional bazaars but has been transformed into a modern commercial district following its reconstruction by known architect Rafael Moneo. Travellers can explore luxury brand stores alongside more traditional market stalls, offering unique items such as clothing, jewelry, furniture, and lamps.

Martyrs' Square is a historic public space in the center of Beirut, named in honor of Lebanese nationalists executed there in 1916. It is surrounded by significant architectural landmarks, including the Mohammad Al-Amin Mosque. Open pavement and surrounding facades define the visitor setting.

Mohammad Al Amin Mosque

Mohammad Al Amin Mosque, an Ottoman-influenced Muslim mosque, has a striking 48-meter blue dome and was reopened in 2008 after extensive renovations. Situated in downtown Beirut on the corner of Martyrs Square, this Sunni mosque stands out with its vast and towering presence compared to the surrounding architecture.

Beit Beirut - Museum and Urban Cultural Center

Beit Beirut, also known as the Barakat Building or Yellow House, is a unique museum and urban cultural center located in a restored 1920s Ottoman-style apartment block. This building holds significant historical value as it served as a strategic sniper nest and witnessed intense combat during the civil war in Beirut.

Al Raouche Rocks

Al Raouche Rocks, also referred to as Pigeon Rocks, is a must-visit destination in Beirut that draws travellers with its limestone formations set against the backdrop of the Mediterranean Sea. This natural formation not only showcases views but also offers a glimpse into Lebanon's rich history, having been shaped by a significant earthquake in the 13th century.

Beirut Lighthouse is a striking blend of functionality and cultural significance, embodying the charm of Beirut's Mediterranean coastline and its recorded history. This modern marvel, located in the Raouche area along Manara Street, offers panoramic views of both the sea and cityscape from afar.

Corniche El Manara

Corniche El Manara, Ain El Mraiseh, Beirut is a popular seaside promenade that stretches for about 5 kilometers from St. George Bay to Ramlet al-Bayda. It was designed during the French Mandate period and offers a glimpse into the city's lifestyle. Travellers can spot affluent locals at upscale cafes and in luxurious cars against the backdrop of the Mediterranean Sea and Mount Lebanon. The area is known for its active nightlife with clubs being popular attractions at night.

Sanayeh Park, also known as Rene Mouawad Garden, is a historic public park in Beirut's Sanayeh district. Horsh Beirut

Horsh Beirut, the largest green space in the city, has been neglected since its completion in 1995. The campaign aims to open it securely and permanently for the public to enjoy fresh air and nature. The area is mainly filled with pine and olive trees, along with various plants like Arbutus unedo and bougainvillea. Despite being a bit run down, efforts are being made to clean up and improve landscaping.

Souk El Tayeb Farmers' Market

Located on Armenia Street in Mar Mikhael, Beirut, Souk El Tayeb is a busy farmers' market that opens every Saturday. The market offers a wide variety of seasonal produce, dairy items, sweets, and crafts from around 100 small producers across Lebanon. Despite the damage caused by the Beirut Port Explosion, the market continues to attract the freshest produce from countryside farms.
