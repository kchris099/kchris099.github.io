A Brief History

Vagharshapat, commonly known as Etchmiadzin, is one of the oldest cities in Armenia and served as the capital of the ancient Kingdom of Armenia. It became the religious center of the country following the adoption of Christianity as the state religion in 301 AD. The city is defined by its collection of early Christian architectural sites, which illustrate the development of Armenian church building. These ecclesiastical structures continue to function as the spiritual headquarters of the Armenian Apostolic Church.

Holy Etchmiadzin - Armenian Apostolic Holy Church

The mother church of the Armenian Apostolic Church, located in Vagharshapat. It is one of the oldest cathedrals in the world and a UNESCO World Heritage site. It remains a notable local point of interest situated in Vagharshapat, Armenia. Visitors can observe the surrounding area and its connection to the city's broader historical landscape. Local guides sometimes include it on walking routes through the centre.
