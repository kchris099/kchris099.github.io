Destination Guide — Bangkok

Explore Bangkok

A Brief History

Bangkok became the capital of Siam in 1782 when King Rama I established the new royal city on Rattanakosin Island. Canals, palace compounds, temples, diplomatic quarters, and later rail and road networks expanded the city across both banks of the Chao Phraya. Its historic core and modern commercial districts still define daily movement through the capital.

Top Sights & Activities

฿500

The Grand Palace

The Grand Palace was established in 1782 as the royal complex of the new Bangkok capital. Its ceremonial halls, courts, and the Temple of the Emerald Buddha remain central to the history of the Chakri monarchy and the formal geography of old Rattanakosin.

฿200

Wat Arun

Wat Arun, the Temple of Dawn, is one of Bangkok's most iconic and visually striking landmarks, situated on the west bank of the Chao Phraya River. Its massive, central prang (spire) is intricately decorated with colorful porcelain and seashells. The temple is particularly impressive when illuminated at night or viewed during the early morning light.

฿100

Wat Suthat

Wat Suthat is one of the oldest and most important Buddhist temples in Bangkok, renowned for its magnificent 19th-century murals and the towering, red Giant Swing located just outside its entrance. The expansive temple complex features a serene atmosphere, a massive bronze Buddha statue, and intricate wood carvings, offering a quiet retreat in the historic district.

฿200

National Museum Bangkok

The National Museum Bangkok occupies the former Front Palace and traces Thai history through court art, inscriptions, sculpture, royal regalia, and archaeology. King Chulalongkorn opened the first public museum in 1874, and the present museum grew from that royal collection.

Free

The Shrine of Erawan

The Erawan Shrine was established in 1956 beside the former Erawan Hotel. It houses a statue of Phra Phrom, the Thai form of Brahma, and became a major urban shrine where residents, workers, and visitors make offerings in the middle of Bangkok's commercial district.

Free

ICONSIAM Thai Street Food

ICONSIAM opened on the Chao Phraya in 2018 as a mixed-use riverfront complex. Its indoor Thai street food section recreates market-style stalls and regional food settings inside a large retail and dining development that links shopping, public space, and river transport.

Free

centralwOrld

centralwOrld occupies a long-established retail site in the Ratchaprasong district and opened in its current form in 2006. It became one of Bangkok's largest shopping complexes and functions as a commercial hub connected to Skytrain lines, event spaces, and office towers.

Free

Terminal 21 Asok

Terminal 21 Asok is a very popular, uniquely themed shopping mall in central Bangkok. Each floor is designed to resemble a famous international city, such as Tokyo, London, or San Francisco, complete with detailed decor and iconic landmarks. The mall offers a wide variety of boutique shops, international brands, and a renowned, affordable food court.

฿200

Jim Thompson House Museum

The Jim Thompson House Museum, located on the Saen Saeb Canal in Bangkok, is a must-visit attraction. Once the home of American entrepreneur and architect Jim Thompson, it now showcases his impressive art collection. Thompson played a pivotal role in revitalizing the Thai silk industry after World War II and was honored for his contributions to Thailand. The museum features six traditional Thai teakwood houses that were carefully collected from different parts of Thailand by Thompson himself.

Free

Bangkok Art and Culture Centre

A contemporary arts center featuring exhibitions, performances, and cultural events, showcasing both local and international artists. The site forms part of Bangkok Art and Culture Centre's documented civic and cultural landscape within Thailand, with archives and visitor information available from municipal or regional heritage authorities.

Free

MBK CENTER

MBK Center is a massive shopping complex in Bangkok, boasting over 2000 shops, restaurants, cafes, and department stores across its 8 floors. It's a paradise for shopaholics with its wide range of brands and dining options. The mall offers everything from clothing and electronics to gifts and furniture. Additionally, travellers can enjoy various activities such as bowling, cinemas, and video games.

Free

Asiatique The Riverfront

Asiatique The Riverfront is a unique open-air mall and night market located along the Chao Praya River in Bangkok. It offers views of the river, featuring a 200-foot-tall Ferris wheel and a two-story merry-go-round. This former international trade port has been transformed into a shopping and cultural hub, with boutiques, restaurants, and traditional Thai performances like Muay Thai Live and mini-puppetry shows.

Free

Siam Paragon

Siam Paragon is a luxurious mall In Bangkok, Thailand. It offers a wide range of high-end and mainstream boutiques, dining options, entertainment facilities, and attractions for travellers of all ages. The mall features international luxury fashion brands, exquisite watches, and even showcases luxurious cars like Maserati. travellers can enjoy delicious food from renowned restaurants at the trendy food court on the ground floor or explore aquatic animals at the indoor Aquarium.

฿1199

SEA LIFE Bangkok Ocean World

SEA LIFE Bangkok Ocean World, situated in the Siam Paragon Mall, is a top-notch metropolitan aquarium in Southeast Asia. It boasts 7 themed exhibition areas showcasing hundreds of marine species. travellers can enjoy informative and fun experiences such as 4-D films and underwater adventures. The presentation of the tanks is impressive, featuring a wide variety of fish including sharks and rays.

Free

Victory Monument

Victory Monument, a prominent military monument in Bangkok's Ratchathewi district, commemorates Thailand's victory in the Franco-Thai War. The area is bustling with market stalls, street vendors, and diverse dining options including Thai and international cuisine. It serves as a major transportation hub with access to the BTS Skytrain and buses to nearby destinations.

฿100

Phaya Thai Palace

Phaya Thai Palace, originally established in 1909, is a royal estate featuring multiple throne halls and lush grounds adorned with fountains. Built by King Rama V in the early 20th century, this magnificent residence offers a glimpse into the glorious past of Thailand. While it was briefly transformed into an international hotel before being converted into a museum, travellers can now explore its historical buildings and verdant gardens.

Free

EmQuartier

EmQuartier is a contemporary mall in Bangkok that offers an array of international brands and upscale dining options. With nearly 50 food outlets, travellers can indulge in authentic Thai cuisine or opt for familiar Western fare. The mall's unique design features a striking spiral staircase concept, futuristic architectural elements, and a 100-meter long rainforest chandelier hanging from the ceiling.

฿1000

Rajadamnern Muay Thai Stadium

Rajadamnern Muay Thai Stadium is a historic sports arena in Bangkok that has been hosting Muay Thai kickboxing matches since 1945. The stadium offers an electrifying atmosphere with passionate fans and young fighters. For a better view of the fights, the club class seating provides an elevated perspective, and affordable drinks are available. Additionally, travellers can explore the Museum of Siam for an informative experience and enjoy a theatrical performance of Muay Thai Live at Asiatique.

Free

The Temple of the Emerald Buddha

The Temple of the Emerald Buddha, also known as Wat Phra Kaew, is a sacred site located within the Grand Palace in Bangkok. It houses the revered Emerald Buddha, which was brought from Laos in the 18th century. The temple is part of a vast compound that includes the former residence of Thai monarchs and spans over 94.5 hectares with more than 100 buildings representing royal history and architectural innovation.

Free

Bangkok City Pillar Shrine

Bangkok City Pillar Shrine is a small but revered shrine that houses the city pillar, which was erected in 1782 when Siam's capital moved to Bangkok. Located near the Grand Palace, it is often overlooked by tourists but regarded by locals. The shrine symbolizes stability of power and is an important cultural project planned by the Bangkok City Government. It features a mix of Buddhist and secular elements, with beautiful Thai-style artwork.

Free

Sanam Luang

Sanam Luang, also known as The Royal Field, is a significant grassy park located opposite the Grand Palace in Bangkok. This oval-shaped field has been used for various royal ceremonies since 1782 and holds historical importance. Surrounded by well-trimmed trees and a walking path, it serves as a venue for cultural events such as the Kite Flying Festival and the Royal Ploughing Ceremony.

Free

Dusit Maha Prasat Throne Hall

Dusit Maha Prasat Hall, located within the Grand Palace complex in Bangkok, is a example of traditional Thai architecture. Originally an audience hall, it now hosts annual royal ceremonies and funerals. Built during the reign of King Rama V to commemorate the Chakri dynasty's centenary, this Renaissance-style building features a distinct Thai roof. The central throne hall is used for public audiences while the top floor houses royal ashes.

Free

Wat Phra Chetuphon Wimon Mangkhalaram Rajwaramahawihan

Wat Phra Chetuphon Wimon Mangkhalaram Rajwaramahawihan, also known as the Temple of the Reclining Buddha, is a significant landmark temple in Thailand. The temple grounds offer a serene atmosphere and are home to a massive 46-meter-long reclining Buddha statue. travellers can explore historic art, statues, and colorful murals depicting over 400 images of the Buddha. Additionally, the temple houses a traditional Thai massage school.

Free

lebua at State Tower

Experience the epitome of luxury at lebua at State Tower, a chic hotel located in a swanky tower. The hotel offers high-end rooftop dining, an outdoor pool, and river views. With 178 expansive suites spanning from the 51st to the 59th floor, each accommodation features a bedroom, living room, and kitchenette with personalized and intuitive service.

฿1000

King Power Mahanakhon

A striking skyscraper with a unique pixelated design, offering panoramic views of Bangkok from its observation deck. The site forms part of King Power Mahanakhon's documented civic and cultural landscape within Thailand, with archives and visitor information available from municipal or regional heritage authorities.

Free

Lumphini Park

Lumphini Park is a sprawling urban oasis in downtown Bangkok, reminiscent of New York's Central Park. Spanning 142 acres, it offers manicured lawns, playgrounds, and an artificial lake with boat rentals. Accessible via public transport, the park provides a tranquil escape from the city's hustle and bustle. travellers can spot water monitors by the lake and enjoy activities like boating and bicycling.

Free

Wat Benchamabophit Dusitwanaram

Wat Benchamabophit Dusitwanaram, commonly referred to as the Marble Temple, is a architectural marvel located in Bangkok. Completed in 1911 during King Rama V's reign, this temple showcases an exquisite blend of Thai and European design elements. Crafted primarily from imported Italian marble, it radiates elegance and grandeur. The temple was designed by Prince Narisara Nuwattiwong and features intricate stained glass windows that enhance its beauty.

Free

Wat Mangkon Kamalawat (Wat Leng Noei Yi)

Wat Mangkon Kamalawat, also known as Wat Leng Noei Yi, is a significant Chinese Buddhist temple located in Bangkok's Chinatown. Established in 1871, the temple is steeped in Thai and Chinese culture and hosts various events such as Chinese New Year celebrations and food festivals. travellers can explore its numerous courtyards and passageways while admiring Buddhist artifacts like the laughing Buddha and other statues.

Free

Yaowarat Rd

Yaowarat Rd, a 1.5-kilometer street cutting through Bangkok's bustling Chinatown, is a must-visit destination. This historic district offers a vibrant mix of markets, shops, and delectable street food that comes alive after sunset. travellers can explore the intricate Chinese architecture adorned with ornate dragon sculptures and marvel at cultural experiences. The area also features attractions like Wat Traimit and Sampeng Lane market.

Free

Lhong 1919

Lhong 1919 is a restored 19th-century Chinese mansion that has been transformed into a mixed-use complex featuring restaurants, stores, and a shrine dedicated to Mazu, the Chinese goddess of sailors. This historic site was once home to Chinese immigrants seeking new opportunities in Thailand. The mansion showcases a blend of Thai and Chinese culture through its art and relics.

Free

Wat Paknam Phasi Charoen

Paknam Phasi Charoen Temple is a vast Buddhist complex with a towering stupa and numerous stone and glass sculptures. It features a giant golden Buddha statue located away from the main tourist trail, offering an impressive view as you approach from the Skytrain or along the canal. The temple, built during the Ayutthaya period, has an interesting history and attracts mae chis due to its meditation policies.

Free

The One Ratchada

A Night Market in Bangkok is a vibrant and popular spot for tourists, offering a mix of street food, trendy bars, local fashion, and unique souvenirs. The site forms part of The One Ratchada's documented civic and cultural landscape within Thailand, with archives and visitor information available from municipal or regional heritage authorities.

Free

Phra Sumen Fort

Phra Sumen Fort, built in 1783, stands as an octagonal bunker with a striking white exterior within a small park along the Chao Phraya River in Bangkok. Despite its proximity to the bustling Khao San Road, this architectural gem remains relatively undiscovered by tourists. The fort and its picturesque surroundings offer a serene spot for relaxation and contemplation.

Free

Democracy Monument

Democracy Monument in Bangkok is a significant historical attraction, symbolizing the Siamese Revolution of 1932. The monument features towering sculptures and fountains, with its art deco style commemorating the shift to a constitutional monarchy. Situated at a bustling intersection, it offers panoramic views of Bangkok from its open-sided top level. travellers can appreciate the monument's dramatic symbolism and vital position connected to other important sites in the city.

Free

Wat Ratchanatdaram Worawihan

Wat Ratchanatdaram Worawihan is a Buddhist temple complex in Bangkok, known for its Loha Prasat, or 'metal castle,' featuring 37 iron spires. Despite being close to major tourist attractions like the Golden Mount and the Grand Palace, it's often overlooked by travellers. The temple offers a serene escape from the bustling city with its landscaped gardens and provides impressive views from its cloistered corridors and compact rooftop area.

Free

Wat Saket Ratchawora Mahawihan

Saket Temple, also known as the Golden Mount, is a significant Buddhist temple and pilgrimage site in Bangkok. It offers extensive city views and showcases the local culture and traditions of the area. The temple's architecture and shrine of Lord Buddha at the top provide a 360-degree view of Bangkok. travellers, including children, can appreciate ancient art and culture while learning about the magnificent history of this place.

Free

The Giant Swing

The Giant Swing, also known as Sao Ching Cha, is a prominent landmark in Bangkok standing in City Hall square. It was originally erected in 1784 as part of the adjacent Devasathan, a Brahmin compound of shrines. This 21-meter tall scarlet frame overlooks the 19th-century Buddhist temple and was once used for a daring ceremony where men would swing to grab pouches of coins.

Free

Wat Ratchabophit Sathitmahasimaram Ratchaworawihan

Wat Ratchabophit Sathitmahasimaram Ratchaworawihan, also known as Wat Ratchabophit, is a magnificent 19th-century Buddhist temple located near the Grand Palace in Bangkok. It was the last temple built by King Rama V and showcases a blend of traditional Thai and European architectural elements, including Gothic features.

Free

Pak Khlong Talat (Flower Market)

Pak Khlong Talat, also known as Bangkok Flower Market, is a bustling and spacious market that offers an extensive variety of flowers, fruits, vegetables, and spices. Open 24 hours a day, the market provides a vibrant sensory experience with its colorful displays and fragrant scents. travellers can witness locals skillfully preparing sacred temple offerings such as delicate lotus blossoms and fragrant jasmine garlands.

Free

Chatuchak Weekend Market

Chatuchak Weekend Market, affectionately known as JJ Market, is a sprawling paradise for shoppers and explorers alike. With over 15,000 stalls brimming with an eclectic mix of goods—from vintage antiques to adorable pets—this market is a treasure trove waiting to be discovered. Originally established in the 1950s by a visionary former prime minister aiming to create flea markets across Thailand, it has evolved into the largest market in the country.
