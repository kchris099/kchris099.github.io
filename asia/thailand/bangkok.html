Explore Bangkok

A Brief History

Bangkok became the capital of Siam in 1782 when King Rama I established the new royal city on Rattanakosin Island. Canals, palace compounds, temples, diplomatic quarters, and later rail and road networks expanded the city across both banks of the Chao Phraya. Its historic core and modern commercial districts still define daily movement through the capital.

Top Sights & Activities

The Grand Palace

The Grand Palace was established in 1782 as the royal complex of the new Bangkok capital. Its ceremonial halls, courts, and the Temple of the Emerald Buddha remain central to the history of the Chakri monarchy and the formal geography of old Rattanakosin.

฿200

Wat Arun

Wat Arun stands on the west bank of the Chao Phraya and took much of its present form in the early Bangkok period. Its central prang, decorated with porcelain and seashell fragments, became one of the city's most recognizable riverfront structures.

฿100

Wat Suthat

Wat Suthat was begun in the reign of Rama I and completed under Rama III. The temple is known for its large ubosot, mural paintings, and the bronze Phra Si Sakayamuni image moved from Sukhothai. The Giant Swing stands beside the temple grounds.

฿200

National Museum Bangkok

The National Museum Bangkok occupies the former Front Palace and traces Thai history through court art, inscriptions, sculpture, royal regalia, and archaeology. King Chulalongkorn opened the first public museum in 1874, and the present museum grew from that royal collection.

Free

The Shrine of Erawan

The Erawan Shrine was established in 1956 beside the former Erawan Hotel. It houses a statue of Phra Phrom, the Thai form of Brahma, and became a major urban shrine where residents, workers, and visitors make offerings in the middle of Bangkok's commercial district.

Free

ICONSIAM Thai Street Food

ICONSIAM opened on the Chao Phraya in 2018 as a mixed-use riverfront complex. Its indoor Thai street food section recreates market-style stalls and regional food settings inside a large retail and dining development that links shopping, public space, and river transport.

Free

centralwOrld

centralwOrld occupies a long-established retail site in the Ratchaprasong district and opened in its current form in 2006. It became one of Bangkok's largest shopping complexes and functions as a commercial hub connected to Skytrain lines, event spaces, and office towers.

Free

Terminal 21 Asok

Terminal 21 Asok opened in 2011 at the Asok intersection. The mall organizes its interiors around travel-themed floors named after world cities and anchors one of Bangkok's busiest interchange points between the BTS and MRT systems.
