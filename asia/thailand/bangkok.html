Bangkok

Rama I moved the capital to Rattanakosin Island in 1782 after Ayutthaya's fall. Canals, royal temples, and later bridges spread Bangkok across the Chao Phraya. The Grand Palace and older canal districts explain the city's origin as a deliberate royal foundation. Chinatown along Yaowarat grew as a commercial counterweight to the royal island. Thonburi across the river was the brief prior capital before Rama I's move.

The Grand Palace

The Grand Palace was established in 1782 as the royal complex of the new Bangkok capital. Its ceremonial halls, courts, and the Temple of the Emerald Buddha remain central to the history of the Chakri monarchy and the formal geography of old Rattanakosin.

Wat Arun

Wat Arun rises on the west bank of the Chao Phraya, centered on a tall prang covered with porcelain and ceramic decoration, and the temple has earlier origins, while most of the present landmark form developed under early Bangkok kings. Steep terraces, ordination halls, statues, and active worship occupy the complex, and restoration frequently changes access to the prang.

Bangkok National Museum occupies the former Front Palace near Sanam Luang.
