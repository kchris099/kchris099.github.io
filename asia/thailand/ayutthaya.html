Destination Guide — Ayutthaya

Explore Ayutthaya

A Brief History

Serving as the striking capital of the Kingdom of Siam for over four centuries, Ayutthaya was one of the world's most populous and powerful trade hubs. Devastated by the Burmese in 1767, its massive, haunting brick ruins now stand as a solemn monument to pre-modern Thai grandeur. Today, this complex historical evolution remains significantly embedded in the civic architecture and vibrant daily life of the community.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

Wat Mahathat

Wat Mahathat is a Buddhist temple in Ayutthaya, known for its colorful shrines and famous Buddha head entwined in tree roots. The temple was once at the heart of Ayutthaya city but was destroyed during the Burmese invasion. Despite the damage, it remains a historical and cultural landmark. Visitors can explore the temple grounds, marvel at its impressive architecture, and learn about Thailand's rich religious heritage.

Free

Wat Ratchaburana

Wat Ratchaburana is an ancient Buddhist temple in Ayutthaya, Thailand, known for its elaborate carvings and a restored tower and tomb. The temple was built as a mark of remembrance to King Intharacharthirat and his two sons who died in an elephant duel over the throne. Inside the crypt, visitors can see frescoes depicting the story of Buddha's life.

Free

Wat Phanan Choeng Worawihan

Wat Phanan Choeng Worawihan, also known as the temple of the seated Buddha, is a historic and ornate temple complex located by the river. Dating back to before the founding of the Ayutthaya Kingdom, it has survived fires and been renovated over time. The main hall houses an impressive 19-meter tall sitting Buddha statue that attracts both locals and visitors.

Free

Wat Yai Chai Mongkol Worawihan

Wat Yai Chai Mongkhon is a restored Buddhist temple in Ayutthaya, featuring stupas and a huge reclining Buddha along with smaller seated ones in a row. The temple was built during the 14th century and is the largest and most important in Ayutthaya. It houses two large chedis built in classic Ayutthaya style, influenced by Ceylon architecture.

Free

Chedi Wat Sam Pluem

This bell-shaped chedi is a popular tourist attraction in the city of Ayutthaya. It welcomes visitors into the old city and used to be part of an important canal system. The surrounding monastery has been destroyed for road construction, but back in the day it was an important part of many other temple complexes. There is a large, ancient 'gangster' pagoda next to it that people often photograph without realizing how close they are to the vehicle.

Free

Wat Lokaya Sutha

Wat Lokkayasutha is a Buddhist temple in Ayutthaya, featuring an enormous reclining Buddha statue that measures approximately 42 meters long. The temple is located about a kilometer behind the Grand Palace and can be accessed by the road behind the Ancient Palace, passing other significant temples like Wat Worapho and Wat Worachettharam. This site is part of the Ayutthaya Historical Park and sits across a small canal from the rest of the park.

Free

Wat Phra Si Sanphet

Wat Phra Si Sanphet is a remarkable temple in Ayutthaya, Thailand. It was constructed during the 14th century and features three restored towers, with the central and eastern ones built in classic Ayutthaya style influenced by Ceylon architecture. The site also includes a viharn that houses a 16-meter high golden Buddha image coated with 170 kilograms of gold. This temple holds great historical significance as it once housed the ashes of several former Thai Kings for centuries.

Free

Wiharn Phra Mongkhon Bophit

Wihan Phra Mongkhon Bophit is a bustling and contemporary temple located just 4 km from the city center of Ayutthaya. It is renowned for housing a colossal, gilded statue of the seated Buddha, which is in Thailand. The temple compound, nestled within the historical park of Ayutthaya near the Grand Palace, attracts numerous visitors due to its rich historical significance.

Free

Wat Phra Ram

Wat Phra Ram is a 14th-century temple in Ayutthaya, Thailand, known for its crumbling yet picturesque religious site with Buddha images carved on the external walls and several ornate stupas. The temple offers an Instagrammable view reflected in the water of Bueng Phra Ram lake, especially at sunset.

Free

Wat Chaiwatthanaram

Wat Chaiwatthanaram is a 17th-century royal Buddhist temple located in a picturesque riverside setting. It is part of the Ayutthaya Historical Park and is renowned as Instagram-worthy temples in Ayutthaya. The temple's preserved ruins make it a must-visit attraction, along with the impressive complex of Wat Yai Chai Mongkol.

Free

Wat Kasattrathirat Worawihan

The Wat Kasattrathirat temple is an active Buddhist temple used by the clergy. The complex has a history that dating back all the way to the 15th century and features buildings that are relatively modern, but it is well maintained. The landscaping is 5-star and must be seen, as well as the peacefulness of the place. There is also a pier where feed fish.

Free

Wat Tha Ka Rong

Nestled near the picturesque Wat Chaiwatthanaram, Wat Tha Ka Rong is a hidden gem that offers a unique blend of culture and entertainment. This vibrant temple complex, often overlooked in historical records, boasts wooden craftsmanship and captivating architectural styles. Visitors are greeted by an array of colorful decor and quirky animatronic figures that add a whimsical touch to the experience.

Free

Bang Pa-In Palace

Bang Pa-In Royal Palace, also known as the Summer Palace, is a sprawling complex located near Ayutthaya. Originally built in the 17th century and extensively restored by King Chulalongkorn in the 19th century, the palace features a striking mix of traditional Thai, Chinese, and European architectural styles set amidst beautifully landscaped gardens and waterways.

Free

Wat Niwet Thammaprawat Ratchaworawihan

Wat Niwet Thammaprawat Ratchaworawihan is a Buddhist temple with a unique twist - it's housed in a Gothic Revival-style building that resembles a Christian church. Built by King Chulalongkorn, the temple reflects Thailand's cultural exchange with the West during his reign. The European cathedral appearance includes colorful stained-glass windows, and visitors can reach the temple by taking a cable car across the river.

Free

Wat Kudi Dao

The Wat Kudi Dao ruined Buddhist monastery is a popular tourist destination in Ayutthaya district, Thailand. Restoration work began in 1711 and was completed in 1715. The temple is known for its beautiful architecture, tranquil atmosphere, and open hours of 8:00am to 4:30pm. Nearby Wat Maheyong ruins are also worth visiting.

Free

Wat Maheyong

Wat Maheyong is a Buddhist monastery located just outside the ancient city of Ayutthaya, in Phra Nakhon Si Ayutthaya Province. The temple was originally built in 1438 A.D. and has been restored several times throughout history. Despite being in ruins, the ordination hall and other buildings are well-preserved. Visitors can explore the peaceful monastery and retreat center, experiencing a mix of old and new architecture.

Free

Wat Na Phra Meru Rachikaram

Wat Na Phra Meru Rachikaram is a significant Ayutthaya-period temple that houses unique Buddha statues, including one in regal garb and another made of green stone from the Dvaravati period. The temple features historical importance, having been the site of peace talks between Thai and Burmese kings in 1569 and serving as a base for a Burmese attack on the royal palace in 1760.

Free

Wat Thammikarat

Wat Thammikarat is a captivating historic temple complex that beautifully blends the past with the present. Nestled in an area rich with history, this active monastery showcases original ruins alongside replicas of statues. As you wander through its grounds, be greeted by vibrant roosters and lion sculptures that add a unique charm to the atmosphere. The architecture here is nothing short of , featuring intricate carvings and towering figures that radiate tranquility.

Free

Wat Phutthaisawan

Wat Phutthaisawan is a historical temple complex situated on the west bank of the Chao Phraya River in Ayutthaya. Established in 1353 by King Ramathibodi, this site holds significant cultural importance as it marks where the first king and his ministers resided before founding the capital city.

Free

King Naresuan Monument

Located in central Ayutthaya, the King Naresuan Monument commemorates the bravery of King Naresuan who is widely considered one of Thailand's greatest monarchs. The huge monument is flanked by many colorful chicken statues and offers a great view of the nearby White Stupa. Visitors can enjoy a bowl of delicious noodle soup after touring the site.

Free

วัดภูเขาทอง

Wat Phu Khao Thong (The Temple of the Golden Mount) is a historic Buddhist temple located just outside the central island of Ayutthaya. It is renowned for its massive, towering white chedi (stupa), which offers visitors who climb its steep stairs expansive, panoramic views of the surrounding flat countryside and the distant ruins of the ancient capital.

Free

Queen Sri Suriyothai Memorial

Ayutthaya Historical Park is a UNESCO World Heritage site located in Thailand, known for its ruins and ancient temples. The park showcases the rich history and cultural heritage of the Ayutthaya Kingdom, which was once a powerful and prosperous city. Visitors can explore the impressive architecture of the temples, statues of Buddha, and intricately carved stone structures that offer a glimpse into the past.

Free

Chao Phrom Market

Chao Phrom Market is a vibrant traditional market in Ayutthaya, dating back to the time of the city's capital. It offers a wide variety of goods, from local delicacies and fresh produce to unique merchandise not found elsewhere. The market never closes and is always bustling with activity, offering everything from fruits, vegetables, fish, and meats to electrical and household goods.
