Ayutthaya

For four centuries Ayutthaya was Siam's capital and a cosmopolitan river port until the Burmese sack of 1767. Brick prang and monastery ruins across the island city are the burnt shell of that pre-Bangkok kingdom. Foreign trading quarters once lined the rivers; Japanese and European villages are marked in ruins and museums. Wat Phra Mahathat's tree-wrapped Buddha head is the ruin visitors photograph most.
