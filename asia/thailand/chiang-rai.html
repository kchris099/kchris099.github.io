Chiang Rai

King Mengrai founded Chiang Rai in 1262 as an early Lanna capital and northern defence point. Chiang Mai later overshadowed it, yet twentieth-century temple art and hill-country cultures revived the city's distinct northern identity. The older moat-and-temple plan still underlies the modern grid. The Golden Triangle proximity tied the city to later border-trade and opium-history tourism.

Khun Korn Waterfall

Khun Korn Waterfall is a waterfall in Chiang Rai, Thailand. Khun Korn Waterfall is the tallest and a dramatic waterfalls in Chiang Rai province, cascading down a sheer 70-meter cliff. Located within a lush national forest park, the falls are accessible via a scenic, moderately challenging 1.4-kilometer hike through dense bamboo and tropical vegetation. Marked tracks lead to the main viewing points.

Singha Park Chiang Rai

Singha Park Chiang Rai is a public park in Chiang Rai, Thailand. Singha Park is a massive, beautifully landscaped agrotourism destination in Chiang Rai, owned by the Boon Rawd Brewery. Visitors can explore the grounds via tram tours or rented bicycles, enjoying the scenic agricultural environment and the well known golden Singha (lion) statue. Boon Rawd farm

Boon Rawd Farm, integrated within the larger Singha Park complex in Chiang Rai, focuses on sustainable agriculture and the cultivation of high-quality produce, including oolong tea, strawberries, and various vegetables. The farm offers educational tours demonstrating modern agricultural techniques and provides fresh, local ingredients for the restaurants located within the park.
