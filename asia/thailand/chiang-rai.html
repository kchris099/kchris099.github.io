Destination Guide — Northern Thailand

Explore Chiang Rai

A Brief History

Established as the original capital of the Lanna Kingdom in 1262, Chiang Rai served as a crucial defensive outpost against northern incursions. Overshadowed by Chiang Mai, its deep historical roots heavily influenced the modern renaissance of its unique, spiritually inspired contemporary arts. The remarkable survival of these cultural and architectural milestones provides a powerful window into the region's storied past.

Wat Rong Khun

Wat Rong Khun, also known as the White Temple, is a contemporary Buddhist temple located in Chiang Rai, Thailand. Designed by artist Chalermchai Kositpipat, it features a unique white exterior with intricate designs and unconventional architecture. Inside, visitors can explore unusual murals and decorations along with odd sculptures. The temple aims to provide a space for learning and meditation to promote the teachings of Buddhism.

Free

Khun Korn Waterfall

Khun Korn Waterfall is the tallest and one of the most dramatic waterfalls in Chiang Rai province, cascading down a sheer 70-meter cliff. Located within a lush national forest park, the falls are accessible via a scenic, moderately challenging 1.4-kilometer hike through dense bamboo and tropical vegetation. The powerful spray provides a refreshing natural retreat.

Free

Singha Park Chiang Rai

Singha Park is a massive, beautifully landscaped agrotourism destination in Chiang Rai, owned by the Boon Rawd Brewery. The expansive park features rolling tea plantations, fruit orchards, flower gardens, and a large lake. Visitors can explore the grounds via tram tours or rented bicycles, enjoying the scenic agricultural environment and the iconic golden Singha (lion) statue.

Free

Boon Rawd farm

Boon Rawd Farm, integrated within the larger Singha Park complex in Chiang Rai, focuses on sustainable agriculture and the cultivation of high-quality produce, including oolong tea, strawberries, and various vegetables. The farm offers educational tours demonstrating modern agricultural techniques and provides fresh, local ingredients for the restaurants located within the park.
