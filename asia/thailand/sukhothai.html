A Brief History

= Borihan Thepthani claimed the city of Sukhothai was founded in 494 CE, followed by its twin city of Chaliang (เชลียง). In contrast, the Legend of the Arhat (Tamnan Nithan Phra Arahant; ตำนานนิทานพระอรหันต์) says Sukhothai was founded in 679 CE by Indrajayadhirāja (อินทราไชยธิราช), who was from the city of Nakhon Luang (นครหลวง; lit. 'capital city'; potentially Lavapura); however, he, in 687, was deposed by Balidhiraja, the elder son of Kalavarnadisharaja of Lavo. The succession of rulers following Balidhiraja remains obscure in the extant historical record.

Top Sights & Activities

฿200

Sukhothai Historical Park

Sukhothai Historical Park is a must-visit destination, part of the UNESCO World Heritage Site that preserves the legacy of the Sukhothai Kingdom from the 12th to 14th centuries. The park features impressive ruins including temples and palaces, with notable sites like Wat Si Chum and Wat Maha That.

Free

King Ramkhamhaeng Monument

The King Ramkhamhaeng Monument is a striking memorial located within the Sukhothai Historical Park, a designated UNESCO World Heritage Site. This monument pays homage to King Ramkhamhaeng, the third ruler of the historic Sukhothai Empire and the creator of the Thai writing system. Visitors can explore this serene and uncrowded site, surrounded by ancient ruins and magnificent Buddha statues.

฿200

Wat Sa Si

Wat Sa Si is a serene and historical Buddhist temple complex located in the Sukhothai Historical Park. Situated on a small island surrounded by lotus flowers, this somewhat isolated wat features three 12th-century Khmer-style towers, making it larger than other temples in the central zone. The main chedi, built towards the end of the 14th century, is well-preserved and showcases Singhalese style influence.

฿200

Wat Mahathat

Wat Mahathat is an expansive complex of ruins from an ancient temple situated in a scenic, park-like environment. The New Year's countdown that took place there was a truly awe-inspiring event, and despite being small in scale, it was undoubtedly striking events I have ever attended. This was my third visit to the site in 2021 alone, primarily because I thoroughly enjoy exploring its surroundings.

฿200

Wat Si Sawai

Wat Si Sawai is a centuries-old sacred site that was originally a Hindu temple dedicated to Vishnu, later transformed into a Buddhist temple. It features three well-preserved Khmer-style towers and ornate pagodas, showcasing the mingling of Hindu and Buddhist influences. The temple's architecture reflects the classic styles of the Angkor period, with striking laterite prangs adorned with mythical figures.

฿200

Wat Si Chum

Wat Si Chum, also known as Wat Srijun, is a landmark temple located in the heart of Lampang, Thailand. The temple features a 49-ft.-tall Buddha surrounded by stone walls and an open ceiling. It has a small ordination hall with multi-tiered roofs and intricately carved eaves in the Burmese style.

฿200

Wat Phra Phai Luang

Wat Phra Phai Luang is a captivating temple located just outside the North Gate of the ancient city walls in Sukhothai Historical Park. This serene site features one remaining Khmer-style prang from the 12th century, showcasing architectural influences from Angkor and Lopburi. Originally, there were three towers here, but only one stands intact today, with remnants of the others still visible. The temple grounds are adorned with several Buddha statues that add to its tranquil atmosphere.

฿200

Wat Chang Lom

Wat Chang Lom is a historical temple located in Sukhothai province, featuring a stupa-shaped pagoda and other important temples such as Wat Phra Prang. The name translates to "temple surrounded by elephants," with 32 elephant statues around its base. The site also includes a vihara, an ordination hall, subordinate chedi, and a boundary wall enclosing the whole temple.

฿100 (match ticket)

Thalay Luang Stadium

Thalay Luang Stadium, nestled in the heart of Sukhothai, is a delightful venue that captures the essence of local sports culture. This well-kept stadium boasts clean facilities and a spacious layout, making it an inviting spot for both avid football fans and curious visitors. The atmosphere here is electric, especially during matches featuring the local team, Sukhothai FC.

Free

San Phra Mae Ya

San Phra Mae Ya is a respected shrine in Sukhothai, located in front of City Hall by the Yom River. It houses an idol of Phra Mae Ya, a stone figure resembling an ancient queen. The shrine is visited by many locals who come to make their prayers and seek good fortune. Visitors can also enjoy the serene park situated at the back of the shrine for relaxation.
