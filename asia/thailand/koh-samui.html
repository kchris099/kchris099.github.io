Koh Samui

Malay and Thai fishers and coconut planters occupied Samui for centuries with scant roads until the 1970s. Airport and resort development then remade the Gulf island. Fisherman's Village and remaining plantation plots hint at the pre-tourism economy. Big Buddha statue and temple headlands offer religious landmarks amid the resort belt. Nang Yuan viewpoint trails recall the island's pre-resort footpaths.

Lad Koh View Point

Lad Koh View Point sits on Koh Samui's eastern coastline as a roadside pull-off above rocky shore and open sea. Short paths reach railings and platforms used for sunrise and daytime coastal views toward neighboring islands. Travellers often combine the stop with beach drives along the east coast rather than treating it as a long hike.

02 AdmissionFree

Wat Phra Yai

A large seated Buddha image dominates Wat Phra Yai on a small island linked to Koh Samui by causeway. Built in the early 1970s, the modern temple complex includes a stairway, bells, shrines, shops and coastal views. The image remains a religious monument within an active worship site, where dress and conduct follow temple customs. The stairway provides broad coastal views.

9.5708° N / 100.0598° E

03 AdmissionFree

Wat Plai Laem

Wat Plai Laem forms a modern temple complex on Koh Samui, centered on a many-armed image of Guanyin, a large laughing Buddha, shrines, and pavilions beside an artificial lake. Chinese and Thai Buddhist imagery reflects local community patronage. Feeding fish and tourist photography occur alongside worship.

9.5713° N / 100.0671° E

04 AdmissionFree

Hin Ta Hin Yai

Grandfather and Grandmother Rocks, also known as Hin Ta Hin Yai, are natural rock formations in Thailand that resemble male and female genitalia. Legend has it that they are named after an old couple who died at sea while traveling to arrange a marriage for their son. These unique formations have become a popular tourist attraction on Ko Samui, drawing visitors to the Fishermans Village along Bo Phut Beach.

9.4522° N / 100.0395° E

05 AdmissionFree

Wat Ratchathammaram Red Temple

Also called Wat Sila Ngu, Wat Ratchathammaram on southern Koh Samui wears terracotta hues and sculptures of sea monsters, warriors, and Hindu figures such as Ganesha and Hanuman. Exterior and main halls can usually be seen outside services. The red temple stands apart from whitewashed island wats.

9.4511° N / 100.0309° E

06 AdmissionFree

Wat Khunaram (Phra Wihan Luang Pho Daeng)

Wat Khunaram on Koh Samui is a small working monastery known for the mummified body of Luang Pho Daeng, displayed in a glass case in meditation posture. Buddhist art and ordinary village temple buildings surround the memorial hall. Travellers stop briefly between beach areas, while locals continue regular offerings at the site.

9.4495° N / 100.0004° E

07 AdmissionFree

Tarnim Magic Garden

Since the 1970s, farmer Nim Thongsuk filled Tarnim Magic Garden with sculptures of Buddha, animals, and mythical beings among forest paths. Lawns and shade structure the walk. Personal craft and planted ground shape the park more than commercial rides.

9.4829° N / 99.9945° E

08 AdmissionFree

Fisherman's Village Night Market

Fisherman's Village in Bophut hosts a large evening market on designated days, when food, clothing, crafts, bars and performances fill the main street. Older wooden shop buildings recall the area's maritime and Chinese-Thai trading past, though most current activity is tourism-oriented; dates, road closures and vendor mix change.

9.5585° N / 100.0314° E

09 AdmissionFree

Samui Elephant Sanctuary Bophut

Located Bophut, the Samui Elephant Sanctuary offers a heartwarming experience for animal lovers and travelers alike. This sanctuary is dedicated to rescuing elephants and providing them with a safe haven where they can roam freely without the burden of riding or forced interactions. Inspired by the Save Elephant Foundation, it is a landmark of ethical tourism on the island, allowing visitors to engage with these gentle giants through feeding sessions and leisurely walks.

9.5481° N / 100.0385° E

10 AdmissionFree

Samui Elephant Haven

Samui Elephant Haven is a privately operated care center in the green hills of Bo Phut for elephants retired from logging and entertainment work. Guided programs focus on observing the animals, learning their individual histories and watching routine feeding or bathing. The forested grounds present the elephants within managed enclosures while explaining the labor and tourism practices that shaped their earlier lives.

9.5439° N / 100.0256° E

11 AdmissionFree

เจดีย์​ศรีสุคตคีรี (เจดีย์เขาหัวจุก)

Khao Hua Jook chedi stands on a hill near Chaweng and Koh Samui's airport, reached by a steep road and stairway. The gilded Sri Sukhot Khiri stupa and surrounding terrace support Buddhist worship and broad views over the coast and runway; the present monument is modern and restored.
