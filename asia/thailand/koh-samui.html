Koh Samui

Malay and Thai fishers and coconut planters occupied Samui for centuries with scant roads until the 1970s. Airport and resort development then remade the Gulf island. Fisherman's Village and remaining plantation plots hint at the pre-tourism economy. Big Buddha statue and temple headlands offer religious landmarks amid the resort belt. Nang Yuan viewpoint trails recall the island's pre-resort footpaths.

Hin Ta Hin Yai

Grandfather and Grandmother Rocks, also known as Hin Ta Hin Yai, are natural rock formations in Thailand that resemble male and female genitalia. Legend has it that they are named after an old couple who died at sea while traveling to arrange a marriage for their son. These unique formations have become a popular tourist attraction on Ko Samui, drawing visitors to the Fishermans Village along Bo Phut Beach.
