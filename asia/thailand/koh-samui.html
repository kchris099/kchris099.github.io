Destination Guide — Southern Thailand

Explore Koh Samui

A Brief History

Inhabited for centuries by fishermen and sea traders from the Malay Peninsula, Koh Samui operated without roads until the 1970s. The rapid expansion of international tourism aggressively transformed the remote, coconut-farming island into a premier Gulf of Thailand resort destination. Decades of careful preservation have ensured that this profound historical narrative remains a vital part of the modern experience.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

Lad Koh View Point

Lad Koh View Point is a must-visit coastal stop on the eastern coastline of Koh Samui. It offers panoramic views of the island's mountains, beaches, and unique rocky features. The viewpoint features a small shaded pavilion with seating and a walking path along the coastline, making it an ideal spot to take a break and enjoy the beautiful roadside scenery. Additionally, visitors can explore Angthong, a 42-island group that forms Thailand's vast marine reserve.

Free

Wat Phra Yai

Nestled in the vibrant Bo Put district of Koh Samui, the Big Buddha Temple (Wat Phra Yai) is home to a gilded statue that dates back to 1972. This landmark not only draws visitors for its spiritual significance but also for its picturesque surroundings. The nearby beach offers a delightful mix of low- to mid-cost bungalows, many featuring excellent dining options and luxurious spas.

Free

Wat Plai Laem

Wat Plai Laem is a vibrant, modern Buddhist temple complex located on the island of Koh Samui. It is most famous for its massive, striking statue of Guanyin, the Goddess of Mercy, featuring 18 arms, which sits on a platform surrounded by a lake. The temple showcases elaborate, colorful Thai and Chinese architectural elements and intricate murals.

Free

Hin Ta Hin Yai

Grandfather and Grandmother Rocks, also known as Hin Ta Hin Yai, are natural rock formations in Thailand that resemble male and female genitalia. Legend has it that they are named after an old couple who died at sea while traveling to arrange a marriage for their son. These unique formations have become a popular tourist attraction on Ko Samui, drawing visitors to the Fishermans Village along Bo Phut Beach.

Free

Wat Ratchathammaram Red Temple

Wat Ratchathammaram Red Temple, also known as Wat Sila Ngu, is a Buddhist temple located in the south of Koh Samui. The terracotta-hued temple is adorned with intricate sculptures of sea monsters, warriors, and Hindu gods such as Ganesha and Hanuman. The ongoing construction adds to its unique charm. Visitors can admire the beautiful staircase leading to the sea and the fascinating bas-relief designs on the red-clay temple hall.

Free

Wat Khunaram (Phra Wihan Luang Pho Daeng)

Wat Khunaram, also known as Phra Wihan Luang Por Daeng, is a small yet bustling Buddhist temple in Koh Samui that boasts an impressive display of Buddhist art. One of the main attractions of this serene and peaceful temple is the mummified monk, whose cool shades are accepted as donations and still uphold his appearance. It's fascinating how three different religions coexist harmoniously in this lovely temple.

Free

Tarnim Magic Garden

Nestled atop a mountain in the serene wilderness of Koh Samui lies the enchanting Tarnim Magic Garden, a hidden gem that beckons nature lovers and art enthusiasts alike. This lush forest park is adorned with an array of captivating sculptures, including depictions of Buddha, various animals, and mythical beings. Created by local farmer Nim Thongsuk since the 1970s, this magical landscape reflects his deep appreciation for both nature and artistry.

Free

Fisherman's Village Night Market

Fisherman's Village Night Market is a vibrant hub of culinary delights and local culture, perfect for those looking to indulge in an authentic experience. As the sun sets, the market comes alive with stalls offering a variety of delicious street food that showcases the region's flavors. From fresh seafood to tantalizing desserts, there's something for every palate.

Free

Samui Elephant Sanctuary Bophut

Nestled in Bophut, the Samui Elephant Sanctuary offers a heartwarming experience for animal lovers and travelers alike. This sanctuary is dedicated to rescuing elephants and providing them with a safe haven where they can roam freely without the burden of riding or forced interactions. Inspired by the Save Elephant Foundation, it stands as a beacon of ethical tourism on the island, allowing visitors to engage with these gentle giants through feeding sessions and leisurely walks.

Free

Samui Elephant Haven

Samui Elephant Haven is a sanctuary on Koh Samui that provides a safe haven for over 15 rescued elephants. Founded by Maew Sariya, the sanctuary offers these majestic creatures the opportunity to roam freely and form their own herd. Visitors can observe the elephants interacting in natural pools and mud pits, foraging on native plants, and playing together in beautiful surroundings.

Free

เจดีย์​ศรีสุคตคีรี (เจดีย์เขาหัวจุก)

Perched on a hill near Chaweng Lake, the Chedi Wat Khao Hua Chuk is a golden temple complex that offers views of Koh Samui's coastline and valleys. The intricate carvings and golden sculptures of Lord Buddha inside the temple are truly captivating. Visitors can also receive blessings from monks and take home a bracelet for their journey.
