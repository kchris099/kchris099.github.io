Chiang Mai

Founded in 1296 as the capital of the independent Lanna Kingdom, Chiang Mai was protected by massive walls and a defensive moat. Its profound Buddhist heritage and distinct northern cultural identity survived centuries of Burmese occupation before eventual integration with modern Thailand. Visitors today can clearly trace the detailed layers of this unique heritage throughout the preserved streets and monuments.

Tha Phae Gate

Tha Phae Gate, a well-preserved city gate with a rich history dating back to the Lanna Kingdom, was originally known as Chiang Ruak Gate. Situated by the Mae Ping River, it served as a vital entrance for trade routes. The gate's name was changed to Tha Phae, meaning 'Harbor of the Floating House,' reflecting its connection to river traders and fishermen. Today, it is an landmark within the ancient city wall of Chiang Mai.
