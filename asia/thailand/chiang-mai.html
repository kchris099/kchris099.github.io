Destination Guide — Chiang Mai

Explore Chiang Mai

A Brief History

Founded in 1296 as the capital of the independent Lanna Kingdom, Chiang Mai was protected by massive walls and a defensive moat. Its profound Buddhist heritage and distinct northern cultural identity survived centuries of Burmese occupation before eventual integration with modern Thailand. Visitors today can clearly trace the intricate layers of this unique heritage throughout the preserved streets and monuments.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

Tha Phae Gate

Tha Phae Gate, a well-preserved city gate with a rich history dating back to the Lanna Kingdom, was originally known as Chiang Ruak Gate. Situated by the Mae Ping River, it served as a vital entrance for trade routes. The gate's name was changed to Tha Phae, meaning 'Harbor of the Floating House,' reflecting its connection to river traders and fishermen. Today, it stands as an landmark within the ancient city wall of Chiang Mai.

Free

Wat Chiang Man

Nestled in the heart of Chiang Mai's historic old town, Wat Chiang Man stands as a testament to the city's rich heritage, being its oldest temple dating back to the late 13th century. This enchanting site features a gold chedi and an array of impressive elephant statues that showcase remarkable craftsmanship. Visitors are drawn not only by the ancient pagoda but also by the serene library known as Ubosot, which serves as a vital space for ceremonies.

Free

Wat Phra Singh Woramahawihan

Wat Phra Singh Woramahawihan is a revered 14th-century Buddhist temple located in Chiang Mai, Thailand. It is home to gold and copper Buddhas, ancient manuscripts, and intricate murals. The temple's main attraction is the Phra Phutta Sihing Buddha statue, which holds great cultural significance and is paraded through the city during the Songkran Festival.

Free

Kruba Srivichai Monument

Kruba Srivichai Monument sits within the Kruba Srivichai Monument area of Chiang Mai, documented in local heritage registers for its civic or cultural role. Visitors approach from the main access road and combine the visit with neighbouring heritage buildings and public spaces in the district.

Free

Wat Phra That Doi Suthep

Wat Phra That Doi Suthep, a revered temple in Northern Thailand, is known for its gold stupa, pagodas, and museum. Perched on a mountainside, the temple offers panoramic views of the city. To explore off-the-beaten-path routes in the region, consider hiring a local trekking guide through a reputable company. This allows access to non-touristic areas and an authentic experience.

Free

Doi Suthep-Pui National Park

Doi Suthep-Pui National Park is a forested sanctuary in Chiang Mai Province, Thailand, known for its natural beauty and cultural landmarks. The park encompasses two mountains, Doi Suthep and Doi Pui, and is home to the revered Wat Phra That Doi Suthep temple. Visitors can climb 309 steps to reach the temple complex and enjoy panoramic views of Chiang Mai.

Free

Bhubing Palace

Bhubing Palace, situated about 13.6 miles from Chiang Mai's city center near Wat Phra That Doi Suthep temple, is a royal residence built in the traditional northern Thai architectural style known as Reun Mu. This elegant palace, constructed in 1961, serves as the winter residence for the Thai Royal Family during their visits to Chiang Mai and also functions as a guest house for distinguished foreign visitors.

Free

Hmong Doi Pui Village

The Hmong Doi Pui Village is a traditional hill tribe settlement located near the summit of Doi Suthep, outside Chiang Mai. While it has become a popular tourist destination, it offers visitors a glimpse into the culture, traditional dress, and daily life of the Hmong people. The village features numerous stalls selling handmade crafts, textiles, and local agricultural products.

Free

Wat Pha Lad

Nestled in the serene foothills of Doi Suthep, Wat Pha Lat is a hidden gem that often escapes the notice of travelers rushing to the more famous Wat Phra That Doi Suthep. This charming Buddhist temple and monastery may not boast the grandeur of some other temples in Chiang Mai, but its true beauty lies in its tranquil woodland setting. Accessible via a scenic hiking trail, visitors are greeted by lush greenery and soothing sounds from nearby waterfalls.

Free

Wat Lok Moli

Nestled just outside the bustling gates of Chiang Mai's old city, Wat Lok Moli is a captivating Buddhist temple that dates back to at least the 1300s. Unlike most temples that face east, this unique site aligns along a north-south axis, adding to its charm. The highlight here is its striking chedi, one of the tallest in the area, which houses the ashes of several kings from the Mengrai dynasty.

Free

Wat Chedi Luang

Nestled in the heart of Chiang Mai's Old City, Wat Chedi Luang is a remarkable temple complex that showcases a partially crumbled chedi dating back to the 15th century. This historical site not only captivates visitors with its impressive architecture but also holds cultural significance as it features a pillar believed to protect the city. As you wander through this ancient structure, feel a deep connection to Chiang Mai's rich heritage and spiritual history.

Free

Wat Phantao

Wat Phantao is a hidden gem nestled in the heart of Chiang Mai, showcasing the rich history and artistry of Lanna architecture. Founded in the 14th century, this intricate teak Buddhist temple captivates visitors with its gilded accents and colorful mosaics. While it may not be as expansive as some neighboring temples, its charm lies in the exquisite details found within its centuries-old teakwood structures.

Free

Chiang Mai Night Market

Chiang Mai's Sunday Night Market, also known as the Walking Market, is a vibrant and bustling event that takes place every Sunday along Ratchadamnoen Road starting at Thapae Gate. The market features a wide array of local produce, prepared foods, crafts, and souvenirs. Visitors can explore handmade goods while witnessing artisans crafting their wares right at their stalls.

Free

Three Kings Monument

The Three Kings Monument, located in the heart of Chiang Mai's old-walled city, pays tribute to the founding fathers of the Lanna Kingdom. This elegant monument and its surrounding courtyard are considered the symbolic center of modern Chiang Mai. The sculpture depicts Kings Mengrai, Ramkamhaeng, and Ngam Muang, who played significant roles in establishing the city.

Free

Wat Umong Suan Putthatham

Nestled just outside the bustling old town of Chiang Mai, Wat Umong Suan Putthatham is a captivating historic temple complex that transports visitors back to the 13th century. This enchanting site features a network of ancient tunnels adorned with intriguing cave paintings and serene Buddha statues, all set against a backdrop of lush greenery and a tranquil lake. The atmosphere here is peaceful, making it an ideal spot for meditation or simply soaking in the natural beauty surrounding you.

Free

Baan Kang Wat

Baan Kang Wat is a charming artisan village situated in the Hand Dong District, just 5km south of downtown Chiang Mai. The village hosts a delightful artists market every Sunday from 8.00am to 1.00pm, showcasing the work of local creatives. This eco-village comprises standalone buildings housing galleries, restaurants, cafes, and workshops for art, design, yoga, and meditation.

Free

Wat Jedyod Royal Temple Wat Chet Yod Phra Aram Luang

Nestled away from the bustling city life, Wat Jed Yod, also known as the Temple of Seven Spires, offers a tranquil escape into history and spirituality. Located just a short drive from the Old City of Chiang Mai, this hidden gem is surrounded by lush fields and serene streets that lead you through picturesque landscapes framed by the majestic Suthep mountain range.

Free

Wat Suan Dok

Wat Suan Dok, also known as the "Field of Flowers Temple," is a tranquil Buddhist temple located about 1 km west of Chiang Mai's old city. The temple was established in the late 14th century on the grounds of a former royal flower garden. One of its most striking features is the impressive 48-meter-high bell-shaped chedi that enshrines sacred relics from Sukhothai.

Free

Wat Sri Suphan - Silver Temple

Nestled in the vibrant "Silver District" of Chiang Mai, Wat Sri Suphan, affectionately known as the Silver Temple, is a example of Lanna-style architecture. Originally constructed during the Mangrai dynasty, this temple may not be vast in size but captivates visitors with its intricate hand-crafted silver decorations that shimmer brilliantly under the sun. As you approach Wat Sri Suphan, you're welcomed by an enchanting display of silver artistry that truly lives up to its name.

Free

Wiang Kum Kam

Wiang Kum Kam is an ancient city and temple complex located just 5 kilometers southeast of the Old City in Chiang Mai, Thailand. This historic settlement was the capital of the Lanna Kingdom, built in the 13th century under King Mangrai the Great. Today, Wiang Kum Kam serves as an open-space museum showcasing Lanna culture from that period. Visitors can explore brick ruins, Buddhist sculptures, and everyday items from this era.

Free

Chiang Mai Gate

Chiang Mai Gate is a bustling area known for its vibrant markets and lively atmosphere. The Wua Lai (Saturday) Market, located opposite the gate, offers a wide array of goods to browse and bargain for every Saturday from 4 pm onwards. Nearby, the renowned Silver Temple, Wat Sri Suphan, showcases intricate decorative elements that shimmer in the late afternoon light.

Free

Chiang Mai Gate Market

Chiang Mai Gate Market is a lively open-air market located on the southern side of the old city wall in Chiang Mai. During the day, it offers a wide variety of street food snacks, grilled seafood, and fresh produce. As night falls, the market transforms as street vendors set up tents and prepare for a bustling evening. The area is also known for its local fruit and vegetable market during the day.

Free

Wua Lai Walking Street Saturday Market

Wua Lai Walking Street is a vibrant outdoor bazaar that spans about a mile and takes place every Saturday evening on Wualai Road near the Silver Temple. It offers an array of traditional street snacks, handmade crafts, souvenirs, and local delicacies. The market is known for its bustling atmosphere and diverse offerings including handcrafted lamps, dolls, soaps, jewelry, clothes, original paintings, and locally designed jewelry.

Free

Wat Chet Lin

Wat Jet Lin is a serene Buddhist temple in Chiang Mai, known for its tranquil atmosphere and rich history. The temple's name originates from the seven water channels used by the Lanna Royal family for bathing. While the exact construction date remains uncertain, it is believed to have origins dating back to the early 16th century. The main prayer hall houses various Buddha statues, including a striking large golden Buddha and a smaller jade statue.

Free

Chiang Mai Night Bazaar

As the sun sets in Chiang Mai, the vibrant Night Bazaar springs to life, transforming a typical street into a bustling hub of activity. Spanning approximately 1 kilometer along Thanon Chang Khlan, this market is a treasure trove for shoppers and food lovers alike. Here, find an eclectic mix of local crafts, souvenirs, and mouthwatering street food that showcases Thailand's rich culinary heritage.

Free

Waroros Market

Warorot Market, also known as Kad Luang, is a bustling 3-floor market in Chiang Mai that offers an array of experiences for visitors. The market is a paradise for food enthusiasts, with its wide selection of fresh produce, local snacks, and ready-to-eat meals. Additionally, the market features affordable clothing, fashion accessories, and household goods. It's a great place to immerse yourself in the local way of life and explore the vibrant atmosphere.

Free

Wat Chai Mongkol

Wat Chai Mongkhon is a serene Buddhist temple located by the riverside in Chiang Mai. It boasts a gilded stupa, intricate bas-relief carvings, and an ornate prayer hall. The temple holds great historical significance and offers visitors numerous captivating details to explore. Notable features include a statue of King Roman 5 under a majestic tree, a charming restaurant overlooking the river, and the opportunity to make special offerings of various animals to the river.

Free

Chiang Mai Night Safari

Chiang Mai Night Safari is a large zoo offering day and evening tram safari rides, kids' activities, and shows. The park features three major animal zones: Predator Prowl, Savanna Safari, and Jaguar Trail. Visitors can enjoy petting tiger cubs, hand-feeding wild animals, and watching dancing fountains and laser light shows. The Savanna Night Safari allows guests to see kangaroos, white rhinos, and water buffalos from an open tram.

Free

Royal Park Rajapruek

Nestled in the heart of Chiang Mai, Royal Park Rajapruek is a botanical garden that invites visitors to immerse themselves in nature's splendor. Spanning approximately 200 acres, this expansive park features meticulously landscaped grounds adorned with over 1,900 species of plants and flowers. Each themed zone showcases unique aspects of Thai culture and horticulture, including the enchanting Thai Tropical Garden and the vibrant Orchid Garden.

Free

Wat Phra That Doi Kham

Nestled atop a lush, forested hill on the outskirts of Chiang Mai lies Wat Phra That Doi Kham, also known as the Golden Temple. This hidden gem is celebrated for its magnificent 17-meter seated Buddha statue, which gleams in white and gold. With roots tracing back over a millennium, this temple offers visitors an enchanting experience filled with intricate shrines and pagodas to explore amidst the serene backdrop of the Doi Suthep Mountain range.

Free

Jing Jai Market

Jing Jai Market is a documented market or commercial centre in Chiang Mai, Thailand, listed in local civic guides for its role in regional trade and daily commerce. The site operates during standard market hours and is open to visitors without charge for browsing. Travellers enter from the surrounding streets and combine the visit with nearby food vendors and craft stalls.

Free

Wat Ku Tao

Wat Ku Tao is a serene Buddhist temple in Chiang Mai, known for its traditional Burmese, Chinese, and Confucian influences. The temple's unique Yunnanese stupa resembles a gourd or a stack of watermelons, symbolizing the lives of Buddha. It was built in 1613 to house the remains of Nawrahta Minsaw and has since become a peaceful retreat for meditation and prayer due to its low number of visitors.

Free

Mae Kha Canal

The Mae Kha Canal in Chiang Mai has recently undergone significant revitalization, transforming from a polluted waterway into a vibrant, picturesque pedestrian promenade. The canal is now lined with colorful flowers, small cafes, and boutique shops, often compared to the canals of Otaru in Japan. It has quickly become a popular spot for evening strolls and photography.
