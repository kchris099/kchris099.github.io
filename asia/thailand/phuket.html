Phuket

Phuket developed as a tin-mining and trading island on the Andaman Sea, with Sino-Portuguese shophouses in Phuket Town and fishing villages along western beaches. Chinese and Malay communities shaped markets and temples before tourism expanded from the 1980s. Rubber plantations and coastal resorts now dominate the economy while Old Town lanes preserve 19th-century merchant architecture.

Tourist Informatio Centre | Phuket Old Town

The tourist information center in Phuket Old Town is a public-service point within the historic commercial district. Phuket Old Town

Phuket Old Town preserves streets shaped by nineteenth- and early twentieth-century tin trade, migration and colonial connections, and the district remains lived and worked in. Sino-Portuguese shophouses, mansions, shrines, markets, cafes and street art now mix heritage with rapid commercial change.

Thao Thep Krasattri and Thao Si Sunthon Monument

The Heroines Monument honors sisters Thao Thep Krasattri and Thao Si Sunthon, credited in local history with organizing resistance to a Burmese invasion of Phuket in 1785. Their bronze figures stand in a busy traffic circle, receiving offerings and ceremonial attention. The monument reflects island identity and later commemoration.

Wat Chalong

Wat Chalong is Phuket's large and most prominent Buddhist temple complex, associated with monks Luang Pho Chaem and Luang Pho Chuang, and firecrackers may be set off in a designated structure, while active religious practice and dress expectations matter more than decorative photography. Ordination halls, gilded statues, murals, a tall chedi, relic traditions, and frequent offerings attract worshippers and tourists.

Kaokard Viewpoint Tower

The Big Buddha, Phuket

Phuket's Big Buddha is a large modern statue on Nakkerd Hill overlooking the southern island, and construction began in the early 2000s around an active Buddhist project supported by donations, with smaller shrines and viewpoints nearby. The Big Buddha, officially known as "Phraphutthamingmongkhol-akenagakhiri Buddha," stands majestically at 45 meters tall atop Nakerd Mountain in Phuket.
