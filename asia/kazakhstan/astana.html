Astana

Astana became Kazakhstan's capital in 1997, replacing Almaty with a planned city on the steppe beside the Ishim River. Norman support's Khan Shatyr, the Bayterek tower, and symmetrical government districts embody post-independence nation-building and oil wealth. The city symbolises Kazakhstan's move to centre administration in the north while preserving nomadic and Soviet legacies in its museums.

Astana Opera

Astana Opera opened in the early twenty-first century as a purpose-built venue for opera, ballet, symphonic music and visiting productions. A formal classical exterior surrounds foyers, rehearsal rooms and a large auditorium equipped for complex staging. The institution supports national companies and international repertoire within the monumental civic architecture of Kazakhstan's capital. The formal interiors match the capital's monumental planning.

Khan Shatyr

A vast transparent tent designed by Foster + Partners covers Khan Shatyr's shopping and entertainment complex in Astana. The cable-net structure moderates the capital's extreme climate while enclosing retail, dining, cinema, rides, and leisure facilities.

Nur-Astana Mosque

Nur-Astana Mosque stands in Astana's newer left-bank district with a large gold dome, four minarets, and broad ceremonial approaches. Opened in the early twenty-first century, it served as one of the capital's principal mosques before larger complexes were built; worship remains its primary function, and visitor access outside prayer follows current religious guidance.

Baiterek

Baiterek rises on Nurzhol Boulevard as a white tower topped by a golden sphere, a civic monument of the capital founded on the steppe. Observation levels look along the formal axis of government buildings toward the presidential palace. The tower's silhouette appears on souvenirs and city branding across Astana (once Nur-Sultan).

Ak Orda Presidential Palace

Ak Orda is the official workplace of Kazakhstan's president on Astana's monumental government axis. A blue-and-gold dome crowns the pale facade facing formal gardens and the Ishim River. Security controls restrict close access to the working palace, while designated public areas provide exterior views within the surrounding civic landscape.

Astana Arena

Located in Astana, Kazakhstan, the Astana Arena is a modern football stadium with a seating capacity of 30,000 and a retractable roof. It is the national stadium for the Kazakhstan national football team and is also home to FC Astana and FC Bayterek. The arena has modern facilities including comfortable seating, contemporary football pitch, and climate control systems suitable for all seasons.

Botanical Garden

The Botanical Garden in Astana, established in 2012 and opened to the public in 2018, is a relatively new but modern park offering various amenities not commonly found in other parks. It has nearly 90,000 trees and shrubs, making it a sight with flowers and trees. Visitors can enjoy jogging, cycling, and skating paths as well as an ice rink during winter.

Assumption Cathedral in Astana is a major Orthodox church with richly finished interiors used for liturgy and pilgrimage stays. Domes and icon screens dominate the prayer hall; support spaces include basic lodging and a church shop. Underground chapels extend worship beyond the main floor.
