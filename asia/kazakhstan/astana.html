Astana

Astana became Kazakhstan's capital in 1997, replacing Almaty with a planned city on the steppe beside the Ishim River. Norman support's Khan Shatyr, the Bayterek tower, and symmetrical government districts embody post-independence nation-building and oil wealth. The city symbolises Kazakhstan's move to centre administration in the north while preserving nomadic and Soviet legacies in its museums.
