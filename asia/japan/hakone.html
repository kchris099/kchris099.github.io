Hakone

Tokugawa officials guarded the Hakone checkpoint on the Tōkaidō between Edo and Kyoto. Nineteenth-century hot-spring hotels then turned the same volcanic pass into a mountain resort facing Mount Fuji. Barrier-station ruins and lakeside ryokan routes map that change from control point to leisure landscape. Sekisho museum displays explain how travellers' papers were checked at the barrier.

Odawara Castle

Odawara Castle is a castle in Hakone, Japan. Odawara Castle is a small but resilient fortress that houses various exhibits and historical artifacts, offering panoramic views from its tower. Owakudani Information Center

Owakudani, also known as Hell Valley, is a geothermic site in Hakone that offers views of Mt. Fuji and the surrounding area. Visitors can take the Hakone Tozan Railway or local buses from Hakone-Yumoto Station to reach this forested and mountainous area. One of the main attractions here is the chance to eat black eggs boiled in hot springs, which are said to add seven years to visitor life for each egg consumed.
