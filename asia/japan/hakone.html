Hakone

Tokugawa officials guarded the Hakone checkpoint on the Tōkaidō between Edo and Kyoto. Nineteenth-century hot-spring hotels then turned the same volcanic pass into a mountain resort facing Mount Fuji. Barrier-station ruins and lakeside ryokan routes map that change from control point to leisure landscape. Sekisho museum displays explain how travellers' papers were checked at the barrier.

Odawara Castle

Captured by Hojo Soun in 1495 as a hilltop fort, the site later grew into the seat of Hojo power in the region. Today's reconstructed tower holds exhibits and opens views over the castle town and coast. Walls, courtyards, and the keep form the route when grounds are open.

02 AdmissionFree

Odawara Castle Park

Parkland around the castle includes museum displays of samurai material and family amusement rides near the moats. The mid-fifteenth-century stronghold once housed the Odawara Hojo during the Warring States period. Walls and courtyards structure walks when the grounds are open.

35.2508° N / 139.1548° E

03 AdmissionFree

Ishigakiyama Castle

Ishigakiyama Castle Historical Park preserves earthworks and stone of the late-sixteenth-century overnight fortress above Odawara. Paths open views toward the city and ocean. Interpretation on site ties the remains to Toyotomi Hideyoshi's campaign against the Hojo.

35.2354° N / 139.1277° E

04 AdmissionFree

Tenzan Onsen

Tenzan Onsen is a traditional hot-spring bathing complex near Hakone Yumoto, reached by a short bus ride from the station. Indoor and outdoor baths use local spring water in a wooded valley setting. Day visitors share the facilities with overnight guests from nearby inns.

35.2252° N / 139.0887° E

05 AdmissionFree

Owakudani Information Center

Owakudani's information center explains the active volcanic valley above Hakone through displays on geology, gas monitoring, trail closures, and local ecology. Steam vents and sulfurous ground surround the developed viewing area, where black-shelled eggs are cooked in hot spring water.

35.2436° N / 139.0197° E

06 AdmissionFree

Hakone Craft House

Hands-on studios inside Hakone Gora Park make up Hakone Craft House, where scheduled workshops cover pottery, glassblowing, sandblasting, glass beads and related crafts. Participants create an object under staff guidance, with some pieces collected later after firing or finishing.

35.2483° N / 139.0457° E

07 AdmissionFree

Hakone Pirate Ship Tōgendai Port

Togendai Port links the Hakone Ropeway with sightseeing boats on Lake Ashi, making it a transfer point at the lake's northern end. The themed Hakone Pirate Ships sail toward Hakone-machi and Moto-Hakone, passing forested shores and possible views of Mount Fuji in clear weather.

35.2374° N / 138.9945° E

08 AdmissionFree

Hakone Shrine

Hakone Shrine stands among cedars on Lake Ashi's shore, long associated with prayers for martial success by figures such as Minamoto no Yoritomo and Tokugawa Ieyasu. A lakeside torii rises from the water near the main precinct. Stone stairs climb through the forest to the worship halls.

35.2048° N / 139.0254° E

09 AdmissionFree

Heiwa-no-Torii (Hakone Shrine)

A vermilion torii rises from Lake Ashi below Hakone Shrine, marking the waterside approach traditionally used by worshippers. Called Heiwa no Torii, the gate was dedicated in the twentieth century and later received its inscription. Stone stairs connect it with the forest shrine above.

35.2028° N / 139.0257° E

10 Admission¥1,700

Hakone Sightseeing Cruise

Hakone Sightseeing Cruise boats link ports on Lake Ashi, giving open views of the caldera lake, surrounding ridges, and, in clear weather, Mount Fuji. Pirate-ship styled vessels are the line's familiar silhouette. The cruise forms a standard segment of the Hakone transport loop.

35.1900° N / 139.0245° E

11 AdmissionFree

Mt.Taikan Observation Deck

Taikanzan Observation Deck belongs to the Hakone Turnpike area in Kanagawa, not the Aso caldera in Kumamoto. From the ridge, clear conditions reveal Lake Ashi, Mount Fuji, the Izu Peninsula and surrounding mountains. The viewpoint is reached by a toll road and is exposed to fog, wind and winter weather, so the landscape differs completely from Daikanbo's view over Mount Aso.

35.1847° N / 139.0484° E

12 AdmissionFree

Daiyuzan Saijoji Temple

Ancient cedar avenues climb through the forested precinct of Daiyuzan Saijoji near Minamiashigara. Founded in the late fourteenth century, the Soto Zen temple honors the monk Doryo, later revered as a protective tengu figure represented by statues and oversized geta. Gates, halls, streams, and long stairways spread across the mountainside beyond the compact form of a city temple.

35.3023° N / 139.0759° E
