Explore Hakone

A Brief History

Serving as an essential checkpoint on the historic Tokaido road linking Edo and Kyoto, Hakone was heavily guarded by the Tokugawa shogunate. Its transition into a premier mountain resort in the 19th century capitalized on its volcanic hot springs and well known views of Mount Fuji. The survival of these cultural and architectural milestones provides a powerful window into the region's storied past. Museums, markets, and street plans in Hakone still show layers from those periods.

Top Sights & Activities

Odawara Castle

Odawara Castle is a castle in Hakone, Japan. Odawara Castle is a small but resilient fortress that houses various exhibits and historical artifacts, offering panoramic views from its tower. Originally a simple hilltop fortification captured by Hojo Soun in 1495, it later expanded to become the seat of Hojo power. Walls, courtyards, or towers form the usual visitor route when open.

Free

Odawara Castle Park

Odawara Castle Park is a fortified historic site in Hakone, Japan. Construction and early use date to around 1870. Odawara Castle Park is a historical site with a museum showing samurai artifacts and offering kids' rides. Originally built in the mid-15th century, Odawara Castle was once home to the powerful Odawara Hojo daimyo during the Warring States period. Walls, courtyards, or towers form the main visitor route when the grounds are open.

Free

Ishigakiyama Castle

Ishigakiyama Castle is a castle in Hakone, Japan. Ishigakiyama Castle Historical Park is a large park that include the remains of a 16th-century castle, offering visitors views of the city and ocean. The park is situated on the site of Ishigakiyama Ichiya Castle, which has an intriguing creation myth and holds historical significance as it represents Odawara City. Walls, courtyards, or towers form the usual visitor route when open.

Free

Tenzan Onsen

Tenzan Onsen is a visitor site in Hakone, Japan. Tenzan Onsen is a recommended traditional Japanese bath located in Hakone, just a short bus ride from Hakone Yumoto station. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest. Tenzan Onsen remains part of the public visitor circuit in Hakone.

Free

Owakudani Information Center

Owakudani, also known as Hell Valley, is a geothermic site in Hakone that offers views of Mt. Fuji and the surrounding area. Visitors can take the Hakone Tozan Railway or local buses from Hakone-Yumoto Station to reach this forested and mountainous area. One of the main attractions here is the chance to eat black eggs boiled in hot springs, which are said to add seven years to visitor life for each egg consumed.

Free

Hakone Craft House

Hakone Craft House offers a unique chance to immerse oneself in traditional Japanese crafts. Visitors can participate in hands-on workshops where they can create their own works of art. The facility provides various experiences such as glassblowing, sandblasting, and pottery making. Even for English speakers with limited Japanese knowledge, it's easy to join the classes and the staff are attentive and helpful.

Free

Hakone Pirate Ship Tōgendai Port

The Hakone Pirate Ship Tōgendai Port offers a unique and memorable experience for visitors. From the former Hakone Port, now known as Moto-Hakone Port, tourists can enjoy sights such as Lake Ashi and Mount Fuji. The still, clear surface of Lake Ashi provides a mesmerizing reflection of Mount Fuji, especially in the early morning mist.

Free

Hakone Shrine

Hakone Shrine is a visitor site in Hakone, Japan. The shrine is believed to bring luck in battle and has been revered by prominent historical figures like shogun Tokugawa Ieyasu and ruler Minamoto no Yoritomo. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Free

Heiwa-no-Torii (Hakone Shrine)

Heiwa no Torii is a and spot in Hakone, located by the shore of Lake Ashi. This big Torii gate offers a photogenic view and is a popular attraction for visitors. The area can get crowded, with queues forming for that perfect photo opportunity. Nearby, the historic Hakone Shrine, dating back to 757, has the chance to purchase Omamori amulets for protection.

¥1,700

Hakone Sightseeing Cruise

Hakone Sightseeing Cruise is a visitor site in Hakone, Japan. The Hakone Sightseeing Cruise offers a unique experience for travelers looking to explore the scenic landscapes of Hakone. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Free

Mt.Taikan Observation Deck

The Mt. Taikan Observation Deck is situated on the edge of the massive Aso Caldera in Kumamoto Prefecture. It offers some of the most expansive and dramatic panoramic views of the five peaks of Mount Aso and the vast, cultivated caldera floor below. It is particularly famous for the 'sea of clouds' phenomenon that frequently occurs in the early morning.

Free

Daiyuzan Saijoji Temple

Daiyuzan Saijoji Temple is a religious building in Hakone, Japan. The exterior and main interior spaces are usually viewable outside service times. Local maps identify Daiyuzan Saijoji Temple within Hakone, Japan. Posted signs mark approaches and opening information for Daiyuzan Saijoji Temple.
