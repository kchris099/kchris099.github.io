Nagoya

= Jōmon period & Kofun period In the Jomon and Yayoi period, the Ōguruwa Shell Midden was discovered before the settlement of Nagoya. In the Kofun period, Nagoya was settled and the Danpusan Kofun and Shiratori Kofun was built in this area. The Atsuta Shrine is of ancient origin, it is home to the Imperial Regalia of Japan, the legendary sword Kusanagi no Tsurugi. According to traditional sources, Yamato Takeru died in 113 AD. The possessions of the dead prince were gathered together along with the sword Kusanagi and his widow venerated his memory in a shrine at her home. Heian period The Seigan-ji was built by the Fujiwara clan in the late Heian period. A member served as the head priest of the nearby Atsuta Shrine, one of the legendary shrines of Japan.

Atsuta-jingu Shrine

Atsuta Jingu, often called Atsuta-san, is one of Japan's major shrines and draws millions of worshippers each year. Tradition links it to the sacred sword Kusanagi among the imperial regalia. Broad wooded grounds, multiple halls, and busy approaches fill a large precinct in southern Nagoya.

Toyota Auto Museum

Toyota Automobile Museum in Nagakute, east of Nagoya, displays the broader development of global motoring. Chronological galleries include vehicles from European, American and Japanese manufacturers, while a cultural wing explores design, advertising and social change; the museum's location and scope distinguish it from Toyota's industry museums in central Nagoya and Toyota City.

Port Of Nagoya Public Aquarium

Two large wings at the Port of Nagoya Public Aquarium present marine life from Japanese waters and polar or oceanic regions. Dolphins, orcas and belugas occupy major tanks, joined by penguins, sea turtles, fish and deep-sea species. A large stadium supports marine-mammal presentations, while educational programs explain animal biology and conservation.

Kiyosu Castle

A reconstructed keep across a bridge draws fewer tourists than Nagoya Castle yet still opens walls and courtyards when grounds allow. Stamp collecting and exterior walks are who make the short trip. The site recalls the town's earlier political weight before Nagoya rose.

05 Admission¥500

Nagoya Castle

Tokugawa Ieyasu's 1612 fortress, rebuilt after wartime loss and reopened around 1959, carries golden roof ornaments visible from station approaches. Exhibits include Edo-period costume and weapon collections inside. Hommaru palace reconstructions continue to expand the on-site story.

06 AdmissionFree

Osu Kannon

Osu Kannon stands beside the covered Osu shopping district in central Nagoya. The temple traces its origin to the fourteenth century and was moved to its current site under Tokugawa Ieyasu, while wartime destruction led to modern reconstruction. A library tradition, monthly flea markets, pigeon-filled grounds, and devotion to Kannon connect the religious precinct with the surrounding commercial neighborhood.

35.1597° N / 136.8994° E

Chubu Electric Power MIRAI TOWER

Completed in 1954, Nagoya's steel broadcasting tower rises from Hisaya-odori Park under the current name Chubu Electric Power MIRAI TOWER. Observation levels look across the central grid, Sakae district, and distant mountains, while lighting changes the nighttime appearance; hotel and event uses have been introduced within the structure, so access extends beyond a conventional television tower deck.

An oval glass-roofed complex holds shops, eateries, and a bus terminal under a shallow water skin on the upper deck. Night lighting turns the structure into a glowing lens beside the nearby TV tower. Paths link the transit deck with surrounding Sakae streets.

Sky Promenade

Midland Square's Sky Promenade occupies the upper levels of a high-rise opposite Nagoya Station. A partly open-air route ascends through glass-walled observation floors overlooking rail lines, downtown towers and the wider Nobi Plain. The exposed design brings wind and changing weather into the experience, distinguishing it from a fully enclosed viewing gallery. Open sections make the weather noticeable at elevation.

Vantelin Dome Nagoya

Vantelin Dome Nagoya is the indoor home of the Chunichi Dragons, seating roughly 40,000 under a geodesic roof. Baseball games fill the calendar, with concerts and other events between seasons. The dome rises near central Nagoya transit lines, a short ride from the castle and downtown shopping districts.

Tokugawa Art Museum

The Tokugawa Art Museum, situated in the historic Nagoya's Tokugawa Park, is a known institution showing an extensive collection of artifacts and cultural relics from the influential Tokugawa family. With over 10,000 pieces including national treasures such as samurai armor and Noh theater artifacts, it offers a fascinating insight into Japanese history and culture.

12 Admission¥500

Higashiyama Zoo and Botanical Gardens

Higashiyama Zoo and Botanical Gardens, located in Nagoya, is a popular tourist spot offering a diverse range of attractions. The zoo has over 125 species of mammals, 70 species of reptiles, and more than 86 species of birds. Adjacent to the zoo is the city's Botanical Gardens featuring an extensive greenhouse with over 7,000 varieties of plants from different environments such as desert and jungle.

Higashiyama Sky Tower

Higashiyama Sky Tower stands on elevated ground beside Nagoya's zoo and botanical garden. Observation floors look across the city toward Mount Ontake, the Central Alps and surrounding districts when visibility is clear. The tower rises further above the plateau, so its outlook combines a high natural base with an enclosed urban observation structure.
