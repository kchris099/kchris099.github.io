Explore Hiroshima

A Brief History

Hiroshima became the capital of Hiroshima Prefecture in the Meiji period and developed as a military port on the Seto Inland Sea. On 6 August 1945 an atomic bomb destroyed the city center. Peace Memorial Park, the A-bomb Dome, and museums preserve that history. Reconstruction from the 1950s created broad avenues and trams. Hiroshima Castle, originally built in the 1590s, was rebuilt in concrete after the war.

Atomic Bomb Dome

The Hiroshima Peace Memorial, originally the Hiroshima Prefectural Industrial Promotion Hall, and now commonly called the Genbaku Dome, Atomic Bomb Dome or A-Bomb Dome, is part of Hiroshima Peace Memorial Park in Hiroshima, Japan, and was designated a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 1996.

Closed

Hiroshima Castle

Hiroshima Castle is a visitor site in Hiroshima. Originally constructed in 1589, this formidable castle served as the political and economic center of the region. Completely destroyed by the atomic bomb in 1945, the replica built in 1958 now is a powerful symbol of the city's historical resilience.

Free (Peace Memorial Museum ¥200)

Peace Memorial Park - Hiroshima

The Peace Memorial Park in Hiroshima is a vast, solemn park dedicated to the legacy of the city as the first in the world to suffer a nuclear attack. The park contains numerous memorials, including the well known A-Bomb Dome, the Cenotaph for the A-Bomb Victims, and the Peace Memorial Museum. It is a powerful global symbol advocating for the abolition of nuclear weapons.

Free (ferry to island ¥100)

Miyajima

Miyajima is a visitor site in Hiroshima, Japan. A sacred island in the Seto Inland Sea, historically revered in Shintoism as an island of gods. It has been a major pilgrimage site for centuries, defined by its preserved ancient shrines and deep spiritual tranquility. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Free

Itsukushima Shrine Otorii Gate

Itsukushima Shrine is a Shinto shrine on the island of Itsukushima, known for its "floating" torii. It is in the city of Hatsukaichi, in Hiroshima Prefecture in Japan, accessible from the mainland by ferry at Miyajimaguchi Station. The shrine complex is listed as a UNESCO World Heritage Site, and the Japanese government has designated several buildings and possessions as National Treasures.

Free

Itsukushima Jinja

Itsukushima Shrine is a Shinto shrine complex on Miyajima Island, recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage site. It is most known for massive, vermilion 'floating' torii gate, which appears to stand on the water during high tide. The shrine's main buildings are constructed on piers over the tidal flats, creating a unique and striking architectural ensemble.

Free

Daishoin

Daishoin is a visitor site in Hiroshima. Daishō-in or Daisyō-in, also called Suishō-ji, is a Japanese Buddhist temple complex with many temples and statues on Mount Misen, the holy mountain on the island of Itsukushima, off the coast of Hatsukaichi, Hiroshima, Japan. It is the 14th temple in the Chūgoku 33 Kannon Pilgrimage and famous for the maple trees and their autumn colors.

Free

Daigan-ji

Daigan-ji is a visitor site in Hiroshima. The Three Great Shrines of Benzaiten are a group of Japanese shrines dedicated to the worship of the goddess Benzaiten. During the Meiji Era separation of Shinto and Buddhism the veneration of the Buddhist water-goddess Benzaiten was replaced by the veneration of the Munakata sanjojin, three Shinto goddesses of the sea. The official veneration of Benzaiten was moved to separate Buddhist temples.

¥350

Shukkeien Garden

Shukkeien Garden is a public park in Hiroshima, Japan. It offers a peaceful escape from the city with its koi pond, tea shop, and flowering trees. The garden is an ideal place for contemplation and relaxation, featuring extended paths, lush green spaces, streams, bridges, and a central pond. Paths, lawns, and shaded areas make up the main public facilities.

Free

Mazda Zoom-Zoom Stadium Hiroshima

Mazda Zoom-Zoom Stadium Hiroshima, also known as Hiroshima Municipal Stadium, is the home of the Hiroshima Toyo Carp baseball team. The stadium offers tours and is conveniently located a short walk from Hiroshima Station. It has a spacious and open design with comfortable seating that adheres to Major League standards. The 600m-long concourse behind the seats allows for easy movement and access to concession stands.

Free

原爆の子の像

The Children's Peace Monument (原爆の子の像) in Hiroshima Peace Memorial Park commemorates child victims of the 1945 atomic bombing, including Sadako Sasaki. Paper crane offerings surround the figure. The memorial stands near the park's central paths and is open as part of the free park grounds.

Free

Okonomimura

Okonomimura is a visitor site in Hiroshima, Japan. Located Hiroshima, Okonomimura is a active food hall that celebrates the city's beloved dish: Hiroshima-style okonomiyaki. The history of Okonomimura dates back to post-war times when numerous food stalls began serving delicious fare in Shintenchi Hiroba. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Free

Flame of Peace

Flame of Peace is a visitor site in Hiroshima, Japan. Records associated with the site include 1945, 1964. The Flame of Peace is a moving monument dedicated to the victims of the 1945 atomic bomb. The eternal flame was ignited in 1964 and has been continuously burning since then. It is a solemn reminder of the tragic event and is a symbol of global cooperation towards creating a safer world by advocating for the elimination of nuclear weapons.

Free

Mitaki Temple

Mitaki Temple is a religious building in Hiroshima, Japan. It's an underrated scenic beauty with deep vegetation, quaint rivers, pagodas, and Buddhist statues. The exterior and main interior spaces are usually viewable outside service times. Mitaki Temple remains part of the public visitor circuit in Hiroshima.
