Kyoto

Kyoto served as Japan's imperial capital from 794 to 1868, when the court moved to Tokyo. Temples, shrines, and gardens from the Heian, medieval, and Edo periods line its eastern hills and river valleys. The city was largely spared from air raids in 1945. Seventeen historic monuments in Kyoto were inscribed as a UNESCO World Heritage site in 1994. Traditional crafts and festivals continue in preserved districts.

Ninenzaka

Ninenzaka, or Ninen-zaka is an ancient stone-paved pedestrian road in Higashiyama-ku, Kyoto, Japan. It connects Kōdai-ji Temple with Kiyomizu-dera and is often walked in sequence with the parallel Sannenzaka lane, together forming one of Kyoto's most photographed historic streetscapes.
