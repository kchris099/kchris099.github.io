Hakodate

Hakodate was one of the first Japanese ports opened to foreign trade after the 1854 Treaty of Kanagawa, drawing consulates and Western-style warehouses onto the waterfront. The Goryokaku star fort marked late-Tokugawa and early-Meiji conflict on Hokkaido. Fire and wartime damage reshaped the centre, while the cable car to Mount Hakodate and morning seafood markets define the modern port city at the island's southern tip.

Goryokaku Park

Located Hakodate, Goryokaku Park is a star-shaped fort dating back to the 1800s that has transformed into a lush park. This historic site, recognized as a national special historic location, was once the battleground for the final clash between the old Shogunate army and new government forces during the Boshin War. Today, travellers can explore its beautifully maintained gardens and admire restored structures like the Hakodate Magistrate's Office.

Kanemori Red Brick Warehouse

Discover a shopping destination with a rich history at the Kanemori Red Brick Warehouse in Hakodate. This circa-1869 warehouse complex has been transformed into a busy commercial district, offering an array of shops where find local snacks, sweets, and unique clothing and accessories. As one of the first ports in Japan to open to foreign trade, Hakodate has a long commercial history, and the red brick warehouses along the bay serve as a evidence of this legacy.

Hachiman-Zaka Slope

Hachiman-Zaka Slope is a known attraction in Hakodate, known for its steep incline and vistas. Named after the nearby Hakodate Hachiman Shrine, this stone-paved street is flanked by trees and offers panoramic views of the Hakodate Bay area and the distant Mashu Maru. Among the 19 sloping streets in Hakodate, Hachiman-Zaka is considered the most as it descends from Mount Hakodate to Hakodate Bay.

Our Lady of the Angels Trappistine Abbey

Our Lady of the Angels Trappistine Abbey is a religious building in Hakodate, Japan. Construction and early use date to around 1898. While some areas are off-limits due to its functioning as a nunnery, travellers can still enjoy the garden and architecture. Yukura Shrine

Yukura Shrine is a visitor site in Hakodate, Japan. It is adorned with a row of striking red torii gates and is known for dispensing fortunes tied around paper squids. The shrine offers a wide range of benefits, including blessings for land management, medical care, hot springs, cultivation, good harvests, prosperous businesses, family safety, and marriage. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.
