Explore Hakodate

A Brief History

Established as a regional settlement beyond an older fortified core, the city developed through industrialization in the late 19th century, post-war reconstruction, and modern civic expansion that reshaped its public spaces, markets, and surrounding landscape. Restored religious buildings, civic buildings, and natural features now define the contemporary visitor experience.

Goryokaku Park

Nestled in the heart of Hakodate, Goryokaku Park is a star-shaped fort dating back to the 1800s that has transformed into a lush green oasis. This historic site, recognized as a national special historic location, was once the battleground for the final clash between the old Shogunate army and new government forces during the Boshin War. Today, travellers can explore its beautifully maintained gardens and admire restored structures like the Hakodate Magistrate's Office.

Free (Goryokaku Tower ¥1200)

Mount Hakodate

Mount Hakodate, standing at 1,096 feet tall, is a popular spot for taking in city views. Accessible by cable car or hiking trail, it offers a panorama of the city lights below. The area also holds historical significance as a cemetery for foreigners with Western religious beliefs dating back to the late 19th century.

Free

Kanemori Red Brick Warehouse

Discover a charming shopping destination with a rich history at the Kanemori Red Brick Warehouse in Hakodate. This circa-1869 warehouse complex has been transformed into a bustling commercial district, offering an array of shops where find local snacks, sweets, and unique clothing and accessories. As one of the first ports in Japan to open to foreign trade, Hakodate boasts a long commercial history, and the red brick warehouses along the bay serve as a testament to this legacy.

Free

Hachiman-Zaka Slope

Hachiman-Zaka Slope is a renowned attraction in Hakodate, known for its steep incline and vistas. Named after the nearby Hakodate Hachiman Shrine, this stone-paved street is flanked by trees and offers panoramic views of the Hakodate Bay area and the distant Mashu Maru. Among the 19 sloping streets in Hakodate, Hachiman-Zaka is considered the most picturesque as it descends from Mount Hakodate to Hakodate Bay.

Free

Old Public Hall of Hakodate Ward

The Old Public Hall of Hakodate Ward is a beautifully renovated civic building dating back to 1910, showcasing elegant Western-style architecture influenced by the city's exposure to Western trade. This historical landmark offers harbor views from its balcony and features a period interior that exudes the grandeur of European monarchs' palaces. Designated as a national important cultural property, the light yellow and light blue exterior adds to its charm.

Free

Our Lady of the Angels Trappistine Abbey

Our Lady of the Angels Trappistine Abbey, founded by 8 French nuns in 1898, is a serene convent that continues to be active today. The elegant French-style architecture and a small shop featuring works by the sisters make it a must-visit destination. While some areas are off-limits due to its functioning as a nunnery, travellers can still enjoy the beautiful garden and architecture.

Free

Yukura Shrine

Yukura Shrine is a charming and serene shrine located at the 'birthplace' of Yunokawa Onsen. It is adorned with a row of striking red torii gates and is known for dispensing fortunes tied around paper squids. The shrine offers a wide range of benefits, including blessings for land management, medical care, hot springs, cultivation, good harvests, prosperous businesses, family safety, and marriage.

Free

Hakodate Hachimangu

Hakodate Hachimangu Shrine, nestled at the base of Mt. Hakodate, is a serene Shinto shrine founded in 1445. The shrine's traditional buildings are surrounded by lush forest, creating a tranquil and spiritual atmosphere. It is a popular destination during Japan's New Year holidays and hosts a vibrant summer festival. Nearby attractions include Tachimachi Cape and foreign cemeteries, making it an ideal location for leisurely walks after visiting the shrine.
