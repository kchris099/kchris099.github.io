Beppu

Geothermal vents were known for centuries before Meiji-era developers organised eight distinct onsen districts into a national spa resort. Hell tours and bathhouses, not castles or temples, are Beppu's historical product: a modern leisure town built on volcanic heat. Kannawa and neighbouring districts each specialised in different bath temperatures and uses.

Lake Kinrin

Kinrin Lake, located near the town center, is a destination known for its morning mists and scenic beauty. The name 'Kinrin' means gold scales, inspired by the glittering surface of the lake when reflecting the evening sun. Visitors can stroll along the footpath around the lake's perimeter, passing by hot springs and a small shrine. The lake is fed by both hot and cold spring waters, creating a mist on colder mornings.

Aso-Kuju National Park Sagiridai Overlook

Yufu in the background. This scenic spot is perfect for travelers looking to take a break from their journey through Japan's mountainous terrain.

Umi Jigoku

Umi Jigoku, also known as Sea Hell, is one of the eight Beppu Hells in Japan. This geothermal pool was formed 1,200 years ago when Tsurumi Volcano erupted and is the largest hot spring among the group. The cobalt-blue water of Umi Jigoku looks magical and reaches temperatures around 98 degrees Celsius. Visitors can admire the vapors cloaking the pool while enjoying a footbath or visiting the nearby cafe.

33.3168° N / 131.4687° E

Beppu Jigoku (Hells of Beppu)

Beppu 'Jigoku Meguri' Hells Tour is a famous hot springs attraction with themed fenced pools and animal enclosures. The tour consists of two groups, one at Kannawa and the other further north. The Kannawa group includes unique hells like Umi Jigoku, Oniishibozu Jigoku, Shira-ike Jigoku, and Kamado Jigoku.

33.3159° N / 131.4697° E

05 AdmissionFree

Kamado Jigoku

Kamado Jigoku is a geothermal park in Beppu, featuring steaming red and blue pools, a footbath, gift shop, and snacks. It's conveniently located near public transportation and offers day-use hot spring bathing facilities with salt springs known for their moisturizing properties. The park showcases various uses of geothermal energy in the city, including boiling water for tea preparation and floor heating using underground steam.

33.3164° N / 131.4724° E

Jumonji Viewpoint sits within the Umi Jigoku area of Beppu, documented in local heritage registers for its civic or cultural role. Visitors approach from the main access road and combine the visit with neighbouring heritage buildings and public spaces in the district.

Global Tower

Global Tower is a visitor site in Beppu, Japan. Global Tower, located near the B-Con Plaza convention center in Beppu, Japan, is a 125-meter tall notable building designed by known architect Arata Isozaki. Visitors can enjoy panoramic views of Beppu City and its natural surroundings from the 100-meter high observation deck. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Takegawara Onsen

Takegawara Onsen is a visitor site in Beppu, Japan. Records associated with the site include 1879. Established in 1879, this classic onsen has traditional wooden architecture with a striking karahafu gable design reminiscent of the Kamakura Era. Visitors can experience the unique sand immersion bath along with classic hot spring baths in an old-fashioned building. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

10 AdmissionFree

Takasakiyama Natural Zoological Garden

Takasakiyama Natural Zoological Garden, also known as Beppus Monkey Park, is a nature reserve located near Oita City. The park is home to wild Japanese macaque monkeys who roam freely on the 628-meter-high Mount Takasaki. Established in 1952, the zoo was created to provide a safe haven for these playful monkeys and mitigate conflicts with local farmers.

33.2583° N / 131.5328° E

Beppu Ropeway

Beppu Ropeway is a visitor site in Beppu, Japan. The Beppu Ropeway is a large aerial tramway that transports visitors up the steep slopes of Mount Tsurumi, near the hot spring city of Beppu. The ten-minute ride offers expansive, panoramic views of Beppu Bay and the surrounding volcanic landscape. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.
