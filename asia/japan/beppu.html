Beppu

Geothermal vents were known for centuries before Meiji-era developers organised eight distinct onsen districts into a national spa resort. Hell tours and bathhouses, not castles or temples, are Beppu's historical product: a modern leisure town built on volcanic heat. Kannawa and neighbouring districts each specialised in different bath temperatures and uses.

Lake Kinrin

Warm spring water enters Lake Kinrin at Yufuin, mixing with cooler streams and helping mist form above the surface on cold mornings. A short path circles the small lake past trees, waterside buildings, and a shrine. The name refers to golden scales said to resemble fish flashing in evening light, while Mount Yufu rises beyond the surrounding town.

33.2667° N / 131.3690° E

02 AdmissionFree

Aso-Kuju National Park Sagiridai Overlook

Sagiridai overlooks the Yufuin basin from a mountain road within Aso-Kuju National Park. A roadside terrace faces the town, patchwork fields and the twin peaks of Mount Yufu, with conditions changing quickly in fog, rain or snow. The site is closer to Yufuin than central Beppu and is a brief driving stop.

33.2600° N / 131.3825° E

Umi Jigoku

Cobalt-blue water and rising steam define Umi Jigoku, one of Beppu's geothermal viewing sites. The pool is close to boiling and is observed from paths through landscaped grounds. Its Japanese name means Sea Hell, referring to the color and intense heat; nearby displays and smaller thermal features interpret the volcanic activity that shapes the area.

33.3168° N / 131.4687° E

04 Admission¥500

Beppu Jigoku (Hells of Beppu)

Beppu 'Jigoku Meguri' Hells Tour is a famous hot springs attraction with themed fenced pools and animal enclosures. The tour consists of two groups, one at Kannawa and the other further north. The Kannawa group includes unique hells like Umi Jigoku, Oniishibozu Jigoku, Shira-ike Jigoku, and Kamado Jigoku.

33.3159° N / 131.4697° E

05 AdmissionFree

Kamado Jigoku

This geothermal park demonstrates how Beppu puts underground heat to work, from boiling water for tea to warming floors with steam. Day-use salt springs on site are known for their moisturizing feel. Public transport stops nearby make the hells circuit easy to reach without a car.

33.3164° N / 131.4724° E

06 AdmissionFree

Jumonji Viewpoint

Above Beppu, the label Jumonji Viewpoint refers to a mountain overlook. Roads climb toward a broad view of Beppu Bay, the urban coast, and surrounding volcanic slopes, with visibility dependent on weather. Visitors approach from the main access road and combine the visit with neighbouring heritage buildings and public spaces in the district.

33.3297° N / 131.4602° E

07 AdmissionFree

Global Tower

Global Tower rises 125 meters beside B-Con Plaza in Beppu, designed by Arata Isozaki as a convention-center landmark. An observation floor near the 100-meter mark looks over the spa city, Beppu Bay, and surrounding hills. The shaft marks the waterfront redevelopment district.

33.2832° N / 131.4861° E

08 AdmissionFree

Beppu Park

Lawns, mature trees, walking paths, sports areas and seasonal flower displays fill Beppu Park near the city center. The grounds occupy land once used by the Japanese military and later by Allied forces before conversion to public recreation; cherry blossoms, plum trees, bamboo and open grass make it a daily neighborhood park.

33.2816° N / 131.4910° E

09 Admission¥300

Takegawara Onsen

A sweeping karahafu gable identifies Takegawara Onsen in central Beppu, where a public bath has operated since the nineteenth century. The present wooden building dates from the 1930s and contains gender-separated hot-spring baths; its well-known sand-bath service has changed during renovation periods.

33.2775° N / 131.5060° E

10 AdmissionFree

Takasakiyama Natural Zoological Garden

Wild Japanese macaques gather on Mount Takasaki at a managed observation area established to reduce conflict between monkey troops and nearby farms. The animals are not enclosed like a conventional zoo and move between the mountain forest and feeding ground; staff monitor troop behavior and explain social relationships, while visitors must follow distance, food, and eye-contact rules.

33.2583° N / 131.5328° E

11 AdmissionFree

Beppu Ropeway

The Beppu Ropeway climbs Mount Tsurumi in about ten minutes, lifting cabins above the hot-spring city to a summit station. Windows frame Beppu Bay and volcanic ridges during the ascent. Trails and viewpoints continue from the upper terminal across the mountain slopes.

33.2779° N / 131.4488° E
