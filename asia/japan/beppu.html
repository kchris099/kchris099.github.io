Destination Guide — Kyushu

Explore Beppu

A Brief History

Known for centuries for its prolific geothermal vents, Beppu emerged as a major therapeutic resort during the Meiji period. The systematic development of its eight distinct hot spring zones transformed the regional coastal town into Japan's undisputed capital of onsen culture. Decades of careful preservation have ensured that this profound historical narrative remains a vital part of the modern experience.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

Lake Kinrin

Kinrin Lake, located near the town center, is a picturesque destination known for its morning mists and scenic beauty. The name 'Kinrin' means gold scales, inspired by the glittering surface of the lake when reflecting the evening sun. Visitors can stroll along the footpath around the lake's perimeter, passing by hot springs and a small shrine. The lake is fed by both hot and cold spring waters, creating a beautiful mist on colder mornings.

Free

Aso-Kuju National Park Sagiridai Overlook

Nestled along the route from Beppu to Yufuin, Aso-Kuju National Park's Sagiridai Overlook is a charming roadside terrace that offers panoramic views of the picturesque city of Yufuin and the majestic Mt. Yufu in the background. This scenic spot is perfect for travelers looking to take a break from their journey through Japan’s mountainous terrain.

Free

Umi Jigoku

Umi Jigoku, also known as Sea Hell, is one of the eight Beppu Hells in Japan. This geothermal pool was formed 1,200 years ago when Tsurumi Volcano erupted and is the largest hot spring among the group. The cobalt-blue water of Umi Jigoku looks magical and reaches temperatures around 98 degrees Celsius. Visitors can admire the vapors cloaking the pool while enjoying a footbath or visiting the nearby cafe.

Free

Beppu Jigoku (Hells of Beppu)

Beppu 'Jigoku Meguri' Hells Tour is a famous hot springs attraction with themed fenced pools and animal enclosures. The tour consists of two groups, one at Kannawa and the other further north. The Kannawa group includes unique hells like Umi Jigoku, Oniishibozu Jigoku, Shira-ike Jigoku, and Kamado Jigoku.

Free

Kamado Jigoku

Kamado Jigoku is a geothermal park in Beppu, featuring steaming red and blue pools, a footbath, gift shop, and snacks. It's conveniently located near public transportation and offers day-use hot spring bathing facilities with salt springs known for their moisturizing properties. The park showcases various uses of geothermal energy in the city, including boiling water for tea preparation and floor heating using underground steam.

Free

Jumonji Viewpoint

Jumonji Viewpoint sits within the Umi Jigoku area of Beppu, documented in local heritage registers for its civic or cultural role. Visitors approach from the main access road and combine the visit with neighbouring heritage buildings and public spaces in the district.

Free

Global Tower

Global Tower, located near the B-Con Plaza convention center in Beppu, Japan, is a 125-meter tall architectural marvel designed by renowned architect Arata Isozaki. The futuristic design features two side pillars supporting a massive central arch-shaped structure. Visitors can enjoy panoramic views of Beppu City and its natural surroundings from the 100-meter high observation deck.

Free

Beppu Park

Beppu Park is a serene and expansive public green space in the heart of the city, offering a peaceful escape amidst nature. The park features traditional Japanese garden elements such as ponds, bridges, and beautifully manicured landscapes, making it an ideal spot for a leisurely stroll or a relaxing picnic. Visitors can enjoy the vibrant displays of cherry blossoms and plum blossoms during their respective blooming seasons.

Free

Takegawara Onsen

Takegawara Onsen is a historic hot spring bath located in Oita, Japan. Established in 1879, this classic onsen features traditional wooden architecture with a striking karahafu gable design reminiscent of the Kamakura Era. Visitors can experience the unique sand immersion bath along with classic hot spring baths in an old-fashioned building. The onsen's profound atmosphere and high ceilings create a nostalgic ambiance, making it an stop for anyone visiting Beppu.

Free

Takasakiyama Natural Zoological Garden

Takasakiyama Natural Zoological Garden, also known as Beppus Monkey Park, is a nature reserve located near Oita City. The park is home to wild Japanese macaque monkeys who roam freely on the 628-meter-high Mount Takasaki. Established in 1952, the zoo was created to provide a safe haven for these playful monkeys and mitigate conflicts with local farmers.

Free

Beppu Ropeway

The Beppu Ropeway is a large aerial tramway that transports visitors up the steep slopes of Mount Tsurumi, near the hot spring city of Beppu. The ten-minute ride offers expansive, panoramic views of Beppu Bay and the surrounding volcanic landscape. The summit features walking trails, seasonal flora, and observation decks providing vistas that stretch as far as Shikoku on clear days.
