Explore Osaka

A Brief History

Osaka grew around Ishiyama Hongan-ji and later Osaka Castle, built by Toyotomi Hideyoshi in the 1580s. The city became a merchant hub with the slogan tenka no daini kaiden, or the nation's kitchen, supplying rice and goods to Edo. Canals and warehouses lined the Yodo River delta. Industrialization and expos in 1970 and 2025 shaped modern districts. Dotonbori and Shinsekai retain showa-era commercial streets.

Top Sights & Activities

¥1200

Osaka Castle

Osaka Castle is one of Japan's most famous and historically significant landmarks. Originally built by Toyotomi Hideyoshi in the late 16th century, the massive fortress played a major role in the unification of Japan. The current main keep is a modern reconstruction housing a historical museum, surrounded by impressive stone walls, wide moats, and expansive public parks.

¥300

Nishinomaru Garden

The Nishinomaru Garden is a spacious, beautifully landscaped park located within the western citadel of Osaka Castle. It features expansive lawns, a traditional tea house, and excellent views of the castle tower. The garden is particularly famous for its hundreds of cherry trees, making it one of the city's premier destinations for hanami (cherry blossom viewing) in the spring.

Free

Dotonbori

Dōtonbori or Dōtombori is a district in Osaka, Japan. Known as one of Osaka's principal tourist and nightlife areas, the area runs along the Dōtonbori canal from Dōtonboribashi Bridge to Nipponbashi Bridge in the Namba district of the city's Chūō ward. Historically a theater district, it is now a popular nightlife and entertainment area characterized by its eccentric atmosphere and large illuminated signboards.

Free

Glico Sign Dotonbori

Glico Sign Dotonbori sits within the Osaka area of Osaka, documented in local heritage registers for its civic or cultural role. Visitors approach from the main access road and combine the visit with neighbouring heritage buildings and public spaces in the district.

¥8600

Universal Studios Japan

Universal Studios Japan is a theme park located in Osaka, Japan. Opened on March 31, 2001, it is one of six Universal Studios theme parks worldwide and was the first to open outside the United States. The park is owned and operated by USJ LLC, a wholly owned subsidiary of Comcast NBCUniversal.

Free

Osaka Tenmangu Shrine

The Osaka Tenmangū Shrine is a Shinto shrine and one of Tenmangū founded in AD 949 in Osaka by Emperor Murakami. The Tenjin Festival is held here annually from 24 July to 25 July. The shrine has been periodically destroyed by fire, with the present entrance hall and gate built in 1845.

¥600

Hep-Five Ferris Wheel

The Hep-Five Ferris Wheel is a striking, bright red observation wheel integrated into the roof of the HEP Five shopping mall in the Umeda district of Osaka. Reaching a height of 106 meters, the 15-minute ride offers dramatic panoramic views of the dense urban skyline, the harbor, and, on clear days, as far as Mount Ikoma.

Free

Osaka City Central Public Hall

The Osaka City Central Public Hall is a magnificent neo-Renaissance building located on Nakanoshima island in central Osaka. Completed in 1918, the elegant red-brick structure features ornate bronze domes and intricate architectural details. It serves as a major cultural venue for concerts and lectures, standing as a prominent symbol of the city's early 20th-century modernization.

Free

Nakanoshima Rose Garden

Nakanoshima is a 3 km long and 50 hectares narrow sandbank in Kita-ku, Osaka city, Japan, that divides the Kyū-Yodo into the Tosabori and Dōjima rivers. Many governmental and commercial offices, museums and other cultural facilities are located on Nakanoshima.

Free

Mido-Suji

Shinsaibashi is a district in the Chūō-ku ward of Osaka, Japan and the city's main shopping area. At its center is Shinsaibashi-suji, a covered shopping street, that is north of Dōtonbori and Sōemonchō, and parallel and east of Mido-suji street. Associated with Shinsaibashi, and west of Mido-suji street, is Amerika-mura, an American-themed shopping area and center of Osaka's youth culture.

¥1500

Tsutenkaku

Tsutenkaku is a historic observation tower and the iconic symbol of the retro Shinsekai district in Osaka. Originally built in 1912 and reconstructed in 1956, the tower features a distinctive, somewhat nostalgic design. Visitors can take an elevator to the observation decks for panoramic city views and to see the statue of Billiken, a popular local deity of good luck.

Free (treasure hall ¥300)

Shitenno-ji

Shitennō-ji is a Buddhist temple in Ōsaka, Japan. It is also known as Arahaka-ji, Nanba-ji/Naniwa-daiji, or Mitsu-ji. The temple is sometimes regarded as the first Buddhist and oldest officially administered temple in Japan, although the temple complex and buildings have been rebuilt over the centuries, with the last reconstruction taking place in 1963.

Free (observation deck ¥1500)

Abeno Harukas - 16th Floor Garden

Abeno Harukas is a multi-purpose commercial facility in Abenosuji Itchome, Abeno-ku, Osaka, Japan. It consists of the New Annex, Eastern Annex and a supertall skyscraper, Abeno Harukas. The building is 300 m tall and has 62 floors; it was the tallest building in Japan from 2014 to 2023, until Azabudai Hills Mori JP Tower seized the title.

Free

Sumiyoshi Taisha

Sumiyoshi-taisha, also known as Sumiyoshi Grand Shrine, is a Shinto shrine in Sumiyoshi-ku, Osaka, Osaka Prefecture, Japan. It is the main shrine of all the Sumiyoshi shrines. It gives its name to a style of shrine architecture known as Sumiyoshi-zukuri.

¥2000

Kuchu Teien Observatory

Kuchu Teien Observatory, also known as the Floating Garden Observatory at Umeda Sky Building, is a modern indoor/outdoor observation deck offering panoramic views of the city. travellers can enjoy not only the vistas but also dine at an eatery, relax at a cafe, and browse through a gift shop.

Free (observation deck ¥2000)

Umeda Sky Building

The Umeda Sky Building is a architectural marvel in Osaka, featuring two connected skyscrapers that offer views of the city from its rooftop garden observatory. This unique structure not only provides an incredible vantage point to appreciate the sprawling urban landscape but also houses a delightful restaurant and lounge where travellers can unwind while soaking in panoramic vistas.

Free

Shinsekai

Shinsekai, a vibrant district in Osaka established in 1912, is a feast for the senses and a true representation of the city's culinary heritage. Dominated by the Tsutenkaku Tower, this area buzzes with life and color. While it may not be the most upscale part of town, Shinsekai has been delighting locals and travellers alike with its beloved soul foods for over a century.

¥500

Tennoji Zoo

Nestled within the lush expanse of Tennoji Park, Tennoji Zoo is a delightful destination for animal lovers and families alike. Established in January 1915, this charming zoo spans approximately 11 hectares and showcases over 1,000 animals from more than 200 species. travellers can marvel at majestic lions, playful chimpanzees, and the adorable koalas that have become a favorite among guests.

Free

Isshin-ji Temple

Isshin-ji Temple, a traditional temple dating back to the 12th century, is a unique blend of ancient history and modern architecture. The temple features strikingly modern steel entrance gates and beautiful gardens. It is known as a site for praying to cure alcoholism and for laying the ashes of the dead to rest, with a tradition dating back to a 17th-century battle in Osaka.

Free

Imamiya Ebisu Jinja

Nestled in the vibrant Naniwa Ward of Osaka, Imamiya Ebisu Jinja is an ancient Shinto shrine that boasts a beautifully sloped tiled roof and serene gardens. This sacred site is dedicated to Ebisu, one of Japan's Seven Lucky Gods, who symbolizes prosperity in business and wealth.

Free

Animate Osaka Nipponbashi

Located in Nipponbashi, Animate Osaka is a sprawling multi-story retailer that offers a wide range of anime and manga-related products. From comics, manga, and dojin magazines to character goods, figures, games, CDs, DVDs, and cosplay costumes, this store has it all. It's known for its extensive collection of merchandise from popular series as well as hosting regular events. Whether you're into classic titles or the latest releases, Animate has something for every anime enthusiast.

Free

SenNichiMae DoguyaSuji cookware Shopping Street

Sennichimae Doguyasuji Shopping Street is a 150-meter-long arcade in Osaka, Japan, offering a wide array of household goods, kitchenware, pottery, and tools. It's the perfect place to find professional kitchen tools and experience Osaka's culinary world to the fullest. The street is lined with stores showcasing an extensive collection of kitchen utensils, cookware, and restaurant supplies. In addition to these items, travellers can also find food shops interspersed throughout the arcade.

Free

Kuromon Market

Kuromon Ichiba Market is a spacious market in Osaka, Japan, known for its diverse range of vendors selling street food, fresh produce, shellfish, and souvenirs. The market offers an incredible variety of stalls featuring live fish, exotic fruits, affordable items from dollar shops, and artisanal souvenir shops.

Free

Hozen-ji

Nestled in a quiet alleyway just off the bustling Dotonbori district, Hozenji is an intimate Buddhist temple that offers a serene escape from the surrounding noise and commercial activity. The temple is adorned with lanterns and features a moss-covered statue of Fudomyoo, the deity of protection. travellers can offer prayers for safe travels before strolling down Hozenji Yokocho, a historic cobbled street lined with izakayas.

Free

Shinsaibashi-Suji Shopping Street

Shinsaibashi-Suji Shopping Street is a vibrant and bustling shopping district in Osaka, renowned for its extensive array of high-end tax-free shops that even offer airport delivery services. This lively street runs parallel to Nagahoridori, where find a mix of traditional and modern retail experiences. The area is steeped in history, with roots tracing back to the merchant Shinsai, who built one of the famous bridges over what was once a canal.

Free

Namba Yasaka Jinja

Nestled in the heart of Osaka, Namba Yasaka Jinja is a captivating shrine that boasts a striking lion-head stage, towering at an impressive 12 meters. This unique structure, built in 1975, symbolizes the swallowing of evil spirits and attracts travellers seeking good fortune during exam seasons and financial year-end. Once part of a larger religious complex, this shrine has been lovingly restored after suffering damage during WWII.

Free

Kyocera Dome Ōsaka

Kyocera Dome Osaka, a futuristic baseball ground and concert arena, has been a landmark in Osaka since its opening in 1997. With a capacity of 51,000, it serves as the home grounds for the Orix Buffaloes and hosts various events, concerts, and sports games. The dome also features restaurants and shops for travellers to enjoy. Additionally, travellers can capture panoramic views of Osaka from Cosmo Tower, including the Kyocera Dome itself.

Free

Hokoku Shrine

Hokoku Shrine is a Shinto shrine situated in Osaka Castle Park, built in the 1800s to pay homage to Toyotomi Hideyoshi, a prominent leader. It is positioned south of the Castle Keep or Hommaru. The shrine enshrines three members of the Toyotomi family and they are revered as symbols of success and good fortune.

¥450

Expo '70 Commemorative Park

Expo '70 Commemorative Park, located on the site of the 1970 World's Fair, is home to a museum, a stadium, and the Tower of the Sun created by artist Taro Okamoto. The park offers diverse natural landscapes that change with the seasons, including running streams, ponds, open grassy areas, and woodlands. travellers can enjoy picturesque sights such as fields of Cosmos and red Kochia grass perfect for photography.

Free (interior tour ¥720)

Tower of the Sun

The Tower of the Sun, a peculiar artwork constructed for the 1970 world's fair, is a cultural symbol of Osaka located in Banpaku Kinenkoen Expo Park. The tower's interior is occasionally open to the public and provides an opportunity to witness its restoration and learn about its design and construction history. Moreover, it features a psychedelic portrayal of evolution and life that captivates travellers' attention.

Free

Panasonic Stadium Suita

Panasonic Stadium Suita, the modern home of Gamba Osaka, offers an experience for soccer fans. This stadium is designed to bring spectators closer to the action, fostering a vibrant atmosphere that energizes both players and supporters alike. During my visit in late March for a thrilling local derby against Cerezo Osaka in the Levain Cup, I was captivated by the electric ambiance created by 26,000 passionate fans.

¥800

Tempozan Ferris Wheel

The Tempozan Ferris Wheel, standing tall at 112.5 meters, is a must-visit attraction in Osaka that promises panoramic views of the surrounding landscape. This impressive wheel can accommodate up to 480 passengers and features four transparent cabins for an unobstructed view of sights like the Akashi Kaikyo Ohashi Bridge and Rokko Mountain on clear days. A ride lasts about 15 minutes, allowing you to soak in the scenery from above.

Free

Daisen-ryo Kofun

Daisen-ryo Kofun, also known as the Tomb of Emperor Nintoku, is a massive burial mound located in Japan. It is the largest tomb in the country and the third largest in the world, with a total length of 486 meters and a perimeter of approximately 2.8 kilometers. The tomb is shaped like a keyhole when viewed from above and is covered in vegetation.
