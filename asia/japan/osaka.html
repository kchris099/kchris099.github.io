Osaka

Osaka grew around Ishiyama Hongan-ji and later Osaka Castle, built by Toyotomi Hideyoshi in the 1580s. The city became a merchant hub with the slogan tenka no daini kaiden, or the nation's kitchen, supplying rice and goods to Edo. Canals and warehouses lined the Yodo River delta. Industrialization and expos in 1970 and 2025 shaped modern districts. Dotonbori and Shinsekai retain showa-era commercial streets.
