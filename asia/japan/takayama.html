Explore Takayama

A Brief History

Takayama is in Japan. Established as a regional settlement beyond an older fortified core, the city developed through industrialization in the late 19th century, post-war reconstruction, and modern civic expansion that reshaped its public spaces, markets, and surrounding landscape. Museums, markets, and street plans in Takayama still show layers from those periods.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

Hidatakayama Sanmachi Historic District

The old townscape of Hidatakayama Sanmachi Historic District is a neighborhood featuring traditional Edo-period architecture from 1600-1868. Travellers can explore houses, shops, and eateries that offer a glimpse into the Edo living styles. The area also houses the Hida Takayama Town Museum and the Kusakabe Heritage House, both located in some of the oldest buildings in Sanmachi. Some homes are open to the public for an immersive experience.

Free

Sanmachi Suji

Sanmachi Suji is a district that transports visitors back to the Edo period, with its streets adorned by traditional merchant houses, inviting restaurants, quaint shops, and local sake breweries. The area is divided into three main sections: chuushin (the center), kamimachi (upper town), and shimomachi (lower town).

¥500

Takayama Jinya

Takayama Jinya is a visitor site in Takayama, Japan. Records associated with the site include 1969. This beautifully restored government building served as an administrative center until 1969 and has traditional tatami rooms that reflect the simplicity of Japanese design. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Free

Hida Kokubun-ji Temple

Hida Kokubun-ji Temple is a religious building in Takayama, Japan. The shrine is known for its architecture, including the bell tower and triple tower structure. Travellers can explore the administrative halls of Takayama Jinya and admire the vermillion Nakabashi Bridge nearby. The temple houses some of the oldest Buddha statues, each depicting its own fabled story. The exterior and main interior spaces are usually viewable outside service times.

Free

Hie Shrine

Hie Shrine is a visitor site in Takayama, Japan. Hie Shrine is a centuries-old Shinto shrine located south of the historic center, surrounded by tall trees and a sacred cedar. It is known for its annual spring festival, the Takayama Matsuri, held on April 14 and 15. Travellers can also explore the nearby Tenshoji Temple, which is part of a walking course established by Nagachika Kanamori. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Free

Miyagawa Morning Markets

The Miyagawa Morning Markets in Takayama, Japan, are a active and busy food market located near the turquoise Miyagawa River. This daily market offers a wide variety of fresh produce, flowers, and handmade souvenirs from local vendors. The markets are rooted in Japanese history, dating back to the Edo period when farmers gathered to sell their goods directly to consumers.

¥500

Yoshijima Traditional House

Yoshijima Traditional House is a visitor site in Takayama, Japan. Construction and early use date to around 1907. The Yoshijima Traditional House is a century-old mansion and former sake brewery in Takayama with traditional architecture. Constructed in 1907 by master carpenter Nishida Isaburo, it served as a residence for a wealthy merchant and also housed sake-making facilities. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Free

Sakurayama Hachimangu Shrine

Sakurayama Hachimangu Shrine is a visitor site in Takayama, Japan. The shrine is known for its active festivals, particularly the Hachiman Festival in autumn, featuring splendid yatai with colorful decorations that captivate onlookers. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Free

Setogawa and Shirakabe Dozou Street

Shirakabe Dozogai Street is a heritage site located in the peaceful town of Hida Furukawa, which has traditional buildings spread along a narrow street. Unlike its commercialized counterpart in Takayama, this old town center exudes an authentic and atmosphere that attracts few tourists daily. The streets are adorned with clear water channels filled with koi fish, adding to the scenery of the area.

Free

Keta Wakamiya Shrine

Keta Wakamiya Shrine is a visitor site in Takayama, Japan. Keta Wakamiya Shrine is a significant site in the Gifu village, where Buddhism and Shintoism coexist harmoniously. The shrine's layout, located between mountains, exudes a sense of dignity and history. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.
