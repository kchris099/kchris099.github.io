Explore Mount Fuji

A Brief History

Mount Fuji, Japan's tallest and most sacred peak, is a perfectly symmetrical, globally iconic stratovolcano that has profoundly inspired artists and pilgrims for centuries. Surrounded by the breathtaking Fuji Five Lakes, the region offers incredibly picturesque landscapes that dramatically change with the seasons, from vibrant spring cherry blossoms to striking autumn foliage. Whether viewing its majestic snow-capped cone from a serene lakeside park or exploring the traditional, crystal-clear spring villages at its base, the Mount Fuji area delivers absolute, unparalleled natural grandeur.

Oshino hakkai

Oshino Hakkai are the eight springs found in Oshino, Yamanashi, Japan. The aquifer water from Mount Fuji comes out to the ground to form these springs. They are a Natural monument of Japan, part of the Mount Fuji World Heritage Site, and one of Yamanashi Prefecture's important tourist attractions.

Free

Arakura Fuji Sengen Shrine

Arakurayama Sengen Park is a block park located at 3353 Arakura, Fujiyoshida, Yamanashi Prefecture, Japan. Installed in October 1959. Located on the hillside of Mount Arakura, it covers an area of about 4.3 hectares.

Oishi Park

A brilliantly situated park on the northern shore of Lake Kawaguchiko. It provides an absolutely breathtaking, unobstructed view of Mount Fuji, beautifully famed for its vibrant seasonal flowers that have inspired countless cultural depictions of the mountain.
