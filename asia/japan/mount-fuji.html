Mount Fuji

Mount Fuji rises to 3,776 meters on the border of Shizuoka and Yamanashi prefectures as an active stratovolcano last erupting in 1707. Pilgrimage routes and shrines such as Fujisan Hongu Sengen Taisha developed from medieval reverence. Woodblock prints popularized its cone in the Edo period. Fuji was inscribed as a UNESCO World Heritage site in 2013 for its cultural landscape. Climbing season runs from July to early September.

Oshino hakkai

Oshino Hakkai are the eight springs found in Oshino, Yamanashi, Japan. The aquifer water from Mount Fuji comes out to the ground to form these springs. They are a Natural monument of Japan, part of the Mount Fuji World Heritage Site, and one of Yamanashi Prefecture's important tourist attractions.

02 AdmissionFree

Hikawa Clock Shop

The Hikawa Clock Shop in Fujikawaguchiko is a small streetfront storefront that became a pilgrimage stop for fans of the BanG Dream! franchise, whose story uses a similar setting. Clocks fill the windows facing the town's tourist strip near Lake Kawaguchi. Mount Fuji appears on clear days beyond the ordinary shop row.

35.4928° N / 138.8039° E

03 AdmissionFree

Arakura Fuji Sengen Shrine

Arakura Fuji Sengen Shrine is located on a mountainside in Fujiyoshida and is famous for offering a well known views in Japan. After climbing nearly 400 stone steps, visitors reach the Chureito Pagoda. From this vantage point, the five-story red pagoda is neatly framed against the majestic backdrop of Mount Fuji, especially during cherry blossom season.

35.5011° N / 138.8016° E

Lawson Fujikawaguchiko Town hall

This Lawson convenience store beside Fujikawaguchiko Town Hall is a well-known Mount Fuji photo stop, with the volcano framed above the shop's blue sign on clear days. Tour buses and rental cars pause along the curb for the view. The store itself remains an ordinary Lawson, selling drinks and snacks to lake-town traffic.

35.4998° N / 138.7675° E

05 AdmissionFree

Oishi Park

Oishi Park follows the northern shore of Lake Kawaguchiko with paths through seasonal flower beds and open views toward Mount Fuji. Lavender, kochia, and other plantings change through the year, while the mountain may disappear behind cloud even in otherwise clear weather; the park also includes visitor and produce facilities, but its primary geographical value is the lake-and-volcano alignment.

35.5229° N / 138.7456° E
