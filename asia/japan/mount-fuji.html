Explore Mount Fuji

A Brief History

Mount Fuji rises to 3,776 meters on the border of Shizuoka and Yamanashi prefectures as an active stratovolcano last erupting in 1707. Pilgrimage routes and shrines such as Fujisan Hongu Sengen Taisha developed from medieval reverence. Woodblock prints popularized its cone in the Edo period. Fuji was inscribed as a UNESCO World Heritage site in 2013 for its cultural landscape. Climbing season runs from July to early September.

Oshino hakkai

Oshino Hakkai are the eight springs found in Oshino, Yamanashi, Japan. The aquifer water from Mount Fuji comes out to the ground to form these springs. They are a Natural monument of Japan, part of the Mount Fuji World Heritage Site, and one of Yamanashi Prefecture's important tourist attractions.

Free

Hikawa Clock Shop

The Japanese music media franchise BanG Dream! features a large ensemble of characters, with its main cast organized into twelve all-female bands of five members each. The band Poppin'Party serves as the protagonists of the franchise's anime television series, making their debut in 2015.

Free

Arakura Fuji Sengen Shrine

Arakura Fuji Sengen Shrine is located on a mountainside in Fujiyoshida and is famous for offering one of the most iconic views in Japan. After climbing nearly 400 stone steps, visitors reach the Chureito Pagoda. From this vantage point, the five-story red pagoda is neatly framed against the majestic backdrop of Mount Fuji, especially impressive during cherry blossom season.

¥500

Lawson Fujikawaguchiko Town hall

Overtourism is congestion or overcrowding from an excess of tourists, resulting in conflicts with locals. The World Tourism Organization defines overtourism as "the impact of tourism on a destination, or parts thereof, that excessively influences perceived quality of life of citizens and/or quality of visitor experiences in a negative way".

Free

Oishi Park

Oishi Park is a beautifully landscaped public park situated on the northern shore of Lake Kawaguchiko. It is renowned for its vibrant, seasonal flower displays, including lavender in the summer and kochia (summer cypress) that turns bright red in the autumn. The park provides unobstructed, panoramic views of Mount Fuji reflecting on the calm waters of the lake.
