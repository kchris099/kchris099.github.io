Destination Guide — Tohoku

Explore Sendai

A Brief History

Founded in 1600 by the formidable daimyo Date Masamune, Sendai was engineered as a powerful northern fortress town. Surviving devastating World War II air raids and modern earthquakes, the 'City of Trees' remains the resilient cultural and economic anchor of the Tohoku region. Decades of careful preservation have ensured that this profound historical narrative remains a vital part of the modern experience.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

Shiogama Shrine

Shiogama Shrine is a hilltop shrine with a tree-lined stairway offering sweeping bay views and spring cherry trees. Located near Sendai city, it's easily accessible from downtown Sendai, just a 30-minute train ride away. The shrine boasts a history of over 120 years and is surrounded by cherry trees, making it a popular hanami spot.

¥2,400

Sendai Umino-Mori Aquarium

If you're looking for an aquatic experience, the Sendai Umino-Mori Aquarium is a must-visit destination on the eastern side of Sendai. This impressive facility boasts around 100 expansive tanks filled with marine life from across the globe, all swimming gracefully in pristine waters. Guests can immerse themselves in educational exhibits about these fascinating creatures and even engage in hands-on activities like feeding penguins or snapping close-up photos with dolphins.

Free

Sendai Dai Kannon (White-Robed Colossus)

Located in the northern part of Sendai City, the Sendai Dai Kannon (Colossus) is a massive 100-meter-tall statue of the goddess Kannon. It stands as one of the tallest Buddha statues in Japan and offers views across the city. The interior of the statue is divided into 12 floors, housing a total of 108 Buddha figurines. travellers can ride up to enjoy panoramic city views and then walk down inside to admire the numerous statues.

Free

National Treasure Osaki Hachiman Shrine

Nestled within a serene forest, the Osaki Hachiman Shrine is a captivating 17th-century Shinto shrine complex that invites travellers to step back in time. Established by the feudal lord Date Masamune in 1607, this sacred site is renowned for its blessings and rich history. As you ascend the stone steps flanked by majestic cedar trees, encounter a striking vermilion torii gate that sets the tone for your visit.

Free

Sendai Castle Honmaru Ruins

Sendai Castle Honmaru Ruins, also known as Aoba Castle, was built in 1602 after Tokugawa Ieyasu's victory at the Battle of Sekigahara. Although it never had a main keep, it featured an impressive honmaru and multi-storied yagura. The castle town was divided into merchant and residential areas to the east and the castle to the west.

¥480

Yagiyama Zoological Park

Nestled on the scenic slopes of Mt. Yagi, Yagiyama Zoological Park is a delightful destination for animal enthusiasts and families alike. Established in 1965, this charming zoo showcases around 600 animals from 115 different species, thoughtfully arranged by their natural habitats. Just a short ten-minute journey from Sendai Station, it’s easily accessible via the Yagiyama Zoological Park Subway Station.

Free

Rakuten Mobile Saikyo Park Miyagi

Nestled in the vibrant city of Sendai, Rakuten Mobile Park Miyagi is a must-visit destination for sports aficionados and baseball lovers alike. Since its inception in 1950, this impressive stadium has become the proud home of the Tohoku Rakuten Golden Eagles. With a seating capacity of 23,000, it offers an exhilarating atmosphere that captures the essence of Japan's beloved pastime.

Free

Sendai Station

Sendai Station serves as a fantastic starting point for exploring the rich cultural and natural wonders of Miyagi Prefecture. Just a short 30-minute drive west leads you to Akiu Onsen, one of Tohoku's renowned hot springs with a history dating back around 1,500 years. Legend has it that Emperor Kinmei found relief from his skin ailment after bathing in its healing waters, while the famous warrior Date Masamune frequented this serene spot.

Free

AER Observation Terrace

AER Observation Terrace is a must-visit in Sendai, offering panoramic views of the city from its 31st-floor observation deck. The terrace provides an excellent vantage point to take in the entire cityscape, with lush vegetation adding to the charm during summer. This contemporary tower is conveniently located just a short walk from Sendai Station, making it easily accessible for travellers.

Free

Sendai Tōshōgū Shrine

Sendai Toshogu Shrine is a serene place founded in the 1600s by feudal lord Date Tadamune. It boasts cherry blossoms, stone lanterns, and a peaceful atmosphere. The shrine is located in a quiet grove and offers parking on-site. travellers can also enjoy a leisurely 40-minute walk from Sendai station to reach this beautiful little shrine.
