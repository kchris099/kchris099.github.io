Sendai

Date Masamune founded Sendai in 1600 as a northern castle domain. Firebombing in 1945 and later earthquakes tested the 'City of Trees,' which rebuilt as Tōhoku's main urban centre. Zuihōden mausoleum recalls the Date lineage behind that fortress origin. Tanabata bamboo decorations each August recall Date-era festival patronage in the rebuilt avenues.

Shiogama Shrine

Shiogama Shrine is a hilltop shrine with a tree-lined stairway offering sweeping bay views and spring cherry trees. Located near Sendai city, it's easily accessible from downtown Sendai, just a 30-minute train ride away. The shrine has a history of over 120 years and is surrounded by cherry trees, making it a popular hanami spot.

Sendai Umino-Mori Aquarium

If visitors are looking for an aquatic experience, the Sendai Umino-Mori Aquarium is a must-visit destination on the eastern side of Sendai. This facility has around 100 expansive tanks filled with marine life from across the globe, all swimming gracefully in pristine waters. Guests can immerse themselves in educational exhibits about these fascinating creatures and even engage in hands-on activities like feeding penguins or snapping close-up photos with dolphins.

Sendai Dai Kannon (White-Robed Colossus)

Located in the northern part of Sendai City, the Sendai Dai Kannon (Colossus) is a massive 100-meter-tall statue of the goddess Kannon. It is one of the tallest Buddha statues in Japan and offers views across the city. The interior of the statue is divided into 12 floors, housing a total of 108 Buddha figurines. Travellers can ride up to enjoy panoramic city views and then walk down inside to admire the numerous statues.

National Treasure Osaki Hachiman Shrine is a visitor site in Sendai, Japan. Records associated with the site include 1607. Established by the feudal lord Date Masamune in 1607, this sacred site is known for its blessings and rich history. Sendai Castle Honmaru Ruins

Sendai Castle Honmaru Ruins, also known as Aoba Castle, was built in 1602 after Tokugawa Ieyasu's victory at the Battle of Sekigahara. Although it never had a main keep, it featured an honmaru and multi-storied yagura. The castle town was divided into merchant and residential areas to the east and the castle to the west.

Yagiyama Zoological Park

Located on the scenic slopes of Mt. Yagi, Yagiyama Zoological Park is a destination for animal enthusiasts and families alike. Established in 1965, this zoo showcases around 600 animals from 115 different species, thoughtfully arranged by their natural habitats. Just a short ten-minute journey from Sendai Station, it's easily accessible via the Yagiyama Zoological Park Subway Station.
