Sapporo

Sapporo was laid out in the Meiji era as a planned grid capital for Hokkaido's colonisation, with American advisors influencing parks and agriculture. The clock tower, beer industry, and later Winter Olympics of 1972 marked its rise. Ainu land and history precede the settler city. Snow festivals, subway lines, and Odori Park's central boulevard now define Japan's largest northern metropolis.

Hōhei Kan

Hōhei Kan, located in Sapporo's Nakajima Park, is a historic 19th-century hotel building that has been repurposed as a museum and event space. Originally opened in 1881, it holds the distinction of being the oldest wooden hotel building in Japan. Throughout its history, it welcomed high-ranking officials and even hosted three emperors. The white and ultramarine blue exterior sets it apart as a landmark.
