Destination Guide — Hokkaido

Explore Sapporo

A Brief History

Established as a regional settlement beyond an older fortified core, the city developed through industrialization in the late 19th century, post-war reconstruction, and modern civic expansion that reshaped its public spaces, markets, and surrounding landscape. Restored religious buildings, civic buildings, and natural features now define the contemporary visitor experience.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

Tanukikoji Shopping Street

Tanukikoji Shopping Street, also known as Raccoon Alley, is a historic covered shopping street in Sapporo that spans 10 blocks and stretches for about 1 kilometer. With over 200 shops offering a diverse range of goods, from traditional kimonos and incense sticks to modern electronics, the arcade captures the lively atmosphere of a bustling bazaar.

¥1,200

Sapporo TV Tower

The Sapporo TV Tower, a famous landmark built in the 1950s, stands at 147.3 meters tall at the end of Odori Park in Sapporo City. Its observation deck, situated 90 meters above ground level, provides panoramic views of the city and beyond. travellers can enjoy night views of Odori Park and experience thrilling transparent glass panels that slope down to their feet.

Free

Hokkaido Shrine Tongu

Hokkaido Shrine Tongu is a traditional Shinto place of worship located in the eastern island of Hokkaido. Established in 1869, it enshrines four deities and hosts the annual Sapporo Matsuri festival between June 14 and 16, featuring religious ceremonies and vibrant processions.

Free

JR Tower

The JR Tower is a prominent 173-meter high skyscraper that opened in 2003. It is directly connected to Sapporo Station, serving as a gateway to various transportation options within the city. The tower features a diverse range of amenities, including a shopping mall, office complex, movie theater, and hotel. travellers can enjoy panoramic views from the observatory on the 38th floor.

Free

Suwa Shrine

Catholic Otaru Church, also known as Tomioka Catholic Church, is one of Japan's oldest Catholic churches. Located in the picturesque city of Otaru, it stands out with its European Medieval Gothic architecture, complete with marvelous stained glass windows and a distinctive red pointed roof. The church's history dates back to the time when Otaru was a bustling port city that opened itself up to trade with the West, attracting Christian missionaries to Japan.

Free (Premium tour ¥1,000)

Sapporo Beer Museum

The Sapporo Beer Museum, located in a former factory, offers free admission and features exhibits, tours, and paid tastings. travellers of all ages can enjoy a tour of the museum to learn about the history of beer in Japan and the beer-making process. The highlight of the tour is a half-hour tasting session with an array of beers available. While sake may seem like a more traditional Japanese drink, experiencing the birth of Hokkaido's famous beer at this museum is also worthwhile.

Free

Hōhei Kan

Hōhei Kan, located in Sapporo's Nakajima Park, is a historic 19th-century hotel building that has been repurposed as a museum and event space. Originally opened in 1881, it holds the distinction of being the oldest wooden hotel building in Japan. Throughout its history, it welcomed high-ranking officials and even hosted three emperors. The white and ultramarine blue exterior sets it apart as a landmark.

Free

Hokkaido Jingu

Hokkaido Jingu, a Shinto shrine built in 1869, is located in Sapporo and is dedicated to four deities, including Emperor Meiji. It was established around 150 years ago and is considered the most important shrine in the city. The shrine honors three gods, with locals often seen making offerings to them. This relatively youthful shrine stands out amidst Japan's famous shrines and holds historical significance as it guards the pioneering career of Hokkaido.

Free

Hokkaido Shrine Mikado

Hokkaido Shrine Mikado, situated in the Chuo District of Sapporo City, is a significant religious site with a rich history dating back to 1869. Initially established as a place of faith for pioneers by Emperor Meiji, it enshrines the Kaitaku Sanshin (Developing Three Deities). The shrine boasts beautiful gardens and is surrounded by parks, offering a peaceful and serene atmosphere.

¥800

Sapporo City Maruyama Zoo

Sapporo City Maruyama Zoo is a top destination for animal lovers, offering an up-close experience with a variety of wildlife. As the tenth-largest zoo in Japan, it prides itself on providing naturalistic enclosures for its animals. travellers can observe polar bears, red pandas, penguins, reptiles, and many other creatures in habitats that closely resemble their natural environments.

¥1,200

Shiroi Koibito Park

Nestled in Sapporo, Shiroi Koibito Park is a delightful destination that feels like stepping into a chocolate wonderland. This enchanting theme park offers an array of attractions for travellers of all ages, from mesmerizing views of a real chocolate assembly line to engaging cookie-making workshops. Families can enjoy the playful atmosphere with playgrounds and interactive exhibits showcasing delectable chocolates and cookies.

Free (Ropeway ¥2,100 round trip)

Mt. Moiwa Summit Observation Deck

Experience the sunset or night view of Sapporo at the Mount Moiwa Observation Deck, located on a 531-meter mountain south of downtown Sapporo. Accessible by ropeway and cable car, this popular spot offers panoramic views of Sapporo, Ishikari Plain, and Ishikari Bay. The night view from this altitude is truly and has been recognized as one of Japan's top three city night views.

Free

Mt. Moiwa

Mt. Moiwa is a renowned peak in Sapporo, offering nighttime panoramas of the city and Ishikari Bay. travellers can ascend to the summit via a cable car or the Mt. Moiwa sightseeing expressway, followed by a ride on the unique Morris Car. The mountain is popular among locals for its accessibility and features five lightly climbed trails ranging from 2.4km to 4.5km in length.
