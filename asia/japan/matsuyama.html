Destination Guide — Shikoku

Explore Matsuyama

A Brief History

Centered around one of Japan's twelve original surviving castles, Matsuyama flourished under the Matsudaira clan during the Edo period. The ancient Dogo Onsen, famously frequented by emperors, has drawn travelers for millennia, significantly shaping the city's literary and healing culture. This rich historical tapestry established a strong foundation that continues to shape the city's dynamic contemporary identity.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

Dogo Onsen Main Building

Dogo Onsen Main Building is a traditional hot spring facility in Matsuyama, Japan, offering public baths, private rooms, and tea and snacks. Visitors can also take a ride on the Botchan Ressha, a small steam train that provides a unique way to explore the city's attractions. Dogo Onsen is one of Japan's oldest and most renowned hot springs, attracting visitors from around the world.

Free

Isaniwa Shrine

Isaniwa Shrine is a striking red shrine adorned with gold leaf detailing and sculptures, offering city views and accessed by steep stone steps. Believed to have been constructed over 1,000 years ago, this significant shrine has a rich history in Matsuyama City. Originally built after Emperor Chuai's visit to Dogo Onsen, it was later relocated and reconstructed in the style of Kyoto's Iwashimizu Hachiman Shrine by lord Sadanaga Matsudaira.

Free

Ishite Temple

Ishiteji Temple is a captivating Buddhist temple located in Matsuyama, known for its renowned gate, pagodas, and intricate network of caves and tunnels. As the 51st stop on the 88 Shikoku Pilgrimage temples, it is filled with hidden treasures waiting to be discovered.

Free

Matsuyama Castle

Matsuyama Castle, a restored 17th-century castle, is located 4km from Matsuyama's city center and can be accessed by chairlift, cable car, or on foot. The castle sits atop a small mountain and offers views of the city and the Seto Inland Sea. It has remained intact since the Edo era, making it one of the oldest castles in Japan.

Free

Shiroyama Park Honmaru Square

Shiroyama Park is a picturesque destination known for its cherry blossoms, offering visitors walking paths and cultural attractions. The park's Sannomaru area was historically significant as the center of clan administration, featuring the lord's palace and facilities for political affairs.

Free

Chojyaganaru Station

Chojyaganaru Station is a key access point and rest area located along the scenic mountain roads of the Mount Daisen region. It provides parking, visitor information, and panoramic viewpoints of the surrounding peaks and dense beech forests. The station serves as a popular starting point for hikers exploring the extensive trail network of the Daisen-Oki National Park.

Free

Birthplace of the Akiyama Brothers

Located in Kachi-machi 2-Chome, Matsuyama City, the Birthplace of the Akiyama Brothers is a museum that recreates the home where Yoshifuru and Saneyuki Akiyama, renowned for their military exploits, were born. The brothers' historical significance is depicted through various exhibits at the museum. Additionally, bronze statues of the brothers are situated on the grounds.

Free

Tobe Zoological Park

Tobe Zoological Park is a diverse animal park featuring over 765 animals from approximately 163 different species. Divided into 10 streets, the park offers separate zones for various animals and hosts interactive events throughout the day. Visitors can enjoy close encounters with hippos, tigers, monkeys, and other exotic creatures. The park also includes a restaurant overlooking the elephant enclosure and cute gift shops offering souvenirs and decorations.

Free

Tsubaki Shrine

Tsubaki Shrine is a remarkable place with prayer halls, totems, and imposing torii gates surrounded by lush foliage. The shrine hosts the New Year Matsuri every February, offering visitors the chance to witness traditional events, enjoy delicious food from various stalls, and purchase good luck charms. The site is also adorned with Japanese poetry on its fence posts.

Free

Taisanji Temple

Taisan-ji is a historic Buddhist temple located in Matsuyama, designated as a National Treasure of Japan. Founded in the 6th century, the temple features a magnificent main hall built in the Kamakura period, showcasing exquisite ancient architecture. It is the 52nd temple on the Shikoku 88 Temple Pilgrimage, drawing numerous pilgrims and visitors seeking its serene, spiritual atmosphere.
