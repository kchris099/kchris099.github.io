Matsuyama

Matsudaira lords held Matsuyama Castle, one of twelve original keeps left in Japan, while Dōgo Onsen drew imperial bathers for centuries. Literary associations with Natsume Sōseki later coloured the spa town's modern identity on Shikoku. Botchan train streetcars nod to Sōseki's novel set in the spa city. The ropeway to the keep ties spa streets to the hilltop fort.

Dogo Onsen Main Building

Dogo Onsen Main Building is a traditional hot spring facility in Matsuyama, Japan, offering public baths, private rooms, and tea and snacks. Visitors can also take a ride on the Botchan Ressha, a small steam train that provides a unique way to explore the city's attractions. Dogo Onsen is one of Japan's oldest and most known hot springs, attracting visitors from around the world.

Isaniwa Shrine

Isaniwa Shrine is a striking red shrine adorned with gold leaf detailing and sculptures, offering city views and accessed by steep stone steps. Believed to have been constructed over 1,000 years ago, this significant shrine has a rich history in Matsuyama City. Originally built after Emperor Chuai's visit to Dogo Onsen, it was later relocated and reconstructed in the style of Kyoto's Iwashimizu Hachiman Shrine by lord Sadanaga Matsudaira.

Ishite Temple

Ishiteji Temple is a Buddhist temple located in Matsuyama, known for its known gate, pagodas, and detailed network of caves and tunnels. As the 51st stop on the 88 Shikoku Pilgrimage temples, it is filled with hidden treasures waiting to be discovered.

Matsuyama Castle

Matsuyama Castle, a restored 17th-century castle, is located 4km from Matsuyama's city center and can be accessed by chairlift, cable car, or on foot. The castle sits atop a small mountain and offers views of the city and the Seto Inland Sea. It has remained intact since the Edo era, making it one of the oldest castles in Japan.

Shiroyama Park Honmaru Square

Shiroyama Park is a destination known for its cherry blossoms, offering visitors walking paths and cultural attractions. The park's Sannomaru area was historically significant as the center of clan administration, featuring the lord's palace and facilities for political affairs.

Chojyaganaru Station

Chojyaganaru Station is a key access point and rest area located along the scenic mountain roads of the Mount Daisen region. It provides parking, visitor information, and panoramic viewpoints of the surrounding peaks and dense beech forests. The station is a popular starting point for hikers exploring the extensive trail network of the Daisen-Oki National Park.

Birthplace of the Akiyama Brothers

Located in Kachi-machi 2-Chome, Matsuyama City, the Birthplace of the Akiyama Brothers is a museum that recreates the home where Yoshifuru and Saneyuki Akiyama, known for their military exploits, were born. The brothers' historical significance is depicted through various exhibits at the museum, bronze statues of the brothers are situated on the grounds.

Tobe Zoological Park

Tobe Zoological Park is a diverse animal park featuring over 765 animals from approximately 163 different species. Divided into 10 streets, the park offers separate zones for various animals and hosts interactive events throughout the day. Visitors can enjoy close encounters with hippos, tigers, monkeys, and other exotic creatures. The park also includes a restaurant overlooking the elephant enclosure and cute gift shops offering souvenirs and decorations.

Tsubaki Shrine

Tsubaki Shrine is a visitor site in Matsuyama, Japan. The shrine hosts the New Year Matsuri every February, offering visitors the chance to witness traditional events, enjoy delicious food from various stalls, and purchase good luck charms. The site is also adorned with Japanese poetry on its fence posts. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Taisanji Temple is a religious building in Matsuyama, Japan. Taisan-ji is a historic Buddhist temple located in Matsuyama, designated as a National Treasure of Japan.
