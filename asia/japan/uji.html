Uji

Uji lies south of Kyoto on the Uji River, known for tea cultivation since the Kamakura period. Byōdō-in temple, built in 1053, appears on the ten-yen coin and was inscribed as part of the UNESCO Historic Monuments of Ancient Kyoto. The Tale of Genji sets episodes in this area. Tea houses and bridges line the riverbank. Uji City formed in 1951 from merged towns and remains a center of matcha production.

Uji Bridge

The First Battle of Uji, alternatively known as "Mochihitos Raising of an Army" in Japan is a battle which took place on June 20, 1180, following Prince Mochihito and Minamoto no Yorimasa's plan to raise an army to overthrow the Taira clan and the issuing of an edict urging the Minamoto clan, major temples, and shrines in the country to revolt. Due to lack of preparation, the plan was discovered by the Taira, and Prince Mochihito and Yorimasa were defeated, dying at the battle.
