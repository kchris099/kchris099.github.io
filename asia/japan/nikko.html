Nikko

Mountain worship here dates to the eighth century. In the seventeenth, Tokugawa Ieyasu's heirs built the lavish Tōshōgū mausoleum that made Nikkō a national sacred site. UNESCO listing covers the shrine-temple complex set in cedar forests as a statement of shogunal legitimacy. A Japanese proverb equates seeing Nikkō with the height of scenic and architectural satisfaction.

Shinkyō Bridge

Shinkyō Bridge, a striking black-and-red bridge surrounded by dense forests known for their active fall foliage, is widely regarded as one of Japan's top three bridges. It sits in a location with thick forests flanking the Daiya River below. Serving as an entrance to Nikko's shrines and temples, the bridge has a rich history dating back to feudal times when only the Shogun was permitted to cross it.

Yōmeimon Gate

Yomeimon Gate is a famous landmark at Nikkō Tōshō-gū, known for its detailed carvings and active paint. The gate is adorned with over 5,100 sculptures, showing the sophisticated techniques of the early 17th century. It is often referred to as the 'Higurashi Gate' due to its beauty that could keep visitors engaged all day.

Nikko Tamozawa Imperial Villa Memorial Park

Tamozawa Imperial Villa in Nikkō was built from 1899 as a retreat for the imperial household and used by three generations through 1947. Wooden corridors link Edo- and Meiji-period rooms set in landscaped gardens. The villa shows how court architecture adapted to a mountain resort setting.

Rin’nōji Taiyū-in (Mausoleum of Iemitsu)

Rin'nōji Taiyū-in (Mausoleum of Iemitsu) is a visitor site in Nikko, Japan. The Taiyu-in is the lavishly decorated mausoleum of Tokugawa Iemitsu, the third Tokugawa shogun, located in Nikko. The complex is designed to face Toshogu, symbolizing Iemitsu's deep respect for his grandfather, Ieyasu. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Tobu World Square

Tobu World Square is an outdoor theme park featuring 1:25 scale models of buildings from around the world. Visitors can explore replicas of architectural wonders from America, Asia, Egypt, Europe, and Japan, including the Statue of Liberty, Eiffel Tower, Great Sphinx of Giza, Duomo di Milano, Tokyo Skytree, and Saint Basils Cathedral. The park offers guided tours and has various eateries for visitors to enjoy.
