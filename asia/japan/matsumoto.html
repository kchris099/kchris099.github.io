Explore Matsumoto

A Brief History

Developed as a prosperous castle town in the late 16th century, Matsumoto grew due to its strategic position in the Japanese Alps. Its notably preserved original wooden castle is a rare architectural survivor, anchoring a city in traditional merchant heritage. The survival of these cultural and architectural milestones provides a powerful window into the region's storied past. Museums, markets, and street plans in Matsumoto still show layers from those periods.

Matsumoto Castle

Matsumoto-jō Castle, located in Matsumoto City, Nagano Prefecture, Japan, is a 16th-century national treasure with striking black walls. Visitors can explore historical weapons exhibits and climb to the top for panoramic views of the city and snow-capped mountains. The castle grounds are adorned with maple and cherry trees, making it a popular spot for viewing red leaves and cherry blossoms in early November. The castle offers evening lighting events until 22:00.

Free

Matsumoto Castle Park

Matsumoto Castle Park is a fortified historic site in Matsumoto, Japan. Matsumoto Castle Park, located in the second largest city of Nagano Prefecture, is a park surrounding the historic Matsumoto Castle. Visitors can explore the castle grounds for free and purchase tickets to tour the interior, which showcases feudal-era artifacts such as armor and matchlock guns. Walls, courtyards, or towers form the main visitor route when the grounds are open.

Free

Yōhashira-jinja Shrine

Yōhashira-jinja Shrine is a visitor site in Matsumoto, Japan. Yohashira-jinja is a prominent Shinto shrine located in the center of Matsumoto, near the historic Nawate Street. Uniquely dedicated to four major Shinto deities, it is considered a powerful site for granting wishes. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Free

Fukashi-jinja Shrine

Fukashi-jinja Shrine is a visitor site in Matsumoto, Japan. This festival brings out the cultural richness and liveliness of Matsumoto. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest. Fukashi-jinja Shrine remains part of the public visitor circuit in Matsumoto.

Free

Gofukuji Temple

Gofukuji Temple, located southwest of the city center in Matsumoto, Nagano, is a Buddhist temple complex surrounded by natural beauty. Throughout the year, visitors can enjoy views of cherry blossoms in spring, hydrangeas in summer, colorful foliage in autumn, and snowfall in winter. The temple grounds are home to two bronze cow statues that symbolize an ancient legend related to its name 'prostrated cow temple.'.

Free

Matsumoto City Alps Park

Matsumoto City Alps Park is a hilltop destination featuring playgrounds, walking trails, and an observation deck that offers views of the Japanese Alps. The park is located in Matsumoto City, Nagano Prefecture and offers a trail from Konashidaira Campsite to Yokoo Sanso along the Azusa River. Visitors can enjoy a leisurely walk while taking in the natural beauty of the area.

Free

Shinshu Sky Park

Shinshu Sky Park is a versatile park located at Matsumoto Airport, offering various amenities such as running paths, sports courts, and cherry blossoms in the spring. Situated 657.5 meters above sea level, it is the highest commercial airport in Japan and has expansive greenery. Adjacent to the park is Alwin Stadium, home to the Matsumoto Yamaga FC football club.

Free

Hotaka Shrine

Hotaka Shrine is a visitor site in Matsumoto, Japan. Hotaka Shrine, located in Nagano Prefecture, is a significant shrine associated with transportation safety and the guardian of the Japan Alps. Originally established by the Azumi people, it enshrines a sea god brought from ancient northern Kyushu. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Free

Alps Azumino National Government Park (Horigane, Hotaka)

Alps Azumino National Government Park, located in Horigane, Hotaka, offers a setting with colorful fields of tulips and seasonal flowers against the backdrop of Mount Jonen. The park provides various activities such as frisbee golf, hiking trails, and craft activities for kids, it hosts a winter illumination event from mid-November to early-January featuring over 700,000 lights that create a display.

Free

Utsukushigahara

Utsukushigahara is a visitor site in Matsumoto, Japan. Records associated with the site include 2000. The alpine plateau has over 200 varieties of flowers and is grazing land for cattle. At an altitude of over 2000 meters, it offers seasonal beauty and starry nights. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.
