Explore Kumamoto

A Brief History

Grown around its formidable 17th-century castle built by Kato Kiyomasa, Kumamoto served as a powerful military stronghold on Kyushu. Long-running the violent Satsuma Rebellion in 1877, the city modernized while significantly honoring its long-standing legacy of martial resilience. The survival of these cultural and architectural milestones provides a powerful window into the region's storied past.

Kumamoto Castle

Kumamoto Castle, originally built in 1607, is a reconstructed castle located in Kumamoto City. It is a history museum and is a symbol of the city's rich heritage. The castle played a significant role during Japan's Warring States period and. Documented dates associated with the site include 1607.

Free

Katō Shrine

Katō Shrine is a historic Shinto shrine dedicated to Kiyomasa Katō, a prominent warlord of the Warring States period who played a significant role in building Kumamoto Castle and establishing the city's foundation. It is quite rare to find a shrine in Japan that venerates an actual historical figure, highlighting the close relationship between Kiyomasa and Kumamoto.

Free

Kumamoto Castle Inari Shrine

Kumamoto Castle Inari Shrine is a lovely local shrine located near the entrance of Kumamoto Castle. It offers colorful Inari shrines, protective charms, and fortune papers. Visitors can also obtain goshuincho here. The shrine has a mixture of traditional elements from before the Meiji era, combining both Kami and Buddha in the same complex.

Free

Sakura-no-baba Josaien

Sakura-no-baba Josaien is a visitor site in Kumamoto, Japan. Sakura-no-baba Josaien is a active tourist district located in the Sakuranobaba area of Kumamoto Castle. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest. Sakura-no-baba Josaien remains part of the public visitor circuit in Kumamoto.

Free

Kumamoto Castle Park (Ninomaru Hiroba)

The Ninomaru Hiroba is a vast, open lawn area situated within the outer defenses of Kumamoto Castle. It provides excellent, unobstructed views of the castle's main keep and the surrounding stone walls. The park is a popular gathering place for locals, frequently hosting cultural festivals, picnics, and seasonal events, particularly during the cherry blossom season.

Free

Kumamoto Station

Kumamoto Station is a modern and efficient transportation hub located in Kumamoto City, Japan. It is a central point for both local commuters and tourists exploring the region. The station's sleek and spacious design offers a comfortable environment for travelers. Nearby, the City Tram provides easy access to major destinations such as Kumamoto Castle and Suizenji Jojuen Garden, making it convenient for tourists to get around the city.

Free

Fujisaki Hachimangu

Fujisaki Hachimangu is a visitor site in Kumamoto, Japan. Fujisaki Hachimangu Shrine is a prominent shrine located in the Chuo Ward of Kumamoto City. It is the main shrine of Kumamoto and is frequented by many locals. Visitors can take the Sendai sightseeing bus loople to reach the shrine, which takes about 20 minutes from getting off to leaving. The grounds and principal structures are the main points of interest.

Free

Monkey D. Luffy statue

The Monkey D. Luffy statue is a bronze monument located in front of the Kumamoto Prefectural Government Office. It was erected to honor Eiichiro Oda, the Kumamoto-born creator of the globally popular manga series 'One Piece,' and to commemorate the region's recovery from the 2016 earthquakes. It is a major pilgrimage site for anime and manga fans.

¥500

Suizenji Jojuen Garden

Suizenji Jojuen Garden is a public park in Kumamoto, Japan. Paths, lawns, and shaded areas make up the main public facilities. Suizenji Jojuen Garden remains part of the public visitor circuit in Kumamoto. Local maps identify Suizenji Jojuen Garden within Kumamoto, Japan.

Free

Kengun Shrine

Kengun Shrine is a significant landmark in Kumamoto City, known for its rich history and welcoming atmosphere. As one of the oldest shrines in the city, it holds a special place in the hearts of locals and visitors alike. The shrine offers a glimpse into traditional Japanese culture and provides an opportunity for prayer and reflection. Visitors can also enjoy interacting with the friendly family who oversees the shrine, as they are eager to share its history.
