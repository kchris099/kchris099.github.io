Explore Mount Aso

A Brief History

Mount Aso, situated in the heart of Kyushu, is one of the largest active volcanoes in the world and a testament to the awesome, untamed power of nature. Its massive caldera encompasses ancient rural villages, expansive green pastures, and mystical shrines that have long revered the mountain as a sacred deity. Beyond the smoking crater, the surrounding region offers breathtaking vistas like the dramatic Takachiho Gorge, where volcanic basalt columns drop into emerald rivers. Exploring Mount Aso is a journey into the spiritual and geological origins of the Japanese archipelago.

Aso Crater

On January 20, 2026, a Robinson R44 Raven II helicopter on a sightseeing tour crashed inside Mount Aso. All three onboard, including two Taiwanese tourists and the pilot, were spotted by emergency agencies' drones but remain inaccessible as of May 2026.

Free

Kusasenrigahama

Mount Aso or Aso Volcano is the second largest active volcano in Japan after Mount Fuji, and among the largest in the world. Common use relates often only to the somma volcano in the centre of Aso Caldera. It stands in Aso Kujū National Park in Kumamoto Prefecture, on the island of Kyushu.

Free

Kamishikimi Kumanoimasu Shrine

A profoundly deeply mystical, incredibly atmospheric forest shrine famously approached via a long, lantern-lined stone staircase. It is incredibly culturally significant, famously appearing deeply intertwined with ancient, powerful Japanese folklore.

Free

Aso Shrine

One of Japan's oldest and most prominent Shinto shrines, with a history spanning over 2,500 years. It is uniquely deeply connected to the volcanic activity of Mount Aso, serving as the center for local agricultural rituals and fiery festivals.

Free

Takachiho Gorge

A spectacular natural gorge carved through volcanic basalt rock by the Gokase River. According to Japanese mythology, it is profoundly tied to the legend of the sun goddess Amaterasu, making it a site of immense spiritual and cultural importance.

Free

Ama-no-Iwato Shrine

A highly sacred Shinto shrine built near the legendary cave where the sun goddess Amaterasu is said to have hidden, plunging the world into darkness. It serves as an incredibly important site for Japanese creation mythology.
