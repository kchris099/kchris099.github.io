Destination Guide — Kyushu

Explore Mount Aso

A Brief History

Mount Aso is an active volcano in central Kyushu with one of the world's largest calderas, formed in eruptions roughly 90,000 years ago. Grasslands called Kusasenri lie inside the rim, used for grazing and tourism. The Nakadake crater releases steam and is monitored for safety. Nearby towns such as Aso and Takamori grew around shrines and routes crossing the caldera. Volcanic soil supports agriculture in surrounding Kumamoto Prefecture.

Aso Crater

On January 20, 2026, a Robinson R44 Raven II helicopter on a sightseeing tour crashed inside Mount Aso. All three onboard, including two Taiwanese tourists and the pilot, were spotted by emergency agencies' drones but remain inaccessible as of May 2026.

Free

Kusasenrigahama

Mount Aso or Aso Volcano is the second largest active volcano in Japan after Mount Fuji, and among the largest in the world. Common use relates often only to the somma volcano in the centre of Aso Caldera. It stands in Aso Kujū National Park in Kumamoto Prefecture, on the island of Kyushu.

Free

Kamishikimi Kumanoimasu Shrine

Kamishikimi Kumanoimasu Shrine is a very atmospheric Shinto shrine located in the forested mountains of Takamori, near Mount Aso. The approach to the shrine consists of a long, moss-covered stone staircase lined with nearly 100 stone lanterns, creating a mystical, otherworldly environment. It is renowned as a 'power spot' and has inspired settings in popular Japanese anime.

Free

Aso Shrine

Aso Shrine is one of the oldest and most prominent Shinto shrines in Japan, with a history spanning over 2,000 years. Located near Mount Aso, it features a distinctive two-story Romon gate, traditionally considered one of the three grandest in the country. The shrine is very connected to the agricultural cycles and volcanic activity of the Aso caldera region.

Free

Takachiho Gorge

Takachiho Gorge is a dramatic natural ravine in Miyazaki Prefecture, formed by the Gokase River cutting through ancient volcanic basalt columns. The sheer, geometric cliffs rise dramatically from the emerald-green water, creating a very scenic and somewhat mystical environment. Visitors can rent rowboats to navigate the narrow gorge and view the beautiful Minainotaki waterfall up close.

Free

Ama-no-Iwato Shrine

Ama-no-Iwato Shrine is a very significant Shinto site located near Takachiho, very tied to Japanese mythology. It is dedicated to the sun goddess Amaterasu, who, according to legend, hid in a nearby cave (the Ama-no-Iwato), plunging the world into darkness. The shrine offers a serene, spiritual atmosphere and guided access to an observation deck viewing the sacred cave across the river.
