Explore Yokohama

A Brief History

Yokohama opened its port in 1859 after Japan signed treaties ending isolation, becoming one of the first places where foreigners could trade and reside. The Bluff and Yamate districts retain consulates and homes from the late 19th century. The Great Kanto earthquake of 1923 and air raids in 1945 damaged the city. Red Brick Warehouse and Minato Mirai skyline reflect later harbor redevelopment on Tokyo Bay.

Top Sights & Activities

Kishamichi Promenade

Minato Mirai 21, often known as simply Minato Mirai and abbreviated as MM, is the central business district of Yokohama, Japan. Initially developed in the 1980s, Minato Mirai 21 was designed as a large master-planned development and new urban center planned to connect Yokohama's traditionally important areas and commercial centers of Kannai and the Yokohama Station area.

Free

Yokohama Red Brick Warehouse

The Yokohama Red Brick Warehouse is a historical building that is used as a complex that includes a shopping mall, banquet hall, and event venues. The complex, officially known as the Newport Pier Bonded Warehouse, was originally used as customs buildings, and has two sections: Warehouse No.1 and No.2. It is operated by Yokohama Akarenga Co.

Free

Yamashita Park

Yamashita Park sits within the Yokohama area of Yokohama, documented in local heritage registers for its civic or cultural role. Visitors approach from the main access road and combine the visit with neighbouring heritage buildings and public spaces in the district.

Free

Yokohama Chinatown

Yokohama Chinatown is the largest Chinatown in Japan and one of the largest in the world. Established in the mid-19th century, the vibrant district is characterized by its numerous ornate, colorful entrance gates and narrow streets packed with hundreds of restaurants and food stalls. It is a major culinary destination, famous for its authentic Chinese cuisine and lively festival atmosphere.

Closed

Gundam Factory Yokohama

Gundam Factory Yokohama is a must-visit complex for fans of the Gundam animated franchise. It features a meticulously designed, 18-meter tall, life-sized moving robot that has been attracting travellers from around the world since its opening in December 2020. The giant robot moves every hour, offering an awe-inspiring experience during the 'startup experiment.'.

¥1000

Yokohama Marine Tower

Yokohama Marine Tower, a prominent waterfront landmark and lighthouse constructed in 1961, features an observation deck offering views of the harbor. Standing at a height of 106 meters (348 feet), this lattice tower was temporarily closed in 2006 but underwent restoration, reopening to the public on Yokohama's 150th anniversary in 2009.

Free

Yokohama Stadium

Yokohama Stadium, established in 1978, serves as the home ground for the Yokohama DeNA BayStars baseball team. Situated in central Yokohama, it's an ideal spot to catch a thrilling baseball game while sipping on a refreshing Kirin beer. The stadium has a seating capacity of nearly 35,000 and hosts games from April to October, with tickets typically ranging from JPY2000 to JPY7000.

Closed

Yokohama Landmark Tower

The Yokohama Landmark Tower is an structure that stands tall in Japan, featuring a luxurious hotel, bustling mall, and modern office spaces. One of its highlights is the Sky Garden observation deck, where travellers can ascend in the country’s fastest elevator to enjoy 360-degree views that stretch all the way to Tokyo and even majestic Mt. Fuji! The tower's lights reflect beautifully on the bay waters below, creating a romantic atmosphere for couples strolling nearby.

Free

Nissan Global Headquarters Gallery

Nestled within the impressive Nissan World Headquarters building, the Nissan Global Headquarters Gallery is a must-visit for car enthusiasts and families alike. Just a short stroll from Yokohama Station, this sleek showroom showcases an exciting blend of cutting-edge new models alongside vintage racing cars that tell the story of automotive history. travellers can immerse themselves in interactive tech displays and even try their hand at driving simulators.

Free

Nogeyama Zoological Gardens

Nestled within the scenic Nogeyama Park, the Nogeyama Zoological Gardens is a delightful destination for animal lovers and families alike. Established in 1951, this charming zoo boasts an impressive collection of over 90 species, including majestic lions and tigers that will surely captivate your imagination. Zoorasia

Zoorasia is a captivating zoo that offers travellers an immersive experience through its expansive open-air habitat zones, showcasing a diverse array of animals. This unique facility allows you to encounter rare species like okapis and Goodfellow's tree kangaroos while exploring exhibits organized by global climate zones.

Free

Kawasaki Daishi

Kawasaki Daishi is a famous Buddhist temple known for its protection against evil spirits and good luck charms. It's a significant cultural site with beautiful architecture and lively events. The site forms part of Kawasaki Daishi's documented civic and cultural landscape within Japan, with archives and visitor information available from municipal or regional heritage authorities.

Free

Soji-ji

Nestled in the vibrant city of Yokohama, Sojiji Temple stands as a remarkable Buddhist temple complex that beautifully marries traditional architecture with serene landscaped grounds. As one of the two head temples of the Soto school of Zen Buddhism, it serves as a significant spiritual sanctuary and is among Japan's largest Buddhist institutions. Founded in 1321 and relocated here after a fire in the 19th century, Sojiji offers travellers an oasis of tranquility amidst urban life.

Free

Nissan Stadium

Nissan Stadium, Japan's largest stadium with a seating capacity of 72,327, is a popular venue for international and J League fixtures. It hosts regular football games and rugby matches throughout the year. The stadium is also home to Yokohama F Marinos, part of Manchester City's City Football Group stable. Additionally, it serves as a venue for baseball games at the nearby Yokohama Stadium. travellers can take guided tours of the stadium when events are not taking place.

Free (aqua museum ¥3500)

Yokohama Hakkeijima Sea Paradise

Yokohama Hakkeijima Sea Paradise, established in 1993, is a well-known amusement park in Yokohama. It offers a wide range of attractions including an aquarium with sealife shows, various amusement rides, a marina, and a hotel. The site forms part of Yokohama Hakkeijima Sea Paradise's documented civic and cultural landscape within Japan, with archives and visitor information available from municipal or regional heritage authorities.
