Destination Guide — Okinawa

Explore Naha

A Brief History

Naha is the capital of Okinawa Prefecture on the main island of the Ryukyu chain. Shuri Castle served as the royal seat of the Ryukyu Kingdom from the 15th century until annexation by Japan in 1879. The city was heavily damaged in the 1945 Battle of Okinawa. Postwar U.S. administration lasted until 1972. Kokusai Street and public markets reflect rebuilding and trade with mainland Japan and East Asia.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

kokusai-dori

Kokusai-dori (International Street) is the bustling, two-kilometer-long main commercial artery of Naha, Okinawa. Lined with palm trees, the vibrant street features hundreds of souvenir shops, traditional Okinawan restaurants, izakayas, and lively bars. It serves as the primary entertainment and shopping hub for tourists, offering a vibrant mix of local culture and modern commerce.

Free

Makishi Public Market

Makishi Public Market is a documented market or commercial centre in Naha, Japan, listed in local civic guides for its role in regional trade and daily commerce. The site operates during standard market hours and is open to visitors without charge for browsing. Travellers enter from the surrounding streets and combine the visit with nearby food vendors and craft stalls.

Free

Heiwa Dori

Heiwa Dōri is a covered shopping arcade and tourist attraction in Naha, Okinawa. The entrance to the arcade is on Kokusai Street, opposite Mitsubishi department store. The name Heiwa Dōri was chosen in a public competition in 1951, and is linked to the Heiwa-kan cinema which used to be located in the mouth of the arcade.

¥400

Shuri Castle

Shuri Castle is a Ryukyuan gusuku castle in the historic Shuri district of Naha, the capital of Okinawa Prefecture, Japan. Between 1429 and 1879, it was the palace of the Ryukyu Kingdom, before becoming largely neglected. In 1945, during the Battle of Okinawa, it was almost completely destroyed.

Free

Cape Manzamo

Cape Manzamo is a scenic rock formation on Okinawa Island, Japan. It is located near Onna Village in the Kunigami District of Okinawa Prefecture. Cape Manzamo is one of the most popular places among both Japanese and international travelers who visit Okinawa Island.

Free

Bise Beach

Bise Beach is a documented coastal feature in Naha, Japan, recognised in regional geographic listings for its natural formation and public access. The shoreline is open to visitors throughout the year and connects to adjacent beachfront districts. Travellers approach from the coastal road or boardwalk and combine the visit with nearby harbour areas and seaside markets.

¥2180

Okinawa Churaumi Aquarium

Okinawa Island, also known as Okinawa Main Island, is the largest of the Okinawa Islands and the Ryukyu Islands of Japan in the Kyushu region. It is the smallest and least populated of the five main islands of Japan. The island is approximately 106 kilometres long, an average 11 kilometres wide, and has an area of 1,206.98 square kilometers.

Free

Okichan Theatre (Free Dolphin Show)

Okichan Theatre (Free Dolphin Show) is a documented performance venue in Naha, Japan, recognised in regional cultural listings for its programming and architectural significance. The venue hosts scheduled performances and admits visitors during box-office hours. Travellers approach from the surrounding cultural district and combine the visit with nearby galleries and historic theatres.

Free

American Village

Mihama American Village is a large, vibrant entertainment and shopping complex located in Chatan, Okinawa. Developed on a former US military base, the area features a distinct American West Coast architectural style, complete with a large Ferris wheel, diverse restaurants, and boutique shops. It is a popular destination for both locals and tourists, offering a lively, multicultural atmosphere.

Free

Depot Island Boardwalk

The Depot Island Boardwalk is a scenic coastal promenade located within the American Village complex in Okinawa. The walkway runs along the oceanfront, lined with cafes, bars, and colorful, eclectic architecture. It is a very popular spot for evening strolls, offering excellent views of the sunset over the East China Sea and a relaxed, resort-like ambiance.

Free

Chatan Park Sunset Beach

Sunset Beach is a popular, artificial public beach located adjacent to the American Village in Chatan, Okinawa. Known for its calm, shallow waters and well-maintained facilities, it is a favored spot for swimming, barbecues, and family outings. As the name suggests, it offers dramatic, unobstructed views of the sun setting over the ocean.

¥600

Former Japanese Navy Underground Headquarters

Former Japanese Navy Underground Headquarters, also known as Kyukaigun Shireibugo, is a significant historical site located in southern Okinawa. This 450-meter-long tunnel complex served as the headquarters for the Japanese Navy during the intense Battle of Okinawa in World War II. Situated about four kilometers south of central Naha City, it stands as a preserved tribute to the victims of this battle.

Free

Umikaji Terrace Senagajima

Umikaji Terrace Senagajima is a popular spot for dining and shopping located on Senaga Island, just outside Naha in Tomigusuku. The shop-lined terraces offer views of the nearby seaside, with over 40 shops selling gourmet dishes and various types of sweets made with unique Okinawan ingredients.

¥2480

DMM Kariyushi Aquarium okinawa

Nestled in Toyosaki, Tomigusuku City, the DMM Kariyushi Aquarium is a captivating destination that opened its doors in May 2020. This modern aquarium showcases an impressive array of marine life, including jellyfish, turtles, and vibrant tropical fish. travellers can also explore a lush rainforest area teeming with frogs and chameleons. Perfect for families visiting Okinawa, it offers both educational experiences and entertainment—making it an ideal spot to escape rainy weather.

¥450

Himeyuri Cenotaph

The Himeyuri Memorial Tower is a small but poignant museum that honors the brave female students and teachers who served as nurses during the 3-month Battle of Okinawa at the end of WWII. The museum provides a glimpse into the inhumane conditions these women endured, and most tragically lost their lives. The short film displayed at the museum is heart-wrenching and will surely move travellers to tears.

Free

Gushikawa Castle

Gushikawa Castle, situated in the Kyan District of Itoman City, is a captivating site believed to have been constructed around the 13th century. The castle ruins boast aesthetically pleasing stone walls that evoke nostalgia for the old Ryukyu Kingdom era. Notably, there is a unique feature—a hole leading down to the beach below.

Free

Peace Memorial Park

Peace Memorial Park in Okinawa commemorates the WWII battle fought there with monuments and open space. The park is beautiful and expansive, with facilities such as eateries, restrooms, picnic areas, and parks available for travellers. Public transportation is convenient, and guided tours in English are recommended to enhance knowledge of the historical events. While some areas require an entrance fee, many outdoor attractions are free.

Free

Cornerstone of Peace

The Cornerstone of Peace stands as a poignant tribute along the serene coastline, featuring engraved granite slabs that honor all lives lost during the Battle of Okinawa, regardless of their nationality or status as soldiers or civilians. This memorial is part of the Okinawa Prefectural Peace Memorial Museum, where travellers can delve into various exhibitions and access an extensive "Information Library" dedicated to peace studies.

Free

Shikina-en Royal Gardens

Shikina-en Royal Gardens, a UNESCO World Heritage site, served as the secondary residence for the royal family of the Ryukyu Kingdom. This historic property features traditional palace buildings, walking paths, and a serene pond at its center. The garden's design includes a large pond shaped like the Chinese character for heart and an elegant gazebo on a small island in the middle of the pond.

Free

Shureimon Gate

Shureimon Gate is a traditional Chinese gate located at Shurijo Castle, originally dating back to the 16th century and later rebuilt. travellers can immerse themselves in the rich cultural experiences offered here, such as enjoying traditional music and savoring A5 Ishigaki beef from the Yaeyama Islands of Okinawa.

¥300

Tamaudun

Tamaudun, a mausoleum located near Shuri Castle in Okinawa, is the resting place of the Second Shō Dynasty's royalty. Constructed in the early 16th century, it suffered damage during World War II but has since been restored and recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage Site. The mausoleum consists of two main rooms - one for kings and queens and another for princes and princesses.

¥2000

Gyokusendo Cave

Nestled within the enchanting Okinawa World, Gyokusendo Cave is a limestone wonder that beckons adventurers and nature lovers alike. Discovered in 1967, this underground marvel boasts over a million stalactites and serene pools of water, creating an otherworldly atmosphere. Spanning 800 meters, the cave's interior is illuminated by ethereal white formations that transport travellers to a mystical realm.

¥2000

Okinawa World

Okinawa World is a unique theme park that offers a comprehensive experience of Okinawa's nature, history, and culture. travellers can explore a replica castle townscape, stroll through a tropical fruit orchard with over 450 fruit trees, and witness the vibrant eisa dance performances during special festivities. The highlight of the park is the massive natural cave called Gyokusendo, known for its formations and well-designed lighting.

¥2500

Valley of Gangala

Valley of Gangala is an enjoyable and enlightening nature reserve offering guided tours through lush surroundings, including natural caves, trees, and landscapes. The trails are easy to navigate without excessive exertion. travellers receive a complimentary thermos of tea and an English guidebook. The knowledgeable tour guides provide informative commentary about the area's history, prehistoric culture in Okinawa, and the land.

¥500

Nakagusuku Castle Ruins

Nakagusuku Castle Ruins are well-preserved remnants of a historic Ryukyu castle, providing insight into Okinawa's rich history and scenic views. The site forms part of Nakagusuku Castle Ruins's documented civic and cultural landscape within Japan, with archives and visitor information available from municipal or regional heritage authorities.

Free

Futenma-Gu (Futenma Shrine)

Nestled in the heart of Okinawa, Futenma Shrine is a captivating Shinto shrine that draws both locals and travelers alike. This serene spot not only offers a glimpse into traditional Japanese spirituality but also features an intriguing cave adorned with stalactites. travellers can embark on guided tours of the cave, which depart every 20 minutes; just remember to sign up beforehand at the designated area.

Free

Naminoue Shrine

Naminoue Shrine, established in 1890, is a significant Shinto shrine located in Naha, Okinawa. Originally built for the Ryukuyuan religion and dedicated to Nirai Kanai, it now overlooks the sea and offers views from the cliffs. The shrine consists of a main hall, worship hall, and smaller shrines. It is situated near Memorial Park and Tsujiwakasa Ryokuchi Park.

Free

Tomari Iyumachi Fish Market

Tomari Iyumachi Fish Market is a bustling seafood market where find fresh local catches at wholesale stands. It's known for offering affordable prices and lower processing fees compared to other places. If you're a fan of Japanese food, this market is a paradise for you, offering an array of delectable options to satisfy your cravings. Whether you have an innate love for Japanese cuisine or have developed a taste for it over time, this market is a must-visit destination.

Free

Tsuboya Yachimun Street

Tsuboya Yachimun Street, also known as Tsuboya Yachimun Dori, is a serene cobblestone street adorned with traditional earthenware workshops, charming cafes, and a pottery museum. The street is home to unique workshops that have been crafting traditional Ryukyu-era pottery for generations. travellers can enjoy leisurely strolls and window shopping while admiring the vibrant and skillfully crafted potteries on display.
