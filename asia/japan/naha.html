Explore Naha

A Brief History

Naha, the vibrant capital of Okinawa, is a stunning, sun-drenched coastal city that proudly serves as the gateway to Japan's subtropical island paradise. Distinctly different from the mainland, Naha deeply preserves the unique, profoundly rich culture of the ancient Ryukyu Kingdom, evident in its magnificent castles, vibrant traditional textiles, and deeply flavorful local cuisine. From the bustling, lively street markets of Kokusai-dori to the breathtaking, crystal-clear turquoise waters nearby, Naha uniquely combines laid-back, tropical island vibes with a rich, colorful historical legacy.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

kokusai-dori

The bustling, deeply historic main street of Naha, often referred to as the 'Miracle Mile'. Having rapidly developed following the devastating Battle of Okinawa, it proudly stands today as the beating heart of Okinawan commerce and resilience.

Free

Makishi Public Market

A deeply authentic, highly historic indoor market that has functioned as 'the kitchen of Okinawa' for decades. It is the absolute center of local culinary culture, famously showcasing the unique, vibrant ingredients native to the Ryukyu Islands.

Free

Heiwa Dori

Heiwa Dōri is a covered shopping arcade and tourist attraction in Naha, Okinawa. The entrance to the arcade is on Kokusai Street, opposite Mitsubishi department store. The name Heiwa Dōri was chosen in a public competition in 1951, and is linked to the Heiwa-kan cinema which used to be located in the mouth of the arcade.

¥400

Shuri Castle

Shuri Castle is a Ryukyuan gusuku castle in the historic Shuri district of Naha, the capital of Okinawa Prefecture, Japan. Between 1429 and 1879, it was the palace of the Ryukyu Kingdom, before becoming largely neglected. In 1945, during the Battle of Okinawa, it was almost completely destroyed.

Free

Cape Manzamo

Cape Manzamo is a scenic rock formation on Okinawa Island, Japan. It is located near Onna Village in the Kunigami District of Okinawa Prefecture. Cape Manzamo is one of the most popular places among both Japanese and international travelers who visit Okinawa Island.

Free

Bise Beach

A remarkably pristine, highly tranquil coastal area beautifully surrounded by ancient, protective Fukugi tree windbreaks. The deeply traditional village layout provides a profound glimpse into historic Okinawan seaside life before modern development.

¥2180

Okichan Theatre (Free Dolphin Show)

A highly entertaining, wonderfully engaging open-air theater beautifully set directly against the deep blue ocean, famously offering incredibly spectacular, completely free acrobatic dolphin performances.

American Village

A uniquely vibrant, highly colorful entertainment complex built on a former US military base. It powerfully reflects the profound, deeply complex cultural synthesis of Okinawan and American influences that defines the modern island.

A deeply colorful, highly vibrant coastal walkway beautifully defining the American Village complex. It incredibly powerfully showcases the uniquely beautiful, deeply synthesized blend of vibrant Okinawan seaside culture and modern entertainment.
