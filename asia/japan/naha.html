Destination Guide — Okinawa

Explore Naha

A Brief History

Naha is the capital of Okinawa Prefecture on the main island of the Ryukyu chain. Shuri Castle served as the royal seat of the Ryukyu Kingdom from the 15th century until annexation by Japan in 1879. The city was heavily damaged in the 1945 Battle of Okinawa. Postwar U.S. administration lasted until 1972. Kokusai Street and public markets reflect rebuilding and trade with mainland Japan and East Asia.

Top Sights & Activities

Free

kokusai-dori

Kokusai-dori (International Street) is the bustling, two-kilometer-long main commercial artery of Naha, Okinawa. Lined with palm trees, the vibrant street features hundreds of souvenir shops, traditional Okinawan restaurants, izakayas, and lively bars. It serves as the primary entertainment and shopping hub for tourists, offering a vibrant mix of local culture and modern commerce.

Free

Makishi Public Market

Makishi Public Market is a documented market or commercial centre in Naha, Japan, listed in local civic guides for its role in regional trade and daily commerce. The site operates during standard market hours and is open to visitors without charge for browsing. Travellers enter from the surrounding streets and combine the visit with nearby food vendors and craft stalls.

Free

Heiwa Dori

Heiwa Dōri is a covered shopping arcade and tourist attraction in Naha, Okinawa. The entrance to the arcade is on Kokusai Street, opposite Mitsubishi department store. The name Heiwa Dōri was chosen in a public competition in 1951, and is linked to the Heiwa-kan cinema which used to be located in the mouth of the arcade.

¥400

Shuri Castle

Shuri Castle is a Ryukyuan gusuku castle in the historic Shuri district of Naha, the capital of Okinawa Prefecture, Japan. Between 1429 and 1879, it was the palace of the Ryukyu Kingdom, before becoming largely neglected. In 1945, during the Battle of Okinawa, it was almost completely destroyed.

Free

Cape Manzamo

Cape Manzamo is a scenic rock formation on Okinawa Island, Japan. It is located near Onna Village in the Kunigami District of Okinawa Prefecture. Cape Manzamo is one of the most popular places among both Japanese and international travelers who visit Okinawa Island.

Free

Bise Beach

Bise Beach is a documented coastal feature in Naha, Japan, recognised in regional geographic listings for its natural formation and public access. The shoreline is open to visitors throughout the year and connects to adjacent beachfront districts. Travellers approach from the coastal road or boardwalk and combine the visit with nearby harbour areas and seaside markets.

¥2180

Okinawa Churaumi Aquarium

Okinawa Island, also known as Okinawa Main Island, is the largest of the Okinawa Islands and the Ryukyu Islands of Japan in the Kyushu region. It is the smallest and least populated of the five main islands of Japan. The island is approximately 106 kilometres long, an average 11 kilometres wide, and has an area of 1,206.98 square kilometers.

Free

Okichan Theatre (Free Dolphin Show)

Okichan Theatre (Free Dolphin Show) is a documented performance venue in Naha, Japan, recognised in regional cultural listings for its programming and architectural significance. The venue hosts scheduled performances and admits visitors during box-office hours. Travellers approach from the surrounding cultural district and combine the visit with nearby galleries and historic theatres.

Free

American Village

Mihama American Village is a large, vibrant entertainment and shopping complex located in Chatan, Okinawa. Developed on a former US military base, the area features a distinct American West Coast architectural style, complete with a large Ferris wheel, diverse restaurants, and boutique shops. It is a popular destination for both locals and tourists, offering a lively, multicultural atmosphere.

Free

Depot Island Boardwalk

The Depot Island Boardwalk is a scenic coastal promenade located within the American Village complex in Okinawa. The walkway runs along the oceanfront, lined with cafes, bars, and colorful, eclectic architecture. It is a very popular spot for evening strolls, offering excellent views of the sunset over the East China Sea and a relaxed, resort-like ambiance.

Free

Chatan Park Sunset Beach

Sunset Beach is a popular, artificial public beach located adjacent to the American Village in Chatan, Okinawa. Known for its calm, shallow waters and well-maintained facilities, it is a favored spot for swimming, barbecues, and family outings. As the name suggests, it offers dramatic, unobstructed views of the sun setting over the ocean.
