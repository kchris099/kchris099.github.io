Destination Guide — Chubu

Explore Hakuba

A Brief History

Historically a quiet agricultural and trading post on the Salt Road through the Japanese Alps, Hakuba remained isolated until the post-war skiing boom. The 1998 Winter Olympics propelled the remote valley onto the global stage, solidifying its status as a winter sports hub. Visitors today can clearly trace the intricate layers of this unique heritage throughout the preserved streets and monuments.

Top Sights & Activities

Hakuba Ski Jumping Stadium

The Hakuba Ski Jumping Stadium is a must-visit spot in Hakuba Village. The Red Soba Flower Festival held between September and October offers the perfect opportunity to capture the beauty of buckwheat flowers in bloom. These flowers eventually become buckwheat noodles, adding an interesting cultural aspect to the visit. The stadium holds historical significance as it was used for Olympic events in 1998, and visitors can still feel the Olympic spirit there.

Free

Hakuba Happo Onsen (Hot Spring)

Hakuba Happo Onsen is one of the six sources in Yunosato. It's a bit pricey at 850 yen for adults, but visitors can enjoy both indoor and outdoor baths. The facility may feel relatively new and small, with only one indoor and one outdoor bath available. Despite its size, the onsen offers excellent views of the mountains from the outdoor pool. Visitors can also purchase or rent towels if needed.

Free

snow peak Land Station Hakuba

snow peak Land Station Hakuba is a documented natural feature in Hakuba, Japan, listed in regional geographic and conservation records for its geological formation and protected status. The site is accessible via marked trails and is open to visitors during daylight hours. Travellers follow the principal path from the trailhead and combine the visit with nearby viewpoints and forest walks.

Free

Oide Park

Nestled in the picturesque Hakuba village, Oide Park is a hidden gem renowned for its cherry blossom views. While this area may not boast an abundance of sakura trees, it compensates with backdrops of the snow-capped Japanese Alps and the serene Hime River flowing gracefully nearby. Ideal for hanami enthusiasts, visitors can enjoy a delightful variety of blooming trees at different stages, creating a vibrant tapestry of colors.

Free

Rhythm Wadano, Hakuba

Rhythm Japan Hakuba is a top choice for ski and snowboard equipment rental in Hakuba. The staff are praised for their helpfulness and knowledge, ensuring that customers are well-equipped and reassured before hitting the slopes. While some holiday workers may lack expertise, the overall service is efficient and friendly. Customers appreciate the convenience of shuttle pick-up/drop-off and gear collection from accommodations.

Free

Hakuba Happo Onsen Obinata no yu

Hakuba Happo Onsen Obinata no yu is a charming onsen located within the Hakuba Highland Hotel, offering a serene and natural setting for visitors to enjoy. The onsen's hot spring water, which originates from the Himekawa River nearby, is known for its gentle and slightly alkaline properties that are beneficial to the skin. Guests can bask in the romantic and spacious outdoor setup while immersing themselves in the rejuvenating waters.

Free

Happo pond

Happo Ike pond is a popular tourist attraction located on the ridge trail leading to Mt. Karamatsu. The lake offers reflections of the surrounding peaks, making it a sight to behold on clear days. Tourists can reach the lake by taking a gondola and lift to Happo Sanso, followed by traversing through forests and mountain paths. The lake sits at an elevation of 2080m and during winter, it freezes over turning into a walkable terrain.

Free

Kurobishidaira

Kurobishidaira sits within the Happo pond area of Hakuba, documented in local heritage registers for its civic or cultural role. Visitors approach from the main access road and combine the visit with neighbouring heritage buildings and public spaces in the district.

Free

Mount Shirouma

Mount Shirouma, standing tall at 2,932 meters (9,620 feet), is a gem nestled in the Hakuba section of the Hida Mountains. Renowned as one of Japan's top climbing destinations, this majestic peak boasts steep trails that lead adventurers through lush greenery during summer and year-round snow fields in its deep-snow gorge.

Free

Hakuba Goryu Alpine Botanical Gardens

The Hakuba Goryu Alpine Botanical Gardens are located high on the slopes of the Hakuba ski resort area, accessible via a scenic gondola ride. The gardens showcase hundreds of rare and colorful alpine plant species native to the Japanese Alps and other mountainous regions. The elevated location also provides expansive views of the rugged Northern Alps.
