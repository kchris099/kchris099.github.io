Japan

Court rule in Kyoto and shogunal power in Edo framed Japanese politics for centuries before the Meiji Restoration of 1868 and reconstruction after 1945. Castle towns, shrine precincts, merchant streets, and treaty-era ports are the places where that shift from samurai government to industrial state is easiest to read.
