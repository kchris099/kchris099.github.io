Explore Kagoshima

A Brief History

Established as a regional settlement beyond an older fortified core, the city developed through industrialization in the late 19th century, post-war reconstruction, and modern civic expansion that reshaped its public spaces, markets, and surrounding landscape. Restored religious buildings, civic buildings, and natural features now define the contemporary visitor experience.

Sengan-en

Sengan-en is a historic Japanese garden and former residence of a feudal lord, showcasing traditional design and volcano views. The garden was created over 300 years ago by the Shimadzu clan, incorporating the surrounding landscape into its layout. travellers can explore bamboo groves, a scenic waterfall, and a serene pond. This picturesque setting was once used for famous poem-composing garden parties hosted by the Shimadzu family.

Free

Former Kagoshima Spinning Mill Engineer's Residence

Shuseikan Foreign Engineers' Residence (1867) is a recreated Western building that served as the residence for English individuals 150 years ago. It offers a glimpse into the historical impact of the modernizing Shimazu clan and their interactions with the West. The house, though small, is an important piece of Japan's modernization history and showcases colonial architecture. Inside, preserved rooms depict the lifestyle of foreign engineers from that era.

Free

Yunohira Observation Deck

Nestled at an impressive 373 meters above sea level on the North Peak of Sakurajima, the Yunohira Observation Deck stands as the highest publicly accessible vantage point on this volcano. travellers are treated to panoramic views that encompass the rugged lava fields, lush wooded slopes of Mount Sakurajima, and the sparkling expanse of Kagoshima Bay. On clear days, soak in unobstructed sights stretching across Kinko Bay and Kagoshima City.

Free

Arimura Lava Observation Deck

Located at the foot of Minamidake on the volcanic island of Sakurajima, the Arimura Lava Observation Deck offers views of the surrounding mountains and landscapes. travellers can explore the Taisho lava field and admire black pines in the area. The observatory provides a lesser-known perspective of Sakurajima and showcases intriguing geological features.

Free

Sakurajima

Sakurajima, an active stratovolcano in Kagoshima, Japan, was once an island until a massive 1914 eruption connected it to the Osumi peninsula. This volcano has erupted around 30 times throughout history and continues to emit steam and ash. With ties to Naples, Italy as its sister city, Kagoshima is often referred to as the 'Naples of the Orient.' The region enjoys a balmy climate most of the year.

Free

Marine Port Kagoshima Square.

Marine Port is a well-maintained and clean port with helpful English maps available at the information desk. While walking from the port to the nearest JR line may take some time, it offers a good view of Sakurajima and beautiful flowers along the way. travellers also have the opportunity to interact with friendly high school students selling matcha mochi, who may even be filming for their local TV station.

Free

Kagoshima City Aquarium

Kagoshima City Aquarium, also known as Suizoku-Kan Ioworld, is a popular attraction located near downtown and next to the Sakurajima Ferry Terminal. The aquarium showcases a diverse range of marine life native to Kagoshima Prefecture, including stingrays, bluefin tuna, Japanese anchovy, a whale shark, squid, octopuses, and the Japanese giant crab. travellers can also enjoy dolphin shows and feedings during their visit.

Free

Main Gate of Tsurumaru Castle

The Main Gate of Tsurumaru Castle in Kagoshima, Japan, has been partially reconstructed, with the moat and outer wall being the only original sections that remain. travellers can enter through the gate and explore the surrounding area, including a building uphill. The traditional architecture showcases Japanese construction at its . Additionally, there is a view of Sakurajima from this vantage point.

Free

Shiroyama Park Observation Deck

Perched atop Shiroyama Park, the Observation Deck provides panoramic views of Kagoshima city and its bay, as well as the active Sakurajima volcano. This popular spot is particularly enchanting after sunset when the city lights create a soothing ambiance. Accessible via the Kagoshima City View bus or by taxi, it's also within walking distance from the city center. The deck offers a picturesque vantage point to admire the natural beauty and urban landscape of Kagoshima.

Free

Terukuni Shrine

Terukuni Shrine is a 19th-century Shinto shrine located in Kagoshima. It is known for its distinctive feature, a tree that has been pruned into the shape of a flying bird. The shrine's giant white torii makes it easily recognizable. Despite being relatively young compared to other temples, having been founded in 1864, the shrine has faced damage from historical events such as the Satsuma Rebellion and World War II air raids.
