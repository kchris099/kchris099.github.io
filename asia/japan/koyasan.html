Koyasan

Koyasan was founded in 816 by the monk Kukai (Kobo Daishi) as the mountain headquarters of Shingon Buddhism. Temple halls, monastic lodgings (shukubo), and the vast Okunoin cemetery grew around his mausoleum over twelve centuries. Pilgrims climb or ride to the plateau above Wakayama. UNESCO listing covers the sacred sites. Lantern-lit paths through cedar groves and dawn services in temple lodgings still organise life on the mountain.

Daimon Gate

Daimon Gate, the grand entrance to Mt. Koya, is a towering structure adorned with carved guardians that dates back to the 11th century. Although it was once lost in a fire, it was reconstructed in 1705 and has stood tall ever since. Standing at an height of 25 meters and overlooking the surrounding mountains, this gate offers views especially during sunset when the vermilion surfaces gleam in the fading light.
