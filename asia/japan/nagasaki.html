Nagasaki

Serving as Japan's sole window to the West during centuries of national isolation, Nagasaki absorbed profound Portuguese, Dutch, and Chinese influences. The catastrophic atomic bombing in 1945 fundamentally redefined the city's legacy toward global peace and reconstruction. Visitors today can clearly trace the detailed layers of this unique heritage throughout the preserved streets and monuments.

Mt. Inasa Overlook

Mount Inasa rises west of central Nagasaki and can be reached by ropeway, bus, road or walking route. A summit observation area overlooks the harbor and shipyards in daylight. After dark, city lights trace the narrow valleys and waterfront districts. Weather strongly affects visibility, but the surrounding topography remains clear even without ranking the night view against other cities.

Glover Garden

On Minami-Yamate hill, former foreign residences recall Nagasaki's treaty-port trade years above the harbor. Paths and lawns link the historic houses with overlooks toward the port. Shinchi Chinatown and Dejima sit in the same broader story of overseas contact below the slope.

03 AdmissionFree

Megane Bridge

Chinese monk Mokusunyo built Megane Bridge across Nagasaki's Nakashima River in 1634. Its two stone arches and their reflections form the shape of spectacles that gave the bridge its nickname. Floods damaged the structure, but reconstruction reused recovered stones, and stepping-stone paths along the river provide close views of the masonry and neighboring historic bridges.

32.7471° N / 129.8801° E

04 AdmissionFree

Suwa Shrine

Nagasaki's Suwa Shrine occupies a forested hillside reached by a long staircase from the city. Founded in the early Edo period, it became a major center for Shinto worship after years of Christian influence and suppression in the region. The Nagasaki Kunchi festival is closely associated with the shrine, bringing neighborhood performances and large crowds each October.

32.7543° N / 129.8819° E

05 AdmissionFree

Peace Park Nagasaki

Nagasaki Peace Park was laid out to commemorate the atomic bombing of 9 August 1945 and covers about 18.6 hectares. Zones for hope, prayer, learning, sport, and plaza gathering organise sculptures, fountains, and lawns. The Peace Statue and nearby hypocentre markers draw remembrance ceremonies each August.

06 AdmissionFree

Peace Statue

Kitamura Seibo's bronze figure marks the tenth anniversary of the atomic bombing at the far end of Peace Park. Other nations' gifts for peace stand nearby in the same open plaza. The raised right hand and extended left arm form the statue's familiar silhouette.

32.7769° N / 129.8640° E

07 AdmissionFree

Hypocenter Park

A damaged pillar from Urakami Cathedral stands beside the hypocenter marker in this small memorial park. Under glass, broken roof tiles, bricks, and glass from the blast remain visible in a cut of soil. The ground-zero reading stays literal and quiet.

32.7732° N / 129.8631° E

08 AdmissionFree

Atomic Bomb Hypocenter Monument

A simple black stone marker identifies the hypocenter of the atomic bomb exploded over Nagasaki on August 9, 1945. Concentric paving and nearby remains of Urakami Cathedral help indicate the physical center of destruction, while Peace Park and the Atomic Bomb Museum lie close by as separate sites.

32.7737° N / 129.8632° E

09 AdmissionFree

Urakami Cathedral

Urakami Cathedral, a large Catholic church in Nagasaki, was reconstructed in 1959 after being obliterated by an atomic bomb during World War II. The parishioners of Urakami, known for the 'Miracle in the East,' defied a 250-year ban on Christianity to build this cathedral with the help of a French priest. Despite being just 500 meters from the bomb's hypocenter, it was completely destroyed.

32.7762° N / 129.8684° E

10 AdmissionFree

Sanno Shrine one-legged Torii Gate

Sanno Shrine's one-legged torii survived the 1945 atomic bombing of Nagasaki when the blast sheared away one pillar yet left the other standing. The damaged gate remains in place as a physical record of that morning. Shrine grounds around it continue ordinary worship while the tilted lintel draws those studying the city's wartime scars.

32.7675° N / 129.8673° E

Nagasaki Penguin Aquarium

Nagasaki Penguin Aquarium focuses on penguin biology and conservation, housing several species in indoor pools and a natural-seawater beach area. Displays also cover local aquatic life, fish, and coastal ecosystems, while scheduled feeding or walking programs depend on animal welfare and weather.
