Explore Himeji

A Brief History

Himeji grew below Himeji Castle, a hilltop fortress largely completed in 1609 for Tokugawa-era lord Ikeda Terumasa. The white plaster and wooden keep survived wars and earthquakes with careful maintenance. The castle was inscribed as a UNESCO World Heritage site in 1993 as one of Japan's surviving feudal complexes. The city lies on the Sanyo Shinkansen route between Osaka and Okayama in Hyogo Prefecture.

Top Sights & Activities

¥500

Himeji Castle

This hilltop castle in Hyōgo Prefecture survives as a rare intact example of feudal Japanese fortification, with 83 buildings linked by walls and gates. White plaster walls earned it the nicknames White Heron or White Egret Castle. The main keep dates to the early 1600s and underwent major restoration before reopening in 2015.

Free

Himeji Park

Parkland rings the castle moats and outer walls where samurai quarters once stood. Cherry trees line paths that lead to viewpoints of the white keep and stone ramparts. The grounds are popular for hanami picnics in spring and serve as a buffer between the monument and the modern city.

¥1000

Koko-en Garden

Opened in 1992 for Himeji's centennial, this complex contains nine walled gardens in Edo-period styles on the site of former samurai mansions. Ponds, tea houses, and bamboo groves sit a short walk south of the castle. Combined tickets with the castle are available at the garden entrance.

Free

Koko-en

Koko-en Gardens, located near Himeji Castle in Japan, is a collection of nine themed traditional Japanese gardens. These gardens were built to commemorate the 100th anniversary of the founding of the Himeji municipality. travellers can enjoy a tranquil vibe as they stroll through the 3.5-hectare area featuring water features and seasonal plants. The garden also includes a tea house where traditional Japanese tea ceremonies are held earlier in the day.

Free

Engyōji

Engyoji, a large Buddhist temple complex founded in 966, is situated on the mountaintop of Mount Shosha, surrounded by lush forest. The area offers a serene hiking trail and boasts impressive temple structures dating back over 1000 years. Known as the 'Hieizan of the West,' Engyoji features numerous halls and pagodas amidst verdant greenery.

Free

HIMEJI STATION

Himeji Station is a bustling hub for both locals and tourists, conveniently located just a 20-minute walk from the Himeji Castle. The Underground Mall Grand Festa offers a vast shopping experience with specialty stores featuring local products, apparel, cosmetics, sake, and traditional sweets. Nearby, Kokoen Garden comprises nine interconnected Edo-era style gardens with ponds, waterfalls, and tea houses.

Free

Himeji Central Park

Himeji Central Park, located in Himeji, Japan, offers a diverse range of attractions for travellers. The park features a scenic drive-through safari park where guests can observe over 1,200 animals and birds from different parts of the world. Additionally, there is an amusement park with thrilling rides such as a Ferris wheel and roller coasters. During the summer months, travellers can cool off in the pool while in winter they can enjoy ice-skating at the park's rink.

Free

Himeji City Aquarium

Himeji City Aquarium, situated in Tegarayama Central Park, offers a delightful experience for travelers interested in marine life. The small mountaintop aquarium features both indoor and outdoor exhibits, showcasing a diverse range of aquatic species including sea turtles, penguins, and jellyfish. Families with children can enjoy the touch pool and interactive displays while learning about different marine creatures. Additionally, the aquarium provides hands-on activities for younger travellers.

Free

Hiromine Shrine

Nestled in the northern part of Himeji city near Mt. Hiromine, Hiromine Shrine boasts a rich history dating back to 733 when it was established by scholar Makibi Kibi. The shrine is dedicated to Susanowo-no-mikoto, the first God in Japanese culture. travellers can enjoy a serene atmosphere and take in panoramic views from Himeji Castle to the Seto Inland Sea.

Free

Harima-no-Kuni Sohsha-Gomon shrine (Old Prefectural Shrine)

Harima no Kuni Sōsha Shrine, located southeast of Himeji Castle, has a rich history dating back to the year 787 when the Hyozu god was moved to Onoe. The shrine's Mitsuyama-taisai festival is a significant cultural event held every 20 years since 1533, featuring rituals and traditional clothing. travellers can admire the beautiful red torii gate and explore the large grounds with smaller torii gates.

Free

Ōtemae Park

Ōtemae Park is a large rectangular paved open space that serves as a community gathering spot for various events. It features some trees, benches, and restrooms, making it more of a public common space than a traditional park. The park hosts a flea market with unique items like flintlock pistols and katanas, making it an interesting stop for travellers to Himeji Castle.

Free

Ikarugadera

The Remains of Aboshi Jinya offer a fascinating glimpse into the region's rich history, which dates back to ancient times when it served as a vital hub for water transport, particularly for timber from upstream. During the Edo period, this area was under the jurisdiction of the Himeji domain but later transitioned to the control of the Kyogoku family from Tatsuno domain. Following their relocation to Marugame, ownership shifted once again to the Marugame clan.
